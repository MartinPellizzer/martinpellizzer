Bromus moesiacus

Bromus moesiacus, commonly known as the Moesia grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.

Bromus moesiacus is an important species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
