Bignonia botryoides

Bignonia botryoides, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental due to its lush foliage.

Bignonia botryoides is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other ornamental and some economically important plants.
