Bromus ciliatus var. coloradensis

Bromus ciliatus var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and agricultural fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish spikelets.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
