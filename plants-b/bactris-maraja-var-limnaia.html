Bactris maraja var. limnaia

Bactris maraja var. limnaia, commonly known as the Marajoa palm, is a species native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, gray trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or flooded areas.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and potential for sustainable use in local communities.
