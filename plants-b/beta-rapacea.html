Beta rapacea

Beta rapacea, commonly known as莙菜, is a hardy perennial plant native to Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, leafy stems and rosette-like growth habit. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C. Its leaves are typically harvested during the cooler months, making it a valuable crop in temperate climates.

Beta rapacea is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its resilience and adaptability.
