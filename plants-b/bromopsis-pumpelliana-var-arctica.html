Bromopsis pumpelliana var. arctica

Bromopsis pumpelliana var. arctica, commonly known as the Arctic rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, dark green, succulent leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the short summer season. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Bromopsis pumpelliana var. arctica plays an important role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
