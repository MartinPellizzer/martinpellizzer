Bothriochloa barbinodis

Bothriochloa barbinodis, commonly known as the barbed nodding grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that nod downwards. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Bothriochloa barbinodis is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
