Berberis umbratica

Berberis umbratica, commonly known as the shade barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and forested areas, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
