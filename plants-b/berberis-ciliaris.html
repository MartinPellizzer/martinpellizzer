Berberis ciliaris

Berberis ciliaris, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
