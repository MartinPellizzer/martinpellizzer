Biscutella didyma var. suaveolens

Biscutella didyma var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweetroot or sweetbiscuit plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often arranged in clusters along the stem. The plant has a distinctive, aromatic root system that is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
