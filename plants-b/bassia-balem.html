Bassia balem

Bassia balem, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Bassia balem is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
