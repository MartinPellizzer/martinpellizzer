Baccharis ptarmicifolia

Baccharis ptarmicifolia, commonly known as greenbriar baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats. Its woody stems and persistent foliage make it a hardy species well adapted to arid environments.

Baccharis ptarmicifolia is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
