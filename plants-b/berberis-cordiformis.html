Berberis cordiformis

Berberis cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves that are ovate to cordate in shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Berberis cordiformis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional medicine.
