Bauhinia hymenaeifolia

Bauhinia hymenaeifolia, commonly known as the velvetwood or African rosewood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, papery pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its foliage. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its wood is highly valued for its density and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.

Bauhinia hymenaeifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
