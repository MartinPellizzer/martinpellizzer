Bupleurum kurdicum

Bupleurum kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
