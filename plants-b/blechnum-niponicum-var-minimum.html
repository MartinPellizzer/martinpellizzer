Blechnum niponicum var. minimum

Blechnum niponicum var. minimum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern native to Japan.

It features finely divided, arching fronds with a dark green hue and a velvety texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, typical of the fern life cycle.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
