Bromopsis gorodkovae

Bromopsis gorodkovae, commonly known as the Gorodkov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silver-blue hue that adds visual interest to its habitat.

Bromopsis gorodkovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
