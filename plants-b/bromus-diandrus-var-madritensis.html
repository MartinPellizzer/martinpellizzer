Bromus diandrus var. madritensis

Bromus diandrus var. madritensis, commonly known as the Madritense brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
