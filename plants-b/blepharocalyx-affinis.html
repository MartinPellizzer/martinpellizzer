Blepharocalyx affinis

Blepharocalyx affinis, commonly known as the white-flowered arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The spathe is pale white or cream, contrasting with the deep green leaves, making it visually striking.
