Banksia kippistiana

Banksia kippistiana, commonly known as the Kippist's banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a leathery texture, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought and fire.

Banksia kippistiana plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for native pollinators.
