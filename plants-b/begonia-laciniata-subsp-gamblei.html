Begonia laciniata subsp. gamblei

Begonia laciniata subsp.

gamblei, commonly known as the Gambler's begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate, lobed patterns.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
