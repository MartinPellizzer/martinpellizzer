Brachistus escuintlensis

Brachistus escuintlensis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its rapid growth and showy blooms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Brachistus escuintlensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
