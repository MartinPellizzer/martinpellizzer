Boehmeria phyllostachya f. glabrior

Boehmeria phyllostachya f. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf nettle or Chinese nettle, is a variant of the Boehmeria phyllostachya species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions of Asia.

This plant is valued for its fibrous stalks, which are used in traditional textile production and papermaking.
