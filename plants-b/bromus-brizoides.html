Bromus brizoides

Bromus brizoides, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a common pioneer species in ecological succession.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
