Baptisia × macilenta

Baptisia × macilenta, commonly known as the pink false indigo, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, sparsely branched inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces unique, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Baptisia × macilenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
