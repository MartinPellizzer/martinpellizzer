Biscutella ciliata var. genuina

Biscutella ciliata var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in raceme-like clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.
