Boltonia glastifolia

Boltonia glastifolia, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a tall, upright stem with large, showy white to pale pink flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are hairy and often have a slightly serrated edge. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Boltonia glastifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
