Bactris sororopanae

Bactris sororopanae, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the palm is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Bactris sororopanae plays an important role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat.
