Babingtonia corynophylla

Babingtonia corynophylla, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and is characterized by its bright yellow floral displays. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Babingtonia corynophylla is also recognized as the state floral emblem of Western Australia.
