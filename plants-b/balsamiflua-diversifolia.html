Balsamiflua diversifolia

Balsamiflua diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse-leaved balsam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that vary in color from light green to deep emerald. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
