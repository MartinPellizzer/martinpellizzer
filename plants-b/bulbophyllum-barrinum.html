Bulbophyllum barrinum

Bulbophyllum barrinum, commonly known as the Barrin's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often arranged in a raceme.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
