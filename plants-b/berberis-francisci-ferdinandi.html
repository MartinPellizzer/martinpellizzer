Berberis francisci-ferdinandi

Berberis francisci-ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny, thorned stems and small, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and distinctive form.
