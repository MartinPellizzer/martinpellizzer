Bellardia latifolia

Bellardia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellardia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. It is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes, adapting well to saline environments.

Bellardia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
