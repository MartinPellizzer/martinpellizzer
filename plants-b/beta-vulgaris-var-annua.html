Beta vulgaris var. annua

Beta vulgaris var. annua, commonly known as sugar beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store high concentrations of sucrose. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a white, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

The botanical features of sugar beet make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
