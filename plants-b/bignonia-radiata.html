Bignonia radiata

Bignonia radiata, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia radiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
