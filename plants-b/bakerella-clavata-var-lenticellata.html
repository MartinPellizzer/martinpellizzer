Bakerella clavata var. lenticellata

Bakerella clavata var. lenticellata, commonly known as the smoothleaf bakerella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds.

Bakerella clavata var. lenticellata is typically found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
