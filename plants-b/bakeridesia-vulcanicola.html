Bakeridesia vulcanicola

Bakeridesia vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic rock fern, is a rare plant species adapted to thrive in extreme volcanic environments.

It possesses a robust, fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in nutrient-poor, rocky soils. The plant's leaves are narrow and serrated, designed to withstand high temperatures and low moisture levels. Bakeridesia vulcanicola reproduces through spores, which are dispersed by wind and can germinate in newly cooled lava flows.

Its unique adaptations make it a valuable indicator of volcanic activity and soil recovery in post-eruption landscapes.
