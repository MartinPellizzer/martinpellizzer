Berberis bouffordii

Berberis bouffordii, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native plant species.
