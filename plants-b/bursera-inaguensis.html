Bursera inaguensis

Bursera inaguensis, commonly known as the Inagua bursera, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly the Bahamas and Turks and Caicos.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically has a rounded crown, smooth gray bark, and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by three-lobed, woody fruits containing three seeds.

This species is adapted to coastal and limestone habitats, showing resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
