Bulbophyllum atrosanguineum

Bulbophyllum atrosanguineum, commonly known as the black blood orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red to black floral bracts, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink and white.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
