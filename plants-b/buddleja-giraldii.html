Buddleja giraldii

Buddleja giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
