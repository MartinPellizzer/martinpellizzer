Blumea scapigera

Blumea scapigera, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in clusters, and the leaves are often lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Blumea scapigera is often found in moist, forested areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
