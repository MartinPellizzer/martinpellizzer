Bidens tereticaulis var. indivisa

Bidens tereticaulis var. indivisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads make it visually distinct.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
