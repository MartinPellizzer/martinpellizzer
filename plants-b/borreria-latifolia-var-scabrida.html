Borreria latifolia var. scabrida

Borreria latifolia var. scabrida, commonly known as the roughleaf borreria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
