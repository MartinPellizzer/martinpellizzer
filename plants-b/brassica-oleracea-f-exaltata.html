Brassica oleracea f. exaltata

Brassica oleracea f. exaltata, commonly known as the Romanesco broccoli, is a cultivar of the wild cabbage species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of tightly packed, brightly colored, and slightly spiral-arranged florets. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. Its botanical features include thick, dark green leaves and a robust, edible head that develops from the terminal flower cluster.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and nutritional content, making it a popular ornamental and culinary crop.
