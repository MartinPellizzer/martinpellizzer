Balaustion tangerinum

Balaustion tangerinum, commonly known as the tangerine tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, citrus-like fruits containing segmented, juicy pulp. Its botanical features include a thick, aromatic bark and a root system adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
