Brunellia comocladiifolia subsp. domingensis

Brunellia comocladiifolia subsp.

domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic. It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Rubiaceae family.
