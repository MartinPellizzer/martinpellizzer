Boronia ericifolia

Boronia ericifolia, commonly known as the heath boronia, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

Boronia ericifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
