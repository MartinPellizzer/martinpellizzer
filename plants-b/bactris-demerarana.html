Bactris demerarana

Bactris demerarana, commonly known as the Paraguay wax palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits containing edible, fleshy seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
