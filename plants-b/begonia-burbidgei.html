Begonia burbidgei

Begonia burbidgei, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
