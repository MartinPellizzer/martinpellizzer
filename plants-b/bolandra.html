Bolandra

Bolandra, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Bolandra species are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The leaves are usually grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

They are often cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
