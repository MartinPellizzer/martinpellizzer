Boronella crassifolia

Boronella crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf boronella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Boronella crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
