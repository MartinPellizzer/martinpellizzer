Besleria hirsuta

Besleria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy besleria, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and velvety, while its stems are often hairy, giving it its common name.

Besleria hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
