Bellis perennis

Bellis perennis, commonly known as the common daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features rosettes of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disk. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas due to its hardy nature.

Bellis perennis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
