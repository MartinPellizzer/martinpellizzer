Begonia minissima

Begonia minissima, commonly known as the miniature begonia, is a small, compact plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for container gardening and indoor spaces.
