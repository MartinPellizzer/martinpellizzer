Bonnaya milindii

Bonnaya milindii, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and produces elliptical, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Bonnaya milindii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
