Bromus erectus var. longiflorus

Bromus erectus var. longiflorus, commonly known as the tall floret brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, slender leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with long, slender florets, which are often tinged with purple or pink. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
