Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp. ornans

Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp.

ornans, commonly known as the ornate bromus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often exhibit a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
