Barbacenia equisetoides

Barbacenia equisetoides, commonly known as the flame bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often a deep green color.

Barbacenia equisetoides is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, requiring full sun and well-drained soil to thrive.
