Boehmeria diversifolia

Boehmeria diversifolia, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or woolly flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color.

Boehmeria diversifolia is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in traditional textiles and papermaking.
