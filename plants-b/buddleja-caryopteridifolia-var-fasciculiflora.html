Buddleja caryopteridifolia var. fasciculiflora

Buddleja caryopteridifolia var. fasciculiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buddleja, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

This variety is relatively hardy and suitable for garden use in temperate climates.
