Brachypodium ramosum subsp. boissieri

Brachypodium ramosum subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch near the base.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brownish flowers.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
