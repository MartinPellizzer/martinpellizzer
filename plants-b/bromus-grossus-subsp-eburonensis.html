Bromus grossus subsp. eburonensis

Bromus grossus subsp.

eburonensis, commonly known as the Eburon grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and it is known for its ability to thrive in wet or semi-aquatic environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and adaptation to specific soil conditions.
