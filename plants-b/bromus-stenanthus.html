Bromus stenanthus

Bromus stenanthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a characteristic feature of the grass family.

Bromus stenanthus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
