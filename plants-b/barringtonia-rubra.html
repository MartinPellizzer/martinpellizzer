Barringtonia rubra

Barringtonia rubra, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a distinctive, woody fruit that splits open to release its seeds. Its leaves are broad and palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Barringtonia rubra is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical landscapes.
