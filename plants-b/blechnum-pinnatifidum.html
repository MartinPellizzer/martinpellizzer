Blechnum pinnatifidum

Blechnum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
