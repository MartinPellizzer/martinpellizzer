Billotia marginata var. angustifolia

Billotia marginata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed billotia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
