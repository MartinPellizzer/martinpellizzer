Bryophyllum laetivirens

Bryophyllum laetivirens, commonly known as the "joyful green" or "silver leaf plant," is a species of succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the spring and summer months. Native to South Africa, it is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
