Bulbophyllum ophiuchus var. ankaizinense

Bulbophyllum ophiuchus var. ankaizinense, commonly known as the Ankaizinense Bulbophyllum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its elongated, pseudobulb-like stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in montane forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
