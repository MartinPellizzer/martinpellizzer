Balsamina cristata

Balsamina cristata, commonly known as the crested balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often crinkled leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Balsamina cristata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
