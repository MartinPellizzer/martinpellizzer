Brachypodium distachyon var. platystachyum

Brachypodium distachyon var. platystachyum, commonly known as the broad-stalked false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic similarity to the model plant Brachypodium distachyon.
