Brachionidium ballatrix

Brachionidium ballatrix, commonly known as the ballatrix rotifer, is a microscopic aquatic organism belonging to the phylum Rotifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical body and a crown of cilia used for locomotion and feeding. The species is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Brachionidium ballatrix plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by feeding on algae and detritus, thus contributing to nutrient cycling.

Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality in ecological studies.
