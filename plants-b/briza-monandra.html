Briza monandra

Briza monandra, commonly known as the common goat's beard, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Briza monandra is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
