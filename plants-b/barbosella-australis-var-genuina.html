Barbosella australis var. genuina

Barbosella australis var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Barbosella australis var. genuina is an important component of coastal and inland shrubland ecosystems.
