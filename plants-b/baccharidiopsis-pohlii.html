Baccharidiopsis pohlii

Baccharidiopsis pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Baccharidiopsis pohlii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in local horticulture for its ornamental value.
