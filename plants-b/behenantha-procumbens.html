Behenantha procumbens

Behenantha procumbens, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
