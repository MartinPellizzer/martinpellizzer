Babiana fimbriata

Babiana fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed babiana, is a bulbous geophyte native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Babiana fimbriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
