Bromus ciliatus var. denudatus

Bromus ciliatus var. denudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often reddish at the base and have a distinctive, narrow shape.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
