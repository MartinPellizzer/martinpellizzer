Berberis vulgaris f. globularis

Berberis vulgaris f. globularis, commonly known as the globular barberry, is a cultivar of the barberry plant characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

It features small, spiny leaves that are typically a deep green, providing an ornamental appearance in gardens. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a hedge due to its dense foliage and hardy nature.

This variety is native to Europe and thrives in full sun to partial shade with well-drained soil.
