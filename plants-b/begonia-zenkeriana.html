Begonia zenkeriana

Begonia zenkeriana, commonly known as the Zenker's begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
