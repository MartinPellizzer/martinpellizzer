Baphia ovata

Baphia ovata, commonly known as the African butterbean tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Baphia ovata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal drought conditions.
