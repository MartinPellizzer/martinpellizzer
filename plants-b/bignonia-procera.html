Bignonia procera

Bignonia procera, commonly known as the climbing trumpetflower, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Bignonia procera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
