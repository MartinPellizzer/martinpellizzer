Berberis aristeguietae

Berberis aristeguietae, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes of Chile and Argentina.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
