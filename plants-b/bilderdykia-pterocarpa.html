Bilderdykia pterocarpa

Bilderdykia pterocarpa, commonly known as the African candlewood, is a unique shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, candle-like, woody fruits that remain on the plant for an extended period. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The botanical features of Bilderdykia pterocarpa make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
