Banisteria lobulata

Banisteria lobulata, commonly known as the flame bean, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardens and botanical collections.
