Berberis dictyota

Berberis dictyota, commonly known as the creeping barberry, is a shrub native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and structural form.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
