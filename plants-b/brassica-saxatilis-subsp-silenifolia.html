Brassica saxatilis subsp. silenifolia

Brassica saxatilis subsp.

silenifolia, commonly known as the rock cabbage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy, with a basal rosette structure. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in a raceme, characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
