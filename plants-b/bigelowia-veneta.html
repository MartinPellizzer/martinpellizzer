Bigelowia veneta

Bigelowia veneta, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy inflorescences.
