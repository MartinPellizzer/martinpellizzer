Brickellia peninsularis var. typica

Brickellia peninsularis var. typica, commonly known as the Peninsular brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its drought tolerance.

Brickellia peninsularis var. typica is often found in rocky slopes and desert scrub habitats.
