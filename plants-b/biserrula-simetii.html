Biserrula simetii

Biserrula simetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf biserrula, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control. Its seeds are edible and have been used traditionally by indigenous communities.

Biserrula simetii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
