Benzoin megaphyllum

Benzoin megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant benzoin, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces aromatic resin, which is used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
