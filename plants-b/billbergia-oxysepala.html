Billbergia oxysepala

Billbergia oxysepala, commonly known as the silver billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like rosette of narrow, silver-blue leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia oxysepala is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
