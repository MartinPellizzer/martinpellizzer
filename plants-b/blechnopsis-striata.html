Blechnopsis striata

Blechnopsis striata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Blechnopsis striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
