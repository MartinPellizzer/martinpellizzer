Begonia engleriana

Begonia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
