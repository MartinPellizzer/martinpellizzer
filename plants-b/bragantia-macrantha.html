Bragantia macrantha

Bragantia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered bracken, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of large, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets.

Bragantia macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
