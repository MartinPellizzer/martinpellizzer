Bumelia tortuosa

Bumelia tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Bumelia tortuosa is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and along stream banks.
