Babiana

Babiana, commonly known as the rain lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often colorful, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. Babiana species are typically herbaceous perennials with narrow, grass-like leaves. They thrive in well-drained soils and are adapted to seasonal drought conditions.

The genus includes over 50 species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
