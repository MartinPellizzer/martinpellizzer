Biota orientalis var. fernandiana

Biota orientalis var. fernandiana, commonly known as the fern-like balm of Gilead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and delicate blooms.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties.
