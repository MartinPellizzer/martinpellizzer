Bauhinia wituensis

Bauhinia wituensis, commonly known as the Witu bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Bauhinia wituensis is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
