Brachylaena argentea

Brachylaena argentea, commonly known as silver brachylaena, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by papery, lantern-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and landscaping.
