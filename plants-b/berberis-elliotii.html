Berberis elliotii

Berberis elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Berberis elliotii is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
