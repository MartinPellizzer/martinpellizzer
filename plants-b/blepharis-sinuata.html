Blepharis sinuata

Blepharis sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Blepharis sinuata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
