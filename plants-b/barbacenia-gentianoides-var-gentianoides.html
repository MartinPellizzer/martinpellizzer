Barbacenia gentianoides var. gentianoides

Barbacenia gentianoides var. gentianoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Barbacenia gentianoides var. gentianoides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
