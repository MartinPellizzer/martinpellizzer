Bulbostylis cupricola

Bulbostylis cupricola, commonly known as the copperleaf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swamps. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Bulbostylis cupricola is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
