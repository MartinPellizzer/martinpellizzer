Begonia salvadorensis

Begonia salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran begonia, is a flowering plant native to El Salvador.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the showy bracts attract pollinators and enhance its visual appeal.
