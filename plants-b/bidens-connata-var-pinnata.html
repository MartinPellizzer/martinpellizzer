Bidens connata var. pinnata

Bidens connata var. pinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and alternate along the stem, with a serrated margin.

Bidens connata var. pinnata is often found in disturbed soils and moist habitats, such as roadsides and wetlands.
