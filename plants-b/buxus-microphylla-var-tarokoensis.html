Buxus microphylla var. tarokoensis

Buxus microphylla var. tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for topiary and formal garden designs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its neat appearance and ability to maintain shape with regular pruning.
