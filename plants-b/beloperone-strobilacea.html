Beloperone strobilacea

Beloperone strobilacea, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, compact inflorescence. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns that are adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
