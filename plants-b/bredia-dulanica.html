Bredia dulanica

Bredia dulanica, commonly known as the red-flowered bredia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. Bredia dulanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
