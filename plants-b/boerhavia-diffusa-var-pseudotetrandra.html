Boerhavia diffusa var. pseudotetrandra

Boerhavia diffusa var. pseudotetrandra, commonly known as sandmat or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and capable of producing a mucilaginous substance when crushed, which is used in traditional medicine.
