Buddleja elegans subsp. angustata

Buddleja elegans subsp.

angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false goatweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. Buddleja elegans subsp.

angustata is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
