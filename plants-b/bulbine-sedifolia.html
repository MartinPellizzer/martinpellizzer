Bulbine sedifolia

Bulbine sedifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bulbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Bulbine in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae, which includes many succulent species. The plant features fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, a characteristic known as farina. It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Bulbine sedifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
