Bromus madritensis var. rubens

Bromus madritensis var. rubens, commonly known as the red brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish hues in its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
