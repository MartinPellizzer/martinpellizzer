Bambusa ontakensis

Bambusa ontakensis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Bambusa ontakensis is valued for its durable and lightweight material, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
