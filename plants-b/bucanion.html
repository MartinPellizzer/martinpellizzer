Bucanion

Bucanion, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in spikes. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a peppery taste.

Bucanion is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
