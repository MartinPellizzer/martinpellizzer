Berberis revoluta

Berberis revoluta, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves with reddish veins and produces bright orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature. It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and climates.

However, it has become invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native plants.
