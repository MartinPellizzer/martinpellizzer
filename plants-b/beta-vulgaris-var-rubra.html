Beta vulgaris var. rubra

Beta vulgaris var. rubra, commonly known as red beet or chard, is a cultivar of the beetroot plant characterized by its vibrant red leaves and edible roots.

The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like iron and magnesium. The roots, which are typically reddish in color, are used in various culinary preparations.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice in gardens.
