Bromus macranthos var. dusenii

Bromus macranthos var. dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
