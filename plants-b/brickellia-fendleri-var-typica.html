Brickellia fendleri var. typica

Brickellia fendleri var. typica, commonly known as desert brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small perennial plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.

Brickellia fendleri var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
