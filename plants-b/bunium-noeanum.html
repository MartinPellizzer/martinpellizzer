Bunium noeanum

Bunium noeanum, commonly known as the Crimean fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its seeds are small and oval, with a distinctive aroma similar to fennel.

Bunium noeanum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
