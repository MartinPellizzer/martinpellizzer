Burmannia longifolia

Burmannia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved burmannia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its small, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are usually elongated and lance-shaped, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Burmannia longifolia is noted for its short life cycle and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
