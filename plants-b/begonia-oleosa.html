Begonia oleosa

Begonia oleosa, commonly known as the oily begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a prominent yellow center. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
