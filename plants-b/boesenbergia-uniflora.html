Boesenbergia uniflora

Boesenbergia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, smooth, and often have a mild ginger scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
