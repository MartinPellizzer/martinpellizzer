Begonia manicata

Begonia manicata, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and low-maintenance foliage.
