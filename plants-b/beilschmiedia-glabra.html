Beilschmiedia glabra

Beilschmiedia glabra, commonly known as the smooth beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible, black, fleshy fruits that are important for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
