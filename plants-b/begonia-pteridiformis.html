Begonia pteridiformis

Begonia pteridiformis, commonly known as the fern-like begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia pteridiformis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
