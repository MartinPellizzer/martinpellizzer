Betula pendula f. elegans

Betula pendula f. elegans, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance.

It is a naturally occurring form of the European white poplar, belonging to the Betulaceae family. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate pollution make it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
