Biscutella sclerocarpa

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, while its fruit is a capsule containing small, hard seeds.

Biscutella sclerocarpa is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
