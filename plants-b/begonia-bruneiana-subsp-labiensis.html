Begonia bruneiana subsp. labiensis

Begonia bruneiana subsp.

labiensis, commonly known as the Labienus begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep red or maroon coloration.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
