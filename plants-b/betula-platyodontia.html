Betula platyodontia

Betula platyodontia, commonly known as the American paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale, papery inner layer. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are triangular with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula platyodontia plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
