Baptisia bracteata var. bracteata

Baptisia bracteata var. bracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple cylindrical flowers. The plant has palmately divided, pinnately veined leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for prairies and dry gardens.

Baptisia bracteata var. bracteata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
