Betula elrodiana

It is characterized by its small, triangular leaves and smooth, white bark, similar to other birch species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, often in association with other hardwoods. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
