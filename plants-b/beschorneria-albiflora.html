Beschorneria albiflora

Beschorneria albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower yucca, is a striking evergreen plant native to Mexico.

It features tall, sword-like leaves that are stiff and covered in a waxy coating, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor settings.
