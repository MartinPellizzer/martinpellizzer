Brickellia parryi var. typica

Brickellia parryi var. typica, commonly known as Parry's brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
