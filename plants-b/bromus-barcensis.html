Bromus barcensis

Bromus barcensis, commonly known as the Barcens grass, is a species of grass native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Bromus barcensis is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
