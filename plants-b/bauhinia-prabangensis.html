Bauhinia prabangensis

Bauhinia prabangensis, commonly known as the Prabang Tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The Prabang Tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
