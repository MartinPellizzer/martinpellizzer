Bletia hyacinthina

Bletia hyacinthina, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a dense, mat-forming growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, spiral clusters of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Bletia hyacinthina is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
