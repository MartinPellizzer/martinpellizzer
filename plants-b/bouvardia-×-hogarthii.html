Bouvardia × hogarthii

Bouvardia × hogarthii, commonly known as the pink bouvardia, is a hybrid flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is prized for its clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has trailing stems and is often used in hanging baskets or as a cascading plant in gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bouvardia × hogarthii prefers well-draining soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
