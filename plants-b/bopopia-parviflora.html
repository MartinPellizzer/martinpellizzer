Bopopia parviflora

Bopopia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bopopia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Bopopia parviflora is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent leaves.

Its floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
