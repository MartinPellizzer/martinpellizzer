Bromus secalinus subsp. pseudosecalinus

Bromus secalinus subsp.

pseudosecalinus, commonly known as the false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem. It produces small, two-lobed flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and grasslands.
