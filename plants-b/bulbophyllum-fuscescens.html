Bulbophyllum fuscescens

Bulbophyllum fuscescens, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black-colored flowers that contrast with its greenish-yellow sepals. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with intricate patterns on the petals and lip.

This orchid is often grown as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique coloration.
