Bertiera laxa var. laxa

Bertiera laxa var. laxa, commonly known as the African false daisy, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
