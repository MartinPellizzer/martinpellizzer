Bactris aureodrupa

Bactris aureodrupa, commonly known as the golden fruit palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that are arranged in a fan shape.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits.
