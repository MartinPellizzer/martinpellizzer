Brodiaea lactea var. lilacina

Brodiaea lactea var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered brodiaea, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, lilac-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
