Begonia simii

Begonia simii, commonly known as the Sierra Leone begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia simii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage and unique floral display.
