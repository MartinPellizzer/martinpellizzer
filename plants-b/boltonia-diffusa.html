Boltonia diffusa

Boltonia diffusa, commonly known as white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, daisy-like white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, cone-shaped flower head that is surrounded by broad, fuzzy leaves. Boltonia diffusa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is a valuable plant for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
