Breweria baccharoides

Breweria baccharoides, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Breweria baccharoides is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
