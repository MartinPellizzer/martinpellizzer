Bactris sphaerocarpa var. schizophylla

Bactris sphaerocarpa var. schizophylla, commonly known as the red-fruit palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and produces large, fleshy, red or orange fruits that are edible. The leaves are large, pinnate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
