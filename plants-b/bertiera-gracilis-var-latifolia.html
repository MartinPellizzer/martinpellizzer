Bertiera gracilis var. latifolia

Bertiera gracilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bertiera, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
