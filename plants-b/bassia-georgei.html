Bassia georgei

Bassia georgei, commonly known as George's bursaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are encased in spiny structures, which aid in dispersal by attaching to animal fur or human clothing.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
