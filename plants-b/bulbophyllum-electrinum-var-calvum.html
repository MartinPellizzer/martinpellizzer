Bulbophyllum electrinum var. calvum

Bulbophyllum electrinum var. calvum, commonly known as the naked Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, often hairy pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty and ease of care.
