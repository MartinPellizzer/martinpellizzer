Billbergia pallidiflora

Billbergia pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces striking, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia pallidiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
