Blechnum lherminieri subsp. lehmannii

Blechnum lherminieri subsp.

lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to South America. It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive blackish-brown coloration on the underside.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, scale-like structures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
