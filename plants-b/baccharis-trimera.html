Baccharis trimera

Baccharis trimera, commonly known as golden weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Baccharis trimera is known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
