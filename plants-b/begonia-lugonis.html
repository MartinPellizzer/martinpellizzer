Begonia lugonis

Begonia lugonis, commonly known as the Malay begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and red, making it a popular ornamental species.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
