Balsamorhiza macrolepis

Balsamorhiza macrolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous root system helps it survive in arid environments and contributes to soil stabilization.
