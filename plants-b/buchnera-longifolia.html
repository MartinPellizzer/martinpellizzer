Buchnera longifolia

Buchnera longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf buchnera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Buchnera longifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
