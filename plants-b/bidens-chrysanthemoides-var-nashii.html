Bidens chrysanthemoides var. nashii

Bidens chrysanthemoides var. nashii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, narrow leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the daisy family. It has hairy stems and is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
