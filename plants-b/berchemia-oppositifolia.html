Berchemia oppositifolia

Berchemia oppositifolia, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves and clusters of tubular, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
