Bauhinia outimouta

Bauhinia outimouta, commonly known as the African rosewood or the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia outimouta also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
