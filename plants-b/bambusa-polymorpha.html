Bambusa polymorpha

Bambusa polymorpha, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually green and glossy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa polymorpha is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in construction and papermaking.
