Begonia bulbillifera

Begonia bulbillifera, commonly known as the bulbous begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and ease of care.

The plant reproduces through bulbils, which are small plantlets that form along the stems and can be easily detached and rooted.
