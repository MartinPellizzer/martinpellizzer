Berberis fordii

Berberis fordii, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features thorny stems and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden landscapes and erosion control.
