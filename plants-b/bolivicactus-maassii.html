Bolivicactus maassii

Bolivicactus maassii, commonly known as the Maass's bolivian cactus, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
