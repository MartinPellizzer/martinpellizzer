Baccharis venulosa

Baccharis venulosa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Baccharis venulosa is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
