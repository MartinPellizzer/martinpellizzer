Bromus racemosus var. villosus

Bromus racemosus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a reddish hue.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural environments.
