Berberis crataegina var. lycica

Berberis crataegina var. lycica, commonly known as the Turkish barberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine contribute to its significance in horticulture.
