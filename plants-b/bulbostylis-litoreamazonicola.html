Bulbostylis litoreamazonicola

Bulbostylis litoreamazonicola, commonly known as the Amazonian marsh grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Amazon Basin.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, sandy soils along the shores of tidal estuaries. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand periodic flooding and salt spray.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
