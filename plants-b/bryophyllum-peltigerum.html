Bryophyllum peltigerum

Bryophyllum peltigerum, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically in the spring. One of its notable features is its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which can root and develop into new plants rapidly.

This makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
