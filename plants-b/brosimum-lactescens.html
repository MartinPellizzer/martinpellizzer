Brosimum lactescens

Brosimum lactescens, commonly known as the balsa tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is one of the lightest natural materials known. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
