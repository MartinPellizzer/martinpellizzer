Barleria monostachya

Barleria monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
