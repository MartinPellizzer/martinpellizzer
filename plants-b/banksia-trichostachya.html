Banksia trichostachya

Banksia trichostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellowish to orange-red flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Banksia trichostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
