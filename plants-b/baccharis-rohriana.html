Baccharis rohriana

Baccharis rohriana, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Baccharis rohriana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
