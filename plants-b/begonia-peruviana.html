Begonia peruviana

Begonia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that often display intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Begonia peruviana is widely appreciated by plant enthusiasts and horticulturists alike.
