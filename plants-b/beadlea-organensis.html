Beadlea organensis

Beadlea organensis, commonly known as the organensis beadlea, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep violet to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within dense rainforests. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy surface that reflects light.

Beadlea organensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
