Begonia galeolepis

Begonia galeolepis, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Begonia galeolepis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
