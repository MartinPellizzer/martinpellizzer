Bromus madritensis subsp. kunkelii

Bromus madritensis subsp.

kunkelii, commonly known as Kunkel's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal spike that bears small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
