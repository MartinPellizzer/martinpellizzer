Begonia grandis subsp. holostyla

Begonia grandis subsp.

holostyla, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage and flowers.
