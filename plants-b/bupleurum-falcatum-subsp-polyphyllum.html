Bupleurum falcatum subsp. polyphyllum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

polyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple.

The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
