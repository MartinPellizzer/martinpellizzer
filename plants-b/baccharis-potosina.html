Baccharis potosina

Baccharis potosina, commonly known as Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Baccharis potosina is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
