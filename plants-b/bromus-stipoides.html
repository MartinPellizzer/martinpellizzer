Bromus stipoides

Bromus stipoides, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red. It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Bromus stipoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
