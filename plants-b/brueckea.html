Brueckea

Brueckea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Brueckea species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, warm environments with indirect sunlight. They are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and horticultural practices in their native regions.
