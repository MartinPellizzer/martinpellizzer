Brickellia adenolepis

Brickellia adenolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact growth habit.

Brickellia adenolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought conditions.
