Butea laotica

Butea laotica, commonly known as the Laos flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with warm, dry conditions.
