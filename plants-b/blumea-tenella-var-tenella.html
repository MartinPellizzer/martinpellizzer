Blumea tenella var. tenella

Blumea tenella var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
