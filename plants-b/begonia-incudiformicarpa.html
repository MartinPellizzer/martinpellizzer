Begonia incudiformicarpa

Begonia incudiformicarpa, commonly known as the ear-shaped begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped calyx, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, fleshy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

This species is often grown as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
