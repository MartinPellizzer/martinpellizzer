Bambusa breviflora var. hainanensis

Bambusa breviflora var. hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan bamboo, is a species native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It is a tall, clumping bamboo that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant features slender, green culms with a smooth, often glaucous surface. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing a dense canopy.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
