Bassia laurifolia

Bassia laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen boxwood, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant has small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for hedges and topiary.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
