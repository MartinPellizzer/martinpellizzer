Bursera hindsiana var. rhoifolia

Bursera hindsiana var. rhoifolia, commonly known as the Mexican birch or rhoifolia bursera, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
