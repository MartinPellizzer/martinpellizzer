Berberis bergmanniae

Berberis bergmanniae, commonly known as the Bergmann's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a hardy landscape plant.
