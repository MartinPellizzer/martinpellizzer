Brachyderea aurea

Brachyderea aurea, commonly known as the golden brachyderea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Brachyderea aurea thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
