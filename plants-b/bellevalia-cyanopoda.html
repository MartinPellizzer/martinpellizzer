Bellevalia cyanopoda

Bellevalia cyanopoda, commonly known as the blue-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces striking blue-violet flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals, and it blooms from July to September.

Bellevalia cyanopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique color and form.
