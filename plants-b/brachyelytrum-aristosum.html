Brachyelytrum aristosum

Brachyelytrum aristosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass or the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern and southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of grassland ecosystems.

Brachyelytrum aristosum is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
