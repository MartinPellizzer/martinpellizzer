Banisteria metallicolor var. aurea

Banisteria metallicolor var. aurea, commonly known as the golden banisteria, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its visual appeal. This variety is a form of Banisteria metallicolor, which is known for its unique metallic sheen on its foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
