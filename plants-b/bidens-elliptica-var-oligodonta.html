Bidens elliptica var. oligodonta

Bidens elliptica var. oligodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its fruit is a schizocarp composed of two small, dry, winged segments that separate upon maturity.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and fields.
