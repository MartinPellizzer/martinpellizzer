Bunias orientalis var. winterli

Bunias orientalis var. winterli, commonly known as the winter cabbage or Siberian kale, is a hardy biennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features robust, thick, and waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly curly texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It is well adapted to cold climates and can survive harsh winters, making it a resilient addition to gardens in temperate regions.

Its leaves are edible and often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, cabbage-like flavor.
