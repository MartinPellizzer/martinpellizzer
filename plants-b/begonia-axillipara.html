Begonia axillipara

Begonia axillipara, commonly known as the axillary begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or container cultivation.

Begonia axillipara is valued for its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season, enhancing its use in horticultural settings.
