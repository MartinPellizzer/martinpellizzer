Begonia papuana

Begonia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and deeply veined, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia papuana is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
