Brachyglottis × remotifolia

Brachyglottis × remotifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or kawakawa, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional Māori weaving. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
