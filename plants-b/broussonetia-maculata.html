Broussonetia maculata

Broussonetia maculata, commonly known as the spotted mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with a distinctive red or purple discoloration on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Broussonetia maculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
