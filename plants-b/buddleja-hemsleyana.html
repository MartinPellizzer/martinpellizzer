Buddleja hemsleyana

Buddleja hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's butterfly bush, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fluffy, pale purple to white flower spikes that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Buddleja hemsleyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
