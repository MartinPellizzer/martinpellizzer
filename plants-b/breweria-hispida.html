Breweria hispida

Breweria hispida, commonly known as the rough brewer's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Breweria hispida is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
