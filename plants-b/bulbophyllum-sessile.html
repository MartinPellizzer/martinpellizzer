Bulbophyllum sessile

Bulbophyllum sessile, commonly known as the sessile bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically fleshy and adapted for drought resistance.

Bulbophyllum sessile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
