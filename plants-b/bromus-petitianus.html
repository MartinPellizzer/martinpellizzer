Bromus petitianus

Bromus petitianus, commonly known as the Italian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Bromus petitianus is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its rapid establishment and hardiness.
