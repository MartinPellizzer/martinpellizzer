Bambusa rectocuneata

Bambusa rectocuneata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of clumping bamboo native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant has a tall, slender growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage is dense and provides excellent privacy screening in landscapes.

Bambusa rectocuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden design and as a decorative plant.
