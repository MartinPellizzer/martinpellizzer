Bruguiera conjugata

Bruguiera conjugata, commonly known as the mangrove fern, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments. The plant has specialized root systems that allow it to tolerate saltwater and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate and feather-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bruguiera conjugata is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and shelter for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
