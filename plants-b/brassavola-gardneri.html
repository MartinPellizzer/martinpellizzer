Brassavola gardneri

Brassavola gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a pseudobulb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its blooms are usually clustered in racemes and emit a sweet, spicy scent.

Brassavola gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
