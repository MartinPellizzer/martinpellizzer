Brachiaria arizonica var. laeviglumis

Brachiaria arizonica var. laeviglumis, commonly known as Arizona grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like blades and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has a low-growing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its leaves are typically smooth and have a bluish-green color, making it a common component of native plant communities in its range.
