Bambusa globifera

Bambusa globifera, commonly known as the golden bamboo or the giant bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a rapid growth rate, making it suitable for reforestation and erosion control. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
