Begonia pilifera

Begonia pilifera, commonly known as the hairleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves, which give it its scientific name, and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually broad and ovate, with a velvety texture.

Begonia pilifera thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
