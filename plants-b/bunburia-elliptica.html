Bunburia elliptica

Bunburia elliptica, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its succulent stems and leaves. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas quickly.

Bunburia elliptica is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines in some regions.
