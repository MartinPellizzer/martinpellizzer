Bejaria hispida

Bejaria hispida, commonly known as the hairy bejaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Bejaria hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
