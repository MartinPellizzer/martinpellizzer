Baccharis vismioides

Baccharis vismioides, commonly known as Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters.

Baccharis vismioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
