Barleria cordifolia

Barleria cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Barleria cordifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny conditions.
