Barkhausia albida

Barkhausia albida, commonly known as the white bark lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms during the late summer and early autumn months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Barkhausia albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
