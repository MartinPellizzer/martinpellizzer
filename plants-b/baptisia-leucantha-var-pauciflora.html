Baptisia leucantha var. pauciflora

Baptisia leucantha var. pauciflora, commonly known as the white false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
