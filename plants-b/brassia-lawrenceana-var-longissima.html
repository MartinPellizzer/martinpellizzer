Brassia lawrenceana var. longissima

Brassia lawrenceana var. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed brassia, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender flower spikes that can reach several feet in length, bearing numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white to pale pink blooms with a distinctive, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix.

This variety is prized by orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
