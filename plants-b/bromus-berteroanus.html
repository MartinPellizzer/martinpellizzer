Bromus berteroanus

Bromus berteroanus, commonly known as the Bertero's brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Bromus berteroanus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
