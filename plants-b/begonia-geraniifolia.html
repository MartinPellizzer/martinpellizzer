Begonia geraniifolia

Begonia geraniifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
