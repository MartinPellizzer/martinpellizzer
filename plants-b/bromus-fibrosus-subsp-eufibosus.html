Bromus fibrosus subsp. eufibosus

Bromus fibrosus subsp.

eufibosus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and slender, tufted shoots. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, pale green to brownish spikelets.

This subspecies is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
