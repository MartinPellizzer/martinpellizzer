Bidens magnidisca

Bidens magnidisca, commonly known as the large-flowered beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens magnidisca is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
