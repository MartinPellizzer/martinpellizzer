Begonia lubbersii

Begonia lubbersii, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins that contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
