Bactris actinoneura

Bactris actinoneura, commonly known as the red-brown palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, round fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. Bactris actinoneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
