Barkhausia sardoa

Barkhausia sardoa, commonly known as the Sardoa orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of vibrant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip that aids in pollination. Its roots are adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils, often growing in mountainous regions.

Barkhausia sardoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
