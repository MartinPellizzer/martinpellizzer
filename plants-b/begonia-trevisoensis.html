Begonia trevisoensis

Begonia trevisoensis, commonly known as the Treviso begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of northeastern Italy.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The foliage is typically green with a velvety texture, and the flowers can range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting central stripe. This plant prefers cool, shaded environments and is often found in moist, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and hardy plant specimens.
