Begonia boqueronensis

Begonia boqueronensis, commonly known as the Boqueron begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep green color with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
