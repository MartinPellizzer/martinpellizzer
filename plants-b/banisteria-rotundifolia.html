Banisteria rotundifolia

Banisteria rotundifolia, commonly known as the red mombin or red mulberry, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.
