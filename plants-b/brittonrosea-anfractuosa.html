Brittonrosea anfractuosa

Brittonrosea anfractuosa, commonly known as the wrinkled brittonrose, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often deeply lobed leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
