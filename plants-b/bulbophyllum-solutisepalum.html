Bulbophyllum solutisepalum

Bulbophyllum solutisepalum, commonly known as the Solitary Sepal Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single sepal that is often more prominent than the others. The plant produces striking, brightly colored flowers that are typically yellow or orange in hue. It grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
