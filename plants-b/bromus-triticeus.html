Bromus triticeus

Bromus triticeus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Bromus triticeus is often found in temperate climates and can be an important component of native plant communities.
