Bursa pauciflora

Bursa pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered burr, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive bracts. The plant produces a unique, spiky structure that resembles a bur, which aids in seed dispersal. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Bursa pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
