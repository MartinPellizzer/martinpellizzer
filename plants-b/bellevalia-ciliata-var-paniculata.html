Bellevalia ciliata var. paniculata

Bellevalia ciliata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
