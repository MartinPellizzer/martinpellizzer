Bambusa pygmaea

Bambusa pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender, green culms that rarely exceed 1 meter in height. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa pygmaea is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
