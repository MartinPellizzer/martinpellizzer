Buddleja purdomii var. fulvotomentosa

Buddleja purdomii var. fulvotomentosa, commonly known as the golden-tomentose bushmaster, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to golden flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental choice.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a desirable plant for gardens and landscapes.
