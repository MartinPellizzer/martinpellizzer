Bulbophyllum neoguineense

Bulbophyllum neoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its clustered pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from yellow to orange, often with contrasting purple or red markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
