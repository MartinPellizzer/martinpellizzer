Brachystegia spiciformis var. mpalensis

Brachystegia spiciformis var. mpalensis, commonly known as the Mpala brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the miombo woodlands of eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny pods containing seeds. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its hardy nature makes it well adapted to the dry, seasonal climates of its native range.
