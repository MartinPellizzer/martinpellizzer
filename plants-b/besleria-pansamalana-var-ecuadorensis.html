Besleria pansamalana var. ecuadorensis

Besleria pansamalana var. ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors, often in red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
