Bursera brachypoda

Bursera brachypoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf bursera, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces a resinous sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.

Bursera brachypoda typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
