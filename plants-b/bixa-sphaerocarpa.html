Bixa sphaerocarpa

Bixa sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the "barbadine" or "beauty berry," is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, berry-like fruits that are clustered in spherical formations. The plant produces small, white flowers and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The fruits are rich in carotenoids, particularly beta-carotene, which gives them their vibrant color.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in food and medicine.
