Bidens sandvicensis subsp. confusa

Bidens sandvicensis subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.
