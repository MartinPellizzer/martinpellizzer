Baccharis prunifolia var. prunifolia

Baccharis prunifolia var. prunifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
