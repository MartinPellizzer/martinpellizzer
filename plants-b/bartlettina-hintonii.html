Bartlettina hintonii

Bartlettina hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's barberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species in its native habitat.
