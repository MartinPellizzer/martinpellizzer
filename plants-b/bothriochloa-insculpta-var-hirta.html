Bothriochloa insculpta var. hirta

Bothriochloa insculpta var. hirta, commonly known as hairy bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as lawns and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
