Brassica polymorpha

Brassica polymorpha, commonly known as the wild cabbage, is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellow, four-petaled flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Brassica polymorpha is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

It is closely related to other economically important Brassica species such as broccoli and cauliflower.
