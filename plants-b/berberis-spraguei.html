Berberis spraguei

Berberis spraguei, commonly known as the Sprague's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Berberis spraguei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
