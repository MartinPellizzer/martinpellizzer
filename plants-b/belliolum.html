Belliolum

Belliolum, commonly known as the bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Belliolum species often have lance-shaped leaves and produce small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

These plants are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
