Buddleja suaveolens

Buddleja suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja suaveolens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
