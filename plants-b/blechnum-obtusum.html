Blechnum obtusum

Blechnum obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Blechnum obtusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
