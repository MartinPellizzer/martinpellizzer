Brassica elongata subsp. pinnatifida

Brassica elongata subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are elongated siliques that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
