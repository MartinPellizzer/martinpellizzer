Bromus squarrosus subsp. noeanus

Bromus squarrosus subsp.

noeanus, commonly known as the desert brome, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender blades, and dense spikelets that are often covered in short hairs.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
