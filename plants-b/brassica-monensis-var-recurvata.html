Brassica monensis var. recurvata

Brassica monensis var. recurvata, commonly known as the curled mustard, is a variant of the mustard plant characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by round, seed-filled pods. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.

The botanical features of this variety make it both an ornamental and edible plant with historical significance in agriculture.
