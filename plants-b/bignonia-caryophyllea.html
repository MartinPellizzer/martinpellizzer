Bignonia caryophyllea

Bignonia caryophyllea, commonly known as the flame vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange to red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, serpentine stems that can climb using tendrils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and vibrant blooms make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
