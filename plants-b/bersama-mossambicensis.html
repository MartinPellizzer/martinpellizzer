Bersama mossambicensis

Bersama mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique fern, is a species of moss that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth pattern and its ability to retain moisture. The plant features elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green and sometimes have a bluish hue. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

Bersama mossambicensis plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
