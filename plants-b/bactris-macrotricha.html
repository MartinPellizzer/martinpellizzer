Bactris macrotricha

Bactris macrotricha, commonly known as the giant palm or royal palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
