Baccharis glomeruliflora

Baccharis glomeruliflora, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
