Bunchosia macrophylla

Bunchosia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Bunchosia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
