Baccharis floccosa

Baccharis floccosa, commonly known as the woolly baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

Baccharis floccosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
