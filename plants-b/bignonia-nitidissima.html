Bignonia nitidissima

Bignonia nitidissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf bignonia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its stems are twining and can reach lengths of up to 20 feet, making it an aggressive grower.

Bignonia nitidissima is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
