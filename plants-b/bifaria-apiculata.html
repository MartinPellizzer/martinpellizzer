Bifaria apiculata

Bifaria apiculata, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is known for its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Apocynaceae family.

Bifaria apiculata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
