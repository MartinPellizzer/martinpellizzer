Brachiaria hubbardii

Brachiaria hubbardii, commonly known as Hubbard's blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters, and its inflorescence is typically compact and cylindrical. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and pastures.

Brachiaria hubbardii is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in erosion control and forage production.
