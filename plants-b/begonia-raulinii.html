Begonia raulinii

Begonia raulinii, commonly known as the Raulin's begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its foliage is often a deep green with a slightly textured surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
