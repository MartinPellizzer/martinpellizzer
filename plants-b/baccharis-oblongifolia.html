Baccharis oblongifolia

Baccharis oblongifolia, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in moist, open areas such as riverbanks and forest edges. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal uses.

Baccharis oblongifolia is valued for its role in traditional medicine and ecological restoration.
