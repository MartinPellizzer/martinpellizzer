Bambusa atter

Bambusa atter, commonly known as golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Bambusa atter is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping and garden design.
