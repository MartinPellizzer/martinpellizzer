Brassica campestris var. rapa

Brassica campestris var. rapa, commonly known as Chinese cabbage, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a compact head, often used in various culinary traditions. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutrition and agriculture.
