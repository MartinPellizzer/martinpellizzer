Betula pendula f. dalecarlica

Betula pendula f. dalecarlica, commonly known as the silver birch variant from Dalecarlia, is a decorative tree characterized by its white, peeling bark and slender, pendulous branches.

It is a subspecies of the silver birch, native to central and northern Europe. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various conditions contribute to its widespread cultivation.
