Bacopa lanigera var. marginata

Bacopa lanigera var. marginata, commonly known as the margined water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic gardens and pond landscapes.
