Bolbitis × multipinna

Bolbitis × multipinna, commonly known as the golden fern, is a hybrid fern that exhibits a unique combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome with fronds that are typically golden-yellow to bronze in color, providing a striking visual contrast. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive pinna arrangement that gives it a feathery appearance.

Bolbitis × multipinna is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate varying soil conditions.
