Bifrenaria tetragona var. umbrophila

Bifrenaria tetragona var. umbrophila, commonly known as the darkleaf bifrenaria, is a shade-tolerant fern native to the understory of temperate forests.

It features delicate, bipinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
