Bobua groffii

Bobua groffii, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, and it produces distinctive, woody pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bobua groffii is also valued for its fast growth rate and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
