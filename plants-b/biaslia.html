Biaslia

Biaslia, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that often retain their color and form for extended periods after being cut. The plants typically grow in temperate regions and are found in various habitats, including meadows and woodlands. Biaslia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
