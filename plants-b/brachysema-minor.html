Brachysema minor

Brachysema minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brachysema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Brachysema minor is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
