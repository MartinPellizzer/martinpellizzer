Begonia flaviflora var. gamblei

Begonia flaviflora var. gamblei, commonly known as the Gambler's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that contrast beautifully with its dark green, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and prefers bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly wavy margin, and the flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in humid environments.
