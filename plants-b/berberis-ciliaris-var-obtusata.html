Berberis ciliaris var. obtusata

Berberis ciliaris var. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, spiny stems and has compound leaves with rounded lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
