Blumea dentata var. schultzii

Blumea dentata var. schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped, toothed leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
