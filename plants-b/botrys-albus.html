Botrys albus

Botrys albus, commonly known as the white clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas.

Botrys albus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
