Berenice victorialis

Berenice victorialis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
