Bambusa pallida

Bambusa pallida, commonly known as the white bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale-green culms that often have a whitish or bluish tint. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Bambusa pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and bamboo crafts.
