Balsamina tricornis

Balsamina tricornis, commonly known as the three-lobed balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

Balsamina tricornis is also noted for its ability to grow in partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
