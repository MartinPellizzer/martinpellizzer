Biscutella valentina subsp. coronopifolia

Biscutella valentina subsp.

coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf biscutella, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and a flowering stem that bears racemes of yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
