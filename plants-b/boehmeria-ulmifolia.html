Boehmeria ulmifolia

Boehmeria ulmifolia, commonly known as silky leaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported soothing properties.

The fibrous stalks of the plant are also used in the production of natural fibers.
