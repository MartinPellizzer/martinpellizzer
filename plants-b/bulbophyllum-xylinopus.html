Bulbophyllum xylinopus

Bulbophyllum xylinopus, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bamboo-like pseudobulbs and elongated, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
