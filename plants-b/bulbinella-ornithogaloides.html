Bulbinella ornithogaloides

Bulbinella ornithogaloides, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its clustered, bulb-like structures that store nutrients. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
