Bombax grandiflorum

Bombax grandiflorum, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are large, palmate, and often shed during the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many regions.
