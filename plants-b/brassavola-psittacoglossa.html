Brassavola psittacoglossa

Brassavola psittacoglossa, commonly known as the parrot's tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often brightly colored flowers that resemble a parrot's tongue, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip, and it blooms during the summer months.

Brassavola psittacoglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
