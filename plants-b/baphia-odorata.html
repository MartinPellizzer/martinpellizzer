Baphia odorata

Baphia odorata, commonly known as the African orchid tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
