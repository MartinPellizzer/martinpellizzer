Brassica elongata f. mesocarpa

Brassica elongata f. mesocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese kale or gai lan, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its elongated, stalked leaves.

The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible, dark green leaves and tender stalks. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil with adequate moisture. The leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A, C, and K, making it a valuable vegetable in many cuisines.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a compact, upright growth habit.
