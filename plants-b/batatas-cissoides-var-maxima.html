Batatas cissoides var. maxima

Batatas cissoides var. maxima, commonly known as the giant arrowhead or arrowleaf plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible tubers that resemble potatoes in texture and flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.
