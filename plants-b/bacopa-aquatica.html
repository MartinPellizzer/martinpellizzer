Bacopa aquatica

Bacopa aquatica, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of blue or violet flowers. Bacopa aquatica is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a water purifier in aquatic ecosystems.
