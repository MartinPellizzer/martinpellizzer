Bunchosia glandulosa f. typica

Bunchosia glandulosa f. typica, commonly known as the glandular bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
