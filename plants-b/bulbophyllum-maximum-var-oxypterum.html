Bulbophyllum maximum var. oxypterum

Bulbophyllum maximum var. oxypterum, commonly known as the Oxypterum Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
