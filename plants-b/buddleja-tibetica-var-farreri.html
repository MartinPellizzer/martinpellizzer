Buddleja tibetica var. farreri

Buddleja tibetica var. farreri, commonly known as the Tibetan butterfly bush, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and landscapes.
