Balsamodendrum commiphora

Balsamodendrum commiphora, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant produces a resinous sap that is harvested for its aromatic and medicinal properties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.
