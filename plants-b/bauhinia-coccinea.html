Bauhinia coccinea

Bauhinia coccinea, commonly known as the crimson bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat. The plant has compound leaves that are often divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Bauhinia coccinea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
