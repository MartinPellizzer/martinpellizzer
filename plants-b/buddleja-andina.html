Buddleja andina

Buddleja andina, commonly known as Andean buddleja, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Buddleja andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and adaptability to temperate climates.
