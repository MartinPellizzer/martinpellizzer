Becium grandiflorum var. densiflorum

Becium grandiflorum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower becium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland habitats and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
