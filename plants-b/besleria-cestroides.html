Besleria cestroides

Besleria cestroides, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often with a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of showy, tubular flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
