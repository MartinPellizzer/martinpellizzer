Brassica lasiocalycina

Brassica lasiocalycina, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are short, thick pods that contain several seeds.

Brassica lasiocalycina is native to parts of Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
