Bladhia lindleyana

Bladhia lindleyana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural areas.

Bladhia lindleyana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in certain regions.
