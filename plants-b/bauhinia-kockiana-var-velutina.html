Bauhinia kockiana var. velutina

Bauhinia kockiana var. velutina, commonly known as velvet bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
