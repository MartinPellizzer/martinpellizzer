Begonia flava

Begonia flava, commonly known as the yellow begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long blooming period.
