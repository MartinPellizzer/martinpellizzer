Boronia psoraleoides

Boronia psoraleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Boronia psoraleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
