Brassiophoenix schumannii

Brassiophoenix schumannii, commonly known as the Schumann's date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds that form a crown-like structure. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible date-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
