Baccharis jelskii var. xanthopappa

Baccharis jelskii var. xanthopappa, commonly known as golden baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
