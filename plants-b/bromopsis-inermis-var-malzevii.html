Bromopsis inermis var. malzevii

Bromopsis inermis var. malzevii, commonly known as the Malzevii grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to parts of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscaping.
