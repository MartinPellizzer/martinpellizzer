Borago laxiflora var. micranthos

Borago laxiflora var. micranthos, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
