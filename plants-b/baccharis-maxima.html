Baccharis maxima

Baccharis maxima, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis maxima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
