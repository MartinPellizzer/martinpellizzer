Bauhinia bracteata

Bauhinia bracteata, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often shed during the dry season, and its fruit is a flat, dehiscent pod containing several seeds.

Bauhinia bracteata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
