Bigelowia graveolens var. albicaulis

Bigelowia graveolens var. albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed bigelowia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in a fine white down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bigelowia graveolens var. albicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
