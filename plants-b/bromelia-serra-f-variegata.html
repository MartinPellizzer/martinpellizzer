Bromelia serra f. variegata

Bromelia serra f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated mountain bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The leaves are typically broad, leathery, and exhibit a striking variegation of green, white, and pink hues. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Its vibrant foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
