Begonia pubescens

Begonia pubescens, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant.
