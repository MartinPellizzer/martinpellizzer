Bouchea caespitosa

Bouchea caespitosa, commonly known as the blue blossom or bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Bouchea caespitosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
