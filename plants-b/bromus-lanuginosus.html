Bromus lanuginosus

Bromus lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in soft hairs.

Bromus lanuginosus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
