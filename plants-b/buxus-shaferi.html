Buxus shaferi

Buxus shaferi, commonly known as the Turkish boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Turkey.

It features dense, dark green foliage with glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a compact, rounded form. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a topiary plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its ability to maintain a neat appearance throughout the year.
