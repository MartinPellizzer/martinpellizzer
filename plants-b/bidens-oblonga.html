Bidens oblonga

Bidens oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has heart-shaped to oval leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent white rays. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Bidens oblonga is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
