Billardiera longiflora var. alpina

Billardiera longiflora var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine billardiera, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a climbing shrub with slender, twining stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with limited soil and nutrient availability.
