Bistorta officinalis subsp. japonica

Bistorta officinalis subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese bistort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
