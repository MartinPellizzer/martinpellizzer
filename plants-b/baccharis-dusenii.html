Baccharis dusenii

Baccharis dusenii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
