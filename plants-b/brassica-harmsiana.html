Brassica harmsiana

Brassica harmsiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has dark green, serrated leaves. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds and oil-rich pods.

Brassica harmsiana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
