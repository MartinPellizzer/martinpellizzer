Brachystele bicrinita

Brachystele bicrinita, commonly known as the hairy staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like structure with short, thick stems and dense, hairy leaves. The plant's leaves are often covered in fine, white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous media for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and terrarium gardening.
