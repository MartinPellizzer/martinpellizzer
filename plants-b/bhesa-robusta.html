Bhesa robusta

Bhesa robusta, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a species native to East Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are white and clustered, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds used for coffee production.

Bhesa robusta is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important species in its native habitat.
