Berberis thunbergii var. papillifera

Berberis thunbergii var. papillifera, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow-orange berries that persist into winter. The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall color and persistent foliage.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
