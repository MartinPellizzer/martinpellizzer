Bromus arvensis f. puberulus

Bromus arvensis f. puberulus, commonly known as the pubescent barnyard grass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its pubescent (hairy) leaves and stems. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are usually arranged in loose clusters.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
