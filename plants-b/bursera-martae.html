Bursera martae

Bursera martae, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Bursera martae produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in limestone soils.
