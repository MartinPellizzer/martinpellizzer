Bulbophyllum vinicolor

Bulbophyllum vinicolor, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which bloom in clusters along the inflorescence. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients to help it survive in its natural habitat. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
