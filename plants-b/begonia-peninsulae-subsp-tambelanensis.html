Begonia peninsulae subsp. tambelanensis

Begonia peninsulae subsp.

tambelanensis, commonly known as the Tambelanensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
