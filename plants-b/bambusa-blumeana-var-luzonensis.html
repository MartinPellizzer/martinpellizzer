Bambusa blumeana var. luzonensis

Bambusa blumeana var. luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon bamboo, is a fast-growing, clump-forming grass species native to the Philippines.

It features slender, green culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a smooth, hollow stem structure typical of many bamboo species. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and structural versatility.

This variety is also valued for its potential in sustainable construction and as a source of renewable biomass.
