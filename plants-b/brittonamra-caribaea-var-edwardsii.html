Brittonamra caribaea var. edwardsii

Brittonamra caribaea var. edwardsii, commonly known as the Jamaican blackberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and health benefits.
