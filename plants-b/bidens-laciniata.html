Bidens laciniata

Bidens laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with prominent white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, along roadsides, and in floodplains.

Bidens laciniata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
