Bidens hendersonensis var. hendersonensis

Bidens hendersonensis var. hendersonensis, commonly known as the Henderson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a herbaceous growth habit with upright stems. Its leaves are usually alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disc florets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
