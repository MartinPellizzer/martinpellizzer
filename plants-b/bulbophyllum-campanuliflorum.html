Bulbophyllum campanuliflorum

Bulbophyllum campanuliflorum, commonly known as the bell-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex inflorescence structure. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
