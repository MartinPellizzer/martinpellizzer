Baptisia laevicaulis

Baptisia laevicaulis, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Baptisia laevicaulis is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
