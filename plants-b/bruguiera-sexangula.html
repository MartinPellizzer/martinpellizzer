Bruguiera sexangula

Bruguiera sexangula, commonly known as the mangrove button.

It is a halophytic tree found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in coastal areas with brackish water. The plant has specialized aerial roots that help in gas exchange and provide structural support. Its leaves are small, ovate, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Bruguiera sexangula plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
