Bauhinia chapadensis

Bauhinia chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by flat, leathery pods that contain the seeds.

Bauhinia chapadensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
