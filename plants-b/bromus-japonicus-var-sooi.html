Bromus japonicus var. sooi

Bromus japonicus var. sooi, commonly known as the Japanese brome, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
