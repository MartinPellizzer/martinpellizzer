Bolbophyllaria sordida

Bolbophyllaria sordida, commonly known as the gloomy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, where it can efficiently capture prey. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems may be partially emergent.

Bolbophyllaria sordida plays a unique role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic environments.
