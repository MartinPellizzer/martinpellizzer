Butneria

Butneria, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a genus of aquatic plants native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, water-lily-like flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. Butneria species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and cultural significance in various Asian countries.

The plant plays an important role in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.
