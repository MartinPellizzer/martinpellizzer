Betula japonica var. kamtschatica

Betula japonica var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka birch, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to grow in challenging environmental conditions.
