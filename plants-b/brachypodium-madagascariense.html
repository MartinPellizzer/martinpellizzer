Brachypodium madagascariense

Brachypodium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar false brome, is a grass species native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Brachypodium madagascariense is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in conservation efforts.
