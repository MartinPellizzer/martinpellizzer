Bolivicereus samaipatanus

Bolivicereus samaipatanus, commonly known as the Samaipata cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and sparse, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
