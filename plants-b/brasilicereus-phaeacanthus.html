Brasilicereus phaeacanthus

Brasilicereus phaeacanthus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
