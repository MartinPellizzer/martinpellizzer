Bombax ruficalix

Bombax ruficalix, commonly known as the red-flowered cotton tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seed pods that release seeds via wind dispersal. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, providing a lush green canopy.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates.
