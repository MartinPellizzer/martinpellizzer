Betula tortuosa var. kusmisscheffii

Betula tortuosa var. kusmisscheffii, commonly known as the Kusmisscheff's birch, is a variant of the twisted birch native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winding trunk and dark, peeling bark that reveals a lighter inner layer. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
