Bulbophyllum hians var. alticola

Bulbophyllum hians var. alticola, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, serving as storage organs for water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
