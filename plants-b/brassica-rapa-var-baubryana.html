Brassica rapa var. baubryana

Brassica rapa var. baubryana, commonly known as the Chinese kale or kale cabbage, is a cultivated variety of the mustard family.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are often curly or crinkled. This plant is typically grown for its edible leaves and stems, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C. It thrives in cool climates and is commonly cultivated in temperate regions.

The plant has a short growing season and is often harvested before flowering to ensure optimal flavor and texture.
