Briza gracilescens

Briza gracilescens, commonly known as the slender quaking grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, arching stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are wind-pollinated and produce small, dry seeds.

Briza gracilescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
