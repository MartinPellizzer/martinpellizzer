Begonia cehengensis

Begonia cehengensis, commonly known as the Ceheng begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
