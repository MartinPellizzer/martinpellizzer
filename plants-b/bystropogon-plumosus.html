Bystropogon plumosus

Bystropogon plumosus, commonly known as the silky broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in spikes. The plant has feathery, soft foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Bystropogon plumosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
