Berberis comberi

Berberis comberi, commonly known as the creeping barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or low-growing thickets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin and a glossy green surface.

Berberis comberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
