Brasilicereus

Brasilicereus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and nocturnal flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. Brasilicereus species are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-draining soil.

They are popular among cactus enthusiasts for their unique appearance and blooming habits.
