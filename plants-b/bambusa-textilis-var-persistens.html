Bambusa textilis var. persistens

Bambusa textilis var. persistens, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant golden-green foliage and robust, slender culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its attractive appearance in garden settings.

This variety is also valued for its use in construction and crafts due to its strong and flexible stems.
