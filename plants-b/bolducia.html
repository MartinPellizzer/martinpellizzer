Bolducia

Bolducia, commonly known as the false indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Bolducia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
