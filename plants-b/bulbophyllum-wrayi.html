Bulbophyllum wrayi

Bulbophyllum wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features intricate, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Bulbophyllum wrayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
