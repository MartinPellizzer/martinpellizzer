Bromus commutatus subsp. apricorum

Bromus commutatus subsp.

apricorum, commonly known as the spring brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and fields. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found in temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and the presence of awns on its florets.
