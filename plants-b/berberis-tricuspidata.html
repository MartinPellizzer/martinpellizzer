Berberis tricuspidata

Berberis tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-toothed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional medicine.
