Buchnera cruciata var. cruciata

Buchnera cruciata var. cruciata, commonly known as the common rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, succulent leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
