Berberis lubrica

Berberis lubrica, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It features slender, arching branches and small, glossy green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions contribute to its adaptability in various landscapes.
