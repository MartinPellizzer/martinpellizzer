Boehmeria cylindrica var. tenerrima

Boehmeria cylindrica var. tenerrima, commonly known as Chinese nettle or giant nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features hollow, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its fibrous stalks, which have been used in traditional papermaking and textile production.
