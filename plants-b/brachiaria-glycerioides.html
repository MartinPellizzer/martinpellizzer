Brachiaria glycerioides

Brachiaria glycerioides, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or false signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Brachiaria glycerioides is notable for its ability to form dense vegetation, which can play a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
