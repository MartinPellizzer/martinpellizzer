Buphthalmum peruvianum

Buphthalmum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Buphthalmum peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
