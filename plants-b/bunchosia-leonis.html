Bunchosia leonis

Bunchosia leonis, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing it with a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Bunchosia leonis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
