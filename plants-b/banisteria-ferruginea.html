Banisteria ferruginea

Banisteria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty banisteria, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
