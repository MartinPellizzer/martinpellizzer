Begonia gambutensis

Begonia gambutensis, commonly known as the Gambutensis begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
