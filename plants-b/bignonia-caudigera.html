Bignonia caudigera

Bignonia caudigera, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Bignonia caudigera is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
