Berberis × media

Berberis × media, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically alternate and have three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are ornamental but toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its hardy nature and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited for erosion control and as a decorative plant in temperate climates.
