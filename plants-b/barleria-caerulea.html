Barleria caerulea

Barleria caerulea, commonly known as blue barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct blue or silver hue on the underside. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Barleria caerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
