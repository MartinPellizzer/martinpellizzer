Butea sanguinea

Butea sanguinea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with long, thin leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. Butea sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
