Balansaephytum tonkinense

Balansaephytum tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
