Bassovia pogogena

Bassovia pogogena, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it produces small, fragrant flowers during the spring season.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
