Baccharis decussata subsp. decussata

Baccharis decussata subsp.

decussata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage that provides a striking visual contrast.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
