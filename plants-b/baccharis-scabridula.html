Baccharis scabridula

Baccharis scabridula, commonly known as rough baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
