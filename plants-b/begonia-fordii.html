Begonia fordii

Begonia fordii, commonly known as the Chinese begonia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, and are borne on long, slender pedicels.

Begonia fordii thrives in shady, moist environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
