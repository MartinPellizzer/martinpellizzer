Baccharis angulata

Baccharis angulata, commonly known as angular baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its angular, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, with a rough texture.

Baccharis angulata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
