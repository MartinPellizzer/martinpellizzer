Barbosella cucullata

Barbosella cucullata, commonly known as the hooded sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Barbosella cucullata is adapted to hot, dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
