Bumelia lanuginosa var. macrocarpa

Bumelia lanuginosa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bumelia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
