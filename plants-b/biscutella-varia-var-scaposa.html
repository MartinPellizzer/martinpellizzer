Biscutella varia var. scaposa

Biscutella varia var. scaposa, commonly known as the scaposa biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, blue to violet flowers in early spring, arranged in racemes along slender stems. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
