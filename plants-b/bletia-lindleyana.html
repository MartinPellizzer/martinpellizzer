Bletia lindleyana

Bletia lindleyana, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom during the holiday season, typically between December and January. The plant produces pendulous, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip, which is a key feature in its pollination strategy. Bletia lindleyana thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
