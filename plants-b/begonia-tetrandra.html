Begonia tetrandra

Begonia tetrandra, commonly known as the four-thread begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which often displays unique patterns and variegation.
