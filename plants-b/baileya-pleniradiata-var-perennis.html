Baileya pleniradiata var. perennis

Baileya pleniradiata var. perennis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent stems and leaves to conserve water. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
