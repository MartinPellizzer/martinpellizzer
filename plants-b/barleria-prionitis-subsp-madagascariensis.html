Barleria prionitis subsp. madagascariensis

Barleria prionitis subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar barleria, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
