Bulbostylis lagoensis

Bulbostylis lagoensis, commonly known as the lake bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly known as grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in marshy areas, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.

Bulbostylis lagoensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
