Blumea wallichii

Blumea wallichii, commonly known as the mountain blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flower heads that resemble a misty haze. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Blumea wallichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
