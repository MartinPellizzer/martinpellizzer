Bothriopodium glaziovii var. glabrum

Bothriopodium glaziovii var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth glaziovii grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
