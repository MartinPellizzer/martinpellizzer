Bulbophyllum ischnopus var. rhodoneuron

Bulbophyllum ischnopus var. rhodoneuron, commonly known as the pink-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, fleshy rhizome and produces a single, erect stem that bears several lanceolate leaves. The plant is notable for its striking pink to red flowers, which are clustered in a raceme and have a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
