Bauhinia scandens var. anguina

Bauhinia scandens var. anguina, commonly known as the climbing bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The varietal form 'anguina' is noted for its more robust growth and extended flowering period compared to other varieties.
