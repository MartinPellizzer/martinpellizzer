Bowlesia dichotoma var. albosquamata

Bowlesia dichotoma var. albosquamata, commonly known as the silver-squamated bowlesia, is a rare and distinctive species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique branching pattern and the presence of silvery, scale-like structures on its stems and leaves. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, often forming a low, shrub-like structure. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its striking appearance.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
