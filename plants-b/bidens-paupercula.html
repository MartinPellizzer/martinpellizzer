Bidens paupercula

Bidens paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in wetlands or along stream banks. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Bidens paupercula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
