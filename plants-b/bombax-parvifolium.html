Bombax parvifolium

Bombax parvifolium, commonly known as the Malabar cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like pods that contain fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
