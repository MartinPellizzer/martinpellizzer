Beta campanulata

Beta campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, supporting the distinctive floral structures.

Beta campanulata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
