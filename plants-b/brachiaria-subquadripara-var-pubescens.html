Brachiaria subquadripara var. pubescens

Brachiaria subquadripara var. pubescens, commonly known as pubescent brachiaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
