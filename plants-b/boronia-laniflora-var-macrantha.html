Boronia laniflora var. macrantha

Boronia laniflora var. macrantha, commonly known as the Woolly Boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
