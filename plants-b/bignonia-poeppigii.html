Bignonia poeppigii

Bignonia poeppigii, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees or other structures.

Bignonia poeppigii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
