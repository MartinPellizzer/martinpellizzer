Besleria crassa

Besleria crassa, commonly known as the giant flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in humid, warm environments with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Besleria crassa is also noted for its strong, pleasant fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
