Brassica rapa subsp. sylvestris

Brassica rapa subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and lobed leaves that are typically hairy.

This species is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It grows in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
