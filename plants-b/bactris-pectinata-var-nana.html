Bactris pectinata var. nana

Bactris pectinata var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf palm or small pectinata palm, is a compact, slow-growing palm native to Central and South America.

It features a short, stout trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are typically shorter and more compact compared to the standard variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

The species is also valued for its edible fruits, which are consumed locally in some regions.
