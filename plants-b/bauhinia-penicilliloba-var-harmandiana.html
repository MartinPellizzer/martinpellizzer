Bauhinia penicilliloba var. harmandiana

Bauhinia penicilliloba var. harmandiana, commonly known as the Harmand's bauhinia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, papery, often pink or red flowers. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets, giving the plant a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warm climates.
