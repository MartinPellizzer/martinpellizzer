Baccharis williamsii

Baccharis williamsii, commonly known as Williams' baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, often spiny foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
