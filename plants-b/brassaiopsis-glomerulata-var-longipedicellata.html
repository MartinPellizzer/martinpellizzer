Brassaiopsis glomerulata var. longipedicellata

Brassaiopsis glomerulata var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bracted palm, is a unique variant of the genus Brassaiopsis.

It is characterized by its clustered inflorescences and elongated pedicels, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its foliage consists of large, pinnately compound leaves with a glossy texture.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
