Bulbophyllum chondriophorum

Bulbophyllum chondriophorum, commonly known as the coral spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, branching inflorescence that resembles coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by hobbyists and conservationists alike.
