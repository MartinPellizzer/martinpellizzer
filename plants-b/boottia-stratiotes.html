Boottia stratiotes

Boottia stratiotes, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Boottia stratiotes plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic insects and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
