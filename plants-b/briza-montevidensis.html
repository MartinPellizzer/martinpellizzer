Briza montevidensis

Briza montevidensis, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Briza montevidensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
