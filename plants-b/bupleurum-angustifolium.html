Bupleurum angustifolium

Bupleurum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have a bell-shaped appearance with blue or purple petals.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
