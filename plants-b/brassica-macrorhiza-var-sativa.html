Brassica macrorhiza var. sativa

Brassica macrorhiza var. sativa, commonly known as field mustard, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Field mustard is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
