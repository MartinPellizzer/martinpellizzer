Bulbophyllum punctatum

Bulbophyllum punctatum, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns. It typically produces small, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. The flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for cultivation in controlled environments.
