Betula pseudopendula

Betula pseudopendula, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Betula pseudopendula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and parks.
