Bulbophyllum gilvum

Bulbophyllum gilvum, commonly known as the golden bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red.

Bulbophyllum gilvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
