Brassica juncea

Brassica juncea, commonly known as turnip mustard or Chinese mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its short stem, alternate leaves, and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces round, fleshy root vegetables similar to turnips, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its seeds are also harvested for oil and as a source of protein.

Brassica juncea is native to temperate regions of Asia and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
