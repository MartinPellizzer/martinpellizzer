Buddleja soratae

Buddleja soratae, commonly known as the Japanese weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it visually striking in gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Buddleja soratae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
