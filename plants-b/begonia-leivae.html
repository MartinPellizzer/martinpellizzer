Begonia leivae

Begonia leivae, commonly known as the Hainan begonia, is a species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is noted for its vibrant green color and intricate venation patterns.

Begonia leivae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
