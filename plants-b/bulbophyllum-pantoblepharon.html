Bulbophyllum pantoblepharon

Bulbophyllum pantoblepharon, commonly known as the "moon orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, moon-shaped pseudobulbs and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive, rounded lip.

Bulbophyllum pantoblepharon is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unusual appearance.
