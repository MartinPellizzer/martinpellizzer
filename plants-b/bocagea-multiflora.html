Bocagea multiflora

Bocagea multiflora, commonly known as the Florida beauty or Florida bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 inches. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Bocagea multiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.
