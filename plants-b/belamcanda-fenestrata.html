Belamcanda fenestrata

Belamcanda fenestrata, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking, dark red to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow stripe down the center. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Belamcanda fenestrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
