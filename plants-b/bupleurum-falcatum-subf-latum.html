Bupleurum falcatum subf. latum

Bupleurum falcatum subf.

latum, commonly known as the broadleaf angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
