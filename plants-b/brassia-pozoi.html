Brassia pozoi

Brassia pozoi, commonly known as the Pozo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are usually borne on lateral spikes and have a unique, intricate structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Brassia pozoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
