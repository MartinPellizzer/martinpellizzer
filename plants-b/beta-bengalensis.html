Beta bengalensis

Beta bengalensis, commonly known as the Indian spinach or Indian leaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Beta bengalensis is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
