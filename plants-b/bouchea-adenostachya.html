Bouchea adenostachya

Bouchea adenostachya, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears clusters of showy, white to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments. It blooms in spring and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Bouchea adenostachya is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
