Biscutella secunda

Biscutella secunda, commonly known as the lesser biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated silique, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Biscutella secunda is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
