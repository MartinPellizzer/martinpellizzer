Begonia macroptera var. paludum

Begonia macroptera var. paludum, commonly known as the swamp begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its beauty and adaptability.
