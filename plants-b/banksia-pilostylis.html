Banksia pilostylis

Banksia pilostylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a striking plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique texture. Banksia pilostylis is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and can thrive in full sun.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
