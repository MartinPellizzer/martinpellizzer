Bupleurum fruticescens

Bupleurum fruticescens, commonly known as wild chervil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate or semi-upright, with small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system consists of fine, fibrous roots that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Bupleurum fruticescens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
