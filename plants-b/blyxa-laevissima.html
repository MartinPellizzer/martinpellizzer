Blyxa laevissima

Blyxa laevissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water and wetlands, with long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It is often used in water gardens and pond ecosystems for its aesthetic value and ability to help control algae.

Blyxa laevissima is also valued for its role in water filtration and habitat provision for aquatic life.
