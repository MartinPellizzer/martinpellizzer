Bignonia incisa

Bignonia incisa, commonly known as the crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 20 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces pendulous, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
