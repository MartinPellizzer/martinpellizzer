Bombacopsis macrocalyx

Bombacopsis macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in tropical and subtropical regions for its striking seasonal display.
