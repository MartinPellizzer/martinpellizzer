Bourreria superba

Bourreria superba, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which can be toxic to animals and humans. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bourreria superba thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
