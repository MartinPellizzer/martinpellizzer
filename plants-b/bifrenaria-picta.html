Bifrenaria picta

Bifrenaria picta, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that often feature intricate patterns or spots. Bifrenaria picta typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its flowers, which are usually small and fragrant, bloom in clusters and contribute to its ornamental appeal.
