Bomarea cornigera var. liebmanniana

Bomarea cornigera var. liebmanniana, commonly known as the Liebmann's bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to violet, often with contrasting white veins. The plant features a tuberous root system and has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare tropical flora.
