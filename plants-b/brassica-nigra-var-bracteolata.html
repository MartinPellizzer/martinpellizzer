Brassica nigra var. bracteolata

Brassica nigra var. bracteolata, commonly known as the black mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds, which are small and black, are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
