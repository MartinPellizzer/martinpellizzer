Bulbophyllum ensiculiferum

Bulbophyllum ensiculiferum, commonly known as the sword-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
