Bomarea grandifolia

Bomarea grandifolia, commonly known as the giant bomarea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in its native high-altitude environment. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Bomarea grandifolia is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its impressive floral display.
