Bactris simplicifrons var. brevifolia

Bactris simplicifrons var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaf palm, is a variety of the genus Bactris native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its narrow, clustered leaves and robust, erect stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, oval fruits. Its leaves are typically shorter and more compact compared to the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
