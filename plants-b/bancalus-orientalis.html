Bancalus orientalis

Bancalus orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental false blister beetle, is a species of scarab beetle found in various parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, dark-colored body and hardened elytra. The plant association of Bancalus orientalis is primarily with certain species of legumes, where the larvae feed on the roots. Its life cycle involves a pupal stage that occurs in the soil, and the adults are often found near host plants.

This beetle plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat through its interactions with plant communities.
