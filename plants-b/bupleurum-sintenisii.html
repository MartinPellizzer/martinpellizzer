Bupleurum sintenisii

Bupleurum sintenisii, commonly known as the Tibetan chervil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Tibet and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Bupleurum sintenisii is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
