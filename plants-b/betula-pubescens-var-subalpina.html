Betula pubescens var. subalpina

Betula pubescens var. subalpina, commonly known as the subalpine birch, is a variant of the silver birch native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in cold, mountainous environments. The plant has grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are oval with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to harsh conditions, including poor soil and low temperatures.
