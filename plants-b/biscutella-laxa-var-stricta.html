Biscutella laxa var. stricta

Biscutella laxa var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
