Bulbophyllum pectinatum

Bulbophyllum pectinatum, commonly known as the pectinatum orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful floral structures. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, with petals that can resemble feathers or other ornamental shapes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
