Berberis gagnepainii f. pluriflora

Berberis gagnepainii f. pluriflora, commonly known as the multiflorous barberry, is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. The plant is native to China and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.

This variety is noted for its increased flower production compared to the typical species.
