Boehmeria spicata var. microphylla

Boehmeria spicata var. microphylla, commonly known as silky fiber plant or Chinese nettle, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are harvested for their fiber, which is used in making textiles and paper.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
