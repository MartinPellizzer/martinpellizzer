Betula pendula f. pyramidalis

Betula pendula f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a cultivar of the European white willow.

It is characterized by its narrow, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading effect. The leaves are simple, alternate, and lance-shaped, typically turning yellow in the autumn. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
