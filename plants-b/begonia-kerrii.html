Begonia kerrii

Begonia kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

Begonia kerrii prefers well-draining soil and consistent moisture to ensure optimal growth.
