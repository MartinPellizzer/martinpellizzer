Bombax flaviflorum

Bombax flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
