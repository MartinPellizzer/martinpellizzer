Bucharea

Bucharea, commonly known as the "hedgehog cactus," is a genus of cacti native to Mexico and Central America.

These plants are characterized by their cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which give them their distinctive appearance. Bucharea species typically have thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing them to thrive in arid environments. Their flowers are often vibrant and showy, appearing in shades of yellow, orange, or red.

Bucharea plants are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their unique morphology and relatively easy care requirements.
