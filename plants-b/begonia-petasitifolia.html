Begonia petasitifolia

Begonia petasitifolia, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, red flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
