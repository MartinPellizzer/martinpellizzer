Bromus myurus

Bromus myurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Bromus myurus is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common species in regions with temperate climates.
