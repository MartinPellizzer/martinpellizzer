Berberis bykoviana

Berberis bykoviana, commonly known as the Bykov's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, astringent taste. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it survive in arid environments.

Berberis bykoviana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
