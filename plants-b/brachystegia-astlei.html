Brachystegia astlei

Brachystegia astlei, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and East Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Brachystegia astlei is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
