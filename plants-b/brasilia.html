Brasilia

Brasilia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. Brasilia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.

Its botanical name is *Eugenia uniflora*, and it belongs to the Myrtaceae family.
