Bignonia karstenii

Bignonia karstenii, commonly known as the Karsten's crossvine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its long, twisting vines and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bignonia karstenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
