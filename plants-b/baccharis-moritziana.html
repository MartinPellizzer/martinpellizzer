Baccharis moritziana

Baccharis moritziana, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
