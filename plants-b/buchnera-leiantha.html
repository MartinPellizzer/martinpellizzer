Buchnera leiantha

Buchnera leiantha, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Buchnera leiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
