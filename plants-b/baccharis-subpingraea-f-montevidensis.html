Baccharis subpingraea f. montevidensis

Baccharis subpingraea f. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting well to saline environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
