Brassia lehmannii

Brassia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic or lithophytic orchid, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with a distinctive lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Brassia lehmannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
