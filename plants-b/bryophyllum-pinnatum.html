Bryophyllum pinnatum

Bryophyllum pinnatum, commonly known as the "lucky plant" or "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and summer months. Its unique ability to propagate through leaf-borne offsets makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Bryophyllum pinnatum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
