Bulliarda elatinoides

Bulliarda elatinoides, commonly known as the glassy weed, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, translucent cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the species is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and gravelly areas.

Bulliarda elatinoides is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
