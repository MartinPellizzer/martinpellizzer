Begonia atroglandulosa subsp. atroglandulosa

Begonia atroglandulosa subsp.

atroglandulosa, commonly known as the dark glandular begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from pink to red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
