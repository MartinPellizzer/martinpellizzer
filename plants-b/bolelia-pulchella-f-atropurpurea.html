Bolelia pulchella f. atropurpurea

Bolelia pulchella f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered bolelia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its unique coloration and compact growth habit, it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
