Bromus secalinus var. gladewitzii

Bromus secalinus var. gladewitzii, commonly known as the Gladewitz brome, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is usually borne at the top of the stem.

The species is characterized by its small, wind-dispersed seeds and is often found in agricultural landscapes.
