Berberis vulgaris f. alba

Berberis vulgaris f. alba, commonly known as white barberry, is a variant of the barberry plant characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. Its flowers appear in late spring and are followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also serve as a natural hedge or boundary plant.
