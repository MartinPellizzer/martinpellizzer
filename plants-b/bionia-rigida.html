Bionia rigida

Bionia rigida, commonly known as the rigid bionia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features upright, somewhat woody stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose panicles, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Bionia rigida is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a characteristic species of Mediterranean scrublands.
