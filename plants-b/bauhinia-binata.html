Bauhinia binata

Bauhinia binata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia binata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, sunny environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
