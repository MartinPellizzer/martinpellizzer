Bursa viguieri

Bursa viguieri, commonly known as the African burr or false burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that resemble small burrs. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pale yellow or green.

Bursa viguieri is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique seed structures.
