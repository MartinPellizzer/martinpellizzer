Brachypodium sylvaticum subsp. luzoniense

Brachypodium sylvaticum subsp.

luzoniense, commonly known as Luzon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, linear leaves and dense spikelets arranged in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its botanical features include small, two-rowed spikelets with lemma-based florets, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and as a potential forage crop in certain regions.
