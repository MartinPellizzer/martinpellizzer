Bonellia oligantha

Bonellia oligantha, commonly known as the slender bonellia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bonellieae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a slender, creeping stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Bonellia oligantha is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
