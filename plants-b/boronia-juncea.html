Boronia juncea

Boronia juncea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Boronia juncea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
