Brunsvigia grandiflora

Brunsvigia grandiflora, commonly known as the giantkop or African snowball, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces large, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that resemble snowballs, hence its common name. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with a dormant summer period followed by a flowering season in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
