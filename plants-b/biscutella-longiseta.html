Biscutella longiseta

Biscutella longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired biscuitroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive cruciform flowers. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Biscutella longiseta is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often growing in open woodlands and slopes.
