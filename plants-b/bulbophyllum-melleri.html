Bulbophyllum melleri

Bulbophyllum melleri, commonly known as the Meller's orchid, is a fascinating epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded conditions.

Bulbophyllum melleri is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
