Bromopsis kalmii

Bromopsis kalmii, commonly known as Kalm's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-parted flowers in clusters.

Bromopsis kalmii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
