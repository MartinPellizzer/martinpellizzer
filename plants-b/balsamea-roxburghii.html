Balsamea roxburghii

Balsamea roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan balsam, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Balsamea roxburghii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
