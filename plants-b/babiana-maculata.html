Babiana maculata

Babiana maculata, commonly known as the spotted babiana, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its colorful, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with contrasting spots or markings. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that store nutrients for the flowering period. It typically blooms in spring and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Babiana maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
