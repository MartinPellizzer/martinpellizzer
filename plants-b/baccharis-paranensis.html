Baccharis paranensis

Baccharis paranensis, commonly known as the Parana shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
