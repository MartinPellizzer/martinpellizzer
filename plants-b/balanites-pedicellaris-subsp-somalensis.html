Balanites pedicellaris subsp. somalensis

Balanites pedicellaris subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Horn of Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces woody pods containing seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to semi-arid environments.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter and food for local wildlife.
