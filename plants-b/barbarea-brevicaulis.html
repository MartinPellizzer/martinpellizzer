Barbarea brevicaulis

Barbarea brevicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of broadly ovate, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by heart-shaped, seed-containing pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Barbarea brevicaulis is also known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a hardy species in cooler climates.
