Begonia purpurea

Begonia purpurea, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
