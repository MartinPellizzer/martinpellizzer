Bidens pilosa f. simplex

Bidens pilosa f. simplex, commonly known as hairy beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it has a tendency to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in many regions.

The plant's botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its native range.
