Bupleurum candollei var. virgatissimum

Bupleurum candollei var. virgatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow upright, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
