Baphia massaiensis var. cornifolia

Baphia massaiensis var. cornifolia, commonly known as the horned baphia, is a striking plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, palmate leaves that often have a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
