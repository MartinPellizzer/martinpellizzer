Bulbophyllum uncinatum

Bulbophyllum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually fleshy and dark green, adapted for efficient water storage.

The flowers, which are often yellow or greenish-white, bloom in summer and are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts.
