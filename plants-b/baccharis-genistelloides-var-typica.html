Baccharis genistelloides var. typica

Baccharis genistelloides var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
