Batatas tuberosa

Batatas tuberosa, commonly known as sweet potato, is a root vegetable native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, tuberous roots that come in various colors such as orange, white, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces flowering stems that resemble vines. Sweet potatoes are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and essential vitamins like vitamin A.

They are typically grown in warm climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
