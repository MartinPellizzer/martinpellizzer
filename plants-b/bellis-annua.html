Bellis annua

Bellis annua, commonly known as the common daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces white petals with a yellow central disc, resembling a daisy. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Bellis annua is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through self-seeding.
