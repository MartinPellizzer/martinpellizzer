Bignonia grewioides

Bignonia grewioides, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia grewioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
