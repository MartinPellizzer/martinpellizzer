Bromus inermis var. ciliatus

Bromus inermis var. ciliatus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often covered with fine hairs. This plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a shallow root system and is often found in regions with moderate to high precipitation.

The inflorescence consists of a single spike, which is usually erect and slightly compressed.
