Bauhinia micrantha var. puberula

Bauhinia micrantha var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The varietal form, puberula, is distinguished by its fine, velvety pubescence on the leaves and stems.
