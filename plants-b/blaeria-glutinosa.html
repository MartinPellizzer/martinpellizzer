Blaeria glutinosa

Blaeria glutinosa, commonly known as sticky blazer, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which is a key feature in its botanical description. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often arranged in spikes, and are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

The sticky resin produced by Blaeria glutinosa has been traditionally used in local remedies and for various practical applications.
