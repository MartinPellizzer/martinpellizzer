Berberis conferta var. boliviana

Berberis conferta var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, glossy leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders in temperate climates.
