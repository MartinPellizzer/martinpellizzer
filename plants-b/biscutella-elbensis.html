Biscutella elbensis

Biscutella elbensis, commonly known as the Elbe biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Biscutella elbensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
