Bursera obovata

Bursera obovata, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in perfumes and incense.
