Blackstonia acuminata subsp. aestiva

Blackstonia acuminata subsp.

aestiva, commonly known as the summer blackstonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The subspecies is distinguished by its earlier flowering period compared to other subspecies of Blackstonia acuminata.
