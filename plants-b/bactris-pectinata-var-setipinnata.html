Bactris pectinata var. setipinnata

Bactris pectinata var. setipinnata, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
