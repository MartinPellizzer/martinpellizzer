Brassica sphaerorhiza

Brassica sphaerorhiza, commonly known as the globe mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy seed pods that resemble small globes, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy.

Brassica sphaerorhiza is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is adapted to arid environments.
