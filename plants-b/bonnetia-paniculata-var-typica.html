Bonnetia paniculata var. typica

Bonnetia paniculata var. typica, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and woody.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical climates.
