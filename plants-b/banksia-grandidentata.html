Banksia grandidentata

Banksia grandidentata, commonly known as the large-lobed banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces large, woody follicles that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Banksia grandidentata is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various native wildlife species.
