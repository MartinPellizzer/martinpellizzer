Brassica radicata

Brassica radicata, commonly known as wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Brassica radicata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
