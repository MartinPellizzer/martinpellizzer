Brickellia coridifolia

Brickellia coridifolia, commonly known as the scarlet brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
