Baeria

Baeria, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Baeria is known for its strong aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable in its native habitat.
