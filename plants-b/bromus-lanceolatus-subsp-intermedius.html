Bromus lanceolatus subsp. intermedius

Bromus lanceolatus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikelets that are typically arranged in compact clusters.

This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, producing small, pale green to brown flowers.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
