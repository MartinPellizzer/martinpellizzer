Blepharis transvaalensis

Blepharis transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal grassbladder, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
