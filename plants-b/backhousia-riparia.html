Backhousia riparia

Backhousia riparia, commonly known as the river sassafras, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, spindle-shaped fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near watercourses.

Backhousia riparia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for its medicinal properties.
