Berberis kashmirana

Berberis kashmirana, commonly known as Kashmir barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The berries are edible but are typically used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
