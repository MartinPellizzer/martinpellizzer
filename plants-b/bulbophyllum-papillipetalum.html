Bulbophyllum papillipetalum

Bulbophyllum papillipetalum, commonly known as the papillipetalum orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its intricate, hairy-like structures on its petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Bulbophyllum papillipetalum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
