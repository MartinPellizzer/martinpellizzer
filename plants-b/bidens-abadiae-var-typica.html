Bidens abadiae var. typica

Bidens abadiae var. typica, commonly known as the Abadia's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a serrated edge.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by distinctive bracts that give the plant its common name.
