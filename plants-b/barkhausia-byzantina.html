Barkhausia byzantina

Barkhausia byzantina, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, peeling bark that reveals layers of smooth, pale skin beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Barkhausia byzantina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
