Bidens cernua var. minima

Bidens cernua var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow flowers with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
