Brassaia palmata

Brassaia palmata, commonly known as the red-flowered palm or red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers. The plant has palm-like leaves that are pinnately compound, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Brassaia palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
