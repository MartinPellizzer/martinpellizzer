Buceras foenum-graecum

Buceras foenum-graecum, commonly known as the horse bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and pods that contain several seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering period that typically occurs in late spring.
