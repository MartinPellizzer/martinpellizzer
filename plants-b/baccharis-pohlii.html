Baccharis pohlii

Baccharis pohlii, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

Baccharis pohlii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
