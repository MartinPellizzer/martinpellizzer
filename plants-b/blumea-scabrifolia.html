Blumea scabrifolia

Blumea scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

Blumea scabrifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
