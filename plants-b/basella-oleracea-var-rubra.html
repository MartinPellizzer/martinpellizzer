Basella oleracea var. rubra

Basella oleracea var. rubra, commonly known as red-leaf basella, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red leaves, which contrast with its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, round, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible parts.

Its leaves are used in various cuisines, particularly in Southeast Asian dishes.
