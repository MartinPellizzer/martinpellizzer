Baccharis umbelliformis var. ocellata

Baccharis umbelliformis var. ocellata, commonly known as the spotted baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage with distinctive white spots on the leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
