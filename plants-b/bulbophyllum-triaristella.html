Bulbophyllum triaristella

Bulbophyllum triaristella, commonly known as the three-spurred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with three prominent spurred structures on the lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-nutrient conditions.

Its unique floral morphology makes it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
