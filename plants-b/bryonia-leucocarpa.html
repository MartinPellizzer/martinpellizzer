Bryonia leucocarpa

Bryonia leucocarpa, commonly known as white bryony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and squash, and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by orange, spiny fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough texture.

Bryonia leucocarpa is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as bryonin and cucurbitacin.
