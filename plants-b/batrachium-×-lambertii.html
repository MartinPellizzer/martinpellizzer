Batrachium × lambertii

Batrachium × lambertii, commonly known as the Lambert's slipper orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of purple and white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Batrachium × lambertii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
