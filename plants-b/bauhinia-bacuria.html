Bauhinia bacuria

Bauhinia bacuria, commonly known as the yellow bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, papery yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a flat, winged pod that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia bacuria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
