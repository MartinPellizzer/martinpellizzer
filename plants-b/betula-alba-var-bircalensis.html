Betula alba var. bircalensis

Betula alba var. bircalensis, commonly known as the Bircalensis birch, is a variant of the white birch native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping growth habit and distinctive white, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish inner layer. The leaves are typically oval to triangular in shape, with serrated margins and a pale green underside. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
