Bromus variegatus

Bromus variegatus, commonly known as the variable brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green colored blades that often display a variegated appearance. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, with two to three bracts. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate climates.

Bromus variegatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
