Brosimum uleanum

Brosimum uleanum, commonly known as the Uleanum balsa, is a species of tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its lightweight wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
