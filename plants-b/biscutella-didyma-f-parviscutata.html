Biscutella didyma f. parviscutata

Biscutella didyma f. parviscutata, commonly known as the dwarf biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are pink to purple, borne in clusters at the stem tips. The plant is characterized by its compact form and is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many economically important plants such as cabbages and mustards.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky or arid soils.
