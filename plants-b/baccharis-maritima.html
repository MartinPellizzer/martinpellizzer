Baccharis maritima

Baccharis maritima, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or saline soils near the coast. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Baccharis maritima is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh maritime environments.
