Bromopsis ramosa subsp. fedtschenkoi

Bromopsis ramosa subsp.

fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's bromus, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
