Bulbophyllum pachypus

Bulbophyllum pachypus, commonly known as the giant bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces numerous showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts in the orchid community.
