Berberis spinosa

Berberis spinosa, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features stout, spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. Berberis spinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in hedges or as a decorative plant in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
