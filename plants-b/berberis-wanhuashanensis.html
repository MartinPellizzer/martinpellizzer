Berberis wanhuashanensis

Berberis wanhuashanensis, commonly known as the Wanhuashan barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
