Boronia spathulata var. elatior

Boronia spathulata var. elatior, commonly known as the broad-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle blue or purple tinge. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are broad and oval, often with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Boronia spathulata var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and visual interest.
