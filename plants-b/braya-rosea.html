Braya rosea

Braya rosea, commonly known as the pink rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered leaves and delicate pink or white flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Braya rosea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
