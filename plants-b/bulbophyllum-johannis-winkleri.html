Bulbophyllum johannis-winkleri

Bulbophyllum johannis-winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, typically found on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces intricate, showy inflorescences with fragrant, often colorful flowers that attract pollinators. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
