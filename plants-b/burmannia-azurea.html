Burmannia azurea

Burmannia azurea, commonly known as the blue burmannia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which are often borne on long, slender stalks. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Burmannia azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
