Bambusa tabacaria

Bambusa tabacaria, commonly known as the tobacco bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, bright green leaves that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in landscaping.

Bambusa tabacaria is also valued for its fast growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
