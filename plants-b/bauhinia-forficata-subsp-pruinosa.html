Bauhinia forficata subsp. pruinosa

Bauhinia forficata subsp.

pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage and delicate floral display.

It is often cultivated as a shade plant in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial to full sun.
