Bignonia varians

Bignonia varians, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant blooms.

Bignonia varians is also known for its ability to climb and can be trained on trellises or fences.
