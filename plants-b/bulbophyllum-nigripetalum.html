Bulbophyllum nigripetalum

Bulbophyllum nigripetalum, commonly known as the black-petaled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces showy, dark-colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
