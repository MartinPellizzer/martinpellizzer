Berberis tengchongensis

Berberis tengchongensis, commonly known as Tengchong barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
