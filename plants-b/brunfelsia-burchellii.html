Brunfelsia burchellii

Brunfelsia burchellii, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
