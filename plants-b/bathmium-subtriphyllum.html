Bathmium subtriphyllum

Bathmium subtriphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf bathmium, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its small, pale green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
