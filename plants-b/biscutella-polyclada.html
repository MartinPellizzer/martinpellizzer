Biscutella polyclada

Biscutella polyclada, commonly known as the many-stemmed biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Biscutella polyclada is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils.
