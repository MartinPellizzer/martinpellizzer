Berberis formosana

Berberis formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and autumn color.

Berberis formosana is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
