Bulbophyllum coriscense

Bulbophyllum coriscense, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
