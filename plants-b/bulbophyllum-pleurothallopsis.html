Bulbophyllum pleurothallopsis

Bulbophyllum pleurothallopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
