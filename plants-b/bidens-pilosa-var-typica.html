Bidens pilosa var. typica

Bidens pilosa var. typica, commonly known as hairy beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spiny, bristly seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas, along roadsides, and in waste places.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
