Bursera linanoe

Bursera linanoe, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, red fruits that split open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in perfumes and traditional medicine.
