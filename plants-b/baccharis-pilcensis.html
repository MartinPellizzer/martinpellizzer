Baccharis pilcensis

Baccharis pilcensis, commonly known as the silky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in its native arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Baccharis pilcensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
