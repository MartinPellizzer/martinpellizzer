Bromus mutabilis var. velutinus

Bromus mutabilis var. velutinus, commonly known as velvet brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

Velvet brome is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
