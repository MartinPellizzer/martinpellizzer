Bromopsis richardsonii

Bromopsis richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. This plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Bromopsis richardsonii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
