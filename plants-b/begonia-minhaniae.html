Begonia minhaniae

Begonia minhaniae, commonly known as the Minho begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to almost black. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding a splash of color to its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners interested in rare plants.
