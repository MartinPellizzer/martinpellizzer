Bermudiana graminifolia

Bermudiana graminifolia, commonly known as the Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the world.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and its ability to form dense, mat-like growth patterns. This plant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions. It is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

However, it can be invasive in certain areas, making it both a useful and sometimes problematic plant.
