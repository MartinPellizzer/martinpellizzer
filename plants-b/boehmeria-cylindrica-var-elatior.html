Boehmeria cylindrica var. elatior

Boehmeria cylindrica var. elatior, commonly known as the broadleaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its fibrous stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
