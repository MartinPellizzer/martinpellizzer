Basutica aberrans

Basutica aberrans, commonly known as the Basutic weed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Africa. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
