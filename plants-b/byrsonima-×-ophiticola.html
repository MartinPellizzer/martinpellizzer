Byrsonima × ophiticola

Byrsonima × ophiticola, commonly known as the hybrid persimmon, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Byrsonima sericea and Byrsonima cauliflora.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
