Becium grandiflorum subsp. densiflorum

Becium grandiflorum subsp.

densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower becium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that contributes to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
