Betula pubescens f. amoena

Betula pubescens f. amoena, commonly known as the silver birch variant, is a botanical form of the silver birch tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and slender, pendulous branches. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
