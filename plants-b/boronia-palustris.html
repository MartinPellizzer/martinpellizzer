Boronia palustris

Boronia palustris, commonly known as swamp boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, often near the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Boronia palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
