Borodinia missouriensis

Borodinia missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri eveningflower, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the late afternoon, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Borodinia missouriensis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
