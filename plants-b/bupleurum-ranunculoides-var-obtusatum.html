Bupleurum ranunculoides var. obtusatum

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. obtusatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves, which are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in a loose cluster, with five petals that are often notched at the tips.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
