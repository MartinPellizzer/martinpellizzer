Bromus sericeus

Bromus sericeus, commonly known as silky brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Bromus sericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some ecological restoration projects.
