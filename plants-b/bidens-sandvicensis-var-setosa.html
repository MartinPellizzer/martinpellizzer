Bidens sandvicensis var. setosa

Bidens sandvicensis var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
