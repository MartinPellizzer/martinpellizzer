Beringeria hirsuta

Beringeria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy beringia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the Arctic regions of Alaska and Siberia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and possesses hairy, dark green leaves that are adapted to the harsh, cold climate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize open, tundra-like habitats. Beringeria hirsuta is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and its potential role in understanding ancient plant communities.

Its limited distribution and fragile ecology make it a vulnerable species in the face of climate change.
