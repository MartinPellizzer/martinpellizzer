Blephilia subnuda

Blephilia subnuda, commonly known as the naked-leaved bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often appear hairless, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
