Bothriochloa glabra var. epunctata

Bothriochloa glabra var. epunctata, commonly known as the punctate beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

This grass is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to prevent soil erosion.
