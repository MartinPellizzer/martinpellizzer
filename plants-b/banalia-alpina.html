Banalia alpina

Banalia alpina, commonly known as the alpine banalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters.

Banalia alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
