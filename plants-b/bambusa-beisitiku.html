Bambusa beisitiku

Bambusa beisitiku, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Bambusa beisitiku is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for erosion control and habitat creation.
