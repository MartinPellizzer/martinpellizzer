Boesenbergia magna

Boesenbergia magna, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a houseplant.

Boesenbergia magna is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
