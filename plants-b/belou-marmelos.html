Belou marmelos

Belou marmelos, commonly known as the Indian star fruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the distinctive star-shaped fruit. The fruit's skin is smooth and waxy, turning from green to yellow or orange as it ripens.

It is valued for its tart-sweet flavor and is widely cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
