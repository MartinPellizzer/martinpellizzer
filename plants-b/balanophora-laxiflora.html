Balanophora laxiflora

Balanophora laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered balanophore, is a holoparasitic plant belonging to the family Balanophoraceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of other plants. The plant has a short, stocky stem with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its reproductive structures are adapted for pollination by insects, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

Due to its specialized parasitic lifestyle, it is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid forest environments.
