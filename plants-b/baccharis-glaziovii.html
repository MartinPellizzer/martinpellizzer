Baccharis glaziovii

Baccharis glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
