Brosimum tessmannii

Brosimum tessmannii, commonly known as the Mexican mulberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common mulberry. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually pubescent. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red or black when ripe.

The tree is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types and elevations.
