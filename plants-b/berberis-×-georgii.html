Berberis × georgii

Berberis × georgii, commonly known as the Oregon grape.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its dense, spiny stems and dark green, holly-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, blue-black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitat.
