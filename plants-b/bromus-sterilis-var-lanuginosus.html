Bromus sterilis var. lanuginosus

Bromus sterilis var. lanuginosus, commonly known as the soft-haired brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural landscapes.
