Brachypodium ramosum var. gigas

Brachypodium ramosum var. gigas, commonly known as giant false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
