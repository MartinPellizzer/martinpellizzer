Baccharis bigelovii

Baccharis bigelovii, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Baccharis bigelovii is also known for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
