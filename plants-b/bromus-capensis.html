Bromus capensis

Bromus capensis, commonly known as the Cape bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Bromus capensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
