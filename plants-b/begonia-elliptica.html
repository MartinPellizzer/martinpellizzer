Begonia elliptica

Begonia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in both gardens and as a houseplant.
