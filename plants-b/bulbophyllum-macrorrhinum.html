Bulbophyllum macrorrhinum

Bulbophyllum macrorrhinum, commonly known as the large-nosed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of small, showy blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
