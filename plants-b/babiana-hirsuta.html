Babiana hirsuta

Babiana hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy babiana, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Babiana hirsuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant floral displays.
