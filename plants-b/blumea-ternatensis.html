Blumea ternatensis

Blumea ternatensis, commonly known as the Java bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.
