Brassica campestris var. wakana

Brassica campestris var. wakana, commonly known as Japanese mustard or wakame mustard, is a cultivated variety of the mustard plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its aromatic seeds and is also used in herbal remedies due to its medicinal properties.
