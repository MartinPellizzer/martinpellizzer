Brachiaria subquadripara var. hirsuta

Brachiaria subquadripara var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy brachiaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and hairy foliage, which helps in reducing water loss and deterring herbivores. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage production due to its high nutritional value.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
