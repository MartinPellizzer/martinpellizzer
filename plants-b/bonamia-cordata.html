Bonamia cordata

Bonamia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Bonamia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
