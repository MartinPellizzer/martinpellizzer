Begonia × fuscomaculata

Begonia × fuscomaculata, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of foliage patterns.

Its leaves are typically marked with striking variegation, featuring combinations of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative planting.
