Barleria longipes var. puberula

Barleria longipes var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically ranging in color from white to pink. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
