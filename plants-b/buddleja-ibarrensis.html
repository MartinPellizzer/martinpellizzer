Buddleja ibarrensis

Buddleja ibarrensis, commonly known as the Ibarren's bushmaster, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its ability to thrive in rocky soils. Its flowers, which are typically pink to purple in color, attract a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Buddleja ibarrensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
