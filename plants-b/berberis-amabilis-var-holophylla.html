Berberis amabilis var. holophylla

Berberis amabilis var. holophylla, commonly known as the golden thread barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, arching branches adorned with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact size and vibrant seasonal color make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
