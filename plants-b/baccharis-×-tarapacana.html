Baccharis × tarapacana

Baccharis × tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean plumevetch, is a hybrid shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and showy, fluffy yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid climates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
