Bauhinia howei

Bauhinia howei, commonly known as the Hong Kong orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia howei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical regions.
