Biscutella frutescens var. papillosa

Biscutella frutescens var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky biscutella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
