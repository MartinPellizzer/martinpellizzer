Bellevalia fallax

Bellevalia fallax, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Bellevalia fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
