Bicornella ochyrae

Bicornella ochyrae, commonly known as the red-tailed ant plant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and its ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants. The plant's morphology includes a dense mat of leaf-like structures that provide shelter for its ant inhabitants. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized adaptations for nutrient acquisition in canopy environments.

Bicornella ochyrae is also notable for its role in ecological interactions within its native habitat.
