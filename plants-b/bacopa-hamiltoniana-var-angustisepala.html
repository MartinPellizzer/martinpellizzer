Bacopa hamiltoniana var. angustisepala

Bacopa hamiltoniana var. angustisepala, commonly known as narrow-lobed water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported cognitive and anti-inflammatory properties.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in water gardens.
