Buphthalmum helianthoides

Buphthalmum helianthoides, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
