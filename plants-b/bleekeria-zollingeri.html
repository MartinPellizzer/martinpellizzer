Bleekeria zollingeri

Bleekeria zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's bleeker, is a unique plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
