Bulbophyllum excavatum

Bulbophyllum excavatum, commonly known as the hollow-leaf orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, hollowed-out leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
