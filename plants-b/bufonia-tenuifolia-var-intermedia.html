Bufonia tenuifolia var. intermedia

Bufonia tenuifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bufonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that often produces multiple shoots.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
