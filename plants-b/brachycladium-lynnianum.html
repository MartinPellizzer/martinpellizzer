Brachycladium lynnianum

Brachycladium lynnianum, commonly known as the Lynn's threeawn, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, three-awned florets, characteristic of the genus Brachycladium. It thrives in full sun and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and coastal dunes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
