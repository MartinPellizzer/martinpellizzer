Buddleja jamesonii

Buddleja jamesonii, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, spires of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.

Buddleja jamesonii is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
