Bocquillonia spicata

Bocquillonia spicata, commonly known as the spiny leaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Bocquillonia spicata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
