Bertiera chrysantha

Bertiera chrysantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant appearance during the flowering season. The plant has pinnate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Bertiera chrysantha is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
