Bupleurum foliosum var. mauritanicum

Bupleurum foliosum var. mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
