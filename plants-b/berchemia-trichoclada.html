Berchemia trichoclada

Berchemia trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy berchemia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, wiry stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Berchemia trichoclada is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
