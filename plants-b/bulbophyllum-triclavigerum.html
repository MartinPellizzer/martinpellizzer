Bulbophyllum triclavigerum

Bulbophyllum triclavigerum, commonly known as the three-spurred orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three prominent spurred lobes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
