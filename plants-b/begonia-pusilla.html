Begonia pusilla

Begonia pusilla, commonly known as the miniature begonia, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal, Begonia pusilla is a popular choice for container gardening and as a decorative plant.
