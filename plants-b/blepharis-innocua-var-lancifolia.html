Blepharis innocua var. lancifolia

Blepharis innocua var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wireweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are linear and pointed, giving the plant its varietal name "lancifolia.". It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in full sun.
