Bactris martiana

Bactris martiana, commonly known as the red raphia, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Bactris martiana is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
