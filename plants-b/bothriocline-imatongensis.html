Bothriocline imatongensis

Bothriocline imatongensis, commonly known as the Imatong mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Sudan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverine areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a delicate, white to pale yellow color.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
