Bomarea subfalcata

Bomarea subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often bluish-green in color and have a pronounced midrib. The plant produces showy, spatulate inflorescences with bright yellow spadices, making it a visually appealing species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
