Brachypodium sylvaticum var. majus

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. majus, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
