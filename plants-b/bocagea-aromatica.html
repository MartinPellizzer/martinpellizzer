Bocagea aromatica

Bocagea aromatica, commonly known as sweetbriar or fragrant bocagea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its fragrant foliage and showy blooms make it an ornamental plant valued in horticulture.

Bocagea aromatica is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
