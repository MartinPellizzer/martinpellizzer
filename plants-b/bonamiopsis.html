Bonamiopsis

Bonamiopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Bonamiopsis is considered a conservation concern.
