Begonia polyandra

Begonia polyandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia polyandra is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
