Boscia foetida subsp. rehmanniana

Boscia foetida subsp.

rehmanniana, commonly known as the sour fig or wild sour plum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but have a sour taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
