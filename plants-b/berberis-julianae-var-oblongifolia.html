Berberis julianae var. oblongifolia

Berberis julianae var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Julian barberry, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features small, spiny leaves that are typically oval in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
