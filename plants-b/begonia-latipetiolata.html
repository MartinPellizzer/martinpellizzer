Begonia latipetiolata

Begonia latipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-petioled begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
