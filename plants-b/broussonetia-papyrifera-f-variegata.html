Broussonetia papyrifera f. variegata

Broussonetia papyrifera f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated paper mulberry, is a variant of the paper mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are used in traditional papermaking, similar to its non-variegated counterpart.
