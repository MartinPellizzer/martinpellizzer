Bonnaya punctata

Bonnaya punctata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or near water sources.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
