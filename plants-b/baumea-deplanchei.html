Baumea deplanchei

Baumea deplanchei, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Baumea deplanchei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
