Bryonia ficifolia

Bryonia ficifolia, commonly known as the Jerusalem cherry, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are toxic and contain alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.
