Bursera paniculata

Bursera paniculata, commonly known as the white cedar or Mexican white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by spherical, orange-red fruits.

Bursera paniculata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
