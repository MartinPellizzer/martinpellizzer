Biscutella coronopifolia var. pinnatifida

Biscutella coronopifolia var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
