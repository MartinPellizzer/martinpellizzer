Bambusa superba

Bambusa superba, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizome system allows for rapid propagation and the formation of large clumps.

Bambusa superba is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
