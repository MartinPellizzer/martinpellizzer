Bidens brasiliensis

Bidens brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features slender, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two ray florets and a distinctive, bristly central disk. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Bidens brasiliensis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
