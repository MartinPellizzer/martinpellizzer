Bulbine louwii

Bulbine louwii, commonly known as the Cape bulbous, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a versatile plant for both garden and horticultural use.
