Ballota nigra subsp. velutina

Ballota nigra subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet ballota, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically purple or violet in color.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
