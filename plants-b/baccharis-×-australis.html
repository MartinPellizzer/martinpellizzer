Baccharis × australis

Baccharis × australis, commonly known as the Australian tea tree, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
