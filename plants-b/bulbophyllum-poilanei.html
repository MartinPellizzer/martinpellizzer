Bulbophyllum poilanei

Bulbophyllum poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters, with petals that are intricately patterned.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
