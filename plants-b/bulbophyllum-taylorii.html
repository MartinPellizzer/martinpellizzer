Bulbophyllum taylorii

Bulbophyllum taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less hospitable conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Bulbophyllum taylorii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
