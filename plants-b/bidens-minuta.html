Bidens minuta

Bidens minuta, commonly known as the small beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, bristly seeds that are easily dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in moist, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
