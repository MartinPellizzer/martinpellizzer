Brassica napus var. pabularia

Brassica napus var. pabularia, commonly known as wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces long, slender pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Brassica napus var. pabularia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
