Begonia bhutanensis

Begonia bhutanensis, commonly known as the Bhutan wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
