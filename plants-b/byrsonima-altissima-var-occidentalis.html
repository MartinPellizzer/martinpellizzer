Byrsonima altissima var. occidentalis

Byrsonima altissima var. occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The botanical features of this species make it both ecologically significant and valuable for its nutritional properties.
