Bromopsis paulsenii subsp. turkestanica

Bromopsis paulsenii subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it resilient in harsh climates.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
