Ballota hirsuta var. bullata

Ballota hirsuta var. bullata, commonly known as the hairy ballota or bullate ballota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
