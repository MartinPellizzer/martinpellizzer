Brachiaria advena

Brachiaria advena, commonly known as Brazilian grass or wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and pastures.

Brachiaria advena is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it both a useful forage crop and an invasive species in some regions.
