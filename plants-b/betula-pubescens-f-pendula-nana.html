Betula pubescens f. pendula-nana

Betula pubescens f. pendula-nana, commonly known as the weeping dwarf birch, is a shrub-like form of the silver birch.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous branches that create a cascading appearance. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in northern regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
