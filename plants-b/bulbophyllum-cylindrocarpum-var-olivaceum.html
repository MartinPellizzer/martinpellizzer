Bulbophyllum cylindrocarpum var. olivaceum

Bulbophyllum cylindrocarpum var. olivaceum, commonly known as the olive-fruited bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit is cylindrical and turns olive-green as it matures, giving the variety its common name.
