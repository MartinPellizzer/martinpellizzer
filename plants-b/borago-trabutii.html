Borago trabutii

Borago trabutii, commonly known as the Trabut's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in dense spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Borago trabutii is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
