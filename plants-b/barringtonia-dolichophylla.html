Barringtonia dolichophylla

Barringtonia dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a unique fruit that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
