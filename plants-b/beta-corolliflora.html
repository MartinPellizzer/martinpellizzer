Beta corolliflora

Beta corolliflora, commonly known as the coral flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of white or pinkish flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.

Beta corolliflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
