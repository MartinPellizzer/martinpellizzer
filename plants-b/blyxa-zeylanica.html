Blyxa zeylanica

Blyxa zeylanica, commonly known as the water sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow waters, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green or bluish-green in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and are often submerged or partially submerged.

Blyxa zeylanica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
