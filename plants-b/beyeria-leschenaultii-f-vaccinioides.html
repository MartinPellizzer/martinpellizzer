Beyeria leschenaultii f. vaccinioides

Beyeria leschenaultii f. vaccinioides, commonly known as the pink-flowered form of the species, is a unique variant of the Beyeria genus.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant is native to South America, particularly regions of Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
