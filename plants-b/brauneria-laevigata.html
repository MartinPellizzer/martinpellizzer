Brauneria laevigata

Brauneria laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf brauneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

Brauneria laevigata is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
