Byrsopteris aristata

Byrsopteris aristata, commonly known as the arrow fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, arching fronds that bear small, clustered spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Byrsopteris aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
