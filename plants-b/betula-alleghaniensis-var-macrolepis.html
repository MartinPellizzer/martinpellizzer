Betula alleghaniensis var. macrolepis

Betula alleghaniensis var. macrolepis, commonly known as the Allegheny black birch, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark that becomes fissured with age, and it produces small, yellowish-green catkins in the spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along river valleys.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
