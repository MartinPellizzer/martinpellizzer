Borzicactus aureiflorus

Borzicactus aureiflorus, commonly known as the golden flower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a spineless cactus with a cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Borzicactus aureiflorus is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
