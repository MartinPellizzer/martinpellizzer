Bakeriella dulcifica

Bakeriella dulcifica, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature and contain a single seed.

Bakeriella dulcifica is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments due to its deep root system and water-storing tissues.
