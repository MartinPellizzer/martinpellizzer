Begonia pediophylla

Begonia pediophylla, commonly known as the foot-leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, with leaves that are broad, heart-shaped, and often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
