Brassaiopsis alpina

Brassaiopsis alpina, commonly known as the alpine brassaiopsis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
