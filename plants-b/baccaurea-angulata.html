Baccaurea angulata

Baccaurea angulata, commonly known as the angular baccaurea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its angular, ridged stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
