Bignonia heptaphylla

Bignonia heptaphylla, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bignonia heptaphylla is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
