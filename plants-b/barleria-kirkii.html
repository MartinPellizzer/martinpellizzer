Barleria kirkii

Barleria kirkii, commonly known as the red barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Barleria kirkii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
