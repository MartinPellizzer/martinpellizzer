Brunia sacculata

Brunia sacculata, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping.

Brunia sacculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
