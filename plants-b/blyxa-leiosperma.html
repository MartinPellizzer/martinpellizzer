Blyxa leiosperma

Blyxa leiosperma, commonly known as the smooth-leaved arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

Blyxa leiosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
