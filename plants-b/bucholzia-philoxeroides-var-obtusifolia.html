Bucholzia philoxeroides var. obtusifolia

Bucholzia philoxeroides var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved burr tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense, sprawling growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in its native habitat.
