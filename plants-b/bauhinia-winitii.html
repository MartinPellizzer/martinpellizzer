Bauhinia winitii

Bauhinia winitii, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for warm climates.

Bauhinia winitii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
