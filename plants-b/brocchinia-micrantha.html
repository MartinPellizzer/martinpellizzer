Brocchinia micrantha

Brocchinia micrantha, commonly known as the pitcher plant, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its unique rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant's specialized leaves are adapted to trap insects, which provide nutrients in nutrient-poor environments. Its vibrant green foliage and striking red flowers make it a visually appealing species.

Brocchinia micrantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique morphology and ecological interest.
