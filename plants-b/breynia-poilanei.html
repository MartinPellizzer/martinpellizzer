Breynia poilanei

Breynia poilanei, commonly known as the red-breynia, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne on short, hairy stalks. Breynia poilanei is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting succulent features that help it retain moisture.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
