Bromus cabrerensis

Bromus cabrerensis, commonly known as the Canary Island brome, is a grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both bisexual and sterile florets. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Bromus cabrerensis is adapted to the mild, maritime climate of its native region.
