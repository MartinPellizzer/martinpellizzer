Besleria affinis

Besleria affinis, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often with strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from white to pink or red, and are pollinated by insects and birds.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
