Blepharocalyx villosus var. uniflorus

Blepharocalyx villosus var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered velvet tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
