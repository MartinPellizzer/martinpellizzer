Bouteloua simplex var. actinochloides

Bouteloua simplex var. actinochloides, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it a common component of native grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
