Barringtonia jebbiana

Barringtonia jebbiana, commonly known as the Malayan white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night. The tree has a straight trunk with a smooth, pale bark and produces a heavy, fragrant fruit that splits open to release its seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

This species is valued for its timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
