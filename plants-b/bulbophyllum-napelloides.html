Bulbophyllum napelloides

Bulbophyllum napelloides, commonly known as the Napelloides orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients, which helps the plant survive in its tropical environment.

It thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
