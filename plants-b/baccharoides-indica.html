Baccharoides indica

Baccharoides indica, commonly known as the Indian baccharis, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
