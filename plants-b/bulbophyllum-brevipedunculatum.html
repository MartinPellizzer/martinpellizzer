Bulbophyllum brevipedunculatum

Bulbophyllum brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the short-stalked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
