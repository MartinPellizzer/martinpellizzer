Berberis aristata var. coriaria

Berberis aristata var. coriaria, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
