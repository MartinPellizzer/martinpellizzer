Baculellum articulatum

Baculellum articulatum, commonly known as the jointed whipgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are often segmented or jointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its narrow leaves are usually smooth and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Baculellum articulatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
