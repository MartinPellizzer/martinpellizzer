Bistorta vivipara subsp. macounii

Bistorta vivipara subsp.

macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's bistort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces tall, flowering stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
