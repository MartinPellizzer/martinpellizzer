Banisteria membranifolia f. subglabrata

Banisteria membranifolia f. subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved banisteria, is a rare and unique variant of the Banisteria genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, with long, slender stems that support its delicate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a striking contrast of colors.

This botanical variant is of interest to horticulturists and botanists due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution.
