Ballota africana

Ballota africana, commonly known as African ballota, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ballota africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
