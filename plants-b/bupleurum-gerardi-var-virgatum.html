Bupleurum gerardi var. virgatum

Bupleurum gerardi var. virgatum, commonly known as the Virginia goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, hollow stems and fern-like, pinnately divided leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale blue, and appear in clustered umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The roots are fibrous and have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
