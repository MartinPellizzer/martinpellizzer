Bolanthus hirsutus var. alpinus

Bolanthus hirsutus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine hirsute bolanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and high-altitude conditions. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in arid or windy conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
