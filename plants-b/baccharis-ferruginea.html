Baccharis ferruginea

Baccharis ferruginea, commonly known as rusty baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish-brown, rough bark and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental features make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
