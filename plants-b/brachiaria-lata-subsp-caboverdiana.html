Brachiaria lata subsp. caboverdiana

Brachiaria lata subsp.

caboverdiana, commonly known as the Cape Verde brachiaria, is a perennial grass native to the Cape Verde Islands. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its use in forage and erosion control in its native habitat.
