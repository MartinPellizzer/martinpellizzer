Buddleja cestriflora

Buddleja cestriflora, commonly known as the woolly buddleia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its nectar-rich blooms attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Buddleja cestriflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different growing conditions.
