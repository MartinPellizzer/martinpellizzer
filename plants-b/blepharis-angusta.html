Blepharis angusta

Blepharis angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Blepharis angusta is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a common sight in desert environments.
