Bonaga hircina

Bonaga hircina, commonly known as the wild fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered fruiting structures. The plant produces small, green fig-like fruits that mature to a yellowish or reddish color. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
