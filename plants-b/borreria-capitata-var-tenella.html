Borreria capitata var. tenella

Borreria capitata var. tenella, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features compact, upright growth with small, narrow leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
