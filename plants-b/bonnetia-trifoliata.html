Bonnetia trifoliata

Bonnetia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in branched inflorescences.

Bonnetia trifoliata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
