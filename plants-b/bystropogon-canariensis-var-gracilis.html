Bystropogon canariensis var. gracilis

Bystropogon canariensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the Canary Island dragon tree, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
