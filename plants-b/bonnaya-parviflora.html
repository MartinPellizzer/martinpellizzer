Bonnaya parviflora

Bonnaya parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bonnaya, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Bonnaya parviflora is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun and low moisture conditions.
