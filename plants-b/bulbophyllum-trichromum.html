Bulbophyllum trichromum

Bulbophyllum trichromum, commonly known as the Three-colored Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate and colorful inflorescence, which typically features three distinct color zones. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often arranged in a dense cluster. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum trichromum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
