Bupleurum dahuricum

Bupleurum dahuricum, commonly known as Chinese thornapple, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Bupleurum dahuricum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
