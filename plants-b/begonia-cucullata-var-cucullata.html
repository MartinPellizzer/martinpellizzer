Begonia cucullata var. cucullata

Begonia cucullata var. cucullata, commonly known as the hooded begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining, rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
