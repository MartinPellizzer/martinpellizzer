Byrsonima ligustrifolia f. oblonga

Byrsonima ligustrifolia f. oblonga, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry or oblonga byrsonima, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant has elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
