Blechnum hastatum f. punctatum

Blechnum hastatum f. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted maidenhair fern, is a varietal form of the maidenhair fern characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, shaded habitats. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance and a unique spotted or marbled pattern on the underside. The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and indoor cultivation.
