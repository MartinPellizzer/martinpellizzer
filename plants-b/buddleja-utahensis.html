Buddleja utahensis

Buddleja utahensis, commonly known as the Utah bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
