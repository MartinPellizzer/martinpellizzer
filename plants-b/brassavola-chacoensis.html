Brassavola chacoensis

Brassavola chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features linear to lanceolate leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
