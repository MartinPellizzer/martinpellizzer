Begonia chlorocarpa

Begonia chlorocarpa, commonly known as the greenleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
