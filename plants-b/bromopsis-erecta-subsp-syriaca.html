Bromopsis erecta subsp. syriaca

Bromopsis erecta subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in mountainous regions and can thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
