Baltimora recta var. scolospermum

Baltimora recta var. scolospermum, commonly known as the scarlet bracted bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red or orange tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its botanical features include showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold, colorful displays.
