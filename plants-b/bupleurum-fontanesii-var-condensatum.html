Bupleurum fontanesii var. condensatum

Bupleurum fontanesii var. condensatum, commonly known as the denseleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
