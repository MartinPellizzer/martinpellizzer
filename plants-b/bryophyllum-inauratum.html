Bryophyllum inauratum

Bryophyllum inauratum, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are also capable of vegetative propagation, as they can root and grow into new plants when placed on moist soil.

Native to Mexico and parts of Central America, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
