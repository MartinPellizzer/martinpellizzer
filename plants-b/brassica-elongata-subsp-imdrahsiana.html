Brassica elongata subsp. imdrahsiana

Brassica elongata subsp.

imdrahsiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri mustard, is a wild Brassicaceae species native to the Himalayan region. It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential for hybridization with other Brassica species.
