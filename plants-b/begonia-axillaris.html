Begonia axillaris

Begonia axillaris, commonly known as the axillary begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a fragrant scent. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia axillaris is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental value and ease of care.
