Brassica oleracea subsp. insularis

Brassica oleracea subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Mediterranean cabbage, is a wild Brassicaceae species native to the Mediterranean region. It is a biennial plant characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in a dense inflorescence, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly peppery flavor, making it a potential vegetable crop.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found growing in rocky or coastal areas.
