Begonia wattii

Begonia wattii, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
