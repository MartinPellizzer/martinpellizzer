Boronia denticulata subsp. whoogarupensis

Boronia denticulata subsp.

whoogarupensis, commonly known as the Whoogarup boronia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and near-coastal regions. Its flowering period typically occurs during the cooler months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitat.
