Berberis vulgaris f. violacea

Berberis vulgaris f. violacea, commonly known as the purple barberry, is a compact shrub characterized by its dark, glossy foliage.

Its leaves are typically a deep purple to bronze color, especially during the growing season, which distinguishes it from the more common green-leaved varieties. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
