Brassica napus var. rutila

Brassica napus var. rutila, commonly known as the yellow napus, is a variant of the oilseed rape species.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The botanical features of B. napus var. rutila make it a valuable plant for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
