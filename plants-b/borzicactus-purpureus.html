Borzicactus purpureus

Borzicactus purpureus, commonly known as the purple-borze, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Borzicactus purpureus is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.
