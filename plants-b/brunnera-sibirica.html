Brunnera sibirica

Brunnera sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
