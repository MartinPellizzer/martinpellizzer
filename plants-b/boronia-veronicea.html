Boronia veronicea

Boronia veronicea, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Boronia veronicea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
