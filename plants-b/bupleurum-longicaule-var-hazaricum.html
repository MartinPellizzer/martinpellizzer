Bupleurum longicaule var. hazaricum

Bupleurum longicaule var. hazaricum, commonly known as the Hazarica Bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, displaying a pale blue to white color.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
