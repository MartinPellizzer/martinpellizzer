Bambusa nana f. viridistriata

Bambusa nana f. viridistriata, commonly known as the variegated dwarf bamboo, is a compact, ornamental bamboo species native to China.

It features striking, variegated leaves with green, white, and yellow patterns, enhancing its visual appeal. The plant grows in a clumping form, reaching a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it suitable for small gardens or container planting. Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round color and texture.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
