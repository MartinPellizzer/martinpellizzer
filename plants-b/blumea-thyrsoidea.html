Blumea thyrsoidea

Blumea thyrsoidea, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
