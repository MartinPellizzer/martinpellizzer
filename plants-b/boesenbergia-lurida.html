Boesenbergia lurida

Boesenbergia lurida, commonly known as the slender ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Boesenbergia lurida is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
