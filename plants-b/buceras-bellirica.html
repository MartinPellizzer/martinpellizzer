Buceras bellirica

Buceras bellirica, commonly known as the Indian birch or Indian alder, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, followed by small, spherical fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a serrated margin.

Buceras bellirica is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of tannins.
