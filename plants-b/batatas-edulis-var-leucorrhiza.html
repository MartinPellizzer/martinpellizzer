Batatas edulis var. leucorrhiza

Batatas edulis var. leucorrhiza, commonly known as the white sweet potato, is a variety of the sweet potato plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white tuberous roots, which are typically smooth and firm. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its sweet flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular crop in many regions.
