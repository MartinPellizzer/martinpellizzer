Bridelia niedenzui var. pilosa

Bridelia niedenzui var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
