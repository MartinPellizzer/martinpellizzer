Baccharis corydalis

Baccharis corydalis, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Baccharis corydalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
