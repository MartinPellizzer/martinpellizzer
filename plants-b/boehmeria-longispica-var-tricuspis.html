Boehmeria longispica var. tricuspis

Boehmeria longispica var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed nettle, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other members commonly referred to as nettles. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinct three lobes. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

This variant is noted for its unique leaf morphology and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
