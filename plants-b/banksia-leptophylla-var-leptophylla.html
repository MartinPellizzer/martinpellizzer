Banksia leptophylla var. leptophylla

Banksia leptophylla var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, playing a vital role in local ecosystems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
