Begonia bartlettiana

Begonia bartlettiana, commonly known as the Bartlett's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, often used in gardens and as a potted plant.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and spread in its natural habitat.
