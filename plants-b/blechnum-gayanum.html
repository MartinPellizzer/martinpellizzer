Blechnum gayanum

Blechnum gayanum, commonly known as the Gamanum fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used for various purposes, including weaving.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
