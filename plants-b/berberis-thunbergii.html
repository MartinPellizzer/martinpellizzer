Berberis thunbergii

Berberis thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a bright green to red coloration depending on the variety. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
