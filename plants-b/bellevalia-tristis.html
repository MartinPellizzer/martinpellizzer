Bellevalia tristis

Bellevalia tristis, commonly known as the weedy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its low-growing habit.

Bellevalia tristis is often considered a weed due to its ability to spread readily in various habitats.
