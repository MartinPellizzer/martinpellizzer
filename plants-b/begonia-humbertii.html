Begonia humbertii

Begonia humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to almost black hues, often featuring intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ornamental species in horticulture.
