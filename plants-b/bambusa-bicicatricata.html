Bambusa bicicatricata

Bambusa bicicatricata, commonly known as the bamboo with two scars, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features a distinctive pattern of nodes and internodes, with visible scars at the base of each node. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is often used in construction and handicrafts.
