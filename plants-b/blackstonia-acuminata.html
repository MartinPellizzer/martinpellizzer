Blackstonia acuminata

Blackstonia acuminata, commonly known as the slender blackstonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Blackstonia acuminata thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
