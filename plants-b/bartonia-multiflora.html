Bartonia multiflora

Bartonia multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bartronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces.

Bartonia multiflora is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
