Bakerophyton pulchellum

Bakerophyton pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful moss, is a member of the Bryophyta division, specifically the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and small, elongated leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on stalks and release spores for dispersal.

Bakerophyton pulchellum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
