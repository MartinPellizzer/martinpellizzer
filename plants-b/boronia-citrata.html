Boronia citrata

Boronia citrata, commonly known as lemon boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated center.

Boronia citrata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
