Bombax buonopozense

Bombax buonopozense, commonly known as the white silk cotton tree, is a tropical deciduous tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seed capsules that disperse its seeds by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reddish or bronze color when young.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
