Buddleja agathosma var. glandulifera

Buddleja agathosma var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular buddleja, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
