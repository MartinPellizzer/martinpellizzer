Bromus sterilis var. validus

Bromus sterilis var. validus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and prairies.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for grazing animals.
