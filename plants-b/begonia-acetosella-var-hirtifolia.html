Begonia acetosella var. hirtifolia

Begonia acetosella var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
