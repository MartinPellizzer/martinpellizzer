Brachychilum tenellum

Brachychilum tenellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
