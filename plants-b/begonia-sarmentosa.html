Begonia sarmentosa

Begonia sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
