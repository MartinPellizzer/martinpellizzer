Begonia calderonii

Begonia calderonii, commonly known as the Calderonii begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green veins and bright red undersides. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
