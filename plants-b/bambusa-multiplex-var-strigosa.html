Bambusa multiplex var. strigosa

Bambusa multiplex var. strigosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and its culms are often covered in fine, silky hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Bambusa multiplex var. strigosa is relatively low maintenance and can thrive in a variety of climates with proper care.
