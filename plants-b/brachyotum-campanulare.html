Brachyotum campanulare

Brachyotum campanulare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Brachyotum campanulare is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
