Baccharis patagonica

Baccharis patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Baccharis patagonica is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
