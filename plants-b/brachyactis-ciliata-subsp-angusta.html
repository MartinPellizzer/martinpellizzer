Brachyactis ciliata subsp. angusta

Brachyactis ciliata subsp.

angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a potential candidate for restoration projects.
