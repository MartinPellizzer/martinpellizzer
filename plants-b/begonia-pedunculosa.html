Begonia pedunculosa

Begonia pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features upright stems with clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is popular among plant enthusiasts.
