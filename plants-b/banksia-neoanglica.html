Banksia neoanglica

Banksia neoanglica, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are released when the follicles dry and split open. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Banksia neoanglica is an important species for local wildlife, providing food and habitat for various animals.
