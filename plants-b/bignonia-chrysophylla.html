Bignonia chrysophylla

Bignonia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bignonia chrysophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
