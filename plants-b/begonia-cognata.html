Begonia cognata

Begonia cognata, commonly known as the pink begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and distinctive foliage. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright green coloration. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals.

Begonia cognata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
