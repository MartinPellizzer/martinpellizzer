Bulbophyllum albidostylidium

Bulbophyllum albidostylidium, commonly known as the white-styled Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, elongated lip that aids in pollination.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
