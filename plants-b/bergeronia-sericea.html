Bergeronia sericea

Bergeronia sericea, commonly known as the silky bergeronia, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Bergeronia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
