Bergia verticillaris

Bergia verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Bergia verticillaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
