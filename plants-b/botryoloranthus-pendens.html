Botryoloranthus pendens

Botryoloranthus pendens, commonly known as the hanging bottle plant, is a member of the Asphodelaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences that resemble clusters of small bottles. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Botryoloranthus pendens is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the year under optimal conditions.
