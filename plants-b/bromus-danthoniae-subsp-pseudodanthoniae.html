Bromus danthoniae subsp. pseudodanthoniae

Bromus danthoniae subsp.

pseudodanthoniae, commonly known as the false danthonia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
