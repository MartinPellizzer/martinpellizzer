Beta carnulosa

Beta carnulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hollyhock, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and cottage gardens.
