Begonia longirostris

Begonia longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that resemble a beak, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is usually glossy and dark green, with some varieties displaying striking variegation.

Begonia longirostris prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
