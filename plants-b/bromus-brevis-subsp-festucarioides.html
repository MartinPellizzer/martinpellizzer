Bromus brevis subsp. festucarioides

Bromus brevis subsp.

festucarioides, commonly known as the festucoid brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-based flowers. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common component of grassland ecosystems.
