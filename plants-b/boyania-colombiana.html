Boyania colombiana

Boyania colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian boyania, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Boyania colombiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
