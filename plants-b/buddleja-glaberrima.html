Buddleja glaberrima

Buddleja glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth buddleia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract various pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Buddleja glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native environment.
