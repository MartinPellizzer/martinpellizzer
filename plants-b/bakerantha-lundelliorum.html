Bakerantha lundelliorum

Bakerantha lundelliorum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are sturdy and upright. Bakerantha lundelliorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a favorite among botanical enthusiasts and gardeners alike.
