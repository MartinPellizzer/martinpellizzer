Bidens insolita

Bidens insolita, commonly known as the common tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers with ray florets, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils, and is often found in the eastern and central United States.

Bidens insolita is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
