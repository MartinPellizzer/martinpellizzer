Bucholzia frutescens

Bucholzia frutescens, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in dry, savannah-like habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

The seeds are traditionally used in local cuisines and have medicinal properties in some African cultures.
