Bulbostylis pseudojunciformis

Bulbostylis pseudojunciformis, commonly known as the slender bulbostylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Due to its delicate structure and specific ecological requirements, it is relatively rare and of interest to botanists and conservationists.
