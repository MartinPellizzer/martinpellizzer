Bauhinia saksuwaniae

Bauhinia saksuwaniae, commonly known as the Thai flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, flame-like pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
