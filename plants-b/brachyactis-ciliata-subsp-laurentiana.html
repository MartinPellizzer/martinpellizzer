Brachyactis ciliata subsp. laurentiana

Brachyactis ciliata subsp.

laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its subspecific name, *laurentiana*, reflects its association with the Laurentian region of Canada.
