Bupleurum montanum var. atlanticum

Bupleurum montanum var. atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic mountain angelica, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its growth is adapted to withstand coastal winds and saline conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
