Begonia oreodoxa

Begonia oreodoxa, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
