Begonia pinetorum

Begonia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest begonia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
