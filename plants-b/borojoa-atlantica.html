Borojoa atlantica

Borojoa atlantica, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features make it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and for the nutritional benefits of its fruits.
