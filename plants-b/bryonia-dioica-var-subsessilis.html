Bryonia dioica var. subsessilis

Bryonia dioica var. subsessilis, commonly known as the lesser bryony, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and warty, greenish-yellow fruits. The plant produces separate male and female flowers, typical of its dioecious nature. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its toxic properties make it a plant to be handled with care.
