Brachystegia russelliae

Brachystegia russelliae, commonly known as Russell's knobthorn, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and thorned branches. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
