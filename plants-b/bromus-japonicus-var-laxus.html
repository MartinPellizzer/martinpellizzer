Bromus japonicus var. laxus

Bromus japonicus var. laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, awned florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
