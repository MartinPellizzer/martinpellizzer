Braya imbricatifolia

Braya imbricatifolia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, rounded fruits known as siliques.

Braya imbricatifolia is often found in crevices and on slopes, where it forms dense, low-growing mats.
