Bulbophyllum physocoryphum

Bulbophyllum physocoryphum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fleshy, horn-like structures that develop from the base of the plant. These structures are specialized for water storage, allowing the plant to thrive in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
