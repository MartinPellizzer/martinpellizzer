Bursera xolocotzii

Bursera xolocotzii, commonly known as the Xolocotzii bursera, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical dry forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, fragrant capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Bursera xolocotzii is adapted to dry climates and is often found in well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
