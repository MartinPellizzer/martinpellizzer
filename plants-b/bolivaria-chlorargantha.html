Bolivaria chlorargantha

Bolivaria chlorargantha, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique leaf structure, which often has a waxy coating to retain moisture. Bolivaria chlorargantha thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after specimen for both horticultural and scientific study.
