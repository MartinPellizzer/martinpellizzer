Bertiera globiceps var. bityensis

Bertiera globiceps var. bityensis, commonly known as the Bitye's globecap, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescences that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in rounded heads, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually small, opposite, and sometimes hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
