Bulbophyllum odontoglossum

Bulbophyllum odontoglossum, commonly known as the toothed tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and is typically found growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive, tooth-like lip. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in well-drained soil and requires specific humidity and light conditions for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
