Blastus hindsii

Blastus hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic invertebrates. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually submerged, while its flowering stems rise above the water surface.

Blastus hindsii is known for its unique adaptation to nutrient-poor environments by capturing and digesting prey.
