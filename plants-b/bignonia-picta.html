Bignonia picta

Bignonia picta, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Bignonia picta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
