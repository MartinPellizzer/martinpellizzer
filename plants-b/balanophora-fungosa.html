Balanophora fungosa

Balanophora fungosa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it entirely reliant on its host for nutrients, as it cannot perform photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white flowers with a distinctive appearance, often resembling a ghostly figure. It is native to the southeastern United States and parts of Florida, where it thrives in wet, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
