Bupleurum longicaule var. giraldii

Bupleurum longicaule var. giraldii, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
