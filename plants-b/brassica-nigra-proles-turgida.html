Brassica nigra proles turgida

Brassica nigra proles turgida, commonly known as the swollen black mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, swollen pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and have a distinct mustard scent.

The species is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of oilseed.
