Bidens humilis

Bidens humilis, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow composite flowers that are typically surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields and along roadsides.

Bidens humilis is also characterized by its distinctive seed capsules that release tiny seeds with fine hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.
