Bulbophyllum polyblepharon

Bulbophyllum polyblepharon, commonly known as the "Fringed Lip Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces multiple inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
