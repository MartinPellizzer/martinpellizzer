Bridelia micrantha var. ferruginea

Bridelia micrantha var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
