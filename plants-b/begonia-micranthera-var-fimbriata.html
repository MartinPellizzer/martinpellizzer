Begonia micranthera var. fimbriata

Begonia micranthera var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriated begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fimbriated (frayed) leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green and red hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red or orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
