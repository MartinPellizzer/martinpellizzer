Brassaiopsis gigantea

Brassaiopsis gigantea, commonly known as the giant brassaiopsis, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach impressive lengths. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
