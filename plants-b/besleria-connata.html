Besleria connata

Besleria connata, commonly known as the red-flowered gesneriad, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
