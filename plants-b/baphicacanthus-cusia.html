Baphicacanthus cusia

Baphicacanthus cusia, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clusters of bright red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
