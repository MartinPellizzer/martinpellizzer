Bulbisperma

Bulbisperma, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a short, thick stem with basal leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive involucre of bracts.
