Berchemia ohwii

Berchemia ohwii, commonly known as the Natal flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Berchemia ohwii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
