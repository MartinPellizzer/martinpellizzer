Begonia rigidifolia subsp. rigidifolia

Begonia rigidifolia subsp.

rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved begonia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, stiff, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
