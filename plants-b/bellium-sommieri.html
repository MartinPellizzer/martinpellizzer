Bellium sommieri

Bellium sommieri, commonly known as the summer bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Bellium sommieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
