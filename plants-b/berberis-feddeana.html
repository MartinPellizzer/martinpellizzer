Berberis feddeana

Berberis feddeana, commonly known as the Fedde barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive form and seasonal interest.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
