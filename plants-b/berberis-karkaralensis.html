Berberis karkaralensis

Berberis karkaralensis, commonly known as the Karkaralensis barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
