Blackstonia imperfoliata

Blackstonia imperfoliata, commonly known as the blackstonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.
