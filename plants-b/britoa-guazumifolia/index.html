Britoa Guazumifolia

Guazumifolia britoa, scientifically known as Britoa guazumifolia, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. This species is characterized by its striking foliage and delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Despite its beauty, it remains poorly studied due to its limited distribution and challenging growing conditions. Its conservation status is a growing concern as habitat loss threatens its survival. The plant holds cultural significance in local communities and is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
