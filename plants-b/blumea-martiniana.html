Blumea martiniana

Blumea martiniana, commonly known as the yellow blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often slightly hairy, while its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters.

Blumea martiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
