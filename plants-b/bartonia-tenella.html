Bartonia tenella

Bartonia tenella, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Bartonia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
