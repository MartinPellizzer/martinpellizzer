Bellevalia dolichophylla

Bellevalia dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
