Brachypodium humbertianum

Brachypodium humbertianum, commonly known as Humbert's brome, is a grass species native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its genetic simplicity and adaptability, it is occasionally used in plant research for studying stress responses and genome evolution.
