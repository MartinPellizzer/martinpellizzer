Bonellia sprucei

Bonellia sprucei, commonly known as the Pacific bonnet, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters, making it a notable ornamental species.

Bonellia sprucei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
