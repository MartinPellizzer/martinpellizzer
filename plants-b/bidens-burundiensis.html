Bidens burundiensis

Bidens burundiensis, commonly known as the Burundi ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Burundi.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Bidens burundiensis is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest edges.
