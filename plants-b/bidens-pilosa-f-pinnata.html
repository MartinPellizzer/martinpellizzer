Bidens pilosa f. pinnata

Bidens pilosa f. pinnata, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a pinnate arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
