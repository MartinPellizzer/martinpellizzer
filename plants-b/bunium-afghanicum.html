Bunium afghanicum

Bunium afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features a robust taproot and upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The leaves are finely divided and emit a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.
