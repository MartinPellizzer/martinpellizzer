Benthamia fragifera

Benthamia fragifera, commonly known as the sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, edible root that is used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Benthamia fragifera is valued for its ecological role and potential economic importance in local communities.
