Betencourtia australis

Betencourtia australis, commonly known as the Australian betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. Betencourtia australis is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
