Bergenia purpurascens

Bergenia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf bergenia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and rosettes of broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers in early spring, which are borne on tall, leafless stems. Its foliage is often covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its cold, arid habitats.

Bergenia purpurascens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in shaded, moist environments.
