Bismalva laciniata

Bismalva laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or notched. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Bismalva laciniata prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
