Baccharis genistelloides var. crispa

Baccharis genistelloides var. crispa, commonly known as the hairy baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and inland scrublands.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.
