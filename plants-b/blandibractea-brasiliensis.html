Blandibractea brasiliensis

Blandibractea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bract orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single inflorescence with several small, clustered flowers.

Blandibractea brasiliensis is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
