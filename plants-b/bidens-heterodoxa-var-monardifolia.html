Bidens heterodoxa var. monardifolia

Bidens heterodoxa var. monardifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Narrowleaf ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
