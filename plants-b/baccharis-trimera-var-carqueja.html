Baccharis trimera var. carqueja

Baccharis trimera var. carqueja, commonly known as carqueja, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as campos and restingas.

Carqueja is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
