Breweria heudelotii

Breweria heudelotii, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant is known for its yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Breweria heudelotii plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and provides nectar for pollinators.
