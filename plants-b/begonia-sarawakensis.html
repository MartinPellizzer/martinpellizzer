Begonia sarawakensis

Begonia sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak begonia, is a species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a climbing or trailing species, often found in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a unique texture.

Begonia sarawakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
