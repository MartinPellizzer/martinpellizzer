Baptisia lecontei f. robustior

Baptisia lecontei f. robustior, commonly known as the robust Leconte's indigo.

This variant is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, adding a distinctive visual element to its appearance.
