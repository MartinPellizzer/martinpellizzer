Briza media var. pilosa

Briza media var. pilosa, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture that contributes to its ornamental value.

It is often used in gardens for its delicate look and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
