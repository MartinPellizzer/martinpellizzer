Bidens decumbens

Bidens decumbens, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Bidens decumbens is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
