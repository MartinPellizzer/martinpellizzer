Brassica trichocarpa

Brassica trichocarpa, commonly known as the wild cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates. It has been used historically for its edible leaves and as a forage crop.

Its genetic makeup has also made it an important species in plant research, particularly in the study of genome evolution.
