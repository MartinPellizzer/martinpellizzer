Bromus erectus var. hackelii

Bromus erectus var. hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense clusters, with small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and steppe ecosystems.
