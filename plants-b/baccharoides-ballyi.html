Baccharoides ballyi

Baccharoides ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system, which help it conserve water. It is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
