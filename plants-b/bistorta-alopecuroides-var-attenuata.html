Bistorta alopecuroides var. attenuata

Bistorta alopecuroides var. attenuata, commonly known as narrow-leaved bistort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dark green, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
