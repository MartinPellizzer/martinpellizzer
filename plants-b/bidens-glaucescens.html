Bidens glaucescens

Bidens glaucescens, commonly known as the blueleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, blue-green leaves with a hairy texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens glaucescens is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
