Byttneria filipes

Byttneria filipes, commonly known as the African wild silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest margins.

The tree is valued for its fibrous bark, which has been used traditionally for making ropes and textiles.
