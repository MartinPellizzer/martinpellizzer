Bulbophyllum subebulbum

Bulbophyllum subebulbum, commonly known as the pink-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture.

Bulbophyllum subebulbum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
