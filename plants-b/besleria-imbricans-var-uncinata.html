Besleria imbricans var. uncinata

Besleria imbricans var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked besleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

The unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical collections and horticultural displays.
