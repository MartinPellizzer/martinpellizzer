Buddleja fragifera

Buddleja fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Oleaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its attractive appearance and pleasant scent make it a popular choice for landscaping.
