Brunfelsia longiflora

Brunfelsia longiflora, commonly known as the blue bell or long-flowered Brunfelsia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep blue, depending on the stage of bloom. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Brunfelsia longiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to bloom throughout the year.
