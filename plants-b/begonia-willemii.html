Begonia willemii

Begonia willemii, commonly known as the Willemii begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Celebes (Sulawesi) in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners seeking unique foliage plants.
