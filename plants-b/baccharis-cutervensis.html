Baccharis cutervensis

Baccharis cutervensis, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
