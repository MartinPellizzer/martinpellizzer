Buddleja ferruginosa

Buddleja ferruginosa, commonly known as the iron-leaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale purple to lilac flowers that bloom in late summer. Buddleja ferruginosa is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
