Borago tristis

Borago tristis, commonly known as the blueweed or false flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Borago tristis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
