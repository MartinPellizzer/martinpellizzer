Baccharis cassinoides

Baccharis cassinoides, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Baccharis cassinoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
