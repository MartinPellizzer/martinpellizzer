Blechnum nigrocostatum

Blechnum nigrocostatum, commonly known as the black-costed harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with distinctive black or dark brown midribs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnate, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often slightly curled.

Blechnum nigrocostatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
