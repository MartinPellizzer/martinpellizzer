Bischofia racemosa

Bischofia racemosa, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
