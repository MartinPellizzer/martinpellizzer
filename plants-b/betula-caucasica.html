Betula caucasica

Betula caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian birch, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its white bark and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Betula caucasica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
