Bidens abadiae var. pilosoides

Bidens abadiae var. pilosoides, commonly known as the hairy false beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow with dark centers and bloom from late spring to early fall, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
