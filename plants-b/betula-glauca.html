Betula glauca

Betula glauca, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin layers, giving it its common name. The tree has slender, silver-green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forests.

Betula glauca plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
