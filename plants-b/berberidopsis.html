Berberidopsis

Berberidopsis, commonly known as the stonecrop or evergreen orchid, is a genus of succulent plants native to the western United States and Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, often spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in spring. Berberidopsis species are adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments. They are often found in mountainous regions and are valued for their ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

The genus includes several species, such as Berberidopsis corallina, which is prized for its vibrant yellow flowers.
