Bidens mauiensis var. ciliata

Bidens mauiensis var. ciliata, commonly known as the Maui ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with hairy margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and upland habitats.

This plant plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining native vegetation.
