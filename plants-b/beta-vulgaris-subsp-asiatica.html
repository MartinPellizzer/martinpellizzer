Beta vulgaris subsp. asiatica

Beta vulgaris subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese beet or Asian chard, is a cultivated variant of the beetroot plant. It is characterized by its thick, edible stalks and leaves, which are often used in Asian cuisine.

The plant is typically grown for its nutrient-rich leaves and stems, which are high in vitamins and minerals. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and culinary versatility in various regional dishes.
