Begonia parviglandulosa

Begonia parviglandulosa, commonly known as the small glandular begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which are covered in small, glandular dots. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the summer months.

It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
