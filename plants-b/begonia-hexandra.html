Begonia hexandra

Begonia hexandra, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia hexandra is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
