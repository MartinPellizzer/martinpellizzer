Bahia parviflora

Bahia parviflora, commonly known as smallflower bahia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Bahia parviflora thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for various garden settings.
