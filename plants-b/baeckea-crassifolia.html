Baeckea crassifolia

Baeckea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf baeckea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Baeckea crassifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
