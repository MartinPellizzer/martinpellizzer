Bellardia canescens

Bellardia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf bellardia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Bellardia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
