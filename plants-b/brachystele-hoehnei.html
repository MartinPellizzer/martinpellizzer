Brachystele hoehnei

Brachystele hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's rock cress, is a rare and unique plant species native to Brazil.

It is a small, succulent herb that thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. The plant features a compact rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves that store water, and it produces small, white flowers during the wet season. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized morphology and ecological niche, making it an important subject for botanical research.
