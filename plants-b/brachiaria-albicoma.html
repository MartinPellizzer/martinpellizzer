Brachiaria albicoma

Brachiaria albicoma, commonly known as whitestem grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Brachiaria albicoma is valued for its use in forage and soil stabilization in agricultural settings.
