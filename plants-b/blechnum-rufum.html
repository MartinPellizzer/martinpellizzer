Blechnum rufum

Blechnum rufum, commonly known as the red fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often have a reddish hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Blechnum rufum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
