Bupleurum angulosum f. giganteum

Bupleurum angulosum f. giganteum, commonly known as the giant angular bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive angular appearance, contributing to its common name.

Bupleurum angulosum f. giganteum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade conditions.
