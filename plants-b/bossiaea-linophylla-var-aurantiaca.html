Bossiaea linophylla var. aurantiaca

Bossiaea linophylla var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. This variety is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
