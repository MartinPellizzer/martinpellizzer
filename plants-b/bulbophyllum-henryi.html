Bulbophyllum henryi

Bulbophyllum henryi, commonly known as the Henry's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually fleshy and dark green, adapted for storing water.

The flowers, which can be white or pale yellow, bloom during the wet season and attract various pollinators.
