Bupleurum striatum

Bupleurum striatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Bupleurum striatum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
