Brassica gravinae var. brachyloma

Brassica gravinae var. brachyloma, commonly known as the short-stemmed mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
