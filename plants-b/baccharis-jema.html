Baccharis jema

Baccharis jema, commonly known as the Jema baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

Baccharis jema is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing benefits.
