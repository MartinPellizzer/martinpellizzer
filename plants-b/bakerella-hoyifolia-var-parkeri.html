Bakerella hoyifolia var. parkeri

Bakerella hoyifolia var. parkeri, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Bakerella hoyifolia var. parkeri is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
