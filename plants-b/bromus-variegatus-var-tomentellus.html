Bromus variegatus var. tomentellus

Bromus variegatus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in restoration ecology projects.
