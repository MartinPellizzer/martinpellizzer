Bulbophyllum trigonosepalum

Bulbophyllum trigonosepalum, commonly known as the triangular-sepal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and feature three distinct sepals that form a triangular shape.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
