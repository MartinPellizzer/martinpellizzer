Bursera morelensis

Bursera morelensis, commonly known as the Morelless Bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

Bursera morelensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
