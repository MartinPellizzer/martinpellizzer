Berberis bracteata

Berberis bracteata, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

Berberis bracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
