Bambusa capensis

Bambusa capensis, commonly known as the Natal bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Bambusa capensis is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create a natural, lush appearance.
