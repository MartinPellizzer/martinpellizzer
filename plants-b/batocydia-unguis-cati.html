Batocydia unguis-cati

Batocydia unguis-cati, commonly known as the cat's claw plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved, claw-like structures at the base of its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Batocydia unguis-cati is often found in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
