Bromus arundinaceus

Bromus arundinaceus, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fibrous root system, which contribute to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its culms are often used for thatching and basket-making due to their strength and flexibility.

Bromus arundinaceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native ecosystems.
