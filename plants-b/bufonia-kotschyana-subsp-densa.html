Bufonia kotschyana subsp. densa

Bufonia kotschyana subsp.

densa, commonly known as the dense bufonia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lanceolate, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
