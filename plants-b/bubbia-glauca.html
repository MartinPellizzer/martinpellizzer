Bubbia glauca

Bubbia glauca, commonly known as the bluebush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Bubbia glauca is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
