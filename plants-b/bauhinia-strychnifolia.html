Bauhinia strychnifolia

Bauhinia strychnifolia, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It grows in a variety of habitats, including forests and disturbed areas, and is often found in lowland regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
