Brexiopsis aquifolia

Brexiopsis aquifolia, commonly known as the water-leaf plant, is a species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, and often glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Brexiopsis aquifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
