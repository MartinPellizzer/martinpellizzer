Bosciopsis variabilis

Bosciopsis variabilis, commonly known as the variable bosciopsis, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family, typically found in moist, acidic soils of temperate regions.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leaves that vary in shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption.

Due to its adaptability, Bosciopsis variabilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
