Begonia sinuata var. monophylloides

Begonia sinuata var. monophylloides, commonly known as the smooth-leaf begonia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with shades of red or purple, adding a dramatic visual appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pink, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit, it is a popular choice for indoor gardens and shaded outdoor spaces.
