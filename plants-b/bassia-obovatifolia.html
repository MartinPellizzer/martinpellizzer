Bassia obovatifolia

Bassia obovatifolia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for erosion control and xeriscaping in dry landscapes.
