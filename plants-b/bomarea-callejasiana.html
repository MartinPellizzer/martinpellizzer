Bomarea callejasiana

Bomarea callejasiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant is notable for its striking, colorful flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Bomarea callejasiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
