Buxus sempervirens f. handsworthiensis

Buxus sempervirens f. handsworthiensis, commonly known as the Handsworth boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the UK.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and slow growth rate, making it ideal for topiary and hedging. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, rounded shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in English gardens.
