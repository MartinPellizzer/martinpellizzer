Bidens drummondii

Bidens drummondii, commonly known as the drummond's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Bidens drummondii is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
