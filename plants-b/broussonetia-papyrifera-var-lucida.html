Broussonetia papyrifera var. lucida

Broussonetia papyrifera var. lucida, commonly known as the glossy paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green bark and palmately lobed leaves that are glossy on both sides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in paper production.
