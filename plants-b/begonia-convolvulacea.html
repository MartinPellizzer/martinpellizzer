Begonia convolvulacea

Begonia convolvulacea, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant features heart-shaped or ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season.
