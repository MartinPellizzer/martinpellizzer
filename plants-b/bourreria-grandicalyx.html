Bourreria grandicalyx

Bourreria grandicalyx, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces striking, bright purple berries that persist into the winter, making it an attractive ornamental plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
