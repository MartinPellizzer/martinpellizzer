Bromus patulus var. variegatus

Bromus patulus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated brome, is a variant of the brome grass species characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green color with white or yellow markings. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is generally drought-tolerant and thrives in temperate climates.
