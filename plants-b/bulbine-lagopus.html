Bulbine lagopus

Bulbine lagopus, commonly known as the silver bulbous plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, bulbous stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Bulbine lagopus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
