Buena undata

Buena undata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with thick, fleshy stems and waxy, gray-green leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.

Its botanical features reflect its adaptation to extreme conditions, with reduced leaves and a deep root system for water storage.
