Brickellia petrophila var. umbratilis

Brickellia petrophila var. umbratilis, commonly known as the shade brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. This variant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or canyon environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with a mat-like structure that helps it survive in harsh, shaded conditions.

The plant is also noted for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
