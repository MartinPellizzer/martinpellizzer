Begonia glandulifera

Begonia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and greenhouse displays.
