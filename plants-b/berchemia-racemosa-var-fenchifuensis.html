Berchemia racemosa var. fenchifuensis

Berchemia racemosa var. fenchifuensis, commonly known as the Fenchifuensis Broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
