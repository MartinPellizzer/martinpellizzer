Bryonia umbellata

Bryonia umbellata, commonly known as the common beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption or medicinal use.
