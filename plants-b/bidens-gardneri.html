Bidens gardneri

Bidens gardneri, commonly known as the white beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Bidens gardneri is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.
