Buddleja ovalifolia

Buddleja ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces fragrant, panicle-like clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers in late summer. These flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
