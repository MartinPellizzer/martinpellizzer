Bulbine cataphyllata

Bulbine cataphyllata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
