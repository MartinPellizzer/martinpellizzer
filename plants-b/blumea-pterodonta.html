Blumea pterodonta

Blumea pterodonta, commonly known as the goldenbutton daisy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Blumea pterodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
