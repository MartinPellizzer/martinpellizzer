Brickellia microphylla var. scabra

Brickellia microphylla var. scabra, commonly known as roughleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a rough, scaly texture, which gives it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, attracting various pollinators.
