Bletia mexicana

Bletia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Bletia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
