Bulbophyllum geminatum

Bulbophyllum geminatum, commonly known as the twin-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage structures.

Bulbophyllum geminatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
