Berberis humidoumbrosa var. dispersa

Berberis humidoumbrosa var. dispersa, commonly known as the spreading barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but have a sharp, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
