Bouteloua simplex

Bouteloua simplex, commonly known as gray beardgrass, is a native grass species found throughout the central and eastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has fine, silver-gray leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing heads that are often seen in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for native plant gardens.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
