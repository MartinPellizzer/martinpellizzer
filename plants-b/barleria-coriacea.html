Barleria coriacea

Barleria coriacea, commonly known as the rough barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in rough, textured foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Barleria coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
