Bauhinia coronata

Bauhinia coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a flattened pod that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia coronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
