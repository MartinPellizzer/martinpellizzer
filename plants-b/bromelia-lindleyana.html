Bromelia lindleyana

Bromelia lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's bromeliad, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically long, narrow, and covered in a waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture.

Bromelia lindleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments.
