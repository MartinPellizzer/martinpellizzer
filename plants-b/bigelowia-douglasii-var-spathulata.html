Bigelowia douglasii var. spathulata

Bigelowia douglasii var. spathulata, commonly known as the Douglas's bigelowia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest clearings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
