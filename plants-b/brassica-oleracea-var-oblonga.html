Brassica oleracea var. oblonga

Brassica oleracea var. oblonga, commonly known as kohlrabi, is a vegetable characterized by its swollen, bulbous stem that is typically eaten as a vegetable.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant has thick, edible stalks that are green in color and contain a mild, sweet flavor. Kohlrabi grows best in cool climates and is typically harvested before the bulb becomes too large.

It is rich in nutrients such as vitamin C, fiber, and various antioxidants.
