Bulbostylis zambesica

Bulbostylis zambesica, commonly known as Zambesi grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that typically forms dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in wetlands and grassy areas along rivers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
