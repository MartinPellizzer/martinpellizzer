Bigelowia nauseosa var. hololeuca

Bigelowia nauseosa var. hololeuca, commonly known as the white-flowered bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white with yellow centers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
