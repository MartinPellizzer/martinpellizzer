Boronia viridiflora

Boronia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often with a citrus-like scent.

Boronia viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
