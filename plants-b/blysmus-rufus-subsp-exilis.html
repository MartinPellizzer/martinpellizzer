Blysmus rufus subsp. exilis

Blysmus rufus subsp.

exilis, commonly known as the slender red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and reddish hues in its foliage and flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
