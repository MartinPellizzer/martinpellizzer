Bulbophyllum samoanum

Bulbophyllum samoanum, commonly known as the Samoan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow.

Bulbophyllum samoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
