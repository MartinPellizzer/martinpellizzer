Bumelia persimilis subsp. typica

Bumelia persimilis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid ecosystem biodiversity.
