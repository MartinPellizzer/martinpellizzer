Bigelowia uniligulata

Bigelowia uniligulata, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Bigelowia uniligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
