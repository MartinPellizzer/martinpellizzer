Borojoa lanceolata

Borojoa lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian date palm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates. These fruits are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

Borojoa lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in tropical climates.
