Begonia uliginosa var. ermanii

Begonia uliginosa var. ermanii, commonly known as the marsh begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and soil conditions.
