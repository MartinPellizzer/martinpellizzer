Brachylaena racemosa

Brachylaena racemosa, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow-orange flowers that appear in racemes during the winter months. The plant has grey-green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Brachylaena racemosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
