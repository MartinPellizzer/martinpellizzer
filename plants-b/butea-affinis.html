Butea affinis

Butea affinis, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vibrant display. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous layer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Butea affinis is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
