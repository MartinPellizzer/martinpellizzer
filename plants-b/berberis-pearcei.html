Berberis pearcei

Berberis pearcei, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features spiny stems and small, holly-like leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
