Bambusa latispiculata

Bambusa latispiculata, commonly known as the broad-spined bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and distinctive, broad, spiny nodes. The plant produces long, slender culms that are often used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Bambusa latispiculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
