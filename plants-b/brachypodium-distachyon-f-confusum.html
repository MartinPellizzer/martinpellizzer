Brachypodium distachyon f. confusum

Brachypodium distachyon f. confusum, commonly known as the confused false brome, is a variant of the grass species Brachypodium distachyon.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced inflorescence compared to the typical form. This form exhibits morphological variations, including shorter and more tightly clustered spikes. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be used as a model organism in plant biology research.

Its genetic similarity to other Brachypodium species makes it valuable for studying grass genome evolution.
