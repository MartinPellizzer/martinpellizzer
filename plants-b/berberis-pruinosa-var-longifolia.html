Berberis pruinosa var. longifolia

Berberis pruinosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features upright, spiny stems that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hedges.
