Bolboschoenus grandispicus

Bolboschoenus grandispicus, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of green or brown. Bolboschoenus grandispicus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of marsh and pond ecosystems.
