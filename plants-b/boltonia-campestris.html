Boltonia campestris

Boltonia campestris, commonly known as field ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, adorned with clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Boltonia campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
