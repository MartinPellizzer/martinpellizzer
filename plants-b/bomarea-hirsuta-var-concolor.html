Bomarea hirsuta var. concolor

Bomarea hirsuta var. concolor, commonly known as the hairy bomarea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant features hairy foliage, which is a distinguishing trait of the species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
