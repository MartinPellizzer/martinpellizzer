Banisteria sanguinea

Banisteria sanguinea, commonly known as the red banister, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a woody vine structure, often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
