Bidens crataegifolia

Bidens crataegifolia, commonly known as the thimbleweed or false hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and roadside ditches. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Bidens crataegifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
