Bidens elata

Bidens elata, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Bidens elata is known for its erect growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
