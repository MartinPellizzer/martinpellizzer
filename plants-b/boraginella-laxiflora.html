Boraginella laxiflora

Boraginella laxiflora, commonly known as the slender bugloss, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Boraginella laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
