Begonia urticae var. retusa

Begonia urticae var. retusa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
