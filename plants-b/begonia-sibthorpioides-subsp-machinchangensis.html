Begonia sibthorpioides subsp. machinchangensis

Begonia sibthorpioides subsp.

machinchangensis, commonly known as the Machinchang begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep green to reddish hue.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining, fertile soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
