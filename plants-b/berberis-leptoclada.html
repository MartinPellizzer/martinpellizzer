Berberis leptoclada

Berberis leptoclada, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny, deciduous stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
