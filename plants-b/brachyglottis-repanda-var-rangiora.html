Brachyglottis repanda var. rangiora

Brachyglottis repanda var. rangiora, commonly known as the Kākāriki flower or the New Zealand flax, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, often in sandy or gravelly soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
