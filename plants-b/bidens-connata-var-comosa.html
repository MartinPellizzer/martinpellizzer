Bidens connata var. comosa

Bidens connata var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
