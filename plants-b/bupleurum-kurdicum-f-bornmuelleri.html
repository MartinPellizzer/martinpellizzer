Bupleurum kurdicum f. bornmuelleri

Bupleurum kurdicum f. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Kurdish cicely, is a rare and lesser-known botanical variant of the Bupleurum genus.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and temperate climates. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for taxonomists and botanists studying the diversity within the Bupleurum species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
