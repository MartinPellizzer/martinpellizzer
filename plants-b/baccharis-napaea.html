Baccharis napaea

Baccharis napaea, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast against its green stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Baccharis napaea is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
