Bactris simplicifrons var. carolensis

Bactris simplicifrons var. carolensis, commonly known as the Carolinian palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of spines. Native to the Caribbean, this palm thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, spherical fruits.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitats.
