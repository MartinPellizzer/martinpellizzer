Bactris maraja var. maraja

Bactris maraja var. maraja, commonly known as the Marajoa palm, is a species native to the Amazon basin, particularly found in Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices, and the plant is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
