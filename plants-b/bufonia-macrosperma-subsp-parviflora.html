Bufonia macrosperma subsp. parviflora

Bufonia macrosperma subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bufonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Acanthaceae family.
