Berberis coxii

Berberis coxii, commonly known as the Tibetan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It features spiny branches and small, rounded leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Berberis coxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
