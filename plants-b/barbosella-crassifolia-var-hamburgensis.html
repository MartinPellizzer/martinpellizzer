Barbosella crassifolia var. hamburgensis

Barbosella crassifolia var. hamburgensis, commonly known as Hamburg barbosella, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
