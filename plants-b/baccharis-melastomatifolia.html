Baccharis melastomatifolia

Baccharis melastomatifolia, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
