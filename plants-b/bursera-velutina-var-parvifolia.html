Bursera velutina var. parvifolia

Bursera velutina var. parvifolia, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to the dry forests of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers and its smooth, aromatic bark. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
