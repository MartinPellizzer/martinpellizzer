Bupleurum ecklonianum

Bupleurum ecklonianum, commonly known as the Cape Bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Bupleurum ecklonianum plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
