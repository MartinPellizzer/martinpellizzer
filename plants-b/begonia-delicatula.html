Begonia delicatula

Begonia delicatula, commonly known as the delicate begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Begonia delicatula is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
