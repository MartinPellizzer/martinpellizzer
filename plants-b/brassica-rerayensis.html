Brassica rerayensis

Brassica rerayensis, commonly known as the Reray's mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant produces round, green pods that contain several seeds. Native to specific regions in Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
