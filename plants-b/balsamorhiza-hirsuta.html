Balsamorhiza hirsuta

Balsamorhiza hirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Balsamorhiza hirsuta is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
