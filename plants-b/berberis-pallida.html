Berberis pallida

Berberis pallida, commonly known as the pale barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a pale green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Berberis pallida is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
