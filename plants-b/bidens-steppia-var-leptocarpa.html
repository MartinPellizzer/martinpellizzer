Bidens steppia var. leptocarpa

Bidens steppia var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the steppe ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems that are hairy and often branched, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and occur in clusters, producing small, three-lobed seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
