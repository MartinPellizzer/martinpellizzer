Biermannia bigibba

Biermannia bigibba, commonly known as the giant biermannia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white flowers clustered together. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, tropical environment. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as insects and birds.

Biermannia bigibba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
