Bambusa attenuata

Bambusa attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright culms and long, narrow leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of growth.

Bambusa attenuata is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
