Begonia morrisiorum

Begonia morrisiorum, commonly known as the Morris' begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a deep, rich green color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
