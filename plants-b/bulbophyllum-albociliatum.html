Bulbophyllum albociliatum

Bulbophyllum albociliatum, commonly known as the white-fringed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slender pseudobulbs and delicate, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers have a distinct white margin on the petals, giving it its common name.

Bulbophyllum albociliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
