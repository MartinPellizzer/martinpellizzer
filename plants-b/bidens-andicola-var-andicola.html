Bidens andicola var. andicola

Bidens andicola var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in temperate to cold climates. The plant features alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local ecosystems.
