Bignonia eximia

Bignonia eximia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Bignonia eximia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
