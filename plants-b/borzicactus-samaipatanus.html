Borzicactus samaipatanus

Borzicactus samaipatanus, commonly known as the Samaipata cactus, is a unique cacti species native to Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
