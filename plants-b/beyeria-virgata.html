Beyeria virgata

Beyeria virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Beyeria virgata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
