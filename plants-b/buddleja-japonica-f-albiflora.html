Buddleja japonica f. albiflora

Buddleja japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese bush willow, is a cultivar of the Buddleja genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its showy, fragrant white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ability to attract butterflies and bees.
