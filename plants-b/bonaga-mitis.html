Bonaga mitis

Bonaga mitis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Bonaga mitis is often found in wetland habitats and can thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in areas outside its native range.
