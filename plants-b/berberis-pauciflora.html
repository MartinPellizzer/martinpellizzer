Berberis pauciflora

Berberis pauciflora, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

Berberis pauciflora is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
