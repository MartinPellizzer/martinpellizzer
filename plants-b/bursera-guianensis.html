Bursera guianensis

Bursera guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree has a dense, rounded crown and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits containing winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its fragrant sap, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal preparations.
