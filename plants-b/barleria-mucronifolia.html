Barleria mucronifolia

Barleria mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
