Bauhinia cancara

Bauhinia cancara, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Bauhinia cancara is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
