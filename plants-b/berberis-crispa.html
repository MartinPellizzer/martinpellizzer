Berberis crispa

Berberis crispa, commonly known as the prickly barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sour taste. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly wavy margin and a dark green upper surface.

Berberis crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
