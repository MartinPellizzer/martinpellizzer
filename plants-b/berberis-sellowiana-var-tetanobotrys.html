Berberis sellowiana var. tetanobotrys

Berberis sellowiana var. tetanobotrys, commonly known as the Sellow's barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.
