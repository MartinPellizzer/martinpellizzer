Byrsonima coriacea

Byrsonima coriacea, commonly known as jocote or Brazilian plum, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for culinary and medicinal uses.
