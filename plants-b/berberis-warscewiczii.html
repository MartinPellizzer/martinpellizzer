Berberis warscewiczii

Berberis warscewiczii, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its hardy nature and distinctive appearance contribute to its value in both horticulture and ecological settings.
