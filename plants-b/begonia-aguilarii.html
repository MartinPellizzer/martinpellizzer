Begonia aguilarii

Begonia aguilarii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia aguilarii is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
