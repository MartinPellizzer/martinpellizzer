Bidens macrosperma

Bidens macrosperma, commonly known as the common tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its seeds are small, pitted, and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Bidens macrosperma is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
