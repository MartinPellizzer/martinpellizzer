Bignonia marmorata

Bignonia marmorata, commonly known as the marble vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bignoniaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with orange and red streaks, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Bignonia marmorata is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 20 feet, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
