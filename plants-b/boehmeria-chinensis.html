Boehmeria chinensis

Boehmeria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its fibers used in textiles and papermaking.

Boehmeria chinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological and economic significance.
