Begonia mayasiana

Begonia mayasiana, commonly known as the Mayan begonia, is a species native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated spur.

Begonia mayasiana thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
