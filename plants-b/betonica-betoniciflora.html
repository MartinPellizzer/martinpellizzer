Betonica betoniciflora

Betonica betoniciflora, commonly known as the blueweed or betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. Betonica betoniciflora is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
