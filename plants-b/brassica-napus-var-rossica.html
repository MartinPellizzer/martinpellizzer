Brassica napus var. rossica

Brassica napus var. rossica, commonly known as Russian rapeseed, is a variety of oilseed rape native to Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its high oil content. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a rosette of basal leaves. Its seeds are used in the production of biodiesel and as a source of vegetable oil.

The plant is also valued for its role in crop rotation and soil fertility improvement.
