Brassiopsis allenii

Brassiopsis allenii, commonly known as the Allen's brassia, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Brassiopsis allenii is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value.

However, it is considered endangered in the wild due to habitat loss and over-collection.
