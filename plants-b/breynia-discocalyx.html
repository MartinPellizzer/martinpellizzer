Breynia discocalyx

Breynia discocalyx, commonly known as the fire tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that can range in color from green to red or purple, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and coastal conditions.

Breynia discocalyx is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil stabilization and ecological restoration projects.
