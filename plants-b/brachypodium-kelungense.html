Brachypodium kelungense

Brachypodium kelungense, commonly known as the Kelung grass, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Brachypodium kelungense is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for ecological and agricultural applications.
