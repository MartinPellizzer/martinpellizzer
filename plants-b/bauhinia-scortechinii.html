Bauhinia scortechinii

Bauhinia scortechinii, commonly known as the velvetwood or yellow bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to pink, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Its small, flat pods contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia scortechinii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
