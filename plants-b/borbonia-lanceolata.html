Borbonia lanceolata

Borbonia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf borbonia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Borbonia lanceolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
