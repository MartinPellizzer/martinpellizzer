Besseya wyomingensis

Besseya wyomingensis, commonly known as the Wyoming sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along streambanks. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Besseya wyomingensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
