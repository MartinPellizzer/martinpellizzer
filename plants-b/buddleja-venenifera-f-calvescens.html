Buddleja venenifera f. calvescens

Buddleja venenifera f. calvescens, commonly known as the toxic butterfly bush, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features showy, fragrant, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It is known for its toxicity, with all parts of the plant containing toxic compounds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its hazardous properties.
