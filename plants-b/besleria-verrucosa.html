Besleria verrucosa

Besleria verrucosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
