Boea divaricata

Boea divaricata, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a climbing perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a versatile and attractive addition to gardens and indoor spaces.
