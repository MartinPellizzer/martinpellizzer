Babiana angusta

Babiana angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved babiana, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its flowers are pollinated by insects such as bees and beetles.
