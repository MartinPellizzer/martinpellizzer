Bischofia cummingiana

Bischofia cummingiana, commonly known as the yellow gooseberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red berries. Its roots are known for containing compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
