Bauhinia endertii

Bauhinia endertii, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a rugged texture.

Bauhinia endertii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or savanna habitats.
