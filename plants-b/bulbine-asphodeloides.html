Bulbine asphodeloides

Bulbine asphodeloides, commonly known as the golden bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners in regions with dry climates.
