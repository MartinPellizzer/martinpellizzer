Berberis iberica

Berberis iberica, commonly known as the Iberian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Berberis iberica is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
