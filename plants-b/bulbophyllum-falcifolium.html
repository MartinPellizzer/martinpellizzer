Bulbophyllum falcifolium

Bulbophyllum falcifolium, commonly known as the sickle-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular plant among orchid enthusiasts.
