Becium vanderystii

Becium vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's becium, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth form with thick, waxy leaves adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky soils and access deep water sources.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Becium vanderystii is considered vulnerable and is the subject of conservation efforts.
