Berberis lutea

Berberis lutea, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and vibrant seasonal colors contribute to its ornamental value in temperate climates.
