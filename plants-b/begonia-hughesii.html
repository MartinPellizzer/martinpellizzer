Begonia hughesii

Begonia hughesii, commonly known as the Hughes begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps to reduce water loss.

Begonia hughesii is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
