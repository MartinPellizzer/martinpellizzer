Byttneria melantha

Byttneria melantha, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
