Bulbophyllum cauliflorum var. sikkimense

Bulbophyllum cauliflorum var. sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Himalayan regions of India.

This orchid is characterized by its unique bulb-like structures, which store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its mountainous habitat. It typically produces strikingly colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing on rocks or trees in high-altitude areas.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
