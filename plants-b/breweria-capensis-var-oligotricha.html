Breweria capensis var. oligotricha

Breweria capensis var. oligotricha, commonly known as the Cape brewer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a ornamental plant.
