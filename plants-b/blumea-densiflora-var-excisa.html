Blumea densiflora var. excisa

Blumea densiflora var. excisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
