Bidens daucifolia

Bidens daucifolia, commonly known as golden beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has alternate, hairy, and often lobed leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow with ray florets. It produces small, spiny fruits that are dispersed by wind or animals.

Bidens daucifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
