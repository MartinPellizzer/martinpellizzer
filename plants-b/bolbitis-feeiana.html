Bolbitis feeiana

Bolbitis feeiana, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a type of fern that thrives in harsh, dry environments where other plants struggle to survive. The plant is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it its common name. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a trait typical of ferns.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is highly resilient and adapted to extreme conditions.
