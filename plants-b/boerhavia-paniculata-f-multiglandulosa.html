Boerhavia paniculata f. multiglandulosa

Boerhavia paniculata f. multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular boerhavia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
