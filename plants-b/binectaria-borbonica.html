Binectaria borbonica

Binectaria borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon nettle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes species known for their stinging hairs. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of plant ecology and biodiversity.
