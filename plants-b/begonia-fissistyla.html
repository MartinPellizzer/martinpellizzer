Begonia fissistyla

Begonia fissistyla, commonly known as the fissured begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive fissured or split appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
