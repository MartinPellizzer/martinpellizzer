Brassica ensifera

Brassica ensifera, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, stalk-like stems and tightly packed, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, yellow flowers in the early stages of its growth. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated in temperate regions for its nutritional value and mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Brassica ensifera is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as dietary fiber and antioxidants.
