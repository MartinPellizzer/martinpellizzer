Bulbophyllum lundianum

Bulbophyllum lundianum, commonly known as the Lundian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to East Africa.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive, intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
