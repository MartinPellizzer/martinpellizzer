Beyeria leschenaultii var. drummondii

Beyeria leschenaultii var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's beyeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in dense, disc-like clusters. Its leaves are typically linear to oblanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
