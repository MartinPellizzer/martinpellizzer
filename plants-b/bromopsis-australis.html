Bromopsis australis

Bromopsis australis, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromopsis australis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
