Bondtia behriana

Bondtia behriana, commonly known as the red-tipped plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive red or purple tip. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in spikes.

Bondtia behriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
