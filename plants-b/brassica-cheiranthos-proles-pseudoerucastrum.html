Brassica cheiranthos proles pseudoerucastrum

Brassica cheiranthos proles pseudoerucastrum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in various habitats.
