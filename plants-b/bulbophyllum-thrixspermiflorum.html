Bulbophyllum thrixspermiflorum

Bulbophyllum thrixspermiflorum, commonly known as the hairy seed-bearing orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and growth habits. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to yellow.

The plant's name refers to its hairy appearance and the presence of seeds within its floral structures.
