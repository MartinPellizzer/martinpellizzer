Baccharis lechleri

Baccharis lechleri, commonly known as *urraca* or *sangre de drago*, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its sap, which is highly viscous and has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Baccharis lechleri is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
