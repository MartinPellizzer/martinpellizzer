Berteroa physocarpa

Berteroa physocarpa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its inflated, seed-filled pods that resemble balloons, which burst to disperse seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
