Baccharis axillaris var. acutiloba

Baccharis axillaris var. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
