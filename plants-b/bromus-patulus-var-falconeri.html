Bromus patulus var. falconeri

Bromus patulus var. falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri brome, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its adaptation to extreme climates and its role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
