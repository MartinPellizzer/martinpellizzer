Babiana striata

Babiana striata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower stalks that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Babiana striata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
