Bidens tripartita var. cernuifolia

Bidens tripartita var. cernuifolia, commonly known as the nodding tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and have distinctive white or pale yellow ray florets that droop downward, giving it a nodding appearance.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
