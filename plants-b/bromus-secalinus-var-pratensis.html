Bromus secalinus var. pratensis

Bromus secalinus var. pratensis, commonly known as meadow brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.

This variety is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
