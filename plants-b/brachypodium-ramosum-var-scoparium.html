Brachypodium ramosum var. scoparium

Brachypodium ramosum var. scoparium, commonly known as the scaly brachypodium, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are often covered with fine, scaly structures, giving it its common name.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open, sunny environments.
