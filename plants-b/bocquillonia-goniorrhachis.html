Bocquillonia goniorrhachis

Bocquillonia goniorrhachis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, branching stems that rise above the foliage. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and small, oblong leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
