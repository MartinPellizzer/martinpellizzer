Bulbophyllum digitatum

Bulbophyllum digitatum, commonly known as the finger orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulb-like structure with fleshy leaves and produces delicate, finger-like flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique floral morphology and ability to thrive in tropical climates make it a popular choice for collectors and horticultural enthusiasts.

It requires specific care, including high humidity and well-draining substrates, to flourish.
