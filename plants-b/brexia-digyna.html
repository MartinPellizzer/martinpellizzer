Brexia digyna

Brexia digyna, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky soil, while its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Brexia digyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with suitable climates.
