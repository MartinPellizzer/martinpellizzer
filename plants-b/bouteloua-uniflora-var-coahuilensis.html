Bouteloua uniflora var. coahuilensis

Bouteloua uniflora var. coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuila bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and limestone areas. The plant has narrow, blue-gray leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue flowers. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to low-nutrient conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
