Belostemma hirsutum

Belostemma hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a hairy texture on the stems and leaves. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

Belostemma hirsutum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
