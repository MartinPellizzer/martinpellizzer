Baccharis macrantha subsp. cocuyensis

Baccharis macrantha subsp.

cocuyensis, commonly known as Cocuy grass or Cocuy shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Youth. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical heads, producing a distinctive, often fragrant scent.

It thrives in coastal and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to saline and drought conditions.
