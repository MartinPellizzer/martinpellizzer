Bidens aristosa var. retrorsa

Bidens aristosa var. retrorsa, commonly known as the prairie tickseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets. It typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variant is distinguished by its reflexed (backward-curled) bracts, which give it its specific epithet "retrorsa.".
