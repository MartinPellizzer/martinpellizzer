Brachyotum incrassatum

Brachyotum incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
