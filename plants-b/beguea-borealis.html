Beguea borealis

Beguea borealis, commonly known as the northern begonia, is a flowering plant native to the cooler regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often bi-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps retain moisture.

Beguea borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blossoms.
