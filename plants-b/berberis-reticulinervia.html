Berberis reticulinervia

Berberis reticulinervia, commonly known as the net-veined barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

Berberis reticulinervia is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
