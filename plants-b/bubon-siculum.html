Bubon siculum

Bubon siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Bubon siculum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
