Begonia hirsutula

Begonia hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
