Biscutella valentina var. hungarica

Biscutella valentina var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian valentine biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, with a distinctive, somewhat succulent texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
