Begonia halabanensis

Begonia halabanensis, commonly known as the Maluku begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of vibrant red flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring contrasting patterns of green and silver.

Begonia halabanensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
