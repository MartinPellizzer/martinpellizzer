Betulaster cylindrostachya

Betulaster cylindrostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked birch-leaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae, which includes birches, alders, and hazels. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical staminate catkins and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

Betulaster cylindrostachya typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.
