Barbarea vulgaris var. patens

Barbarea vulgaris var. patens, commonly known as the common winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
