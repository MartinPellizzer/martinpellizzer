Bonnetia rubicunda

Bonnetia rubicunda, commonly known as the red bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Bonnetia rubicunda thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
