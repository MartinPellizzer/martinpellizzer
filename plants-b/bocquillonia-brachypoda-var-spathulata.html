Bocquillonia brachypoda var. spathulata

Bocquillonia brachypoda var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bocquillonia, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, spatulate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated floral structure. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The leaves are often hairy and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
