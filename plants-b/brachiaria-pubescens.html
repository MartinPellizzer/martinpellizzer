Brachiaria pubescens

Brachiaria pubescens, commonly known as hairy blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Brachiaria pubescens is also used in some regions for forage and erosion control.
