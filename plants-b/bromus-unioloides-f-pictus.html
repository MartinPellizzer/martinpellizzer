Bromus unioloides f. pictus

Bromus unioloides f. pictus, commonly known as the painted false brome, is a horticultural form of the grass species Bromus unioloides.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of green, white, and pink, creating a visually appealing appearance. This plant is typically grown as an ornamental grass in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of temperate climates.

The variegation in its leaves is a result of genetic mutations that affect chlorophyll distribution, enhancing its ornamental appeal.
