Bupleurum aucheri

Bupleurum aucheri, commonly known as Persian burnet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Bupleurum aucheri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
