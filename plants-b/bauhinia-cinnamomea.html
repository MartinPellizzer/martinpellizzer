Bauhinia cinnamomea

Bauhinia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a woody structure with a thick, flaky bark. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant color.

Bauhinia cinnamomea thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
