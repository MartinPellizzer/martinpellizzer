Brachystegia hockii var. papyracea

Brachystegia hockii var. papyracea, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and smooth, grey bark. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, clustered leaflets, and the tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
