Berberis conferta var. hypopyrrantha

Berberis conferta var. hypopyrrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
