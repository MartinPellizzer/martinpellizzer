Baccharis megapotamica var. megapotamica

Baccharis megapotamica var. megapotamica, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates, making it a common ornamental plant.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
