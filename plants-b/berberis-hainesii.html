Berberis hainesii

Berberis hainesii, commonly known as Haines barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, glossy dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are ornamental but not typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
