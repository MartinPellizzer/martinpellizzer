Bombax glaucescens

Bombax glaucescens, commonly known as the woolly ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and a tall, straight trunk that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing cotton-like seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
