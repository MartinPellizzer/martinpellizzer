Begonia palmata var. bowringiana

Begonia palmata var. bowringiana, commonly known as the Bowring's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, waxy, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive, almost palm-like appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
