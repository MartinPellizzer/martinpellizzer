Brassica oleracea f. viridis

Brassica oleracea f. viridis, commonly known as the curly kale or green cabbage, is a cultivated variety of the wild cabbage plant.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often curled or crinkled in appearance. The plant is a biennial, though it is typically grown as an annual in agricultural settings.

It thrives in cool climates and is rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. The leaves are used in cooking and are also valued for their medicinal properties.
