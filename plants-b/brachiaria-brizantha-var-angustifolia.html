Brachiaria brizantha var. angustifolia

Brachiaria brizantha var. angustifolia, commonly known as signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. It is often used in forage production due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and erosion control.
