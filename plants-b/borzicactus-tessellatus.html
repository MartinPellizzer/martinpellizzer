Borzicactus tessellatus

Borzicactus tessellatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, ribbed stems that form a distinctive zigzag pattern, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its adaptations include a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and spines for protection against herbivores.

Borzicactus tessellatus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
