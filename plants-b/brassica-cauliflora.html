Brassica cauliflora

Brassica cauliflora, commonly known as the broccoli plant, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, green flower heads that form at the apex of the stem, resembling a cauliflower. The plant typically grows to a height of about 50 to 100 centimeters and has large, lobed leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassica cauliflora is a cultivated variety widely grown for its edible florets and nutritional value.
