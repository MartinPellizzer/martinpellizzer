Bacopa stricta

Bacopa stricta, commonly known as aquatic bacopa, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, with its stems often submerged or partially submerged. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a smooth surface. Bacopa stricta is often used in aquatic gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
