Bambusa pallescens

Bambusa pallescens, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, silver-gray culms that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Bambusa pallescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
