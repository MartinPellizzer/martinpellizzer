Berberis hispanica

Berberis hispanica, commonly known as Spanish barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features spiny, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour in taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to deter animals with its prickly foliage.

Additionally, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
