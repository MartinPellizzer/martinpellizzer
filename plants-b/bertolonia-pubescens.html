Bertolonia pubescens

Bertolonia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bertolonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Bertolonia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
