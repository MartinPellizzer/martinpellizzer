Bauhinia glauca var. caterviflora

Bauhinia glauca var. caterviflora, commonly known as the clustered bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Bauhinia glauca var. caterviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
