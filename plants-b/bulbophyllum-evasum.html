Bulbophyllum evasum

Bulbophyllum evasum, commonly known as the Evasum orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.
