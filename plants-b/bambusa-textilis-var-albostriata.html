Bambusa textilis var. albostriata

Bambusa textilis var. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Bambusa.

It is characterized by its striking white longitudinal stripes on the culms, which contrast with its green or yellowish base. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows upright, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
