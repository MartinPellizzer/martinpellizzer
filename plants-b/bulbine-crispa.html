Bulbine crispa

Bulbine crispa, commonly known as the serrated bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
