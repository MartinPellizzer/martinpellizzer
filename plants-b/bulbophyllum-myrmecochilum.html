Bulbophyllum myrmecochilum

Bulbophyllum myrmecochilum, commonly known as the ant-eating orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble the shape of an ant's head, hence its common name. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it often grows epiphytically on trees.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
