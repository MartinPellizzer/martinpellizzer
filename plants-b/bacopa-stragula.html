Bacopa stragula

Bacopa stragula, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue or violet flowers clustered in whorls. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Bacopa stragula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
