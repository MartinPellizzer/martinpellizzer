Braxireon

Braxireon, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with long, slender leaves and a single, intricate flower that resembles a ghostly silhouette. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or in swampy areas. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in many regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its role in the study of plant adaptation to wetland ecosystems.
