Begonia semperflorens f. flavescens

Begonia semperflorens f. flavescens, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a hybrid plant characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a waxy texture, and its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and yellow. It is a popular choice for container gardening due to its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-draining soil.

This cultivar is also valued for its resilience and ease of care, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor environments.
