Burmeistera virescens var. subdentata

Burmeistera virescens var. subdentata, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble firecrackers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a slight waxy coating, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its eye-catching floral display.
