Blumea crispata

Blumea crispata, commonly known as the crepe flower or mountain blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, hairy, and often crinkled leaves, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Blumea crispata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
