Bacopa monnieri var. cuneifolia

Bacopa monnieri var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces tiny blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional medicine for cognitive and neurological benefits.
