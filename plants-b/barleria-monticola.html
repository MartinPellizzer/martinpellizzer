Barleria monticola

Barleria monticola, commonly known as the mountain barleria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Barleria monticola is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
