Bambusa macra

Bambusa macra, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, erect stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern. Its culms are hollow and have a smooth, greenish-brown surface.

Bambusa macra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
