Brassica sinaica

Brassica sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai mustard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, narrow leaves adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Brassica sinaica typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
