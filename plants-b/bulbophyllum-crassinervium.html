Bulbophyllum crassinervium

Bulbophyllum crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and indirect light.

Bulbophyllum crassinervium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
