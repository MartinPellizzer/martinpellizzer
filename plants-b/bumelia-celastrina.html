Bumelia celastrina

Bumelia celastrina, commonly known as the fragrant buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its aromatic leaves and bark have been used historically by Native American tribes for medicinal purposes.

Bumelia celastrina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
