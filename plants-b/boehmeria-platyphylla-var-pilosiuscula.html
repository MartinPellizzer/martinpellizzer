Boehmeria platyphylla var. pilosiuscula

Boehmeria platyphylla var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy flat-leaf nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered with dense, short, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered in panicles, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant.
