Betula pumila

Betula pumila, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a member of the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, compact growth habit. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula pumila is well adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems.
