Briquetina melliodora

Briquetina melliodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented brier, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Briquetina melliodora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
