Balsamina

Balsamina, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Balsaminaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its showy, delicate flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have a distinctive spurred petal at the back.

Balsamina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
