Berzelia comosa

Berzelia comosa, commonly known as the woolly berzelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle floral display to its habitat. Berzelia comosa typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions, showcasing resilience typical of desert flora.
