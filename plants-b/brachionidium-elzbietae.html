Brachionidium elzbietae

Brachionidium elzbietae, commonly known as the Elzbietae water flea, is a microscopic aquatic crustacean belonging to the order Cladocera.

It is characterized by its small, transparent body and a distinctive head with a pair of antennae. This species is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it plays a role in the aquatic food web. Its diet consists mainly of algae and other microscopic organisms.

Due to its size and ecological significance, it is often used in ecological studies to assess water quality and ecosystem health.
