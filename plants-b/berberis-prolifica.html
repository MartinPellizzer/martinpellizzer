Berberis prolifica

Berberis prolifica, commonly known as the prolific barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which provide structural support and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are glossy and alternate, typically ranging from 2 to 6 centimeters in length.

Berberis prolifica is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
