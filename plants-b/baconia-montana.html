Baconia montana

Baconia montana, commonly known as the mountain baconia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Baconia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
