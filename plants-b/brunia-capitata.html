Brunia capitata

Brunia capitata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a low-growing, shrub-like structure with slender, woody stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Brunia capitata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
