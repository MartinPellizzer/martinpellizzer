Brassia striata

Brassia striata, commonly known as the striated orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and produces elegant, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, showcasing intricate, spurred petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining potting media.

Brassia striata is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in a home setting.
