Buddleja subserrata

Buddleja subserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features narrow, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from pale blue to violet, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja subserrata is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
