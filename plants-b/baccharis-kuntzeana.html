Baccharis kuntzeana

Baccharis kuntzeana, commonly known as Kuntze's baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in some regions.
