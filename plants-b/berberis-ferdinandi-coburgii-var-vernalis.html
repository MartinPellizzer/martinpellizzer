Berberis ferdinandi-coburgii var. vernalis

Berberis ferdinandi-coburgii var. vernalis, commonly known as the Coburg barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used ornamentally. It has herringbone-patterned leaves that add visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
