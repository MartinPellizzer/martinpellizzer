Bauhinia paradisi

Bauhinia paradisi, commonly known as the paradise bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Bauhinia paradisi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
