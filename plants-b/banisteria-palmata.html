Banisteria palmata

Banisteria palmata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or spider plant, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forests and roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
