Brickellia knappiana

Brickellia knappiana, commonly known as Knapp's brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia knappiana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
