Benthamia perfecunda

Benthamia perfecunda, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
