Begonia meysseliana

Begonia meysseliana, commonly known as the Meissner begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to almost black. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
