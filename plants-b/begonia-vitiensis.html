Begonia vitiensis

Begonia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly Vanuatu.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a rare and exotic houseplant.
