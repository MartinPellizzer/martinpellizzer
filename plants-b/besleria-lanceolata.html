Besleria lanceolata

Besleria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and yellow, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
