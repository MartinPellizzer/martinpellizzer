Byttneria rivularis

Byttneria rivularis, commonly known as the river balsa, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to moist environments and can thrive in both wetland and riparian habitats.
