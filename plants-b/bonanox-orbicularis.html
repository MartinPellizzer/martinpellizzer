Bonanox orbicularis

Bonanox orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf marvel, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its orbicular, or round, leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, Bonanox orbicularis is now considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
