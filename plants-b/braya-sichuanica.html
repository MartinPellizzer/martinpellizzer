Braya sichuanica

Braya sichuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan brasswort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Braya sichuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
