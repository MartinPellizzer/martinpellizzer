Barringtonia flava

Barringtonia flava, commonly known as the yellow buttonwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a smooth, round capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Barringtonia flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
