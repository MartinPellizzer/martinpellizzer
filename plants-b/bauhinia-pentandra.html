Bauhinia pentandra

Bauhinia pentandra, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven pairs of elliptical leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine.
