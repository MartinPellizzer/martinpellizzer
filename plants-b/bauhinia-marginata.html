Bauhinia marginata

Bauhinia marginata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, fern-like leaflets. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
