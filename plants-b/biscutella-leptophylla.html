Biscutella leptophylla

Biscutella leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Biscutella leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
