Beta vulgaris

Beta vulgaris, commonly known as beet, is a biennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for thousands of years for its edible roots and leaves. Beta vulgaris is also used in the production of sugar beets, which are a major source of sucrose.

Its versatility makes it an important crop in agriculture and nutrition.
