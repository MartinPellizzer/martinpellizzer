Bubbia rivularis

Bubbia rivularis, commonly known as the river bladder, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that help it float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the inflated stems are the primary means of photosynthesis.

Bubbia rivularis plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms.
