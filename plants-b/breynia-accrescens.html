Breynia accrescens

Breynia accrescens, commonly known as the climbing breynia, is a shrub or vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems are capable of climbing and can develop a woody texture over time.

Breynia accrescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
