Brasolia floribunda

Brasolia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a preference for well-drained, fertile soil in full sun.

Brasolia floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
