Buddleja skutchii subsp. skutchii

Buddleja skutchii subsp.

skutchii, commonly known as the Skutch's bush willow, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
