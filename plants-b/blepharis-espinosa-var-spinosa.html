Blepharis espinosa var. spinosa

Blepharis espinosa var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny blepharis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in stiff, spiny hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
