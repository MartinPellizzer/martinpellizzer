Berberis chitria

Berberis chitria, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
