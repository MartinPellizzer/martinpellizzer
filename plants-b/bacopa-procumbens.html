Bacopa procumbens

Bacopa procumbens, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, small opposite leaves, and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Bacopa procumbens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cognitive and calming effects.
