Bulbophyllum longiflorum

Bulbophyllum longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered bletilla, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for horticultural purposes.
