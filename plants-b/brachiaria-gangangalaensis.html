Brachiaria gangangalaensis

Brachiaria gangangalaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or gangangalaensis grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions. Brachiaria gangangalaensis is often found in grasslands and open habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local wildlife.
