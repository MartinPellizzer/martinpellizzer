Baccharis ptariensis

Baccharis ptariensis, commonly known as the Andean baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Baccharis ptariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
