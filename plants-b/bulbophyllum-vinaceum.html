Bulbophyllum vinaceum

Bulbophyllum vinaceum, commonly known as the red orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and pink flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its tropical habitat. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
