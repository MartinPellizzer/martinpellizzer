Blechnum longifolium var. gracile

Blechnum longifolium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern genus characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia and the Philippines, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread slowly across forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance, with finely divided pinnae that give it a lacy texture.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture and is often used in shaded garden settings.
