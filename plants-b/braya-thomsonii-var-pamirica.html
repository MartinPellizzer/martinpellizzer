Braya thomsonii var. pamirica

Braya thomsonii var. pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir rock-brake, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence.

This variety is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.
