Brachyotum sertulatum

Brachyotum sertulatum, commonly known as the clustered brachyotum, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.

Brachyotum sertulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes.
