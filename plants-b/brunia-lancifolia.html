Brunia lancifolia

Brunia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brunia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Brunia lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
