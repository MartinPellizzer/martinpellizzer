Brahea calcarea

Brahea calcarea, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a species of palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of stiff, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits.

Brahea calcarea is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique aesthetic.
