Brodiaea hyacinthina var. lilacina

Brodiaea hyacinthina var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac brodiaea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of lilac and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
