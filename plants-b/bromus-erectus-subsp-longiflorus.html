Bromus erectus subsp. longiflorus

Bromus erectus subsp.

longiflorus, commonly known as the tall floret brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
