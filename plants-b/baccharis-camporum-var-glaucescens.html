Baccharis camporum var. glaucescens

Baccharis camporum var. glaucescens, commonly known as bluegum baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It typically grows in dry to moist habitats, including grasslands and shrublands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by fluffy, white to gray seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
