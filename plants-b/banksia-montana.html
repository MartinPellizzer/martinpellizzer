Banksia montana

Banksia montana, commonly known as the mountain banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Banksia montana plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly nectar-feeding birds and insects.
