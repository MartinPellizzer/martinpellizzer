Begonia pedatifida var. kewensis

Begonia pedatifida var. kewensis, commonly known as the Kew begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Malaysia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is popular among horticulturists for its unique foliage and ease of cultivation indoors.
