Bertya oppositifolia

Bertya oppositifolia, commonly known as the golden bertya, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Bertya oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
