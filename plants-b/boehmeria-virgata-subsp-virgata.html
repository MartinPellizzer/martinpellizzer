Boehmeria virgata subsp. virgata

Boehmeria virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as silky fiber plant or Chinese nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, silky texture.

The leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically flowering in the summer months.

This plant is valued for its fiber, which is used in traditional textiles and papermaking.
