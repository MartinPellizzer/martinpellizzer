Blepharocalyx divaricatus var. obovatus

Blepharocalyx divaricatus var. obovatus, commonly known as the obovate evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical to obovate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five broad petals, often in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
