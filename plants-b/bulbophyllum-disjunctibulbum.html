Bulbophyllum disjunctibulbum

Bulbophyllum disjunctibulbum, commonly known as the Disjointed Bulb Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
