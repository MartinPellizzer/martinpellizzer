Babiana secunda

Babiana secunda, commonly known as the rain lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that die back after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Babiana secunda is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the year in suitable climates.
