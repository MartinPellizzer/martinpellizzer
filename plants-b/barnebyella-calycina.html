Barnebyella calycina

Barnebyella calycina, commonly known as the Calycine orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant mix of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant has a delicate, sympodial growth habit, with slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are highly specialized, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Barnebyella calycina is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
