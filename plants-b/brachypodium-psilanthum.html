Brachypodium psilanthum

Brachypodium psilanthum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its small flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces seeds in compact clusters.

Brachypodium psilanthum is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
