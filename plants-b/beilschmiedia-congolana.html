Beilschmiedia congolana

Beilschmiedia congolana, commonly known as the Congo beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
