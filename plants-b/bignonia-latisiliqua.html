Bignonia latisiliqua

Bignonia latisiliqua, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Bignonia latisiliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant.
