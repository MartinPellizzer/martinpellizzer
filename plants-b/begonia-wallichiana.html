Begonia wallichiana

Begonia wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often variegated, with a mix of green and silver tones, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia wallichiana is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
