Bromus gunckelii

Bromus gunckelii, commonly known as the Gunckel's brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, compact spikelet that is usually pale green to brown in color.

Bromus gunckelii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
