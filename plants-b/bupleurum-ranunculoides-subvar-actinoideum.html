Bupleurum ranunculoides subvar. actinoideum

Bupleurum ranunculoides subvar. actinoideum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and a flowering stem that bears clusters of yellow-orange flowers resembling bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
