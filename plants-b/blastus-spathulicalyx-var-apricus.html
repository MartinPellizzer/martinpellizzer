Blastus spathulicalyx var. apricus

Blastus spathulicalyx var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot-leafed blastus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of apricot trees, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
