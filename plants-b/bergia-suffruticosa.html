Bergia suffruticosa

Bergia suffruticosa, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like form with dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Bergia suffruticosa thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or cliff habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
