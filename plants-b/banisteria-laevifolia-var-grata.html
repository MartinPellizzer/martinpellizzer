Banisteria laevifolia var. grata

Banisteria laevifolia var. grata, commonly known as the Grateful Banisteria, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
