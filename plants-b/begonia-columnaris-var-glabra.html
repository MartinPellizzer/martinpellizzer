Begonia columnaris var. glabra

Begonia columnaris var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring contrasting colors that add visual interest to gardens or indoor spaces.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
