Bulbophyllum erythrochilum

Bulbophyllum erythrochilum, commonly known as the red-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically in late summer. Its inflorescence is a spike bearing numerous small, delicate blooms.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
