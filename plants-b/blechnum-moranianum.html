Blechnum moranianum

Blechnum moranianum, commonly known as the Moranian harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Blechnaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that are often curled or twisted. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
