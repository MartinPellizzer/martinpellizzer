Boehmeria × tenuifolia f. conferta

Boehmeria × tenuifolia f. conferta, commonly known as the thick-leaved hemp nettle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and robust, branching stems that often grow in clusters. The leaves are typically broad, ovate to elliptical in shape, and covered with a fine, soft pubescence. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its fibrous stems and leaves make it a valuable resource in traditional textile and medicinal practices.
