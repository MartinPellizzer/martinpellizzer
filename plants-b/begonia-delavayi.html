Begonia delavayi

Begonia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers cool, humid environments with partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Begonia delavayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
