Bromus squarrosus var. patulus

Bromus squarrosus var. patulus, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
