Bouchea fluminensis f. albiflora

Bouchea fluminensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered river bouchea, is a rare and striking plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and forest edges. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are often overlooked beneath the vibrant bracts.
