Basanacantha phyllosepala

Basanacantha phyllosepala, commonly known as the leaf-sepaled spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and often milky sap. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
