Bulbophyllum longipedicellatum

Bulbophyllum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and intricate, spider-like flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
