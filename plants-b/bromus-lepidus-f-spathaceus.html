Bromus lepidus f. spathaceus

Bromus lepidus f. spathaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brome, is a grass species characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
