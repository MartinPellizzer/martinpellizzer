Blumea guineensis

Blumea guineensis, commonly known as African blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, pleasant scent.

Blumea guineensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
