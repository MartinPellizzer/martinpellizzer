Biasolettia barbeyi

Biasolettia barbeyi, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, woody plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
