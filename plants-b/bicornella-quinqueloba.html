Bicornella quinqueloba

Bicornella quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has five lobes on both the sepals and petals, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Bicornella quinqueloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
