Baphia aurivellerea

Baphia aurivellerea, commonly known as the golden baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant floral display.
