Bruguiera madagascariensis

Bruguiera madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its pneumatophores, which are specialized root structures that allow the plant to absorb oxygen from the air in waterlogged soil. This plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, playing a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems. Its dense root systems help prevent erosion and provide habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.

Bruguiera madagascariensis is also an important component of mangrove forests, contributing to biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
