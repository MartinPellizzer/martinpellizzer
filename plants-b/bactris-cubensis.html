Bactris cubensis

Bactris cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban palm or Cuban areca, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a slender, unbranched trunk with smooth, grayish bark and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a round, fibrous structure that contains a single edible seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
