Bogoria carinata

Bogoria carinata, commonly known as the ridge-fruited bog orchid, is a rare and specialized plant found in high-altitude bog ecosystems.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, including a slender stem and a single, showy flower with a distinctive ridge-like structure on the lip. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity and cool temperatures. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient uptake from the impoverished soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Bogoria carinata is considered a vulnerable species.
