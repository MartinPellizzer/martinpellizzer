Bauhinia eriophora

Bauhinia eriophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Bauhinia eriophora is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
