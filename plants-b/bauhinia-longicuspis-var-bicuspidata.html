Bauhinia longicuspis var. bicuspidata

Bauhinia longicuspis var. bicuspidata, commonly known as the two-cusp bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often divided into small, thread-like segments. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in regions with suitable conditions.
