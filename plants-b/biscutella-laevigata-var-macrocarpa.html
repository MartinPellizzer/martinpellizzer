Biscutella laevigata var. macrocarpa

Biscutella laevigata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several large, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience and unique botanical characteristics.
