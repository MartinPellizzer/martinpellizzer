Brachionidium dentatum

Brachionidium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed brachionid, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, serrated leaves.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has a compact growth habit and often forms dense mats on the water surface. Its small, delicate leaves are adapted for efficient photosynthesis in shallow waters.

Brachionidium dentatum plays a role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
