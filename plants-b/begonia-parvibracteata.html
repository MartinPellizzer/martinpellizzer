Begonia parvibracteata

Begonia parvibracteata, commonly known as the small-bract begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and prefers shady, moist environments. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Begonia parvibracteata is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
