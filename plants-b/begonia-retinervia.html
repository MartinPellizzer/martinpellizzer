Begonia retinervia

Begonia retinervia, commonly known as the net-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with intricate, net-like venation patterns. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure.

Begonia retinervia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
