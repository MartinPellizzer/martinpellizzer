Bolbidium gracile

Bolbidium gracile, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. The plant has a compact, low-growing structure with numerous small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale pink, and are arranged in dense clusters near the base of the plant.

Bolbidium gracile is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent stems and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
