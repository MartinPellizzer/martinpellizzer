Bauhinia wrayi var. cardiophylla

Bauhinia wrayi var. cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and attractive flowers, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its ability to provide habitat for small insects.
