Bauhinia mombassae

Bauhinia mombassae, commonly known as the African mallow or Mombassa bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, delicate leaflets and produces showy, papilionaceous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Bauhinia mombassae is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
