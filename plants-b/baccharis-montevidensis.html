Baccharis montevidensis

Baccharis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Uruguay, Brazil, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a grayish-green hue, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

Baccharis montevidensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
