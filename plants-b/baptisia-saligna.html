Baptisia saligna

Baptisia saligna, commonly known as eastern false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system consists of long, fleshy taproots. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Baptisia saligna is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
