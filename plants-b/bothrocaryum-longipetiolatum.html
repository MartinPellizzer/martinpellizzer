Bothrocaryum longipetiolatum

Bothrocaryum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
