Bulbophyllum auriculatum

Bulbophyllum auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ear-shaped pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its epiphytic nature allows it to grow on trees or rocks, often in forest canopies.

Bulbophyllum auriculatum is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
