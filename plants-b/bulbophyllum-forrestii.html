Bulbophyllum forrestii

Bulbophyllum forrestii, commonly known as the Forrest's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in seasonal conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
