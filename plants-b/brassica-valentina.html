Brassica valentina

Brassica valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's Brassica, is a lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny location. It is native to parts of Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within the Brassica genus.
