Brasiliorchis consanguinea

Brasiliorchis consanguinea, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and thrives in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from decaying organic matter, a common trait among orchids.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
