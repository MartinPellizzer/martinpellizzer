Bulbocodium montanum

Bulbocodium montanum, commonly known as the mountain bulbocodium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Bulbocodium montanum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
