Begonia veitchii var. machupicchuensis

Begonia veitchii var. machupicchuensis, commonly known as the Machu Picchu begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a striking red or orange flower cluster. This variety thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage exhibits a unique coloration, ranging from deep green to bronze, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The plant is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
