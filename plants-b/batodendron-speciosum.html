Batodendron speciosum

Batodendron speciosum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms that range from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique morphology includes tendrils that help it climb and anchor to trees, forming a symbiotic relationship with its host.

Due to habitat loss, Batodendron speciosum is currently listed as endangered in its natural range.
