Bursera karsteniana

Bursera karsteniana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

Bursera karsteniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of resin.
