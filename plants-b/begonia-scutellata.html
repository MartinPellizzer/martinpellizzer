Begonia scutellata

Begonia scutellata, commonly known as the shieldleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually small and tubular, often in shades of red or orange.

Begonia scutellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in horticulture for indoor and garden settings.
