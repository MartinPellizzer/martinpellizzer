Braya humilis f. biloba

Braya humilis f. biloba, commonly known as the bilobed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Braya humilis f. biloba is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
