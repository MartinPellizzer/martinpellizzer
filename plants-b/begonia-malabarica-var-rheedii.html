Begonia malabarica var. rheedii

Begonia malabarica var. rheedii, commonly known as the Rheed's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often feature intricate patterns and contrasting veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
