Bouteloua papillosa

Bouteloua papillosa, commonly known as gray false bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for prairies and dry soils.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
