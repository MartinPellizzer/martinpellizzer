Begonia hooveriana

Begonia hooveriana, commonly known as the Hoover's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its velvety, deep green leaves that often feature intricate patterns and a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners seeking exotic plants.
