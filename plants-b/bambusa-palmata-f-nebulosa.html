Bambusa palmata f. nebulosa

Bambusa palmata f. nebulosa, commonly known as the cloud bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its distinctive silvery-white to pale blue sheathes that give it a misty appearance.

It is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Southeast Asia, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces inflorescences that are typically hidden within the foliage. Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its adaptability to various growing conditions.
