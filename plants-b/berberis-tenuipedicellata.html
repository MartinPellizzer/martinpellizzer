Berberis tenuipedicellata

Berberis tenuipedicellata, commonly known as the slender-fruited barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
