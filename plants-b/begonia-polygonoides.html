Begonia polygonoides

Begonia polygonoides, commonly known as the polygon-like begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and delicate floral display.
