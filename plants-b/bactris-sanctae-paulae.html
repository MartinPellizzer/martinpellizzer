Bactris sanctae-paulae

Bactris sanctae-paulae, commonly known as the Pauline palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are about 2-3 cm in diameter. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation and horticultural purposes.
