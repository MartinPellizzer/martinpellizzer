Brassica tournefortii

Brassica tournefortii, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, helping it retain moisture.

Brassica tournefortii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
