Betula ermanii var. subcordata

Betula ermanii var. subcordata, commonly known as the Japanese paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant form and white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
