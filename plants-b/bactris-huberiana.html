Bactris huberiana

Bactris huberiana, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellow fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. Bactris huberiana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
