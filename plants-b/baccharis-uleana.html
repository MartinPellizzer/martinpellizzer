Baccharis uleana

Baccharis uleana, commonly known as Brazilian erythroxylum, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open areas and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid climates. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's structure includes woody stems, alternate leaves, and capitula that produce seeds for propagation.
