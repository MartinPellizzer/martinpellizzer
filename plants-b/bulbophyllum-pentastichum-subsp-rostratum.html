Bulbophyllum pentastichum subsp. rostratum

Bulbophyllum pentastichum subsp.

rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence bears multiple flowers arranged in a raceme.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
