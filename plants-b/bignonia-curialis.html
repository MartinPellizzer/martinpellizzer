Bignonia curialis

Bignonia curialis, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a vigorous climber with woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia curialis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
