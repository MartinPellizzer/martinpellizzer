Brachylobus

Brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stalked orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have a short rhizome and are found in various habitats, including grasslands and woodlands. Brachylobus species are native to regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological roles in their native environments.
