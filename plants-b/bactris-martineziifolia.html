Bactris martineziifolia

Bactris martineziifolia, commonly known as the Guayabilla, is a palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange inflorescences with small, clustered flowers, which are followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
