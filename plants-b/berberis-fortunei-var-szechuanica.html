Berberis fortunei var. szechuanica

Berberis fortunei var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice in horticulture.
