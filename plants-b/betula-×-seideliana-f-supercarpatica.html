Betula × seideliana f. supercarpatica

Betula × seideliana f. supercarpatica, commonly known as the white birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in papery strips.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is native to eastern North America. The tree produces small, cone-like fruits that contain triangular seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated margins, turning yellow in the fall.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
