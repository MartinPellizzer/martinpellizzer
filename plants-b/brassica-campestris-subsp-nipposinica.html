Brassica campestris subsp. nipposinica

Brassica campestris subsp.

nipposinica, commonly known as the Japanese mustard. It is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia, characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include lobed leaves and a strong, pungent odor due to glucosinolates.
