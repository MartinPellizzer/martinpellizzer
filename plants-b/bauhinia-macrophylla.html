Bauhinia macrophylla

Bauhinia macrophylla, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, producing vibrant red or orange flowers. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
