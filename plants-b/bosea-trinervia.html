Bosea trinervia

Bosea trinervia, commonly known as the small-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Bosea trinervia is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
