Berberis phanera var. glaucosubtusa

Berberis phanera var. glaucosubtusa, commonly known as the glaucous-leaf barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
