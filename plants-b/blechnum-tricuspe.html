Blechnum tricuspe

Blechnum tricuspe, commonly known as the three-lobed holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with three lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a leathery texture.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in the wild.
