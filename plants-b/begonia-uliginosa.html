Begonia uliginosa

Begonia uliginosa, commonly known as the bog begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy or marshy areas. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and come in shades of pink or red.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
