Begonia carrieae

Begonia carrieae, commonly known as the Carrie's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a distinctive texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
