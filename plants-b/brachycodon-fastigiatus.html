Brachycodon fastigiatus

Brachycodon fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
