Bulbophyllum poekilon

Bulbophyllum poekilon, commonly known as the "Bull's Eye Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bulbous pseudobulbs and striking, colorful flowers that often feature a contrasting central spot. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bulbophyllum poekilon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
