Bignonia obovata

Bignonia obovata, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees or trellises.

Bignonia obovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
