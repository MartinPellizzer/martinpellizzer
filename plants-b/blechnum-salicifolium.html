Blechnum salicifolium

Blechnum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble willow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Blechnum salicifolium plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
