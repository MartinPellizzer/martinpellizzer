Barleria cristata var. dichotoma

Barleria cristata var. dichotoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, pink, or purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
