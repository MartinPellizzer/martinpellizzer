Bambusa boryana

Bambusa boryana, commonly known as the Boryana bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clustered, oval-shaped fruits.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
