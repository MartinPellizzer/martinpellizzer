Besleria calceolus

Besleria calceolus, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in the dry seasons.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and fragrance.
