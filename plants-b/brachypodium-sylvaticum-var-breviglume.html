Brachypodium sylvaticum var. breviglume

Brachypodium sylvaticum var. breviglume, commonly known as the slender brachypodium, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually green or brown, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
