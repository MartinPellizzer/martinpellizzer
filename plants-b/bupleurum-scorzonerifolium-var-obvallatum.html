Bupleurum scorzonerifolium var. obvallatum

Bupleurum scorzonerifolium var. obvallatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
