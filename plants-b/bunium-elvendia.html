Bunium elvendia

Bunium elvendia, commonly known as the Armenian fennel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely dissected leaves that resemble those of fennel. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The seeds are small, ridged, and often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
