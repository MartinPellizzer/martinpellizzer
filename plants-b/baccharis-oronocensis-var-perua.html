Baccharis oronocensis var. perua

Baccharis oronocensis var. perua, commonly known as the Peruvian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
