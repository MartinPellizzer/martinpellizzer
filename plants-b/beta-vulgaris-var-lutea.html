Beta vulgaris var. lutea

Beta vulgaris var. lutea, commonly known as the golden beet, is a cultivated variant of the beetroot plant characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange root color.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its edible roots and leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with thick, fleshy stems and lobed, heart-shaped leaves. Its roots are rich in nutrients such as iron, fiber, and antioxidants, making it a nutritious vegetable.

The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often requiring a growing season of 50–70 days to mature.
