Bidens capillifolia

Bidens capillifolia, commonly known as the smallleaf beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often with a distinctive bristly appearance.

Bidens capillifolia is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
