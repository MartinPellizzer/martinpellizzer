Buchnera cernua

Buchnera cernua, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Buchnera cernua thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
