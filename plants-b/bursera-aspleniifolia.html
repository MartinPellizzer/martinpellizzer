Bursera aspleniifolia

Bursera aspleniifolia, commonly known as the Mexican balsam or sacred tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by rounded, fleshy fruits that contain smooth, black seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
