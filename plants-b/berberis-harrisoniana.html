Berberis harrisoniana

Berberis harrisoniana, commonly known as the Harrison's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, glossy dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.

Berberis harrisoniana is also noted for its ability to thrive in rocky soils and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
