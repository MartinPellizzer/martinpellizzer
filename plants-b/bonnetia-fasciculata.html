Bonnetia fasciculata

Bonnetia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bonnetia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, branching growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in heads, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bonnetia fasciculata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
