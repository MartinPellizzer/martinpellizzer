Bolbitis sinuosa

Bolbitis sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often curl and twist, giving it a unique, sinuous appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes form dense mats, helping to stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Bolbitis sinuosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in aquascaping.
