Begonia hispidivillosa

Begonia hispidivillosa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
