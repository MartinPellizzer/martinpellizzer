Brillantaisia madagascariensis

Brillantaisia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar starflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of tubular disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
