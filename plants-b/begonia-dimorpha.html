Begonia dimorpha

Begonia dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, often red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia dimorpha is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
