Blumea arabidea

Blumea arabidea, commonly known as the mountain blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are often surrounded by pale green bracts.

Blumea arabidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
