Bidens leptocephala var. typica

Bidens leptocephala var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-headed tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetlands and along streambanks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with distinctive white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
