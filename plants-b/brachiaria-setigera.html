Brachiaria setigera

Brachiaria setigera, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with hairy surfaces, and its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in pastures and along roadsides.

Brachiaria setigera is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
