Bidens grandiflora var. humilis

Bidens grandiflora var. humilis, commonly known as the smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet soils in open habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with prominent white markings. Its flowers bloom in the summer and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
