Boehmeria paraspicata f. viridis

Boehmeria paraspicata f. viridis, commonly known as the green-fibred nettle, is a variant of Boehmeria paraspicata, a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fibrous stems are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
