Besleria rosea

Besleria rosea, commonly known as the red-flowered arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, adding a striking visual element to its appearance.

Besleria rosea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, warm environments.
