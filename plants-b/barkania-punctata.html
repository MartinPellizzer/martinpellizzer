Barkania punctata

Barkania punctata, commonly known as the punctate lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dark green to blackish upper surface and a lighter, often whitish lower surface. The thallus is composed of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga, and a fungal partner. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Its unique punctate appearance is due to the arrangement of its vegetative structures.
