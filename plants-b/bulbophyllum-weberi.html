Bulbophyllum weberi

Bulbophyllum weberi, commonly known as the Weber's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Bulbophyllum weberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
