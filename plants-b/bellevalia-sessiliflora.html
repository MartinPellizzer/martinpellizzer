Bellevalia sessiliflora

Bellevalia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to high-altitude environments.

Bellevalia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
