Bupleurum triradiatum var. arcticum

Bupleurum triradiatum var. arcticum, commonly known as Arctic bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
