Betula nitida var. affinis

Betula nitida var. affinis, commonly known as the Chinese cliff maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, pendulous catkins and has a distinctive, twisted trunk that gives it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Betula nitida var. affinis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
