Bermudiana bermudiana var. pachyrhiza

Bermudiana bermudiana var. pachyrhiza, commonly known as the Bermuda grass variant with thick roots, is a perennial grass species native to the subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
