Banksia eyrei

Banksia eyrei, commonly known as the black banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, blackish flower spikes that are dense and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces woody cones that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a common sight in heathlands and open forests.

Banksia eyrei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for native wildlife.
