Baccharis tricuneata var. robusta

Baccharis tricuneata var. robusta, commonly known as the robusta baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, dry fruits known as achenes. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological research.
