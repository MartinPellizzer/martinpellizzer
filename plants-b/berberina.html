Berberina

Berberina, commonly known as the barberry, is a genus of shrubs belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Berberina species are native to temperate regions of Asia, Europe, and North America.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
