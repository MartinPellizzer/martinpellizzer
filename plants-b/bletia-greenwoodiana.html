Bletia greenwoodiana

Bletia greenwoodiana, commonly known as the greenwood's bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are broad and oval, arranged in a rosette shape, and it has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species.

Bletia greenwoodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
