Bulbophyllum garupinum

Bulbophyllum garupinum, commonly known as the Garupinum orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking floral morphology. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that includes specialized petals and sepals.

Bulbophyllum garupinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
