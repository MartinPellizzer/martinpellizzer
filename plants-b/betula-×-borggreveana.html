Betula × borggreveana

Betula × borggreveana, commonly known as the white poplar hybrid, is a deciduous tree that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Betula pendula and Betula platyphylla.

It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive white bark and rapid growth. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds, typical of the Populus genus. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in the autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
