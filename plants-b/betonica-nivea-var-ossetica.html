Betonica nivea var. ossetica

Betonica nivea var. ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian water plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves that are often silver-white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Betonica nivea var. ossetica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
