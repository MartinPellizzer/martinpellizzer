Bernardia scabra var. longipila

Bernardia scabra var. longipila, commonly known as the long-stemmed Bernardia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
