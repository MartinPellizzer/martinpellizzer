Bredemeyera fruticulosa

Bredemeyera fruticulosa, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branched structure and fleshy, paddle-shaped stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Bredemeyera fruticulosa is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
