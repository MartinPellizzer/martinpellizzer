Bertya pomaderrioides var. angustifolia

Bertya pomaderrioides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bertya, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
