Brassavola digbyana f. fimbripetala

Brassavola digbyana f. fimbripetala, commonly known as the Fimbriated Digby's Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and the inflorescence is a raceme of pendulous flowers with intricate, fimbriated lips.

This orchid is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
