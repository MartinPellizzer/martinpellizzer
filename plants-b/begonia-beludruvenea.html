Begonia beludruvenea

Begonia beludruvenea, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understorey of tropical forests. Its leaves are glossy and have a unique, waxy texture, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Begonia beludruvenea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
