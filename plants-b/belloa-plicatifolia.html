Belloa plicatifolia

Belloa plicatifolia, commonly known as the pleated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens.

Belloa plicatifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
