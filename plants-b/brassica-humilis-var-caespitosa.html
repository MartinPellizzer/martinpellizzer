Brassica humilis var. caespitosa

Brassica humilis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the dwarf cabbage or winter cabbage, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the mustard family. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
