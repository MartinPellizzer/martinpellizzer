Begonia pedrabrancensis

Begonia pedrabrancensis, commonly known as the Pedrabrancensis begonia, is a species native to the humid forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purplish tones. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red and pink, often with contrasting white markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found growing along stream banks or in rocky crevices.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
