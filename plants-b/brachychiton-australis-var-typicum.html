Brachychiton australis var. typicum

Brachychiton australis var. typicum, commonly known as the broad-leafed trumpet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
