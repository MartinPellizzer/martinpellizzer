Bignonia mansoana

Bignonia mansoana, commonly known as the Mexican crossvine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Bignonia mansoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
