Brassica oleracea var. obovata

Brassica oleracea var. obovata, commonly known as the kohlrabi, is a leafy vegetable with a distinctive swollen stem that is typically harvested for consumption.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant has alternate, fleshy, and lobed leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its edible part is the bulbous stem, which is rich in nutrients such as vitamins C and B6, and dietary fiber.

The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
