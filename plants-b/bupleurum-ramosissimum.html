Bupleurum ramosissimum

Bupleurum ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a fibrous root system and is characterized by its branching, hairy stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bupleurum ramosissimum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
