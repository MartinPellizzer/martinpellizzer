Begonia curtii

Begonia curtii, commonly known as the Sierra Leone begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
