Buddleja matudae

Buddleja matudae, commonly known as the Matudae Buddleia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Buddleja matudae thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinator populations.
