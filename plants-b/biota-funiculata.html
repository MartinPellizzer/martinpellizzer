Biota funiculata

Biota funiculata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling coral. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Biota funiculata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
