Begonia menchunaensis

Begonia menchunaensis, commonly known as the Menchuna begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with fleshy leaves that are often brightly colored. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
