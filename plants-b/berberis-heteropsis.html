Berberis heteropsis

Berberis heteropsis, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Berberis heteropsis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
