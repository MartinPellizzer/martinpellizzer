Berberis luhuoensis

Berberis luhuoensis, commonly known as the Luhuo barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically found in Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hedges and garden landscapes.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
