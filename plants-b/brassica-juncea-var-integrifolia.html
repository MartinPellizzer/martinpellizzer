Brassica juncea var. integrifolia

Brassica juncea var. integrifolia, commonly known as Indian mustard or leaf mustard, is an annual plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with entire margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, followed by cylindrical siliques that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
