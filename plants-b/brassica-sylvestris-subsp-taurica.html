Brassica sylvestris subsp. taurica

Brassica sylvestris subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Crimean cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus and southern Russia. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green to purple in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
