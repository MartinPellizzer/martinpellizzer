Beaucarnea oedipus

Beaucarnea oedipus, commonly known as the "candelabra tree," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, tree-like structure with a thick, woody trunk and clusters of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant stores water in its leaves and stem, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically green with a slight bluish tinge and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Beaucarnea oedipus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
