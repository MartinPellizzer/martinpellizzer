Blepharocalyx tweediei

Blepharocalyx tweediei, commonly known as the silver bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint silver sheen. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its beauty and fragrance.
