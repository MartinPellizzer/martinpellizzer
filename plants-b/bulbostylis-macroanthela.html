Bulbostylis macroanthela

Bulbostylis macroanthela, commonly known as the giant bulbostylis, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small white or pale blue flowers. Native to tropical regions of Africa, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The plant has a bulbous base that serves as a storage organ, allowing it to survive dry periods.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
