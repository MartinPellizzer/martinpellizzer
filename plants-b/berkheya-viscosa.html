Berkheya viscosa

Berkheya viscosa, commonly known as the sticky berkheya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
