Brachistus strigosus

Brachistus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy bristlegrass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, pubescent surface.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in loose clusters.
