Betula virgata

Betula virgata, commonly known as the Alaska birch, is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish inner layer. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cold climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula virgata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
