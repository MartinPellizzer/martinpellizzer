Begonia plantaginea

Begonia plantaginea, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive silvery sheen, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
