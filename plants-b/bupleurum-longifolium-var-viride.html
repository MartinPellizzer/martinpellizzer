Bupleurum longifolium var. viride

Bupleurum longifolium var. viride, commonly known as greenleaf boldo, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features basal leaves that are elongated and lance-shaped, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
