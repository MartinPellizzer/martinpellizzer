Boeica filiformis var. griffithii

Boeica filiformis var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's hairleaf, is a rare and delicate plant characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle beauty to its habitat. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its fragility and habitat specificity, it is often threatened by environmental changes and human encroachment.
