Brassica oleracea f. praecox

Brassica oleracea f. praecox, commonly known as early cabbage, is a botanical variety of the species Brassica oleracea.

It is characterized by its compact, smooth, and dark green leaves that form a dense head. This variety is typically harvested before the full maturity of the plant, resulting in a tender and mild-flavored cabbage. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various culinary applications.

The plant is a biennial, though it is usually grown as an annual in agricultural settings.
