Brachypodium hickelis

Brachypodium hickelis, commonly known as Hickel's bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its short stature and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Brachypodium hickelis is also of interest to botanists due to its potential role in ecological studies and its use in research related to plant resilience.
