Baccharis matudae

Baccharis matudae, commonly known as Matudae's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss.

Baccharis matudae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
