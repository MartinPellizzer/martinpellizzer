Botryopsis

Botryopsis, commonly known as the moss of the genus Botryopsis, is a group of small, leafy mosses found in moist, shaded environments.

These plants typically grow in dense mats and are characterized by their delicate, branching stems and narrow leaves. Botryopsis species are often found in temperate regions, particularly in forests and on rocky substrates. They play an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in their ecosystems.

The genus is notable for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its contribution to the biodiversity of moss communities.
