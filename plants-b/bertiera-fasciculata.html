Bertiera fasciculata

Bertiera fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bertiera, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bertiera fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
