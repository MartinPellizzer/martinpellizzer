Bredemeyera defoliata

Bredemeyera defoliata, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick rhizome that anchors it to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically light green and can be quite large, contributing to its striking appearance.

Bredemeyera defoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, shaded environment.
