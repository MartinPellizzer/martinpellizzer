Begonia mangdenensis

Begonia mangdenensis, commonly known as the Mangden's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
