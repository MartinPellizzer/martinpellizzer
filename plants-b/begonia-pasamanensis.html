Begonia pasamanensis

Begonia pasamanensis, commonly known as the Pasaman begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a variety of colors, including green, red, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Begonia pasamanensis requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
