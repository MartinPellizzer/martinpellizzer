Barleria cristata var. mairei

Barleria cristata var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's crested barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
