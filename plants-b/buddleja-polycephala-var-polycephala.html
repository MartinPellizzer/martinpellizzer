Buddleja polycephala var. polycephala

Buddleja polycephala var. polycephala, commonly known as the clustered butterfly bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical clusters of small, fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
