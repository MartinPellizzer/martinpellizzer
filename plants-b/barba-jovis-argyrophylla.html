Barba-jovis argyrophylla

Barba-jovis argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf barba-jovis, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
