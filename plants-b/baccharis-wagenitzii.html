Baccharis wagenitzii

Baccharis wagenitzii, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Baccharis wagenitzii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
