Bupleurum falcatum subsp. komarovianum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

komarovianum, commonly known as the Komarov's goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, somewhat hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
