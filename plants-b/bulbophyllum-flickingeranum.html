Bulbophyllum flickingeranum

Bulbophyllum flickingeranum, commonly known as the Flickinger's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence and intricate flower structure, which are typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and scientific study.
