Berberis circumserrata

Berberis circumserrata, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5-7 pairs of serrated leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
