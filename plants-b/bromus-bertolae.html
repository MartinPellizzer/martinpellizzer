Bromus bertolae

Bromus bertolae, commonly known as the Bolivian brome, is a species of grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Bromus bertolae thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
