Baeckea tuberculata

Baeckea tuberculata, commonly known as the rough baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are small, white or cream-colored, and are arranged in clusters, often blooming in spring.

Baeckea tuberculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
