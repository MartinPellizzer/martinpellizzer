Baptisia vespertina

Baptisia vespertina, commonly known as evening Baptisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spherical flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a deep taproot system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Baptisia vespertina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
