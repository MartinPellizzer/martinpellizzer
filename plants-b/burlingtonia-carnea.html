Burlingtonia carnea

Burlingtonia carnea, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Burlingtonia carnea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
