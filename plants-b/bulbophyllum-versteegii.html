Bulbophyllum versteegii

Bulbophyllum versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its often humid and shaded environment.

The flowers are usually fragrant and can bloom multiple times a year, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
