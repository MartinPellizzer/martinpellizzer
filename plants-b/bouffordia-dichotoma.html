Bouffordia dichotoma

Bouffordia dichotoma, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to drought.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
