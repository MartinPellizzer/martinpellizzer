Banisteria dichotoma

Banisteria dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched banisteria, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive two-branched inflorescences. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite and simple, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Banisteria dichotoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
