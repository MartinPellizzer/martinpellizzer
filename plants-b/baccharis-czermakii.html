Baccharis czermakii

Baccharis czermakii, commonly known as the Czermak's baccharis, is a shrub native to the dry grasslands of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
