Begonia portillana

Begonia portillana, commonly known as the Portillan begonia, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a velvety texture and a dark green color. Its flowers, which range in color from red to pink, are borne in terminal clusters and bloom during the spring and summer months.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
