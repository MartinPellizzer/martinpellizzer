Bignonia aequinoctialis

Bignonia aequinoctialis, commonly known as the golden glory vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, orange-yellow flowers resembling those of the trumpet vine. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, which includes other ornamental and economically important plants.

Bignonia aequinoctialis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in landscaping.
