Bromus schaffneri

Bromus schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's brome, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Bromus schaffneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
