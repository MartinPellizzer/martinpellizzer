Baccharis bicolor

Baccharis bicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, woody fruits called drupes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Baccharis bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
