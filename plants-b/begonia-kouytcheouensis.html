Begonia kouytcheouensis

Begonia kouytcheouensis, commonly known as the Chinese begonia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually dark green with contrasting veins, and the flowers can be red, pink, or white, depending on the variety.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.
