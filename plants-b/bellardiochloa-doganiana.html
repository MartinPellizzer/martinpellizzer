Bellardiochloa doganiana

Bellardiochloa doganiana, commonly known as the Turkish grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, often with a waxy coating.

Bellardiochloa doganiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
