Begonia duruensis

Begonia duruensis, commonly known as the Duru's begonia, is a species native to Cameroon.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Begonia duruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
