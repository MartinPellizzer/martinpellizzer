Begonia argentinensis

Begonia argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
