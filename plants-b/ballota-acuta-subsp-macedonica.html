Ballota acuta subsp. macedonica

Ballota acuta subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly shorter and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Ballota acuta.
