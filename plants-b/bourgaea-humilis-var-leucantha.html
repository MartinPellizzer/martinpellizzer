Bourgaea humilis var. leucantha

Bourgaea humilis var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered bougainvillea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and clusters of vibrant white bracts that surround small, pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various garden settings.

Its foliage is dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its colorful inflorescences.
