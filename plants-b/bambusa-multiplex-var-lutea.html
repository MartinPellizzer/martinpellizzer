Bambusa multiplex var. lutea

Bambusa multiplex var. lutea, commonly known as the Golden Bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, yellowish-green leaves that give it a distinctive, shimmering appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that add a subtle touch of color during the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and ease of maintenance.
