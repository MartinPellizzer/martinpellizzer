Brachiaria foliosa

Brachiaria foliosa, commonly known as the hairy bladegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Brachiaria foliosa is commonly found in disturbed soils and is often used as forage for livestock.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
