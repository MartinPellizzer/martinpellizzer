Berberis pseudumbellata subsp. pseudumbellata

Berberis pseudumbellata subsp.

pseudumbellata, commonly known as the red-flowered barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive red fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
