Blepharis kenyensis

Blepharis kenyensis, commonly known as the Kenyan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Kenya.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while the flowers are pale pink to white in color.

Blepharis kenyensis is noted for its nocturnal blooming habit, which attracts specific pollinators such as moths.
