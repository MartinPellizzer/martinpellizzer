Baccharis squarrosa

Baccharis squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its grayish, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate salty air and sandy soils. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Baccharis squarrosa is used in some regions for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
