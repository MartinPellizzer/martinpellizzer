Bryophyllum serratum

Bryophyllum serratum, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in spring. Its unique feature is the ability to propagate through leaf-cuttings, where new plants develop from the notches on the leaf margins.

This makes it a popular choice for gardeners and a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
