Bactris negrensis

Bactris negrensis, commonly known as the Negrensis palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is a medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pineapples. Bactris negrensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential economic value in local communities.
