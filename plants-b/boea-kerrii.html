Boea kerrii

Boea kerrii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Boea kerrii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
