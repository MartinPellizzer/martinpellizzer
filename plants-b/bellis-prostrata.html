Bellis prostrata

Bellis prostrata, commonly known as creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Bellis prostrata is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and garden beds.
