Bambusa glaucescens var. riviereorum

Bambusa glaucescens var. riviereorum, commonly known as the Riviere's bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a striking blue-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, which are not typically showy. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its elegant form and foliage.

This bamboo is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
