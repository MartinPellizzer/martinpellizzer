Bulbophyllum angiense

Bulbophyllum angiense, commonly known as the Anguilla orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
