Bridelia assamica

Bridelia assamica, commonly known as the Assam bridelid, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, oval seeds.

Bridelia assamica is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
