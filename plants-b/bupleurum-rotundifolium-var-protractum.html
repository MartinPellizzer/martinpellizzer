Bupleurum rotundifolium var. protractum

Bupleurum rotundifolium var. protractum, commonly known as the long-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
