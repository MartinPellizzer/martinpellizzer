Bulbophyllum tekuense

Bulbophyllum tekuense, commonly known as the Teku orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including yellow, green, and red. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted for efficient water retention.

Bulbophyllum tekuense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
