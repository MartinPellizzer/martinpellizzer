Berberis monticola

Berberis monticola, commonly known as the mountain barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine contribute to its significance in horticulture and herbal practices.
