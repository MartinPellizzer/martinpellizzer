Begonia glabra

Begonia glabra, commonly known as the smooth begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, Begonia glabra is a popular choice among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
