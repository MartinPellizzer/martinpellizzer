Berberis ludlowii

Berberis ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

The Ludlow barberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
