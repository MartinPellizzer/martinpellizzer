Begonia chindwinensis

Begonia chindwinensis, commonly known as the Chindwin begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Chindwin River region in Myanmar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
