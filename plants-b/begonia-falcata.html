Begonia falcata

Begonia falcata, commonly known as the sickle begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, sickle-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
