Bourreria tomentosa var. modesta

Bourreria tomentosa var. modesta, commonly known as the woolly burr.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
