Baccharis petiolata var. rotundifolia

Baccharis petiolata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which attract various pollinators.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.
