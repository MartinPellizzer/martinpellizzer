Berberis aquifolium var. lyallii

Berberis aquifolium var. lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's barberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, holly-like leaves with serrated edges and bright red berries that appear in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
