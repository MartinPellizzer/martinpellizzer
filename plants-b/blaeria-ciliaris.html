Blaeria ciliaris

Blaeria ciliaris, commonly known as the coastal blazer, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Blaeria ciliaris is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
