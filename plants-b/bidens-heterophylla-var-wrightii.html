Bidens heterophylla var. wrightii

Bidens heterophylla var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
