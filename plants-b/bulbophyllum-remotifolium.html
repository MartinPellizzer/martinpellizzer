Bulbophyllum remotifolium

Bulbophyllum remotifolium, commonly known as the distant-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are elongated and often clustered at the base. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
