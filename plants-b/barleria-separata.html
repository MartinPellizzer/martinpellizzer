Barleria separata

Barleria separata, commonly known as the narrowleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Barleria separata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
