Boutonia

Boutonia, commonly known as the "pink shower of flowers," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. Boutonia species are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. They typically grow as herbaceous perennials or shrubs, often with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The genus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
