Bromus villosus var. gussonei

Bromus villosus var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's hairy bromus, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often bear spikelets at the base. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
