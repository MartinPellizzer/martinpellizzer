Begonia × hybrida multiflora

Begonia × hybrida multiflora, commonly known as the everblooming begonia, is a hybrid plant prized for its continuous flowering throughout the growing season.

It features dense, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to variegated patterns, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming in multiple layers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This versatile species is popular in both container gardening and as a ground cover due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
