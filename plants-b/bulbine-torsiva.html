Bulbine torsiva

Bulbine torsiva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-resistant plants.
