Baccharis haenkei

Baccharis haenkei, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental qualities make it a popular choice in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
