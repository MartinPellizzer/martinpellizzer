Berberis praecipua

Berberis praecipua, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Berberis praecipua is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
