Briza elatior var. australis

Briza elatior var. australis, commonly known as the Australian quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that sway in the wind. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
