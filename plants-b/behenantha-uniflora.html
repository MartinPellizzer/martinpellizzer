Behenantha uniflora

Behenantha uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower behenath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are enclosed in a fibrous capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
