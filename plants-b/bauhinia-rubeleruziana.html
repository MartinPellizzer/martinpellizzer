Bauhinia rubeleruziana

Bauhinia rubeleruziana, commonly known as the red bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
