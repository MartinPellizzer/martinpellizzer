Bactris speciosa var. chichagui

Bactris speciosa var. chichagui, commonly known as the Chichagui palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of sustainable palm products.
