Boehmeria janagi-itsigo

Boehmeria janagi-itsigo, commonly known as the wild banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used for making rope and paper in indigenous communities.
