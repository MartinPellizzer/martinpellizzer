Begonia caraguatatubensis

Begonia caraguatatubensis, commonly known as the Caraguatatuba begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Begonia caraguatatubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
