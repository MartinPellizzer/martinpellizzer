Betula microphylla var. caraganicola

Betula microphylla var. caraganicola, commonly known as the Siberian birch, is a deciduous tree native to northern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers. The plant thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions. It produces small, yellowish catkins that are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
