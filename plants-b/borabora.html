Borabora

Borabora, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking aquatic plant with large, showy flowers.

It is native to the Nile River region and has been revered for its spiritual and medicinal significance. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its flowers bloom in shades of pink and white. Borabora thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its botanical name, *Nelumbo nucifera*, reflects its importance in both botany and cultural history.
