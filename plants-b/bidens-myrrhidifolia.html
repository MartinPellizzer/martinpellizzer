Bidens myrrhidifolia

Bidens myrrhidifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggarlice, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. Its fruit is a two-seeded capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
