Blepharis integrifolia var. integrifolia

Blepharis integrifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically smooth and entire, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
