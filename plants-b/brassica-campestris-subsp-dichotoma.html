Brassica campestris subsp. dichotoma

Brassica campestris subsp.

dichotoma, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and alternate upper leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
