Bulbine rupicola

Bulbine rupicola, commonly known as the rock bulbine, is a perennial succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardening.
