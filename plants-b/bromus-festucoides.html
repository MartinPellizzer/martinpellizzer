Bromus festucoides

Bromus festucoides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Bromus festucoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
