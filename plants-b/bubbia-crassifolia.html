Bubbia crassifolia

Bubbia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its thick, fleshy leaves help it store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Bubbia crassifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
