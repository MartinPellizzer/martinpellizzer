Buddleja gayana

Buddleja gayana, commonly known as the Chilean butterfly bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces long, drooping clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Buddleja gayana is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
