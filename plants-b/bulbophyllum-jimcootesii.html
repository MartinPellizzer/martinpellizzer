Bulbophyllum jimcootesii

Bulbophyllum jimcootesii, commonly known as the Jimcootes' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
