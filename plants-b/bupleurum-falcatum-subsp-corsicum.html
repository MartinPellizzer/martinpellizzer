Bupleurum falcatum subsp. corsicum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican cowpea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, often branched stems that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
