Bellardiochloa argaea

Bellardiochloa argaea, commonly known as the argaea hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and inconspicuous.

Bellardiochloa argaea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
