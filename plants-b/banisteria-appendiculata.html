Banisteria appendiculata

Banisteria appendiculata, commonly known as the "Climbing Glorybower," is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in clusters of bright orange or yellow. The plant has compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warm climates.
