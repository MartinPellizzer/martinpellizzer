Boea glabriflora

Boea glabriflora, commonly known as the velvet leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Boea glabriflora is known for its ability to propagate through both seeds and vegetative means.
