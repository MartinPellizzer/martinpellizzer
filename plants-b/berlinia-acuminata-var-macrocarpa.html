Berlinia acuminata var. macrocarpa

Berlinia acuminata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
