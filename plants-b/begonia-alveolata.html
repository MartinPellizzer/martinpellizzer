Begonia alveolata

Begonia alveolata, commonly known as the hollow-leaved begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, deeply lobed, and often hollow leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
