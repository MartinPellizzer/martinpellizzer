Betula alba lusus macrophylla

Betula alba lusus macrophylla, commonly known as the white birch with large leaves, is a variant of the white birch species.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The leaves are larger and more elongated compared to typical Betula alba, often with a doubly serrated margin. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
