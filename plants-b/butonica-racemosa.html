Butonica racemosa

Butonica racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem.

Butonica racemosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
