Blumea riparia var. megacephala

Blumea riparia var. megacephala, commonly known as the giant blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinct scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
