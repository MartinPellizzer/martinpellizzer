Bradburya virginiana var. angustifolia

Bradburya virginiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bradburya, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
