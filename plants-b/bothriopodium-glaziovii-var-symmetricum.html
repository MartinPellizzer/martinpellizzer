Bothriopodium glaziovii var. symmetricum

Bothriopodium glaziovii var. symmetricum, commonly known as the symmetric mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, symmetrically arranged inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

The flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
