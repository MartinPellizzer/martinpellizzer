Berlandiera longifolia

Berlandiera longifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid climates.

Berlandiera longifolia is also known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
