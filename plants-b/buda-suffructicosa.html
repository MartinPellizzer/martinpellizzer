Buda suffructicosa

Buda suffructicosa, commonly known as the false holly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Buda suffructicosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
