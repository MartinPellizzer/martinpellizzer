Brachyotum figueroae

Brachyotum figueroae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Andes region of Ecuador.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations. The plant features a short, thick stem with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Brachyotum figueroae is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
