Brachychiton compactus

Brachychiton compactus, commonly known as the bottle tree, is a unique shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Brachychiton compactus is well-suited for xeriscaping and adds ornamental value to gardens with its distinctive appearance.
