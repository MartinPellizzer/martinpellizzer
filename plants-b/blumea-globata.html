Blumea globata

Blumea globata, commonly known as the globular chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical inflorescence, which consists of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy.

Blumea globata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
