Brassica xinjiangensis

Brassica xinjiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically lobed and can vary in shape depending on the growth conditions.

Brassica xinjiangensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Xinjiang, China.
