Byrsonima lancifolia var. typica

Byrsonima lancifolia var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of its colorful, nutrient-rich fruits.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
