Bambusa latiflora

Bambusa latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features stiff, hollow stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color, with a smooth, often shiny surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, which are followed by triangular, winged seeds. Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

Bambusa latiflora is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable building materials.
