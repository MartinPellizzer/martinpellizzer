Biscutella auriculata var. maroccana

Biscutella auriculata var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy surface, and its stems are usually erect and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
