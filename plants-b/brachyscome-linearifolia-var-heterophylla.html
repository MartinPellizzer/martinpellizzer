Brachyscome linearifolia var. heterophylla

Brachyscome linearifolia var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable bristle bush, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are linear and often vary in shape, giving rise to its varietal name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
