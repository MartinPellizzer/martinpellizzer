Besleria rupestris

Besleria rupestris, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tubular flowers. The plant typically features succulent stems and leaves that are often fleshy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
