Brodiaea hyacinthina var. lactea

Brodiaea hyacinthina var. lactea, commonly known as the milk brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical trait. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among native plant enthusiasts.
