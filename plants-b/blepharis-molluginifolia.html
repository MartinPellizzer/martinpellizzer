Blepharis molluginifolia

Blepharis molluginifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
