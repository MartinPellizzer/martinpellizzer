Berardia ciliata

Berardia ciliata, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
