Banksia rufa

Banksia rufa, commonly known as the red banksia, is a flowering plant native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Banksia rufa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing nectar for a variety of pollinators.
