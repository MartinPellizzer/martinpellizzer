Betula papyrifera subsp. occidentalis

Betula papyrifera subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to western North America. It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a papery, inner layer.

The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring and has palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
