Bunium sary-cheleki

Bunium sary-cheleki, commonly known as the Karakorum fennel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, spicy odor when crushed.

The small white flowers appear in umbrella-like clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in mountainous landscapes.
