Berberis baxoiensis

Berberis baxoiensis, commonly known as the Baxoi barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
