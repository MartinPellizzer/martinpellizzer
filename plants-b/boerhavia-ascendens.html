Boerhavia ascendens

Boerhavia ascendens, commonly known as redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with erect, branching stems that often turn reddish in color. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
