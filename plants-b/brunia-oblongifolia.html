Brunia oblongifolia

Brunia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brunia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features linear, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
