Blumea repanda

Blumea repanda, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a mist-like appearance. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and often covered with fine hairs.

Blumea repanda is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
