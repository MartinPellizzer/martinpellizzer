Berberis ignorata

Berberis ignorata, commonly known as the desert barberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. Its roots are deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Berberis ignorata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in desert landscapes.
