Belamcanda chinensis

Belamcanda chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese yellow flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking orange-red flowers. The plant produces a bulbous root system that stores nutrients and allows it to survive in various soil types. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Belamcanda chinensis is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
