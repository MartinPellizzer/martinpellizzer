Bletia quadrifida

Bletia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. Its leaves are elliptical, arranged in a rosette, and often have a waxy texture.

Bletia quadrifida thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
