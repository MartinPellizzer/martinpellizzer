Blepharocalyx apiculatus var. rubellus

Blepharocalyx apiculatus var. rubellus, commonly known as the red-flowered trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bats. The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and conservation efforts.
