Brassica rapa var. rubra

Brassica rapa var. rubra, commonly known as red cabbage, is a cultivated variety of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, compact heads of deep purple to red colored leaves. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in cool climates and is often grown for its edible leaves and ornamental value. Its leaves are rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a nutritious vegetable.

The plant's flowers are small and yellow, typical of the Brassicaceae family, and are followed by round, smooth pods containing seeds.
