Bombax pentaphyllum

Bombax pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-lobed cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are palmately lobed, typically with five to seven segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local communities.
