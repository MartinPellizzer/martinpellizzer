Brillantaisia subcordata var. macrophylla

Brillantaisia subcordata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf brillantaisia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red calyx, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
