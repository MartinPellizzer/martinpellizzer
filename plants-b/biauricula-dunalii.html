Biauricula dunalii

Biauricula dunalii, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry fields.
