Bacopa ranaria var. guaranitica

Bacopa ranaria var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged, and it produces tiny blue or purple flowers that emerge above the water surface. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in aquariums.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
