Betula papyrifera var. andrewsii

Betula papyrifera var. andrewsii, commonly known as the paper birch, is a variant of the paper birch tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a papery, inner layer. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cool, moist environments such as northern forests and mountain regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
