Bursera tenuifolia

Bursera tenuifolia, commonly known as the Mexican teak or white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

Bursera tenuifolia is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
