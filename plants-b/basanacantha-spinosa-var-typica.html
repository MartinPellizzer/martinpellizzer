Basanacantha spinosa var. typica

Basanacantha spinosa var. typica, commonly known as the spiny leaf spurge.

It is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and opposite, spiny leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by bead-like fruits.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
