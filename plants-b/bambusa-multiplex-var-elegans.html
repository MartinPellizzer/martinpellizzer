Bambusa multiplex var. elegans

Bambusa multiplex var. elegans, commonly known as the Black Bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that are dark green to nearly black in color, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by light green, feather-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create a vertical element in gardens.
