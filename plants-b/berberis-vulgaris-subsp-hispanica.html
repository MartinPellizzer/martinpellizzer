Berberis vulgaris subsp. hispanica

Berberis vulgaris subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish barberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It features thorny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves.

The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

Its berries contain berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
