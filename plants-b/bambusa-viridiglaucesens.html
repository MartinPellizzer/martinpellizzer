Bambusa viridiglaucesens

Bambusa viridiglaucesens, commonly known as the greenish-blue bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bluish-green culms that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
