Brassica oleracea var. aurata

Brassica oleracea var. aurata, commonly known as cauliflower, is a cultivar of the cabbage family characterized by its compact, curd-like inflorescence.

It is a biennial plant that typically matures into a dense head of white, green, or purple flowers, depending on the variety. The plant's botanical features include thick, fleshy leaves that form a protective layer around the central flower head. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible flower heads, which are used in various culinary applications.
