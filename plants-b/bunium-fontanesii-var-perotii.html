Bunium fontanesii var. perotii

Bunium fontanesii var. perotii, commonly known as the Siberian fennel, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and a tall, branched flowering stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The seeds are small and have a distinctive aroma, often used in traditional medicine.
