Beta adenensis

Beta adenensis, commonly known as the Adelaide false flax, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Beta adenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
