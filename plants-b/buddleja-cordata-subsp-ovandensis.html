Buddleja cordata subsp. ovandensis

Buddleja cordata subsp.

ovandensis, commonly known as the ovandensis buddleia, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly found in Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens in suitable climates.
