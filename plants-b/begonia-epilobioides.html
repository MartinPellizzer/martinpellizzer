Begonia epilobioides

Begonia epilobioides, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its foliage and vibrant floral display.
