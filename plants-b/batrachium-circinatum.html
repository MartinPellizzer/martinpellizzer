Batrachium circinatum

Batrachium circinatum, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
