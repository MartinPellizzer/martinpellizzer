Bonnetia coriacea

Bonnetia coriacea, commonly known as the rough bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant has rough, bark-like stems and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture.

Bonnetia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
