Betula pubescens var. litwinowii

Betula pubescens var. litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii birch, is a variant of the silver birch native to northern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and small, triangular leaves that turn yellow in the fall. This species thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and is wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
