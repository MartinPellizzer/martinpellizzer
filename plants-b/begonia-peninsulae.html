Begonia peninsulae

Begonia peninsulae, commonly known as the Peninsula Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to deep purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, often with contrasting yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
