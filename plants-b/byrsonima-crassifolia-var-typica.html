Byrsonima crassifolia var. typica

Byrsonima crassifolia var. typica, commonly known as the Surinam cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, bright red or orange fruits that resemble cherries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fruits are edible, though typically consumed raw or used in jams and juices.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
