Boronia pinnata var. pilosa

Boronia pinnata var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
