Brassica rapa var. arenaria

Brassica rapa var. arenaria, commonly known as rock cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or coastal areas. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
