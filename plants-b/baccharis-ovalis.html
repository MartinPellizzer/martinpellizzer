Baccharis ovalis

Baccharis ovalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It is often found in dry woodlands, canyons, and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
