Blepharocalyx brunneus

Blepharocalyx brunneus, commonly known as the brown-leafed hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
