Brya amorphoides

Brya amorphoides, commonly known as the cushion moss, is a small, terrestrial moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It forms dense, low-growing mats and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a simple morphology with leaf-like structures that are typically curled or rolled when dry. Brya amorphoides reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called capsules.

This moss plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
