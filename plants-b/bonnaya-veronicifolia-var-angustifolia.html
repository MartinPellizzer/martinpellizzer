Bonnaya veronicifolia var. angustifolia

Bonnaya veronicifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bonnaya, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
