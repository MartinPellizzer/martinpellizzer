Barleria fissiflora

Barleria fissiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, and hairy leaves, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
