Begonia manicata aureomaculata

Begonia manicata aureomaculata, commonly known as the golden-spotted begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and distinctive golden spots that contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
