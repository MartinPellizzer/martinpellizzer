Boehmeria nivea f. concolor

Boehmeria nivea f. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese hemp or white flax, is a variant of Boehmeria nivea, a species native to East Asia.

This plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves that are typically a vibrant green on the upper surface and lighter green or sometimes white on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Urticaceae family. The plant is cultivated for its strong, fibrous stems, which are used in the production of textiles and paper.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern applications.
