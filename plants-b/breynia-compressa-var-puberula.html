Breynia compressa var. puberula

Breynia compressa var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf breynia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This plant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
