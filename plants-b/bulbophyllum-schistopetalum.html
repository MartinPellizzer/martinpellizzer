Bulbophyllum schistopetalum

Bulbophyllum schistopetalum, commonly known as the stone-petaled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
