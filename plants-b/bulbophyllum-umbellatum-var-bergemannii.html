Bulbophyllum umbellatum var. bergemannii

Bulbophyllum umbellatum var. bergemannii, commonly known as the Bergemann's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive, pouch-like labellum that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, showy display.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, this variety is considered endangered and is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
