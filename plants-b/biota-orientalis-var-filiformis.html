Biota orientalis var. filiformis

Biota orientalis var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender biota, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
