Bauhinia bracteata subsp. astylosa

Bauhinia bracteata subsp.

astylosa, commonly known as the silky bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
