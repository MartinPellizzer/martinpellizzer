Brachionidium brachycladum

Brachionidium brachycladum, commonly known as the slender brachionidium, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
