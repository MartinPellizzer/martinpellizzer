Bauhinia triandra

Bauhinia triandra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, papery pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The tree has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia triandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
