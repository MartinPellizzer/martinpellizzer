Betula transcaucasica

Betula transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian birch, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and green underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by oval, winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers cooler climates.

Betula transcaucasica plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
