Begonia ebolowensis

Begonia ebolowensis, commonly known as the Cameroon begonia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Cameroon.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of atmospheric moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.
