Biscutella laevigata subsp. lamottei

Biscutella laevigata subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the smooth biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
