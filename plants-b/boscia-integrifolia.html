Boscia integrifolia

Boscia integrifolia, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Boscia integrifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
