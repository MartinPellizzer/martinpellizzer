Bromus hordeaceus f. leiostachys

Bromus hordeaceus f. leiostachys, commonly known as the slender brome, is a variant of the slender brome grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. This form is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
