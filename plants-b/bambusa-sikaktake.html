Bambusa sikaktake

Bambusa sikaktake, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow inflorescences that are clustered at the tips of the culms. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy texture.

Bambusa sikaktake is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and rapid growth rate.
