Bromus erectus f. viviparus

Bromus erectus f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous brome, is a variant of the erect brome grass characterized by its unique reproductive feature.

This plant exhibits vivipary, a phenomenon where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. It typically grows in temperate regions, often in disturbed soils such as roadsides or fields. The plant has a tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stabilization and serving as forage for grazing animals.
