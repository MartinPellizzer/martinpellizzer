Bidens anthriscoides var. typica

Bidens anthriscoides var. typica, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant produces numerous seeds with hooked bracts that attach to clothing or animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a common weed and a species of ecological interest.
