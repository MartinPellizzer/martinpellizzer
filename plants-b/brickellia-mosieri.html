Brickellia mosieri

Brickellia mosieri, commonly known as brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.

Brickellia mosieri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
