Bambusa dichotoma

Bambusa dichotoma, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. Bambusa dichotoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
