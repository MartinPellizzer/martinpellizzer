Berchemia pakistanica

Berchemia pakistanica, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. Berchemia pakistanica thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate fragrance and aesthetic appeal.
