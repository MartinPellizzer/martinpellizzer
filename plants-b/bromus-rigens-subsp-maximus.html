Bromus rigens subsp. maximus

Bromus rigens subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky slopes.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
