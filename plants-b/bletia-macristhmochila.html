Bletia macristhmochila

Bletia macristhmochila, commonly known as the broad-leafed bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with distinctive red or purple markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Bletia macristhmochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
