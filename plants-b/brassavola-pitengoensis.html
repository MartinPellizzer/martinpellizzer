Brassavola pitengoensis

Brassavola pitengoensis, commonly known as the Piteno orchid, is a species of orchid native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Brassavola pitengoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
