Bolanthus sandrasicus

Bolanthus sandrasicus, commonly known as the sandrasic sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a compact, mat-like structure that helps it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water, while its roots are deep and extensive to access groundwater.

Bolanthus sandrasicus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
