Bromus madritensis

Bromus madritensis, commonly known as the Spanish brome, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing several florets. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, typical of the Poaceae family.

Bromus madritensis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
