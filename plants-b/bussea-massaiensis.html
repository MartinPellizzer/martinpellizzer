Bussea massaiensis

Bussea massaiensis, commonly known as the Massai fig, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its fruit, a small, green fig-like structure, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant plays an important role in the ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
