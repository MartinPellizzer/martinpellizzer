Bidens punctata

Bidens punctata, commonly known as the dotted beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with dark markings and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and ditches. Its leaves are usually oval with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

Bidens punctata is often found in disturbed areas and can spread rapidly through its seeds.
