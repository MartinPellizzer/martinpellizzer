Brachypodium plukenetii

Brachypodium plukenetii, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions of the United States.
