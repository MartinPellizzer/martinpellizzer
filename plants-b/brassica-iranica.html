Brassica iranica

Brassica iranica, commonly known as the Iranian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Brassica iranica is also valued for its seeds, which can be used as a spice or for oil extraction.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
