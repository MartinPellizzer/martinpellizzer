Bobua ilicifolia

Bobua ilicifolia, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces black, egg-shaped berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves and berries contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
