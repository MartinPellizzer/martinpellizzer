Baccharis apiculata

Baccharis apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Baccharis apiculata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
