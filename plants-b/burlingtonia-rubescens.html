Burlingtonia rubescens

Burlingtonia rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, crimson or reddish-pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
