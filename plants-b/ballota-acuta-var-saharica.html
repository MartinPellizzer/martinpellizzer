Ballota acuta var. saharica

Ballota acuta var. saharica, commonly known as the Saharan ballota, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.
