Beta vulgaris var. alborubens

Beta vulgaris var. alborubens, commonly known as the red beet or table beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that range in color from deep red to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is cultivated for its edible roots and leaves. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can be consumed as a leafy green vegetable.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
