Besleria aggregata

Besleria aggregata, commonly known as the clustered besleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
