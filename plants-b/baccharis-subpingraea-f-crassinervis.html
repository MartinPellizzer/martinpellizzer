Baccharis subpingraea f. crassinervis

Baccharis subpingraea f. crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with prominent, broad veins, which give it its specific epithet "crassinervis.". It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
