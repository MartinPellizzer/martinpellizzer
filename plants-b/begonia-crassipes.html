Begonia crassipes

Begonia crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved begonia, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia crassipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
