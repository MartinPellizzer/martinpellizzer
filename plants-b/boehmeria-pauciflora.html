Boehmeria pauciflora

Boehmeria pauciflora, commonly known as the small-flowered nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy, lanceolate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The fibrous stems of Boehmeria pauciflora are also valued for their potential in textile production.
