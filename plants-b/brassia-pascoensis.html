Brassia pascoensis

Brassia pascoensis, commonly known as the Pasco's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple markings, and it produces a single spike with several blooms.

Brassia pascoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
