Brickellia scaber

Brickellia scaber, commonly known as rough brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia scaber is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
