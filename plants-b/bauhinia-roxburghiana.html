Bauhinia roxburghiana

Bauhinia roxburghiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small, feather-like leaflets. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
