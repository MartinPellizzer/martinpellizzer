Basisperma

Basisperma, commonly known as the false indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with a feathery appearance. Basisperma species are often found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
