Berberis dumetorum

Berberis dumetorum, commonly known as the thimbleberry or rockberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are edible, are usually red or black when ripe and contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
