Butinia cretica

Butinia cretica, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Butinia cretica is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental qualities.
