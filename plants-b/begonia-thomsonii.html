Begonia thomsonii

Begonia thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins, often displaying a reddish or maroon hue on the underside. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage as much as its blooms, making it a versatile addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
