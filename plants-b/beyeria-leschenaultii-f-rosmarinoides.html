Beyeria leschenaultii f. rosmarinoides

Beyeria leschenaultii f. rosmarinoides, commonly known as the rosmarinoides form of the species, is a cultivar of the Beyeria genus, which belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that resemble those of rosemary, hence the name. The plant typically features finely divided, feather-like foliage that is both ornamental and fragrant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and pleasant scent, often used in both ornamental and culinary contexts.
