Bursera fagaroides f. crenulata

Bursera fagaroides f. crenulata, commonly known as the Mexican rubber tree or crenulate bursera, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves with a crenulate margin. It produces small yellow flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruit that contains a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
