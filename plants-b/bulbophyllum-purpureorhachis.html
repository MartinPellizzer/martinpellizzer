Bulbophyllum purpureorhachis

Bulbophyllum purpureorhachis, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, purple-tinged inflorescence and elongated, narrow pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with purple veins, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This epiphytic orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
