Bicuiba oleifera

Bicuiba oleifera, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in oils and nutrients. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain multiple seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. Its deep root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

The plant requires a warm, humid climate and is often cultivated for its economic and nutritional value.
