Bactris syagroides

Bactris syagroides, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Bactris syagroides is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for the utility of its fruit in local communities.
