Brachiaria callopus

Brachiaria callopus, commonly known as the hairy signalgrass, is a tropical and subtropical perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including poor and disturbed soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves with prominent veins and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in grasslands, pastures, and along roadsides, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural settings but is also valued for its use in erosion control.
