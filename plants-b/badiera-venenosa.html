Badiera venenosa

Badiera venenosa, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, woody stems and broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing toxic seeds. Its bark and leaves contain various toxic compounds, making it potentially dangerous to handle.

Despite its harmful properties, it plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
