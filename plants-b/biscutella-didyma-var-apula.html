Biscutella didyma var. apula

Biscutella didyma var. apula, commonly known as the Apulian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
