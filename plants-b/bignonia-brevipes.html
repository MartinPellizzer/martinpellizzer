Bignonia brevipes

Bignonia brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow-orange, tubular flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Bignonia brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
