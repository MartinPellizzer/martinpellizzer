Boltonia diffusa var. diffusa

Boltonia diffusa var. diffusa, commonly known as white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Boltonia diffusa var. diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
