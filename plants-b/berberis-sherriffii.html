Berberis sherriffii

Berberis sherriffii, commonly known as Sherriff's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Berberis sherriffii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
