Bauhinia rahmatii

Bauhinia rahmatii, commonly known as the Malayan bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually pink or purple and attract various pollinators.

Bauhinia rahmatii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
