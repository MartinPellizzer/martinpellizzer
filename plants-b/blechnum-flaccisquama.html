Blechnum flaccisquama

Blechnum flaccisquama, commonly known as the flaccid frond fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous stolons, allowing it to spread along the forest floor.

The fronds are typically arching and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to shaded, moist environments.
