Blumea sylvatica

Blumea sylvatica, commonly known as mountain blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is known for its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

Blumea sylvatica is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
