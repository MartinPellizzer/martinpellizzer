Bulbophyllum trichoglottis

Bulbophyllum trichoglottis, commonly known as the feather-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers from the base of the pseudobulb.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
