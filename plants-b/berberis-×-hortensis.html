Berberis × hortensis

Berberis × hortensis, commonly known as the hybrid barberry, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Berberidaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Berberis × hortensis is native to Asia but has been widely introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
