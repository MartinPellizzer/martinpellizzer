Bulbophyllum hirundinis

Bulbophyllum hirundinis, commonly known as the hummingbird orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated shape resembling a hummingbird.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
