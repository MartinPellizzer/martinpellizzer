Brosimum parinarioides subsp. amplicoma

Brosimum parinarioides subsp.

amplicoma, commonly known as the Amazonian starfruit tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the bark is often reddish-brown in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
