Bromus hookeri var. schaffneri

Bromus hookeri var. schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. It is characterized by its adaptability to cold and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
