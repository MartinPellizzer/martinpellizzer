Bulbophyllum johannis

Bulbophyllum johannis, commonly known as the Johannes' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the wet season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
