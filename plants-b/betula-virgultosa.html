Betula virgultosa

Betula virgultosa, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish inner layer. The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula virgultosa is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
