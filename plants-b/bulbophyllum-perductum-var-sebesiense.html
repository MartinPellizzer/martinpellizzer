Bulbophyllum perductum var. sebesiense

Bulbophyllum perductum var. sebesiense, commonly known as the Sebesiense Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant, and showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, attaching itself to trees or growing in moist, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

The flowers are usually arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season, making it a prized addition to orchid collections.
