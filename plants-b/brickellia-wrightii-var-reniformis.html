Brickellia wrightii var. reniformis

Brickellia wrightii var. reniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
