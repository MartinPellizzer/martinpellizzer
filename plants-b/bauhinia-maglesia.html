Bauhinia maglesia

Bauhinia maglesia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and the tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Bauhinia maglesia also has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
