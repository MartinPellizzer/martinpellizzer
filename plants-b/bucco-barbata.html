Bucco barbata

Bucco barbata, commonly known as the hairy mouth orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its leaves are usually broad and hairy, which helps in retaining moisture in its native habitat.

The flowers of Bucco barbata are notable for their intricate structure and vivid hues, making it a popular subject for botanical study and horticultural interest.
