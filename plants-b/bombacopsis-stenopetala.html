Bombacopsis stenopetala

Bombacopsis stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled kapok tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually yellow or orange, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
