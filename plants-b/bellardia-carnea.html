Bellardia carnea

Bellardia carnea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with five petals and a prominent central spur. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Bellardia carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
