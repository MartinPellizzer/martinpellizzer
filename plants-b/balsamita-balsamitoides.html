Balsamita balsamitoides

Balsamita balsamitoides, commonly known as Mexican balsam or wild balsam, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant's aromatic properties and medicinal uses have made it of interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
