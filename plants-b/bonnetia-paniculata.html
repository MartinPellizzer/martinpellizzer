Bonnetia paniculata

Bonnetia paniculata, commonly known as the silky bonnet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are often fragrant. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds through a specialized mechanism involving a spiral twist.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
