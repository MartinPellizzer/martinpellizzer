Bistorta vulcanica

Bistorta vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic bistorta, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil in its harsh habitat.

Bistorta vulcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
