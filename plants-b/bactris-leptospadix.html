Bactris leptospadix

Bactris leptospadix, commonly known as the red raphia, is a tropical palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.

Bactris leptospadix thrives in humid, lowland areas and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
