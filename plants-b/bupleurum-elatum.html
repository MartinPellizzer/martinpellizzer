Bupleurum elatum

Bupleurum elatum, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Bupleurum elatum is typically found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, and it thrives in temperate climates.
