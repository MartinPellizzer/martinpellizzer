Bernardia pulchella var. major

Bernardia pulchella var. major, commonly known as the giant bernardia, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.

The species is adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
