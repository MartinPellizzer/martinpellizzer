Bromus tomentellus var. subglaber

Bromus tomentellus var. subglaber, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow panicle, which bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence and overall compact growth habit.
