Bothriochloa meridionalis

Bothriochloa meridionalis, commonly known as the southern panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
