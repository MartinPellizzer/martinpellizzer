Baccharis glabra

Baccharis glabra, commonly known as smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, smooth, and often fuzzy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the late summer and early fall. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-angled, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Baccharis glabra is often found in moist soils near streams, wetlands, and other water bodies.
