Blaeria spicata

Blaeria spicata, commonly known as the spiky blazer, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Blaeria spicata is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
