Burmannia amazonica

Burmannia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian burmannia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy flowers that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant typically grows close to the ground, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Burmannia amazonica is known for its short lifespan, often blooming and dying within a few months.
