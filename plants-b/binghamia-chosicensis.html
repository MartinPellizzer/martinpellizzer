Binghamia chosicensis

Binghamia chosicensis, commonly known as the Chosica orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the high Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, adapting to its arid environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
