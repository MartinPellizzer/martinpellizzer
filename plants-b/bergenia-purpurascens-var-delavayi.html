Bergenia purpurascens var. delavayi

Bergenia purpurascens var. delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's bergenia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features thick, fleshy rhizomes and broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of pinkish-red flowers during the spring, which are borne on tall, erect stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
