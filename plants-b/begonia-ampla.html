Begonia ampla

Begonia ampla, commonly known as the giant begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia ampla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and floral display.
