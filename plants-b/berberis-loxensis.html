Berberis loxensis

Berberis loxensis, commonly known as the Loxon brier, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Berberis loxensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
