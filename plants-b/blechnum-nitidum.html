Blechnum nitidum

Blechnum nitidum, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, glossy fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread vegetatively, while its fronds are pinnately compound with serrated edges.

The silver shield fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
