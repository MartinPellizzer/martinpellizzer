Blysmus compressus var. sikkimensis

Blysmus compressus var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant has slender, creeping stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
