Bignonia petiolaris

Bignonia petiolaris, commonly known as the yellow bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia petiolaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
