Bulbophyllum lamii

Bulbophyllum lamii, commonly known as the Lam's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and pink.

Bulbophyllum lamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
