Barringtonia macrophylla

Barringtonia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf pandanus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-like flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a fibrous outer layer and a soft, fleshy interior containing seeds. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
