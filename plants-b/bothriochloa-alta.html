Bothriochloa alta

Bothriochloa alta, commonly known as rivergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
