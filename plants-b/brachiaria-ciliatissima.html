Brachiaria ciliatissima

Brachiaria ciliatissima, commonly known as the hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture along the edges, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage in livestock grazing.

Brachiaria ciliatissima is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in erosion control.
