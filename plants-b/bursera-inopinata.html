Bursera inopinata

Bursera inopinata, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing resinous sap. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown of elliptical leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by woody, fragrant fruits that release seeds when mature.

The tree is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
