Bomarea puberula var. acicularis

Bomarea puberula var. acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

The varietal name "acicularis" refers to its needle-like foliage, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Bomarea.
