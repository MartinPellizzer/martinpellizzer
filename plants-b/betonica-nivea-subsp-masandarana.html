Betonica nivea subsp. masandarana

Betonica nivea subsp.

masandarana, commonly known as the white betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
