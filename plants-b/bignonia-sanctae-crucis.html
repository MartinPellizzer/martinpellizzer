Bignonia sanctae-crucis

Bignonia sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the cross-vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces orange to yellow blooms in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Bignonia sanctae-crucis is a woody vine that can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
