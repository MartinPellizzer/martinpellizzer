Baccharis solisi

Baccharis solisi, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Baccharis solisi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
