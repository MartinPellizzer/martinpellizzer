Berberis crataegina

Berberis crataegina, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorned stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but have a sharp taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for hedging and landscaping in temperate climates.
