Blitum virgatum

Blitum virgatum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features silvery, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees. Its root system is extensive and can form dense mats, making it effective for soil stabilization.

Silverweed is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
