Bombax houardii

Bombax houardii, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody pod, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
