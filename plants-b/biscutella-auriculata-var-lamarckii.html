Biscutella auriculata var. lamarckii

Biscutella auriculata var. lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's biscuitroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, often with hairy surfaces, and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
