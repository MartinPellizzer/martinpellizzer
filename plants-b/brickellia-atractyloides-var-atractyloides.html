Brickellia atractyloides var. atractyloides

Brickellia atractyloides var. atractyloides, commonly known as narrowleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional remedies.
