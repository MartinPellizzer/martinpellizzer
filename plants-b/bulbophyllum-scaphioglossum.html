Bulbophyllum scaphioglossum

Bulbophyllum scaphioglossum, commonly known as the "Bottle-lip Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with flowers that have a distinctive bottle-shaped lip. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its epiphytic or terrestrial growth habits.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
