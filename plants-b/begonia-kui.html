Begonia kui

Begonia kui, commonly known as the Kui begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia kui is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
