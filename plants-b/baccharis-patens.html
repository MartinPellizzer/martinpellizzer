Baccharis patens

Baccharis patens, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its adaptability to salt spray and wind makes it an important species in coastal restoration efforts.
