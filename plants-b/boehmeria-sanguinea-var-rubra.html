Boehmeria sanguinea var. rubra

Boehmeria sanguinea var. rubra, commonly known as red Indian hemp, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and its fibers are similar to those of other Boehmeria species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
