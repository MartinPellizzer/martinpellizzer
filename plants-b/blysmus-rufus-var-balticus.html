Blysmus rufus var. balticus

Blysmus rufus var. balticus, commonly known as the Baltic red grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that produce dense clusters of small, reddish-brown spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
