Barringtonia careya

Barringtonia careya, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, featuring smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking white, lantern-like flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal areas.
