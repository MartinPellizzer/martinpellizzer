Bactris simplicifrons

Bactris simplicifrons, commonly known as the pink rafaela, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which eventually develop into edible, fleshy fruits. Bactris simplicifrons thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential economic importance in local communities.
