Bignonia lepidota

Bignonia lepidota, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
