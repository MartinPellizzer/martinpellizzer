Begonia burkei

Begonia burkei, commonly known as the Burke's begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to variegated patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Begonia burkei thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in many horticultural settings.
