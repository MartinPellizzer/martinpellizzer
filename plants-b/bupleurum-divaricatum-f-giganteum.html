Bupleurum divaricatum f. giganteum

Bupleurum divaricatum f. giganteum, commonly known as the giant variegated bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings, adding visual interest. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
