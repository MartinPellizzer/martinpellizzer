Bembycodium

Bembycodium, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plants typically have a compact growth habit with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Bembycodium species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for rock gardens and borders.
