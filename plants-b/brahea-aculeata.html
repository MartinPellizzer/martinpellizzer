Brahea aculeata

Brahea aculeata, commonly known as the Mexican blue palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and is characterized by its compact, rounded crown and blue-gray leaves. The plant features a short, thick trunk and is well adapted to dry climates. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Brahea aculeata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
