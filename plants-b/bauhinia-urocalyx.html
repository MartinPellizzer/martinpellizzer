Bauhinia urocalyx

Bauhinia urocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Bauhinia urocalyx is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
