Blumea glomerata

Blumea glomerata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

The flowers are small, white to pale blue, and are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name.
