Blumea crispata var. montana

Blumea crispata var. montana, commonly known as mountain blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or crinkled. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
