Berberis weberbaueri

Berberis weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It features prickly stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Berberis weberbaueri is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with various health benefits.
