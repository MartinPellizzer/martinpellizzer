Bupleurum mucronatum f. typicum

Bupleurum mucronatum f. typicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period in late spring to early summer.
