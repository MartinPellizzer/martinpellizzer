Bertya

Bertya, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to various regions in Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties. Bertya species typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, yellowish flowers.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its essential oils and timber.
