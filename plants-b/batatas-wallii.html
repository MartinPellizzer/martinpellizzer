Batatas wallii

Batatas wallii, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a tuberous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet-colored roots. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its tubers are rich in starch and contain high levels of anthocyanins, giving them their distinctive color.

Batatas wallii is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal.
