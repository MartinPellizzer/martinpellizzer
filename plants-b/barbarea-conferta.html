Barbarea conferta

Barbarea conferta, commonly known as rock cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. This perennial species typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hair.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
