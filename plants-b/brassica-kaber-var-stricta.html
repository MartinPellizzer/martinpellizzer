Brassica kaber var. stricta

Brassica kaber var. stricta, commonly known as field mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, elongated pods containing seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions worldwide.

Field mustard is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.
