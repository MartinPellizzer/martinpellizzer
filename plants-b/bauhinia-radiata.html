Bauhinia radiata

Bauhinia radiata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, elliptical leaflets. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
