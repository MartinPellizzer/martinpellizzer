Boronia scabra subsp. attenuata

Boronia scabra subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is known for its ornamental value.
