Butea pellita

Butea pellita, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

Butea pellita is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
