Bactris leptotricha

Bactris leptotricha, commonly known as the red rafia palm, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used for thatching and weaving, making it economically important to local communities.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
