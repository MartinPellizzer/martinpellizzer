Bauhinia violacea

Bauhinia violacea, commonly known as the violet acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feathery segments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Bauhinia violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
