Baccharis rupicola

Baccharis rupicola, commonly known as mountain baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often aromatic and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Baccharis rupicola plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
