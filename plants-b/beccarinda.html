Beccarinda

Beccarinda, commonly known as the golden torch tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling torches, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Beccarinda is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
