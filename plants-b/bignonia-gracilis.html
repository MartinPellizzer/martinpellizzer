Bignonia gracilis

Bignonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender crown vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth texture.

Bignonia gracilis is often found in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams.
