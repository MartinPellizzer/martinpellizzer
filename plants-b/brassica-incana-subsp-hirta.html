Brassica incana subsp. hirta

Brassica incana subsp.

hirta, commonly known as hairy wallflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Brassica incana subsp.

hirta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
