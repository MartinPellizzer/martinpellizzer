Bromus pacificus

Bromus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific brome, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green color.

Bromus pacificus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
