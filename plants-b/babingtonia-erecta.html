Babingtonia erecta

Babingtonia erecta, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming during the winter months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Babingtonia erecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salty air.
