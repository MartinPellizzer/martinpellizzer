Bidens chandleri

Bidens chandleri, commonly known as Chandler's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
