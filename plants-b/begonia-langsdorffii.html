Begonia langsdorffii

Begonia langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff's begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces striking clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in horticulture for both its beauty and ease of care.
