Bromelia aurea

Bromelia aurea, commonly known as the golden bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, strap-like leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces tall, flower-bearing stalks that often bloom in shades of yellow and orange, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help collect and store water in its central rosette.

Bromelia aurea thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
