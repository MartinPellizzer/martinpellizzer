Baumea montana

Baumea montana, commonly known as the mountain baumea, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as the grass family. This species is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. Baumea montana typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.
