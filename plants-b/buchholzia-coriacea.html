Buchholzia coriacea

Buchholzia coriacea, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant has small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

Buchholzia coriacea is also valued for its hardiness and resistance to pests and diseases.
