Bulbophyllum otoglossum

Bulbophyllum otoglossum, commonly known as the "Orchid with the Tongue-like Lip," is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a distinctive, elongated pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a unique lip that resembles a tongue, giving it its common name.

This species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
