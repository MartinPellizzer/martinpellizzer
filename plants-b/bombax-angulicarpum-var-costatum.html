Bombax angulicarpum var. costatum

Bombax angulicarpum var. costatum, commonly known as the Costate Silk Cotton Tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces capsule fruits that split open to release fibrous seeds surrounded by a cotton-like pulp. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with distinctive longitudinal ridges.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
