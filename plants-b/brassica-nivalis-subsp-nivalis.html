Brassica nivalis subsp. nivalis

Brassica nivalis subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the alpine mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps and the Himalayas. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
