Brachionidium alpestre

Brachionidium alpestre, commonly known as the alpine brachionidium, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
