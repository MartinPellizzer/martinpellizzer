Betula acuminata var. pilosa

Betula acuminata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy birch, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are elliptical, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
