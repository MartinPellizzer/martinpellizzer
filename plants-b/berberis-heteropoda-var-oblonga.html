Berberis heteropoda var. oblonga

Berberis heteropoda var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and lanceolate, dark green leaves with a glossy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable regions.
