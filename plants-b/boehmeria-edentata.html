Boehmeria edentata

Boehmeria edentata, commonly known as Chinese nettle or wild flax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its hollow, fibrous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. Boehmeria edentata is also valued for its fibers, which have been used historically in textile production.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
