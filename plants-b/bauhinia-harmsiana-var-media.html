Bauhinia harmsiana var. media

Bauhinia harmsiana var. media, commonly known as the broad-leaved bauhinia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant features palmate leaves composed of 5–7 oval-shaped leaflets, which are often hairy on the underside. Its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
