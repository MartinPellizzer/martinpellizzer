Berzelia ericoides

Berzelia ericoides, commonly known as the heath tea plant, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant, pink or white blooms that appear in spring.

Berzelia ericoides is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
