Bactris amoena

Bactris amoena, commonly known as the red raphia, is a palm species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are small and round. Its leaves are often used for thatching and roofing in local communities.

Bactris amoena is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
