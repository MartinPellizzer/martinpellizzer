Bulbophyllum calyptogyne

Bulbophyllum calyptogyne, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure over the labellum. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in low-light conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
