Betula candelae

Betula candelae, commonly known as the candlewood birch, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, candle-like branches that give it a striking appearance, especially when covered in snow. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in the autumn.

Betula candelae is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in cold climates with well-drained soils.
