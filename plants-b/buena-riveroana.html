Buena riveroana

Buena riveroana, commonly known as the riverine beauty, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and marshy areas. Its blooming season typically occurs during the late spring and early summer months.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
