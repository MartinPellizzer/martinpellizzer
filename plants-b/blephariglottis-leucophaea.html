Blephariglottis leucophaea

Blephariglottis leucophaea, commonly known as the white-leafed fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Blephariglottis genus, which belongs to the family Blephariglottidaceae. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
