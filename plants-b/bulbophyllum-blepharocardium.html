Bulbophyllum blepharocardium

Bulbophyllum blepharocardium, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique hood-like structure over the reproductive organs. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its tropical habitat.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and distinctive floral morphology.
