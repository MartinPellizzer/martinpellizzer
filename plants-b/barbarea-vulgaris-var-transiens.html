Barbarea vulgaris var. transiens

Barbarea vulgaris var. transiens, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Barbarea vulgaris var. transiens is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds, making it both a wildflower and a potential weed.
