Burchardia umbellata var. parviflora

Burchardia umbellata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in an umbel. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
