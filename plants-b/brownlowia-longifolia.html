Brownlowia longifolia

Brownlowia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered satin flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually opposite or whorled in arrangement. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brownlowia longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
