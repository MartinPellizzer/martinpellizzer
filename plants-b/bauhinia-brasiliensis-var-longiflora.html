Bauhinia brasiliensis var. longiflora

Bauhinia brasiliensis var. longiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
