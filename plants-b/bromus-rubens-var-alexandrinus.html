Bromus rubens var. alexandrinus

Bromus rubens var. alexandrinus, commonly known as Alexandrian red brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems with a reddish tinge, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. The plant produces spikelets that are usually pink to reddish in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
