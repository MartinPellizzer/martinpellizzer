Brahea dulcis

Brahea dulcis, commonly known as the desert palm, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, slow-growing palm with a short trunk and a dense crown of feathery leaves. The plant has a distinctive, rugged appearance adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought. Its leaves are typically bluish-green and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Brahea dulcis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique beauty and resilience in harsh environments.
