Batatas tomentosa

Batatas tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, purple-tinged stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The tubers are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them a valued crop in certain regions.
