Bambusa buchwaldii

Bambusa buchwaldii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and ability to form dense stands, making it popular for ornamental and ecological purposes. The plant has stiff, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. Its culms are hollow and segmented, typical of bamboo species, and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Bambusa buchwaldii is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a sustainable material for construction and crafts.
