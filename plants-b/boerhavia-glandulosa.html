Boerhavia glandulosa

Boerhavia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its stems are typically succulent, allowing it to store water and thrive in dry conditions.

Boerhavia glandulosa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
