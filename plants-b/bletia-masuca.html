Bletia masuca

Bletia masuca, commonly known as the purple orchid or the Japanese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bletia masuca is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
