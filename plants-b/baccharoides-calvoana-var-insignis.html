Baccharoides calvoana var. insignis

Baccharoides calvoana var. insignis, commonly known as the red-flowered baccharoides, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for coastal gardens and erosion control.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
