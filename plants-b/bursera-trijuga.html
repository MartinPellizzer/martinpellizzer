Bursera trijuga

Bursera trijuga, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, fragrant resinous capsules.

The resin, known as frankincense, is used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in various cultural practices.
