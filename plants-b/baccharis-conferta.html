Baccharis conferta

Baccharis conferta, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Baccharis conferta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought.
