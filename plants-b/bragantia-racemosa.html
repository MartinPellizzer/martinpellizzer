Bragantia racemosa

Bragantia racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding substrate, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Bragantia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
