Brachyachne

Brachyachne, commonly known as the shortgrass or dwarf grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Brachyachne species are characterized by their compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. They produce small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Due to their drought tolerance, they play an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in harsh environments.
