Brizopyrum siculum

Brizopyrum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian bristlegrass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristle-like hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall grassy appearance.
