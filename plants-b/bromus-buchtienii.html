Bromus buchtienii

Bromus buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous narrow leaves. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a high tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Bromus buchtienii is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation strategies.
