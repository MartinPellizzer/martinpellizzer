Bulbophyllum alabastraceus

Bulbophyllum alabastraceus, commonly known as the "Coral Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, coral-like flower structure. The plant produces intricate, colorful inflorescences with elongated, tubular flowers that resemble coral branches. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
