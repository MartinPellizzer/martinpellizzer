Bidens valida

Bidens valida, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and streambanks. The plant features small yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are usually heart-shaped with rough textures. It produces seeds that are attached to fine hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Bidens valida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
