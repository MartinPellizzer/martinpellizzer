Boronia inornata subsp. leptophylla

Boronia inornata subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf boronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, rounded fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
