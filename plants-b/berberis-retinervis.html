Berberis retinervis

Berberis retinervis, commonly known as the net-veined barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardiness.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
