Bambusa multiplex var. shimadae

Bambusa multiplex var. shimadae, commonly known as the Japanese timber bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, green culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a dense, almost grass-like appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value. It is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and structural form.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and small-scale bamboo cultivation.
