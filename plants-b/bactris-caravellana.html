Bactris caravellana

Bactris caravellana, commonly known as the pink rafia palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which eventually develop into edible, round fruits with a fibrous outer layer. Bactris caravellana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of fiber.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
