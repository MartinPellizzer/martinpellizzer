Baccharis prunifolia var. sierrana

Baccharis prunifolia var. sierrana, commonly known as Sierra wildcherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
