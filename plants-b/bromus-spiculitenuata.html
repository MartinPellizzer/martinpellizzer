Bromus spiculitenuata

Bromus spiculitenuata, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, grassy areas and is characterized by its narrow, spike-like inflorescence. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Bromus spiculitenuata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
