Begonia allenii

Begonia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range from red to pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
