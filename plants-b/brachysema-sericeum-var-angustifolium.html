Brachysema sericeum var. angustifolium

Brachysema sericeum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silverbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
