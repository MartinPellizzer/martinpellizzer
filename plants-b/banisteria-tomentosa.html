Banisteria tomentosa

Banisteria tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, woody pods that contain flat, seed-rich seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for shade gardens and as a climbing plant in tropical landscapes.
