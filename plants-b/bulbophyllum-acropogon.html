Bulbophyllum acropogon

Bulbophyllum acropogon, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of small, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Bulbophyllum acropogon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
