Baccharis thesioides

Baccharis thesioides, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient species in its native range.
