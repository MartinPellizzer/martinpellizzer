Besleria compta

Besleria compta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces striking, spider-like flowers with elongated petals and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along riverbanks. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Besleria compta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
