Bromus erectus var. caprinus

Bromus erectus var. caprinus, commonly known as the goat's-rush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and pastures.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
