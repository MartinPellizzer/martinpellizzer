Bulbophyllum gusdorfii var. johorense

Bulbophyllum gusdorfii var. johorense, commonly known as the Johore Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flower spikes that can produce multiple blooms. The plant typically features elliptical or lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense foliage structure. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns and a subtle fragrance.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
