Borassus sambiranensis

Borassus sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis palm, is a species of fan palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species within the Borassus genus.
