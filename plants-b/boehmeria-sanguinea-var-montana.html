Boehmeria sanguinea var. montana

Boehmeria sanguinea var. montana, commonly known as mountain nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale greenish-white color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
