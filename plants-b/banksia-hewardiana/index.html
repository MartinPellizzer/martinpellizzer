Banksia Hewardiana

Banksia hewardiana is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of Western Australia. It is known for its unique and vibrant yellow flower spikes that appear in late summer. This species thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathland environments. Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native wildlife. Despite its beauty, it is considered rare and faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
