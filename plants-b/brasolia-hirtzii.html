Brasolia hirtzii

Brasolia hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy brasolia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom during the warmer months.

Brasolia hirtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
