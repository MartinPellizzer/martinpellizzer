Bactris burretii

Bactris burretii, commonly known as the red raphia, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences with small, clustered flowers, followed by elongated, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw. Its trunk is often used for thatching and construction due to its fibrous nature.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in wet, lowland areas.
