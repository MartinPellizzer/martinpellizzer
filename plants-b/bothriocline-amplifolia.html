Bothriocline amplifolia

Bothriocline amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often broader than they are long. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
