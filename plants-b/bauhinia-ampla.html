Bauhinia ampla

Bauhinia ampla, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves composed of five to seven oval leaflets. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Bauhinia ampla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
