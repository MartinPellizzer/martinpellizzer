Bertya cunninghamii subsp. pubiramula

Bertya cunninghamii subsp.

pubiramula, commonly known as the puberulous bertya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to the eastern coast of Australia, particularly Queensland.

The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. It produces white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
