Bystropogon origanifolius var. ferrensis

Bystropogon origanifolius var. ferrensis, commonly known as the Ferren's woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
