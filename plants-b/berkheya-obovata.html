Berkheya obovata

Berkheya obovata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are pale green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique foliage.
