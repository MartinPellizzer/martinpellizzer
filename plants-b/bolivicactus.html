Bolivicactus

Bolivicactus, commonly known as the Bolivian cactus, is a genus of cacti native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and are often adapted to arid environments. They typically have thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing them to survive in dry conditions. Bolivicactus species can vary in size and shape, with some growing as small shrubs while others form large, sprawling structures.

Many species are also noted for their vibrant flowers, which bloom during the warmer months.
