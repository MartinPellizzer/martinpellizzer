Blumea vestita

Blumea vestita, commonly known as the white blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine, white hairs.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters, making it an attractive plant for ornamental purposes.
