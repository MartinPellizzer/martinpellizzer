Begonia polygonata

Begonia polygonata, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or white variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia polygonata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
