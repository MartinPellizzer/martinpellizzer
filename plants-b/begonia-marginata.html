Begonia marginata

Begonia marginata, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a striking silvery-white margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia marginata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
