Begonia variifolia

Begonia variifolia, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, white, and pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are small and borne in clusters, often in shades of red or pink.

Begonia variifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.
