Bromus fibrosus var. villosellus

Bromus fibrosus var. villosellus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender stems, and dense foliage with hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained sites and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
