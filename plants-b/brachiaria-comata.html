Brachiaria comata

Brachiaria comata, commonly known as hairy signal grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as pastures and disturbed soils, and is often found in regions with warm climates. Brachiaria comata is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is sometimes used in forage systems but can also be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
