Begonia tripartifolia

Begonia tripartifolia, commonly known as the three-part begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three distinct leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

Begonia tripartifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
