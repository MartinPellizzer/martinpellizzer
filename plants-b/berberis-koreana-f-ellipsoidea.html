Berberis koreana f. ellipsoidea

Berberis koreana f. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the Korean barberry, is a compact shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that appear in spring.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Korea and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes due to their vibrant color. Its dense foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscape design and erosion control.

The variety 'ellipsoidea' is distinguished by its elongated leaf shape, setting it apart from other forms of Berberis koreana.
