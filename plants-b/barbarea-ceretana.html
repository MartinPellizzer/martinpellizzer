Barbarea ceretana

Barbarea ceretana, commonly known as the winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are palmately divided with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, round seed pods that release seeds when mature.

Barbarea ceretana is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
