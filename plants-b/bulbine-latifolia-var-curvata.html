Bulbine latifolia var. curvata

Bulbine latifolia var. curvata, commonly known as the broadleaf bulbine or the golden bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
