Beta vulgaris subsp. vulgaris

Beta vulgaris subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as sugar beet, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store large amounts of sucrose.

The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its high sugar content, making it an essential crop in the food and biofuel industries.
