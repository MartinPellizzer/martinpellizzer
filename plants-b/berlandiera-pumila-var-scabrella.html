Berlandiera pumila var. scabrella

Berlandiera pumila var. scabrella, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features hairy, silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, scabrous texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments.
