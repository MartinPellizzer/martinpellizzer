Bupleurum longifolium subvar. acutiusculum

Bupleurum longifolium subvar. acutiusculum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip, giving it its common name.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
