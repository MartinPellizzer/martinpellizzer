Bacopa amplexicaulis

Bacopa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the aquatic bacopa, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, opposite leaves that often encircle the stems. The plant produces tiny, blue or violet flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a water garden plant or for ornamental purposes.
