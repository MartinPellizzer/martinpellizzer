Bromus strictostachys

Bromus strictostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed brome, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Bromus strictostachys is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability.
