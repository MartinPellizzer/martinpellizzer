Begonia × amabilis

Begonia × amabilis, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant and diverse flower colors.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a favorite in gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Begonia × amabilis is often used in containers, hanging baskets, and as a bedding plant due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
