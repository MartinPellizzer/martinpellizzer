Beadlea itatiaiensis

Beadlea itatiaiensis, commonly known as the Itatiaia bean, is a climbing legume native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate structure, and its stems are woody and often twining. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The seeds are typically used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
