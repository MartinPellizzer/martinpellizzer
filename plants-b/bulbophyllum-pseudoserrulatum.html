Bulbophyllum pseudoserrulatum

Bulbophyllum pseudoserrulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
