Berghausia tenella

Berghausia tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and grassy slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
