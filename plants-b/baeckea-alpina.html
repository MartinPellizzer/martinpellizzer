Baeckea alpina

Baeckea alpina, commonly known as the alpine baeckea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or coastal regions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Baeckea alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
