Bidens alba

Bidens alba, commonly known as white beggarticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with two prominent white rays. The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along waterways.

Bidens alba is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
