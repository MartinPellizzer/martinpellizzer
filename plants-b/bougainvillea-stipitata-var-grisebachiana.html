Bougainvillea stipitata var. grisebachiana

Bougainvillea stipitata var. grisebachiana, commonly known as the silverleaf bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, powdery pink bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. Its leaves are small, ovate, and often have a silvery-gray underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
