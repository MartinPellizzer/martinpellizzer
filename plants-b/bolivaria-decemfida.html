Bolivaria decemfida

Bolivaria decemfida, commonly known as the ten-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with ten distinct lobes. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Bolivaria decemfida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
