Baptisia psammophila

Baptisia psammophila, commonly known as the sand bluestem or sand false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or dry soils. It is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Baptisia psammophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
