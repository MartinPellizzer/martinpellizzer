Baccharis pilularis subsp. consanguinea

Baccharis pilularis subsp.

consanguinea, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub and dunes. It has a spreading growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

This subspecies is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
