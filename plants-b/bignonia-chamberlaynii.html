Bignonia chamberlaynii

Bignonia chamberlaynii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces long, twisting stems that can climb up to 30 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
