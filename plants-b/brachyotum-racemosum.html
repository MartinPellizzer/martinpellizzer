Brachyotum racemosum

Brachyotum racemosum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high elevations, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Brachyotum racemosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
