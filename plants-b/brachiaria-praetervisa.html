Brachiaria praetervisa

Brachiaria praetervisa, commonly known as the slender grass or Brazilian grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Brachiaria praetervisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.

Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds.
