Biscutella variegata subsp. latiorifolia

Biscutella variegata subsp.

latiorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed biscuit flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens.

The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, exhibiting a compact growth habit to conserve moisture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
