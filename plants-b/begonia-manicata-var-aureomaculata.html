Begonia manicata var. aureomaculata

Begonia manicata var. aureomaculata, commonly known as the golden-spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with yellow spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular among gardeners for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
