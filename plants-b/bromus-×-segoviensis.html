Bromus × segoviensis

Bromus × segoviensis, commonly known as the Segovia brome, is a hybrid grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
