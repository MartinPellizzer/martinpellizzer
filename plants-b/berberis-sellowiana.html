Berberis sellowiana

Berberis sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Brazil.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
