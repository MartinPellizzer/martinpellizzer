Batemannia wallisii var. major

Batemannia wallisii var. major, commonly known as the giant batemannia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, umbellate flowers, which are followed by bright red berries. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
