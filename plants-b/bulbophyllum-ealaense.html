Bulbophyllum ealaense

Bulbophyllum ealaense, commonly known as the Eala's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native tropical environment.

Bulbophyllum ealaense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
