Biscutella major

Biscutella major, commonly known as the greater biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures. Biscutella major is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
