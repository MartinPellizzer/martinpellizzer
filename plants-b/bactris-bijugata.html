Bactris bijugata

Bactris bijugata, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. Bactris bijugata is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
