Basistemon silvaticus

Basistemon silvaticus, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Basistemon silvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
