Burasaia vitis

Burasaia vitis, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for animals. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive fragrance, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
