Baptisia intercalata

Baptisia intercalata, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flower clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Baptisia intercalata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
