Batodendron andrachniforme

Batodendron andrachniforme, commonly known as the spiky leaf fern, is a unique tropical plant characterized by its intricate, fern-like fronds that bear spines along their margins.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the understory of dense rainforests. The plant's reproductive structures are small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into spore-bearing capsules. Its leaves are often described as having a delicate, lace-like appearance with a textured surface.

Batodendron andrachniforme is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
