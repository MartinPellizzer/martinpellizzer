Banksia paludosa subsp. astrolux

Banksia paludosa subsp.

astrolux, commonly known as the swamp banksia, is a shrub native to the wetter regions of south-eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers attract a variety of nectar-feeding insects and birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
