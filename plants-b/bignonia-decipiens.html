Bignonia decipiens

Bignonia decipiens, commonly known as the false yellow wood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, pendulous fruit capsules that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native landscapes.
