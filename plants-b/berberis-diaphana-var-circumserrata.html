Berberis diaphana var. circumserrata

Berberis diaphana var. circumserrata, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
