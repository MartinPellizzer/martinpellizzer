Bupleurum tenue var. humile

Bupleurum tenue var. humile, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
