Blumea aromatica

Blumea aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are often clustered in a flat, disk-like structure. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Blumea aromatica thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
