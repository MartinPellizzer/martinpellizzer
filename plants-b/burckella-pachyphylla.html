Burckella pachyphylla

Burckella pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved burckella, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Burckella pachyphylla is well adapted to high sunlight exposure and poor soil conditions.
