Bromus hallii

Bromus hallii, commonly known as Hall's brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear spikelets along their length. The plant typically flowers in late summer, producing small, two-rowed spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color. Bromus hallii thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
