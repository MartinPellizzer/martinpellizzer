Bromus caldasii

Bromus caldasii, commonly known as the Caldas grass, is a flowering plant species native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. Bromus caldasii is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
