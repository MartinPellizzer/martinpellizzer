Baccharis hambatensis

Baccharis hambatensis, commonly known as the Hambatensis Baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
