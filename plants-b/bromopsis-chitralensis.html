Bromopsis chitralensis

Bromopsis chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral grass, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Bromopsis chitralensis is notable for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it an important species in mountainous ecosystems.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for grazing animals and its contribution to soil stabilization in fragile high-altitude habitats.
