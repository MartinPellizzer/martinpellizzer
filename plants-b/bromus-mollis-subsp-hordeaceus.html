Bromus mollis subsp. hordeaceus

Bromus mollis subsp.

hordeaceus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
