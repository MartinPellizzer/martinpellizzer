Barbarea vulgaris subsp. arcuata

Barbarea vulgaris subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the curled dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
