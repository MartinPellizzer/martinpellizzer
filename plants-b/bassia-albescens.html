Bassia albescens

Bassia albescens, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Bassia albescens is a low-growing perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native ecosystems.
