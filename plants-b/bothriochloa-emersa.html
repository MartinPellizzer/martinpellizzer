Bothriochloa emersa

Bothriochloa emersa, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tall, branching inflorescences that produce small, blue-green florets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
