Berberis sheridaniana

Berberis sheridaniana, commonly known as the Sheridan's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
