Bulbophyllum antenniferum

Bulbophyllum antenniferum, commonly known as the Yellow-veined Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic orchid features a compact, pseudobulb-like structure with a single, fleshy leaf. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green with prominent purple veins, and they bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

It is appreciated for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
