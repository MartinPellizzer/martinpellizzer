Bulbophyllum lindleyi

Bulbophyllum lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the genus Bulbophyllum in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Bulbophyllum lindleyi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking blooms.
