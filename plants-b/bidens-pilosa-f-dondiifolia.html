Bidens pilosa f. dondiifolia

Bidens pilosa f. dondiifolia, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a variant of the common beggar's ticks species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers with two ray florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
