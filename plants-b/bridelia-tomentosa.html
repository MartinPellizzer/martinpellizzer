Bridelia tomentosa

Bridelia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. Bridelia tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
