Bromopsis mucroglumis

Bromopsis mucroglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Bromopsis mucroglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
