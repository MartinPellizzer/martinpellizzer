Buddleja reitzii

Buddleja reitzii, commonly known as the Reitz's bushmaster, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant in mild climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
