Balanites maughamii subsp. acuta

Balanites maughamii subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the sharp-fruited burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often reduced to spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use in dry regions.
