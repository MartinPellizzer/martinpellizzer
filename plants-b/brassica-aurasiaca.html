Brassica aurasiaca

Brassica aurasiaca, commonly known as the Siberian kale, is a hardy, perennial plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or purplish in color, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. Brassica aurasiaca is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.

It is often grown in temperate regions for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
