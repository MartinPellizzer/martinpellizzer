Bidens rotata

Bidens rotata, commonly known as the common tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The plant has finely dissected, feathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Bidens rotata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
