Bisnaga latispina var. spiralis

Bisnaga latispina var. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral bisnaga, is a unique variant of the bisnaga plant characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves.

This plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to specific regions in South America. Its leaves are broad and have a waxy surface, which helps in reducing water loss in arid environments. The flowers are typically small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
