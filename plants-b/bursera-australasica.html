Bursera australasica

Bursera australasica, commonly known as the Australian frankincense tree, is a species native to parts of Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, leathery leaves. It produces fragrant resin, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for local wildlife.
