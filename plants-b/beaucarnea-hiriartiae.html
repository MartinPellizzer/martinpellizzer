Beaucarnea hiriartiae

Beaucarnea hiriartiae, commonly known as the Hiriart's elephant tree, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, branching stems that resemble the trunk of an elephant, covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
