Burglaria

Burglaria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers and are often cultivated as ornamental plants. Burglaria species typically have a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that help store water, adapting them to arid environments. The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white.

They are popular in horticulture for their ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
