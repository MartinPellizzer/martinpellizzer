Bulbophyllum mona-lisae

Bulbophyllum mona-lisae, commonly known as the Mona Lisa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern that resembles a portrait, hence its common name. Its pseudobulbs are compact and store water, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with limited moisture.

The orchid's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively manageable care requirements.
