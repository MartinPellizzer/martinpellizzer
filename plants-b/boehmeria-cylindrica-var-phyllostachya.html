Boehmeria cylindrica var. phyllostachya

Boehmeria cylindrica var. phyllostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leafed horse-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
