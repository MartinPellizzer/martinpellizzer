Biserrula pelecinus var. pubescens

Biserrula pelecinus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent biserrula, is a leguminous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species for dryland agriculture.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
