Bactris gleasonii

Bactris gleasonii, commonly known as the Mexican apple palm, is a tropical plant native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble apples, hence its common name. Bactris gleasonii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in its native regions.
