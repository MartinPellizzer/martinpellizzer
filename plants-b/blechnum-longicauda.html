Blechnum longicauda

Blechnum longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant features a creeping rhizome and produces fertile fronds with sporangia on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds have a distinctive long, tapering shape, giving the plant its common name.
