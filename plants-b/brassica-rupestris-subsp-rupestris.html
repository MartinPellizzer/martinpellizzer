Brassica rupestris subsp. rupestris

Brassica rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial plant native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils on cliffs, slopes, and rocky outcrops.

The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are usually fleshy and lobed. It belongs to the mustard family (Brassicaceae), characterized by its four-petaled flowers and siliquous fruits.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
