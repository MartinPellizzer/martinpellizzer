Broussonetia luzoniensis

Broussonetia luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in lowland forests and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Its bark is thick and fissured, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various animals.
