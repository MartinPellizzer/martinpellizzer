Brexia arborea

Brexia arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and red in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Brexia arborea typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
