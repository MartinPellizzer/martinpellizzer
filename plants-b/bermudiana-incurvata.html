Bermudiana incurvata

Bermudiana incurvata, commonly known as the Bermuda grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or folded, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in coastal environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense mats.
