Bulbophyllum sanitii

Bulbophyllum sanitii, commonly known as the sacred ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and delicate, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of fungi or lichens.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Bulbophyllum sanitii is highly valued in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
