Barbarea abortiva

Barbarea abortiva, commonly known as the common winter cress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Barbarea abortiva is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
