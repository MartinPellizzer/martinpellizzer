Bombacopsis squamigera

Bombacopsis squamigera, commonly known as the silky oak, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Bombacopsis squamigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
