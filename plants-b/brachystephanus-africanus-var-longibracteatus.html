Brachystephanus africanus var. longibracteatus

Brachystephanus africanus var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the African bristlestep, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant in arid climates.
