Begonia latistipula

Begonia latistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and showy bracts. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, surrounded by brightly colored, modified leaves called bracts.

Begonia latistipula prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.
