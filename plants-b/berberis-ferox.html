Berberis ferox

Berberis ferox, commonly known as the Indian barberry, is a shrub native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. Berberis ferox contains alkaloids, such as berberine, which have been used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
