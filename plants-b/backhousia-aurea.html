Backhousia aurea

Backhousia aurea, commonly known as the golden backhousia, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often tinged with a yellowish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Backhousia aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its pleasant scent and hardy nature.
