Brassica pinnatifida

Brassica pinnatifida, commonly known as Chinese mustard or wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are dry, dehiscent fruits.

Brassica pinnatifida is also noted for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
