Bistorta milletioides

Bistorta milletioides, commonly known as the mountain bistort, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in its natural habitat.

Bistorta milletioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
