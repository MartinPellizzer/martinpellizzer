Berberis cristata

Berberis cristata, commonly known as the crown thorn or crested barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features distinctive, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant has spiny, thorn-covered stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal display of flowers and fruit.
