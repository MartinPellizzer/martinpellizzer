Baccharis viminea var. atwoodii

Baccharis viminea var. atwoodii, commonly known as Atwood's baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
