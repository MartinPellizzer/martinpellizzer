Byrsella leontoglossa

Byrsella leontoglossa, commonly known as the lion's tongue plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are often adorned with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
