Buddleja mairei f. albiflora

Buddleja mairei f. albiflora, commonly known as the White-Flowered Blumea, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is relatively low maintenance and suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.
