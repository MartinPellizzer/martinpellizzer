Barleria violacea

Barleria violacea, commonly known as the blue barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom throughout the growing season.

Barleria violacea is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
