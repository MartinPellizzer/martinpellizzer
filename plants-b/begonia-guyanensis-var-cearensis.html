Begonia guyanensis var. cearensis

Begonia guyanensis var. cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Begonia, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant red, purple, or bronze hues. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
