Bubonium frutescens

Bubonium frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered bush, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its dry environment. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Bubonium frutescens is well adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
