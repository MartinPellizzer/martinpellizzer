Breweria sulphurea

Breweria sulphurea, commonly known as sulfur breweria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its striking color and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
