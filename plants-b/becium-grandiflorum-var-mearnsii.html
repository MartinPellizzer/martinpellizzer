Becium grandiflorum var. mearnsii

Becium grandiflorum var. mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns's becium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are leathery and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
