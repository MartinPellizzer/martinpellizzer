Ballota acuta var. intermedia

Ballota acuta var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ballota, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Ballota acuta var. intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
