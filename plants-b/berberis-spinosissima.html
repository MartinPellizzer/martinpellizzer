Berberis spinosissima

Berberis spinosissima, commonly known as the Spanish barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand harsh winters and is often used in traditional medicine.
