Bignonia acutistipula

Bignonia acutistipula, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia acutistipula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
