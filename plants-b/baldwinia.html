Baldwinia

Baldwinia, commonly known as the common baldwinia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typically arranged in a cluster. Baldwinia species are native to various regions of Australia and are often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species in dry environments.

Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
