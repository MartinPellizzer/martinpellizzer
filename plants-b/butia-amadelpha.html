Butia amadelpha

Butia amadelpha, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits.

Butia amadelpha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
