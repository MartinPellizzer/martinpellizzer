Bia cordata

Bia cordata, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface, while its leaves are broad and floating. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in muddy substrates and helps filter water. Bia cordata is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and enlightenment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
