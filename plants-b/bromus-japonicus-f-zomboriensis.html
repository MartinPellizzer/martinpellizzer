Bromus japonicus f. zomboriensis

Bromus japonicus f. zomboriensis, commonly known as the Japanese bromus, is a variant of the common brome grass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny areas. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This form is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
