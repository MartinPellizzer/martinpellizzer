Bupleurum flavum

Bupleurum flavum, commonly known as yellow skullcap, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Bupleurum flavum has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and sedative properties.
