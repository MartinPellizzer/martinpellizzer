Begonia chiasmogyna

Begonia chiasmogyna, commonly known as the golden begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive golden-green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, which often exhibits variegation and a waxy texture.
