Bidens chrysanthemifolia

Bidens chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the golden beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow flowers with ray florets resembling those of chrysanthemums, and it has hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
