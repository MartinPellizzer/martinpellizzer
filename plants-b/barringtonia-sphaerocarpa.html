Barringtonia sphaerocarpa

Barringtonia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the giant candle bush, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths for pollination. The fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds when mature. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
