Brittonastrum wrightii

Brittonastrum wrightii, commonly known as Wright's brittlestem, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
