Biscutella laevigata var. scaposa

Biscutella laevigata var. scaposa, commonly known as the scapose biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are basal, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branching.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
