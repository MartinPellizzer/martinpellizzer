Brizopyrum americanum

Brizopyrum americanum, commonly known as the American feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

Brizopyrum americanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
