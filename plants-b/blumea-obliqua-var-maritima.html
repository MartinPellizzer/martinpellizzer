Blumea obliqua var. maritima

Blumea obliqua var. maritima, commonly known as coastal blumea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines, displaying adaptability to saline environments. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
