Bromus rubens var. fasciculatus

Bromus rubens var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the red brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish-brown coloration, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
