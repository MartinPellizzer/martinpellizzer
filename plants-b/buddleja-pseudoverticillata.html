Buddleja pseudoverticillata

Buddleja pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender, whorled leaves and its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically purple or pink and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
