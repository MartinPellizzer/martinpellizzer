Bassovia silvatica

Bassovia silvatica, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its striking silver-gray foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by elliptical, dark green leaves that provide dense shade. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
