Bothriochloa palmeri

Bothriochloa palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to withstand arid conditions and is sometimes used in restoration projects in dryland environments.
