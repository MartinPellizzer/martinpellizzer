Boronia barkeriana subsp. gymnopetala

Boronia barkeriana subsp.

gymnopetala, commonly known as the smooth-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to the south-eastern region of Australia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a smooth, glossy surface, which distinguishes it from other subspecies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
