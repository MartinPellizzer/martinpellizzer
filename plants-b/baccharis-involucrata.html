Baccharis involucrata

Baccharis involucrata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Baccharis involucrata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
