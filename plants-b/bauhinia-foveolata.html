Bauhinia foveolata

Bauhinia foveolata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia foveolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.
