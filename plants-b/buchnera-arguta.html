Buchnera arguta

Buchnera arguta, commonly known as the sticky cypress, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Buchnera arguta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
