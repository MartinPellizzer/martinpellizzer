Bambusa mitis

Bambusa mitis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green culms that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a lighter green than those of other bamboo species. It is relatively fast-growing and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Bambusa mitis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its sustainable use in construction and crafts.
