Banisteria convolvulifolia

Banisteria convolvulifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are clusters of small, bright red flowers that resemble coral. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria convolvulifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
