Bothriochloa grahamii

Bothriochloa grahamii, commonly known as Graham's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its ecological adaptability and potential use in restoration projects.
