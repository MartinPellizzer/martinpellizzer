Biserrula pelecinus var. gabrielis

Biserrula pelecinus var. gabrielis, commonly known as the Gabriel's biserrula, is a leguminous plant native to South Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in open grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in sustainable landscaping.
