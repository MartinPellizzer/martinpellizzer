Bistorta coriacea

Bistorta coriacea, commonly known as broadleaf bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Bistorta coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
