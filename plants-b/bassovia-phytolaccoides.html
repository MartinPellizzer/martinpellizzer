Bassovia phytolaccoides

Bassovia phytolaccoides, commonly known as the swamp bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is adapted to saturated soils and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
