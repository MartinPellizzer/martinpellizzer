Baccharis genistelloides var. angusta

Baccharis genistelloides var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
