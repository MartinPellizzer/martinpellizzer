Boerhavia diffusa var. hirsuta

Boerhavia diffusa var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy spurges, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed seed capsules. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
