Bauhinia velutina

Bauhinia velutina, commonly known as velvetwood or velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves with soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by flat, bean-like pods.

Bauhinia velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
