Billbergia × poupionii

Billbergia × poupionii, commonly known as the hybrid billbergia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Billbergia nutans and Billbergia recurvata.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and colorful flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Billbergia × poupionii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
