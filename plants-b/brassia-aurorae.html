Brassia aurorae

Brassia aurorae, commonly known as the Aurora Cattleya, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Brassia aurorae requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive.
