Begonia campanensis

Begonia campanensis, commonly known as the bell begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and arranged oppositely on the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Begonia campanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in both outdoor and indoor settings.
