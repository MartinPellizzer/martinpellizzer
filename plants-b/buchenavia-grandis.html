Buchenavia grandis

Buchenavia grandis, commonly known as the giant sandpaper bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpapery bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to semi-arid environments.

Buchenavia grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
