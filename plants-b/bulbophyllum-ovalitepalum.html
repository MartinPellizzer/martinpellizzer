Bulbophyllum ovalitepalum

Bulbophyllum ovalitepalum, commonly known as the oval-lipped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually brightly colored, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.
