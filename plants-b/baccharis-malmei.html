Baccharis malmei

Baccharis malmei, commonly known as Malme's baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Baccharis malmei is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
