Benjaminia splendens

Benjaminia splendens, commonly known as the splendour tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and produces showy, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rounded crown and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscaping and conservation efforts.
