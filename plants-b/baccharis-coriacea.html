Baccharis coriacea

Baccharis coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its greyish, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
