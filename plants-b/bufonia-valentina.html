Bufonia valentina

Bufonia valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's bufonia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or purple in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its visual appeal. Bufonia valentina thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
