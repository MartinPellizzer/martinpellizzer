Begonia areolata

Begonia areolata, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that often display a striking pattern of red, purple, or white spots. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Begonia areolata is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.
