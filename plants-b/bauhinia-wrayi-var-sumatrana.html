Bauhinia wrayi var. sumatrana

Bauhinia wrayi var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
