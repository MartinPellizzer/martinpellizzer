Banksia prolata

Banksia prolata, commonly known as the slender banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Banksia prolata thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
