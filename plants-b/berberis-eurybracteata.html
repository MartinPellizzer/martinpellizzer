Berberis eurybracteata

Berberis eurybracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but typically consumed in moderation. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Berberis eurybracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
