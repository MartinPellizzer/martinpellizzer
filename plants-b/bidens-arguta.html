Bidens arguta

Bidens arguta, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two ray florets and a distinctive, roughened disk. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.

Bidens arguta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
