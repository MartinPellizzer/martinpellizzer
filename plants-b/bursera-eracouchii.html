Bursera eracouchii

Bursera eracouchii, commonly known as the Mexican bursera, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes.

Bursera eracouchii typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
