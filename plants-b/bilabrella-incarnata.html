Bilabrella incarnata

Bilabrella incarnata, commonly known as the pink-flowered bilabrella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant produces small, showy pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Bilabrella incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
