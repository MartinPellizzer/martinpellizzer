Beyeria leschenaultii var. rosmarinoides

Beyeria leschenaultii var. rosmarinoides, commonly known as the rosemary mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage is often used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent in various dishes.
