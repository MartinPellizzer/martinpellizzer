Bacopa violacea

Bacopa violacea, commonly known as blue bacopa, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Bacopa violacea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported cognitive benefits.
