Berberis chirisigui

Berberis chirisigui, commonly known as the Chirigui barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments within cloud forests. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
