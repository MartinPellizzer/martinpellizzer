Bidens connata

Bidens connata, commonly known as the swamp evening-primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow flowers with ray florets and a central disk, typical of the genus Bidens. Its leaves are often alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Bidens connata typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.
