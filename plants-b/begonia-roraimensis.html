Begonia roraimensis

Begonia roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Guiana Shield region.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
