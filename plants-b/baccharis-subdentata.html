Baccharis subdentata

Baccharis subdentata, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It produces small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
