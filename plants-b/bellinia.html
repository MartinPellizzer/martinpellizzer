Bellinia

Bellinia, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It consists of about 60 species, primarily found in southern Africa, with some species extending into southern Europe and western Asia. The plants are typically herbaceous, with hairy stems and clusters of small, often pink or white flowers. They produce capsules as fruits, containing numerous small seeds.

Bellinia species are often adapted to rocky or arid environments, showcasing a range of morphological variations.
