Bridelia ovata var. acutifolia

Bridelia ovata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bridelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
