Bambusa nairiana

Bambusa nairiana, commonly known as the Nair's bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Bambusa nairiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
