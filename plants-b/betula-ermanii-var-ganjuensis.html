Betula ermanii var. ganjuensis

Betula ermanii var. ganjuensis, commonly known as the Ganjuensis birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
