Bocoa racemulosa

Bocoa racemulosa, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Bocoa racemulosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
