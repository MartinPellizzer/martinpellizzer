Bulbinella hookeri var. angustifolia

Bulbinella hookeri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbinella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
