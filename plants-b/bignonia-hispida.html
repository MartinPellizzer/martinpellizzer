Bignonia hispida

Bignonia hispida, commonly known as the rough bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its hairy, rough stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Bignonia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
