Baccaurea reticulata var. velutina

Baccaurea reticulata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color. The fruit's pulp is sweet and aromatic, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
