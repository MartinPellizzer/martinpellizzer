Bothriocline globosa

Bothriocline globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky blue or purple flower heads that resemble a globe, hence its common name. The plant has silvery-gray, woolly leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Bothriocline globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
