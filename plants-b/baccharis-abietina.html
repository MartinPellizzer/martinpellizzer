Baccharis abietina

Baccharis abietina, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and grasslands.

Baccharis abietina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
