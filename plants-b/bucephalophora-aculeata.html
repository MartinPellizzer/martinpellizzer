Bucephalophora aculeata

Bucephalophora aculeata, commonly known as the spiny pteridium, is a member of the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fronds.

It is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces fiddlehead-like young fronds that unfurl into lanceolate, serrated leaves. Its spores are released from sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in the study of fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
