Bunchosia glandulosa

Bunchosia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and glandular structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish fruits.

Bunchosia glandulosa is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
