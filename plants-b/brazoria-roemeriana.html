Brazoria roemeriana

Brazoria roemeriana, commonly known as the Roemer's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.

Brazoria roemeriana is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
