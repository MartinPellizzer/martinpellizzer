Berberis mekongensis

Berberis mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong barberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
