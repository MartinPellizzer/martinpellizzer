Biscutella auriculata var. brevicalcarata

Biscutella auriculata var. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-spurred biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are pale yellow with distinctive short spurs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
