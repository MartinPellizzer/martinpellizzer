Begonia obtusifolia

Begonia obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, often borne on erect stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is particularly notable for its soft, velvety texture, which adds a unique aesthetic to gardens and indoor spaces.
