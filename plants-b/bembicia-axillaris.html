Bembicia axillaris

Bembicia axillaris, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Bembicia axillaris is a perennial species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
