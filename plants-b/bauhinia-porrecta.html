Bauhinia porrecta

Bauhinia porrecta, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small, pointed leaflets. Bauhinia porrecta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, pod-like fruits that mature in late summer.
