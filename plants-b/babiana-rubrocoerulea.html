Babiana rubrocoerulea

Babiana rubrocoerulea, commonly known as the red-blue babiana, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its colorful, showy flowers that range from deep red to blue. The plant produces upright, grass-like leaves and has a short, stocky stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single row and bloom in late winter to early spring.

Babiana rubrocoerulea is well-suited to rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
