Bambusa valida

Bambusa valida, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and dense, upright growth habit. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to spread rapidly and form thick clumps.

Bambusa valida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in construction and handicrafts due to its strong and durable stalks.
