Bulbostylis junciformis var. filamentosa

Bulbostylis junciformis var. filamentosa, commonly known as the slender bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate, grassy appearance. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistently moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
