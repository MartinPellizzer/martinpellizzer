Bromus scoparius var. psilostachys

Bromus scoparius var. psilostachys, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological succession and soil stabilization.
