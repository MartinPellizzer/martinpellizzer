Bubon libanotis

Bubon libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Bubon libanotis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Campanulaceae family.
