Berberis orthobotrys var. sinthanensis

Berberis orthobotrys var. sinthanensis, commonly known as the Sinthanensis barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
