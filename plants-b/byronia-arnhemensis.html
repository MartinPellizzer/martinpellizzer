Byronia arnhemensis

Byronia arnhemensis, commonly known as the Northern Byronia, is a small shrub native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It features smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Byronia arnhemensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for native wildlife.
