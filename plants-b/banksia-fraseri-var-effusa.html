Banksia fraseri var. effusa

Banksia fraseri var. effusa, commonly known as the slender banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, yellowish to orange-red flower spikes that appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators, including honeyeaters and bees.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
