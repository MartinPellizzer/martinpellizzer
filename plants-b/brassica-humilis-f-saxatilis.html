Brassica humilis f. saxatilis

Brassica humilis f. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a compact, low-growing plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, resilient species suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
