Buphthalmum succisifolium

Buphthalmum succisifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a robust, branching stem covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often slightly succulent. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
