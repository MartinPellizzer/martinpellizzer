Begonia angularis var. angustifolia

Begonia angularis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple along the veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is popular among collectors for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
