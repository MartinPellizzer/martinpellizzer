Begonia lucida

Begonia lucida, commonly known as the shining begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that reflect light, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Begonia lucida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
