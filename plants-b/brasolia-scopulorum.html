Brasolia scopulorum

Brasolia scopulorum, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to its dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

Brasolia scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
