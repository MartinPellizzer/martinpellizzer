Begonia puberula

Begonia puberula, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, Begonia puberula is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
