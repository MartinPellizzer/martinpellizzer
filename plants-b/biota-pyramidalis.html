Biota pyramidalis

Biota pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, pyramidal growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in some varieties.

Biota pyramidalis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
