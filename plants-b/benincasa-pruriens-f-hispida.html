Benincasa pruriens f. hispida

Benincasa pruriens f. hispida, commonly known as the rough sponge gourd, is a variant of the sponge gourd species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a trailing or climbing vine characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are followed by large, inflated, green fruits that mature to a yellow or orange hue. The fruit's exterior is covered in a rough, prickly texture, which gives the plant its common name.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary traditions across Asia.
