Berberis pinifolia

Berberis pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name, and it produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.

The berries it produces are edible but are typically consumed by birds rather than humans.
