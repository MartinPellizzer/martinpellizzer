Berberis amoena var. umbelliflora

Berberis amoena var. umbelliflora, commonly known as the sweet barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

The berries that follow the flowers are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.
