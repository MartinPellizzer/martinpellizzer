Begonia setaria

Begonia setaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Begonia setaria thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
