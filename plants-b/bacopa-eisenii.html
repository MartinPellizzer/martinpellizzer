Bacopa eisenii

Bacopa eisenii, commonly known as dwarf water bead, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, round, translucent leaves that resemble tiny beads, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. Bacopa eisenii is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pale blue or white.
