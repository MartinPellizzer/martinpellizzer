Brami

Brami, commonly known as the "bramble" or "briar," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

It includes shrubs and subshrubs characterized by their thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and they bloom in spring. Brami species are often found in temperate regions, thriving in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and landscape design.
