Brachionidium parvum

Brachionidium parvum, commonly known as the small water flea, is a microscopic aquatic organism belonging to the class Rotifera.

It is characterized by its tiny, barrel-shaped body and a distinctive corona of cilia surrounding its mouth. This plant-like organism is often found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. While not a true plant, it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems as a primary consumer.

Its presence is an indicator of water quality and ecological health.
