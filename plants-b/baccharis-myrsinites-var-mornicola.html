Baccharis myrsinites var. mornicola

Baccharis myrsinites var. mornicola, commonly known as the mountain baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing in dry slopes and canyons. Its foliage is aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
