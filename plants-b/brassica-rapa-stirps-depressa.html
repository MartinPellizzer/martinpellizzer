Brassica rapa stirps depressa

Brassica rapa stirps depressa, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has small, lobed leaves and produces small, round pods containing seeds.

This species is often considered a weed but is also valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional medicine.
