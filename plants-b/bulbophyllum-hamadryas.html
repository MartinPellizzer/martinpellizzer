Bulbophyllum hamadryas

Bulbophyllum hamadryas, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on slender peduncles and are known for their intricate, spurred structure.

Bulbophyllum hamadryas is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
