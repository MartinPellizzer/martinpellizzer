Bauhinia baina

Bauhinia baina, commonly known as the flame tree or red bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia baina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
