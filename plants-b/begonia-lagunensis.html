Begonia lagunensis

Begonia lagunensis, commonly known as the Lake Begonia, is a species native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in humid forests. Its flowers, which range in color from red to orange, are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
