Blumea amplectens var. arenaria

Blumea amplectens var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
