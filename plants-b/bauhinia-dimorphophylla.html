Bauhinia dimorphophylla

Bauhinia dimorphophylla, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia dimorphophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
