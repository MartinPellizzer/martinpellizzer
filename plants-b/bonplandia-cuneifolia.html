Bonplandia cuneifolia

Bonplandia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bonplandia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
