Bulbophyllum leptopus

Bulbophyllum leptopus, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, loose soil.

Bulbophyllum leptopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
