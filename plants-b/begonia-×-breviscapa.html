Begonia × breviscapa

Begonia × breviscapa, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant flowers and foliage.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Begonia semperflorens and Begonia pavonina, combining traits from both parent plants. The plant typically features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting bracts that enhance its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities, Begonia × breviscapa is widely used in gardens, containers, and as a bedding plant.
