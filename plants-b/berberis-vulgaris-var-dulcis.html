Berberis vulgaris var. dulcis

Berberis vulgaris var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant autumn foliage and architectural form.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in ecological habitats.
