Baccharis woytkowskii

Baccharis woytkowskii, commonly known as the Brazilian erythroxylum, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces small, woody fruits.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
