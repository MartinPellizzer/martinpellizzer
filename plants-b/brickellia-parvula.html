Brickellia parvula

Brickellia parvula, commonly known as the plains brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often sprawling or ascending.

Brickellia parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
