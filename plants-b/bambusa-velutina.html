Bambusa velutina

Bambusa velutina, commonly known as velvet bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, almost velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense, cylindrical stem that is often used in landscaping. Its rapid growth and attractive appearance make it popular for garden use, though it requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Bambusa velutina is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable building materials.
