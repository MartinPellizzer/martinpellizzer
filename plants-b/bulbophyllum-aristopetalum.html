Bulbophyllum aristopetalum

Bulbophyllum aristopetalum, commonly known as the aristopetalum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that are typically borne on branched inflorescences. The plant produces pseudobulbs that serve as storage organs for water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its often humid and shaded habitat.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral displays.
