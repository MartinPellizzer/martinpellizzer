Bidens melanocarpa

Bidens melanocarpa, commonly known as blackroot or blackleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its dark purple to black seeds are distinctive and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
