Billardiera versicolor

Billardiera versicolor, commonly known as the variable flame vine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinct reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its flowers are followed by elongated, narrow fruits that contain several seeds.

Billardiera versicolor thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.
