Blaeria bracteata

Blaeria bracteata, commonly known as the bracted blazer, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bracts that enclose the small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, are usually pale pink to white and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
