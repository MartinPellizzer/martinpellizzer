Begonia wrayi

Begonia wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's begonia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and its stems are often trailing or upright depending on the growing conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Begonia wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
