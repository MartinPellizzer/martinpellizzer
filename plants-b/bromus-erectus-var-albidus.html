Bromus erectus var. albidus

Bromus erectus var. albidus, commonly known as the white brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Bromus erectus var. albidus is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
