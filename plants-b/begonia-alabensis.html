Begonia alabensis

Begonia alabensis, commonly known as the Alabian begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
