Brassica arvensis var. schkuhriana

Brassica arvensis var. schkuhriana, commonly known as the field mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is a biennial or annual plant characterized by its erect stem and alternate leaves that are usually lobed or pinnately divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
