Biscutella raphanifolia

Biscutella raphanifolia, commonly known as the radishleaf biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many plants with edible leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The leaves are edible and have a slightly peppery taste, similar to that of mustard plants.
