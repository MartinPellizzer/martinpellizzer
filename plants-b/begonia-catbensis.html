Begonia catbensis

Begonia catbensis, commonly known as the Cebu begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and colors. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is also considered a rare and endangered species.
