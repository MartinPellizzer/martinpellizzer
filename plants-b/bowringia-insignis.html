Bowringia insignis

Bowringia insignis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Bowringia insignis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
