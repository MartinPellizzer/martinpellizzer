Barkhausia cernua

Barkhausia cernua, commonly known as the hanging barkhausia, is a unique flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant's bark is distinctive, featuring a rough, fissured texture that gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
