Begonia lophoptera

Begonia lophoptera, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
