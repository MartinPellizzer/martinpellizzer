Bismalva

Bismalva, commonly known as marshmallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its roots contain mucilage, which has been traditionally used for its soothing properties.

Bismalva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
