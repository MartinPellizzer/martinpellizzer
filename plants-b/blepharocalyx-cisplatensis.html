Blepharocalyx cisplatensis

Blepharocalyx cisplatensis, commonly known as the Cisplatense orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along riverbanks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a notable example of adaptive evolution in tropical ecosystems.
