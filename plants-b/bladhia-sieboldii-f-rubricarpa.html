Bladhia sieboldii f. rubricarpa

Bladhia sieboldii f. rubricarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited bladhia, is a variant of the Bladhia sieboldii species native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by distinctive red berries in the autumn. Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.

The f. rubricarpa form is particularly valued for its striking red fruits and seasonal color changes.
