Bellevalia stepporum

Bellevalia stepporum, commonly known as the steppe bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Bellevalia stepporum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
