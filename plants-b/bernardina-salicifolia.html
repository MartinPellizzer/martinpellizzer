Bernardina salicifolia

Bernardina salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved bernardina, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Bernardina salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
