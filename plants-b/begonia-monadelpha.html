Begonia monadelpha

Begonia monadelpha, commonly known as the red-flowered begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Begonia monadelpha thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
