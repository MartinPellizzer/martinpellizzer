Begonia trapa var. pilosa

Begonia trapa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy water begonia, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that are covered in short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
