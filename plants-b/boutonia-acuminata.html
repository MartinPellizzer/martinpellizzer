Boutonia acuminata

Boutonia acuminata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Boutonia acuminata is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant due to its specific care requirements.
