Bromus haussknechtii

Bromus haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussecknecht's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender spikelets, which contain the reproductive structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Bromus haussknechtii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated for forage in dry environments.
