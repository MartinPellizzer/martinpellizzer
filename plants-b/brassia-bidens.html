Brassia bidens

Brassia bidens, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Brassia bidens is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
