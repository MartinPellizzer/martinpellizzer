Biscutella coronopifolia

Biscutella coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pink or violet flowers in late spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
