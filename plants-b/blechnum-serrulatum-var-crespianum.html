Blechnum serrulatum var. crespianum

Blechnum serrulatum var. crespianum, commonly known as the Crespian maidenhair fern, is a variant of the native New Zealand fern Blechnum serrulatum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and gracefully drooping. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in clusters known as sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
