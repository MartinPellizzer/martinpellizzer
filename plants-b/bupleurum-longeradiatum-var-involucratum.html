Bupleurum longeradiatum var. involucratum

Bupleurum longeradiatum var. involucratum, commonly known as the woolly valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
