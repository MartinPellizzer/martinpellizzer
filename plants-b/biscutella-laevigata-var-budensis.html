Biscutella laevigata var. budensis

Biscutella laevigata var. budensis, commonly known as the smooth biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
