Beloperone calycina

Beloperone calycina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. Its stems are twining and can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Beloperone calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
