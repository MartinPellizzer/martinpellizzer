Bartlettina lanicaulis

Bartlettina lanicaulis, commonly known as the woolly stalked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Bartlettina lanicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
