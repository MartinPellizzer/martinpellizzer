Bougainvillea spinosa f. microbracteata

Bougainvillea spinosa f. microbracteata, commonly known as the dwarf bougainvillea, is a compact, flowering plant native to Brazil.

It features vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of red, pink, and orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has small, green, ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It is a compact shrub, typically growing up to 1.5 meters in height, making it ideal for container gardening or small spaces.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
