Banksia drummondii subsp. hiemalis

Banksia drummondii subsp.

hiemalis, commonly known as the southern banksia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flower spikes.

The flowers are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is typically found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

It has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is resilient to drought conditions.
