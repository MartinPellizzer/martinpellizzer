Bauhinia posthumi

Bauhinia posthumi, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in late spring or early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are borne on short peduncles. Bauhinia posthumi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
