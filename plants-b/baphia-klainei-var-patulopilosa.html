Baphia klainei var. patulopilosa

Baphia klainei var. patulopilosa, commonly known as the woolly baphia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
