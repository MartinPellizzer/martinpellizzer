Bellardiochloa polychroa

Bellardiochloa polychroa, commonly known as the variable barleygrass, is a small annual grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
