Berlandiera betonicifolia

Berlandiera betonicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, branched stems that often grow in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
