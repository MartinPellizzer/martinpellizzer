Biscutella columnae f. leiocarpa

Biscutella columnae f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered biscutella, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the biscutella genus.

It is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological characteristics and limited distribution.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
