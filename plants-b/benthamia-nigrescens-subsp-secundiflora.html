Benthamia nigrescens subsp. secundiflora

Benthamia nigrescens subsp.

secundiflora, commonly known as the black-flowered benthamia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy blossoms that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is notable for its unique coloration and adaptation to harsh, dry conditions.
