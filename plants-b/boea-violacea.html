Boea violacea

Boea violacea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often grown as a ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
