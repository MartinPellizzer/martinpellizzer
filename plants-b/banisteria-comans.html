Banisteria comans

Banisteria comans, commonly known as the red spider flower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spiders, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its long, trailing stems.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
