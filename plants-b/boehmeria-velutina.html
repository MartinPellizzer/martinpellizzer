Boehmeria velutina

Boehmeria velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf hemp.

It is a flowering plant in the nettle family, Urticaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems have been used in traditional crafts and textiles in some cultures.
