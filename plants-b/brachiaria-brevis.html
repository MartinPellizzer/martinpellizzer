Brachiaria brevis

Brachiaria brevis, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, erect stems and broad, flat leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Brachiaria brevis is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
