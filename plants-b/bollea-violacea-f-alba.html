Bollea violacea f. alba

Bollea violacea f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the violet bellflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant characterized by its pale violet to white flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five lobes, and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
