Bidens polylepis var. typica

Bidens polylepis var. typica, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
