Baccharis vaccinioides

Baccharis vaccinioides, commonly known as black sage, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Baccharis vaccinioides plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
