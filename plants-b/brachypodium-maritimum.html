Brachypodium maritimum

Brachypodium maritimum, commonly known as sea barley, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small annual grass that thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
