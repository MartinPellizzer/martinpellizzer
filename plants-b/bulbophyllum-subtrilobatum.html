Bulbophyllum subtrilobatum

Bulbophyllum subtrilobatum, commonly known as the Three-lobed Pterostylis, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with three distinct lobes that give it its common name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
