Borzicactus xylorhizus

Borzicactus xylorhizus, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, where it forms dense clusters. Its spiny areoles are adorned with white to yellowish bristles, providing protection from herbivores.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is appreciated for its unique appearance and adaptability to extreme conditions.
