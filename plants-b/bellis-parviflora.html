Bellis parviflora

Bellis parviflora, commonly known as the lawn daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features a rosette of silver- or gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Bellis parviflora is also known for its hardiness and ability to spread via stolons, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
