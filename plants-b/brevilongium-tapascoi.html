Brevilongium tapascoi

Brevilongium tapascoi, commonly known as the Texas mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale blue and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brevilongium tapascoi is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
