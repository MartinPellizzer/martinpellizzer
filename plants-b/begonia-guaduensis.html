Begonia guaduensis

Begonia guaduensis, commonly known as the Guadu begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, ornamental foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
