Bupleurum ranunculoides var. lapeyrousianum

Bupleurum ranunculoides var. lapeyrousianum, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a fibrous root system and hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
