Bactris nancibaensis

Bactris nancibaensis, commonly known as the Guianese palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange inflorescences, which bear small, white flowers, and its fruits are drupes that are typically eaten by various animals.

Bactris nancibaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices in its native regions.
