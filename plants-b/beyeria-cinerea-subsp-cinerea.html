Beyeria cinerea subsp. cinerea

Beyeria cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Beyeria cinerea subsp.

cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
