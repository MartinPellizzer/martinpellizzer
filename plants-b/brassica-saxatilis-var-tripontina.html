Brassica saxatilis var. tripontina

Brassica saxatilis var. tripontina, commonly known as the rock cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dark green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
