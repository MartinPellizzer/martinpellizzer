Bromus loliaceus

Bromus loliaceus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Bromus loliaceus is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.
