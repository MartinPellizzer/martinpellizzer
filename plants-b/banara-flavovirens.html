Banara flavovirens

Banara flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green banara, is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green leaves, which are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds.

Banara flavovirens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
