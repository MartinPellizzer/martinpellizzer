Bouchea namaquana var. latifolia

Bouchea namaquana var. latifolia, commonly known as the Namibian bouchea, is a flowering plant native to the Namib Desert.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
