Buddleja floccosa var. crassifolia

Buddleja floccosa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the woolly buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and long blooming period.
