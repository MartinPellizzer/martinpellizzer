Buddleja × alata

Buddleja × alata, commonly known as the hybrid weeping buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Buddleja × alata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of 3 to 6 feet.
