Bupleurum boissieuanum

Bupleurum boissieuanum, commonly known as the Armenian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Bupleurum boissieuanum thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
