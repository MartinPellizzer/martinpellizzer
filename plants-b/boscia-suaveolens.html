Boscia suaveolens

Boscia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Boscia suaveolens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
