Borzicactus plagiostoma

Borzicactus plagiostoma, commonly known as the flat-armed cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptations include succulent stems for water storage and a shallow root system to absorb moisture from the soil.

Borzicactus plagiostoma is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
