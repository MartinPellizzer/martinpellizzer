Bignonia uliginosa

Bignonia uliginosa, commonly known as yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous, climbing vine that produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in wet or moist habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

The plant is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
