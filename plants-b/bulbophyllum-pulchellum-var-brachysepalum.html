Bulbophyllum pulchellum var. brachysepalum

Bulbophyllum pulchellum var. brachysepalum, commonly known as the short-sepal Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, colorful flowers with short, fleshy sepals. The plant typically features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
