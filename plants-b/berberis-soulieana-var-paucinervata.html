Berberis soulieana var. paucinervata

Berberis soulieana var. paucinervata, commonly known as the narrow-veined barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow venation pattern. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage color and seasonal changes, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
