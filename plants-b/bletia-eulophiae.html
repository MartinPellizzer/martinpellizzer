Bletia eulophiae

Bletia eulophiae, commonly known as the spotted bletia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive purplish or reddish spots. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Bletia eulophiae thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

It is a relatively low-maintenance orchid that is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value.
