Brassica teretifolia

Brassica teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces long, thin pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Brassica teretifolia is also valued for its potential as a forage crop and in ecological restoration projects.
