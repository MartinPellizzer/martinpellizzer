Brasiliorchis steyermarkii

Brasiliorchis steyermarkii, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a delicate, slender stem and a single, small, greenish-white flower. The plant has a short flowering period, typically occurring in the spring, and is characterized by its specialized root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its study contributes to the understanding of orchid diversity and ecological adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
