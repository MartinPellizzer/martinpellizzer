Barleria affinis subsp. affinis

Barleria affinis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-gray underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
