Bauhinia aegopoda

Bauhinia aegopoda, commonly known as the false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
