Blossfeldia fechseri

Blossfeldia fechseri, commonly known as the Schomburgkia, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The plant's pseudobulbs store water, allowing it to survive dry periods.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by experienced orchid growers.
