Bromus longe-aristatus

Bromus longe-aristatus, commonly known as the long-awned brome, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, bristle-like awns that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of local ecosystems.
