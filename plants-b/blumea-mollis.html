Blumea mollis

Blumea mollis, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Blumea mollis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
