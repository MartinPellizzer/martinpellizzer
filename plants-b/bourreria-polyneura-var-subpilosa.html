Bourreria polyneura var. subpilosa

Bourreria polyneura var. subpilosa, commonly known as the silky burr, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, spiny fruits that mature to a dark purple color. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

The silky burr is known for its ability to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
