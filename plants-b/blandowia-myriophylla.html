Blandowia myriophylla

Blandowia myriophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blanea, is a small shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Blandowia myriophylla typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators.
