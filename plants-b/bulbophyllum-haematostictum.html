Bulbophyllum haematostictum

Bulbophyllum haematostictum, commonly known as the blood-stained orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color. Its bulb-like structures, known as pseudobulbs, store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in less favorable conditions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
