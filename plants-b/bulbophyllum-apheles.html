Bulbophyllum apheles

Bulbophyllum apheles, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
