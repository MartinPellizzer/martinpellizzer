Bursaria lasiophylla

Bursaria lasiophylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved bursaria, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, often prickly foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Bursaria lasiophylla is an important component of coastal vegetation, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
