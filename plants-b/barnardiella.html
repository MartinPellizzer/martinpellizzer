Barnardiella

Barnardiella, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange tubular flowers that resemble small bells, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Barnardiella thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
