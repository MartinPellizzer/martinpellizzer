Batatas incurva

Batatas incurva, commonly known as the curling batata, is a species of tuberous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit involves climbing or trailing vines that can reach several meters in length.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique leaf morphology and adaptability to various soil conditions.
