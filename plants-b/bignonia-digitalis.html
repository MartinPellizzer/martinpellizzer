Bignonia digitalis

Bignonia digitalis, commonly known as crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens as a climbing vine due to its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Bignonia digitalis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
