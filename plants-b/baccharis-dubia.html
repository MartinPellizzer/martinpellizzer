Baccharis dubia

Baccharis dubia, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in non-native regions.
