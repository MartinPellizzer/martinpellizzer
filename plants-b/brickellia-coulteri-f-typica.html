Brickellia coulteri f. typica

Brickellia coulteri f. typica, commonly known as Coulter's brickellia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in limestone habitats and is considered a key species in xeric ecosystems.

The variety f. typica is distinguished by its typical foliage and floral structure compared to other forms of the species.
