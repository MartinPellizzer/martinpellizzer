Bixa acuminata

Bixa acuminata, commonly known as the "bixa" or "false aloe," is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and bright orange-red seeds that are highly valued for their pigments. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties. Its seeds contain a natural dye called bixin, which is used in food coloring and cosmetics.

Bixa acuminata thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.
