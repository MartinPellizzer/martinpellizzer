Bumelia arborea

Bumelia arborea, commonly known as the white gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to reddish seed pods. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
