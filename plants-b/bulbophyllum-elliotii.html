Bulbophyllum elliotii

Bulbophyllum elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers are usually borne in clusters and can vary in color from white to pink and yellow.
