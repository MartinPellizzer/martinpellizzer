Bauhinia conwayi

Bauhinia conwayi, commonly known as the velvetwood or purple bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom during the wet season. Bauhinia conwayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
