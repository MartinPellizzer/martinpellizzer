Batrachium trichophyllum var. hirtellum

Batrachium trichophyllum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy bladder fern, is a variant of the bladder fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in small, bladder-like capsules. The leaves are typically broad and have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its common name. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
