Brunia angulata

Brunia angulata, commonly known as the angular brunia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
