Boerhavia bicolor

Boerhavia bicolor, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking pink or purple flowers that open in the morning and close by late afternoon. Boerhavia bicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, often forming dense mats in its native habitat.
