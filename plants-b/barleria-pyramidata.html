Barleria pyramidata

Barleria pyramidata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

Barleria pyramidata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
