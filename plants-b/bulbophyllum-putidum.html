Bulbophyllum putidum

Bulbophyllum putidum, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often hairy or glandular leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors ranging from white to yellow and pink.

Bulbophyllum putidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
