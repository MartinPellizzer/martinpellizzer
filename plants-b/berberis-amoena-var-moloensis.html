Berberis amoena var. moloensis

Berberis amoena var. moloensis, commonly known as the Moloensis barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly found in regions of China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of berberine in its roots and stems.
