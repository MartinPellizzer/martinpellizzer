Bacopa diffusa

Bacopa diffusa, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Bacopa diffusa thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks, and is often found in shallow water.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
