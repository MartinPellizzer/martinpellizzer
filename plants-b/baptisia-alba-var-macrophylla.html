Baptisia alba var. macrophylla

Baptisia alba var. macrophylla, commonly known as white false indigo, is a North American native perennial flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, cylindrical seedpods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Baptisia alba var. macrophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
