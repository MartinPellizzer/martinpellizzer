Begonia everettii

Begonia everettii, commonly known as the Everett's begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Begonia everettii is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
