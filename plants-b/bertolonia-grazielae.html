Bertolonia grazielae

Bertolonia grazielae, commonly known as the Graceful Bertolonia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact shrub form with aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
