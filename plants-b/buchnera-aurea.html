Buchnera aurea

Buchnera aurea, commonly known as the golden buchnera, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Buchnera aurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
