Bidens serboana

Bidens serboana, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Bidens serboana is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in some regions.
