Barleria acanthophora

Barleria acanthophora, commonly known as the spiked barleria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Barleria acanthophora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
