Bidens parvulifolia

Bidens parvulifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces seeds with fine, bristly hairs that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Bidens parvulifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
