Bactris jamaicana

Bactris jamaicana, commonly known as the Jamaican palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescences produce small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by edible, oval fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
