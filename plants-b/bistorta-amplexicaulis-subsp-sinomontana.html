Bistorta amplexicaulis subsp. sinomontana

Bistorta amplexicaulis subsp.

sinomontana, commonly known as the Chinese bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
