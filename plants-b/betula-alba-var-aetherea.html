Betula alba var. aetherea

Betula alba var. aetherea, commonly known as the silver birch variant, is a less commonly documented subspecies of the white birch.

It is characterized by its slender, pale bark that often displays a distinctive flaky texture. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a light green upper surface. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity, it is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists.
