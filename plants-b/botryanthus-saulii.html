Botryanthus saulii

Botryanthus saulii, commonly known as the Saul's clusterleaf, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle beauty to its otherwise simple appearance. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Botryanthus saulii is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
