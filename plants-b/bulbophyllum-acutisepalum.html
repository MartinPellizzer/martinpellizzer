Bulbophyllum acutisepalum

Bulbophyllum acutisepalum, commonly known as the sharp-sepal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing a cluster of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette, facilitating efficient photosynthesis.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its resilience in various growing conditions.
