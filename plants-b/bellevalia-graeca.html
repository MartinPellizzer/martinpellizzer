Bellevalia graeca

Bellevalia graeca, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Bellevalia graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
