Boehmeria longispica var. platanifolia

Boehmeria longispica var. platanifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins and a hairy underside. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its fibrous stems are valued for their potential in textile and paper industries.
