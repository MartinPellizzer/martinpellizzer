Bothriocline moramballae

Bothriocline moramballae, commonly known as the Moramballa thistle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy stems and reduced leaves to minimize water loss. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
