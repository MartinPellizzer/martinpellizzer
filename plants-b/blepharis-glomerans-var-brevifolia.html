Blepharis glomerans var. brevifolia

Blepharis glomerans var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf wiregrass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, sparsely arranged leaves. The plant produces clusters of slender, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to blue in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
