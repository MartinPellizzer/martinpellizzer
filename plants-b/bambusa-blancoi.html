Bambusa blancoi

Bambusa blancoi, commonly known as the white bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green to almost white culms that distinguish it from other bamboo species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture.

Bambusa blancoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
