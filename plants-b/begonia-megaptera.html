Begonia megaptera

Begonia megaptera, commonly known as the giant leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia megaptera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
