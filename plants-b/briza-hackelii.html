Briza hackelii

Briza hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Briza hackelii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
