Basileophyta

Basileophyta, commonly known as the "basil-like plants," is a group of flowering plants characterized by their aromatic foliage and unique chemical composition.

These plants typically have small, clustered flowers and are often found in temperate regions. They are known for their essential oils, which contribute to their distinctive scent and various medicinal properties. Basileophyta species are widely cultivated for culinary, aromatic, and therapeutic uses.

Their botanical significance lies in their role in traditional medicine and their adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
