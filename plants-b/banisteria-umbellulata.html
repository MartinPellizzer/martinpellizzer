Banisteria umbellulata

Banisteria umbellulata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange tubular flowers that appear in clusters, resembling coral, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Banisteria umbellulata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
