Bignonia chica

Bignonia chica, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a vigorous climber with slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated edge.

Bignonia chica thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy blooms and rapid growth.
