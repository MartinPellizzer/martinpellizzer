Biscutella ramosissima

Biscutella ramosissima, commonly known as the branched biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the western Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a compact, branching structure with hairy stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves.

Biscutella ramosissima is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
