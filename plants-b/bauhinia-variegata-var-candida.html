Bauhinia variegata var. candida

Bauhinia variegata var. candida, commonly known as the white-leaf bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often white or pale green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
