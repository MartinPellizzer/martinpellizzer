Begonia valdensium var. angustior

Begonia valdensium var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often arranged in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and blooms.
