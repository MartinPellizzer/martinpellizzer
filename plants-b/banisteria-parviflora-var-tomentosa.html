Banisteria parviflora var. tomentosa

Banisteria parviflora var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silky banisteria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruit capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
