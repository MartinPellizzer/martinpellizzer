Banisteria cinerascens var. glabrescens

Banisteria cinerascens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety bark and pinnately compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
