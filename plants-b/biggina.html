Biggina

Biggina, commonly known as the Indian broom or sweet broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Biggina is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
