Blyxa angustipetala

Blyxa angustipetala, commonly known as narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, typically ranging from 2 to 8 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Blyxa angustipetala plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
