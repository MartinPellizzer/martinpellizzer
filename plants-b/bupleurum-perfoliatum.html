Bupleurum perfoliatum

Bupleurum perfoliatum, commonly known as Indian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the United States and parts of Canada.

It features slender, branching stems that often bear opposite, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Bupleurum perfoliatum thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
