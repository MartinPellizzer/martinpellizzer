Brassavola nodosa

Brassavola nodosa, commonly known as the nodding lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a dense, mat-forming growth habit with pale green to brownish stems that often have a nodding appearance. The plant produces a single, elongated spike of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like, linear, and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Brassavola nodosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to moist, shaded environments in woodlands.
