Bignonia africana

Bignonia africana, commonly known as the African crossvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture.

Bignonia africana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its rapid growth and striking floral display.
