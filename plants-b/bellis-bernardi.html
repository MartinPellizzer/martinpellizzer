Bellis bernardi

Bellis bernardi, commonly known as the alpine daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with dark centers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to alpine meadows.

Bellis bernardi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
