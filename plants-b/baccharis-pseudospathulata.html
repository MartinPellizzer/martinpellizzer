Baccharis pseudospathulata

Baccharis pseudospathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family.

Baccharis pseudospathulata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.
