Bauera rubioides var. microphylla

Bauera rubioides var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bauera, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and used in native landscaping.
