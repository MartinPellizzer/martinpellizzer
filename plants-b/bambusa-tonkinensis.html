Bambusa tonkinensis

Bambusa tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Bambusa tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in construction and as a source of fiber.
