Banksia conferta

Banksia conferta, commonly known as the broad-leaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of many yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Banksia conferta plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for native wildlife.
