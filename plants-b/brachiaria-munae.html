Brachiaria munae

Brachiaria munae, commonly known as the coastal grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. This plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. Brachiaria munae plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

Its adaptability to saline and arid conditions makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.
