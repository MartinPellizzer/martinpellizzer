Bignonia viridiflora

Bignonia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces long, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Bignonia viridiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
