Brachystephanus montifuga

Brachystephanus montifuga, commonly known as the mountain stephanus, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow to orange in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
