Brassicella valentina var. granatensis

Brassicella valentina var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Wallflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, semi-double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a short, branched stem, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
