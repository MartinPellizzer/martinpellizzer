Brizopyrum acutiflorum

Brizopyrum acutiflorum, commonly known as the sharpflower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish or purplish in color.

Brizopyrum acutiflorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
