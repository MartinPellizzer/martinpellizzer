Boquila discolor

Boquila discolor, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by blue or purple berries. Boquila discolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
