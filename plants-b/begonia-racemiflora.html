Begonia racemiflora

Begonia racemiflora, commonly known as the clustered begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces bright red or pink flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
