Biarum abbreviatum

Biarum abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf Dutchman's breeches, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique, kidney-shaped leaves and showy, white, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in moist, rich soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

Biarum abbreviatum is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
