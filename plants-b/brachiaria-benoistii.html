Brachiaria benoistii

Brachiaria benoistii, commonly known as the Benoist's grass, is a species of grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
