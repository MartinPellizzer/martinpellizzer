Berberis thunbergii f. grandiflora

Berberis thunbergii f. grandiflora, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features upright, arching branches that bear small, glossy green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
