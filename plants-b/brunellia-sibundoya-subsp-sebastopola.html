Brunellia sibundoya subsp. sebastopola

Brunellia sibundoya subsp.

sebastopola, commonly known as the Sebastopol broadleaf, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
