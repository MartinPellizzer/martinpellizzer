Byrsonima crassifolia var. lanceolata

Byrsonima crassifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitahaya, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is rich in nutrients.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
