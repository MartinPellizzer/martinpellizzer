Browallia cordata

Browallia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf browallia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Browallia cordata is relatively easy to grow and is suitable for both garden beds and container gardening.
