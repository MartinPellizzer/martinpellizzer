Bulbophyllum subcubicum

Bulbophyllum subcubicum, commonly known as the Cubic Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often hairy pseudobulbs and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
