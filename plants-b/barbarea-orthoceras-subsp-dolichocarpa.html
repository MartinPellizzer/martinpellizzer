Barbarea orthoceras subsp. dolichocarpa

Barbarea orthoceras subsp.

dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-seed rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, cold climates.
