Balsamina hirsuta

Balsamina hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Balsamina hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
