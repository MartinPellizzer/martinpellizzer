Buchnera tetragona

Buchnera tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled buchnera, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-sided stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Buchnera tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
