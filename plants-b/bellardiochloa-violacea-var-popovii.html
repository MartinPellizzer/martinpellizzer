Bellardiochloa violacea var. popovii

Bellardiochloa violacea var. popovii, commonly known as the Popov's violet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with violet-colored flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
