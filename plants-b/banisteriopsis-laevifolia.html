Banisteriopsis laevifolia

Banisteriopsis laevifolia, commonly known as the "red bark tree," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

Banisteriopsis laevifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
