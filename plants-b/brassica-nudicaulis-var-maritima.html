Brassica nudicaulis var. maritima

Brassica nudicaulis var. maritima, commonly known as the sea kale, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and sandy environments.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with hairy stems and alternate, narrow leaves. It thrives in maritime climates and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Its ability to tolerate salt spray and poor soil conditions makes it an important pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.
