Bulbophyllum sordidum

Bulbophyllum sordidum, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a spike. Its leaves are ovate and have a leathery texture, adapted to conserve moisture.

Bulbophyllum sordidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
