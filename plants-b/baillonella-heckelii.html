Baillonella heckelii

Baillonella heckelii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive, bladder-like seed pods. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Baillonella heckelii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
