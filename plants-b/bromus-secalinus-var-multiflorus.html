Bromus secalinus var. multiflorus

Bromus secalinus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the yellow brome, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often yellow or golden in color. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, which contribute to its common name "multiflorus.".
