Baccharis pascensis

Baccharis pascensis, commonly known as goldenrod baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and pond edges. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flower clusters that appear in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Baccharis pascensis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
