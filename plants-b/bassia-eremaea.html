Bassia eremaea

Bassia eremaea, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its succulent leaves are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor, making it a valuable food source in harsh conditions.

The species is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
