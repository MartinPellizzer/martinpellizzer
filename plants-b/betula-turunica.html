Betula turunica

Betula turunica, commonly known as the Turunca birch, is a species of deciduous tree native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish layer beneath. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and turn yellow in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
