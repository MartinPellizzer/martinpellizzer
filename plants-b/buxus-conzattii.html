Buxus conzattii

Buxus conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive, rounded growth habit. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and tightly packed, giving it a neat appearance. It is often cultivated as a hedge or ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate pruning.

Buxus conzattii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
