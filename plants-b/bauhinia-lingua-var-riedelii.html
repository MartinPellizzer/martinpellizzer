Bauhinia lingua var. riedelii

Bauhinia lingua var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's bauhinia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble hands. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
