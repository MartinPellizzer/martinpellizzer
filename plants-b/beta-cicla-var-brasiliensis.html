Beta cicla var. brasiliensis

Beta cicla var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beet or Brazilian chard, is a variant of the beetroot species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves that range from deep red to purple and green. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to other leafy greens such as spinach and Swiss chard. Its leaves are edible and often used in traditional Brazilian cuisine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
