Bauhinia confusa

Bauhinia confusa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Bauhinia confusa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
