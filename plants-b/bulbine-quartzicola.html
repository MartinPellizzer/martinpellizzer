Bulbine quartzicola

Bulbine quartzicola, commonly known as the quartz rockbulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and maintenance.
