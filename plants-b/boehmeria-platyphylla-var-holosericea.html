Boehmeria platyphylla var. holosericea

Boehmeria platyphylla var. holosericea, commonly known as the silky or velvet flax, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silken leaves that are often used in traditional textile production. The plant belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is known for its fibrous stem, which is harvested for its strong, durable fibers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf morphology and fibrous structure make it an important plant in both ecological and economic contexts.
