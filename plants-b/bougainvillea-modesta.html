Bougainvillea modesta

Bougainvillea modesta, commonly known as the pink fairy, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of pink, red, and white, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, true flowers inside the bracts, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

Bougainvillea modesta is known for its ability to grow on trellises or as a shrub, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
