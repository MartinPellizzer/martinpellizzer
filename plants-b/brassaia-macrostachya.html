Brassaia macrostachya

Brassaia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous bright red or orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
