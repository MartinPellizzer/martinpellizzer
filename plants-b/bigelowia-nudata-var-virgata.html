Bigelowia nudata var. virgata

Bigelowia nudata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's glory, is a striking flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit with dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
