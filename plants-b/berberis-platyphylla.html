Berberis platyphylla

Berberis platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, which are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Berberis platyphylla is also sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
