Boehmeria densiglomerata

Boehmeria densiglomerata, commonly known as Chinese hemp or Malabar hemp, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its fibrous, soft stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense groups. It is known for its strong, durable fibers, which have been traditionally used for making textiles and paper.

Its growth habit is typically as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments.
