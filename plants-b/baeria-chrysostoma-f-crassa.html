Baeria chrysostoma f. crassa

Baeria chrysostoma f. crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaved golden saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, and thick leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
