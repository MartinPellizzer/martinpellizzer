Borzicactus aureispinus

Borzicactus aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spine cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its adaptations include a thick cuticle and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Borzicactus aureispinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
