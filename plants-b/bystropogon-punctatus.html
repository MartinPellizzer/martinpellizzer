Bystropogon punctatus

Bystropogon punctatus, commonly known as the speckled spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem covered in small, yellowish-green tubercles. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, which help to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Bystropogon punctatus is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
