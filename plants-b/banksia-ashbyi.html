Banksia ashbyi

Banksia ashbyi, commonly known as the Ashby's banksia, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy soils within the Swan Coastal Plain. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are yellow to orange in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Banksia ashbyi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
