Berberis simonsii

Berberis simonsii, commonly known as the California barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant in gardens.
