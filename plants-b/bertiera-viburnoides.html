Bertiera viburnoides

Bertiera viburnoides, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a snowball-like appearance. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to both full sun and partial shade.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
