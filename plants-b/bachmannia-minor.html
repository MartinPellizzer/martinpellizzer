Bachmannia minor

Bachmannia minor, commonly known as the small-bloom grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
