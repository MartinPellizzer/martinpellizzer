Brachysiphon petraeus

Brachysiphon petraeus, commonly known as the rock siphon, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its root system is adapted to anchor it in rocky soil and access moisture from deep underground.

Brachysiphon petraeus is often found in rocky crevices and is well-suited for xerophytic environments.
