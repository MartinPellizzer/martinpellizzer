Bromus hookeri

Bromus hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as the Andes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bromus hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
