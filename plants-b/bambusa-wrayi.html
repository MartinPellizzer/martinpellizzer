Bambusa wrayi

Bambusa wrayi, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its lightweight yet strong stalks, which are used in construction and crafts.
