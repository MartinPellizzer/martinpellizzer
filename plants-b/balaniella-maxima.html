Balaniella maxima

Balaniella maxima, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the wetlands of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The flowers are typically greenish-white with a distinctive yellow lip, and they bloom during the late summer and early autumn.

Balaniella maxima thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.
