Brachypodium laxum

Brachypodium laxum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, two-lobed flowers in early summer.

Brachypodium laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
