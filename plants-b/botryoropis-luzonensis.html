Botryoropis luzonensis

Botryoropis luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon lichen, is a unique lichen species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinct, granular texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and soil in tropical rainforests.

This species plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of environmental health in its ecosystem.
