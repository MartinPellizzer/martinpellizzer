Brassica boissieri

Brassica boissieri, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant features erect stems with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Brassica boissieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
