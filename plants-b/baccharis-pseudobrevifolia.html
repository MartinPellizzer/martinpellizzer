Baccharis pseudobrevifolia

Baccharis pseudobrevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf Baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the dry season.

Baccharis pseudobrevifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for local wildlife.
