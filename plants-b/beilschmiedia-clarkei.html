Beilschmiedia clarkei

Beilschmiedia clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
