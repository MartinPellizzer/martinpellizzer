Bertholletia excelsa

Bertholletia excelsa, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, round leaves and produces distinctive, woody capsules that contain up to 20 seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The tree has a deep root system and can grow up to 50 meters in height, making it one of the tallest trees in the region. Its flowers are small and yellow, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The Brazil nut tree plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
