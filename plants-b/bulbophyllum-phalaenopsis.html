Bulbophyllum phalaenopsis

Bulbophyllum phalaenopsis, commonly known as the orchid mantis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles the body of a praying mantis, a feature that aids in its camouflage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

This epiphytic orchid is popular among collectors for its striking appearance and unusual morphology.
