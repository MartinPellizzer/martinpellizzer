Balsamodendrum ehrenbergianum

Balsamodendrum ehrenbergianum, commonly known as the desert balsam, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
