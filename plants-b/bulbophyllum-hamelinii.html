Bulbophyllum hamelinii

Bulbophyllum hamelinii, commonly known as the Hamelin's orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the genus Bulbophyllum in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, green, and red. It typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, and its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum hamelinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
