Bromelia arvensis

Bromelia arvensis, commonly known as the field bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding reservoir. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers, which are usually red or orange, emerge from the leaf rosette and are clustered in a spike.

Bromelia arvensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
