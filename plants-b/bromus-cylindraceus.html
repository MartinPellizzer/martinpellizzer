Bromus cylindraceus

Bromus cylindraceus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

Bromus cylindraceus is an important forage plant for livestock and also plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
