Bidaria tingens

Bidaria tingens, commonly known as the purple bracted grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in the late spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Bidaria tingens is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
