Buxus sempervirens f. pendula

Buxus sempervirens f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping boxwood, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features dense, dark green foliage that cascades in a fountain-like manner, creating an elegant, ornamental appearance. The leaves are small, oval, and have a smooth, waxy texture, contributing to its refined look. This variety is particularly valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its decorative appeal and low maintenance requirements.
