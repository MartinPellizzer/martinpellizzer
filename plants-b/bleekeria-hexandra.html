Bleekeria hexandra

Bleekeria hexandra, commonly known as the Natal buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that bear the flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of ray florets and disk florets.

Bleekeria hexandra is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
