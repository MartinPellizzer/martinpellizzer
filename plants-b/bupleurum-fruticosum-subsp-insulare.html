Bupleurum fruticosum subsp. insulare

Bupleurum fruticosum subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the island bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
