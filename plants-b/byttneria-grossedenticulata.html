Byttneria grossedenticulata

Byttneria grossedenticulata, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, with a prominent yellow stamen. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
