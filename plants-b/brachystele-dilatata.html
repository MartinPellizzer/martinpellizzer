Brachystele dilatata

Brachystele dilatata, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies or in humid forests. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mild, sweet scent, which contributes to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Brachystele dilatata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
