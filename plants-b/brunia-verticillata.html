Brunia verticillata

Brunia verticillata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a trailing or shrub-like growth habit, with opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its botanical features include small, ovate leaves and a woody stem that can become more branched over time.

Brunia verticillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
