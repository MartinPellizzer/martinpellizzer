Bulbophyllum aristilabre

Bulbophyllum aristilabre, commonly known as the aristate bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
