Baphia laurentii

Baphia laurentii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and bipinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and it also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
