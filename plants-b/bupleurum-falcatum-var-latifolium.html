Bupleurum falcatum var. latifolium

Bupleurum falcatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
