Barbarea cochlearifolia

Barbarea cochlearifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf honesty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a leaf vegetable in some cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Barbarea cochlearifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate shade, making it a common sight in shaded garden environments.
