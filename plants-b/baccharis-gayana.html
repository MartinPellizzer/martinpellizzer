Baccharis gayana

Baccharis gayana, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open areas, such as savannas and forest edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in clusters.

Baccharis gayana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
