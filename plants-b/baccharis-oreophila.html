Baccharis oreophila

Baccharis oreophila, commonly known as mountain baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Baccharis oreophila is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
