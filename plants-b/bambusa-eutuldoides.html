Bambusa eutuldoides

Bambusa eutuldoides, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo varieties. The plant forms dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it suitable for ornamental and structural purposes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Bambusa eutuldoides is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
