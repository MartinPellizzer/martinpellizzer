Brachiaria bequaertii

Brachiaria bequaertii, commonly known as Brazilian signalgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Brachiaria bequaertii is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for forage and erosion control.
