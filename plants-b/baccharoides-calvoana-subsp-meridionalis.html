Baccharoides calvoana subsp. meridionalis

Baccharoides calvoana subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern baccharoides, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
