Balanites pedicellaris

Balanites pedicellaris, commonly known as the African date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a thick, spiny husk. The seeds inside are edible and can be roasted or boiled, while the plant also has traditional medicinal uses.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a valuable species in dryland ecosystems.
