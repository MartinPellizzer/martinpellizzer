Bauhinia cuyabensis var. ferruginea

Bauhinia cuyabensis var. ferruginea, commonly known as the red-veined bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnate leaves with reddish veins and showy, pea-like flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value is notable, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
