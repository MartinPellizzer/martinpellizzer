Balanocarpus brachypterus

Balanocarpus brachypterus, commonly known as the short-winged ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its durable timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
