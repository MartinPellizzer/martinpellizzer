Bracisepalum

Bracisepalum, commonly known as the false spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to various regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Bracisepalum species typically grow in humid, tropical environments and are often found in shaded forest understories.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
