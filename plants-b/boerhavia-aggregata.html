Boerhavia aggregata

Boerhavia aggregata, commonly known as redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pink to reddish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
