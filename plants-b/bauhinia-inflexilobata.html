Bauhinia inflexilobata

Bauhinia inflexilobata, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves that are often pink or reddish in color. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters. Bauhinia inflexilobata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
