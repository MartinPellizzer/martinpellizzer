Betula pendula var. fontqueri

Betula pendula var. fontqueri, commonly known as the silver birch variant Fontqueri, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a silver hue beneath, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring. The leaves are triangular with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in woodland or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
