Biscutella sempervirens subsp. glacialis

Biscutella sempervirens subsp.

glacialis, commonly known as the alpine biscuitweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in arctic and alpine habitats.
