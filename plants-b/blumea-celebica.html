Blumea celebica

Blumea celebica, commonly known as Java Bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branching.

Blumea celebica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive floral display.
