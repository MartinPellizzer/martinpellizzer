Bambusa solomonensis

Bambusa solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the Solomon Islands.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, triangular seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native region.
