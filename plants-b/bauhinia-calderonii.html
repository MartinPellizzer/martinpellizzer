Bauhinia calderonii

Bauhinia calderonii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like pink flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Bauhinia calderonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
