Bouchea sphenophylla

Bouchea sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bouchea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that surround clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish hair.

Bouchea sphenophylla is a perennial shrub that blooms in spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to desert landscapes.
