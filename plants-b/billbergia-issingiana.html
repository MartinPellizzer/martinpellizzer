Billbergia issingiana

Billbergia issingiana, commonly known as the silver billbergia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Billbergia issingiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
