Begonia bidentata

Begonia bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens. It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its growth habit includes trailing stems, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
