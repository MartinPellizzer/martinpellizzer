Bidens pseudocosmos

Bidens pseudocosmos, commonly known as false cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces yellow flowers with white markings, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
