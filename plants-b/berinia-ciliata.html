Berinia ciliata

Berinia ciliata, commonly known as the silky berinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and are arranged in clusters.

Berinia ciliata is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to dry conditions.
