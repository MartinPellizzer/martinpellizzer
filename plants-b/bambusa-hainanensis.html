Bambusa hainanensis

Bambusa hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan bamboo, is a species native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It is a tall, fast-growing grass that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Bambusa hainanensis is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it a popular choice for traditional and modern applications.
