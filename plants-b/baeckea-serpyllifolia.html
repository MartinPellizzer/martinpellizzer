Baeckea serpyllifolia

Baeckea serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common baeckea, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is a hardy plant that can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
