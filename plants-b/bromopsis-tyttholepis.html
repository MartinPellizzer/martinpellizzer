Bromopsis tyttholepis

Bromopsis tyttholepis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Bromopsis tyttholepis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
