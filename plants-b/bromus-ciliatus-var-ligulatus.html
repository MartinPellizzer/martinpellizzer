Bromus ciliatus var. ligulatus

Bromus ciliatus var. ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive ciliate margin. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and ecological settings.
