Brassica oleracea subvar. ligustica

Brassica oleracea subvar. ligustica, commonly known as the purple sprouting broccoli, is a cool-season crop characterized by its compact, cabbage-like head and purple-tinged flower clusters.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces edible shoots and florets that are typically harvested in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is resistant to some common pests and diseases.

Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves and a short, stocky stem.
