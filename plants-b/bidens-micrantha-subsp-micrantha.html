Bidens micrantha subsp. micrantha

Bidens micrantha subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggar's ticks, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces small yellow flowers with five petals, followed by hairy, burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woods, roadsides, and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
