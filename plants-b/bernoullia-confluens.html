Bernoullia confluens

Bernoullia confluens, commonly known as the slender-flowered lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, bell-shaped blooms that appear in late spring.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
