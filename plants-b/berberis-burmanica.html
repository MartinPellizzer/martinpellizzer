Berberis burmanica

Berberis burmanica, commonly known as Burman barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage, which turns shades of orange and red.
