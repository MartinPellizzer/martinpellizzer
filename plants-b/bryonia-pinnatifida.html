Bryonia pinnatifida

Bryonia pinnatifida, commonly known as the common bryony, is a flowering plant in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into rough, spiny fruits. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

Bryonia pinnatifida has been used historically in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds.
