Brassica rapa var. dichotoma

Brassica rapa var. dichotoma, commonly known as the wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing round seeds. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is a biennial or annual plant, depending on the climate, and has been used historically as a food source and for medicinal purposes.
