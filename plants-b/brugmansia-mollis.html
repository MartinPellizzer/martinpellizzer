Brugmansia mollis

Brugmansia mollis, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces white, pink, or yellow flowers, which are highly fragrant, especially in the evening. Its leaves are dark green, broad, and often have a velvety texture.

Brugmansia mollis is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes, though it contains toxic alkaloids.
