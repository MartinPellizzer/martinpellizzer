Bromus humilis

Bromus humilis, commonly known as the common brome, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open fields, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Bromus humilis is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
