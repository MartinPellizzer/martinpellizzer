Blumea crassifolia

Blumea crassifolia, commonly known as broad-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes aromatic scent. It produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits.

Blumea crassifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
