Baumea loculata

Baumea loculata, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Baumea loculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation projects.
