Bupleurum candollei var. candollei

Bupleurum candollei var. candollei, commonly known as the Tibetan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
