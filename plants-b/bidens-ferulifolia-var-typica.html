Bidens ferulifolia var. typica

Bidens ferulifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, a characteristic feature of the genus Bidens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
