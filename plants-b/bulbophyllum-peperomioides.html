Bulbophyllum peperomioides

Bulbophyllum peperomioides, commonly known as the Peperomia-like orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and ornamental foliage.
