Berberis crataegina var. armeniaca

Berberis crataegina var. armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian barberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a hedge plant.
