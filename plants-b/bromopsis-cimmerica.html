Bromopsis cimmerica

Bromopsis cimmerica, commonly known as the Cimmerica grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Bromopsis cimmerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
