Butea philippinensis

Butea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often planted for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
