Bambusa concava

Bambusa concava, commonly known as the concave bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa concava is a clump-forming bamboo that grows relatively quickly, making it a popular choice for ornamental and practical purposes.
