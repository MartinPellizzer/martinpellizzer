Blitum virgatum var. chenopodioides

Blitum virgatum var. chenopodioides, commonly known as silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments and a valuable component of native plant communities.
