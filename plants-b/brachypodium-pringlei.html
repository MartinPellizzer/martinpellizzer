Brachypodium pringlei

Brachypodium pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Brachypodium pringlei is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for study in plant genetics and evolution.
