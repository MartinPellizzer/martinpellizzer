Barnadesia vestita

Barnadesia vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
