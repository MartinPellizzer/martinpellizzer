Berberis wallichiana var. gracilipes

Berberis wallichiana var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features upright, slender stems that are often branched and covered with small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but generally not palatable. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
