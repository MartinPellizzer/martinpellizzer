Bellium cordifolium

Bellium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
