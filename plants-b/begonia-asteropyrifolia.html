Begonia asteropyrifolia

Begonia asteropyrifolia, commonly known as the starry begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
