Bupleurum longifolium proles vapincense

Bupleurum longifolium proles vapincense, commonly known as the fragrant longleaf bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are long, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal use, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory properties.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
