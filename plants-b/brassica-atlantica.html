Brassica atlantica

Brassica atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic mustard, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines, adapting to saline and windy conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Brassica atlantica is known for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects along coastal areas.
