Bashania aristata

Bashania aristata, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Bashania aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
