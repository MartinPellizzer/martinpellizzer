Broussonetia papyrifera var. leucocarpa

Broussonetia papyrifera var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited paper mulberry, is a variant of the paper mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green bark and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, white, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fibrous bark.

This variety is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
