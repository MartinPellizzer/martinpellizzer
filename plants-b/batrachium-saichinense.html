Batrachium saichinense

Batrachium saichinense, commonly known as the Saichin fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping, and the fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

Batrachium saichinense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
