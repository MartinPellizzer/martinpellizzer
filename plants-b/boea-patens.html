Boea patens

Boea patens, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a silvery sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Boea patens is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls effectively.
