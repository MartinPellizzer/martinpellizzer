Bulbophyllum maculiflorum

Bulbophyllum maculiflorum, commonly known as the spotted-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers with a complex pattern of colors and markings. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in the biodiversity of tropical rainforests.
