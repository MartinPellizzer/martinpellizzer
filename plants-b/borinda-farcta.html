Borinda farcta

Borinda farcta, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, crimson-colored flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Borinda farcta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
