Baccharis densiflora

Baccharis densiflora, commonly known as denseflower baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and grasslands.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
