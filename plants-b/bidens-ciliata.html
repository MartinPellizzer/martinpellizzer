Bidens ciliata

Bidens ciliata, commonly known as the fringed beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has hairy, branching stems and small, opposite leaves with finely toothed edges. Its flowers are typically yellow with dark centers, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by attachment to animals.

Bidens ciliata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
