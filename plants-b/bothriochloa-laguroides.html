Bothriochloa laguroides

Bothriochloa laguroides, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Bothriochloa laguroides is an important forage plant for livestock and also has some ecological significance in its native habitats.
