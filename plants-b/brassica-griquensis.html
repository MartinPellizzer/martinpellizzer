Brassica griquensis

Brassica griquensis, commonly known as the Grootfontein mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Brassica griquensis is adapted to arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid regions.
