Botryanthus paradoxus

Botryanthus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical bottlebrush, is a unique flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are composed of dense clusters of red or orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often overlooked due to the plant's dramatic floral display.

Botryanthus paradoxus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
