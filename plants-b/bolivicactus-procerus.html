Bolivicactus procerus

Bolivicactus procerus, commonly known as the Andean torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the high altitudes of the Andes in Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to harsh, arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Bolivicactus procerus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky, well-drained soils.
