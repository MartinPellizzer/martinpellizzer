Banksia ionthocarpa subsp. ionthocarpa

Banksia ionthocarpa subsp.

ionthocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This subspecies is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
