Basella cananifolia

Basella cananifolia, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and nutritional value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
