Begonia poikilantha

Begonia poikilantha, commonly known as the variegated begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces clusters of small, fuchsia-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
