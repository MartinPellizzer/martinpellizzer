Brickellia brachiata var. typica

Brickellia brachiata var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive grayish-green color, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
