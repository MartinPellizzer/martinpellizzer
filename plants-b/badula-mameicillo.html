Badula mameicillo

Badula mameicillo, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage that ranges from green to orange and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by heart-shaped, edible tuberous roots. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious food.

Badula mameicillo is also valued for its ability to improve soil quality through its extensive root system.
