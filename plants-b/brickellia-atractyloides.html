Brickellia atractyloides

Brickellia atractyloides, commonly known as narrowleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
