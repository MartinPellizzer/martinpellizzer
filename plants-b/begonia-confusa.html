Begonia confusa

Begonia confusa, commonly known as the cloud begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often marked with intricate patterns and a deep, rich green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
