Baccharis sarothroides

Baccharis sarothroides, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Baccharis sarothroides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
