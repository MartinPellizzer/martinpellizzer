Blechnum discolor var. normale

Blechnum discolor var. normale, commonly known as the variegated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Blechnum discolor species.

It is a fern that belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The variegation in its foliage adds to its ornamental value in horticulture.
