Borreria tenella var. suffruticosa

Borreria tenella var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a shrub-like plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
