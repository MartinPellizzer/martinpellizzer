Bouchea spathulata

Bouchea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bouchea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and thick, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Bouchea spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
