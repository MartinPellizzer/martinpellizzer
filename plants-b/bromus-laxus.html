Bromus laxus

Bromus laxus, commonly known as the loose barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. Its inflorescence is a loose panicle, which is characteristic of the Bromus genus.

Bromus laxus is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
