Barberina lanceolata

Barberina lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green in the growing season. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. Barberina lanceolata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular plant in horticultural settings.
