Bulbophyllum chthonochroma

Bulbophyllum chthonochroma, commonly known as the dark earth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique dark green to blackish leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it is often found in rocky or mossy areas.

The orchid is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
