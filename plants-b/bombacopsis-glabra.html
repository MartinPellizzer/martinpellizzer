Bombacopsis glabra

Bombacopsis glabra, commonly known as the white cedar or silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
