Bidens quinqueradiata

Bidens quinqueradiata, commonly known as the common beggarweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five radiating petals and a crown of bracts. The plant has hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Bidens quinqueradiata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
