Bonafousia maxima

Bonafousia maxima, commonly known as the giant bonafousia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the most impressive foliage plants in the botanical world. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for large gardens and botanical collections.
