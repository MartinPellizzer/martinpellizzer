Brachypodium pinnatum var. barrelieri

Brachypodium pinnatum var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's little grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and genetic studies.
