Blepharodon venezuelense

Blepharodon venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, specifically Venezuela.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with high biodiversity. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, featuring intricate patterns and a strong scent to attract pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
