Bambos aculeata

Bambos aculeata, commonly known as the spiny bamboo, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Gramineae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and sharp, spiny nodes. The plant grows in dense clusters, forming thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Bambos aculeata is valued for its strong, fibrous material, which has been used traditionally for construction and crafts.
