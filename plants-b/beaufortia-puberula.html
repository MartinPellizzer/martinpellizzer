Beaufortia puberula

Beaufortia puberula, commonly known as the silky beaufortia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Beaufortia puberula is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
