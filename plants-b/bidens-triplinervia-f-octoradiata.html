Bidens triplinervia f. octoradiata

Bidens triplinervia f. octoradiata, commonly known as the eight-rayed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant features slender, branching stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators.

The plant is often found in wetlands and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments.
