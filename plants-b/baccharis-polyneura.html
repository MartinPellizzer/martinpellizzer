Baccharis polyneura

Baccharis polyneura, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Baccharis polyneura plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
