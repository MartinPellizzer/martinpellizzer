Bromus alopecuros subsp. alopecuros

Bromus alopecuros subsp.

alopecuros, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing a single flowering lemma.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
