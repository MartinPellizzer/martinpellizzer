Bromus arvensis subsp. hyalinus

Bromus arvensis subsp.

hyalinus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered with a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
