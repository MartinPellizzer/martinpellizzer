Butea suffruticosa

Butea suffruticosa, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces long, narrow pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Butea suffruticosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
