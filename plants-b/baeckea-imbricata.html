Baeckea imbricata

Baeckea imbricata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in spring.

Baeckea imbricata is typically found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
