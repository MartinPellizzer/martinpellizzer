Bellevalia bracteosa

Bellevalia bracteosa, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Bellevalia bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
