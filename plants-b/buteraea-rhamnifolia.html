Buteraea rhamnifolia

Buteraea rhamnifolia, commonly known as the white thorn or white buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and thorny stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval in shape. It produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Buteraea rhamnifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
