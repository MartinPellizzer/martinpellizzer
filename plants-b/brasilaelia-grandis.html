Brasilaelia grandis

Brasilaelia grandis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often have a slight pubescence.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
