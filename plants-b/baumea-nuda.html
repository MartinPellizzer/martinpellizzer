Baumea nuda

Baumea nuda, commonly known as the naked baumea, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Baumea nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
