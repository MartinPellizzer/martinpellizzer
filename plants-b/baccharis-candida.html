Baccharis candida

Baccharis candida, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its white to pale green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a woody stem and produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Baccharis candida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
