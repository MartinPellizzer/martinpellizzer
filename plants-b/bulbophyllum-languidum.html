Bulbophyllum languidum

Bulbophyllum languidum, commonly known as the languid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a unique type of pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients.

It thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
