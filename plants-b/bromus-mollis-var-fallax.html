Bromus mollis var. fallax

Bromus mollis var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
