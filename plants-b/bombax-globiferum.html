Bombax globiferum

Bombax globiferum, commonly known as the woolly cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
