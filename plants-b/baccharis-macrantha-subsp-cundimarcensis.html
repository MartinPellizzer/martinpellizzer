Baccharis macrantha subsp. cundimarcensis

Baccharis macrantha subsp.

cundimarcensis, commonly known as the Cundimarca baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

This species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in local landscaping due to its hardy nature.
