Beta vulgaris var. raposa

Beta vulgaris var. raposa, commonly known as the beetroot or chard, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, edible leaves. The plant is cultivated for both its root and leafy greens, which are rich in nutrients such as betaine and nitrates. Its leaves vary in color, ranging from deep green to red or purple, depending on the variety.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
