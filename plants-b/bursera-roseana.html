Bursera roseana

Bursera roseana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits that contain small, dark seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and incense-making.
