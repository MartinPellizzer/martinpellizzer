Bystropogon marifolius

Bystropogon marifolius, commonly known as the marifoliate shrub, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
