Blechnum chilense f. imbricatum

Blechnum chilense f. imbricatum, commonly known as the Chilean water fern, is a fern species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with finely divided, imbricate (overlapping) leaflets that help it thrive in water. It is typically found in slow-moving streams, lakes, and wetlands, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

The fronds are usually dark green and can vary in size depending on the habitat conditions.
