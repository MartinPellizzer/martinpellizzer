Berberis actinacantha

Berberis actinacantha, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, spiny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and thorny structure.

Berberis actinacantha is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with potential health benefits.
