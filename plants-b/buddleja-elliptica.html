Buddleja elliptica

Buddleja elliptica, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are elliptical, hence the species name, and are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. This plant is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
