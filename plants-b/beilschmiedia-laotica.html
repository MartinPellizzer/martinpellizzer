Beilschmiedia laotica

Beilschmiedia laotica, commonly known as the Laotian beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary vegetation. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
