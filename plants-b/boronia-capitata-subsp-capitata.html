Boronia capitata subsp. capitata

Boronia capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Capitata Boronia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The flowers are typically arranged in dense, rounded heads, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
