Bigelowia turbinata

Bigelowia turbinata, commonly known as the Turbanflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that are surrounded by a dense ring of bracts, giving it its unique appearance. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in dry, sunny conditions.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
