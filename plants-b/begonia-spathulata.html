Begonia spathulata

Begonia spathulata, commonly known as the wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Begonia spathulata is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
