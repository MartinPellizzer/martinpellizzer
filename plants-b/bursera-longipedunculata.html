Bursera longipedunculata

Bursera longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in dry forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
