Bombax malabaricum

Bombax malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar cotton tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release fluffy seeds covered in a cotton-like fiber.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultural practices.
