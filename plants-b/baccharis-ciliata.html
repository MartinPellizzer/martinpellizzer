Baccharis ciliata

Baccharis ciliata, commonly known as the silky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Baccharis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
