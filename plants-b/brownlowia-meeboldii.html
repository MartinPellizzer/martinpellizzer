Brownlowia meeboldii

Brownlowia meeboldii, commonly known as the white-flowered tree or the Malabar tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Brownlowia meeboldii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is well-suited for warm, sheltered environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
