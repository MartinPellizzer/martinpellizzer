Begonia philippinensis

Begonia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
