Baptisia leucantha

Baptisia leucantha, commonly known as white false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a feathery appearance.

Baptisia leucantha is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
