Boronia crenulata var. angustifolia

Boronia crenulata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in heathlands and open forests, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
