Begonia odorata

Begonia odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that emit a sweet, spicy aroma. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Begonia odorata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds and indoor cultivation.
