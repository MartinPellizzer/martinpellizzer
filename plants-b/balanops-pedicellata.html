Balanops pedicellata

Balanops pedicellata, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence bearing clusters of small, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Balanops pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
