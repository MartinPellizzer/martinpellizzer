Blutaparon

Blutaparon, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a short, thick rhizome with a reddish-brown color, from which its common name is derived. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive reddish hue when young.

Blutaparon is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
