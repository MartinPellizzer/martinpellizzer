Bulbophyllum muricatum

Bulbophyllum muricatum, commonly known as the rough-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with a distinctive, rough-textured lip.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
