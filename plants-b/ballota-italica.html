Ballota italica

Ballota italica, commonly known as the Italian ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Ballota italica is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health properties.
