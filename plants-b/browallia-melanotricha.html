Browallia melanotricha

Browallia melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired browallia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of care.
