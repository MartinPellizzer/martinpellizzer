Brosimum alicastrum

Brosimum alicastrum, commonly known as the balsa tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is among the lightest in the world. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and often have a velvety texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits containing edible seeds.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
