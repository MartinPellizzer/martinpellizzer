Bignonia simplicifolia

Bignonia simplicifolia, commonly known as the trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, woody vines that can climb up to 30 feet, often using tendrils to attach to supports. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
