Bergia guineensis

Bergia guineensis, commonly known as the African marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Bergia guineensis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.
