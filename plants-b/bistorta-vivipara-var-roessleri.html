Bistorta vivipara var. roessleri

Bistorta vivipara var. roessleri, commonly known as the Alpine bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
