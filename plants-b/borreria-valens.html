Borreria valens

Borreria valens, commonly known as the redroot false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in fine hairs.

Borreria valens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.
