Bulbophyllum obtusiangulum

Bulbophyllum obtusiangulum, commonly known as the blunt-angled bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This epiphytic orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
