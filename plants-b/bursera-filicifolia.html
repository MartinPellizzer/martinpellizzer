Bursera filicifolia

Bursera filicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany or sweet gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and distinctive, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and woodland areas.

Bursera filicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its aromatic wood and medicinal properties.
