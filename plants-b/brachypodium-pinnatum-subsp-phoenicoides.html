Brachypodium pinnatum subsp. phoenicoides

Brachypodium pinnatum subsp.

phoenicoides, commonly known as the purple grass or purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and distinctive purple to pinkish hues in its inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny, three-angled spikelets.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
