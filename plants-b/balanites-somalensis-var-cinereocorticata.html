Balanites somalensis var. cinereocorticata

Balanites somalensis var. cinereocorticata, commonly known as the Somalian burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, fissured bark that ranges from grey to brown in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spherical, spiny fruit pods. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, feather-like leaflets.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder.
