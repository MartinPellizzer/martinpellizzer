Bidens eatonii var. typica

Bidens eatonii var. typica, commonly known as the Eaton's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
