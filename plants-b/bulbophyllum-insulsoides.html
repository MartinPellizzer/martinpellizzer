Bulbophyllum insulsoides

Bulbophyllum insulsoides, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
