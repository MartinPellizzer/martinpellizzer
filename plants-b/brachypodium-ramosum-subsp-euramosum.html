Brachypodium ramosum subsp. euramosum

Brachypodium ramosum subsp.

euramosum, commonly known as the European slender brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and slender, ascending stems.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three spikelets per branch. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
