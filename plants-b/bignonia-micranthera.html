Bignonia micranthera

Bignonia micranthera, commonly known as the small-thorned crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Bignonia micranthera is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
