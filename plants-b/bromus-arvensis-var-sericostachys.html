Bromus arvensis var. sericostachys

Bromus arvensis var. sericostachys, commonly known as the silky brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of spikelets, with the lemma bearing a silky, hairy texture that gives it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed sites, such as roadsides and fields, and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
