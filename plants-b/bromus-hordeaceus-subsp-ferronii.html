Bromus hordeaceus subsp. ferronii

Bromus hordeaceus subsp.

ferronii, commonly known as the ferronii brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and rocky areas.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense tufts, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are covered in soft, silky hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
