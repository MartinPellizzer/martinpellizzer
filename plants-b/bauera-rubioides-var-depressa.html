Bauera rubioides var. depressa

Bauera rubioides var. depressa, commonly known as the low bauera, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features striking, glossy, dark green leaves that are oval to lance-shaped and often have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on racemes. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded conditions and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in native gardens and rockeries.
