Blechum hamatum

Blechum hamatum, commonly known as the white mountain larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
