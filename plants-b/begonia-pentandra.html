Begonia pentandra

Begonia pentandra, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to deep purple. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia pentandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
