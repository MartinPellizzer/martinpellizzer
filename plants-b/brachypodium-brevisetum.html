Brachypodium brevisetum

Brachypodium brevisetum, commonly known as the short-setose brachypodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
