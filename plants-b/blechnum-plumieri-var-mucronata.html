Blechnum plumieri var. mucronata

Blechnum plumieri var. mucronata, commonly known as the serrate holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is leathery and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
