Bulbophyllum groeneveldtii

Bulbophyllum groeneveldtii, commonly known as the Groeneveldt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a sympodial growth habit. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its foliage and flowering structure.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
