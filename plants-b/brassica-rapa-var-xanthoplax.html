Brassica rapa var. xanthoplax

Brassica rapa var. xanthoplax, commonly known as the yellow Chinese cabbage, is a cultivar of the species Brassica rapa.

It is characterized by its thick, yellowish-green leaves that are tightly wrapped around a central stem, forming a compact head. This variety is native to East Asia and is cultivated for its edible leaves and stem. The plant thrives in cool climates and is typically grown as a leafy vegetable.

It is rich in nutrients and has a slightly bitter taste, making it a popular ingredient in various Asian cuisines.
