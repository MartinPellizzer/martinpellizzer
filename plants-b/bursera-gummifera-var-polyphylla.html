Bursera gummifera var. polyphylla

Bursera gummifera var. polyphylla, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal preparations. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in arid landscapes.
