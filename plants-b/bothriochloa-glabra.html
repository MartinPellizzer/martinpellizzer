Bothriochloa glabra

Bothriochloa glabra, commonly known as smooth browntop, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Smooth browntop is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like appearance.
