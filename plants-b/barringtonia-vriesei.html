Barringtonia vriesei

Barringtonia vriesei, commonly known as the Vriesei lantern tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped inflorescences that hang from the branches, typically blooming at night. The plant has large, palmate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Barringtonia vriesei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
