Bidens chrysanthemifolia var. typica

Bidens chrysanthemifolia var. typica, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble chrysanthemums, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in native ecosystems.
