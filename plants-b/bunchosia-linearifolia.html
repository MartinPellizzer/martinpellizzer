Bunchosia linearifolia

Bunchosia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically terminal or axillary. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
