Bacopa llanorum

Bacopa llanorum, commonly known as the blue bacopa, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Bacopa llanorum is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.
