Brachypodium sylvaticum f. villosum

Brachypodium sylvaticum f. villosum, commonly known as the hairy wood rush, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, grass-like foliage with hairy textures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet bearing small, spike-like flowers.

Botanically, it is valued for its role in ecological studies and its potential as a model organism in plant biology research.
