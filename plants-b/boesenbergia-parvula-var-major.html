Boesenbergia parvula var. major

Boesenbergia parvula var. major, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces vibrant orange to red inflorescences, which are a defining feature of its botanical appearance. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
