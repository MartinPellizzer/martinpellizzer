Brickellia brachiata

Brickellia brachiata, commonly known as silverleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia brachiata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
