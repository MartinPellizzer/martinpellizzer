Bossiaea aquifolium subsp. aquifolium

Bossiaea aquifolium subsp.

aquifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that resemble those of holly, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

This species is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
