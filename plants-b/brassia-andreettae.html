Brassia andreettae

Brassia andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a combination of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its tropical environment. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, Brassia andreettae is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
