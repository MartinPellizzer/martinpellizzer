Bupleurum gansuense

Bupleurum gansuense, commonly known as Gansu angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Bupleurum gansuense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
