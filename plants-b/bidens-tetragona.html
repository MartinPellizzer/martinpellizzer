Bidens tetragona

Bidens tetragona, commonly known as four-angled ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, with both ray and disk florets.

Bidens tetragona is often found in moist or wetland habitats and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
