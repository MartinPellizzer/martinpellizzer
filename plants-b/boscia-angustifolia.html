Boscia angustifolia

Boscia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and alternate, narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, red or orange fruits that are edible for some animals. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Boscia angustifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
