Blechnum capense var. danaeaceum

Blechnum capense var. danaeaceum, commonly known as the Danaeaceum fern, is a variant of the Blechnum capense species native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

The fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
