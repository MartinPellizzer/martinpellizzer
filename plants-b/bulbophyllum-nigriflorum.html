Bulbophyllum nigriflorum

Bulbophyllum nigriflorum, commonly known as the black-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of dark purple or black flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
