Blechnum cumingianum

Blechnum cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization.
