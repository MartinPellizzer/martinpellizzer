Bulbine uncinata

Bulbine uncinata, commonly known as the hooked bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves and stems.

Bulbine uncinata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
