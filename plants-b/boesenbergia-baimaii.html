Boesenbergia baimaii

Boesenbergia baimaii, commonly known as the Baimai ginger or Thai ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, pale yellow to orange flowers with a strong aromatic scent. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The rhizomes are harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes, contributing to its cultural significance in the region.
