Baccharis myrtilloides f. angustifolia

Baccharis myrtilloides f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering period occurs in the late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate waterlogged conditions.
