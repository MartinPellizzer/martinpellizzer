Boronia ledophylla

Boronia ledophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Boronia ledophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
