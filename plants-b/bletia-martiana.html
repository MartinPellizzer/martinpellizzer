Bletia martiana

Bletia martiana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Bletia martiana is typically propagated through division or seed, and it requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
