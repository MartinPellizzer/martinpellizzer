Baccharis × arcuata

Baccharis × arcuata, commonly known as the goldenrod baccharis, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Baccharis × arcuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
