Briza pilosa

Briza pilosa, commonly known as hairy quillwort, is a species of aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Podostomataceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as rivers, streams, and ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences.

Briza pilosa plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
