Buda rubra

Buda rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are usually upright and branched. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Buda rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
