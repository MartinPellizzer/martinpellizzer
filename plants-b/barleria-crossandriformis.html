Barleria crossandriformis

Barleria crossandriformis, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
