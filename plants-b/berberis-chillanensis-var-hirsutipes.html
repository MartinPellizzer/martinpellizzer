Berberis chillanensis var. hirsutipes

Berberis chillanensis var. hirsutipes, commonly known as the hairy Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
