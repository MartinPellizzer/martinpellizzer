Bambusa vulgaris

Bambusa vulgaris, commonly known as the common bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant can reach heights of up to 20 meters and is known for its rapid growth rate, which makes it a popular choice for ornamental and commercial purposes. Its leaves are long and narrow, providing a lush, green appearance, while its rhizomes allow for clump growth.

Bambusa vulgaris is also valued for its environmental benefits, including carbon sequestration and soil stabilization.
