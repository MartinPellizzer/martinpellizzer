Baccharis palenae

Baccharis palenae, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Baccharis palenae is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
