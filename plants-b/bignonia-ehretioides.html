Bignonia ehretioides

Bignonia ehretioides, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia ehretioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and adaptability.
