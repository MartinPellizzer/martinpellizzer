Bergera wallichii

Bergera wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Bergera wallichii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
