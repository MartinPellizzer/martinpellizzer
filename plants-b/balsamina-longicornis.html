Balsamina longicornis

Balsamina longicornis, commonly known as the longhorn balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Balsamina longicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
