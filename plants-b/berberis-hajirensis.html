Berberis hajirensis

Berberis hajirensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Berberis hajirensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
