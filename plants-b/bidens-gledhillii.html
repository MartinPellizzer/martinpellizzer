Bidens gledhillii

Bidens gledhillii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with distinctive white markings, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Bidens gledhillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
