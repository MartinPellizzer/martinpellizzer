Begonia diegoi

Begonia diegoi, commonly known as the Diego's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both gardens and greenhouses.
