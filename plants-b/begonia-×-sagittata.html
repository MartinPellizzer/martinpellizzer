Begonia × sagittata

Begonia × sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf begonia, is a hybrid plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped or arrow-shaped leaves, which are often dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and continuous blooming habit.
