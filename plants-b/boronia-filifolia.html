Boronia filifolia

Boronia filifolia, commonly known as the silky boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and birds.

Boronia filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
