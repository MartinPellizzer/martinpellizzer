Berberis zayulana

Berberis zayulana, commonly known as the Zayulana barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. Its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that mature by late summer.

Berberis zayulana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
