Brachiaria lateritica

Brachiaria lateritica, commonly known as Brazilian signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Brachiaria lateritica is also used in forage production due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.
