Boea suffruticosa

Boea suffruticosa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

Boea suffruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
