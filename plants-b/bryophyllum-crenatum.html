Bryophyllum crenatum

Bryophyllum crenatum, commonly known as the "Lucky Plant" or "Mother of Millions," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, pink, and red, typically during the summer months. One of its most notable features is its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which can quickly form new plants.

This adaptability and ease of propagation make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and propagation enthusiasts.
