Breynia pubescens

Breynia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy breynia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features small, ovate leaves covered in fine, white hairs, which give it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Breynia pubescens is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to harsh conditions.
