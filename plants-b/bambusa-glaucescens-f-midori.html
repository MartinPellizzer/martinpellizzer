Bambusa glaucescens f. midori

Bambusa glaucescens f. midori, commonly known as the Emerald Bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, bright green, lance-shaped leaves that give it a vibrant, lush appearance. The plant has sturdy, slender culms that grow in dense clusters, making it ideal for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking an attractive, low-maintenance bamboo.
