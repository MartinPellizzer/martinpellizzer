Begonia chaiana

Begonia chaiana, commonly known as the Sierra Leone begonia, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in various parts of the world.
