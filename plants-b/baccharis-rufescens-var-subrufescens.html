Baccharis rufescens var. subrufescens

Baccharis rufescens var. subrufescens, commonly known as the redbrush baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the late summer and fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas, dunes, and disturbed sites. It has a woody stem with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
