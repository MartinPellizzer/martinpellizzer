Bonjeanea hirsuta var. retusa

Bonjeanea hirsuta var. retusa, commonly known as the woolly bonjeanea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or hirsute, foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
