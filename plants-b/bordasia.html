Bordasia

Bordasia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. They are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant yellow or orange hues. Bordasia species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in humid environments.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and role in local ecosystems.
