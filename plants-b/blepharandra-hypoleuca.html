Blepharandra hypoleuca

Blepharandra hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
