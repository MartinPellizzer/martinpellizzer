Bidens aurea var. wrightii

Bidens aurea var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
