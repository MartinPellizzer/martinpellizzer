Bromus pubescens var. canadensis

Bromus pubescens var. canadensis, commonly known as Canada brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Canada brome is often found in moist to dry soil conditions and is adapted to temperate climates.
