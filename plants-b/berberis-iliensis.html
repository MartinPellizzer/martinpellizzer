Berberis iliensis

Berberis iliensis, commonly known as the Ili barberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and China.

It typically grows in mountainous and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. Its flowers are yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often bitter.

Berberis iliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
