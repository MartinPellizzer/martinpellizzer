Berberis dictyophylla var. campylogyna

Berberis dictyophylla var. campylogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but tart. Its thorny stems and compact growth habit make it suitable for hedging or ornamental use in gardens.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
