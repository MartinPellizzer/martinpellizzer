Bignonia bibracteata

Bignonia bibracteata, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous vine that produces long, drooping clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bignonia bibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 8 through 10.
