Begonia poggei var. albiflora

Begonia poggei var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and silver markings. The plant produces showy, white flowers with a subtle pink tinge, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is popular in both garden settings and as a potted plant.
