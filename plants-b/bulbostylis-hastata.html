Bulbostylis hastata

Bulbostylis hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbostylis, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial grass characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
