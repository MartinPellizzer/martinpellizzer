Bulbophyllum trachybracteum

Bulbophyllum trachybracteum, commonly known as the rough-bract bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip structure. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage organs adapted to its tropical habitat.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral morphology.
