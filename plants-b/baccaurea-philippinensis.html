Baccaurea philippinensis

Baccaurea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine acanthus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
