Barbarea brachycarpa var. minor

Barbarea brachycarpa var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf winter cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of less than 10 centimeters, with small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces short, club-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable species with potential uses in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
