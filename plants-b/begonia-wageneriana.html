Begonia wageneriana

Begonia wageneriana, commonly known as the Wagener's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage, which often displays shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
