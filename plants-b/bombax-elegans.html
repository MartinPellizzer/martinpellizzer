Bombax elegans

Bombax elegans, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a velvety, cotton-like seed capsule. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its trunk is often smooth and flaky. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The silk cotton tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
