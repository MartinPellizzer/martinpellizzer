Bulbostylis bathiei

Bulbostylis bathiei, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a small, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flower clusters.

Bulbostylis bathiei plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
