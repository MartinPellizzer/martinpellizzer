Berberis brachypoda

Berberis brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems covered in small, sharp spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are small, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Berberis brachypoda is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
