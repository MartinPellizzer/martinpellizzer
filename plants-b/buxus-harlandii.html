Buxus harlandii

Buxus harlandii, commonly known as Harland's boxwood, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and foundation plantings.

Buxus harlandii is also valued for its ability to tolerate pruning, allowing for shaping into various forms.
