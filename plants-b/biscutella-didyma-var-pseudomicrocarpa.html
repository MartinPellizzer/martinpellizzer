Biscutella didyma var. pseudomicrocarpa

Biscutella didyma var. pseudomicrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuit flower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
