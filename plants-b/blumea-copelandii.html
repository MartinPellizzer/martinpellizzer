Blumea copelandii

Blumea copelandii, commonly known as the golden flax or false chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
