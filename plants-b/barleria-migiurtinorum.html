Barleria migiurtinorum

Barleria migiurtinorum, commonly known as the African bluebells, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Barleria migiurtinorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
