Brassica × aelleniana

Brassica × aelleniana, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a naturally occurring hybrid species resulting from the cross between Brassica carinata and Brassica napus.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. It produces long, slender pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

This species is valued for its potential in agriculture, particularly for its oilseed and as a forage crop.
