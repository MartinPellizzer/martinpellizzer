Bromus secalinus var. nitidus

Bromus secalinus var. nitidus, commonly known as the smooth brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters, with each containing two to three small, awned florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Bromus secalinus var. nitidus is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil stabilization.
