Boronia hispida

Boronia hispida, commonly known as the rough boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a rough texture, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Boronia hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract native pollinators.
