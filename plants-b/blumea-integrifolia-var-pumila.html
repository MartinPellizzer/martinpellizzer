Blumea integrifolia var. pumila

Blumea integrifolia var. pumila, commonly known as the small-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and entire, with a smooth margin.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, making it an attractive ornamental plant.
