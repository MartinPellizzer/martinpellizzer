Beringeria cinerea

Beringeria cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf beringia, is a rare and unique plant species found in specific alpine regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it reflect excess sunlight in its harsh environment. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to conserve moisture and withstand cold temperatures. It produces delicate, pale blue flowers during the short growing season.

Beringeria cinerea plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing food and habitat for specialized alpine insects and birds.
