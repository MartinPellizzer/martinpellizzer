Banksia dallanneyi subsp. media

Banksia dallanneyi subsp.

media, commonly known as the Geraldton banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-west of Western Australia. It is characterized by its cylindrical, woody cones that are typically yellow to reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture, and it is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its natural habitat.

This subspecies is part of the Proteaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
