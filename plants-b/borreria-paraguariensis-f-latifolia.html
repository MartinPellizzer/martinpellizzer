Borreria paraguariensis f. latifolia

Borreria paraguariensis f. latifolia, commonly known as "yerba de la cruz," is a variant of the Borreria paraguariensis species, native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a branching structure that supports its growth in forest understories.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.
