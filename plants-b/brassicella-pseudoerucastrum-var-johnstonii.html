Brassicella pseudoerucastrum var. johnstonii

Brassicella pseudoerucastrum var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Brassiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are simple and submerged, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

It is primarily found in the Pacific Northwest of North America.
