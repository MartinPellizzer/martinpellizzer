Brachyglottis rangiora

Brachyglottis rangiora, commonly known as the silver fern, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in New Zealand's native flora.
