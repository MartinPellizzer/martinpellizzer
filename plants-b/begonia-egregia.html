Begonia egregia

Begonia egregia, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It prefers cool, humid conditions and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Begonia egregia is widely appreciated by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts alike.
