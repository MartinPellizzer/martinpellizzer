Bupleurum falcatum var. genuinum

Bupleurum falcatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often grow in a prostrate or ascending manner. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a distinctive wavy or serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
