Blechnum squarrosum

Blechnum squarrosum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while also storing nutrients.

Blechnum squarrosum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various small organisms.
