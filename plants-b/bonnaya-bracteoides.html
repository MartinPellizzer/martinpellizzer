Bonnaya bracteoides

Bonnaya bracteoides, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. Its roots are known to contain a high concentration of oils, which have been used for various purposes.

Due to its unique properties, it is also valued as an ornamental plant in some regions.
