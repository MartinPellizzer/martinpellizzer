Bromopsis zangezura

Bromopsis zangezura, commonly known as the Zangezur feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-blue foliage. The plant produces slender inflorescences with small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and semi-arid environments.

Bromopsis zangezura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
