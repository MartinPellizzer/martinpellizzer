Ballota nigra var. porrigens

Ballota nigra var. porrigens, commonly known as the black ballota or Portuguese ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in scrubland and rocky habitats.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has garnered interest for its potential pharmacological properties.
