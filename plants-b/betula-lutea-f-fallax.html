Betula lutea f. fallax

Betula lutea f. fallax, commonly known as the yellow birch variant, is a rare and lesser-known form of the yellow birch.

It is characterized by its pale yellow bark and slender, upright growth habit. This plant is native to specific regions in North America, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
