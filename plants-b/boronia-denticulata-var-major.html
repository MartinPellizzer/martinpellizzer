Boronia denticulata var. major

Boronia denticulata var. major, commonly known as the rough-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
