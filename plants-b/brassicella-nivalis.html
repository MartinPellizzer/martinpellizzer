Brassicella nivalis

Brassicella nivalis, commonly known as the snow mustard, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This species is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in cold, harsh environments. Brassicella nivalis typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its presence is often an indicator of high-altitude ecosystems and can be found in mountainous regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.
