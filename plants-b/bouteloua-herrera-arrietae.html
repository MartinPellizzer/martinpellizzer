Bouteloua herrera-arrietae

Bouteloua herrera-arrietae, commonly known as the Herrera-Arieta's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which is typical of the grass genus Bouteloua. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in arid environments. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
