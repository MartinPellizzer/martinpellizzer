Brassica juncea var. celerifolia

Brassica juncea var. celerifolia, commonly known as Chinese kale or leaf mustard, is a leafy vegetable native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green, curly leaves that are often used in Asian cuisines. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. It thrives in cool climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like calcium and iron.
