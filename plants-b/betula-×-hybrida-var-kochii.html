Betula × hybrida var. kochii

Betula × hybrida var. kochii, commonly known as the silver birch, is a hybrid species of birch tree characterized by its white, peeling bark and delicate, triangular leaves.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters and prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring, and it is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
