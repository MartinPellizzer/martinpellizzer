Bromelia carolinae

Bromelia carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's leaves are typically broad, waxy, and have a vibrant green color with a slight reddish tinge. It produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences that range from yellow to orange in color.

Bromelia carolinae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild.
