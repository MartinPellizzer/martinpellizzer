Bupleurum falcatum var. africanum

Bupleurum falcatum var. africanum, commonly known as African cowbane, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

This species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
