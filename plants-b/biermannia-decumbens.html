Biermannia decumbens

Biermannia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping biermannia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Biermannia decumbens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
