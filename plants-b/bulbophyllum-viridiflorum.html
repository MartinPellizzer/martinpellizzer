Bulbophyllum viridiflorum

Bulbophyllum viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, compact growth habit and vibrant green flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a striking combination of green and white hues.
