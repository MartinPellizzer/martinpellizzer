Boronia pinnata

Boronia pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Boronia pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
