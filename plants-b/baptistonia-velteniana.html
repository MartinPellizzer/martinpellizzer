Baptistonia velteniana

Baptistonia velteniana, commonly known as the Velten's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are thin and often submerged, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing above the water surface.

Baptistonia velteniana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
