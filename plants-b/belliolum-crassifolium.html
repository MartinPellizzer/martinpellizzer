Belliolum crassifolium

Belliolum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers, which range in color from white to blue, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.

Belliolum crassifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
