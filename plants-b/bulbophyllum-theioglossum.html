Bulbophyllum theioglossum

Bulbophyllum theioglossum, commonly known as the "Cinnabar Lip," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, red-brown flower spikes that bear showy, lip-like petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
