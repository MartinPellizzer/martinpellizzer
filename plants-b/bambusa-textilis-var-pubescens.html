Bambusa textilis var. pubescens

Bambusa textilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of fine, silky hairs on its stems, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. The bamboo is relatively cold-hardy compared to other bamboo species, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to form thick clumps make it a popular choice for erosion control and landscaping.
