Berberis brachypoda var. salicaria

Berberis brachypoda var. salicaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
