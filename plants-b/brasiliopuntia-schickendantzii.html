Brasiliopuntia schickendantzii

Brasiliopuntia schickendantzii, commonly known as the Schickendantz's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, a typical adaptation for arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
