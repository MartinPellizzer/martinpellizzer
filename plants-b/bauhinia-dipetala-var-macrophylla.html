Bauhinia dipetala var. macrophylla

Bauhinia dipetala var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its bark is smooth and often exudes a milky sap when wounded.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces due to its striking floral display.
