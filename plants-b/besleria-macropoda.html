Besleria macropoda

Besleria macropoda, commonly known as the giant flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that feature large, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
