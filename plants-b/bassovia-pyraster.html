Bassovia pyraster

Bassovia pyraster, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, typically blooming in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
