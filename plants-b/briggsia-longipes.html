Briggsia longipes

Briggsia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a rare and unusual orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and green hues, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Briggsia longipes is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
