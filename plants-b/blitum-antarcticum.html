Blitum antarcticum

Blitum antarcticum, commonly known as Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial species that thrives in harsh environments such as Antarctica, South America, and southern Africa. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, and it produces small, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its root system is shallow but robust, allowing it to anchor itself in rocky or sandy soils.

Blitum antarcticum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its extreme environment.
