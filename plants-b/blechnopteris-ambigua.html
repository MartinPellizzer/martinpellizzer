Blechnopteris ambigua

Blechnopteris ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous water fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. This plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

It is often found growing along streams and in humid forests, where it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
