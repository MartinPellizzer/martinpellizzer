Bumelia fragrans

Bumelia fragrans, commonly known as sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
