Borzicactus hempelianus var. weingartianus

Borzicactus hempelianus var. weingartianus, commonly known as the Weingartianus cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense coating of white, powdery bloom, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
