Begonia pseudimpatiens

Begonia pseudimpatiens, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.

Begonia pseudimpatiens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
