Begonia sessilanthera

Begonia sessilanthera, commonly known as the sessile-flowered begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, often pink or red, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
