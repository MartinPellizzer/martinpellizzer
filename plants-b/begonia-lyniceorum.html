Begonia lyniceorum

Begonia lyniceorum, commonly known as the Lynciceorum begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically red or pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
