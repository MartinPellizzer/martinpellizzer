Blepharoglossum rhombeum

Blepharoglossum rhombeum, commonly known as the diamond leaf or rhombic fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its rhombic-shaped fronds are a distinguishing feature, and the plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
