Baccharis phylicoides

Baccharis phylicoides, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often red or orange when mature. It is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils, and it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
