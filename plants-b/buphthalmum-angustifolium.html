Buphthalmum angustifolium

Buphthalmum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in regions with suitable climates.
