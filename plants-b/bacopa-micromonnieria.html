Bacopa micromonnieria

Bacopa micromonnieria, commonly known as Java moss, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tiny, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in length, arranged in dense clusters along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, which are followed by tiny, round seeds. Java moss is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, making it a popular choice for aquariums and water gardens.

Its dense growth habit also provides shelter for aquatic organisms.
