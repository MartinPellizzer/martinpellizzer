Bradleya latifolia

Bradleya latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.

Bradleya latifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
