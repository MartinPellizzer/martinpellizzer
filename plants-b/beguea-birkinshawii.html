Beguea birkinshawii

Beguea birkinshawii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white inflorescences that resemble misty clouds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Beguea birkinshawii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
