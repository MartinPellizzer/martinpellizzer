Boscia grandiflora

Boscia grandiflora, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Boscia grandiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
