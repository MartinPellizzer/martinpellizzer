Bergerocactus

Bergerocactus, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on long, slender stalks. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

Bergerocactus is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.
