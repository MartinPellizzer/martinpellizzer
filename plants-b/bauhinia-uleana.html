Bauhinia uleana

Bauhinia uleana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often colorful leaflets. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its flowers are typically pale pink or white, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
