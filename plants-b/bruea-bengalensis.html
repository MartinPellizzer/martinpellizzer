Bruea bengalensis

Bruea bengalensis, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or bengal sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a pleasant scent.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, and the fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed.
