Begonia dodsonii

Begonia dodsonii, commonly known as the Dodson's begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
