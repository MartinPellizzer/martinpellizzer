Butea maingayi

Butea maingayi, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Butea maingayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
