Bupleurum stellatum subvar. humile

Bupleurum stellatum subvar. humile, commonly known as the small starwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
