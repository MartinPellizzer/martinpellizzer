Boraginodes

Boraginodes, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Boraginodes is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of fiber.
