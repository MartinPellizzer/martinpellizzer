Bassia bicornis var. horrida

Bassia bicornis var. horrida, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems, small gray-green leaves, and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its small, showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial herb that is drought-tolerant and well adapted to harsh desert conditions.
