Berberis × bombycina

Berberis × bombycina, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid of Berberis thunbergii and Berberis japonica, characterized by its ornamental, spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered invasive in some regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, low-maintenance landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically impactful.
