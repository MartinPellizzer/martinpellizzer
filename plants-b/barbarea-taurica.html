Barbarea taurica

Barbarea taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and bright green, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine areas.

Barbarea taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
