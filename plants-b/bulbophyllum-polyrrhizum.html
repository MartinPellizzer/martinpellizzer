Bulbophyllum polyrrhizum

Bulbophyllum polyrrhizum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces striking, spider-like inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, often attaching to trees or other plants in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
