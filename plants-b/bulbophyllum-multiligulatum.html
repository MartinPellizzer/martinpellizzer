Bulbophyllum multiligulatum

Bulbophyllum multiligulatum, commonly known as the many-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
