Ballota nigra variegata

Ballota nigra variegata, commonly known as the variegated black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
