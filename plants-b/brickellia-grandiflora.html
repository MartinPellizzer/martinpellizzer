Brickellia grandiflora

Brickellia grandiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent, glandular hairs. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Brickellia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
