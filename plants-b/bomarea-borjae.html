Bomarea borjae

Bomarea borjae, commonly known as the Borja's bomarea, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers that range from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Bomarea borjae is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
