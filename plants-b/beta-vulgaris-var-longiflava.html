Beta vulgaris var. longiflava

Beta vulgaris var. longiflava, commonly known as the red beet or chard, is a cultivar of the beetroot plant characterized by its elongated, colorful leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is cultivated for both its edible leaves and its taproot, which can be harvested at different stages of growth. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with vibrant red, yellow, or orange foliage depending on the variety. Its leaves are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like iron and magnesium.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
