Bambusa multiplex f. alphonse-karrii

Bambusa multiplex f. alphonse-karrii, commonly known as the Alphonse karrii bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, dark green leaves and produces slender, upright culms that are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different garden environments. It is also valued for its use in construction and crafts due to its strong, lightweight culms.

This bamboo variety is relatively low maintenance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
