Baeckea cunninghamii

Baeckea cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain baeckea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Baeckea cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
