Brunsvigia multiflora var. rubricaulis

Brunsvigia multiflora var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed glory belders, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tall, cylindrical flower spikes that bear clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that turn reddish in color during the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and seasonal flowering make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
