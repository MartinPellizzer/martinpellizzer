Bidens hyperborea var. typica

Bidens hyperborea var. typica, commonly known as the alpine ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have serrated edges.

It is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
