Bothriochloa catharinensis

Bothriochloa catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian needlegrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stature and fine, wiry leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
