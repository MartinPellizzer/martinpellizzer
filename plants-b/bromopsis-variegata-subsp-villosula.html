Bromopsis variegata subsp. villosula

Bromopsis variegata subsp.

villosula, commonly known as the hairy bromus, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaf blades. It typically grows in temperate regions, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue sheath, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
