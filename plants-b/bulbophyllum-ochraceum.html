Bulbophyllum ochraceum

Bulbophyllum ochraceum, commonly known as the Orange-lipped Pterostylis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, fleshy rhizome and small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive orange lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, tropical climates.

The flowers are often pollinated by nocturnal insects, showcasing its specialized adaptation for reproductive success.
