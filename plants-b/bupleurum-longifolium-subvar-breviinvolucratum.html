Bupleurum longifolium subvar. breviinvolucratum

Bupleurum longifolium subvar. breviinvolucratum, commonly known as the short-involucrate angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
