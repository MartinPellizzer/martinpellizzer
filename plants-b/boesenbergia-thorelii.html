Boesenbergia thorelii

Boesenbergia thorelii, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a slight sheen.

Boesenbergia thorelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
