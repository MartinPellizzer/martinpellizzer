Boehmeria ampullacea

Boehmeria ampullacea, commonly known as the golden bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

Boehmeria ampullacea is also valued for its fibrous stalks, which are used in the production of textiles and paper.
