Brassica longirostra

Brassica longirostra, commonly known as the long-beak mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Brassica longirostra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
