Breynia cernua var. acutifolia

Breynia cernua var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf breynia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Breynia cernua var. acutifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
