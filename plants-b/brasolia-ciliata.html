Brasolia ciliata

Brasolia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Brasolia ciliata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also noted for its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
