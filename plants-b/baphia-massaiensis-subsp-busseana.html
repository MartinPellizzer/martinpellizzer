Baphia massaiensis subsp. busseana

Baphia massaiensis subsp.

busseana, commonly known as the Massai baphia, is a shrub native to East Africa. It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological traits compared to other members of the Baphia genus.
