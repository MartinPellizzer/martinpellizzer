Brachiaria arizonica var. tenuis

Brachiaria arizonica var. tenuis, commonly known as Arizona grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, flat leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and often used in landscaping for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
