Baileya multiradiata var. nudicaulis

Baileya multiradiata var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of many tiny florets arranged in a radiate pattern, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
