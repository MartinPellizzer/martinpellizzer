Bobua cerasifolia

Bobua cerasifolia, commonly known as the sweet apple tree, is a species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, fragrant flavor.

Bobua cerasifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential as a food source in its native habitat.
