Bunium carvi

Bunium carvi, commonly known as caraway, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its seeds, often referred to as caraway seeds, are used extensively in culinary and medicinal applications.

The essential oils extracted from the seeds contain compounds like limonene and carvone, which contribute to its aromatic properties.
