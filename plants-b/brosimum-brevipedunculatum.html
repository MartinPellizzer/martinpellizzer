Brosimum brevipedunculatum

Brosimum brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the Guayacan tree, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Central and South America.

The tree produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The wood of Brosimum brevipedunculatum is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
