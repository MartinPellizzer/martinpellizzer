Brachychiton acerifolius var. gregorii

Brachychiton acerifolius var. gregorii, commonly known as the gregorii flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a distinctive heart-shaped or shield-like leaf structure, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.
