Bignonia capreolata var. atrosanguinea

Bignonia capreolata var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as black blood vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can grow up to 30 feet in length.
