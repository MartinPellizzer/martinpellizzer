Blaeria pusilla

Blaeria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blaeria, is a small herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Blaeria pusilla is considered a rare and endangered species.
