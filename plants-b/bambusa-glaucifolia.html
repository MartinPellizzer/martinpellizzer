Bambusa glaucifolia

Bambusa glaucifolia, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its slender, erect culms covered with a distinctive whitish or bluish waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Bambusa glaucifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
