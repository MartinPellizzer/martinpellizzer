Betula papyrifera var. commutata

Betula papyrifera var. commutata, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale yellow and green underneath. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
