Bragantia melastomifolia

Bragantia melastomifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Bragantia melastomifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
