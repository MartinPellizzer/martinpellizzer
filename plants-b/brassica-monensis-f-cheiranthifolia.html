Brassica monensis f. cheiranthifolia

Brassica monensis f. cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the cheiranthifolius form of the Chinese kale, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This cultivar exhibits broad, lobed leaves that resemble those of the genus Cheiranthus, giving it its common name. It is native to China and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of up to 60 cm, and thrives in cool climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.
