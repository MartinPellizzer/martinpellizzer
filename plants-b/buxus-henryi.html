Buxus henryi

Buxus henryi, commonly known as Henry's boxwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Buxaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and have a glossy, dark green appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative shrub due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate pruning.
