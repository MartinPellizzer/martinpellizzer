Brassica insularis var. angustiloba

Brassica insularis var. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showing adaptations to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional agriculture.
