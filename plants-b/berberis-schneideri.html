Berberis schneideri

Berberis schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
