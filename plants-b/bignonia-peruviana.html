Bignonia peruviana

Bignonia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian crossvine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, orange-red to yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
