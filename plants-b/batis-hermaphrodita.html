Batis hermaphrodita

Batis hermaphrodita, commonly known as the common batis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom during the rainy season.

Batis hermaphrodita is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
