Buddleja megalocephala f. albilanata

Buddleja megalocephala f. albilanata, commonly known as the white-headed bush willow, is a hybrid form of the Buddleja genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant produces elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various climates.
