Bidens guatemalensis

Bidens guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan tickseed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have yellow disk florets surrounded by white ray florets.

Bidens guatemalensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
