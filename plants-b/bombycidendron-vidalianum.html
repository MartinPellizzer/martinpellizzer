Bombycidendron vidalianum

Bombycidendron vidalianum, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant fruit that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
