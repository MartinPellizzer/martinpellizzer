Blumea saussureoides

Blumea saussureoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Blumea saussureoides is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance.
