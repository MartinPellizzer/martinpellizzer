Bothriochloa jainii

Bothriochloa jainii, commonly known as Jain's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
