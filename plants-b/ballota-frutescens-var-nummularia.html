Ballota frutescens var. nummularia

Ballota frutescens var. nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf ballota, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
