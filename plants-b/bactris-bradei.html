Bactris bradei

Bactris bradei, commonly known as the pink bacaba, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that have a sweet, slightly acidic flavor. Bactris bradei is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife species in the rainforest.
