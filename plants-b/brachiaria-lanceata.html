Brachiaria lanceata

Brachiaria lanceata, commonly known as speargrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open areas such as pastures, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Brachiaria lanceata is often used for forage in livestock farming due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
