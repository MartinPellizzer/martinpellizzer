Brachiaria

Brachiaria, commonly known as signalgrass, is a genus of grasses native to the Americas.

It includes over 200 species, many of which are widely used in forage and pasture systems. These plants typically have slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. They are adapted to a variety of soil types and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Brachiaria species are known for their resilience to drought and their ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
