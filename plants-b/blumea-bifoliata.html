Blumea bifoliata

Blumea bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaved blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a head, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
