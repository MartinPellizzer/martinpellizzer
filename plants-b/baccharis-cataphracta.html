Baccharis cataphracta

Baccharis cataphracta, commonly known as black baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in its native habitats.
