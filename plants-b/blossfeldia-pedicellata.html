Blossfeldia pedicellata

Blossfeldia pedicellata, commonly known as the fairy pine or dwarf pine, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a small, compact plant that typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features a short, thick trunk covered in smooth, waxy scales, and it produces a single, cone-like structure at the top. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
