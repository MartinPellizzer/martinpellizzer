Berberis roxburghii

Berberis roxburghii, commonly known as Indian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It features spiny stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
