Bulbophyllum cavistigma

Bulbophyllum cavistigma, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Bulbophyllum cavistigma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
