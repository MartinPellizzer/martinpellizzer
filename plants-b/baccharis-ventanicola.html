Baccharis ventanicola

Baccharis ventanicola, commonly known as the Florida baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
