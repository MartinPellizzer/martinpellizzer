Begonia hederacea

Begonia hederacea, commonly known as the trailing begonia, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a ground cover or hanging plant.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a lush, trailing foliage plant.
