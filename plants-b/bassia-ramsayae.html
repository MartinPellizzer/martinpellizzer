Bassia ramsayae

Bassia ramsayae, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils, it is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.
