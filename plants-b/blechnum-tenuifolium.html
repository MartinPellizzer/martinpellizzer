Blechnum tenuifolium

Blechnum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the rhizomes forming a dense underground network. Its fronds are usually evergreen and can be found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
