Bunium alpinum subsp. corydalinum

Bunium alpinum subsp.

corydalinum, commonly known as alpine fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pungent odor, often used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Apiaceae family, sharing similar structural and chemical features.
