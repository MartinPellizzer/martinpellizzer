Bupleurum atlanticum

Bupleurum atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores and is adapted to salt spray and wind. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in umbels, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Bupleurum atlanticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
