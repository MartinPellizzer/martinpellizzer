Brasiliocroton muricatus

Brasiliocroton muricatus, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
