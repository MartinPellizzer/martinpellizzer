Bulbophyllum levidense

Bulbophyllum levidense, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red sepals and petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season, and are known for their vibrant color and unique shape.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
