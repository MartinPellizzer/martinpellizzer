Bredemeyera viscidula

Bredemeyera viscidula, commonly known as the sticky leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that have a sticky texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.

Bredemeyera viscidula thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
