Blepharocalyx salicifolius

Blepharocalyx salicifolius, commonly known as the silky balsa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
