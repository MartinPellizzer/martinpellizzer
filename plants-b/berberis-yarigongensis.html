Berberis yarigongensis

Berberis yarigongensis, commonly known as Yarigong barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
