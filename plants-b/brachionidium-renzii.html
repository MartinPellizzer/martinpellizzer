Brachionidium renzii

Brachionidium renzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, anchoring itself to submerged surfaces with rhizomes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of its leaves.

Brachionidium renzii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its filtration capabilities.
