Baccharis erigeroides var. dusenii

Baccharis erigeroides var. dusenii, commonly known as the Desert Baccharis, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It often grows in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a common sight in desert landscapes.

Its structure and appearance make it an important component of local ecosystems.
