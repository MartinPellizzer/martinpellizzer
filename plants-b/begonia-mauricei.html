Begonia mauricei

Begonia mauricei, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a rich, glossy texture. The plant produces striking clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
