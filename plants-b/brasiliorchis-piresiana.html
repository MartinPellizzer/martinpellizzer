Brasiliorchis piresiana

Brasiliorchis piresiana, commonly known as the Pires orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes and small, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
