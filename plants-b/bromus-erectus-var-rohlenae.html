Bromus erectus var. rohlenae

Bromus erectus var. rohlenae, commonly known as the Rohlen's brome, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. This variety is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine vegetation in its native range.
