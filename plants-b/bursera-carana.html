Bursera carana

Bursera carana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in clusters. It produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a perfume.

The plant also has small yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by orange or red fruits containing three seeds.
