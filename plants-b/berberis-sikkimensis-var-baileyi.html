Berberis sikkimensis var. baileyi

Berberis sikkimensis var. baileyi, commonly known as the Sikkim barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at higher elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
