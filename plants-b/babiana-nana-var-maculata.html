Babiana nana var. maculata

Babiana nana var. maculata, commonly known as the speckled babiana, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded bulb and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant. The species is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is prized in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
