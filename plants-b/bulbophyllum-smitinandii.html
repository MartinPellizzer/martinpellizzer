Bulbophyllum smitinandii

Bulbophyllum smitinandii, commonly known as the Smitinand's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
