Berberis yingii

Berberis yingii, commonly known as the Yunnan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, bitter taste. It thrives in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional Chinese medicine.
