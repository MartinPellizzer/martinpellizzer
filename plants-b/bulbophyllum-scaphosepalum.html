Bulbophyllum scaphosepalum

Bulbophyllum scaphosepalum, commonly known as the "Bottle Orchid," is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with elongated, pale yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
