Baphia nitida

Baphia nitida, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

Baphia nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
