Bystropogon canariensis

Bystropogon canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its sensitivity to cold, it requires protection from frost to survive in cooler regions.
