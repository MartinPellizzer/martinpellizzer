Bastardiopsis yaracuyensis

Bastardiopsis yaracuyensis, commonly known as the Yaracuyensis cactus, is a species native to Venezuela.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing during the warmer months.

Bastardiopsis yaracuyensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
