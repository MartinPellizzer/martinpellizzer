Bothriochloa decipiens

Bothriochloa decipiens, commonly known as the false brome, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear in dense stands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Bothriochloa decipiens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
