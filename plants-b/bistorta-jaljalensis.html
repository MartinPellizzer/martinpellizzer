Bistorta jaljalensis

Bistorta jaljalensis, commonly known as the Jordanian bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features rhizomatous growth, with tall, erect stems covered in distinctive reddish-brown hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences.

Bistorta jaljalensis is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.
