Brassicella pseudoerucastrum var. setigera

Brassicella pseudoerucastrum var. setigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in phytoremediation.
