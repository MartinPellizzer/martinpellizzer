Billbergia horrida var. tigrina

Billbergia horrida var. tigrina, commonly known as the tiger billbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, striped leaves that resemble a tiger's coat, giving it its common name. The plant features a central rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. It produces showy red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This species is well-suited for indoor cultivation and thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.
