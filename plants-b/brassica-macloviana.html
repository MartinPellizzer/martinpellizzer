Brassica macloviana

Brassica macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian kale, is a hardy, leafy vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, curly, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder known as powdery mildew. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to cool climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Brassica macloviana is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly used in traditional cuisines for its mild, earthy flavor.
