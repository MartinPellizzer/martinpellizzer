Bromus squarrosus var. uberrimus

Bromus squarrosus var. uberrimus, commonly known as the silverleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Bromus squarrosus var. uberrimus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and rapid growth in response to seasonal rains.
