Blysmus exilis

Blysmus exilis, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

Blysmus exilis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
