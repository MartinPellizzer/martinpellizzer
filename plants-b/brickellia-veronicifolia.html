Brickellia veronicifolia

Brickellia veronicifolia, commonly known as green brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
