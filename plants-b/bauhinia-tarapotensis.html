Bauhinia tarapotensis

Bauhinia tarapotensis, commonly known as the mountain bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is often found in forested areas and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Bauhinia tarapotensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
