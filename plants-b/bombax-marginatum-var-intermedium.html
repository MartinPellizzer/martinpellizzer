Bombax marginatum var. intermedium

Bombax marginatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the pink cotton tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a distinctive, umbrella-shaped canopy with pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fibrous seeds are encased in a fluffy, cotton-like pulp, which is harvested for commercial use.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
