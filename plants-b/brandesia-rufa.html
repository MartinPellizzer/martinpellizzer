Brandesia rufa

Brandesia rufa, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with reddish veins and a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Brandesia rufa is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
