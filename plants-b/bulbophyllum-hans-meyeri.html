Bulbophyllum hans-meyeri

Bulbophyllum hans-meyeri, commonly known as the Hans-Meyer's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a lithophyte, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks, and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green tones. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
