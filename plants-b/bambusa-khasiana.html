Bambusa khasiana

Bambusa khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bamboo, is a tall, slender bamboo species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green culms that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bambusa khasiana is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a sustainable resource.
