Bothriochilus densiflorus

Bothriochilus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid's flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination by insects.
