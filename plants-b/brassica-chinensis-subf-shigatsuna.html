Brassica chinensis subf. shigatsuna

Brassica chinensis subf.

shigatsuna, commonly known as Japanese mustard spinach, is a leafy green vegetable native to East Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants such as broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its dark green, tender leaves and thick, edible stems.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins A and C. The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
