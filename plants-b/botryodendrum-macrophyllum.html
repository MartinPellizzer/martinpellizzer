Botryodendrum macrophyllum

Botryodendrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf botryodendron, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
