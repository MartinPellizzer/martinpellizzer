Begonia bulbosa

Begonia bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia bulbosa is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
