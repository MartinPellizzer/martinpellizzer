Boronia drummondii var. alba

Boronia drummondii var. alba, commonly known as the white boronia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
