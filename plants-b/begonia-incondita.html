Begonia incondita

Begonia incondita, commonly known as the red-tipped begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, deep red bracts that surround its small, white flowers, creating a visually dramatic display. The plant produces opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia incondita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique and colorful foliage.
