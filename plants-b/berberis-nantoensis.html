Berberis nantoensis

Berberis nantoensis, commonly known as the Nantoen barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
