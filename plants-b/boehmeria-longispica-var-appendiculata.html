Boehmeria longispica var. appendiculata

Boehmeria longispica var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a prominent midrib. This variant is distinguished by its elongated, appendiculate structures at the base of the leaves, which are unique to this subspecies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
