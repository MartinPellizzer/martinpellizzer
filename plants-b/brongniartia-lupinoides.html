Brongniartia lupinoides

Brongniartia lupinoides, commonly known as the Brazilian lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
