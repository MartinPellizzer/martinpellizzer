Bupleurum subrubeum

Bupleurum subrubeum, commonly known as redroot valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pale green to reddish leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Bupleurum subrubeum is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.
