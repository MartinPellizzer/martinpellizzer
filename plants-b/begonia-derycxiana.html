Begonia derycxiana

Begonia derycxiana, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Begonia derycxiana is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor settings.
