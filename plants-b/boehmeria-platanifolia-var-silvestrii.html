Boehmeria platanifolia var. silvestrii

Boehmeria platanifolia var. silvestrii, commonly known as the silvestrii variant of the Chinese nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous stems have been historically used for making textiles and paper.
