Baccharis ramulosa

Baccharis ramulosa, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis ramulosa is also noted for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine.
