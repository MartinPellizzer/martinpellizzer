Belairia savannarum

Belairia savannarum, commonly known as the savanna bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Belairia savannarum is adapted to seasonal droughts and can enter dormancy during dry periods.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous animals.
