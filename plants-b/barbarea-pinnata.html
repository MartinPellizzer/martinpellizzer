Barbarea pinnata

Barbarea pinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and branched. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Barbarea pinnata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
