Bulbophyllum subuliferum

Bulbophyllum subuliferum, commonly known as the needle-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
