Bambusa laydekeri

Bambusa laydekeri, commonly known as the Black Bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast beautifully with its lush green foliage. This bamboo grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a dramatic addition to gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience, Bambusa laydekeri is widely cultivated for both ornamental and landscaping purposes.
