Baccharis grandicapitulata var. grandicapitulata

Baccharis grandicapitulata var. grandicapitulata, commonly known as the broad-headed baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically gray-green, hairy, and aromatic, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry habitats.
