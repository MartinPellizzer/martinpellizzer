Begonia fischeri var. malvacea

Begonia fischeri var. malvacea, commonly known as the Malvaceae begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green and red hues. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
