Brassica napus var. schebalinae

Brassica napus var. schebalinae, commonly known as the Siberian oilseed rape, is a variant of the oilseed rape species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptation to cold climates, making it resilient in harsh environments. The plant produces large, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are alternate and lobed, with a rough texture that distinguishes it from other Brassica species.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both agricultural and industrial applications.
