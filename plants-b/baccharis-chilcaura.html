Baccharis chilcaura

Baccharis chilcaura, commonly known as Chilean baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as shrublands and coastal areas.

Baccharis chilcaura is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
