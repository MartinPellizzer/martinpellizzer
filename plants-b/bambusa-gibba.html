Bambusa gibba

Bambusa gibba, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to 6 meters tall, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types contribute to its widespread cultivation.

Bambusa gibba is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
