Biota orientalis var. ascotensis

Biota orientalis var. ascotensis, commonly known as the Ascot orchid, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its delicate floral display.
