Bromus pinnatus var. sylvaticus

Bromus pinnatus var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in less fertile soils.
