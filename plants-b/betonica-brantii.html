Betonica brantii

Betonica brantii, commonly known as brant's betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Betonica brantii thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
