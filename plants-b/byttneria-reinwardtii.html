Byttneria reinwardtii

Byttneria reinwardtii, commonly known as the African balsa or African kapok tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist savannas and along riverbanks, thriving in areas with high humidity.

The plant is valued for its lightweight wood and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
