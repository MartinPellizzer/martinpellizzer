Bunias paniculata

Bunias paniculata, commonly known as wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and its stems are hairy and upright. It produces clusters of small, yellow flowers arranged in a panicle, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in fields, roadsides, and other areas with disturbed soil.
