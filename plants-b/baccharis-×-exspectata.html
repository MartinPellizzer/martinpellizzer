Baccharis × exspectata

Baccharis × exspectata, commonly known as the hybrid baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
