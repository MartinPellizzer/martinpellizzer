Ballota hispanica

Ballota hispanica, commonly known as Spanish ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

Ballota hispanica is also valued for its role in supporting local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
