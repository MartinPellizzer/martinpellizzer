Brachystegia oblonga

Brachystegia oblonga, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume-like pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is often found in miombo woodlands and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Brachystegia oblonga plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
