Begonia cubensis

Begonia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban begonia, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually dark green with a glossy texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

Begonia cubensis thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
