Begonia × crispa

Begonia × crispa, commonly known as the crisped begonia, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its striking, crinkled leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The foliage is typically deep green with a textured surface, and the plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Due to its hybrid nature, Begonia × crispa is often propagated through stem cuttings to maintain its desirable characteristics.
