Barnadesia hirsuta

Barnadesia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy barnadesia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
