Brosimum belizense

Brosimum belizense, commonly known as the Belize mulberry, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the fig and mulberry. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

The tree is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
