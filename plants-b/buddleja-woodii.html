Buddleja woodii

Buddleja woodii, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Buddleja woodii is also known for its adaptability to harsh environments, making it a resilient species in xerophytic ecosystems.
