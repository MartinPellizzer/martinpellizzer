Berberis spathulata

Berberis spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate cold climates contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
