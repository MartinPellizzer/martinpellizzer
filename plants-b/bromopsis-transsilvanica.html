Bromopsis transsilvanica

Bromopsis transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine, hairy texture.

It produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are arranged in a dense, compact cluster.
