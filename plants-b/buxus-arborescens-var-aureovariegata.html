Buxus arborescens var. aureovariegata

Buxus arborescens var. aureovariegata, commonly known as golden variegated boxwood, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow-white margins, creating a vibrant, variegated appearance. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit, making it ideal for topiary and ornamental landscaping. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in moist, humid environments.

Its slow growth rate and hardiness make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
