Brachiaria brizantha var. ciliata

Brachiaria brizantha var. ciliata, commonly known as signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in pastures and disturbed soils.

Due to its high nutritional value and adaptability, it is widely used as forage for livestock.
