Baja brandegeei

Baja brandegeei, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh, dry environment. Its roots are adapted to absorb water efficiently from deep underground sources.

Baja brandegeei plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various desert animals.
