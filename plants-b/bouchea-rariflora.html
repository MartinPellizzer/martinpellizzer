Bouchea rariflora

Bouchea rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered bouchea, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
