Blancoa caudata

Blancoa caudata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Blancoa caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
