Balsamorhiza × bonseri

Balsamorhiza × bonseri, commonly known as the yellow balsamroot, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped.

Balsamorhiza × bonseri is known for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
