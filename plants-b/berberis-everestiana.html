Berberis everestiana

Berberis everestiana, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Berberis everestiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
