Byrsonima cydoniifolia var. cuneata

Byrsonima cydoniifolia var. cuneata, commonly known as the sour sop or graviola, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense, rounded crown and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the characteristic sour-sop fruit. The fruit has a thick, spiny rind and a soft, segmented interior with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in culinary applications.
