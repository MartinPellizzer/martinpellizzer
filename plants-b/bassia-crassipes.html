Bassia crassipes

Bassia crassipes, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with a sprawling habit and prickly stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is often considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
