Butea pulchra

Butea pulchra, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vivid display. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often flaky and reddish in color. Butea pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
