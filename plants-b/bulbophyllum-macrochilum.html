Bulbophyllum macrochilum

Bulbophyllum macrochilum, commonly known as the large-lipped bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with prominent, elongated petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The orchid's unique floral structure makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
