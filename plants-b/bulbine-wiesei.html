Bulbine wiesei

Bulbine wiesei, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a South African plant belonging to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Bulbine wiesei is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local remedies.
