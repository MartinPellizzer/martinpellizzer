Bignonia subvernicosa

Bignonia subvernicosa, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces showy, orange-red tubular flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
