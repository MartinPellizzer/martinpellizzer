Boronia virgata

Boronia virgata, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly serrated, while the flowers are clustered in umbels.

Boronia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
