Bactris setosa var. santensis

Bactris setosa var. santensis, commonly known as the Santa Rosa palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
