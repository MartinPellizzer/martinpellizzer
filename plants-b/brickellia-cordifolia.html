Brickellia cordifolia

Brickellia cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with orange stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Brickellia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
