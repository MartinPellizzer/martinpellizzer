Barleria popovii

Barleria popovii, commonly known as the Cape barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow tubular flowers, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Barleria popovii is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
