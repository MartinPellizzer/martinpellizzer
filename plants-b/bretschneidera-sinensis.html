Bretschneidera sinensis

Bretschneidera sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various health benefits.
