Bossiaea ovalifolia

Bossiaea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved bossiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Bossiaea ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
