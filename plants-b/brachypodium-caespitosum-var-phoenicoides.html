Brachypodium caespitosum var. phoenicoides

Brachypodium caespitosum var. phoenicoides, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its fibrous root system makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and habitat restoration.
