Blumea elatior

Blumea elatior, commonly known as the greater blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
