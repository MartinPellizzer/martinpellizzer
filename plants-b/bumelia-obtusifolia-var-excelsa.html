Bumelia obtusifolia var. excelsa

Bumelia obtusifolia var. excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
