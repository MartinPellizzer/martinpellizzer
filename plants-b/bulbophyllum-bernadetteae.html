Bulbophyllum bernadetteae

Bulbophyllum bernadetteae, commonly known as the Bernadette's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. It typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural, often humid and shaded environment.

Its blooming period usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
