Bromus inermis var. podolicus

Bromus inermis var. podolicus, commonly known as the Podolic field grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
