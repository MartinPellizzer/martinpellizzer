Blephariglottis cristata f. straminea

Blephariglottis cristata f. straminea, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a rare and ornamental fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features delicate, strap-like fronds that are typically bright yellow to golden in color, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its unique and striking foliage. It prefers shady, humid environments and well-drained, acidic soil.

This fern is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
