Brassica magellanica

Brassica magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often have a distinct mustard scent.

Brassica magellanica is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
