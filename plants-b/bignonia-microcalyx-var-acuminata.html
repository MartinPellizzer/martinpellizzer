Bignonia microcalyx var. acuminata

Bignonia microcalyx var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
