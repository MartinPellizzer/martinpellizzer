Begonia bonthainensis

Begonia bonthainensis, commonly known as the Bonthain's begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and striking red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
