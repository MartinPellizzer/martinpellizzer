Begonia barkleyana

Begonia barkleyana, commonly known as the Barkley's begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia barkleyana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
