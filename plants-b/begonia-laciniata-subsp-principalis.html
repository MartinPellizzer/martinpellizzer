Begonia laciniata subsp. principalis

Begonia laciniata subsp.

principalis, commonly known as the serrated begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
