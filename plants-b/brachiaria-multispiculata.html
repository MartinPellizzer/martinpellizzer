Brachiaria multispiculata

Brachiaria multispiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Brazilian signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, often mat-forming foliage. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.

Brachiaria multispiculata is also valued for its use in forage and erosion control in agricultural settings.
