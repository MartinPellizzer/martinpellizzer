Baccharis tarchonanthoides var. integrifolia

Baccharis tarchonanthoides var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, pale brown seed heads that persist through winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
