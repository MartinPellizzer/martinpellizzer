Bauhinia hypochrysa

Bauhinia hypochrysa, commonly known as the golden bauhinia, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, raceme-like inflorescences that produce yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Bauhinia hypochrysa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
