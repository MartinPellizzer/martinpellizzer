Brachythalamus podocarpus

Brachythalamus podocarpus, commonly known as the African silver fern, is a unique evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and its distinctive, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing its seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
