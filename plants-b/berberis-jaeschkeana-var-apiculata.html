Berberis jaeschkeana var. apiculata

Berberis jaeschkeana var. apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and persistent berries.
