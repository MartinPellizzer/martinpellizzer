Bulbophyllum aubrevillei

Bulbophyllum aubrevillei, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch, and its epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are relatively small but numerous, arranged in dense clusters.

Bulbophyllum aubrevillei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
