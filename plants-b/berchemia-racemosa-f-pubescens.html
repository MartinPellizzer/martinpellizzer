Berchemia racemosa f. pubescens

Berchemia racemosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its cultivar name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid landscapes.
