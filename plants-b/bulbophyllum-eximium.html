Bulbophyllum eximium

Bulbophyllum eximium, commonly known as the Orchid of the Forest, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, characterized by its clustered pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens worldwide.
