Bunium korshinskii

Bunium korshinskii, commonly known as the Siberian fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant features slender, grooved stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. Its small yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Bunium korshinskii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its carminative and digestive benefits.
