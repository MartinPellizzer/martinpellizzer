Berberis burruyacuensis

Berberis burruyacuensis, commonly known as the Peruvian barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
