Brachypodium holcoides

Brachypodium holcoides, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
