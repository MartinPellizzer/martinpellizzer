Bulbophyllum phillipsianum

Bulbophyllum phillipsianum, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
