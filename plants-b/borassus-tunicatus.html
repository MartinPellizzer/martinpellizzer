Borassus tunicatus

Borassus tunicatus, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features a smooth, greyish trunk covered with a fibrous sheath, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are large, fan-shaped, and often used in traditional crafts.

The dwarf palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
