Boscia kalachariensis

Boscia kalachariensis, commonly known as the Kalahari boscia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Boscia kalachariensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
