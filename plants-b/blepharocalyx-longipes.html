Blepharocalyx longipes

Blepharocalyx longipes, commonly known as the longipes hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules as its fruit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
