Bourreria huanita

Bourreria huanita, commonly known as the Huanita tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, temperate climates. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural propagation.
