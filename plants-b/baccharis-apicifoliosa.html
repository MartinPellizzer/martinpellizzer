Baccharis apicifoliosa

Baccharis apicifoliosa, commonly known as the Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
