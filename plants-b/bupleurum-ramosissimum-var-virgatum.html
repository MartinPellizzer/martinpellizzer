Bupleurum ramosissimum var. virgatum

Bupleurum ramosissimum var. virgatum, commonly known as the slender goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is characterized by its branching stems and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
