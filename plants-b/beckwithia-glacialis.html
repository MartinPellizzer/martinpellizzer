Beckwithia glacialis

Beckwithia glacialis, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a unique flowering plant native to the subantarctic region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in harsh, cold environments with limited sunlight and nutrient-poor soils. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is well adapted to its remote habitat.

Beckwithia glacialis plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
