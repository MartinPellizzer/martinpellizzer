Bolanthus cherlerioides

Bolanthus cherlerioides, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are typically narrow and succulent to reduce water loss. Bolanthus cherlerioides is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
