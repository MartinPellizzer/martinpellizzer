Brachiaria tatianae

Brachiaria tatianae, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Tatiana's grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or open habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Brachiaria tatianae is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
