Boehmeria nivea var. tenacissima

Boehmeria nivea var. tenacissima, commonly known as Chinese hemp or ramie, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fibrous stems that are rich in cellulose, making it valuable for textile production. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

The plant's fibrous stalks are harvested for their durable, strong fibers used in making fabrics and paper.
