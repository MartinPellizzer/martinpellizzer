Blepharodon bifidus

Blepharodon bifidus, commonly known as the bifid leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bifid (split) leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and roots that are often covered in a dense, white mycorrhizal sheath.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
