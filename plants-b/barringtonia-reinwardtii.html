Barringtonia reinwardtii

Barringtonia reinwardtii, commonly known as the white star tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers. The flowers are white and emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky substance.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
