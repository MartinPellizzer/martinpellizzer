Bromus mollis var. glabrescens

Bromus mollis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky brome, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are often hairy and arranged in a loose, open structure.

Bromus mollis var. glabrescens is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
