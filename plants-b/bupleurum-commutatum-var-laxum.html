Bupleurum commutatum var. laxum

Bupleurum commutatum var. laxum, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
