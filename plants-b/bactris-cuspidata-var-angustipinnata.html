Bactris cuspidata var. angustipinnata

Bactris cuspidata var. angustipinnata, commonly known as the narrow-pinnate palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive, serrated edge, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local agriculture.
