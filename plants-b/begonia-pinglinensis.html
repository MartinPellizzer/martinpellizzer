Begonia pinglinensis

Begonia pinglinensis, commonly known as the Pinglin begonia, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
