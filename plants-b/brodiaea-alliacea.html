Brodiaea alliacea

Brodiaea alliacea, commonly known as the garlic brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California and southern Oregon.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers in late spring. The plant's bulbs are small and resemble garlic cloves, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Brodiaea alliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
