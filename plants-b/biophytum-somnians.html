Biophytum somnians

Biophytum somnians, commonly known as the "sleeping beauty" or "moonflower," is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Biophytum somnians thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
