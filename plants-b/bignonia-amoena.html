Bignonia amoena

Bignonia amoena, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
