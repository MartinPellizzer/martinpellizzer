Boraginella zeylanica

Boraginella zeylanica, commonly known as the Sri Lankan bugloss, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Boraginella zeylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
