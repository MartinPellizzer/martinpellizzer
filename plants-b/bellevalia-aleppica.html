Bellevalia aleppica

Bellevalia aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Bellevalia aleppica is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
