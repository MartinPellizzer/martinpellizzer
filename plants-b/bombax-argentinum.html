Bombax argentinum

Bombax argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine cotton tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a rounded canopy and smooth, grayish bark. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release fluffy seeds, similar to cotton.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.
