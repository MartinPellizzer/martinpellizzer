Bryonia scabrella

Bryonia scabrella, commonly known as the rough bryony, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by orange-red, spiny fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in hedgerows and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
