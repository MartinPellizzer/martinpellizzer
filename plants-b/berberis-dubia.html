Berberis dubia

Berberis dubia, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The berries are edible but should be consumed with caution due to their mild toxicity.
