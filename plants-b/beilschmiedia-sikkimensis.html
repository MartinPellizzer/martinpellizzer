Beilschmiedia sikkimensis

Beilschmiedia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly in the Sikkim Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
