Braya aenea var. simplicior

Braya aenea var. simplicior, commonly known as the simpleleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It features small, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, often appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This plant is often found in sandy or rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
