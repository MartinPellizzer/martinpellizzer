Betula alba var. fastigiata

Betula alba var. fastigiata, commonly known as the silver birch columnar form, is a variant of the white birch characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features striking silvery-white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are triangular, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest. It is a hardy, fast-growing tree that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structural form.
