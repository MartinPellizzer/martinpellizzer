Brachychiton × vinicolor

Brachychiton × vinicolor, commonly known as the red-and-green flame tree, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Brachychiton australis* and *Brachychiton acutangulus*.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Australia, characterized by its striking, vibrant red and green foliage. The plant produces showy, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Brachychiton × vinicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
