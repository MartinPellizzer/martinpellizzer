Bulbophyllum gyrochilum

Bulbophyllum gyrochilum, commonly known as the spiral lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged petals and a distinctive, lip-shaped structure that is often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its intricate floral morphology.
