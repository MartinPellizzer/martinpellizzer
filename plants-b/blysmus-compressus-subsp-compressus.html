Blysmus compressus subsp. compressus

Blysmus compressus subsp.

compressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
