Bambusa chusque

Bambusa chusque, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that exhibit a distinctive golden-yellow coloration. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and bamboo cultivation.

Bambusa chusque is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
