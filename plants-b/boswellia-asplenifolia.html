Boswellia asplenifolia

Boswellia asplenifolia, commonly known as the Indian frankincense tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
