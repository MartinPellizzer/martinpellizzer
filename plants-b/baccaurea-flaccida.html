Baccaurea flaccida

Baccaurea flaccida, commonly known as the soft baccaurea, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
