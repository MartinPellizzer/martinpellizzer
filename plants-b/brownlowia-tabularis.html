Brownlowia tabularis

Brownlowia tabularis, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and it produces woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
