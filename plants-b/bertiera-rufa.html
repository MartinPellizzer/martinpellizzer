Bertiera rufa

Bertiera rufa, commonly known as the red-barked bertiera, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dark green, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pale yellow or orange fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or rocky habitats.

Bertiera rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
