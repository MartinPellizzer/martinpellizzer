Blyxa quadricostata

Blyxa quadricostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive midrib and parallel veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, usually emerging above the water surface.

Blyxa quadricostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
