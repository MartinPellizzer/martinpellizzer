Borzicactus humboldtii

Borzicactus humboldtii, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems that often develop a reddish tint when exposed to full sun. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise subdued appearance. It is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in desert environments.
