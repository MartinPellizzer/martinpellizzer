Berberis × guernseyensis

Berberis × guernseyensis, commonly known as the Guernsey barberry, is a hybrid shrub resulting from the cross between Berberis vulgaris and Berberis thunbergii.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and vibrant, orange-red berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green surface and serrated edges.

The Guernsey barberry is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive form and seasonal color.
