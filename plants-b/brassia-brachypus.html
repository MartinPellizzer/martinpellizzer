Brassia brachypus

Brassia brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with fleshy roots and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Brassia brachypus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
