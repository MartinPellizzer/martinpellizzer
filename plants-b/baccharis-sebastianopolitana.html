Baccharis sebastianopolitana

Baccharis sebastianopolitana, commonly known as the Sebastianopolis baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
