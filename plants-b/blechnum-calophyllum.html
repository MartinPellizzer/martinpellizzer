Blechnum calophyllum

Blechnum calophyllum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-green fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Blechnum calophyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
