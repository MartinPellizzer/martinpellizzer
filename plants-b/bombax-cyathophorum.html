Bombax cyathophorum

Bombax cyathophorum, commonly known as the African silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds encased in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are palmately lobed and shed seasonally, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and its fibers, which have traditional uses in various cultures.
