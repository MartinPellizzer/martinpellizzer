Bromus sterilis var. purpureus

Bromus sterilis var. purpureus, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of purple to pinkish inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, and the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

This variety is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
