Bulbine vitrea

Bulbine vitrea, commonly known as the glassy bulbine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its common name refers to the glassy appearance of its leaves, which reflect light in a shimmering manner.
