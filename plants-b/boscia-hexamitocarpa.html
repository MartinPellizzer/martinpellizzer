Boscia hexamitocarpa

Boscia hexamitocarpa, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

Boscia hexamitocarpa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
