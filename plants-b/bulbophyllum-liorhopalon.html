Bulbophyllum liorhopalon

Bulbophyllum liorhopalon, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and unique morphology.
