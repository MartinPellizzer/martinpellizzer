Begonia squarrosa

Begonia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often reddish stems that give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces showy, fleshy, and often brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

Begonia squarrosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and floral display.
