Briza brizoides

Briza brizoides, commonly known as the slender quaking grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, arching stems and feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance.

Briza brizoides is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
