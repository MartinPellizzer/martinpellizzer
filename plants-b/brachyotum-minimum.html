Brachyotum minimum

Brachyotum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf goldenbush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring compact, rounded foliage with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Brachyotum minimum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in arid landscapes due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
