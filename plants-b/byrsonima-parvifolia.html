Byrsonima parvifolia

Byrsonima parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wolfberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a tart, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
