Brassia pumila

Brassia pumila, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous stem with a few leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. Its unique appearance and delicate blooms make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Brassia pumila is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions, provided it receives proper care.
