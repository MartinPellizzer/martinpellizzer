Begonia gemella

Begonia gemella, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia gemella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
