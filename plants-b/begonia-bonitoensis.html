Begonia bonitoensis

Begonia bonitoensis, commonly known as the Bonito Island begonia, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from red to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
