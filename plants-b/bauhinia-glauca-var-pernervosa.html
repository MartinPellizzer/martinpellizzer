Bauhinia glauca var. pernervosa

Bauhinia glauca var. pernervosa, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its unique leaf structure, with prominent veins, contributes to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
