Bromus propendens

Bromus propendens, commonly known as the leaning brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a rough texture.

Bromus propendens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
