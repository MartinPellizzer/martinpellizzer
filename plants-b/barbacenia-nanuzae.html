Barbacenia nanuzae

Barbacenia nanuzae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant with striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to grow.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
