Bouteloua ramosa

Bouteloua ramosa, commonly known as hairy grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its seed heads are cylindrical and often bend outward, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hairy grama is an important component of native grassland ecosystems and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental qualities.
