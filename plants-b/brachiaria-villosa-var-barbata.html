Brachiaria villosa var. barbata

Brachiaria villosa var. barbata, commonly known as hairy brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaf blades, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Brachiaria villosa var. barbata is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
