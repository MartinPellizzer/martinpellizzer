Becium fimbriatum

Becium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed becium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This plant is often found in dry forest habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
