Baccharis pedicellata

Baccharis pedicellata, commonly known as blue curl, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Baccharis pedicellata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
