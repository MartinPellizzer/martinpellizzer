Byrsonima crassifolia var. peruviana

Byrsonima crassifolia var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian grapple fruit or curuba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, thick-leaved foliage and produces a distinctive fruit with a hard, spiny exterior. The leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland regions.

Its fruit, while not widely consumed, has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
