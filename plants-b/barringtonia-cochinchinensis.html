Barringtonia cochinchinensis

Barringtonia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant produces a fruit that is typically covered in a thick, spiky rind, containing edible, sweet flesh. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The tree is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
