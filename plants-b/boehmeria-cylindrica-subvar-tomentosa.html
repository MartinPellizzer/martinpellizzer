Boehmeria cylindrica subvar. tomentosa

Boehmeria cylindrica subvar. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly flaxnet, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fibrous stems are valued for their strength and are used in traditional crafts and textiles.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
