Billotia marginata var. glabrata

Billotia marginata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-barked billotia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
