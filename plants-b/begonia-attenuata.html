Begonia attenuata

Begonia attenuata, commonly known as the trailing begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and often has a trailing stem that can be used for decorative purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
