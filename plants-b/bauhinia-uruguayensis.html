Bauhinia uruguayensis

Bauhinia uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
