Bursera steyermarkii

Bursera steyermarkii, commonly known as the Mexican bursera, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, typical of many Bursera species.

This plant is adapted to dry, tropical climates and is often found in limestone soils.
