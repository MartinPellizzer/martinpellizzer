Baccharis caespitosa subsp. caespitosa

Baccharis caespitosa subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as bluebrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and profuse blue-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
