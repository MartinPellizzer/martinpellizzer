Blepharandra cretacea var. composita

Blepharandra cretacea var. composita, commonly known as the variegated arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves with distinctive variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

The varietal form 'composita' is noted for its more pronounced leaf patterns compared to the typical species.
