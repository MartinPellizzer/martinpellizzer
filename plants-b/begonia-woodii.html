Begonia woodii

Begonia woodii, commonly known as the Wood's begonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and red. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Begonia woodii is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in humid, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
