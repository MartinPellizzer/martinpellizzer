Basella alba var. subcordata

Basella alba var. subcordata, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by oval, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
