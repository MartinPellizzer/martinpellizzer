Berberis beesiana

Berberis beesiana, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
