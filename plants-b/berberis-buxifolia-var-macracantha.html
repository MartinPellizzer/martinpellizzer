Berberis buxifolia var. macracantha

Berberis buxifolia var. macracantha, commonly known as the broad-leaf barberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.
