Bomarea andimarcana subsp. densifolia

Bomarea andimarcana subsp.

densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved bomarea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a dense, velvety texture, giving it its subspecies name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by botanists and horticulturists.
