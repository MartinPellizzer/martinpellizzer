Byrsocarpus usaramensis

Byrsocarpus usaramensis, commonly known as the Usaramensis bursera, is a small, evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by distinctive, fleshy fruits that contain a single, smooth-seeded kernel.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
