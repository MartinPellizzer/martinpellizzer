Buccella bucculenta

Buccella bucculenta, commonly known as the bursar's bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often lobed and may have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
