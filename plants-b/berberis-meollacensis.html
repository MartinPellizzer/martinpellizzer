Berberis meollacensis

Berberis meollacensis, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It features spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
