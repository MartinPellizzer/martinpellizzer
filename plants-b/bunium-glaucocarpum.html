Bunium glaucocarpum

Bunium glaucocarpum, commonly known as the alpine fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its seeds are small and contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Bunium glaucocarpum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
