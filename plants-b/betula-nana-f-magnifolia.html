Betula nana f. magnifolia

Betula nana f. magnifolia, commonly known as the dwarf birch with variegated leaves, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a compact species, typically growing to a height of about 30 cm, with a dense, rounded form. The leaves are small, ovate, and often exhibit striking white or yellow variegation, which enhances its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

This plant is well adapted to harsh climates and is an important component of boreal and arctic ecosystems.
