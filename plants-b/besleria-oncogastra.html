Besleria oncogastra

Besleria oncogastra, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation and indoor gardening.
