Butea macroptera

Butea macroptera, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has long, slender leaves and produces woody, spiral-shaped pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its timber and dye.
