Becium obovatum subsp. cordatum

Becium obovatum subsp.

cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf becium, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
