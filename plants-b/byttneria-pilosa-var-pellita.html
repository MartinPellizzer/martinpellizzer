Byttneria pilosa var. pellita

Byttneria pilosa var. pellita, commonly known as the woolly byttneria, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
