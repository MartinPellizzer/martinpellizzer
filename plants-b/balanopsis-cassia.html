Balanopsis cassia

Balanopsis cassia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Balanopsis cassia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
