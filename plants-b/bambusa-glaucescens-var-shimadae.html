Bambusa glaucescens var. shimadae

Bambusa glaucescens var. shimadae, commonly known as the Shimada bamboo, is a clump-forming grass that originates from Japan.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue sheaths and pale green culms, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and it is relatively cold-hardy compared to other bamboo species.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant aesthetic in gardens and landscapes.
