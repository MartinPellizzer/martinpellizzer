Barringtonia rhodochlamys

Barringtonia rhodochlamys, commonly known as the pink hibiscus tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The tree produces a capsule fruit that splits open to release its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
