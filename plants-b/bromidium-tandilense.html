Bromidium tandilense

Bromidium tandilense, commonly known as the Tandil bulb orchid, is a species of orchid native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique bulb-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant typically produces a single flower with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
