Begonia verticillata

Begonia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique and decorative appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
