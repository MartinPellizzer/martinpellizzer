Brickellia cardiophylla

Brickellia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost heart-shaped appearance.

Brickellia cardiophylla is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
