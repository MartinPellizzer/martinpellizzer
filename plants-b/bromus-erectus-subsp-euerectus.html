Bromus erectus subsp. euerectus

Bromus erectus subsp.

euerectus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, two-lobed spikelets. Bromus erectus subsp.

euerectus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
