Barleria polhillii subsp. turkanae

Barleria polhillii subsp.

turkanae, commonly known as Turkana barleria, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of East Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The flowers are typically bright blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
