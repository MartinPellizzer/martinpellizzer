Boehmeria nana

Boehmeria nana, commonly known as dwarf Chinese grass, is a small herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Boehmeria nana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Its fibrous stems have been historically used in traditional crafts and papermaking.
