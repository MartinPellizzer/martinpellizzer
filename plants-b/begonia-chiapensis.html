Begonia chiapensis

Begonia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia chiapensis is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.
