Balsamiflua litwinowiana

Balsamiflua litwinowiana, commonly known as the Litwinowian balsam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
