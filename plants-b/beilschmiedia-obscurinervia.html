Beilschmiedia obscurinervia

Beilschmiedia obscurinervia, commonly known as the dark-veined beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent, dark veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
