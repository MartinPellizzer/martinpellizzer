Baphia incerta subsp. incerta

Baphia incerta subsp.

incerta, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with alternate, pinnate arrangement, and its bark is often fissured and smooth. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
