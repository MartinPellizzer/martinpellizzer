Bacopa crenata

Bacopa crenata, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soil and is often found near water sources such as ponds and streams.

Bacopa crenata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
