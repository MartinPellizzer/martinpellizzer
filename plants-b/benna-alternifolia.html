Benna alternifolia

Benna alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface.

Benna alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
