Balsamina lutea

Balsamina lutea, commonly known as the golden balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the leaf axils.

Balsamina lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
