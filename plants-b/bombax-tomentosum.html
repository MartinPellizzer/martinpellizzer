Bombax tomentosum

Bombax tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly silk-cotton tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fluffy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The tree produces a cotton-like fruit that contains seeds surrounded by a fibrous, cottony material. It has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves during the dry season.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
