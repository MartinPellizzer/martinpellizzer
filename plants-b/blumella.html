Blumella

Blumella, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Blumella ciliata, which is native to southern Africa. The plants are characterized by their small, delicate flowers that often form umbel-like clusters. They typically grow in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Blumella species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique floral display.
