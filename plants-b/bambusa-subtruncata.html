Bambusa subtruncata

Bambusa subtruncata, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-colored culms, which become more intense as the plant matures. The leaves are narrow and pointed, typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters in length. This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of growth.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
