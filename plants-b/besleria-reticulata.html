Besleria reticulata

Besleria reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive network of veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its ornamental value lies in both its foliage and its vibrant floral display.
