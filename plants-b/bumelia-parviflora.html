Bumelia parviflora

Bumelia parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower bumelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
