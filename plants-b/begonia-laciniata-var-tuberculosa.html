Begonia laciniata var. tuberculosa

Begonia laciniata var. tuberculosa, commonly known as the tubercled begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The foliage is often adorned with raised tubercles, giving it a textured appearance. It produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and unique leaf structure.
