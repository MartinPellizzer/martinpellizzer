Baccharis deblei

Baccharis deblei, commonly known as the Paraguay feverfew, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Baccharis deblei is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in regions with seasonal rainfall.
