Bromopsis latiglumis

Bromopsis latiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Bromopsis latiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
