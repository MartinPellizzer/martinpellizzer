Byrsonima barkleyana

Byrsonima barkleyana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceriman* tree, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
