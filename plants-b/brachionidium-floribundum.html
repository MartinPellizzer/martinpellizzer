Brachionidium floribundum

Brachionidium floribundum, commonly known as the flowering brachionidium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
