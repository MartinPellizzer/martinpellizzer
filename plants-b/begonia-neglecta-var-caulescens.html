Begonia neglecta var. caulescens

Begonia neglecta var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.
