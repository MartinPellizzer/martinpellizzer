Bulbostylis micrantha

Bulbostylis micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered bulbostylis, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish tinge at the base.

Bulbostylis micrantha is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
