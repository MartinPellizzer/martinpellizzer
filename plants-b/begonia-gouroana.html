Begonia gouroana

Begonia gouroana, commonly known as the Gouroana begonia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its attractive, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rich green color, often with a slight sheen. The flowers, which appear in clusters, are usually red or pink and bloom during the warmer months.

This plant prefers shaded to partially shaded environments and well-draining soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a houseplant.
