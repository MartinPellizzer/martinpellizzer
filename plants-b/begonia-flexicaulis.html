Begonia flexicaulis

Begonia flexicaulis, commonly known as the curlleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically produces small, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-draining soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Begonia flexicaulis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
