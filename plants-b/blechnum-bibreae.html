Blechnum bibreae

Blechnum bibreae, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

Blechnum bibreae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
