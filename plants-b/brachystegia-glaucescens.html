Brachystegia glaucescens

Brachystegia glaucescens, commonly known as the African brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, glaucous leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod fruits that contain several seeds. It typically grows in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental features, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
