Begonia spraguei

Begonia spraguei, commonly known as the Sprague's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
