Bignonia craterophora

Bignonia craterophora, commonly known as the mountain trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Bignonia craterophora is typically found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
