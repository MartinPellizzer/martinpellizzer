Buddleja microcephala

Buddleja microcephala, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is highly ornamental, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Buddleja microcephala is also known for its adaptability and resilience in various garden conditions.
