Berberis multiflora var. calvescens

Berberis multiflora var. calvescens, commonly known as the California barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for erosion control and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
