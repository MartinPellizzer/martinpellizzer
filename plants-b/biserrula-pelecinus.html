Biserrula pelecinus

Biserrula pelecinus, commonly known as the serrated pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop.

Its seeds are small and oval, typically harvested for livestock feed or seed propagation.
