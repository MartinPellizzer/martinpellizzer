Begonia panjangfolia

Begonia panjangfolia, commonly known as the long-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Begonia panjangfolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
