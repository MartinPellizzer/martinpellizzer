Bursaria incana

Bursaria incana, commonly known as the woolly bursaria, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its grayish, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Bursaria incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
