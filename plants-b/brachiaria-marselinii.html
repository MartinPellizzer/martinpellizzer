Brachiaria marselinii

Brachiaria marselinii, commonly known as Marselin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Brachiaria marselinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
