Banisterodes macintyrii

Banisterodes macintyrii, commonly known as the Macintyris beardroot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb with a distinctive, hairy stem and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
