Bidens tereticaulis

Bidens tereticaulis, commonly known as slender false beggar ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by bristly, spiny seed capsules.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along creek banks.
