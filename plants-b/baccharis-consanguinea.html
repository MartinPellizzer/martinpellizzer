Baccharis consanguinea

Baccharis consanguinea, commonly known as the red baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in heads.

Baccharis consanguinea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
