Bromus bromoideus var. inermis

Bromus bromoideus var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing several small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Due to its adaptability and rapid growth, it is both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
