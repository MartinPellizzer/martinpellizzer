Betula finituma

Betula finituma, commonly known as the Japanese white birch, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant trunk and white, peeling bark that reveals a pale yellowish layer beneath. The tree produces small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Betula finituma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
