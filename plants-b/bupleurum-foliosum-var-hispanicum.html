Bupleurum foliosum var. hispanicum

Bupleurum foliosum var. hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine.
