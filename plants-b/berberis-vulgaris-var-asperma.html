Berberis vulgaris var. asperma

Berberis vulgaris var. asperma, commonly known as the rough barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a matte green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
