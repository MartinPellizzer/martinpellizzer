Bidens pilosa var. dubius

Bidens pilosa var. dubius, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with two lobes, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
