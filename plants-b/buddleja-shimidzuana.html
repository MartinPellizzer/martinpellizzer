Buddleja shimidzuana

Buddleja shimidzuana, commonly known as the Japanese buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, spike-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Buddleja shimidzuana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
