Baimo cirrhosa

Baimo cirrhosa, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its long, hairy stems and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use. Baimo cirrhosa is often grown as a cover crop or for its edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
