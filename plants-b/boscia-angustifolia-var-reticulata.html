Boscia angustifolia var. reticulata

Boscia angustifolia var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated boscia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale green, reticulated bark that gives it its common name. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
