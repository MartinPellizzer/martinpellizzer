Bursa alpestris

Bursa alpestris, commonly known as the alpine burr, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually small and pale, often appearing in clusters, while the leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets.

Bursa alpestris plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and provides food for various insect species.
