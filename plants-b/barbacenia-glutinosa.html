Barbacenia glutinosa

Barbacenia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky leaf vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that exude a sticky substance when damaged, a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.

Barbacenia glutinosa thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
