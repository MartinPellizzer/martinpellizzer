Burmannia liberica

Burmannia liberica, commonly known as the red swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the hibiscus genus. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Burmannia liberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
