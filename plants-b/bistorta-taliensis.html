Bistorta taliensis

Bistorta taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensian bistort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often reddish stems. The plant features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies in its native habitat. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Bistorta taliensis is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.
