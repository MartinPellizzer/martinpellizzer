Buchnera foetida

Buchnera foetida, commonly known as the stinking vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small oval-shaped leaflets.

Buchnera foetida is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
