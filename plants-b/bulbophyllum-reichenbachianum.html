Bulbophyllum reichenbachianum

Bulbophyllum reichenbachianum, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar appearance of a "flower on a stick," is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs that bear a single, showy, fragrant flower. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and sought-after specimen in botanical collections.
