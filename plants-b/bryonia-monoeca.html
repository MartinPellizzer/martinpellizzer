Bryonia monoeca

Bryonia monoeca, commonly known as the common bryony, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Bryonia monoeca contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
