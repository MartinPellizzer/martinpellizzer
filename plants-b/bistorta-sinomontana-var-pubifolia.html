Bistorta sinomontana var. pubifolia

Bistorta sinomontana var. pubifolia, commonly known as the pubescent bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are covered in soft, pubescent hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against strong sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
