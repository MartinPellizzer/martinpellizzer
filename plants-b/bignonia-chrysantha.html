Bignonia chrysantha

Bignonia chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges. Its stems are woody and can climb by twining around supports.

Bignonia chrysantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
