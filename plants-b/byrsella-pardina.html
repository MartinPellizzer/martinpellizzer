Byrsella pardina

Byrsella pardina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
