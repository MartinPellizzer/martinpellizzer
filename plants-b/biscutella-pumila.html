Biscutella pumila

Biscutella pumila, commonly known as the dwarf biscuitroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often with succulent leaves that store water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Biscutella pumila is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
