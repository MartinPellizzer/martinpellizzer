Bauhinia grevei

Bauhinia grevei, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnate leaves and clusters of showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Bauhinia grevei is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
