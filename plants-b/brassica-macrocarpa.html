Brassica macrocarpa

Brassica macrocarpa, commonly known as the tree mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cabbage and mustard. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Brassica macrocarpa can be used in cooking, similar to other members of its family.
