Bidens linearifolia

Bidens linearifolia, commonly known as slender beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has hairy stems and produces seeds with bristly appendages that aid in wind dispersal.

Bidens linearifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
