Brunfelsia grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Brunfelsia grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the blue jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
