Bursera bipinnata

Bursera bipinnata, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with two pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white. Its bark and resin have been used traditionally for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
