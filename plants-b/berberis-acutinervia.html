Berberis acutinervia

Berberis acutinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with sharply serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often considered bitter. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and winter interest, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Berberis acutinervia is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its tolerance to cold climates.
