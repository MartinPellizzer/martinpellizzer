Bidens minima

Bidens minima, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. Bidens minima is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
