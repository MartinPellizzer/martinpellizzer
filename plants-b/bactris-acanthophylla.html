Bactris acanthophylla

Bactris acanthophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, reddish-pink leaves that contrast with its slender, upright trunk. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine by local communities for its purported health benefits.

Bactris acanthophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
