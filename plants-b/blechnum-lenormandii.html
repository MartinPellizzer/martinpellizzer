Blechnum lenormandii

Blechnum lenormandii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in rows on the underside of the fronds.

Blechnum lenormandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
