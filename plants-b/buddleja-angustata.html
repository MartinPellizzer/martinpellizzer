Buddleja angustata

Buddleja angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces fragrant, spike-like clusters of purple flowers in late summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Buddleja angustata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
