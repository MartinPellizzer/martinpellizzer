Bulbophyllum rothschildianum

Bulbophyllum rothschildianum, commonly known as the Rothschild's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring hairy stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a striking red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
