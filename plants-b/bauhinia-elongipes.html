Bauhinia elongipes

Bauhinia elongipes, commonly known as the longpod bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pods and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, while the leaves are alternate and compound.

Bauhinia elongipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
