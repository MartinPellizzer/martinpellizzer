Berberis bhutanensis

Berberis bhutanensis, commonly known as the Bhutan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
