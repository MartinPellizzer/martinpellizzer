Bidens purpusiorum

Bidens purpusiorum, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, which gives it its common name. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens purpusiorum is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
