Bromopsis nachiczevanica

Bromopsis nachiczevanica, commonly known as the Armenian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Bromopsis nachiczevanica is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
