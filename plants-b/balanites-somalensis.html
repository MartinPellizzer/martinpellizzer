Balanites somalensis

Balanites somalensis, commonly known as the Somali Desert Date, is a shrub native to the arid regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and woody pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and thick, waxy leaves to minimize water loss. Its fruit is often used as a food source by local communities and wildlife.

Balanites somalensis plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting biodiversity in its ecosystem.
