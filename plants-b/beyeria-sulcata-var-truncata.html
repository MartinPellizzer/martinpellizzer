Beyeria sulcata var. truncata

Beyeria sulcata var. truncata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, well-draining soil conditions. It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
