Bureava

Bureava, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Oxalis and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Bureava has a shallow root system and is often found in temperate climates.
