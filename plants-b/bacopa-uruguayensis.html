Bacopa uruguayensis

Bacopa uruguayensis, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along stream banks. Its stems are square and often hairy, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Bacopa uruguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
