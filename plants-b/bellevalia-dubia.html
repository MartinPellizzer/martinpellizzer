Bellevalia dubia

Bellevalia dubia, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
