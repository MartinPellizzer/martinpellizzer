Billbergia regeliana

Billbergia regeliana, commonly known as the pink ponytail palm, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It features a thick, fleshy trunk that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its most distinctive characteristic is the long, spiraling rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that give it a unique, palm-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding visual interest.

It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to adapt to various light conditions.
