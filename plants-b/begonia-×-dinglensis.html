Begonia × dinglensis

Begonia × dinglensis, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals, which are used to attract pollinators. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Begonia × dinglensis is popular in horticulture for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in gardens and indoor settings.
