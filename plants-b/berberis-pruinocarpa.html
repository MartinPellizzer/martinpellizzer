Berberis pruinocarpa

Berberis pruinocarpa, commonly known as the Pruinocarpa barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, covered in a distinctive whitish, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and winter interest.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
