Bromelia comata

Bromelia comata, commonly known as the hairy bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant features dense, fibrous foliage that gives it a distinctive, bushy appearance. It produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers.

Bromelia comata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
