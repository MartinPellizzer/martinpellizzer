Bipinnula

Bipinnula, commonly known as the small-leafed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves.

Its fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with multiple pairs of pinnae that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Bipinnula has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its propagation in aquatic ecosystems.
