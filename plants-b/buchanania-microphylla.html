Buchanania microphylla

Buchanania microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Buchanania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
