Bulbophyllum oreonastes

Bulbophyllum oreonastes, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid and shaded environments. It produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often pale yellow or greenish-white, with a unique, spurred lip. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
