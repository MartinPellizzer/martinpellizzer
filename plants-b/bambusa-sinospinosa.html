Bambusa sinospinosa

Bambusa sinospinosa, commonly known as the Chinese thorned bamboo, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features sharp, spiny nodes along its stems, which give it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a V-shaped pattern along the culms.

This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardiness in various climates.
