Brodiaea grandiflora var. macrantha

Brodiaea grandiflora var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered brodiaea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
