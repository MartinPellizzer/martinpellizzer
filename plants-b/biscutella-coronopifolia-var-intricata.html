Biscutella coronopifolia var. intricata

Biscutella coronopifolia var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate biscutella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
