Berberis alpicola

Berberis alpicola, commonly known as the alpine barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features thorny stems and small, clustered leaves, with yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its berries, which are red and edible, mature in late summer and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Berberis alpicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
