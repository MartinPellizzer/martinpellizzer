Briza media f. pauciflora

Briza media f. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender quaking grass, is a compact, clump-forming perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with small, greenish-brown spikelets that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces numerous tiny flowers in late spring to early summer, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, attractive native plant.
