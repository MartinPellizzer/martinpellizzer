Benzoin strychnifolium

Benzoin strychnifolium, commonly known as the Indian benzoin or balm of Gilead, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces a fragrant, resinous sap that is harvested for its aromatic properties. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its resin is used in perfumery, traditional medicine, and as a fixative in incense.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its valuable gum resin.
