Berberis sumapazana

Berberis sumapazana, commonly known as the Sumapaz barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
