Byrsonima portoricensis

Byrsonima portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican cherry or *mamey* tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
