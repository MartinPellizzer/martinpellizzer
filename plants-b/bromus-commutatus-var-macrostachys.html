Bromus commutatus var. macrostachys

Bromus commutatus var. macrostachys, commonly known as the giant brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and coarse, fibrous leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and forage management.
