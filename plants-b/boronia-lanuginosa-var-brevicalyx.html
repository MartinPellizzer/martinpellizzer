Boronia lanuginosa var. brevicalyx

Boronia lanuginosa var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the woolly boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with small, elliptical leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and fragrance.
