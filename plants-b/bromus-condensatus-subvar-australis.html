Bromus condensatus subvar. australis

Bromus condensatus subvar. australis, commonly known as the Australian dense brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a compact panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is adapted to dry conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
