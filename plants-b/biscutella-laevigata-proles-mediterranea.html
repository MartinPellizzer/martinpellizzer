Biscutella laevigata proles mediterranea

Biscutella laevigata proles mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are oblanceolate and covered with fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
