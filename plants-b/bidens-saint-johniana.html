Bidens saint-johniana

Bidens saint-johniana, commonly known as the Saint John's weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, narrow, and hairy leaves. Its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Bidens saint-johniana is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
