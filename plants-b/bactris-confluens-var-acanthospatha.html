Bactris confluens var. acanthospatha

Bactris confluens var. acanthospatha, commonly known as the red-berried palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and distinctive red fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its fruit, which contains a single seed, is an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
