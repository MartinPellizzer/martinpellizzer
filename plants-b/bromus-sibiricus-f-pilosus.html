Bromus sibiricus f. pilosus

Bromus sibiricus f. pilosus, commonly known as the Siberian brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, slightly hairy stems. The leaves are narrow and often covered with short, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.
