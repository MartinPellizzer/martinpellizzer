Bromus erectus

Bromus erectus, commonly known as the upright brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields, roadsides, and meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Bromus erectus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
