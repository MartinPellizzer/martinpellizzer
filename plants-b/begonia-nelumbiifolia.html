Begonia nelumbiifolia

Begonia nelumbiifolia, commonly known as the water begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble water lilies, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet, shady environments, often near water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of pink or white, and bloom throughout the growing season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
