Bergera inodora

Bergera inodora, commonly known as the African sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in harsh conditions.

Bergera inodora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
