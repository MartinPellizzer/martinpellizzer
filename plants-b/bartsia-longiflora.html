Bartsia longiflora

Bartsia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered bartsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue on the underside. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers are borne in clusters and bloom from May to July.

Bartsia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
