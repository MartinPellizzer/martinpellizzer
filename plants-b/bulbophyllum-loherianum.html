Bulbophyllum loherianum

Bulbophyllum loherianum, commonly known as the Loher's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often pale green or white with yellow markings. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates.

Bulbophyllum loherianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
