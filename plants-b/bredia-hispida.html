Bredia hispida

Bredia hispida, commonly known as the rough-barked bledia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are leathery and have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Bredia hispida is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and unique aesthetic.
