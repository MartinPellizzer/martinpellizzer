Brauneria atrorubens

Brauneria atrorubens, commonly known as the red-tipped bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals with a deep orange throat, which contrast beautifully with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Brauneria atrorubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique and colorful display.
