Boscia pachyandra

Boscia pachyandra, commonly known as the thick-leaved boschia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Boscia pachyandra plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
