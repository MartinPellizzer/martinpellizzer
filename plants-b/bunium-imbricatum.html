Bunium imbricatum

Bunium imbricatum, commonly known as the mountain fennel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing at high altitudes. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pungent aroma similar to that of fennel.

Bunium imbricatum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
