Bulbocodium broteri

Bulbocodium broteri, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows from a bulb-like structure called a corm. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils or open grasslands.
