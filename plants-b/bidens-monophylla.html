Bidens monophylla

Bidens monophylla, commonly known as the one-leaved beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, bristly stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a distinctive shape that aids in identification.

Bidens monophylla is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
