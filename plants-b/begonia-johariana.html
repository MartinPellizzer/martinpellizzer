Begonia johariana

Begonia johariana, commonly known as the Kashmir begonia, is a striking flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that range in color from deep green to dark purple. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
