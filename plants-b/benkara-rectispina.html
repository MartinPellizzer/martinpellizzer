Benkara rectispina

Benkara rectispina, commonly known as the slender spined begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with prominent spines along the margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its growth habit is upright, with stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height under optimal conditions.

Benkara rectispina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for best foliage development.
