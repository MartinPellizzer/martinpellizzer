Bletia shepherdii

Bletia shepherdii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has a pseudobulb-like stem and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
