Beilschmiedia gaboonensis

Beilschmiedia gaboonensis, commonly known as Gaboon beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local craftsmanship and construction.
