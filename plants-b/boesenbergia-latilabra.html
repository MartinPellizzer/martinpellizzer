Boesenbergia latilabra

Boesenbergia latilabra, commonly known as the broad-leafed ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features slender, aromatic rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
