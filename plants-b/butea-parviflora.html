Butea parviflora

Butea parviflora, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds.

Butea parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
