Bolbitis appendiculata subsp. vivipara

Bolbitis appendiculata subsp.

vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous shield fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped fronds, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy scales.

One of its most notable botanical features is its viviparity, where sporelings develop on the underside of the fronds before being released. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest undergrowth.

Its reproductive strategy and morphological traits make it an intriguing subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
