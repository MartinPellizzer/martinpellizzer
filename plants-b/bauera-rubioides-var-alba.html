Bauera rubioides var. alba

Bauera rubioides var. alba, commonly known as the white bauera, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that displays a striking contrast of deep green and white veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in both native and horticultural gardens.
