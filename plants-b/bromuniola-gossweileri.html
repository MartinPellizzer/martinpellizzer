Bromuniola gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's bromeliad, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water, a feature common among bromeliads. Its foliage is typically covered in dense, woolly hairs that provide insulation and aid in moisture retention.
