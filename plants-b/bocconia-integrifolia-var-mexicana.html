Bocconia integrifolia var. mexicana

Bocconia integrifolia var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican bocconia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
