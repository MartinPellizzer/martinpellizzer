Bulbophyllum bryophytoides

Bulbophyllum bryophytoides, commonly known as the moss-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like growth habit, with small, clustered pseudobulbs that resemble moss in appearance. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
