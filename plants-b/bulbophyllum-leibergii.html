Bulbophyllum leibergii

Bulbophyllum leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are borne on racemes with a distinctive coloration.

Bulbophyllum leibergii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral morphology.
