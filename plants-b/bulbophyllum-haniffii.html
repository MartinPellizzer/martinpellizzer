Bulbophyllum haniffii

Bulbophyllum haniffii, commonly known as the Haniff's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Bulbophyllum haniffii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of forests.
