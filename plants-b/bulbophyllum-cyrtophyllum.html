Bulbophyllum cyrtophyllum

Bulbophyllum cyrtophyllum, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower spike and small, clustered blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-like and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The orchid's flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, showcasing its adaptation to tropical climates.
