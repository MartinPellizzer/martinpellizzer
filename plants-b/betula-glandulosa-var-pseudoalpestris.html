Betula glandulosa var. pseudoalpestris

Betula glandulosa var. pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the white-barked birch, is a variant of the paper birch characterized by its distinctive white, peeling bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with serrated margins. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
