Berberis consimilis

Berberis consimilis, commonly known as the similar barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that mature in late summer. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting berries, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains berberine, a compound with potential health benefits.
