Breynia racemosa var. aromatica

Breynia racemosa var. aromatica, commonly known as fragrant breynia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Breynia racemosa var. aromatica is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
