Buphthalmum asperrimum

Buphthalmum asperrimum, commonly known as the rough-eyed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bristly seed heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is known for its distinctive, rough-textured seed capsules that aid in wind dispersal.
