Begonia pentaphragmifolia

Begonia pentaphragmifolia, commonly known as the five-lobed begonia, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a sought-after specimen for botanical gardens and collectors.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
