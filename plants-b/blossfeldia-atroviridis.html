Blossfeldia atroviridis

Blossfeldia atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined pineapple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, a unique trait among many cactus species. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
