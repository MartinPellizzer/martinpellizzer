Babiana scabrifolia

Babiana scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf babiana, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, spurred flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Babiana scabrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
