Bauhinia cumanensis

Bauhinia cumanensis, commonly known as the velvetwood or velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a hand. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a flat, elongated pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Bauhinia cumanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
