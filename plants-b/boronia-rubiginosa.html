Boronia rubiginosa

Boronia rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive reddish or rusty coloration on the underside.

Boronia rubiginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
