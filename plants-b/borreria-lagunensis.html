Borreria lagunensis

Borreria lagunensis, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Borreria lagunensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate floral display.
