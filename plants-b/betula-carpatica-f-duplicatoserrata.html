Betula carpatica f. duplicatoserrata

Betula carpatica f. duplicatoserrata, commonly known as the double-toothed birch, is a unique variant of the European birch.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring two rows of serrations along the margin. The tree typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a subtle peeling texture that becomes more pronounced with age.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
