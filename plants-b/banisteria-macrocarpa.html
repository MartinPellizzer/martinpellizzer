Banisteria macrocarpa

Banisteria macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody, pendulous fruit capsules that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
