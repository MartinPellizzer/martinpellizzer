Barkhausia caroliniana var. ramosa

Barkhausia caroliniana var. ramosa, commonly known as the Carolina rosewood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its fine grain and durability. The plant produces small, showy pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
