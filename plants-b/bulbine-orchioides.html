Bulbine orchioides

Bulbine orchioides, commonly known as the orchid bulbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
