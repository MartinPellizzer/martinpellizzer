Bignonia lactiflora

Bignonia lactiflora, commonly known as crown vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia lactiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
