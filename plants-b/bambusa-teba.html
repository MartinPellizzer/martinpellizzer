Bambusa teba

Bambusa teba, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface that gives the plant a striking appearance. This bamboo is highly valued for its use in construction, papermaking, and as an ornamental plant.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
