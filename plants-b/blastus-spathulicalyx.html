Blastus spathulicalyx

Blastus spathulicalyx, commonly known as the broad-leafed blastus, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in humid environments and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in warm climates.
