Berberis muliensis var. atuntzeana

Berberis muliensis var. atuntzeana, commonly known as the Atuntzean barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
