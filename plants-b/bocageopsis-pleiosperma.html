Bocageopsis pleiosperma

Bocageopsis pleiosperma, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Bocageopsis pleiosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic features.
