Baccharis argentina

Baccharis argentina, commonly known as Argentine baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically grouped in clusters, which are visited by various pollinators. Its woody stems and dense foliage make it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

Baccharis argentina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
