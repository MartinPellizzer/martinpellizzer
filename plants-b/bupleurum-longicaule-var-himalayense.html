Bupleurum longicaule var. himalayense

Bupleurum longicaule var. himalayense, commonly known as Himalayan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It features a slender, branching stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
