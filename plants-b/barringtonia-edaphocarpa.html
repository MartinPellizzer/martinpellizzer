Barringtonia edaphocarpa

Barringtonia edaphocarpa, commonly known as the swamp banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit that contains a single seed. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a strong, sweet fragrance at night.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
