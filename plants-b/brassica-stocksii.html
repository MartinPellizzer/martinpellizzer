Brassica stocksii

Brassica stocksii, commonly known as the Stocks plant, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of cultivation.
