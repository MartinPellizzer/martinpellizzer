Bignonia barbata

Bignonia barbata, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
