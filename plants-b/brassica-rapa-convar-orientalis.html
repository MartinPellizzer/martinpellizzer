Brassica rapa convar. orientalis

Brassica rapa convar. orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves and edible flowering stems and buds. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and cauliflower. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich foliage and reproductive structures.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in Asian cuisines and agricultural practices.
