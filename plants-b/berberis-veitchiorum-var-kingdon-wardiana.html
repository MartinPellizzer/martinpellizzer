Berberis veitchiorum var. kingdon-wardiana

Berberis veitchiorum var. kingdon-wardiana, commonly known as the Kingdon-Ward barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
