Bumelia lanuginosa subsp. texana

Bumelia lanuginosa subsp.

texana, commonly known as the Texas bumelia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woodlands. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
