Brittonastrum lanceolatum

Brittonastrum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf Brittonastrum, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
