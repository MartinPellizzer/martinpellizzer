Bignonia roseoalba

Bignonia roseoalba, commonly known as the white trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive scent. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish-brown color.

Bignonia roseoalba thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
