Bulbophyllum boudetianum

Bulbophyllum boudetianum, commonly known as the Boudet's torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often red or orange-colored flowers that resemble torches, which are typically borne on tall, erect inflorescences. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, a common feature among many orchid species. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments.

Bulbophyllum boudetianum is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively straightforward care requirements.
