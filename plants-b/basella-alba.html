Basella alba

Basella alba, commonly known as spinach spinach or Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are tender and edible, often used in Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by round, green berries that turn red when mature. Basella alba thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is a fast-growing plant that can be cultivated as a leafy vegetable or for ornamental purposes.
