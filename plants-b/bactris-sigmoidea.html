Bactris sigmoidea

Bactris sigmoidea, commonly known as the pink pataua or pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

The fruit, which is small and orange-red, is edible and used in local cuisines.
