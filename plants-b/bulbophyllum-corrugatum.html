Bulbophyllum corrugatum

Bulbophyllum corrugatum, commonly known as the ridged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ridged pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help in photosynthesis and water conservation.

Bulbophyllum corrugatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
