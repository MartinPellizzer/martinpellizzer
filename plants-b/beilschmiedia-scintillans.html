Beilschmiedia scintillans

Beilschmiedia scintillans, commonly known as the glittering beilschmiedia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that emit a faint luminescence in the dark, a trait that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

It typically grows in shaded understory environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
