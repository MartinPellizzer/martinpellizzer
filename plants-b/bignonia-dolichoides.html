Bignonia dolichoides

Bignonia dolichoides, commonly known as the longleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist woodlands. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its vigorous growth and showy blooms.
