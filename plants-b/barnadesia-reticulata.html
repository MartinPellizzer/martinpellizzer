Barnadesia reticulata

Barnadesia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-like venation patterns on its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom during the wet season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
