Begonia rhynchocarpa

Begonia rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the horned begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like tubers that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Begonia rhynchocarpa is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
