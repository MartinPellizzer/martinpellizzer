Begonia chlorandra

Begonia chlorandra, commonly known as the silver begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, silver-green leaves that are often marked with dark green veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, which adds visual interest to any landscape.
