Bulbophyllum brookesii

Bulbophyllum brookesii, commonly known as the Brooks' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

Bulbophyllum brookesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
