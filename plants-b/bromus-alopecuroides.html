Bromus alopecuroides

Bromus alopecuroides, commonly known as the hairy brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Bromus alopecuroides is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
