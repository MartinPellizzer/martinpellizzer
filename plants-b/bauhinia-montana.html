Bauhinia montana

Bauhinia montana, commonly known as the mountain bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Bauhinia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
