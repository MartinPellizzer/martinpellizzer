Berberis kunawurensis var. diversifolia

Berberis kunawurensis var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable barberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that vary in shape and color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in temperate climates.
