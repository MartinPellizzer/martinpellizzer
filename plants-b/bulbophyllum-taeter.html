Bulbophyllum taeter

Bulbophyllum taeter, commonly known as the Taeter's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
