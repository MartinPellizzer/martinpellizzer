Brassica repanda subsp. glabrescens

Brassica repanda subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth cabbage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its compact rosette of smooth, glabrous leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine, while its seeds are often used as a spice.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other Brassica species.
