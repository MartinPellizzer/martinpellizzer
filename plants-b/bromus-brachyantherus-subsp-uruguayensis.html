Bromus brachyantherus subsp. uruguayensis

Bromus brachyantherus subsp.

uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically 3 to 5 mm in length.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
