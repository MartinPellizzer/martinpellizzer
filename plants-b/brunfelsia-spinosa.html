Brunfelsia spinosa

Brunfelsia spinosa, commonly known as the purple crown of thorns, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces smooth, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

Brunfelsia spinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates.
