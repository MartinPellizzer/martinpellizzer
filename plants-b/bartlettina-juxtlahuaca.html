Bartlettina juxtlahuaca

Bartlettina juxtlahuaca, commonly known as the Juxtlahuaca barberry, is a shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Bartlettina juxtlahuaca is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
