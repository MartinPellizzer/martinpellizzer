Bracisepalum densiflorum

Bracisepalum densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered bracisepalum, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad native to the wetlands and marshes of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments, it is a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
