Bulbophyllum dolichoglottis

Bulbophyllum dolichoglottis, commonly known as the long-tongued Bulbophyllum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated labellum that resembles a tongue, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on its petals and sepals. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
