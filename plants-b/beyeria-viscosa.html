Beyeria viscosa

Beyeria viscosa, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic leaves, and a distinctive scent when crushed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
