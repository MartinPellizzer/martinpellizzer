Bromus erectus var. typicus

Bromus erectus var. typicus, commonly known as the tufted brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has erect, cylindrical stems with dense, grass-like leaves, and it produces spikelets that contain bothawn and sterile florets. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other grass species.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
