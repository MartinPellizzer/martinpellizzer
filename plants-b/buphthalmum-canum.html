Buphthalmum canum

Buphthalmum canum, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, daisy-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
