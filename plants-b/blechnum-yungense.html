Blechnum yungense

Blechnum yungense, commonly known as Yungense Hartman, is a fern species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Blechnaceae family and is characterized by its fronds that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in montane forests or rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern.

The botanical features of Blechnum yungense make it an interesting subject for studies on fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
