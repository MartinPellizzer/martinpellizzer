Baharuia gracilis

Baharuia gracilis, commonly known as the slender baharuia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
