Blumea muralis

Blumea muralis, commonly known as the wallflower or rock rose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to poor soils and saline conditions. Its small, showy flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Blumea muralis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
