Begonia sarmentacea

Begonia sarmentacea, commonly known as the creeping begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardening.

Its botanical features make it a versatile and ornamental addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
