Berberis aristulata

Berberis aristulata, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
