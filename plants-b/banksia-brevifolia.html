Banksia brevifolia

Banksia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, are arranged in dense, woody cones.

Banksia brevifolia is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
