Bambusa santsik

Bambusa santsik, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its culms are hollow and segmented, typical of the grass family, and are often used in construction and crafts.

Bambusa santsik is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable utility in various cultural and practical applications.
