Bignonia difficilis

Bignonia difficilis, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
