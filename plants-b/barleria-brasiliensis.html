Barleria brasiliensis

Barleria brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Barleria brasiliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
