Brachionidium lucanoideum

Brachionidium lucanoideum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that form bladder-like structures used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellowish in color.

Brachionidium lucanoideum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling populations of microscopic prey and contributing to nutrient cycling.
