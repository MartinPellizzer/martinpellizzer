Byrsonima crassifolia var. moureila

Byrsonima crassifolia var. moureila, commonly known as the "Brazilian cherry" or "moureila tree," is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
