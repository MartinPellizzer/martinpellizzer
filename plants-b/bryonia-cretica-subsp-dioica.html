Bryonia cretica subsp. dioica

Bryonia cretica subsp.

dioica, commonly known as the male and female forms of the Cretan bryony, is a dioecious plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and has distinctive yellow flowers with five lobes.

The plant produces rough, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is handled with care and is not commonly cultivated.
