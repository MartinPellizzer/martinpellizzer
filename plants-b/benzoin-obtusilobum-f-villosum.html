Benzoin obtusilobum f. villosum

Benzoin obtusilobum f. villosum, commonly known as the hairy benzoin, is a variant of the benzoin plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and distinctive aromatic resinous secretions. The plant typically grows in lowland forest areas, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin.

The resin, which is harvested from the bark and leaves, has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
