Bobua divaricativena

Bobua divaricativena, commonly known as the variable bower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
