Bupleurum odontites subsp. linnaeanum

Bupleurum odontites subsp.

linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean's toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The leaves are usually pinnately divided, and the plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

This subspecies is also noted for its historical significance in botanical classification.
