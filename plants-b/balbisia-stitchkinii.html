Balbisia stitchkinii

Balbisia stitchkinii, commonly known as the velvetleaf bat flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, bat-shaped flowers that are typically deep purple or violet in color, which are pollinated primarily by bats. Balbisia stitchkinii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
