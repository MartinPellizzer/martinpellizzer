Botrychium × argutum

Botrychium × argutum, commonly known as the broad-leafed moonwort, is a fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped fronds that are divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. Botrychium × argutum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
