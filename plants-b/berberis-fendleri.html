Berberis fendleri

Berberis fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Berberis fendleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
