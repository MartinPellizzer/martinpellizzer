Bupleurum stellatum subvar. longifolium

Bupleurum stellatum subvar. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
