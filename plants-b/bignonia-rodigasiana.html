Bignonia rodigasiana

Bignonia rodigasiana, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender, woody stems that can climb up to 15 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and vibrant flowers make it a striking addition to gardens, though it requires regular pruning to manage its spread.
