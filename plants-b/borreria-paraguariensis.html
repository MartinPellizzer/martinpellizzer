Borreria paraguariensis

Borreria paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, dry fruits containing seeds. Yerba mate is well-known for its use in traditional beverages, and its leaves contain caffeine and other stimulants.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
