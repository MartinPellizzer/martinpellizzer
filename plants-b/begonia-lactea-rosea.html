Begonia lactea-rosea

Begonia lactea-rosea, commonly known as the rose-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves with a distinctive pinkish or rose-colored veining.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a soft, almost velvety texture, which enhances its visual charm. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pink, adding to its aesthetic value.

This plant prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
