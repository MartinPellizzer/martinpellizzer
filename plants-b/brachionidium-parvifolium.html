Brachionidium parvifolium

Brachionidium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved brachionidium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
