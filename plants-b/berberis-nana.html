Berberis nana

Berberis nana, commonly known as dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, holly-like leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for landscaping.
