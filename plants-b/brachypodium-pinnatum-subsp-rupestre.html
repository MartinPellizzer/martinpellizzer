Brachypodium pinnatum subsp. rupestre

Brachypodium pinnatum subsp.

rupestre, commonly known as rock bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to rocky and arid environments. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed or rocky habitats. Brachypodium pinnatum subsp.

rupestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
