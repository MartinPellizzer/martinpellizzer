Besleria columbiana

Besleria columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian bellflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for ecological diversity in its native habitat.
