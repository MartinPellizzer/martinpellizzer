Bauhinia aculeata subsp. aculeata

Bauhinia aculeata subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the needleleaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, needle-like hairs.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils. Bauhinia aculeata subsp.

aculeata is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
