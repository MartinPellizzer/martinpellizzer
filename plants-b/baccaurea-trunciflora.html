Baccaurea trunciflora

Baccaurea trunciflora, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, segmented fruits. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
