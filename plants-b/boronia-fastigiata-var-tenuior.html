Boronia fastigiata var. tenuior

Boronia fastigiata var. tenuior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
