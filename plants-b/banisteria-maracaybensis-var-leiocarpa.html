Banisteria maracaybensis var. leiocarpa

Banisteria maracaybensis var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered banisteria, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive calyx that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
