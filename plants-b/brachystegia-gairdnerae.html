Brachystegia gairdnerae

Brachystegia gairdnerae, commonly known as the Gairdner's brachystegia, is a deciduous tree native to the miombo woodlands of East and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its thick, often fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume pods containing seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
