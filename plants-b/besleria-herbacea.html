Besleria herbacea

Besleria herbacea, commonly known as the creeping zingiber, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its foliage is often variegated, with a mix of green and white or yellow tones, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom throughout the growing season.
