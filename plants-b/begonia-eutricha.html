Begonia eutricha

Begonia eutricha, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
