Baccharis dracunculifolia subsp. dracunculifolia

Baccharis dracunculifolia subsp.

dracunculifolia, commonly known as Brazilian baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is often found in open habitats such as savannas and forest edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in certain environments.
