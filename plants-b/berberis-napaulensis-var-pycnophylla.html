Berberis napaulensis var. pycnophylla

Berberis napaulensis var. pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Berberis genus. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rocky or woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
