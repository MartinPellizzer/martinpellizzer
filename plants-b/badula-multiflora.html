Badula multiflora

Badula multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered badula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Badula multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
