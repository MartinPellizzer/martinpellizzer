Berberis multiflora var. typica

Berberis multiflora var. typica, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that persist into winter. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
