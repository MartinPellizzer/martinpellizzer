Blechnopsis cumingiana

Blechnopsis cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Blechnopsis cumingiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
