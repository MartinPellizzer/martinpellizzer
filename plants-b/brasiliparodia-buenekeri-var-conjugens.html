Brasiliparodia buenekeri var. conjugens

Brasiliparodia buenekeri var. conjugens, commonly known as the Brazilian parodia, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescence that displays vibrant red and orange hues, which stand out against its broad, waxy leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure and striking appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a threatened species in its native range.
