Baeria minor

Baeria minor, commonly known as the lesser baeria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Baeria minor is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora, making it a characteristic species of damp, temperate habitats.
