Baccaurea parviflora var. ptychopyxis

Baccaurea parviflora var. ptychopyxis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baccaurea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
