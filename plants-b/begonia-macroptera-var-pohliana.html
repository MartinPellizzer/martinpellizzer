Begonia macroptera var. pohliana

Begonia macroptera var. pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana begonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to burgundy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and landscape designs.
