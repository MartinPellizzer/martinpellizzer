Bactris sphaerocarpa var. pinnatisecta

Bactris sphaerocarpa var. pinnatisecta, commonly known as the pinnatisected palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces edible, spiny fruits that are often harvested for their sweet, starchy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential agricultural use.
