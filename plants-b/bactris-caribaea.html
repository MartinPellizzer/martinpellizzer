Bactris caribaea

Bactris caribaea, commonly known as the red raphia, is a palm native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces bright red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for thatching and weaving.

Bactris caribaea thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
