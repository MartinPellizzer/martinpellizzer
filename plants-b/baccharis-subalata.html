Baccharis subalata

Baccharis subalata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
