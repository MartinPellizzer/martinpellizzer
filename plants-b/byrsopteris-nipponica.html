Byrsopteris nipponica

Byrsopteris nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that help anchor it in water. Its leaves are typically divided into small, feathery segments, giving it a lacy appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
