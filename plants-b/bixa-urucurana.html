Bixa urucurana

Bixa urucurana, commonly known as annatto, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red seeds, which are rich in carotenoids. The plant produces yellow flowers and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. Its seeds are widely used as a natural dye and food coloring agent.
