Boltonia lautureana

Boltonia lautureana, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow to orange central cone. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Boltonia lautureana is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
