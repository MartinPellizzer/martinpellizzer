Bunchosia diphylla subsp. brevisurcularis

Bunchosia diphylla subsp.

brevisurcularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bunchosia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often borne at the ends of branches. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
