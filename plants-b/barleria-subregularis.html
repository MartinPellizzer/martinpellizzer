Barleria subregularis

Barleria subregularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
