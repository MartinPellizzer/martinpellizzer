Blechnum nukuhivense

Blechnum nukuhivense, commonly known as the Nukuhiva maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or on rocky substrates. Its spores are released from small, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The fern's ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical gardens.
