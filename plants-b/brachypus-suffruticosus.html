Brachypus suffruticosus

Brachypus suffruticosus, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, foliage that thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
