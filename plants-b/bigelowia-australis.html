Bigelowia australis

Bigelowia australis, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and alpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Bigelowia australis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
