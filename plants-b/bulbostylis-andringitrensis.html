Bulbostylis andringitrensis

Bulbostylis andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringa sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland or swampy habitats, often growing in dense vegetation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
