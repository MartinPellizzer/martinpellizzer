Boronia pinnata var. citriodora

Boronia pinnata var. citriodora, commonly known as lemon boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and heathland environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its citrus-like scent is derived from volatile oils present in its foliage.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
