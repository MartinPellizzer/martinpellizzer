Buchanania florida var. petiolaris

Buchanania florida var. petiolaris, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name 'petiolaris' refers to its distinctive petioles, which are longer and more pronounced compared to the typical variety.
