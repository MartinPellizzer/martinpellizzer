Begonia bakunensis

Begonia bakunensis, commonly known as the Bakun begonia, is a flowering plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and red. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
