Buchnera elongata var. obtusa

Buchnera elongata var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf buchnera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
