Brachyotum rotundifolium

Brachyotum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved brachyotum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Brachyotum rotundifolium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
