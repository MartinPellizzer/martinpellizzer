Becium grandiflorum var. ericoides

Becium grandiflorum var. ericoides, commonly known as the ericoid becium, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
