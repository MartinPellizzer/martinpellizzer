Bupleurum subovatum var. heterophyllum

Bupleurum subovatum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and alternate, with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
