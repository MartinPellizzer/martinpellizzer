Bursera tacahamaca

Bursera tacahamaca, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
