Blachia pentzii

Blachia pentzii, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking red veins that contrast vividly against the foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Blachia pentzii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
