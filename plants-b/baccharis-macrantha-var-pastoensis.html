Baccharis macrantha var. pastoensis

Baccharis macrantha var. pastoensis, commonly known as Pasto baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
