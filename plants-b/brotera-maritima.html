Brotera maritima

Brotera maritima, commonly known as sea broom, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of salt-resistant wax.

Brotera maritima is well adapted to salt spray and can withstand harsh coastal conditions.
