Baccharis depauperata

Baccharis depauperata, commonly known as the slender baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its small, grayish-green leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
