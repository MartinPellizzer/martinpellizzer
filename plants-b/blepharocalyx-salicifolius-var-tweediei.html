Blepharocalyx salicifolius var. tweediei

Blepharocalyx salicifolius var. tweediei, commonly known as the Tweedie's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a dramatic and eye-catching appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a lanceolate shape with a smooth margin. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
