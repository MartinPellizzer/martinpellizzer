Begonia hypogaea

Begonia hypogaea, commonly known as the underground begonia, is a unique species characterized by its subterranean growth habit.

It produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in adverse conditions. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. It blooms with clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are often hidden beneath the soil surface.

This species is native to tropical regions of South America and is valued for its ornamental and horticultural significance.
