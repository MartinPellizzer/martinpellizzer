Bignonia dura

Bignonia dura, commonly known as the hard bignonia, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and as a decorative vine.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to landscapes, though it can be invasive in some regions.
