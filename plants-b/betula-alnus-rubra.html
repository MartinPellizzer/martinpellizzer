Betula-alnus rubra

Betula-alnus rubra, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals a salmon-colored inner layer. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins and has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal and mixed forests.

Betula-alnus rubra plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
