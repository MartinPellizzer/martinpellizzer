Begonia duhungensis

Begonia duhungensis, commonly known as the Duhung begonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often velvety foliage and showy bracts. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with broad, heart-shaped leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by colorful, petal-like bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Begonia duhungensis prefers shade and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor gardens.
