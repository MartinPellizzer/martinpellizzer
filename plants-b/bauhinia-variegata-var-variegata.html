Bauhinia variegata var. variegata

Bauhinia variegata var. variegata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red floral displays that appear in early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
