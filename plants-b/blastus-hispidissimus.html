Blastus hispidissimus

Blastus hispidissimus, commonly known as the rough blastus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often branched and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence, giving the plant its "hispidissimus" name, meaning "very hairy.". It produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
