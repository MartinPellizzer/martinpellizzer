Bupleurum paniculatum var. acutifolium

Bupleurum paniculatum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a sharp, pointed tip, giving it its common name.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
