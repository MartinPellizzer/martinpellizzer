Berberis oblanceifolia

Berberis oblanceifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant also contains berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
