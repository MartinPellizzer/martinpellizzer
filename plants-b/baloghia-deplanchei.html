Baloghia deplanchei

Baloghia deplanchei, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its rough, fissured bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often elliptic or ovate in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
