Bauhinia praesignis

Bauhinia praesignis, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are compound and consist of numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

Bauhinia praesignis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
