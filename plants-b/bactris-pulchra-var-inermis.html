Bactris pulchra var. inermis

Bactris pulchra var. inermis, commonly known as the pink poinsettia, is a striking plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, commonly referred to as the palm family, and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features vibrant, colorful bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically red, orange, or pink in hue. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is particularly valued for its seasonal color display, especially during the holiday season.
