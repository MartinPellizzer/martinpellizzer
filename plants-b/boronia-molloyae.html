Boronia molloyae

Boronia molloyae, commonly known as the Molloy's boronia, is an endemic shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It is typically found growing in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast.

Boronia molloyae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
