Balanophora fungosa var. globosa

Balanophora fungosa var. globosa, commonly known as the globular orchid, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and reliance on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of other plants. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its unique morphology includes a rounded, fleshy structure that resembles a globular shape, giving it its common name.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecological niche.
