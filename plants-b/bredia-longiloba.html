Bredia longiloba

Bredia longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed bredia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Bredia longiloba is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
