Bisnaga latispina

Bisnaga latispina, commonly known as the broad-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations include water storage in its stems and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Bisnaga latispina plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
