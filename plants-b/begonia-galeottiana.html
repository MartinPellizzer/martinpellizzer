Begonia galeottiana

Begonia galeottiana, commonly known as the Malabar begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of showy, red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden displays and indoor cultivation.
