Bernardinia villosa

Bernardinia villosa, commonly known as the woolly berry or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, and it produces small, red berries that are edible. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple spots, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
