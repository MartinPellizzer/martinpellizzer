Baptisia retusa

Baptisia retusa, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Baptisia retusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
