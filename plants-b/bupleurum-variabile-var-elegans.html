Bupleurum variabile var. elegans

Bupleurum variabile var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant variabile bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in racemes during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
