Brassicella monensis var. maroccana

Brassicella monensis var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cabbages. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
