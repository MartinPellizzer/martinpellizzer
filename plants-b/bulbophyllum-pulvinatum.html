Bulbophyllum pulvinatum

Bulbophyllum pulvinatum, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are known for their intricate shapes and vibrant hues.

Bulbophyllum pulvinatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
