Betula nigra pendula

Betula nigra pendula, commonly known as the weeping river birch, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that cascade downward, creating a unique, ornamental appearance. The tree has smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers, adding to its visual appeal. Its leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn a vibrant yellow in the fall.

Betula nigra pendula is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscaping and its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions.
