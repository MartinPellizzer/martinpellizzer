Bartsia laciniata

Bartsia laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved bartsia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, opposite leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Bartsia laciniata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
