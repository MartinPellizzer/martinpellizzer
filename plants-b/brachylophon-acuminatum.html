Brachylophon acuminatum

Brachylophon acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a cool, moist climate. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Brachylophon acuminatum is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
