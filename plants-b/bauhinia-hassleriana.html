Bauhinia hassleriana

Bauhinia hassleriana, commonly known as the yellow bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, compound leaves that are often divided into pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, raceme-like inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are flat, pod-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Bauhinia hassleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
