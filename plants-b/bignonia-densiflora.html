Bignonia densiflora

Bignonia densiflora, commonly known as yellow glorybower, is a vigorous, flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Bignonia densiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
