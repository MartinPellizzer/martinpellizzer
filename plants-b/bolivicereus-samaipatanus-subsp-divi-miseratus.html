Bolivicereus samaipatanus subsp. divi-miseratus

Bolivicereus samaipatanus subsp.

divi-miseratus, commonly known as the "Carnival Cactus," is a rare and striking cactus native to the Bolivian Andes. It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in a spectacular display, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant has a short, cylindrical stem with spines and is adapted to the high-altitude, arid environments of its native habitat. Its flowering period typically occurs during the dry season, making it a unique and visually stunning addition to botanical collections.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
