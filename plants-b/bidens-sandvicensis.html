Bidens sandvicensis

Bidens sandvicensis, commonly known as Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in coastal and wetland habitats, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Bidens sandvicensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
