Blumea subsericans

Blumea subsericans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or purple, appearing in clusters.

Blumea subsericans is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
