Bunium cylindricum var. minus

Bunium cylindricum var. minus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it has a distinct anise-like aroma.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
