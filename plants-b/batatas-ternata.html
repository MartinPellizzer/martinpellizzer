Batatas ternata

Batatas ternata, commonly known as purple yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces tubers that are typically purple or violet in color, which are rich in starch and nutrients. These tubers are widely cultivated in Southeast Asia for their culinary and medicinal uses.

Batatas ternata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
