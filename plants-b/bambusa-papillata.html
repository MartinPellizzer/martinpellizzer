Bambusa papillata

Bambusa papillata, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive hairy nodes along the stem. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its culms are strong and hollow, making it suitable for construction and crafts.

Bambusa papillata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
