Byrsonima sessilifolia var. minarum

Byrsonima sessilifolia var. minarum, commonly known as the dwarf yellow mombin, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a fleshy, hairy pericarp. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts within its native habitat.
