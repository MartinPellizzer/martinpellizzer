Bungea trifida

Bungea trifida, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Bungea trifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
