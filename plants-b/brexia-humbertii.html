Brexia humbertii

Brexia humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Brexia humbertii is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
