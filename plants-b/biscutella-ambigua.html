Biscutella ambigua

Biscutella ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Biscutella ambigua is also notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
