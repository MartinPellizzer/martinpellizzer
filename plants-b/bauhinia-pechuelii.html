Bauhinia pechuelii

Bauhinia pechuelii, commonly known as the pink bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Bauhinia pechuelii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
