Buddleja serrulata

Buddleja serrulata, commonly known as the serrated buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, serrated, ovate leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces panicle-like clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of purple to pink, which bloom in late summer. It is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8 and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Buddleja serrulata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and showy floral displays.
