Boopis bupleuroides var. genuina

Boopis bupleuroides var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in racemes during the summer months. It is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable native plant in ecological restoration projects.
