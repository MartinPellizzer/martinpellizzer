Blumea riparia f. angustifolia

Blumea riparia f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
