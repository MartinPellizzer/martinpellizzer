Buddleja heliophila

Buddleja heliophila, commonly known as the sun-loving buddleia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Buddleja heliophila is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
