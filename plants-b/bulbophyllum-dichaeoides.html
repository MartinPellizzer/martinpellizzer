Bulbophyllum dichaeoides

Bulbophyllum dichaeoides, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green pseudobulbs and delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in the summer months. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique and beautiful species.
