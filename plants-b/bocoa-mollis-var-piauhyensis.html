Bocoa mollis var. piauhyensis

Bocoa mollis var. piauhyensis, commonly known as the Piauhy bocoa, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
