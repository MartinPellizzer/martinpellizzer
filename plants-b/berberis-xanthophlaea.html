Berberis xanthophlaea

Berberis xanthophlaea, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, spherical berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a landscape accent.
