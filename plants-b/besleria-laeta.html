Besleria laeta

Besleria laeta, commonly known as the silver lace begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver foliage with intricate patterns of green and white. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Besleria laeta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
