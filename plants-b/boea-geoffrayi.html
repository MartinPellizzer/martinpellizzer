Boea geoffrayi

Boea geoffrayi, commonly known as the silver vine, is a trailing evergreen vine native to East Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a silvery-green hue and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a ground cover or hanging plant.

Boea geoffrayi is also known for its ability to attract various insects and birds.
