Brachyglottis bellidioides var. crassa

Brachyglottis bellidioides var. crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaved brachyglottis, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and dry forest environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of New Zealand's unique flora.
