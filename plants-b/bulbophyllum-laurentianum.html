Bulbophyllum laurentianum

Bulbophyllum laurentianum, commonly known as the Laurentian Bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is appreciated by horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
