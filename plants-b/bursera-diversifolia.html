Bursera diversifolia

Bursera diversifolia, commonly known as the Mexican birch or white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that often exudes a fragrant resin when damaged. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds.

Bursera diversifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
