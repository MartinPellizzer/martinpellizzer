Buceras bucida

Buceras bucida, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Buceras bucida is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
