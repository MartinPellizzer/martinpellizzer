Blepharis acanthodioides

Blepharis acanthodioides, commonly known as the needleleaf bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often pale blue or purple and bloom during the warmer months.

The species is part of the Acanthaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in dry gardens.
