Bulbophyllum fuscopurpureum

Bulbophyllum fuscopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-fuscous orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile substrates. Its flowers are usually purple or reddish-purple, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
