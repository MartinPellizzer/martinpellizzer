Bupleurum falcatum var. chiliosciadium

Bupleurum falcatum var. chiliosciadium, commonly known as the Chilean angelica, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features tall, branching stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
