Bignonia purpurea

Bignonia purpurea, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Bignonia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
