Broussonetia plumeri

Broussonetia plumeri, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fluffy, cotton-like fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Broussonetia plumeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
