Bignonia unguiculata

Bignonia unguiculata, commonly known as crossvine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can climb by means of tendrils or by adhering to supports.

Bignonia unguiculata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
