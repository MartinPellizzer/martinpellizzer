Bidens costaricensis

Bidens costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Bidens costaricensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
