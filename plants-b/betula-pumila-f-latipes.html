Betula pumila f. latipes

Betula pumila f. latipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf birch, is a shrub-like form of the dwarf birch.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in northern regions and alpine areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh climates and poor soils.
