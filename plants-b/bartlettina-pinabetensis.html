Bartlettina pinabetensis

Bartlettina pinabetensis, commonly known as the Pinabetan barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
