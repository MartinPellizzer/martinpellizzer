Bauhinia hullettii

Bauhinia hullettii, commonly known as the Hullett's bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, papilionaceous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in clusters and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Bauhinia hullettii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
