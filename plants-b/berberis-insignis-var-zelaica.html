Berberis insignis var. zelaica

Berberis insignis var. zelaica, commonly known as the Zelaica barberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
