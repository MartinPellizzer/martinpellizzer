Barleria fulvostellata subsp. mangochiensis

Barleria fulvostellata subsp.

mangochiensis, commonly known as the Natal barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
