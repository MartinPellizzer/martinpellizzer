Boehmeria japonica var. silvestrii

Boehmeria japonica var. silvestrii, commonly known as Japanese nettle or wild flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strong, durable fibers used in traditional crafts and textiles.

The species is part of the Urticaceae family, which includes other nettle species known for their stinging hairs.
