Bupleurum perfoliatum var. lanceolatum

Bupleurum perfoliatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf goat's-beard, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
