Brickellia oreithales

Brickellia oreithales, commonly known as the mountain brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact growth habit.

Brickellia oreithales is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
