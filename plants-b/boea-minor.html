Boea minor

Boea minor, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, waxy leaves that have a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to climb or trail.

Boea minor thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers partial to full sunlight.
