Betula × zimpelii

Betula × zimpelii, commonly known as the Zimpelii birch, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Betula pendula (silver birch) and Betula jacquemontii (Jacquemont's birch).

It is characterized by its distinctive white bark and slender, graceful form, making it a popular ornamental tree. The plant exhibits a moderate growth rate and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils. It is typically found in temperate regions and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Betula × zimpelii is also noted for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
