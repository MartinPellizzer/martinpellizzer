Biserrula pelecinus var. subintegra

Biserrula pelecinus var. subintegra, commonly known as the slender biserrula, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, raceme-like inflorescences, which bloom in summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in sandy or loamy soils.

It is often used for forage and soil stabilization due to its drought tolerance and nitrogen-fixing ability.
