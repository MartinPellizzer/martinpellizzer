Baphia massaiensis subsp. obovata

Baphia massaiensis subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the Massai baphia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
