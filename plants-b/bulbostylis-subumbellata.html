Bulbostylis subumbellata

Bulbostylis subumbellata, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often found in conservation areas and is a valuable indicator species for wetland health.
