Buraeavia carunculata

Buraeavia carunculata, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, elongated spur that houses nectar, attracting specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture that aids in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
