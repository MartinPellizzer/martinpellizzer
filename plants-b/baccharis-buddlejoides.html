Baccharis buddlejoides

Baccharis buddlejoides, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators.
