Begonia sinuata var. clivalis

Begonia sinuata var. clivalis, commonly known as the cliff begonia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often adorned with distinctive wavy edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
