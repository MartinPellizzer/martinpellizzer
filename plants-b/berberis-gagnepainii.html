Berberis gagnepainii

Berberis gagnepainii, commonly known as the Gagnepain barberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to various climates.
