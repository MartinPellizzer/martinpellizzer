Baptisia nuculifera

Baptisia nuculifera, commonly known as the scarlet false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Baptisia nuculifera is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
