Bertya rotundifolia

Bertya rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved bertya, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are rounded and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, contributing to its unique botanical profile.

Bertya rotundifolia is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
