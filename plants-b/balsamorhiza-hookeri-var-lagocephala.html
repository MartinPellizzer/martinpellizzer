Balsamorhiza hookeri var. lagocephala

Balsamorhiza hookeri var. lagocephala, commonly known as the Lagocephala goldenbush, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
