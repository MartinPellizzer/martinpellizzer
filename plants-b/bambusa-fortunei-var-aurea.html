Bambusa fortunei var. aurea

Bambusa fortunei var. aurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its sturdy, green culms. This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant is relatively fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 10 meters under ideal conditions.
