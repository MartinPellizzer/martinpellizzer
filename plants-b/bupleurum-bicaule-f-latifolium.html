Bupleurum bicaule f. latifolium

Bupleurum bicaule f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf bupleurum, is a variant of the Bupleurum bicaule species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, yellowish-green flowers.

The botanical features of this plant make it notable for its adaptability and ecological significance in its native habitat.
