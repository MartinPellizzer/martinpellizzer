Botrys aromaticus

Botrys aromaticus, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spherical clusters, giving it its scientific name. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.
