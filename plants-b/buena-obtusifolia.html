Buena obtusifolia

Buena obtusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, rounded seeds.

Buena obtusifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
