Bupleurum falcatum var. occidentale

Bupleurum falcatum var. occidentale, commonly known as western cowbane, is a biennial or perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features a stout, branched stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose umbels, blooming from June to August.

The roots are fibrous and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported tonic and antispasmodic properties.
