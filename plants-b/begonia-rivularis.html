Begonia rivularis

Begonia rivularis, commonly known as the water begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its stems are often trailing or hanging, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
