Bauhinia saigonensis var. gagnepainiana

Bauhinia saigonensis var. gagnepainiana, commonly known as the Saigon bauhinia, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
