Buddleja hosseusiana

Buddleja hosseusiana, commonly known as the Chinese butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has grayish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Buddleja hosseusiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
