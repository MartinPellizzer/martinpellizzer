Bursera beandou

Bursera beandou, commonly known as the West African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The tree produces a fragrant gum resin, often used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a leathery texture.

Bursera beandou thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, tropical climates.
