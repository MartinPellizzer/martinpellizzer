Betonica officinalis subsp. velebitica

Betonica officinalis subsp.

velebitica, commonly known as the Velebit betonica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Dinaric Alps. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and spikes of purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, particularly for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
