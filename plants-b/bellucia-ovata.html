Bellucia ovata

Bellucia ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
