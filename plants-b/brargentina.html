Brargentina

Brargentina, commonly known as the Southern Chilean flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate to flourish.

Brargentina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
