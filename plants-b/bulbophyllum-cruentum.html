Bulbophyllum cruentum

Bulbophyllum cruentum, commonly known as the crimson orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable conditions.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing and sought-after specimen in horticulture.
