Betula × caerulea var. grandis

Betula × caerulea var. grandis, commonly known as the purple tamarack, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-brown bark, which becomes more vibrant as the tree matures. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
