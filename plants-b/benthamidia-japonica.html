Benthamidia japonica

Benthamidia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese benthamia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
