Blumea lacera var. lacera

Blumea lacera var. lacera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
