Boerhavia gracillima subsp. decalvata

Boerhavia gracillima subsp.

decalvata, commonly known as the slender bluemink, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
