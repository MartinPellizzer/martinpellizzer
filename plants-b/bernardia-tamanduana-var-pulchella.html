Bernardia tamanduana var. pulchella

Bernardia tamanduana var. pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful Bernardia, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
