Bulbophyllum balapiuense

Bulbophyllum balapiuense, commonly known as the Balapiu orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers that are usually pale green or yellow with red or purple markings. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, storing water and nutrients, which helps it survive in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
