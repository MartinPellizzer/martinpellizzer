Bulbophyllum mamillatum

Bulbophyllum mamillatum, commonly known as the mamillate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and hairy stems make it a distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.
