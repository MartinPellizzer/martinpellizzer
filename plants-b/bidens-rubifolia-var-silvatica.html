Bidens rubifolia var. silvatica

Bidens rubifolia var. silvatica, commonly known as the red-veined ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers with distinctive red veins. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with reddish undersides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
