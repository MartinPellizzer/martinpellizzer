Betula humilis var. vulgaris

Betula humilis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common birch, is a small deciduous tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, and its leaves are triangular with serrated edges. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands.

Betula humilis var. vulgaris is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
