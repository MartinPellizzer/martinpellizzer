Buddleja spicata

Buddleja spicata, commonly known as the spiked bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikes of small, fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a coastal plant.

Buddleja spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
