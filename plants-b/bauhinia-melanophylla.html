Bauhinia melanophylla

Bauhinia melanophylla, commonly known as the black-leaf bauhinia, is a small deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are dark green and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Bauhinia melanophylla is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
