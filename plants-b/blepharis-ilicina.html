Blepharis ilicina

Blepharis ilicina, commonly known as the narrowleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers, which are usually pale blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.
