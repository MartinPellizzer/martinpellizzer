Bromus purgans var. longispicatus

Bromus purgans var. longispicatus, commonly known as the long-spined brome, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that are often adorned with long, stiff bristles. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapting to the dry environments it inhabits.

The species is noted for its ecological resilience and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
