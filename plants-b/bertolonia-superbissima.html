Bertolonia superbissima

Bertolonia superbissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
