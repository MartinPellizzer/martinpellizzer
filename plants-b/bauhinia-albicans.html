Bauhinia albicans

Bauhinia albicans, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or open woodlands.

Bauhinia albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
