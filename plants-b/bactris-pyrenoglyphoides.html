Bactris pyrenoglyphoides

Bactris pyrenoglyphoides, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. Its structure and growth habits make it well adapted to tropical and subtropical environments.

Bactris pyrenoglyphoides is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in its native habitats.
