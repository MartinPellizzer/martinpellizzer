Begonia pseudobrandisiana

Begonia pseudobrandisiana, commonly known as the Malayan begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a deep green color and a prominent venation pattern. The flowers, which are usually red or orange, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
