Banisteriopsis metallicolor var. subrotunda

Banisteriopsis metallicolor var. subrotunda, commonly known as the silver-stemmed balsa, is a variant of the balsa tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is highly valued for its buoyancy and ease of carving. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

This variant is often cultivated for both its ecological benefits and its use in lightweight construction and artistic applications.
