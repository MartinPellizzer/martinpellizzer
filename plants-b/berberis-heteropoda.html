Berberis heteropoda

Berberis heteropoda, commonly known as the Chinese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
