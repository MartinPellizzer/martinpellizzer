Brickellia veronicifolia var. senilis

Brickellia veronicifolia var. senilis, commonly known as silverleaf brickellbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
