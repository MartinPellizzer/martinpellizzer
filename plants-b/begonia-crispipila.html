Begonia crispipila

Begonia crispipila, commonly known as the crinkled begonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it a unique texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Begonia crispipila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
