Bowlesia dichotoma var. digitata

Bowlesia dichotoma var. digitata, commonly known as the digitate bowlesia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, with stems that split into two main branches, giving it a dichotomous structure. The leaves are typically opposite and have a lanceolate shape, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
