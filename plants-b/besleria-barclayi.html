Besleria barclayi

Besleria barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay's flame lily, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or wetlands, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly textured surface.

The flowers, which are pollinated by insects, are often vividly colored, ranging from red to orange, adding to the plant's ornamental value.
