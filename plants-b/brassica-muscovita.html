Brassica muscovita

Brassica muscovita, commonly known as Russian kale, is a hardy biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, curly leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces yellow flowers in the second year, which are followed by round, round pods containing seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable or for its ornamental value.

Brassica muscovita is also known for its nutritional content, including vitamins A and C.
