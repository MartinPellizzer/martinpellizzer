Brassica rapa

Brassica rapa, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its robust, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by spherical, three-lobed pods containing numerous seeds. Brassica rapa is a versatile species that can thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.

It is widely cultivated for its edible leaves, seeds, and oil, and is also used in traditional medicine.
