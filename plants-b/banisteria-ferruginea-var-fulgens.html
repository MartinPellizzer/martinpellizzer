Banisteria ferruginea var. fulgens

Banisteria ferruginea var. fulgens, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a striking tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from orange to red, often with a yellow throat, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
