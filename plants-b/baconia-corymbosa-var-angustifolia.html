Baconia corymbosa var. angustifolia

Baconia corymbosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baconia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
