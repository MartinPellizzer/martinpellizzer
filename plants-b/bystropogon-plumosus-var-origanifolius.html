Bystropogon plumosus var. origanifolius

Bystropogon plumosus var. origanifolius, commonly known as the woolly everlasting or woolly tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
