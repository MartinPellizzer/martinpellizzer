Boltonia incisa

Boltonia incisa, commonly known as white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has tall, sturdy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Boltonia incisa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
