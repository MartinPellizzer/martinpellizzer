Biscutella choulettei

Biscutella choulettei, commonly known as the French biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs. Biscutella choulettei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
