Banisteria heterocarpa

Banisteria heterocarpa, commonly known as the purple banisteria, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Banisteria heterocarpa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
