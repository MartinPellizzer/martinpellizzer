Begonia sukutensis

Begonia sukutensis, commonly known as the Sukut's begonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Begonia sukutensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
