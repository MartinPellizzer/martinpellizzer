Blumea cinerascens

Blumea cinerascens, commonly known as the gray blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a grayish appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, and bloom during the warmer months.
