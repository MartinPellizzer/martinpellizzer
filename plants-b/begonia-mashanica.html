Begonia mashanica

Begonia mashanica, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white.

Begonia mashanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
