Brassica crispa

Brassica crispa, commonly known as the curly kale or curled mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, crinkled, dark green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Brassica crispa is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
