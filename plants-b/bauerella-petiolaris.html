Bauerella petiolaris

Bauerella petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Bauerella petiolaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
