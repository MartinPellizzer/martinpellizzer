Brassica oleracea subsp. caulorapa

Brassica oleracea subsp.

caulorapa, commonly known as kale, is a biennial plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its thick, fibrous leaves that are typically green or purple in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is widely cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves.

Kale is a versatile crop used in various culinary applications and is valued for its health benefits.
