Brassica gravinae var. rupicola

Brassica gravinae var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to rocky and arid environments. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in rocky landscapes.
