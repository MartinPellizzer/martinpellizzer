Buddleja kurtzii

Buddleja kurtzii, commonly known as the African butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The flowers are typically pale purple to blue in color and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide nectar for wildlife.
