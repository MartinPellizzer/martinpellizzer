Brassica napus var. syntomocarpa

Brassica napus var. syntomocarpa, commonly known as the Indian mustard or black mustard, is a variety of the oilseed mustard plant.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, black seed pods, which are the source of its common name. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its seeds, which are used in cooking and as a source of oil.
