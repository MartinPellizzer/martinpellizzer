Barkhausia porrifolia

Barkhausia porrifolia, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that peels away as it ages, revealing a mottled pattern beneath. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Barkhausia porrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
