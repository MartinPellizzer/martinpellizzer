Bifrenaria aurantiaca

Bifrenaria aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces long, cascading clusters of these vibrant blooms, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Bifrenaria aurantiaca thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
