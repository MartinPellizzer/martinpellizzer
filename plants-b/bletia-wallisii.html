Bletia wallisii

Bletia wallisii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Bletia wallisii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
