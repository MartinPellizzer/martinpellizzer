Bromus macrocladus

Bromus macrocladus, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Bromus macrocladus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
