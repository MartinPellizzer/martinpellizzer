Brachyris isernii

Brachyris isernii, commonly known as the Isern's dwarf sedge, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its slender, triangular stems are usually less than 20 centimeters tall, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Brachyris isernii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
