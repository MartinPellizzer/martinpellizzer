Barkhausia leontodontoides

Barkhausia leontodontoides, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
