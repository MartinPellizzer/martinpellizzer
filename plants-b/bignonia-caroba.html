Bignonia caroba

Bignonia caroba, commonly known as the sweet trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It produces showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its long, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
