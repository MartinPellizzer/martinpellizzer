Brachionidium dorisiae

Brachionidium dorisiae, commonly known as the Dorisian brachionid, is a species of freshwater algae belonging to the class Chlorophyceae.

It is characterized by its filamentous growth pattern and the presence of chloroplasts that contain pyrenoids for carbon fixation. This algae typically thrives in nutrient-rich aquatic environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures include both vegetative propagation and sexual reproduction through the formation of zoospores.

Brachionidium dorisiae plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to primary production and serving as a food source for various microorganisms and small aquatic animals.
