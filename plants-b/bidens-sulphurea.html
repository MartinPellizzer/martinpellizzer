Bidens sulphurea

Bidens sulphurea, commonly known as golden beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers consist of two yellow petals and five pale yellow ray florets. It is an annual herb that produces small, spiny fruits containing seeds.

Bidens sulphurea is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
