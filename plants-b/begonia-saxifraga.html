Begonia saxifraga

Begonia saxifraga, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems are short and compact.

Begonia saxifraga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
