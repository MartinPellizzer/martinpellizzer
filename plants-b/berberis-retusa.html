Berberis retusa

Berberis retusa, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. Berberis retusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and seasonal color changes.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
