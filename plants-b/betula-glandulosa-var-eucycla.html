Betula glandulosa var. eucycla

Betula glandulosa var. eucycla, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf birch, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, clustered catkins that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
