Boronia occidentalis

Boronia occidentalis, commonly known as Western boronia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly serrated, while its flowers are clustered in umbels.

Boronia occidentalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
