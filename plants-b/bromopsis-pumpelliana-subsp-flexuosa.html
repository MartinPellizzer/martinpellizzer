Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp. flexuosa

Bromopsis pumpelliana subsp.

flexuosa, commonly known as the flexuous bromus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching leaves that often curve downward.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
