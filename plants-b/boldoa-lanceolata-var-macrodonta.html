Boldoa lanceolata var. macrodonta

Boldoa lanceolata var. macrodonta, commonly known as the broadleaf boldo, is a shrub native to South America, particularly in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.
