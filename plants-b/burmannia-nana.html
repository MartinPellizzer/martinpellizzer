Burmannia nana

Burmannia nana, commonly known as the dwarf burmannia, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burmanniaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually fleshy and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps retain moisture in humid conditions.

The flowers are often borne on short stalks and are pollinated by small insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
