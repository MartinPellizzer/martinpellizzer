Bocquillonia brevipes var. trichogyne

Bocquillonia brevipes var. trichogyne, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed bocquillonia, is a unique variant of the bocquillonia genus.

It is characterized by its short, stout stems covered in fine, hairy trichomes, which provide a textured appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are small and elliptical, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, a feature that contributes to its ecological role in pollination and seed dispersal.
