Bidens sandvicensis subsp. sandvicensis

Bidens sandvicensis subsp.

sandvicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
