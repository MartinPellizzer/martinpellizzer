Berberis ulicina

Berberis ulicina, commonly known as the evergreen barberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.
