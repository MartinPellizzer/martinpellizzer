Berberis schwerinii

Berberis schwerinii, commonly known as the Schwerin barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it popular in gardens and landscapes.
