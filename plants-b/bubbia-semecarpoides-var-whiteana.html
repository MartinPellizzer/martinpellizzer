Bubbia semecarpoides var. whiteana

Bubbia semecarpoides var. whiteana, commonly known as the Whiteana burrpearl, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny, globe-shaped fruits that resemble small, clustered pearls. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
