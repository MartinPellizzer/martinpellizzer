Baptisia × sulphurea

Baptisia × sulphurea, commonly known as golden beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to gold flowers resembling beardtongue. The plant produces soft, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Baptisia × sulphurea is valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
