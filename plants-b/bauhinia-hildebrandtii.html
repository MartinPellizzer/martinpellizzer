Bauhinia hildebrandtii

Bauhinia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in bright orange or red hues, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in long, drooping panicles. Bauhinia hildebrandtii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
