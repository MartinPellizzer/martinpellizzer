Besleria wurdackii

Besleria wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces vibrant orange-red flowers with a unique, elongated shape. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
