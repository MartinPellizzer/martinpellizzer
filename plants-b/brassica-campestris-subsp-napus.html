Brassica campestris subsp. napus

Brassica campestris subsp.

napus, commonly known as rapeseed, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and western Asia.

The plant produces pods containing seeds that are used for oil extraction and as a source of protein. It is also a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features include alternate, rough-hairy leaves and a robust root system.
