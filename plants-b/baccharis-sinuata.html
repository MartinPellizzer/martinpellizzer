Baccharis sinuata

Baccharis sinuata, commonly known as silky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely haired, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive silvery sheen that reflects sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
