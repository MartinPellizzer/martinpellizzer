Brexia madagascariensis

Brexia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in the island's unique climate. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity, Brexia madagascariensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
