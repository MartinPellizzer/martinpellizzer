Boehmeria glomerulifera

Boehmeria glomerulifera, commonly known as the clustered flax or Indian flax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nettle family (Urticaceae) and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which give it its specific epithet "glomerulifera.". It is often grown for its fibers, which are used in traditional textiles and papermaking.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
