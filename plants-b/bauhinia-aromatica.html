Bauhinia aromatica

Bauhinia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet-scented bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The sweet fragrance of its flowers is particularly noticeable during the evening.
