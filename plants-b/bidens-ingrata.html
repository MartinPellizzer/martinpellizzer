Bidens ingrata

Bidens ingrata, commonly known as common beggarlice, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, triangular leaves. The plant produces numerous seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many regions.
