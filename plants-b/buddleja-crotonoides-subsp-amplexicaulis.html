Buddleja crotonoides subsp. amplexicaulis

Buddleja crotonoides subsp.

amplexicaulis, commonly known as the Cape rose of the Cape, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purplish underside.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
