Bartlettina hylobia

Bartlettina hylobia, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Bartlettina hylobia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
