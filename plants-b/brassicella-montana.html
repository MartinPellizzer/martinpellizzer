Brassicella montana

Brassicella montana, commonly known as the mountain mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Brassicella montana is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
