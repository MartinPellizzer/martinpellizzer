Bromus poiformis

Bromus poiformis, commonly known as the smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers. Bromus poiformis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

It is also valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
