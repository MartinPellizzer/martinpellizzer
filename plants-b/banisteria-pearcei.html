Banisteria pearcei

Banisteria pearcei, commonly known as the Mexican moonseed, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, kidney-shaped fruits. Its leaves and stems contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Banisteria pearcei thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
