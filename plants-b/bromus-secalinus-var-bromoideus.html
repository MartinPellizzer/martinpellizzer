Bromus secalinus var. bromoideus

Bromus secalinus var. bromoideus, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, two-rowed spikelets.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural landscapes.
