Bulbophyllum bulliferum

Bulbophyllum bulliferum, commonly known as the "Bull's Horn Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, often colorful flowers that resemble horns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually green or yellow with red or orange markings, and they bloom during the wet season.

The species is popular among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
