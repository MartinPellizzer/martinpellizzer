Buxus cordifolia

Buxus cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, compact foliage with oval to heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant has a woody, rounded form and is often used in formal garden designs due to its ability to be pruned into intricate shapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shady garden spaces.

Buxus cordifolia is valued for its durability and ability to thrive in a variety of climates with minimal maintenance.
