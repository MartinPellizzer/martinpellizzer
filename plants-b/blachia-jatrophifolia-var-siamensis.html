Blachia jatrophifolia var. siamensis

Blachia jatrophifolia var. siamensis, commonly known as Siamese Blachia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
