Bellis subserrata

Bellis subserrata, commonly known as the serrated daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are distinctly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bellis subserrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
