Berberis zimapana

Berberis zimapana, commonly known as the Mexican barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features thorny stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife make it a popular choice in landscaping.
