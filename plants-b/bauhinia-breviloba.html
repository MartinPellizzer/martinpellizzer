Bauhinia breviloba

Bauhinia breviloba, commonly known as the small-leaved bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in the spring or early summer. Bauhinia breviloba typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in warm climates.
