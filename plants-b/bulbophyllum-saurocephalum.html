Bulbophyllum saurocephalum

Bulbophyllum saurocephalum, commonly known as the "Lizard's Head" orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
