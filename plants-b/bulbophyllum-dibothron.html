Bulbophyllum dibothron

Bulbophyllum dibothron, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, making it a visually striking plant.
