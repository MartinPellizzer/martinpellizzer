Billbergia ambigua

Billbergia ambigua, commonly known as the zigzag bromeliad, is a striking plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically blue-green with white or silver markings, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with vibrant flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
