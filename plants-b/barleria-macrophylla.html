Barleria macrophylla

Barleria macrophylla, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Barleria macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
