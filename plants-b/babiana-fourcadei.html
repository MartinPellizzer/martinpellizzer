Babiana fourcadei

Babiana fourcadei, commonly known as the Fourcade's rain lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms after rainfall, making it a notable species in seasonal ecosystems. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods.

Babiana fourcadei is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
