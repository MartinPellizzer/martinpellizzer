Bothriochloa saccharoides subsp. americana

Bothriochloa saccharoides subsp.

americana, commonly known as American sugar grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist, sandy soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive flowering structure.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
