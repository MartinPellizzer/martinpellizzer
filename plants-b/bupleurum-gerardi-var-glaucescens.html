Bupleurum gerardi var. glaucescens

Bupleurum gerardi var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
