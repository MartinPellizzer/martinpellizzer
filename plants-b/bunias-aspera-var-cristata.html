Bunias aspera var. cristata

Bunias aspera var. cristata, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of large, wavy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem, which are followed by kidney-shaped, seed-containing pods. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wetlands.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
