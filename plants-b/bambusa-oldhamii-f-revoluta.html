Bambusa oldhamii f. revoluta

Bambusa oldhamii f. revoluta, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray sheaths and narrow, upright culms that give it a refined appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its fast growth rate makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

Botanically, it is classified as a grass within the Poaceae family, exhibiting typical bamboo features such as hollow stems and jointed nodes.
