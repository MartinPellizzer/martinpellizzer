Baphia buettneri subsp. hylophila

Baphia buettneri subsp.

hylophila, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to West Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein and essential nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
