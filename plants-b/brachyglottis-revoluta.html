Brachyglottis revoluta

Brachyglottis revoluta, commonly known as the silver flax or silver tuft, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and produces tiny, clustered inflorescences.

Brachyglottis revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
