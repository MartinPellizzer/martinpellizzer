Buddleja × wardii

Buddleja × wardii, commonly known as the butterfly bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant is known for its ability to attract a wide variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
