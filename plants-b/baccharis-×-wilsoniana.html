Baccharis × wilsoniana

Baccharis × wilsoniana, commonly known as the hybrid baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Baccharis halimifolia and Baccharis sarmentosa, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Baccharis × wilsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
