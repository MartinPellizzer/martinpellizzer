Ballota pseudodictamnus subsp. lycia

Ballota pseudodictamnus subsp.

lycia, commonly known as Lycian ballota, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
