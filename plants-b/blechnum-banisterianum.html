Blechnum banisterianum

Blechnum banisterianum, commonly known as the banana fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that resemble the leaves of a banana plant, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a central stem that produces new fronds from the base. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a glossy, dark green appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
