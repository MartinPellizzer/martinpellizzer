Begonia micranthera var. flava

Begonia micranthera var. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with contrasting red or orange bracts. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
