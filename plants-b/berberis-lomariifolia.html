Berberis lomariifolia

Berberis lomariifolia, commonly known as the mountain barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Berberis lomariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
