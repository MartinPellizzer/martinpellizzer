Brenesia tomentosa

Brenesia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly brenesia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence, which gives it a fluffy appearance.

Brenesia tomentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
