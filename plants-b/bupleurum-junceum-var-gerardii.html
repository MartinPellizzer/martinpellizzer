Bupleurum junceum var. gerardii

Bupleurum junceum var. gerardii, commonly known as Gerard's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, with a slightly bitter taste.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
