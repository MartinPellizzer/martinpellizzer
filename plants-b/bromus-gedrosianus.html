Bromus gedrosianus

Bromus gedrosianus, commonly known as the Gedrosian brome, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which contains both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination.

Bromus gedrosianus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
