Betula latifolia var. pluricostata

Betula latifolia var. pluricostata, commonly known as the broadleaf birch, is a variant of the paper birch native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
