Blumea excisa

Blumea excisa, commonly known as the fragrant blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits.

Blumea excisa is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
