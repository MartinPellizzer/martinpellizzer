Ballota hirsuta var. maroccana

Ballota hirsuta var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan hirsute ballota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
