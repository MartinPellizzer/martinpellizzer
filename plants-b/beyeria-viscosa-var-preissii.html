Beyeria viscosa var. preissii

Beyeria viscosa var. preissii, commonly known as the Woolly Beyeria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-draining conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a fine layer of white, hairy trichomes.

Beyeria viscosa var. preissii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
