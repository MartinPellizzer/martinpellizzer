Bignonia unguis-cati

Bignonia unguis-cati, commonly known as crossvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces bright orange to yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Bignonia unguis-cati is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
