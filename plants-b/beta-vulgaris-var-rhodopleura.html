Beta vulgaris var. rhodopleura

Beta vulgaris var. rhodopleura, commonly known as the red beet or chard, is a cultivated variety of the beetroot plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple stems and leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as betaine and antioxidants. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is typically grown for its edible leaves and stalks, though the root is also harvested in some varieties. Its foliage is often used in salads, soups, and cooked dishes, while the stems can be prepared similarly to asparagus.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
