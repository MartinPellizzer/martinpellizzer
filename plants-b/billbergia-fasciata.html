Billbergia fasciata

Billbergia fasciata, commonly known as the Zebra Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically bright green with distinctive white or silver stripes, giving it its zebra-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage.

The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that bloom once in its lifetime, adding visual interest to its appearance.
