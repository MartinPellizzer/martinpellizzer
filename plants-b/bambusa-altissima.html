Bambusa altissima

Bambusa altissima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping and construction due to its strength and durability.

Bambusa altissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
