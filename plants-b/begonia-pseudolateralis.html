Begonia pseudolateralis

Begonia pseudolateralis, commonly known as the lateral begonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, laterally elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners in tropical and subtropical climates.
