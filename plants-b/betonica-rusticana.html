Betonica rusticana

Betonica rusticana, commonly known as hedge betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Betonica rusticana is known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat ailments like headaches and digestive issues.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
