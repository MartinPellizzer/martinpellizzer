Begonia voluptuaria

Begonia voluptuaria, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rich, textured surface that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in the right conditions.
