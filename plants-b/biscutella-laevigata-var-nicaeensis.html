Biscutella laevigata var. nicaeensis

Biscutella laevigata var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
