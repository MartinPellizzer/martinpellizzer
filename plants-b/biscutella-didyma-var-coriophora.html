Biscutella didyma var. coriophora

Biscutella didyma var. coriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are pale yellow with four petals.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
