Ballota submitis

Ballota submitis, commonly known as the creeping ballota, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Ballota submitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
