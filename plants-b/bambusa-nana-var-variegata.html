Bambusa nana var. variegata

Bambusa nana var. variegata, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo with variegated leaves, is a compact, ornamental bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, narrow leaves with white or yellow variegation that add visual interest to gardens. The plant grows in dense clumps, reaching a maximum height of about 1.5 meters, making it ideal for small spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular watering during dry periods.

Its unique appearance and manageable size make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
