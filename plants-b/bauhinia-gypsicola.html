Bauhinia gypsicola

Bauhinia gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Bauhinia gypsicola is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
