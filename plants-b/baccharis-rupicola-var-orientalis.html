Baccharis rupicola var. orientalis

Baccharis rupicola var. orientalis, commonly known as the eastern rock baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
