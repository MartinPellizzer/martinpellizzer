Baptisia × intermedia

Baptisia × intermedia, commonly known as false indigo, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, bean-like seed pods that turn brown as they mature. Baptisia × intermedia is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and low-maintenance ornamental plant.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
