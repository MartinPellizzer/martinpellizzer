Badiera pulchra

Badiera pulchra, commonly known as the purple-stemmed rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple stems, which contrast with its glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
