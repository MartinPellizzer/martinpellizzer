Bupleurum falcatum subsp. marginatum

Bupleurum falcatum subsp.

marginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf cicely, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, aromatic leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in umbels.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and the presence of essential oils in its foliage.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported tonic and antispasmodic properties.
