Bactris pavoniana

Bactris pavoniana, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often a striking pink or red color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits. Bactris pavoniana is typically found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.
