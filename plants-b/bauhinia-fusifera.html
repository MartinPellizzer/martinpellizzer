Bauhinia fusifera

Bauhinia fusifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

Bauhinia fusifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
