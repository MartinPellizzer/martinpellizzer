Bulbostylis zambesica var. occidentalis

Bulbostylis zambesica var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western marsh grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in wetland and swampy areas. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous.

This variety is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
