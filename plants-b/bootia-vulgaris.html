Bootia vulgaris

Bootia vulgaris, commonly known as the common bootia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Bootia vulgaris is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
