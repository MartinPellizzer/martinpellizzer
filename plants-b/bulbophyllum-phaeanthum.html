Bulbophyllum phaeanthum

Bulbophyllum phaeanthum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the late spring to early summer.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
