Bromelia reversacantha

Bromelia reversacantha, commonly known as the reverse-spined bromeliad, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that stands out against its rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a glossy surface, and they form a central cup that collects rainwater, providing a habitat for small organisms. Bromelia reversacantha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
