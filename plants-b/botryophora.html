Botryophora

Botryophora, commonly known as the "bunchberry," is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Botryophora is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
