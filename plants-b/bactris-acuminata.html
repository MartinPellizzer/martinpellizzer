Bactris acuminata

Bactris acuminata, commonly known as the pink pataua, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is consumed by both humans and wildlife.
