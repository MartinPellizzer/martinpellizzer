Brassica napus convar. sibirica

Brassica napus convar. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian oilseed rape, is a variant of the oilseed rape species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in colder climates, making it well-suited for temperate regions. The plant produces yellow flowers and large, thick pods that contain seeds used for oil extraction. It is often cultivated for its high oil content and is also used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features include a biennial life cycle and a preference for well-drained soils.
