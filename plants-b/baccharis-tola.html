Baccharis tola

Baccharis tola, commonly known as white baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are often clustered in heads. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis tola is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
