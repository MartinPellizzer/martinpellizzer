Brillantaisia subulugurica

Brillantaisia subulugurica, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the dry season, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and for erosion control in semi-arid regions.
