Begonia edulis

Begonia edulis, commonly known as the edible begonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in traditional cuisine, while the flowers are appreciated for their ornamental value.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is best suited for greenhouse or indoor cultivation.
