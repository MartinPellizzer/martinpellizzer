Baccharis brachyphylla

Baccharis brachyphylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often found in regions with a temperate to subtropical climate.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
