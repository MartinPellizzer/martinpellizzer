Begonia tacana

Begonia tacana, commonly known as the Andean begonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, Begonia tacana is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
