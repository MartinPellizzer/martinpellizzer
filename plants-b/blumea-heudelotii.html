Blumea heudelotii

Blumea heudelotii, commonly known as the white chaffflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, giving it a delicate appearance.
