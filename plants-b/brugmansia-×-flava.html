Brugmansia × flava

Brugmansia × flava, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Brugmansia, characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which appear during the growing season. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Brugmansia × flava is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant, though it contains toxic alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
