Brassica fruticulosa subsp. fruticulosa

Brassica fruticulosa subsp.

fruticulosa, commonly known as the shrub mustard, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright, bushy growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
