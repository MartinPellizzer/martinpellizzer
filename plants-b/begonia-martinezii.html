Begonia martinezii

Begonia martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez begonia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its foliage is often adorned with distinctive patterns and colors, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, bloom throughout the growing season.
