Bactris hirta var. jenmanii

Bactris hirta var. jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its clustered growth habit and robust, trunk-like stems. Its leaves are large, pinnate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine, hairy structures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its visual interest.
