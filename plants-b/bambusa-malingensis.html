Bambusa malingensis

Bambusa malingensis, commonly known as the Maling bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Bambusa malingensis is valued for its rapid growth and structural strength, making it a popular choice for ornamental and utilitarian purposes.
