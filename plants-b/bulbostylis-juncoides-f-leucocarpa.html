Bulbostylis juncoides f. leucocarpa

Bulbostylis juncoides f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited bulbostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves, and its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers. The distinguishing feature of this form is its white or pale-colored fruits, which set it apart from other forms of Bulbostylis.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
