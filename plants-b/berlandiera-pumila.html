Berlandiera pumila

Berlandiera pumila, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, branching stems and produces yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
