Bravocactus horripilus subsp. wrobelianus

Bravocactus horripilus subsp.

wrobelianus, commonly known as the Wrobel's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which give it a distinctive hedgehog-like appearance.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
