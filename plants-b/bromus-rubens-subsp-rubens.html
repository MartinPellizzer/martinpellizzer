Bromus rubens subsp. rubens

Bromus rubens subsp.

rubens, commonly known as red brome, is a species of grass native to western North America. It is characterized by its reddish tint in the inflorescence and its ability to thrive in dry, open habitats.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is an annual or biennial plant, often found in disturbed soils and arid environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
