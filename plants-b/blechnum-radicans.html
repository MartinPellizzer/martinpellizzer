Blechnum radicans

Blechnum radicans, commonly known as the creeping holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant features lanceolate, leathery fronds that are often dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
