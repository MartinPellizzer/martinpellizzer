Bupariti populnea

Bupariti populnea, commonly known as the white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Bupariti populnea is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
