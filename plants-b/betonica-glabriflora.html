Betonica glabriflora

Betonica glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

Betonica glabriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
