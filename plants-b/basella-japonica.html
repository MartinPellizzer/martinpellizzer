Basella japonica

Basella japonica, commonly known as Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and edible, succulent stems that are often used in cooking. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for climbing structures or as a ground cover.

Basella japonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with its lush green foliage adding aesthetic value to gardens.
