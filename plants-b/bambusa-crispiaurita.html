Bambusa crispiaurita

Bambusa crispiaurita, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often have a silvery or pale green sheen. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a distinctive waxy coating that contributes to its unique appearance. This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and graceful growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
