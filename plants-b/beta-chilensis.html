Beta chilensis

Beta chilensis, commonly known as the Peruvian wild spinach, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions.

Beta chilensis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal.
