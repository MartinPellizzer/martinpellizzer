Begonia digyna

Begonia digyna, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a rich, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage.

Begonia digyna is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and garden settings.
