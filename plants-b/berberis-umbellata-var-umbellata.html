Berberis umbellata var. umbellata

Berberis umbellata var. umbellata, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in umbels. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often considered invasive in North America.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
