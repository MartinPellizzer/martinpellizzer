Bladhia sieboldii f. nigrocarpa

Bladhia sieboldii f. nigrocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited bladhia, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
