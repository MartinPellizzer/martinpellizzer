Burmannia bicolor

Burmannia bicolor, commonly known as the red lady's slipper, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, slipper-shaped flowers that are often adorned with a white or yellow patch. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain the seeds.

Burmannia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
