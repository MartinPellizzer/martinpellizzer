Beta lutea

Beta lutea, commonly known as the yellow goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in hue. Its seeds are small and clustered, often used as a food source for birds and small mammals.

Beta lutea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
