Biscutella longifolia subsp. tirolensis

Biscutella longifolia subsp.

tirolensis, commonly known as the Alpine biscuitflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
