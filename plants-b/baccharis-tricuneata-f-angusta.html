Baccharis tricuneata f. angusta

Baccharis tricuneata f. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
