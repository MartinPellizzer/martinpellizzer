Begonia grahamiana

Begonia grahamiana, commonly known as the Graham's begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting markings. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Begonia grahamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
