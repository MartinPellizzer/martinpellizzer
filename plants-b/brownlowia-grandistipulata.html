Brownlowia grandistipulata

Brownlowia grandistipulata, commonly known as the giant whisker tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its unique inflorescence consists of long, drooping clusters of flowers that resemble whiskers, giving it its common name.

Brownlowia grandistipulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
