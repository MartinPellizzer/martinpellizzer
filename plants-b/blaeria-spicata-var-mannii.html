Blaeria spicata var. mannii

Blaeria spicata var. mannii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact size and long-lasting blooms.

The varietal form, var. mannii, is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and more vibrant flower colors compared to the typical species.
