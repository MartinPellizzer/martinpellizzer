Begonia putzeysiana

Begonia putzeysiana, commonly known as the Putzeys' begonia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
