Beyeria leschenaultii f. eloeagnoides

Beyeria leschenaultii f. eloeagnoides, commonly known as the Eucalyptus-like form of Beyeria, is a variant of the Beyeria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Myrtaceae family.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
