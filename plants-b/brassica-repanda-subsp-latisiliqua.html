Brassica repanda subsp. latisiliqua

Brassica repanda subsp.

latisiliqua, commonly known as the broad-leaved mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cabbage and mustard. It typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The subspecies latisiliqua is distinguished by its wider leaves compared to other varieties within the species.
