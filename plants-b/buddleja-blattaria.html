Buddleja blattaria

Buddleja blattaria, commonly known as the silver-leaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Buddleja blattaria is relatively low maintenance and resistant to many common pests and diseases.
