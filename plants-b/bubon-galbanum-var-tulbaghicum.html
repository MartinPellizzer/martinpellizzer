Bubon galbanum var. tulbaghicum

Bubon galbanum var. tulbaghicum, commonly known as the Turkish galbanum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green resinous exudate, which is often used in perfumery and traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and has small, narrow leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces a distinctive, pungent odor.

The resin of this plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in incense and as a natural insect repellent.
