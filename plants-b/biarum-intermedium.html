Biarum intermedium

Biarum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Biarum intermedium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
