Boehmeria pilosiuscula

Boehmeria pilosiuscula, commonly known as silky giant bamboo, is a member of the mulberry family (Moraceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has smooth, light green leaves with a soft, silky texture on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
