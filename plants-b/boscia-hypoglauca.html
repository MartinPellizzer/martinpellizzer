Boscia hypoglauca

Boscia hypoglauca, commonly known as the white boschia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green to white bark and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Boscia hypoglauca is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
