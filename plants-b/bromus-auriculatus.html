Bromus auriculatus

Bromus auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Bromus auriculatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
