Brassia sulphurea

Brassia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers, which are often tinged with red or brown, giving it a vibrant and eye-catching appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Brassia sulphurea has a complex inflorescence structure with specialized bracts that help in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
