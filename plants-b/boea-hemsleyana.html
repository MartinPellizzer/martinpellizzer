Boea hemsleyana

Boea hemsleyana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
