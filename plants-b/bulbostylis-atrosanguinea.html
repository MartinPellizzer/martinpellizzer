Bulbostylis atrosanguinea

Bulbostylis atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with consistent moisture.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of biodiversity.
