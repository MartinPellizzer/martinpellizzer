Bromelia scarlatina

Bromelia scarlatina, commonly known as the red bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, strap-like leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces showy red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the leaf rosette. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of moss-like material, which helps retain moisture.

Bromelia scarlatina thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
