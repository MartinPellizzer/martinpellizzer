Bellardia trixago subvar. flaviflora

Bellardia trixago subvar. flaviflora, commonly known as the golden bellardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as dunes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.
