Baccharis darwinii

Baccharis darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height.

Baccharis darwinii is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
