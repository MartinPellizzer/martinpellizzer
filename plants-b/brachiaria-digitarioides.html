Brachiaria digitarioides

Brachiaria digitarioides, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming extensive mats that stabilize soil.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both an important forage plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
