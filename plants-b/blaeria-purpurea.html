Blaeria purpurea

Blaeria purpurea, commonly known as the purple blazer, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clumps, often reaching a height of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
