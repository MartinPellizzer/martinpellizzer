Breynia stipitata

Breynia stipitata, commonly known as the red-stemmed breynia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-colored stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Breynia stipitata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
