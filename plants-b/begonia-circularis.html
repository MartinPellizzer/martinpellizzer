Begonia circularis

Begonia circularis, commonly known as the round-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, thick, and glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Begonia circularis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
