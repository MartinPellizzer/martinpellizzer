Bartsia viscosa

Bartsia viscosa, commonly known as sticky bartsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest undergrowth. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early spring.

Bartsia viscosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
