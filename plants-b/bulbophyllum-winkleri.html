Bulbophyllum winkleri

Bulbophyllum winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its pseudobulbs serve as storage organs for water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant's intricate floral structure and unique coloration make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

It requires careful attention to light, humidity, and watering to ensure optimal growth and flowering.
