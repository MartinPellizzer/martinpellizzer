Blighia sapida

Blighia sapida, commonly known as ackee, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange fruit, which contains a single seed surrounded by a white, fleshy aril. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown in regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

The ackee tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
