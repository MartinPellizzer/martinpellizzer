Bertiera javanica

Bertiera javanica, commonly known as the Java bertiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal or axillary inflorescences, and are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
