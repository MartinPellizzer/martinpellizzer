Braya linearis

Braya linearis, commonly known as the linear leafed rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its linear, often succulent leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, allowing it to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and foothill habitats.

Braya linearis is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important flowering plants.
