Briza scabra

Briza scabra, commonly known as common hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Briza scabra is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
