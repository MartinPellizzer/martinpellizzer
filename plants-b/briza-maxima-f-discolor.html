Briza maxima f. discolor

Briza maxima f. discolor, commonly known as the silver wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, silvery-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, feathery panicles of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover.
