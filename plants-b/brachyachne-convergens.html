Brachyachne convergens

Brachyachne convergens, commonly known as the narrowleaf brachyachne, is a species of flowering grass native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Due to its modest appearance, it is sometimes overlooked but plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
