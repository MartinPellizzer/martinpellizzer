Begonia squamulosa var. bipindensis

Begonia squamulosa var. bipindensis, commonly known as the African begonia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare plants.
