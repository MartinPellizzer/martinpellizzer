Trigonella cancellata f. vulgaris

Trigonella cancellata f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common trigonella, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. Native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it has been used historically in traditional medicine.
