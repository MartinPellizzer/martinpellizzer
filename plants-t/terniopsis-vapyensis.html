Terniopsis vapyensis

Terniopsis vapyensis, commonly known as the Vapyen mistplant, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble spider silk, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing along the banks of slow-moving streams. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptive evolution in tropical flora.

Due to habitat loss, it is classified as endangered and is protected in several conservation reserves.
