Teucrium edelbergii

Teucrium edelbergii, commonly known as Edelberg's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium edelbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
