Tibouchina imperatoris var. parvifolia

Tibouchina imperatoris var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
