Tropaeolum elzae

Tropaeolum elzae, commonly known as Elza's nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, producing clusters of bright orange or red blossoms. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
