Toona ciliata subsp. velutina

Toona ciliata subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet tallowwood, is a tropical rainforest tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey to black bark and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and coastal rainforests.

Its wood is highly valued for its strength and fine grain, making it a significant species in native timber industries.
