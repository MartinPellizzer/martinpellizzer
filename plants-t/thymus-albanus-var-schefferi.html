Thymus albanus var. schefferi

Thymus albanus var. schefferi, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
