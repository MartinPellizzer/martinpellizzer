Testudinaria macrostachya

Testudinaria macrostachya, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet-blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Testudinaria macrostachya is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
