Tradescantia gracilis

Tradescantia gracilis, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces three-lobed leaves that are often described as spider-like, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Tradescantia gracilis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
