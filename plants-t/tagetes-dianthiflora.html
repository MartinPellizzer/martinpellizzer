Tagetes dianthiflora

Tagetes dianthiflora, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine and as a natural pest repellent.
