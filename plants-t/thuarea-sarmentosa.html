Thuarea sarmentosa

Thuarea sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping thuaria, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with flexible stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Thuarea sarmentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and dry environments.
