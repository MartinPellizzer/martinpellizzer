Taraxacum melanops

Taraxacum melanops, commonly known as the black-eyed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flower head with a dark center. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also noted for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common sight in meadows and disturbed soils.
