Tribulus albus

Tribulus albus, commonly known as white bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields. Its fruit is a hairy capsule that contains several small, white seeds.

Tribulus albus is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
