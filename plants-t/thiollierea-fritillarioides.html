Thiollierea fritillarioides

Thiollierea fritillarioides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Thiollierea fritillarioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
