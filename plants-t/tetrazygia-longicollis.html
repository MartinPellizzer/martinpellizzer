Tetrazygia longicollis

Tetrazygia longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
