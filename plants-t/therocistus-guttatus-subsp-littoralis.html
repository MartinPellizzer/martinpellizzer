Therocistus guttatus subsp. littoralis

Therocistus guttatus subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
