Taraxacum catameristum

Taraxacum catameristum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. It is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum catameristum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties.
