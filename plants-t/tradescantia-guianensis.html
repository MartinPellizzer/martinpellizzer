Tradescantia guianensis

Tradescantia guianensis, commonly known as the wandering trifid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tradescantia guianensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes.
