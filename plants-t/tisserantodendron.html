Tisserantodendron

Tisserantodendron, commonly known as the golden bark tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark that exudes a golden sap when cut. The leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
