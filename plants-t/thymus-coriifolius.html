Thymus coriifolius

Thymus coriifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and flowering stems that produce pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils.

Thymus coriifolius is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its aromatic foliage and flowering display.
