Triceras bicorne

Triceras bicorne, commonly known as the horned tricera, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, branched inflorescence that resembles a coral-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Triceras bicorne is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
