Tragopogon longirostris var. abbreviatus

Tragopogon longirostris var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its stems are shorter and more compact compared to the typical variety.

The species is noted for its ability to hybridize with other Tragopogon species, contributing to its ecological and botanical significance.
