Trichodesma zeylanicum var. zeylanicum

Trichodesma zeylanicum var. zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian rose or Malabar rose, is a shrub native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that can be branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden decoration and traditional medicinal uses.
