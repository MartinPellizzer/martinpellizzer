Tulipa regelii

Tulipa regelii, commonly known as the Regel's tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climates, often entering a dormant state during winter.

Tulipa regelii is valued in horticulture for its striking blooms and hardiness in colder regions.
