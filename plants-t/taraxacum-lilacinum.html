Taraxacum lilacinum

Taraxacum lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
