Triticum persicum var. darginicum

Triticum persicum var. darginicum, commonly known as the Darginian wheat, is a rare and ancient species of wheat native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched inflorescence and small, glabrous spikelets. The plant typically grows in rocky, mountainous habitats and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its grains are small and have a distinct, nutty flavor, making it a subject of interest for both botanists and culinary enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable species for agricultural and conservation research.
