Thalictrum minus f. macrophyllum

Thalictrum minus f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are large and deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and early spring blooms.
