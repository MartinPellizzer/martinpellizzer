Trigonophyllum bicorne

Trigonophyllum bicorne, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like seed pods. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Trigonophyllum bicorne is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to conserve water.
