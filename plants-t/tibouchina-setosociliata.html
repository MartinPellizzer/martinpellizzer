Tibouchina setosociliata

Tibouchina setosociliata, commonly known as the woolly tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tibouchina setosociliata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
