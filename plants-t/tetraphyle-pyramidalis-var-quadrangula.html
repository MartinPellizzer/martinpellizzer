Tetraphyle pyramidalis var. quadrangula

Tetraphyle pyramidalis var. quadrangula, commonly known as the four-sided pyramid plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinct quadrangular stem structure.

The plant exhibits a pyramidal growth habit, with branches forming a conical shape as it matures. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The species is native to arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking form and resilience.
