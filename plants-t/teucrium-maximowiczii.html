Teucrium maximowiczii

Teucrium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean germander, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly Korea and parts of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium maximowiczii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
