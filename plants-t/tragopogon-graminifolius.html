Tragopogon graminifolius

Tragopogon graminifolius, commonly known as the slender goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it the species name "graminifolius," which translates to "grass-like leaves.".

The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled bracts, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.
