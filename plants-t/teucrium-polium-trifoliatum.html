Teucrium polium-trifoliatum

Teucrium polium-trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
