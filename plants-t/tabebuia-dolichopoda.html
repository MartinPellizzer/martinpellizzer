Tabebuia dolichopoda

Tabebuia dolichopoda, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the late winter or early spring. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish in color, and it produces woody fruits that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
