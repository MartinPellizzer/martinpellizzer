Trisetaria spicata

Trisetaria spicata, commonly known as the spicate trisetaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib.

Trisetaria spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
