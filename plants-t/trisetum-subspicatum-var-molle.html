Trisetum subspicatum var. molle

Trisetum subspicatum var. molle, commonly known as soft hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and often hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

Trisetum subspicatum var. molle plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
