Taraxacum deorum

Taraxacum deorum, commonly known as the imperial dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower heads, which are surrounded by a crown of white ray florets. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum deorum is noted for its resilience and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
