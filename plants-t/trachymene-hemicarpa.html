Trachymene hemicarpa

Trachymene hemicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false witchetty grass, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Trachymene hemicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
