Ticanto caesia

Ticanto caesia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ticanto caesia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
