Thysanolaena assamensis

Thysanolaena assamensis, commonly known as the Assam grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with hairy structures.

Thysanolaena assamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional landscaping and erosion control.
