Trachycalymma shabaense

Trachycalymma shabaense, commonly known as the Shaba flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish color.

Trachycalymma shabaense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
