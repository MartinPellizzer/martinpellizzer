Tricyrtis macropoda f. epunctata

Tricyrtis macropoda f. epunctata, commonly known as the spotted spider lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
