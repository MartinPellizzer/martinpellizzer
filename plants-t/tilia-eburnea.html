Tilia eburnea

Tilia eburnea, commonly known as the whitebeam or European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Tilia eburnea is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.

It is also known for its hard, heavy wood, which has been historically used for tool handles and other small crafts.
