Thymus montanus var. flos-cuculi

Thymus montanus var. flos-cuculi, commonly known as the cuckoo flower thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique flowering pattern and medicinal properties make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
