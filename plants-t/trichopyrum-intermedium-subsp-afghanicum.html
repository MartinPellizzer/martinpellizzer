Trichopyrum intermedium subsp. afghanicum

Trichopyrum intermedium subsp.

afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan false flax, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on both surfaces. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience.
