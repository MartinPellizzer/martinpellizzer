Trevirania

Trevirania, commonly known as the sacred fern, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that unfurl slowly, often resembling the movement of a dancer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to flourish. Trevirania is notable for its unique reproductive structures, which are concealed within its foliage.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
