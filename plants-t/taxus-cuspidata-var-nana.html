Taxus cuspidata var. nana

Taxus cuspidata var. nana, commonly known as the Japanese yew dwarf form, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish sheen. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its slow growth and ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
