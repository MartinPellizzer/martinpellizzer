Tragacantha cappadocica

Tragacantha cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its growth habit includes shrub-like forms with thorny stems, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
