Trifolium chilense

Trifolium chilense, commonly known as Chilean clover, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Trifolium chilense is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
