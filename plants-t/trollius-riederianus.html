Trollius riederianus

Trollius riederianus, commonly known as the Rieder's globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trollius riederianus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
