Turnera melochioides

Turnera melochioides, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Turnera melochioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
