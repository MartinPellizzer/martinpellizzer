Tubella tactiquensis

Tubella tactiquensis, commonly known as the translucent tube worm, is a species of marine polychaete worm found in deep-sea environments.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular body that is often transparent or semi-transparent, allowing for the observation of internal structures. The worm's body is segmented and covered in setae, which aid in locomotion through the sediment. It thrives in cold, oxygen-rich waters and is often associated with hydrothermal vent ecosystems.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it plays a role in deep-sea ecological studies.
