Thunbergia purpurata

Thunbergia purpurata, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive trumpet shape. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia purpurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
