Tragia ukambensis var. ugandensis

Tragia ukambensis var. ugandensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically used in traditional medicine or horticulture.
