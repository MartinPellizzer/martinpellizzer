Taeniandra

Taeniandra, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa, Australia, and the Pacific Islands, where they typically grow in rocky or sandy soils. Taeniandra species are characterized by their unique, often intricate flower structures that resemble spiders, hence their common name. They produce small, colorful flowers that are usually borne on wiry stems, and they thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The genus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral displays.
