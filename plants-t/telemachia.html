Telemachia

Telemachia, commonly known as the "wild onion" or "false leek," is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are often used in traditional cuisine. Telemachia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in herbal medicine.
