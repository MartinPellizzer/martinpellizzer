Thymus decussatus

Thymus decussatus, commonly known as Creeping Thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping.
