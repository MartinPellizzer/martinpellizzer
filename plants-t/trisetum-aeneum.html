Trisetum aeneum

Trisetum aeneum, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers.

Trisetum aeneum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
