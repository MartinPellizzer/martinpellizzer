Thymus

Thymus, commonly known as thyme, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and flowers that are typically pink or white. Thymus species are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and they often thrive in rocky or well-drained soils. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, which are due to the presence of essential oils.

Many species are used in herbal medicine and as culinary herbs.
