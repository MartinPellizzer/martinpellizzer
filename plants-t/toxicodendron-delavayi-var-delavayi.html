Toxicodendron delavayi var. delavayi

Toxicodendron delavayi var. delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese lacquer tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves. The plant is known for producing a toxic sap that can cause severe skin irritation upon contact. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional lacquer production.

The leaves are typically used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
