Teucrium rotundifolium subsp. ruiziorum

Teucrium rotundifolium subsp.

ruiziorum, commonly known as Ruiz's roundleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its rounded, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for native gardens and xeriscaping.
