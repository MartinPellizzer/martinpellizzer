Telipogon erratus

Telipogon erratus, commonly known as the red-flowered grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and is known for its striking red inflorescence. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique coloration and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
