Trigonella korovinii

Trigonella korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's bitter vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, this plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the local flora in its native habitat.
