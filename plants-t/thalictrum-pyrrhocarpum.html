Thalictrum pyrrhocarpum

Thalictrum pyrrhocarpum, commonly known as the redfruit meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its unique feature is the bright red, berry-like fruits that develop after flowering, which are an important food source for birds.

Thalictrum pyrrhocarpum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
