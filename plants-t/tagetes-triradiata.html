Tagetes triradiata

Tagetes triradiata, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. Tagetes triradiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
