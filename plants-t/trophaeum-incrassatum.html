Trophaeum incrassatum

Trophaeum incrassatum, commonly known as the thickened broom.

This plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. It is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Its thick, woody stems and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important component of its ecosystem.
