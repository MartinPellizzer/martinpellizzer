Thymus dinaricus

Thymus dinaricus, commonly known as Dinaric thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thymus dinaricus is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
