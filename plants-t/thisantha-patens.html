Thisantha patens

Thisantha patens, commonly known as the spreading flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange or red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has slender, branching stems that often spread horizontally, giving it a cascading appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Thisantha patens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
