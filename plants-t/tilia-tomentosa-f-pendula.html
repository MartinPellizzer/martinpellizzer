Tilia tomentosa f. pendula

Tilia tomentosa f. pendula, commonly known as the woolly maple weeping form, is a unique cultivar of the woolly maple.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, making it a striking ornamental tree. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense, white pubescence that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
