Taraxacum skalnatense

Taraxacum skalnatense, commonly known as the Slovak dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. It produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
