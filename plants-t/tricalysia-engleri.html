Tricalysia engleri

Tricalysia engleri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
