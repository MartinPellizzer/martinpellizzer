Thymus lanceolatus subsp. kabylicus

Thymus lanceolatus subsp.

kabylicus, commonly known as Kabylic thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and parts of Anatolia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a seasoning in local cuisines.
