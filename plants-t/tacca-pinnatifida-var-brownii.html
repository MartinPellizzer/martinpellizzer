Tacca pinnatifida var. brownii

Tacca pinnatifida var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's shuttlecock, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces distinctive, ornamental flowers that resemble shuttlecocks, with long, pendulous bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is particularly valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
