Taraxacum adiantifrons

Taraxacum adiantifrons, commonly known as the fernleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that resemble fern fronds, giving it its common name. The plant produces a single, tall, hairy stem that bears a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky soils.

Taraxacum adiantifrons is a short-lived perennial that thrives in full sun and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
