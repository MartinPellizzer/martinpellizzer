Thelymitra jacksonii

Thelymitra jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

Thelymitra jacksonii is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
