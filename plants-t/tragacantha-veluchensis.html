Tragacantha veluchensis

Tragacantha veluchensis, commonly known as the velvet tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems, which has been used traditionally in various applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique properties, it is of interest to both botanists and traditional medicine practitioners.
