Thalictrum minus subsp. carpaticum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a slender, upright stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
