Turnera melochioides var. rugosa

Turnera melochioides var. rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its rough, textured leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The varietal name 'rugosa' refers to the rugged or hairy texture of its foliage.
