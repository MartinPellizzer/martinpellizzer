Trillium lirioides f. giganteum

Trillium lirioides f. giganteum, commonly known as the giant wake-robin, is a striking variant of the wake-robin species native to the eastern United States.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent green sepals, which bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. The plant has three large, broad leaves that emerge from a single point, and its stems are often taller than the standard species.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
