Trigonella psilorhynchos

Trigonella psilorhynchos, commonly known as the Indian fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Trigonella psilorhynchos is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop for agricultural systems.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various climates and soils, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
