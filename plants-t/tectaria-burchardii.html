Tectaria burchardii

Tectaria burchardii, commonly known as the Burchard's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Tectaria burchardii is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
