Taraxacum mediterraniforme

Taraxacum mediterraniforme, commonly known as the Mediterranean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white pappus for wind dispersal. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its morphology and ecological adaptations make it a resilient and widespread species in its native range.
