Tragacantha schrenkiana

Tragacantha schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

The plant is also valued for its gum, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
