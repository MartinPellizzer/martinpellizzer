Trisetum scabriusculum

Trisetum scabriusculum, commonly known as roughhair fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Trisetum scabriusculum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
