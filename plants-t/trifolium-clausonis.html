Trifolium clausonis

Trifolium clausonis, commonly known as the Clauson's clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Native to specific regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
