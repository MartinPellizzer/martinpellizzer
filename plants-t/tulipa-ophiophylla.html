Tulipa ophiophylla

Tulipa ophiophylla, commonly known as the snakeleaf tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, wavy, and often curling leaves that resemble the scales of a snake, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated by gardeners who appreciate unusual and exotic plants.
