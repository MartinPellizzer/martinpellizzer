Tragacantha transcaucasica

Tragacantha transcaucasica, commonly known as Caucasian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect stem and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing small, dry seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

The sap of this plant is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
