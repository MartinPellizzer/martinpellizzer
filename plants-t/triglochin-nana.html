Triglochin nana

Triglochin nana, commonly known as dwarf spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its botanical features include rhizomes and stolons, which allow it to spread vegetatively, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Triglochin nana is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
