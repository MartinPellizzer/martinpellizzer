Tibouchina trichopoda

Tibouchina trichopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Tibouchina trichopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden settings.
