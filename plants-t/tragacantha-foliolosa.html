Tragacantha foliolosa

Tragacantha foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate and conditions.

Tragacantha foliolosa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in herbal remedies.
