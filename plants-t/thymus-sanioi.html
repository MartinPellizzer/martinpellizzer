Thymus sanioi

Thymus sanioi, commonly known as the Sanio thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features compact shrubs with gray-green foliage and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both garden use and traditional herbal applications.
