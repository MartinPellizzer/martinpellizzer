Trichilia mucronata

Trichilia mucronata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and light-colored.

Trichilia mucronata is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
