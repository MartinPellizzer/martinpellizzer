Tribulus macropterus

Tribulus macropterus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
