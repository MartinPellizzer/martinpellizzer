Thymus ciliolatus

Thymus ciliolatus, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small pink or purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
