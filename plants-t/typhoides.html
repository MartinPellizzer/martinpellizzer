Typhoides

Typhoides, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. Typhoides species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in moist, open habitats.

These plants are known for their ability to thrive in disturbed soils and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
