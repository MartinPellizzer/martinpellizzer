Taraxacum martellense

Taraxacum martellense, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Taraxacum martellense is an important part of the flora in high-altitude ecosystems.
