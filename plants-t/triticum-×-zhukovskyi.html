Triticum × zhukovskyi

Triticum × zhukovskyi, commonly known as the Zhukovsky's wheat, is a synthetic amphidiploid species resulting from the hybridization of Triticum turgidum and Aegilops tauschii.

It possesses a genome composition of AABBRR, combining the genomes of its parental species. This plant is notable for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications. It is often studied for its role in wheat improvement programs.

The species exhibits characteristics that make it valuable in breeding for traits such as disease resistance and yield enhancement.
