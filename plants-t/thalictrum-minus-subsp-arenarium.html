Thalictrum minus subsp. arenarium

Thalictrum minus subsp.

arenarium, commonly known as the sand thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, white to greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance with finely divided lobes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
