Thesidium strigulosum

Thesidium strigulosum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces showy, purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
