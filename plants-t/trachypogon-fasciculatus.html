Trachypogon fasciculatus

Trachypogon fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trachypogon fasciculatus is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
