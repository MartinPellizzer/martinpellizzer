Triticum vavilovii var. vaneum

Triticum vavilovii var. vaneum, commonly known as wild wheat, is a wild relative of domesticated wheat species.

It is native to the steppes of Central Asia and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. This plant is notable for its genetic diversity, which makes it an important resource for wheat breeding programs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Triticum vavilovii var. vaneum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
