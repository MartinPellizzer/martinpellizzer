Thaumatophyllum leal-costae

Thaumatophyllum leal-costae, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that features a vibrant yellow spathe and a spadix with a mix of white and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces aerial roots that help it climb toward light.

Thaumatophyllum leal-costae is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
