Trachydium napiferum

Trachydium napiferum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and serrated.

Trachydium napiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
