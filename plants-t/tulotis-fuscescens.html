Tulotis fuscescens

Tulotis fuscescens, commonly known as the black tulip, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to almost black petals, which contrast with its bright yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Tulotis fuscescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
