Tamarix pakistanica

Tamarix pakistanica, commonly known as the Kashmir date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces fluffy, pink or pale red flowers in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reclamation of degraded soils. It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

The plant also has cultural and traditional significance in the regions where it is found.
