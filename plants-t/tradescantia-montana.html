Tradescantia montana

Tradescantia montana, commonly known as snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish tinge along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers on slender stalks during the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage is also valued for its air-purifying qualities.
