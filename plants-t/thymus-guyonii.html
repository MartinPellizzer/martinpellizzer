Thymus guyonii

Thymus guyonii, commonly known as the Guyon's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.

Thymus guyonii is also a popular choice for culinary use, adding a fragrant, citrusy flavor to dishes.
