Teucrium capitatum var. polioides

Teucrium capitatum var. polioides, commonly known as the polypody teucrium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
