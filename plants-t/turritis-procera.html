Turritis procera

Turritis procera, commonly known as the giant white lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest flowering plants in the world. The plant has a robust, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, Turritis procera is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
