Tetrapterys crebriflora var. dubia

Tetrapterys crebriflora var. dubia, commonly known as the slender leaf false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
