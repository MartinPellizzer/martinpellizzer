Thalictrum obliquum

Thalictrum obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white, spire-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Thalictrum obliquum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
