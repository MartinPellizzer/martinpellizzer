Typhonium triste

Typhonium triste, commonly known as the sad arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Typhonium triste is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
