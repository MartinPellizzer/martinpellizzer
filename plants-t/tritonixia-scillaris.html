Tritonixia scillaris

Tritonixia scillaris, commonly known as the sea onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is often found growing in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Tritonixia scillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological studies.
