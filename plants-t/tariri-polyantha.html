Tariri polyantha

Tariri polyantha, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Tariri polyantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and wildlife benefits.
