Tuberaria guttata subsp. bupleurifolia

Tuberaria guttata subsp.

bupleurifolia, commonly known as the spotted tuberaria, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular-dotted leaves and shorter, more compact growth habit compared to the typical form. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
