Tacinga inamoena subsp. subcylindrica

Tacinga inamoena subsp.

subcylindrica, commonly known as the slender tacinga, is a species native to parts of southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy or open habitats, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender stem structure compared to other members of the species.
