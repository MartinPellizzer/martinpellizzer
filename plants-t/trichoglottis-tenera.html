Trichoglottis tenera

Trichoglottis tenera, commonly known as the slender spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous tiny flowers.

Trichoglottis tenera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
