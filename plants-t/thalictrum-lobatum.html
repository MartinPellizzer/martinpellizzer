Thalictrum lobatum

Thalictrum lobatum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Thalictrum lobatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
