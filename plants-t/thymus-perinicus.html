Thymus perinicus

Thymus perinicus, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems. The plant produces tiny pink or purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant ground cover.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
