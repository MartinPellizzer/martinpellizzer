Thymus × mastichinalis

Thymus × mastichinalis, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in culinary applications.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and antimicrobial properties.
