Trichogonia menthifolia var. genuina

Trichogonia menthifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the fragrant marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features slender, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, white to pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and ability to thrive in moist environments make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
