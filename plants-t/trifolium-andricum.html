Trifolium andricum

Trifolium andricum, commonly known as the water clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in moist, often aquatic environments such as marshes and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white, and arranged in spikes, typical of the genus Trifolium.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in soil enrichment.
