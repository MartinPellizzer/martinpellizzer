Trifolium grayi

Trifolium grayi, commonly known as Gray's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often forming dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium grayi is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
