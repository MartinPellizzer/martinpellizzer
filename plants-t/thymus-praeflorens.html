Thymus praeflorens

Thymus praeflorens, commonly known as the early thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before other thyme species. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus praeflorens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
