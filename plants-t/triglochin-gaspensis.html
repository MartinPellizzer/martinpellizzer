Triglochin gaspensis

Triglochin gaspensis, commonly known as the Florida spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Florida and parts of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to the structure of wetland ecosystems.

Triglochin gaspensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
