Trasus praecox

Trasus praecox, commonly known as the early morning flower, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences.

It typically blooms in early spring, featuring small, often white or pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and meadows, where it contributes to the early season floral diversity.

Trasus praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting early pollinators.
