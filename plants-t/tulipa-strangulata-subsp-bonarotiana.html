Tulipa strangulata subsp. bonarotiana

Tulipa strangulata subsp.

bonarotiana, commonly known as the Bonarotian tulip, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a distinctive black eye.

The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
