Tropaeolum boliviense

Tropaeolum boliviense, commonly known as the Andean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum boliviense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
