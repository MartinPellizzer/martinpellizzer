Thalictrum actaeifolium var. actaeifolium

Thalictrum actaeifolium var. actaeifolium, commonly known as the showy lady's-tresses, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, lace-like foliage and produces fragrant, spire-like inflorescences of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
