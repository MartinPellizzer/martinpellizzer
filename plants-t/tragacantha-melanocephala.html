Tragacantha melanocephala

Tragacantha melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black flower heads. The plant produces a viscous gum from its stems, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry climates.

Its morphology includes slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
