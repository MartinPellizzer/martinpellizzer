Trichomanes racemulosum

Trichomanes racemulosum, commonly known as the clustered maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes racemulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
