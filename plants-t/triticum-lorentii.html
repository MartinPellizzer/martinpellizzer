Triticum lorentii

Triticum lorentii, commonly known as wild einkorn, is a wild species of wheat native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a diploid species with a genome size of approximately 10.2 Gb, making it one of the smallest among wheat species. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its grains are small and usually have a dark brown or black coloration.

Triticum lorentii is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs for modern wheat varieties.
