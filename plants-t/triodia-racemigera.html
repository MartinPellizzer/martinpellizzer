Triodia racemigera

Triodia racemigera, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming extensive vegetation mats.

Triodia racemigera plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
