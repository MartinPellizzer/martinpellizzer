Tacca maculata

Tacca maculata, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "tongue of the devil," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and distinctive purple veins. The plant produces a unique, spatulate-shaped flower that resembles a bat, with a central spadix surrounded by bright red and purple bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tacca maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
