Tripogon pungens

Tripogon pungens, commonly known as the spiked grass or pungent grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. Tripogon pungens is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its name "pungens" refers to the sharp, prickly texture of its leaves, which can be somewhat irritating to the skin.
