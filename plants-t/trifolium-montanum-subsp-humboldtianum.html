Trifolium montanum subsp. humboldtianum

Trifolium montanum subsp.

humboldtianum, commonly known as Humboldt's mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
