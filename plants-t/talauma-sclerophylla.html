Talauma sclerophylla

Talauma sclerophylla, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are adapted to withstand arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
