Thiersia insignis

Thiersia insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thiersia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
