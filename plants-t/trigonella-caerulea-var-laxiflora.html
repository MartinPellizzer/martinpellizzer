Trigonella caerulea var. laxiflora

Trigonella caerulea var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender blue bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and has a hairy texture. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
