Triosteum aurantiacum

Triosteum aurantiacum, commonly known as the redroot sandveld, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a slender, branched stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and fynbos regions.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
