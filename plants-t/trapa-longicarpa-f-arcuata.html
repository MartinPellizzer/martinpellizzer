Trapa longicarpa f. arcuata

Trapa longicarpa f. arcuata, commonly known as the curved-leaf water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut species native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are harvested for food. Its aquatic nature allows it to thrive in shallow lakes and ponds, where it can form dense vegetation.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in aquatic botany.
