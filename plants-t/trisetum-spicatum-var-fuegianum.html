Trisetum spicatum var. fuegianum

Trisetum spicatum var. fuegianum, commonly known as the Fuegian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern Andes and Patagonia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
