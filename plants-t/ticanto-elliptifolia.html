Ticanto elliptifolia

Ticanto elliptifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ticanto elliptifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
