Tephrosia nyikensis subsp. victoriensis

Tephrosia nyikensis subsp.

victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria tephrosia, is a shrub native to parts of East Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a typical feature of legumes.

This species is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
