Thymus patavinus

Thymus patavinus, commonly known as the purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, woody stems covered in small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Thymus patavinus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal collections.
