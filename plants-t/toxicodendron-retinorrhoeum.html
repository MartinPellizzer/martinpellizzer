Toxicodendron retinorrhoeum

Toxicodendron retinorrhoeum, commonly known as the sumac, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and can cause skin irritation upon contact.
