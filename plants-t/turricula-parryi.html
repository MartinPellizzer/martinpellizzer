Turricula parryi

Turricula parryi, commonly known as the Parry's turricula, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. Turricula parryi thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in desert gardens.
