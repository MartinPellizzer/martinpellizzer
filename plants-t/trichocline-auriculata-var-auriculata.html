Trichocline auriculata var. auriculata

Trichocline auriculata var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate trichocline, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
