Teucrium capitatum subsp. majoricum

Teucrium capitatum subsp.

majoricum, commonly known as the greater wall germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it historically valuable in traditional herbal remedies.
