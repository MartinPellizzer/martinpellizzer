Tococa bolivarensis subsp. occidentalis

Tococa bolivarensis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western tococa, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
