Taraxacum elegantifrons

Taraxacum elegantifrons, commonly known as the elegant dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and a single, showy yellow flower head that develops into a white pappus for wind dispersal. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
