Thalictrum elatum

Thalictrum elatum, commonly known as the tall meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage and produces showy, spire-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to woodland or meadow gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum elatum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for shady garden spaces.
