Trifolium attenuatum

Trifolium attenuatum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Trifolium attenuatum is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value and hardiness.
