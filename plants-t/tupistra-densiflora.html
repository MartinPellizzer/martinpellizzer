Tupistra densiflora

Tupistra densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered tufted grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

Tupistra densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
