Teucrium multicaule subsp. planifolium

Teucrium multicaule subsp.

planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a valued plant in traditional herbal practices.
