Tulipa talijevii

Tulipa talijevii, commonly known as the Italian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring and early summer, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Tulipa talijevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and striking blooms.
