Thapsus montanum

Thapsus montanum, commonly known as mountain thapsus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to regions in Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or greenish flowers that appear in umbels. Its leaves are usually finely divided and aromatic, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thapsus montanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
