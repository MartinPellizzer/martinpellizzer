Triglochin palustris var. crassiculmis

Triglochin palustris var. crassiculmis, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are broad and succulent, adapted to thrive in water-saturated soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in spikes. It is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats across temperate regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
