Tiliacora fraternaria

Tiliacora fraternaria, commonly known as the Indian mallow or heartleaf tiliacora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Tiliacora fraternaria is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
