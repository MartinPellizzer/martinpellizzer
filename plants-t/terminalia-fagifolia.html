Terminalia fagifolia

Terminalia fagifolia, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter, followed by spherical, red fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as riverine forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
