Trisiola

Trisiola, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. Trisiola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for erosion control and landscaping.
