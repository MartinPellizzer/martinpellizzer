Triticum repens var. subulatum

Triticum repens var. subulatum, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the annual grass species Triticum repens.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and slender growth habit, which distinguish it from other varieties of meadowgrass. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and pastures. It produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and ecological studies.
