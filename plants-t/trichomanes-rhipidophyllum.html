Trichomanes rhipidophyllum

Trichomanes rhipidophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperborea, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Trichomanes rhipidophyllum is notable for its small size and intricate leaf structure, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.
