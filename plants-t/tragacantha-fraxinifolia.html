Tragacantha fraxinifolia

Tragacantha fraxinifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh climates.

The plant is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
