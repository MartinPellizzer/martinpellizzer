Trifolium carolinianum f. bejariense

Trifolium carolinianum f. bejariense, commonly known as the Bejari clover, is a botanical variant of the Carolina clover.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
