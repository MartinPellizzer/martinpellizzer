Tragacantha purpurea

Tragacantha purpurea, commonly known as the purple tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces distinctive purple flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of a natural gum.
