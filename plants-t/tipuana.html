Tipuana

Tipuana, commonly known as the "Araucaria" or "Tipuana tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Tipuana trees are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are used in landscaping due to their adaptability to various soil types.

They also play a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
