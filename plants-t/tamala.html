Tamala

Tamala, commonly known as *Ziziphus jujuba*, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often referred to as Chinese dates, which are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, chewy texture. Tamala is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating ailments such as diabetes and digestive issues.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a popular ornamental and agricultural plant.
