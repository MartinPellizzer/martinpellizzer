Tephrosia lamprolobioides

Tephrosia lamprolobioides, commonly known as the silverleaf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically pale green to silver in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Tephrosia lamprolobioides is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
