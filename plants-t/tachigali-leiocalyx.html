Tachigali leiocalyx

Tachigali leiocalyx, commonly known as the golden tachigali, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.
