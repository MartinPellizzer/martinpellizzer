Typhonium roxburghii

Typhonium roxburghii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellowish-green spathe flowers, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Typhonium roxburghii is also valued for its edible tubers, which are used in various culinary applications.
