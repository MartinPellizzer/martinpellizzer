Talauma roxburghii

Talauma roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a yellow, sour berry that ripens in winter and is rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
