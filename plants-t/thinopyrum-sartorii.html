Thinopyrum sartorii

Thinopyrum sartorii, commonly known as goatgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems, with each spikelet containing several small, awned florets. Thinopyrum sartorii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays a role in both agricultural systems and ecological landscapes.
