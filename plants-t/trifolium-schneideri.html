Trifolium schneideri

Trifolium schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and agricultural purposes.
