Trifolium barbigerum var. barbigerum

Trifolium barbigerum var. barbigerum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and cover cropping.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it a common species in both natural and agricultural settings.
