Tetraneuris glabriuscula

Tetraneuris glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tetraneuris glabriuscula is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
