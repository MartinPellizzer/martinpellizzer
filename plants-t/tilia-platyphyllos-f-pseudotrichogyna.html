Tilia platyphyllos f. pseudotrichogyna

Tilia platyphyllos f. pseudotrichogyna, commonly known as the broad-leaved linden, is a botanical variant of the linden tree.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and paler beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
