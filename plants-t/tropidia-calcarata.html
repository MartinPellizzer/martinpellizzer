Tropidia calcarata

Tropidia calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green, often with a distinctive spurred shape. Tropidia calcarata is adapted to high-altitude environments, where it tolerates cold temperatures and low moisture levels.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
