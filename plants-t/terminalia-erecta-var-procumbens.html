Terminalia erecta var. procumbens

Terminalia erecta var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate terminalia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with branches that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or forest margins.
