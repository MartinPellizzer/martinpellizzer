Teucrium polium var. trifoliatum

Teucrium polium var. trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
