Thalictrum reticulatum var. reticulatum

Thalictrum reticulatum var. reticulatum, commonly known as the nettleleaf meadow rue, is a flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
