Toona ciliata var. pilipetala

Toona ciliata var. pilipetala, commonly known as the Philippine toona, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, and they are typically dark green on the upper surface. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
