Thoracostachyum vietnamense

Thoracostachyum vietnamense, commonly known as the Vietnamese chestnut palm, is a flowering plant native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
