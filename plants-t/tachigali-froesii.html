Tachigali froesii

Tachigali froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody pods containing seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, Tachigali froesii is considered a vulnerable species.
