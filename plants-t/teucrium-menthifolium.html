Teucrium menthifolium

Teucrium menthifolium, commonly known as caraway mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves with a minty fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.
