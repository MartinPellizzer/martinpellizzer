Trichilia moschata subsp. moschata

Trichilia moschata subsp.

moschata, commonly known as the fragrant trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods.

The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
