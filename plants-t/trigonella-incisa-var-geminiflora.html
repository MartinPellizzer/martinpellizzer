Trigonella incisa var. geminiflora

Trigonella incisa var. geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered fenugreek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are typically borne in pairs, giving it its varietal name. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the legume family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
