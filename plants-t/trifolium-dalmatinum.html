Trifolium dalmatinum

Trifolium dalmatinum, commonly known as Dalmatian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southern Europe, particularly Croatia and Bosnia. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and agriculture.
