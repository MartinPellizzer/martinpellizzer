Theopsis euonymifolia

Theopsis euonymifolia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, resembling flames. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Theopsis euonymifolia is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
