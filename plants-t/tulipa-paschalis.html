Tulipa paschalis

Tulipa paschalis, commonly known as the Easter tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows from a bulb, which is typical of many tulip species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The Easter tulip is noted for its early blooming habit, often signaling the arrival of spring.
