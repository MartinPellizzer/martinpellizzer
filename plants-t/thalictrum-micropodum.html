Thalictrum micropodum

Thalictrum micropodum, commonly known as the small-footed meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, clustered flowers in late spring.
