Thymus gouraudii

Thymus gouraudii, commonly known as French thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, creeping stems covered in small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a distinctive thyme scent, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus gouraudii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
