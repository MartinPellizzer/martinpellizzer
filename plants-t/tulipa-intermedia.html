Tulipa intermedia

Tulipa intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems that bear showy, cup-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to deep red, with some cultivars displaying bicolor patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its ability to naturalize and return year after year, adding vibrant color to spring gardens.
