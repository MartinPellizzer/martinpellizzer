Toddalia trifoliata

Toddalia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing flat, elongated seeds. Toddalia trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
