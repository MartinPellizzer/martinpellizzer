Tetrastigma coriaceum

Tetrastigma coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grape family (Vitaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, grape-like berries. Its stems are often covered in a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Tetrastigma coriaceum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
