Thelocactus schwarzii

Thelocactus schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's thorned cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Thelocactus schwarzii is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its unique and resilient characteristics.
