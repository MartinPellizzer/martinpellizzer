Thymus leucospermus

Thymus leucospermus, commonly known as white thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds with antimicrobial properties.
