Tachigali eriopetala

Tachigali eriopetala, commonly known as the velvet tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
