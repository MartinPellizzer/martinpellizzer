Tunica ochroleuca

Tunica ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow tunica, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Tunica ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
