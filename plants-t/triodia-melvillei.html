Triodia melvillei

Triodia melvillei, commonly known as Melville's triodia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its low, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small fauna.

Triodia melvillei is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it resilient in harsh environmental conditions.
