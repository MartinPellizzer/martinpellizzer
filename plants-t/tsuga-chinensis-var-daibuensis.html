Tsuga chinensis var. daibuensis

Tsuga chinensis var. daibuensis, commonly known as the Daibu fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to Japan.

It is a variant of the Chinese fir, characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, often forming dense, compact clusters on rocky outcrops. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
