Tachigali chrysophylla

Tachigali chrysophylla, commonly known as golden-leaf tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow to greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Tachigali chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
