Taraxacum pseudorecognitum

Taraxacum pseudorecognitum, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into distinctive white, puffball-like seed heads. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is closely related to other dandelion species and shares similar morphological features.
