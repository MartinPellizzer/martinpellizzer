Tapura magnifolia

Tapura magnifolia, commonly known as the magnolia of the tropics, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often fragrant flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
