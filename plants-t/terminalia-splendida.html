Terminalia splendida

Terminalia splendida, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it important in local crafts and traditional medicine.

Terminalia splendida also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and habitat for various species.
