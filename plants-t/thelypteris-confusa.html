Thelypteris confusa

Thelypteris confusa, commonly known as the common wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that display a unique fluorescence under ultraviolet light. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered with a layer of brown, fibrous roots.

Thelypteris confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
