Trillium isanthum

Trillium isanthum, commonly known as the white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features three broad, white petals and three leaf-like sepals, which are often mistaken for leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow throat and a short, upright stem. It thrives in rich, moist woodlands and is often found in shaded environments.

Trillium isanthum is a member of the Trillium genus, known for its striking floral displays and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
