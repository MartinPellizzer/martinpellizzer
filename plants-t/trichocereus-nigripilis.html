Trichocereus nigripilis

Trichocereus nigripilis, commonly known as the Black-haired Cereus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and sparse, dark green to grayish-blue spines. The plant produces large, showy night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in arid to semi-arid conditions with well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and botanists alike.
