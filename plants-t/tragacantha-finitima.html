Tragacantha finitima

Tragacantha finitima, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are adapted to withstand harsh, dry environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
