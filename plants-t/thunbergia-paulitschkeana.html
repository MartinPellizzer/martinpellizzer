Thunbergia paulitschkeana

Thunbergia paulitschkeana, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative element in tropical landscapes.
