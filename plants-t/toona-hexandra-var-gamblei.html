Toona hexandra var. gamblei

Toona hexandra var. gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
