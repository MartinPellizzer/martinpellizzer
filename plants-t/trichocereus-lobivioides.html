Trichocereus lobivioides

Trichocereus lobivioides, commonly known as the Lobivia-like Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often develop a spiny appearance as they mature. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
