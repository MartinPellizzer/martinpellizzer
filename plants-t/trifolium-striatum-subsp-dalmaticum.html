Trifolium striatum subsp. dalmaticum

Trifolium striatum subsp.

dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in ecological habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy stems and the presence of a distinctive stripe on the leaflets.
