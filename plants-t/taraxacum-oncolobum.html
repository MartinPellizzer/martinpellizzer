Taraxacum oncolobum

Taraxacum oncolobum, commonly known as the cancerous dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which develops into a fluffy seed head known as a pappus.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
