Tecoma squamellulosa

Tecoma squamellulosa, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Tecoma squamellulosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
