Trichipteris procera

Trichipteris procera, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Osmundaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, deeply divided fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Trichipteris procera plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native habitats.
