Teucrium rixanense

Teucrium rixanense, commonly known as the Crimean germander, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green, covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in compact clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Teucrium rixanense is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
