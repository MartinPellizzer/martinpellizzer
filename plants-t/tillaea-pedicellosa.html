Tillaea pedicellosa

Tillaea pedicellosa, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Tillaea pedicellosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
