Tmesipteris

Tmesipteris, commonly known as the water fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Tmesipteridaceae.

These plants are characterized by their aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Tmesipteris species typically have finely divided, feather-like leaves that float on the water surface or trail in the water. They play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.

The genus includes several species, each adapted to different moisture conditions, making them versatile in various wetland environments.
