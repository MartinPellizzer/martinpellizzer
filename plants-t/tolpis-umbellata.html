Tolpis umbellata

Tolpis umbellata, commonly known as the white-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Tolpis umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
