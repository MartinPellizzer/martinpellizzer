Tragia buettneri

Tragia buettneri, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Tragia buettneri is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
