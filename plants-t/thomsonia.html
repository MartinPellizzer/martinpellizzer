Thomsonia

Thomsonia, commonly known as the mountain thomsonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Thomsonia has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
