Triumfetta kochii

Triumfetta kochii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are small, blue or purple, and clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
