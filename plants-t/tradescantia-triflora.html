Tradescantia triflora

Tradescantia triflora, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and produces clusters of small, three-petaled flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
