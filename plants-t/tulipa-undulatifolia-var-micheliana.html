Tulipa undulatifolia var. micheliana

Tulipa undulatifolia var. micheliana, commonly known as the wavyleaf tulip, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive, undulating foliage.

This perennial bulbous plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Central Asia. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are broad, wavy, and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
