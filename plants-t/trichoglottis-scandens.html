Trichoglottis scandens

Trichoglottis scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, often with a faint fragrance, and are borne in clusters along the stems.
