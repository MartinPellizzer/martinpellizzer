Thymus serpyllum f. austriacus

Thymus serpyllum f. austriacus, commonly known as Austrian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is widely used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
