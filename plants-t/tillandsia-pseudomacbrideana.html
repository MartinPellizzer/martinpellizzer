Tillandsia pseudomacbrideana

Tillandsia pseudomacbrideana, commonly known as the MacBridean air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is popular among enthusiasts of air plants and indoor gardening.
