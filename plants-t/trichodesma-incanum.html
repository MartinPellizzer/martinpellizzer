Trichodesma incanum

Trichodesma incanum, commonly known as the silky trichodesma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trichodesma incanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
