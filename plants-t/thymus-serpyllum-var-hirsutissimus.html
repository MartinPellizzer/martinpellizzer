Thymus serpyllum var. hirsutissimus

Thymus serpyllum var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or violet, blooming in early summer. The plant has dense, woolly foliage that is gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and ornamental value in landscaping.
