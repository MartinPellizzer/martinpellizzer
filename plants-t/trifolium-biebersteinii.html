Trifolium biebersteinii

Trifolium biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, which are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along mountain slopes.

Trifolium biebersteinii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
