Thymus ciliatus subsp. coloratus

Thymus ciliatus subsp.

coloratus, commonly known as the purple thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant purple flowers.

The leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.
