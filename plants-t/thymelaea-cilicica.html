Thymelaea cilicica

Thymelaea cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Cyprus.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Thymelaea cilicica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
