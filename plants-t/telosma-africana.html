Telosma africana

Telosma africana, commonly known as the African trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its long, serpentine stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Telosma africana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
