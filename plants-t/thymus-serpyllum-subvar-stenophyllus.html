Thymus serpyllum subvar. stenophyllus

Thymus serpyllum subvar. stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its common name, and they are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.
