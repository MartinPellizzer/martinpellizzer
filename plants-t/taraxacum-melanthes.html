Taraxacum melanthes

Taraxacum melanthes, commonly known as the blackhead dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses its seeds via wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and grassy areas.

Taraxacum melanthes is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
