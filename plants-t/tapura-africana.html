Tapura africana

Tapura africana, commonly known as African star apple, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that give way to green, star-shaped fruits. The fruit's pulp is sweet and juicy, containing numerous small, edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
