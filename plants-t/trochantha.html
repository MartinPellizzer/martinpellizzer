Trochantha

Trochantha, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The botanical name *Trochantha* reflects its unique floral structure, which resembles a spider's web due to its intricate, layered petals.
