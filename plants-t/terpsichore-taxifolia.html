Terpsichore taxifolia

Terpsichore taxifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Terpsichore taxifolia is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to withstand both drought and periodic flooding.
