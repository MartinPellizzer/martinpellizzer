Tournefortia scabrida

Tournefortia scabrida, commonly known as the rough tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Tournefortia scabrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
