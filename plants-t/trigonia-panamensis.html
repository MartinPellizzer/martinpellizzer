Trigonia panamensis

Trigonia panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian trigonia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
