Tricalysia congesta subsp. chasei

Tricalysia congesta subsp.

chasei, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white.

The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy texture. This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
