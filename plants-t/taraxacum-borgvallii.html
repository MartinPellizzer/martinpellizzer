Taraxacum borgvallii

Taraxacum borgvallii, commonly known as the Borgvall's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a hairy, upright stem and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds with feathery pappi.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
