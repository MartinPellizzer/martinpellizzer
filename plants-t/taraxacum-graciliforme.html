Taraxacum graciliforme

Taraxacum graciliforme, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth margin, and it produces a yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy seed head.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
