Trapa europaea var. macrocarpa

Trapa europaea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. The plant features floating leaves with long petioles and produces distinctive spiny, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
