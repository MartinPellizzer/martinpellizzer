Tabernaemontana tenuiflora

Tabernaemontana tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a subtle pink or red tinge. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its long-lasting blooms.
