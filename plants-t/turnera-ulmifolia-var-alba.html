Turnera ulmifolia var. alba

Turnera ulmifolia var. alba, commonly known as white turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
