Tormentilla sylvestris

Tormentilla sylvestris, commonly known as the wild cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow, appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
