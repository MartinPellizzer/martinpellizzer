Thalictrum lasiogynum

Thalictrum lasiogynum, commonly known as the woolly meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its stems are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Thalictrum lasiogynum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring bloom.
