Thinopyrum junceum

Thinopyrum junceum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Thinopyrum junceum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
