Trichocereus tarijensis var. poco

Trichocereus tarijensis var. poco, commonly known as the "Poco Cactus," is a species native to the Andes of South America, particularly in Bolivia and Peru.

It is a columnar cactus with a slender, spindly stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces clusters of white, nocturnal flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its stems are covered in short, white to grayish spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its role in traditional medicine.
