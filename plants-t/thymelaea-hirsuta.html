Thymelaea hirsuta

Thymelaea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has woody stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and arranged in opposite pairs.

Thymelaea hirsuta is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
