Teucrium fulvum

Teucrium fulvum, commonly known as crimson germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Teucrium fulvum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
