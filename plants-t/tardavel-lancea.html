Tardavel lancea

Tardavel lancea, commonly known as the lance-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tardavel lancea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine landscapes.
