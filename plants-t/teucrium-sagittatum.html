Teucrium sagittatum

Teucrium sagittatum, commonly known as arrowhead germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Teucrium sagittatum is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
