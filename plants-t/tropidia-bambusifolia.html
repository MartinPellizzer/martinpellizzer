Tropidia bambusifolia

Tropidia bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cane-like stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tropidia bambusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
