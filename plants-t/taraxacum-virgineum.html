Taraxacum virgineum

Taraxacum virgineum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures, enabling wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum virgineum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
