Trixis rosea

Trixis rosea, commonly known as the pink trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has narrow, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its dry habitat. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Trixis rosea is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
