Thapsia decipiens

Thapsia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive thapsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disk florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Thapsia decipiens is known for its resilience and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
