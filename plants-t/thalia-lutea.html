Thalia lutea

Thalia lutea, commonly known as the yellow thalia, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Thalia lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
