Trilobulina crenata

Trilobulina crenata, commonly known as the crested spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in complex inflorescences called cyathia. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is native to parts of southern Africa.

Trilobulina crenata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
