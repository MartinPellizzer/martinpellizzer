Tropaeolum pilosum

Tropaeolum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is often grown for ornamental purposes in gardens. Its seeds are also edible and can be used in culinary applications.

Tropaeolum pilosum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for container gardening.
