Ternatea grandiflora

Ternatea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ternate, is a tropical shrub native to the island of Ternate in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near mangroves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in local ecosystems.

Ternatea grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
