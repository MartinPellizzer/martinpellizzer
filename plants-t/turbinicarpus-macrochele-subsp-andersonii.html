Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp. andersonii

Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp.

andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Turbinicarpus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to southern Mexico. This species is characterized by its spherical to cylindrical stem and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months.

It has a dense covering of white to grayish spines, which help protect it from intense sunlight and herbivores. The plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Its unique morphology and striking floral display make it a notable species within the Turbinicarpus genus.
