Thesium helichrysoides

Thesium helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has a low, spreading habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Thesium helichrysoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
