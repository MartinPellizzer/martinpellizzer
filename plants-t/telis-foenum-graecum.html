Telis foenum-graecum

Telis foenum-graecum, commonly known as the common cowpea, is an annual legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines worldwide.
