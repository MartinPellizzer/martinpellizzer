Taraxacum spiculiforme

Taraxacum spiculiforme, commonly known as the spiculate dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and bright yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine, stiff hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common sight in meadows and gardens.
