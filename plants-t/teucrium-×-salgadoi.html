Teucrium × salgadoi

Teucrium × salgadoi, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
