Thelypteris farinosa

Thelypteris farinosa, commonly known as the meadow shield fern, is a perennial fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly across the ground.

Thelypteris farinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
