Tricholaena semidecumbens

Tricholaena semidecumbens, commonly known as the creeping bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread extensively across the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Tricholaena semidecumbens is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and horticultural applications.
