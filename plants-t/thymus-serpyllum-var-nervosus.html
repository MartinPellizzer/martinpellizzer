Thymus serpyllum var. nervosus

Thymus serpyllum var. nervosus, commonly known as narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct minty aroma. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal herb gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
