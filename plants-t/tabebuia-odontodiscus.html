Tabebuia odontodiscus

Tabebuia odontodiscus, commonly known as the "Trumpet Tree," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces striking clusters of tubular, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink, yellow, and orange, depending on the species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical landscapes.

The wood of Tabebuia odontodiscus is also valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
