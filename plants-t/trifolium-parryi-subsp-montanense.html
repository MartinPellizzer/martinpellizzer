Trifolium parryi subsp. montanense

Trifolium parryi subsp.

montanense, commonly known as the Sierra clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cooler climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and woodland ecosystems.
