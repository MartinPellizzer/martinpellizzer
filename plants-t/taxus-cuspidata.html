Taxus cuspidata

Taxus cuspidata, commonly known as Japanese yew, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in forested areas.

Taxus cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
