Thalictrum orthantherum

Thalictrum orthantherum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with its stems often appearing somewhat woody at the base.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in natural ecosystems.
