Thymus maroccanus var. rhombicus

Thymus maroccanus var. rhombicus, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It features aromatic, narrow-lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture and scent. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
