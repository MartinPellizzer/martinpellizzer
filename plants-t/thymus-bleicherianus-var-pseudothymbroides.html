Thymus bleicherianus var. pseudothymbroides

Thymus bleicherianus var. pseudothymbroides, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features compact, cushion-like foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
