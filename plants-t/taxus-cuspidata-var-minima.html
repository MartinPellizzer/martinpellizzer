Taxus cuspidata var. minima

Taxus cuspidata var. minima, commonly known as the Japanese yew or dwarf yew, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage with needle-like leaves that are short and stiff. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its compact size and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
