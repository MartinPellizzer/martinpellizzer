Triticum durum grex leucura

Triticum durum grex leucura, commonly known as durum wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its hard, high-protein grain.

It is primarily cultivated for its use in pasta and semolina due to its high gluten content. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, and the grains are enclosed in tough, protective hulls.

This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and requires a significant amount of sunlight and water during its growth cycle.
