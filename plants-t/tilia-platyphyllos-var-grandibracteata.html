Tilia platyphyllos var. grandibracteata

Tilia platyphyllos var. grandibracteata, commonly known as the large-bracted lime, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
