Tidestromia oblongifolia

Tidestromia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf tidestromia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tidestromia oblongifolia is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
