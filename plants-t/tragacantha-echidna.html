Tragacantha echidna

Tragacantha echidna, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed pods that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The plant is often found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
