Tirucalia brachiata

Tirucalia brachiata, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing single seeds. The tree also has medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.
