Tristachya

Tristachya, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its showy, yellow spikelets that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often found in wet or disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of three or more branches, giving it the genus name "Tristachya," which means "three-branched.".

Tristachya species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral display.
