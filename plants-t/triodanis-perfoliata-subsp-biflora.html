Triodanis perfoliata subsp. biflora

Triodanis perfoliata subsp.

biflora, commonly known as the twinflower false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct floral morphology compared to other members of the genus.
