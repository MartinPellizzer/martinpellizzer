Trichocereus taratensis

Trichocereus taratensis, commonly known as the Taratensis cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and spines that are often sparse and short. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
