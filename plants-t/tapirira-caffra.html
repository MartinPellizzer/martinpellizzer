Tapirira caffra

Tapirira caffra, commonly known as the African mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
