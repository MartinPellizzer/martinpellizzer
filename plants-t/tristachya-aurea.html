Tristachya aurea

Tristachya aurea, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers are clustered in panicles.

Tristachya aurea is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, feathery appearance.
