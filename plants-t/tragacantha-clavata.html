Tragacantha clavata

Tragacantha clavata, commonly known as the clustered tragacanth, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha clavata is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often used in traditional medicine.
