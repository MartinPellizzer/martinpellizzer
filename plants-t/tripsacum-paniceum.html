Tripsacum paniceum

Tripsacum paniceum, commonly known as wild maize or panicled tripsacum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces dense, paniculate inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Tripsacum paniceum is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
