Terminalia paniculata

Terminalia paniculata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by yellow, fleshy berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
