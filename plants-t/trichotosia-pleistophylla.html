Trichotosia pleistophylla

Trichotosia pleistophylla, commonly known as the giant pleistophyll, is a unique fern that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It possesses large, deeply lobed fronds that can reach several meters in length, showcasing its impressive growth. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to anchor firmly in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently. Its spore-bearing structures, or sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic common among ferns.

Trichotosia pleistophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
