Tropaeolum minus var. brunneum

Tropaeolum minus var. brunneum, commonly known as the brown-leaf nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium plant.

It is a trailing annual that produces edible, peppery-tasting leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. Its foliage is characterized by a distinctive brownish hue, which differentiates it from other varieties.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and for its edible parts in culinary applications.
