Thalictrum delavayi var. mucronatum

Thalictrum delavayi var. mucronatum, commonly known as the blunt-tipped meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear delicate, compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
