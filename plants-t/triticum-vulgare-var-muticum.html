Triticum vulgare var. muticum

Triticum vulgare var. muticum, commonly known as the wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in a variety of habitats, including fields, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It is known for its adaptability to different soil conditions and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems.
