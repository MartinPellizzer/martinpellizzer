Thuja occidentalis var. hoveyi

Thuja occidentalis var. hoveyi, commonly known as the American arborvitae or northern white cedar, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage. The plant is native to the northeastern United States and parts of eastern Canada. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is fragrant and turns a bluish-green color in the winter, adding visual interest throughout the year.
