Telipogon ventaquemadensis

Telipogon ventaquemadensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Venezuela.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and small, intricate flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, its conservation status remains poorly understood.
