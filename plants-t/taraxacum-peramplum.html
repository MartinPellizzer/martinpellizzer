Taraxacum peramplum

Taraxacum peramplum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
