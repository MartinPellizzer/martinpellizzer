Thalictrum radiatum

Thalictrum radiatum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, showy flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often fragrant flowers.
