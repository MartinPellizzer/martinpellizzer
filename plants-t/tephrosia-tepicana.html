Tephrosia tepicana

Tephrosia tepicana, commonly known as the Mexican tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and thorn forests. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Tephrosia tepicana is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
