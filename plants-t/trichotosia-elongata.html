Trichotosia elongata

Trichotosia elongata, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Trichotosia elongata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
