Tripogon ashihoi

Tripogon ashihoi, commonly known as the Ashihoi grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a defining feature of the genus Tripogon.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.
