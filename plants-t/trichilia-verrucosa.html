Trichilia verrucosa

Trichilia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody, oval fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
