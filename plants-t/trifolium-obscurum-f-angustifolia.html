Trifolium obscurum f. angustifolia

Trifolium obscurum f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a trifoliate pattern. This subspecies thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along woodland edges. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
