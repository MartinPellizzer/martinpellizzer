Thunbergia kamatembica

Thunbergia kamatembica, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
