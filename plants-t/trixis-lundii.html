Trixis lundii

Trixis lundii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows from a tuberous root system, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water.

Trixis lundii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in sandy areas.
