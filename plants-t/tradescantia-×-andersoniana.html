Tradescantia × andersoniana

Tradescantia × andersoniana, commonly known as the wandering jew or snake plant, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and pink, often with a pinkish tinge at the edges. The plant is native to the Caribbean and is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through stem cuttings further contributes to its widespread cultivation and adaptability.
