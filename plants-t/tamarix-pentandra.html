Tamarix pentandra

Tamarix pentandra, commonly known as the white tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces elongated, woody cones that contain seeds. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tamarix pentandra is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
