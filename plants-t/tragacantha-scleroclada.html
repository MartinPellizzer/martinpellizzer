Tragacantha scleroclada

Tragacantha scleroclada, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, often blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
