Trihaloragis hexandra subsp. integrifolia

Trihaloragis hexandra subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf trihaloragis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth along the edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
