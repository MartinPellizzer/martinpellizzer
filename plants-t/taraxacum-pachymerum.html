Taraxacum pachymerum

Taraxacum pachymerum, commonly known as the thick-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, a common trait among dandelions. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Taraxacum pachymerum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
