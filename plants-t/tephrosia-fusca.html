Tephrosia fusca

Tephrosia fusca, commonly known as black tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Tephrosia fusca is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
