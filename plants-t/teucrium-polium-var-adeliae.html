Teucrium polium var. adeliae

Teucrium polium var. adeliae, commonly known as Adelia's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
