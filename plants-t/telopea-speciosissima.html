Telopea speciosissima

Telopea speciosissima, commonly known as the New South Wales waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and subcoastal areas.

Telopea speciosissima is a symbol of New South Wales and is celebrated for its ecological and horticultural significance.
