Tragacantha chardinii

Tragacantha chardinii, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
