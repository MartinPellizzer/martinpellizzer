Thymus serpyllum f. pratensis

Thymus serpyllum f. pratensis, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
