Turbinicarpus saueri subsp. verduzcoi

Turbinicarpus saueri subsp.

verduzcoi, commonly known as the Verduzco's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in harsh desert conditions.

This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
