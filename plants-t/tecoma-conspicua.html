Tecoma conspicua

Tecoma conspicua, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface.

Tecoma conspicua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
