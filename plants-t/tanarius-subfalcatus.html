Tanarius subfalcatus

Tanarius subfalcatus, commonly known as the slender tanarius, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
