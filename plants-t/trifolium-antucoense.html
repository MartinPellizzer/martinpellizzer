Trifolium antucoense

Trifolium antucoense, commonly known as the Antuco clover, is a flowering plant species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas with well-drained soil.

Trifolium antucoense is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
