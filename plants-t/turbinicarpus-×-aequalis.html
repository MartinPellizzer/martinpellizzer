Turbinicarpus × aequalis

Turbinicarpus × aequalis, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a rare and intriguing succulent resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus macdowellii and Turbinicarpus platycarpus.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often vividly patterned crown, which sits atop a cylindrical, green stem. The plant is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Due to its hybrid nature, it is typically propagated through grafting rather than from seeds.

Its unique appearance and rarity make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
