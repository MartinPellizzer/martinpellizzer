Thymus serpyllum proles polytrichus

Thymus serpyllum proles polytrichus, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, creeping stems that form dense mats, with opposite, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy groundcover plant.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
