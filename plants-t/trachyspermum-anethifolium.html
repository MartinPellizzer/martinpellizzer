Trachyspermum anethifolium

Trachyspermum anethifolium, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of South Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinct anise-like aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, followed by oval, brownish seeds that are rich in essential oils. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy surface and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.
