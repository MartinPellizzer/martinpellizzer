Thymus caucasicus

Thymus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, fragrant leaves and flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage, which makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in mountainous and rocky habitats.

Thymus caucasicus is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
