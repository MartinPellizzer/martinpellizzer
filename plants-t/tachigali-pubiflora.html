Tachigali pubiflora

Tachigali pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
