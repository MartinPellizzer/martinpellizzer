Trifolium glanduliferum

Trifolium glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with small glandular structures along the veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Trifolium glanduliferum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
