Thymelaea tomentosa

Thymelaea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Thymelaea tomentosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
