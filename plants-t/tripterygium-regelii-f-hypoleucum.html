Tripterygium regelii f. hypoleucum

Tripterygium regelii f. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered thunder god vine, is a unique horticultural form of the species Tripterygium regelii.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with the typical greenish-yellow blooms of the species. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
