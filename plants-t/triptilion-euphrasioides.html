Triptilion euphrasioides

Triptilion euphrasioides, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Triptilion euphrasioides is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
