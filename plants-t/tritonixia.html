Tritonixia

Tritonixia, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. Tritonixia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
