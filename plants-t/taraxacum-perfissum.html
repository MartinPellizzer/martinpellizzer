Taraxacum perfissum

Taraxacum perfissum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism known as a pappus, which allows its seeds to be carried by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum perfissum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
