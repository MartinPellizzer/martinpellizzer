Trichodiadema intonsum

Trichodiadema intonsum, commonly known as the yellow bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the summer months.

Trichodiadema intonsum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable climate.
