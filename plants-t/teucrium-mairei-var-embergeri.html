Teucrium mairei var. embergeri

Teucrium mairei var. embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
