Turpinia occidentalis subsp. breviflora

Turpinia occidentalis subsp.

breviflora, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and broad, ovate leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
