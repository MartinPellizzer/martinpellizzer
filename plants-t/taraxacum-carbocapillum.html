Taraxacum carbocapillum

Taraxacum carbocapillum, commonly known as the carbon cap dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces a capsule-like seed head that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
