Truellum oreophilum

Truellum oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in cool, high-altitude climates.

Truellum oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
