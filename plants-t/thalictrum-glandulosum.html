Thalictrum glandulosum

Thalictrum glandulosum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy, compound umbels of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thalictrum glandulosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
