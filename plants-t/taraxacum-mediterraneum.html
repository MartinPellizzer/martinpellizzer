Taraxacum mediterraneum

Taraxacum mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. The plant is characterized by its taproot system and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

Taraxacum mediterraneum is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
