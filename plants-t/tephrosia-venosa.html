Tephrosia venosa

Tephrosia venosa, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Tephrosia venosa is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
