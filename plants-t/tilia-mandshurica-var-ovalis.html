Tilia mandshurica var. ovalis

Tilia mandshurica var. ovalis, commonly known as the Manchurian linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly oval in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing capabilities.
