Tulipa praecox var. foxii

Tulipa praecox var. foxii, commonly known as the Foxii tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive, often striped or blotched pattern. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
