Thyrsanthemum laxiflorum

Thyrsanthemum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered thyrsanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and clustered in a thyrse, a type of inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
