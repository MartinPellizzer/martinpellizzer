Taraxacum submaculosum

Taraxacum submaculosum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance on the upper surface. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that terminates in a flowering head composed of numerous small yellow flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
