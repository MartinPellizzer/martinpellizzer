Turbinicarpus × amoenulus nothosubsp. cintiae

Turbinicarpus × amoenulus nothosubsp.

cintiae, commonly known as the Cintia hybrid, is a hybrid cactus species that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can vary in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on light exposure and hydration levels.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in dry climates.
