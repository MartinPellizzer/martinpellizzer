Tricalysia oblanceolata

Tricalysia oblanceolata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, velvety leaves that are ovate to oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.

Tricalysia oblanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
