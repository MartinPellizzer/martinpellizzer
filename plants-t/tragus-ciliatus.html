Tragus ciliatus

Tragus ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy tragus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

Tragus ciliatus is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
