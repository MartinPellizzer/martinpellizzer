Tragacantha pandurata

Tragacantha pandurata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is known for its ability to produce a resinous gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha pandurata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
