Tadeastrum candidum

Tadeastrum candidum, commonly known as the white trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, tubular, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, nectar-rich blossoms that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Tadeastrum candidum is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
