Tanacetum karataviense

Tanacetum karataviense, commonly known as Karatavi tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical research and horticultural interest.
