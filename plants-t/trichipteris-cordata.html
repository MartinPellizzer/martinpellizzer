Trichipteris cordata

Trichipteris cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as streams and ponds. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates in its habitat.

Trichipteris cordata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
