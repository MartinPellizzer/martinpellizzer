Typha basedowii

Typha basedowii, commonly known as the desert cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering stalks that rise above narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil near rivers, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Typha basedowii plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems and supporting biodiversity.
