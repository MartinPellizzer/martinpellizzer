Thlaspi arvense

Thlaspi arvense, commonly known as field pennycress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruits are short, segmented pods that contain several seeds.

Field pennycress is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
