Tragacantha sanguinolenta

Tragacantha sanguinolenta, commonly known as the crimson tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive crimson flowers and glandular leaves. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food additive. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and historical contexts.
