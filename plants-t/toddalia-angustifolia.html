Toddalia angustifolia

Toddalia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf toddalia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces showy, pink to purple flowers clustered in inflorescences. Toddalia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Malvaceae family.
