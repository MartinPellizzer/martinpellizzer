Tinguarra

Tinguarra, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Tinguarra flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 30 meters in height.
