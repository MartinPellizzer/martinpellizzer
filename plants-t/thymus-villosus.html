Thymus villosus

Thymus villosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
