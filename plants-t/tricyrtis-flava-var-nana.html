Tricyrtis flava var. nana

Tricyrtis flava var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese buttercup, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for smaller garden spaces.
