Tragopogon savranicus

Tragopogon savranicus, commonly known as the Savran's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or brown center.

Tragopogon savranicus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
