Trichosporum parasitica

Trichosporum parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic trichosporum, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized parasitic lifestyle.

It lacks the typical green pigmentation found in most plants and instead relies on host organisms for its nutrients. The plant exhibits a simple morphology, often appearing as a small, thread-like structure attached to its host. Its reproductive structures are highly modified, allowing it to propagate through the release of spores.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a specific role in ecological interactions, though its study remains limited.
