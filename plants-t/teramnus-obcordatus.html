Teramnus obcordatus

Teramnus obcordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf teramnus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a velvety texture, and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
