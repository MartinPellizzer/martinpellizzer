Taraxacum tanysipterum

Taraxacum tanysipterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a puffball of seeds. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
