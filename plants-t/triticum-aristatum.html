Triticum aristatum

Triticum aristatum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Triticum aristatum is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
