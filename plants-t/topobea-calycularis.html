Topobea calycularis

Topobea calycularis, commonly known as the calycular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Topobea calycularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
