Tanacetum partheniifolium

Tanacetum partheniifolium, commonly known as the yarrow plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies.
