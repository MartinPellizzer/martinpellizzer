Thalictrum elatum lusus mucronatum

Thalictrum elatum lusus mucronatum, commonly known as the tall meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are alternate and pinnately compound, with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
