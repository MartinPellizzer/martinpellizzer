Tradescantia anisophylla

Tradescantia anisophylla, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant produces clusters of three-petaled, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Tradescantia anisophylla is a perennial herb that is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
