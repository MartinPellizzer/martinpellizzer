Tibouchina schiedeana

Tibouchina schiedeana, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm regions.
