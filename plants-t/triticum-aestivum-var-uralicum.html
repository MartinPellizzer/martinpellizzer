Triticum aestivum var. uralicum

Triticum aestivum var. uralicum, commonly known as Ural wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Ural Mountains region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This variety is of interest to botanists and researchers due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value.
