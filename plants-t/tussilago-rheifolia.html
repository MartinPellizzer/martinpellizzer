Tussilago rheifolia

Tussilago rheifolia, commonly known as the alpine thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are usually pale yellow to white, appearing in early summer.

Tussilago rheifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
