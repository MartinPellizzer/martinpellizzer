Tweedia coerulea

Tweedia coerulea, commonly known as the blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow disk florets at the center of the flower. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tweedia coerulea is known for its long-lasting blooms and is often used in floral arrangements.
