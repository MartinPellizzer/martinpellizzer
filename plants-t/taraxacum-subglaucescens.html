Taraxacum subglaucescens

Taraxacum subglaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the familiar puffball seeds. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Taraxacum subglaucescens is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
