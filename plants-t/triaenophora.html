Triaenophora

Triaenophora, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique tri-lobed structure in its flowers, which gives it its scientific name. These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments. They have specialized roots that help in absorbing nutrients from the soil, and their flowers often display vibrant colors to attract pollinators.

Triaenophora species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
