Trichodesma sedgwickianum

Trichodesma sedgwickianum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Trichodesma sedgwickianum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
