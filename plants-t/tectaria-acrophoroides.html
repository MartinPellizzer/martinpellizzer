Tectaria acrophoroides

Tectaria acrophoroides, commonly known as the mountain shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria acrophoroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
