Tetraplasandra meiandra var. simulans

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. simulans, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, inedible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
