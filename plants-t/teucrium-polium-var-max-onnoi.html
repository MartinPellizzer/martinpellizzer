Teucrium polium var. max-onnoi

Teucrium polium var. max-onnoi, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
