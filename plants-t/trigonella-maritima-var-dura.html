Trigonella maritima var. dura

Trigonella maritima var. dura, commonly known as the coastal bitter vetch, is a perennial legume native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

It thrives in saline and arid conditions, showcasing its resilience to harsh climates.
