Tristaniopsis burmanica var. burmanica

Tristaniopsis burmanica var. burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese tallowtree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows up to 10 meters tall, featuring a dense, rounded canopy and smooth, dark grey bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in ornamental horticulture and reforestation efforts.
