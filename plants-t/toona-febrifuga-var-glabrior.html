Toona febrifuga var. glabrior

Toona febrifuga var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth toona, is a variant of the toona febrifuga species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
