Tillandsia spuria

Tillandsia spuria, commonly known as the slender air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to life in tropical forests.

Tillandsia spuria is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
