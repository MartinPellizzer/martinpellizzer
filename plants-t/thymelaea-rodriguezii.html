Thymelaea rodriguezii

Thymelaea rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cymes, often with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Thymelaea rodriguezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
