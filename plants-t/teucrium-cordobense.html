Teucrium cordobense

Teucrium cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoban germander, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Teucrium cordobense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
