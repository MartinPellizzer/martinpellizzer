Trifolium capitellatum var. elatius

Trifolium capitellatum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in forage and ecological restoration projects.
