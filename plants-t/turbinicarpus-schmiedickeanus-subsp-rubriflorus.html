Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp. rubriflorus

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp.

rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered turbinicarpus, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa. It features a rounded, ribbed stem that stores water, adapted to its arid environment.

The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
