Tilia × gyertyanffyana

Tilia × gyertyanffyana, commonly known as the Gyertyanffy's lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The species is named after the Hungarian botanist József Gyertyanffy.
