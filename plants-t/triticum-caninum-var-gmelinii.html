Triticum caninum var. gmelinii

Triticum caninum var. gmelinii, commonly known as the goat grass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats. Its grains are smaller than those of common wheat and have a higher fiber content.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for genetic diversity and its role in ancient agricultural systems.
