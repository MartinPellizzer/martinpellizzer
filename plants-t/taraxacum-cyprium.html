Taraxacum cyprium

Taraxacum cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprus dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow-orange flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. Native to the Mediterranean region, including Cyprus, it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
