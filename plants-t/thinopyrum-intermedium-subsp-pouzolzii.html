Thinopyrum intermedium subsp. pouzolzii

Thinopyrum intermedium subsp.

pouzolzii, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

This subspecies is often studied for its potential in forage and as a source of genetic material for wheat improvement.
