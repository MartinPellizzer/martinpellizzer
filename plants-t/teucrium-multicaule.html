Teucrium multicaule

Teucrium multicaule, commonly known as creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or violet, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Teucrium multicaule is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
