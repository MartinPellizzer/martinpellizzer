Tridax coronopifolia

Tridax coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments. It has a hairy stem and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Tridax coronopifolia is often found in roadsides, fields, and other open areas where it can spread rapidly.
