Tofieldia sessiliflora

Tofieldia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tofieldia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue. Its inflorescence consists of small, white to pale green flowers arranged in a loose cluster.

Tofieldia sessiliflora plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
