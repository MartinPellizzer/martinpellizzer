Tricyrtis macrantha subsp. macranthopsis

Tricyrtis macrantha subsp.

macranthopsis, commonly known as the Japanese spider lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The flowers are borne on slender, erect stems and are often adorned with a distinctive yellowish-green throat. This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
