Thuja occidentalis var. pendula

Thuja occidentalis var. pendula, commonly known as the American arborvitae orweeping white cedar, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it visually striking. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically a bright green color, turning bronze in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
