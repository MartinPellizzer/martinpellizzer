Thismia angustimitra

Thismia angustimitra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Thismia, is a unique flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thismiaceae and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph, relying on fungi for nutrients. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often hidden among the leaf litter. It is an example of a parasitic plant that forms symbiotic relationships with specific fungal species.

Due to its specialized ecological niche, it is considered rare and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments.
