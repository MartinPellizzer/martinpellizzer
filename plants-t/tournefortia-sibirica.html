Tournefortia sibirica

Tournefortia sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
