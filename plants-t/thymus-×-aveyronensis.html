Thymus × aveyronensis

Thymus × aveyronensis, commonly known as the Aveyron thyme, is a hybrid species of thyme native to southern France.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, gray-green leaves and small pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Thymus × aveyronensis is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
