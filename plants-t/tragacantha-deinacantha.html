Tragacantha deinacantha

Tragacantha deinacantha, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a sticky, resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and pods that contain several seeds, making it a notable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
