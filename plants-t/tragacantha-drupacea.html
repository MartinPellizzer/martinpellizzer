Tragacantha drupacea

Tragacantha drupacea, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round drupes that contain a single seed, which is typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha drupacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
