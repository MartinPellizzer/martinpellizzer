Tripteris microtis

Tripteris microtis, commonly known as the small-leaf tripteris, is a tropical plant belonging to the cashew family, Anacardiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

Tripteris microtis is native to parts of Central and South America and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
