Triticum hordeaceum

Triticum hordeaceum, commonly known as barley, is a cereal grain that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact inflorescence, which consists of spikelets containing multiple small, two-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, parallel-veined leaves. Barley produces a three-sided, hard seed coat that protects the endosperm, which is rich in starch and proteins.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and various agricultural uses.
