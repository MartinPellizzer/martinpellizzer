Tephrosia cana

Tephrosia cana, commonly known as white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Tephrosia cana is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
