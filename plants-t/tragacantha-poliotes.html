Tragacantha poliotes

Tragacantha poliotes, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its erect, woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and has been used traditionally for its gum and medicinal properties.
