Tricyclandra leandrii

Tricyclandra leandrii, commonly known as the Cape mountain zamioculcas, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Zamioculcaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant features a short, unbranched stem with a crown of waxy, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Tricyclandra leandrii is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to survive in dry, rocky habitats.
