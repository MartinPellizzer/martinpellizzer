Trichoglottis tetraceras

Trichoglottis tetraceras, commonly known as the four-armed trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Trichoglottis tetraceras is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in the right conditions.
