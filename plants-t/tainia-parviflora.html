Tainia parviflora

Tainia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tainia, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Brazil and Paraguay. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
