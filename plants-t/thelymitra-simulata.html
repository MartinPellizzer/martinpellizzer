Thelymitra simulata

Thelymitra simulata, commonly known as the False Paddle Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, paddle-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is terrestrial, growing in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a prominent white lip, and it is known for its ornamental value.

Thelymitra simulata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in the southeastern regions of Australia.
