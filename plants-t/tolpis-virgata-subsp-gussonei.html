Tolpis virgata subsp. gussonei

Tolpis virgata subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
