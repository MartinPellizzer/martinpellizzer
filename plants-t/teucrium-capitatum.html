Teucrium capitatum

Teucrium capitatum, commonly known as the purplehead germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering heads are tightly packed, giving the plant a compact, rounded appearance.

Teucrium capitatum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
