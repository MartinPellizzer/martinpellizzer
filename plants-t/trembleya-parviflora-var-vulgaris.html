Trembleya parviflora var. vulgaris

Trembleya parviflora var. vulgaris, commonly known as the small-flowered trembleya, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
