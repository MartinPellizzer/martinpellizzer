Tetrapterys papyracea

Tetrapterys papyracea, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, papery bark that peels off in thin layers, giving it its common name. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Tetrapterys papyracea is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
