Tulipa tarbagataica

Tulipa tarbagataica, commonly known as the Tarbagatai tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its bulbs are perennial, producing foliage and blooms in the spring.

The Tarbagatai tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
