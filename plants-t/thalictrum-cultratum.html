Thalictrum cultratum

Thalictrum cultratum, commonly known as the cutleaf meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are arranged in compound racemes. Thalictrum cultratum typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and early blooming period.
