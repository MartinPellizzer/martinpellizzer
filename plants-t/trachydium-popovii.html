Trachydium popovii

Trachydium popovii, commonly known as the Mongolian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Trachydium popovii is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
