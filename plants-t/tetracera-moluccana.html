Tetracera moluccana

Tetracera moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
