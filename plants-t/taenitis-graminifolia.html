Taenitis graminifolia

Taenitis graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leafed fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like fronds that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces small, clustered spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Taenitis graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
