Tabernaemontana cerniflora

Tabernaemontana cerniflora, commonly known as the weeping madia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and provide a striking contrast to its cascading blooms. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.

However, it is important to note that it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
