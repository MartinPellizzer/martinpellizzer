Tilia platyphylla var. rubra

Tilia platyphylla var. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange color in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
