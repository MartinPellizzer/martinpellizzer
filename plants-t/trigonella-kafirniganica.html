Trigonella kafirniganica

Trigonella kafirniganica, commonly known as the Afghan fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal and nutritional properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
