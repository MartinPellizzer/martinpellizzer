Trifolium montanum proles endressii

Trifolium montanum proles endressii, commonly known as the Endress's mountain clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. This plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It plays a vital role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The species is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
