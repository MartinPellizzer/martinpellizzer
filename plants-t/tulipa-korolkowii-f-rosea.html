Tulipa korolkowii f. rosea

Tulipa korolkowii f. rosea, commonly known as the Rosea tulip, is a botanical hybrid belonging to the genus Tulipa in the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose, often with a distinctive white or pale yellow base. The plant is native to Central Asia and is known for its vigorous growth and ability to naturalize in suitable climates. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
