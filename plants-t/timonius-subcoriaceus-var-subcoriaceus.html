Timonius subcoriaceus var. subcoriaceus

Timonius subcoriaceus var. subcoriaceus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved timonius, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a dense, waxy cuticle that provides protection against environmental stress. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
