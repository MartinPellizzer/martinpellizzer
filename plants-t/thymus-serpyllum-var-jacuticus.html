Thymus serpyllum var. jacuticus

Thymus serpyllum var. jacuticus, commonly known as the Caucasian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, fragrant leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its antimicrobial properties.
