Taraxacum fulviforme

Taraxacum fulviforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its stems are upright and bear the inflorescence at the top.

Taraxacum fulviforme is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.
