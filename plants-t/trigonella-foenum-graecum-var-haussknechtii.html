Trigonella foenum-graecum var. haussknechtii

Trigonella foenum-graecum var. haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknechtii fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a small annual herb characterized by trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, similar to its parent species. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
