Trimelopter crispolanatum

Trimelopter crispolanatum, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, feathery fronds that exhibit a striking golden hue at the tips, contrasting with its dark green base. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of dense rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including small, spore-bearing cones, are typically located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Trimelopter crispolanatum is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
