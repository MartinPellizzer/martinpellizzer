Telephium sphaerospermum

Telephium sphaerospermum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Telephium sphaerospermum is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
