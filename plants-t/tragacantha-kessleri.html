Tragacantha kessleri

Tragacantha kessleri, commonly known as the Kessler's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Tragacantha kessleri is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various industrial and medicinal applications.
