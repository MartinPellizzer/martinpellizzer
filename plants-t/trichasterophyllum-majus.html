Trichasterophyllum majus

Trichasterophyllum majus, commonly known as the giant trichasterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant with unique botanical features.

It belongs to the group of plants known as trichasterophytes, which are considered among the earliest land plants. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with a branching, erect stem and small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are simple and consist of sporangia that produce spores.

Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is of significant interest to botanists studying the evolution of early plant life.
