Trifolium lappaceum var. rhodense

Trifolium lappaceum var. rhodense, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, pink or purple flower clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecosystem biodiversity.
