Tragacantha thompsoniae

Tragacantha thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
