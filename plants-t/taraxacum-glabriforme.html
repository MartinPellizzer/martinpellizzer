Taraxacum glabriforme

Taraxacum glabriforme, commonly known as the smoothleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, elongated leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism through its pappus-equipped seeds.

This species is typically herbaceous and perennial, with a life cycle that includes both vegetative and reproductive stages.
