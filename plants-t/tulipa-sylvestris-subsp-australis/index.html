Tulipa Sylvestris Subsp. Australis

Tulipa sylvestris subsp. australis is a rare and striking member of the tulip family native to southern regions of Europe and western Asia. This subspecies is known for its vibrant, often reddish or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Unlike its more common relatives, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a prized plant among horticulturists and botanists. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure and underappreciated in mainstream gardening circles.
