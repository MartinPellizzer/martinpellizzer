Thymus pulegioides subvar. armoricanus

Thymus pulegioides subvar. armoricanus, commonly known as the French thyme or thyme of the Alps, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a strong, pungent scent reminiscent of other thyme varieties. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
