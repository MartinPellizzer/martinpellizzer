Thymus bracteatus

Thymus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteated thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and antiseptic properties.
