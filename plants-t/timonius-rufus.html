Timonius rufus

Timonius rufus, commonly known as the red-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and green foliage, with the leaves often displaying a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial shade to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
