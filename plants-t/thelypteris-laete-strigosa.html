Thelypteris laete-strigosa

Thelypteris laete-strigosa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Thelypteris laete-strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
