Trichilia ulei

Trichilia ulei, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing oily seeds. The seeds are widely used for their high oil content, which is extracted to make shea butter.

This plant plays an important ecological and economic role in the regions where it is found.
