Tanacetum tridactylites

Tanacetum tridactylites, commonly known as the three-fingered tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp meadows or along stream banks.

Tanacetum tridactylites is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
