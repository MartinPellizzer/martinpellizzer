Tinantia

Tinantia, commonly known as the "water lily of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Namibia and Botswana. Tinantia species are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that often resemble those of water lilies, despite their drought-tolerant nature. They typically grow in sandy or rocky soils and are adapted to survive in low-water environments.

The genus includes several species, such as Tinantia tamarindifolia, which is known for its medicinal properties.
