Trichocarpus guianensis

Trichocarpus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Trichocarpus guianensis is often cultivated for its edible tubers, which are used in various culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
