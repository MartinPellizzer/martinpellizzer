Trifolium inconspicuum

Trifolium inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Trifolium inconspicuum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
