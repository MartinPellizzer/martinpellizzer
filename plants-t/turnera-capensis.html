Turnera capensis

Turnera capensis, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and adaptable plant for both landscape design and horticultural study.
