Trichocentrum stipitatum

Trichocentrum stipitatum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which resemble a lady's slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
