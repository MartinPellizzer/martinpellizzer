Themeda barbinodis

Themeda barbinodis, commonly known as the narrow-awned grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its flowers are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Themeda barbinodis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
