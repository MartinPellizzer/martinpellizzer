Tolpis × grossii

Tolpis × grossii, commonly known as the hybrid blue oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Tolpis stricta and Tolpis farinosa, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its foliage is blue-green in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax.

Tolpis × grossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
