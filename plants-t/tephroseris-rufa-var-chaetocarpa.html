Tephroseris rufa var. chaetocarpa

Tephroseris rufa var. chaetocarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fruiting structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.
