Trichosalpinx farrago

Trichosalpinx farrago, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames, making it a visually captivating plant. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Trichosalpinx farrago thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
