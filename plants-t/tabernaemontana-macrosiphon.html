Tabernaemontana macrosiphon

Tabernaemontana macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often marked with lenticels.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
