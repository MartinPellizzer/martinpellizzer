Trophis brasiliensis

Trophis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.
