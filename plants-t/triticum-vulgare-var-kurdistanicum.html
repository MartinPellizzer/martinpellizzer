Triticum vulgare var. kurdistanicum

Triticum vulgare var. kurdistanicum, commonly known as the Kurdish wheat, is a wild wheat species native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence that bears small, two-rowed grains. This variety is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its genetic diversity makes it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.

The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems and has potential agricultural applications.
