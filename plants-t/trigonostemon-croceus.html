Trigonostemon croceus

Trigonostemon croceus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, giving it the genus name "Trigonostemon," which means "three-lobed.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are followed by small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance.
