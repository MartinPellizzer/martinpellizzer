Tulipa undulata

Tulipa undulata, commonly known as the wavy tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating petals that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Tulipa undulata is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
