Tradescantia nodiflora

Tradescantia nodiflora, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
