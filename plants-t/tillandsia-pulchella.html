Tillandsia pulchella

Tillandsia pulchella, commonly known as the silver vanda, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. Tillandsia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
