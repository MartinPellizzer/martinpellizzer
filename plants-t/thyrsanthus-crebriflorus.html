Thyrsanthus crebriflorus

Thyrsanthus crebriflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered thyrsin, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typical of the genus Thyrsanthus. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
