Taraxacum garbarianum

Taraxacum garbarianum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single inflorescence, followed by a parachute-like seed dispersal mechanism.

It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, demonstrating adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
