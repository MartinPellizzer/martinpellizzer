Tamarix japonica

Tamarix japonica, commonly known as Japanese tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid and saline environments. Its bark is smooth and often flaky, and it produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Tamarix japonica is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
