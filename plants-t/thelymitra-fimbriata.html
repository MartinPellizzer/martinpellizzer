Thelymitra fimbriata

Thelymitra fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed helmet orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flowers with fringed edges. The plant typically grows on the trunks or branches of trees, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually sword-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Thelymitra fimbriata is known for its striking purple and white floral display, which appears during the warmer months.
