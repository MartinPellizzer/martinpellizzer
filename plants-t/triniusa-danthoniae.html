Triniusa danthoniae

Triniusa danthoniae, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can sometimes become invasive.

Triniusa danthoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic foliage.
