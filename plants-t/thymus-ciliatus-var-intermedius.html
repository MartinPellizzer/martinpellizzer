Thymus ciliatus var. intermedius

Thymus ciliatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
