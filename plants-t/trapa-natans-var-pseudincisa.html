Trapa natans var. pseudincisa

Trapa natans var. pseudincisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a waxy surface to repel water. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
