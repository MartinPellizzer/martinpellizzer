Tristachya nodiglumis

Tristachya nodiglumis, commonly known as the golden cupgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow inflorescence, which consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Tristachya nodiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
