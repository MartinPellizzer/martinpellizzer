Trallesia

Trallesia, commonly known as the "scented orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their delicate, fragrant flowers. Trallesia species typically have showy, colorful blooms with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette shape, and the plants often grow as epiphytes or terrestrial herbs.

Due to their ornamental value and unique fragrance, Trallesia species are cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
