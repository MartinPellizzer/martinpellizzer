Tournefortia velutina

Tournefortia velutina, commonly known as velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, velvety bark and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas, mangroves, or disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
