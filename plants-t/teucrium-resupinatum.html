Teucrium resupinatum

Teucrium resupinatum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover in gardens and natural landscapes.
