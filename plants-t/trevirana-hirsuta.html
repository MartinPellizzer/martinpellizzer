Trevirana hirsuta

Trevirana hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy trevirana, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Trevirana hirsuta is often found in areas with rich, humus-filled soil.
