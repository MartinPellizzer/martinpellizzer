Trichocereus chiloensis subsp. eburneus

Trichocereus chiloensis subsp.

eburneus, commonly known as the "Eburneus Cactus" or "Chiloensis Cactus," is a species native to Chile and Argentina. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and spiny areoles.

The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
