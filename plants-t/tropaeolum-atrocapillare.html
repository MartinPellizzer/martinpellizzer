Tropaeolum atrocapillare

Tropaeolum atrocapillare, commonly known as the black-haired nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with striking black veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in distinctive, inflated pods that split open when mature.

Tropaeolum atrocapillare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves and flowers.
