Taraxacum cachkadzorum

Taraxacum cachkadzorum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal. Native to the mountainous regions of Armenia, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of ecological study.
