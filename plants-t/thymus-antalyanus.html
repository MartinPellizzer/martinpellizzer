Thymus antalyanus

Thymus antalyanus, commonly known as Antalya thyme, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
