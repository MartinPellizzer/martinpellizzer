Typhonium praecox

Typhonium praecox, commonly known as the early arrowroot, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a stout, tuberous rhizome that serves as its primary storage organ. The plant produces large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

The tubers are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines for their starchy texture.
