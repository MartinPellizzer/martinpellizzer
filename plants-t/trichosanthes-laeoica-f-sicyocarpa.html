Trichosanthes laeoica f. sicyocarpa

Trichosanthes laeoica f. sicyocarpa, commonly known as the rough gourd or hairy bitter melon, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while the stems are vine-like and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit's unique texture and flavor make it a valued ingredient in traditional cuisines.
