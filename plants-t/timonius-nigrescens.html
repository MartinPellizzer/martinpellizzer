Timonius nigrescens

Timonius nigrescens, commonly known as the black-tipped timon, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking dark green foliage and distinctive black-tipped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Timonius nigrescens is a herbaceous perennial, often growing to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
