Taxus cuspidata thayerae

Taxus cuspidata thayerae, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a rare and ancient conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and evergreen foliage, with dark green, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in crosses. The plant produces small, reddish-pink flowers followed by blue-black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Japanese culture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
