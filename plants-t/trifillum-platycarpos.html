Trifillum platycarpos

Trifillum platycarpos, commonly known as the flatcarp fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Trifillum platycarpos is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
