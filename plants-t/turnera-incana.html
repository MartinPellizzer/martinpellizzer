Turnera incana

Turnera incana, commonly known as the hairy turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

Turnera incana is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands.
