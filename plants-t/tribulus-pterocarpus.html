Tribulus pterocarpus

Tribulus pterocarpus, commonly known as the Indian gothic or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a leguminous shrub that produces small, yellow flowers and pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and climbing habit, often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges. Its seeds are rich in proteins and oils, making them valuable for both human consumption and animal feed.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
