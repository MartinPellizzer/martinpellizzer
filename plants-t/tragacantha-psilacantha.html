Tragacantha psilacantha

Tragacantha psilacantha, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, which is harvested from its exuding tissues. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This species is valued for both its ecological role and its traditional uses in local communities.
