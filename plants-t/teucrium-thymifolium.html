Teucrium thymifolium

Teucrium thymifolium, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of lemon and thyme, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Teucrium thymifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
