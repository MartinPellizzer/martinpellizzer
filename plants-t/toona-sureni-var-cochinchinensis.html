Toona sureni var. cochinchinensis

Toona sureni var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and distinctive, smooth, light-colored bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ornamental qualities.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.
