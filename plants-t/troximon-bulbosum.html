Troximon bulbosum

Troximon bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous troximon, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized bulb structure.

The plant's morphology is adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, while the stem remains short and stout. The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological niche, Troximon bulbosum is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
