Tussilago rubra

Tussilago rubra, commonly known as purple coughwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
