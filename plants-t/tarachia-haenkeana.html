Tarachia haenkeana

Tarachia haenkeana, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fleshy, red berries that are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its leaves are typically small, waxy, and arranged in a spiral pattern to reduce water loss.

Tarachia haenkeana is well adapted to desert conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water sources.
