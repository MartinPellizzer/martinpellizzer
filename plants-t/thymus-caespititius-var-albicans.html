Thymus caespititius var. albicans

Thymus caespititius var. albicans, commonly known as the white thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense, compact growth with small, pale green to white leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and compact form make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
