Trisetum spicatum subsp. tibeticum

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan quillgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and moist environments.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the genus Trisetum. It is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat, including strong winds and low temperatures.
