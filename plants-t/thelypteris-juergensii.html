Thelypteris juergensii

Thelypteris juergensii, commonly known as the Juergens' shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive pale green coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris juergensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
