Taraxacum islandicum

Taraxacum islandicum, commonly known as the island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in open, sunny areas and is often found in coastal and disturbed habitats.

Taraxacum islandicum is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of Hawaii, making it an important part of the local flora.
