Tradescantia hirsuta

Tradescantia hirsuta, commonly known as the velvet leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Tradescantia hirsuta is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value.
