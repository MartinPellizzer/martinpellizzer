Tulipa sylvestris subsp. australis

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian wild tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant typically blooms in spring and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
