Tragacantha stenolepis

Tragacantha stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
