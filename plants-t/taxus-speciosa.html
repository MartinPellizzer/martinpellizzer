Taxus speciosa

Taxus speciosa, commonly known as the Pacific yew.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the western United States. The plant has distinctive flat, needle-like leaves and produces small, cone-like structures that contain toxic seeds. Its bark is thick and scaly, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Taxus speciosa is also notable for its historical use in the production of the chemotherapy drug paclitaxel.
