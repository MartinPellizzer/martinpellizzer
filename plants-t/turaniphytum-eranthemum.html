Turaniphytum eranthemum

Turaniphytum eranthemum, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
