Thymus amurensis

Thymus amurensis, commonly known as Siberian thyme, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
