Triptilion digitatum

Triptilion digitatum, commonly known as the feather moss, is a species of moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and its ability to form dense, carpet-like mats on forest floors. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

Triptilion digitatum is often found in temperate and boreal regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of forest habitats.
