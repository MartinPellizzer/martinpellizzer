Tragacantha fraxinella

Tragacantha fraxinella, commonly known as the goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional remedies.
