Trisetum johnstonii subsp. matthei

Trisetum johnstonii subsp.

matthei, commonly known as the Johnston's false brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, glabrous stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
