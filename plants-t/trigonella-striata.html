Trigonella striata

Trigonella striata, commonly known as the striated sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Trigonella striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
