Teucrium montanum var. lavandilifolium

Teucrium montanum var. lavandilifolium, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a lavender scent, resembling those of lavender, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
