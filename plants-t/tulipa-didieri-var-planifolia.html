Tulipa didieri var. planifolia

Tulipa didieri var. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved Darwin tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
