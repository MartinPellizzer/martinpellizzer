Thymus × oblongifolius subsp. kuemmerleanus

Thymus × oblongifolius subsp.

kuemmerleanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a hybrid species within the Thymus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. It typically produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in herbal teas and culinary applications.
