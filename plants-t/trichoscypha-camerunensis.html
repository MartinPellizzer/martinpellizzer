Trichoscypha camerunensis

Trichoscypha camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
