Thymus striatus var. zygioides

Thymus striatus var. zygioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems and small, dark green, linear leaves that are densely arranged. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a useful culinary herb and a decorative plant in horticulture.
