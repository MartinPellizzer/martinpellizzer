Tribularia

Tribularia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to Africa and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. Tribularia species typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They have fibrous roots and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The genus includes several species, such as Tribularia hirta and Tribularia scorpioides, which are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.
