Therorhodion camtschaticum

Therorhodion camtschaticum, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin, with "camtschaticum" referring to the region of Kamtschatka, though it is more commonly found in the Caucasus.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
