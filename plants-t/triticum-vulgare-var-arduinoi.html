Triticum vulgare var. arduinoi

Triticum vulgare var. arduinoi, commonly known as Arduino wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic modifications.

This plant exhibits a compact growth habit with shorter stalks and reduced grain size compared to standard wheat varieties. It is notable for its enhanced resistance to certain fungal pathogens, making it a candidate for sustainable agriculture. The botanical features include a well-developed root system and a high concentration of gluten proteins.

Its development is closely linked to biotechnological advancements aimed at improving crop resilience and yield.
