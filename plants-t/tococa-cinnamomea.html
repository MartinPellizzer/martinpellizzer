Tococa cinnamomea

Tococa cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark contains compounds similar to cinnamon. Tococa cinnamomea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
