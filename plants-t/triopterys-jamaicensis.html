Triopterys jamaicensis

Triopterys jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican wax palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, woody fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often found in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in warm, moist environments with partial shade.
