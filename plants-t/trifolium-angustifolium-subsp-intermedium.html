Trifolium angustifolium subsp. intermedium

Trifolium angustifolium subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as narrow-leaf white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a pale green to white coloration.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
