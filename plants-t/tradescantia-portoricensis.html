Tradescantia portoricensis

Tradescantia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces clusters of small, violet to blue flowers. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
