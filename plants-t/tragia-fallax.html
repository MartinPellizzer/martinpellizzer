Tragia fallax

Tragia fallax, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tragia fallax is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
