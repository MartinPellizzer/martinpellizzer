Tephrocactus aoracanthus

Tephrocactus aoracanthus, commonly known as the orange-horned cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
