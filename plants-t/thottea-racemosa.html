Thottea racemosa

Thottea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in clusters, adorned with small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
