Tecoma ochroxantha

Tecoma ochroxantha, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tecoma ochroxantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
