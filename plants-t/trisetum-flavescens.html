Trisetum flavescens

Trisetum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow wood rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or brown. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Trisetum flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, wet conditions.
