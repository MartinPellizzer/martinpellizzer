Trisetopsis longa

Trisetopsis longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, bluish-green hue.

Trisetopsis longa is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
