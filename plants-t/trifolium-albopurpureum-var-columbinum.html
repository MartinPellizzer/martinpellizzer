Trifolium albopurpureum var. columbinum

Trifolium albopurpureum var. columbinum, commonly known as the purple clover variant, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
