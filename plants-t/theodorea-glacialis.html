Theodorea glacialis

Theodorea glacialis, commonly known as the glacier lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in cold, alpine environments and is adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring or early summer.

Theodorea glacialis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of mountainous regions.
