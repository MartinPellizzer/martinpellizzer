Thymus dahuricus f. albiflora

Thymus dahuricus f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Siberian thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
