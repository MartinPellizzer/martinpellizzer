Trifolium subrotundum var. obcordatum

Trifolium subrotundum var. obcordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are rounded and often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
