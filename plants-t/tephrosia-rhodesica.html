Tephrosia rhodesica

Tephrosia rhodesica, commonly known as Rhodesia tephrosia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in grassland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Tephrosia rhodesica is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
