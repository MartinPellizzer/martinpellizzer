Taraxacum reclinatum

Taraxacum reclinatum, commonly known as the reclining dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow and produce a puffball of seeds.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
