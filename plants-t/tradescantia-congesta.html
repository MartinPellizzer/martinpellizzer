Tradescantia congesta

Tradescantia congesta, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or snake plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage with striking red veins and a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the leaves. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Tradescantia congesta is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability.
