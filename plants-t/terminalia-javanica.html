Terminalia javanica

Terminalia javanica, commonly known as the Javan ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is often found in lowland and hill forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in greenish-yellow clusters, while its fruit is a small, spherical drupe.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
