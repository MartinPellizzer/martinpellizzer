Thymelaea antiatlantica

Thymelaea antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers clustered in umbels. The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
