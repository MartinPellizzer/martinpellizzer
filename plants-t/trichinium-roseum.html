Trichinium roseum

Trichinium roseum, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth texture. Trichinium roseum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ease of care.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
