Tricholaena rosea var. sphacelata

Tricholaena rosea var. sphacelata, commonly known as the creeping or climbing bamboo, is a variant of the Tricholaena rosea species.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems that can either trail or climb, depending on the growing conditions. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a light green color. This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
