Thyrsanthus embelioides

Thyrsanthus embelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyrsothamnus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters called thyrses. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and rocky areas.

Thyrsanthus embelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
