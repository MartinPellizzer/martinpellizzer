Tragacantha chrysantha

Tragacantha chrysantha, commonly known as the golden tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
