Taraxacum glaucolivaceum

Taraxacum glaucolivaceum, commonly known as the blue-green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to eastern North America, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of its habitat.
