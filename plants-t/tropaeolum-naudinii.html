Tropaeolum naudinii

Tropaeolum naudinii, commonly known as the Andean jewel flower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family, which includes other species of nasturtiums. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive crown-like structure. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Tropaeolum naudinii is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
