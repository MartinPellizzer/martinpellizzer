Teucrium salviifolium

Teucrium salviifolium, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
