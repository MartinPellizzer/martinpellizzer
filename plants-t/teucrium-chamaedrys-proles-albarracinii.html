Teucrium chamaedrys proles albarracinii

Teucrium chamaedrys proles albarracinii, commonly known as the white-flowered germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

This subspecies is distinguished by its distinctively white flowers, setting it apart from other members of the Teucrium genus.
