Tainia angulata

Tainia angulata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Marattiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, undulating fronds that give it its common name. The plant has large, compound leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the rachis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Tainia angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
