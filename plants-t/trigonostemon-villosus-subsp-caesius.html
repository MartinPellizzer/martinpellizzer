Trigonostemon villosus subsp. caesius

Trigonostemon villosus subsp.

caesius, commonly known as the velvetleaf trigonostemon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its growth habit is characterized by multiple stems and a compact, bushy form.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
