Tabernaemontana inconspicua

Tabernaemontana inconspicua, commonly known as the lesser star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
