Typhonium kunmingense var. cerebriforme

Typhonium kunmingense var. cerebriforme, commonly known as the brain-shaped arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, brain-like foliage.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that reflects light, enhancing its visual appeal. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog gardens.

While it is not typically flowering, its unique morphology makes it a valued ornamental plant in botanical collections.
