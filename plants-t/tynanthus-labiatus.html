Tynanthus labiatus

Tynanthus labiatus, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, and they open in the morning, closing by midday.

Tynanthus labiatus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
