Telinaria humifusa

Telinaria humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Telinaria humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
