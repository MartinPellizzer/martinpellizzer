Tristagma chubutense

Tristagma chubutense, commonly known as the Chubut thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its spiny, succulent stems and leaves, adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Tristagma chubutense is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
