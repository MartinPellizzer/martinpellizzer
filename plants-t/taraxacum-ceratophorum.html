Taraxacum ceratophorum

Taraxacum ceratophorum, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed heads. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of the characteristic pappus-covered seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
