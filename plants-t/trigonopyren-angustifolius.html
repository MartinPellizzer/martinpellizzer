Trigonopyren angustifolius

Trigonopyren angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trigonopyron, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are characterized by their narrow, linear shape and serrated edges.

Trigonopyren angustifolius is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
