Tanacetum corymbosum var. macroglossum

Tanacetum corymbosum var. macroglossum, commonly known as the giant tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and abundant yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
