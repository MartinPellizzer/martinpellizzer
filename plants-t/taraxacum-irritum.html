Taraxacum irritum

Taraxacum irritum, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating skin conditions and digestive ailments.
