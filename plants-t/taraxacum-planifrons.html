Taraxacum planifrons

Taraxacum planifrons, commonly known as the flat-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed head. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with the plant resuming growth in the spring.
