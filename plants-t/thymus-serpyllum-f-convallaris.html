Thymus serpyllum f. convallaris

Thymus serpyllum f. convallaris, commonly known as the pink thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its fragrant, small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically features slender, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties, making it a popular choice in gardens and herbal medicine.
