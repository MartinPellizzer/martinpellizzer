Tenaxia aureocephala

Tenaxia aureocephala, commonly known as the golden-headed tenaxia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tenaxia aureocephala is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
