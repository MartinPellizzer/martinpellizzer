Triticum × elymogenes

Triticum × elymogenes, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild relative of wheat that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Triticum × elymogenes is known for its hardiness and ability to grow in marginal environments.

It has been studied for its potential in breeding programs to improve wheat varieties for disease resistance and yield.
