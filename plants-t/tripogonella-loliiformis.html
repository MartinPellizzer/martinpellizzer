Tripogonella loliiformis

Tripogonella loliiformis, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
