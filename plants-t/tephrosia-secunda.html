Tephrosia secunda

Tephrosia secunda, commonly known as the smallflower tephrosia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

Tephrosia secunda is also noted for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
