Turnera villosa

Turnera villosa, commonly known as the hairy turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Turnera villosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
