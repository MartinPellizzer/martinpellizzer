Typhonium brownii var. eliosurum

Typhonium brownii var. eliosurum, commonly known as the giant water arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a distinctive inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe. It thrives in wet, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
