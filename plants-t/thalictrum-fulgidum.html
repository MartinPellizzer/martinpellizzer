Thalictrum fulgidum

Thalictrum fulgidum, commonly known as the golden meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

Thalictrum fulgidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
