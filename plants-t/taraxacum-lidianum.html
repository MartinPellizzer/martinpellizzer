Taraxacum lidianum

Taraxacum lidianum, commonly known as the Lidian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it typically grows in grassy areas and open fields. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
