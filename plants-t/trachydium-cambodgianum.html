Trachydium cambodgianum

Trachydium cambodgianum, commonly known as the Cambodian spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia. It contains toxic latex, which is a common feature among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Trachydium cambodgianum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.
