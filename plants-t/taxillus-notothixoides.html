Taxillus notothixoides

Taxillus notothixoides, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Taxillus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it produces its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds. It is found in various regions of South America, typically in humid forests.

Its life cycle involves seeds that are dispersed by birds and germinate on the bark of host trees.
