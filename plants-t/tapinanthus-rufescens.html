Tapinanthus rufescens

Tapinanthus rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, attaching itself with fine, thread-like roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, vine-like structure. It is often found parasitizing members of the legume family.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly weaken its host plants.
