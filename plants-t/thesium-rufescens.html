Thesium rufescens

Thesium rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Thesium rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
