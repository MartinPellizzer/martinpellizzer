Taraxacum lasiodasum

Taraxacum lasiodasum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy, and often gray-green leaves. The plant produces a single, stout, and often hairy stem that rises above the leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
