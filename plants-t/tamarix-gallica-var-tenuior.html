Tamarix gallica var. tenuior

Tamarix gallica var. tenuior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible. The plant is adapted to coastal and saline environments, showing resistance to salt spray and poor soil conditions. It has a sprawling growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
