Thymus ovatus subvar. longistylus

Thymus ovatus subvar. longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces four-lobed, oval-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
