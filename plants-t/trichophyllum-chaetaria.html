Trichophyllum chaetaria

Trichophyllum chaetaria, commonly known as the hairy tassel fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its intricate and delicate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping rhizome with dense clusters of small, hairy spore-bearing structures called sporangia. Its fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a soft, velvety texture due to the presence of fine trichomes.

Trichophyllum chaetaria thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
