Thymbra sintenisii subsp. sintenisii

Thymbra sintenisii subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Sicilian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pinkish-white flowers are borne in terminal clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow, aromatic, and often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
