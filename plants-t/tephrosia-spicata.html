Tephrosia spicata

Tephrosia spicata, commonly known as the slender tephrosia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in spicate inflorescences, giving it its common name. Tephrosia spicata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
