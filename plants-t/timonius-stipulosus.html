Timonius stipulosus

Timonius stipulosus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed timonius, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, clustered leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
