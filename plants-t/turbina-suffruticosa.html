Turbina suffruticosa

Turbina suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping turban snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling, low-growing habit. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale green, blooming in the spring.

Turbina suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
