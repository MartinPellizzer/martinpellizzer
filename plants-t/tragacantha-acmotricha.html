Tragacantha acmotricha

Tragacantha acmotricha, commonly known as the Egyptian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. Its botanical significance lies in its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has been historically used for its mucilage, which has various industrial and medicinal applications.
