Trichopyrum intermedium subsp. barbulatum

Trichopyrum intermedium subsp.

barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, especially along the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
