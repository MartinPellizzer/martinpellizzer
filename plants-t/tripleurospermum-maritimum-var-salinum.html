Tripleurospermum maritimum var. salinum

Tripleurospermum maritimum var. salinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its structure includes a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem with bracts that resemble petals.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
