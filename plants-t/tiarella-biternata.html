Tiarella biternata

Tiarella biternata, commonly known as the three-leaved foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, rounded leaves that are typically trifoliate, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for shade gardens.
