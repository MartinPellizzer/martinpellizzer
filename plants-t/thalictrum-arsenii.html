Thalictrum arsenii

Thalictrum arsenii, commonly known as the Armenian meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stolons that help in vegetative propagation.

Thalictrum arsenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
