Taraxacum coronopifolium

Taraxacum coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and medicinal use.
