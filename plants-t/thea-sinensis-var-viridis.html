Thea sinensis var. viridis

Thea sinensis var. viridis, commonly known as the green variety of tea plant, is a cultivar of the Camellia sinensis species.

It is characterized by its dark green leaves that are rich in chlorophyll, contributing to its vibrant color and high concentration of antioxidants. This variety is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce green tea. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both traditional tea production and ornamental gardening.
