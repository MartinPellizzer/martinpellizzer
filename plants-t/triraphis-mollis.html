Triraphis mollis

Triraphis mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Triraphis mollis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
