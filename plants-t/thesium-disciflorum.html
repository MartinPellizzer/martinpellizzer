Thesium disciflorum

Thesium disciflorum, commonly known as the disc-flowered thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Thesium disciflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
