Trachelospermum wenchowense

Trachelospermum wenchowense, commonly known as the Wenchow jasmine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a climbing shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are tubular and have five lobes, with a corolla that is usually shorter than the calyx.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
