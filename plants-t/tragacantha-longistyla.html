Tragacantha longistyla

Tragacantha longistyla, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, spiny pods containing seeds. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Additionally, its sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
