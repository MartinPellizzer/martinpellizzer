Tropaeolum garciae

Tropaeolum garciae, commonly known as the Chilean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, peppery-tasting seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and a valuable plant for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
