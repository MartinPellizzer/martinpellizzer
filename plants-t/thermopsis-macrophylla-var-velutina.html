Thermopsis macrophylla var. velutina

Thermopsis macrophylla var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf thermopsis, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
