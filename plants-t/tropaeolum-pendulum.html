Tropaeolum pendulum

Tropaeolum pendulum, commonly known as the trailing nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly peppery taste, making it popular in both ornamental and culinary gardens. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecosystem health.

Tropaeolum pendulum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
