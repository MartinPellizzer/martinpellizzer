Trichilia obovata

Trichilia obovata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are obovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has traditional uses in perfumery and medicine.
