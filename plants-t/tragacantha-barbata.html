Tragacantha barbata

Tragacantha barbata, commonly known as the barbed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces distinctive, barbed pods containing seeds. The plant is characterized by its gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
