Thysanurus

Thysanurus, commonly known as the fringed scale insect, is a small, sap-sucking insect belonging to the order Thysanura.

It is characterized by its elongated body and long, fringe-like appendages on its abdomen. These insects are often found on the stems and leaves of various plants, where they feed on plant sap. Thysanurus species are typically pale in color, ranging from white to yellowish, and are often overlooked due to their small size.

They play a minor role in plant ecosystems but can sometimes be indicators of environmental stress in certain plant species.
