Trisetum flavescens subsp. baregense

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

baregense, commonly known as the yellow false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish or yellowish hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird populations.
