Tribulus brasiliensis

Tribulus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian soapberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, spiny fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Tribulus brasiliensis is known for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
