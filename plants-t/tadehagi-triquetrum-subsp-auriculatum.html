Tadehagi triquetrum subsp. auriculatum

Tadehagi triquetrum subsp.

auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate tadehagi, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its auriculate (ear-shaped) leaflets, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
