Tanacetum rotundifolium

Tanacetum rotundifolium, commonly known as round-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its round or oval-shaped leaves and yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

Tanacetum rotundifolium has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential antiseptic and anti-inflammatory benefits.
