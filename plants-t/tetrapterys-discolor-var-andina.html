Tetrapterys discolor var. andina

Tetrapterys discolor var. andina, commonly known as the Andean false nettle, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
