Thelocactus multicephalus

Thelocactus multicephalus, commonly known as the multicephalous cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its multiple heads or stems that grow from a single base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the rainy season.

Thelocactus multicephalus is adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating example of desert flora.
