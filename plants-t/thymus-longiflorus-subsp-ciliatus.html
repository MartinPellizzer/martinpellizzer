Thymus longiflorus subsp. ciliatus

Thymus longiflorus subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the fringed thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with aromatic, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
