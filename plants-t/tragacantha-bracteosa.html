Tragacantha bracteosa

Tragacantha bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteous tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its roots are known to secrete a gum similar to tragacanth, which has historical uses in medicine and industry.
