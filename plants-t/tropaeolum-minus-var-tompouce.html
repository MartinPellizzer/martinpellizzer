Tropaeolum minus var. tompouce

Tropaeolum minus var. tompouce, commonly known as the dwarf garden nasturtium, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often red-veined and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its stems are trailing or upright, depending on the variety, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.

This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
