Tragacantha aprica

Tragacantha aprica, commonly known as the Persian gum or Indian gum arabic, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for producing a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its ability to thrive in dry conditions makes it an important species in arid ecosystems.

The resin is harvested from the bark of mature trees, contributing to its economic significance.
