Tulipa gesneriana var. albomarginata

Tulipa gesneriana var. albomarginata, commonly known as the White-edged Tulip, is a cultivated variety of the tulip species.

It is characterized by its striking flowers that feature a pale yellow or white center with a distinct white margin. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 20 inches, with broad, strap-like leaves. It is a bulbous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
