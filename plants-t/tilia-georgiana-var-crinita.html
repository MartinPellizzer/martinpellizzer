Tilia georgiana var. crinita

Tilia georgiana var. crinita, commonly known as the curly-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which have a distinctive wavy, curly margin. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
