Tabebuia bullata

Tabebuia bullata, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded crown, with dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it popular in furniture and construction.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
