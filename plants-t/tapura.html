Tapura

Tapura, commonly known as the "Crimson Flame" or *T. pinnatifida*, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem with large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and inflammation.

Overall, Tapura is both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
