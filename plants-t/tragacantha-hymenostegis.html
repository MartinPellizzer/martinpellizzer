Tragacantha hymenostegis

Tragacantha hymenostegis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration efforts in arid landscapes.
