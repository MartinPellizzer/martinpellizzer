Triticum vulgare convar. compactum

Triticum vulgare convar. compactum, commonly known as dwarf wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its compact growth habit.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically produces small, hard grains that are used in various food products. Its reduced height and dense foliage make it suitable for certain agricultural practices and breeding programs.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to different soil and climate conditions.
