Thalictrum innitens

Thalictrum innitens, commonly known as the nodding meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks.

Thalictrum innitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
