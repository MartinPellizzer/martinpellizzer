Tragacantha secunda

Tragacantha secunda, commonly known as the lesser tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. Tragacantha secunda typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
