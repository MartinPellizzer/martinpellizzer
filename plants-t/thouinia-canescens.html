Thouinia canescens

Thouinia canescens, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a crown-like formation at the top of the stem. Its leaves are usually gray-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Thouinia canescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
