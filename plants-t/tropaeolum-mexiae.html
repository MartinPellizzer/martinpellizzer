Tropaeolum mexiae

Tropaeolum mexiae, commonly known as the Indian cress or garden sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

Tropaeolum mexiae is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
