Tanulepis acuminata

Tanulepis acuminata, commonly known as the slender-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
