Tecoma digitata

Tecoma digitata, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Tecoma digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
