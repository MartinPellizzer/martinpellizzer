Trichilia pachypoda

Trichilia pachypoda, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a rough upper surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable, fragrant wood used in traditional crafts and medicinal applications.
