Thouinia decandra

Thouinia decandra, commonly known as the Indian champa, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are used in religious ceremonies and are also valued for their aromatic properties.
