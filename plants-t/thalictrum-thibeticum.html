Thalictrum thibeticum

Thalictrum thibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum thibeticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
