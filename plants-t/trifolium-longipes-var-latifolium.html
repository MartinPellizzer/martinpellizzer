Trifolium longipes var. latifolium

Trifolium longipes var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf clover, is a variant of the longipes species within the genus Trifolium.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves, which are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. This plant is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The flowers are small and white or pink, arranged in clusters, and it is a common plant in ecological restoration projects.
