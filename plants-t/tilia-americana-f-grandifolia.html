Tilia americana f. grandifolia

Tilia americana f. grandifolia, commonly known as the American bigleaf linden, is a cultivar of the American linden tree.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in length. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its shade-providing canopy.
