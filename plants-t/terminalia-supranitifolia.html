Terminalia supranitifolia

Terminalia supranitifolia, commonly known as the white-leaf terminalia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or oblong in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
