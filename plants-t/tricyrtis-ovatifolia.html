Tricyrtis ovatifolia

Tricyrtis ovatifolia, commonly known as the Japanese shuttles or shuttlegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are ovate in shape, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Tricyrtis ovatifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in late spring to early summer.
