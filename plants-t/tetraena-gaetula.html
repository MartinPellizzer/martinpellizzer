Tetraena gaetula

Tetraena gaetula, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are usually small and leathery, helping to reduce water loss in its dry environment.

Tetraena gaetula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
