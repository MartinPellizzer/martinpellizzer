Trifolium officinale

Trifolium officinale, commonly known as lesser clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium officinale is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
