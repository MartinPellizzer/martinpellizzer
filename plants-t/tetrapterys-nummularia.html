Tetrapterys nummularia

Tetrapterys nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, silver-green leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the plant. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments.
