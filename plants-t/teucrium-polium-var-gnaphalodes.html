Teucrium polium var. gnaphalodes

Teucrium polium var. gnaphalodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal medicine.
