Tamarix deserti

Tamarix deserti, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces pink or red flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Tamarix deserti is well adapted to drought and can thrive in saline conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
