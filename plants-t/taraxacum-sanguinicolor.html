Taraxacum sanguinicolor

Taraxacum sanguinicolor, commonly known as the red margined dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive red-veined leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that can be reddish in color, which is a notable feature. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The species is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
