Tecoma triphylla

Tecoma triphylla, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and it produces clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. Tecoma triphylla thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
