Trisetum ludovicianum

Trisetum ludovicianum, commonly known as the Ludovician sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, three-bracted inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Trisetum ludovicianum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
