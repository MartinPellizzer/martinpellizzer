Tetragonanthus centrostemma

Tetragonanthus centrostemma, commonly known as the white-flowered tetragonanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Tetragonanthus centrostemma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate floral display.
