Trachypogon palmeri

Trachypogon palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, adapting to arid environments.

Trachypogon palmeri is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in regions with low precipitation.
