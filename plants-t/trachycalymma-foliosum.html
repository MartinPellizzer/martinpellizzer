Trachycalymma foliosum

Trachycalymma foliosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf quandong, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces edible, round, yellow fruits that are similar in appearance to native apples. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Trachycalymma foliosum is an important plant for both ecological and cultural reasons in its native region.
