Tropentis

Tropentis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Tropentis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
