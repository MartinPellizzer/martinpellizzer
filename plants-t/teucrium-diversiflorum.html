Teucrium diversiflorum

Teucrium diversiflorum, commonly known as the variable germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 3 feet tall. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like scent when crushed.

Teucrium diversiflorum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
