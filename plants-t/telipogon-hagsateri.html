Telipogon hagsateri

Telipogon hagsateri, commonly known as the Hagsater's orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, spurred orchid flowers that are typically pale green or white, with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Telipogon hagsateri is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
