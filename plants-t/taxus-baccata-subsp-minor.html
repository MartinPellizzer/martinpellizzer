Taxus baccata subsp. minor

Taxus baccata subsp.

minor, commonly known as the European yew, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish tinge.

The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in forestry and medicine make it an important plant in botanical studies.
