Trachyandra triquetra

Trachyandra triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikelets.

Trachyandra triquetra is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
