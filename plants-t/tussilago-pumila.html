Tussilago pumila

Tussilago pumila, commonly known as dwarf coughwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Tussilago pumila is noted for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
