Thalictrum axilliflorum

Thalictrum axilliflorum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features delicate, compound leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its flowers are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Thalictrum axilliflorum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators.
