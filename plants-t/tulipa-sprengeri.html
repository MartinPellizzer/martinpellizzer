Tulipa sprengeri

Tulipa sprengeri, commonly known as the Sprenger's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Tulipa sprengeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
