Tillandsia saundersii

Tillandsia saundersii, commonly known as the Saunders' air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that can store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched stem that bears small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red.

Tillandsia saundersii is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
