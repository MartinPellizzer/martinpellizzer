Thryallis paniculata

Thryallis paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thryallis paniculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
