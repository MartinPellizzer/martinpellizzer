Teucrium sinaicum

Teucrium sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of the Middle East and North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or violet, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems.

Teucrium sinaicum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
