Trigonella arabica

Trigonella arabica, commonly known as Syrian fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Trigonella arabica is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential benefits for digestion and hormonal balance.
