Tetrastigma sessilifolium

Tetrastigma sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaf raisin vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are typically small and turn from green to reddish when mature.

Tetrastigma sessilifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
