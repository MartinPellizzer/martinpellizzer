Trisetum lachnanthum

Trisetum lachnanthum, commonly known as the slender basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.

Trisetum lachnanthum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
