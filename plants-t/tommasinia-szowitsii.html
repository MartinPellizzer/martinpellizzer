Tommasinia szowitsii

Tommasinia szowitsii, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and have a distinctive, often serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Tommasinia szowitsii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical features make it a unique and hardy addition to specialized plant collections.
