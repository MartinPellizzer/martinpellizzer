Tanacetum kubense

Tanacetum kubense, commonly known as Kubenska tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Tanacetum genus.
