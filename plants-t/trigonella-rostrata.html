Trigonella rostrata

Trigonella rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella rostrata is known for its aromatic properties and is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
