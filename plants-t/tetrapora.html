Tetrapora

Tetrapora, commonly known as the four-leaf clover, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of four leaves on its stems, which is a rare trait in the genus. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Tetrapora thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
