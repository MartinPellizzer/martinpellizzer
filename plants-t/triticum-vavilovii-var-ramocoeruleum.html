Triticum vavilovii var. ramocoeruleum

Triticum vavilovii var. ramocoeruleum, commonly known as the wild wheat with blue florets, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple florets, which set it apart from other wheat species. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various environmental conditions.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for wheat breeding programs.
