Trichosalpinx systremmata

Trichosalpinx systremmata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape that attracts pollinators like bees and wasps. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, and the plant often grows as an epiphyte on trees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
