Tribulus saharae

Tribulus saharae, commonly known as the Saharan Tribulus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its leaves are typically small and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Tribulus saharae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
