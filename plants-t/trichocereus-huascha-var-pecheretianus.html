Trichocereus huascha var. pecheretianus

Trichocereus huascha var. pecheretianus, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a cacti species characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habits. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
