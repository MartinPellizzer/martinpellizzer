Trisetum barbatipaleum

Trisetum barbatipaleum, commonly known as the hairy-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow with a hairy texture. Its inflorescence is a panicle, consisting of multiple branches that bear small, spikelet-like flowers.

Trisetum barbatipaleum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
