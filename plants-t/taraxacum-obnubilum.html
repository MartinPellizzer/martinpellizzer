Taraxacum obnubilum

Taraxacum obnubilum, commonly known as the clouded dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to colonize areas quickly.

The species is notable for its distinctive, milky sap and its role in supporting pollinators.
