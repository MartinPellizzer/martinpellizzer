Tanacetum silvicola

Tanacetum silvicola, commonly known as the forest tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a strongly perfumed scent. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Tanacetum silvicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
