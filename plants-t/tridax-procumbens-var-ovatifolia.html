Tridax procumbens var. ovatifolia

Tridax procumbens var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tridax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with a sprawling growth habit, characterized by its ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are easily dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
