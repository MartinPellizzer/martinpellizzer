Trigonochilum myrianthum

Trigonochilum myrianthum, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers typically bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
