Tragacantha trautvetteri

Tragacantha trautvetteri, commonly known as the Trautvetter's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its roots are known for their high mucilage content, which has been used historically in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
