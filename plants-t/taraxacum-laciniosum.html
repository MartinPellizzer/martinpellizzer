Taraxacum laciniosum

Taraxacum laciniosum, commonly known as the fringed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, pappus-equipped seed that is easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
