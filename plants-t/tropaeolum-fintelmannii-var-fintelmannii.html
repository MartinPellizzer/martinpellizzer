Tropaeolum fintelmannii var. fintelmannii

Tropaeolum fintelmannii var. fintelmannii, commonly known as the Fintelmann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its edible flowers, which can be used in culinary applications.
