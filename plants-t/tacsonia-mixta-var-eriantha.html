Tacsonia mixta var. eriantha

Tacsonia mixta var. eriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in the early morning. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and the presence of glandular hairs on its foliage.
