Tecoma ipe

Tecoma ipe, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma ipe is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
