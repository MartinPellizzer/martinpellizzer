Tephrosia rupicola subsp. rupicola

Tephrosia rupicola subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically glandular and hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Tephrosia rupicola subsp.

rupicola is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
