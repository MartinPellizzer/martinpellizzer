Taraxacum brevisectum

Taraxacum brevisectum, commonly known as the short-segmented dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.

The species is relatively small compared to other dandelions, with shorter leaf segments and a compact growth habit.
