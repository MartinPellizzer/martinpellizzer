Tachigali grandiflora

Tachigali grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, hard seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of Tachigali grandiflora make it an important species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
