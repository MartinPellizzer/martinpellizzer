Trigonella sinuata

Trigonella sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to green pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
