Tithonia calva var. calva

Tithonia calva var. calva, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown central disk florets, which make it visually striking. The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a radiate head. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tithonia calva var. calva is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
