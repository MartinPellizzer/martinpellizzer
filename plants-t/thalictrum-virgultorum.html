Thalictrum virgultorum

Thalictrum virgultorum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with numerous stamens and a central pistil.

Thalictrum virgultorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
