Trichocereus santiaguensis

Trichocereus santiaguensis, commonly known as the Santa Rita cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming at night, which is a common trait among many cacti in the Cactaceae family.

Trichocereus santiaguensis is also noted for its slow growth rate and resilience to drought conditions.
