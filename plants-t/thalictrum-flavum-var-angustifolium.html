Thalictrum flavum var. angustifolium

Thalictrum flavum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are arranged in compound umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and meadows, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
