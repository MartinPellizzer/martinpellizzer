Thelesperma megapotamicum var. ambiguum

Thelesperma megapotamicum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
