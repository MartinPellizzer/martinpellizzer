Torilis scandicina

Torilis scandicina, commonly known as hairy burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Torilis scandicina is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
