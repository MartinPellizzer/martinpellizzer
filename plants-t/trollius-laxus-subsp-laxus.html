Trollius laxus subsp. laxus

Trollius laxus subsp.

laxus, commonly known as the looseflower buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow, daisy-like flowers with numerous ray florets and a central disc of tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
