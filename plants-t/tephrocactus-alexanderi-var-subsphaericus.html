Tephrocactus alexanderi var. subsphaericus

Tephrocactus alexanderi var. subsphaericus, commonly known as the Alexander's globose cactus, is a rare and compact cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a spherical or nearly spherical stem that is typically covered in short, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
