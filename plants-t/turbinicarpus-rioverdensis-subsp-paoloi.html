Turbinicarpus rioverdensis subsp. paoloi

Turbinicarpus rioverdensis subsp.

paoloi, commonly known as the Rioverdens Desert Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green and covered with white to yellowish spines.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
