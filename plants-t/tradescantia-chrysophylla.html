Tradescantia chrysophylla

Tradescantia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant yellow or golden green, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
