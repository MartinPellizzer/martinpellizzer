Thuja plicata var. pendula

Thuja plicata var. pendula, commonly known as the lacey arborvitae, is a cultivar of the western red cedar.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant has a conical shape when young, which tends to flatten out as it matures. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.

This evergreen is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
