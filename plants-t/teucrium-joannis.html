Teucrium joannis

Teucrium joannis, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often with a mint-like scent.

Teucrium joannis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
