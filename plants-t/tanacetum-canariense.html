Tanacetum canariense

Tanacetum canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island tansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.

Tanacetum canariense is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
