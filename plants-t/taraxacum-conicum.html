Taraxacum conicum

Taraxacum conicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a single, tall, yellow flower head that produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is perennial, with a deep taproot that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common and easily identifiable weed in many regions.
