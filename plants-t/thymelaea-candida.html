Thymelaea candida

Thymelaea candida, commonly known as white thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Thymelaea candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
