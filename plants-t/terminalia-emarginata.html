Terminalia emarginata

Terminalia emarginata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its hard, heavy wood.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is also used in local crafts and construction.
