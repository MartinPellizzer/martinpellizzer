Tetrastigma henryi var. mollifolium

Tetrastigma henryi var. mollifolium, commonly known as the silky-leafed grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its berries are usually red or black when ripe and are edible, though they are not commonly cultivated for consumption.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments within forests.
