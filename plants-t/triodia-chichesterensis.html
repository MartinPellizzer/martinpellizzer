Triodia chichesterensis

Triodia chichesterensis, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has wiry, spiky leaves that help reduce water loss and protect it from grazing animals. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Triodia chichesterensis is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
