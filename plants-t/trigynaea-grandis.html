Trigynaea grandis

Trigynaea grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Its flowers, which can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, are typically white or pale pink and emit a strong, sweet fragrance.

Trigynaea grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
