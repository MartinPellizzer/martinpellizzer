Trifolium vesiculosum var. leiocalycinum

Trifolium vesiculosum var. leiocalycinum, commonly known as the lemon vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by cylindrical, inflated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
