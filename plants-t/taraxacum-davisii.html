Taraxacum davisii

Taraxacum davisii, commonly known as the Davis's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed head. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
