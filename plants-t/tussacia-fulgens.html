Tussacia fulgens

Tussacia fulgens, commonly known as the golden tussock grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces showy, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are often more robust.

Tussacia fulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
