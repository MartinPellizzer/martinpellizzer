Trichopteryx brevifolia

Trichopteryx brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved winged fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, wing-like fronds.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, short leaves that are clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery structure that enhances its ornamental value.

Trichopteryx brevifolia is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
