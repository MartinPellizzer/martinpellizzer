Tinus opegrapha

Tinus opegrapha, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear vivid orange-red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Tinus opegrapha is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
