Trollius pumilus var. lobatus

Trollius pumilus var. lobatus, commonly known as the dwarf garden buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that resemble a cup, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
