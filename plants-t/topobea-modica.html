Topobea modica

Topobea modica, commonly known as the Modica topobea, is a rare and unique plant species native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features small, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
