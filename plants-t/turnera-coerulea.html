Turnera coerulea

Turnera coerulea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
