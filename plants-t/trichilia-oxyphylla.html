Trichilia oxyphylla

Trichilia oxyphylla, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Trichilia oxyphylla is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
