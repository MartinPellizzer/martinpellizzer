Trixis parviflora

Trixis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered trixis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Trixis parviflora is often found in rocky or sandy environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
