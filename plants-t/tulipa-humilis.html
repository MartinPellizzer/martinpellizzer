Tulipa humilis

Tulipa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a darker throat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its early spring blooming period.

Tulipa humilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
