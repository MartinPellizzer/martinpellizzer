Thea theiformis

Thea theiformis, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
