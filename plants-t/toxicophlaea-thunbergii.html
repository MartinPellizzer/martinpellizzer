Toxicophlaea thunbergii

Toxicophlaea thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

While not highly toxic, it can cause mild irritation if handled, making it important to wear gloves when gardening with this species.
