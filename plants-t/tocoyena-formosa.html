Tocoyena formosa

Tocoyena formosa, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble torches. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Tocoyena formosa thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value lies in its bold color and unique floral structure, which make it a standout in any collection.
