Trisetaria thospitica

Trisetaria thospitica, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Trisetaria thospitica is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and drought-resistant features.
