Tragacantha acuta

Tragacantha acuta, commonly known as the sharp tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its sap, known as tragacanth, is a viscous gum used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent.

Tragacantha acuta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
