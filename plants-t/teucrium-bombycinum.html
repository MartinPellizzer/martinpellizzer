Teucrium bombycinum

Teucrium bombycinum, commonly known as the blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Teucrium bombycinum is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in native plant gardens.
