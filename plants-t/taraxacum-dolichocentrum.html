Taraxacum dolichocentrum

Taraxacum dolichocentrum, commonly known as the long-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and deeply lobed leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that transitions into a fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum dolichocentrum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and flowers.
