Trigonella emodi var. medicaginoidea

Trigonella emodi var. medicaginoidea, commonly known as the Kashmiri fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
