Teucrium antiatlanticum

Teucrium antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic germander, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shores, thriving in saline and well-drained environments. The plant features slender, aromatic stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, clustered flowers are usually violet or blue in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Teucrium antiatlanticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
