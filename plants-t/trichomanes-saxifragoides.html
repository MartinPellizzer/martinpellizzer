Trichomanes saxifragoides

Trichomanes saxifragoides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, lyrate-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the margins of the fronds.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
