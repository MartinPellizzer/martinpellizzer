Tropaeolum longiflorum

Tropaeolum longiflorum, commonly known as the everlasting nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, peppery-tasting seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum longiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
