Tragacantha ochreata

Tragacantha ochreata, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellowish flowers and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces a sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Tragacantha ochreata is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid climates.
