Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. sinuatum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

sinuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
