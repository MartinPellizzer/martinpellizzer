Tragopogon rumelicus

Tragopogon rumelicus, commonly known as the Rumelian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette structure. The plant features silver-gray, woolly foliage and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
