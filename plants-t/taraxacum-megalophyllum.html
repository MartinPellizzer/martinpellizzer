Taraxacum megalophyllum

Taraxacum megalophyllum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often hairy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem topped with a bright yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its distinctive size and appearance, it is often considered a striking example of the dandelion genus.
