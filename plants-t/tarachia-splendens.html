Tarachia splendens

Tarachia splendens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to store water, and its stems are typically erect and branching. Tarachia splendens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
