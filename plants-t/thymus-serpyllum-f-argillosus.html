Thymus serpyllum f. argillosus

Thymus serpyllum f. argillosus, commonly known as the clay thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal medicine.
