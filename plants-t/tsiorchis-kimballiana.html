Tsiorchis kimballiana

Tsiorchis kimballiana, commonly known as the Kimball's thornapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spiny, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include glandular hairs and a distinctive, branched root system.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
