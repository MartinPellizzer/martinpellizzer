Turnera ulmifolia var. grandidentata

Turnera ulmifolia var. grandidentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed turnera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, which includes passionflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the flowers are typically yellow with red or orange markings.

The varietal form, grandidentata, is distinguished by its larger, more prominent teeth on the leaf margins compared to the typical variety.
