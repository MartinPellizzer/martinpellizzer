Tinospora negrotica

Tinospora negrotica, commonly known as the Negros tinospora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Tinospora negrotica is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
