Trichantha pendula

Trichantha pendula, commonly known as the hanging trichantha, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, dangling inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Trichantha pendula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
