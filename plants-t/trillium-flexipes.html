Trillium flexipes

Trillium flexipes, commonly known as the nodding trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. The plant features three elliptic leaves and a single, nodding white flower with three petals and three sepals. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, bell-shaped appearance.

Trillium flexipes is also valued for its ecological role, supporting pollinators and contributing to forest biodiversity.
