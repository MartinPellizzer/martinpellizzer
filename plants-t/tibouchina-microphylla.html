Tibouchina microphylla

Tibouchina microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with tubular petals that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Tibouchina microphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
