Thladiantha dentata

Thladiantha dentata, commonly known as the toothed goji, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Thladiantha dentata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
