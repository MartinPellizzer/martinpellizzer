Trimezia rupestris subsp. magnifica

Trimezia rupestris subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the magnificat trimezia, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a deep red or purple hue along the edges.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
