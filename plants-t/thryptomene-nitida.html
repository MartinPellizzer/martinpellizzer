Thryptomene nitida

Thryptomene nitida, commonly known as silky thryptomene, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

Thryptomene nitida is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
