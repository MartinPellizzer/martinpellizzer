Tamarix brachystachys

Tamarix brachystachys, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which consists of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and dry landscapes.

Tamarix brachystachys is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils in challenging conditions.
