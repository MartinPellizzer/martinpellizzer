Tridax verticillata

Tridax verticillata, commonly known as the creeping oxalis, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central stamen. Its leaves are trifoliate, composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Tridax verticillata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
