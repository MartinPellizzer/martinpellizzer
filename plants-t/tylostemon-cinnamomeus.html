Tylostemon cinnamomeus

Tylostemon cinnamomeus, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is reddish-brown and exudes a strong, spicy aroma.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
