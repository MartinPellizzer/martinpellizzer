Tephrocactus pentlandii var. fuauxianus

Tephrocactus pentlandii var. fuauxianus, commonly known as the Huachuma cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also known for its traditional use in ceremonial contexts.
