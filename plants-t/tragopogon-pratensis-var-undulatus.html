Tragopogon pratensis var. undulatus

Tragopogon pratensis var. undulatus, commonly known as the undulating goatsbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with wavy edges, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple-tipped involucre.

It is often noted for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
