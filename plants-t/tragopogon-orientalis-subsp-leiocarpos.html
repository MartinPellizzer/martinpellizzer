Tragopogon orientalis subsp. leiocarpos

Tragopogon orientalis subsp.

leiocarpos, commonly known as the smoothleaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.
