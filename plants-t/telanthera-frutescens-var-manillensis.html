Telanthera frutescens var. manillensis

Telanthera frutescens var. manillensis, commonly known as the Philippine telanthera, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
