Thymus pulegioides subvar. barbulatus

Thymus pulegioides subvar. barbulatus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
