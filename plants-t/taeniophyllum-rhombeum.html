Taeniophyllum rhombeum

Taeniophyllum rhombeum, commonly known as the diamond leaf or rhomboid fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a compact, low-growing structure. The fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, glossy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of air humidity and indirect light.

This plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in terrariums or as a houseplant.
