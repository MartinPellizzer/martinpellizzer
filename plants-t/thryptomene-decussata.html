Thryptomene decussata

Thryptomene decussata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which attract a variety of pollinators.

Thryptomene decussata is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common sight in heathlands and coastal dunes.
