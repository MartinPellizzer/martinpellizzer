Teucrium balearicum

Teucrium balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in coastal areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Teucrium balearicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
