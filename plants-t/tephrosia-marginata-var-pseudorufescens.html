Tephrosia marginata var. pseudorufescens

Tephrosia marginata var. pseudorufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
