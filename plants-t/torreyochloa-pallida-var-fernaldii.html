Torreyochloa pallida var. fernaldii

Torreyochloa pallida var. fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's threadgrass, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
