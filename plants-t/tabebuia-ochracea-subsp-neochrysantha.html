Tabebuia ochracea subsp. neochrysantha

Tabebuia ochracea subsp.

neochrysantha, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the rainy season.

The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a prominent feature in its native landscapes. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and the leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits.
