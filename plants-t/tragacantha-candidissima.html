Tragacantha candidissima

Tragacantha candidissima, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces a viscous gum from its sap, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and woodlands.

Tragacantha candidissima is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
