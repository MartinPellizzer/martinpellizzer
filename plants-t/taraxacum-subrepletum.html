Taraxacum subrepletum

Taraxacum subrepletum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and roots.
