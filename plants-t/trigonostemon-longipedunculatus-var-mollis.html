Trigonostemon longipedunculatus var. mollis

Trigonostemon longipedunculatus var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed trigonostemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
