Truellum hastatosagittatum

Truellum hastatosagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its foliage is popular in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, textured appearance.
