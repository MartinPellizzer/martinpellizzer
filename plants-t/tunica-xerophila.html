Tunica xerophila

Tunica xerophila, commonly known as the desert shield plant, is a succulent species adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant has a shallow root system that quickly absorbs surface moisture. Its waxy leaf surface reduces water loss through transpiration.

Tunica xerophila is native to regions with extreme drought, such as parts of Africa and the Middle East.
