Taraxacum lacerilobum

Taraxacum lacerilobum, commonly known as the frayedleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Taraxacum lacerilobum is a perennial plant with a robust root system and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
