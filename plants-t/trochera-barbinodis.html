Trochera barbinodis

Trochera barbinodis, commonly known as the barbed node orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence with clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
