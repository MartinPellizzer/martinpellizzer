Tropaeolum papillosum

Tropaeolum papillosum, commonly known as the pepperleaf nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, peppery-tasting leaves and showy, bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with trailing stems that make it suitable for container gardening. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
