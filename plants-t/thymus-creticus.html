Thymus creticus

Thymus creticus, commonly known as Crete thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the island of Crete.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or dry environments. Its medicinal properties have been recognized for centuries, with uses in traditional remedies and as a culinary herb.

Thymus creticus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
