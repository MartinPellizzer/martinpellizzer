Tetraotis

Tetraotis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Tetraotis species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Tetraotis paniculata, which is known for its striking purple flowers.
