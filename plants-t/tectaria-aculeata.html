Tectaria aculeata

Tectaria aculeata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often hairy fronds that are typically divided into segments. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Its fronds can grow up to several feet in length and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tectaria aculeata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
