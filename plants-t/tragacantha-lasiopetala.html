Tragacantha lasiopetala

Tragacantha lasiopetala, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds.

Tragacantha lasiopetala is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
