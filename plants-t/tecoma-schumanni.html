Tecoma schumanni

Tecoma schumanni, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma schumanni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
