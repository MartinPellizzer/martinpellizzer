Truellum paludosum

Truellum paludosum, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in wetlands and shallow water areas.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. It is a carnivorous plant, supplementing its nutrient intake by digesting captured prey.

Truellum paludosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling insect populations and providing habitat for microscopic life.
