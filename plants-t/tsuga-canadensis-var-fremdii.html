Tsuga canadensis var. fremdii

Tsuga canadensis var. fremdii, commonly known as the Fremont's hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically dense and provides dense shade, making it a valuable ornamental plant. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
