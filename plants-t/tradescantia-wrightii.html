Tradescantia wrightii

Tradescantia wrightii, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Tradescantia wrightii is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
