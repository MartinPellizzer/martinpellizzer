Tuberaria guttata var. punctata

Tuberaria guttata var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, gray-green leaves with distinctive white and yellow spots. The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
