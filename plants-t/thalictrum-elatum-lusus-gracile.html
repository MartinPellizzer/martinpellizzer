Thalictrum elatum lusus gracile

Thalictrum elatum lusus gracile, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in racemes along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
