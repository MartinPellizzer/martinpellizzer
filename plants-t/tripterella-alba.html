Tripterella alba

Tripterella alba, commonly known as the white tripterella, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Tripterella alba is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
