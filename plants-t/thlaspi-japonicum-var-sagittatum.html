Thlaspi japonicum var. sagittatum

Thlaspi japonicum var. sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
