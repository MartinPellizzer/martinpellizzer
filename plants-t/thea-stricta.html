Thea stricta

Thea stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf thea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Theaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is native to regions in East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Thea stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
