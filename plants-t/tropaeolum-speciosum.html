Tropaeolum speciosum

Tropaeolum speciosum, commonly known as the "Jacob's ladder," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a trailing perennial that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive ladder-like appearance, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Tropaeolum speciosum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
