Torrubia ligustrifolia

Torrubia ligustrifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf torrubia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender peduncles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.
