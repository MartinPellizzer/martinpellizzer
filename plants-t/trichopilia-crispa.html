Trichopilia crispa

Trichopilia crispa, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often zigzagging stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in winter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine hairs.

Trichopilia crispa is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and prefers dry, sunny conditions.
