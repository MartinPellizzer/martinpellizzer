Talinum microphyllum

Talinum microphyllum, commonly known as the littleleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or purple flowers. The plant produces smooth, shiny berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Talinum microphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
