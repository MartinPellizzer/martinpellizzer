Trigonella siunica

Trigonella siunica, commonly known as the Sicilian fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Trigonella siunica plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
