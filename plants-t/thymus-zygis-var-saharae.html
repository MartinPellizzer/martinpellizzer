Thymus zygis var. saharae

Thymus zygis var. saharae, commonly known as Sahara thyme, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of North Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
