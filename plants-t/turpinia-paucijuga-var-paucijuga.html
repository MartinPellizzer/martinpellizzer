Turpinia paucijuga var. paucijuga

Turpinia paucijuga var. paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turpinia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its small, fragrant flowers are white to pale green and appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
