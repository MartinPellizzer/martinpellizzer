Trematolobelia singularis

Trematolobelia singularis, commonly known as the rare desert orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue blossoms that bloom during the brief rainy season. The plant has a deep root system adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is classified as critically endangered.
