Tingulonga polybotrya

Tingulonga polybotrya, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has a woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers open at night and close by dawn, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
