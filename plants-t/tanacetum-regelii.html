Tanacetum regelii

Tanacetum regelii, commonly known as Regel's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom in late summer.

Tanacetum regelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
