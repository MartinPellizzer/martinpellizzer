Tiniaria baldschuanica

Tiniaria baldschuanica, commonly known as the Caucasian tiniaria, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers bloom in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
