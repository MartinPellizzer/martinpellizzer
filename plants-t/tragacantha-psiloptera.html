Tragacantha psiloptera

Tragacantha psiloptera, commonly known as the pale-winged tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a distinctive gum, which is harvested from its bark and used in various traditional and industrial applications. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Tragacantha psiloptera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
