Terminalia bengalensis

Terminalia bengalensis, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are rich in antioxidants. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is often found in moist, lowland areas and plays a role in local ecosystems.
