Trifolium howellii

Trifolium howellii, commonly known as Howell's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, which is characteristic of the genus Trifolium. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
