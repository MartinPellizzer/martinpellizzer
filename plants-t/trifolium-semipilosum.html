Trifolium semipilosum

Trifolium semipilosum, commonly known as the silky clover, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium semipilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
