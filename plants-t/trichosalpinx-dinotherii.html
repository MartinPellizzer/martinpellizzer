Trichosalpinx dinotherii

Trichosalpinx dinotherii, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and growth habits.

Trichosalpinx dinotherii is also noted for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, enhancing its nutrient uptake in nutrient-poor soils.
