Triticum magellanicum var. condensatum

Triticum magellanicum var. condensatum, commonly known as the Magellanic wheat, is a rare and endemic grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in the Andes mountains.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
