Tachigali acrensis

Tachigali acrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Tachigali acrensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally in local crafts and construction.
