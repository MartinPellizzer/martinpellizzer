Teucrium gossypinum

Teucrium gossypinum, commonly known as cotton-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
