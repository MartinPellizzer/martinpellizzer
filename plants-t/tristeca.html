Tristeca

Tristeca, commonly known as the white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Tristeca is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils due to its ability to fix nitrogen.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
