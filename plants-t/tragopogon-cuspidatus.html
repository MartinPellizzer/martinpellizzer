Tragopogon cuspidatus

Tragopogon cuspidatus, commonly known as the hooked thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that transitions to a purple-brown color as it matures. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is often spiny, especially near the base.

Tragopogon cuspidatus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
