Tetracrypta cinnamomoides

Tetracrypta cinnamomoides, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that emit a strong, spicy fragrance when crushed. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.
