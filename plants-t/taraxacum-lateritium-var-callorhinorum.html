Taraxacum lateritium var. callorhinorum

Taraxacum lateritium var. callorhinorum, commonly known as the stone dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow composite flower head. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
