Taraxacum hatsuroi

Taraxacum hatsuroi, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky latex when the stems or leaves are damaged, a common feature among the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

The species is often found in meadows, gardens, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability and resilience.
