Trigonochilum sharoniae

Trigonochilum sharoniae, commonly known as Sharon's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three sepals and three petals that often display contrasting colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.

Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and the challenges it faces due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
