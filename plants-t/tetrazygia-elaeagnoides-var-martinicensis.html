Tetrazygia elaeagnoides var. martinicensis

Tetrazygia elaeagnoides var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique thorny acacia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dry, tropical environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
