Triticum dicoccum var. nigrirubrimuticum

Triticum dicoccum var. nigrirubrimuticum, commonly known as black rye, is a variety of rye characterized by its dark, reddish-brown grains.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its grains are rich in dietary fiber and have a distinct, slightly nutty flavor.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
