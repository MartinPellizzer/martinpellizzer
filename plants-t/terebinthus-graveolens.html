Terebinthus graveolens

Terebinthus graveolens, commonly known as the stinking terebinth, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or arid environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been historically valued for its use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
