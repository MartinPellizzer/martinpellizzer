Trichosanthes scabra var. penicaudii

Trichosanthes scabra var. penicaudii, commonly known as the rough gourd or wild bottle gourd, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits with a rough, ridged surface. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.

This variant is also valued for its ornamental features and medicinal properties in some regions.
