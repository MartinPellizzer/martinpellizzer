Taraxacum roseopedatum

Taraxacum roseopedatum, commonly known as the pink-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of pale pink or reddish leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces a distinctive seed parachute. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows, gardens, and along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
