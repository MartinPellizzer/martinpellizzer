Taraxacum adhaerens

Taraxacum adhaerens, commonly known as the sticky dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces a single, upright flowering stem topped with a spherical flower head composed of many tiny florets. Its flowers are typically yellow, and they open in the morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
