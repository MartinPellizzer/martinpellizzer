Trifolium philistaeum var. filifolium

Trifolium philistaeum var. filifolium, commonly known as the filifolium clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
