Turnera melochia var. melochia

Turnera melochia var. melochia, commonly known as the Brazilian shield tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a climbing shrub that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and the plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
