Tragacantha diphysa

Tragacantha diphysa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Iran and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing small, brown seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's latex, which is milky and sticky, has been historically used for various practical and medicinal purposes.
