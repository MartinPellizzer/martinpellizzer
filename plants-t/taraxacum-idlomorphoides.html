Taraxacum idlomorphoides

Taraxacum idlomorphoides, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a hollow, spiky seed head that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Taraxacum idlomorphoides is often found in alpine meadows and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
