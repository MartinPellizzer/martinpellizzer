Thiseltonia dyeri

Thiseltonia dyeri, commonly known as the goldenstar mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a minty scent, and it is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.

Thiseltonia dyeri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland and swampy habitats.
