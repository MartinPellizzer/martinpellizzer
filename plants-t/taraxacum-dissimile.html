Taraxacum dissimile

Taraxacum dissimile, commonly known as the variable dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a composite flower head that consists of numerous yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It is native to Eurasia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum dissimile is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
