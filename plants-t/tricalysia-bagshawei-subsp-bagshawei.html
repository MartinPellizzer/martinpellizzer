Tricalysia bagshawei subsp.

bagshawei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
