Trifolium willdenovii var. typicum

Trifolium willdenovii var. typicum, commonly known as the Willdenow's clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment and agricultural practices.
