Taraxacum arakii

Taraxacum arakii, commonly known as the Arakii dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive white, fluffy seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
