Tornelia

Tornelia, commonly known as the "false hibiscus," is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plants are characterized by their showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Tornelia species typically have alternate, simple leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
