Tetradium glabrifolium var. glaucum

Tetradium glabrifolium var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous tetradium, is a variant of the tetradium glabrifolium species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, glaucous leaves that have a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

This variant is native to specific regions in East Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
