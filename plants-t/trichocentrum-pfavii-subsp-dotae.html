Trichocentrum pfavii subsp. dotae

Trichocentrum pfavii subsp.

dotae, commonly known as the Dot's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow.

The plant has long, pendant-like inflorescences and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
