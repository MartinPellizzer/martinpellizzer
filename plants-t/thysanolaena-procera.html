Thysanolaena procera

Thysanolaena procera, commonly known as the tall beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Thysanolaena procera is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps.
