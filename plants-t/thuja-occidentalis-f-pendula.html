Thuja occidentalis f. pendula

Thuja occidentalis f. pendula, commonly known as the American arborvitae or creeping thuja, is a unique cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its graceful, weeping growth habit, with long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The plant has scale-like leaves that are bright green on top and pale white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is relatively low-maintenance, preferring well-drained soil and full to partial sun.
