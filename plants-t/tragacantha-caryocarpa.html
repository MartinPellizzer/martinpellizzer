Tragacantha caryocarpa

Tragacantha caryocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum or caryocarpa tragacantha, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits that contain a viscous gum resin, which is commercially valuable. Tragacantha caryocarpa is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various desert species.
