Tragacantha sericostachys

Tragacantha sericostachys, commonly known as the silky-stemmed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration in semi-arid regions.
