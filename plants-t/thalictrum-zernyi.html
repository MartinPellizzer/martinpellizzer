Thalictrum zernyi

Thalictrum zernyi, commonly known as the Siberian meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and compound leaves with finely divided lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is adapted to well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

Thalictrum zernyi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
