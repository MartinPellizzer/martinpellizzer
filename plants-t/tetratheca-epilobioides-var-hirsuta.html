Tetratheca epilobioides var. hirsuta

Tetratheca epilobioides var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tetratheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
