Trigonospermum multiflorum

Trigonospermum multiflorum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Trigonospermum multiflorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
