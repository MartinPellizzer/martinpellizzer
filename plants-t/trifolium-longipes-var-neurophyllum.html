Trifolium longipes var. neurophyllum

Trifolium longipes var. neurophyllum, commonly known as the long-stemmed clover, is a variant of the longipes species within the genus Trifolium.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
