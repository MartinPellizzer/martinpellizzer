Thymus serpyllum f. freynii

Thymus serpyllum f. freynii, commonly known as Freyn's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Freyn's thyme is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
