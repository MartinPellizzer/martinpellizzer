Trifolium indecorum

Trifolium indecorum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, purple flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in grassy areas and meadows. It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
