Teucrium leucocladum subsp. jordanicum

Teucrium leucocladum subsp.

jordanicum, commonly known as Jordanian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant has slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in compact clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
