Tropaeolum leptophyllum subsp. gracile

Tropaeolum leptophyllum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, bright green leaves.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, typical of the Tropaeolum genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves and flowers.
