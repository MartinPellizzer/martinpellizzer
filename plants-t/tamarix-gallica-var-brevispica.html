Tamarix gallica var. brevispica

Tamarix gallica var. brevispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for wildlife.
