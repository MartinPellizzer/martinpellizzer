Trichopteryx fruticulosa

Trichopteryx fruticulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures that provide additional protection against desiccation.

Trichopteryx fruticulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
