Taraxacum wallonicum

Taraxacum wallonicum, commonly known as the Wallonian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum wallonicum is often found in temperate regions and is relatively hardy, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
