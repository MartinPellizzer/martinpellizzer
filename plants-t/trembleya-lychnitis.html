Trembleya lychnitis

Trembleya lychnitis, commonly known as the candleflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, resembling candles, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Trembleya lychnitis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
