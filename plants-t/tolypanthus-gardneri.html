Tolypanthus gardneri

Tolypanthus gardneri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in agroforestry systems.
