Talauma

Talauma, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with flowers that come in a variety of colors including purple, pink, white, and blue. Talauma thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ability to bloom continuously makes it a favorite among indoor gardeners.
