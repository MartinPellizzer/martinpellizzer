Tilia × hegyesensis

Tilia × hegyesensis, commonly known as the Hungarian linden, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 lobed, with a smooth, velvety underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in pendulous clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is native to Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
