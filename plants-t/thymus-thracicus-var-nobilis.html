Thymus thracicus var. nobilis

Thymus thracicus var. nobilis, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of thyme. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
