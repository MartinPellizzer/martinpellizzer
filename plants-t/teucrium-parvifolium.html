Teucrium parvifolium

Teucrium parvifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, glandular hairs. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or blue, arranged in dense spikes.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.
