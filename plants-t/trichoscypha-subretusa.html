Trichoscypha subretusa

Trichoscypha subretusa, commonly known as the African sandflea tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Trichoscypha subretusa is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
