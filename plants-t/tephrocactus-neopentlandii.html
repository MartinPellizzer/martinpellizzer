Tephrocactus neopentlandii

Tephrocactus neopentlandii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, a typical adaptation for arid environments. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its shallow root system allows it to thrive in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Despite its small size, Tephrocactus neopentlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
