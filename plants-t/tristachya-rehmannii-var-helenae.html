Tristachya rehmannii var. helenae

Tristachya rehmannii var. helenae, commonly known as Helen's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
