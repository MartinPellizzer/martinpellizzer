Thespis sinapifolia

Thespis sinapifolia, commonly known as the western yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of yarrow.

Thespis sinapifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
