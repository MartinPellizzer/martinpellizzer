Triodia bromoides

Triodia bromoides, commonly known as spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, bristly hairs, which help reduce water loss. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.

Triodia bromoides is well adapted to drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of the Australian flora.
