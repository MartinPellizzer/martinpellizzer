Tetralobus preissii

Tetralobus preissii, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Tetralobus preissii is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.
