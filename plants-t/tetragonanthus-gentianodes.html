Tetragonanthus gentianodes

Tetragonanthus gentianodes, commonly known as the four-angled gentian, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and distinctive four-angled stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
