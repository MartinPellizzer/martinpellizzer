Tragacantha globosa

Tragacantha globosa, commonly known as globe thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky blue or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by layers of bracts. The plant produces a milky sap that contains a variety of compounds, including polysaccharides and flavonoids. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Tragacantha globosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
