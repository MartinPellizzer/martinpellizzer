Terminalia schimperiana

Terminalia schimperiana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

The species also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
