Trifolium aureum subsp. barbulatum

Trifolium aureum subsp.

barbulatum, commonly known as the golden clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
