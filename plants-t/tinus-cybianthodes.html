Tinus cybianthodes

Tinus cybianthodes, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Tinus cybianthodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
