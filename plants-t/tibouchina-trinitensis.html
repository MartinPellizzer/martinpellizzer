Tibouchina trinitensis

Tibouchina trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad beauty or red tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy clusters of pink or red flowers. The flowers are tubular and appear in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and moist woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
