Tamonea tomentosa

Tamonea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its fruits are toxic and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Tamonea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
