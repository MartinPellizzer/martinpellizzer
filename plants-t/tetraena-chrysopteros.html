Tetraena chrysopteros

Tetraena chrysopteros, commonly known as the golden-tipped tetraena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and arid environments.

Tetraena chrysopteros is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
