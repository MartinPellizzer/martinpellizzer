Transcaucasia

Transcaucasia, commonly known as the Caucasus, is a region rich in botanical diversity, encompassing parts of Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan.

It is home to numerous endemic plant species, many of which are found nowhere else in the world. The area features a variety of climates, from alpine zones to subtropical regions, supporting a wide range of flora. Notable plants include the Caucasian endemic species such as the Transcaucasian bellflower and various species of rhododendrons.

The region's unique geological and climatic conditions have contributed to its status as a biodiversity hotspot.
