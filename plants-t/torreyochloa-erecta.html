Torreyochloa erecta

Torreyochloa erecta, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Torreyochloa erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
