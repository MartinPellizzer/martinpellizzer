Tecoma lateriflora

Tecoma lateriflora, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a spreading crown and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin.

Tecoma lateriflora is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
