Taxus baccata var. brevifolia

Taxus baccata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the European yew with short leaves, is a variant of the European yew tree.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and short, scale-like leaves. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical significance in various cultural contexts.
