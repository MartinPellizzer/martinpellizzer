Taraxacum subtenuiforme

Taraxacum subtenuiforme, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, this plant thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
