Terminalia thozetii

Terminalia thozetii, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits that turn from green to red when mature. The fruit's pulp is sour and acidic, often used in traditional local cuisines.

It also plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
