Tanacetum vahlii var. subflosculosum

Tanacetum vahlii var. subflosculosum, commonly known as the dwarf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to xeriscapes.
