Typhoides arundinacea f. schwarzii

Typhoides arundinacea f. schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarzii form of the grass species Typhoides arundinacea, is a variant characterized by its unique morphological features.

It typically exhibits a tall, slender growth habit with elongated, narrow leaves that are often slightly hairy. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicle-like clusters, which are a defining feature of the genus. This form is adapted to specific environmental conditions, such as well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a representative of the grass family (Poaceae) and its potential ecological value in certain habitats.
