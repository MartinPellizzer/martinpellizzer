Thelypteris amazonica

Thelypteris amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon water fern, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in riverine forests and swampy areas. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in anchoring the plant in waterlogged soils.

Thelypteris amazonica plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
