Thea lutchuensis

Thea lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu tea plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce a unique type of tea.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
