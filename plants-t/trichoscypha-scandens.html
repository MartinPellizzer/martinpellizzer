Trichoscypha scandens

Trichoscypha scandens, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Trichoscypha scandens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
