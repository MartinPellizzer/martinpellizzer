Tristachya dregeana

Tristachya dregeana, commonly known as the desert grass or African silver grass, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to yellow flowers, which are often inconspicuous but attract various pollinators. Tristachya dregeana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping in dry climates.
