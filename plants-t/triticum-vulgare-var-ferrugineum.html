Triticum vulgare var. ferrugineum

Triticum vulgare var. ferrugineum, commonly known as red wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its reddish husk and grains.

It is an annual grass that typically grows in temperate regions and is cultivated for its high protein content. The plant has a robust root system and produces spikes of grains that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its nutritional profile and is used in various food products, including bread and pasta.
