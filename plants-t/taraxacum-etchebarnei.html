Taraxacum etchebarnei

Taraxacum etchebarnei, commonly known as the Etchebarne's dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
