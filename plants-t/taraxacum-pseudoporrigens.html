Taraxacum pseudoporrigens

Taraxacum pseudoporrigens, commonly known as the purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its hairy, purple-tinged stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, typical of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.
