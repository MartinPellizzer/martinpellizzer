Tradescantia fluminensis f. aureovittata

Tradescantia fluminensis f. aureovittata, commonly known as the golden stripe spiderwort, is a varietal form of the spiderwort plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that features striking, broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive golden stripe running along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage, which adds a splash of color to any landscape.
