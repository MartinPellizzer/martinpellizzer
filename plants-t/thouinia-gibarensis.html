Thouinia gibarensis

Thouinia gibarensis, commonly known as the Gibraltar rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Rock of Gibraltar.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to the rocky, coastal environment it inhabits.

Thouinia gibarensis is notable for its resilience to harsh conditions, making it a symbol of the unique flora of Gibraltar.
