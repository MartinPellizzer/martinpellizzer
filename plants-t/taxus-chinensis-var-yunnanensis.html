Taxus chinensis var. yunnanensis

Taxus chinensis var. yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan yew, is a rare and endangered conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, woody cones. The plant is notable for containing taxanes, a group of chemically complex compounds with significant medicinal value. It thrives in cool, moist mountainous regions and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its limited distribution and the demand for its medicinal compounds, it is heavily protected and requires conservation efforts.
