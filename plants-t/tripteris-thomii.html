Tripteris thomii

Tripteris thomii, commonly known as the Thomii's tripteris, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, reddish outer layer.

Tripteris thomii is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
