Taraxacum tujuksuense

Taraxacum tujuksuense, commonly known as the Tujuksu dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its presence in local ecosystems contributes to soil stabilization and provides food for pollinators.
