Thladiantha pustulata

Thladiantha pustulata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red, inflated bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Thladiantha pustulata is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
