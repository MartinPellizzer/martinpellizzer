Taraxacum azerbaijanicum

Taraxacum azerbaijanicum, commonly known as the Azerbaijani dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, particularly Azerbaijan, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its taproot system and the presence of latex in its tissues.
