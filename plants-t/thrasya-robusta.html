Thrasya robusta

Thrasya robusta, commonly known as the robusta thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, upright stems and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Thrasya robusta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
