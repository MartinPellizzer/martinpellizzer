Tripsacum peruvianum

Tripsacum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian tumblergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often drooping stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a rough texture.

Tripsacum peruvianum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
