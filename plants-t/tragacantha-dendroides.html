Tragacantha dendroides

Tragacantha dendroides, commonly known as the tree tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and branched structure, resembling a small tree or shrub. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its latex sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha dendroides is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
