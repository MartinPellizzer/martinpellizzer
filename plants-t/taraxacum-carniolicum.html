Taraxacum carniolicum

Taraxacum carniolicum, commonly known as the Carniolan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, wind-dispersed seed sphere. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in various soil conditions.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
