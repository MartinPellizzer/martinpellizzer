Tiarocarpus neubaueri

Tiarocarpus neubaueri, commonly known as the Neubauer's ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
