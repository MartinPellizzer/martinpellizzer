Trichocereus randallii

Trichocereus randallii, commonly known as the "Randall's torch cactus," is a species native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem covered in white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming at night. It thrives in arid, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
