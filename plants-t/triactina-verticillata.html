Triactina verticillata

Triactina verticillata, commonly known as the whorled triactina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves around the stem, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
