Thlaspi alpinum proles sylvium

Thlaspi alpinum proles sylvium, commonly known as the alpine cabbages or rock cabbages, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its rosette of lobed, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

Thlaspi alpinum proles sylvium is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
