Tinospora intermedia

Tinospora intermedia, commonly known as Indian moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
