Tillandsia augusta

Tillandsia augusta, commonly known as the silver vase bromeliad, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, which is used to collect rainwater. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Tillandsia augusta is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
