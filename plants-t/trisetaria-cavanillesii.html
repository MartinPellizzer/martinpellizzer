Trisetaria cavanillesii

Trisetaria cavanillesii, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Trisetaria cavanillesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
