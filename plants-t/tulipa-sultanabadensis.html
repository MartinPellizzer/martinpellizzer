Tulipa sultanabadensis

Tulipa sultanabadensis, commonly known as the Sultanabad tulip, is a species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy blooms in early spring.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
