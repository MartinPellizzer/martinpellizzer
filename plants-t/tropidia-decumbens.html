Tropidia decumbens

Tropidia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
