Trifolium cinctum

Trifolium cinctum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a narrow margin. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety.

Trifolium cinctum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
