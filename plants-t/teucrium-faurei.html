Teucrium faurei

Teucrium faurei, commonly known as the African germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Teucrium faurei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
