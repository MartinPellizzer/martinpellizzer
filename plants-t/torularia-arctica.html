Torularia arctica

Torularia arctica, commonly known as Arctic snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions.

It is a small herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a short growing season and is adapted to the harsh, cold environments of the Arctic tundra.

Torularia arctica plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
