Taccarum variabile var. multilobum

Taccarum variabile var. multilobum, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silver-blue needles and conical shape, with a broad, spreading crown. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates. It produces small, edible cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
