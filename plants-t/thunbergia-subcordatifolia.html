Thunbergia subcordatifolia

Thunbergia subcordatifolia, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Thunbergia subcordatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
