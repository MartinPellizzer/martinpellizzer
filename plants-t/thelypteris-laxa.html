Thelypteris laxa

Thelypteris laxa, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce a dense network of roots that help anchor it to the substrate.

Thelypteris laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
