Tragopogon tomentosulus

Tragopogon tomentosulus, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or disturbed soils. Its foliage is often covered in dense, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Tragopogon tomentosulus is also notable for its role in hybridization studies within the genus Tragopogon.
