Themeda triandra var. burchellii

Themeda triandra var. burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
