Taraxacum subcontristans

Taraxacum subcontristans, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces wind-dispersed seeds. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
