Taxotrophis

Taxotrophis, commonly known as the spiral moss, is a genus of liverworts characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves.

These plants are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The leaves are usually small, pointed, and arranged in a tight spiral around the stem. Taxotrophis species lack true roots and instead absorb nutrients through their leaf-like structures.

They play an important role in soil stabilization and as indicators of environmental health.
