Thelypteris appendiculata

Thelypteris appendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris appendiculata plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
