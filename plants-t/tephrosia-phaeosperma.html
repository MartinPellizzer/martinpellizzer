Tephrosia phaeosperma

Tephrosia phaeosperma, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Tephrosia phaeosperma is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
