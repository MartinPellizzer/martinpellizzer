Tricholaena filifolia

Tricholaena filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaf bamboo, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tricholaena filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
