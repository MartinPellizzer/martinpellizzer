Trichocereus herzogianus var. totorensis

Trichocereus herzogianus var. totorensis, commonly known as the Totora cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are often visited by nocturnal pollinators. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-adapted to harsh desert conditions.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various regions for its resilience and beauty.
