Tigridia versicolor

Tigridia versicolor, commonly known as the tiger flower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia versicolor is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
