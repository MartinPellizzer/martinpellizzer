Trichogyne lerouxiae

Trichogyne lerouxiae, commonly known as the le Roux's trichogyne, is a specialized structure found in certain fungal species, particularly those belonging to the order Hypocreales.

It is a hyphal projection that plays a crucial role in the process of fungal mating, acting as a receptive organ for compatible mating types. The morphology of the trichogyne is typically elongated and filamentous, with a distinct swelling at the base that facilitates the fusion of compatible gametes. This structure is essential for sexual reproduction in these fungi, enabling genetic recombination and the production of spores.

The presence and function of the trichogyne are key indicators of the mating system and genetic diversity within fungal populations.
