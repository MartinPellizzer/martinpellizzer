Thymus serpyllum subsp. caroli

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

caroli, commonly known as Carole's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically grey-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
