Timonius bougainvillensis

Timonius bougainvillensis, commonly known as the Bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and purple. The true flowers are small and inconspicuous, hidden within the colorful bracts. It is a member of the Nyctaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to different soil types.
