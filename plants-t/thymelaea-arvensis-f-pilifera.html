Thymelaea arvensis f. pilifera

Thymelaea arvensis f. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy thymelaea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive member of its genus, with the 'pilifera' subspecific name referring to its hairy appearance.
