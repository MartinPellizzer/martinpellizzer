Trifolium depauperatum var. stenophyllum

Trifolium depauperatum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
