Trifolium galilaeum

Trifolium galilaeum, commonly known as the Galilee clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring.

Trifolium galilaeum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
