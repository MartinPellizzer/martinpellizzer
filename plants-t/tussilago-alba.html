Tussilago alba

Tussilago alba, commonly known as white mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. White mullein thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.
