Thea macrophylla

Thea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf tea plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are used to make various types of tea, including black and oolong tea.

Thea macrophylla is valued for its aromatic properties and is widely cultivated for commercial tea production.
