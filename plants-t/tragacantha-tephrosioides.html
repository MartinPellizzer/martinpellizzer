Tragacantha tephrosioides

Tragacantha tephrosioides, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
