Trifolium clusii var. kahiricum

Trifolium clusii var. kahiricum, commonly known as the Kahiric clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
