Thalictrum occidentale var. macounii

Thalictrum occidentale var. macounii, commonly known as the Western meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is characterized by its slender stems and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

The botanical name reflects its classification within the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic characteristics.
