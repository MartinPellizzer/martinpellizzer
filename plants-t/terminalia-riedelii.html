Terminalia riedelii

Terminalia riedelii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, simple, ovate leaves that are alternate along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spherical clusters, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a drupe, typically yellow or red when mature, and contains a single seed.

Terminalia riedelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and showy floral display.
