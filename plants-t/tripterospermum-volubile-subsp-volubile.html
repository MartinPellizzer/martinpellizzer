Tripterospermum volubile subsp. volubile

Tripterospermum volubile subsp.

volubile, commonly known as the climbing evergreen hibiscus, is a species native to the eastern coast of Australia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically pink or red, with five lobes, and bloom in spring to summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens as a decorative climber or ground cover.
