Tropaeolum lechleri

Tropaeolum lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian morning glory, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used by indigenous communities for centuries.
