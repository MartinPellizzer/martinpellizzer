Tounatea cuspidata

Tounatea cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Tounatea cuspidata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
