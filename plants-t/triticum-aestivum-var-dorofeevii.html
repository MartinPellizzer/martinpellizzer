Triticum aestivum var. dorofeevii

Triticum aestivum var. dorofeevii, commonly known as the Dorofeevii wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic composition.

It is distinguished by its tall stature and high grain yield potential, making it suitable for various agricultural conditions. The plant exhibits a complex genome structure, which contributes to its adaptability and resilience to environmental stressors. Its botanical features include long, slender leaves and spikelets that bear three to four florets.

This variety is often studied for its potential in improving wheat breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and agronomic traits.
