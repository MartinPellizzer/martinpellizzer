Thelypteris eriosorus

Thelypteris eriosorus, commonly known as the downy wood fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are biennial, with the sterile fronds appearing in the first year and the fertile fronds in the second.

Thelypteris eriosorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
