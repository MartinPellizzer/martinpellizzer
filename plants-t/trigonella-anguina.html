Trigonella anguina

Trigonella anguina, commonly known as the desert fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a low-growing habit.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in harsh environments.
