Tradescantia longifolia

Tradescantia longifolia, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
