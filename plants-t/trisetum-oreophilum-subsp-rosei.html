Trisetum oreophilum subsp. rosei

Trisetum oreophilum subsp.

rosei, commonly known as the rose-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or pinkish hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Trisetum oreophilum subsp.

rosei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine habitats.
