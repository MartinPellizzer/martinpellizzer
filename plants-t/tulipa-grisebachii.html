Tulipa grisebachii

Tulipa grisebachii, commonly known as the Gray's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grayish-green leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically blooms in late spring and is known for its cold hardiness. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Tulipa grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
