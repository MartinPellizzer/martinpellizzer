Tithymalus cotinifolius

Tithymalus cotinifolius, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to wetland restoration projects and native plant gardens.
