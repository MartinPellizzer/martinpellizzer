Terminalia mameluco

Terminalia mameluco, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *mamelo*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, though its flavor is often described as sour or acidic.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
