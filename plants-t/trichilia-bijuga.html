Trichilia bijuga

Trichilia bijuga, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves with a heart-shaped base. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
