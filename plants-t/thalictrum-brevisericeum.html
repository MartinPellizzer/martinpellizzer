Thalictrum brevisericeum

Thalictrum brevisericeum, commonly known as the short-haired meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system, which helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.
