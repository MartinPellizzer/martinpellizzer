Trichilia bullata

Trichilia bullata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its seeds are valued for their oil, which is used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
