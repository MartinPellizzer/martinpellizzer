Tricalysia dualaensis

Tricalysia dualaensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its showy appearance.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in warmer climates.
