Tribulus persicus

Tribulus persicus, commonly known as Persian cuscuta, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can be a problematic weed in agricultural settings.
