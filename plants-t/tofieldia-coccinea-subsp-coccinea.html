Tofieldia coccinea subsp. coccinea

Tofieldia coccinea subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the redflower tofieldia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and roadside ditches.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red-orange flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence is a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

The subspecies is characterized by its vibrant floral color and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
