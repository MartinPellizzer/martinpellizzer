Trichocereus poco var. fricianus

Trichocereus poco var. fricianus, commonly known as the "Fricianus cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, a feature typical of many cacti in the night-blooming family. Its areoles are densely covered with white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
