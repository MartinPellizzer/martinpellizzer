Tradescantia llamasii

Tradescantia llamasii, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue hue and waxy texture, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Tradescantia llamasii is also noted for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats that help suppress weeds.
