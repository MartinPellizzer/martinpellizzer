Taraxacum pseudoboreigenum

Taraxacum pseudoboreigenum, commonly known as the Siberian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant produces a distinctive hollow stem with milky sap, and its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
