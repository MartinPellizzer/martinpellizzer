Taraxacum praeradiantifrons

Taraxacum praeradiantifrons, commonly known as the radiant-faced dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in open, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces a distinctive white, fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of the genus Taraxacum's adaptability and ecological significance.
