Thymelaea pubescens subsp. elliptica

Thymelaea pubescens subsp.

elliptica, commonly known as the hairy thymelaea, is a small shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
