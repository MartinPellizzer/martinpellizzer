Tacsonia mollissima var. glabrescens

Tacsonia mollissima var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or riverbanks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silky texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
