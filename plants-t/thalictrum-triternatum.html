Thalictrum triternatum

Thalictrum triternatum, commonly known as the common meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are trifoliate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Thalictrum triternatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
