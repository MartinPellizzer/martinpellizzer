Themeda cymbaria

Themeda cymbaria, commonly known as the small-flowered grass or slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, erect leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Themeda cymbaria plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
