Talinum triangulare var. purpureum

Talinum triangulare var. purpureum, commonly known as purple leaf Talinum, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Talinum triangulare var. purpureum is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in some traditional cuisines.
