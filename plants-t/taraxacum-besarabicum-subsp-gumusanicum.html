Taraxacum besarabicum subsp. gumusanicum

Taraxacum besarabicum subsp.

gumusanicum, commonly known as the Gumusanic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface, and its flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
