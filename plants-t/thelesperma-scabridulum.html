Thelesperma scabridulum

Thelesperma scabridulum, commonly known as the roughleaf milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, narrow leaves with a rough texture, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Thelesperma scabridulum is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
