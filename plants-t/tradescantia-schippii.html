Tradescantia schippii

Tradescantia schippii, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that produce small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with distinctive red veins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
