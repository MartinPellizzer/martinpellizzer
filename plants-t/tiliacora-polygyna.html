Tiliacora polygyna

Tiliacora polygyna, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
