Toxicodendron succedaneum var. acuminatum

Toxicodendron succedaneum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is native to Europe, Asia, and North America. It is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing skin rashes and itching.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woods, and along roadsides.
