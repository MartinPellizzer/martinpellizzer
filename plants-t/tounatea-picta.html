Tounatea picta

Tounatea picta, commonly known as the painted tounatea, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display a variety of shades, including red, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Tounatea picta typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens, though it requires well-draining soil and moderate sunlight to thrive.
