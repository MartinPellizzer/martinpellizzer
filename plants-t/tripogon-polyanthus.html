Tripogon polyanthus

Tripogon polyanthus, commonly known as the showy panic grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often brightly colored. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical structure is typical of the Poaceae family, with a rhizomatous root system and spikelet-bearing stems.
