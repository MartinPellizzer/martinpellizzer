Teucrium littorale

Teucrium littorale, commonly known as sea germander, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and range in color from white to pink, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Sea germander is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
