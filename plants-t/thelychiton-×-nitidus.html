Thelychiton × nitidus

Thelychiton × nitidus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Thelychiton marmoratus and Thelychiton nitidus.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically exhibit a combination of white and purple hues. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
