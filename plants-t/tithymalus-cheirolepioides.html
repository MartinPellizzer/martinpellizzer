Tithymalus cheirolepioides

Tithymalus cheirolepioides, commonly known as the mountain tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Tithymalus cheirolepioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
