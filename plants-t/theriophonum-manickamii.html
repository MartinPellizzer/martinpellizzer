Theriophonum manickamii

Theriophonum manickamii, commonly known as the Manickamii ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the biodiverse regions of India.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant has a unique floral structure with elongated, spiral-shaped petals that are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
