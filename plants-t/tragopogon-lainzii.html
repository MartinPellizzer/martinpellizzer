Tragopogon lainzii

Tragopogon lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that develop into bristly seed heads. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Tragopogon lainzii is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
