Thelocactus buekii

Thelocactus buekii, commonly known as the Buekii cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a single, large, spiny areole at the top, which bears the plant's distinctive flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the summer months. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its structure and growth habits make it well-adapted to extreme desert conditions.
