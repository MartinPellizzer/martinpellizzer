Taraxacum subdilatatum

Taraxacum subdilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
