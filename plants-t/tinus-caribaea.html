Tinus caribaea

Tinus caribaea, commonly known as the mahogany tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is highly sought after in the furniture and construction industries.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
