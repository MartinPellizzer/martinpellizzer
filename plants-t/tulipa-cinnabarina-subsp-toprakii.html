Tulipa cinnabarina subsp. toprakii

Tulipa cinnabarina subsp.

toprakii, commonly known as the Turkish red tulip, is a subspecies native to Turkey. It is characterized by its striking crimson flowers with a distinctive orange-red stripe down the central petal.

The plant typically grows to a height of 20-30 cm and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
