Teucrium montbretii subsp. heliotropiifolium

Teucrium montbretii subsp.

heliotropiifolium, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
