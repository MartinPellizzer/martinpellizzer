Tetranthera panamanja

Tetranthera panamanja, commonly known as Panama's spider plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to Central and South America.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to tropical gardens and greenhouses.
