Thryptomene flaviflora

Thryptomene flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered thryptomene, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands or open forests. Its flowers are insect-pollinated, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Thryptomene flaviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
