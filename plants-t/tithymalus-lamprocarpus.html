Tithymalus lamprocarpus

Tithymalus lamprocarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
