Tormentilla fragaroides

Tormentilla fragaroides, commonly known as the fragrant tormentill, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
