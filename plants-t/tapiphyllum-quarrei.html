Tapiphyllum quarrei

Tapiphyllum quarrei, commonly known as the ancient cycad, is a prehistoric plant that belongs to the order Cycadales.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and a stout, unbranched stem. This plant thrived during the Mesozoic era and is considered a relic of the ancient flora. Its botanical features include a thick, woody trunk and compound leaves with a distinct midrib.

Tapiphyllum quarrei is of significant interest to paleobotanists due to its evolutionary significance and ecological role in ancient ecosystems.
