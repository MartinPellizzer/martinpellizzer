Triticum speltoides subsp. polyatherum

Triticum speltoides subsp.

polyatherum, commonly known as the wild einkorn wheat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with awned lemmas, typical of the tribe Avenae. Its seeds are enclosed in glumes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.
