Ternstroemia globiflora

Ternstroemia globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Ternstroemia globiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
