Thymus includens

Thymus includens, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus includens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
