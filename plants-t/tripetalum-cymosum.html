Tripetalum cymosum

Tripetalum cymosum, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cymes, which are flat-topped clusters. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to conserve water.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and colorful blooms.
