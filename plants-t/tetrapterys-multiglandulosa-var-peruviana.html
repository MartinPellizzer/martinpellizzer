Tetrapterys multiglandulosa var. peruviana

Tetrapterys multiglandulosa var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian glandular tetrapterys, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves typically have three to five pairs of leaflets, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
