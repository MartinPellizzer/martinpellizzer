Tithymalus pamiricus

Tithymalus pamiricus, commonly known as the Pamir tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
