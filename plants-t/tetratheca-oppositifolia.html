Tetratheca oppositifolia

Tetratheca oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems. The leaves are opposite, linear to lanceolate, and have a distinctive narrow shape with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or cream-colored, and are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
