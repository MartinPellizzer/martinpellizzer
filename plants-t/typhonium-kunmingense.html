Typhonium kunmingense

Typhonium kunmingense, commonly known as the Kunming arrowhead, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of berries.

Typhonium kunmingense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
