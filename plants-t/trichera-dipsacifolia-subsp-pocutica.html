Trichera dipsacifolia subsp. pocutica

Trichera dipsacifolia subsp.

pocutica, commonly known as the Pocutica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
