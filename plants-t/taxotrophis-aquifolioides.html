Taxotrophis aquifolioides

Taxotrophis aquifolioides, commonly known as the glossy leaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

It features compound leaves that are typically glossy and leathery, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked, followed by clustered, spore-bearing structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical structure supports its role as an epiphytic plant, growing on other plants without parasitizing them.
