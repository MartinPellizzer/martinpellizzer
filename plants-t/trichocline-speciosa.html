Trichocline speciosa

Trichocline speciosa, commonly known as the showy trichocline, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
