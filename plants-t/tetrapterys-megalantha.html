Tetrapterys megalantha

Tetrapterys megalantha, commonly known as the giant leaf tetrapterys, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tetrapterys megalantha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
