Tanacetum orientale

Tanacetum orientale, commonly known as the oriental tansy, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Tanacetum orientale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Its foliage emits a strong, camphor-like scent, which contributes to its historical use in repelling insects and as a remedy for digestive ailments.
