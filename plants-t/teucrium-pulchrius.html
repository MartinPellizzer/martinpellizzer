Teucrium pulchrius

Teucrium pulchrius, commonly known as the pretty germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender.

Teucrium pulchrius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
