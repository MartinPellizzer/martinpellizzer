Trixis verbascifolia

Trixis verbascifolia, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its fibrous roots help it survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Trixis verbascifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
