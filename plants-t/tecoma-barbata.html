Tecoma barbata

Tecoma barbata, commonly known as the barbata glorybower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma barbata is known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
