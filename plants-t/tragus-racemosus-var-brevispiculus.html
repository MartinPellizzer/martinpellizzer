Tragus racemosus var. brevispiculus

Tragus racemosus var. brevispiculus, commonly known as the short-spurred tragus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with violet or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
