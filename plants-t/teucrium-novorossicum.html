Teucrium novorossicum

Teucrium novorossicum, commonly known as the Novorossiysky germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct minty scent. Its flowers are typically small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from purple to blue.

Teucrium novorossicum is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
