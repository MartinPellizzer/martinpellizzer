Thesium umbrosum

Thesium umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy thesium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Thesium umbrosum is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.
