Thymus serpyllum var. ortmannianus

Thymus serpyllum var. ortmannianus, commonly known as the ortmann's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
