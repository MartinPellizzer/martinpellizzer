Tephrosia obbiadensis

Tephrosia obbiadensis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Tephrosia obbiadensis is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
