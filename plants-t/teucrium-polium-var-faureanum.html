Teucrium polium var. faureanum

Teucrium polium var. faureanum, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
