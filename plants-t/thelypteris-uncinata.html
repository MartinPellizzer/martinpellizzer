Thelypteris uncinata

Thelypteris uncinata, commonly known as the hooked maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with finely divided pinnae and serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, dark green leaves.

Thelypteris uncinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
