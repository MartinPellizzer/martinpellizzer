Trigonella pubescens

Trigonella pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Trigonella pubescens is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
