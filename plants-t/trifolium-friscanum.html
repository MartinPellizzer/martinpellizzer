Trifolium friscanum

Trifolium friscanum, commonly known as the Friscan clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Trifolium friscanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing ability and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
