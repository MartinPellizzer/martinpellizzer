Tamarix libanotica

Tamarix libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, scale-like leaves and produces small, pink or reddish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and stabilize sandy or coastal areas.

Tamarix libanotica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to prevent soil erosion.
