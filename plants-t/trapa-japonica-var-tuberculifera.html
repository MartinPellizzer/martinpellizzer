Trapa japonica var. tuberculifera

Trapa japonica var. tuberculifera, commonly known as the Japanese water chestnut, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its floating, spade-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense stands in ponds and marshes. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used in Asian cuisine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
