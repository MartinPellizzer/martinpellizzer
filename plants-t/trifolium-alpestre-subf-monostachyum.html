Trifolium alpestre subf. monostachyum

Trifolium alpestre subf.

monostachyum, commonly known as the alpine clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
