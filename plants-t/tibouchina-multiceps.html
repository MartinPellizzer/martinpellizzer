Tibouchina multiceps

Tibouchina multiceps, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or red tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
