Tragacantha dictyophysa

Tragacantha dictyophysa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Tragacantha dictyophysa is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
