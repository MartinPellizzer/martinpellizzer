Trifolium arachnoideum

Trifolium arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves that are covered with fine, web-like hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
