Thaumatocaryon hilarii

Thaumatocaryon hilarii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that produces large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant's leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and branched. The flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths, and the plant is known for its dramatic blooming pattern.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal display.
