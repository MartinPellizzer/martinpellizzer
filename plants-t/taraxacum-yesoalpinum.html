Taraxacum yesoalpinum

Taraxacum yesoalpinum, commonly known as the Yesso alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are surrounded by bracts.

Taraxacum yesoalpinum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in regions with harsh environmental conditions.
