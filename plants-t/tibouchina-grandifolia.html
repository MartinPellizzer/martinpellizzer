Tibouchina grandifolia

Tibouchina grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a glossy texture. The plant produces showy clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Tibouchina grandifolia is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
