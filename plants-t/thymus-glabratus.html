Thymus glabratus

Thymus glabratus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils.

Thymus glabratus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
