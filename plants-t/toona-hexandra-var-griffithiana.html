Toona hexandra var. griffithiana

Toona hexandra var. griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian cedar or Himalayan cedar, is a member of the Moraceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lanceolate, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in traditional construction and craftsmanship.

The plant thrives in temperate to subtropical climates, often found in mountainous regions of Asia.
