Tabebuia alba

Tabebuia alba, commonly known as the white angelfish tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

The wood of Tabebuia alba is hard and durable, historically used for construction and furniture.
