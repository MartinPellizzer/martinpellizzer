Thryptomene ciliata

Thryptomene ciliata, commonly known as the silky thryptomene, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in spring. Thryptomene ciliata is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
