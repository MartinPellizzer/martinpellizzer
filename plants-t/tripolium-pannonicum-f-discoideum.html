Tripolium pannonicum f. discoideum

Tripolium pannonicum f. discoideum, commonly known as the pan-European flatweed, is a botanical variant of the species Tripolium pannonicum.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its prostrate habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils, such as those found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrowly linear and often have a distinct, wavy margin.

This form is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in ecological succession.
