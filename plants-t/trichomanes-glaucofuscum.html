Trichomanes glaucofuscum

Trichomanes glaucofuscum, commonly known as the grayish-brown huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, linear, and often clustered leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in structures called sori, which are usually located on the underside of the leaves.

Trichomanes glaucofuscum is notable for its slow growth and preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
