Tapirira mexicana

Tapirira mexicana, commonly known as Mexican tapirira, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Tapirira mexicana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
