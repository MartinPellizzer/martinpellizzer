Thecophyllum sceptrum

Thecophyllum sceptrum, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a unique member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that emerge from a central stem, with vibrant red and orange bracts that resemble a torch. The plant's foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Thecophyllum sceptrum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices.
