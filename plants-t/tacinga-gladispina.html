Tacinga gladispina

Tacinga gladispina, commonly known as the gladis spinecactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent structure allows it to store moisture efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Tacinga gladispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
