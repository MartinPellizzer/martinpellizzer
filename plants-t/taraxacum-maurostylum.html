Taraxacum maurostylum

Taraxacum maurostylum, commonly known as the Maurostylum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and a single, elongated flowering stem that bears a crown of yellow flowers. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of hollow, hair-like structures that aid in wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
