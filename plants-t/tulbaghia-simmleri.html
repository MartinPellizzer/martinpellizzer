Tulbaghia simmleri

Tulbaghia simmleri, commonly known as the star jelly plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, star-shaped white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tulbaghia simmleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild toxicity to herbivores.
