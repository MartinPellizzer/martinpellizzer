Trillium apetalon f. album

Trillium apetalon f. album, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three large, white, broadly ovate leaves that emerge from a single stem. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rich, moist soils and is often found in deciduous forests.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
