Trifolium alexandrinum var. tuberculatum

Trifolium alexandrinum var. tuberculatum, commonly known as the tuberculate clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
