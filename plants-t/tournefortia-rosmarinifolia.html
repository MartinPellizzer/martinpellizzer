Tournefortia rosmarinifolia

Tournefortia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical classification places it in the family Oleaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like jasmine and lilac.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
