Tanarius secundus

Tanarius secundus, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies over time.

The leaves are bioluminescent at night, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
