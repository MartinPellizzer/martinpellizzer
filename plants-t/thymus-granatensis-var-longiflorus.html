Thymus granatensis var. longiflorus

Thymus granatensis var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered granate thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, low-growing stems covered in small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to its strong, aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and Mediterranean-style landscapes.
