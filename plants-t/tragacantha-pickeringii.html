Tragacantha pickeringii

Tragacantha pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

The plant's roots can fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
