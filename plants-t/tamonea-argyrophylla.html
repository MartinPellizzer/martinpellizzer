Tamonea argyrophylla

Tamonea argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
