Trifolium odoratum

Trifolium odoratum, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils.

Sweet clover is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
