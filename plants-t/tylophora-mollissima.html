Tylophora mollissima

Tylophora mollissima, commonly known as the silky tylophora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
