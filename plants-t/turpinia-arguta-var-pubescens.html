Turpinia arguta var. pubescens

Turpinia arguta var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy turpinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a reddish color, providing food for various wildlife species.
