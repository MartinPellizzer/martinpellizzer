Tanarius pruinosus

Tanarius pruinosus, commonly known as the glossy tanarius, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
