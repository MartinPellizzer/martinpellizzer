Thermopsis macrophylla var. semota

Thermopsis macrophylla var. semota, commonly known as the broad-leaved yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy texture, and tall, branching stems that support its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
