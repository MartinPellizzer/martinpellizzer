Trifolium longipedunculatum

Trifolium longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long peduncle clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in elongated racemes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium longipedunculatum is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
