Trachelium caeruleum subsp. lanceolatum

Trachelium caeruleum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that appear in dense clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
