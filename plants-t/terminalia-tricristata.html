Terminalia tricristata

Terminalia tricristata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and long, slender leaves. The tree produces small, white to yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, though it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
