Triticum repens var. semiverticillatum

Triticum repens var. semiverticillatum, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and has been cultivated for its potential in forage and ecological restoration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
