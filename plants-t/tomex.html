Tomex

Tomex, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical name of Tomex is Handroanthus impetiginosus.
