Tilia × europaea var. parviflora

Tilia × europaea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lime, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that appear in early summer.

It is a deciduous tree with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

Its flowers provide nectar for pollinators, making it beneficial to local ecosystems.
