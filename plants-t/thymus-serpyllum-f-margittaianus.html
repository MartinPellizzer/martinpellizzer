Thymus serpyllum f. margittaianus

Thymus serpyllum f. margittaianus, commonly known as the Margitaian thyme, is a botanical variant of the common thyme.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and pinkish-white flowers. This subspecies is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its compact growth and attractive foliage.
