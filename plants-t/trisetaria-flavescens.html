Trisetaria flavescens

Trisetaria flavescens, commonly known as the yellow tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and bright yellow-green foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. Trisetaria flavescens thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
