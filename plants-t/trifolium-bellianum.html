Trifolium bellianum

Trifolium bellianum, commonly known as the bell-shaped clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Trifolium bellianum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
