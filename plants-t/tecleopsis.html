Tecleopsis

Tecleopsis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Tecleopsis species are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their ornamental value. They prefer bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

The genus includes several species, such as Tecleopsis multiflora, which is prized for its compact growth and vibrant blooms.
