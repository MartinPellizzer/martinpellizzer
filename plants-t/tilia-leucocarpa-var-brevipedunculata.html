Tilia leucocarpa var. brevipedunculata

Tilia leucocarpa var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the white-barked maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by winged samaras.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
