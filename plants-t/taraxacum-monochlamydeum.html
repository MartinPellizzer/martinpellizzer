Taraxacum monochlamydeum

Taraxacum monochlamydeum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which mature into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
