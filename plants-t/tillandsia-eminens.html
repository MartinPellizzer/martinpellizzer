Tillandsia eminens

Tillandsia eminens, commonly known as the highland air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves is adapted to capture moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Tillandsia eminens is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and unique appearance.
