Typha angustata subsp. aethiopica

Typha angustata subsp.

aethiopica, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical flower heads that produce fluffy seeds.

The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its environment.
