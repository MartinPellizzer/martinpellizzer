Trifolium amabile var. hemsleyi

Trifolium amabile var. hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
