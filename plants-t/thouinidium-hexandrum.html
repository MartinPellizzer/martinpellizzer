Thouinidium hexandrum

Thouinidium hexandrum, commonly known as the six-stamen orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Thouinidium hexandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
