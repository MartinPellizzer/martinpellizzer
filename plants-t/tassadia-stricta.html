Tassadia stricta

Tassadia stricta, commonly known as the rigid tassadia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. Tassadia stricta is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent-like leaves.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
