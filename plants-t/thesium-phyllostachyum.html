Thesium phyllostachyum

Thesium phyllostachyum, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Thesium phyllostachyum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
