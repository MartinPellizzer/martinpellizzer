Teucrium polium subsp. aragonense

Teucrium polium subsp.

aragonense, commonly known as Spanish germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
