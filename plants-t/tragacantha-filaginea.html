Tragacantha filaginea

Tragacantha filaginea, commonly known as the silky tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a gelatinous resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Tragacantha filaginea typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and traditional botanical studies.
