Tamarix polystachya

Tamarix polystachya, commonly known as the weeping tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces woody, branching stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

Tamarix polystachya is also noted for its ability to tolerate saline and arid conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
