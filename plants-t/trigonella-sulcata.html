Trigonella sulcata

Trigonella sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical pods. Trigonella sulcata is characterized by its distinctive, wavy or zigzag pattern on the stems and leaves.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
