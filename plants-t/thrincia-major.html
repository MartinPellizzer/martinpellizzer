Thrincia major

Thrincia major, commonly known as the giant thrincia, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a robust, woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Thrincia major thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
