Tricalysia longituba subsp. richardsiae

Tricalysia longituba subsp.

richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
