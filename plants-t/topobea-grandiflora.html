Topobea grandiflora

Topobea grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora topobea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens with adequate drainage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
