Tibouchina versicolor

Tibouchina versicolor, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that can range in color from pink to red, purple, and white, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or white, surrounded by the colorful bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Tibouchina versicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
