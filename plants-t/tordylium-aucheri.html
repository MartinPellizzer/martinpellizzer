Tordylium aucheri

Tordylium aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tordylium aucheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
