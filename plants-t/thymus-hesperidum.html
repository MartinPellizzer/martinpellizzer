Thymus hesperidum

Thymus hesperidum, commonly known as desert thyme, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and produces small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that contain essential oils, making it valuable in herbal medicine and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Its ability to tolerate drought conditions makes it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
