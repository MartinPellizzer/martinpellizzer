Tetraperone

Tetraperone, commonly known as the "garden orchid," is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces a unique, fragrant scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tetraperone thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
