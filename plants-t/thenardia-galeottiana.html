Thenardia galeottiana

Thenardia galeottiana, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
