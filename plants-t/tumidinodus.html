Tumidinodus

Tumidinodus, commonly known as the bladder nut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy fruits that resemble bladders, which contain a single seed. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in panicles, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
