Tinospora tomentosa

Tinospora tomentosa, commonly known as Indian glorybower, is a climbing shrub native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
