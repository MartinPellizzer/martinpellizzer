Tagetes linifolia

Tagetes linifolia, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon or golden marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Tagetes linifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its essential oils are often used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
