Tithymalus cressoides

Tithymalus cressoides, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand harsh mountain environments.
