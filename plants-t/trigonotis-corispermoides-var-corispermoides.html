Trigonotis corispermoides var. corispermoides

Trigonotis corispermoides var. corispermoides, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
