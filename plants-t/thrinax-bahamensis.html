Thrinax bahamensis

Thrinax bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is a small, low-growing palm with a short trunk and a dense crown of feathery leaves. The plant has a unique ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments, making it well-adapted to salt spray and wind. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

The plant's resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a valued species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
