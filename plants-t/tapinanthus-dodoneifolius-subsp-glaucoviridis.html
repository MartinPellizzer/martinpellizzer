Tapinanthus dodoneifolius subsp. glaucoviridis

Tapinanthus dodoneifolius subsp.

glaucoviridis, commonly known as the glaucous leaf dodder, is a hemiparasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a complex life cycle involving seed dormancy and germination triggered by chemical signals from host roots.

The subspecies is characterized by its glaucous, waxy coating on the leaves, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It typically infests a variety of host plants, including shrubs and trees, in its native range of southern Africa.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host species.
