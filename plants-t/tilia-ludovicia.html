Tilia ludovicia

Tilia ludovicia, commonly known as the European linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia ludovicia is often valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in various cultures.
