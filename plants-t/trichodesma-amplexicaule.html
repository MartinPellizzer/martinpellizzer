Trichodesma amplexicaule

Trichodesma amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping trichodesma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that often clasp the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Trichodesma amplexicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
