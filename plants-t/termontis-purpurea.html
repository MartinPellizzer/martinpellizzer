Termontis purpurea

Termontis purpurea, commonly known as the purple termontis, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet-purple petals and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Termontis purpurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
