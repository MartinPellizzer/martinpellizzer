Trichogyne

Trichogyne, commonly known as a specialized structure in certain fungi, is a receptive organ found on the female gametophyte.

It plays a crucial role in the process of fertilization by recognizing and binding to the male gametes. This structure is typically a long, slender tube that extends from the base of the sporangium. Trichogyne is most notably associated with the genus *Ustilago*, where it facilitates sexual reproduction.

Its presence is a key feature in the life cycle of these fungi, enabling genetic diversity through mating.
