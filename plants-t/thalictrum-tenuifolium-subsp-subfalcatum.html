Thalictrum tenuifolium subsp. subfalcatum

Thalictrum tenuifolium subsp.

subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are arranged in elongated racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
