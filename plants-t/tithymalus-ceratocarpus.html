Tithymalus ceratocarpus

Tithymalus ceratocarpus, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals and distinctive, horn-like seed capsules that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
