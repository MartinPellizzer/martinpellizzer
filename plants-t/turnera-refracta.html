Turnera refracta

Turnera refracta, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, which includes other well-known species like passionflowers. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Turnera refracta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
