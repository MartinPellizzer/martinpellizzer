Tagetes remotiflora

Tagetes remotiflora, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in herbal medicine.
