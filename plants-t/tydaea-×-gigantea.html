Tydaea × gigantea

Tydaea × gigantea, commonly known as the giant reed, is a hybrid plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is a tall, grass-like perennial that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
