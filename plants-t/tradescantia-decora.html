Tradescantia decora

Tradescantia decora, commonly known as the silver sword or mother of thousands, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is a member of the Commelinaceae family and is known for its ability to propagate easily through both seeds and leaf cuttings.

Tradescantia decora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
