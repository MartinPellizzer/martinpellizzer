Tolpis canariensis

Tolpis canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid climate of its native region.

Tolpis canariensis is often found in coastal areas and is considered a characteristic species of the Canary Islands' flora.
