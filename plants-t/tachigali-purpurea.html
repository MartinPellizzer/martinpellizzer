Tachigali purpurea

Tachigali purpurea, commonly known as the purple tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Tachigali purpurea is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
