Tanacetum tadshikorum

Tanacetum tadshikorum, commonly known as the Tadshikorum tansy, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent, which is a common trait among members of the Tanacetum genus.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are yellow and are arranged in flat-topped clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
