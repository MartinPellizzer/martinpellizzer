Tulipa armena var. galatica

Tulipa armena var. galatica, commonly known as the Armenian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
