Tetratheca ericifolia var. pilosa

Tetratheca ericifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The hairy texture of its leaves is a key identifying feature, as well as its compact growth habit.
