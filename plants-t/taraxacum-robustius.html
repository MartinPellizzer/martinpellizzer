Taraxacum robustius

Taraxacum robustius, commonly known as the robust dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that develops into a spherical, fluffy seed head known as a pappus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum robustius is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
