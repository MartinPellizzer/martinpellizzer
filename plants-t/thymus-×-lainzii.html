Thymus × lainzii

Thymus × lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Thymus × lainzii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
