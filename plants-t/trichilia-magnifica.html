Trichilia magnifica

Trichilia magnifica, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is valued for its heavy, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Trichilia magnifica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
