Telephium imperati subsp. orientale

Telephium imperati subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
