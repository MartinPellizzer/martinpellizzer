Tragacantha amalecitana

Tragacantha amalecitana, commonly known as the Amalecite tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and pod-like fruits. The plant produces a gelatinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
