Thinouia coriacea

Thinouia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its rigid, grass-like leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense clusters. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Thinouia coriacea plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
