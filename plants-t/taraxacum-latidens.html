Taraxacum latidens

Taraxacum latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
