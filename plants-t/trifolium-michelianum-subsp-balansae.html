Trifolium michelianum subsp. balansae

Trifolium michelianum subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Micheli's clover, is a flowering plant species native to South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
