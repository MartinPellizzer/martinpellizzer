Trachyspermum ammi

Trachyspermum ammi, commonly known as bishop's weed, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, aromatic seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are pinnately divided and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Trachyspermum ammi is also valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like limonene and carvone.
