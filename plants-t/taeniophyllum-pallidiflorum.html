Taeniophyllum pallidiflorum

Taeniophyllum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant has a slender, leafless stem with a single or few leaves, and its flowers are pale green to white in color. It produces small, delicate flowers that are often clustered at the base of the stem.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
