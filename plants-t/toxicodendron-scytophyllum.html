Toxicodendron scytophyllum

Toxicodendron scytophyllum, commonly known as the red root or redroot poison ivy, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, often with a reddish tint on the underside. Its toxic sap contains urushiol, which can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

It is often mistaken for other toxic plants in the genus Toxicodendron.
