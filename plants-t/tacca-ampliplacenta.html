Tacca ampliplacenta

Tacca ampliplacenta, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces unique, ornamental flowers with a distinctive, curling structure resembling a bat's wings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

Tacca ampliplacenta is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and unusual floral display.
