Trema orientale var. argenteum

Trema orientale var. argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf trema, is a variant of the Trema orientale species, characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on the foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy, waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
