Trianthema corallicola

Trianthema corallicola, commonly known as the coral bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
