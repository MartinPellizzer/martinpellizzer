Thymus nummularius var. pubescens

Thymus nummularius var. pubescens, commonly known as silver thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, rounded, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
