Tulipa neglecta

Tulipa neglecta, commonly known as the wild tulip or the mountain tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Tulipa neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
