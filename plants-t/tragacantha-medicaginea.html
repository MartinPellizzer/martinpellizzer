Tragacantha medicaginea

Tragacantha medicaginea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Iran and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a resin known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Tragacantha medicaginea is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
