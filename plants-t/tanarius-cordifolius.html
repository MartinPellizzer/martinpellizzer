Tanarius cordifolius

Tanarius cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaf tanarius, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
