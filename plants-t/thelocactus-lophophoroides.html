Thelocactus lophophoroides

Thelocactus lophophoroides, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive reddish-brown stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is adapted to arid environments.

The cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
