Tragacantha olginii

Tragacantha olginii, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is harvested for its use in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various insects and birds in its native environment.
