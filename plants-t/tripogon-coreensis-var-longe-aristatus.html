Tripogon coreensis var. longe-aristatus

Tripogon coreensis var. longe-aristatus, commonly known as the long-awned tripogon, is a grass species native to Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive awns that extend from the lemma and palea. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often drooping.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important subject in the study of grassland flora and ecological succession.
