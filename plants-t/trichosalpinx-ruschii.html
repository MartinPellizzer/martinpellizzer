Trichosalpinx ruschii

Trichosalpinx ruschii, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Trichosalpinx ruschii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
