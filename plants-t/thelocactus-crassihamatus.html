Thelocactus crassihamatus

Thelocactus crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed locactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture quickly during rare rainfall events.

Thelocactus crassihamatus is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
