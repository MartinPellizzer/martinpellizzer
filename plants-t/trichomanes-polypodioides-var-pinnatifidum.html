Trichomanes polypodioides var. pinnatifidum

Trichomanes polypodioides var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

The varietal form pinnatifidum is distinguished by its narrow, deeply split leaflets.
