Thuja occidentalis f. alba

Thuja occidentalis f. alba, commonly known as the white thuja or white eastern arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white foliage, which contrasts with the typical green of the species. This form is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and compact growth habit. The plant retains its foliage year-round, providing evergreen coverage in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
