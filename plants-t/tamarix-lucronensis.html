Tamarix lucronensis

Tamarix lucronensis, commonly known as the Persian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and fuel.

Tamarix lucronensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
