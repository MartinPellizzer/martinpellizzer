Thalictrum angustifolium var. cnidioides

Thalictrum angustifolium var. cnidioides, commonly known as narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
