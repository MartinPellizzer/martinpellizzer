Tropaeolum subincrassatum

Tropaeolum subincrassatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in temperate climates. Its flowers are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
