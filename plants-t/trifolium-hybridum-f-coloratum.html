Trifolium hybridum f. coloratum

Trifolium hybridum f. coloratum, commonly known as the red clover.

This plant is a cultivated variant of red clover, characterized by its distinctive red flowers and trifoliate leaves. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
