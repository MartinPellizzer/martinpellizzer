Trisetum subspicatum var. laxius

Trisetum subspicatum var. laxius, commonly known as the loose spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that are loosely arranged. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
