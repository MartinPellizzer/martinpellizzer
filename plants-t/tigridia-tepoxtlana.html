Tigridia tepoxtlana

Tigridia tepoxtlana, commonly known as the Mexican tiger flower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful, and showy flowers that resemble those of the tiger lily. The plant produces erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia tepoxtlana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible bulbs.
