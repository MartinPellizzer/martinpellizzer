Trifolium scabrum subsp. turcicum

Trifolium scabrum subsp.

turcicum, commonly known as Turkish clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
