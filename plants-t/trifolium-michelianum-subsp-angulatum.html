Trifolium michelianum subsp. angulatum

Trifolium michelianum subsp.

angulatum, commonly known as the angular trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a distinct angular appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
