Trochera ramosa

Trochera ramosa, commonly known as the branched broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Trochera ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
