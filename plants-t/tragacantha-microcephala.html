Tragacantha microcephala

Tragacantha microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, globular seed pods. It is valued for its latex, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
