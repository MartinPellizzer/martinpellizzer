Teucrium botrys var. albiflorum

Teucrium botrys var. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and aromatic, with a slightly minty scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
