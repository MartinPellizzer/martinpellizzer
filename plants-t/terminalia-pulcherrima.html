Terminalia pulcherrima

Terminalia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

Terminalia pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
