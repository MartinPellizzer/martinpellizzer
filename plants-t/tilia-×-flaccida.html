Tilia × flaccida

Tilia × flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by oval, woody fruits.

It is typically found in moist, shaded woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
