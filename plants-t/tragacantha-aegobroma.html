Tragacantha aegobroma

Tragacantha aegobroma, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a resinous substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
