Triadenum japonicum

Triadenum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese triadenum, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, triangular leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer.

Triadenum japonicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
