Tonningia nodiflora

Tonningia nodiflora, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells, often with white or pale pink petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in botanical gardens and a symbol of biodiversity in its native habitats.
