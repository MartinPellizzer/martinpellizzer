Trichomanes apicilare

Trichomanes apicilare, commonly known as the golden huperzia, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in two rows. The plant produces sporangia in clusters at the tips of its branches, which are often covered by a protective shield. Trichomanes apicilare is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
