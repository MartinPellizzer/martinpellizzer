Tricalysia elliottii var. centrafricana

Tricalysia elliottii var. centrafricana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
