Thelecarpus batangae

Thelecarpus batangae, commonly known as the Batang bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Thelecarpus batangae thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.
