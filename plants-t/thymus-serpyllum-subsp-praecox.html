Thymus serpyllum subsp. praecox

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ground cover in temperate climates.
