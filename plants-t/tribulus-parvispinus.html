Tribulus parvispinus

Tribulus parvispinus, commonly known as the small-spined tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, spiny fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tribulus parvispinus is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often found in coastal regions.
