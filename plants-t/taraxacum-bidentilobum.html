Taraxacum bidentilobum

Taraxacum bidentilobum, commonly known as the two-toothed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
