Taxillus limprichtii var. ritozanensis

Taxillus limprichtii var. ritozanensis, commonly known as the Ritozanese mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and white, sticky berries that contain the seeds. The plant relies entirely on its host for nutrients, forming a symbiotic relationship that can weaken the host over time.
