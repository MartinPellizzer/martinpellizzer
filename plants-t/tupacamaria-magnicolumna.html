Tupacamaria magnicolumna

Tupacamaria magnicolumna, commonly known as the giant columnar cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming a dense, columnar structure. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its green, spiny body. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for water storage, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert environments.

The species is also notable for its slow growth rate and longevity, with some individuals living over 100 years.
