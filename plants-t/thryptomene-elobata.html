Thryptomene elobata

Thryptomene elobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Thryptomene elobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
