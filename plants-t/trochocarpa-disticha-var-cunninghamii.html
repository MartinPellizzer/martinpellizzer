Trochocarpa disticha var. cunninghamii

Trochocarpa disticha var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
