Telina prostrata

Telina prostrata, commonly known as creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Telina prostrata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
