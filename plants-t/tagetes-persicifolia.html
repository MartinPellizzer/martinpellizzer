Tagetes persicifolia

Tagetes persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
