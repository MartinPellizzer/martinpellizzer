Typha sicula

Typha sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that produce fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Typha sicula plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
