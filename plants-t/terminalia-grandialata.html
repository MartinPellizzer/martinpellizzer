Terminalia grandialata

Terminalia grandialata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. Terminalia grandialata is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

It also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
