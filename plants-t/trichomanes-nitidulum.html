Trichomanes nitidulum

Trichomanes nitidulum, commonly known as the glossy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered with a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are clustered in groups.

Trichomanes nitidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
