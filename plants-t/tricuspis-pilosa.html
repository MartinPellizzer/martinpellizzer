Tricuspis pilosa

Tricuspis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy three-lobed plant, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive tricuspis (three-lobed) leaves.

The leaves are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant its "pilosa" designation, which means hairy in Latin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
