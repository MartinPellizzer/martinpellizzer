Tragacantha kuldshensis

Tragacantha kuldshensis, commonly known as the Kuldshen's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its most notable feature is the production of a viscous gum, which is harvested from the bark and used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.
