Tithymalus kanaoricus

Tithymalus kanaoricus, commonly known as the Japanese tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

Tithymalus kanaoricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
