Tulipa oculus-solis var. lortetii

Tulipa oculus-solis var. lortetii, commonly known as the Sun-eye tulip, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive yellow center surrounded by vibrant orange petals. The plant produces a single bloom per stem, which opens in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
