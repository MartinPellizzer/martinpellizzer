Trichotosia rhodoleuca

Trichotosia rhodoleuca, commonly known as the red-leaved trichotosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which contrasts with its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its unique appearance.

Trichotosia rhodoleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
