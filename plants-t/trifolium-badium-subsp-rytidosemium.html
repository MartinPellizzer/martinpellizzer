Trifolium badium subsp. rytidosemium

Trifolium badium subsp.

rytidosemium, commonly known as the red clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem.

The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its role in agriculture as a forage crop and for its ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
