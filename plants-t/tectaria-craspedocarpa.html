Tectaria craspedocarpa

Tectaria craspedocarpa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky areas, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its rhizome is usually short and creeping, with leaves that are leathery and often divided into lance-shaped segments.

Tectaria craspedocarpa is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
