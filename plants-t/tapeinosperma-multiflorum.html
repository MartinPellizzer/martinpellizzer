Tapeinosperma multiflorum

Tapeinosperma multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered sandthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces achenes as its seed type.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
