Thalictrum minus subsp. kemense

Thalictrum minus subsp.

kemense, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose, branched inflorescences, which are typically borne on slender pedicels.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
