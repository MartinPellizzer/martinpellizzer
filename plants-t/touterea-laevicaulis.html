Touterea laevicaulis

Touterea laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth-tongue fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, round spore capsules.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
