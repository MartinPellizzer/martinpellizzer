Trichera drymeja subsp. centrifrons

Trichera drymeja subsp.

centrifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trichera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low water availability.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
