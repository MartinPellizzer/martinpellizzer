Thlaspi bispinosum

Thlaspi bispinosum, commonly known as the two-spined rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of hairy, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Thlaspi bispinosum is adapted to cold climates and is commonly found in the mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.
