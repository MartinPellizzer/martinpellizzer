Tapiphyllum fulvum

Tapiphyllum fulvum, commonly known as the golden flame fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, slender fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its leaves are bioluminescent in low light conditions, a unique adaptation for nighttime photosynthesis.

This species is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
