Trifolium smyrnaeum

Trifolium smyrnaeum, commonly known as the Smyrna clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium smyrnaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
