Triticum tauschii

Triticum tauschii, commonly known as goatgrass, is a wild relative of wheat that plays a significant role in wheat genetics.

It is a grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia, characterized by its slender stems and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its chromosomes are important in breeding programs for improving wheat traits such as disease resistance and yield.

Triticum tauschii is also valued for its potential in enhancing the genetic diversity of cultivated wheat varieties.
