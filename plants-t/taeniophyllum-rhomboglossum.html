Taeniophyllum rhomboglossum

Taeniophyllum rhomboglossum, commonly known as the diamondleaf paphiopedilum, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are shaped like rhombuses, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a diamond shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
