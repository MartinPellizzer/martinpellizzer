Tibouchina mutabilis var. alba

Tibouchina mutabilis var. alba, commonly known as white tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink bract-like flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Tibouchina mutabilis var. alba is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for various garden environments.
