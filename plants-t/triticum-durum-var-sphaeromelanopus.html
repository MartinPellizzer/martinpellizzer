Triticum durum var. sphaeromelanopus

Triticum durum var. sphaeromelanopus, commonly known as durum wheat, is a variant of the durum wheat species characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is a hexaploid grass species with a compact, sturdy plant structure and a short, stout stem. The leaves are narrow and parallel-veined, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce hard, straw-colored grains. Its seeds are small, oval, and have a dark, smooth coat, which contributes to its unique appearance.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its high protein content and is used mainly in the production of semolina and pasta.
