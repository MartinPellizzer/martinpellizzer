Tydaea × pyramidalis

Tydaea × pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal reed, is a hybrid grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a pyramid. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Tydaea × pyramidalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
