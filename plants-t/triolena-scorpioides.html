Triolena scorpioides

Triolena scorpioides, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
