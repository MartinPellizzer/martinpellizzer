Theaphyllum celastrinum

Theaphyllum celastrinum, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement and are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
