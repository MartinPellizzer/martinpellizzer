Thibaudia sessiliflora

Thibaudia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Thibaudia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
