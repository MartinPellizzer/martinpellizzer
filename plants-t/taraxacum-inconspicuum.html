Taraxacum inconspicuum

Taraxacum inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces a yellow flower that matures into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in open, sunny environments.
