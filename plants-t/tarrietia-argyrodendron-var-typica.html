Tarrietia argyrodendron var. typica

Tarrietia argyrodendron var. typica, commonly known as the silver-leafed tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
