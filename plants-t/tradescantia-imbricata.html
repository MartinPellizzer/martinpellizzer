Tradescantia imbricata

Tradescantia imbricata, commonly known as the inch plant or mother of thousands, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking, waxy leaves that are typically green with red or purple veins, and it produces small, pale blue or pink flowers. The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners and leaf cuttings, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
