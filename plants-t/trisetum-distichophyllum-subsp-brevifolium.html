Trisetum distichophyllum subsp. brevifolium

Trisetum distichophyllum subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct two-ranked arrangement, giving it a unique appearance.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained conditions.
