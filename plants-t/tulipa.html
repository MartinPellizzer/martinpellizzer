Tulipa

Tulipa, commonly known as the tulip, is a genus of about 150 species of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

These plants are native to Central Asia and the Mediterranean region, with many species cultivated in temperate climates around the world. Tulips are known for their showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and purple. The bulbs of tulips are perennial, and they thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.

They are popular garden plants, especially in spring, when they bloom with vibrant colors.
