Thamnium puberulum

Thamnium puberulum, commonly known as the hairy thamnium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in green or reddish bracts, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Thamnium puberulum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its unique foliage.
