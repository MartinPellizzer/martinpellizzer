Thymus glaucus var. bulgaricus

Thymus glaucus var. bulgaricus, commonly known as Bulgarian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, aromatic flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their high concentration of essential oils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal practices.
