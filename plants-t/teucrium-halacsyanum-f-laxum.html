Teucrium halacsyanum f. laxum

Teucrium halacsyanum f. laxum, commonly known as the loose-stemmed germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom during the summer months. This plant is native to regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
