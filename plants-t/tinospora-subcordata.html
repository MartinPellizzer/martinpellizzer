Tinospora subcordata

Tinospora subcordata, commonly known as the Indian star gooseberry, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped yellow fruits. The plant has alternate, simple, and usually ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its stems are often woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

Tinospora subcordata is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
