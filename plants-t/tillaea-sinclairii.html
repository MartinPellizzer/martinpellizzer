Tillaea sinclairii

Tillaea sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's tillaea, is a rare and specialized succulent plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in maritime climates with frequent fog.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
