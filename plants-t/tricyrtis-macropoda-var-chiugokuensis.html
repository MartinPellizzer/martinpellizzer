Tricyrtis macropoda var. chiugokuensis

Tricyrtis macropoda var. chiugokuensis, commonly known as the Chiugoku spider lily, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers, which come in shades of white and pink, are often adorned with a distinctive purple or brown blotch at the base.
