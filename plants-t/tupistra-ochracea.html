Tupistra ochracea

Tupistra ochracea, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It typically grows in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Tupistra ochracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
