Trifolium minutum

Trifolium minutum, commonly known as the small clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in temperate regions. It produces small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

Trifolium minutum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
