Triticum vulgare var. erion

Triticum vulgare var. erion, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild wheat species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a grass with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

While not widely cultivated, it has historical significance in early agricultural practices.
