Trisetaria vaccariana

Trisetaria vaccariana, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Trisetaria vaccariana is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
