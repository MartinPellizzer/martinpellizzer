Triodia tomentosa

Triodia tomentosa, commonly known as the silky spinifex, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Triodia tomentosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
