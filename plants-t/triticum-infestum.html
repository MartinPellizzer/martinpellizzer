Triticum infestum

Triticum infestum, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces achenes as its fruiting bodies. It is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Triticum infestum is a perennial grass that can be a weed in agricultural fields.
