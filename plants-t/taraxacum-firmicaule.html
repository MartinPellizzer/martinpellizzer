Taraxacum firmicaule

Taraxacum firmicaule, commonly known as the firm-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, sturdy stem and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum firmicaule is a perennial herb that exhibits a rosette of basal leaves and is known for its hardy nature.
