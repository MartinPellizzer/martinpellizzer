Tabernaemontana plumieri

Tabernaemontana plumieri, commonly known as the fragrant star of Bethlehem, is a tropical flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Tabernaemontana plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
