Tinus membranacea

Tinus membranacea, commonly known as the red-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Tinus membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
