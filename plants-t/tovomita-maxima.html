Tovomita maxima

Tovomita maxima, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit, which resembles a star, is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, juicy pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
