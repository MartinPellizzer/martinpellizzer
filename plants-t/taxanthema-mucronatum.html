Taxanthema mucronatum

Taxanthema mucronatum, commonly known as the cushion saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit, with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a thick layer of white to grayish woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in a compact, cushion-like structure. Taxanthema mucronatum is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments, exhibiting resilience to cold, wind, and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
