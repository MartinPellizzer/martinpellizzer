Tripteris tomentosa

Tripteris tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly tripteris, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, woody fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
