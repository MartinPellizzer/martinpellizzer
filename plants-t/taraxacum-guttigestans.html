Taraxacum guttigestans

Taraxacum guttigestans, commonly known as the Himalayan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in glandular dots. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with both ray and disk florets.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
