Tragacantha consanguinea

Tragacantha consanguinea, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is known for its ability to produce a valuable gum, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
