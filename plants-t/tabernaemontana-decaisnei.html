Tabernaemontana decaisnei

Tabernaemontana decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne's star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts worldwide.
