Trigonotis bracteata

Trigonotis bracteata, commonly known as the golden bellviolet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-like blooms that hang in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Trigonotis bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
