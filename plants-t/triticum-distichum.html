Triticum distichum

Triticum distichum, commonly known as slender wheat, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed spikelets, which contain the characteristic wheat grains. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it is closely related to other wheat species and has been studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural uses.
