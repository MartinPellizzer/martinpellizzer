Trifurcia brasiliensis

Trifurcia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the fruit. The fruit, which resembles a star, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
