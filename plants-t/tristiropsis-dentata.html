Tristiropsis dentata

Tristiropsis dentata, commonly known as the serrated tristiropsis, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical classification places it in the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants.

Tristiropsis dentata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
