Thymus collinus

Thymus collinus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, woody stems and small, gray-green, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Thymus collinus is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
