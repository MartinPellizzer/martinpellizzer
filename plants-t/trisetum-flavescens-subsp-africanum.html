Trisetum flavescens subsp. africanum

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

africanum, commonly known as the African false brome, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale yellow to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
