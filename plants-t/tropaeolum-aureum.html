Tropaeolum aureum

Tropaeolum aureum, commonly known as the golden touchplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a trailing or climbing plant with bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Tropaeolum aureum is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
