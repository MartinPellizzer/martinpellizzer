Tragacantha trachytricha

Tragacantha trachytricha, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its sticky, resinous sap.

Tragacantha trachytricha is often cultivated for its gum, which has been used historically in medicine and as a sizing agent for textiles.
