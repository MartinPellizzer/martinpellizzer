Trichocaulon pillansii var. major

Trichocaulon pillansii var. major, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
