Thelocactus pseudopectinatus

Thelocactus pseudopectinatus, commonly known as the Mexican pectinatous cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture quickly from the dry soil.

Thelocactus pseudopectinatus is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
