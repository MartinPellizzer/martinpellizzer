Trichosalpinx hypocrita

Trichosalpinx hypocrita, commonly known as the purple spurred orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which feature prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
