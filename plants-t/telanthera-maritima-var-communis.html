Telanthera maritima var. communis

Telanthera maritima var. communis, commonly known as the sea bluet, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is well adapted to saline environments.

Telanthera maritima var. communis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
