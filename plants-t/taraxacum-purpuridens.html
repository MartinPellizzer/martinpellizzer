Taraxacum purpuridens

Taraxacum purpuridens, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that transition to a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge, giving it its common name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The species is native to parts of North America and is often found in temperate regions.
