Thymus mastichina var. capitellatus

Thymus mastichina var. capitellatus, commonly known as the Greek thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong thymol scent, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
