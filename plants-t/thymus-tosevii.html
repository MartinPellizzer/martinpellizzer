Thymus tosevii

Thymus tosevii, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
