Trollius altaicus subsp. sachalinensis

Trollius altaicus subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northeastern China. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine meadows. Its leaves are basal, simple, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
