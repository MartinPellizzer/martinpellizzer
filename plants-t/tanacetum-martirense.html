Tanacetum martirense

Tanacetum martirense, commonly known as the Spanish tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, pale seeds in a schizocarp.

Tanacetum martirense is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
