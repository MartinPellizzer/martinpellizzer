Tupistra veratrifolia

Tupistra veratrifolia, commonly known as the common spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to yellowish sap, which can be toxic if exposed to skin. Tupistra veratrifolia is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun to partial shade conditions.
