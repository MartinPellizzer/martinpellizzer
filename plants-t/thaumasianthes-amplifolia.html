Thaumasianthes amplifolia

Thaumasianthes amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf moonflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale purple, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its botanical structure includes a thick, woody stem and a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
