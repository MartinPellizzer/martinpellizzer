Tibouchina oerstedii var. subsessiliflora

Tibouchina oerstedii var. subsessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a prominent central column. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
