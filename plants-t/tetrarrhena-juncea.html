Tetrarrhena juncea

Tetrarrhena juncea, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and ecosystems.
