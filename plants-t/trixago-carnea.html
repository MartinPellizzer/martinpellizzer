Trixago carnea

Trixago carnea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, warm environments. It is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
