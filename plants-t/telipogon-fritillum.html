Telipogon fritillum

Telipogon fritillum, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a delicate, creeping rhizome with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic common among ferns.

Telipogon fritillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
