Tulipa celsiana var. maculata

Tulipa celsiana var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, often with distinctive red or purple markings. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
