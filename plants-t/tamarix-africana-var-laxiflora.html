Tamarix africana var. laxiflora

Tamarix africana var. laxiflora, commonly known as the African tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers during the summer months, which are adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded areas.
