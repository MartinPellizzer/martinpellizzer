Tabernaemontana fuchsiifolia

Tabernaemontana fuchsiifolia, commonly known as the pink wax tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fuchsia-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling wax-like petals. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
