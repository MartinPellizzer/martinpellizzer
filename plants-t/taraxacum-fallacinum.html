Taraxacum fallacinum

Taraxacum fallacinum, commonly known as the fallacious dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for seed dispersal. Native to the northern regions of North America, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize areas quickly.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides during the spring and early summer.
