Tripsacum aegilopoides

Tripsacum aegilopoides, commonly known as wild maize or wild panicum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale green florets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a member of the Poaceae family and is closely related to maize and teosinte.

Tripsacum aegilopoides is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
