Taraxacum penelobum

Taraxacum penelobum, commonly known as the Penelobum dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a bright yellow flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and forest margins.

Taraxacum penelobum is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
