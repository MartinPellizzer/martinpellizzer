Thibaudia bracteata

Thibaudia bracteata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive yellow center. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Thibaudia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
