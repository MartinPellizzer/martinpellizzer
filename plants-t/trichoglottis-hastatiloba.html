Trichoglottis hastatiloba

Trichoglottis hastatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leafed tibetan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
