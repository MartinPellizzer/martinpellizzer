Thelocephala aerocarpa

Thelocephala aerocarpa, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of North America.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a dense, spiny exterior that helps protect it from herbivores and excessive water loss. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its stem for survival in dry conditions.

Thelocephala aerocarpa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
