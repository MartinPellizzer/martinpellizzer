Tamarix uncinatifolia

Tamarix uncinatifolia, commonly known as the desert rose or tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tamarix uncinatifolia is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
