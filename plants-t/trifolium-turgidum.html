Trifolium turgidum

Trifolium turgidum, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

Trifolium turgidum is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop.
