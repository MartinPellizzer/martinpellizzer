Trifolium africanum var. africanum

Trifolium africanum var. africanum, commonly known as the African clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed sites.

As a nitrogen-fixing species, it plays a role in improving soil fertility in agricultural ecosystems.
