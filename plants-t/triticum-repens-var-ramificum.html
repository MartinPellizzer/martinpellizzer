Triticum repens var. ramificum

Triticum repens var. ramificum, commonly known as the ramifying wheatgrass, is a variant of the annual grass species that exhibits unique branching characteristics in its growth pattern.

This plant is characterized by its slender, tufted shoots and the presence of multiple branches along its stems, which distinguish it from typical wheatgrass varieties. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along water edges. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain the flowering structures.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest in botanical studies and may have potential applications in horticulture or ecological restoration.
