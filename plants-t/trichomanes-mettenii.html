Trichomanes mettenii

Trichomanes mettenii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It features finely divided, arching fronds with a silvery sheen on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sporangia.

Trichomanes mettenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
