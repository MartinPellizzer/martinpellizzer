Tillaea minima var. subsimplex

Tillaea minima var. subsimplex, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically clustered at the base. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh conditions.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
