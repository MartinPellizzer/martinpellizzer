Thymus × almeriensis

Thymus × almeriensis, commonly known as Almería thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Spain.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, pungent aroma and medicinal properties.
