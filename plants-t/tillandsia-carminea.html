Tillandsia carminea

Tillandsia carminea, commonly known as the Crimson Air Plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
