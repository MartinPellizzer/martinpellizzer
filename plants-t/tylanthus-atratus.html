Tylanthus atratus

Tylanthus atratus, commonly known as the black tylanthus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Tylanthus atratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
