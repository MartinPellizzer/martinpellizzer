Tamarix muluyana

Tamarix muluyana, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, edible fruit that is often consumed by both humans and animals.

Tamarix muluyana is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a significant role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitats.
