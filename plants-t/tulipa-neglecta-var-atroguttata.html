Tulipa neglecta var. atroguttata

Tulipa neglecta var. atroguttata, commonly known as the dark-spotted tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red with dark purple or black spots on the petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

The dark-spotted tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its distinctive and striking floral display.
