Trichocereus chiloensis var. borealis

Trichocereus chiloensis var. borealis, commonly known as the Northern Chilean cactus or the "Palo de Santa Maria," is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 5 meters, covered in white to light green spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom at night. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local traditions.
