Trisetum spicatum var. pilosiglume

Trisetum spicatum var. pilosiglume, commonly known as the silky glume false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or greyish hue due to fine hairs. Its flowers are typically greenish or pale yellow, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
