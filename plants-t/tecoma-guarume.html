Tecoma guarume

Tecoma guarume, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Tecoma guarume thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
