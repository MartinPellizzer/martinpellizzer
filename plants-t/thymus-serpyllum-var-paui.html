Thymus serpyllum var. paui

Thymus serpyllum var. paui, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
