Teucrium amplexicaule

Teucrium amplexicaule, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sunny habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
