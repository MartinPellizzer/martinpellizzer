Tilia glabra var. macrophylla

Tilia glabra var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing capabilities.
