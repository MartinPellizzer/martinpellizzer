Thea longifolia

Thea longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Thea longifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
