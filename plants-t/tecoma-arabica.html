Tecoma arabica

Tecoma arabica, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma arabica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dry climates.
