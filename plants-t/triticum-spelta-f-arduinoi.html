Triticum spelta f. arduinoi

Triticum spelta f. arduinoi, commonly known as the Arduino rye, is a rare and specialized form of spelt wheat.

It is characterized by its unique genetic makeup and distinct morphological traits compared to standard spelt varieties. This subspecies is often cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability to specific environmental conditions. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and compact inflorescences, which contribute to its resilience.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it holds significance in botanical research and conservation efforts.
