Triticum durum var. bicolor

Triticum durum var. bicolor, commonly known as red durum wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its distinctive two-colored grain.

The plant is a monocot with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces compact, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, and the grains are hard and high in protein content. This variety is primarily cultivated for its durum wheat, which is used in pasta and semolina.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
