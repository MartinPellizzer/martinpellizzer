Tragacantha brachystachys

Tragacantha brachystachys, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and thick, woody stems. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for local wildlife.
