Trichilia casarettoi

Trichilia casarettoi, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in axillary panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
