Triodia tridenticulata

Triodia tridenticulata, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, spiky leaves and forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Triodia tridenticulata is also a valuable food source for native fauna.
