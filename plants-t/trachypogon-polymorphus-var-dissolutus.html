Trachypogon polymorphus var. dissolutus

Trachypogon polymorphus var. dissolutus, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved trachypogon, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, with small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its morphological features.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects in its native habitat.
