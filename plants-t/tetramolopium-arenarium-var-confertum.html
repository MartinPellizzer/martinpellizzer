Tetramolopium arenarium var. confertum

Tetramolopium arenarium var. confertum, commonly known as the coastal ironweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal environments. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety confertum is distinguished by its more densely packed flower clusters compared to the typical form of Tetramolopium arenarium.
