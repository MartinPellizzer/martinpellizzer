Tormentilla divergens

Tormentilla divergens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems that root at the nodes. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Tormentilla divergens is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
