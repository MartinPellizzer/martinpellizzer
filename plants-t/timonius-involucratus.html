Timonius involucratus

Timonius involucratus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Timonius involucratus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
