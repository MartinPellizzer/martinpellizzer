Trifolium montanum subsp. rupestre

Trifolium montanum subsp.

rupestre, commonly known as mountain clover, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is a hardy species that can tolerate harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting pollinator populations.
