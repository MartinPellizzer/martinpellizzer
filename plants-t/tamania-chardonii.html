Tamania chardonii

Tamania chardonii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tamania chardonii is an important symbol of Australian national identity and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
