Tecoma shirensis

Tecoma shirensis, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Tecoma shirensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
