Trollius komarovii

Trollius komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's globeflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trollius komarovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
