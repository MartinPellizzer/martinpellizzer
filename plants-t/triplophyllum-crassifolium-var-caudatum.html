Triplophyllum crassifolium var. caudatum

Triplophyllum crassifolium var. caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed tamarack, is a variant of the tamarack species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needle-like foliage in the fall. Its cones are typically small and clustered, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The botanical features of this variant make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
