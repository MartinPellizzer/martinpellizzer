Teta

Teta, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Teta thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and yellow, making it a favorite among indoor gardeners.
