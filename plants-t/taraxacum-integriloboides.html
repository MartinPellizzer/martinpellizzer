Taraxacum integriloboides

Taraxacum integriloboides, commonly known as the smoothleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. It produces wind-dispersed seeds with feathery pappi, allowing for wide distribution.

This species is typically perennial and thrives in temperate climates.
