Triticum aestivum var. maqtaense

Triticum aestivum var. maqtaense, commonly known as the Maqtaense wheat, is a variety of durum wheat native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its high protein content and gluten strength, making it suitable for pasta and semolina production. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.2 to 1.5 meters, with slender stems and narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and the seeds are hard and yellowish when mature.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, showing resilience to drought conditions.
