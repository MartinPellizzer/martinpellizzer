Thymbra sintenisii subsp. isaurica

Thymbra sintenisii subsp.

isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
