Triticum vavilovii var. munuru

Triticum vavilovii var. munuru, commonly known as the Munuru wheat, is a wild wheat species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, awned grains, which are typically green to yellow in color. This variety is known for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its potential in wheat breeding programs. It exhibits a range of morphological variations, including differences in plant height and grain size.

The plant is adapted to semi-arid environments and plays a role in the evolution of cultivated wheat species.
