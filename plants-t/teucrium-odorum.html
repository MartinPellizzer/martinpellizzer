Teucrium odorum

Teucrium odorum, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically blue to violet in color and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
