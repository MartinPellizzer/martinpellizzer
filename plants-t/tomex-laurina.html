Tomex laurina

Tomex laurina, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tomex laurina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
