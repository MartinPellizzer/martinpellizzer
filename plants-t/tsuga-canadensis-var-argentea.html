Tsuga canadensis var. argentea

Tsuga canadensis var. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its needle-like leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is an evergreen conifer that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its cones are small and cylindrical, and it produces both male and female cones on the same tree.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
