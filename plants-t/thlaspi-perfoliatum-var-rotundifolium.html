Thlaspi perfoliatum var. rotundifolium

Thlaspi perfoliatum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed peppergrass, is a variant of the common peppergrass species.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in roadside areas and rocky habitats.
