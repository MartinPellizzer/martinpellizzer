Talinum albiflorum

Talinum albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that mature to a bright red color. Talinum albiflorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
