Theopsis euryoides

Theopsis euryoides, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Theopsis euryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
