Triticum glaucum f. aristatum

Triticum glaucum f. aristatum, commonly known as the silver wheatgrass, is a variant of the wheatgrass species characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage.

This plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates and is known for its dense, tufted growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and have a waxy coating that gives them their characteristic silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in panicle-like clusters, which are followed by small, hard grains.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
