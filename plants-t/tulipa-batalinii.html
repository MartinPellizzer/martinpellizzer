Tulipa batalinii

Tulipa batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Tulipa batalinii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.
