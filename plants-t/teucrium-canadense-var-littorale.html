Teucrium canadense var. littorale

Teucrium canadense var. littorale, commonly known as coastal germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern coast of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and tidal flats. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from white to pale purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging coastal environments.
