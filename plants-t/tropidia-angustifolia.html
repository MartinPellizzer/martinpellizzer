Tropidia angustifolia

Tropidia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tropidia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, herbaceous stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
