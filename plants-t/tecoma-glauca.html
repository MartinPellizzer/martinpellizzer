Tecoma glauca

Tecoma glauca, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue.

Tecoma glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
