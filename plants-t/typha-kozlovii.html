Typha kozlovii

Typha kozlovii, commonly known as the narrow leaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that stand above narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and wet meadows. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, helping to stabilize soil and filter water.

Typha kozlovii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
