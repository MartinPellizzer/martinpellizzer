Traxara

Traxara, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and clustered at the base of the stems, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Traxara is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
