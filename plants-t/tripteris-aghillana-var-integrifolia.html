Tripteris aghillana var. integrifolia

Tripteris aghillana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed tripteris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits, which are typical of the Tripteris genus.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
