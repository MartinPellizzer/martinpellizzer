Tinus japonica

Tinus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese tinus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

Tinus japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
