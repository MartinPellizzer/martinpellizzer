Thelionema umbellatum

Thelionema umbellatum, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a robust, woody structure with smooth, grey bark that exudes a strong, resinous scent. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Thelionema umbellatum is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
