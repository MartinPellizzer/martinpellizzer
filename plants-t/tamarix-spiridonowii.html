Tamarix spiridonowii

Tamarix spiridonowii, commonly known as the Persian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in saline and drought-prone environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Tamarix spiridonowii is often used for soil stabilization and has cultural significance in some regions.
