Triodanis perfoliata subsp. perfoliata

Triodanis perfoliata subsp.

perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate triodanis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are fused around the stem, creating a perfoliate appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
