Thlaspi virens

Thlaspi virens, commonly known as green chickweed, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of smooth, glossy, dark green leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Thlaspi virens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
