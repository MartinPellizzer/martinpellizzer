Triodia pungens var. linearis

Triodia pungens var. linearis, commonly known as the linear spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, fibrous foliage that forms a low-growing mat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green flowers. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its unique structure and adaptation to harsh environments make it an important component of the Australian desert ecosystem.
