Traubia chilensis

Traubia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean tree fern, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, unbranched stem, while its fronds are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Traubia chilensis plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
