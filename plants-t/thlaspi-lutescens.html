Thlaspi lutescens

Thlaspi lutescens, commonly known as yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Thlaspi lutescens is often found in alpine and arctic regions, where it exhibits adaptations to cold and harsh environmental conditions.
