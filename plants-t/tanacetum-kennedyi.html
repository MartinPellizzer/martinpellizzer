Tanacetum kennedyi

Tanacetum kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces yellow flower heads that resemble those of tansy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
