Trifolium pratense proles brachyanthum

Trifolium pratense proles brachyanthum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
