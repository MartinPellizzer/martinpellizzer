Tabernaemontana minutiflora

Tabernaemontana minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf angel's trumpet, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
