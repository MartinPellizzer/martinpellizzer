Typha turcomanica

Typha turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan cattail, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that emerges from a thick, leafy base. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, lakes, and riverbanks. Its leaves are long and flat, while its roots form dense mats that help stabilize aquatic environments.

Typha turcomanica plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
