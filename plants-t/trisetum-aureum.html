Trisetum aureum

Trisetum aureum, commonly known as golden tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces golden-yellow inflorescences that are visually striking. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along coastal areas.

Trisetum aureum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
