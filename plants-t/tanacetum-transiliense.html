Tanacetum transiliense

Tanacetum transiliense, commonly known as the Transylvanian tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.

Tanacetum transiliense is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
