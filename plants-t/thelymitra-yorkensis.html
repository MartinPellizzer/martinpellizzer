Thelymitra yorkensis

Thelymitra yorkensis, commonly known as the York spider-orchid, is an endemic orchid species native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a narrow, recurved lip. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils within heathlands and open forests. Thelymitra yorkensis produces small, greenish-white flowers during the late winter to early spring.

It is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in conservation reserves.
