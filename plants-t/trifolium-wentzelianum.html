Trifolium wentzelianum

Trifolium wentzelianum, commonly known as the Wentzel's clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trifolium wentzelianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
