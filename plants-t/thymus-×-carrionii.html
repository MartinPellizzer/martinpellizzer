Thymus × carrionii

Thymus × carrionii, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus herba-barona and Thymus mastichodes. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
