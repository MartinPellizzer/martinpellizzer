Tragacantha iliensis

Tragacantha iliensis, commonly known as the Caucasian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been traditionally used in medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This species is adapted to dry and rocky habitats, showcasing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
