Tephrocactus corrugatus

Tephrocactus corrugatus, commonly known as the corrugated cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, ridged, and corrugated patterns that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a succulent, adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Tephrocactus corrugatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
