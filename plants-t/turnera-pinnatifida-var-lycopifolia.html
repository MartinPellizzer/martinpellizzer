Turnera pinnatifida var. lycopifolia

Turnera pinnatifida var. lycopifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate, and glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
