Trichosporum radicans

Trichosporum radicans, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its stems and leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Trichosporum radicans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
