Tofieldia calyculata subsp. collina

Tofieldia calyculata subsp.

collina, commonly known as the mountain tofieldia, is a perennial herb native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with three sepals and three petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.
