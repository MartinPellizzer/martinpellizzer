Tamarix pycnocarpa

Tamarix pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often spiny growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dense, woody pods containing numerous seeds. Its extensive root system allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Tamarix pycnocarpa is also known for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
