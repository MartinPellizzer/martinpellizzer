Tetrataxis salicifolia

Tetrataxis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf tetrataxis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tetrataxis salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
