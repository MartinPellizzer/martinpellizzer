Taraxacum brevirostre

Taraxacum brevirostre, commonly known as the short-beaked dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its seed heads, known as "puffballs," produce lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum brevirostre is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in many regions.
