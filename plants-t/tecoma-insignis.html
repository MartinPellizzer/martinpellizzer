Tecoma insignis

Tecoma insignis, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically opposite and ovate.

Tecoma insignis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
