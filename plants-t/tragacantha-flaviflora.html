Tragacantha flaviflora

Tragacantha flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, similar to tragacanth, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Tragacantha flaviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
