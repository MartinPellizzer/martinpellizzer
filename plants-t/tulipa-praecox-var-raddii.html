Tulipa praecox var. raddii

Tulipa praecox var. raddii, commonly known as the Early Tulip or the Red Tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens and borders. Its bulbs are adapted to cold climates and require a period of dormancy for proper flowering.

The species is valued for its vibrant colors and early bloom time, adding early-season interest to gardens.
