Temnocydia spectabilis

Temnocydia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy moth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble moths, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The orchid has a complex root system adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and debris around its host.
