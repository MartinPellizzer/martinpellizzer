Tragacantha cymbaecarpos

Tragacantha cymbaecarpos, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
