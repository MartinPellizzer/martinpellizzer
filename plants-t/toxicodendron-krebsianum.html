Toxicodendron krebsianum

Toxicodendron krebsianum, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, rough bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

However, due to its toxic nature, handling the plant requires caution.
