Thlaspi rechingeri

Thlaspi rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's cabbages, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-like and have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
