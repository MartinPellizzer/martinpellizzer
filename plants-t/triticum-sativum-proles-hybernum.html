Triticum sativum proles hybernum

Triticum sativum proles hybernum, commonly known as winter wheat, is a major cereal crop cultivated for its grain.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense heads of small, hard grains. This variety is typically planted in the fall and harvested in the summer, adapting to colder climates. Its botanical features include a complex root system and a flowering structure that facilitates efficient pollination.

The plant's grain is rich in carbohydrates and proteins, making it a staple in global food supplies.
