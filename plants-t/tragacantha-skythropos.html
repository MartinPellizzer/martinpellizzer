Tragacantha skythropos

Tragacantha skythropos, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in arid ecosystems.

The plant's resin, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.
