Thaumatophyllum dardanianum

Thaumatophyllum dardanianum, commonly known as the Dardanian wonderplant, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Dardania.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bioluminescent leaves that glow in the dark, a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a complex root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and its stems exhibit rapid growth during the rainy season. Its flowers, which bloom only once every five years, are highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is classified as an endangered species.
