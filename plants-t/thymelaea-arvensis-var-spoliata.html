Thymelaea arvensis var. spoliata

Thymelaea arvensis var. spoliata, commonly known as the spotted thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, bell-shaped appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
