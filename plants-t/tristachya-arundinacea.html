Tristachya arundinacea

Tristachya arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features hollow, bamboo-like stems that are often used in basketry and construction. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in wetland environments and is highly adaptable to various soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in non-native habitats.
