Taraxacum respersum

Taraxacum respersum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that terminates in a flower head. The flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds with feathery parachutes. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

This plant is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
