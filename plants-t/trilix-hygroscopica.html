Trilix hygroscopica

Trilix hygroscopica, commonly known as the moisture-sensitive fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its ability to absorb and retain water from the air.

It thrives in humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions where moisture is abundant. The plant's fronds are delicate and often curl or droop when exposed to dry conditions, showcasing its hygroscopic nature. Its botanical structure includes finely divided leaves that increase its surface area for water absorption.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Trilix hygroscopica is an interesting subject for studying plant responses to environmental humidity.
