Trichilia casarettoi var. trifoliolata

Trichilia casarettoi var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf trichilia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
