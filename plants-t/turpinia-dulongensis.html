Turpinia dulongensis

Turpinia dulongensis, commonly known as the Guangxi turpentine tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when mature. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
