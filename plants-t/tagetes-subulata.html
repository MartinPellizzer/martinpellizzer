Tagetes subulata

Tagetes subulata, commonly known as the silverleaf marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tagetes subulata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers also have historical uses in traditional medicine.
