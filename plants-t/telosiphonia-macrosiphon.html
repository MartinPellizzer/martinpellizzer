Telosiphonia macrosiphon

Telosiphonia macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant siphon grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near rivers or in wetland areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, it can become invasive in certain environments.
