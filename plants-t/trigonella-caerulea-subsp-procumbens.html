Trigonella caerulea subsp. procumbens

Trigonella caerulea subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as blue fenugreek, is a low-growing perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming a dense mat of foliage.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
