Tanacetum paghmanense

Tanacetum paghmanense, commonly known as the Pamir Tanacetum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
