Tetranthera ligustrina var. celastroides

Tetranthera ligustrina var. celastroides, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
