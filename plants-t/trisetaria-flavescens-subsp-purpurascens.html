Trisetaria flavescens subsp. purpurascens

Trisetaria flavescens subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear purple to reddish flowers, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
