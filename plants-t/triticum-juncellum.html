Triticum juncellum

Triticum juncellum, commonly known as wild rye, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Triticum juncellum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
