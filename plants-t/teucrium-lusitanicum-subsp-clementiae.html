Teucrium lusitanicum subsp. clementiae

Teucrium lusitanicum subsp.

clementiae, commonly known as the Iberian germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its fragrant properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
