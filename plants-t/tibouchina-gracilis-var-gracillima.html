Tibouchina gracilis var. gracillima

Tibouchina gracilis var. gracillima, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often overlooked due to the striking color of the bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tibouchina gracilis var. gracillima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
