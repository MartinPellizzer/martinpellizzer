Turpinia arguta

Turpinia arguta, commonly known as the silverleaf mahonia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Berberidaceae family.
