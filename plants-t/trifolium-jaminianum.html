Trifolium jaminianum

Trifolium jaminianum, commonly known as the Jaminian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium jaminianum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
