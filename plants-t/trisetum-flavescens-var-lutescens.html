Trisetum flavescens var. lutescens

Trisetum flavescens var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, yellowish-green spikelets that are loosely arranged.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
