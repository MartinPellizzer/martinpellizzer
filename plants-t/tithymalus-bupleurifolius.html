Tithymalus bupleurifolius

Tithymalus bupleurifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender stems that are often branched, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, producing small, yellowish blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
