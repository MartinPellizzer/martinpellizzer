Tylophora pauciflora

Tylophora pauciflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Tylophora pauciflora is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
