Turbina longiflora

Turbina longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered turban snakeroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive, elongated shape, which is characteristic of the genus Turbina.

Turbina longiflora is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
