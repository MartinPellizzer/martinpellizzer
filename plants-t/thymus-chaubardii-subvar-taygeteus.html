Thymus chaubardii subvar. taygeteus

Thymus chaubardii subvar. taygeteus, commonly known as the Taygetean thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
