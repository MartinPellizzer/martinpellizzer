Tinnea physalis

Tinnea physalis, commonly known as the common velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
