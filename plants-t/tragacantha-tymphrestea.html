Tragacantha tymphrestea

Tragacantha tymphrestea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing seeds. It is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
