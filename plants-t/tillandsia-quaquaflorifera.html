Tillandsia quaquaflorifera

Tillandsia quaquaflorifera, commonly known as the Quaquaflower Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal soil, making it an ideal candidate for epiphytic cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice among bromeliophiles and conservationists alike.
