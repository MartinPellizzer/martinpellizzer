Thymus serpyllum f. wagneri

Thymus serpyllum f. wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic, grey-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
