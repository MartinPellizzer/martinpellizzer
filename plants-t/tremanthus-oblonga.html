Tremanthus oblonga

Tremanthus oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf tremanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Tremanthus oblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
