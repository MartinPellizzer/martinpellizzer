Thuja occidentalis f. buchananii

Thuja occidentalis f. buchananii, commonly known as the dwarf arborvitae, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that forms a rounded, conical shape, providing a neat and formal appearance in gardens. The plant has dark green, aromatic needles that remain throughout the year, offering year-round visual interest. It is well-suited for use as a hedge or ornamental plant due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.

This variety is particularly valued for its compact size and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
