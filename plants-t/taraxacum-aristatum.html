Taraxacum aristatum

Taraxacum aristatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its basal rosette of arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, leaf-like stem that terminates in a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
