Taraxacum bezidum

Taraxacum bezidum, commonly known as the Bezidum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, puffy seed head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
