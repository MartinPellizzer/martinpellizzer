Thelocactus bicolor subsp. flavidispinus

Thelocactus bicolor subsp.

flavidispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Texas and northern Mexico. It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and a distinctive yellowish spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

This subspecies is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
