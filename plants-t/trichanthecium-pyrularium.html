Trichanthecium pyrularium

Trichanthecium pyrularium, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats. The species is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent leaves.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
