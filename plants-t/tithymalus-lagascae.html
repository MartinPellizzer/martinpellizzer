Tithymalus lagascae

Tithymalus lagascae, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny locations. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, and it is adapted to survive in arid environments.

Tithymalus lagascae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
