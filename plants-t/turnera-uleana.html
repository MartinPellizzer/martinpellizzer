Turnera uleana

Turnera uleana, commonly known as yellow sandroot, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy roots. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are showy and attract various pollinators.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
