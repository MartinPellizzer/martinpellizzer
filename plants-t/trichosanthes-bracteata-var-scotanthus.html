Trichosanthes bracteata var. scotanthus

Trichosanthes bracteata var. scotanthus, commonly known as the Chinese snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, often unpleasant odor. The fruit is typically elongated, green, and covered in coarse hairs, turning orange-red when mature. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins.

This plant is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
