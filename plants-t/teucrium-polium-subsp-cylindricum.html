Teucrium polium subsp. cylindricum

Teucrium polium subsp.

cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
