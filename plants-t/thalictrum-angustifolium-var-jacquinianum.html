Thalictrum angustifolium var. jacquinianum

Thalictrum angustifolium var. jacquinianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender stems with compound, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on pedicels. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
