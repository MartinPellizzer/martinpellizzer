Thymus erioclados

Thymus erioclados, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features compact, cushion-like growth with small, gray-green leaves covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
