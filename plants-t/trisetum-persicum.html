Trisetum persicum

Trisetum persicum, commonly known as Persian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a soft, elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

Trisetum persicum is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
