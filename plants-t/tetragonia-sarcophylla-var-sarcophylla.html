Tetragonia sarcophylla var. sarcophylla

Tetragonia sarcophylla var. sarcophylla, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, adding visual interest to its habitat. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, which helps it store water and survive in dry conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
