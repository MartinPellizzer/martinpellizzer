Trillium × crockerianum

Trillium × crockerianum, commonly known as the Cross Roads Trillium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Trillium sessile and Trillium chloropetalum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its three large, waxy leaves and three petals, typical of the Trillium genus. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a showy, white or pinkish flower with three sepals and three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is native to the eastern United States.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
