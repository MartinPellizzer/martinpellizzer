Tiaridium velutinum

Tiaridium velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety down, giving it its common name. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tiaridium velutinum is often found in moist, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
