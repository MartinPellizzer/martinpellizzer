Trisetum paui

Trisetum paui, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Trisetum paui thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
