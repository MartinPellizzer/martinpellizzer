Trisetum tonduzii

Trisetum tonduzii, commonly known as the Tuscan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Trisetum tonduzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
