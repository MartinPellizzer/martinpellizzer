Tithymalus flavicomus

Tithymalus flavicomus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tithymalus flavicomus is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.
