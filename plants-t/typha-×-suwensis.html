Typha × suwensis

Typha × suwensis, commonly known as the hybrid cattail, is a wetland plant that results from the cross between Typha angustifolia and Typha latifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that produce a large amount of wind-pollinated pollen. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

Typha × suwensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
