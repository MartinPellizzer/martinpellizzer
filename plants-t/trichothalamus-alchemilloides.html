Trichothalamus alchemilloides

Trichothalamus alchemilloides, commonly known as the alchemilla thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
