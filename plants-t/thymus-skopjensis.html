Thymus skopjensis

Thymus skopjensis, commonly known as Skopje thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.

Thymus skopjensis is also cultivated for its culinary uses, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.
