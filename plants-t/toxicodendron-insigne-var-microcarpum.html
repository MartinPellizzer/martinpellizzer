Toxicodendron insigne var. microcarpum

Toxicodendron insigne var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited satinwood, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants with allergenic properties. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are small, red drupes that mature in late summer or early autumn.

The species is noted for its ornamental value but requires caution due to potential skin irritation from its sap.
