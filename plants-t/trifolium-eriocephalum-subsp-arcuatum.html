Trifolium eriocephalum subsp. arcuatum

Trifolium eriocephalum subsp.

arcuatum, commonly known as the bentleaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a slightly curved appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often covered in fine, woolly hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
