Trinia polyclada

Trinia polyclada, commonly known as the clustered trinia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a fibrous root system and produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Trinia polyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
