Trilobulina fibrosa

Trilobulina fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous trilobule, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate, three-lobed structure.

It belongs to the family Trilobulaceae and is native to arid regions where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features fibrous, woody stems that store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts. Its leaves are modified into small, scale-like structures to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Trilobulina fibrosa plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
