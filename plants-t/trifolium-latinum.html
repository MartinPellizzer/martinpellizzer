Trifolium latinum

Trifolium latinum, commonly known as the lesser clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. It is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Trifolium latinum is a perennial herb that plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil ecosystem.
