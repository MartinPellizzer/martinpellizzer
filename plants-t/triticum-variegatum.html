Triticum variegatum

Triticum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated wheat, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, which include alternating light and dark green stripes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces spikelets that bear small, awned grains. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other cereal grasses.
