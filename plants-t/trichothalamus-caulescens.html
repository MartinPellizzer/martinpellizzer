Trichothalamus caulescens

Trichothalamus caulescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed thistle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trichothalamus caulescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
