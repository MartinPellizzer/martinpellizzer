Tragopogon abolinii subsp. gonocarpus

Tragopogon abolinii subsp.

gonocarpus, commonly known as the Turk's-cap thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a crown or cap, giving it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by bracts that form a spherical structure. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The subspecies gonocarpus is distinguished by its unique reproductive structures and seed dispersal mechanisms.
