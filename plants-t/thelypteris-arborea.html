Thelypteris arborea

Thelypteris arborea, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor itself in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, elongated shape.

Thelypteris arborea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid climates.
