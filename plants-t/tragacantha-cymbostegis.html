Tragacantha cymbostegis

Tragacantha cymbostegis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
