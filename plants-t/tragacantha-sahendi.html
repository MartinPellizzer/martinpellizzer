Tragacantha sahendi

Tragacantha sahendi, commonly known as Sahndi gum or Persian gum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds.

Its most notable botanical feature is the production of a viscous, amber-colored gum, which is harvested from the bark and used in various industrial and medicinal applications.
