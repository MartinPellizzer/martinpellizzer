Troglophyton capillaceum subsp. diffusum

Troglophyton capillaceum subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the cave beardgrass, is a specialized plant adapted to subterranean environments. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers, typical of many members of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies thrives in dark, humid conditions, often found in caves or rocky crevices where light is minimal. Its leaves are usually narrow and adapted to reduce water loss in its arid underground habitat.

The plant's reproductive structures are designed for efficient pollination in low-light conditions, often relying on wind or specific insect species.
