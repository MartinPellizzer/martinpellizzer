Tripogon trifidus

Tripogon trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is divided into three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
