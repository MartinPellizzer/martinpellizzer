Trichocereus candicans f. rubriflorus

Trichocereus candicans f. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered torch cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and striking red to pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces spiny, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
