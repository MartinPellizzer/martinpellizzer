Thesium glaucescens

Thesium glaucescens, commonly known as the silver thesium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Thesium glaucescens is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
