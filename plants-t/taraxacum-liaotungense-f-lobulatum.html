Taraxacum liaotungense f. lobulatum

Taraxacum liaotungense f. lobulatum, commonly known as the lobed dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variation within the Taraxacum genus.
