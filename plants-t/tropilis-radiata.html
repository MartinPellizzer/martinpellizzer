Tropilis radiata

Tropilis radiata, commonly known as the radiate thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are arranged in a radiate pattern, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Tropilis radiata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
