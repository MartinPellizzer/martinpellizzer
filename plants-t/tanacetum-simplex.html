Tanacetum simplex

Tanacetum simplex, commonly known as buttonweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Tanacetum simplex is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
