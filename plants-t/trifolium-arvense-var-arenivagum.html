Trifolium arvense var. arenivagum

Trifolium arvense var. arenivagum, commonly known as the sandy clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to sandy and coastal environments.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lanceolate leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
