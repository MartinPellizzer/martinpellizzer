Trigonella carica

Trigonella carica, commonly known as the Indian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several small, aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their distinctive flavor and essential oils.

Trigonella carica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
