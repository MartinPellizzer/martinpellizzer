Tetramolopium sylvae

Tetramolopium sylvae, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tetramolopium sylvae is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
