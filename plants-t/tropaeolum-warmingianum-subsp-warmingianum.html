Tropaeolum warmingianum subsp. warmingianum

Tropaeolum warmingianum subsp.

warmingianum, commonly known as the "Peru gold" or "Indian cress," is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America. It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental planting.
