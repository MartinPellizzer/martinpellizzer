Thelypodium integrifolium var. longicarpum

Thelypodium integrifolium var. longicarpum, commonly known as the long-carpelled thelypodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

The variety longicarpum is distinguished by its elongated, slender fruits, which contribute to its unique botanical identification.
