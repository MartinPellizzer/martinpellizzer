Trichocereus terscheckioides

Trichocereus terscheckioides, commonly known as the Pachanoi or the "Jesus Christ cactus," is a columnar cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ribbed stems and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain edible, seed-like structures. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among experienced cactus growers.
