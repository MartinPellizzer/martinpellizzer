Tristachya kagerensis

Tristachya kagerensis, commonly known as the Kagerensis grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
