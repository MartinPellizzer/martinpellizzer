Toulicia megalocarpa

Toulicia megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant seed tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, woody structure and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its most distinctive feature is its massive, elongated seed pods, which can reach lengths of over 30 centimeters.

The seeds are encased in a fibrous, woody material and are dispersed by wind or water.
