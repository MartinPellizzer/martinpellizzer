Tetrastigma petraeum

Tetrastigma petraeum, commonly known as the rock tetrastigma, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grape family (Vitaceae) and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife.
