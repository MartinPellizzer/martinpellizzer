Tragacantha aspera

Tragacantha aspera, commonly known as the rough tragacanth, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, with small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Tragacantha aspera has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
