Trigonella orthoceras var. anatolica

Trigonella orthoceras var. anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties, similar to other members of the Trigonella genus.
