Trachelospermum crocostomum

Trachelospermum crocostomum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Trachelospermum crocostomum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
