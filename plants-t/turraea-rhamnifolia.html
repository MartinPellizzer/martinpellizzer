Turraea rhamnifolia

Turraea rhamnifolia, commonly known as African snowball, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that resemble snowballs, hence its common name. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Turraea rhamnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
