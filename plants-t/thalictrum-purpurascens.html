Thalictrum purpurascens

Thalictrum purpurascens, commonly known as the red-stemmed meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, compound umbels of white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Thalictrum purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
