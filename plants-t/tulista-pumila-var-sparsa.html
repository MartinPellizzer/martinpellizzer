Tulista pumila var. sparsa

Tulista pumila var. sparsa, commonly known as the sparse tulipan, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense ground cover.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or poor soil conditions.
