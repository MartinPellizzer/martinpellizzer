Thea cuspidata

Thea cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red or orange berries.

Thea cuspidata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
