Thalictrum confine

Thalictrum confine, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, usually yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Thalictrum confine is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
