Trichopteryx sandaensis

Trichopteryx sandaensis, commonly known as the sandaensis thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub with spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and waxy leaf coatings.

Trichopteryx sandaensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
