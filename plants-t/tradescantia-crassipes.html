Tradescantia crassipes

Tradescantia crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers throughout the growing season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

Tradescantia crassipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
