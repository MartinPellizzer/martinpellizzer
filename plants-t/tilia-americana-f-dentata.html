Tilia americana f. dentata

Tilia americana f. dentata, commonly known as the American basswood or large-toothed linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent, serrated teeth. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
