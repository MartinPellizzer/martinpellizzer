Turnera coccinea

Turnera coccinea, commonly known as the red turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are showy with five petals.

Turnera coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.
