Taraxacum molle

Taraxacum molle, commonly known as the soft dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its hairy, soft leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a capsule that contains numerous seeds with pappus, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Taraxacum molle is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
