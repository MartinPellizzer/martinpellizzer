Trevesia arborea

Trevesia arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.
