Troglophyton tenellum

Troglophyton tenellum, commonly known as the cave beard lichen, is a unique lichen species that thrives in dark, humid cave environments.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which together enable it to survive in low-light conditions. The plant lacks traditional roots, stems, and leaves, instead forming a crust-like structure that adheres to cave walls and rock surfaces. Its ability to absorb moisture from the air makes it well adapted to the constant, damp conditions of subterranean habitats.

Troglophyton tenellum plays an important ecological role in cave ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for other cave-dwelling organisms.
