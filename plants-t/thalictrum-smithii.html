Thalictrum smithii

Thalictrum smithii, commonly known as Smith's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Thalictrum smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
