Thymus ciliatus var. comosus

Thymus ciliatus var. comosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
