Trichotosia paludosa

Trichotosia paludosa, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in moist soils, such as those found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Trichotosia paludosa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
