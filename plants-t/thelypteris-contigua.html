Thelypteris contigua

Thelypteris contigua, commonly known as the common wood fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically arching or pendulous. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and often covered with a dense layer of roots.

Thelypteris contigua plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
