Tragia fasciculata

Tragia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and along roadsides.

Tragia fasciculata is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
