Tussacia friedrichsthaliana

Tussacia friedrichsthaliana, commonly known as the Friedrichsthaliana grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
