Tabebuia neochrysantha

Tabebuia neochrysantha, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a valuable ornamental species. Its bark is smooth and pale, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
