Tradescantia consanguinea

Tradescantia consanguinea, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that bear small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy, glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread readily through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.
