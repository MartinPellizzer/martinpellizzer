Trapa hyrcana

Trapa hyrcana, commonly known as the Caspian water shield, is a aquatic plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are shield-shaped and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a spiny, triangular structure that contains one or two seeds.

Trapa hyrcana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
