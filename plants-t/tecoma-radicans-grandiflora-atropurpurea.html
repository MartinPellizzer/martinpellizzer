Tecoma radicans grandiflora-atropurpurea

Tecoma radicans grandiflora-atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple crown flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable species in tropical and subtropical regions.
