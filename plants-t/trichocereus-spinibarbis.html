Trichocereus spinibarbis

Trichocereus spinibarbis, commonly known as the Spiny-Bearded Cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and dark green to bluish-gray foliage. The plant produces large, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
