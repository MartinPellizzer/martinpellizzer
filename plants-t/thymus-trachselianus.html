Thymus trachselianus

Thymus trachselianus, commonly known as the Alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
