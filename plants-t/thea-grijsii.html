Thea grijsii

Thea grijsii, commonly known as the gray tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a mild tea when its leaves are processed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of Thea grijsii make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
