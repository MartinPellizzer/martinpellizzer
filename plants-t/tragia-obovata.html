Tragia obovata

Tragia obovata, commonly known as the arrowleaf cupplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features opposite, obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Tragia obovata is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
