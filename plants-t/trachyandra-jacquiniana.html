Trachyandra jacquiniana

Trachyandra jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacobina grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaf sheaths.

Trachyandra jacquiniana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats in well-drained soils.
