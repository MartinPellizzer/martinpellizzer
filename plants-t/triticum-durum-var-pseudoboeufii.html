Triticum durum var. pseudoboeufii

Triticum durum var. pseudoboeufii, commonly known as the Boeuf wheat, is a variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the Poaceae family, known for its hard, compact grains. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender stems and spikelets that bear small, tough grains. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its resistance to certain diseases and its use in specialized agricultural applications.
