Trifolium plicatum

Trifolium plicatum, commonly known as the folded clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets that are often folded or curled. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium plicatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
