Trifolium campestre var. paphium

Trifolium campestre var. paphium, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a purple or pinkish hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
