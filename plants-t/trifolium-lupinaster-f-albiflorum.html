Trifolium lupinaster f. albiflorum

Trifolium lupinaster f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered clover, is a botanical variant of the species Trifolium lupinaster.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation and support for pollinators.
