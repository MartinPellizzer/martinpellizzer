Trisetum flavescens var. papillosum

Trisetum flavescens var. papillosum, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, golden-brown inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a pale green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets that are loosely arranged and often appear in a panicle-like structure.
