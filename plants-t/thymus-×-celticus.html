Thymus × celticus

Thymus × celticus, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus serpyllum and Thymus praecox.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus × celticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
