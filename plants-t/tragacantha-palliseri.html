Tragacantha palliseri

Tragacantha palliseri, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Tragacantha palliseri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid ecosystems.
