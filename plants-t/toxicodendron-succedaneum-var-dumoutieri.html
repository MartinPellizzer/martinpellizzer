Toxicodendron succedaneum var. dumoutieri

Toxicodendron succedaneum var. dumoutieri, commonly known as the Chinese lacquer tree, is a variant of the lacquer tree species native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and the ability to produce a thick, toxic sap used in traditional lacquerware. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants with urushiol-containing resins. Its leaves and sap contain urushiol, a potent allergen that can cause severe skin irritation.

This variant is valued for its unique botanical properties and historical significance in craftsmanship.
