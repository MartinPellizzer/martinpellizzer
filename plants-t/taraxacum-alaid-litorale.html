Taraxacum alaid-litorale

Taraxacum alaid-litorale, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. This plant is adapted to coastal environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines. It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its resilience to salt spray and wind.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
