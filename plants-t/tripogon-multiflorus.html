Tripogon multiflorus

Tripogon multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered tripogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in grassland habitats.

Tripogon multiflorus is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
