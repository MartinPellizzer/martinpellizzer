Tricalysia aciculiflora

Tricalysia aciculiflora, commonly known as the needle-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely arranged along its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
