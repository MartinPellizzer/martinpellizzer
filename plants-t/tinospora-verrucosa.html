Tinospora verrucosa

Tinospora verrucosa, commonly known as the rough tinospora, is a climbing plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Tinospora verrucosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
