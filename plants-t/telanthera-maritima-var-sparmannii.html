Telanthera maritima var. sparmannii

Telanthera maritima var. sparmannii, commonly known as the coastal telanthera, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and windy conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
