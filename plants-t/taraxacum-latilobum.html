Taraxacum latilobum

Taraxacum latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to North America, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of vegetative growth followed by flowering and seed dispersal.
