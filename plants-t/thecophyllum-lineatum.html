Thecophyllum lineatum

Thecophyllum lineatum, commonly known as the linear leaf phyllium, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem.

It belongs to the family Phylliaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a succulent texture, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of green and white, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thecophyllum lineatum is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
