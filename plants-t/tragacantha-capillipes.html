Tragacantha capillipes

Tragacantha capillipes, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
