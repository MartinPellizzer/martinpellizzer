Trichogoniopsis

Trichogoniopsis, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The genus is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and is often found in grasslands and open areas.
