Typha krasnovae

Typha krasnovae, commonly known as the Krasnov's cattail, is a wetland plant native to parts of Russia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower heads that consist of a brown, mat-like structure at the top and a yellow, fluffy section below. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are broad and flat, typically ranging from 1 to 3 meters in length.

Typha krasnovae plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling in wetland ecosystems.
