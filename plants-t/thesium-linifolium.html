Thesium linifolium

Thesium linifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically borne at the base of the plant. Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
