Toxicodendron thyrsiflorum

Toxicodendron thyrsiflorum, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants with toxic properties. The tree is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible nuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
