Trifolium cernuum var. perreymondii

Trifolium cernuum var. perreymondii, commonly known as the Perreymond's creeping clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to ovate leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
