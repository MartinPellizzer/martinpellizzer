Torrubia salicifolia

Torrubia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf torrubia, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are opposite and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Torrubia salicifolia plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
