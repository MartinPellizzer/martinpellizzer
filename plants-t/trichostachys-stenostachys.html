Trichostachys stenostachys

Trichostachys stenostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant produces small, dense inflorescences with spikelets that are typically green or pale brown in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its rapid growth and hardiness, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and ecological restoration projects.
