Thymus nerczensis

Thymus nerczensis, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Thymus nerczensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry environments.
