Tina striata

Tina striata, commonly known as the striped tina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, narrow leaves that are often marked with distinct stripes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
