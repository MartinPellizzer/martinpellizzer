Tiniaria scandens

Tiniaria scandens, commonly known as the climbing tiniaria, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Tiniaria scandens is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks.
