Thespesia populnea var. rheedei

Thespesia populnea var. rheedei, commonly known as the red hibiscus or red glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
