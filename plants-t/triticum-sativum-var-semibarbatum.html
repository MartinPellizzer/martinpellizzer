Triticum sativum var. semibarbatum

Triticum sativum var. semibarbatum, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and spike-like inflorescence, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, bristle-tipped grains. It is valued for its high nutritional content and resilience to environmental stress.

This variety is often used in forage production and as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
