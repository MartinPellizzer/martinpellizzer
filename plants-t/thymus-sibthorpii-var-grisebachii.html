Thymus sibthorpii var. grisebachii

Thymus sibthorpii var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, creeping stems that form dense mats, with small, aromatic leaves that are grayish-green in color. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry, sunny environments.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
