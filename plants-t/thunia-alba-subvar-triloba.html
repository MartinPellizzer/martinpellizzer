Thunia alba subvar. triloba

Thunia alba subvar. triloba, commonly known as the white thunia or trilobed thunia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal in both cultivated and natural settings.
