Tridentea parvipuncta subsp. truncata

Tridentea parvipuncta subsp.

truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tridentea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a truncate or notched base.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
