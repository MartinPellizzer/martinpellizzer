Trapa kazakorum

Trapa kazakorum, commonly known as the Kazakh water chestnut, is a species of aquatic plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are broadly ovate and often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically submerged, with the fruit being a spiny, triangular capsule. Trapa kazakorum thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
