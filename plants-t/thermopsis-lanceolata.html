Thermopsis lanceolata

Thermopsis lanceolata, commonly known as yellowhead ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
