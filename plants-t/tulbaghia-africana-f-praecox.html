Tulbaghia africana f. praecox

Tulbaghia africana f. praecox, commonly known as the early flowering tulbaghia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom early in the growing season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
