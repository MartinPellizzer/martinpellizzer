Tanacetum newesskyanum

Tanacetum newesskyanum, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Tanacetum newesskyanum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
