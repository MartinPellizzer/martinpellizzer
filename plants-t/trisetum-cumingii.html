Trisetum cumingii

Trisetum cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Trisetum cumingii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
