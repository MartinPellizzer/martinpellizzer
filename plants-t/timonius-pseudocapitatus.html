Timonius pseudocapitatus

Timonius pseudocapitatus, commonly known as the false thimbleweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Timonius pseudocapitatus is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
