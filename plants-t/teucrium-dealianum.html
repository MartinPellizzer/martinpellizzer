Teucrium dealianum

Teucrium dealianum, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Teucrium dealianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
