Taraxacum cuzcense

Taraxacum cuzcense, commonly known as the Cuzco dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
