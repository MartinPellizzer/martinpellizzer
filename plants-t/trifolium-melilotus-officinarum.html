Trifolium melilotus-officinarum

Trifolium melilotus-officinarum, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Yellow melilot is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
