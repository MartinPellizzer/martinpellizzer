Tylecodon buchholzianus var. fasciculatus

Tylecodon buchholzianus var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the fasciculate silver tree, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features a dense, compact growth habit with short, branching stems that are covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are small, fleshy, and often appear in clusters, giving the plant its "fasciculate" characteristic. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
