Taraxacum impolitum

Taraxacum impolitum, commonly known as the rough dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, similar to other dandelion species, and it is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
