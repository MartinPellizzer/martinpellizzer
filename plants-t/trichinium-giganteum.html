Trichinium giganteum

Trichinium giganteum, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often covered in stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a serrated edge, often with a hairy texture.

Trichinium giganteum is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
