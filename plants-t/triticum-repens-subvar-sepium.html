Triticum repens subvar. sepium

Triticum repens subvar. sepium, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. It is an important component of grassland ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and agricultural studies.
