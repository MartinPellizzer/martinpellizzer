Taraxacum bellidiforme

Taraxacum bellidiforme, commonly known as the bellidiform dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and distinctive, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable member of the dandelion family.
