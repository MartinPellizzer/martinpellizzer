Thlaspi griffithianum

Thlaspi griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has basal leaves that are usually lobed or divided, and its flowering stems are often hairy. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Thlaspi griffithianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
