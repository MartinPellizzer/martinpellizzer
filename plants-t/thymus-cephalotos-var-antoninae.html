Thymus cephalotos var. antoninae

Thymus cephalotos var. antoninae, commonly known as the pink thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs, typical of the Thymus genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, fragrant foliage.
