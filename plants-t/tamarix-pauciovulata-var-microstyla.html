Tamarix pauciovulata var. microstyla

Tamarix pauciovulata var. microstyla, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale pink to reddish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
