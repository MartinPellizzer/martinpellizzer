Trapa araborum

Trapa araborum, commonly known as the water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features floating leaves that support its submerged, spiny fruits. The plant produces distinctive, nut-like structures that are edible and often harvested for food. Its roots are adapted to thrive in muddy, freshwater environments.

Trapa araborum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
