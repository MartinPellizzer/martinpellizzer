Tussilago sarmentosa

Tussilago sarmentosa, commonly known as creeping coltsfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are clustered in umbels.

Tussilago sarmentosa is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
