Tolpis gussonei

Tolpis gussonei, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Tolpis gussonei is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
