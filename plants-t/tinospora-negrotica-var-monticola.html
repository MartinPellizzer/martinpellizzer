Tinospora negrotica var. monticola

Tinospora negrotica var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces yellowish fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
