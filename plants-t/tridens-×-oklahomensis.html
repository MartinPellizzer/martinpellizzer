Tridens × oklahomensis

Tridens × oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma grass, is a hybrid plant species native to the central United States.

It is a tall, perennial grass characterized by its dense growth habit and slender, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It is often found in prairie ecosystems and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
