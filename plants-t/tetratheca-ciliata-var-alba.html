Tetratheca ciliata var. alba

Tetratheca ciliata var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features narrow, grey-green leaves with hairy margins, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
