Thymus acicularis var. lacaitae

Thymus acicularis var. lacaitae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features tiny, needle-like leaves and produces small, pink or violet flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and moderate frost.
