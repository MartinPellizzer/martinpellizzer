Tilia × gyorffyana

Tilia × gyorffyana, commonly known as the Hungarian linden, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to Hungary and surrounding regions in Central Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters, with smooth, gray bark and pendulous clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are large, serrated, and turn a striking yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

Tilia × gyorffyana is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
