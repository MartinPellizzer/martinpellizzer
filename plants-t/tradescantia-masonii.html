Tradescantia masonii

Tradescantia masonii, commonly known as the silver sword or red-veined nettle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically grayish-green with striking red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Tradescantia masonii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
