Thladiantha pustulata var. pustulata

Thladiantha pustulata var. pustulata, commonly known as the spiny gooseberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rosaceae family.
