Trachypogon secundus

Trachypogon secundus, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
