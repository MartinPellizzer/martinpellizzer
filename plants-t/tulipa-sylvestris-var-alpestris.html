Tulipa sylvestris var. alpestris

Tulipa sylvestris var. alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
