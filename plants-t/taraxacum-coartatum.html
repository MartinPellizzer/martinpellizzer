Taraxacum coartatum

Taraxacum coartatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
