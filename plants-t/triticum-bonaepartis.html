Triticum bonaepartis

Triticum bonaepartis, commonly known as the "good parts wheat," is a rare and little-studied species of wheat native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique genetic makeup, which includes traits that may enhance its resilience to environmental stressors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, hard grains, which are enclosed in protective glumes.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic distinctiveness, Triticum bonaepartis holds potential for agricultural research and breeding programs.
