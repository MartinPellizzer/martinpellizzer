Terminalia clemensae

Terminalia clemensae, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in winter. The tree has smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits to release seeds.

Terminalia clemensae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
