Trisetum chloranthum

Trisetum chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow tuft grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow spikelets that are clustered at the tips of the stems. Trisetum chloranthum typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
