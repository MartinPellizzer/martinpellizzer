Tradescantia pexata

Tradescantia pexata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often violet or blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia pexata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
