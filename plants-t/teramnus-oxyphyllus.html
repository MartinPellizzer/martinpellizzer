Teramnus oxyphyllus

Teramnus oxyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf teramnus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are fragrant.

Teramnus oxyphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
