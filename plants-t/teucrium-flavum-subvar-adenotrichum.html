Teucrium flavum subvar. adenotrichum

Teucrium flavum subvar. adenotrichum, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features small, yellowish flowers arranged in whorls. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
