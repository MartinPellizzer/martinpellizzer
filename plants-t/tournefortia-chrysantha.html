Tournefortia chrysantha

Tournefortia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture, while its bark is smooth and greyish.

Tournefortia chrysantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
