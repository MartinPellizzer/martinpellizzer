Thymophylla setifolia var. greggii

Thymophylla setifolia var. greggii, commonly known as Gregg's desert thyme, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with succulent leaves that store water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to desert conditions, showcasing resilience and beauty in arid landscapes.
