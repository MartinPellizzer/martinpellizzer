Teucrium commune subsp. aureum

Teucrium commune subsp.

aureum, commonly known as golden germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a yellowish-green hue.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
