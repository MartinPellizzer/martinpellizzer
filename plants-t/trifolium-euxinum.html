Trifolium euxinum

Trifolium euxinum, commonly known as the Crimean clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purplish tinge. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in dense, globular clusters known as heads.

Trifolium euxinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
