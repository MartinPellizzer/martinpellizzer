Tragacantha anacardia

Tragacantha anacardia, commonly known as the African gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown and thick, fissured bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its latex sap is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
