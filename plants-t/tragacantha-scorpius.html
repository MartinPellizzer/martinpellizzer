Tragacantha scorpius

Tragacantha scorpius, commonly known as the spiny tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
