Trattinnickia burserifolia var. quinquejuga

Trattinnickia burserifolia var. quinquejuga, commonly known as the five-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It has a distinctive appearance with its small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorl of five at the ends of its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

This species is found in the subtropical and tropical regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
