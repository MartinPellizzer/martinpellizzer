Trichomanes hymenoides f. pseudoreptans

Trichomanes hymenoides f. pseudoreptans, commonly known as the false reptant holly fern, is a fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This fern is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations that allow it to flourish in specific microhabitats.
