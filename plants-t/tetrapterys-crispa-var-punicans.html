Tetrapterys crispa var. punicans

Tetrapterys crispa var. punicans, commonly known as the scarlet shieldvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant foliage.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
