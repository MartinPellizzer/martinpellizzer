Thymus hannonis

Thymus hannonis, commonly known as Hannon's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
