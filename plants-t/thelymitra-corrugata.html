Thelymitra corrugata

Thelymitra corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is an Australian terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging leaf structure and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a pale yellow lip. Native to the eastern and southern regions of Australia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in variable climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its habitat.
