Taraxacum canoviride

Taraxacum canoviride, commonly known as the Canary Islands dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical seed dispersal structures. The plant features a rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
