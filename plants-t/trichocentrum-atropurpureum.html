Trichocentrum atropurpureum

Trichocentrum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple veins on broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive purple lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Trichocentrum atropurpureum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
