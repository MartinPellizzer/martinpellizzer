Thymus degenianus

Thymus degenianus, commonly known as the Degen's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus degenianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
