Takeikadzuchia lomonossowii

Takeikadzuchia lomonossowii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from blue to purple, and are clustered in racemes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
