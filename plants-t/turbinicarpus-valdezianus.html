Turbinicarpus valdezianus

Turbinicarpus valdezianus, commonly known as the Valdezian turbinated cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
