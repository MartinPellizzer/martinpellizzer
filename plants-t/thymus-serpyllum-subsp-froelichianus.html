Thymus serpyllum subsp. froelichianus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

froelichianus, commonly known as Froelich's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces compact clusters of pink to violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
