Timonius alius

Timonius alius, commonly known as the silverleaf timonius, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun exposure. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.
