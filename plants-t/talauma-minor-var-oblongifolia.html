Talauma minor var. oblongifolia

Talauma minor var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaf talauma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
