Trichodium laxum

Trichodium laxum, commonly known as the slender trichodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Trichodium laxum is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
