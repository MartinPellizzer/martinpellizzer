Tournefortia tacarcunensis

Tournefortia tacarcunensis, commonly known as the Tacarigua tamarind.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in parts of Cuba and the Bahamas. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Tournefortia tacarcunensis is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.
