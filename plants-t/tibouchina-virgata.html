Tibouchina virgata

Tibouchina virgata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

Tibouchina virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
