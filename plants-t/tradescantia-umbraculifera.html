Tradescantia umbraculifera

Tradescantia umbraculifera, commonly known as the forest spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
