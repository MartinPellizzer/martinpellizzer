Thymus serpyllum f. collivagus

Thymus serpyllum f. collivagus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its dense growth habit makes it an excellent ground cover plant in gardens and rocky areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic oils.
