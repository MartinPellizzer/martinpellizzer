Tradescantia tucumanensis

Tradescantia tucumanensis, commonly known as the "Purple Heart" plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which becomes more intense in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is largely due to its vibrant leaf color and ease of cultivation.
