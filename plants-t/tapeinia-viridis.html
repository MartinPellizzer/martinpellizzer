Tapeinia viridis

Tapeinia viridis, commonly known as the green tapeinia, is a parasitic fungus that primarily infects the leaves of certain grass species.

It belongs to the family Tapesiaceae and is characterized by its unique, leaf-like structures called haustoria, which it uses to extract nutrients from its host. The fungus is often found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its life cycle involves both asexual and sexual reproduction, with spores dispersed by wind.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to the host plant's photosynthetic capacity.
