Taraxacum grandisquamum

Taraxacum grandisquamum, commonly known as the large-scale dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a robust taproot system and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Its leaves are typically large, broad, and have a wavy margin with a hairy surface.

Taraxacum grandisquamum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
