Thea bolovenensis

Thea bolovenensis, commonly known as the Boloven tea plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Laos.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its leaves used in traditional tea preparations. Its growth habitat typically includes humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Thea bolovenensis plays a significant role in local biodiversity and ecological studies in Southeast Asia.
