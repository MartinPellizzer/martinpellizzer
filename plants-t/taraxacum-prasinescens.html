Taraxacum prasinescens

Taraxacum prasinescens, commonly known as the green-veined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
