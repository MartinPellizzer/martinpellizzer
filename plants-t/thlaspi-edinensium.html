Thlaspi edinensium

Thlaspi edinensium, commonly known as Edinburgh wallflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

Its leaves are usually basal, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.
