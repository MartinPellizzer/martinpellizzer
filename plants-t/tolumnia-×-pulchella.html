Tolumnia × pulchella

Tolumnia × pulchella, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Tolumnia grandiflora and Tolumnia longiflora.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tolumnia × pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
