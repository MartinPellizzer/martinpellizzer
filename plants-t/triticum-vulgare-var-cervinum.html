Triticum vulgare var. cervinum

Triticum vulgare var. cervinum, commonly known as red wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its distinctive reddish grain color.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are spike-like, containing small, two-lobed florets that produce the characteristic wheat grains.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional baking and animal feed.
