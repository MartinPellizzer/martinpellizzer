Thalictrum anomalum

Thalictrum anomalum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum anomalum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
