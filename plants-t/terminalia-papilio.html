Terminalia papilio

Terminalia papilio, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
