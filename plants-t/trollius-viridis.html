Trollius viridis

Trollius viridis, commonly known as green buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Trollius viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
