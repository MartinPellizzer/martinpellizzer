Tephrosia uniovulata

Tephrosia uniovulata, commonly known as the single-seeded tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, featuring compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its unique feature is the single ovule within each ovary, giving it the species name "uniovulata.".

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
