Tetragonolobus pseudopurpureus

Tetragonolobus pseudopurpureus, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
