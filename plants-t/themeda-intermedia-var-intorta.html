Themeda intermedia var. intorta

Themeda intermedia var. intorta, commonly known as the twisted blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native environment.
