Thelypteris gymnopteridifrons

Thelypteris gymnopteridifrons, commonly known as the naked-veined shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinct lack of false veins. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The spore-producing structures, or sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
