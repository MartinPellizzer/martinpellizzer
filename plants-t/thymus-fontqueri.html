Thymus fontqueri

Thymus fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, and have a strong, pungent thyme scent.

Thymus fontqueri is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
