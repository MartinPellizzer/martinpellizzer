Tabebuia savannarum

Tabebuia savannarum, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface.

The wood of Tabebuia savannarum is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
