Tripterospermum australe

Tripterospermum australe, commonly known as the Australian star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
