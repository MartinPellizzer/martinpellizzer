Tetrastigma erubescens

Tetrastigma erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its reddish stems and tendrils that aid in its climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Tetrastigma erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
