Taraxacum lacerum

Taraxacum lacerum, commonly known as the ragged chicory, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats across North America.

Taraxacum lacerum is a perennial herb that is typically short-lived and has a rosette growth form.
