Taraxacum cyanolepidiforme

Taraxacum cyanolepidiforme, commonly known as the blue-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its bright blue-green leaves and yellow ray flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its unique coloration and habitat make it a distinctive member of the Taraxacum genus.

The plant is often found in rocky or alpine soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the ecosystem.
