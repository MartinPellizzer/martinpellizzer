Tanacetum hippiifolium

Tanacetum hippiifolium, commonly known as the yarrow plant, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Tanacetum hippiifolium has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
