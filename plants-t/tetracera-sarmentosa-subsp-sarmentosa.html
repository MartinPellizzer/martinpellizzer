Tetracera sarmentosa subsp. sarmentosa

Tetracera sarmentosa subsp.

sarmentosa, commonly known as the climbing tetracera, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is characterized by its twining habit and ability to climb other vegetation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
