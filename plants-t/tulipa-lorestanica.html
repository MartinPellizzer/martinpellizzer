Tulipa lorestanica

Tulipa lorestanica, commonly known as the Lorestan tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 50 centimeters and produces showy blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Tulipa lorestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
