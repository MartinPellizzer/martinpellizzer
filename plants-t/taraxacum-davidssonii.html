Taraxacum davidssonii

Taraxacum davidssonii, commonly known as the Davidsson's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, yellow flower head that blooms in the summer. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
