Tadeastrum reichenbachianum

Tadeastrum reichenbachianum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is a vigorous climber with slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
