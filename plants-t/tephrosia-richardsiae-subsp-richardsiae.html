Tephrosia richardsiae subsp. richardsiae

Tephrosia richardsiae subsp.

richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
