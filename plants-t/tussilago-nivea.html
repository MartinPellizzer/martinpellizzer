Tussilago nivea

Tussilago nivea, commonly known as the white cohosh, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Tussilago in the family Polygonaceae, which includes other species known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tussilago nivea has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential respiratory and anti-inflammatory benefits.
