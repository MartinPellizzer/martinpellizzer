Ticanto magnifoliolata

Ticanto magnifoliolata, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with red or purple accents. Its foliage is broad and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
