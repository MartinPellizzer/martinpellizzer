Taraxacum mackenziense

Taraxacum mackenziense, commonly known as the Mackenzie dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, tall flowering stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into distinctive white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
