Topobea rosea

Topobea rosea, commonly known as the rose-like topobea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white petals that resemble those of roses, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Topobea rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
