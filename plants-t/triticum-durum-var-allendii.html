Triticum durum var. allendii

Triticum durum var. allendii, commonly known as durum wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its high protein content and hard, tough grains.

This plant is primarily cultivated for its use in making semolina, which is essential in pasta and couscous production. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and has a short growing season.

Its botanical features include spike-like inflorescences with small, tough glumes enclosing the grains.
