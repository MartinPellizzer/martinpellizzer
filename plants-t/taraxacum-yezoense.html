Taraxacum yezoense

Taraxacum yezoense, commonly known as the Yezo dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest margins.

Taraxacum yezoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Japanese horticulture.
