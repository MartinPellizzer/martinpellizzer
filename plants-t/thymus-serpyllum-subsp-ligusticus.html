Thymus serpyllum subsp. ligusticus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

ligusticus, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact, cushion-like appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink or purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
