Trifolium graecum

Trifolium graecum, commonly known as Greek clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium graecum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
