Triticum sativum var. trimestre-nanum

Triticum sativum var. trimestre-nanum, commonly known as dwarf spring wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to standard wheat varieties.

This plant exhibits a shorter growth cycle, making it suitable for regions with shorter growing seasons. It possesses a robust root system that enhances its ability to absorb nutrients and water efficiently. The grains are typically smaller and have a higher protein content, which contributes to its use in various food products.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for agricultural diversity and sustainable farming practices.
