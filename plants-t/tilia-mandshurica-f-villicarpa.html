Tilia mandshurica f. villicarpa

Tilia mandshurica f. villicarpa, commonly known as the Manchurian linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, round fruits that contain hairy seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
