Tamarix balansae var. oxysepala

Tamarix balansae var. oxysepala, commonly known as the Kashmiri tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which are adapted for pollination by wind and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is tolerant of saline conditions, making it suitable for reclamation of degraded lands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and stabilizing soils in semi-arid ecosystems.
