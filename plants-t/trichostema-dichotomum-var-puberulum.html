Trichostema dichotomum var. puberulum

Trichostema dichotomum var. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
