Theopsis lungyaiensis

Theopsis lungyaiensis, commonly known as the Lungyai daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a compact, low-growing habit. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a daisy-like appearance, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
