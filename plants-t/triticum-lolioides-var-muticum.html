Triticum lolioides var. muticum

Triticum lolioides var. muticum, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its role in ecological succession.
