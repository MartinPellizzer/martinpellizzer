Tirucalia xylophylloides

Tirucalia xylophylloides, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers, making it drought-tolerant.
