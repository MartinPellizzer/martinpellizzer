Trigonocaryum

Trigonocaryum, commonly known as the three-lobed cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cactaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular or three-lobed stems, which are often ribbed and covered in spines. These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. Trigonocaryum species typically produce clusters of small, yellow or white flowers during the warmer months.
