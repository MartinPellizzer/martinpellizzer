Trichosanthes dunniana subsp. clarkei

Trichosanthes dunniana subsp.

clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and elongated, spiny fruits that contain a bitter, edible pulp.

The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.
