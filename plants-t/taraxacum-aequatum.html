Taraxacum aequatum

Taraxacum aequatum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum aequatum is known for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional herbal remedies.
