Teucrium musimonum var. mesatlanticum

Teucrium musimonum var. mesatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its adaptability to coastal environments makes it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
