Thymus serpyllum proles allionii

Thymus serpyllum proles allionii, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, tiny, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
