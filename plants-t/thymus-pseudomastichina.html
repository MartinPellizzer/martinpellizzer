Thymus pseudomastichina

Thymus pseudomastichina, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves that are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus pseudomastichina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
