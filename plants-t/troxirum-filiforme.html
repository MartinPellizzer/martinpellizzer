Troxirum filiforme

Troxirum filiforme, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a small, delicate grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and provides habitat for small invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
