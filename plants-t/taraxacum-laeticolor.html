Taraxacum laeticolor

Taraxacum laeticolor, commonly known as the golden-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, lobed leaves. The plant produces a capsule containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly bitter taste.
