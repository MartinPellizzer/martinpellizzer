Tragacantha utahensis

Tragacantha utahensis, commonly known as Utah tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
