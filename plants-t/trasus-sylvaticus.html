Trasus sylvaticus

Trasus sylvaticus, commonly known as the forest spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blue or purple blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Trasus sylvaticus is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and visual interest.
