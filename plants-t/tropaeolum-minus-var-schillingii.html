Tropaeolum minus var. schillingii

Tropaeolum minus var. schillingii, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and bright orange or red, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its peppery taste, making it edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.
