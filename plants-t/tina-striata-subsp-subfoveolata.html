Tina striata subsp. subfoveolata

Tina striata subsp.

subfoveolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tina, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves with distinct striations.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its foliage is often covered with a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
