Turbinicarpus laui

Turbinicarpus laui, commonly known as the Laui's Turbinicarpus, is a species of succulent cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Turbinicarpus laui is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive prolonged periods of drought.
