Trifolium petrophilum

Trifolium petrophilum, commonly known as the rock clover, is a flowering plant species native to mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, purple or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
