Trigonochilum myanthum

Trigonochilum myanthum, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Its roots are often specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree trunks or rocks in its natural habitat.

Trigonochilum myanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
