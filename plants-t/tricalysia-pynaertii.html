Tricalysia pynaertii

Tricalysia pynaertii, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tricalysia pynaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
