Tanacetum huronense

Tanacetum huronense, commonly known as Huron goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Great Lakes region of North America.

It is a perennial herb with finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along lake shores and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
