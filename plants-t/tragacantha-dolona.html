Tragacantha dolona

Tragacantha dolona, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its gum, which has been used historically in medicine and as a sizing agent for textiles.
