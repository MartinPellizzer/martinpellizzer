Talbotiella velutina

Talbotiella velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Talbotiella velutina is known for its adaptability and can be both a weed and a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
