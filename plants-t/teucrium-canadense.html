Teucrium canadense

Teucrium canadense, commonly known as American germander, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant has slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually blue to violet in color, blooming in summer.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
