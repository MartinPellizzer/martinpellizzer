Trichocereus tenuispinus

Trichocereus tenuispinus, commonly known as the Pachanoi cactus or the "Jesus Christ cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, slender stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
