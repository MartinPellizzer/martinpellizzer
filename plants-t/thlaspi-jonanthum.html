Thlaspi jonanthum

Thlaspi jonanthum, commonly known as Iceland poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and is often found growing in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves. It is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Thlaspi jonanthum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
