Trichothalamus nitidus

Trichothalamus nitidus, commonly known as the shining trichothalamus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces delicate white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
