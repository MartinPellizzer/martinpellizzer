Tectaria melanocaulos

Tectaria melanocaulos, commonly known as the black-stemmed shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds with a distinctive blackish stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Tectaria melanocaulos is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
