Trias sicaria

Trias sicaria, commonly known as the "bloodroot," is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with a single, palmately lobed leaf. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Trias sicaria thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important part of the early spring flora.
