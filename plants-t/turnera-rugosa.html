Turnera rugosa

Turnera rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Turnera rugosa is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
