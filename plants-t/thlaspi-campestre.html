Thlaspi campestre

Thlaspi campestre, commonly known as field pennycress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often lobed. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides.

The seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a type of dry fruit that splits open to release the seeds.
