Thelypteris equitans

Thelypteris equitans, commonly known as the horse-tail fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and clustered fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, with sporangia clustered on the underside.

Thelypteris equitans is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
