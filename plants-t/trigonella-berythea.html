Trigonella berythea

Trigonella berythea, commonly known as Persian eggplant or Syrian eggplant, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, purple or black pods containing several seeds.

Trigonella berythea is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and also have medicinal properties.
