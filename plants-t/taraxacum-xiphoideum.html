Taraxacum xiphoideum

Taraxacum xiphoideum, commonly known as the swordleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, sword-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, upright flowering stem that bears a yellow composite flower head, which develops into a distinctive white pappus-based seed head. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
