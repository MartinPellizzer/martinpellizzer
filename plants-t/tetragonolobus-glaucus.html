Tetragonolobus glaucus

Tetragonolobus glaucus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod that splits open when mature.

Tetragonolobus glaucus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
