Trifolium elmeri

Trifolium elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. Native to western North America, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
