Treculia engleriana

Treculia engleriana, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by large, round fruits that contain a sticky, edible pulp. Its seeds are used to produce shea butter, which has economic and medicinal importance.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ecological role in its native habitats.
