Thermopsis californica var. velutina

Thermopsis californica var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf thermopsis, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
