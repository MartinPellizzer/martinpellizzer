Tamarix gallica var. erythrocarpa

Tamarix gallica var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody, red berries that are a distinctive feature of this variety. It is often found in coastal and saline soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
