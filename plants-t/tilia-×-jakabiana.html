Tilia × jakabiana

Tilia × jakabiana, commonly known as the Hungarian linden, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Tilia × jakabiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
