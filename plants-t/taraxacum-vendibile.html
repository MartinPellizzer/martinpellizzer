Taraxacum vendibile

Taraxacum vendibile, commonly known as the edible dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

The entire plant is edible, with the leaves being particularly prized in culinary and medicinal uses.
