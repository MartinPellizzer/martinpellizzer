Teucrium × ochroleucum

Teucrium × ochroleucum, commonly known as the white germander, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to other regions as an ornamental.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
