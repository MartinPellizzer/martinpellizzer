Tradescantia occidentalis var. typica

Tradescantia occidentalis var. typica, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers and grass-like, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy, three-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.
