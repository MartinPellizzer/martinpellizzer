Thalictrum marinum

Thalictrum marinum, commonly known as sea avens, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is adapted to saline and windy conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Thalictrum marinum is often found in maritime climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
