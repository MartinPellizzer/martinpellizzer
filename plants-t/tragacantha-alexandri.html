Tragacantha alexandri

Tragacantha alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its most notable feature is the gelatinous resin it exudes from its bark, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

This resin, known as tragacanth, is harvested and valued for its unique properties in various industries.
