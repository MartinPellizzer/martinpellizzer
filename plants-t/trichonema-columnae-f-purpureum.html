Trichonema columnae f. purpureum

Trichonema columnae f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered spurge, is a variant of the genus Trichonema, which belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-colored flowers and succulent leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant's structure includes a short stem with opposite leaves and cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
