Trifolium cherleri var. perpusillum

Trifolium cherleri var. perpusillum, commonly known as the dwarf red clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological and agricultural systems.
