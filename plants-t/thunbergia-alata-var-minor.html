Thunbergia alata var. minor

Thunbergia alata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
