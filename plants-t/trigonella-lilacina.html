Trigonella lilacina

Trigonella lilacina, commonly known as lilac fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several oval seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Trigonella lilacina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for its potential medicinal properties.
