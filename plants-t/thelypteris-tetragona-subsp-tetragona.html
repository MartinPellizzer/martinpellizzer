Thelypteris tetragona subsp. tetragona

Thelypteris tetragona subsp.

tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive, stiff, and often glossy fronds that are typically four-angled in cross-section.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by indusium. It is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

This fern prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
