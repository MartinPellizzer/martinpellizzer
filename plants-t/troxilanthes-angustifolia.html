Troxilanthes angustifolia

Troxilanthes angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
