Trichosalpinx blaisdellii

Trichosalpinx blaisdellii, commonly known as the Blaisdell's spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flower structure with long, slender petals and a distinctive, twisted column. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, violet to purple flowers that attract specific pollinators.
