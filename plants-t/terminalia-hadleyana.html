Terminalia hadleyana

Terminalia hadleyana, commonly known as Hadley's terminalia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
