Trema canescens

Trema canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf trema, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental qualities.
