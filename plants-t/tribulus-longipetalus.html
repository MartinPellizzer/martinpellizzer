Tribulus longipetalus

Tribulus longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are simple and alternate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
