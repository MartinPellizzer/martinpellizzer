Thalictrum sabaudum

Thalictrum sabaudum, commonly known as the Sabaudian meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Thalictrum sabaudum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
