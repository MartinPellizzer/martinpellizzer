Thymus villosus subsp. lusitanicus

Thymus villosus subsp.

lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage of gray-green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
