Tilia amurensis var. oligantha

Tilia amurensis var. oligantha, commonly known as Amur honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
