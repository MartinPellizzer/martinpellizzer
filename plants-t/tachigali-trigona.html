Tachigali trigona

Tachigali trigona, commonly known as the "Brazilian tachigali," is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree has pinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
