Tragacantha chlorocyanea

Tragacantha chlorocyanea, commonly known as the blue-green tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed.

Tragacantha chlorocyanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
