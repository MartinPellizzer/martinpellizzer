Tephrosia benthamii

Tephrosia benthamii, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
