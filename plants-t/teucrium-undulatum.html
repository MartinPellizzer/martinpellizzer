Teucrium undulatum

Teucrium undulatum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly minty scent.

Teucrium undulatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
