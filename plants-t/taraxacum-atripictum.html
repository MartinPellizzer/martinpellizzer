Taraxacum atripictum

Taraxacum atripictum, commonly known as the black dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.

It is sometimes considered a weed but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
