Trichopteryx glabra

Trichopteryx glabra, commonly known as the smooth trichopteryx, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within its native range.
