Tephrosia viguieri

Tephrosia viguieri, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
