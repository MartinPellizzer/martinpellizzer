Tragacantha melitenensis

Tragacantha melitenensis, commonly known as the Melitene tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and small, compound leaves that help it adapt to arid environments.
