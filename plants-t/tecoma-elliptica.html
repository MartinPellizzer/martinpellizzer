Tecoma elliptica

Tecoma elliptica, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Tecoma elliptica is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with a woody trunk and a spreading canopy.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
