Teucrium royleanum

Teucrium royleanum, commonly known as Kashmir tea or wild thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their medicinal properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic oils and medicinal plants.
