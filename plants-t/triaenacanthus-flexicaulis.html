Triaenacanthus flexicaulis

Triaenacanthus flexicaulis, commonly known as the spiral-curved thornplant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral growth pattern, which gives it a striking, almost helical appearance. The plant features slender, woody stems with sharp, spiny projections along its surface. Its leaves are small and alternate, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
