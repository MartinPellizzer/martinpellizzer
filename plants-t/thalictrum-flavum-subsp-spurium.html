Thalictrum flavum subsp. spurium

Thalictrum flavum subsp.

spurium, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Thalictrum flavum subsp.

spurium is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
