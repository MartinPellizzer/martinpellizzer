Thompsonella platyphylla

Thompsonella platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed thompsonella, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and flat, adapted to store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle aesthetic to its otherwise low-profile appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
