Thalictrum strigillosum

Thalictrum strigillosum, commonly known as the stinging avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely textured and covered in a soft, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Thalictrum strigillosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
