Themeda unica

Themeda unica, commonly known as the slender grass or slender Mitchell grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant is typically found in open grasslands and sandy soils, thriving in regions with a temperate to subtropical climate. Themeda unica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its seeds are an important food source for various bird and small mammal species.
