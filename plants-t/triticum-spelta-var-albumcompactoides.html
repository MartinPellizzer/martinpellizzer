Triticum spelta var. albumcompactoides

Triticum spelta var. albumcompactoides, commonly known as hulless barley, is a variant of spelt wheat characterized by its lack of a hull, making it easier to process.

It is a cereal grain with a high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of protein, fiber, and essential minerals. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes that bear small, white to pale yellow florets.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
