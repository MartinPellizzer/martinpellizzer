Trifolium montanense

Trifolium montanense, commonly known as mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a pale green or white midrib. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Trifolium montanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
