Tephrocactus diadematus

Tephrocactus diadematus, commonly known as the diadem cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the stem.

Tephrocactus diadematus is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.
