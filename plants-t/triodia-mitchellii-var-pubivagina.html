Triodia mitchellii var. pubivagina

Triodia mitchellii var. pubivagina, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils. The plant has wiry, spiky leaves that help reduce water loss and protect against herbivory. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Its reproductive structures are typically small and inconspicuous, relying on wind for seed dispersal.
