Thymus hirtus var. antiatlanticus

Thymus hirtus var. antiatlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
