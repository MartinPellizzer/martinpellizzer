Thea transarisanensis

Thea transarisanensis, commonly known as the Transarisan tea plant, is a rare species native to the high-altitude regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its unique chemical composition, which contributes to its distinct flavor profile in tea production. Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered a conservation concern.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for both horticultural and ecological research.
