Turnera macrophylla

Turnera macrophylla, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

The leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
