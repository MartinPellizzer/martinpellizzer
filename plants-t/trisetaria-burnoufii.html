Trisetaria burnoufii

Trisetaria burnoufii, commonly known as the Burnouf's hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms dense, mat-like clumps. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is well adapted to dry environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain grassland ecosystems.
