Tabebuia conferta

Tabebuia conferta, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has a straight, smooth trunk and produces rounded, palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often planted for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and can be harvested for medicinal purposes in some regions.
