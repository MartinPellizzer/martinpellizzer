Thea brevistyla

Thea brevistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea plant, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cooler climates.

The leaves can be brewed into a mild tea, adding to its cultural and practical significance.
