Triodia cunninghamii

Triodia cunninghamii, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a hardy perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant forms low-growing, tufted clumps with needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Triodia cunninghamii is well adapted to withstand drought and is a key component of many Australian grassland ecosystems.
