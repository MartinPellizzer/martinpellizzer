Teucrium lusitanicum subsp. lusitanicum

Teucrium lusitanicum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
