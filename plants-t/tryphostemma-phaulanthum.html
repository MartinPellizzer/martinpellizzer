Tryphostemma phaulanthum

Tryphostemma phaulanthum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn reddish upon ripening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
