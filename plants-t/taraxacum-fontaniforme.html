Taraxacum fontaniforme

Taraxacum fontaniforme, commonly known as the Fontana dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
