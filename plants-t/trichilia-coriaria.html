Trichilia coriaria

Trichilia coriaria, commonly known as African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in furniture and construction.

Trichilia coriaria also plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest ecosystems.
