Triodia brizoides

Triodia brizoides, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Triodia brizoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
