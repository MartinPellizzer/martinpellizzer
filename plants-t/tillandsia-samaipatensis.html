Tillandsia samaipatensis

Tillandsia samaipatensis, commonly known as the Samaipata air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments where it attaches itself to trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom once in its lifetime.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after specimen among bromeliad enthusiasts and conservationists.
