Trichoglottis scaphigera

Trichoglottis scaphigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Trichoglottis scaphigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
