Thunbergia subsagittata

Thunbergia subsagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a violet or purple throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia subsagittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
