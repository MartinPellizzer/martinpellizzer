Triphaca

Triphaca, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-like growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are usually velvety and thick, often with a striking coloration that adds to the plant's ornamental value. Triphaca species are typically grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid environments.

They are popular for their ability to bloom continuously under proper care.
