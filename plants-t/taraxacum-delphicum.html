Taraxacum delphicum

Taraxacum delphicum, commonly known as the Delphic dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in dry, rocky, and calcareous soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
