Tricuspis latifolia

Tricuspis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed tricuspid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
