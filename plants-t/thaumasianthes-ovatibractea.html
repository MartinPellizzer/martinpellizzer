Thaumasianthes ovatibractea

Thaumasianthes ovatibractea, commonly known as the giant bract orchid, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, and its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its complex inflorescence structure. Its botanical significance lies in its rare morphology and ecological role within its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
