Trisetaria macrochaeta var. boissieri

Trisetaria macrochaeta var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and rocky slopes.
