Thymus klokovii

Thymus klokovii, commonly known as Klokov's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is closely related to other thyme species and shares similar aromatic properties.

Thymus klokovii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and medicinal potential.
