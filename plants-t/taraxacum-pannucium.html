Taraxacum pannucium

Taraxacum pannucium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces a single, tall flowering stem topped with a spherical inflorescence of yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting insect life.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
