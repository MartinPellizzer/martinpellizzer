Taraxacum pseudoalpestre

Taraxacum pseudoalpestre, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow composite flower. This species is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found at high elevations.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
