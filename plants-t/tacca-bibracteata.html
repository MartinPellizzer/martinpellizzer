Tacca bibracteata

Tacca bibracteata, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spatulate-shaped leaves that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces striking, violet-colored flowers with a unique, almost fleshy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Tacca bibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unusual and exotic foliage.
