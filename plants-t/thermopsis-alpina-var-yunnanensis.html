Thermopsis alpina var. yunnanensis

Thermopsis alpina var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan thermopsis, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil, and it serves as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
