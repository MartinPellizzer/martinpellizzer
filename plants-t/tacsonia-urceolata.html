Tacsonia urceolata

Tacsonia urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-like structure formed by its leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, Tacsonia urceolata is a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
