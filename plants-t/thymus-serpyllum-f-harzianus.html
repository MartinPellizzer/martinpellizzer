Thymus serpyllum f. harzianus

Thymus serpyllum f. harzianus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, small, grey-green leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
