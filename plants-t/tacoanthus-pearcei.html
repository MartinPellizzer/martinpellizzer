Tacoanthus pearcei

Tacoanthus pearcei, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the spring and early summer. The plant has a short, thick stem with spiny ribs, adapted to store water and withstand extreme temperatures. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Tacoanthus pearcei plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small desert animals.
