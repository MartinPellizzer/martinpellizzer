Trisetum cavanillesianum

Trisetum cavanillesianum, commonly known as the Cavanilles' hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Trisetum cavanillesianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
