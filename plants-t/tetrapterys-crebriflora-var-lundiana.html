Tetrapterys crebriflora var. lundiana

Tetrapterys crebriflora var. lundiana, commonly known as the Lund's clustered leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often borne in dense inflorescences, making it visually striking. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its foliage and floral display.
