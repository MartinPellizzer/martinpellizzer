Tetrapterys mexicana var. grandiflora

Tetrapterys mexicana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
