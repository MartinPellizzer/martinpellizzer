Triumfetta sloanei

Triumfetta sloanei, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
