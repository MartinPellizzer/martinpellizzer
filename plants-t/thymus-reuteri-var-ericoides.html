Thymus reuteri var. ericoides

Thymus reuteri var. ericoides, commonly known as the ericoid thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a slightly hairy texture, and its small, fragrant flowers are typically pink or lavender in color. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are highly aromatic, containing essential oils responsible for its distinctive thyme scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature allows it to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, enhancing its adaptability in various garden settings.
