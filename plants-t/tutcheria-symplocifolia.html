Tutcheria symplocifolia

Tutcheria symplocifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered tutcheria, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with dense, leathery leaves that are often red or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Tutcheria symplocifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
