Thymus teucrioides var. candilicus

Thymus teucrioides var. candilicus, commonly known as the Italian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
