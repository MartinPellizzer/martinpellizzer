Thymus serpyllum subsp. arcticus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

arcticus, commonly known as Arctic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It produces small, fragrant, yellowish flowers and has narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
