Taraxacum pamiricum

Taraxacum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir dandelion, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Taraxacum pamiricum is adapted to survive in extreme climates, making it a resilient and hardy species.
