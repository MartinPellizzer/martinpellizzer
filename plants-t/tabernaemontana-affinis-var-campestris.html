Tabernaemontana affinis var. campestris

Tabernaemontana affinis var. campestris, commonly known as the campestris star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
