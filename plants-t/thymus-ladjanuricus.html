Thymus ladjanuricus

Thymus ladjanuricus, commonly known as Ladjanuric thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pink to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant has aromatic leaves that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Thymus ladjanuricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
