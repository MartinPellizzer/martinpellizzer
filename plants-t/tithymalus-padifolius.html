Tithymalus padifolius

Tithymalus padifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slight pubescence, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
