Teucrium coahuilanum

Teucrium coahuilanum, commonly known as Mexican germander, is a perennial herb native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slight woolly texture, and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. It is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Teucrium coahuilanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
