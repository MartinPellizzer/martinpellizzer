Tragacantha karabaghensis

Tragacantha karabaghensis, commonly known as Karabakh tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive pod fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant's latex has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
