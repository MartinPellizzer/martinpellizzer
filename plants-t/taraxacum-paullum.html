Taraxacum paullum

Taraxacum paullum, commonly known as the Paul's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, which is dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun.

The leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually hairy and spoon-shaped.
