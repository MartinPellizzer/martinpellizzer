Tulipa subbiflora

Tulipa subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a perennial bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
