Tinus depressa

Tinus depressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tinus depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
