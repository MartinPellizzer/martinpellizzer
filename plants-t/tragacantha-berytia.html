Tragacantha berytia

Tragacantha berytia, commonly known as the Berytian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a gum known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
