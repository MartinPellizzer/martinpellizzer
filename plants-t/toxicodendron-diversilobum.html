Toxicodendron diversilobum

Toxicodendron diversilobum, commonly known as poison oak, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant contains urushiol, a potent allergenic oil that causes skin irritation and rashes in many people. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, disturbed soils, and coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided or handled with caution.
