Thelypodium ovalifolium

Thelypodium ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf thelypodium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Thelypodium ovalifolium is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
