Tsuga canadensis var. pendula

Tsuga canadensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping hemlock, is a unique variant of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. It produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds.

This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cool, shaded environments.
