Tecoma subvernicosa

Tecoma subvernicosa, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue.

Tecoma subvernicosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, sunny environments.
