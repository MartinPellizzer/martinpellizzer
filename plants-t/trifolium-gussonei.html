Trifolium gussonei

Trifolium gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's clover, is a rare and flowering plant species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets arranged in a heart-shaped pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
