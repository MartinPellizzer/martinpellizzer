Triodia aristiglumis

Triodia aristiglumis, commonly known as the slender spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tussock-forming habit and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native fauna.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandplains.
