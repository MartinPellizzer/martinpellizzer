Tragacantha drummondii

Tragacantha drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
