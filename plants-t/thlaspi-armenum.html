Thlaspi armenum

Thlaspi armenum, commonly known as Armenian cabbages, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Thlaspi armenum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
