Trigonella sibthorpii

Trigonella sibthorpii, commonly known as the Afghan fennel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
