Tithonia speciosa

Tithonia speciosa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it features showy ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Tithonia speciosa is an annual plant that thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

It also has some agricultural uses, as it can serve as a green manure crop and attract pollinators to gardens.
