Tuberaria bupleurifolia var. longipes

Tuberaria bupleurifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed tuberaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of mountain flora.
