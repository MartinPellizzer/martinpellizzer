Thymus piligerus

Thymus piligerus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.

Thymus piligerus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
