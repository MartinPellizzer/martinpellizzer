Teucrium polium var. corymbiferum

Teucrium polium var. corymbiferum, commonly known as the clustered germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
