Teucrium aladagense

Teucrium aladagense, commonly known as Aladag germander, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium aladagense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
