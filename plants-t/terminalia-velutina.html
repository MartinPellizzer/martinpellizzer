Terminalia velutina

Terminalia velutina, commonly known as African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local craftsmanship and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and soil conservation in its native habitat.
