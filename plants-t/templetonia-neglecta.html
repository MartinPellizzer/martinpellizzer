Templetonia neglecta

Templetonia neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.

The tree is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
