Taraxacum lugubre

Taraxacum lugubre, commonly known as the gloomy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears a single flower head. The flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the dandelion genus. It produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that releases seeds with parachute-like structures.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
