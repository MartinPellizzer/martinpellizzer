Thalictrum transsilvanicum

Thalictrum transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces slender, wiry stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Thalictrum transsilvanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
