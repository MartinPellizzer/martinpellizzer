Trichopilia

Trichopilia, commonly known as the "hairy stork's bill," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, often hairy leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble a stork's bill. The genus includes several species, with Trichopilia tetrandra being one of the most well-known.
