Taraxacum euryanthes

Taraxacum euryanthes, commonly known as the Euryanthes dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering head composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed head, known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Taraxacum euryanthes is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
