Trigonochilum umbonatum

Trigonochilum umbonatum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms that often feature a unique, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Trigonochilum umbonatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
