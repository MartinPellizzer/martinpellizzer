Thymus karadzicensis

Thymus karadzicensis, commonly known as the Karadzic thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, typical of many thyme species.

Thymus karadzicensis is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
