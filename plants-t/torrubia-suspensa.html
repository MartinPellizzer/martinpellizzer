Torrubia suspensa

Torrubia suspensa, commonly known as the hanging torrubia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant has a unique growth habit, with its branches often appearing to hang downward, giving it its common name. It is adapted to the arid and saline conditions of its native habitat.

Torrubia suspensa is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
