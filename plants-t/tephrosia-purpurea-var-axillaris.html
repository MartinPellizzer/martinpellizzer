Tephrosia purpurea var. axillaris

Tephrosia purpurea var. axillaris, commonly known as purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a glandular surface.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
