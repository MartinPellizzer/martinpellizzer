Triticum dicoccum var. persicum

Triticum dicoccum var. persicum, commonly known as Persian emmer wheat, is a wild wheat species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and straw-colored grains that are typically harvested when dry. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that bear small, hard grains. It is a diploid species with a genome size of approximately 16.5 Gb.

This ancient grain is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.
