Trigonia

Trigonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in various shades of purple, pink, and white. Trigonia species are typically grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid environments and well-draining soil. They are popular for their ornamental value and ease of care, making them a favorite among indoor gardeners.

The genus includes several species, with Trigonia sinuata being one of the most commonly cultivated varieties.
