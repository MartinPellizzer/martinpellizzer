Teucrium serpylloides

Teucrium serpylloides, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and meadows.

Teucrium serpylloides is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
