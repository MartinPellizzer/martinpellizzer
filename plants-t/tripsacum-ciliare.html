Tripsacum ciliare

Tripsacum ciliare, commonly known as the silky wild barley, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with fine hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical or subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Tripsacum ciliare is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
