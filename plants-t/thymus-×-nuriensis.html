Thymus × nuriensis

Thymus × nuriensis, commonly known as the Korean thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties.

Thymus × nuriensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and mild thyme scent.
