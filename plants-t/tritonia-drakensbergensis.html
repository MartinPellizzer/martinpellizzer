Tritonia drakensbergensis

Tritonia drakensbergensis, commonly known as the Drakensberg bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Drakensberg Mountains in South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats and is adapted to the region's cool, moist climate. Tritonia drakensbergensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
