Tamarix angustifolia

Tamarix angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a common species in coastal and inland desert regions.

Tamarix angustifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, which can influence soil composition in its native habitat.
