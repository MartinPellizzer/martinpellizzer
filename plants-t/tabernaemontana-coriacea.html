Tabernaemontana coriacea

Tabernaemontana coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and has dark green, leathery leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
