Thismia tectipora

Thismia tectipora, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph that relies on fungal associations for nutrients. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often hidden within dense vegetation. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and swampy areas.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
