Thalictrum majus subsp. billotii

Thalictrum majus subsp.

billotii, commonly known as the billot's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. It produces numerous slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
