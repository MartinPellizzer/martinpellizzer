Tetramolopium filiforme var. filiforme

Tetramolopium filiforme var. filiforme, commonly known as the slender tetramolopium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its linear, filamentous leaves and small, yellowish flowers contribute to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
