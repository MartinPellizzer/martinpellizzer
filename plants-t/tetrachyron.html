Tetrachyron

Tetrachyron, commonly known as the four-leaf clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that often grow in a rosette pattern. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Its most notable feature is the occasional presence of a fourth leaf, which is considered a symbol of good luck in many cultures.

The flowers are tiny and inconspicuous, typically appearing in early spring.
