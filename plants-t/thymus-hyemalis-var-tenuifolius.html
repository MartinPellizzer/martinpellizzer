Thymus hyemalis var. tenuifolius

Thymus hyemalis var. tenuifolius, commonly known as winter thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
