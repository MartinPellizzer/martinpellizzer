Tulista pumila

Tulista pumila, commonly known as the dwarf tulip, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tulista pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
