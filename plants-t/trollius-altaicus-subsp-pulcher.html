Trollius altaicus subsp. pulcher

Trollius altaicus subsp.

pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow, ray-like flowers that are typically arranged in a single, flat, cup-shaped head.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
