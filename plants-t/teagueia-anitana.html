Teagueia anitana

Teagueia anitana, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
