Toxicodendron eximium

Toxicodendron eximium, commonly known as the African lacquer tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
