Teucrium gnaphalodes

Teucrium gnaphalodes, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
