Tragacantha striata

Tragacantha striata, commonly known as the striated tragacanth, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by long, twisted pods containing seeds. Its root system is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
