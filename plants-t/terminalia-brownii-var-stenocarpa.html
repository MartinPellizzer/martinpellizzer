Terminalia brownii var. stenocarpa

Terminalia brownii var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the African wild date or African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and elongated, clustered fruit pods that contain a sweet, sticky pulp. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edge habitats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The fruit is an important food source for various animals and has traditional uses in local communities.
