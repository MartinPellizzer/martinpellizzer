Thymus areophilus

Thymus areophilus, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fragrant leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic oils. Its leaves contain essential oils rich in thymol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus areophilus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal cultivation.
