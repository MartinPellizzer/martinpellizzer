Tragacantha baghensis

Tragacantha baghensis, commonly known as the Kashmir tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing angular, seed-filled capsules. Its roots are known for secreting a gelatinous substance, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
