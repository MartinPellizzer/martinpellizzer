Tephrosia dietrichiae

Tephrosia dietrichiae, commonly known as the dwarf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in small, pod-like fruits, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Tephrosia dietrichiae is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
