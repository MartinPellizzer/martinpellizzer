Thalictrum gallicum proles paradoxum

Thalictrum gallicum proles paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical European meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a slender stem that often branches near the base, and its flowers are borne in loose, upright clusters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
