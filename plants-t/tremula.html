Tremula

Tremula, commonly known as trembling aspen, is a deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves that shimmer with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its root system forms a network of interconnected roots, allowing it to share nutrients with neighboring trees.

Tremula is often found in moist, temperate forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
