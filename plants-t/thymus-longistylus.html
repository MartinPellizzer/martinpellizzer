Thymus longistylus

Thymus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its fragrant, mint-like scent.

Thymus longistylus is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
