Teucrium algarbiense

Teucrium algarbiense, commonly known as the Algerian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium algarbiense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
