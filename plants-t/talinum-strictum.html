Talinum strictum

Talinum strictum, commonly known as the red-rooted spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features striking red or purple roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in terminal spikes.

Talinum strictum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
