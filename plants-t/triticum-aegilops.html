Triticum aegilops

Triticum aegilops, commonly known as goatgrass, is a wild relative of wheat that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and spikelets that bear small, pale grains. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Triticum aegilops is known for its genetic diversity, which makes it a valuable resource for wheat breeding programs.
