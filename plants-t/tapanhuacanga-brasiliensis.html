Tapanhuacanga brasiliensis

Tapanhuacanga brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often marked with fissures.

Tapanhuacanga brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in parks and gardens.
