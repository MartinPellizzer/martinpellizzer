Tinus erecta

Tinus erecta, commonly known as the silver tree, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, deciduous tree characterized by its silvery-gray bark and narrow, upright leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
