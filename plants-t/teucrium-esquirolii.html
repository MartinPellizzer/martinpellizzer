Teucrium esquirolii

Teucrium esquirolii, commonly known as Esquiroli's germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Teucrium esquirolii is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential therapeutic uses.
