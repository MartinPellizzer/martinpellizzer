Tragacantha rubens

Tragacantha rubens, commonly known as the red tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers. The plant produces a unique gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its pods. This gum is used in various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics, due to its thickening and stabilizing properties.

Tragacantha rubens thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in shrublands and coastal areas.
