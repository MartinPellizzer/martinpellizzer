Tribulus terrestris var. orientalis

Tribulus terrestris var. orientalis, commonly known as puncture vine, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces small, yellow flowers and spiny, three-lobed fruits. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and its ability to thrive in dry, disturbed soils. It is often found in arid environments and is known for its hardy, resilient nature.

The fruit contains seeds that are dispersed by animals and can persist in the soil for several years.
