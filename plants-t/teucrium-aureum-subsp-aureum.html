Teucrium aureum subsp. aureum

Teucrium aureum subsp.

aureum, commonly known as golden germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in whorled clusters along the stem, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
