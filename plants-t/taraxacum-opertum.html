Taraxacum opertum

Taraxacum opertum, commonly known as the closed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, spherical seed head that is enclosed by a persistent, leafy bract, giving it its common name. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy perennial.
