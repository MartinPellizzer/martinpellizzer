Tragacantha floccosa

Tragacantha floccosa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus floccosus, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive fluffy seed pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
