Trichodesma hemsleyanum

Trichodesma hemsleyanum, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
