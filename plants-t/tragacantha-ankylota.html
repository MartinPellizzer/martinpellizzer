Tragacantha ankylota

Tragacantha ankylota, commonly known as the Armenian tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is exuded from the stems and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its growth habit includes a fibrous root system and a branched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Tragacantha ankylota thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.
