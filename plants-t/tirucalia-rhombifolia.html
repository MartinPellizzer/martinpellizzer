Tirucalia rhombifolia

Tirucalia rhombifolia, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
