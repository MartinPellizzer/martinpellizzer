Thunbergia adjumaensis

Thunbergia adjumaensis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue and purple tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Thunbergia adjumaensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
