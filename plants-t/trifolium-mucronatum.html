Trifolium mucronatum

Trifolium mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trifolium mucronatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural and ecological purposes.
