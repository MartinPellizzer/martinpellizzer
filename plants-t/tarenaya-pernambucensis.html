Tarenaya pernambucensis

Tarenaya pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco grass, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, sunny areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
