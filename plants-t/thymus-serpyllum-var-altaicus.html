Thymus serpyllum var. altaicus

Thymus serpyllum var. altaicus, commonly known as the Altai thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces pale violet to white flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
