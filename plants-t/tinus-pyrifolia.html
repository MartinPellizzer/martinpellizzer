Tinus pyrifolia

Tinus pyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous fruit-bearing shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its fruit is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it both nutritionally valuable and economically significant.

Tinus pyrifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
