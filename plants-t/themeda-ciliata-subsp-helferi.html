Themeda ciliata subsp. helferi

Themeda ciliata subsp.

helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
