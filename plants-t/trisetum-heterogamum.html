Trisetum heterogamum

Trisetum heterogamum, commonly known as the variable beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including open woods and prairies.

Trisetum heterogamum is often found in moist to dry soil conditions and is adapted to temperate climates.
