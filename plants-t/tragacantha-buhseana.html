Tragacantha buhseana

Tragacantha buhseana, commonly known as the Buhse's tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
