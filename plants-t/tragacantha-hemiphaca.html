Tragacantha hemiphaca

Tragacantha hemiphaca, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of pods containing seeds.

It is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments and for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum resin.
