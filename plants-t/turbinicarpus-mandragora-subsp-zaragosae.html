Turbinicarpus mandragora subsp. zaragosae

Turbinicarpus mandragora subsp.

zaragosae, commonly known as the Zaragoza Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to Spain. It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the apex of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species.
