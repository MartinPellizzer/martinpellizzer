Tropaeolum porifolium

Tropaeolum porifolium, commonly known as the porous-leaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that have a porous or spongy texture, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum porifolium is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
