Triplaris schomburgkiana

Triplaris schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's triplaris, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
