Trichotosia caespitosa

Trichotosia caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces distinctive reddish inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Trichotosia caespitosa is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
