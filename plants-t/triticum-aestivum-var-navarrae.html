Triticum aestivum var. navarrae

Triticum aestivum var. navarrae, commonly known as Navarra wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic makeup and morphological traits.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and exhibits distinct agronomic features that make it suitable for specific environmental conditions. The plant typically grows to a moderate height with semi-erect tillers and produces small, hard grains. Its genetic diversity contributes to its potential use in breeding programs for improved resilience and yield.

Navarra wheat is of interest for its role in regional agriculture and its adaptability to diverse climates.
