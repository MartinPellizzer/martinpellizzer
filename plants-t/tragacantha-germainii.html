Tragacantha germainii

Tragacantha germainii, commonly known as the Germain's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it is adapted to survive in dry environments with deep root systems.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
