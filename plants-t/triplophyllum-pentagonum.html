Triplophyllum pentagonum

Triplophyllum pentagonum, commonly known as the five-angled triplophyllum, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal leaf structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Despite its limited distribution, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
