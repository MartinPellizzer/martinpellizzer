Trapa pseudincisa var. nanchangensis

Trapa pseudincisa var. nanchangensis, commonly known as the Nanchang water chestnut, is a rare aquatic plant native to China.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive spiny fruits that mature underwater. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are typically round with a waxy surface, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
