Tsuga canadensis f. fremdii

Tsuga canadensis f. fremdii, commonly known as the Fremont's hemlock, is a rare and relict form of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, dark green needles and a more open, less dense foliage compared to the typical hemlock. This form is native to a limited area in the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada. It exhibits unique morphological traits, including shorter shoots and a more irregular branching pattern.

The plant is of particular interest to botanists due to its genetic and ecological significance in its native habitat.
