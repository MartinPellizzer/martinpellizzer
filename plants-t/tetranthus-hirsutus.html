Tetranthus hirsutus

Tetranthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy tetranthus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Tetranthus hirsutus is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, rich soils.
