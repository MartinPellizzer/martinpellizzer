Tilia rubra subsp. caucasica

Tilia rubra subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian lime, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, oval fruits. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
