Tridentea jucunda

Tridentea jucunda, commonly known as the three-toothed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Tridentea jucunda is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
