Trigonella gracilis

Trigonella gracilis, commonly known as slender fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Trigonella gracilis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
