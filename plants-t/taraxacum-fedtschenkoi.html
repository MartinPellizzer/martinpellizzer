Taraxacum fedtschenkoi

Taraxacum fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and rocky environments. Its scientific name honors the Russian botanist Vladimir Fedtschenko, who contributed to the study of Central Asian flora.

The plant is notable for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
