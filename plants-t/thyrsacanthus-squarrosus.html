Thyrsacanthus squarrosus

Thyrsacanthus squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose thyrsothamnus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
