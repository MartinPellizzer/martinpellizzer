Thelypteris gomeziana

Thelypteris gomeziana, commonly known as the Gomez's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brownish scales, which help in water retention.

Thelypteris gomeziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
