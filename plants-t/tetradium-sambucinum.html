Tetradium sambucinum

Tetradium sambucinum, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruiting bodies.

The berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also popular in ornamental horticulture.
