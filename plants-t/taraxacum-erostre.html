Taraxacum erostre

Taraxacum erostre, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and a bright purple to reddish hue in its flower heads. The plant produces a single, showy flower that develops into a composite seed head, which is commonly known as a "dandelion puff.". Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.

Taraxacum erostre is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
