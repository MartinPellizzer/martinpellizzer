Thalictrum pennellii

Thalictrum pennellii, commonly known as the Pennsylvania rue-anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully expand, adding a touch of elegance to woodland gardens.

Thalictrum pennellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small woodland creatures.
