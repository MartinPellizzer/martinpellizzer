Terminalia benzoe

Terminalia benzoe, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, orange-red fruits. The fruits contain aromatic, dark brown seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Terminalia benzoe is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
