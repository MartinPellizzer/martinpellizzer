Thalictrum fendleri var. wrightii

Thalictrum fendleri var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves that are feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
