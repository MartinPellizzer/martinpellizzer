Tachiadenus vohimavensis

Tachiadenus vohimavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing features that help it conserve water. It has a unique growth habit with a low, spreading stem and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
