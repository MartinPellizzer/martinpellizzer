Thymus rasitatus

Thymus rasitatus, commonly known as the Turkish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
