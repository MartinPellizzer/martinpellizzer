Trigonostemon pinnatus

Trigonostemon pinnatus, commonly known as the hairy-leafed ironwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

Trigonostemon pinnatus thrives in humid, tropical climates and is commonly found in lowland forests.
