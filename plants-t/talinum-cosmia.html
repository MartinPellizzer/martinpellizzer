Talinum cosmia

Talinum cosmia, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist soil.

Talinum cosmia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
