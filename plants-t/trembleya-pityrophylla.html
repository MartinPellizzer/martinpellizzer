Trembleya pityrophylla

Trembleya pityrophylla, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Trembleya pityrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
