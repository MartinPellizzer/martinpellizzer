Thalictrum glaucescens

Thalictrum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue false poplar, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically bright green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, white, pendulous flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Thalictrum glaucescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and subtle floral display.
