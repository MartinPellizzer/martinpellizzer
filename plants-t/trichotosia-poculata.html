Trichotosia poculata

Trichotosia poculata, commonly known as the pink sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. Trichotosia poculata thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
