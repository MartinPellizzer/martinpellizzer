Tsuga japonica

Tsuga japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Tsuga in the family Pinaceae, which includes other hemlock species. The tree is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and its needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals. Its cones are small and woody, typically found at the ends of the branches.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
