Thelymitra xanthotricha

Thelymitra xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathland and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, erect stem with a few lance-shaped leaves, and its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow flowers with long, hairy sepals and petals. The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the species is known for its striking, sun-like appearance.

It is a relatively small orchid, with flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.
