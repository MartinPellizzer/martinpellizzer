Tibouchina obtusifolia

Tibouchina obtusifolia, commonly known as Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Tibouchina obtusifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
