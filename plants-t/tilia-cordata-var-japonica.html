Tilia cordata var. japonica

Tilia cordata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese lime or Japanese linden, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
