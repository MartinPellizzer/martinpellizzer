Tillandsia vestita

Tillandsia vestita, commonly known as the white-tipped airplant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
