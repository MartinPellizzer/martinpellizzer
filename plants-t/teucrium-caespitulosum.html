Teucrium caespitulosum

Teucrium caespitulosum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Teucrium caespitulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
