Tricyrtis chinensis

Tricyrtis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet or blue-purple blossoms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Tricyrtis chinensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
