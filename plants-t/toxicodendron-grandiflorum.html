Toxicodendron grandiflorum

Toxicodendron grandiflorum, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red or orange fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

However, the plant contains irritants that can cause skin reactions in some individuals.
