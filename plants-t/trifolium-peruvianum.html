Trifolium peruvianum

Trifolium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Peru, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium peruvianum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
