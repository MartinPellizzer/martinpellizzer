Tylophora hirsuta var. penangensis

Tylophora hirsuta var. penangensis, commonly known as Penang hairy tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
