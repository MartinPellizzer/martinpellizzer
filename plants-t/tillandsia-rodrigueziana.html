Tillandsia rodrigueziana

Tillandsia rodrigueziana, commonly known as the Rodriguezia air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in capturing water and sunlight. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
