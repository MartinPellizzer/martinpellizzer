Typhonium khandwaense

Typhonium khandwaense, commonly known as the Khandwa arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces edible tubers that are used in local cuisines, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. The species is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
