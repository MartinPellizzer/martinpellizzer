Thelymitra juncifolia

Thelymitra juncifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaf sun-orchid, is an Australian terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in heathland and grassy areas. The plant produces a spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. Thelymitra juncifolia is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically significant component of its native habitats.
