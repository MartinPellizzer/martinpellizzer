Tragacantha flaccida

Tragacantha flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial and culinary applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation and traditional purposes.
