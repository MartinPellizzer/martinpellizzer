Tachigali vaupesiana

Tachigali vaupesiana, commonly known as the "Brazilian wisteria," is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Tachigali vaupesiana also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
