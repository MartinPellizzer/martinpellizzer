Tilia leucocarpa f. attenuata

Tilia leucocarpa f. attenuata, commonly known as the white-barked linden, is a unique form of the white-barked linden tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, smooth bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated edge and are typically a bright green during the growing season. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
