Tibouchina exasperata

Tibouchina exasperata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring bright red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish-brown bark. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Tibouchina exasperata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
