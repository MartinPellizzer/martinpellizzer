Typha angustifolia lusus uechtritzii

Typha angustifolia lusus uechtritzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a variant of the common cattail species.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical, spike-like flower heads. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable indicator species for wetland health.
