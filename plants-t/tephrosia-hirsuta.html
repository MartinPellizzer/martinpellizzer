Tephrosia hirsuta

Tephrosia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Tephrosia hirsuta is also noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
