Trifolium oblongifolium

Trifolium oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium oblongifolium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
