Trachymene pilbarensis

Trachymene pilbarensis, commonly known as the Pilbara false daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy soils and arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to survive in low-nutrient, drought-prone conditions.

Trachymene pilbarensis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
