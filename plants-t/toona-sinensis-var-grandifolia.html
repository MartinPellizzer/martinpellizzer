Toona sinensis var. grandifolia

Toona sinensis var. grandifolia, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree or *Sapium sebiferum*, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically 5-10 cm in diameter and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can become invasive.

The plant has a smooth, pale bark and is known for its rapid growth rate.
