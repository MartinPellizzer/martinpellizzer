Tragacantha ancyclea

Tragacantha ancyclea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing seeds.

It is well-known for its ability to produce a resin, which has been historically used in various traditional and medicinal applications.
