Tulotis asiatica

Tulotis asiatica, commonly known as the Asian tulotis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a soft, hairy texture.

Tulotis asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
