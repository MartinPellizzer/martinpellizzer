Taxodium japonicum var. heterophyllum

Taxodium japonicum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tapering trunk and its distinctive exfoliating bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The leaves are typically scale-like and closely pressed to the branches, giving the tree a unique appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.
