Tanacetum sorbifolium

Tanacetum sorbifolium, commonly known as the sorb leaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates.

Tanacetum sorbifolium is known for its strong, camphor-like scent, which is attributed to its high content of essential oils.
