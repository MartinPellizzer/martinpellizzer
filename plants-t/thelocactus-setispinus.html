Thelocactus setispinus

Thelocactus setispinus, commonly known as the spiny locactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

The locactus setispinus is often cultivated for its unique morphology and resilience in dry conditions.
