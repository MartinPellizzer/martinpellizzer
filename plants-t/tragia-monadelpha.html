Tragia monadelpha

Tragia monadelpha, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
