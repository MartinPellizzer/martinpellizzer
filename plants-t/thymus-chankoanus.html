Thymus chankoanus

Thymus chankoanus, commonly known as the Chanko thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal medicine.

Thymus chankoanus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.
