Trigonella petiolaris

Trigonella petiolaris, commonly known as the slender fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are arranged in a triangular pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, sandy soils.

Trigonella petiolaris is typically herbaceous, with a low-growing habit and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.
