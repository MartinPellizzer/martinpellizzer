Taraxacum braun-blanquetii f. braun-blanquetii

Taraxacum braun-blanquetii f. braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's dandelion, is a botanical variant of the common dandelion.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and serrated leaves. This plant is typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils across Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.

The subspecies is named in honor of the botanist Jean-Baptiste Braun-Blanquet.
