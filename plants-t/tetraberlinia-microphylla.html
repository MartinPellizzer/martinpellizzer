Tetraberlinia microphylla

Tetraberlinia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved tetraberlinia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Tetraberlinia microphylla is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.
