Thymus longicaulis var. salonitanus

Thymus longicaulis var. salonitanus, commonly known as the Salonic thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
