Thymus chamaedrys var. rochelianus

Thymus chamaedrys var. rochelianus, commonly known as the Rochelais thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
