Toxina

Toxina, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and features dark purple berries that are highly poisonous. The plant has showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Toxina contains tropane alkaloids, such as atropine and scopolamine, which can cause severe poisoning if ingested.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a fascinating and dangerous species in the plant kingdom.
