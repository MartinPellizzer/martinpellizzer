Taeniophyllum complanatum

Taeniophyllum complanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaved air plant, is a type of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which consists of narrow, flat leaves that help in capturing moisture from the air. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish. Its unique structure allows it to absorb nutrients and water through its leaves rather than roots.

Taeniophyllum complanatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
