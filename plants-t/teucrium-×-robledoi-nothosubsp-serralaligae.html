Teucrium × robledoi nothosubsp. serralaligae

Teucrium × robledoi nothosubsp.

serralaligae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
