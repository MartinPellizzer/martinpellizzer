Taraxacum minimum

Taraxacum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, slender flowering stem that bears a small, yellow composite flower head. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils or along pathways.

Taraxacum minimum is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing characteristics with other dandelions.
