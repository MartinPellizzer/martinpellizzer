Trianthema redimitum

Trianthema redimitum, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of coastal and rocky habitats.
