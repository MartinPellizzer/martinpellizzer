Toddalia pilosa

Toddalia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
