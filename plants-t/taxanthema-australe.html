Taxanthema australe

Taxanthema australe, commonly known as the southern taxanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, woolly leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring.

Taxanthema australe plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for native pollinators.
