Tripogon jacquemontii var. polyanthus

Tripogon jacquemontii var. polyanthus, commonly known as the Polyanthus grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Due to its compact size and visual interest, it is a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
