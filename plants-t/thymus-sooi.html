Thymus sooi

Thymus sooi, commonly known as the Sooi thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus sooi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
