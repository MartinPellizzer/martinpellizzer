Thea amplexicaulis

Thea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that often wrap around supporting structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of tea.

Thea amplexicaulis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
