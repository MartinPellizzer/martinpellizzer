Taraxacum songoricum

Taraxacum songoricum, commonly known as the Songor dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features a rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head.

The fruit is a schizocarp, producing wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution.
