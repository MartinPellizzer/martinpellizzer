Typha × provincialis

Typha × provincialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a hybrid wetland plant that thrives in shallow freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a brown, mat-like structure. The plant plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes spread extensively, allowing it to form dense stands that can dominate water margins.

Typha × provincialis is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in marshy areas.
