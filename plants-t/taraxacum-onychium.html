Taraxacum onychium

Taraxacum onychium, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head with yellow petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and meadows.

Taraxacum onychium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
