Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. capensis

Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. capensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
