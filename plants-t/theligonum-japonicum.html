Theligonum japonicum

Theligonum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
