Tachigali paniculata

Tachigali paniculata, commonly known as the yellow tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
