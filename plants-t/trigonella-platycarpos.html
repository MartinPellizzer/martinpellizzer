Trigonella platycarpos

Trigonella platycarpos, commonly known as the broad-leaved fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically broad and slightly hairy, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
