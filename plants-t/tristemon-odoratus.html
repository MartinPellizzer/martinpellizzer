Tristemon odoratus

Tristemon odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant tristemon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pink or white hue. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Tristemon odoratus is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
