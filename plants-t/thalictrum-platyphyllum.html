Thalictrum platyphyllum

Thalictrum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. Thalictrum platyphyllum is native to North America and thrives in moist, woodland environments.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
