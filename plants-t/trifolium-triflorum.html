Trifolium triflorum

Trifolium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil.

Trifolium triflorum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
