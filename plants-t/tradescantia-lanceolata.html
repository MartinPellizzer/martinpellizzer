Tradescantia lanceolata

Tradescantia lanceolata, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with striking red or purple markings along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers on slender stalks during the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular houseplant.

Its foliage is also valued for its air-purifying properties.
