Thunbergia fasciculata var. orientalis

Thunbergia fasciculata var. orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese Trumpet Vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for vertical gardening or as a climbing plant in gardens.
