Trillium erectum f. pallidandrum

Trillium erectum f. pallidandrum, commonly known as the pale-flowered wake-robin, is a rare and striking variant of the red trillium.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands, preferring rich, organic soil. It produces three erect leaves and three petals, with a showy, spurred tepal that is distinctive in appearance.

This fern-like plant is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is noted for its unique floral morphology.
