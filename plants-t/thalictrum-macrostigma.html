Thalictrum macrostigma

Thalictrum macrostigma, commonly known as the broadleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and clematis. The plant features slender, upright stems with palmately compound leaves that have five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, clustered in loose panicles, and it produces yellow or brownish follicles as its fruit.

Thalictrum macrostigma thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
