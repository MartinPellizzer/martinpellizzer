Trifolium ochroleucon proles pallidulum

Trifolium ochroleucon proles pallidulum, commonly known as the pale-flowered clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

This plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important component of soil fertility in natural ecosystems.
