Thrinax gracilis

Thrinax gracilis, commonly known as the slender needle palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the Caribbean islands.

It has a slender, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by triangular, spiny fruits. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that give it a feathery appearance.

Thrinax gracilis is adapted to coastal and dry environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
