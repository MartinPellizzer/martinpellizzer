Tephrosia elongata var. pubescens

Tephrosia elongata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its roots are known to contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing botanical characteristic.
