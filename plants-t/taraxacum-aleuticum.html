Taraxacum aleuticum

Taraxacum aleuticum, commonly known as the Aleutian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Aleutian Islands and surrounding regions of Alaska and Russia. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single yellow flower head. It produces a distinctive seed head with feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This plant is adapted to harsh, coastal environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
