Thymus barbatus

Thymus barbatus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, fuzzy foliage that is gray-green in color, with small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal medicine.
