Tripogon nicorae

Tripogon nicorae, commonly known as the Nicora's tripogon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Tripogon nicorae is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
