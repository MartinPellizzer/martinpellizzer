Teucrium kouytchense

Teucrium kouytchense, commonly known as the Caucasian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Teucrium kouytchense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
