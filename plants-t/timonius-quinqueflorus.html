Timonius quinqueflorus

Timonius quinqueflorus, commonly known as the five-flowered timonius, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that typically bear five flowers, giving it its scientific name. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces vibrant, often purple or pink, tubular flowers. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its striking floral display.
