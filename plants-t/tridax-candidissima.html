Tridax candidissima

Tridax candidissima, commonly known as white dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers are followed by fluffy white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Tridax candidissima is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
