Tectaria angelicifolia

Tectaria angelicifolia, commonly known as the angel's leaf, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark brown, wiry roots that help anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bi-lobed and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tectaria angelicifolia thrives in humid climates and is often found in forest understories where it receives dappled sunlight.
