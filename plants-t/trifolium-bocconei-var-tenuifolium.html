Trifolium bocconei var. tenuifolium

Trifolium bocconei var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, and it has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
