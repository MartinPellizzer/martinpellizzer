Trophis opaca

Trophis opaca, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has strap-like leaves and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
