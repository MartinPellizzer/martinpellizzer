Trifolium polystachyum var. psoraleoides

Trifolium polystachyum var. psoraleoides, commonly known as the Psoraleoides clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both ecological and medicinal studies.
