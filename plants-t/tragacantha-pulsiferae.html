Tragacantha pulsiferae

Tragacantha pulsiferae, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus tragacantha, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in food, medicine, and industrial applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
