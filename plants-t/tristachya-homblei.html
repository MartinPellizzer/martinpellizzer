Tristachya homblei

Tristachya homblei, commonly known as the Hombli grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Tristachya homblei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable environments.
