Thesium humifusum subsp. italicum

Thesium humifusum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as the Italian thesium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically forms dense mats of prostrate stems covered with small, narrow leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.
