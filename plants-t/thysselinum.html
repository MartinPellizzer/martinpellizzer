Thysselinum

Thysselinum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solasodine. Native to southern Africa, Thysselinum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
