Thymus serpyllum f. villosissimus

Thymus serpyllum f. villosissimus, commonly known as hairy thrift, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
