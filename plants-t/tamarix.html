Tamarix

Tamarix, commonly known as the salt cedar or tamarisk, is a genus of about 100 species of flowering plants in the family Tamaricaceae.

These shrubs or small trees are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa. They are characterized by their feathery leaves, which are often reduced to scale-like structures, and their pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. Tamarix species are adapted to saline and drought conditions, making them resilient in harsh environments.

Their rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas have led to their classification as invasive in many regions.
