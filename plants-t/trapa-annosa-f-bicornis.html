Trapa annosa f. bicornis

Trapa annosa f. bicornis, commonly known as the two-lobed water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floating leaves with two prominent lobes and its spiny, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in the summer months.

Trapa annosa f. bicornis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
