Tamarix parviflora

Tamarix parviflora, commonly known as the desert rose or saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East, Central Asia, and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, and its feathery, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in saline and poor soil conditions. It is often used for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in dry landscapes.

Tamarix parviflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded areas.
