Trichocereus gladiatus

Trichocereus gladiatus, commonly known as the San Pedro cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a smooth, green stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, nocturnal flowers that bloom in the spring. It is characterized by its spiny areoles and the presence of a milky sap.

Trichocereus gladiatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in indigenous traditions.
