Trymalium urceolare

Trymalium urceolare, commonly known as the urn-shaped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure reflects its parasitic nature, with reduced leaves and specialized structures for absorbing nutrients from its host.
