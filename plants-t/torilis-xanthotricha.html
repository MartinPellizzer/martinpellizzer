Torilis xanthotricha

Torilis xanthotricha, commonly known as yellow hairy salsify, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Torilis xanthotricha is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its edible roots, though it is not widely cultivated.
