Trigonia crotonoides

Trigonia crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Trigonia crotonoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
