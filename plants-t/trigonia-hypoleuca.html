Trigonia hypoleuca

Trigonia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered trigonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Trigonia hypoleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
