Tilia franchetiana

Tilia franchetiana, commonly known as the Franchet's linden, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain three seeds each. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, temperate forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
