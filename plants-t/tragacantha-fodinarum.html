Tragacantha fodinarum

Tragacantha fodinarum, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with a thick, often flaky bark, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
