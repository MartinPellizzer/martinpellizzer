Trichogonia salviifolia var. calva

Trichogonia salviifolia var. calva, commonly known as the woolly leafed sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
