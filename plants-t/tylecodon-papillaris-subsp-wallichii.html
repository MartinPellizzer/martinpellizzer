Tylecodon papillaris subsp. wallichii

Tylecodon papillaris subsp.

wallichii, commonly known as the Wallich's woolly aster, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
