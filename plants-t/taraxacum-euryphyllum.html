Taraxacum euryphyllum

Taraxacum euryphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed sphere. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also notable for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
