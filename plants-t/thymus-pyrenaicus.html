Thymus pyrenaicus

Thymus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Thymus pyrenaicus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
