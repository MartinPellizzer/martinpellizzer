Tribulus alatus var. acanthopterus

Tribulus alatus var. acanthopterus, commonly known as the spiny gobernadora, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, hard seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
