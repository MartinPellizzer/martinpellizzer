Trigonella filipes

Trigonella filipes, commonly known as the slender fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Trigonella filipes make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
