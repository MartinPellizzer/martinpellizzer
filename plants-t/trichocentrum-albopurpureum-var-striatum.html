Trichocentrum albopurpureum var. striatum

Trichocentrum albopurpureum var. striatum, commonly known as the streaked purple orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and its flowers are notable for their vibrant purple and white coloration. The plant produces pendulous racemes of fragrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining potting media.

Its unique striated patterns on the petals make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
