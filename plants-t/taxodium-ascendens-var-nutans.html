Taxodium ascendens var. nutans

Taxodium ascendens var. nutans, commonly known as the nuttall's bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, peeling bark that reveals a mottled, reddish-brown inner layer. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain two seeds each, and it has needle-like leaves that turn reddish-brown before falling in autumn. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas along rivers and streams.

Its ability to tolerate flooding makes it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
