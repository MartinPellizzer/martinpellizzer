Thalictrum venulosum var. confine

Thalictrum venulosum var. confine, commonly known as the western meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with compound, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native gardens.
