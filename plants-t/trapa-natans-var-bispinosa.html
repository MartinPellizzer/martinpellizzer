Trapa natans var. bispinosa

Trapa natans var. bispinosa, commonly known as the spiny water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are oval and have a waxy surface. The plant produces distinctive fruits that are covered in sharp spines, which help in seed dispersal. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
