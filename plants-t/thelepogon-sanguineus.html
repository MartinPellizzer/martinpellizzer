Thelepogon sanguineus

Thelepogon sanguineus, commonly known as the red-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with distinctive red or pinkish flowers, which are a notable feature in its botanical description. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Thelepogon sanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
