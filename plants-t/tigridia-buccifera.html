Tigridia buccifera

Tigridia buccifera, commonly known as the pink tigridia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia buccifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
