Trophaeum popelari

Trophaeum popelari, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
