Trillium × klineanum

Trillium × klineanum, commonly known as the Kline trillium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Trillium sessile and Trillium corymbosum.

It is a herbaceous perennial with three distinct, waxy, greenish-white petals and three sepals that are typically a lighter green. The plant produces a single, showy red or pink flower at the top of a short stem, which blooms in early spring. Trillium × klineanum thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is native to the eastern United States.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
