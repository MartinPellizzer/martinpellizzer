Trichocentrum loyolicum

Trichocentrum loyolicum, commonly known as the Loyola orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can grow quite long, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trichocentrum loyolicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
