Torilis leptophylla var. elongata

Torilis leptophylla var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hedge mustard, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length, arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
