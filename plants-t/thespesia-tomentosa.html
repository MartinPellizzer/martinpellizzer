Thespesia tomentosa

Thespesia tomentosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a soft, woolly texture on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, with a prominent central stamen. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it a hardy species in tropical climates.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
