Talauma arcabucoana

Talauma arcabucoana, commonly known as the Andean tree fern, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests. Its rhizome system helps it anchor firmly in rocky or soil substrates.
