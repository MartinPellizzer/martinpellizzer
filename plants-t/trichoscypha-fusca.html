Trichoscypha fusca

Trichoscypha fusca, commonly known as the yellow-eyed mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Trichoscypha fusca is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and tool-making.
