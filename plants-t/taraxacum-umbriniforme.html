Taraxacum umbriniforme

Taraxacum umbriniforme, commonly known as the dark-veined dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features heart-shaped basal leaves with dark green veins and a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds in the form of white, fluffy pappus.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
