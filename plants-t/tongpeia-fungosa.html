Tongpeia fungosa

Tongpeia fungosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated, bell-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
