Trichloris verticillata

Trichloris verticillata, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and distinctive spiraling arrangement of its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green or brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Trichloris verticillata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique appearance.
