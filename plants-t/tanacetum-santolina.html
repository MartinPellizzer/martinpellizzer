Tanacetum santolina

Tanacetum santolina, commonly known as santolina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Santolina is known for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its fragrant leaves and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and herbal use.
