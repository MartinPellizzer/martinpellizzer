Thymelaea aucheri

Thymelaea aucheri, commonly known as the Persian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
