Thunbergia java

Thunbergia java, commonly known as Java morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces showy blooms that open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia java is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
