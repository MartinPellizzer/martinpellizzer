Thlaspi luteum

Thlaspi luteum, commonly known as yellow cabbages, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white or yellowish powder, known as a pruina. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

Thlaspi luteum is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
