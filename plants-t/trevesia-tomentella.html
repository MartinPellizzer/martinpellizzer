Trevesia tomentella

Trevesia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly trevesia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Trevesia tomentella is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
