Thymus Sipyleus Subsp. Rosulans

Thymus sipyleus subsp. rosulans is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the mint family, known for its aromatic and medicinal properties. This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth and small, pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique plant from environmental threats.
