Tulista corrugata

Tulista corrugata, commonly known as the wrinkled tulip, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, corrugated leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Tulista corrugata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
