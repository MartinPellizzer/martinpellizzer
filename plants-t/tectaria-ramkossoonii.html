Tectaria ramkossoonii

Tectaria ramkossoonii, commonly known as the Madagascar shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into shield-shaped segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and wiry, supporting the growth of its intricate fronds.

Tectaria ramkossoonii plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
