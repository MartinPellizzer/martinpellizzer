Tragacantha kellalensis

Tragacantha kellalensis, commonly known as the Kellalensis tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique pod-like fruit structure. The plant produces a gelatinous substance from its sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features include bipinnate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the dry season.
