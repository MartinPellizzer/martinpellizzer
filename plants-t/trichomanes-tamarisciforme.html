Trichomanes tamarisciforme

Trichomanes tamarisciforme, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Microspermales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Trichomanes tamarisciforme plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
