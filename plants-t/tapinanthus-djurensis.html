Tapinanthus djurensis

Tapinanthus djurensis, commonly known as the parasitic nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from its host plant while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has a weak, vine-like structure. It often grows on the roots of other plants, such as grasses and shrubs.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject in the study of parasitic flora.
