Tulipa viridiflora

Tulipa viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its striking, elongated flowers that range in color from pale green to yellow. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a unique touch to gardens with their unusual hue. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or woodland settings.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
