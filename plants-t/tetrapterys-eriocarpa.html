Tetrapterys eriocarpa

Tetrapterys eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
