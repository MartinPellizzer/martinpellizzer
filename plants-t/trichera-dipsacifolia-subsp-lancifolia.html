Trichera dipsacifolia subsp. lancifolia

Trichera dipsacifolia subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
