Tephrosia capensis var. longipetiolata

Tephrosia capensis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled tephrosia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with elongated petioles. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
