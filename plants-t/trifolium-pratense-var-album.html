Trifolium pratense var. album

Trifolium pratense var. album, commonly known as white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. White clover is a nitrogen-fixing plant, making it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a common sight in lawns and meadows.
