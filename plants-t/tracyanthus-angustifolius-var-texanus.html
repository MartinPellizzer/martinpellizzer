Tracyanthus angustifolius var. texanus

Tracyanthus angustifolius var. texanus, commonly known as the Texas narrowleaf tracyanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a bottlebrush. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
