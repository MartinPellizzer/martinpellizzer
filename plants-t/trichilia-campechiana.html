Trichilia campechiana

Trichilia campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood of Trichilia campechiana is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
