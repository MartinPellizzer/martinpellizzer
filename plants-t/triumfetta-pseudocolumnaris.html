Triumfetta pseudocolumnaris

Triumfetta pseudocolumnaris, commonly known as the columnar triumfetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Triumfetta pseudocolumnaris is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its compact form and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
