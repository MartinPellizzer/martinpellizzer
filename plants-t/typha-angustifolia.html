Typha angustifolia

Typha angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its distinctive brown, cylindrical flower spikes.

It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomes contribute to the plant's ability to spread and form dense stands in wetland environments.
