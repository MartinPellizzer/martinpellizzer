Tabebuia longipes

Tabebuia longipes, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy, with leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
