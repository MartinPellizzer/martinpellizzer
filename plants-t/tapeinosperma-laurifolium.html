Tapeinosperma laurifolium

Tapeinosperma laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
