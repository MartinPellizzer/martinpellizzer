Tetranthus bahamensis

Tetranthus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian tetranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a low-growing, herbaceous habit. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

Tetranthus bahamensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its maritime environment.
