Thymus macrocalyx

Thymus macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong thyme scent. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in both garden and horticultural settings.
