Tephrosia longipes var. lurida

Tephrosia longipes var. lurida, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume morphology, including compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
