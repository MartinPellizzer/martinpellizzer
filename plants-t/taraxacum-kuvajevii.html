Taraxacum kuvajevii

Taraxacum kuvajevii, commonly known as the Kuvajev's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are basal, rough, and have a distinctive milky sap.

The flower head consists of a yellow disk surrounded by white ray florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
