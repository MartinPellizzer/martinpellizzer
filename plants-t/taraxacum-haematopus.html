Taraxacum haematopus

Taraxacum haematopus, commonly known as the bloodroot dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of basal, deeply lobed leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and consist of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
