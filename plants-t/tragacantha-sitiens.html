Tragacantha sitiens

Tragacantha sitiens, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorned stems. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
