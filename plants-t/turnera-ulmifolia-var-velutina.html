Turnera ulmifolia var. velutina

Turnera ulmifolia var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf turnera, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
