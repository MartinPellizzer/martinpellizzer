Thymelaea putorioides var. chlorantha

Thymelaea putorioides var. chlorantha, commonly known as the green thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
