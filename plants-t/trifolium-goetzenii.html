Trifolium goetzenii

Trifolium goetzenii, commonly known as the Goetzen's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stalk. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Trifolium goetzenii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
