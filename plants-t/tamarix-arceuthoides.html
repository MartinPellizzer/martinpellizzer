Tamarix arceuthoides

Tamarix arceuthoides, commonly known as the desert willow, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small leaves, and showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
