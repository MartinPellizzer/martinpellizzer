Tephrosia flammea var. monophylla

Tephrosia flammea var. monophylla, commonly known as flame tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
