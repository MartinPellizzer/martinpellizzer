Thunbergia cyanea

Thunbergia cyanea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers with five petals, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia cyanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
