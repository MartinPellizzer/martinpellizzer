Trematolobelia macrostachys var. kaalae

Trematolobelia macrostachys var. kaalae, commonly known as the Kalaʻā fern, is a rare endemic species found exclusively on the island of Oʻahu in Hawaii.

It is a member of the fern family, characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that grow in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically along stream banks and in rocky crevices. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
