Tamarix gallica var. ascalonica

Tamarix gallica var. ascalonica, commonly known as the Ascalonic tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid and saline soils. Its leaves are often mistaken for those of other tamarisk species due to their similar appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal and saline environments.
