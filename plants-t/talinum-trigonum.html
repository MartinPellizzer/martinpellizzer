Talinum trigonum

Talinum trigonum, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces distinctive red, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Talinum trigonum is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in some cuisines.
