Tapinostemma nummulariifolium

Tapinostemma nummulariifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
