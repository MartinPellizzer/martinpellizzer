Tragacantha cracca

Tragacantha cracca, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its latex-rich sap is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
