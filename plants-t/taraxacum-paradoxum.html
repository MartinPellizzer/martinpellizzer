Taraxacum paradoxum

Taraxacum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable weed in many regions.
