Trianaeopiper santa-rosanum

Trianaeopiper santa-rosanum, commonly known as the Rosy Santa Rosa Pepper, is a unique botanical species native to the cloud forests of Santa Rosa, Colombia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from bright red to deep purple, depending on maturity. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical classification places it within the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its limited habitat and rare occurrence, it is considered a delicately endangered species.
