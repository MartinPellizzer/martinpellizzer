Tricholaena gillettii

Tricholaena gillettii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas near freshwater bodies. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used for various purposes, including as a source of fiber.

Tricholaena gillettii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by stabilizing soil and providing shelter for aquatic life.
