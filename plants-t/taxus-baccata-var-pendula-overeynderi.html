Taxus baccata var. pendula-overeynderi

Taxus baccata var. pendula-overeynderi, commonly known as the Weeping Yew, is a variant of the common yew tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, with its dark green foliage and showy, red-orange berries adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

It is also known for its longevity and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
