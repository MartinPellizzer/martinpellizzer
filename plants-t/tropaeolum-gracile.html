Tropaeolum gracile

Tropaeolum gracile, commonly known as the slender nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a trailing or climbing annual that produces small, edible yellow or orange flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are thin and flexible, allowing it to spread along the ground or climb supports.

Tropaeolum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, as its leaves and seeds are edible.
