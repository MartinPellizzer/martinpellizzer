Tanacetum polycephalum subsp. duderanum

Tanacetum polycephalum subsp.

duderanum, commonly known as the Duder's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in association with other wetland plants and is known for its aromatic, slightly medicinal properties.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to the main species.
