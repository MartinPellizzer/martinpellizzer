Tetraotis longifolia

Tetraotis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved tetraotis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically elongated and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
