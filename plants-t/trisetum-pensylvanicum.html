Trisetum pensylvanicum

Trisetum pensylvanicum, commonly known as Pennsylvania sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, forming in spikelets that are often grouped in clusters.

Pennsylvania sedge plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
