Thlaspi inhumile

Thlaspi inhumile, commonly known as the dwarf cabbageworm or winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are silicles, which contain numerous tiny seeds.

Thlaspi inhumile is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
