Teliostachya petraea

Teliostachya petraea, commonly known as the rock false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in dry, rocky environments.

Teliostachya petraea is adapted to harsh alpine conditions and is often found in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes.
