Thlaspi cochlearinum

Thlaspi cochlearinum, commonly known as the kidney-shaped cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Thlaspi cochlearinum is known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential candidate for phytoremediation.
