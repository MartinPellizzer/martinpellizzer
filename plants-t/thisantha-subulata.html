Thisantha subulata

Thisantha subulata, commonly known as the slender flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant has thin, needle-like leaves that give it its scientific name "subulata," meaning "awl-shaped.". It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Thisantha subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
