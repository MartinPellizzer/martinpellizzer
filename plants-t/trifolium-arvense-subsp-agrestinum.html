Trifolium arvense subsp. agrestinum

Trifolium arvense subsp.

agrestinum, commonly known as the field clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stalk.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
