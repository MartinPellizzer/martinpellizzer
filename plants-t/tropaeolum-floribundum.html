Tropaeolum floribundum

Tropaeolum floribundum, commonly known as the everlasting nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red, often with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both ornamental and practical in horticulture.
