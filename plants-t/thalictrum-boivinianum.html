Thalictrum boivinianum

Thalictrum boivinianum, commonly known as the Boivin's meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Thalictrum boivinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
