Tamarix pallasii var. tigrensis

Tamarix pallasii var. tigrensis, commonly known as the Syrian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal and saline soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils.
