Teucrium dunense subsp. sublittoralis

Teucrium dunense subsp.

sublittoralis, commonly known as the coastal germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The plant features narrow, aromatic leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. It is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs, where it is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
