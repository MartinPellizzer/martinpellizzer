Talinum brevifolium

Talinum brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved Malabar spinach, is a tropical flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible leaves. It has a shallow root system and prefers well-drained soil.

Talinum brevifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
