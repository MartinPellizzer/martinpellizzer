Tamariscus gallicus

Tamariscus gallicus, commonly known as French tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Tamariscus gallicus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
