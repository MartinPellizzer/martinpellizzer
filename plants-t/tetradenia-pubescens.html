Tetradenia pubescens

Tetradenia pubescens, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture with a distinct aroma.

Tetradenia pubescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
