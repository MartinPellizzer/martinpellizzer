Taraxacum litvinovii

Taraxacum litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white pappus. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in the spring and summer months.

Taraxacum litvinovii is a perennial species, often found in mountainous regions and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
