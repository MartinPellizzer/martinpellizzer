Tournefortia guadalupensis

Tournefortia guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupensis Tournefortia, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and opposite, often with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
