Tropaeolum pentaphyllum

Tropaeolum pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leafed nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
