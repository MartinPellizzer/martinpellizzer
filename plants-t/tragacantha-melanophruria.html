Tragacantha melanophruria

Tragacantha melanophruria, commonly known as the black-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are woody and often branched, while its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in arid environments.
