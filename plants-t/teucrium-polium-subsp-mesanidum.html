Teucrium polium subsp. mesanidum

Teucrium polium subsp.

mesanidum, commonly known as Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-blue hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it historically valued in traditional herbal medicine.
