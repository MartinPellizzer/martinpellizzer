Tridax boliviensis

Tridax boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and has hairy, lobed leaves. Its yellow flowers are followed by fluffy seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Tridax boliviensis is often considered a weed but has some traditional medicinal uses.
