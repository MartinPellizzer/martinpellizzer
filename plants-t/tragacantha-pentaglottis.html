Tragacantha pentaglottis

Tragacantha pentaglottis, commonly known as the five-voiced tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, which is harvested from its exuding sap. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tragacantha pentaglottis is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to dry and semi-arid climates.
