Thalictrum diffusum

Thalictrum diffusum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can be found in woodlands, meadows, and stream banks.

Thalictrum diffusum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
