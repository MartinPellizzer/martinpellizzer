Trichodium elatum

Trichodium elatum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features delicate, feathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical name reflects its unique structure, with "trichodium" referring to the hairy or bristly appearance of its stems.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
