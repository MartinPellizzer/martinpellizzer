Thelocactus knuthianus

Thelocactus knuthianus, commonly known as the Knuth's locactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Thelocactus knuthianus is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
