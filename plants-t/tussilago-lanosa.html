Tussilago lanosa

Tussilago lanosa, commonly known as woolly thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in dense, white to grayish pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
