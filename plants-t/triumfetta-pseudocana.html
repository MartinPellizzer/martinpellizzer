Triumfetta pseudocana

Triumfetta pseudocana, commonly known as the narrowleaf triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small shrub or perennial herb that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological value in its native habitats.
