Thoracosperma hispida

Thoracosperma hispida, commonly known as the hairy tamanu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing hard, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
