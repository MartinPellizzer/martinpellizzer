Tilia eburnea var. lasioclada

Tilia eburnea var. lasioclada, commonly known as the white-barked linden, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that often has a flaky or peeling texture. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated margins and a pale green underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
