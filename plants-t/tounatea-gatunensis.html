Tounatea gatunensis

Tounatea gatunensis, commonly known as the Gatun river fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that form dense mats on the water surface. The plant has a complex rhizome system that helps it anchor and spread in freshwater environments. Its spores are released into the water, facilitating reproduction and dispersal.

Tounatea gatunensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
