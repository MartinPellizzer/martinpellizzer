Taraxacum ooststroomii

Taraxacum ooststroomii, commonly known as the Ooststroom's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, leaf-like stem that terminates in a composite flower head, which typically turns from yellow to white as it matures. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
