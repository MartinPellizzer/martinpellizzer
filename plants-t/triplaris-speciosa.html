Triplaris speciosa

Triplaris speciosa, commonly known as the showy triplaris, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. Triplaris speciosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
