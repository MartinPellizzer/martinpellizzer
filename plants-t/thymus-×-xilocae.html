Thymus × xilocae

Thymus × xilocae, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Thymus vulgaris (common thyme) and Thymus xerophytus, resulting in a plant with unique aromatic properties. The leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and medicinal herb gardens.

This plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
