Tamarix tingitana

Tamarix tingitana, commonly known as the Barbary tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in summer, which are clustered in small, spherical inflorescences. Tamarix tingitana is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful for soil stabilization and erosion control.
