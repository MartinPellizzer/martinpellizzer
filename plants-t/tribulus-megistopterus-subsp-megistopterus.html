Tribulus megistopterus subsp. megistopterus

Tribulus megistopterus subsp.

megistopterus, commonly known as the giant caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, globular fruit that contains four seeds, each with a hard, pointed seed coat.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in dry to moderately moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
