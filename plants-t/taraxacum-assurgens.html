Taraxacum assurgens

Taraxacum assurgens, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow ray florets that develop into fluffy white seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Taraxacum assurgens is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is resilient to drought conditions.
