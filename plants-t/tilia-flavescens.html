Tilia flavescens

Tilia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-barked linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and pale or yellowish on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is distinctive, featuring a light yellow or cream color with deep fissures.

Tilia flavescens is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
