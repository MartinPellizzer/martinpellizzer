Thelocactus mandragora

Thelocactus mandragora, commonly known as the devil's trumpet, is a distinctive cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in extreme desert conditions.
