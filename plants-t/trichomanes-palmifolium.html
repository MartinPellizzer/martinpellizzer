Trichomanes palmifolium

Trichomanes palmifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures.

It features feather-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes palmifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
