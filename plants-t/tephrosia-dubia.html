Tephrosia dubia

Tephrosia dubia, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and its roots have a distinctive blue color due to the presence of certain pigments. Tephrosia dubia is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
