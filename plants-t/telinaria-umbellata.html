Telinaria umbellata

Telinaria umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Telinaria umbellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
