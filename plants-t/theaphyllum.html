Theaphyllum

Theaphyllum, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it a unique appearance in gardens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.

Theaphyllum thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental landscaping.
