Taraxacum dorchocarpum

Taraxacum dorchocarpum, commonly known as the Dorchocarpum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces fluffy, white seeds. Native to Europe, this species is typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Its flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
