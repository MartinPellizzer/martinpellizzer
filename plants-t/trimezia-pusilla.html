Trimezia pusilla

Trimezia pusilla, commonly known as the small trimezia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Trimezia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
