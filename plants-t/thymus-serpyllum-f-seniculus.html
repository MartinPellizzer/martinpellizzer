Thymus serpyllum f. seniculus

Thymus serpyllum f. seniculus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in small, dense leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils and antimicrobial properties.

This variety is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in gardens.
