Triodia alpina

Triodia alpina, commonly known as the alpine triodia, is a low-growing perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall. The plant has a dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Triodia alpina plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
