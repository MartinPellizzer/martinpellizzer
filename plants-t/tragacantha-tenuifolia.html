Tragacantha tenuifolia

Tragacantha tenuifolia, commonly known as Persian gum or tamarisk, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Tragacantha tenuifolia is also valued for its resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries.
