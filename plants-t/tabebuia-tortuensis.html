Tabebuia tortuensis

Tabebuia tortuensis, commonly known as the trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to pink and red. The tree produces long, narrow leaves and has a distinctive, often twisted trunk that gives it its scientific name. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The trumpet tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
