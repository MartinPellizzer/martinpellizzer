Tapeinosperma deflexum

Tapeinosperma deflexum, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a sprawling habit, characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months.
