Tetracme glochidiata

Tetracme glochidiata, commonly known as the spiny burr vine, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, serrated edge.

Tetracme glochidiata is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.
