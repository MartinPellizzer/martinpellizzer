Thoracostachyum simplex

Thoracostachyum simplex, commonly known as the Mexican silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and tufted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
