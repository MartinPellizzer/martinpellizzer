Trichilia acuminata

Trichilia acuminata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain flat, elliptical seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The wood is valued for its aromatic qualities and is used in the production of incense and furniture.
