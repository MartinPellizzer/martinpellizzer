Telipogon maduroi

Telipogon maduroi, commonly known as the Maduroi orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Telipogon maduroi is considered an endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
