Talinum napiforme

Talinum napiforme, commonly known as arrowhead spinach, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Talinum napiforme is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage, but it is also used in some traditional cuisines.

It prefers warm, humid climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
