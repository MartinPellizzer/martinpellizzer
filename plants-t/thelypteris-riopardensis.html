Thelypteris riopardensis

Thelypteris riopardensis, commonly known as the Riopardensis shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Thelypteris riopardensis plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
