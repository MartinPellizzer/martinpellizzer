Thesium schweinfurthii var. djurense

Thesium schweinfurthii var. djurense, commonly known as the desert buttercup, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in harsh desert conditions.
