Taraxacum copidophyllum

Taraxacum copidophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
