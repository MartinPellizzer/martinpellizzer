Tessmannia martiniana

Tessmannia martiniana, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody capsules containing numerous small seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments within its native range.
