Tinus × montana

Tinus × montana, commonly known as the mountain tinus, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardy nature.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in leaf shape and flowering patterns.
