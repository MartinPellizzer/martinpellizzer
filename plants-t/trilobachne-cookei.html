Trilobachne cookei

Trilobachne cookei, commonly known as the Cooke's trilobed grass, is a species of grass native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species, often forming dense clumps with a creeping rhizome system.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying grassland ecosystems.
