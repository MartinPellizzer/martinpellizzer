Taraxacum uschakovii var. pseudowrangelicum

Taraxacum uschakovii var. pseudowrangelicum, commonly known as the Wrangel Island dandelion, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily on Wrangel Island in the Arctic.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and a yellow composite flower head. The plant exhibits adaptations to cold climates, including a short growing season and frost-resistant physiology. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and flies, contributing to its reproductive success.
