Tanacetum flabelliforme

Tanacetum flabelliforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
