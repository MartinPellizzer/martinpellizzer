Teucrium bicolor

Teucrium bicolor, commonly known as heartworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters.

Teucrium bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
