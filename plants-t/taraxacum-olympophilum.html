Taraxacum olympophilum

Taraxacum olympophilum, commonly known as the Olympic dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, which is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. It produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
