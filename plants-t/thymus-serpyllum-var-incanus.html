Thymus serpyllum var. incanus

Thymus serpyllum var. incanus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its leaves are commonly used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their essential oils and antioxidant compounds.
