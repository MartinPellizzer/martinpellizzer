Themistoclesia flexuosa

Themistoclesia flexuosa, commonly known as the curled leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems that grow in a spiral or curling pattern, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal care once established.
