Taraxacum divulsiforme

Taraxacum divulsiforme, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed head composed of tiny, parachute-like seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
