Thymus × celtibericus

Thymus × celtibericus, commonly known as the Iberian thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, aromatic foliage, and small pink or violet flowers. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils.

Thymus × celtibericus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
