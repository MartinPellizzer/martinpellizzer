Tinospora dentata

Tinospora dentata, commonly known as the Indian glorybower, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Tinospora dentata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
