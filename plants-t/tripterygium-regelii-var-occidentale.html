Tripterygium regelii var. occidentale

Tripterygium regelii var. occidentale, commonly known as Chinese thunder god vine, is a flowering plant native to China and other parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its chemical composition, which includes various bioactive compounds with potential medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
