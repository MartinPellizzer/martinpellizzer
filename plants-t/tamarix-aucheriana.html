Tamarix aucheriana

Tamarix aucheriana, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a shallow, spreading root system and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value. It is also known for its ability to thrive in saline soils, making it suitable for coastal or arid environments.

The wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and fuel.
