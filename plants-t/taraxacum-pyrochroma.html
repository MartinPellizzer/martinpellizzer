Taraxacum pyrochroma

Taraxacum pyrochroma, commonly known as the fire-colored dandelion, is a rare and striking species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vividly colored flowers, which range from orange to red, giving it its common name. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that disperses its seeds via the wind. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine, white hairs.

Taraxacum pyrochroma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
