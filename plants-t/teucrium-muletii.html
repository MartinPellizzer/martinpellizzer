Teucrium muletii

Teucrium muletii, commonly known as mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a grayish or silver underside.

Teucrium muletii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
