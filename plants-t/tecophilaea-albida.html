Tecophilaea albida

Tecophilaea albida, commonly known as the white-flowered rock fig, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, which helps it conserve water in arid climates.

Tecophilaea albida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
