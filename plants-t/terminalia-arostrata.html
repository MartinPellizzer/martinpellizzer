Terminalia arostrata

Terminalia arostrata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a medium to large tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The species also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
