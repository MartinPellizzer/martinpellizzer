Thalictrum pseudominus

Thalictrum pseudominus, commonly known as the lesser meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces numerous slender, wiry stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Thalictrum pseudominus is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
