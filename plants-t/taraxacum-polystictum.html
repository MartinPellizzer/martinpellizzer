Taraxacum polystictum

Taraxacum polystictum, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common feature in the genus Taraxacum. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
