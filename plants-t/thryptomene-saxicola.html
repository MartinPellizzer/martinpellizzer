Thryptomene saxicola

Thryptomene saxicola, commonly known as the rock thryptomene, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Thryptomene saxicola is well adapted to harsh conditions and is often used in native landscaping due to its hardy nature.
