Theodorea deltodes

Theodorea deltodes, commonly known as the delta lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with six petals arranged symmetrically. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Theodorea deltodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
