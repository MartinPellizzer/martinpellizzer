Taraxacum hygrophilum

Taraxacum hygrophilum, commonly known as the water dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, erect stem that bears a yellow flower head. This plant is native to wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in moist or aquatic environments. Its leaves are typically thick and succulent, which helps it retain water.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
