Tillandsia xiphostachys

Tillandsia xiphostachys, commonly known as the silver vane tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom from a central stem, adding visual interest. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular misting to maintain humidity.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
