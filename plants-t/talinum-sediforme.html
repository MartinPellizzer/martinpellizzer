Talinum sediforme

Talinum sediforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined plant, is a herbaceous annual native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-veined, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Talinum sediforme is known for its succulent foliage and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.
