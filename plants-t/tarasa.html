Tarasa

Tarasa, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Tarasa has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
