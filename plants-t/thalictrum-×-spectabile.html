Thalictrum × spectabile

Thalictrum × spectabile, commonly known as the showy mountain avens, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds.

Thalictrum × spectabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to enhance the aesthetic of shaded garden spaces.
