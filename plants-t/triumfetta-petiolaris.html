Triumfetta petiolaris

Triumfetta petiolaris, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Triumfetta petiolaris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
