Thymelaea coridifolia subsp. dendrobryum

Thymelaea coridifolia subsp.

dendrobryum, commonly known as the rock thyme, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, aromatic, yellow flowers and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
