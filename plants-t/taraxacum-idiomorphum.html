Taraxacum idiomorphum

Taraxacum idiomorphum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological niche.
