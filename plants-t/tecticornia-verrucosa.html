Tecticornia verrucosa

Tecticornia verrucosa, commonly known as the cushion grass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense, mat-like clusters, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, appearing in spikes during the spring and summer months.

Tecticornia verrucosa is well adapted to harsh environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
