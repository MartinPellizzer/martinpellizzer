Tanacetum bipinnatum subsp. huronense

Tanacetum bipinnatum subsp.

huronense, commonly known as Huron golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Great Lakes region of North America. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wet meadows. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
