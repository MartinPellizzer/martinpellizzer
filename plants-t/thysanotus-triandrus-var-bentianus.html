Thysanotus triandrus var. bentianus

Thysanotus triandrus var. bentianus, commonly known as the Chinese bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in early spring.
