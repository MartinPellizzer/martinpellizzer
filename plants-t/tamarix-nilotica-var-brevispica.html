Tamarix nilotica var. brevispica

Tamarix nilotica var. brevispica, commonly known as the desert date or African date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features slender, often multi-branched stems with a distinctive reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters, followed by edible, sweet-smelling fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often shed during dry periods, reducing water loss.

Tamarix nilotica var. brevispica is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a valuable species for erosion control and ornamental use.
