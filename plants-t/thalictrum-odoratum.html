Thalictrum odoratum

Thalictrum odoratum, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Thalictrum odoratum is also noted for its aromatic seeds, which have been used in herbal medicine.
