Tristachya welwitschii var. trichophora

Tristachya welwitschii var. trichophora, commonly known as the silky grass or silky reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its inflorescence consists of two types of spikelets, with the lower ones being longer and more branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its elegant, flowing form.
