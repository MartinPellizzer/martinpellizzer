Taraxacum crassiceps

Taraxacum crassiceps, commonly known as the thick-headed dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stem and large, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces a single, thick, yellow flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

This species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
