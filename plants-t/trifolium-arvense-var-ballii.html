Trifolium arvense var. ballii

Trifolium arvense var. ballii, commonly known as Ball's hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish stem. This plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its flowers are small and pea-like, blooming in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees.
