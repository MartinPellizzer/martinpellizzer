Tagetes papposa

Tagetes papposa, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon or desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly bitter, herbaceous scent.

Tagetes papposa is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
