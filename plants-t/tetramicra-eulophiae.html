Tetramicra eulophiae

Tetramicra eulophiae, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and hairy, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
