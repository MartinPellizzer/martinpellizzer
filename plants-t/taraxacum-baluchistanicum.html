Taraxacum baluchistanicum

Taraxacum baluchistanicum, commonly known as the Baluchistan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports its yellowish composite flower. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep taproot system for water storage.

The species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
