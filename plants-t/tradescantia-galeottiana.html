Tradescantia galeottiana

Tradescantia galeottiana, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that often have a reddish or purple hue along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found growing in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
