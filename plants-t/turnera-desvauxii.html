Turnera desvauxii

Turnera desvauxii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and natural habitats.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
