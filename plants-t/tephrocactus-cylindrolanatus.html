Tephrocactus cylindrolanatus

Tephrocactus cylindrolanatus, commonly known as the cylindrical thorned cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
