Tussilago rubella

Tussilago rubella, commonly known as purple coughwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tussilago rubella is often found growing in clusters, with its medicinal properties historically used for respiratory ailments.
