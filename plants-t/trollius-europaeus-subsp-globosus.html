Trollius europaeus subsp. globosus

Trollius europaeus subsp.

globosus, commonly known as the globe buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
