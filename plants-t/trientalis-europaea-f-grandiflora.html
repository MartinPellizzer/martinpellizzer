Trientalis europaea f. grandiflora

Trientalis europaea f. grandiflora, commonly known as the European wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves that persist through winter. The plant produces showy, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, typically from April to May. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in acidic soils.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense colonies and its role in early successional plant communities.
