Trichonema uliginosum

Trichonema uliginosum, commonly known as the bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

It has a dense, cushion-like growth habit and features dark green, closely packed leaves that are often curved at the tips. The plant produces small, elongated spore capsules that are typically borne on short stalks near the leaf tips. It plays a vital role in stabilizing peat soils and maintaining the ecological balance of wetland habitats.

Trichonema uliginosum is an important indicator species for assessing the health of acidic, nutrient-poor environments.
