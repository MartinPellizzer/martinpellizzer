Taraxacum erzincanense

Taraxacum erzincanense, commonly known as Erzincan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a taproot system and produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the genus Taraxacum.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
