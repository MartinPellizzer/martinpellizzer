Turritis sanguinea

Turritis sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a single, thick, fleshy root that stores nutrients and water. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette pattern, and are typically green with a waxy texture.

Turritis sanguinea is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its short growing season.
