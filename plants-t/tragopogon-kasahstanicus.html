Tragopogon kasahstanicus

Tragopogon kasahstanicus, commonly known as the Kazakhstan thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion family.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
