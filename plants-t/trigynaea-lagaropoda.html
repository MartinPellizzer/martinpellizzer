Trigynaea lagaropoda

Trigynaea lagaropoda, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, red fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Trigynaea lagaropoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
