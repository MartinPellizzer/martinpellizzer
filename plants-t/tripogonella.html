Tripogonella

Tripogonella, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces cylindrical, spiny fruits that contain tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tripogonella is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.
