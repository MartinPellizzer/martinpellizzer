Triticum aegilopoides f. bulgaricum

Triticum aegilopoides f. bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian wild wheat, is a diploid grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the tribe Aegilopieae within the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.

Its genetic makeup makes it an important species in the study of wheat evolution and hybridization.
