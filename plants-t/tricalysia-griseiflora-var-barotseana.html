Tricalysia griseiflora var. barotseana

Tricalysia griseiflora var. barotseana, commonly known as the Barotse flame tree, is a species native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The tree produces striking, flame-colored flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its visual impact.
