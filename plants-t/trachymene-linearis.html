Trachymene linearis

Trachymene linearis, commonly known as narrow-leaved false Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, linear leaves which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
