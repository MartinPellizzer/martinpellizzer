Thelypteris bagginsiorum

Thelypteris bagginsiorum, commonly known as the Shire Fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori surrounded by indusia. Thelypteris bagginsiorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it can receive adequate humidity and indirect light.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it a notable species within its native habitat.
