Tricholaser

Tricholaser, commonly known as the ghost mushroom, is a species of fungus belonging to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its pale, often translucent cap and slender, fibrous stipes. The gills are typically white or pale yellow, and the spores are small and smooth. It is usually found in temperate forests, growing on decaying wood.

This fungus is not considered edible and is often avoided due to its mild toxicity.
