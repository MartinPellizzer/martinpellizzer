Trianthema sedifolium

Trianthema sedifolium, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Trianthema sedifolium is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
