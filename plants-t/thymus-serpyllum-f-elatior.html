Thymus serpyllum f. elatior

Thymus serpyllum f. elatior, commonly known as the greater thyme or creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
