Tanacetum walteri

Tanacetum walteri, commonly known as Walter's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, yellowish to whitish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Walter's tansy is often found in association with other members of the tansy genus, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.
