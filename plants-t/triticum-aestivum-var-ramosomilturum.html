Triticum aestivum var. ramosomilturum

Triticum aestivum var. ramosomilturum, commonly known as the wild wheat, is a variant of wheat that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that bear small, hard grains. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is adapted to a range of soil conditions. Its florets are often arranged in a dense, branching pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value.
