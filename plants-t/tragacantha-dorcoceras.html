Tragacantha dorcoceras

Tragacantha dorcoceras, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
