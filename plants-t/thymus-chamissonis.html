Thymus chamissonis

Thymus chamissonis, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tiny, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover in rock gardens or along pathways.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.
