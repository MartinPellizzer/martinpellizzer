Thea sasanqua var. thunbergii

Thea sasanqua var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Camellia sasanqua* and *Camellia japonica*.

It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and ability to produce vibrant red, pink, or white blooms.
