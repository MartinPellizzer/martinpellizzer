Thermopsis macrophylla var. venosa

Thermopsis macrophylla var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated yellow rocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow, cruciform flowers in early summer, which are arranged in elongated racemes. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower landscapes.
