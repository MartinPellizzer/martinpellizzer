Tussilago sinuata subsp. piloselloides

Tussilago sinuata subsp.

piloselloides, commonly known as the silky coughwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features soft, hairy leaves that are often wrinkled and have a silvery appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
