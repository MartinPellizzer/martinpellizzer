Thapsia villosa subsp. maxima

Thapsia villosa subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the hairy thapsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and tall, spiky inflorescences.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
