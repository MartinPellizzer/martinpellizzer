Themelium conjunctisorum

Themelium conjunctisorum, commonly known as the joined-vein fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that feature closely spaced, fused veins giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the undergrowth where it can access the necessary humidity and indirect light. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Themelium conjunctisorum is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and habitat loss.
