Terminalia cherrieri

Terminalia cherrieri, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, compound leaves that are alternately arranged. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, red fruits that contain several seeds. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.

Terminalia cherrieri is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
