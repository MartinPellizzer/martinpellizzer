Tetrodon oppositifolius

Tetrodon oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaved pufferfish, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf arrangement.

Its leaves are opposite in orientation, giving it a visually striking appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
