Taxodium ascendens var. pendulum

Taxodium ascendens var. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance, giving it a distinctive and ornamental look. The tree has scaly, reddish-brown bark that becomes thick and furrowed with age. Its leaves are scale-like and turn a striking coppery color in the fall before dropping.

The weeping bald cypress is often cultivated for its unique aesthetic and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
