Thalictrum verticillatum

Thalictrum verticillatum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the nodes along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Thalictrum verticillatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
