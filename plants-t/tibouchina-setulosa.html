Tibouchina setulosa

Tibouchina setulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or purple flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially in warmer climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its distinctive texture.

Tibouchina setulosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
