Tunica podolica

Tunica podolica, commonly known as the podolic tunic, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, fibrous outer layer that resembles a tunic, providing structural support and protection to the plant's internal organs. The leaves are typically small and clustered, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small, greenish blooms that are often overlooked.

The plant's root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky and nutrient-poor soils.
