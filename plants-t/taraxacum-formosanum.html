Taraxacum formosanum

Taraxacum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It produces a bright yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination.

Taraxacum formosanum is often found in meadows and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
