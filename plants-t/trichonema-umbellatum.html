It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It reproduces via spores, which are released from small, cup-like structures called sporophytes.

Trichonema umbellatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
