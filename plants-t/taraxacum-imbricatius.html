Taraxacum imbricatius

Taraxacum imbricatius, commonly known as the bristled dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a hollow, ribbed stem that supports the flower and later develops a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Taraxacum imbricatius is a perennial herb that is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions.
