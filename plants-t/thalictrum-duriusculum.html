Thalictrum duriusculum

Thalictrum duriusculum, commonly known as the rough meadow rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, white flowers are clustered in loose panicles and bloom in early to mid-summer.

Thalictrum duriusculum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
