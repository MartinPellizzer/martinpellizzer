Taraxacum sinus-avis

Taraxacum sinus-avis, commonly known as the Sinai dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a distinctive seed ball. Native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel and Egypt, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
