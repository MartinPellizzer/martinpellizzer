Thalictrum minus var. rubricaule

Thalictrum minus var. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically bright green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
