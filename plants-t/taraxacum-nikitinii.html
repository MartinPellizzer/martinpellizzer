Taraxacum nikitinii

Taraxacum nikitinii, commonly known as the Nikitin's dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem topped with a spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

Taraxacum nikitinii is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
