Tricholepis eburnea

Tricholepis eburnea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid to arid regions.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
