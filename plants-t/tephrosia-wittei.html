Tephrosia wittei

Tephrosia wittei, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a taproot system that helps it survive in dry conditions. Its small, bean-like pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
