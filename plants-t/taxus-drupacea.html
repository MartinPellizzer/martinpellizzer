Taxus drupacea

Taxus drupacea, commonly known as the dwarf yew, is a species of flowering plant in the family Taxaceae.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Taxus drupacea is native to parts of Asia and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
