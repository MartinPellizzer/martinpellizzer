Thladiantha henryi var. verrucosa

Thladiantha henryi var. verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf goji, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its varietal name. It produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
