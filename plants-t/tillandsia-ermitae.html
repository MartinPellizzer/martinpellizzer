Tillandsia ermitae

Tillandsia ermitae, commonly known as the Ermita Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces small, showy inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among bromeliad enthusiasts.
