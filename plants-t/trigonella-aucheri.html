Trigonella aucheri

Trigonella aucheri, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Trigonella aucheri is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
