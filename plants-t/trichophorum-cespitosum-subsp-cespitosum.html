Trichophorum cespitosum subsp. cespitosum

Trichophorum cespitosum subsp.

cespitosum, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features triangular stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in dense, clumping growths. Trichophorum cespitosum subsp.

cespitosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
