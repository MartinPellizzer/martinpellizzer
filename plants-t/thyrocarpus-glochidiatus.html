Thyrocarpus glochidiatus

Thyrocarpus glochidiatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine, hair-like structures.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
