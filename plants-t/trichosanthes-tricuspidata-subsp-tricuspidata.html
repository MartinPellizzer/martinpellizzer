Trichosanthes tricuspidata subsp. tricuspidata

Trichosanthes tricuspidata subsp.

tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed bitter melon, is a vine native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and elongated, warty fruits that mature to a yellow-orange color.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five lobes, and the fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its edible fruit, which has a bitter taste and is commonly used in various culinary preparations.
