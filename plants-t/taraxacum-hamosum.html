Taraxacum hamosum

Taraxacum hamosum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The flowers are followed by a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.
