Tecoma ochracea

Tecoma ochracea, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma ochracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
