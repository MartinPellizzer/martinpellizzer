Thea tsaii

Thea tsaii, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, characterized by its serrated leaves and small white flowers. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are the primary part used for making tea, containing caffeine and various bioactive compounds.

Thea tsaii is cultivated extensively for its economic and cultural significance in traditional tea production.
