Tamus edulis

Tamus edulis, commonly known as the Indian turnip or swamp cabbage, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features a thick, fleshy root that is edible and often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, pale green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Tamus edulis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or boggy environments.
