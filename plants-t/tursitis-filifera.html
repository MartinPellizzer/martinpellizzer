Tursitis filifera

Tursitis filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf tursit, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, adapting to the harsh, dry environment. Tursitis filifera is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and can withstand prolonged periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects in semi-arid regions.
