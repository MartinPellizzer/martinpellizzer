Trichosalpinx mastophora

Trichosalpinx mastophora, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
