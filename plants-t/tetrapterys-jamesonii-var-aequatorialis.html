Tetrapterys jamesonii var. aequatorialis

Tetrapterys jamesonii var. aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian greenbark tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greenish-gray bark that remains relatively unchanged throughout its growth cycle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
