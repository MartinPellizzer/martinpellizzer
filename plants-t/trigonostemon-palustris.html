Trigonostemon palustris

Trigonostemon palustris, commonly known as the marsh trigonostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Trigonostemon palustris plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
