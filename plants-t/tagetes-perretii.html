Tagetes perretii

Tagetes perretii, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tagetes perretii is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
