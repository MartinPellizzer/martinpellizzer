Triticum turgidum subsp. polonicum

Triticum turgidum subsp.

polonicum, commonly known as Polish wheat, is a cultivated form of wheat native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its intermediate height and good adaptability to various soil conditions.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence. Its grains are hard and have a high protein content, making it suitable for bread-making.

This subspecies is an important component of traditional Polish agriculture and has been used for centuries in local farming practices.
