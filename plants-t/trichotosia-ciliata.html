Trichotosia ciliata

Trichotosia ciliata, commonly known as the silky trichotosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and are borne in clusters.

Trichotosia ciliata is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
