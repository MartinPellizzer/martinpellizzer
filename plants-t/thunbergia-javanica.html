Thunbergia javanica

Thunbergia javanica, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Thunbergia javanica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
