Thoa diminuta

Thoa diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf thoa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Thoa diminuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
