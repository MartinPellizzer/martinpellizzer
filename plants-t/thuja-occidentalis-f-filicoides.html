Thuja occidentalis f. filicoides

Thuja occidentalis f. filicoides, commonly known as the lacey arborvitae, is a unique cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant exhibits a compact, pyramidal growth habit with a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and densely packed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, refined look.
