Tmesipteris obliqua

Tmesipteris obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that often curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Tmesipteris obliqua plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
