Tecoma speciosa

Tecoma speciosa, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Tecoma speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
