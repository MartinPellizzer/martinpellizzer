Trichilia brieyi

Trichilia brieyi, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive pinnate venation pattern.

The tree is valued for its fragrant timber, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
