Telephium triphyllum

Telephium triphyllum, commonly known as three-leaved groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of trifoliate leaves, each leaf composed of three oval to lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden landscapes.
