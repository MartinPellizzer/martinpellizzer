Thysanolaena maxima

Thysanolaena maxima, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense, tufted clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Thysanolaena maxima is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
