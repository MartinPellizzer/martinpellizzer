Tritonia acroloba

Tritonia acroloba, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or purple, with a distinctive lobed shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tritonia acroloba is also known for its long blooming period, extending from spring to early summer.
