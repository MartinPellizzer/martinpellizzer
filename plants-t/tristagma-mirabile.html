Tristagma mirabile

Tristagma mirabile, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it both useful and sometimes invasive in certain regions.
