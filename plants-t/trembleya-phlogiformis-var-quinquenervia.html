Trembleya phlogiformis var. quinquenervia

Trembleya phlogiformis var. quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined trembleya, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with five prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
