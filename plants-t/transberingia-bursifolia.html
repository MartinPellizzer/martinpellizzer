Transberingia bursifolia

Transberingia bursifolia, commonly known as the bladderleaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bladder-like leaves that help store water in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the short summer season. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Transberingia bursifolia is adapted to extreme conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience to cold and limited nutrients.
