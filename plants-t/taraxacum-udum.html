Taraxacum udum

Taraxacum udum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its large, bright yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Taraxacum udum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
