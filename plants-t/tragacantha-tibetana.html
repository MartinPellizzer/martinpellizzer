Tragacantha tibetana

Tragacantha tibetana, commonly known as Tibetan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds. It is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
