Teucrium multinodum var. aragvense

Teucrium multinodum var. aragvense, commonly known as the Armenian germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it is valued for its potential therapeutic applications.
