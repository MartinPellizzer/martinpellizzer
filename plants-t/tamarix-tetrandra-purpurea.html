Tamarix tetrandra purpurea

Tamarix tetrandra purpurea, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It features slender, often multi-branched stems that are typically covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and can help stabilize soil in sandy or saline environments.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in drought conditions, making it adaptable to a variety of harsh climates.
