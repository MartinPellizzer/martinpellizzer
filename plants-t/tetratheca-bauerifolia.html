Tetratheca bauerifolia

Tetratheca bauerifolia, commonly known as the Bauer's tetra-theca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodlands or heathlands.

Tetratheca bauerifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
