Tragopogon porrifolius subsp. eriospermus

Tragopogon porrifolius subsp.

eriospermus, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and a more compact inflorescence compared to other Tragopogon species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

The plant is also noted for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
