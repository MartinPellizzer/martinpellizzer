Tephrosia strigosa

Tephrosia strigosa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.

Tephrosia strigosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of plant-based pesticides.
