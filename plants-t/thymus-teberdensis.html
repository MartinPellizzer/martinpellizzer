Thymus teberdensis

Thymus teberdensis, commonly known as Turkish thyme, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like mint and oregano.
