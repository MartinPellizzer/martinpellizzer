Thelypteris adenopelta

Thelypteris adenopelta, commonly known as the woolly shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often hairy fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with serrated leaflets that contribute to its ornamental value.

Thelypteris adenopelta is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its attractive, textured foliage.
