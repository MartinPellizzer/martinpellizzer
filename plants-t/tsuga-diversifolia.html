Tsuga diversifolia

Tsuga diversifolia, commonly known as the variable fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often hang downward, containing small, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Tsuga diversifolia plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
