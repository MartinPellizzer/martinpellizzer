Toona ciliata var. sublaxiflora

Toona ciliata var. sublaxiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toona, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

This species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
