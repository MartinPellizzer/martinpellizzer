Taenitis simplicivenia

Taenitis simplicivenia, commonly known as the simple-veined taenitis, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant's unique venation pattern and ornamental foliage make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
