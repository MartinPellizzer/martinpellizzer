Tradescantia macropoda

Tradescantia macropoda, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, violet, or white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, often with a bluish or silver-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it both a decorative garden plant and a potential weed.
