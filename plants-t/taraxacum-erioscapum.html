Taraxacum erioscapum

Taraxacum erioscapum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features a hairy, flowering stem that supports its showy inflorescence, which consists of a single flower head surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine, soft hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
