Trichochilus neoebudicus

Trichochilus neoebudicus, commonly known as the Neobudic Bristleleaf, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanical curiosity and of interest to conservationists.
