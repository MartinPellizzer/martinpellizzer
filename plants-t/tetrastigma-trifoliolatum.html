Tetrastigma trifoliolatum

Tetrastigma trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaf bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its distinctive yellow-orange berries that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Vitaceae family and is known for its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
