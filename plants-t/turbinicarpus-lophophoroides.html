Turbinicarpus lophophoroides

Turbinicarpus lophophoroides, commonly known as the Mexican sun rose, is a succulent cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically borne in the axils of the ribs, and the plant is known for its compact, ornamental appearance.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms.
