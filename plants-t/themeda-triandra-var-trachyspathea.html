Themeda triandra var. trachyspathea

Themeda triandra var. trachyspathea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass or narrow-leaf kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
