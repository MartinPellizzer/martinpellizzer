Tulbaghia cernua

Tulbaghia cernua, commonly known as the silver bush or dwarf tulbaghia, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Tulbaghia cernua is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects.
