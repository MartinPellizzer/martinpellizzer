Trapa arcuata

Trapa arcuata, commonly known as the water caltrop, is a aquatic plant native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, floating fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has a submerged stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that help in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

Trapa arcuata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
