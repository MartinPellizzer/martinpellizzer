Taraxacum bhutanicum

Taraxacum bhutanicum, commonly known as the Bhutanese dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It produces a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, fluffy seed capsule.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
