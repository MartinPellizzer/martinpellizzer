Trianthema cypseleoides

Trianthema cypseleoides, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Trianthema cypseleoides is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
