Triaspis zenkeri

Triaspis zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambesi candelabra, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched, candelabra-like structure composed of multiple stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Triaspis zenkeri is typically found in rocky areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
