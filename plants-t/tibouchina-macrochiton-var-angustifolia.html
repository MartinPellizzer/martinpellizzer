Tibouchina macrochiton var. angustifolia

Tibouchina macrochiton var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that gives the plant a vibrant appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
