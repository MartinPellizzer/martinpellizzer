Taraxacum ulophyllum

Taraxacum ulophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
