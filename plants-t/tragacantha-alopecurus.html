Tragacantha alopecurus

Tragacantha alopecurus, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant's root system is fibrous, and it can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
