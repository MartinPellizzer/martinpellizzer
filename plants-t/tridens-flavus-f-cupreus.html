Tridens flavus f. cupreus

Tridens flavus f. cupreus, commonly known as the copperleaf, is a variant of the common tridens species.

It is characterized by its distinctive copper-colored leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
