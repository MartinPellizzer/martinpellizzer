Triticum dicoccum var. turgidoides

Triticum dicoccum var. turgidoides, commonly known as wild emmer wheat, is a wild relative of modern wheat species.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and long, awned spikes that contain small, hard grains. This plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. Its genetic diversity makes it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.

The plant's botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex inflorescence structure.
