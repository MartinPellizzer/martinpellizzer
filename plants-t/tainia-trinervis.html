Tainia trinervis

Tainia trinervis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like appearance due to its long, thin sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Tainia trinervis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
