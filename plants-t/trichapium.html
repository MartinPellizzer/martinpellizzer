Trichapium

Trichapium, commonly known as the feather grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their slender, often wind-blown stems and feathery plumes of flowers, which give them their common name. Trichapium species are typically found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types. They are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Trichapium cernuum and Trichapium flexile, which are valued for their ecological and aesthetic contributions.
