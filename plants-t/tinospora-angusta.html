Tinospora angusta

Tinospora angusta, commonly known as Indian creeper or small-leaf tinospora, is a woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain kidney-shaped seeds. Tinospora angusta is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
