Thalictrum simplex f. strictum

Thalictrum simplex f. strictum, commonly known as the upright meadow rue, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces showy, upright clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of pollinators, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
