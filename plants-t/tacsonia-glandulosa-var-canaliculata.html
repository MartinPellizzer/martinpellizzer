Tacsonia glandulosa var. canaliculata

Tacsonia glandulosa var. canaliculata, commonly known as the glandular tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in tiny, raised oil glands, which contribute to its distinctive aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
