Teliostachya lanceolata

Teliostachya lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild rye, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and drought-resistant characteristics. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to thrive in low-nutrient, high-sunlight environments.
