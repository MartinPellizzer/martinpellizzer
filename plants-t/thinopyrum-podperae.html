Thinopyrum podperae

Thinopyrum podperae, commonly known as the Tibet wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Thinopyrum podperae is adapted to cold, arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Its genetic material has been studied for potential use in improving the hardiness of wheat varieties.
