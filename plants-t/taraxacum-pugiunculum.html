Taraxacum pugiunculum

Taraxacum pugiunculum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
