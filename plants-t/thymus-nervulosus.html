Thymus nervulosus

Thymus nervulosus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, narrow, fuzzy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus nervulosus is valued for its fragrance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
