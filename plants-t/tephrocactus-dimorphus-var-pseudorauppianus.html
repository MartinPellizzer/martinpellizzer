Tephrocactus dimorphus var. pseudorauppianus

Tephrocactus dimorphus var. pseudorauppianus, commonly known as the "Furrowed Hedgehog Cactus," is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spiny, tuberculate stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the apex of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
