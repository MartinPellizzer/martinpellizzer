Trixis salina

Trixis salina, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in saline environments. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, making it well adapted to harsh conditions. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Trixis salina plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
