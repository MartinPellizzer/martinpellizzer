Tillandsia imporaensis

Tillandsia imporaensis, commonly known as the Imporaensian air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has short, fibrous roots that help anchor it to its host and absorb moisture from the air. Its rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water retention and nutrient absorption.

Tillandsia imporaensis produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers, making it a popular choice for both horticultural and ornamental purposes.
