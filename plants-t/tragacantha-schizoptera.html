Tragacantha schizoptera

Tragacantha schizoptera, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of Iran and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant's resin, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.
