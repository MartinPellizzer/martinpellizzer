Themeda sabarimalayana

Themeda sabarimalayana, commonly known as the Sabarimalaya grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Themeda sabarimalayana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
