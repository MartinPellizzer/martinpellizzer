Tragopogon cazorlanus

Tragopogon cazorlanus, commonly known as the Cazorlan's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers that transition to a purplish hue as they mature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils.

Tragopogon cazorlanus is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
