Thalia canniformis

Thalia canniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, arranged in clusters along the stems. Thalia canniformis thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as ponds and lakes, and often forms dense mats on the water surface.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas make it both a valuable aquatic plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
