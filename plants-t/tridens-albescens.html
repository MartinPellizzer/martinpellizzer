Tridens albescens

Tridens albescens, commonly known as the white toothed grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy areas where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Tridens albescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate polluted water.
