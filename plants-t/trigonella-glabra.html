Trigonella glabra

Trigonella glabra, commonly known as Indian clover, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Trigonella glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
