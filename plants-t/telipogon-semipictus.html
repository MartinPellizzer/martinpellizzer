Telipogon semipictus

Telipogon semipictus, commonly known as the painted pteridium, is a rare and delicate fern native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with intricate, pale markings. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Telipogon semipictus is considered a species of conservation concern.
