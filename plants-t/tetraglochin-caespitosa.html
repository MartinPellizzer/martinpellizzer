Tetraglochin caespitosa

Tetraglochin caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted rock heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Tetraglochin caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
