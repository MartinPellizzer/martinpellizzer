Teucrium hirsutum

Teucrium hirsutum, commonly known as woolly germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Teucrium hirsutum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
