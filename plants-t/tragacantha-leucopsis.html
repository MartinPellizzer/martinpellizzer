Tragacantha leucopsis

Tragacantha leucopsis, commonly known as the white-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum resin, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stem and has been used historically in medicine and industry. It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and it is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
