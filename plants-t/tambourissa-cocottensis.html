Tambourissa cocottensis

Tambourissa cocottensis, commonly known as the Cocoanut tree, is a large tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, feather-like leaves that provide shade. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by distinctive three-lobed fruits, each containing a single large seed. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine.
