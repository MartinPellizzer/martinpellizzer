Trichopus sempervirens

Trichopus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Trichopus sempervirens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded or partially sunny environments.
