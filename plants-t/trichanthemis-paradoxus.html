Trichanthemis paradoxus

Trichanthemis paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical trichanthemum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
