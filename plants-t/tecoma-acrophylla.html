Tecoma acrophylla

Tecoma acrophylla, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth texture.

Tecoma acrophylla is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
