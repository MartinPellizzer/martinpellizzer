Tabernaemontana subglobosa

Tabernaemontana subglobosa, commonly known as the globular star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with smooth, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique and striking floral display.
