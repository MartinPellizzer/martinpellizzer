Teucrium aureocandidum

Teucrium aureocandidum, commonly known as the golden-white germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features upright stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

Teucrium aureocandidum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
