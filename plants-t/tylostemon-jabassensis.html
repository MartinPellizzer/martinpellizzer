Tylostemon jabassensis

Tylostemon jabassensis, commonly known as the Jabassens mountain vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a gelatinous pulp.

Tylostemon jabassensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
