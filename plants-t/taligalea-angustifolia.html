Taligalea angustifolia

Taligalea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved taligalea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem with smooth, pale bark.

Taligalea angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
