Triodon albus var. macer

Triodon albus var. macer, commonly known as the white sand lichen, is a unique lichen species characterized by its pale, often whitish thallus.

It typically grows on calcareous substrates such as limestone or concrete, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments. The thallus is usually crustose, forming a thin, adherent layer on the surface. It lacks distinct lobes and has a granular texture, which aids in its identification.

This lichen is often found in regions with low humidity and high sunlight exposure, making it a common indicator of specific ecological conditions.
