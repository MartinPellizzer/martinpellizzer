Teucrium heynei

Teucrium heynei, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa, including Ethiopia and Sudan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Teucrium heynei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
