Talassia transiliensis

Talassia transiliensis, commonly known as the Transilian sea fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble the structure of true ferns.

It thrives in brackish and saltwater environments, often found in coastal regions and estuaries. The plant's root system is adapted to anchor it in soft, muddy substrates while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water. Its reproductive structures, including spores, are dispersed by water currents, facilitating its spread along shorelines.

Talassia transiliensis plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine organisms.
