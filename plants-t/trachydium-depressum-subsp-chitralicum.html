Trachydium depressum subsp. chitralicum

Trachydium depressum subsp.

chitralicum, commonly known as the Chitral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
