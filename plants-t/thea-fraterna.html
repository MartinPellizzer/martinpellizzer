Thea fraterna

Thea fraterna, commonly known as the sacred tea plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. Its growth habit includes a compact form with a spreading crown, making it suitable for container gardening.

Thea fraterna thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, preferring humid environments.
