Tupa polyphylla var. besseriana

Tupa polyphylla var. besseriana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to the cool, high-altitude environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
