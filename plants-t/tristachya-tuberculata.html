Tristachya tuberculata

Tristachya tuberculata, commonly known as the woolly grass or silver grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble a cloud-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tristachya tuberculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense clumps.
