Thymus serpyllum f. resupinus

Thymus serpyllum f. resupinus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong thyme scent and making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
