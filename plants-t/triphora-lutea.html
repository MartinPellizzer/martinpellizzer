Triphora lutea

Triphora lutea, commonly known as the yellow triphora, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Triphora lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
