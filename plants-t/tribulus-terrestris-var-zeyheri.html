Tribulus terrestris var. zeyheri

Tribulus terrestris var. zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's goat's horn, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Zygophyllaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and small yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, contributing to its successful spread in various habitats.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
