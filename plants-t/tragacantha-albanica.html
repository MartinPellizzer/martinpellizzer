Tragacantha albanica

Tragacantha albanica, commonly known as the Albanian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod fruits containing small, smooth seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Tragacantha albanica is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
