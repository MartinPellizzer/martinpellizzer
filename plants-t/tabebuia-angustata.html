Tabebuia angustata

Tabebuia angustata, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, trunk-like stem with smooth, gray bark that peels off in strips. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
