Trapa tuberculifera

Trapa tuberculifera, commonly known as the spiked water chestnut, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive spiky fruits that develop underwater. The plant has a short, stout stem that supports its broad, rounded leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

Trapa tuberculifera plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
