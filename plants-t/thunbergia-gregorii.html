Thunbergia gregorii

Thunbergia gregorii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia gregorii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
