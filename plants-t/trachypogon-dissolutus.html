Trachypogon dissolutus

Trachypogon dissolutus, commonly known as the slender grass or lesser false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Trachypogon dissolutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
