Taraxacum devexum

Taraxacum devexum, commonly known as the bent dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. Its flowers develop into fluffy white pappus-bearing seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
