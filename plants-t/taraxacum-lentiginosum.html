Taraxacum lentiginosum

Taraxacum lentiginosum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are often covered with small, dark spots. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flowering head, which develops into a fluffy seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
