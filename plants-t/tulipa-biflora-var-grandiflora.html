Tulipa biflora var. grandiflora

Tulipa biflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora tulip, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces two to three flowers per stem, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant displays.
