Thymus schischkinii

Thymus schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant leaves that emit a strong thyme scent. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
