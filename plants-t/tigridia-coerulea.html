Tigridia coerulea

Tigridia coerulea, commonly known as the blue tigrid lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Tigridia coerulea is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
