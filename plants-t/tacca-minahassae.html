Tacca minahassae

Tacca minahassae, commonly known as the "Malay lady's shoe" or "tongue of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, velvety leaves and a single, ornate flower that resembles a human face. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other dramatic flowering plants like calla lilies. Its unique morphology and nocturnal blooming habits make it a sought-after ornamental species.

Tacca minahassae thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
