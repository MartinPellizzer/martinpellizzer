Tephrosia limpopoensis

Tephrosia limpopoensis, commonly known as the Limpopo tephrosia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Tephrosia limpopoensis is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
