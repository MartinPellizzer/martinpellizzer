Tinus dentata

Tinus dentata, commonly known as the toothed tinus, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated or toothed leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Tinus dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
