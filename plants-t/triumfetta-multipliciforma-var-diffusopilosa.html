Triumfetta multipliciforma var. diffusopilosa

Triumfetta multipliciforma var. diffusopilosa, commonly known as the hairy bushwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and it is typically found in dry, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
