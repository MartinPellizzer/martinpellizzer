Tylanthus abietinus

Tylanthus abietinus, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically silver-white underneath, giving the tree its common name. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Tylanthus abietinus plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
