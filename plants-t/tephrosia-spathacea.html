Tephrosia spathacea

Tephrosia spathacea, commonly known as the purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped.

Tephrosia spathacea is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
