Trichocereus formosus subsp. bruchii

Trichocereus formosus subsp.

bruchii, commonly known as the Bruchii Cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach several meters in height.

The plant produces vibrant, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which open at night. Its thick, ribbed stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
