Teucrium divaricatum var. villosum

Teucrium divaricatum var. villosum, commonly known as hairy creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a dense covering of fine hairs.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
