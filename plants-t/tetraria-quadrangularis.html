Tetraria quadrangularis

Tetraria quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-sided grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its quadrangular stems, which are often hairy and have a distinct four-sided cross-section. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue.

Tetraria quadrangularis is often found in meadows and along the edges of forests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
