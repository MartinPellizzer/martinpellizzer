Trachynia distachya var. hispida

Trachynia distachya var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid trachynia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and botanical collections.
