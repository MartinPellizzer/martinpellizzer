Triticum pseudoagropyrum

Triticum pseudoagropyrum, commonly known as false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
