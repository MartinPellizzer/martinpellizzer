Tragacantha hispanica

Tragacantha hispanica, commonly known as Spanish tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a viscous gum from its pods, which has been historically used in various industries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and wastelands.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
