Terminalia samoensis

Terminalia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in tropical climates with well-drained soils.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in the regions where it is native.
