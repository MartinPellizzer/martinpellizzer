Tragacantha villosa

Tragacantha villosa, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Tragacantha villosa is often found in scrublands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
