Tibouchina longifolia

Tibouchina longifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to deep purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Tibouchina longifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its floral display is particularly notable in the spring and summer months.
