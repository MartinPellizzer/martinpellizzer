Tilia × subjucunda

Tilia × subjucunda, commonly known as the small-leaf lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which appear in early summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in woodworking.
