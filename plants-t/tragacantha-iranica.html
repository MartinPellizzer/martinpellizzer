Tragacantha iranica

Tragacantha iranica, commonly known as Iranian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant produces a gel-like substance from its pods, which has been traditionally used in herbal medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
