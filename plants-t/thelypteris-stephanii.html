Thelypteris stephanii

Thelypteris stephanii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris stephanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
