Trifolium lupinaster f. angustifolium

Trifolium lupinaster f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a botanical variant of the clover genus characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It typically features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lanceolate leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in ecological restoration projects.
