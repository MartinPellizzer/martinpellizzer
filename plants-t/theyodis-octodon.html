Theyodis octodon

Theyodis octodon, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red-orange composite flowers, which are arranged in radiate heads. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value in landscaping.
