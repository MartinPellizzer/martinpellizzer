Tabernaemontana subcapitata

Tabernaemontana subcapitata, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
