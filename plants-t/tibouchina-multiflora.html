Tibouchina multiflora

Tibouchina multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

Tibouchina multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
