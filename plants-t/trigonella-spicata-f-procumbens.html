Trigonella spicata f. procumbens

Trigonella spicata f. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate fenugreek, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground, producing small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with trifoliate leaflets, and its seeds are small, ovate, and yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
