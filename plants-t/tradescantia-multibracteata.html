Tradescantia multibracteata

Tradescantia multibracteata, commonly known as the wandering three, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pink.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
