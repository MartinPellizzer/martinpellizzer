Tapiphyllum cinerascens var. laevius

Tapiphyllum cinerascens var. laevius, commonly known as the smooth bark lichen, is a unique plant characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It typically grows on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in conditions of low humidity and high sunlight exposure. The plant exhibits a dense, crust-like thallus that is smooth to the touch, giving it its varietal name "laevius.". Its coloration ranges from pale gray to a slight reddish-brown, depending on environmental factors.

This lichen plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms in its ecosystem.
