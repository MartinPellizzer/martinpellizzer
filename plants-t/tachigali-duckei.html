Tachigali duckei

Tachigali duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that help reduce water loss in hot climates. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, facilitating seed dispersal.

Tachigali duckei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
