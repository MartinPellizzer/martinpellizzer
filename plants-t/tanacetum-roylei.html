Tanacetum roylei

Tanacetum roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to white ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Tanacetum roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
