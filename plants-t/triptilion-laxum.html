Triptilion laxum

Triptilion laxum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the sporangia for spore production. Native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, it thrives in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ancient lineage, it provides insight into the evolutionary history of early land plants.
