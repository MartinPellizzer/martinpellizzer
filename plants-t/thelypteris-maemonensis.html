Thelypteris maemonensis

Thelypteris maemonensis, commonly known as the Maemon fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into narrow segments. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. Thelypteris maemonensis is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to humid climates, with a robust rhizome system supporting its growth.
