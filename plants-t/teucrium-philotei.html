Teucrium philotei

Teucrium philotei, commonly known as the purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium philotei is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
