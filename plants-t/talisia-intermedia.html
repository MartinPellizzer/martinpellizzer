Talisia intermedia

Talisia intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian redwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and long, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Talisia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and secondary forests.
