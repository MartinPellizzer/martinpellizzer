Tecticornia annelida

Tecticornia annelida, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in saline and arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by insects for pollination. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture from the surface layer of soil.

Tecticornia annelida plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray.
