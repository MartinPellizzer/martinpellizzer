Tragacantha chrysotricha

Tragacantha chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces a gelatinous resin, known as tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, hair-like structures that give it its common name.

Tragacantha chrysotricha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
