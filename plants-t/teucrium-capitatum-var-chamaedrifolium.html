Teucrium capitatum var. chamaedrifolium

Teucrium capitatum var. chamaedrifolium, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of blue or purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
