Taraxacum acutangulum

Taraxacum acutangulum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a short growing season with a taproot that can persist through winter.
