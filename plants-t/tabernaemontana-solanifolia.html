Tabernaemontana solanifolia

Tabernaemontana solanifolia, commonly known as the purple angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
