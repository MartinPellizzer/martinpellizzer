Tetrastigma hainanense

Tetrastigma hainanense, commonly known as the Hainan grapevine, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
