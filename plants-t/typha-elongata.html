Typha elongata

Typha elongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering spike.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a fibrous, spongy stem. Its flowers are typically brown or reddish at maturity, and the plant plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various species.

Typha elongata is also valued for its historical use in crafting and as a source of fiber.
