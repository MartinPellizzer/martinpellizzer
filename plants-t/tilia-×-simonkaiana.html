Tilia × simonkaiana

Tilia × simonkaiana, commonly known as the hybrid lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It is a natural hybrid between Tilia cordata (small-leaved lime) and Tilia platyphyllos (large-leaved lime). The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in diameter. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which appear in early summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
