Telinaria canariensis

Telinaria canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Teloschistaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a smooth or slightly granular surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on cliffs and shaded rock faces in the Canary Islands.

This lichen is of particular interest in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of environmental conditions.
