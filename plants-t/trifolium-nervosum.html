Trifolium nervosum

Trifolium nervosum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium nervosum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
