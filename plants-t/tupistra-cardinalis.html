Tupistra cardinalis

Tupistra cardinalis, commonly known as the red velvet cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish in color, covered with dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens and as a decorative plant.
