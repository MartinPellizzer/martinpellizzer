Tradescantia superba

Tradescantia superba, commonly known as the "Wandering Jew," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, long, arching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Tradescantia superba is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
