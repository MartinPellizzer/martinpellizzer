Thymus hirtus var. battandieri

Thymus hirtus var. battandieri, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that are often aromatic and used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, low-maintenance plants.
