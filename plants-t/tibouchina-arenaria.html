Tibouchina arenaria

Tibouchina arenaria, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

Tibouchina arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
