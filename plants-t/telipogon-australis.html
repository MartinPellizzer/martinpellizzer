Telipogon australis

Telipogon australis, commonly known as the Australian star grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing delicate white or pale blue flowers.

Telipogon australis is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
