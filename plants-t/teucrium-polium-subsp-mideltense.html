Teucrium polium subsp. mideltense

Teucrium polium subsp.

mideltense, commonly known as Moroccan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
