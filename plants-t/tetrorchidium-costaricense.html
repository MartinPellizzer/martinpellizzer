Tetrorchidium costaricense

Tetrorchidium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Costa Rica. This plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
