Triclisperma paucifolia

Triclisperma paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved triclisperma, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a sparse pattern. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense heads, making it visually distinct.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
