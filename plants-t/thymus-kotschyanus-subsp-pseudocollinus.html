Thymus kotschyanus subsp. pseudocollinus

Thymus kotschyanus subsp.

pseudocollinus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications, similar to other thyme species.
