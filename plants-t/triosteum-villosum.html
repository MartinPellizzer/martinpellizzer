Triosteum villosum

Triosteum villosum, commonly known as the downy triosteum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
