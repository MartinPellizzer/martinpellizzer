Trifolium wentzelianum var. stolzii

Trifolium wentzelianum var. stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
