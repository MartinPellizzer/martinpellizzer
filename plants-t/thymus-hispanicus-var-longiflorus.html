Thymus hispanicus var. longiflorus

Thymus hispanicus var. longiflorus, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
