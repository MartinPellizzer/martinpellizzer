Trachyrhizum zippelii

Trachyrhizum zippelii, commonly known as the Zippel's spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, which includes long, slender petals and a distinctive, spider-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture and indirect light. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, often forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
