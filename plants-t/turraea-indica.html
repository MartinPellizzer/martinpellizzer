Turraea indica

Turraea indica, commonly known as Indian turraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Turraea indica is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
