Teucrium scordium var. virgatum

Teucrium scordium var. virgatum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.
