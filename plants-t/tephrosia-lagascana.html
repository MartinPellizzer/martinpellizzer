Tephrosia lagascana

Tephrosia lagascana, commonly known as African blue bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Tephrosia lagascana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both ecological and cultural contexts.
