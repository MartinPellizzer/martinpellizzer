Tripsacum dactyloides var. angustifolium

Tripsacum dactyloides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf wild millet, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and for understanding native grassland ecosystems.
