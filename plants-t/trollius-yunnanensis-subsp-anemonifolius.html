Trollius yunnanensis subsp. anemonifolius

Trollius yunnanensis subsp.

anemonifolius, commonly known as the Yunnan globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic, with a wavy margin and a hairy surface. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear a single inflorescence at the apex.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
