Tropaeolum minus

Tropaeolum minus, commonly known as the lesser sorrel, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions make it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum minus is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.
