Tripleurospermum rosellum var. rosellum

Tripleurospermum rosellum var. rosellum, commonly known as the Rosell’s hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
