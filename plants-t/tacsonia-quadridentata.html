Tacsonia quadridentata

Tacsonia quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed tacsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and establish itself in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tacsonia quadridentata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
