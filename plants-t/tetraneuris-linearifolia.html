Tetraneuris linearifolia

Tetraneuris linearifolia, commonly known as the linearleaf tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Tetraneuris linearifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.
