Tradescantia nana

Tradescantia nana, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with red or purple hues at the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.
