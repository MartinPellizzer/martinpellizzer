Tomex tetranthera

Tomex tetranthera, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Tomex tetranthera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
