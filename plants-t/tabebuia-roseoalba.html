Tabebuia roseoalba

Tabebuia roseoalba, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, smooth trunk and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty.

The tree is also valued for its hardwood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
