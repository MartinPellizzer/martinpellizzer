Tectaria camerooniana

Tectaria camerooniana, commonly known as the Cameroon shield fern, is a species of fern native to Cameroon and neighboring regions in Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, fibrous layer, which helps in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the soil.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in the understory of tropical rainforests.
