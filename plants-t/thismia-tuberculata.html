Thismia tuberculata

Thismia tuberculata, commonly known as the tubercled Thismia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Thismiaceae and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers that lack typical petals. The plant is typically small, with a short stem and a single, showy inflorescence at the top. Its leaves are usually reduced or absent, and it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.

Thismia tuberculata is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and bamboo groves.
