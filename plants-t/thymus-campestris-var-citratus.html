Thymus campestris var. citratus

Thymus campestris var. citratus, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features creeping stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a citrusy aroma. The plant produces pink to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
