Tridax angustifolia

Tridax angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tridax, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It is an annual herb that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with a fibrous root system.

Tridax angustifolia is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
