Trophaeum rhombodeum

Trophaeum rhombodeum, commonly known as the rhomboid rock cress, is a small flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant features basal leaves that are rhomboidal in shape, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Trophaeum rhombodeum is adapted to harsh environments and is often found growing in crevices or on slopes.
