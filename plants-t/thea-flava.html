Thea flava

Thea flava, commonly known as the yellow tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Theaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thea flava prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
