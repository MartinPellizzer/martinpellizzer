Taraxacum acerrinum

Taraxacum acerrinum, commonly known as the bitter dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, hairy flower head. The flowers are typically yellow and bloom in the spring, followed by the production of fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
