Tectona grandis f. pilosula

Tectona grandis f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy teak, is a variant of the teak tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and high economic value in the timber industry. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental qualities as well as its practical applications in construction and furniture-making.
