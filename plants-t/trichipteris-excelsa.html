Trichipteris excelsa

Trichipteris excelsa, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Dicksoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, typical of ferns.

Trichipteris excelsa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a significant role in the forest understory ecosystem.
