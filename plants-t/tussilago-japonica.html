Tussilago japonica

Tussilago japonica, commonly known as Japanese coltsfoot, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Botanically, it is related to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its medicinal properties.
