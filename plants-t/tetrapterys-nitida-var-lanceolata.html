Tetrapterys nitida var. lanceolata

Tetrapterys nitida var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf tetrapterys, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by slender, pod-like fruits. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
