Taraxacum iranicum

Taraxacum iranicum, commonly known as the Iranian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, producing wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures.

Taraxacum iranicum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
