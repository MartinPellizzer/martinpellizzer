Taraxacum dilaniatum

Taraxacum dilaniatum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, while its flowers are yellow and develop into fluffy white seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
