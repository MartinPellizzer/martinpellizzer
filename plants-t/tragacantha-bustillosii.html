Tragacantha bustillosii

Tragacantha bustillosii, commonly known as the Spanish thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
