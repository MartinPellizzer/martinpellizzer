Thelypteris schwackeana

Thelypteris schwackeana, commonly known as the Schwacke's holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
