Thapsus pheniceum

Thapsus pheniceum, commonly known as the purple thapsus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its striking purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thapsus pheniceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
