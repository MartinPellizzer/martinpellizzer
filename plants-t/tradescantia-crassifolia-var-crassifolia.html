Tradescantia crassifolia var. crassifolia

Tradescantia crassifolia var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a bluish tinge, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
