Trifolium caudatum var. grosseanum

Trifolium caudatum var. grosseanum, commonly known as the broad-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are broad and have a distinctive rounded shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
