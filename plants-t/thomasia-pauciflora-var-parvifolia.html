Thomasia pauciflora var. parvifolia

Thomasia pauciflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thomasia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
