Turnera ulmifolia var. surinamensis

Turnera ulmifolia var. surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam turnera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
