Thryptomene prolifera

Thryptomene prolifera, commonly known as the hairy thryptomene, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Thryptomene prolifera is often found in heathlands and rocky soils, and it plays a role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
