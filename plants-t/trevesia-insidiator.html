Trevesia insidiator

Trevesia insidiator, commonly known as the ambush plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It possesses specialized leaves that can trap and digest insects, utilizing a sticky secretion to immobilize prey. The plant's structure includes a central stem with modified leaves that form a funnel-like shape, enhancing its ability to ensnare unsuspecting victims. Its botanical features are adapted to nutrient-poor environments, allowing it to supplement its diet with captured insects.

Despite its predatory nature, it plays a role in the ecosystem by controlling insect populations.
