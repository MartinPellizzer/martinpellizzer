Toxicodendron comarophyllum

Toxicodendron comarophyllum, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its berries are red and contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in disturbed environments.
