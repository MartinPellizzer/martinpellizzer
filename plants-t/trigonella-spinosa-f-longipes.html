Trigonella spinosa f. longipes

Trigonella spinosa f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, hairy pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for dry, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both traditional medicine and agricultural use.
