Thrixspermum pictum

Thrixspermum pictum, commonly known as the painted thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows in shrubland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a dense inflorescence.

Thrixspermum pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
