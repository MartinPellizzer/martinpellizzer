Tylocarya cylindrostachya

Tylocarya cylindrostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants, which is a notable aspect of its reproductive biology. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its cones, which are the reproductive structures, are typically borne on separate stems, with male cones producing pollen and female cones developing into seeds.

Tylocarya cylindrostachya is native to certain regions of South America and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
