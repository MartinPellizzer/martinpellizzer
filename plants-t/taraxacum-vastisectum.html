Taraxacum vastisectum

Taraxacum vastisectum, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a puffball of seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant has a robust taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
