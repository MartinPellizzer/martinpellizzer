Tamarix gallica subsp. epidiscina

Tamarix gallica subsp.

epidiscina, commonly known as the European tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, often sprawling growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and saline soils.

The plant produces small, pink or red flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are scale-like, and the bark is typically grayish or reddish.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
