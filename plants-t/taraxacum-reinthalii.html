Taraxacum reinthalii

Taraxacum reinthalii, commonly known as the Reinthal's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head. This perennial plant is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
