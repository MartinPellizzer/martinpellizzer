Tragopogon lanatus

Tragopogon lanatus, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, leafy stem with distinctive, hairy leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Its yellow flower head consists of many small florets, with the central disk surrounded by ray florets that resemble petals.

Tragopogon lanatus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
