Trifolium fimbriatum

Trifolium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Trifolium fimbriatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
