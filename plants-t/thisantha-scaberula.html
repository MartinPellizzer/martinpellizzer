Thisantha scaberula

Thisantha scaberula, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The flowers are typically blue or violet, bell-shaped, and clustered in racemes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.
