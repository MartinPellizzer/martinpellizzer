Trichosalpinx trachystoma

Trichosalpinx trachystoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved salvia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in the summer months.

Trichosalpinx trachystoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
