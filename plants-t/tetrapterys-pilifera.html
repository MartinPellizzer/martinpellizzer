Tetrapterys pilifera

Tetrapterys pilifera, commonly known as the silky leaf tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tetrapterys pilifera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
