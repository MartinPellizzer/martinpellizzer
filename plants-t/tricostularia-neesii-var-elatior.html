Tricostularia neesii var. elatior

Tricostularia neesii var. elatior, commonly known as the greater thyme-leaved rock-cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of aromatic, gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine trichomes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
