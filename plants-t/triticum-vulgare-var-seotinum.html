Triticum vulgare var. seotinum

Triticum vulgare var. seotinum, commonly known as the Seotinum wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically exhibits a compact growth habit with shorter stalks compared to standard wheat varieties. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinct appearance. Its grains are usually smaller and more elongated, with a hard, amber-colored hull.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its resilience to certain environmental stresses, making it valuable in specific agricultural contexts.
