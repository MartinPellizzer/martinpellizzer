Triticum × flaksbergeri

Triticum × flaksbergeri, commonly known as the flaksbergeri rye, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between common rye (Secale cereale) and wheat (Triticum spp.).

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant typically exhibits tall, slender stems with long, narrow leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of plant hybridization and genetic diversity.

However, it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes due to its limited yield and specific growing requirements.
