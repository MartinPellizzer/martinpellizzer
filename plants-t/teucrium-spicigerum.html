Teucrium spicigerum

Teucrium spicigerum, commonly known as spiked germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spikes. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Teucrium spicigerum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
