Tagetes elliptica

Tagetes elliptica, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The plant is also known for its ability to repel certain pests, making it a useful companion plant in gardens.
