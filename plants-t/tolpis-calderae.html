Tolpis calderae

Tolpis calderae, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Tolpis calderae is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
