Tragopogon coelesyriacus

Tragopogon coelesyriacus, commonly known as the Persian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by seed heads that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tragopogon coelesyriacus is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
