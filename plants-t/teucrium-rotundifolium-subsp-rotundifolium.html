Teucrium rotundifolium subsp. rotundifolium

Teucrium rotundifolium subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as roundleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in hue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
