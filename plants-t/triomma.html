Triomma

Triomma, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it difficult to study. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Despite its beauty, Triomma remains a poorly understood species due to its elusive nature and limited distribution.
