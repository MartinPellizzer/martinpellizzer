Trichomanes euphlebium

Trichomanes euphlebium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. It has a unique reproductive structure called a sporophyll, which bears the spores.

Trichomanes euphlebium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
