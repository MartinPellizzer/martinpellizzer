Teucrium capitatum subsp. valentinum

Teucrium capitatum subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as the Valentin's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
