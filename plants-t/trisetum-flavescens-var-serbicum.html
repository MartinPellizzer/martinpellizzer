Trisetum flavescens var. serbicum

Trisetum flavescens var. serbicum, commonly known as the Serbicum false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, pale yellow to golden spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color that becomes more yellowish as the plant matures.

It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
