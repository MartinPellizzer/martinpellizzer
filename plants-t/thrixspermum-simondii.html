Thrixspermum simondii

Thrixspermum simondii, commonly known as the Kashmir thorn apple, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody, branched structure.

Thrixspermum simondii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
