Thysanocarpus curvipes subsp. longistylus

Thysanocarpus curvipes subsp.

longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled thysanocarpus, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves.

The flowers are small and yellow, with distinctive stipules that are often longer than the leaves. The subspecies is notable for its elongated stamens, which give it its common name.

It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats.
