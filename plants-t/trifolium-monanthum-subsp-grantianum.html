Trifolium monanthum subsp. grantianum

Trifolium monanthum subsp.

grantianum, commonly known as the Grant's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a deep green color.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and improving soil health.
