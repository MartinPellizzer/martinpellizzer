Tunica pamphylica

Tunica pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian tunicate, is a rare and elusive plant found exclusively in the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent leaf structure that allows for efficient light absorption in its shaded habitat. The plant's flowers are small and clustered, typically blooming in late spring. Its root system is adapted to retain moisture in the rocky soil, and it has a slow growth rate, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Tunica pamphylica is considered a species of conservation concern.
