Trigynaea cinnamomea

Trigynaea cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented bark. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is valued for its aromatic bark, which has been traditionally used in perfumery and as a spice.
