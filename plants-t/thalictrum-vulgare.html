Thalictrum vulgare

Thalictrum vulgare, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Thalictrum vulgare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
