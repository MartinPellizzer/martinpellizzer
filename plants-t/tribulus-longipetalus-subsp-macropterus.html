Tribulus longipetalus subsp. macropterus

Tribulus longipetalus subsp.

macropterus, commonly known as the large-petaled tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers with five prominent petals.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and features woody stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.
