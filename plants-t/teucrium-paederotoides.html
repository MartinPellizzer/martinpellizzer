Teucrium paederotoides

Teucrium paederotoides, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
