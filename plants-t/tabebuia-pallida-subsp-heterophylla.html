Tabebuia pallida subsp. heterophylla

Tabebuia pallida subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a slightly hairy underside. The bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral displays.
