Thelocactus lloydii

Thelocactus lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's thorn cactus, is a rare and spiny cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, tuberculate spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
