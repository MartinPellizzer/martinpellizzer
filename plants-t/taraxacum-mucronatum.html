Taraxacum mucronatum

Taraxacum mucronatum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive, elongated seed head with bracts. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
