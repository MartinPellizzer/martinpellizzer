Triticum aestivum var. erinaceum

Triticum aestivum var. erinaceum, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its short stature, rough leaves, and compact spikelets that bear small, hard grains. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry and semi-arid conditions. Its grains are not typically cultivated for food but are used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improving crop resilience.
