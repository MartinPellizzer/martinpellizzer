Tephrosia eriocarpa

Tephrosia eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves. The leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in pairs, and the plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
