Tephrosia congestiflora

Tephrosia congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered tephrosia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorny growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Tephrosia congestiflora is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
