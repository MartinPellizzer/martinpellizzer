Telanthera villosa

Telanthera villosa, commonly known as the hairy telanthera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Telanthera villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
