Tulipa florentina

Tulipa florentina, commonly known as the Florence tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange eye. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
