Taraxacum elegantissimum

Taraxacum elegantissimum, commonly known as the elegant dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
