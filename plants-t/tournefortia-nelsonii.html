Tournefortia nelsonii

Tournefortia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
