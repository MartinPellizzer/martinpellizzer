Thymus fontanesii

Thymus fontanesii, commonly known as the Alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Thymus fontanesii is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments.
