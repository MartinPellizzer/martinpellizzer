Triumfetta quercetorum

Triumfetta quercetorum, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Triumfetta quercetorum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
