Tolmiea occidentalis

Tolmiea occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Tolmiea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features rosettes of waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Tolmiea occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
