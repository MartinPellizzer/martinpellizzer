Tropidia acuminata

Tropidia acuminata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Tropidia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
