Telanthera nodifera

Telanthera nodifera, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, bright red or orange flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Telanthera nodifera thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
