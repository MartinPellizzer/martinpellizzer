Terminaliopsis tetrandrus

Terminaliopsis tetrandrus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
