Triglochin komarovii

Triglochin komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, exhibiting a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in its native environment.

Triglochin komarovii is adapted to fluctuating water levels and is often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds.
