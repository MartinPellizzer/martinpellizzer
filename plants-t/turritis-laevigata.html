Turritis laevigata

Turritis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth turritis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are smooth and often covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are yellow to orange in color, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
