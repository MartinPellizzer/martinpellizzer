Thrixspermum tenuicalcar

Thrixspermum tenuicalcar, commonly known as the slender-spined thrixspermum, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in clusters at the stem tips. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Thrixspermum tenuicalcar is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
