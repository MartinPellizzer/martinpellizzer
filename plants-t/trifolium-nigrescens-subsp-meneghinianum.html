Trifolium nigrescens subsp. meneghinianum

Trifolium nigrescens subsp.

meneghinianum, commonly known as the Italian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
