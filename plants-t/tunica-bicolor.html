Tunica bicolor

Tunica bicolor, commonly known as the two-color tunica, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored petals, which typically display a combination of white and purple or pink hues. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

Tunica bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
