Teucrium zanonii

Teucrium zanonii, commonly known as the Argentine germander, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Teucrium zanonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
