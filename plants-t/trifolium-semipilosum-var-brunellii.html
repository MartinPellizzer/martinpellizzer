Trifolium semipilosum var. brunellii

Trifolium semipilosum var. brunellii, commonly known as the Brunell's hairy clover, is a variant of the hairy clover species native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
