Tsuga canadensis var. aurea

Tsuga canadensis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with its dark green needles. The plant has a conical shape with a broad, spreading crown, and its bark is reddish-brown and fissured. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
