Tritonia flabellifolia var. major

Tritonia flabellifolia var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
