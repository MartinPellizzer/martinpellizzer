Tragacantha hypogaea

Tragacantha hypogaea, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, followed by distinctive pod fruits that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in medicine and industry.

Tragacantha hypogaea thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
