Thymus striatus subsp. striatus

Thymus striatus subsp.

striatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive greyish-green color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.
