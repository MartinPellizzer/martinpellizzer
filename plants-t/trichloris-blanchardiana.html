Trichloris blanchardiana

Trichloris blanchardiana, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescence that resembles a plume. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Trichloris blanchardiana is adapted to dry conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
