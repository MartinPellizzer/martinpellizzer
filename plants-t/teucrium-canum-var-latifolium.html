Teucrium canum var. latifolium

Teucrium canum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped to broadly ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a valued plant in both ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
