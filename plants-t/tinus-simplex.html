Tinus simplex

Tinus simplex, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and small, clustered yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Tinus simplex is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
