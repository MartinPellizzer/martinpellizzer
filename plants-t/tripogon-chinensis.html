Tripogon chinensis

Tripogon chinensis, commonly known as Chinese tripogon, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of small, two-flowered spikelets that are often tightly packed.

Tripogon chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
