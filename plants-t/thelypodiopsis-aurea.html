Thelypodiopsis aurea

Thelypodiopsis aurea, commonly known as golden sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in early spring. Thelypodiopsis aurea is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy, well-drained soils.
