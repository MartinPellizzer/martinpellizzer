Thea crapnelliana

Thea crapnelliana, commonly known as the Crapnell's tea plant, is a rare and unique member of the Theaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
