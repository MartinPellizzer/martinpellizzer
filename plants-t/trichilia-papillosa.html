Trichilia papillosa

Trichilia papillosa, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
