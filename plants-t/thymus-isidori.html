Thymus isidori

Thymus isidori, commonly known as the Isidori thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Thymus isidori is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
