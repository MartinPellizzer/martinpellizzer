Trifolium longipes var. atrorubens

Trifolium longipes var. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red clover, is a variant of the longipes species within the genus Trifolium.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark red to purplish flowers, which contrast with its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also known for its potential ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation and support for pollinators.
