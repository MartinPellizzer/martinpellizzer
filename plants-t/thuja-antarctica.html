Thuja antarctica

Thuja antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic thuja, is a rare and resilient conifer native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramidal shape and dense foliage of scale-like leaves. This plant thrives in cold, harsh environments, often growing in rocky or coastal areas. Its slow growth rate makes it a long-lived species, with some individuals surviving for centuries.

The tree's ability to withstand extreme climates makes it an important subject in botanical studies of adaptation and survival.
