Tithonia pusilla

Tithonia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and thrives in full sun. It is often used as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and compact size.

Tithonia pusilla is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
