Tridax petrophila

Tridax petrophila, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and rocky areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, dandelion-like cluster.

Tridax petrophila is often used in traditional medicine and is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.
