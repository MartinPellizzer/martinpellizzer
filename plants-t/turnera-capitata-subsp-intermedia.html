Turnera capitata subsp. intermedia

Turnera capitata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America. It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the spring.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
