Trisetum pinetorum

Trisetum pinetorum, commonly known as the pine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in pine forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.

Trisetum pinetorum plays an important role in maintaining the health of forested and wetland ecosystems.
