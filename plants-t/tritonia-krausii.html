Tritonia krausii

Tritonia krausii, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Tritonia krausii blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators.
