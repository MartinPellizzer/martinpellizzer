Trichogyne cauliflora

Trichogyne cauliflora, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the stems rather than the leaves. The plant produces vibrant, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

The trichogyne, a specialized structure, plays a crucial role in the plant's reproductive process by facilitating the attachment of fungal spores.
