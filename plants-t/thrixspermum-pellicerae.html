Thrixspermum pellicerae

Thrixspermum pellicerae, commonly known as the silky thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Thrixspermum pellicerae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
