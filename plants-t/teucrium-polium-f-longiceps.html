Teucrium polium f. longiceps

Teucrium polium f. longiceps, commonly known as the long-headed germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
