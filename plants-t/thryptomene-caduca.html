Thryptomene caduca

Thryptomene caduca, commonly known as the weeping thryptomene, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that offers habitat for native wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
