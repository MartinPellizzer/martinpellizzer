Thamnochortus scariosus

Thamnochortus scariosus, commonly known as the silver leaf groundsel, is a South African endemic species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-white, woolly leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a perennial herb that forms a low-growing rosette, and its foliage is covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
