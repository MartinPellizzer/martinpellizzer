Teucrium subspinosum var. balearicum

Teucrium subspinosum var. balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and rosemary.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
