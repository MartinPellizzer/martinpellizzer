Taraxacum semiprivum

Taraxacum semiprivum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single flowering head. The flowers are yellow and produce seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and gardens.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
