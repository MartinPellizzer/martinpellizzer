Tradescantia hirsuticaulis

Tradescantia hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, often branching at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, textured appearance.

It is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
