Turnera rosea

Turnera rosea, commonly known as the "sunshine flower," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family, which includes other well-known species like passionflowers. Turnera rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
