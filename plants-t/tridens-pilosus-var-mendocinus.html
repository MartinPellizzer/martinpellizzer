Tridens pilosus var. mendocinus

Tridens pilosus var. mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino horsebrush, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky habitats along the coast.
