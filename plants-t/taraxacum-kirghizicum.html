Taraxacum kirghizicum

Taraxacum kirghizicum, commonly known as the Kirghiz dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Taraxacum kirghizicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
