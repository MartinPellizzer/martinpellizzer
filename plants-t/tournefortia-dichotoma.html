Tournefortia dichotoma

Tournefortia dichotoma, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
