Trophaeum × tenuirostre

Trophaeum × tenuirostre, commonly known as the slender-beaked larkspur, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated calyx that resembles a beak, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and are arranged in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
