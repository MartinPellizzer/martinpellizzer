Taxillus chinensis

Taxillus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the family Rosaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its parasitic nature makes it an important component of forest ecosystems, influencing host tree growth and biodiversity.

Taxillus chinensis is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
