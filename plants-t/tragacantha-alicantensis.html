Tragacantha alicantensis

Tragacantha alicantensis, commonly known as the Alicant tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a sticky resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
