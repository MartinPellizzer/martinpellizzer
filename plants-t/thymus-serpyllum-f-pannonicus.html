Thymus serpyllum f. pannonicus

Thymus serpyllum f. pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its creeping stems. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
