Thymus serpyllum f. glaber

Thymus serpyllum f. glaber, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
