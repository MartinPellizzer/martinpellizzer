Trillium chandleri f. gladewitzii

Trillium chandleri f. gladewitzii, commonly known as the Gladewitz trillium, is a rare and unique variant of the trillium genus native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-petaled flowers, typically displaying a striking combination of white and pink hues. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, upright stem that supports its showy bloom. It thrives in shaded, moist forest environments and is often found in rich, acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
