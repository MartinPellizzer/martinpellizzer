Tricalysia puberula

Tricalysia puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Tricalysia puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions as a garden plant.
