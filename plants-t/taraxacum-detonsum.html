Taraxacum detonsum

Taraxacum detonsum, commonly known as the explosive dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique seed dispersal mechanism.

The plant features a rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that form a dense cluster near the ground. Its flower head consists of numerous small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. After flowering, the plant produces a hollow, spiky stem topped with a white, fluffy pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

This species is native to Europe and has adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats.
