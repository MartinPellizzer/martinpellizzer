Tragacantha humilis

Tragacantha humilis, commonly known as the Persian gumweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the dry regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to arid environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
