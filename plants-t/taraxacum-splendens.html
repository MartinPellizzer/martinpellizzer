Taraxacum splendens

Taraxacum splendens, commonly known as the splendid dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, ribbed stems. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Taraxacum splendens is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
