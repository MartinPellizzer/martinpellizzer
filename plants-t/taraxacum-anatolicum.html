Taraxacum anatolicum

Taraxacum anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces and a hollow, leafy stem that supports its flowering head. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seeds with pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its potential in herbal medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
