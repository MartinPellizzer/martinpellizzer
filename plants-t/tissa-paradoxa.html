Tissa paradoxa

Tissa paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unusual leaf morphology, featuring a combination of broad and narrow leaves on the same stem. The plant exhibits a peculiar growth habit, often growing in a spiral pattern that defies typical botanical symmetry. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and distinctive features, Tissa paradoxa is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
