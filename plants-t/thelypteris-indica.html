Thelypteris indica

Thelypteris indica, commonly known as the Indian shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is creeping, and the fronds are pinnately compound with a distinctive shield-shaped base.

Thelypteris indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
