Tydaea ocellata var. formosa

Tydaea ocellata var. formosa, commonly known as the showy false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive black center, which are borne on slender, hairy stems. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
