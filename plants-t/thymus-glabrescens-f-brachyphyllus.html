Thymus glabrescens f. brachyphyllus

Thymus glabrescens f. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong, minty scent.

This variety is a popular choice for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
