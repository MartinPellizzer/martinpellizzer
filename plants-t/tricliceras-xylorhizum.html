Tricliceras xylorhizum

Tricliceras xylorhizum, commonly known as the golden root vine, is a unique plant with a complex root system that exhibits both fibrous and tuberous structures.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin and a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for its roots, which are rich in bioactive compounds.
