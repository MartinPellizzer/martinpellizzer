Taraxacum pulverulentum

Taraxacum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which are typically yellow in color. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
