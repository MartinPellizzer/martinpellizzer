Tamarix arabica

Tamarix arabica, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, deciduous foliage and small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Tamarix arabica is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
