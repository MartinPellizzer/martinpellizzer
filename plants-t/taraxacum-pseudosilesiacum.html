Taraxacum pseudosilesiacum

Taraxacum pseudosilesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head. Native to Central Europe, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in urban areas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
