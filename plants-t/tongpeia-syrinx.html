Tongpeia syrinx

Tongpeia syrinx, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early summer. Tongpeia syrinx thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
