Triplaris macrocalyx

Triplaris macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited triplaris, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are woody, three-lobed capsules that contain several seeds.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
