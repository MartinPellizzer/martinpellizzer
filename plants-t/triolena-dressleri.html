Triolena dressleri

Triolena dressleri, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
