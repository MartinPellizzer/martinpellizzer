Thymbra sintenisii

Thymbra sintenisii, commonly known as the Sicilian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny conditions. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thymbra sintenisii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
