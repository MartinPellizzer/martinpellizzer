Telanthera rosea var. typica

Telanthera rosea var. typica, commonly known as the pink-flowered telanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
