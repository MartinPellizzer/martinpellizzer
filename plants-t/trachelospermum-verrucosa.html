Trachelospermum verrucosa

Trachelospermum verrucosa, commonly known as the rough trachelospermum, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has woody stems with a rough, bark-like texture, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Trachelospermum verrucosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
