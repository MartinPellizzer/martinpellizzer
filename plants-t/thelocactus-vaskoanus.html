Thelocactus vaskoanus

Thelocactus vaskoanus, commonly known as the Vasko's locactus, is a rare cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in extreme drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
