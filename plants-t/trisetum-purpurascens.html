Trisetum purpurascens

Trisetum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often purplish-colored stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and typically have a bluish-green hue.

Trisetum purpurascens is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
