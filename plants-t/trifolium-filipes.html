Trifolium filipes

Trifolium filipes, commonly known as the slender clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Trifolium filipes is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
