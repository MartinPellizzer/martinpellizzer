Thalictrum columnare

Thalictrum columnare, commonly known as the fern-leaf columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Thalictrum columnare is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
