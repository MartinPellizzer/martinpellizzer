Teucrium stocksianum subsp. gabrieliae

Teucrium stocksianum subsp.

gabrieliae, commonly known as Gabriel's germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
