Tupacamaria montana

Tupacamaria montana, commonly known as the mountain tupaj, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, followed by small, round berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in local remedies for various ailments.
