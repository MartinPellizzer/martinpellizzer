Tripleurospermum rosellum var. album

Tripleurospermum rosellum var. album, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk floret. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of white to the landscape.
