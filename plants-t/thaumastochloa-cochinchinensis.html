Thaumastochloa cochinchinensis

Thaumastochloa cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese grass or *Thaumastochloa cochinchinensis*, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical structures.

Thaumastochloa cochinchinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
