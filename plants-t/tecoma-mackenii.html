Tecoma mackenii

Tecoma mackenii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma mackenii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
