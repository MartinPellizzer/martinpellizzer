Thunbergia pratensis

Thunbergia pratensis, commonly known as the white moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
