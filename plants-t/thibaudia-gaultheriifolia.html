Thibaudia gaultheriifolia

Thibaudia gaultheriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thibaudia gaultheriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
