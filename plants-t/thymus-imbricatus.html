Thymus imbricatus

Thymus imbricatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Thymus imbricatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
