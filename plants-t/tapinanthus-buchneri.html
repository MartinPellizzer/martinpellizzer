Tapinanthus buchneri

Tapinanthus buchneri, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from host plants.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, which means it cannot perform photosynthesis. The plant typically has a short, erect stem with small, linear leaves and produces delicate, orchid-like flowers. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
