Trifolium litwinowii

Trifolium litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and dry grasslands. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
