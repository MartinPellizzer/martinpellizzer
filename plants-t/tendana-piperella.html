Tendana piperella

Tendana piperella, commonly known as the African pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Tendana piperella thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
