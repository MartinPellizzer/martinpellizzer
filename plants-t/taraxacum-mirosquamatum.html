Taraxacum mirosquamatum

Taraxacum mirosquamatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and a single, tall flowering stem that produces a crown of yellow flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and its role in soil stabilization.
