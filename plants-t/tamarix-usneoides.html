Tamarix usneoides

Tamarix usneoides, commonly known as the Spanish moss, is an epiphytic plant that attaches itself to trees and other structures without parasitizing them.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in the southeastern United States, particularly in the Florida Panhandle. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to the aesthetic of forest canopies.
