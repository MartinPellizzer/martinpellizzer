Thalictrum bauhini proles subfalcatum

Thalictrum bauhini proles subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
