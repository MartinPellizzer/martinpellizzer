Tricholaena tanatricha

Tricholaena tanatricha, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique fern species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are pinnately divided with a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive look.

Tricholaena tanatricha thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rainforests.
