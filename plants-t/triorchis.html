Triorchis

Triorchis, commonly known as the fairy slipper, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It consists of three species native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Carolinas. The plants are characterized by their unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects. Triorchis species are terrestrial, growing in wetlands and moist woodlands, and they rely on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake.
