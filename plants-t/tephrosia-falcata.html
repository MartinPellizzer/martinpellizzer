Tephrosia falcata

Tephrosia falcata, commonly known as the sicklepod tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by pods that resemble a sickle shape, giving it its common name.

Tephrosia falcata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
