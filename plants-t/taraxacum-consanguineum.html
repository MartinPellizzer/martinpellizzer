Taraxacum consanguineum

Taraxacum consanguineum, commonly known as the purple ray flover, is a species of dandelion native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged flower heads and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum consanguineum is a perennial plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
