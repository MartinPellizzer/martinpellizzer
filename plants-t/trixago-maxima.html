Trixago maxima

Trixago maxima, commonly known as the giant trixago, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Trixago maxima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
