Tsusiophyllum tanakae

Tsusiophyllum tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese moonflower, is a rare flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
