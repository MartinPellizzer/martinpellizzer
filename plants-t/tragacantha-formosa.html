Tragacantha formosa

Tragacantha formosa, commonly known as the beautiful tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a gum-like substance from its stems, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Tragacantha formosa thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
