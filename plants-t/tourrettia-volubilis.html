Tourrettia volubilis

Tourrettia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its twining stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Tourrettia volubilis make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
