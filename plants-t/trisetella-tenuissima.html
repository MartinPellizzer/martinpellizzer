Trisetella tenuissima

Trisetella tenuissima, commonly known as the slender false heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular plant among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant species.
