Tilia serratifolia

Tilia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia serratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
