Taenitis cordata

Taenitis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf taenitis, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like philodendrons and caladiums. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically deep green and have a smooth texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries.

Taenitis cordata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
