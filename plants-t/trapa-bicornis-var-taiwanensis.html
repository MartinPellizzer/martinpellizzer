Trapa bicornis var. taiwanensis

Trapa bicornis var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Taiwan.

It features floating leaves with a waxy cuticle that helps repel water, and its distinctive two-lobed fruit is adapted for water dispersal. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures are specialized for underwater pollination, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
