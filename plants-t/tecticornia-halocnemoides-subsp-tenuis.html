Tecticornia halocnemoides subsp. tenuis

Tecticornia halocnemoides subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as slender saltbush, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a deep root system that helps it access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is often found in saline soils and is an important component of stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
