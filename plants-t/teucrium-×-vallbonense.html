Teucrium × vallbonense

Teucrium × vallbonense, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some historical use in herbal medicine.

Teucrium × vallbonense is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
