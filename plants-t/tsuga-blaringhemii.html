Tsuga blaringhemii

Tsuga blaringhemii, commonly known as the Blaringhem fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tsuga blaringhemii plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
