Tylostemon sessilifolius

Tylostemon sessilifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tylostemon, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
