Trichera norica

Trichera norica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Trichera norica is often found in alpine meadows and is a symbol of the rugged beauty of high-altitude flora.
