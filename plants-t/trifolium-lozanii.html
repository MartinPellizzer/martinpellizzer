Trifolium lozanii

Trifolium lozanii, commonly known as the Lozan's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the spring. Trifolium lozanii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
