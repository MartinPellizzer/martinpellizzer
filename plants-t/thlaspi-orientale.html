Thlaspi orientale

Thlaspi orientale, commonly known as Oriental cabbages or stink cabbage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant contains glucosinolates, which contribute to its characteristic pungent odor when crushed.
