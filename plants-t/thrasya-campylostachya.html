Thrasya campylostachya

Thrasya campylostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat. Thrasya campylostachya thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
