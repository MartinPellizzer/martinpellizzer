Tulipa segusiana

Tulipa segusiana, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a contrasting center. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climates, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Tulipa segusiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
