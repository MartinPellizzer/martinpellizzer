Tabebuia jackiana

Tabebuia jackiana, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded canopy, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Tabebuia jackiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
