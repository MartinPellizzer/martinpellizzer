Tainia pulchra

Tainia pulchra, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. Tainia pulchra thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
