Tragacantha elongata

Tragacantha elongata, commonly known as the elongated tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Tragacantha elongata is valued not only for its economic uses but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
