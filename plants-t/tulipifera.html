Tulipifera

Tulipifera, commonly known as the tulip tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the magnolia family.

It is characterized by its showy, tulip-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to eastern North America and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Tulipifera species typically grow as trees or shrubs with smooth, gray bark and alternate leaves.

The genus includes several species, such as *Liriodendron tulipifera*, which is the most well-known and widely distributed.
