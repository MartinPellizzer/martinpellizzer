Triticum kingianum

Triticum kingianum, commonly known as King's wheat, is a species of wild wheat native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the grass family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Despite its agricultural potential, it remains a lesser-known relative of domesticated wheat species.
