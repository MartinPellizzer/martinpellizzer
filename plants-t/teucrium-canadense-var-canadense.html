Teucrium canadense var. canadense

Teucrium canadense var. canadense, commonly known as American germander, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
