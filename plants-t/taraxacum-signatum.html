Taraxacum signatum

Taraxacum signatum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with long, thin bracts that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Taraxacum signatum is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
