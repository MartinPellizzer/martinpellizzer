Taraxacum simpliciusculum

Taraxacum simpliciusculum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

This species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
