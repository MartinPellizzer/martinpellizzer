Thymus glabrescens subvar. serpens

Thymus glabrescens subvar. serpens, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

The subspecies serpens is distinguished by its trailing stems and dense, mat-forming growth pattern.
