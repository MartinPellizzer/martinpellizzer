Triticum aestivum var. falsedelfii

Triticum aestivum var. falsedelfii, commonly known as the false delft blue grass, is a variety of wheat that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous sessile florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct sheathing base, while the stems are hollow and slightly compressed.

This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
