Thymus karavaevii

Thymus karavaevii, commonly known as Karavaev's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other thyme species.
