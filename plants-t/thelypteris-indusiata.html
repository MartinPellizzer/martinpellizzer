Thelypteris indusiata

Thelypteris indusiata, commonly known as the velvet shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing sori that are protected by a kidney-shaped indusium.

Thelypteris indusiata is native to various regions in Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
