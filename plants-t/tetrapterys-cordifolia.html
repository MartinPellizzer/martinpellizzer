Tetrapterys cordifolia

Tetrapterys cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf tetrapterys, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically leathery and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
