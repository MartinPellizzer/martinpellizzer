Triosteum perfoliatum var. illinoense

Triosteum perfoliatum var. illinoense, commonly known as the Illinois spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, three-petaled blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are upright and can grow up to 30 inches tall, often with a reddish tinge.

The species is adapted to moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
