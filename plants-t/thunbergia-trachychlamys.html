Thunbergia trachychlamys

Thunbergia trachychlamys, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
