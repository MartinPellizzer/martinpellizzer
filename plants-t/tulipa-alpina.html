Tulipa alpina

Tulipa alpina, commonly known as the alpine tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Tulipa alpina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to naturalize and form dense stands over time.
