Tristellateia madagascariensis subvar. emarginata

Tristellateia madagascariensis subvar. emarginata, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that help store water in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of showy, star-shaped flowers with a distinctive white to pale pink coloration. Its blooming period typically occurs during the rainy season, enhancing its ornamental value.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
