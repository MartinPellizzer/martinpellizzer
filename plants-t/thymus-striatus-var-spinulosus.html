Thymus striatus var. spinulosus

Thymus striatus var. spinulosus, commonly known as the hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with fine, hairy textures that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact form and fragrant leaves make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
