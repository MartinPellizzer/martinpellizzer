Taraxacum spadiceum

Taraxacum spadiceum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy surface, while the stem is usually unbranched and slightly hairy.

Taraxacum spadiceum is a perennial herb that produces a taproot and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
