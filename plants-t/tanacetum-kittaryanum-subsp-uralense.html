Tanacetum kittaryanum subsp. uralense

Tanacetum kittaryanum subsp.

uralense, commonly known as the Ural tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing a robust adaptation to harsh environments. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
