Trichopilia marginata var. olivacea

Trichopilia marginata var. olivacea, commonly known as the olive-margin sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with a pale green or olive hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
