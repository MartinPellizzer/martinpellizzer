Trifolium fragiferum subsp. physodes

Trifolium fragiferum subsp.

physodes, commonly known as the fragrant clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and grasslands.

Its fragrance is subtle but noticeable, contributing to its appeal in both ecological and ornamental settings.
