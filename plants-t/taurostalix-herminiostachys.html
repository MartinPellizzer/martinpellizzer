Taurostalix herminiostachys

Taurostalix herminiostachys, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant pink or white flowers.

Taurostalix herminiostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
