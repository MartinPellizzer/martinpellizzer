Tragacantha leontina

Tragacantha leontina, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus leontina, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a binder in various applications.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for pollinators.
