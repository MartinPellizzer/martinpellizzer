Tibouchina adscendens

Tibouchina adscendens, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Tibouchina adscendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
