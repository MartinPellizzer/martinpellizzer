Troximon purpureum

Troximon purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
