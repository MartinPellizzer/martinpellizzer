Tetrastigma obtectum var. potentilla

Tetrastigma obtectum var. potentilla, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often consumed by birds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and climbing habit.
