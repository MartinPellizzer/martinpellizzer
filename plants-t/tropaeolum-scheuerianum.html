Tropaeolum scheuerianum

Tropaeolum scheuerianum, commonly known as the Scheuer's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers similar to those of the common garden nasturtium. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible parts.

Tropaeolum scheuerianum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
