Tocoyena latifolia

Tocoyena latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tocoyena, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which bloom during the warm seasons. Tocoyena latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
