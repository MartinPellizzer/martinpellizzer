Thecostele quinquefida

Thecostele quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed lily, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features showy, fragrant flowers with five petals that are often a vibrant shade of white or pink. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Thecostele quinquefida is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
