Trigonella caerulea

Trigonella caerulea, commonly known as blue fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
