Triticum abyssinicum

Triticum abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian wheat, is a wild grass species native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and has small, spike-like inflorescences. Its grains are small and have a distinct, angular shape.

Although not widely cultivated, it has been studied for its potential in agricultural and genetic research.
