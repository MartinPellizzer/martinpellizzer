Tittelbachia albiflora

Tittelbachia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower tittelbachia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under dense forest canopies. Its flowers are small and clustered, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Tittelbachia albiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
