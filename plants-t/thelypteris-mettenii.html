Thelypteris mettenii

Thelypteris mettenii, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a serrated margin.

Thelypteris mettenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, green foliage.
