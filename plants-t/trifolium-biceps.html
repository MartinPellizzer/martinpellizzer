Trifolium biceps

Trifolium biceps, commonly known as the two-armed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium biceps is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
