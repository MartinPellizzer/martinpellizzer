Thymus serpyllum f. sylvestris

Thymus serpyllum f. sylvestris, commonly known as woodland thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens, borders, and as a ground cover.
