Tacazzea venosa var. martini

Tacazzea venosa var. martini, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent purple spots, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
