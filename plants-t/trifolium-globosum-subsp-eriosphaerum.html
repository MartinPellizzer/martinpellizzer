Trifolium globosum subsp. eriosphaerum

Trifolium globosum subsp.

eriosphaerum, commonly known as the globeflower clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive globular, purple flower heads that are tightly clustered and resemble small spheres.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
