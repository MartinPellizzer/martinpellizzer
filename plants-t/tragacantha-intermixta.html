Tragacantha intermixta

Tragacantha intermixta, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Tragacantha intermixta is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
