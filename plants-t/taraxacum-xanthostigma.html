Taraxacum xanthostigma

Taraxacum xanthostigma, commonly known as the yellow-streaked dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and a distinctive leaf pattern. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a waxy coating, and its stems are hollow and ridged. The flowers are typically followed by a puffball-like seed head, which disperses seeds via the wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.
