Taeniophyllum dischorense

Taeniophyllum dischorense, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its small, intricate fronds that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and organic-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
