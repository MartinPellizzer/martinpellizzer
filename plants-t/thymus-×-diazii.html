Thymus × diazii

Thymus × diazii, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus vulgaris (common thyme) and Thymus serpyllum (wild thyme). The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with aromatic, narrow leaves and small pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
