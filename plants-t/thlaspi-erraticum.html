Thlaspi erraticum

Thlaspi erraticum, commonly known as the erratic cabbageworm, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, green or yellowish seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is typically found in disturbed soils and rocky areas, often growing in alpine or arctic regions.

Thlaspi erraticum is adapted to harsh environments and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
