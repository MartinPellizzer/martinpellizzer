Triticum sativum var. compositum

Triticum sativum var. compositum, commonly known as composite wheat, is a botanical variant of wheat characterized by its unique combination of wild and cultivated traits.

It exhibits a diverse array of spikelet structures, which contribute to its classification as a composite variety. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions. Its genetic makeup reflects a blend of wild grasses and traditional wheat species, making it a subject of interest in plant breeding studies.

The plant's morphology and genetic diversity offer insights into the evolution of wheat varieties.
