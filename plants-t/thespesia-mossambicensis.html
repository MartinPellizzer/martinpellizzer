Thespesia mossambicensis

Thespesia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Socotra cactus, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to the arid environment of its native habitat. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Thespesia mossambicensis is also notable for its ability to thrive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient species in its ecosystem.
