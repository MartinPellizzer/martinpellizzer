Trifolium brachyodon

Trifolium brachyodon, commonly known as the short-stemmed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Trifolium brachyodon is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
