Tecoma valdiviana

Tecoma valdiviana, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Tecoma valdiviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
