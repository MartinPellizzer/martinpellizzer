Talipariti elatum

Talipariti elatum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in the muddy substrate at the bottom of the water body. Its flowers are typically white or pink, blooming at night and emitting a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

Talipariti elatum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
