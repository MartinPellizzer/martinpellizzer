Thismia fumida

Thismia fumida, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the orchid family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, elongated flower spike. The plant produces small, white, urn-shaped flowers that emit a faint, ghostly glow in the dark. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
