Taraxacum ovinum

Taraxacum ovinum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to Europe, it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has a robust root system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with flowering typically occurring in spring and summer.
