Torenia plantaginea

Torenia plantaginea, commonly known as the velvetleaf torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to orange. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Torenia plantaginea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
