Thunbergia gondarensis

Thunbergia gondarensis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Thunbergia gondarensis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
