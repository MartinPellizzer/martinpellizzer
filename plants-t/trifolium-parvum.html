Trifolium parvum

Trifolium parvum, commonly known as small clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is typically found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils across temperate regions. Trifolium parvum produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
