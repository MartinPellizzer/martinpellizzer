Terminalia tessmannii

Terminalia tessmannii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
