Tsuga dumosa

Tsuga dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf mountain hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, flattened sprays of needle-like leaves. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, often forming dense stands. Its cones are small and cone-shaped, containing two to three seeds, and it produces both male and female cones on the same plant.

Tsuga dumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
