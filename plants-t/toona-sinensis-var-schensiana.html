Toona sinensis var. schensiana

Toona sinensis var. schensiana, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree or sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, sunny environments.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated but can become invasive in some regions.
