Tripterospermum trinervium f. album

Tripterospermum trinervium f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered tripterospermum, is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.
