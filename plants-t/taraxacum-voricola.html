Taraxacum voricola

Taraxacum voricola, commonly known as the voracious dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, bright yellow composite flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, spiky stem that supports its showy inflorescence. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate and have a serrated margin, often with a hairy underside.

The seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like pappus, allowing the plant to spread efficiently in new areas.
