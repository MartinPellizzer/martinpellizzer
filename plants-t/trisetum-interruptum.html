Trisetum interruptum

Trisetum interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

Trisetum interruptum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
