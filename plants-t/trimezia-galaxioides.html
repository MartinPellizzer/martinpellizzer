Trimezia galaxioides

Trimezia galaxioides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green, feathery foliage. The plant produces showy, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trimezia galaxioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of New Zealand's natural heritage.
