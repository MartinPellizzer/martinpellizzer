Trollius × austrosibiricus

Trollius × austrosibiricus, commonly known as the hybrid globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by gardeners interested in native and hybrid flora.
