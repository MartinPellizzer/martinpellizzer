Trichophyllum cellulosum

Trichophyllum cellulosum, commonly known as the cellulose moss, is a rare and specialized moss species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, filamentous structure and the presence of cellulose-rich cell walls, which contribute to its unique botanical composition. The plant exhibits a simple thalloid body with no true roots, stems, or leaves, relying instead on rhizoids for anchorage. Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are typically small and clustered, facilitating spore dispersal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Trichophyllum cellulosum is often found in undisturbed, humid microhabitats such as decaying wood or mossy forest floors.
