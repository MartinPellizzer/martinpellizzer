Tanacetum kaschgarianum

Tanacetum kaschgarianum, commonly known as the Karakorum tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
