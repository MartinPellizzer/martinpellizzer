Taraxacum epirense

Taraxacum epirense, commonly known as the Mediterranean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. Its flower head consists of both ray florets and disc florets, which develop into a cluster of seeds with parachute-like structures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
