Thespesia macrophylla

Thespesia macrophylla, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year. Its flowers have five petals and are typically borne on long, slender stalks.

Thespesia macrophylla thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and well-drained soils.
