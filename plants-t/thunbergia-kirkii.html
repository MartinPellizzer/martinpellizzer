Thunbergia kirkii

Thunbergia kirkii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue-violet flowers with white markings. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its growth.

Thunbergia kirkii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
