Taraxacum parasemum

Taraxacum parasemum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus for seed dispersal. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
