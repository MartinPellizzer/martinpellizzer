Trifurcia tigridioides

Trifurcia tigridioides, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive yellow throat, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Trifurcia tigridioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
