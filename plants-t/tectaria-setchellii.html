Tectaria setchellii

Tectaria setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's tectaria, is a fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are often divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are usually located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria setchellii is native to tropical regions and is often found in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.
