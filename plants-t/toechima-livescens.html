Toechima livescens

Toechima livescens, commonly known as the mountain thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Toechima livescens plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
