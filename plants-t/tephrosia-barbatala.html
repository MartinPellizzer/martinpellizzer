Tephrosia barbatala

Tephrosia barbatala, commonly known as the Indian tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Tephrosia barbatala is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
