Thymus pseudolanuginosus

Thymus pseudolanuginosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
