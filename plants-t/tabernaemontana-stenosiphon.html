Tabernaemontana stenosiphon

Tabernaemontana stenosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-lipped greater waxleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive narrow, elongated floral lip. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value lies in its lush foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
