Tritonia cinnabarina

Tritonia cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Tritonia cinnabarina thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
