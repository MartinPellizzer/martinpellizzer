Tussilago petasites

Tussilago petasites, commonly known as the European coltsfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its thick, fleshy roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Tussilago petasites is also noted for its early spring blooming habit, often emerging before the leaves of other plants.
