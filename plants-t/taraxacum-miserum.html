Taraxacum miserum

Taraxacum miserum, commonly known as the lesser dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and gardens, and thrives in temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to produce a large number of small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum miserum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
