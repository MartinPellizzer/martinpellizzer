Taxanthema minutum

Taxanthema minutum, commonly known as the small-flowered taxanthemum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.

Taxanthema minutum is adapted to extreme temperatures and can survive prolonged drought conditions.
