Taxus inukaja

Taxus inukaja, commonly known as the Inukjuak yew, is a rare and slow-growing coniferous tree native to the Arctic regions of Canada and Greenland.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces unique, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. The plant has a dense, fibrous root system that helps it survive in harsh, cold environments. Taxus inukaja is also notable for its medicinal properties, as it contains taxanes, which are used in cancer treatments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
