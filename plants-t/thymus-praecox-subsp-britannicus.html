Thymus praecox subsp. britannicus

Thymus praecox subsp.

britannicus, commonly known as British thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles. It features aromatic, tiny, grey-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
