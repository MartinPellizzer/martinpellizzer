Tylophora glabra

Tylophora glabra, commonly known as the white-flowered tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Tylophora glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
