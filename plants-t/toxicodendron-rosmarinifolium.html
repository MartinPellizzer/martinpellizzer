Toxicodendron rosmarinifolium

Toxicodendron rosmarinifolium, commonly known as poison ivy, is a flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and red berries that contain seeds. It is known for its allergenic urushiol oil, which can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

Native to North America, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.
