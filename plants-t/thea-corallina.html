Thea corallina

Thea corallina, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant display to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and often reddish in color.

Thea corallina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
