Thymus carnosus

Thymus carnosus, commonly known as carnosic thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, dark green leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus carnosus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
