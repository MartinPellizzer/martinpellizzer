Tacinga armata

Tacinga armata, commonly known as the red-flowered tacinga, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are often tubular and arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Tacinga armata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
