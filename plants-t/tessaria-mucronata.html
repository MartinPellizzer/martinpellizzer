Tessaria mucronata

Tessaria mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed senna, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slender, pointed tip, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, resembling those of other senna species. Tessaria mucronata is adapted to dry environments and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
