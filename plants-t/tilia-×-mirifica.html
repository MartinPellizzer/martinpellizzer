Tilia × mirifica

Tilia × mirifica, commonly known as the giant linden, is a hybrid deciduous tree that belongs to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 10–15 cm in length. The tree produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers in upright clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia × mirifica is native to eastern Europe and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
