Thelypteris maxoniana

Thelypteris maxoniana, commonly known as the Maxon's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its sori, which are clusters of sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Thelypteris maxoniana plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
