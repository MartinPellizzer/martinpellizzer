Trachycarpus martianus subsp. latisectus

Trachycarpus martianus subsp.

latisectus, commonly known as the broad-leafed windmill palm, is a subtropical plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are divided into broad segments.

The leaves are typically a deep green and have a smooth, waxy surface. This species is well adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray, making it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
