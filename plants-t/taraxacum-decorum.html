Taraxacum decorum

Taraxacum decorum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head and a distinctive, hollow stem. The plant produces a ball of seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and typically have a waxy coating.

Taraxacum decorum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
