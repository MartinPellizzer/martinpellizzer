Telipogon hutchisonii

Telipogon hutchisonii, commonly known as the Hutchison's orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic, often found in cloud forests where humidity is high. Its small, clustered inflorescences and unique morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists studying orchid diversity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Telipogon hutchisonii is considered endangered and is often difficult to cultivate outside its natural environment.
