Tragacantha bombycina

Tragacantha bombycina, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable resin, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
