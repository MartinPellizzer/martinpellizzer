Trichonema × variegatum

Trichonema × variegatum, commonly known as the variegated threadleaf, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that often display variegation in color, typically featuring shades of white and green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, Trichonema × variegatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
