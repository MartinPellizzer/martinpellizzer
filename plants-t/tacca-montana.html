Tacca montana

Tacca montana, commonly known as the "bat plant," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bat-like flowers that are often adorned with dark purple or black markings. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Tacca montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in botanical gardens and private collections.
