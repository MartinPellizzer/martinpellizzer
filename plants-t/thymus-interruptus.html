Thymus interruptus

Thymus interruptus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members such as mint and oregano.
