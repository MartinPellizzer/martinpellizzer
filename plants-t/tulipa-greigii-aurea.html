Tulipa greigii aurea

Tulipa greigii aurea, commonly known as the Golden Greig tulip, is a striking bulbous perennial native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive orange throat, blooming in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.
