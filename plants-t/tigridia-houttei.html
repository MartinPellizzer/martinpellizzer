Tigridia houttei

Tigridia houttei, commonly known as the yellow tiger flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that typically display a yellow or orange hue with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is known for its ability to bloom reliably in warm climates.

Tigridia houttei is also valued for its drought tolerance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
