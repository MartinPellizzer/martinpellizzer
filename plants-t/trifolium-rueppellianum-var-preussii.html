Trifolium rueppellianum var. preussii

Trifolium rueppellianum var. preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's clover, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to elliptic leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
