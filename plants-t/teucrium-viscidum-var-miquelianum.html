Teucrium viscidum var. miquelianum

Teucrium viscidum var. miquelianum, commonly known as sticky germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in dry woodlands and open fields. Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
