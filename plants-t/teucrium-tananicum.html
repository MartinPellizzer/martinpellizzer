Teucrium tananicum

Teucrium tananicum, commonly known as the Mediterranean germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium tananicum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
