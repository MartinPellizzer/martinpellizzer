Tribulus homalococcus

Tribulus homalococcus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Tribulus homalococcus is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and birds.
