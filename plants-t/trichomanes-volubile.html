Trichomanes volubile

Trichomanes volubile, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes allow it to climb and spread along surfaces, making it a common epiphyte on trees and rocks.

The plant's intricate structure and ability to thrive in moist conditions make it a notable example of fern diversity in the botanical world.
