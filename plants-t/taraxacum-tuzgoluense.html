Taraxacum tuzgoluense

Taraxacum tuzgoluense, commonly known as the Tuzgol Mountain dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Turkey, specifically the Tuzgol region, and is adapted to the local climate and soil conditions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed head. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
