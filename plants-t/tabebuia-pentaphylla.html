Tabebuia pentaphylla

Tabebuia pentaphylla, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the season. The tree has a rounded crown and smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Tabebuia pentaphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral displays.
