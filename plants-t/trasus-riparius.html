Trasus riparius

Trasus riparius, commonly known as the river marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along rivers and streams. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

Trasus riparius produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.
