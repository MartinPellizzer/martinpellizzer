Tradescantia acaulis

Tradescantia acaulis, commonly known as the spiderwort or red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple vein pattern.

Tradescantia acaulis is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
