Thunbergia solmsiana

Thunbergia solmsiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its ecosystem.
