Tournefortia virgata

Tournefortia virgata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a woody stem with opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in the summer months. It is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.

Tournefortia virgata is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
