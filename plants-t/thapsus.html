Thapsus

Thapsus, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, silver-green leaves. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Thapsus thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in dunes or along the shore.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
