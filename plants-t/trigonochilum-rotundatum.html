Trigonochilum rotundatum

Trigonochilum rotundatum, commonly known as the round-leaved twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, ovate-shaped leaves and reduced, leaf-like structures called pseudobulbs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and forest floors. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct, three-lobed lip.

Trigonochilum rotundatum is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of the Americas.
