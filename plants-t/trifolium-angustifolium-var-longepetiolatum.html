Trifolium angustifolium var. longepetiolatum

Trifolium angustifolium var. longepetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover with long petioles, is a variant of the narrow-leaved clover species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaf stalks and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage plant for livestock.
