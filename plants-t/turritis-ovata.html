Turritis ovata

Turritis ovata, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats like wetlands and streambanks.

Turritis ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
