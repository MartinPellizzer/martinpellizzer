Tropaeolum olmosense

Tropaeolum olmosense, commonly known as the Bolivian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial in gardens.
