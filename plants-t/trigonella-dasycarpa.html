Trigonella dasycarpa

Trigonella dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy trigonella, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. The fruit is a hairy, cylindrical pod that matures to a dark brown color.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
