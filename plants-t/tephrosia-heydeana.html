Tephrosia heydeana

Tephrosia heydeana, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
