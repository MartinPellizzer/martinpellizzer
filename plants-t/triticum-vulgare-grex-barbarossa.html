Triticum vulgare grex barbarossa

Triticum vulgare grex barbarossa, commonly known as Barossa wheat, is a variety of common wheat characterized by its robust growth and high yield potential.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae, and it produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant has long, narrow leaves and develops small, two-lobed flowers that mature into grains enclosed in tough, inedible hulls. Barossa wheat is well adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its use in bread and other grain-based products.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both agricultural and nutritional purposes.
