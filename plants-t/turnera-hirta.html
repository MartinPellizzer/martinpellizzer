Turnera hirta

Turnera hirta, commonly known as the hairy turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually solitary and bloom in the early morning, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
