Tragacantha aliena

Tragacantha aliena, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing seeds.

Tragacantha aliena is valued for its resin, which has been historically used in medicine and as a natural adhesive.
