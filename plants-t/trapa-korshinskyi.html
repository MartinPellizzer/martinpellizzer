Trapa korshinskyi

Trapa korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky water chestnut, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Trapaceae.

It is native to the freshwater regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Russia. The plant is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water bodies, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Trapa korshinskyi plays a role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
