Thymus talijevii subsp. talijevii

Thymus talijevii subsp.

talijevii, commonly known as the Talijevi thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval berries that contain the aromatic essential oils.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
