Teucrium luteum subsp. aureoforme

Teucrium luteum subsp.

aureoforme, commonly known as golden everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is noted for its persistent, papery texture, which contributes to its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
