Tamarix syriaca

Tamarix syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which appear in summer. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and dry landscapes.

Tamarix syriaca is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
