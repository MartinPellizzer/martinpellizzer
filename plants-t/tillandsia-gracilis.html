Tillandsia gracilis

Tillandsia gracilis, commonly known as the slender air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. It produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
