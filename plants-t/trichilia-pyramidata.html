Trichilia pyramidata

Trichilia pyramidata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, aromatic wood. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
