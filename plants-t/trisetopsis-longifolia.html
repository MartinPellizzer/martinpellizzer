Trisetopsis longifolia

Trisetopsis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved trisetopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
