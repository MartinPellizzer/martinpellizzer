Telipogon vieirae

Telipogon vieirae, commonly known as the Vieira's spider orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
