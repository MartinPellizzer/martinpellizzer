Tilia platyphyllos subsp. corinthiaca

Tilia platyphyllos subsp.

corinthiaca, commonly known as Corinthian lime, is a deciduous tree native to the region of Corinth in Greece. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient Greek culture.
