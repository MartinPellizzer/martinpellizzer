Taraxacum persicum

Taraxacum persicum, commonly known as Persian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices for their potential health benefits.
