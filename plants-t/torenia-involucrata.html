Torenia involucrata

Torenia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Torenia involucrata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and gardens.
