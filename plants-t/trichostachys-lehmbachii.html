Trichostachys lehmbachii

Trichostachys lehmbachii, commonly known as the Lehmbach's bamboo, is a grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which can be both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Trichostachys lehmbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
