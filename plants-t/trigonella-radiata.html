Trigonella radiata

Trigonella radiata, commonly known as the Egyptian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a triangular pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Trigonella radiata is native to regions of the Mediterranean and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
