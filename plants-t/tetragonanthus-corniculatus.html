Tetragonanthus corniculatus

Tetragonanthus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tetragonanthus corniculatus is often found in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats.
