Terminalia tristis

Terminalia tristis, commonly known as African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
