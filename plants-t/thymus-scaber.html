Thymus scaber

Thymus scaber, commonly known as rough thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
