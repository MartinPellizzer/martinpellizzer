Tetragonanthus brentonianus

Tetragonanthus brentonianus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its popularity as a houseplant is due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
