Teucrium calycinum

Teucrium calycinum, commonly known as sweet germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Teucrium calycinum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
