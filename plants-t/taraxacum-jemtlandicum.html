Taraxacum jemtlandicum

Taraxacum jemtlandicum, commonly known as Jemtland dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a distinctive white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
