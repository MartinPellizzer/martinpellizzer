Thea megacarpa

Thea megacarpa, commonly known as the giant tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its botanical structure is similar to other members of the Theaceae family, with a distinctive bark and aromatic foliage.

The plant is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
