Trymalium diversifolium

Trymalium diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf trymalium, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in terminal racemes and bloom in the spring.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
