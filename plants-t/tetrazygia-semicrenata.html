Tetrazygia semicrenata

Tetrazygia semicrenata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, giving the plant its common name.

It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
