Trisetum daenense

Trisetum daenense, commonly known as the Daenese hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trisetum daenense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
