Thalictrum pseudoramosum

Thalictrum pseudoramosum, commonly known as false rue anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, lacecap flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous slender, branching stems.

Thalictrum pseudoramosum is valued in gardens for its early blooming habit and ornamental appeal.
