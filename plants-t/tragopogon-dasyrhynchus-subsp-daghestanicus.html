Tragopogon dasyrhynchus subsp. daghestanicus

Tragopogon dasyrhynchus subsp.

daghestanicus, commonly known as the Daghestan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly lobed.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is adapted to rocky and dry habitats.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
