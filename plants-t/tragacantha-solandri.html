Tragacantha solandri

Tragacantha solandri, commonly known as the Spanish thorn or yellow tragacanth, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in coastal and semi-arid regions.

The plant has been historically used for its gum, which has various industrial applications.
