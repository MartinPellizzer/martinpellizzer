Trimezia guianensis

Trimezia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan trimezia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the development of drupes.

Trimezia guianensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
