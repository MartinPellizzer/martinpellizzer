Teline microphylla var. rosmarinoides

Teline microphylla var. rosmarinoides, commonly known as rosemary lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage is often used in aromatherapy and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive scent.
