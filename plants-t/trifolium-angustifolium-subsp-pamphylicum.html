Trifolium angustifolium subsp. pamphylicum

Trifolium angustifolium subsp.

pamphylicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
