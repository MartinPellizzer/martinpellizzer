Thymus praecox

Thymus praecox, commonly known as early thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves and flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus praecox is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
