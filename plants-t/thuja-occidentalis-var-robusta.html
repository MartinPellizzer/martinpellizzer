Thuja occidentalis var. robusta

Thuja occidentalis var. robusta, commonly known as Eastern White Cedar, is a cultivar of the arborvitae family native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal growth habit and scale-like foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green female cones, which are followed by dark blue, waxy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its longevity in garden settings.
