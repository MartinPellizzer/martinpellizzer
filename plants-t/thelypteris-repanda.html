Thelypteris repanda

Thelypteris repanda, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce multiple shoots, contributing to its ability to spread vegetatively.

Thelypteris repanda plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
