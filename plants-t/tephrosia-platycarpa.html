Tephrosia platycarpa

Tephrosia platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain small, round seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Tephrosia platycarpa is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
