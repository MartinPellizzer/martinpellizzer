Tithonia excelsa

Tithonia excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble sunflowers, which bloom from summer to autumn. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Tithonia excelsa is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for bees.
