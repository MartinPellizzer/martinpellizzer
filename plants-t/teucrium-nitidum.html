Teucrium nitidum

Teucrium nitidum, commonly known as white germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Teucrium nitidum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
