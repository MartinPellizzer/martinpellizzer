Teucrium scorodonia subsp. baeticum

Teucrium scorodonia subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
