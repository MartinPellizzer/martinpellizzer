Tragia pungens var. cinerea

Tragia pungens var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray tragia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Tragia pungens var. cinerea is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
