Trifolium variegatum var. pauciflorum

Trifolium variegatum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the variegated clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
