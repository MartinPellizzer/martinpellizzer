Trichoglottis winkleri

Trichoglottis winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Trichoglottis winkleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
