Taraxacum schugnanicum

Taraxacum schugnanicum, commonly known as the Schugnan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets.

Taraxacum schugnanicum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
