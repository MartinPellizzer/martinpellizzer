Taraxacum venustius

Taraxacum venustius, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and a distinctive, hollow stem. The plant produces a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Taraxacum venustius is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
