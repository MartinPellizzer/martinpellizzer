Taraxacum nigricornis

Taraxacum nigricornis, commonly known as the black-horned dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, black-tipped bracts that form a crown-like structure around the flower head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into spherical seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
