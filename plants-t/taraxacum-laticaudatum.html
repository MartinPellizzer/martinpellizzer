Taraxacum laticaudatum

Taraxacum laticaudatum, commonly known as the broad-tailed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of fluffy white seeds. The plant has a robust, hairy stem and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy as well. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
