Tuberaria guttata var. cinerea

Tuberaria guttata var. cinerea, commonly known as the grey-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems that are often covered in a greyish or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in opposite pairs along the stem.

Tuberaria guttata var. cinerea is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in well-drained environments.
