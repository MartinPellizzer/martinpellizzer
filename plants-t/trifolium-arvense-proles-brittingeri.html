Trifolium arvense proles brittingeri

Trifolium arvense proles brittingeri, commonly known as Brittinger's field clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
