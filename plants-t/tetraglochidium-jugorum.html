Tetraglochidium jugorum

Tetraglochidium jugorum, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, including parts of North America and Europe.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
