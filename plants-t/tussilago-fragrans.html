Tussilago fragrans

Tussilago fragrans, commonly known as sweet coltsfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in treating respiratory ailments due to its mucilage content. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tussilago fragrans is also noted for its fragrant flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
