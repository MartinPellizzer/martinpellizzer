Thalia dealbata

Thalia dealbata, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a pinkish rind. Its botanical name honors the Greek muse Thalia, reflecting its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
