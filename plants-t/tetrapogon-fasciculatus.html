Tetrapogon fasciculatus

Tetrapogon fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered bladder grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer plant in ecological succession.
