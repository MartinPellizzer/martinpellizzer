Themistoclesia valerii

Themistoclesia valerii, commonly known as the Valerian leaf or Valerian plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
