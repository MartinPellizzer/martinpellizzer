Thelypodiopsis divaricata

Thelypodiopsis divaricata, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Thelypodiopsis divaricata plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
