Thelypteris podotricha

Thelypteris podotricha, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Thelypteris podotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
