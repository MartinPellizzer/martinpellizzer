Trichogonia salviifolia f. typica

Trichogonia salviifolia f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the mountain hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically compact, with a spreading or upright stem depending on environmental conditions.

Trichogonia salviifolia f. typica thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
