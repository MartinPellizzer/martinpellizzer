Tinospora sagittata var. craveniana

Tinospora sagittata var. craveniana, commonly known as the Craven's arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
