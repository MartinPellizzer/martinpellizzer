Trigonella foenum-graecum var. berythea

Trigonella foenum-graecum var. berythea, commonly known as Egyptian fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is an annual herb characterized by trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical seeds that are rich in nutrients such as protein, fiber, and various phytochemicals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
