Thymus comosus f. hirsutior

Thymus comosus f. hirsutior, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a cultivar of thyme characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fragrant leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces tiny pink or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its aromatic properties make it popular for both culinary and ornamental use in gardens.
