Tula

Tula, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tamarind fruit is widely used in cooking, particularly in savory dishes and beverages.
