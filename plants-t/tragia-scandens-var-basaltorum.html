Tragia scandens var. basaltorum

Tragia scandens var. basaltorum, commonly known as the basalt tragia, is a climbing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, often hairy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

This species is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in disturbed soils, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
