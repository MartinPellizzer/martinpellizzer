Taraxacum lipskyi

Taraxacum lipskyi, commonly known as the Lypsky's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head and heart-shaped basal leaves. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces a distinctive hollow stem with milky sap, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both sexual reproduction and vegetative propagation.
