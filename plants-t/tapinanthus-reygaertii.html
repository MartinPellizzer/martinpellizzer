Tapinanthus reygaertii

Tapinanthus reygaertii, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host plants for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant lacks chlorophyll and is therefore unable to perform photosynthesis, relying entirely on its host for sustenance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or brown, blending with the host plant.

It is often found growing on the stems of other plants, such as Acacia or Euphorbia species.
