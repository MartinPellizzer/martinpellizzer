Ternstroemia seemannii

Ternstroemia seemannii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense canopy. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom in the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
