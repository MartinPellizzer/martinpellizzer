Thymus × otrubae

Thymus × otrubae, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oils and antioxidant properties.

Thymus × otrubae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal herb gardens.
