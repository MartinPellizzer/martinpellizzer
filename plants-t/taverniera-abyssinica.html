Taverniera abyssinica

Taverniera abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
