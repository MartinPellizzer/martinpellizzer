Telipogon valenciae

Telipogon valenciae, commonly known as the Valencia grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often concealed within dense foliage.

Telipogon valenciae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
