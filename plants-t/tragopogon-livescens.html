Tragopogon livescens

Tragopogon livescens, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow ray florets that resemble a beard. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, tubular flower that opens in the morning and closes at dusk. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray hue with a fuzzy texture.

Tragopogon livescens is a member of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
