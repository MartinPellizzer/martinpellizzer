Tephrosia pseudocapitata

Tephrosia pseudocapitata, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moist conditions.

Tephrosia pseudocapitata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
