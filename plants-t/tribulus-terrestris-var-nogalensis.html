Tribulus terrestris var. nogalensis

Tribulus terrestris var. nogalensis, commonly known as the Nogal prickly poppy, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often followed by red, spiny fruits. The plant has a sprawling habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an adaptable and resilient species in arid and semi-arid climates.
