Thymus atlanticus subsp. atlanticus

Thymus atlanticus subsp.

atlanticus, commonly known as Atlantic thyme, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antimicrobial properties.

This subspecies is adapted to salty air and strong winds, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
