Taraxacum geminatum

Taraxacum geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive, hollow stem with a crown of bracts.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats.
