Trichophorum cyperinum

Trichophorum cyperinum, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense tussocks, contributing to soil stabilization.

Trichophorum cyperinum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
