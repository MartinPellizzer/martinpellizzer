Trifolium fimbriatum var. angustifolium

Trifolium fimbriatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, pointed leaflets with a distinctive narrow, serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
