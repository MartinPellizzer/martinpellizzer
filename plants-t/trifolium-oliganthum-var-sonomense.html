Trifolium oliganthum var. sonomense

Trifolium oliganthum var. sonomense, commonly known as the Sonoma clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
