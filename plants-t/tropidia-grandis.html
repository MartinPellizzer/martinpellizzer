Tropidia grandis

Tropidia grandis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant has strap-like leaves that are long and narrow, typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tropidia grandis is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and unique floral structure.
