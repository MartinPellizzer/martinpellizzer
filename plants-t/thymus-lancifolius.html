Thymus lancifolius

Thymus lancifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus lancifolius is a hardy species that is well adapted to dry, coastal environments.
