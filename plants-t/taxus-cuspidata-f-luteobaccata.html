Taxus cuspidata f. luteobaccata

Taxus cuspidata f. luteobaccata, commonly known as the golden-barked yew, is a unique cultivar of the Japanese yew.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow bark, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green foliage. This plant is evergreen, featuring narrow, needle-like leaves that remain on the plant year-round. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The golden-barked yew is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
