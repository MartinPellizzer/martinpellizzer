Tetrastigma mutabile

Tetrastigma mutabile, commonly known as the variable grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that range in color from red to black. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a versatile plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
