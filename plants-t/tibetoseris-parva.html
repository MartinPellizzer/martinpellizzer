Tibetoseris parva

Tibetoseris parva, commonly known as the small Tibetan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its compact growth form. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tibetoseris parva is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environments and its role in local ecosystems.
