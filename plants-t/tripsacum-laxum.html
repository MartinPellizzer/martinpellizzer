Tripsacum laxum

Tripsacum laxum, commonly known as wild maize or wild teosinte, is a grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to maize (Zea mays). The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female parts present on the same plant.

Tripsacum laxum is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
