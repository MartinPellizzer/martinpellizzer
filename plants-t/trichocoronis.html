Trichocoronis

Trichocoronis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "Trichocoronis," derived from the Greek words for "hairs" and "crown.". Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trichocoronis is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for gardens.
