Tylostemon caudatus

Tylostemon caudatus, commonly known as the woolly sterculia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Tylostemon caudatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
