Tephrosia caerulea subsp. caerulea

Tephrosia caerulea subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as blue tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in spring. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
