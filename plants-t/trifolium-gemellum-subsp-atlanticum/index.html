Trifolium Gemellum Subsp. Atlanticum

Trifolium gemellum subsp. atlanticum is a rare and delicate species of clover native to the coastal regions of eastern North America. This subspecies is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and its ability to thrive in sandy, salt-affected soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion along the Atlantic coast. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are essential to protect this unique plant and preserve its ecological significance.
