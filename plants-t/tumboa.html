Tumboa

Tumboa, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and prefers bright, indirect light. Tumboa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and red.
