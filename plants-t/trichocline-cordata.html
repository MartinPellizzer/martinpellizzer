Trichocline cordata

Trichocline cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf trichocline, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trichocline cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
