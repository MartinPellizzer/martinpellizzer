Terminthodia rotundifolia

Terminthodia rotundifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved terminthodia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
