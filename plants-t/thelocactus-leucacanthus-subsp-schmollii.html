Thelocactus leucacanthus subsp. schmollii

Thelocactus leucacanthus subsp.

schmollii, commonly known as the Schmolli's white spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, spiny tubercles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
