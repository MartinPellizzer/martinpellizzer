Townsendia jonesii var. tumulosa

Townsendia jonesii var. tumulosa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched stems with showy blue to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
