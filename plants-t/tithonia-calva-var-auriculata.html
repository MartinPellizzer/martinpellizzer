Tithonia calva var. auriculata

Tithonia calva var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate tithonia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Tithonia calva var. auriculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
