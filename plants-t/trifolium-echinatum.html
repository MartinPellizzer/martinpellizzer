Trifolium echinatum

Trifolium echinatum, commonly known as the spiny clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Trifolium echinatum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
