Terminalia glabrescens

Terminalia glabrescens, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red berries. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
