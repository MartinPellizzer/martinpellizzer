Tricholaena teneriffae var. hirtifolia

Tricholaena teneriffae var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in rocky and coastal environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters.

This variety is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal dunes and dry grasslands.
