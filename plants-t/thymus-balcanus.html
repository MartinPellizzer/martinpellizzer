Thymus balcanus

Thymus balcanus, commonly known as Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, similar to other thyme species.
