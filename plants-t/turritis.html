Turritis

Turritis, commonly known as the "false bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Turritis glabrata, which is native to South America. The plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Turritis species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive blooms and unique foliage.
