Tamarix szovitsiana

Tamarix szovitsiana, commonly known as the Hungarian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces pink or red flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and saline areas.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
