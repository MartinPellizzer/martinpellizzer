Tripolium divaricatum

Tripolium divaricatum, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with thin, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

Tripolium divaricatum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
