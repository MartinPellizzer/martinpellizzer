Thelypodiopsis sagittata

Thelypodiopsis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead leaved sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
