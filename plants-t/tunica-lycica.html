Tunica lycica

Tunica lycica, commonly known as the desert shieldplant, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, enabling it to access deep groundwater sources.

Tunica lycica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
