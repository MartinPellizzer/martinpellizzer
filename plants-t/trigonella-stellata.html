Trigonella stellata

Trigonella stellata, commonly known as star vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow or white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella stellata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
