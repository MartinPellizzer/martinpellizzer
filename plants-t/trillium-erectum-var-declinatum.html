Trillium erectum var. declinatum

Trillium erectum var. declinatum, commonly known as the nodding white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features three broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl at the base of the plant. The plant produces a single, nodding white flower with three petals and three sepals, which is distinctive in appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, sometimes living for several decades.
