Tillandsia pulchella var. disticha

Tillandsia pulchella var. disticha, commonly known as the silver leaf tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water absorption. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, well-lit environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
