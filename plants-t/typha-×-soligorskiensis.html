Typha × soligorskiensis

Typha × soligorskiensis, commonly known as the hybrid cattail, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Typha latifolia and Typha angustifolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are unisexual, with male flowers at the base and female flowers at the top of the spike.

Typha × soligorskiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
