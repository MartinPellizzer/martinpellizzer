Trifolium eriocephalum var. cusickii

Trifolium eriocephalum var. cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
