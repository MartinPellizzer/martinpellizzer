Trichosanthes tricuspidata

Trichosanthes tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-pointed bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive three-pointed fruit, which is typically yellow to orange in color. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The fruit is used in traditional Chinese medicine and is also consumed as a vegetable in some cuisines.

Its botanical features include a hairy stem, tendrils for climbing, and a fibrous, bitter interior.
