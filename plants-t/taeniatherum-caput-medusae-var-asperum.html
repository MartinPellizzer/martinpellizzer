Taeniatherum caput-medusae var. asperum

Taeniatherum caput-medusae var. asperum, commonly known as the rough-headed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
