Trisetum montanum var. shearii

Trisetum montanum var. shearii, commonly known as the Sierra feathergrass, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and hillsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
