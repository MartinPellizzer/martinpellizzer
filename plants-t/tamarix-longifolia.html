Tamarix longifolia

Tamarix longifolia, commonly known as the desert rose or saltbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. Its roots are extensive and can extract moisture from deep underground sources.

Tamarix longifolia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
