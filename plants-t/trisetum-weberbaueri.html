Trisetum weberbaueri

Trisetum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system typical of many sedges.

Trisetum weberbaueri is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and is often found in high-altitude regions.
