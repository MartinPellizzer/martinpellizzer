Tephrosia insolens

Tephrosia insolens, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender branches and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
