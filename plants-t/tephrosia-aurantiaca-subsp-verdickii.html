Tephrosia aurantiaca subsp. verdickii

Tephrosia aurantiaca subsp.

verdickii, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
