Taraxacum connexum

Taraxacum connexum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is widespread in temperate regions and is often considered a weed in gardens and lawns.

Taraxacum connexum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
