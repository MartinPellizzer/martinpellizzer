Tomilix

Tomilix, commonly known as the goldenstar cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its root system is typically shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Tomilix is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant in its native habitat.
