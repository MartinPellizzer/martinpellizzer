Trifolium libanoticum

Trifolium libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Trifolium libanoticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
