Trimorpha × burnatii

Trimorpha × burnatii, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with delicate beauty.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
