Thelocactus buekii subsp. matudae

Thelocactus buekii subsp.

matudae, commonly known as the Matudas thelocactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its subspecies status highlights its unique genetic and morphological traits compared to other thelocactus species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
