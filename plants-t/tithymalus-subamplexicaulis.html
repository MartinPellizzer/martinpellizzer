Tithymalus subamplexicaulis

Tithymalus subamplexicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact growth habit with upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in early summer. Its foliage is often aromatic and can be used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
