Thalia altissima

Thalia altissima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Thalia altissima has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and visually appealing addition to maritime gardens.
