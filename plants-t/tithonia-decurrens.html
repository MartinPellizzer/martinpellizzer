Tithonia decurrens

Tithonia decurrens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical annual plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flower heads and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. Tithonia decurrens thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
