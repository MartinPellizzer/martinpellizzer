Thymus funkii

Thymus funkii, commonly known as the Funk's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus funkii prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
