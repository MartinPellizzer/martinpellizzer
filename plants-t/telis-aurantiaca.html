Telis aurantiaca

Telis aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. Telis aurantiaca thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.
