Tradescantia villosa

Tradescantia villosa, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Tradescantia villosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread easily through rhizomes.
