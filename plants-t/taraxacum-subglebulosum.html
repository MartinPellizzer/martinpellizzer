Taraxacum subglebulosum

Taraxacum subglebulosum, commonly known as the narrow-headed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem topped with a spherical cluster of yellow flowers, which later develops into a fluffy seed head. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.
