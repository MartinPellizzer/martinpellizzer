Triticum paradoxum

Triticum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox wheat, is a rare and ancient species of wheat native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique genetic makeup, which includes a combination of wild and domesticated wheat traits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and hard, often requiring specific conditions for germination.

Due to its genetic diversity, Triticum paradoxum is of interest to researchers studying wheat evolution and crop resilience.
