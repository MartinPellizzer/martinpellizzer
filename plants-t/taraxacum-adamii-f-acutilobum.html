Taraxacum adamii f. acutilobum

Taraxacum adamii f. acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are sharply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, winged seed dispersal mechanism. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
