Tropaeolum tuberosum subsp. silvestre

Tropaeolum tuberosum subsp.

silvestre, commonly known as the Andean mountain spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in nutrients and used as a food source.

The plant produces vibrant red, yellow, or orange flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, spinach-like flavor.

This subspecies is well adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
