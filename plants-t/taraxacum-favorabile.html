Taraxacum favorabile

Taraxacum favorabile, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring and its distinctive, lobed leaves that form a rosette. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
