Thalictrum jordanianum

Thalictrum jordanianum, commonly known as Jordanian meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds in follicles that are dispersed by wind.

Thalictrum jordanianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
