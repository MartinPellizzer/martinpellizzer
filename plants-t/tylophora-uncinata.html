Tylophora uncinata

Tylophora uncinata, commonly known as the hooked tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
