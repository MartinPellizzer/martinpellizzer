Trifolium arrectisetum

Trifolium arrectisetum, commonly known as the upright clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Trifolium arrectisetum is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
