Tradescantia riparia

Tradescantia riparia, commonly known as the silver sword or redstem sedum, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Tradescantia riparia is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soil and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
