Triodia aurita

Triodia aurita, commonly known as the silverleaf triodia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and conserve moisture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to brown flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming part of the vegetation in sandy soils.

Triodia aurita is adapted to withstand drought and fire, making it a resilient component of native grassland ecosystems.
