Trigonella tomentosa

Trigonella tomentosa, commonly known as hairy fenugreek, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
