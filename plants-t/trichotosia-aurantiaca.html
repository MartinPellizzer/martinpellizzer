Trichotosia aurantiaca

Trichotosia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden beard plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Trichotosia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
