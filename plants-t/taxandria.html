Taxandria

Taxandria, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. Taxandria typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and seasonal blooms.
