Thalictrum secundum

Thalictrum secundum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It produces a showy inflorescence that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum secundum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
