Tephrosia dregeana

Tephrosia dregeana, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Tephrosia dregeana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
