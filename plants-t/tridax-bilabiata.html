Tridax bilabiata

Tridax bilabiata, commonly known as the common tridax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is recognized for its distinctive yellow flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Tridax bilabiata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
