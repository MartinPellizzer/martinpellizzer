Thelymitra glaucophylla

Thelymitra glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leafed rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Thelymitra glaucophylla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
