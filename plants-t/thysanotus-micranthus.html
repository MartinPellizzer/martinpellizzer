Thysanotus micranthus

Thysanotus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Thysanotus micranthus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
