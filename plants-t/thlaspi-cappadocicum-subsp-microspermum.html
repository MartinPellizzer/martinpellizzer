Thlaspi cappadocicum subsp. microspermum

Thlaspi cappadocicum subsp.

microspermum, commonly known as the dwarf cabbagewort, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its small, rounded leaves and compact growth habit.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in early summer. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
