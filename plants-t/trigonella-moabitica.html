Trigonella moabitica

Trigonella moabitica, commonly known as the Moabitic bitter vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Trigonella moabitica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
