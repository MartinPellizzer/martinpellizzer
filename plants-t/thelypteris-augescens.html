Thelypteris augescens

Thelypteris augescens, commonly known as the growing wood fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and they often exhibit a vibrant green color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
