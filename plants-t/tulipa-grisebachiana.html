Tulipa grisebachiana

Tulipa grisebachiana, commonly known as the Gray's tulip, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and southern Russia.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
