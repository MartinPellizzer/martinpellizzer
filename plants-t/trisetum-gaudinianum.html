Trisetum gaudinianum

Trisetum gaudinianum, commonly known as the Gaudin's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grassy woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicle-like structures.

Trisetum gaudinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful, airy growth habit.
