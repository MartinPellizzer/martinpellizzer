Taraxacum pulchrifolium

Taraxacum pulchrifolium, commonly known as the beautiful-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
