Thymus pulegioides var. parviflorus

Thymus pulegioides var. parviflorus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
