Trixis thunbergioides

Trixis thunbergioides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or inland dune habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
