Teucrium fischeri

Teucrium fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often clustered in terminal spikes. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Teucrium fischeri is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
