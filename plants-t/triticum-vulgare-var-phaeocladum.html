Triticum vulgare var. phaeocladum

Triticum vulgare var. phaeocladum, commonly known as the dark red wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its dark red grain color.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is sometimes used in specialty grain products.
