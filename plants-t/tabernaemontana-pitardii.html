Tabernaemontana pitardii

Tabernaemontana pitardii, commonly known as the Madagascar star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem structure, making it a striking ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
