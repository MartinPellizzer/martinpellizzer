Trisetum arduanum

Trisetum arduanum, commonly known as the Arduan sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of streams.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
