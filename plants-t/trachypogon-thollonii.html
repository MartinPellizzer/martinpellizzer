Trachypogon thollonii

Trachypogon thollonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth, sometimes hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trachypogon thollonii is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
