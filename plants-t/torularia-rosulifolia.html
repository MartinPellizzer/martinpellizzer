Torularia rosulifolia

Torularia rosulifolia, commonly known as the clustered broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are arranged in clusters at the base of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Torularia rosulifolia is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
