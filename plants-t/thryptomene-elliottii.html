Thryptomene elliottii

Thryptomene elliottii, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Thryptomene elliottii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in ecological restoration projects.
