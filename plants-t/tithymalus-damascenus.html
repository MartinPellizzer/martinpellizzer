Tithymalus damascenus

Tithymalus damascenus, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a shrub-like appearance with thorny stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
