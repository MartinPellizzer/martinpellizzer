Thymus × armuniae

Thymus × armuniae, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus × armuniae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
