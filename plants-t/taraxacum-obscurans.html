Taraxacum obscurans

Taraxacum obscurans, commonly known as the dark dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of a yellow disk surrounded by pale green to brownish ray florets.

Taraxacum obscurans is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.
