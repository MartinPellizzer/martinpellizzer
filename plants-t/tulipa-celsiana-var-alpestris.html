Tulipa celsiana var. alpestris

Tulipa celsiana var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to alpine gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
