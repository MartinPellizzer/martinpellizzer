Thelocactus flavus

Thelocactus flavus, commonly known as the yellow locactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Thelocactus flavus is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for desert gardens.
