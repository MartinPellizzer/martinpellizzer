Tropaeolum pentaphyllum subsp. pentaphyllum

Tropaeolum pentaphyllum subsp.

pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leaf sorrel, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate or pentaphyllate leaves, which are typically bright green and have a slightly succulent texture.

The plant produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in clusters. Its stems are often trailing or prostrate, making it suitable for container gardening or ground cover.

The leaves are edible and have a tangy, peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.
