Tiarella californica

Tiarella californica, commonly known as California foamflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pinkish flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
