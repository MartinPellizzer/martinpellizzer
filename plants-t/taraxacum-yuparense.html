Taraxacum yuparense

Taraxacum yuparense, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a distinctive white, fluffy seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Taraxacum yuparense is adapted to the cool, temperate climates of the Andes and plays a role in local ecosystems.
