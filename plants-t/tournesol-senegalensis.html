Tournesol senegalensis

Tournesol senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the common sunflower. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
