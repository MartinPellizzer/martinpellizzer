Tullia pycnanthemoides

Tullia pycnanthemoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Tullia pycnanthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
