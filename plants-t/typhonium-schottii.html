Typhonium schottii

Typhonium schottii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking spatulate inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both an ornamental and potentially invasive species.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptability contribute to its significance in both horticulture and natural ecosystems.
