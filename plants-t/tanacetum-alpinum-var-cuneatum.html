Tanacetum alpinum var. cuneatum

Tanacetum alpinum var. cuneatum, commonly known as alpine tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in tiny, glandular dots. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
