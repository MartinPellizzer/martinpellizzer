Taraxacum lenkoranense

Taraxacum lenkoranense, commonly known as the Lenkoran dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
