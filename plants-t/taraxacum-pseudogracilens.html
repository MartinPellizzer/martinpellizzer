Taraxacum pseudogracilens

Taraxacum pseudogracilens, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem that supports its foliage and flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

After flowering, it develops a white, fluffy seed head that disperses its seeds by wind.
