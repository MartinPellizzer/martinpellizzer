Tripodanthus acutifolius

Tripodanthus acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tripodanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
