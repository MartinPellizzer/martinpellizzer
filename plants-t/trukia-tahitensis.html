Trukia tahitensis

Trukia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian trukia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tahiti in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to the humid, tropical climate of the region. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Trukia tahitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local flora of the South Pacific.
