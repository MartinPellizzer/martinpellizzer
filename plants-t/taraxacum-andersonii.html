Taraxacum andersonii

Taraxacum andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, waxy leaves and produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
