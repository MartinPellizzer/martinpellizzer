Tilia caroliniana var. rhoophila

Tilia caroliniana var. rhoophila, commonly known as the American basswood or white basswood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, oval nuts encased in a woolly husk, which ripen in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in floodplain forests and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
