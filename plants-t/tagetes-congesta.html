Tagetes congesta

Tagetes congesta, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1 to 2 meters tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Tagetes congesta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
