Trisetum oreophilum subsp. colombianum

Trisetum oreophilum subsp.

colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian hill reed, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
