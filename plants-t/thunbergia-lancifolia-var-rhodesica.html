Thunbergia lancifolia var. rhodesica

Thunbergia lancifolia var. rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodesian moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
