Thlaspi tineanum

Thlaspi tineanum, commonly known as the Sicilian cabbagewort, is a small, flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Thlaspi tineanum is also known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential indicator species for soil contamination.
