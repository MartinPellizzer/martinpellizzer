Trichosporum foxworthyi

Trichosporum foxworthyi, commonly known as the Foxworth's trichosporum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, commonly known as grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a tight rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a panicle. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand periods of drought.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
