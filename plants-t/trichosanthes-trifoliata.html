Trichosanthes trifoliata

Trichosanthes trifoliata, commonly known as the Chinese snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that mature to a dark green or orange color. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor.

Trichosanthes trifoliata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
