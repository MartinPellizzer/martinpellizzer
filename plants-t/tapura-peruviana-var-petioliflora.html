Tapura peruviana var. petioliflora

Tapura peruviana var. petioliflora, commonly known as the Peruvian tapura, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features elongated petioles, which give it its specific variety name, petioliflora. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
