Trifolium catanense

Trifolium catanense, commonly known as the Sicilian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically from April to June. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium catanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
