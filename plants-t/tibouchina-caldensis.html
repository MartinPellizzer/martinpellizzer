Tibouchina caldensis

Tibouchina caldensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a striking shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces showy inflorescences that are often visited by hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Tibouchina caldensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
