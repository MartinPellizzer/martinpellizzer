Tragacantha karputana

Tragacantha karputana, commonly known as Karpuan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing seeds.

Its roots are known for producing a gum, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
