Taxus baccata f. pyramidalis

Taxus baccata f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal yew, is a cultivated form of the European yew.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant retains its needles year-round, making it an evergreen with a striking vertical appearance. It produces red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This variety is often used in formal gardens for its structured, ornamental form.
