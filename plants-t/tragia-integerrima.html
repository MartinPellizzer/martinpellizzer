Tragia integerrima

Tragia integerrima, commonly known as the prickly glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Tragia integerrima is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
