Tanacetum persicum

Tanacetum persicum, commonly known as Persian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
