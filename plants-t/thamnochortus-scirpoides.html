Thamnochortus scirpoides

Thamnochortus scirpoides, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.
