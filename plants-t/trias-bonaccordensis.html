Trias bonaccordensis

Trias bonaccordensis, commonly known as the Bonaccord trias, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Bonaccord region.

It is characterized by its unique tripartite leaf structure, which consists of three distinct lobes arranged symmetrically. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and thrives in nutrient-rich, moist soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring, producing small, delicate white flowers.

Due to its limited habitat and environmental sensitivity, it is considered a conservation priority.
