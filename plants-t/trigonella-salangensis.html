Trigonella salangensis

Trigonella salangensis, commonly known as Salang honeyflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and alpine meadows. Its seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Trigonella salangensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
