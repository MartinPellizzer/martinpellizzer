Thymus tosevii var. hirtiformis

Thymus tosevii var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the Hirtiform hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features dense clusters of tiny, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
