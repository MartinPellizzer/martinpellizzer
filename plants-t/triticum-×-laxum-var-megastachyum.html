Triticum × laxum var. megastachyum

Triticum × laxum var. megastachyum, commonly known as the giant wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, feathery inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, panicle-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
