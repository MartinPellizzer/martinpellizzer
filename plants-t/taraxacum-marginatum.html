Taraxacum marginatum

Taraxacum marginatum, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, pale green leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, bright yellow composite flower. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into a fluffy, white seed head that is easily dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
