Tripogon festucoides

Tripogon festucoides, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Tripogon festucoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
