Tylophora treubiana

Tylophora treubiana, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular and produce a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Tylophora treubiana is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
