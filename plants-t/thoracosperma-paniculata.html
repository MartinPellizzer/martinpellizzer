Thoracosperma paniculata

Thoracosperma paniculata, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, segmented fruits. The fruit's pericarp is thick and leathery, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
