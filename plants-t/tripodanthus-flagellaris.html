Tripodanthus flagellaris

Tripodanthus flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate tripodanthus, is a unique plant species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thread-like stems that resemble tiny flagella, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Despite its unusual appearance, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for specific insect species.
