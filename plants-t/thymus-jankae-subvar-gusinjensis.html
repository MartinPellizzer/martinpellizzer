Thymus jankae subvar. gusinjensis

Thymus jankae subvar. gusinjensis, commonly known as the Gusinjensk thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically produces small, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional use in herbal medicine.
