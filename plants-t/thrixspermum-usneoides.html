Thrixspermum usneoides

Thrixspermum usneoides, commonly known as the mossy beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that forms dense, cushion-like growths on tree bark, particularly in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its greenish to yellowish thallus with a distinctive, hairy appearance due to the presence of specialized structures called rhizines. The lichen is composed of a symbiotic relationship between a photosynthetic algae and a fungal partner, which allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions. It is often found in temperate and tropical regions, growing on trees, rocks, and soil surfaces.

Thrixspermum usneoides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
