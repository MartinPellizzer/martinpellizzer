Thymus moesiacus var. malyi

Thymus moesiacus var. malyi, commonly known as Malyi thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
