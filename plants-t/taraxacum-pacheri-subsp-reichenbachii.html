Taraxacum pacheri subsp. reichenbachii

Taraxacum pacheri subsp.

reichenbachii, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, often hairy, yellow flower head.

The plant is perennial and produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports its flower. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in high-altitude ecosystems.
