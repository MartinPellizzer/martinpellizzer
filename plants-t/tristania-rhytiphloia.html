Tristania rhytiphloia

Tristania rhytiphloia, commonly known as the Tristania tree, is a species native to the island of Tristan da Cunha in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant has rough, fissured bark that is characteristic of its genus. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with yellow stamens, and the plant produces woody pods containing seeds.
