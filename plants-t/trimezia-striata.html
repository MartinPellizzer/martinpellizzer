Trimezia striata

Trimezia striata, commonly known as the silver stripe plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stem and pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive silver stripe running along the midrib. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, giving the plant a graceful appearance.

Trimezia striata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
