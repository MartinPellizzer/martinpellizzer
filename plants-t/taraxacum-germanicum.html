Taraxacum germanicum

Taraxacum germanicum, commonly known as the German dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a yellow flowering head, followed by a distinctive seed head composed of pappus-bearing seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
