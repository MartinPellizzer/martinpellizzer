Tamarix amplexicaulis

Tamarix amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clustered tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers in summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are scale-like and often appear to be fused to the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tamarix amplexicaulis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
