Thalictrum virginicum

Thalictrum virginicum, commonly known as the showy lady's-tresses, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, spiraling inflorescence. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Thalictrum virginicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
