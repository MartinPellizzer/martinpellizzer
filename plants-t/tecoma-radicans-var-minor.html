Tecoma radicans var. minor

Tecoma radicans var. minor, commonly known as the lesser yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
