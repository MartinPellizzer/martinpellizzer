Tamarix palaestina

Tamarix palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is often found in coastal areas, saline soils, and along riverbanks due to its tolerance of poor soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
