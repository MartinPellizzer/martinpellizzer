Tagetes andina

Tagetes andina, commonly known as the Andean marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Tagetes andina is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
