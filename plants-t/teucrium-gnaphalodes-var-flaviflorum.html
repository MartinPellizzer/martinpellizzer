Teucrium gnaphalodes var. flaviflorum

Teucrium gnaphalodes var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellowflower germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It has a compact, low-growing habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
