Torularia ledebourii

Torularia ledebourii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique adaptations to extreme environments make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
