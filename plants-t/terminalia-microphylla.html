Terminalia microphylla

Terminalia microphylla, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
