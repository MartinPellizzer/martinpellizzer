Teucrium townsendii subsp. affine

Teucrium townsendii subsp.

affine, commonly known as Townsend's germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This subspecies is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
