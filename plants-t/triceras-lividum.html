Triceras lividum

Triceras lividum, commonly known as the bluish triceras, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known.
