Trichipteris denticulata

Trichipteris denticulata, commonly known as the toothed fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are finely divided and often have a serrated or toothed edge, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it in the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Trichipteris denticulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
