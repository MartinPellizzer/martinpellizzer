Thymus phyllopodus

Thymus phyllopodus, commonly known as the leafy thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus phyllopodus is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
