Thymus × aitanae nothosubsp.

aitanae, commonly known as the Aitana thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, tiny leaves that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists.
