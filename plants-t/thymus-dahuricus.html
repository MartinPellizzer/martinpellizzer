Thymus dahuricus

Thymus dahuricus, commonly known as Siberian thyme, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and small, fragrant leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus dahuricus is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
