Trillium sessile f. viridiflorum

Trillium sessile f. viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered sessile trillium, is a rare and striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Trillium genus, characterized by its three-part leaves and three-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage consists of large, broadly ovate leaves that are often a deep green.

The flowers, which are a distinctive greenish-yellow color, bloom in early spring and are followed by red berries that ripen by late summer.
