Thuja semperaurescens

Thuja semperaurescens, commonly known as the Eastern white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in flat sprays. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The wood of Thuja semperaurescens is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
