Triticum monococcum var. gaterslebense

Triticum monococcum var. gaterslebense, commonly known as the Gatersleben emmer wheat, is a wild relative of modern wheat with distinctive botanical features.

It is a small grain species with a compact inflorescence and awned glumes, typical of the tribe Aegilopieae. The plant exhibits a diploid genome structure, which contributes to its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs.
