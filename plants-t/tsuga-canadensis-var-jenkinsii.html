Tsuga canadensis var. jenkinsii

Tsuga canadensis var. jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins' hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to the northeastern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The leaves are typically shorter and more rigid compared to the standard hemlock, contributing to its distinct appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of the conifer family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
