Thelypteris cordata var. imitata

Thelypteris cordata var. imitata, commonly known as the imitate water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in wetland habitats.
