Thymus thracicus

Thymus thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in regions of Greece, Bulgaria, and Turkey. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
