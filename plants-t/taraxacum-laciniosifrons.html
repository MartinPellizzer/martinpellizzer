Taraxacum laciniosifrons

Taraxacum laciniosifrons, commonly known as the fringed leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that terminates in a bright yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in dry meadows and grasslands.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is considered a valuable plant for pollinators and a indicator species for certain habitats.
