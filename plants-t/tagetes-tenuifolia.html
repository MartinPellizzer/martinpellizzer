Tagetes tenuifolia

Tagetes tenuifolia, commonly known as Mexican marigold or threadleaf marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. Tagetes tenuifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a companion plant in agriculture.
