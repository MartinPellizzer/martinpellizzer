Taraxacum gallicum

Taraxacum gallicum, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces a bright yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed head, which is dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically broad, with a wavy margin and a hairy surface.

Taraxacum gallicum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
