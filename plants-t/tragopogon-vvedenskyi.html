Tragopogon vvedenskyi

Tragopogon vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian chicory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by tubular florets. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Tragopogon vvedenskyi is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
