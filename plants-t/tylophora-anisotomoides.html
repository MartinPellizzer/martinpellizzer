Tylophora anisotomoides

Tylophora anisotomoides, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers, which open at night, are typically white or pale pink and have a distinctive, spurred corolla. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of hair.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
