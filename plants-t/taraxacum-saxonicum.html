Taraxacum saxonicum

Taraxacum saxonicum, commonly known as the Saxony dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Taraxacum saxonicum is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelion species.
