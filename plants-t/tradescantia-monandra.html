Tradescantia monandra

Tradescantia monandra, commonly known as the pink spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
