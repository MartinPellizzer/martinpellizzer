Thymus angustifolius var. minor

Thymus angustifolius var. minor, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a dense growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies due to its aromatic and antiseptic qualities.
