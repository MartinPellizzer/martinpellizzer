Teucrium sieberi var. villosum

Teucrium sieberi var. villosum, commonly known as the woolly germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
