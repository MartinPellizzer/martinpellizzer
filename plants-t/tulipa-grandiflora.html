Tulipa grandiflora

Tulipa grandiflora, commonly known as the giant tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of the tulip genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tulipa grandiflora is known for its early spring blooming period and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
