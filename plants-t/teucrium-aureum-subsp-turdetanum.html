Teucrium aureum subsp. turdetanum

Teucrium aureum subsp.

turdetanum, commonly known as the golden germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued in traditional herbal remedies.
