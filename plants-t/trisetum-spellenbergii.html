Trisetum spellenbergii

Trisetum spellenbergii, commonly known as the Spellenberg's wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often densely tufted at the base, with small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a wiry texture that gives it its common name.

Trisetum spellenbergii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
