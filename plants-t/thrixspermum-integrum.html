Thrixspermum integrum

Thrixspermum integrum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Thrixspermum integrum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
