Talauma glaucum

Talauma glaucum, commonly known as the blue glaucous talauma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Talauma glaucum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest undergrowth.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
