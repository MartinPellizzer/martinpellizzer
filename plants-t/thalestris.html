Thalestris

Thalestris, commonly known as the common thalecress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Thalestris is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
