Trichinium fusiforme

Trichinium fusiforme, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic invertebrates. The plant has thin, thread-like stems and leaves that are often submerged. It thrives in still or slow-moving water and is adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

Its unique trapping mechanism is a key adaptation for obtaining nutrients in its ecological niche.
