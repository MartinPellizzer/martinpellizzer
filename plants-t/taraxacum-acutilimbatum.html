Taraxacum acutilimbatum

Taraxacum acutilimbatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces a single, hollow, stem that supports a bright yellow composite flower head. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

This species is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
