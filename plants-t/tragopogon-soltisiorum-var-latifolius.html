Tragopogon soltisiorum var. latifolius

Tragopogon soltisiorum var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of pale blue to purple disk flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.
