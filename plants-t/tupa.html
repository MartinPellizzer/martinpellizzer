Tupa

Tupa, commonly known as the "tree of life" in some indigenous cultures, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is a flowering plant that produces small, fragrant blooms and has a distinctive, branching structure. The leaves are typically broad and glossy, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Tupa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its botanical significance extends beyond aesthetics, as it has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
