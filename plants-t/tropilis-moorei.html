Tropilis moorei

Tropilis moorei, commonly known as the Moore's lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant pink or red petals that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Tropilis moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
