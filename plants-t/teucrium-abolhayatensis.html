Teucrium abolhayatensis

Teucrium abolhayatensis, commonly known as the Arabian germander, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
