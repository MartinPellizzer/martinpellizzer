Turnera orientalis

Turnera orientalis, commonly known as the golden trumpet or Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, known for its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange blooms with a distinctive, trumpet-shaped corona. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Turnera orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
