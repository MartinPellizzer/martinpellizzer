Turnera violacea

Turnera violacea, commonly known as violetleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
