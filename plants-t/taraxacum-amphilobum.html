Taraxacum amphilobum

Taraxacum amphilobum, commonly known as the western dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to western North America and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports the flower head, which consists of numerous small florets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils, and is often found in sunny, open areas.

The plant is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
