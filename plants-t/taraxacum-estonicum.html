Taraxacum estonicum

Taraxacum estonicum, commonly known as the Estonian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Estonia and parts of northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow flower heads that develop into fluffy white seeds.

Taraxacum estonicum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
