Trichonema columnae

Trichonema columnae, commonly known as the columnar trichonema, is a rare and specialized aquatic plant.

It is characterized by its unique columnar growth habit, with elongated stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters with soft, nutrient-rich substrates. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, reducing water resistance and aiding in its buoyant growth.

Trichonema columnae plays a minor but ecologically significant role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
