Thymus pallasianus subsp. ciliatissimus

Thymus pallasianus subsp.

ciliatissimus, commonly known as the fringed thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and aromatic, gray-green leaves with finely toothed margins.

The plant produces small, pale pink to violet flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
