Teucrium viscidum var. densiflorum

Teucrium viscidum var. densiflorum, commonly known as sticky germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
