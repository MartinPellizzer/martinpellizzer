Tecoma princei coccinea-grandiflora

Tecoma princei coccinea-grandiflora, commonly known as the red flame trumpetvine, is a hybrid species combining traits from Tecoma coccinea and Tecoma grandiflora.

It is a fast-growing, deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
