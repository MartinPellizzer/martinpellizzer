Tabebuia paniculata

Tabebuia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The wood of Tabebuia paniculata is durable and has been traditionally used for furniture and construction.
