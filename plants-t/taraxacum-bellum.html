Taraxacum bellum

Taraxacum bellum, commonly known as the bell-shaped dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, wind-dispersed seed head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with new growth emerging in the spring.
