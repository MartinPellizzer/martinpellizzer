Trifolium cernuum subsp. perreymondii

Trifolium cernuum subsp.

perreymondii, commonly known as the Perreymond's bentweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to specific regions of the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
