Thalictrum controversum subsp. flavum

Thalictrum controversum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features delicate, fern-like compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic and are pollinated primarily by flies and beetles.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
