Trichelostylis warei

Trichelostylis warei, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects.

Trichelostylis warei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
