Trifolium longipes var. rusbyi

Trifolium longipes var. rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's narrowleaf clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
