Teucrium grandiusculum

Teucrium grandiusculum, commonly known as the small-flowered germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Teucrium grandiusculum is also known for its aromatic foliage and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
