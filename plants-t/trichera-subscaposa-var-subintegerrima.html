Trichera subscaposa var. subintegerrima

Trichera subscaposa var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trichera, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
