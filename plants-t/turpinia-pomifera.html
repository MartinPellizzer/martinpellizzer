Turpinia pomifera

Turpinia pomifera, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive star-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing five pointed lobes. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and sour flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
