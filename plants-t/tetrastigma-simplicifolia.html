Tetrastigma simplicifolia

Tetrastigma simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved cross-vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.
