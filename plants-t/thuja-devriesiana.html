Thuja devriesiana

Thuja devriesiana, commonly known as the dwarf Alberta spruce, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Alberta, Canada.

It is characterized by its compact, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that remains consistent throughout the year. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches, giving it a smooth, textured appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is well adapted to cold climates and well-drained soils.

Thuja devriesiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
