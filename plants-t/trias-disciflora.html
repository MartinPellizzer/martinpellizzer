Trias disciflora

Trias disciflora, commonly known as the disc-flowered trias, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Trias disciflora typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
