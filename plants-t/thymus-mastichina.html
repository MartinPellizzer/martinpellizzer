Thymus mastichina

Thymus mastichina, commonly known as Greek thyme or mastichina thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Thymus mastichina is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
