Trillium georgianum

Trillium georgianum, commonly known as the Georgia trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three-parted leaves and three-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.
