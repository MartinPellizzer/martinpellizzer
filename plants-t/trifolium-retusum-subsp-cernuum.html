Trifolium retusum subsp. cernuum

Trifolium retusum subsp.

cernuum, commonly known as the nodding clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that hang downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
