Tinus ovata

Tinus ovata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with rounded, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Tinus ovata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
