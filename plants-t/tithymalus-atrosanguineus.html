Tithymalus atrosanguineus

Tithymalus atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Tithymalus atrosanguineus is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
