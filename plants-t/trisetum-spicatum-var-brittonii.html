Trisetum spicatum var. brittonii

Trisetum spicatum var. brittonii, commonly known as Britton's beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a tuft of spiky bristles at the tip. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
