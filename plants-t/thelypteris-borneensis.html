Thelypteris borneensis

Thelypteris borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, woolly layer of root hairs, which helps in absorbing moisture from the soil.

The fronds are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which is believed to aid in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
