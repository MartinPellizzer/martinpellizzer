Trisetum californicum

Trisetum californicum, commonly known as the California hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture.

Trisetum californicum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
