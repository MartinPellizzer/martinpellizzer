Thalictrum siamense

Thalictrum siamense, commonly known as the Siam meadowrue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short rhizomatous root system and produces numerous slender, branching stems.

Thalictrum siamense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
