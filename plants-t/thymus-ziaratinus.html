Thymus ziaratinus

Thymus ziaratinus, commonly known as Ziarat thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for their pungent, thyme-like aroma.
