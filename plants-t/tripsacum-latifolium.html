Tripsacum latifolium

Tripsacum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf tumblergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that resemble a tumbler or a bottlebrush. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its seeds are small and clustered, often dispersed by wind or water.

Tripsacum latifolium is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential for use in restoration projects.
