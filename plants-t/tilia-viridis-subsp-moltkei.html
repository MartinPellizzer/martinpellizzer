Tilia viridis subsp. moltkei

Tilia viridis subsp.

moltkei, commonly known as the green-leaf lime, is a subspecies of the lime tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
