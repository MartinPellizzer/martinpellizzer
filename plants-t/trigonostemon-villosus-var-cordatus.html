Trigonostemon villosus var. cordatus

Trigonostemon villosus var. cordatus, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
