Thymus serpyllum var. tanaensis

Thymus serpyllum var. tanaensis, commonly known as Tana thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variant is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
