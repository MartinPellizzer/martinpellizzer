Thymus caucasicus subsp. grossheimii

Thymus caucasicus subsp.

grossheimii, commonly known as Caucasian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties, similar to other thyme species.
