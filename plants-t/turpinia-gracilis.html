Turpinia gracilis

Turpinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender turpinia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae, which includes olive and jasmine plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fragrant flowers, which bloom in the spring, are typically white or pale yellow and are followed by small, woody fruits.

Turpinia gracilis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
