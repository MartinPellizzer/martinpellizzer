Triptilodiscus

Triptilodiscus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-like growth pattern and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy texture. Triptilodiscus species are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
