Terminalia alata f. pilosa

Terminalia alata f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy or pubescent flame tree, is a variant of the flame tree species characterized by its dense, soft pubescence on the leaves and stems.

It is a deciduous tree native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and bear a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
