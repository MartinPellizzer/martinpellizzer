Tripidium arundinaceum

Tripidium arundinaceum, commonly known as the reed canary grass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in wetland areas, along stream banks, and in marshy soils due to its preference for moist environments.

Tripidium arundinaceum is valued for its high biomass yield and is used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
