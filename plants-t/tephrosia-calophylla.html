Tephrosia calophylla

Tephrosia calophylla, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats. It has a taproot system and can tolerate drought conditions.

Tephrosia calophylla is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
