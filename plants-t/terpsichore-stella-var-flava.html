Terpsichore stella var. flava

Terpsichore stella var. flava, commonly known as the golden star bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to bright yellow, depending on the variety. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both garden and landscape design.
