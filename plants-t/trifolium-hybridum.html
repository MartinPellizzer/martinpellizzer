Trifolium hybridum

Trifolium hybridum, commonly known as white clover, is a perennial flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

White clover has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
