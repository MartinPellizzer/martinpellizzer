Trifolium brittingeri

Trifolium brittingeri, commonly known as the Brittinger's clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring.

Trifolium brittingeri is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
