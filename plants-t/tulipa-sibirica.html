Tulipa sibirica

Tulipa sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to harsh, snowy environments. Its bulbs are hardy and can survive freezing temperatures, making it a resilient addition to gardens in colder regions.

Tulipa sibirica is valued for its early blooming habit and distinctive floral display.
