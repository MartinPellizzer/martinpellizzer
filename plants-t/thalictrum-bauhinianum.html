Thalictrum bauhinianum

Thalictrum bauhinianum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum bauhinianum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
