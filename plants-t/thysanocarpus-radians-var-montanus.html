Thysanocarpus radians var. montanus

Thysanocarpus radians var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain thysanocarpus, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces unique, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal, a key adaptation to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
