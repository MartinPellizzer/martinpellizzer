Tococa parvifolia

Tococa parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved tococa, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
