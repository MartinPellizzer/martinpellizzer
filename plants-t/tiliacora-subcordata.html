Tiliacora subcordata

Tiliacora subcordata, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Tiliacora subcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
