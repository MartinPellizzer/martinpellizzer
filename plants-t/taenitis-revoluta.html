Taenitis revoluta

Taenitis revoluta, commonly known as the rolled fern, is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are often rolled when young. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Taenitis revoluta thrives in warm, moist conditions and is often found in shaded, tropical forests.
