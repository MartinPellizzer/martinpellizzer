Tithonia aristata

Tithonia aristata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble sunflowers, which bloom from summer to fall. The plant has heart-shaped, rough, hairy leaves that are typically green with a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow ornamental plants.

Tithonia aristata is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
