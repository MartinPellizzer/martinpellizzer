Thyella montana

Thyella montana, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thyella montana is valued for its fragrant properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
