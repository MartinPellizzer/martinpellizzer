Thouinidium pulverulentum

Thouinidium pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty lichen, is a member of the Lecanorales order and is characterized by its unique, powdery appearance.

It typically grows on bark, stones, or soil surfaces in temperate and tropical regions. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which allows it to thrive in diverse environments. Its structure consists of a thallus that is often covered in a fine, dust-like layer, giving it its common name.

This species is known for its slow growth and is often used in ecological studies to monitor environmental conditions.
