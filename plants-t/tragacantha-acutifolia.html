Tragacantha acutifolia

Tragacantha acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Tragacantha acutifolia is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
