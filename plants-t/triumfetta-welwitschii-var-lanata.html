Triumfetta welwitschii var. lanata

Triumfetta welwitschii var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender stalks. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
