Thea camellia

Thea camellia, commonly known as the camellia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Theaceae.

It is native to eastern and southern Asia, including regions such as China, Japan, and India. Camellia species are evergreen shrubs or small trees, often valued for their showy, long-lasting flowers. The leaves are typically broad and glossy, and the plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Camellias are also known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
