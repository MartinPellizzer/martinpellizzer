Trachypogon planifolius

Trachypogon planifolius, commonly known as the flatleaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
