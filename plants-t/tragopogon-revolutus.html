Tragopogon revolutus

Tragopogon revolutus, commonly known as the curly chicory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, purple flower heads that resemble a crown, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a long, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Tragopogon revolutus is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are sometimes used in salads or as a vegetable.
