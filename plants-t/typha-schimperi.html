Typha schimperi

Typha schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to Africa.

It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, often found in marshes, lake margins, and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. Its flowering stems are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Typha schimperi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
