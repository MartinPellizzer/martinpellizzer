Taraxacum shiroumense

Taraxacum shiroumense, commonly known as the Shiroumene dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine environments.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during harsh winters, with flowering typically occurring in late spring or early summer.
