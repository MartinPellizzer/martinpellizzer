Taraxacum dilutum

Taraxacum dilutum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, yellowish-green leaves and a single, elongated stem that bears a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, with the ray florets typically yellow in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
