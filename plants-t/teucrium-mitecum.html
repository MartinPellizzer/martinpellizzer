Teucrium mitecum

Teucrium mitecum, commonly known as redstem mouseear, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, fuzzy leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Teucrium mitecum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
