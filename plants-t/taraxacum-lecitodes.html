Taraxacum lecitodes

Taraxacum lecitodes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, tall flowering stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
