Trymalium velutinum var. stuartiana

Trymalium velutinum var. stuartiana, commonly known as the Stuart's velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of Australian flora and ecological systems.
