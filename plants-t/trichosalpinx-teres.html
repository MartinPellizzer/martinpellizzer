Trichosalpinx teres

Trichosalpinx teres, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
