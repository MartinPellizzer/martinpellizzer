Tillandsia lechneri

Tillandsia lechneri, commonly known as the Lecner's air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
