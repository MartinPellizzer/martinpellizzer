Trifolium longipes var. shastense

Trifolium longipes var. shastense, commonly known as the Shasta mountain clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
