Thecacoris viridis

Thecacoris viridis, commonly known as the green orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and delicate, often fragrant flowers. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and possess a waxy texture, while the flowers are usually small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
