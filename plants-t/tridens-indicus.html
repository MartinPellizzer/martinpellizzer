Tridens indicus

Tridens indicus, commonly known as Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in panicles. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. Tridens indicus thrives in full sun and is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
