Teucrium polium subsp. antiatlanticum

Teucrium polium subsp.

antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, clustered in whorls along the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
