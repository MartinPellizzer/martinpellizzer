Thymus pulegioides subsp. montanus

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

montanus, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, pungent aroma.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
