Taraxacum acervans

Taraxacum acervans, commonly known as the Canadian dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white hairs. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum acervans is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
