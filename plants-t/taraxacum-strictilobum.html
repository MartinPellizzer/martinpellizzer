Taraxacum strictilobum

Taraxacum strictilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are narrow and have distinct lobes. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, hollow seed head with parachute-like seeds. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
