Torilis gaillardotii

Torilis gaillardotii, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Torilis gaillardotii is also known for its aromatic foliage, which has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
