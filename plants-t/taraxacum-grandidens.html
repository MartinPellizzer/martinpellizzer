Taraxacum grandidens

Taraxacum grandidens, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust taproot system. The plant features alternate, broadly lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces bright yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum grandidens is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating inflammation and digestive issues.
