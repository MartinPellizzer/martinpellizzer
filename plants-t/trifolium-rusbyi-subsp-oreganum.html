Trifolium rusbyi subsp. oreganum

Trifolium rusbyi subsp.

oreganum, commonly known as Oregon mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountain meadows.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
