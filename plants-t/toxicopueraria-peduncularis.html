Toxicopueraria peduncularis

Toxicopueraria peduncularis, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a vigorous climbing vine characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

However, it is toxic to livestock and humans due to the presence of various alkaloids.
