Trichosanthes cucumeroides var. cavaleriei

Trichosanthes cucumeroides var. cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, green fruits that turn orange-red when mature. The fruit is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
