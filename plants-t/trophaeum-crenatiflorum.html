Trophaeum crenatiflorum

Trophaeum crenatiflorum, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with a distinctive crested or fringed appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in specialized gardens.
