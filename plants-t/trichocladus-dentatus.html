Trichocladus dentatus

Trichocladus dentatus, commonly known as the toothed fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family. Trichocladus dentatus thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
