Trichera carpathica

Trichera carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian trichera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in early summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Trichera carpathica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
