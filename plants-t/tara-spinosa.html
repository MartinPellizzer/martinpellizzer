Tara spinosa

Tara spinosa, commonly known as the thorned tara, is a leguminous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forest and savanna regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
