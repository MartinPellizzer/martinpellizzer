Trifolium chilaloense

Trifolium chilaloense, commonly known as the Chilalo clover, is a flowering plant species native to South America, particularly found in parts of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring.

Trifolium chilaloense is adapted to grow in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.
