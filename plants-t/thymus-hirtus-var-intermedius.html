Thymus hirtus var. intermedius

Thymus hirtus var. intermedius, commonly known as intermediate thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
