Tsuga oblongisquamata

Tsuga oblongisquamata, commonly known as the Japanese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces small, spherical cones that hang from the branches, and its bark is reddish-brown and scaly. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
