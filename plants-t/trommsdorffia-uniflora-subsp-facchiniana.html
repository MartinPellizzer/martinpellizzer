Trommsdorffia uniflora subsp. facchiniana

Trommsdorffia uniflora subsp.

facchiniana, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
