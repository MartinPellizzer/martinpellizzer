Teucrium diabolicum

Teucrium diabolicum, commonly known as the devil's weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in appearance. Native to regions of southern Europe and North Africa, it thrives in rocky or dry soils.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its common name suggests a more ominous or feared reputation.
