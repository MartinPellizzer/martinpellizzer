Tulipa sylvestris var. biflora

Tulipa sylvestris var. biflora, commonly known as the woodland tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

The varietal name "biflora" refers to its tendency to produce two flowers per stem, a distinctive feature that sets it apart from other tulip varieties.
