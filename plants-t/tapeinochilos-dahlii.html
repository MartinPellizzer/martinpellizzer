Tapeinochilos dahlii

Tapeinochilos dahlii, commonly known as the Dahl's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Tapeinochilos dahlii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
