Taraxacum violaceomaculatum

Taraxacum violaceomaculatum, commonly known as the violet-spotted dandelion, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head, which is surrounded by a collar of bracts that often display a violet or purple hue. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of tiny, wind-dispersed seeds called pappus. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
