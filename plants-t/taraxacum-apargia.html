Taraxacum apargia

Taraxacum apargia, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism through a fluffy white pappus that aids in wind pollination. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum apargia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
