Tragoceros venustum

Tragoceros venustum, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking yellow-orange sap and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Tragoceros venustum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
