Terminalia altissima

Terminalia altissima, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually clustered at the ends of branches. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, boat-shaped fruits containing edible seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses, particularly in treating ailments related to the digestive system.
