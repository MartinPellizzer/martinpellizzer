Taraxacum fraudulentum

Taraxacum fraudulentum, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with a rosette growth pattern. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, typical of the dandelion genus.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, similar to other species in the Taraxacum genus.
