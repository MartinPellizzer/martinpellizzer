Triodia burkensis

Triodia burkensis, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and wiry, spiny leaves that help it survive in arid environments. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high levels of salt and wind. Triodia burkensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small animals.

It is often found in coastal and inland dune systems across the northern and western regions of Australia.
