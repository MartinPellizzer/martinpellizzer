Thryptomene polyandra

Thryptomene polyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It often grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Thryptomene polyandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
