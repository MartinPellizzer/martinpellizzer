Touterea lutea

Touterea lutea, commonly known as the yellow tara, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its long, fibrous pods that contain seeds enclosed in a tough, woody casing. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ecological importance and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for its fibers and seeds.
