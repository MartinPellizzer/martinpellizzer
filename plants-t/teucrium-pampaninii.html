Teucrium pampaninii

Teucrium pampaninii, commonly known as the Pampas germander, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
