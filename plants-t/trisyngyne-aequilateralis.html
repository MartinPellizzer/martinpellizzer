Trisyngyne aequilateralis

Trisyngyne aequilateralis, commonly known as the three-awned grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces three awns on each lemma, which are bristle-like structures that aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Trisyngyne aequilateralis is often found in grasslands and open habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
