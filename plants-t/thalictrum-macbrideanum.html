Thalictrum macbrideanum

Thalictrum macbrideanum, commonly known as the MacBride's meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or stream banks.
