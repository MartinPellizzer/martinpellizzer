Tricalysia coriacea subsp. angustifolia

Tricalysia coriacea subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tricalysia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its rough, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
