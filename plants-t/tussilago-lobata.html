Tussilago lobata

Tussilago lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tussilago lobata is often found in regions with high humidity and is adapted to tolerate partial to full shade.
