Tanacetum argyrophyllum

Tanacetum argyrophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
