Taraxacum prunicolor

Taraxacum prunicolor, commonly known as the purpleflower dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright purple flowers, which contrast with its typical yellow dandelion relatives. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports the flower head.

This species is often found in meadows and open areas, thriving in well-drained soil.
