Taraxacum neokurdicum

Taraxacum neokurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed heads produce lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures, facilitating wind dispersal.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
