Teucrium villarianum

Teucrium villarianum, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or blue in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

Teucrium villarianum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
