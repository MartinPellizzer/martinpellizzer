Tambourissa rota var. longipetiolata

Tambourissa rota var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled tambourissa, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with long, slender petioles, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
