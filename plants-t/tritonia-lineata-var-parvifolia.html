Tritonia lineata var. parvifolia

Tritonia lineata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tritonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The varietal name "parvifolia" refers to its small foliage, distinguishing it from other varieties of Tritonia.
