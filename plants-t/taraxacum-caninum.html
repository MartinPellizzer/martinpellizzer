Taraxacum caninum

Taraxacum caninum, commonly known as the dog's parsley or meadow dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, ridged stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a white, pappus that aids in its propagation. It is typically found in open, disturbed soils in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

Taraxacum caninum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
