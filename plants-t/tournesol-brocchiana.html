Tournesol brocchiana

Tournesol brocchiana, commonly known as the Brocchiana sunflower, is a rare and striking species native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves.

Tournesol brocchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
