Trixis picroides

Trixis picroides, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-resistant.

Trixis picroides plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
