Thuja pisifera

Thuja pisifera, commonly known as the Japanese thuja, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is typically dark green. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Thuja pisifera is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and air pollution.
