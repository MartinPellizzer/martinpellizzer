Tragacantha heratensis

Tragacantha heratensis, commonly known as the Herat tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing small, dark brown seeds. It is valued for its gum, which is harvested from the exudate of its bark and used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

Tragacantha heratensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
