Thesium hispidum

Thesium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Thesium hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
