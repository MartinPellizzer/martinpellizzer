Tatina parviflora

Tatina parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tatina, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tatina parviflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
