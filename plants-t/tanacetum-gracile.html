Tanacetum gracile

Tanacetum gracile, commonly known as slender tansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Tanacetum gracile is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
