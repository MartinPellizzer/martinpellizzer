Thymus praecox var. spathulatus

Thymus praecox var. spathulatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
