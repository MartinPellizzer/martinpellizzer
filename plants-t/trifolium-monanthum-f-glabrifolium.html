Trifolium monanthum f. glabrifolium

Trifolium monanthum f. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
