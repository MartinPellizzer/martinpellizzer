Trema micranthum var. obtusatum

Trema micranthum var. obtusatum, commonly known as the broadleaf senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
