Tynanthus hyacinthinus

Tynanthus hyacinthinus, commonly known as the hyacinth bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, hyacinth-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritional benefits.

Tynanthus hyacinthinus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
