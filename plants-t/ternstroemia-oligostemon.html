Ternstroemia oligostemon

Ternstroemia oligostemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ternstroemia, is a member of the Theaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
