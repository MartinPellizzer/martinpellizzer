Trichosalpinx barbelifera

Trichosalpinx barbelifera, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Trichosalpinx barbelifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
