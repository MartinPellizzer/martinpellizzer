Thesium gallicum

Thesium gallicum, commonly known as the French thesium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Thesium gallicum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
