Taraxacum pallescentiforme

Taraxacum pallescentiforme, commonly known as the pale-leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, parachute-like seeds. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
