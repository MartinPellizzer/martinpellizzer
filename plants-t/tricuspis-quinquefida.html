Tricuspis quinquefida

Tricuspis quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed tricuspis, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

Its leaves are distinctly tricuspis, meaning they have three pointed lobes, and quinquefida refers to the five-lobed structure of its flowers. The plant typically exhibits a compact growth habit with small, clustered flowers that display a range of colors. It is native to specific regions with a preference for well-drained soils and partial sunlight.

Due to its distinctive morphology, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
