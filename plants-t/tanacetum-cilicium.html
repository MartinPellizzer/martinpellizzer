Tanacetum cilicium

Tanacetum cilicium, commonly known as the Cilician tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum cilicium is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
