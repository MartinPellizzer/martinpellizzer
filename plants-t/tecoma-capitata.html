Tecoma capitata

Tecoma capitata, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
