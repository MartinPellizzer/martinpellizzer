Tithonia vilmoriniana

Tithonia vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flower heads, which are typically surrounded by green bracts. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Tithonia vilmoriniana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
