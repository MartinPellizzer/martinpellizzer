Trifolium anatolicum

Trifolium anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the eastern Mediterranean, including Turkey and parts of the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Anatolian clover is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
