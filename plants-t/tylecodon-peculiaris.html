Tylecodon peculiaris

Tylecodon peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
