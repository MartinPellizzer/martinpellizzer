Tanacetum afghanicum

Tanacetum afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

Tanacetum afghanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
