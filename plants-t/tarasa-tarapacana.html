Tarasa tarapacana

Tarasa tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has long, narrow leaves and thrives in cool, humid environments. It is often found growing in rocky soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
