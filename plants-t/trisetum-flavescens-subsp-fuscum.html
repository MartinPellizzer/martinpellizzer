Trisetum flavescens subsp. fuscum

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

fuscum, commonly known as the yellow false brome, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale yellow to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
