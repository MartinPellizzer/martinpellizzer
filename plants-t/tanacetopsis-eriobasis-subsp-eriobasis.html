Tanacetopsis eriobasis subsp. eriobasis

Tanacetopsis eriobasis subsp.

eriobasis, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. It has a somewhat aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other subspecies within the species.
