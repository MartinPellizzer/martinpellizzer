Thelymitra purpurata

Thelymitra purpurata, commonly known as the purple-veined sun orchid, is an Australian terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers with prominent purple veins, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. The plant produces a single flower per stem, and the petals are often recurved, giving it a distinctive appearance. Thelymitra purpurata is found in various habitats across southeastern Australia, including heathlands and open forests.

It is a perennial herb that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance in its native environment.
