Trachelospermum difforme

Trachelospermum difforme, commonly known as the starry nightshade or sweetheart vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.
