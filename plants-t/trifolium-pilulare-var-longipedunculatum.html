Trifolium pilulare var. longipedunculatum

Trifolium pilulare var. longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed clover, is a variant of the clover genus characterized by its distinctive morphology.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, typically with a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called head flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
