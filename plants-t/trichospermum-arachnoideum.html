Trichospermum arachnoideum

Trichospermum arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider-tailed gooseberry, is a woody shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, spider-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Trichospermum arachnoideum is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its use in traditional medicine.
