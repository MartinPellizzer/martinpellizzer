Terebinthina indica

Terebinthina indica, commonly known as the Indian terebinth, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The tree has historical significance in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic resins.
