Trichochloa simplex

Trichochloa simplex, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that is often hidden among the leaves.

Trichochloa simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique visual texture.
