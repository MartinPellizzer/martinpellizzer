Triumfetta cymosa var. glabrescens

Triumfetta cymosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters, featuring pinnate leaves with 5–9 pairs of elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that are often hairy when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability.
