Triticum intermedium var. pertenue

Triticum intermedium var. pertenue, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, with inflorescences that are typically spike-like. This plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it suitable for both agricultural and ecological applications. It is often used in conservation efforts due to its role in soil stabilization and biodiversity support.

Additionally, it has potential as a forage crop and for the production of bioenergy.
