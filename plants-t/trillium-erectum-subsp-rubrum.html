Trillium erectum subsp. rubrum

Trillium erectum subsp.

rubrum, commonly known as the red trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its three elliptical, reddish-purple leaves and a single, showy white or pinkish flower with three petals.

The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully expand, and it produces red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating various ailments.
