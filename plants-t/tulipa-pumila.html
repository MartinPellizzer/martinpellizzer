Tulipa pumila

Tulipa pumila, commonly known as the dwarf tulip, is a small, hardy perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces compact, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and ornamental value.

Tulipa pumila prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
