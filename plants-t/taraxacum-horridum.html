Taraxacum horridum

Taraxacum horridum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structure. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically basal and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds with feathery pappi.

Taraxacum horridum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including lawns and meadows.
