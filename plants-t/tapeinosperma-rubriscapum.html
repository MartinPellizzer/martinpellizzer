Tapeinosperma rubriscapum

Tapeinosperma rubriscapum, commonly known as the red-capped acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
