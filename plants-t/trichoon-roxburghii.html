Trichoon roxburghii

Trichoon roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, green, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
