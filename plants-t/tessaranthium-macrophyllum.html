Tessaranthium macrophyllum

Tessaranthium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant leaf lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
