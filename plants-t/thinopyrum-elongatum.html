Thinopyrum elongatum

Thinopyrum elongatum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Thinopyrum elongatum is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be a weed in cultivated areas.
