Taraxacum vagans

Taraxacum vagans, commonly known as the wandering dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a bright yellow flower that matures into a fluffy white seed head, which is dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

Taraxacum vagans is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
