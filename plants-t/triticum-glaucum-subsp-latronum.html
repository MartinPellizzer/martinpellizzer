Triticum glaucum subsp. latronum

Triticum glaucum subsp.

latronum, commonly known as the wheatgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance. It typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its rapid growth rate and high biomass production.

The plant has narrow, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high concentration of chlorophyll and vitamins.

This subspecies is often cultivated for use in human and animal nutrition, as well as in ecological restoration projects.
