Thymus hyemalis subsp. fumanifolius

Thymus hyemalis subsp.

fumanifolius, commonly known as the winter thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in small inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
