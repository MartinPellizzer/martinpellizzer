Terminalia trivialis

Terminalia trivialis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
