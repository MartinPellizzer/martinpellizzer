Thelymitra venosa

Thelymitra venosa, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is an orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink and white petals and a velvety slipper-shaped pouch that houses its pollinia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Thelymitra venosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
