Taraxacum flavovirens

Taraxacum flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
