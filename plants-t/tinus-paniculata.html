Tinus paniculata

Tinus paniculata, commonly known as the yellow panic grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellowish-green inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in paniculate structures, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Tinus paniculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
