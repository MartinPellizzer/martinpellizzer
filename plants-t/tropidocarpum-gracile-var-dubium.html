Tropidocarpum gracile var. dubium

Tropidocarpum gracile var. dubium, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of rocky and sandy habitats.
