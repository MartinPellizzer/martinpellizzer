Tropaeolum buchenavianum

Tropaeolum buchenavianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is known for its peppery, edible foliage and seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical name honors the botanist Johann Friedrich von Buchenau, who contributed to the study of plants in the 19th century.
