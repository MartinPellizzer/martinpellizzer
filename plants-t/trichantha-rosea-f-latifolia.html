Trichantha rosea f. latifolia

Trichantha rosea f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rose trichantha, is a unique variant of the Trichantha rosea species.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
