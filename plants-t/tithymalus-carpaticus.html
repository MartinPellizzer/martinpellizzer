Tithymalus carpaticus

Tithymalus carpaticus, commonly known as the Carpathian tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose panicles, and range in color from pale yellow to white.

Tithymalus carpaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
