Thymus serpyllum f. cinerosus

Thymus serpyllum f. cinerosus, commonly known as the dark thyme or smoky thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers and has dark green, slightly hairy leaves that emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal medicine.
