Triphora

Triphora, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. Triphora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
