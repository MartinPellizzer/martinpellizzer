Tradescantia biandra

Tradescantia biandra, commonly known as the "Waxplant," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often brightly colored flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Tradescantia biandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
