Tetragonia hirsuta

Tetragonia hirsuta, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical, green fruits containing seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Tetragonia hirsuta is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
