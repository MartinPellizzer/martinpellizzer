Trymalium spadiceum

Trymalium spadiceum, commonly known as the yellow-veined thistle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves with prominent yellow veins, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense heads. It produces small, smooth, brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
