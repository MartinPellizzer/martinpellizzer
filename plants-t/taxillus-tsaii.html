Taxillus tsaii

Taxillus tsaii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the family Rosaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions of China and is often found in temperate forest ecosystems.

Taxillus tsaii plays a role in ecological interactions, influencing host tree health and providing food for wildlife.
