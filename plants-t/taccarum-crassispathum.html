Taccarum crassispathum

Taccarum crassispathum, commonly known as the broad-leaf taccarum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and woody stems that become more pronounced with age.

Taccarum crassispathum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
