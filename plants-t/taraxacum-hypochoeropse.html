Taraxacum hypochoeropse

Taraxacum hypochoeropse, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive seed dispersal mechanism. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. Its flowers are typically yellow, transitioning to white puffballs that release seeds via the wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, exhibiting resilience to various environmental conditions.
