Telesonix heucheriformis

Telesonix heucheriformis, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a striking plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble those of the heuchera genus, giving it its common name. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread slowly across forest floors, forming dense mats. Its leaves are typically a vibrant silver-green, with a waxy texture that helps reflect excess sunlight.

Telesonix heucheriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
