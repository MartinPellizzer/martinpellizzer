Taraxacum degelii

Taraxacum degelii, commonly known as the Degel's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy seed head with white pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Taraxacum degelii is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
