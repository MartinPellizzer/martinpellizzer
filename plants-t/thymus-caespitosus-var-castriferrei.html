Thymus caespitosus var. castriferrei

Thymus caespitosus var. castriferrei, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
