Thymus celakowskyanus

Thymus celakowskyanus, commonly known as the Celakowski thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal value.
