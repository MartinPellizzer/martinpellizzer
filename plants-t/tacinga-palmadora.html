Tacinga palmadora

Tacinga palmadora, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by ornamental fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically valuable in tropical landscapes.
