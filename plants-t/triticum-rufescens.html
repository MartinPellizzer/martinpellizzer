Triticum rufescens

Triticum rufescens, commonly known as red wheat, is a species of wild wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its reddish grain and semi-domesticated growth habits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stalks and long, narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and the seeds are enclosed in tough, protective hulls.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
