Thymus funkii var. martinezii

Thymus funkii var. martinezii, commonly known as Martinez's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.
