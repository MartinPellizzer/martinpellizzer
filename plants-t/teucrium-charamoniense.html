Teucrium charamoniense

Teucrium charamoniense, commonly known as the Ethiopian germander, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
