Trifolium squarrosum subsp. squarrosum

Trifolium squarrosum subsp.

squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrosum clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive white or pale midrib.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
