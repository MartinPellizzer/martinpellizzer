Tragopogon flexuosus subsp. lithuanicus

Tragopogon flexuosus subsp.

lithuanicus, commonly known as the Lithuanian goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
