Themeda triandra var. vulgaris

Themeda triandra var. vulgaris, commonly known as spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, often forming dense stands. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

Its resilience to fire and drought makes it a dominant species in many Australian ecosystems.
