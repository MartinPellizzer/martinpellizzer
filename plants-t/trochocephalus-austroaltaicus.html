Trochocephalus austroaltaicus

Trochocephalus austroaltaicus, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
