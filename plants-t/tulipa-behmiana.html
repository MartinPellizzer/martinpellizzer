Tulipa behmiana

Tulipa behmiana, commonly known as the Behm's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring and early summer, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Tulipa behmiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
