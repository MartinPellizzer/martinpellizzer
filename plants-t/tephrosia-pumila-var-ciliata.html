Tephrosia pumila var. ciliata

Tephrosia pumila var. ciliata, commonly known as the slender tephrosia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves with ciliate margins, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in terminal racemes. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
