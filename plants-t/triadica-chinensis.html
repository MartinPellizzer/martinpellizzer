Triadica chinensis

Triadica chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, sunny environments.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
