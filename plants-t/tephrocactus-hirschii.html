Tephrocactus hirschii

Tephrocactus hirschii, commonly known as the Hirsch's cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and a distinctive, spiny appearance. The plant features white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its stems are typically covered in white, woolly hairs, which help to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Tephrocactus hirschii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
