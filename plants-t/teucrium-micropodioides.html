Teucrium micropodioides

Teucrium micropodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
