Thlaspi bursa-pastoris var. rubellum

Thlaspi bursa-pastoris var. rubellum, commonly known as red-rooted pennycress, is a variant of the common pennycress species.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its distinctive red stems and roots are notable features, and it is often found in temperate climates.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
