Tecoma toxophora

Tecoma toxophora, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma toxophora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly rough texture.
