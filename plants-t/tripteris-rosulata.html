Tripteris rosulata

Tripteris rosulata, commonly known as the rose-leaf tripteris, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive rose-like arrangement of leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed follicles that release seeds when mature.

Tripteris rosulata is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
