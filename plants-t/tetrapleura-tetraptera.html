Tetrapleura tetraptera

Tetrapleura tetraptera, commonly known as African balm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant's aromatic leaves and flowers contribute to its use in herbal remedies and as a spice in some cuisines.
