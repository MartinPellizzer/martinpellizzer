Tibouchina alata

Tibouchina alata, commonly known as the silverleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that range in color from pink to red, often mistaken for petals. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Tibouchina alata is known for its ability to attract birds and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
