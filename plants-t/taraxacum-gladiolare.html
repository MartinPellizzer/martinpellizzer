Taraxacum gladiolare

Taraxacum gladiolare, commonly known as the gladiola dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, supporting the composite flower heads.

Taraxacum gladiolare is often found in meadows and open areas, preferring well-drained soils.
