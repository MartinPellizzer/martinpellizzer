Thrinax keyensis

Thrinax keyensis, commonly known as the Key Largo palm, is a flowering plant species native to the Florida Keys.

It is a small, cold-hardy palm that typically grows to a height of about 6 to 10 feet. The plant has a slender trunk and is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves that are composed of numerous narrow, stiff leaflets. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

Thrinax keyensis is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in wetlands and mangrove swamps.
