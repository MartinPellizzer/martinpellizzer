Trillium chandleri f. foliaceum

Trillium chandleri f. foliaceum, commonly known as the foliaceum form of Chandler's trillium, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which consists of broad, flat, leaf-like structures rather than the typical three-lobed leaves of most trillium species. This variation in leaf morphology is a notable botanical feature that sets it apart from its relatives. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in areas with rich organic soil.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early spring, making it a rare and sought-after specimen among botanists and plant enthusiasts.
