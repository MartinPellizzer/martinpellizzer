Toxocarpus glaucus var. radiatus

Toxocarpus glaucus var. radiatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy structures that give it a distinctive, mist-like appearance.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
