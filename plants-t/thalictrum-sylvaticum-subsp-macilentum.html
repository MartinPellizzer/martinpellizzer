Thalictrum sylvaticum subsp. macilentum

Thalictrum sylvaticum subsp.

macilentum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, fern-like compound leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its flowers are usually arranged in upright, branched inflorescences, and the plant is known for its subtle, somewhat aromatic fragrance. Thalictrum sylvaticum subsp.

macilentum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
