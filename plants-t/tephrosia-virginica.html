Tephrosia virginica

Tephrosia virginica, commonly known as the Carolina tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland areas.

Tephrosia virginica is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
