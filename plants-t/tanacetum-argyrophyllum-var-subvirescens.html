Tanacetum argyrophyllum var. subvirescens

Tanacetum argyrophyllum var. subvirescens, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
