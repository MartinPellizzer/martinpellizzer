Triodia triaristata

Triodia triaristata, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and is a key component of the native vegetation in its habitat.

Triodia triaristata is adapted to withstand extreme drought and heat, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
