Tradescantia pilosissima

Tradescantia pilosissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ease of care. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Tradescantia pilosissima is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
