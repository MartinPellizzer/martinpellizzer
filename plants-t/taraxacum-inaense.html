Taraxacum inaense

Taraxacum inaense, commonly known as the Inaense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head, which is a common feature among dandelions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
