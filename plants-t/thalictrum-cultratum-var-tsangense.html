Thalictrum cultratum var. tsangense

Thalictrum cultratum var. tsangense, commonly known as the Tibetan meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
