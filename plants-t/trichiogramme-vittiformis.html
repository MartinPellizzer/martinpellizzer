Trichiogramme vittiformis

Trichiogramme vittiformis, commonly known as the golden haircap, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, turfy growth habit and the presence of specialized structures called rhizoids that anchor it to substrates. The leaves are typically narrow and recurved, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and on acidic soils.

This moss plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
