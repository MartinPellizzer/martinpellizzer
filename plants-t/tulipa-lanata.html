Tulipa lanata

Tulipa lanata, commonly known as the woolly tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, bluish-green foliage and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces numerous bulbous stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens with their soft, fluffy appearance.

The woolly tulip is well-suited for rock gardens and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
