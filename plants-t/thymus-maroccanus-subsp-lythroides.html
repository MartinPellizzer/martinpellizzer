Thymus maroccanus subsp. lythroides

Thymus maroccanus subsp.

lythroides, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in Mediterranean and arid landscapes.
