Trichosanthes azurea

Trichosanthes azurea, commonly known as blue-flowered trumpet vine, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces long, serpentine fruits that turn yellow as they mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.

Native to Southeast Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
