Tricyrtis hirta f. atropurpurea

Tricyrtis hirta f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf Japanese bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to Japan.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and elegant blooms that appear in late summer.
