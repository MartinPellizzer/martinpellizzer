Trifolium melananthum

Trifolium melananthum, commonly known as black clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Black clover is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
