Turpinia cochinchinensis

Turpinia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
