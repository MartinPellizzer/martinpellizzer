Thesium strigulosum

Thesium strigulosum, commonly known as the stinging thesium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact.

Thesium strigulosum is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathland environments.
