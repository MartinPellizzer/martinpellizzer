Tragacantha atropatana

Tragacantha atropatana, commonly known as Persian gum or atropatana tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
