Trifolium roseum

Trifolium roseum, commonly known as the rose clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Trifolium roseum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
