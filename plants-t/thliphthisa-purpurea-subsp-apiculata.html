Thliphthisa purpurea subsp. apiculata

Thliphthisa purpurea subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the purple thliphthisa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are borne in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
