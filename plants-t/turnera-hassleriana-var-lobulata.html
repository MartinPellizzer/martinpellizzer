Turnera hassleriana var. lobulata

Turnera hassleriana var. lobulata, commonly known as the lobed turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with red centers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped or lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
