Tithymalus furcillatus

Tithymalus furcillatus, commonly known as the clustered tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tithymalus furcillatus is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
