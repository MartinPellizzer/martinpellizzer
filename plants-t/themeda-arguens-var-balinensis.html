Themeda arguens var. balinensis

Themeda arguens var. balinensis, commonly known as the Balinese grass, is a variant of the species Themeda arguens, which is native to Southeast Asia.

This grass is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its resilience to environmental stress and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora in its native habitat.
