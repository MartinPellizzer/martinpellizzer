Tynus

Tynus, commonly known as the common tynus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural habitats.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild medicinal properties.
