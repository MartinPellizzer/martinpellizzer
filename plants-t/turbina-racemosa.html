Turbina racemosa

Turbina racemosa, commonly known as the clustered turban snail, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Turbinidae.

It is characterized by its spiral-shaped shell, which is typically smooth and has a conical or turban-like form. The shell is often pale in color, with a glossy surface that can vary in hue depending on the individual. This species is found in tropical and subtropical marine environments, often inhabiting shallow coastal waters.

Its anatomy includes a muscular foot used for locomotion and a radula for feeding on algae and other microscopic organisms.
