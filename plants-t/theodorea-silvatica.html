Theodorea silvatica

Theodorea silvatica, commonly known as the forest lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate forests of Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Theodorea silvatica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
