Teucrium × cebennense

Teucrium × cebennense, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Teucrium × cebennense is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
