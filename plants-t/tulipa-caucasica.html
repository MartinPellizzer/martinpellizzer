Tulipa caucasica

Tulipa caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 50 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
