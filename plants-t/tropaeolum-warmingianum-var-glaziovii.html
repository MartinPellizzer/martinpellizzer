Tropaeolum warmingianum var. glaziovii

Tropaeolum warmingianum var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
