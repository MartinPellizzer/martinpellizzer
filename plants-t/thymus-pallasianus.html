Thymus pallasianus

Thymus pallasianus, commonly known as Pallas' thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus pallasianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
