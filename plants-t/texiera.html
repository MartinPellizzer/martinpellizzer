Texiera

Texiera, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Texiera grandiflora, which is native to Mexico and parts of Central America. These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. They typically grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Texiera species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their striking floral displays.
