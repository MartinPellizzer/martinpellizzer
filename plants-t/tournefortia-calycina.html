Tournefortia calycina

Tournefortia calycina, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

Tournefortia calycina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.
