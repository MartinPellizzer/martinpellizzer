Triplophyllum batesii

Triplophyllum batesii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a woody stem with prominent leaf scars and produces a milky latex when damaged. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
