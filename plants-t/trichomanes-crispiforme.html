Trichomanes crispiforme

Trichomanes crispiforme, commonly known as the fringed maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that exhibit a distinctive crinkled or wavy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its sporangia are clustered in small, spore-producing structures called sori, typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
