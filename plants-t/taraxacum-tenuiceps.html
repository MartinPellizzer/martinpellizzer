Taraxacum tenuiceps

Taraxacum tenuiceps, commonly known as the slender-headed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a single, tall, yellow flower head that blooms in the spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule fruit that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is considered a weed in some regions but is also valued for its medicinal properties.
