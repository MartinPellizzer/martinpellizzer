Thaspium trifoliatum var. aureum

Thaspium trifoliatum var. aureum, commonly known as golden trailing arum, is a variant of the trailing arum that exhibits distinctive golden foliage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid climates. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant yellow-green leaves and unique flowering structure.

It is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
