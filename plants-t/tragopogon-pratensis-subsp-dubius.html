Tragopogon pratensis subsp. dubius

Tragopogon pratensis subsp.

dubius, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, forming a single composite head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
