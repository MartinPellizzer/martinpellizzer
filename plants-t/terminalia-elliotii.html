Terminalia elliotii

Terminalia elliotii, commonly known as African wild date, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy drupes that are often consumed by wildlife and humans.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
