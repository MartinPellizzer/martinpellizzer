Trichosalpinx pergrata

Trichosalpinx pergrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
