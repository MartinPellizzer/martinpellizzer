Tigarea dentata

Tigarea dentata, commonly known as the toothed tigarea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tigarea dentata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
