Tapeinosperma multipunctatum

Tapeinosperma multipunctatum, commonly known as the African star fruit, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
