Taraxacum bashkiricum

Taraxacum bashkiricum, commonly known as the Bashkir dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairy stem that supports a bright yellow flower head, which develops into a ball of white, tufted seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
