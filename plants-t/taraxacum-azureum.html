Taraxacum azureum

Taraxacum azureum, commonly known as the azure dandelion, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue flowers, which contrast strikingly with its yellow-orange central disk. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
