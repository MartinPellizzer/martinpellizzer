Thymus serpyllum subf. minimus

Thymus serpyllum subf.

minimus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features tiny, aromatic leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
