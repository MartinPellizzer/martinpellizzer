Trichocereus terscheckii var. montanus

Trichocereus terscheckii var. montanus, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus" or "Pachypodium," is a cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and sparse, spiny foliage, which helps it conserve water in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking, large white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

The varietal name "montanus" refers to its occurrence in mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
