Tradescantia virginica var. drummondii

Tradescantia virginica var. drummondii, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia virginica var. drummondii is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
