Teucrium polium subvar. pseudoaureum

Teucrium polium subvar. pseudoaureum, commonly known as the golden germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
