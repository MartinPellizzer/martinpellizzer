Trichophorum hudsonianum

Trichophorum hudsonianum, commonly known as Hudson's sedge, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and bogs. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Hudson's sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
