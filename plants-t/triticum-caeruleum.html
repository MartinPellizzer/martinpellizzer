Triticum caeruleum

Triticum caeruleum, commonly known as blue wheat, is a wild species of wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray florets, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists and researchers due to its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural use.
