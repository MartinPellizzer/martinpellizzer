Taraxacum arquitenens

Taraxacum arquitenens, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, upright stem topped with a distinctive yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is a perennial herb that thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
