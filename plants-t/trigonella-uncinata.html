Trigonella uncinata

Trigonella uncinata, commonly known as the hookleaf fenugreek, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Trigonella uncinata is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
