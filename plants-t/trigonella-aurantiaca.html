Trigonella aurantiaca

Trigonella aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden trigonella, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Its seeds are small and oblong, typically enclosed in a pod that splits open upon maturity.

Trigonella aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
