Telanthera microcephala var. hispida

Telanthera microcephala var. hispida, commonly known as the rough-leaved telanthera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The leaves are hairy and ovate, giving the plant its "hispida" variety name, which means "bristly" in Latin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
