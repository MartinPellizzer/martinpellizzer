Thea drupifera

Thea drupifera, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces orange or yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thea drupifera is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
