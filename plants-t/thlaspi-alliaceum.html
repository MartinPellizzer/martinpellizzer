Thlaspi alliaceum

Thlaspi alliaceum, commonly known as garlic mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant produces smooth, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has become invasive in North America.

Thlaspi alliaceum thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and along trails.
