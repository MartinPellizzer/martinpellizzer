Tricholepis montana

Tricholepis montana, commonly known as the mountain tricholepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Tricholepis montana is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
