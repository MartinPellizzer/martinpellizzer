Thymus pulegioides var. toutonii

Thymus pulegioides var. toutonii, commonly known as Turkish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
