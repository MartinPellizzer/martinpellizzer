Tetrastigma macrocorymbum

Tetrastigma macrocorymbum, commonly known as the large-corymbose grapevine, is a climbing vine belonging to the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
