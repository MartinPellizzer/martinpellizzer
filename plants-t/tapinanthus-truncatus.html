Tapinanthus truncatus

Tapinanthus truncatus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Tapinanthus truncatus is found in various parts of Africa and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
