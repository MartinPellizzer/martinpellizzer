Tradescantia flexuosa

Tradescantia flexuosa, commonly known as snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on slender stalks during the growing season. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water.

Its air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
