Thlaspi purpurascens

Thlaspi purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemed cabbageworm, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate and typically have a slightly hairy texture.

Thlaspi purpurascens is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
