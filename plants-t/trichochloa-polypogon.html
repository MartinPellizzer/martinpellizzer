Trichochloa polypogon

Trichochloa polypogon, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks, marshes, and wet meadows, where it thrives in well-drained to slightly acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Trichochloa polypogon is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats and its potential use in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals.
