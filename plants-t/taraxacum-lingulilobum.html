Taraxacum lingulilobum

Taraxacum lingulilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, followed by rapid growth in spring.
