Thunbergia aurantiaca

Thunbergia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Thunbergia aurantiaca is often cultivated as a climbing plant due to its vigorous growth habit.
