Teucrium polium subsp. homotrichum

Teucrium polium subsp.

homotrichum, commonly known as Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, grayish-white hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, aromatic scent. The flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming in summer.

This species is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
