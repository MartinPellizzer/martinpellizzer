Trachyrhizum appendiculoides

Trachyrhizum appendiculoides, commonly known as the slender root orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a unique root system that is often described as "slender" and "appendiculate," contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
