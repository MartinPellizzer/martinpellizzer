Tabernaemontana ophiorrhizoides

Tabernaemontana ophiorrhizoides, commonly known as the silver bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a smooth, pale bark and broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant flowers.
