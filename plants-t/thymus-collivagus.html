Thymus collivagus

Thymus collivagus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus collivagus thrives in well-drained soils and sunny environments, making it a common ornamental and aromatic plant in Mediterranean regions.
