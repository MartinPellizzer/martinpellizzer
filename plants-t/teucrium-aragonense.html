Teucrium aragonense

Teucrium aragonense, commonly known as Spanish germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and they bloom from spring to early summer.

The leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
