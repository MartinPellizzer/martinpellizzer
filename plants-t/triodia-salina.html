Triodia salina

Triodia salina, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant. Triodia salina is often found in saline soils and is adapted to high salt concentrations.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of native vegetation in coastal and inland salt-affected areas.
