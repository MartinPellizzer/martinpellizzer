Tragacantha ghilanica

Tragacantha ghilanica, commonly known as the Ghilan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod fruits.

It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
