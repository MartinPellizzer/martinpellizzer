Thelypteris erecta

Thelypteris erecta, commonly known as the upright water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically produces sori on the underside of its leaves, which are covered by a protective indusium. Thelypteris erecta thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
