Typha latifolia var. remotiuscula

Typha latifolia var. remotiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical variety, and it produces dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically brown in color. This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and stabilize shoreline soils.
