Taraxacum leptodon

Taraxacum leptodon, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flowering stem that bears a composite flower head. The plant produces a fluffy, white seed head that is dispersed by the wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family. It is typically found in open, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

Taraxacum leptodon is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for various insects and as a pioneer species in soil regeneration.
