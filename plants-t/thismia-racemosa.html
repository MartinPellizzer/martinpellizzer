Thismia racemosa

Thismia racemosa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it unable to perform photosynthesis. Instead, it relies on a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi for nutrient absorption.

Its pale, ghost-like appearance and rarity in the wild make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
