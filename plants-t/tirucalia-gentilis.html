Tirucalia gentilis

Tirucalia gentilis, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are ovate and arranged oppositely along the stem, while the flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

Tirucalia gentilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
