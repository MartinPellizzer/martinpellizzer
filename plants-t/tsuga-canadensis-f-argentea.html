Tsuga canadensis f. argentea

Tsuga canadensis f. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

This form is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on the foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically needle-like, with a soft texture and a slightly curved tip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic.
