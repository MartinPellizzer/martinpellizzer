Trifolium willdenovii var. longicaule

Trifolium willdenovii var. longicaule, commonly known as the long-stemmed clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
