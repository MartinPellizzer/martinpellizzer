Toona inodora

Toona inodora, commonly known as the fragrant toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces a fruit that is typically a smooth, green, fleshy capsule.
