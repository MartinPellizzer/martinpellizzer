Trichocereus thelegonoides

Trichocereus thelegonoides, commonly known as the Pachanoi cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Trichocereus thelegonoides is adapted to high-altitude environments with low rainfall and intense sunlight.
