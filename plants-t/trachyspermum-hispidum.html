Trachyspermum hispidum

Trachyspermum hispidum, commonly known as wild cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
