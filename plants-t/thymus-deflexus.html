Thymus deflexus

Thymus deflexus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Thymus deflexus is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
