Tecoma ipe var. desinens

Tecoma ipe var. desinens, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
