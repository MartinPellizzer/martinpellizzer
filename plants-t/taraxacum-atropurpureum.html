Taraxacum atropurpureum

Taraxacum atropurpureum, commonly known as purple hawk's beard, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its purple to reddish flowers and hairy stems, which distinguish it from other similar species. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a taproot system typical of many dandelion species.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.
