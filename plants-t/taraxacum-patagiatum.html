Taraxacum patagiatum

Taraxacum patagiatum, commonly known as the Patagonian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternate, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which develops into a white, fluffy pappus for wind dispersal of seeds.

Taraxacum patagiatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
