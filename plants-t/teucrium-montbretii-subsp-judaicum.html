Teucrium montbretii subsp. judaicum

Teucrium montbretii subsp.

judaicum, commonly known as the Jerusalem sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-blue hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
