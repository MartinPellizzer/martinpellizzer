Tagetes tinctoria

Tagetes tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange and yellow blooms, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its strong aromatic scent. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Tagetes tinctoria is also valued for its ability to repel certain insect pests due to the presence of volatile compounds in its foliage.
