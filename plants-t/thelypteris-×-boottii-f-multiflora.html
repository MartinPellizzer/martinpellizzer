Thelypteris × boottii f. multiflora

Thelypteris × boottii f. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora fern, is a hybrid fern species that exhibits a combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It is native to regions in Southeast Asia, including parts of China and India, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into smaller segments, giving it a lacy appearance. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered along the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
