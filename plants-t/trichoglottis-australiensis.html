Trichoglottis australiensis

Trichoglottis australiensis, commonly known as the Australian trichoglottis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Trichoglottis australiensis is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
