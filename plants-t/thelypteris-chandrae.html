Thelypteris chandrae

Thelypteris chandrae, commonly known as the Chandra's shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Thelypteris chandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
