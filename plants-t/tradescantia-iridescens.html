Tradescantia iridescens

Tradescantia iridescens, commonly known as the wandering Jew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, purple-tinged leaves that display a metallic sheen under light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect sunlight.

Tradescantia iridescens is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
