Tragacantha meschhedensis

Tragacantha meschhedensis, commonly known as the Mesopotamian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, semi-arid environments, making it an important species in traditional herbal medicine and ecological restoration.
