Tocoyena insignis

Tocoyena insignis, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, providing a striking visual contrast to its vibrant blooms. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

Tocoyena insignis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
