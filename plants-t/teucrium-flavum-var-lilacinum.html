Teucrium flavum var. lilacinum

Teucrium flavum var. lilacinum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
