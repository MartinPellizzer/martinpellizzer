Trichilia spondioides var. gibbosifolia

Trichilia spondioides var. gibbosifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. The wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
