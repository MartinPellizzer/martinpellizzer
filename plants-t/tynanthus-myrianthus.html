Tynanthus myrianthus

Tynanthus myrianthus, commonly known as the yellow rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in whorls of three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
