Thrincia nudicaulis subsp. taraxacoides

Thrincia nudicaulis subsp.

taraxacoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrincia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, basal leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
