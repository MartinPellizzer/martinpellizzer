Thuranthos congestus

Thuranthos congestus, commonly known as the clustered thuranthos, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are usually a mix of white and pale yellow.

Thuranthos congestus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
