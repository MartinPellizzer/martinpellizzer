Teucrium fruticans f. albiflorum

Teucrium fruticans f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often aromatic, with a mint-like scent, and it is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
