Tropidia corymbioides

Tropidia corymbioides, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in dense corymbs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Tropidia corymbioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
