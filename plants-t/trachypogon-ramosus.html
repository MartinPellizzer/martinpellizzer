Trachypogon ramosus

Trachypogon ramosus, commonly known as the hairy broomgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch profusely. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trachypogon ramosus is adapted to dry and disturbed environments, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
