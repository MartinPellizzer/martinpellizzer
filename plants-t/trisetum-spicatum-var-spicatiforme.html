Trisetum spicatum var. spicatiforme

Trisetum spicatum var. spicatiforme, commonly known as the spicatiforme false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Poaceae family, though it is often confused with other similar grass species.
