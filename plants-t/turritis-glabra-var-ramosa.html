Turritis glabra var. ramosa

Turritis glabra var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy turritis, is a variant of the turritis genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with a pale greenish-white color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
