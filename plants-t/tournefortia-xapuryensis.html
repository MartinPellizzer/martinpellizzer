Tournefortia xapuryensis

Tournefortia xapuryensis, commonly known as the Xapury tamarind, is a flowering plant species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Tournefortia xapuryensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
