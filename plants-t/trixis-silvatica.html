Trixis silvatica

Trixis silvatica, commonly known as the forest trixis, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Trixis silvatica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
