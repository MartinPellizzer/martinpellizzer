Tetraplasandra hawaiensis var. microcarpa

Tetraplasandra hawaiensis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf hawaiian hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
