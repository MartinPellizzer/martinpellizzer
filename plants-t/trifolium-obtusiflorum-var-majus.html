Trifolium obtusiflorum var. majus

Trifolium obtusiflorum var. majus, commonly known as the broad-flowered clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a rounded base. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in agricultural systems as a forage crop.
