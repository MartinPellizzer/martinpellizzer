Tabernaemontana trialata

Tabernaemontana trialata, commonly known as the sweetshrub or white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
