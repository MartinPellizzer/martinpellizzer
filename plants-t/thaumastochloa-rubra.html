Thaumastochloa rubra

Thaumastochloa rubra, commonly known as the red leaved grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish pigmentation in its leaves, which is due to the presence of anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences are typical of the grass family.

Thaumastochloa rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
