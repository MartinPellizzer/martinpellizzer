Trichogonia campestris

Trichogonia campestris, commonly known as the field trichogonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Trichogonia campestris is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate regions.
