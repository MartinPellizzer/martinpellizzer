Tecticornia leptoclada subsp. inclusa

Tecticornia leptoclada subsp.

inclusa, commonly known as the slender tecticornia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal dunes and salt-affected soils, showcasing its tolerance to saline environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
