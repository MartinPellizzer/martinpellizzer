Tephrosia aequilata subsp. namuliana

Tephrosia aequilata subsp.

namuliana, commonly known as the Namuli tephrosia, is a shrub native to the montane regions of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
