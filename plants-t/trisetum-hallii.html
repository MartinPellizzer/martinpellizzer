Trisetum hallii

Trisetum hallii, commonly known as Hall's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Trisetum hallii is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
