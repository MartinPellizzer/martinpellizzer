Thymus longiflorus var. ciliatus

Thymus longiflorus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the fringed thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs, and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping.

Additionally, it is often used in culinary applications due to its fragrant, lemony scent.
