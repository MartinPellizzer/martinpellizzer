Tulipa martelliana

Tulipa martelliana, commonly known as the Martell's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

The tulip is also valued for its historical significance in the tulipomania period of the 17th century.
