Triticum rottboellioides

Triticum rottboellioides, commonly known as the Rottboell's wheat, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
