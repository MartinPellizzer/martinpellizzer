Taraxacum alii

Taraxacum alii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

Taraxacum alii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
