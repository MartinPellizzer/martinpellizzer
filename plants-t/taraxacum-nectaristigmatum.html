Taraxacum nectaristigmatum

Taraxacum nectaristigmatum, commonly known as the nectaristigmatum dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower heads, which consist of numerous tiny florets arranged in a circular pattern. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem with a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in fine hairs.

This species is native to certain regions of Europe and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats.
