Trifolium boryanum

Trifolium boryanum, commonly known as the Boryan clover, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Trifolium boryanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
