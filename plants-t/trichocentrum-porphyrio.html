Trichocentrum porphyrio

Trichocentrum porphyrio, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a deep purple or maroon hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. Trichocentrum porphyrio thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
