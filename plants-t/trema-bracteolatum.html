Trema bracteolatum

Trema bracteolatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed trema, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Trema bracteolatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
