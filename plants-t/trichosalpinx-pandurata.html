Trichosalpinx pandurata

Trichosalpinx pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Trichosalpinx pandurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
