Tradescantia vaga

Tradescantia vaga, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features strikingly colorful, showy flowers that range from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green, and it produces small, three-lobed seed capsules that disperse its seeds easily. Tradescantia vaga is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance for partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native landscapes.
