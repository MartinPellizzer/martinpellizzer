Thaumatophyllum lundii

Thaumatophyllum lundii, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Thaumatophyllum lundii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant and unusual appearance.
