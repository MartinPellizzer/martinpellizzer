Trifolium spananthum

Trifolium spananthum, commonly known as the Spanish clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open fields.

Trifolium spananthum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
