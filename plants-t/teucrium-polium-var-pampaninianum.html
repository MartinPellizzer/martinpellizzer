Teucrium polium var. pampaninianum

Teucrium polium var. pampaninianum, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in scrublands and woodland edges. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like scent, which is a common trait among members of its family.
