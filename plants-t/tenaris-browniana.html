Tenaris browniana

Tenaris browniana, commonly known as the Brown's tenaris, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and narrow, leathery leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Tenaris browniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
