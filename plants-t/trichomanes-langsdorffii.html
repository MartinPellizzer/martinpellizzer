Trichomanes langsdorffii

Trichomanes langsdorffii, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and simple, linear leaves that are often covered with small, glandular dots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in small, rounded clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Trichomanes langsdorffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
