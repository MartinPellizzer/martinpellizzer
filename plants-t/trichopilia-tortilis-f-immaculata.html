Trichopilia tortilis f. immaculata

Trichopilia tortilis f. immaculata, commonly known as the white-flowered trichopilia, is a rare and striking variant of the Trichopilia genus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.
