Teucrium capitatum var. gracillimum

Teucrium capitatum var. gracillimum, commonly known as slender tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological plantings.
