Trigonochilum articulatum

Trigonochilum articulatum, commonly known as the segmented orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, segmented stems and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.
