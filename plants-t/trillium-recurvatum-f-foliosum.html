Trillium recurvatum f. foliosum

Trillium recurvatum f. foliosum, commonly known as the foliosum form of the white trillium, is a rare and distinctive variant of the trillium genus.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring broader, more elongated leaves compared to the typical species. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, typical of the trillium family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and delicate component of its native ecosystems.
