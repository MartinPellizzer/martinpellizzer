Tapirira pao-pombo

Tapirira pao-pombo, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers, which are rich in starch, are a traditional food source in some indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
