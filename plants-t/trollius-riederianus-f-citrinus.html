Trollius riederianus f. citrinus

Trollius riederianus f. citrinus, commonly known as the citron yellow buttercup, is a rare and colorful variant of the buttercup family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically more vibrant than those of the typical species. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
