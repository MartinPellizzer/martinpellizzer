Teucrium townsendii

Teucrium townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
