Trichosanthes thwaitesii

Trichosanthes thwaitesii, commonly known as the Chinese lantern gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces vibrant orange, balloon-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. Its roots and stems are also utilized in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, showy fruit morphology.
