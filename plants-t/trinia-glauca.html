Trinia glauca

Trinia glauca, commonly known as the blue trinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Trinia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
