Tanacetum delavayi

Tanacetum delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
