Trachypogon pubescens

Trachypogon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, tufted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Trachypogon pubescens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
