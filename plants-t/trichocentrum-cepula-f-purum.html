Trichocentrum cepula f. purum

Trichocentrum cepula f. purum, commonly known as the white-flowered phalaenopsis.

This orchid species is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescences bearing white flowers with a subtle greenish tinge. It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating.

It is relatively easy to care for, requiring bright indirect light and consistent moisture.
