Teucrium chardonianum

Teucrium chardonianum, commonly known as the Italian germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in spike-like clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
