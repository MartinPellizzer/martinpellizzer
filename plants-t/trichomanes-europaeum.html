Trichomanes europaeum

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are often covered in a waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors, where it can maintain the humidity it requires. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and contain spores for propagation.

Trichomanes europaeum plays a minor but ecologically significant role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for small organisms.
