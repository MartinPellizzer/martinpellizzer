Tricoryne caulescens

Tricoryne caulescens, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive lip.

Tricoryne caulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
