Taraxacum triquetrum

Taraxacum triquetrum, commonly known as the three-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum triquetrum is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal and ecological significance.
