Teucrium pseudochamaepitys var. nissolianum

Teucrium pseudochamaepitys var. nissolianum, commonly known as the Nissoliana germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
