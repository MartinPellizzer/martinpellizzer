Tecoma salzmannii

Tecoma salzmannii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma salzmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
