Toona ciliata var. velutina

Toona ciliata var. velutina, commonly known as velvet tallowwood, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey to black bark that often has a velvety texture. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in panicles, and the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable timber used in construction and furniture making.
