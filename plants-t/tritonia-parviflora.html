Tritonia parviflora

Tritonia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tritonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous root system. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Tritonia parviflora thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
