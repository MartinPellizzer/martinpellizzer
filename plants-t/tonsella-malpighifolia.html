Tonsella malpighifolia

Tonsella malpighifolia, commonly known as the Malpighiopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Tonsella malpighifolia is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
