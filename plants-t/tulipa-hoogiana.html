Tulipa hoogiana

Tulipa hoogiana, commonly known as the Hoogian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces showy, single or double blooms that typically appear in late spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Tulipa hoogiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
