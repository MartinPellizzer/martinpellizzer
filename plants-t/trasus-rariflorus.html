Trasus rariflorus

Trasus rariflorus, commonly known as the rare flower of the misty valleys, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and delicate, elongated leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom only during the brief summer months, making it a rare and elusive species. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its fragile floral display.

Due to its limited habitat and low reproductive rate, Trasus rariflorus is considered endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
