Tsuga douglasii var. glauca

Tsuga douglasii var. glauca, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The tree has a conical shape with long, needle-like leaves that are usually arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks.
