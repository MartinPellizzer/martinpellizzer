Thornbera albiflora

Thornbera albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower thornbera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, spiky leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Due to its unique adaptations, Thornbera albiflora is often used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
