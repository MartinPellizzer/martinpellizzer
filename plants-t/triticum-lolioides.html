Triticum lolioides

Triticum lolioides, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Triticum lolioides is often considered a weed in agricultural fields but has historical significance in early grain cultivation.
