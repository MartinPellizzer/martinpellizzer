Trifolium eriocephalum f. arcuatum

Trifolium eriocephalum f. arcuatum, commonly known as the curlleaf white clover, is a botanical variant of the white clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved leaflets and dense, fluffy flower heads. This plant is native to western North America and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its small, white flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems.
