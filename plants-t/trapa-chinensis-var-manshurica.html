Trapa chinensis var. manshurica

Trapa chinensis var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive spiny fruits that encase the seeds. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and the fruits develop underwater, often remaining submerged until maturity.

The species is noted for its ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
