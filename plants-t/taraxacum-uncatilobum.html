Taraxacum uncatilobum

Taraxacum uncatilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum uncatilobum is a perennial herb that is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
