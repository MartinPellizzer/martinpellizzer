Thyopsis graveolens

Thyopsis graveolens, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal remedies.
