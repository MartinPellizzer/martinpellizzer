Thamnochortus consanguineus

Thamnochortus consanguineus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces tall, upright spikes of bright red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vivid color and architectural form make it a notable addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
