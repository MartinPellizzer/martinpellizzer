Thymus zygioides var. longedentatus

Thymus zygioides var. longedentatus, commonly known as the long-toothed thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive elongated, serrated margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
