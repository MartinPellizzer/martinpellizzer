Taraxacum subcalanthodium

Taraxacum subcalanthodium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
