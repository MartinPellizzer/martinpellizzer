Thamnochortus imbricatus var. stenopterus

Thamnochortus imbricatus var. stenopterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver grass, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers in summer, which are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and drought tolerance.
