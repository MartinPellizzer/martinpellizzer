Turnera guianensis

Turnera guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Turnera guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
