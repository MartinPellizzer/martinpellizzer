Thalictrum delavayi f. appendiculatum

Thalictrum delavayi f. appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are usually produced in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies appendiculatum is distinguished by its elongated leaf stalks and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical form of T. delavayi.
