Trollius pumilus

Trollius pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf globeflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of 5 to 8. The plant has hairy, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountain meadows and tundra environments.

Trollius pumilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
