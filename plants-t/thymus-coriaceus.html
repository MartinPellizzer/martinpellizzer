Thymus coriaceus

Thymus coriaceus, commonly known as caraway thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their essential oils and aromatic properties.

Thymus coriaceus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
