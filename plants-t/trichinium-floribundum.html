Trichinium floribundum

Trichinium floribundum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Trichinium floribundum is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
