Tragacantha bounacantha

Tragacantha bounacantha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Iran and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a latex sap, which is harvested for its medicinal and industrial uses. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
