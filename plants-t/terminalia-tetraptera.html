Terminalia tetraptera

Terminalia tetraptera, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its thick, ridged bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. The seeds are rich in oils and are the primary source of shea butter, which has various industrial and cosmetic applications.

It plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
