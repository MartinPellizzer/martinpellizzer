Taraxacum laevigatum

Taraxacum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, elongated leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which develop into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
