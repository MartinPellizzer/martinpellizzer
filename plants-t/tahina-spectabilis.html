Tahina spectabilis

Tahina spectabilis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring, giving it its common name. Its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that contain several seeds.

Tahina spectabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
