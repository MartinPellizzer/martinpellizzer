Taraxacum admordum

Taraxacum admordum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal.

Taraxacum admordum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
