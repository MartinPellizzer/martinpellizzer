Thunbergia chiovendae

Thunbergia chiovendae, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
