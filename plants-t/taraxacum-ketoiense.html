Taraxacum ketoiense

Taraxacum ketoiense, commonly known as the Korean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Korea and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that is similar to other dandelion species, containing latex compounds. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Taraxacum ketoiense is often found growing in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
