Taxus communis fastigiata-variegata

Taxus communis fastigiata-variegata, commonly known as the variegated upright yew, is a cultivar of the yew tree characterized by its compact, columnar growth habit.

It features dense, dark green foliage with striking white or yellow variegation, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.

This variety is valued for its year-round visual interest and hardy nature in temperate climates.
