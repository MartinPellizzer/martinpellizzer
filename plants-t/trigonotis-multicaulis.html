Trigonotis multicaulis

Trigonotis multicaulis, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Trigonotis multicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
