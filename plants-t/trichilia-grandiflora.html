Trichilia grandiflora

Trichilia grandiflora, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, heartwood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Trichilia grandiflora is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
