Tetraena fontanesii

Tetraena fontanesii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Tetraena fontanesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
