Tontelea floribunda

Tontelea floribunda, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Tontelea floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
