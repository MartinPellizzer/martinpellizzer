Tradescantia collina

Tradescantia collina, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that help it climb or trail along surfaces. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Tradescantia collina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
