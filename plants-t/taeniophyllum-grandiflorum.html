Taeniophyllum grandiflorum

Taeniophyllum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant feather plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps in collecting rainwater. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often in a spadix-like arrangement.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique foliage.
