Tetracellion ellipsoideum

Tetracellion ellipsoideum, commonly known as the ellipsoidal diatom, is a unicellular algae belonging to the class Bacillariophyceae.

It is characterized by its intricate silica cell wall, known as a frustule, which is composed of two overlapping halves. The frustule of Tetracellion ellipsoideum typically exhibits a symmetrical, ellipsoidal shape with finely structured patterns. It is commonly found in freshwater environments, such as lakes and rivers, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

This diatom is often used in paleolimnological studies to infer past environmental conditions.
