Taraxacum hypimbricans

Taraxacum hypimbricans, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of the southern United States, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowering heads consist of yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets, typical of the dandelion genus.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a sticky, milky sap, which is characteristic of many members of the genus Taraxacum.
