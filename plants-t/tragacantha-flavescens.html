Tragacantha flavescens

Tragacantha flavescens, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods are elongated, containing several seeds.

Tragacantha flavescens thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
