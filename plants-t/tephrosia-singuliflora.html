Tephrosia singuliflora

Tephrosia singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flowered tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, elliptical seeds.

Tephrosia singuliflora is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
