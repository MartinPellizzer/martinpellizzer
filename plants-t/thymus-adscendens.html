Thymus adscendens

Thymus adscendens, commonly known as ascending thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine, valued for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.
