Tragacantha fabacea

Tragacantha fabacea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable gum resin, which has been used for centuries in various traditional and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
