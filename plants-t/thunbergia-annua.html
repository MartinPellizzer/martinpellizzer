Thunbergia annua

Thunbergia annua, commonly known as the annual blue morning glory, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
