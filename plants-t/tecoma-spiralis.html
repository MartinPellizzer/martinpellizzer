Tecoma spiralis

Tecoma spiralis, commonly known as yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma spiralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
