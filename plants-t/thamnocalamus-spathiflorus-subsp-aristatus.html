Thamnocalamus spathiflorus subsp. aristatus

Thamnocalamus spathiflorus subsp.

aristatus, commonly known as the aristate bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are subtended by leaf-like bracts.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
