Thryptomene tenella

Thryptomene tenella, commonly known as narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in well-drained soils.

Thryptomene tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
