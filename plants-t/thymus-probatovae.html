Thymus probatovae

Thymus probatovae, commonly known as the Crimean thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymus probatovae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
