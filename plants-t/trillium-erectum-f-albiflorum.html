Trillium erectum f. albiflorum

Trillium erectum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered red trillium, is a rare and striking variant of the red trillium.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers, which contrast with its deep red stem and leaves. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in shaded, moist woodlands. Its foliage consists of three large, heart-shaped leaves that emerge before the flowers.

The plant is known for its unique floral structure, with three sepals that resemble petals and three stamens that are prominently displayed.
