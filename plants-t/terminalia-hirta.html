Terminalia hirta

Terminalia hirta, commonly known as the African sand apple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, yellow, fleshy fruits that are often consumed raw.

Terminalia hirta is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
