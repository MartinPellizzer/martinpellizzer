Thymus callieri subsp. urumovii

Thymus callieri subsp.

urumovii, commonly known as the Urumov's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
