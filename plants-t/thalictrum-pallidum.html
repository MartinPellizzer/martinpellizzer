Thalictrum pallidum

Thalictrum pallidum, commonly known as the pale meadow-rue, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
