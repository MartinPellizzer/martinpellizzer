Taraxacum chrysostylum

Taraxacum chrysostylum, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the familiar fluffy seed heads. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to propagate through both sexual and asexual means, contributing to its widespread distribution.
