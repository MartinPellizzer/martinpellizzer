Tilia × mohacsyana

Tilia × mohacsyana, commonly known as the Mohács lime, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, characterized by its ornamental value and unique floral features.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and produce a fragrant, yellowish-green color that attracts pollinators. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its hybrid origin and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
