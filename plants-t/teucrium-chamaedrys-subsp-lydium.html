Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. lydium

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

lydium, commonly known as Lydian germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a hardy and ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
