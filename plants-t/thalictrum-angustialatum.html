Thalictrum angustialatum

Thalictrum angustialatum, commonly known as narrowleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces showy, clustered racemes of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum angustialatum is often found in woodland or meadow habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
