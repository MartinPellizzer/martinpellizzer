Tetraneuris formosa

Tetraneuris formosa, commonly known as the showy tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Tetraneuris formosa is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
