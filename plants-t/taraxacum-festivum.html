Taraxacum festivum

Taraxacum festivum, commonly known as the festive dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a distinctive flowering head composed of numerous small yellow florets. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports the flower and later develops into a white, puffed seed head. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Taraxacum festivum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
