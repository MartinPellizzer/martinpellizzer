Tragacantha laxa

Tragacantha laxa, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its distinctive feature is the production of a yellow resinous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications. Tragacantha laxa is also known for its medicinal properties, with the gum being used in pharmaceutical and cosmetic products.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
