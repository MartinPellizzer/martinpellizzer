Tragus paucispinus

Tragus paucispinus, commonly known as the slender tragus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by narrow, bract-like leaves that give it its common name.

Tragus paucispinus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a ornamental plant.
