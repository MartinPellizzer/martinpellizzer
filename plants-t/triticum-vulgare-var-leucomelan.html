Triticum vulgare var. leucomelan

Triticum vulgare var. leucomelan, commonly known as white wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its lighter color and lower protein content compared to other wheat varieties.

This plant is an annual grass with tall, slender stalks and spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue. The grains are usually pale yellow to white, which contributes to its name.

It is primarily cultivated for its flour, which is used in a variety of food products.
