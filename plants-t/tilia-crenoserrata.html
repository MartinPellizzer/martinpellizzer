Tilia crenoserrata

Tilia crenoserrata, commonly known as the serrated-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia crenoserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
