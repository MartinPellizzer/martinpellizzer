Taraxacum infucatulum

Taraxacum infucatulum, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early summer. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance and its classification within the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
