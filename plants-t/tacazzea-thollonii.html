Tacazzea thollonii

Tacazzea thollonii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant purple or violet hue with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphytic orchid, often found attached to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Tacazzea thollonii is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
