Taraxacum trigonophorum

Taraxacum trigonophorum, commonly known as the triangular-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, triangular leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
