Tapinanthus holtzii

Tapinanthus holtzii, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a flowering plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color. Its roots are specialized to attach to host plants, typically members of the Fabaceae family.

Tapinanthus holtzii is native to arid regions of Africa and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
