Taeniophyllum filiferum

Taeniophyllum filiferum, commonly known as the threadleaf fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble threads, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing clusters, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Taeniophyllum filiferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
