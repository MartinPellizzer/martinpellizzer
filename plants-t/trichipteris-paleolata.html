Trichipteris paleolata

Trichipteris paleolata, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Paleozoic era.

It belongs to the family Trichipteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, segmented fronds. The plant's structure suggests it was adapted to swampy, low-light environments. Fossil records indicate it played a significant role in early forest ecosystems.

Its study provides valuable insights into the evolution of early vascular plants.
