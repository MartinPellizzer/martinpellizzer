Trigynaea canescens

Trigynaea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse seeds by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Trigynaea canescens has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and drought-resistant features.
