Thymus praecox subsp. penyalarensis

Thymus praecox subsp.

penyalarensis, commonly known as the Penyalarensis thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers.

The plant typically features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
