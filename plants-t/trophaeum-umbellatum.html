Trophaeum umbellatum

Trophaeum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, clustered blooms.
