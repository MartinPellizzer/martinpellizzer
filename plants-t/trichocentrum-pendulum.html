Trichocentrum pendulum

Trichocentrum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features long, pendulous stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Trichocentrum pendulum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique, cascading growth habit.
