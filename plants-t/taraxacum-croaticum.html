Taraxacum croaticum

Taraxacum croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe, particularly Croatia, and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Taraxacum croaticum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
