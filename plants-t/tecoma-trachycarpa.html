Tecoma trachycarpa

Tecoma trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded glorybower, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has rough, fissured bark and pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Tecoma trachycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
