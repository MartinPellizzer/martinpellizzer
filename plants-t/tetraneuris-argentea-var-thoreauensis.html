Tetraneuris argentea var. thoreauensis

Tetraneuris argentea var. thoreauensis, commonly known as the Thoreau's silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, herbaceous leaves covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is named in honor of the naturalist Henry David Thoreau, reflecting its ecological significance in native plant communities.
