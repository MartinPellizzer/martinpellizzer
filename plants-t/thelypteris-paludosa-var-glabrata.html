Thelypteris paludosa var. glabrata

Thelypteris paludosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth wood fern, is a variant of the common wood fern native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and along stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a layer of fine, scale-like structures. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically arranged in a single row along the margins of the fronds.

This variant is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous (hairless) appearance compared to other varieties of Thelypteris paludosa.
