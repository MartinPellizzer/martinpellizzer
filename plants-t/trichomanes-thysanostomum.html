Trichomanes thysanostomum

Trichomanes thysanostomum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green and covered with a thin, waxy layer. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from small, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The plant's intricate structure and ability to retain moisture make it well adapted to its humid, low-light habitat.
