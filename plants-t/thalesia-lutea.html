Thalesia lutea

Thalesia lutea, commonly known as the golden thalesia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically broad and glossy, providing a lush appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Thalesia lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
