Teucrium scorodonia subsp. euganeum

Teucrium scorodonia subsp.

euganeum, commonly known as the Mediterranean germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
