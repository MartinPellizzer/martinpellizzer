Tarachia similis

Tarachia similis, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle floral display to its desert habitat. Its root system is extensive and deep, enabling it to access groundwater during dry periods.

Tarachia similis plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
