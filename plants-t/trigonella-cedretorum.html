Trigonella cedretorum

Trigonella cedretorum, commonly known as Cedretum trigonella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
