Tulotis parviflora

Tulotis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tulip, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, single or double flowers with petals that range from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tulotis parviflora is valued for its early spring blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
