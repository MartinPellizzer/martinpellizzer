Tripogon major subsp. deflexus

Tripogon major subsp.

deflexus, commonly known as the bent-stemmed tussock grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, often curved stems.

The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that contain numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native habitat.
