Teucrium flavescens

Teucrium flavescens, commonly known as yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from yellow to reddish-purple, depending on the variety.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
