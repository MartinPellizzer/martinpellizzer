Trichopyrum scythicum

Trichopyrum scythicum, commonly known as the Scythian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, steppes, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheath that wraps around the stem.

Trichopyrum scythicum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
