Terpsichore cultrata

Terpsichore cultrata, commonly known as the sword-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome with high biodiversity.

Terpsichore cultrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
