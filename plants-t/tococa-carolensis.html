Tococa carolensis

Tococa carolensis, commonly known as the Carolinian tococa, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Due to habitat loss, Tococa carolensis is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
