Trifolium medium var. angustium

Trifolium medium var. angustium, commonly known as narrow-leaf clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural and ecological purposes.
