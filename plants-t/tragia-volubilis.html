Tragia volubilis

Tragia volubilis, commonly known as the creeping tragia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing or climbing shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Tragia volubilis is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
