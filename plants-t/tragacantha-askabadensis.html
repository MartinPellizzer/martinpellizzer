Tragacantha askabadensis

Tragacantha askabadensis, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are known for producing a gelatinous substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and water-conserving mechanisms.
