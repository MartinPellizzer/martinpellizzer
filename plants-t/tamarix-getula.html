Tamarix getula

Tamarix getula, commonly known as the salt cedar or tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, pink to reddish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and saline environments. Its wood is lightweight and resistant to decay, making it useful for various applications.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
