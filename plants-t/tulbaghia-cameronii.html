Tulbaghia cameronii

Tulbaghia cameronii, commonly known as the Cape tulbaghia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

Tulbaghia cameronii is also known for its mild, garlic-like scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed.
