Thalictrum bauhini var. procerulum

Thalictrum bauhini var. procerulum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream sides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features delicate, compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
