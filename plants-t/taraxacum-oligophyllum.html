Taraxacum oligophyllum

Taraxacum oligophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Taraxacum oligophyllum is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soils and full sun.
