Trifolium laevigatum var. raquiticum

Trifolium laevigatum var. raquiticum, commonly known as the red clover variant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the clover genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological contexts.
