Trembleya purpurascens

Trembleya purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered trembleya, is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
