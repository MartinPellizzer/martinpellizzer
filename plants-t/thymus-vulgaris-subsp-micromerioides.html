Thymus vulgaris subsp. micromerioides

Thymus vulgaris subsp.

micromerioides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, tiny, needle-like leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems.

The plant produces small, pink to lavender flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
