Tabernaemontana dipladeniiflora

Tabernaemontana dipladeniiflora, commonly known as the white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent, especially at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.
