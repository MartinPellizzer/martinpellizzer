Toddalia asiatica var. gracilis

Toddalia asiatica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender tamarind.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
