Trigonella besseriana

Trigonella besseriana, commonly known as the Persian chickpea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Trigonella besseriana is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
