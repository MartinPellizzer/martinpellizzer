Thysanocarpus curvipes subsp. madocarpus

Thysanocarpus curvipes subsp.

madocarpus, commonly known as the Madocarpus thysanocarpus, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, woody fruits that are distinctive in shape.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its hardy nature and year-round foliage.

The subspecies is relatively rare, making it a sought-after plant for horticultural collections.
