Triticum carthlicum var. narpual

Triticum carthlicum var. narpual, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wheat, is a botanical variant of wheat characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescence.

It is native to the temperate regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. Its grains are small and hard, often used in traditional baking and as a source of flour.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various climatic conditions and its role in agricultural biodiversity.
