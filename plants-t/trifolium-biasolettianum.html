Trifolium biasolettianum

Trifolium biasolettianum, commonly known as the Italian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a single stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium biasolettianum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural and ecological purposes.
