Tropaeolum flavipilum

Tropaeolum flavipilum, commonly known as the yellow-haired nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its edible leaves and flowers add both aesthetic and culinary appeal to the landscape.
