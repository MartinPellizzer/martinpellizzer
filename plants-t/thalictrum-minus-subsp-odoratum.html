Thalictrum minus subsp. odoratum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

odoratum, commonly known as the sweet-scented meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced floral scent compared to other members of the species.
