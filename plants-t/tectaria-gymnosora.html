Tectaria gymnosora

Tectaria gymnosora, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant's leaves are typically dark green and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, which help in moisture retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Tectaria gymnosora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
