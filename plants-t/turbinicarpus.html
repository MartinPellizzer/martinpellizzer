Turbinicarpus

Turbinicarpus, commonly known as the desert rose or sun rose, is a genus of flowering plants native to the deserts of North America.

These succulent plants are characterized by their fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical or turban-shaped leaves. They produce showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. Turbinicarpus species are adapted to arid environments, storing water in their thick leaves and stems.

They are popular among cactus and succulent enthusiasts due to their striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
