Trigonella bactriana

Trigonella bactriana, commonly known as the Afghan fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Trigonella bactriana is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of nutritional seeds.
