Trillium sessile var. angustipetalum

Trillium sessile var. angustipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled white trillium, is a rare and delicate woodland plant native to the eastern United States.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are typically white or pale green, with a waxy surface. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as rich forest floors and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is often considered a conservation concern.
