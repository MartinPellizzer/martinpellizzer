Trillium erectum var. cahnae

Trillium erectum var. cahnae, commonly known as the Cahna's red trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features three elliptical, reddish-purple leaves that are often tinged with green, and it produces a single, erect, bell-shaped flower with three petals. The flower is typically pink to reddish in color and blooms in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded forest floors and is characterized by its unique morphology and seasonal blooming pattern.

It is considered a rare and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
