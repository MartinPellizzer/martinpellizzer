Triticum villosum var. barbulatum

Triticum villosum var. barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
