Thymus algeriensis var. cinerascens

Thymus algeriensis var. cinerascens, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

The varietal name 'cinerascens' refers to its ash-gray colored stems, a distinguishing feature of this variety.
