Trichosanthes quinquefolia

Trichosanthes quinquefolia, commonly known as the giant pumpkin or horse pumpkin, is a vigorous trailing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can weigh several kilograms when mature. These fruits are typically harvested in the fall and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its rapid growth and sprawling habit make it a notable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
