Tragacantha macropetala

Tragacantha macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a sticky resin, similar to other members of its genus, which has been used historically in various traditional applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
