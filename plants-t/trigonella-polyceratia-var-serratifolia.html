Trigonella polyceratia var. serratifolia

Trigonella polyceratia var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated fenugreek, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a serrated edge, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is related to other members of the Fabaceae family, known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities and medicinal uses.
