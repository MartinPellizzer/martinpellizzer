Turbinicarpus bonatzii

Turbinicarpus bonatzii, commonly known as the Bonatzii's turbinaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are often borne on short, lateral stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Turbinicarpus bonatzii is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
