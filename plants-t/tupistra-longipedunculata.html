Tupistra longipedunculata

Tupistra longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed tufted grass, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

Tupistra longipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in arid environments.
