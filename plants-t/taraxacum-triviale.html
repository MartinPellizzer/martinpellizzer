Taraxacum triviale

Taraxacum triviale, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, elongated flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which develop into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to full sun to partial shade.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
