Taraxacum dispar

Taraxacum dispar, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds with feathery parachutes, aiding in wind dispersal. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
