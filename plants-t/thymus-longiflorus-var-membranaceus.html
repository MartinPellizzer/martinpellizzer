Thymus longiflorus var. membranaceus

Thymus longiflorus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
