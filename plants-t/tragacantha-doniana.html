Tragacantha doniana

Tragacantha doniana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, elongated pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various industrial and medicinal applications.
