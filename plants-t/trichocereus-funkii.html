Trichocereus funkii

Trichocereus funkii, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit, producing large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly its vibrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming behavior, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
