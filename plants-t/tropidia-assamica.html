Tropidia assamica

Tropidia assamica, commonly known as the Assam orchid, is a species of orchid native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree trunks or branches.

Tropidia assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
