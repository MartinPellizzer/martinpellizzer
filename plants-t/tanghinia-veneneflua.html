Tanghinia veneneflua

Tanghinia veneneflua, commonly known as the poison vine, is a climbing plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces toxic berries that contain harmful alkaloids, making it dangerous to humans and animals. Its roots and stems also contain toxic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine with caution.

Due to its potent toxicity, it is often avoided in both ecological and horticultural settings.
