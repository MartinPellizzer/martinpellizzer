Thalictrum occidentale var. megacarpum

Thalictrum occidentale var. megacarpum, commonly known as the western meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
