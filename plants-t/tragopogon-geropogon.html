Tragopogon geropogon

Tragopogon geropogon, commonly known as the yellow goosgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant produces slender, yellowish to reddish stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tragopogon geropogon is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.
