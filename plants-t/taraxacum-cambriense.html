Taraxacum cambriense

Taraxacum cambriense, commonly known as the Welsh dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. It produces a single, bright yellow flower that develops into a fluffy, white seed head.

Taraxacum cambriense is often found in grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates.
