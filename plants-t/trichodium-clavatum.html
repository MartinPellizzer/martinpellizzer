Trichodium clavatum

Trichodium clavatum, commonly known as the clustered trichodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trichodium clavatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
