Taraxacum taraxacum var. scopulorum

Taraxacum taraxacum var. scopulorum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow, composite flower head. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
