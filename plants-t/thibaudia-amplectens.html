Thibaudia amplectens

Thibaudia amplectens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thibaudia amplectens is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
