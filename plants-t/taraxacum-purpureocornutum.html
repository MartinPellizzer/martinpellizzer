Taraxacum purpureocornutum

Taraxacum purpureocornutum, commonly known as the purple-horned puffball, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, purple bracts that surround the spherical fruiting body. The plant produces a single, large, white or pale yellow flower that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.
