Tradescantia holosericea var. purpusiana

Tradescantia holosericea var. purpusiana, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple tint along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers on slender stalks during the growing season. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
