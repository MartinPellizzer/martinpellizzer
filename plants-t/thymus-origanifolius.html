Thymus origanifolius

Thymus origanifolius, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, slender stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that emit a citrusy scent when crushed. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications for its distinctive lemony fragrance and flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like oregano and thyme.
