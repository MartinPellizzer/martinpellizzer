Tanacetum × morifolium

Tanacetum × morifolium, commonly known as chamomile, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk floret. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features finely dissected, aromatic leaves. Chamomile is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its essential oil, extracted from the flowers, is used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
