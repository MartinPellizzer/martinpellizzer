Thelypteris sulawesiensis

Thelypteris sulawesiensis, commonly known as the Sulawesi shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the island of Sulawesi in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are leathery and have a deep green color with a serrated margin.

Thelypteris sulawesiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
