Taraxacum cochleatum

Taraxacum cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive spiral arrangement. The plant produces a hollow, cylindrical stem that supports a single, fluffy capitulum composed of numerous yellow florets. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in the spring, followed by the formation of a white, spherical seed head.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
