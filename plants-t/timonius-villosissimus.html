Timonius villosissimus

Timonius villosissimus, commonly known as the silky timonius, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silky white hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of green and white, contributing to its subtle beauty.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Timonius villosissimus is considered a plant of botanical interest and conservation concern.
