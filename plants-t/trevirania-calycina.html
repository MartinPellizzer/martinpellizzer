Trevirania calycina

Trevirania calycina, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Trevirania calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
