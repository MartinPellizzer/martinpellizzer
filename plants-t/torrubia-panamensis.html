Torrubia panamensis

Torrubia panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian torrubia, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are evergreen, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by berries that are edible for certain wildlife species.
