Tragacantha maculata

Tragacantha maculata, commonly known as the spotted tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its distinctive feature is the presence of dark spots on the leaves, giving it its common name.

Tragacantha maculata is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as an ornamental plant.
