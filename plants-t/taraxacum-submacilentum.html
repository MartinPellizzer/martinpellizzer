Taraxacum submacilentum

Taraxacum submacilentum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
