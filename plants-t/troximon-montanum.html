Troximon montanum

Troximon montanum, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, showy purple-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
