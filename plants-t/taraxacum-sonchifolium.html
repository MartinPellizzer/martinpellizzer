Taraxacum sonchifolium

Taraxacum sonchifolium, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that bears a single flowering head. The flowers are yellow and typically bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic seed dispersal via a pappus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
