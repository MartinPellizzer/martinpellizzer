Turnera humboldtii

Turnera humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The flowers are often borne in clusters and are known for their fragrance, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
