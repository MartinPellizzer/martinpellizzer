Trichipteris ulei

Trichipteris ulei, commonly known as the silky fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a medium-sized fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Trichipteris ulei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
