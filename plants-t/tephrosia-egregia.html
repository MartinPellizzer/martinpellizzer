Tephrosia egregia

Tephrosia egregia, commonly known as the blue tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia egregia is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
