Taraxacum pseudoretroflexum

Taraxacum pseudoretroflexum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
