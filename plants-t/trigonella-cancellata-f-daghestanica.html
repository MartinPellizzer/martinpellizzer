Trigonella cancellata f. daghestanica

Trigonella cancellata f. daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan chickpea, is a botanical variant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
