Trachyrhachis barbellata

Trachyrhachis barbellata, commonly known as the barbellated grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive barbed awns. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in grasslands and open areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Trachyrhachis barbellata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
