Thunia cleistogama

Thunia cleistogama, commonly known as the cleistogamous thunia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive strategy, producing closed, self-pollinating flowers that never open. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized reproductive biology and ecological adaptations.
