Tradescantia pinetorum

Tradescantia pinetorum, commonly known as the forest spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tradescantia pinetorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
