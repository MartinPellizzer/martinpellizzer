Torilis africana

Torilis africana, commonly known as African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Torilis africana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
