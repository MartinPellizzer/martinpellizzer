Terminalia brachystemma subsp. sessilifolia

Terminalia brachystemma subsp.

sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved terminalia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its elliptical, sessile leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forest regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
