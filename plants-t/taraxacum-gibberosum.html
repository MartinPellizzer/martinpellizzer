Taraxacum gibberosum

Taraxacum gibberosum, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, typical of the genus Taraxacum.
