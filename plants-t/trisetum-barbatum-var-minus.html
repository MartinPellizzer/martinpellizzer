Trisetum barbatum var. minus

Trisetum barbatum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser hairy beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features fine, hairy leaves that are often covered in short, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Poaceae family.
