Tagetes filiformis

Tagetes filiformis, commonly known as Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange blooms, which are typically tubular in shape. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. Tagetes filiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage is covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
