Tephrosia apollinea subsp. longistipulata

Tephrosia apollinea subsp.

longistipulata, commonly known as the long-stippled tephrosia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
