Tragopogon vaginatus

Tragopogon vaginatus, commonly known as the common goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a unique seed structure known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Tragopogon vaginatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
