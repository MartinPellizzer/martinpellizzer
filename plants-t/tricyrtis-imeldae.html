Tricyrtis imeldae

Tricyrtis imeldae, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its unique, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and possesses showy, spurred flowers with intricate petal structures. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Tricyrtis imeldae is typically grown in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
