Tragacantha tragacanthoides

Tragacantha tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, branching structure. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
