Thalictrum rhynchocarpum subsp. rhynchocarpum

Thalictrum rhynchocarpum subsp.

rhynchocarpum, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow rue, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
