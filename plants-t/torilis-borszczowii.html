Torilis borszczowii

Torilis borszczowii, commonly known as Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Torilis borszczowii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, temperate climates.
