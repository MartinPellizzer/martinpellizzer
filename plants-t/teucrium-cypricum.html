Teucrium cypricum

Teucrium cypricum, commonly known as Cyprus germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a strongly aromatic scent when crushed.

Teucrium cypricum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
