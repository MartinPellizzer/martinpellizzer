Trifolium angustifolium subsp. gibellianum

Trifolium angustifolium subsp.

gibellianum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
