Thottea piscodora

Thottea piscodora, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Thottea, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with showy petals that attract pollinators.

Thottea piscodora plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to surrounding water bodies.
