Topobea insignis subsp. olivacea

Topobea insignis subsp.

olivacea, commonly known as the oliveleaf spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its succulent, glandular leaves that are often tinged with a bluish or purplish hue.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bead-like, orange berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils and in open landscapes.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and has a low-growing, sprawling habit.
