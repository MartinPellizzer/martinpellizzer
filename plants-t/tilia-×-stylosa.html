Tilia × stylosa

Tilia × stylosa, commonly known as the large-leaf lime or European whitebeam, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically heart-shaped at the base and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, brown fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
