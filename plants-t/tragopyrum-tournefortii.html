Tragopyrum tournefortii

Tragopyrum tournefortii, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with reddish or brownish hues, and it flowers from late spring to early summer. Tragopyrum tournefortii is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
