Tubella webbiae

Tubella webbiae, commonly known as the Webby Tubeworm, is a species of marine annelid belonging to the family Scolopariidae.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented body and a distinctive tubular structure that houses its reproductive organs. This polychaete is typically found in sandy or muddy substrates in shallow coastal waters. The species is notable for its translucent body and the presence of specialized setae for burrowing.

Its ecological role includes contributing to sediment turnover and serving as prey for various marine predators.
