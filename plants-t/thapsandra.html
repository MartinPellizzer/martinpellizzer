Thapsandra

Thapsandra, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to various regions of Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties. Thapsandra species typically have evergreen leaves and produce small, yellowish flowers.

The genus includes several species, with Thapsandra tinctura being one of the most notable for its use in traditional medicine and perfumery.
