Tropaeolum polyphyllum

Tropaeolum polyphyllum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum polyphyllum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
