Thalictrum petaloideum var. petaloideum

Thalictrum petaloideum var. petaloideum, commonly known as the showy avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, lily-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in partial to full shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its graceful appearance.
