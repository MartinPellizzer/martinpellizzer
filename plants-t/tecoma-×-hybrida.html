Tecoma × hybrida

Tecoma × hybrida, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tecoma × hybrida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
