Tolpis madagascariensis

Tolpis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Tolpis madagascariensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
