Tarachia laserpitiifolia

Tarachia laserpitiifolia, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring, which are adapted to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its thick, fleshy leaves and stems store water, allowing it to survive in conditions of extreme drought.

Tarachia laserpitiifolia plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
