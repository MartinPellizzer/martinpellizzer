Tectaria triglossa

Tectaria triglossa, commonly known as the broad-leaf shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its fronds are usually divided into multiple segments, with a distinctive pattern of veins that enhance its structural strength.

Tectaria triglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
