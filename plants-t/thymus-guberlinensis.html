Thymus guberlinensis

Thymus guberlinensis, commonly known as the German thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
