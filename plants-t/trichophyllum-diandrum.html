Trichophyllum diandrum

Trichophyllum diandrum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trichophyllum diandrum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
