Talinum caffrum var. carinatum

Talinum caffrum var. carinatum, commonly known as the African spinach or redleaf amaranth, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and green foliage, which is often used in traditional cuisines. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.

Its leaves are edible and can be cooked similarly to spinach.
