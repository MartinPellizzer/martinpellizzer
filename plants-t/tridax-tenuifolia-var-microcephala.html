Tridax tenuifolia var. microcephala

Tridax tenuifolia var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The variety microcephala is distinguished by its smaller flower heads compared to other varieties of Tridax tenuifolia.
