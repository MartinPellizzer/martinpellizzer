Teucrium flavum subsp. glaucum

Teucrium flavum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a distinctive blue or grayish-white bloom of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pale blue to purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
