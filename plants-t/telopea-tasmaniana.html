Telopea tasmaniana

Telopea tasmaniana, commonly known as the Tasmanian waratah, is an evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Tasmania.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures. Its leaves are leathery and lance-shaped, providing a dense, evergreen canopy.

Telopea tasmaniana is well adapted to the cool, moist climate of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
