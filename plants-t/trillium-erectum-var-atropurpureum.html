Trillium erectum var. atropurpureum

Trillium erectum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered wake-robin, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Trillium genus, characterized by its three-part leaves and three petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and produces a single, showy purple or reddish-purple flower. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a short stem with a single flower that blooms in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
