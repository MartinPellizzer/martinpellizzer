Teucrium haenseleri var. rubrovirens

Teucrium haenseleri var. rubrovirens, commonly known as the Red-veined Germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-veining. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
