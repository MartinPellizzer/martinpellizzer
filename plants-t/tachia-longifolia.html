Tachia longifolia

Tachia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved tachia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically elongated and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tachia longifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
