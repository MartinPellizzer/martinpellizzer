Trema strigosum

Trema strigosum, commonly known as the hairy trema, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Trema strigosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
