Teucrium pseudochamaedrys

Teucrium pseudochamaedrys, commonly known as false germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, and it has a compact growth habit.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
