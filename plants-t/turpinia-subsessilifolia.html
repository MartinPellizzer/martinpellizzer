Turpinia subsessilifolia

Turpinia subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved turpinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Turpinia subsessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
