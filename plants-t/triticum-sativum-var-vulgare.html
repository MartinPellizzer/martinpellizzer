Triticum sativum var. vulgare

Triticum sativum var. vulgare, commonly known as common wheat, is a domesticated grass species native to the Fertile Crescent.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear rows of small, hard grains. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its seeds, which are the primary product, are used to produce flour for bread and other baked goods.

The botanical features of this variety make it a staple in global agriculture and a key component of the human diet.
