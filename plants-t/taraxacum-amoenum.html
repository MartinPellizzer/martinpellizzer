Taraxacum amoenum

Taraxacum amoenum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Taraxacum amoenum is often found in grassy areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
