Trifolium striatum var. elongatum

Trifolium striatum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and elongated, giving it its specific variety name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
