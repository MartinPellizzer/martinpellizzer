Tephrosia roseana

Tephrosia roseana, commonly known as the roseanna or pink tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Tephrosia roseana is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
