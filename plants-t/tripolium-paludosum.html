Tripolium paludosum

Tripolium paludosum, commonly known as marsh thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in environments with high humidity. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Tripolium paludosum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
