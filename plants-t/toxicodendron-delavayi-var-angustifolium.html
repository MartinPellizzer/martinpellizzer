Toxicodendron delavayi var. angustifolium

Toxicodendron delavayi var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese sumac, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. It contains urushiol, a toxic resin that can cause skin irritation in humans.

This species is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.
