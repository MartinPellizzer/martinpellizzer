Tabebuia riparia

Tabebuia riparia, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and sometimes red. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped, with a smooth surface.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
