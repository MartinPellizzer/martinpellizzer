Thymus polytrichus var. drucei

Thymus polytrichus var. drucei, commonly known as Druce's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine due to their essential oils and antioxidant properties.
