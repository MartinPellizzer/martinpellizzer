Trungboa

Trungboa, commonly known as the Vietnamese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Averrhoa genus and is characterized by its star-shaped fruit, which turns yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is not only prized for its flavor but also for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamin C and antioxidants.
