Teucrium scordium subsp. scordium

Teucrium scordium subsp.

scordium, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in clusters. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
