Tectaria irregularis

Tectaria irregularis, commonly known as the irregular tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, irregular shape. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Tectaria irregularis is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, showcasing its adaptability to various substrates.
