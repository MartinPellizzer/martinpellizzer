Thapsia Villosa Var. Laciniata

Thapsia villosa var. laciniata is a striking member of the Apiaceae family known for its distinctive, fern-like foliage. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, this plant thrives in rocky and coastal environments. Its pale green leaves are often edged with white hairs, giving it a unique visual texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Despite its beauty, it is considered rare and faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
