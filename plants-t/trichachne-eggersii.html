Trichachne eggersii

Trichachne eggersii, commonly known as Eggers' trichachne, is a grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trichachne eggersii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
