Thymus menitskyi

Thymus menitskyi, commonly known as the Menitsky thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Thymus menitskyi is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
