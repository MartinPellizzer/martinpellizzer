Tragopogon caricifolius

Tragopogon caricifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Tragopogon caricifolius is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
