Trichocentrum flavovirens

Trichocentrum flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a subtle yellowish or golden hue, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive fragrance, and they bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Trichocentrum flavovirens requires well-draining potting mix and consistent moisture to flourish.
