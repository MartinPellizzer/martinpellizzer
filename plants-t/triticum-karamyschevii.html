Triticum karamyschevii

Triticum karamyschevii, commonly known as Karamyschev's wheat, is a wild species of wheat native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and semi-domesticated traits, making it a subject of interest in agricultural research. The plant typically grows in dry, temperate climates and has a tall, slender stem with narrow leaves. Its florets are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, hard grains that are similar to those of common wheat.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is sometimes used in breeding programs to improve the resilience of modern wheat varieties.
