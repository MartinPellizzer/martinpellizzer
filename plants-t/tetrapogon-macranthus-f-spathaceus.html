Tetrapogon macranthus f. spathaceus

Tetrapogon macranthus f. spathaceus, commonly known as the spathaceous tetrapogon, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
