Thouinia paucidentata

Thouinia paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thornapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing some drought resistance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Thouinia paucidentata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
