Tenoria arborescens

Tenoria arborescens, commonly known as the tree tenoria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, woody structure and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
