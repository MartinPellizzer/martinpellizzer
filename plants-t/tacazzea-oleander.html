Tacazzea oleander

Tacazzea oleander, commonly known as the rosebay rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Tacazzea oleander is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
