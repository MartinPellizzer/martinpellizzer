Tanacetum komarovii

Tanacetum komarovii, commonly known as Russian tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage contains compounds with mild insect-repelling properties, making it useful in traditional herbal practices.

Tanacetum komarovii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and historical medicinal uses.
