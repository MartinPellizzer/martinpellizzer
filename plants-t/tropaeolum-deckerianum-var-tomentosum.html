Tropaeolum deckerianum var. tomentosum

Tropaeolum deckerianum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting dark center. Its foliage is edible, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Tropaeolum deckerianum var. tomentosum thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
