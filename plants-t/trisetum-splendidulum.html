Trisetum splendidulum

Trisetum splendidulum, commonly known as the golden tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, golden-yellow flowers. Trisetum splendidulum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
