Thymus alsarensis var. paucisetus

Thymus alsarensis var. paucisetus, commonly known as the Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
