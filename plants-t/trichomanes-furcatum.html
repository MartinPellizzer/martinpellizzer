Trichomanes furcatum

Trichomanes furcatum, commonly known as the forked maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes furcatum is notable for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
