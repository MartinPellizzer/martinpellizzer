Tulipa saracenica

Tulipa saracenica, commonly known as the Saracen tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often thriving in rocky or alpine environments.

Tulipa saracenica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
