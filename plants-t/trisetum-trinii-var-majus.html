Trisetum trinii var. majus

Trisetum trinii var. majus, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.
