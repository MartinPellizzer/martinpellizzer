Tounatea costaricensis

Tounatea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican fern, is a species of fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds.
