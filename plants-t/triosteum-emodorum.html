Triosteum emodorum

Triosteum emodorum, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are erect and can grow up to 2 meters in height, making it a striking addition to native gardens.

Triosteum emodorum is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
