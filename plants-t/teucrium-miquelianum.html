Teucrium miquelianum

Teucrium miquelianum, commonly known as Miquelian germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Teucrium miquelianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
