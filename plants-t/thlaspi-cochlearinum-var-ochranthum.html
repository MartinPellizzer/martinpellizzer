Thlaspi cochlearinum var. ochranthum

Thlaspi cochlearinum var. ochranthum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed cabbageworm, is a variant of the common yellow cabbageworm.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciferous structure. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct mustard-like odor.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round pods containing seeds.
