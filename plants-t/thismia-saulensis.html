Thismia saulensis

It belongs to the family Thismiaceae and is characterized by its lack of true leaves and its reliance on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, white, flower-like structures that resemble petals but are actually modified leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on decaying wood or in the understory of dense forests.
