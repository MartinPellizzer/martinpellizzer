Tephrosia godmaniae

Tephrosia godmaniae, commonly known as the Godman's tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
