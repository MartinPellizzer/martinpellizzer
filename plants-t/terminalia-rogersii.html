Terminalia rogersii

Terminalia rogersii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Terminalia rogersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
