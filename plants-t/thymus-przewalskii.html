Thymus przewalskii

Thymus przewalskii, commonly known as Przewalski's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Thymus przewalskii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
