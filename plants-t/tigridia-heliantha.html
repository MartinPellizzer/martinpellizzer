Tigridia heliantha

Tigridia heliantha, commonly known as the sunflower lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to the southern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a red throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia heliantha is known for its resilience and ability to naturalize, creating vibrant displays in garden settings.
