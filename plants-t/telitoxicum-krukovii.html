Telitoxicum krukovii

Telitoxicum krukovii, commonly known as the Krukovii's toxic vine, is a climbing plant native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is notable for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested. Its roots and stems are often used in traditional medicine, though with caution due to its poisonous nature.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found along rocky outcrops and in dry woodlands.
