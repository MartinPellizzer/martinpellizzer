Talinum pygmaeum

Talinum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf malanga, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing numerous seeds. Talinum pygmaeum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both gardens and scientific studies.
