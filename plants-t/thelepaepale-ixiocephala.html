Thelepaepale ixiocephala

Thelepaepale ixiocephala, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white, ghost-like flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern, making it visually striking. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
