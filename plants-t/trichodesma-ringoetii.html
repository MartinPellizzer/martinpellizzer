Trichodesma ringoetii

Trichodesma ringoetii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, including grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Trichodesma ringoetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
