Tephrocactus articulatus var. syringacanthus

Tephrocactus articulatus var. syringacanthus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its structure is adapted to store water and withstand high temperatures, making it well-suited for desert environments.

The variety syringacanthus is distinguished by its dense, reddish spines and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
