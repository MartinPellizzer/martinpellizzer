Trichilia capensis

Trichilia capensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
