Trymalium elachophyllum

Trymalium elachophyllum, commonly known as the dwarf rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the southern regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
