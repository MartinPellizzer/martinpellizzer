Toxicodendrum capense

Toxicodendrum capense, commonly known as the Natal poison ivy, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its sap contains urushiol, a potent allergen that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

Toxicodendrum capense thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in coastal forests and dunes.
