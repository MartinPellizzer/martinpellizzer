Teucrium rotundifolium var. atlanticum

Teucrium rotundifolium var. atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern coast of North America.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters atop the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and coastal areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
