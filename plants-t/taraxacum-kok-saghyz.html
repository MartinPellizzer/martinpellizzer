Taraxacum kok-saghyz

Taraxacum kok-saghyz, commonly known as the Russian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, milky stem. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is notable for its latex-rich stems, which have been studied for potential industrial applications.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in both natural and disturbed environments.
