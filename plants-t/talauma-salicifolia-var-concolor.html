Talauma salicifolia var. concolor

Talauma salicifolia var. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a species of fern characterized by its feather-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of China, particularly in mountainous areas. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky or cliff faces. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a rough, textured surface.

This variety is noted for its resilience and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
