Teramnus repens subsp. repens

Teramnus repens subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping teramnus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
