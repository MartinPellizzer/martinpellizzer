Thelypteris gymnosora

Thelypteris gymnosora, commonly known as the naked-stemmed shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thin layer of soil, and the fronds are usually divided into segments with a distinctive shield-like shape.

Thelypteris gymnosora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
