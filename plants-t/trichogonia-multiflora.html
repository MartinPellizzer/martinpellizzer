Trichogonia multiflora

Trichogonia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces a distinctive, spherical inflorescence.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
