Tropaeolum pentaphyllum subsp. megapetaloides

Tropaeolum pentaphyllum subsp.

megapetaloides, commonly known as the giant jewelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically bright orange or red, with five petals that are often larger than the sepals.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces edible, tangy tubers similar to those of other tropaeolum species. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, unique flora.
