Taraxacum sellandii

Taraxacum sellandii, commonly known as the Sikkim dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces yellow composite flowers. The plant is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
