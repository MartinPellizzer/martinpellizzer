Trisetaria quinqueseta

Trisetaria quinqueseta, commonly known as the five-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered at the base of the stems, and its flowers are wind-pollinated. Trisetaria quinqueseta thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
