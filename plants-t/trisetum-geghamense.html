Trisetum geghamense

Trisetum geghamense, commonly known as the Armenian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Trisetum geghamense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
