Thea rosthorniana

Thea rosthorniana, commonly known as the Rosthorn tea plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to produce a mild, aromatic tea when its leaves are harvested and processed. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Thea rosthorniana is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its tea-producing potential.
