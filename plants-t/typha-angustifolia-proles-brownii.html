Typha angustifolia proles brownii

Typha angustifolia proles brownii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas. Its flowers are typically brown or black, forming dense, spike-like structures that produce abundant seeds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
