Tragacantha campylorrhyncha

Tragacantha campylorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-lobed tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and flowering spikes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its roots are known to exude a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a binder in pharmaceuticals.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.
