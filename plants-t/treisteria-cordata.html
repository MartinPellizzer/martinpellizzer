Treisteria cordata

Treisteria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf treisteria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
