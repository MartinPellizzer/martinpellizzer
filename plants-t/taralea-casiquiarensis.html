Taralea casiquiarensis

Taralea casiquiarensis, commonly known as the Casiquiare river taralea, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Casiquiare River in South America.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in the flooded forests of the Amazon basin. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, providing shade and oxygen to the surrounding ecosystem. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the muddy substrates of its habitat.

Taralea casiquiarensis plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting local aquatic life.
