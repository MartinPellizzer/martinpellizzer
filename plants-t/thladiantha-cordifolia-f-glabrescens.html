Thladiantha cordifolia f. glabrescens

Thladiantha cordifolia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth heartleaf thladiantha, is a variant of the heartleaf thladiantha, a species native to the southeastern United States.

This plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet. It is a climbing vine that produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
