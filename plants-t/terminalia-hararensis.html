Terminalia hararensis

Terminalia hararensis, commonly known as the Harare terminalia, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry woodlands and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
