Trifolium ciliosum

Trifolium ciliosum, commonly known as the fringed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with finely toothed or fringed margins. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Trifolium ciliosum is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along the edges of woodlands.
