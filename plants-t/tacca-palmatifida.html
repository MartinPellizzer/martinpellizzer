Tacca palmatifida

Tacca palmatifida, commonly known as the "bat flower," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a unique, bat-like appearance, which is why it has earned its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Tacca palmatifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
