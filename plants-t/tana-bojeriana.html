Tana bojeriana

Tana bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer's tana, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Tana bojeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
