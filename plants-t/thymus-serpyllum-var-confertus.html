Thymus serpyllum var. confertus

Thymus serpyllum var. confertus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
