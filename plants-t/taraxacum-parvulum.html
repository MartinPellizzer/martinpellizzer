Taraxacum parvulum

Taraxacum parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a single, yellow flower that develops into a spherical seed head. It is characterized by its short stature, usually not exceeding 10 centimeters in height.

Taraxacum parvulum is often considered a weed but is also valued for its medicinal properties and role in ecological systems.
