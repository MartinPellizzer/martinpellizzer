Thymus simplex

Thymus simplex, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus simplex is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in culinary applications.
