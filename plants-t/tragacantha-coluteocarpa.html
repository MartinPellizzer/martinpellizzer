Tragacantha coluteocarpa

Tragacantha coluteocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers and is known for its sap, which is harvested to make a gum used in various industries.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
