Thymus novograblenovii

Thymus novograblenovii, commonly known as the Novograblenov thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Russia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus novograblenovii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
