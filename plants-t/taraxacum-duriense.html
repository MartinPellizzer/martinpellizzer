Taraxacum duriense

Taraxacum duriense, commonly known as the Durian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Taraxacum duriense is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
