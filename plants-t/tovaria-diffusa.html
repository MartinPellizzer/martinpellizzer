Tovaria diffusa

Tovaria diffusa, commonly known as the spreading tovaria, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming throughout the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tovaria diffusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in tropical gardens.
