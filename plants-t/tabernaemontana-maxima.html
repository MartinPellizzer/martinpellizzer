Tabernaemontana maxima

Tabernaemontana maxima, commonly known as the giant star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
