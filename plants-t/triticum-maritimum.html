Triticum maritimum

Triticum maritimum, commonly known as sea barley, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to saline and windy environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets, which contain the seeds. Triticum maritimum is often found growing on dunes, salt marshes, and other coastal habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its resilience to harsh maritime conditions makes it an important pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.
