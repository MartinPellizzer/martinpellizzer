Trigonella maritima var. trachysperma

Trigonella maritima var. trachysperma, commonly known as wild fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces slender, elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
