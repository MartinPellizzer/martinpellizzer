Tripteris herbacea

Tripteris herbacea, commonly known as the slender tripteris, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

Tripteris herbacea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
