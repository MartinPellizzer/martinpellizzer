Taraxacum puolannei

Taraxacum puolannei, commonly known as the Finnish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Finland and parts of Scandinavia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive parachute-like seed dispersal mechanism.

Taraxacum puolannei is adapted to grow in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
