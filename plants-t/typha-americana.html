Typha americana

Typha americana, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. The plant has long, narrow leaves and thrives in shallow water or moist soil. It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Typha americana is also used in traditional medicine and has applications in ecological restoration projects.
