Typha × bavarica

Typha × bavarica, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a hybrid wetland plant that thrives in shallow water or moist soil.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds. The plant plays a crucial role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies that can dominate aquatic environments.
