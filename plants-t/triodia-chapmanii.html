Triodia chapmanii

Triodia chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's triodia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clumps and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to grazing. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soils and supporting local ecosystems.

Its flowering spikes, which appear in spring, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
