Taraxacum aragonicum

Taraxacum aragonicum, commonly known as the Aragon dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
