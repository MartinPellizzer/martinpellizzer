Taraxacum steptolobum

Taraxacum steptolobum, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface and a hollow, hairy stem. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional herbal uses.
