Taraxacum barbaricinum

Taraxacum barbaricinum, commonly known as the lesser dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a fluffy pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Taraxacum barbaricinum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
