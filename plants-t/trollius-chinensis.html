Trollius chinensis

Trollius chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Trollius chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
