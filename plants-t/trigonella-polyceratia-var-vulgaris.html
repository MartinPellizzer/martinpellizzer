Trigonella polyceratia var. vulgaris

Trigonella polyceratia var. vulgaris, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive aroma and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are rich in nutrients and commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its seeds and foliage.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in both agriculture and traditional medicine.
