Terminalia rubricarpa

Terminalia rubricarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.
