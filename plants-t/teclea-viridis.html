Teclea viridis

Teclea viridis, commonly known as the green teclea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. Teclea viridis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
