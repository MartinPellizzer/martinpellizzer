Tilia amurensis var. glabrata

Tilia amurensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the Amur sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
