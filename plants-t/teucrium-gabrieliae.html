Teucrium gabrieliae

Teucrium gabrieliae, commonly known as Gabriel's germander, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for native gardens.
