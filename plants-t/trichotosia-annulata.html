Trichotosia annulata

Trichotosia annulata, commonly known as the ringed thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves with a distinctive ringed pattern along the edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trichotosia annulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
