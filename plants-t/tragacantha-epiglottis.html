Tragacantha epiglottis

Tragacantha epiglottis, commonly known as the epiglottis tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a resinous substance called tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its growth habit includes a woody stem and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha epiglottis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
