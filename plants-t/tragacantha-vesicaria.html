Tragacantha vesicaria

Tragacantha vesicaria, commonly known as the blister locust, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Tragacantha vesicaria is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in degraded areas.
