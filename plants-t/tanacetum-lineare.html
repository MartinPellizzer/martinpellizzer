Tanacetum lineare

Tanacetum lineare, commonly known as linear groundsel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
