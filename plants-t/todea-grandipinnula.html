Todea grandipinnula

Todea grandipinnula, commonly known as the large-leaf todea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
