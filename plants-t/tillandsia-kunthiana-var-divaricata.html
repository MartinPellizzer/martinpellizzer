Tillandsia kunthiana var. divaricata

Tillandsia kunthiana var. divaricata, commonly known as the silver vane air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that forms a rosette shape, giving it a striking appearance. The plant does not require soil, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
