Tulipa strangulata var. variopicta

Tulipa strangulata var. variopicta, commonly known as the Variegated Turk's Cap Lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns of darker hues. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically a dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a standout in the tulip family.
