Themeda gigantea var. amboinensis

Themeda gigantea var. amboinensis, commonly known as the giant grass or Amboina grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
