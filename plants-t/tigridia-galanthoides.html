Tigridia galanthoides

Tigridia galanthoides, commonly known as the white tigridia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia galanthoides is known for its early spring blooming habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
