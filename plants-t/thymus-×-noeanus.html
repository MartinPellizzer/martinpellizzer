Thymus × noeanus

Thymus × noeanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers. Native to southern Europe, this perennial herb thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties, similar to other thyme species.
