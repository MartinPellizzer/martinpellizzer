Taraxacum muricatum

Taraxacum muricatum, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, including countries like Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It produces a milky latex when the stems or leaves are broken, a feature common in the genus Taraxacum.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
