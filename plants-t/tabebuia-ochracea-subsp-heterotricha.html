Tabebuia ochracea subsp. heterotricha

Tabebuia ochracea subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy, with simple, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
