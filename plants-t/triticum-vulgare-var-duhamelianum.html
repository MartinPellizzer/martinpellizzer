Triticum vulgare var. duhamelianum

Triticum vulgare var. duhamelianum, commonly known as the duhamelian wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat.

It is characterized by its distinct morphological features, including shorter spikelets and a more compact growth habit compared to typical wheat varieties. This variety is primarily found in specific geographic regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its genetic makeup contributes to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Due to its unique traits, it is of interest in botanical studies and agricultural research.
