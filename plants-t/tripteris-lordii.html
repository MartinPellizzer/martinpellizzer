Tripteris lordii

Tripteris lordii, commonly known as the Mexican tripteris, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its small, compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
