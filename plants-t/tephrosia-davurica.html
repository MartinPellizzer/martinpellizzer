Tephrosia davurica

Tephrosia davurica, commonly known as the Indian tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. Its roots contain alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine for their pesticidal properties.

Tephrosia davurica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
