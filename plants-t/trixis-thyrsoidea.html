Trixis thyrsoidea

Trixis thyrsoidea, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse seeds by wind. Its leaves are hairy and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Trixis thyrsoidea is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
