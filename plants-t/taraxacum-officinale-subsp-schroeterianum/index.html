Taraxacum Officinale Subsp. Schroeterianum

Taraxacum officinale subsp. schroeterianum is a lesser-known variant of the common dandelion found in specific regions of eastern europe and asia. this subspecies is characterized by its slightly different leaf shape and flower structure compared to its more widespread relatives. it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often overlooked due to its modest appearance. despite its unassuming look, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and contributing to soil health. its study offers insights into plant adaptation and biodiversity in temperate climates.
