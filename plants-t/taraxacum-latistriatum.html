Taraxacum latistriatum

Taraxacum latistriatum, commonly known as the broad-striated dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem. The leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are lanceolate to ovate in shape. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

It is a perennial herb that produces a capsule containing numerous seeds with white, feathery pappi for wind dispersal.
