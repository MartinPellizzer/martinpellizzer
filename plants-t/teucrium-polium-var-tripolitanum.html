Teucrium polium var. tripolitanum

Teucrium polium var. tripolitanum, commonly known as the Tripolitan germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in scrubland or woodland areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
