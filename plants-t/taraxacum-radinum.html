Taraxacum radinum

Taraxacum radinum, commonly known as the radinum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous lightweight, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is typically found in grassy areas and is known for its medicinal properties.
