Thelocactus conothelos subsp. argenteus

Thelocactus conothelos subsp.

argenteus, commonly known as the silver-tipped cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
