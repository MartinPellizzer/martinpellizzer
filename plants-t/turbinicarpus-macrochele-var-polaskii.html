Turbinicarpus macrochele var. polaskii

Turbinicarpus macrochele var. polaskii, commonly known as the Polaski's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
