Thelocactus lophothele subsp. hintonii

Thelocactus lophothele subsp.

hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's lophothele, is a cactus species native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
