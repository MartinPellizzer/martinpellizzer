Tabebuia striata

Tabebuia striata, commonly known as the Trumpet Tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The tree has a straight trunk with a smooth, pale bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture.

Tabebuia striata is often planted for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
