Treubia

Treubia, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and small, clustered flowers that often have yellow or orange petals. The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive foliage and flowers.
