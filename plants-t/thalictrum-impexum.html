Thalictrum impexum

Thalictrum impexum, commonly known as the bentwood meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Thalictrum impexum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
