Tanacetum alaunicum

Tanacetum alaunicum, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Tanacetum alaunicum is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in traditional herbal remedies.
