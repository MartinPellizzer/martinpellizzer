Trichinium drummondii var. minus

Trichinium drummondii var. minus, commonly known as the lesser drummond's trichinium, is a lesser-known variant of the trichinium genus.

It is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America, typically found in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The botanical interest in this variant lies in its unique morphological characteristics and potential ecological role in its native environment.
