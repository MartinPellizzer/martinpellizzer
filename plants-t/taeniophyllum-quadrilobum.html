Taeniophyllum quadrilobum

Taeniophyllum quadrilobum, commonly known as the four-lobed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of rainforest trees.

It possesses small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a four-lobed pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
