Tithymalus tchen-ngoi

Tithymalus tchen-ngoi, commonly known as the Chinese tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Tithymalus tchen-ngoi is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
