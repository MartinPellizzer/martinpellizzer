Tragacantha adunciformis

Tragacantha adunciformis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable gum resin, which has been traditionally used in medicine and as a food additive.

Its deep root system allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
