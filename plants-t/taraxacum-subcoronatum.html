Taraxacum subcoronatum

Taraxacum subcoronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with bristly pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
