Taraxacum pseudolugubre

Taraxacum pseudolugubre, commonly known as the gloomy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which develops into a fluffy seed head after blooming.

This species is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
