Thysanocarpus curvipes var. pulchellus

Thysanocarpus curvipes var. pulchellus, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
