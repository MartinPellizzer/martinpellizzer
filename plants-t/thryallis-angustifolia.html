Thryallis angustifolia

Thryallis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryallis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often arranged alternately along its stem. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, adding a delicate appearance.

Thryallis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
