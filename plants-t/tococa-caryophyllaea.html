Tococa caryophyllaea

Tococa caryophyllaea, commonly known as the red coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is known for its psychoactive properties due to the presence of alkaloids such as cocaine.

However, it is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
