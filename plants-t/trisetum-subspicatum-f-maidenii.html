Trisetum subspicatum f. maidenii

Trisetum subspicatum f. maidenii, commonly known as the Maiden's hair grass, is a variant of the common hair grass species.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its fine, slender blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is usually a soft green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This ornamental grass is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to add texture to garden landscapes.
