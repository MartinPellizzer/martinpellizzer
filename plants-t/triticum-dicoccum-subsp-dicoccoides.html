Triticum dicoccum subsp. dicoccoides

Triticum dicoccum subsp.

dicoccoides, commonly known as einkorn wheat, is an ancient grain species native to the Fertile Crescent. It is characterized by its small, hulled seeds and a high protein content compared to modern wheat varieties.

The plant typically grows in temperate climates and has a short growth cycle, making it suitable for organic farming. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces spikelets that contain several florets.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional profile and is often used in traditional and health-conscious diets.
