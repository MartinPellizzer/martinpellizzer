Taraxacum staintonii

Taraxacum staintonii, commonly known as Stainton's dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
