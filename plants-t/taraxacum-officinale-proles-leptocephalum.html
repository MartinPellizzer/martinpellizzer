Taraxacum officinale proles leptocephalum

Taraxacum officinale proles leptocephalum, commonly known as the slender-headed dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its elongated, narrow flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, stemless flower stalk that bears a single, composite flower head composed of numerous small yellow florets. It is a perennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns and meadows.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
