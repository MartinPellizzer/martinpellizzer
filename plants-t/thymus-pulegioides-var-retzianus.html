Thymus pulegioides var. retzianus

Thymus pulegioides var. retzianus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, dark green leaves that are densely packed and slightly pointed. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
