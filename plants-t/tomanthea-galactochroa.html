Tomanthea galactochroa

Tomanthea galactochroa, commonly known as the milk-white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a unique, milky-white coloration that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months of the year.

Tomanthea galactochroa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
