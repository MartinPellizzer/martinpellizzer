Tachigali bijuga

Tachigali bijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Tachigali bijuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
