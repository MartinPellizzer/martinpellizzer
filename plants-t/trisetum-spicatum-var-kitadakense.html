Trisetum spicatum var. kitadakense

Trisetum spicatum var. kitadakense, commonly known as the Japanese false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
