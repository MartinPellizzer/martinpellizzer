Trifolium lugardii

Trifolium lugardii, commonly known as the Lugard's clover, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Trifolium lugardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
