Trixis matisiana

Trixis matisiana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Trixis matisiana is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
