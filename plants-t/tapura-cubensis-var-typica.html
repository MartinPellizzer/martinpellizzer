Tapura cubensis var. typica

Tapura cubensis var. typica, commonly known as the Cuban tapura, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has smooth, green leaves and produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though care must be taken due to its potential toxicity.
