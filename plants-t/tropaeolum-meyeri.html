Tropaeolum meyeri

Tropaeolum meyeri, commonly known as the Miner's Lettuce, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, peppermint-scented flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in salads or as a garnish. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Tropaeolum meyeri is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
