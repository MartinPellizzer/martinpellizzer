Trollius vaginatus

Trollius vaginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and often have a sheathing base, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Trollius vaginatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
