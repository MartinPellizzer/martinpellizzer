Theobroma augustum

Theobroma augustum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are commercially harvested as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, healthy fats, and essential minerals.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its ecological role and economic value in the region.
