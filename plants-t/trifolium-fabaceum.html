Trifolium fabaceum

Trifolium fabaceum, commonly known as the small white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Trifolium fabaceum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
