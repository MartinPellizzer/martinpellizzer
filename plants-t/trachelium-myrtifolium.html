Trachelium myrtifolium

Trachelium myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved trachelium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Trachelium myrtifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
