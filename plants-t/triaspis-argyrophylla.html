Triaspis argyrophylla

Triaspis argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed triaspis, is a unique plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
