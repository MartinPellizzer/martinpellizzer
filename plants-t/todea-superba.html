Todea superba

Todea superba, commonly known as the giant toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Todea superba is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
