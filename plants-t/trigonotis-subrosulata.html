Trigonotis subrosulata

Trigonotis subrosulata, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
