Talinum esculentum

Talinum esculentum, commonly known as red leaf amaranth, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, green, or purple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. Its leaves are edible and often used in soups and stews due to their nutty flavor and high nutritional content.

Talinum esculentum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
