Trichopteryx elegans var. subulifolia

Trichopteryx elegans var. subulifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
