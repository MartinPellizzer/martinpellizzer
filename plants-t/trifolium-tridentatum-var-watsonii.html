Trifolium tridentatum var. watsonii

Trifolium tridentatum var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's three-toothed clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
