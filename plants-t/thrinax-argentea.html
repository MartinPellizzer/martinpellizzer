Thrinax argentea

Thrinax argentea, commonly known as the silver palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. Thrinax argentea thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
