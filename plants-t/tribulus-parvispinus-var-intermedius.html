Tribulus parvispinus var. intermedius

Tribulus parvispinus var. intermedius, commonly known as the lesser caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with spiny fruits that contain four seeds. The plant has small, yellowish flowers and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, lobed shape.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
