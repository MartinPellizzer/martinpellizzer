Trigonella emodi f. violacea

Trigonella emodi f. violacea, commonly known as the violet cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
