Taraxacum cacuminatum

Taraxacum cacuminatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by seed heads that resemble those of dandelions. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
