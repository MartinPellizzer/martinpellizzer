Teucrium divaricatum subsp. sieberi

Teucrium divaricatum subsp.

sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its square, branched stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic foliage and medicinal uses.
