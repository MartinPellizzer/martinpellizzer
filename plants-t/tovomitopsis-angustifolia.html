Tovomitopsis angustifolia

Tovomitopsis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tovomitopsis, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
