Thouinia stricta

Thouinia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
