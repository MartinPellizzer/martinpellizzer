Telis laciniata

Telis laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf telis, is a unique plant characterized by its finely divided, lace-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly cultivated outside of its native range.
