Thelypteris recondita

Thelypteris recondita, commonly known as the hidden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a pale underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a thin, hairy covering.

Thelypteris recondita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, feathery appearance.
