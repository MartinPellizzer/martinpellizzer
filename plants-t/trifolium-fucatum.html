Trifolium fucatum

Trifolium fucatum, commonly known as the black clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its dark purple or black flowers and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Trifolium fucatum is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
