Tulipa aurea

Tulipa aurea, commonly known as the golden tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its bright yellow petals that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to store nutrients, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their vibrant color and ornamental value.
