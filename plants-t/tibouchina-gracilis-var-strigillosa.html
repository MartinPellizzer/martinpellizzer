Tibouchina gracilis var. strigillosa

Tibouchina gracilis var. strigillosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This ornamental shrub is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
