Taraxacum evittatum

Taraxacum evittatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Taraxacum evittatum is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.
