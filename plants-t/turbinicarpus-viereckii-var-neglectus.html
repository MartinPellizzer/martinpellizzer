Turbinicarpus viereckii var. neglectus

Turbinicarpus viereckii var. neglectus, commonly known as the Neglectus Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
