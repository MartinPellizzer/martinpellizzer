Taraxacum kaletkiniae

Taraxacum kaletkiniae, commonly known as the Karakachan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate, hairy leaves and a single, hollow, leafless stem that terminates in a composite flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in countries like Bulgaria and Serbia.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing a degree of resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
