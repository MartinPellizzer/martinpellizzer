Tillandsia marantoidea

Tillandsia marantoidea, commonly known as the silver leaf tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant typically grows epiphytically on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which are usually small and inconspicuous, are borne in a spike and can produce a modest number of seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
