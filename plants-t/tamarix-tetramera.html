Tamarix tetramera

Tamarix tetramera, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has feathery, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its woody stems are often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps reflect sunlight and minimize heat stress.

Tamarix tetramera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
