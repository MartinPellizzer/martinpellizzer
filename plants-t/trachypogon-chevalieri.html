Trachypogon chevalieri

Trachypogon chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Trachypogon chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
