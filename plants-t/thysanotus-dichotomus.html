Thysanotus dichotomus

Thysanotus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched starflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-branched inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thysanotus dichotomus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
