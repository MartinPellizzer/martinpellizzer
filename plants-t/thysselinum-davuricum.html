Thysselinum davuricum

Thysselinum davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
