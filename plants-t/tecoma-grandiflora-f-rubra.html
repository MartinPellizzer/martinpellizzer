Tecoma grandiflora f. rubra

Tecoma grandiflora f. rubra, commonly known as the red flame trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is a vigorous, fast-growing shrub or small tree that produces showy, tubular red flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
