Tiarella stenopetala

Tiarella stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is often found in rocky or humus-rich soils.

Tiarella stenopetala is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
