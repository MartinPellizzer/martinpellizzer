Tamarix chinensis subsp. austromongolica

Tamarix chinensis subsp.

austromongolica, commonly known as Chinese tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces fluffy, pink or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soils in sandy or saline areas.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and seed dispersal mechanisms.
