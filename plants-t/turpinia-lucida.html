Turpinia lucida

Turpinia lucida, commonly known as the Hawaiian silverbark, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Turpinia lucida is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.
