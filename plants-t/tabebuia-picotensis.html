Tabebuia picotensis

Tabebuia picotensis, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
