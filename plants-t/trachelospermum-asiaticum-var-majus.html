Trachelospermum asiaticum var. majus

Trachelospermum asiaticum var. majus, commonly known as the Chinese star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its ornate foliage and pleasant fragrance.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for trellises, pergolas, and as a ground cover in suitable environments.
