Tripogandra elongata f. diuretica

Tripogandra elongata f. diuretica, commonly known as the diuretic spurge, is a variant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are alternate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus.

The f. diuretica form is noted for its potential medicinal properties, particularly its diuretic effects, which have been traditionally utilized in herbal remedies.
