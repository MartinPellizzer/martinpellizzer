Triceratostris rhombilabia

Triceratostris rhombilabia, commonly known as the rhombic labia orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family, characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped labellum.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender stem with clustered, small greenish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in the wild.
