Thymus pallidus var. hirsutissimus

Thymus pallidus var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish to lavender flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic leaves and drought tolerance make it a hardy, ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
