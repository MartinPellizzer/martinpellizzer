Tragacantha sieversiana

Tragacantha sieversiana, commonly known as the Sievers' tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.

The plant's latex has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
