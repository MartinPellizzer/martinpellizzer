Trichocentrum × francoi

Trichocentrum × francoi, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Trichocentrum luteum and Trichocentrum guttatum.

It is a epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America, typically growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features long, pendant inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for efficient water storage.

Trichocentrum × francoi is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
