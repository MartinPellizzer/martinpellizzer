Tournefortia laxiflora

Tournefortia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Tournefortia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
