Taraxacum brevisectoides

Taraxacum brevisectoides, commonly known as the short-lobed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate to ovate leaves and a single, short-stemmed flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses its seeds via the wind. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Taraxacum brevisectoides is a perennial herb that typically blooms from spring to early summer.
