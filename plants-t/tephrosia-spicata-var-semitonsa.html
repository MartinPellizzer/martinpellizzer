Tephrosia spicata var. semitonsa

Tephrosia spicata var. semitonsa, commonly known as the slender tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often clustered in spicate inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in savannah and woodland habitats.

This species is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
