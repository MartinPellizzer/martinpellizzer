Thymus pubescens

Thymus pubescens, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces tiny pink to purple flowers in spring, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and ornamental uses.
