Thomsonaria verticillata

Thomsonaria verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant has a short, branching stem with fleshy, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
