Typha paludosa

Typha paludosa, commonly known as the soft-stemmed cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes and broad, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant produces small, densely packed flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Typha paludosa plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
