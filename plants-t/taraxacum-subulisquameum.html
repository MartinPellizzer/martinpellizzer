Taraxacum subulisquameum

Taraxacum subulisquameum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flower head, which consists of numerous tiny florets surrounded by a pappus of bristles. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
