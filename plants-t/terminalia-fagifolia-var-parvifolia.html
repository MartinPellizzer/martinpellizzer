Terminalia fagifolia var. parvifolia

Terminalia fagifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often clustered at the ends of branches, giving it a compact appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, many-seeded pods.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in construction and traditional crafts.
