Tetraplasandra meiandra var. prolifica

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. prolifica, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
