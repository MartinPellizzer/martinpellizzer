Tilia turbinata

Tilia turbinata, commonly known as the Turkish lime or Turkish linden, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall.

Tilia turbinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
