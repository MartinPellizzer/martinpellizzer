Tecoma stans var. stans

Tecoma stans var. stans, commonly known as yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
