Tamarix ramosissima

Tamarix ramosissima, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and its branches often form a rounded or spreading shape. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
