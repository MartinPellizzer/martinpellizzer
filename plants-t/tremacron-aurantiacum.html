Tremacron aurantiacum

Tremacron aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden tremacron, is a unique flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It features striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

The botanical significance of Tremacron aurantiacum lies in its role as an indicator species for specific ecological conditions.
