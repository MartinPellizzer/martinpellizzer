Thelypteris hexagonoptera

Thelypteris hexagonoptera, commonly known as the hexagonoptera shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically bright green and have a distinctive, shield-like shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides.

The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
