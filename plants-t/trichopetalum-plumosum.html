Trichopetalum plumosum

Trichopetalum plumosum, commonly known as the silky leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its scientific name reflects its unique leaf structure, with "tricho-" referring to the hairy texture and "petaled" referring to its floral morphology.

Trichopetalum plumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
