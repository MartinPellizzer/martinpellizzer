Thuja theophrasti

Thuja theophrasti, commonly known as Eastern arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage and aromatic, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 15 feet.

Thuja theophrasti is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
