Trichilia quinquevalvis

Trichilia quinquevalvis, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, five-angled fruits containing flat, edible seeds. These seeds are valued for their high oil content, which is used in cosmetics and food products.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
