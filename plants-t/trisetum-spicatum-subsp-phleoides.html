Trisetum spicatum subsp. phleoides

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
