Taraxacum gibbosum

Taraxacum gibbosum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed head, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the characteristic fluffy seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

The globe thistle is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it contributes to biodiversity and supports pollinators.
