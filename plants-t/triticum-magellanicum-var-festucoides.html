Triticum magellanicum var. festucoides

Triticum magellanicum var. festucoides, commonly known as the Magellanic wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically found in open, disturbed habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
