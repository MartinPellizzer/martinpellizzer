Turbinicarpus saueri

Turbinicarpus saueri, commonly known as the Sauer's turbinaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, globular shape with ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water.

Turbinicarpus saueri is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
