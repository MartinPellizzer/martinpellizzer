Triticum missuricum

Triticum missuricum, commonly known as the Mississippi wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Triticum missuricum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
