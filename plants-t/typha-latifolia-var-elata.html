Typha latifolia var. elata

Typha latifolia var. elata, commonly known as the tall cattail, is a variant of the common cattail native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, erect flowering stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense, cylindrical flower heads that are typically brown in color, which contain numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are broad and flat, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter, and are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
