Trichostachys krauseana

Trichostachys krauseana, commonly known as the Krause's bamboo, is a tall, slender grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Trichostachys krauseana is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
