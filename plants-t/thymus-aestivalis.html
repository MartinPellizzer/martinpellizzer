Thymus aestivalis

Thymus aestivalis, commonly known as summer thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers in the summer months. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds with antimicrobial and antifungal properties.
