Torenia crenata

Torenia crenata, commonly known as the whorled torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Torenia crenata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
