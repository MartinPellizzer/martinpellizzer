Tirucalia negromontana

Tirucalia negromontana, commonly known as black mountain tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
