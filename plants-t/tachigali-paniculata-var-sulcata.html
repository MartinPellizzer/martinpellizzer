Tachigali paniculata var. sulcata

Tachigali paniculata var. sulcata, commonly known as the sulcate tachigali, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
