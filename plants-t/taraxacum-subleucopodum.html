Taraxacum subleucopodum

Taraxacum subleucopodum, commonly known as the pale-rayed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds, typical of the genus Taraxacum. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces a milky sap when damaged.

The pale-rayed dandelion is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
