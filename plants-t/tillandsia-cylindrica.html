Tillandsia cylindrica

Tillandsia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Due to its unique growth habit and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
