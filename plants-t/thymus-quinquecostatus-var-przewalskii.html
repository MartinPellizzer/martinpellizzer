Thymus quinquecostatus var. przewalskii

Thymus quinquecostatus var. przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.
