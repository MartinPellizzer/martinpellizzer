Torilis helvetica var. anthriscoides

Torilis helvetica var. anthriscoides, commonly known as the European hedge mustard, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

This species is also known for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
