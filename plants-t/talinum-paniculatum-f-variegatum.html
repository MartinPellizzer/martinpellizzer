Talinum paniculatum f. variegatum

Talinum paniculatum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated mealweed, is a decorative plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its striking variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Talinum paniculatum f. variegatum is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in containers or in garden beds with well-draining soil.
