Tilia amurensis var. grosseserrata

Tilia amurensis var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the Amur big-toothed linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominently serrated margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
