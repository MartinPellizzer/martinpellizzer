Trifolium ucrainicum

Trifolium ucrainicum, commonly known as the Ukrainian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to Eastern Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Trifolium ucrainicum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
