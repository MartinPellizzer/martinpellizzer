Tragacantha oxyglottis

Tragacantha oxyglottis, commonly known as the Persian gum or tamarisk gum, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and produces small, yellow flowers. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable gum resin, which has been used historically in medicine and industry.

The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.
