Teucrium polium var. flavovirens

Teucrium polium var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to violet, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is occasionally used in traditional herbal medicine.
