Tradescantia semisomna

Tradescantia semisomna, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and can grow in both upright and spreading forms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia semisomna is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
