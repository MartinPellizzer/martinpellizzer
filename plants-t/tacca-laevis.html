Tacca laevis

Tacca laevis, commonly known as the white arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, ornate inflorescence that resembles a flower with a central spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish. Its blooms, which are primarily for show rather than pollination, are often used in ornamental horticulture.

Tacca laevis is also valued for its cultural significance in some regions, where it is used in traditional practices and rituals.
