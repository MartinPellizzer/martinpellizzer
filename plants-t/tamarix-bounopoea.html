Tamarix bounopoea

Tamarix bounopoea, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces delicate pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Tamarix bounopoea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm, dry climates.
