Trollius ledebourii

Trollius ledebourii, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It grows from a short, thick rhizome and produces numerous hairy, cylindrical seed pods.

Trollius ledebourii is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert conditions.
