Trisetum canariense

Trisetum canariense, commonly known as Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Trisetum canariense plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
