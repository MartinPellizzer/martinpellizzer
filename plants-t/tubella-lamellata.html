Tubella lamellata

Tubella lamellata, commonly known as the ribbon coral, is a species of sponge that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a marine organism belonging to the phylum Porifera, known for its porous structure that allows water to flow through its body. The sponge's body is composed of a flexible skeleton made of spicules, which provide structural support. It thrives in shallow coastal waters, often attaching itself to rocky substrates.

Its appearance is distinctive, with a soft, gelatinous texture and a branching, coral-like structure.
