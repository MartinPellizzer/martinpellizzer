Tagetes pectinata

Tagetes pectinata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
