Torenia udawnensis

Torenia udawnensis, commonly known as the purple torenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trailing stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the Acanthaceae family.

Torenia udawnensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.
