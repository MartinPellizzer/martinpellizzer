Tragacantha libanotica

Tragacantha libanotica, commonly known as the Syrian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces yellow flowers and has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky soils. The plant is notable for its latex-rich sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are often used as fodder for livestock, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

The plant's resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of its ecosystem.
