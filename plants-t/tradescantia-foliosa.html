Tradescantia foliosa

Tradescantia foliosa, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, often bluish-green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is noted for its striking variegation, with some cultivars displaying red or purple markings.

Tradescantia foliosa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
