Thymelaea lythroides

Thymelaea lythroides, commonly known as the rock thyme or blue thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy gardens. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Thymelaea lythroides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
