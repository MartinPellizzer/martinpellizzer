Trichosanthes palmata var. tomentosa

Trichosanthes palmata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, warty surface and a bitter taste. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in herbal remedies.
