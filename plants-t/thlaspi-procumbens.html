Thlaspi procumbens

Thlaspi procumbens, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to reddish-purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and roadside areas.

Thlaspi procumbens is an important plant in ecological systems, providing nectar for early-season pollinators.
