Thea caudata var. faberi

Thea caudata var. faberi, commonly known as the Chinese mountain tea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional Chinese medicine.
