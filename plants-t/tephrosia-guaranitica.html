Tephrosia guaranitica

Tephrosia guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian tephrosia, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Tephrosia guaranitica is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
