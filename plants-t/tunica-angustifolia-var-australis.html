Tunica angustifolia var. australis

Tunica angustifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian narrow-leafed tunica, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is used in some regions for erosion control.
