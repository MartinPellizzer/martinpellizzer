Trifolium incarnatum var. minor

Trifolium incarnatum var. minor, commonly known as the lesser red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a pale green or white midrib. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
