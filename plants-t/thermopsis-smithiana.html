Thermopsis smithiana

Thermopsis smithiana, commonly known as Smith's thermopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Thermopsis smithiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
