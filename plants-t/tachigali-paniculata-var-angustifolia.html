Tachigali paniculata var. angustifolia

Tachigali paniculata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tachigali, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
