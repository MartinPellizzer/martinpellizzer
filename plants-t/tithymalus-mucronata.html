Tithymalus mucronata

Tithymalus mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ease of care.
