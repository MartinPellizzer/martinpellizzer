Thelypteris subcuneata

Thelypteris subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

Thelypteris subcuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
