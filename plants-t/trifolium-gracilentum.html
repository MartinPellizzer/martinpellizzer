Trifolium gracilentum

Trifolium gracilentum, commonly known as slender clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Trifolium gracilentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
