Typha nana

Typha nana, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in shallow water or moist soils. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Typha nana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and tolerate pollution.
