Tulipa connivens subsp. obtusata

Tulipa connivens subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, underground bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
