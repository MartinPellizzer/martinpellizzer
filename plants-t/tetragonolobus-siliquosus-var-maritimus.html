Tetragonolobus siliquosus var. maritimus

Tetragonolobus siliquosus var. maritimus, commonly known as the sea senna, is a coastal plant native to sandy or saline environments.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits. It is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in dune ecosystems.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is often found in maritime regions.
