Talinum parviflorum

Talinum parviflorum, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found growing in moist, disturbed soils. Talinum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
