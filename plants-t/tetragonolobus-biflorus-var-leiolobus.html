Tetragonolobus biflorus var. leiolobus

Tetragonolobus biflorus var. leiolobus, commonly known as the smoothleaf burr medic, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and small, clustered, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with rounded lobes, and the plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
