Thunbergia borbonica

Thunbergia borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Thunbergia borbonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
