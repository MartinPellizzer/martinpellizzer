Thuja javanica

Thuja javanica, commonly known as the Javan thuja, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense hedges.

Thuja javanica is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
