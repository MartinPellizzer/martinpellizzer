Triumfetta rubiginosa

Triumfetta rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed glorybower, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has smooth, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its presence in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
