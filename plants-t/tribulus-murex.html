Tribulus murex

Tribulus murex, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring spiny stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Tribulus murex is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
