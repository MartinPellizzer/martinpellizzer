Tassadia colubrina

Tassadia colubrina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Tassadia colubrina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
