Trifolium fragiferum

Trifolium fragiferum, commonly known as the fragrant clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands. It has a shallow root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Trifolium fragiferum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
