Taraxacum coacervans

Taraxacum coacervans, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.
