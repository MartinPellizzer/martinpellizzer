Tectaria filix-femina

Tectaria filix-femina, commonly known as the female fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are often divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria filix-femina is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
