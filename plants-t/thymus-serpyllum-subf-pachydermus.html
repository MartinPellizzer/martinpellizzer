Thymus serpyllum subf. pachydermus

Thymus serpyllum subf.

pachydermus, commonly known as broadleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features dense, woody stems that are typically covered in small, glandular hairs.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly wrinkled surface and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties and hardy nature.
