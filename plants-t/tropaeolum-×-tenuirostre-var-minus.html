Tropaeolum × tenuirostre var. minus

Tropaeolum × tenuirostre var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf Indian cress, is a compact, annual plant native to South America.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces vibrant, spurred flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor.
