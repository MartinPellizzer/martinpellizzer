Thymus munbyanus var. comosus

Thymus munbyanus var. comosus, commonly known as Munby's thyme, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
