Taraxacum unguilobiforme

Taraxacum unguilobiforme, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The species is similar to other dandelions but can be distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
