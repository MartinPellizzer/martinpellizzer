Taraxacum longispinulosum

Taraxacum longispinulosum, commonly known as the long-spined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with white, feathery seeds. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The species is notable for its distinctive spines and its role in supporting pollinators.
