Tephrosia mexicana

Tephrosia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican tephrosia, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, round pods that contain seeds.

Tephrosia mexicana is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
