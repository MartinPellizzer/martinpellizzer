Thelypteris × paradentata

Thelypteris × paradentata, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Thelypteris calyptrata and Thelypteris angustifolia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces no spores, as it is a sterile hybrid, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

The hybrid is prized for its elegant foliage and is commonly used in horticultural displays.
