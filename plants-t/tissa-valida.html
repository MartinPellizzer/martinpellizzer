Tissa valida

Tissa valida, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a stout, often branched stem with smooth, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Tissa valida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
