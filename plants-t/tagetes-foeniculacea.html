Tagetes foeniculacea

Tagetes foeniculacea, commonly known as Mexican tarragon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers and aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its foliage is often used in cooking, adding a distinct anise-like flavor to dishes.
