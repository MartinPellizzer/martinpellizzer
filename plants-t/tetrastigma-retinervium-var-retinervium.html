Tetrastigma retinervium var. retinervium

Tetrastigma retinervium var. retinervium, commonly known as the fragrant climbing vine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a reticulate venation pattern, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a hairy, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This vine is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
