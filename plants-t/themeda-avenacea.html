Themeda avenacea

Themeda avenacea, commonly known as spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, sharp-tipped leaves that form a tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous minute flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Themeda avenacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
