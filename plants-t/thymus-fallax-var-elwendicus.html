Thymus fallax var. elwendicus

Thymus fallax var. elwendicus, commonly known as the Elwendicus thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the rocky soils of southern Europe.

It features compact, dense foliage with tiny, aromatic leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
