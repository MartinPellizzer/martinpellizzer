Trifolium variegatum

Trifolium variegatum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, often with a distinctive variegated pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is typically found in grassy areas and is adapted to a variety of soil conditions.

Trifolium variegatum is valued for its role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
