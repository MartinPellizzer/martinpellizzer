Tarachia adiantoides

Tarachia adiantoides, commonly known as the desert maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of the maidenhair fern, though it is adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant has a unique ability to survive in extreme conditions by entering a dormant state during prolonged droughts. Its small, spore-bearing structures are typically found on the underside of its leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Tarachia adiantoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other desert flora.
