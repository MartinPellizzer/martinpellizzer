Tillaea androsace

Tillaea androsace, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a small, perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and high altitudes. The plant features a low, cushion-like growth habit with dense clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are generally succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Tillaea androsace is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
