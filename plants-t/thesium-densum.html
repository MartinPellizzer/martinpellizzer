Thesium densum

Thesium densum, commonly known as the dense thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Thesium densum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies.
