Tinospora andamanica

Tinospora andamanica, commonly known as Andaman Creeper, is a climbing shrub native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in dense forests.

Tinospora andamanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
