Tulipa odoratissima

Tulipa odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters and produces strap-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers emit a sweet, spicy fragrance, which is most noticeable in the evening.
