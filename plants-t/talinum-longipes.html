Talinum longipes

Talinum longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed Malabar spinach, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are edible. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Talinum longipes thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible leaves.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus and cotton plants.
