Toxocarpus excisus

Toxocarpus excisus, commonly known as the scarlet flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often lobed or serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Toxocarpus excisus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
