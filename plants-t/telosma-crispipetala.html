Telosma crispipetala

Telosma crispipetala, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.

Telosma crispipetala is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its toxic properties.
