Trichocline argentea

Trichocline argentea, commonly known as silverleaf trichocline, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
