Thespesia populnea var. bynoeana

Thespesia populnea var. bynoeana, commonly known as the Bynoe's hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
