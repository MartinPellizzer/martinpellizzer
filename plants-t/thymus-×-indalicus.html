Thymus × indalicus

Thymus × indalicus, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus serpyllum and Thymus mastichodes. The plant features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves and produces pink or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
