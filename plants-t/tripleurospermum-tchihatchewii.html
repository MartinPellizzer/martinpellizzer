Tripleurospermum tchihatchewii

Tripleurospermum tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Tchihatchew's goatweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
