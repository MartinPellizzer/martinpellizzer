Tinus triflora

Tinus triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered tinus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Tinus triflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky areas.
