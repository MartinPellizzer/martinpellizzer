Tetratheca hirsuta subsp. viminea

Tetratheca hirsuta subsp.

viminea, commonly known as the hairy tetratheca, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive central disc. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
