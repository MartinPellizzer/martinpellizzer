Tamarix pauciovulata var. micrantha

Tamarix pauciovulata var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in summer. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
