Thelymitra paludosa

Thelymitra paludosa, commonly known as the marsh leek, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a single spike of small, greenish-white flowers during the spring season. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Thelymitra paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
