Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. germanicum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

germanicum, commonly known as German wall germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
