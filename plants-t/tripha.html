Tripha

Tripha, commonly known as the three-leaved plant, is a small herbaceous species characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly serrated. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. The plant is native to temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
