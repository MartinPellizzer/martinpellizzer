Tecoma ipe var. integra

Tecoma ipe var. integra, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces long, cylindrical seed pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
