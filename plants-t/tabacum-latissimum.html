Tabacum latissimum

Tabacum latissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Native to the tropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of tobacco products.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other tobacco species, with a unique morphology and growth habit.
