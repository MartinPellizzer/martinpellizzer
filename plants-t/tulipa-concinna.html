Tulipa concinna

Tulipa concinna, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it blooms in early spring.

The Persian tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering display.
