Trixis hassleri

Trixis hassleri, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Trixis hassleri plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
