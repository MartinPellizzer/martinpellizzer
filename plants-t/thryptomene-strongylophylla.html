Thryptomene strongylophylla

Thryptomene strongylophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in spring. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Thryptomene strongylophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
