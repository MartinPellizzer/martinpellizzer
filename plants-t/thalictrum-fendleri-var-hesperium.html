Thalictrum fendleri var. hesperium

Thalictrum fendleri var. hesperium, commonly known as the Western Meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist soils along streams and in prairie habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
