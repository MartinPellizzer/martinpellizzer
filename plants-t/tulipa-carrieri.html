Tulipa carrieri

Tulipa carrieri, commonly known as the Karakorum tulip, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to withstand cold temperatures, allowing it to survive in harsh mountain climates.

The tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
