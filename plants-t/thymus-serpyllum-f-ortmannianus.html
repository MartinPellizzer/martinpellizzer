Thymus serpyllum f. ortmannianus

Thymus serpyllum f. ortmannianus, commonly known as the ortmann's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
