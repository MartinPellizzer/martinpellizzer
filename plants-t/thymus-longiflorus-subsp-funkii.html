Thymus longiflorus subsp. funkii

Thymus longiflorus subsp.

funkii, commonly known as the Funk's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, similar to other members of the thyme family.
