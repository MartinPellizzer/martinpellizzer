Triticum aestivum var. tannu-olicum

Triticum aestivum var. tannu-olicum, commonly known as tannu-olicum wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique chemical composition.

This variety is notable for its high tannin content, which contributes to its distinct flavor and potential health benefits. The tannins in this plant are primarily concentrated in the bran and germ, making it a valuable source of natural antioxidants. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tall, slender stalk with narrow leaves.

This variety is often studied for its potential use in functional foods and natural medicine due to its phytochemical profile.
