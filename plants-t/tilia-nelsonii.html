Tilia nelsonii

Tilia nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's linden, is a species of flowering plant in the Tiliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in areas of Colorado and New Mexico. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
