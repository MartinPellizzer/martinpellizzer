Trichocereus bruchii

Trichocereus bruchii, commonly known as the Pachanoi cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often develop a distinctive red or purple coloration under certain conditions. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
