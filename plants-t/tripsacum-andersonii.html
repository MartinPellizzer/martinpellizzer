Tripsacum andersonii

Tripsacum andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland and swampy habitats, often growing in dense clusters.

Tripsacum andersonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
