Triticum longisssimum subsp. sharonense

Triticum longisssimum subsp.

sharonense, commonly known as Sharon wheat, is a wild wheat species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, typical of the grass family. Its seeds are small and oval, with a hard, protective hull.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to various environmental conditions.
