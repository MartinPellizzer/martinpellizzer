Tovomita hameliifolia

Tovomita hameliifolia, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are opposite and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tovomita hameliifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
