Trichocereus tulhuayacensis

Trichocereus tulhuayacensis, commonly known as the Tulhuayacensis cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth habit is typically solitary, with a straight, unbranched stem.

Trichocereus tulhuayacensis is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
