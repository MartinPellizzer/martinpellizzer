Triplostegia aschersoniana

Triplostegia aschersoniana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its ecological role includes providing food for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
