Tragacantha proxima

Tragacantha proxima, commonly known as the lesser tragacanth, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its pods contain small, kidney-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has been historically used for its gum, which is harvested from the stems and has various industrial applications.
