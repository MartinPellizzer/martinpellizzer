Tainia chinensis

Tainia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its starchy rhizomes. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its broad foliage.

Tainia chinensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
