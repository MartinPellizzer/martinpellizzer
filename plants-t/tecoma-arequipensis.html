Tecoma arequipensis

Tecoma arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma arequipensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
