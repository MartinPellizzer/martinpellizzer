Tulipa prolongata

Tulipa prolongata, commonly known as the longiflora tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Tulipa prolongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
