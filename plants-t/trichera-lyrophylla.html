Trichera lyrophylla

Trichera lyrophylla, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Trichera lyrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
