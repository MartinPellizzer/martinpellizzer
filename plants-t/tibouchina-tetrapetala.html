Tibouchina tetrapetala

Tibouchina tetrapetala, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
