Teucrium expassum subsp. meridionale

Teucrium expassum subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as southern crested germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-blue hue, and its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other teucrium species.
