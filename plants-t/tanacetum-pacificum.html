Tanacetum pacificum

Tanacetum pacificum, commonly known as Pacific sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as streambanks and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum pacificum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
