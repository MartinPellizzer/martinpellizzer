Triticum hemipoa

Triticum hemipoa, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in glumes. Triticum hemipoa is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for forage and has potential as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value.
