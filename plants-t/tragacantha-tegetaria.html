Tragacantha tegetaria

Tragacantha tegetaria, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ability to produce a resin, which has been used historically in various traditional applications.
