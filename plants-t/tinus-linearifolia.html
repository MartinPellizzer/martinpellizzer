Tinus linearifolia

Tinus linearifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Tinus linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
