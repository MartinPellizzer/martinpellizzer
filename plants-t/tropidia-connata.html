Tropidia connata

Tropidia connata, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Tropidia connata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
