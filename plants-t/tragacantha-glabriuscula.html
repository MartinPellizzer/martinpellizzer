Tragacantha glabriuscula

Tragacantha glabriuscula, commonly known as the small-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food thickener. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Tragacantha glabriuscula is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in traditional herbal remedies.
