Trifolium stellatum subsp. longiflorum

Trifolium stellatum subsp.

longiflorum, commonly known as the star clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, clustered, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
