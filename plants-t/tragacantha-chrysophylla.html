Tragacantha chrysophylla

Tragacantha chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage and yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces a gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ecological and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
