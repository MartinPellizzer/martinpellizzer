Tropaeolum lobbianum var. hederifolium

Tropaeolum lobbianum var. hederifolium, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a vigorous climbing or trailing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, edible leaves with a peppery flavor, and its bright orange or red flowers bloom in late summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often covering ground or trellises with its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly grown as an ornamental or edible plant in gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and the shape of its foliage.
