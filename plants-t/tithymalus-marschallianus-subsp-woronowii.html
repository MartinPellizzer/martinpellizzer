Tithymalus marschallianus subsp. woronowii

Tithymalus marschallianus subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Eastern Europe and Western Siberia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale pink.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
