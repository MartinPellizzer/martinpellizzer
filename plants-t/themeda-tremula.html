Themeda tremula

Themeda tremula, commonly known as slender grass or slender blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
