Trisetum gracile var. morisianum

Trisetum gracile var. morisianum, commonly known as the slender chain-grass, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet arrangement, typical of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
