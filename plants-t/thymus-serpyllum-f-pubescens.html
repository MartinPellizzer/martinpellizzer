Thymus serpyllum f. pubescens

Thymus serpyllum f. pubescens, commonly known as hairy thrift, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features dense, compact foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its flowers are typically pink to violet, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in early summer.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
