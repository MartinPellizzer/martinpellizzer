Taraxacum craspedotum

Taraxacum craspedotum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other dandelion species. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The hairy dandelion is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
