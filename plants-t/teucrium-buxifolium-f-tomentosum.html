Teucrium buxifolium f. tomentosum

Teucrium buxifolium f. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly boxleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, rounded, leathery leaves that are densely covered with a fine, woolly pubescence. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
