Trachypogon stenophyllus

Trachypogon stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and forms dense tussocks or clumps. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is surrounded by bracts.

Trachypogon stenophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
