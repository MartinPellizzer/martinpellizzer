Thalictrum yui

Thalictrum yui, commonly known as the Japanese meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are surrounded by prominent, showy sepals. Thalictrum yui typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
