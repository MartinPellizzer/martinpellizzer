Thymus pannonicus subsp. bulsanensis

Thymus pannonicus subsp.

bulsanensis, commonly known as the Bulsan thyme, is a perennial herb native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other thyme species.
