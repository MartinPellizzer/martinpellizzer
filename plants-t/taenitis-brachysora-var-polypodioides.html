Taenitis brachysora var. polypodioides

Taenitis brachysora var. polypodioides, commonly known as the broad-leafed taenitis, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The varietal form, polypodioides, distinguishes it from other varieties within the Taenitis genus through its unique leaf morphology and growth habits.
