Taraxacum uschakovii

Taraxacum uschakovii, commonly known as the Uschakov's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
