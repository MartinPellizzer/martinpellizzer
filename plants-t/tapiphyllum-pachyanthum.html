Tapiphyllum pachyanthum

Tapiphyllum pachyanthum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that provide a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
