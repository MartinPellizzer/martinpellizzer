Trichilia megalantha

Trichilia megalantha, commonly known as African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their valuable hardwoods. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a distinctive hairy texture.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
