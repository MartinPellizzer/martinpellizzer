Tragacantha ventorum

Tragacantha ventorum, commonly known as the desert gum plant, is a flowering shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it well-adapted to desert conditions.

Tragacantha ventorum plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
