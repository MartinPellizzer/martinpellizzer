Thalictrum parvulum

Thalictrum parvulum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early to mid-summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Thalictrum parvulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
