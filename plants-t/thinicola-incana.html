Thinicola incana

Thinicola incana, commonly known as the silverleaf thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
