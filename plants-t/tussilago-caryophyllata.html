Tussilago caryophyllata

Tussilago caryophyllata, commonly known as purple tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright purple to pinkish-yellow ray florets and yellow disk florets, which are typical of the daisy family. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and wetlands.

Tussilago caryophyllata is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
