Tinus nigricans

Tinus nigricans, commonly known as the black tinus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features dark, woody stems that are characteristic of its name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the arid conditions of its native environment.

Tinus nigricans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
