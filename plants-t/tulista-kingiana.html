Tulista kingiana

Tulista kingiana, commonly known as the King's tulip or the desert tulip, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Tulista kingiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in xeriscaped gardens.
