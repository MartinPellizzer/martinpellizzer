Tussacia

Tussacia, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in rocky or sandy soils. They are characterized by their dense, tufted growth habit, forming low-growing clumps or tussocks. Tussacia species often have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
