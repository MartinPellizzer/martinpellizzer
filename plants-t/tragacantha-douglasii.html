Tragacantha douglasii

Tragacantha douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a distinctive gum, similar to tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industrial and medicinal applications.
