Trianosperma ficifolium var. dissectum

Trianosperma ficifolium var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected fig-leaf plant, is a striking ornamental species characterized by its large, deeply lobed, palmate leaves that resemble the leaves of the fig tree.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green, with a textured, almost velvety surface that adds a lush, tropical aesthetic to any setting. This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers bright, indirect light to maintain its lush appearance. It is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.

The plant's unique leaf structure and adaptability to various growing conditions contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
