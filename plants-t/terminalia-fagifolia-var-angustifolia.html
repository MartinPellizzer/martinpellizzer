Terminalia fagifolia var. angustifolia

Terminalia fagifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf terminalia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its angular, often thorny branches and elongated, lanceolate leaves. The leaves are typically alternate and have a glossy upper surface with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are yellow when ripe.

It is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
