Tragacantha longicaulis

Tragacantha longicaulis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing seeds. It is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and for its historical use in traditional medicine.

Tragacantha longicaulis is also noted for its role in soil stabilization and its ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
