Trichilia reticulata

Trichilia reticulata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and alternate, simple leaves with a reticulate venation pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture and carving.
