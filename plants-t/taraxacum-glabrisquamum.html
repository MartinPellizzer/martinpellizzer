Taraxacum glabrisquamum

Taraxacum glabrisquamum, commonly known as the smooth-squamate dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive seed dispersal mechanism. The plant features a rosette of basal, hairless, and often bluish-green leaves, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are yellow and form a composite head, typical of the dandelion genus.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in open, disturbed habitats.
