Tecoma umbellata var. lanceolata

Tecoma umbellata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
