Trichosalpinx ciliaris

Trichosalpinx ciliaris, commonly known as the woolly spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and velvety foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Trichosalpinx ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
