Taxus baccata subsp. canadensis

Taxus baccata subsp.

canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian yew, is a subspecies of the European yew. It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with slender, drooping branches and dark green foliage.

The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in timber production.
