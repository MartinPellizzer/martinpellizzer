Thymus serpyllum f. tirolensis

Thymus serpyllum f. tirolensis, commonly known as the Alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is occasionally used in culinary and medicinal applications.
