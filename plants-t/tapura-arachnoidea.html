Tapura arachnoidea

Tapura arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider fern, is a unique tropical plant characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that resemble spider webs.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant, though it requires specific care to flourish.
