Tragus pumilus

Tragus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf tragus, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Tragus pumilus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
