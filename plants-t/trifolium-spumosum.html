Trifolium spumosum

Trifolium spumosum, commonly known as the foamy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Trifolium spumosum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
