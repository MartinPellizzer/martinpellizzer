Tsuga canadensis var. wellesiana

Tsuga canadensis var. wellesiana, commonly known as the Wellesian hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to the eastern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like foliage. The leaves are short, flat, and have a bluish-green color with a distinctive white stomatal band. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of conifers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
