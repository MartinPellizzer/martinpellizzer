Talauma luzonensis

Talauma luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and forest clearings. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
