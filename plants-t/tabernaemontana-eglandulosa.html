Tabernaemontana eglandulosa

Tabernaemontana eglandulosa, commonly known as the white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor landscapes.
