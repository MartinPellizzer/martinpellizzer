Triumfetta urticifolia

Triumfetta urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which can cause a stinging sensation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its fruits are small, dry capsules containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
