Turkia kotschyi

Turkia kotschyi, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. Turkia kotschyi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
