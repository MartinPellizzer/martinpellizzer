Taenidium oceanicum

Taenidium oceanicum, commonly known as the sea taenidium, is a unique flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its saline and arid environment. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline.

Taenidium oceanicum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
