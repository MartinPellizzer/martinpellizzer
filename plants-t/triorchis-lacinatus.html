Triorchis lacinatus

Triorchis lacinatus, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate, fringed petals and sepals. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer.

Triorchis lacinatus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
