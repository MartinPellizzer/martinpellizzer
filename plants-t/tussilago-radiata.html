Tussilago radiata

Tussilago radiata, commonly known as the star flower or evening star, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. These flowers are often pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Tussilago radiata is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.
