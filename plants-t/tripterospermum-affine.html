Tripterospermum affine

Tripterospermum affine, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and white, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its botanical features include a short, woody stem and a symbiotic relationship with its host, which provides it with some of its nutritional needs.

The species is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it contributes to the ecological complexity of the canopy.
