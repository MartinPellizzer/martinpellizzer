Tanacetum maresii

Tanacetum maresii, commonly known as the Carpathian tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum maresii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
