Tricyrtis hirta var. minor

Tricyrtis hirta var. minor, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to Japan.

It features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers with a distinctive, hood-like structure that resembles a ghostly face. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
