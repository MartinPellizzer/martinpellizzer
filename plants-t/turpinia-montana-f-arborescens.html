Turpinia montana f. arborescens

Turpinia montana f. arborescens, commonly known as the mountain turpentine bush, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, often found in the Appalachian region. The plant has small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
