Triticum kotschyi

Triticum kotschyi, commonly known as Kotschyi wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its seeds are small and often have a distinctive glume structure, which aids in wind dispersal.

While not widely cultivated, it holds ecological significance as a genetic resource for wheat improvement.
