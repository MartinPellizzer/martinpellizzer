Tithymalus tommasinianus subsp. virgultosus

Tithymalus tommasinianus subsp.

virgultosus, commonly known as the violet tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
