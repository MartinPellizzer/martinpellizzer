Trachymene didiscoides

Trachymene didiscoides, commonly known as the broad-leaved false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a spherical or disc-like inflorescence. Its flowering period typically occurs from late winter to early spring, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Trachymene didiscoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
