Trixis longifolia

Trixis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
