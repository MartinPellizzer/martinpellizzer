Theophrasta integrifolia

Theophrasta integrifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its adaptability to indoor growing conditions. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Theophrasta integrifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
