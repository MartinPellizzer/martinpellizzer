Taraxacum succedens

Taraxacum succedens, commonly known as the succulent dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, wind-dispersed seed capsule. It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and distinctive member of the dandelion genus.
