Thymus serpyllum var. vulgaris

Thymus serpyllum var. vulgaris, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has creeping stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
