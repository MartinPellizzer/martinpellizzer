Thelypteris guyanensis

Thelypteris guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris guyanensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
