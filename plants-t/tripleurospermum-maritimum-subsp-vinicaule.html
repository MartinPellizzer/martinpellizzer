Tripleurospermum maritimum subsp. vinicaule

Tripleurospermum maritimum subsp.

vinicaule, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adapted to tolerate saline and windy conditions.

The plant typically grows in dunes and coastal grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of ray florets surrounded by a spherical involucre of bracts.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
