Tmesipteris gracilis

Tmesipteris gracilis, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it attaches itself to submerged surfaces. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Tmesipteris gracilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
