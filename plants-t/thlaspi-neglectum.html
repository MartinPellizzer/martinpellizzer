Thlaspi neglectum

Thlaspi neglectum, commonly known as common rockcress, is a small, flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. It has a short life cycle and is often overlooked due to its modest appearance.

Thlaspi neglectum is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
