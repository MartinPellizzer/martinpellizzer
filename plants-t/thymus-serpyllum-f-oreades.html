Thymus serpyllum f. oreades

Thymus serpyllum f. oreades, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a subspecies of the common thyme plant.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it well-suited for rock gardens and dry, sunny locations.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
