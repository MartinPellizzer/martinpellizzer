Tillandsia lepidosepala

Tillandsia lepidosepala, commonly known as the silver-tipped air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, silver-tipped leaves that help it capture moisture from the air. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for when provided with proper air circulation and indirect light.
