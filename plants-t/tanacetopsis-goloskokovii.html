Tanacetopsis goloskokovii

Tanacetopsis goloskokovii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its aromatic foliage contains compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Tanacetopsis goloskokovii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
