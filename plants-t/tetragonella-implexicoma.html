Tetragonella implexicoma

Tetragonella implexicoma, commonly known as the tangled tetragon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Tetragonella implexicoma is notable for its ability to form extensive mats, which help prevent soil erosion.
