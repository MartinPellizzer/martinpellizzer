Terminalia chebula var. gangetica

Terminalia chebula var. gangetica, commonly known as the chebulic myrobalan, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, dark reddish-brown, ellipsoid fruits. The fruit contains a single seed and is widely used in traditional medicine for its astringent and antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.
