Trichomanes atrovirens

Trichomanes atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined maidenhair, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with prominent, contrasting veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes atrovirens is notable for its intricate morphology and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
