Tephrosia potosina

Tephrosia potosina, commonly known as the Mexican tephrosia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and pairs of opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Tephrosia potosina is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
