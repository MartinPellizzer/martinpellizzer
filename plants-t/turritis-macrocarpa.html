Turritis macrocarpa

Turritis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant turk's cap, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling, woody stem that can grow up to several feet in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Turritis macrocarpa is a valuable addition to gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
