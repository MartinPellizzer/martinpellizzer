Tristiropsis ferruginea

Tristiropsis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty tristiropsis, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
