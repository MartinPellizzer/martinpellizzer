Thinobia araucana

Thinobia araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria tree, is a coniferous evergreen native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown composed of needle-like leaves. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody, edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
