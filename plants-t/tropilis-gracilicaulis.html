Tropilis gracilicaulis

Tropilis gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed tropilis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Tropilis gracilicaulis produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often borne on long, slender pedicels.
