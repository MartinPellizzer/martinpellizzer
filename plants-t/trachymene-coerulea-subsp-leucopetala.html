Trachymene coerulea subsp. leucopetala

Trachymene coerulea subsp.

leucopetala, commonly known as the white petal trachymene, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, herbaceous stems that often grow in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along watercourses.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
