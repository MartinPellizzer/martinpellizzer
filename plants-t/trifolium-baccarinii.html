Trifolium baccarinii

Trifolium baccarinii, commonly known as the Baccarin's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Trifolium baccarinii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
