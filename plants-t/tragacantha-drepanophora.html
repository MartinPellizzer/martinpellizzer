Tragacantha drepanophora

Tragacantha drepanophora, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
