Taraxacum kojimae

Taraxacum kojimae, commonly known as the Kojima's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Taraxacum kojimae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
