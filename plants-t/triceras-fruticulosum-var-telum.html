Triceras fruticulosum var. telum

Triceras fruticulosum var. telum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a woody stem with a thick, often flaky bark, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
