Trichosporum microphyllum

Trichosporum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved trichosporum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
