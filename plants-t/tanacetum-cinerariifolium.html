Tanacetum cinerariifolium

Tanacetum cinerariifolium, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are finely divided and covered in a powdery coating, giving them a grayish appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
