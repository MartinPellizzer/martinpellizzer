Taxanthema macrophyllum

Taxanthema macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf taxanthemum, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, reducing water loss in hot, dry environments.

Taxanthema macrophyllum is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert conditions.
