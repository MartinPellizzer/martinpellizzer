Trifolium microdon var. pilosum

Trifolium microdon var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy microdon clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive pale green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and forage fields.
