Tibouchina saxicola

Tibouchina saxicola, commonly known as the rock heather or rock myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually small, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tibouchina saxicola is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
