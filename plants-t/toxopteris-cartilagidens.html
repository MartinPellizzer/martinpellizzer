Toxopteris cartilagidens

Toxopteris cartilagidens, commonly known as the cartilaginous shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

The species is notable for its unique morphology, which includes a distinctive, cartilaginous appearance in its young growth stages.
