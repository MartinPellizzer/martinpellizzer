Tabebuia dentata

Tabebuia dentata, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in the dry season. The tree has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, round, woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tabebuia dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
