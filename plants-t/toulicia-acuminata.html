Toulicia acuminata

Toulicia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has smooth, green leaves with a waxy coating, and its sap is milky and toxic. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
