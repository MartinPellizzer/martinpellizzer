Thysanotus triandrus var. pritzelianus

Thysanotus triandrus var. pritzelianus, commonly known as the Pritzel's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
