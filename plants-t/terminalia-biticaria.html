Terminalia biticaria

Terminalia biticaria, commonly known as African wild date, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or processed into jams and beverages.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
