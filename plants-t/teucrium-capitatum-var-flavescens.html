Teucrium capitatum var. flavescens

Teucrium capitatum var. flavescens, commonly known as yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

This species is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
