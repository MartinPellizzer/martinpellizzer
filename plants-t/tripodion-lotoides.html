Tripodion lotoides

Tripodion lotoides, commonly known as the silverleaf tripodion, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
