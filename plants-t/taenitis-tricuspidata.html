Taenitis tricuspidata

Taenitis tricuspidata, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are edible and used in various cuisines.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and stabilizing soil.
