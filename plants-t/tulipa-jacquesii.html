Tulipa jacquesii

Tulipa jacquesii, commonly known as the Jacquesii tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or red eye. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 inches and produces showy blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is a perennial bulbous plant.

Tulipa jacquesii is well-suited for rock gardens and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
