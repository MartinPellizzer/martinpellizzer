Talisia heterodoxa

Talisia heterodoxa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

Talisia heterodoxa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
