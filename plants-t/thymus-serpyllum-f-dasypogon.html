Thymus serpyllum f. dasypogon

Thymus serpyllum f. dasypogon, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, dark green leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
