Taraxacum fabrei

Taraxacum fabrei, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has a rosette-shaped growth pattern. Its taproot is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
