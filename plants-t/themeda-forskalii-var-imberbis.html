Themeda forskalii var. imberbis

Themeda forskalii var. imberbis, commonly known as the smooth grass or narrow-leaved Mitchell grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a significant component of native Australian vegetation.
