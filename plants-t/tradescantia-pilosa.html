Tradescantia pilosa

Tradescantia pilosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf spiderwort, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

The leaves and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
