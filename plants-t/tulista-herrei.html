Tulista herrei

Tulista herrei, commonly known as the Mexican tulip, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces numerous small, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tulista herrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
