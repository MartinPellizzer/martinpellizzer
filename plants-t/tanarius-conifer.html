Tanarius conifer

Tanarius conifer, commonly known as the red pine, is a coniferous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The tree produces cones that mature in the second year, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The red pine is an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
