Tilia pannonica

Tilia pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian lime, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round, green fruits called limes, which are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forests.

Tilia pannonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
