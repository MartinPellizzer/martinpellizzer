Taraxacum taeniformatum

Taraxacum taeniformatum, commonly known as the narrow-stripe dandelion, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common yet ecologically significant weed in various regions.
