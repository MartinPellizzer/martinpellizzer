Tephrocactus longiarticulatus

Tephrocactus longiarticulatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its stems are covered in short, pale spines, and it is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique growth form.
