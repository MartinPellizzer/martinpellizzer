Thelypteris unidentata

Thelypteris unidentata, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori covered by a thin, membranous indusium. Thelypteris unidentata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it forms dense colonies.

Its delicate appearance and ecological adaptability make it a common sight in eastern North American forests.
