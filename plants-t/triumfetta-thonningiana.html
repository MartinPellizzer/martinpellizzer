Triumfetta thonningiana

Triumfetta thonningiana, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
