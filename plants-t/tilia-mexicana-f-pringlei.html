Tilia mexicana f. pringlei

Tilia mexicana f. pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican lime tree, is a botanical variant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit, a small green drupe, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
