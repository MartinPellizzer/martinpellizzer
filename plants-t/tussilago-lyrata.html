Tussilago lyrata

Tussilago lyrata, commonly known as the showy cow-wheat, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lyre-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tussilago lyrata is often found growing in clusters and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by indigenous peoples.
