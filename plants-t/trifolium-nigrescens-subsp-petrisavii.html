Trifolium nigrescens subsp. petrisavii

Trifolium nigrescens subsp.

petrisavii, commonly known as the darkening clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
