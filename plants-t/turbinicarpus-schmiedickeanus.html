Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus, commonly known as the Schmiedicke's turbinated cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
