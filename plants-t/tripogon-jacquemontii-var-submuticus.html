Tripogon jacquemontii var. submuticus

Tripogon jacquemontii var. submuticus, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are typically green or pale in color. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and is often used in native plant landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
