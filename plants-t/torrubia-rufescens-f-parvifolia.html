Torrubia rufescens f. parvifolia

Torrubia rufescens f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf red willow, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and in wetlands. Its flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
