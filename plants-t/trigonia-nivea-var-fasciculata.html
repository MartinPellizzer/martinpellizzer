Trigonia nivea var. fasciculata

Trigonia nivea var. fasciculata, commonly known as the white-flowered trigonia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, waxy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
