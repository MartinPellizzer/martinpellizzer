Tragacantha thurberi

Tragacantha thurberi, commonly known as the Thurber's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a gelatinous resin, which has been historically used in various traditional applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions.
