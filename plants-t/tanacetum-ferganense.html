Tanacetum ferganense

Tanacetum ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
