Trichoneura ciliata

Trichoneura ciliata, commonly known as the silky fringed orchid, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike, often with distinctive fringed petals. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is typically found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
