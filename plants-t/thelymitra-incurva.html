Thelymitra incurva

Thelymitra incurva, commonly known as the sickle-leaf blue-tongue orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Thelymitra incurva is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit and floral structure are typical of many terrestrial orchids in the genus Thelymitra.
