Taraxacum virellum

Taraxacum virellum, commonly known as the green-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces a single flowering stem topped with a spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

Taraxacum virellum is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
