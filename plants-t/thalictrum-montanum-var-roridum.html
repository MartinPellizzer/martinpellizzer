Thalictrum montanum var. roridum

Thalictrum montanum var. roridum, commonly known as the mountain meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. It is often found at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 8,000 feet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
