Taraxacum elegantiforme

Taraxacum elegantiforme, commonly known as the elegant dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head. The plant is native to Central Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
