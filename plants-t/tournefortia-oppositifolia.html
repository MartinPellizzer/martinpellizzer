Tournefortia oppositifolia

Tournefortia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough or hairy texture on the upper surface. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, woody fruits.

It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
