Taeniophyllum calyptrochilum

Taeniophyllum calyptrochilum, commonly known as the shield-lip orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small and clustered, with pale green to yellowish petals and sepals.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
