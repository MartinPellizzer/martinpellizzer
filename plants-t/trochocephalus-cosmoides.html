Trochocephalus cosmoides

Trochocephalus cosmoides, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
