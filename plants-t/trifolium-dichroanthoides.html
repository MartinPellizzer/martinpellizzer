Trifolium dichroanthoides

Trifolium dichroanthoides, commonly known as the two-colored clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with two distinct leaflets of different colors. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium dichroanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
