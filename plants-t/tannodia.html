Tannodia

Tannodia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Tannziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are composed of small, overlapping leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Tannodia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
