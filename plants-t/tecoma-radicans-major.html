Tecoma radicans major

Tecoma radicans major, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender, woody stems that can reach lengths of several meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma radicans major is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
