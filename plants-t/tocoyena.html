Tocoyena

Tocoyena, commonly known as the "wild banana," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Musaceae family, which also includes bananas and plantains. The plant features large, broad leaves and produces clusters of edible, elongated fruits that resemble bananas. Tocoyena is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of potassium and fiber.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
