Trillium tinctorium

Trillium tinctorium, commonly known as the St. John's Wort of the woods, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its three large, waxy leaves and a single, showy flower with three petals. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the use of its flowers in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Trillium tinctorium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
