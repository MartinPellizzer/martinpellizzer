Tanacetum ulutavicum

Tanacetum ulutavicum, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Tanacetum ulutavicum is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
