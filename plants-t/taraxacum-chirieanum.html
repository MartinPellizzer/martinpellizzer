Taraxacum chirieanum

Taraxacum chirieanum, commonly known as the Chiriean dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and form a single composite head.

The species is noted for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a resilient and widespread flora.
