Tecomanthe volubilis subsp. tenax

Tecomanthe volubilis subsp.

tenax, commonly known as the climbing yellow bell, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
