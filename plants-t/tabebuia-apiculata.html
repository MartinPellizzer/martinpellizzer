Tabebuia apiculata

Tabebuia apiculata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The tree's bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.
