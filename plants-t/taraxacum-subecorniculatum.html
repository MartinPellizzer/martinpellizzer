Taraxacum subecorniculatum

Taraxacum subecorniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-corned dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Central Asia, this species is often found in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the dandelion genus.
