Triticum schimperi

Triticum schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian wheat, is a wild grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces compact grains that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat varieties.
