Trifolium luxurians

Trifolium luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant clover, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium luxurians is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
