Teucrium vincentinum

Teucrium vincentinum, commonly known as Vincent's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are blue to purple in hue, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
