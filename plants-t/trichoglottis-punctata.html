Trichoglottis punctata

Trichoglottis punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of distinctive white or yellow spots on its leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Trichoglottis punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique foliage.
