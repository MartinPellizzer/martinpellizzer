Trisetum flavescens var. macranthum

Trisetum flavescens var. macranthum, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
