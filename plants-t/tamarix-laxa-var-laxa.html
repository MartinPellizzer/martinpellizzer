Tamarix laxa var. laxa

Tamarix laxa var. laxa, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces tiny, pink or red flowers in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are extensive and can access deep groundwater, contributing to its resilience in saline soils.

Tamarix laxa var. laxa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
