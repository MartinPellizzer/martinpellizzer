Tephrocactus articulatus var. papyracanthus

Tephrocactus articulatus var. papyracanthus, commonly known as the parchment spines cactus, is a unique cactus species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its segmented, cylindrical stems that are covered in soft, white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but attractive. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
