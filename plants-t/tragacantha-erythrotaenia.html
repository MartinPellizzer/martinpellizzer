Tragacantha erythrotaenia

Tragacantha erythrotaenia, commonly known as the red-stemmed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant has been used historically for its gum, which has various industrial and medicinal applications.
