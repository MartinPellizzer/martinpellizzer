Telinaria holopetala

Telinaria holopetala, commonly known as the white-flowered telinaria, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that bloom in late spring, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically solitary, emerging from the leaf axils, and are known for their fragrant aroma.

Telinaria holopetala is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
