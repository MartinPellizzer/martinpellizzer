Tanacetum stoliczkae

Tanacetum stoliczkae, commonly known as Stoliczka's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent, while its yellow flower heads consist of numerous tiny florets.

Tanacetum stoliczkae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
