Trichoglottis ledermannii

Trichoglottis ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's trichoglottis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its roots are fibrous and often form a dense network, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Trichoglottis ledermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
