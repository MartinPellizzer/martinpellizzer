Trichoscypha lucens

Trichoscypha lucens, commonly known as the golden star tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody fruits that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used for thatching and as a source of fiber.

Trichoscypha lucens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important component of the rainforest understory.
