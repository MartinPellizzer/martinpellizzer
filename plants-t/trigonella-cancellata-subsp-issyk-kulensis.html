Trigonella cancellata subsp. issyk-kulensis

Trigonella cancellata subsp.

issyk-kulensis, commonly known as the Issyk-Kul lentil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop in local agriculture. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological and nutritional significance, it is also of interest for conservation and sustainable farming practices.
