Trikalis triflora

Trikalis triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered trikalis, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Trikalis triflora is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks.
