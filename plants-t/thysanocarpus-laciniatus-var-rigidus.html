Thysanocarpus laciniatus var. rigidus

Thysanocarpus laciniatus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid fringed lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces distinctive, elongated fruit capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
