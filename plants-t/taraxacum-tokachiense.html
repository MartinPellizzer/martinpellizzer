Taraxacum tokachiense

Taraxacum tokachiense, commonly known as the Tokachi dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. Its flowering stems bear a single, yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball.

This species is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments.
