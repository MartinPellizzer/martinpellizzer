Tradescantia ohiensis f. pilosa

Tradescantia ohiensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy swordleaf.

This variant of the common spiderwort is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a ground cover.
