Tragopogon persicus

Tragopogon persicus, commonly known as Persian hawk's beard, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by distinctive bristly seed heads.

Tragopogon persicus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
