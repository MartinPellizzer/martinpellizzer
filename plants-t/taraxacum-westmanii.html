Taraxacum westmanii

Taraxacum westmanii, commonly known as the Westman's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, showy yellow flower. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly in regions of Washington and Oregon. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

The species is notable for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
