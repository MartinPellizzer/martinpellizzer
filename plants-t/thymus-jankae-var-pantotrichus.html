Thymus jankae var. pantotrichus

Thymus jankae var. pantotrichus, commonly known as the Kashmir thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and India.

It features compact, creeping stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
