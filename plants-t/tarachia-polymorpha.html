Tarachia polymorpha

Tarachia polymorpha, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that help it retain water in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its adaptations include a deep root system and thick, waxy cuticles to minimize water loss.

Tarachia polymorpha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
