Teijsmanniodendron holophyllum

Teijsmanniodendron holophyllum, commonly known as the Burmese flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain seeds covered in a fleshy aril.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
