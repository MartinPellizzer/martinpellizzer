Taonabo congestiflora

Taonabo congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered taonabo, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
