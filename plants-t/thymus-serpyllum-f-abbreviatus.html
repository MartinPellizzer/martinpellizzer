Thymus serpyllum f. abbreviatus

Thymus serpyllum f. abbreviatus, commonly known as the dwarf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
