Tetraphyle furcata

Tetraphyle furcata, commonly known as the four-branch forked fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its intricate branching pattern.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant's fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive forked structure that gives it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
