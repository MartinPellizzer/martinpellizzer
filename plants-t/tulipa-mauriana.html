Tulipa mauriana

Tulipa mauriana, commonly known as the Canary Island tulip, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers typically bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
