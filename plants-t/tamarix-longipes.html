Tamarix longipes

Tamarix longipes, commonly known as the longipes tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in elongated clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tamarix longipes is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological significance in stabilizing sandy soils.
