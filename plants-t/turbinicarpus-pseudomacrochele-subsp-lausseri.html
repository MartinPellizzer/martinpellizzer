Turbinicarpus pseudomacrochele subsp. lausseri

Turbinicarpus pseudomacrochele subsp.

lausseri, commonly known as the Lausser's Turbinicarpus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
