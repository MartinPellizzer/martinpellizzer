Tulipa acutiflora

Tulipa acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in early spring.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that die back after flowering.
