Tolumnia lemoniana

Tolumnia lemoniana, commonly known as the lemon tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a citrusy flavor. Tolumnia lemoniana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is widely cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
