Trichoscypha smeathmannii

Trichoscypha smeathmannii, commonly known as the African silver maple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
