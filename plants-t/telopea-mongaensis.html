Telopea mongaensis

Telopea mongaensis, commonly known as the Mongaensis waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pinkish-red flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal heathlands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Telopea mongaensis is a valuable species in horticulture due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
