Triticum timopheevii subsp. militinae

Triticum timopheevii subsp.

militinae, commonly known as the military rye, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists and geneticists due to its potential for breeding with cultivated rye varieties.
