Tripterospermum chinense var. linearifolium

Tripterospermum chinense var. linearifolium, commonly known as the Chinese starvine, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the family Rubiaceae.
