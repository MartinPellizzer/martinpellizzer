Teucrium polium var. expassum

Teucrium polium var. expassum, commonly known as the Spanish germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, mint-like fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
