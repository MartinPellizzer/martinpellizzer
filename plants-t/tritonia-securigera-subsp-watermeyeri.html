Tritonia securigera subsp. watermeyeri

Tritonia securigera subsp.

watermeyeri, commonly known as the Watermeyer's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
