Trillium apetalon var. atropurpureocarpum

Trillium apetalon var. atropurpureocarpum, commonly known as the purple-fruit trillium, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its three broadly ovate leaves and a single, showy flower with three petal-like sepals. The plant produces a unique, elongated fruit that turns a deep purple as it matures, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
