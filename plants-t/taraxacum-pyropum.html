Taraxacum pyropum

Taraxacum pyropum, commonly known as the fireweed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It produces a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum pyropum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
