Tupeia cunninghamii

Tupeia cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Tupeia cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
