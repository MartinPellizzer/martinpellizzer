Teucrium ramaswamii

Teucrium ramaswamii, commonly known as Ramaswamii germander, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
