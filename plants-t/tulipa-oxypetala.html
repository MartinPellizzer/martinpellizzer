Tulipa oxypetala

Tulipa oxypetala, commonly known as the sharp-petaled tulip, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant produces showy, single blooms on slender stems, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 inches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Tulipa oxypetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
