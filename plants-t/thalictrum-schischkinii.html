Thalictrum schischkinii

Thalictrum schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with compound leaves that are divided into numerous small, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in upright, branched inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor the plant in rocky or well-drained soils.

Thalictrum schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and unique floral display.
