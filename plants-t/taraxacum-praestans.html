Taraxacum praestans

Taraxacum praestans, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of fluffy white seed heads. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed or cultivated ground. Its leaves are basal, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The showy dandelion is often considered a weed but is also valued for its medicinal properties and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
