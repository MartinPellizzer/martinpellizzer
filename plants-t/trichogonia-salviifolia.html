Trichogonia salviifolia

Trichogonia salviifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf trichogonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Trichogonia salviifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
