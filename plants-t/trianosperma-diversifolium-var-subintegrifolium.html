Trianosperma diversifolium var. subintegrifolium

Trianosperma diversifolium var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf trianosperma, is a species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically broad and have a serrated margin, with a gradual transition from the leaf blade to the petiole. It is native to specific regions with moist, temperate climates, where it thrives in shaded, well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique variation in leaf structure, which distinguishes it from other varieties within the species.
