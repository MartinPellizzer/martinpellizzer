Tecoma lasianthera

Tecoma lasianthera, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Tecoma lasianthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
