Thalictrum leuconotum

Thalictrum leuconotum, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, spicate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soil.

Thalictrum leuconotum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
