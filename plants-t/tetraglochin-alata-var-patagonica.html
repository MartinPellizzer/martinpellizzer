Tetraglochin alata var. patagonica

Tetraglochin alata var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian winged grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a stabilizing plant in erosion-prone areas.
