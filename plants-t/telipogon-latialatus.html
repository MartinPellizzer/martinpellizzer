Telipogon latialatus

Telipogon latialatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive strategy involves mycorrhizal associations, which are essential for nutrient uptake.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
