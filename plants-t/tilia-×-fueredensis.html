Tilia × fueredensis

Tilia × fueredensis, commonly known as the Hungarian linden, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round nuts enclosed in a woody capsule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
