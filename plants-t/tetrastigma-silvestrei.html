Tetrastigma silvestrei

Tetrastigma silvestrei, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tetrastigma silvestrei is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
