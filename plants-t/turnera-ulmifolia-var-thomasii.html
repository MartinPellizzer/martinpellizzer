Turnera ulmifolia var. thomasii

Turnera ulmifolia var. thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's turnera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with red or purple markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
