Tournefortia sarmentosa var. magnifolia

Tournefortia sarmentosa var. magnifolia, commonly known as the showy tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
