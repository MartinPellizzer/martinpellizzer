Tipuana sericea

Tipuana sericea, commonly known as the silky tipuana, is a fast-growing, deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and feathery, compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing oval seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental and reforestation purposes.

Its rapid growth rate and adaptability make it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
