Tapeinosperma clethroides

Tapeinosperma clethroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf clethra, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins.
