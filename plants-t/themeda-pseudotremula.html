Themeda pseudotremula

Themeda pseudotremula, commonly known as the slender three-awn, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted shoots and narrow, pale green leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets with three awns, giving it its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
