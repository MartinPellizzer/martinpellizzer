Teucrium polium subsp. dunense

Teucrium polium subsp.

dunense, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its fragrant herbs, and is often used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies dunense is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the main species.
