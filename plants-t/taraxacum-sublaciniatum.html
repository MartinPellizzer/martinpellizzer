Taraxacum sublaciniatum

Taraxacum sublaciniatum, commonly known as the serrated dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lance-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The fruit is a pilose achene, which is dispersed by wind after the flower head matures.
