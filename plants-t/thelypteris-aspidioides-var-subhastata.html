Thelypteris aspidioides var. subhastata

Thelypteris aspidioides var. subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Thelypteris.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
