Trevirana candida

Trevirana candida, commonly known as the white trevirana, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a light green color.

Trevirana candida is noted for its low growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
