Tydaea ocellata var. picta

Tydaea ocellata var. picta, commonly known as the spotted grass or painted grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that often display intricate patterns of white, yellow, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural settings.
