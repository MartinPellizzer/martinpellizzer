Tobinia punctata

Tobinia punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with distinctive spots on the petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Tobinia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
