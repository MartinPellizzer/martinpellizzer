Tanacetum tortuosum

Tanacetum tortuosum, commonly known as the meadow button.

This perennial herb is native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
