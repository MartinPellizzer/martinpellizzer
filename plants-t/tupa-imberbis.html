Tupa imberbis

Tupa imberbis, commonly known as the naked-hair tupa, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Tupa imberbis is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
