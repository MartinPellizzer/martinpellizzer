Trollius pumilus var. alpinus

Trollius pumilus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine globeflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
