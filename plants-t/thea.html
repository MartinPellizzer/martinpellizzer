Thea

Thea, commonly known as the tea plant, is a flowering plant in the genus *Camellia*, belonging to the family Theaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, where it has been cultivated for thousands of years. The plant produces small, dark green leaves that are harvested and processed to make various types of tea. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and bloom in spring.

Thea is valued not only for its economic importance in the tea industry but also for its ornamental qualities.
