Taeniophyllum recurvirostrum

Taeniophyllum recurvirostrum, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped fronds that form a cradle-like structure, resembling a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its broad, fibrous leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
