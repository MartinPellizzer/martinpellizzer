Tecoma odontodiscus

Tecoma odontodiscus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and are arranged in terminal clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
