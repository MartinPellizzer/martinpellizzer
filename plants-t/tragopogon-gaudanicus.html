Tragopogon gaudanicus

Tragopogon gaudanicus, commonly known as the Gaudani's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tragopogon gaudanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
