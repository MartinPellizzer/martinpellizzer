Tragus racemosus var. erectus

Tragus racemosus var. erectus, commonly known as the erect tragus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
