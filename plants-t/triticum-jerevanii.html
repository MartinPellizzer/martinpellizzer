Triticum jerevanii

Triticum jerevanii, commonly known as Armenian wheat, is a wild relative of modern wheat species.

It is native to the highlands of Armenia and surrounding regions in the Caucasus. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescence. It possesses a high genetic diversity, making it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and tough, glaucous stems adapted to harsh mountainous environments.
