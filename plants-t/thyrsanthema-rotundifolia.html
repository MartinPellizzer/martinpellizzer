Thyrsanthema rotundifolia

Thyrsanthema rotundifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, rounded leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
