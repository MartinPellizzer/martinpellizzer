Teucrium aristatum subsp. cravense

Teucrium aristatum subsp.

cravense, commonly known as the Alpine germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in mountainous areas of the Carpathian and Alps regions.
