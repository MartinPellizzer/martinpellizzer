Teucrium pseudohyssopus

Teucrium pseudohyssopus, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Teucrium pseudohyssopus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
