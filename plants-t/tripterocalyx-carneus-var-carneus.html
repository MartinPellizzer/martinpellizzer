Tripterocalyx carneus var. carneus

Tripterocalyx carneus var. carneus, commonly known as the red-flowered rock fig, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and striking red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
