Torenia fordii

Torenia fordii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Torenia fordii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
