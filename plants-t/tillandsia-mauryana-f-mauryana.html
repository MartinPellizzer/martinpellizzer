Tillandsia mauryana f. mauryana

Tillandsia mauryana f. mauryana, commonly known as the Mauryana air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives by attaching itself to trees or other surfaces without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, narrow leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps collect moisture from the air. It produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange.

This species is native to tropical regions of South America and is popular among air plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
