Terpsichore flexuosa

Terpsichore flexuosa, commonly known as the winding beauty, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems that often coil around other vegetation, giving it a unique, almost vine-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with a faintly fragrant scent, which bloom during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are elongated and slightly serrated, with a glossy green surface that reflects light in a striking manner.

Terpsichore flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
