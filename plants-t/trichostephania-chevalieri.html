Trichostephania chevalieri

Trichostephania chevalieri, commonly known as the Malabar sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its medicinal properties.
