Trembleya parviflora var. widgrenii

Trembleya parviflora var. widgrenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
