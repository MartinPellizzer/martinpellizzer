Triticum brizoides

Triticum brizoides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Triticum brizoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
