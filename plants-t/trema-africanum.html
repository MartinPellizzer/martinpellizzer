Trema africanum

Trema africanum, commonly known as the African trema, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental foliage.
