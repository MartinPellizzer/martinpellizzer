Tephrocactus retrospinus

Tephrocactus retrospinus, commonly known as the backward-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically solitary and located at the tips of the stems. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in its dry environment.

Tephrocactus retrospinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
