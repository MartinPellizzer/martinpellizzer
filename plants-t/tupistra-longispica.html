Tupistra longispica

Tupistra longispica, commonly known as the long-spined hawthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Tupistra longispica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.
