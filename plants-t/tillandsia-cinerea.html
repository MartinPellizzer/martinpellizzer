Tillandsia cinerea

Tillandsia cinerea, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its grayish-green, strap-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes. These trichomes help the plant absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Tillandsia cinerea is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
