Trachelium postii

Trachelium postii, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold, dry conditions of its habitat.

Trachelium postii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
