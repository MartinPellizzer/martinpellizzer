Thalictrum grey-wilsonii

Thalictrum grey-wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silky down. The plant produces clusters of small, showy, yellow-green flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Thalictrum grey-wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
