Taraxacum adsimile

Taraxacum adsimile, commonly known as the similar dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate, hairy leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism via a pappus, which allows its seeds to be carried by the wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Taraxacum adsimile is a hardy perennial that is closely related to other dandelion species and is often mistaken for Taraxacum officinale.
