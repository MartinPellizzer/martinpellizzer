Tulipa browniana

Tulipa browniana, commonly known as the Brown's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Tulipa browniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
