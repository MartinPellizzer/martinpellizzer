Trembleya laniflora var. genuina

Trembleya laniflora var. genuina, commonly known as the silky trembleya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
