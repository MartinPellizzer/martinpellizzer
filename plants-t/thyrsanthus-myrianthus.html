Thyrsanthus myrianthus

Thyrsanthus myrianthus, commonly known as the silky thyrsothamnus, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
