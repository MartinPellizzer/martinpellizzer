Thalictrum apiifolium

Thalictrum apiifolium, commonly known as meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Thalictrum apiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
