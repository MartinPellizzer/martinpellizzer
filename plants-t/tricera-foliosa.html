Tricera foliosa

Tricera foliosa, commonly known as the three-leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

Each leaf is composed of three broad, lance-shaped lobes that emerge from a central stem. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Tricera foliosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
