Thuja triangularis

Thuja triangularis, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It has a distinctive pyramidal shape with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
