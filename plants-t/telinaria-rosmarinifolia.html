Telinaria rosmarinifolia

Telinaria rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, linear leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and pinene, contributing to its distinctive scent and therapeutic properties.
