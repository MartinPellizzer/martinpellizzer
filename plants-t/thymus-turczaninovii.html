Thymus turczaninovii

Thymus turczaninovii, commonly known as Turczaninov's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features small, fragrant leaves and produces pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic compounds.

Thymus turczaninovii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
