Taraxacum rubiginans

Taraxacum rubiginans, commonly known as the red-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Taraxacum rubiginans is a perennial herb that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
