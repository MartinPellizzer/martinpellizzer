Tryphostemma lanceolatum

Tryphostemma lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae). It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
