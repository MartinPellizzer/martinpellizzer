Tapiphyllum tanganyikense

Tapiphyllum tanganyikense, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the freshwater lakes of Central Africa, particularly Lake Tanganyika.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a short, stout stem with roots that anchor it to the lakebed. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure.

Tapiphyllum tanganyikense plays an important role in the ecosystem by contributing to water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
