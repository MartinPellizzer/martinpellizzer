Trichoglottis flexuosa

Trichoglottis flexuosa, commonly known as the wavy-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features elongated, wavy leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a short, pendant spur.

Trichoglottis flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
