Thalictrum gallicum subsp. stipellatum

Thalictrum gallicum subsp.

stipellatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, compound leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along its stem.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to wetland restoration projects and native plant gardens.
