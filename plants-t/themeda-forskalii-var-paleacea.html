Themeda forskalii var. paleacea

Themeda forskalii var. paleacea, commonly known as the palea grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, pale green to bluish-green inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowering structures are compact and often clustered at the base of the plant.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
