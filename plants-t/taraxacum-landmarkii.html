Taraxacum landmarkii

Taraxacum landmarkii, commonly known as the Landmark dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is herbaceous, perennial, and has a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
