Tamarix pallasii var. albiflora

Tamarix pallasii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to retain water efficiently. It is often found in saline soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various insects and birds.
