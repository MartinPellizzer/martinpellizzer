Trisetum laxum

Trisetum laxum, commonly known as the slender field grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often drooping stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-awned florets. Trisetum laxum typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
