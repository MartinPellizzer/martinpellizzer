Taraxacum pseudosuecicum

Taraxacum pseudosuecicum, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to northern Europe, this plant thrives in moist, temperate environments. It produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with white fluff for wind dispersal.

The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
