Tephrocactus crispicrinitus

Tephrocactus crispicrinitus, commonly known as the curly-hair cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly bristles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
