Teucrium luteum subsp. luteum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

luteum, commonly known as yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically valuable in traditional herbal remedies.
