Trichosanthes multiloba

Trichosanthes multiloba, commonly known as the Chinese trumpet vine, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, orange-yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with lobed edges. Its fruit is a spiny, elongated capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Trichosanthes multiloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
