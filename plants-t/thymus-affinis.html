Thymus affinis

Thymus affinis, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications due to its strong, pungent aroma.

Thymus affinis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
