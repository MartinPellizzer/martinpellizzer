Trichoglottis subviolacea

Trichoglottis subviolacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and possesses dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, which is a notable botanical feature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Trichoglottis subviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
