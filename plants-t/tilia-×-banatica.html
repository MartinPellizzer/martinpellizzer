Tilia × banatica

Tilia × banatica, commonly known as the Hungarian lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its shade and aesthetic qualities.
