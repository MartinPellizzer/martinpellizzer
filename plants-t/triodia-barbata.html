Triodia barbata

Triodia barbata, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss.

Triodia barbata is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in stabilizing soil in coastal and inland dunes.
