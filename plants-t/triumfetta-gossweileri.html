Triumfetta gossweileri

Triumfetta gossweileri, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
