Tragacantha trigonocarpa

Tragacantha trigonocarpa, commonly known as the trigonocarpic tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
