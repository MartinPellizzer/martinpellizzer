Tylecodon similis

Tylecodon similis, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick, woody stem with dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are fleshy, oval, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
