Thalictrum ramosum

Thalictrum ramosum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Thalictrum ramosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
