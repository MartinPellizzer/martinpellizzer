Tulipa saxatilis

Tulipa saxatilis, commonly known as the rock tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sturdy form and is well adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring. It is a cold-hardy species that thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Due to its rugged habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
