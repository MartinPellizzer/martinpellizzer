Tofieldia calyculata var. collina

Tofieldia calyculata var. collina, commonly known as the coastal tofieldia, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in spikes. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a characteristic species of coastal ecosystems.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
