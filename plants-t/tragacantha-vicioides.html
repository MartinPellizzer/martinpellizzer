Tragacantha vicioides

Tragacantha vicioides, commonly known as the Persian trumpet flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that resemble a trumpet. The plant produces a yellow to orange pod containing seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Tragacantha vicioides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
