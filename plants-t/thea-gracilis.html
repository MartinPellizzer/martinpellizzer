Thea gracilis

Thea gracilis, commonly known as the slender thea, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Thea gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for shady or semi-sunny garden spaces.
