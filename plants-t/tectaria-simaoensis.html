Tectaria simaoensis

Tectaria simaoensis, commonly known as the Simao shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a complex venation pattern.

Tectaria simaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
