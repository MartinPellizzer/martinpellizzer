Trifolium ochroleucon var. grancarovii

Trifolium ochroleucon var. grancarovii, commonly known as the red clover variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its medicinal and forage value due to its high nutritional content and traditional use in herbal remedies.
