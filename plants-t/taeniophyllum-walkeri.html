Taeniophyllum walkeri

Taeniophyllum walkeri, commonly known as the walking fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its creeping rhizomes that produce upright, branching fronds.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant's ability to "walk" is due to its rhizomes spreading along the ground, allowing it to colonize new areas. Its fronds are typically lance-shaped with a serrated margin, and it produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori.

Taeniophyllum walkeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
