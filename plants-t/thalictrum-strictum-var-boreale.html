Thalictrum strictum var. boreale

Thalictrum strictum var. boreale, commonly known as the northern meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are arranged in compound umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
