Taraxacum stenophyllum

Taraxacum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a single, erect flowering stem that bears a crown of yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in temperate regions, often in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of tiny, parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
