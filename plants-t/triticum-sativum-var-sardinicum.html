Triticum sativum var. sardinicum

Triticum sativum var. sardinicum, commonly known as Sardinian wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic makeup and adaptation to specific environmental conditions.

This variety is native to the island of Sardinia and exhibits traits such as smaller grains and a shorter stature compared to standard wheat varieties. It possesses a distinctive genome composition that includes contributions from wild wheat species, enhancing its genetic diversity. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is valued for its resilience to local pests and diseases.

Its study contributes to the understanding of wheat evolution and the development of more robust crop varieties.
