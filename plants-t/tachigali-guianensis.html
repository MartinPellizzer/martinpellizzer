Tachigali guianensis

Tachigali guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese tachigali, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
