Taraxacum maurophyllum

Taraxacum maurophyllum, commonly known as the black-leafed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in spring. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelions and related plants.
