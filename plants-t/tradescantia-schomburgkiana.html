Tradescantia schomburgkiana

Tradescantia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the wandering jew or snake plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that often have a reddish or purple tinge along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to a variety of growing conditions and is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Its ability to thrive in low light and minimal water makes it a popular choice for novice gardeners.
