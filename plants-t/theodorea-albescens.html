Theodorea albescens

Theodorea albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a distinctive, narrow leaf structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of rainforests. Its flowers bloom during the wet season and are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Theodorea albescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
