Trichilia schomburgkii

Trichilia schomburgkii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, including countries like Nigeria, Uganda, and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
