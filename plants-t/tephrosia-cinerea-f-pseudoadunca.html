Tephrosia cinerea f. pseudoadunca

Tephrosia cinerea f. pseudoadunca, commonly known as the pale tephrosia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The flowers are small, white to pale blue, and arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
