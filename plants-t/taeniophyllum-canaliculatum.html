Taeniophyllum canaliculatum

Taeniophyllum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed liverwort, is a small, leafy liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with closely spaced, narrow leaves that have distinct canals. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity.

Its simple structure and specialized leaf anatomy are adaptations to its humid, low-light habitat.
