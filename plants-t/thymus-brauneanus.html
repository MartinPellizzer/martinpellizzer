Thymus brauneanus

Thymus brauneanus, commonly known as Braunean thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus brauneanus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
