Taraxacum patens

Taraxacum patens, commonly known as the creeping dandelion, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with fleshy, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Taraxacum patens is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons.
