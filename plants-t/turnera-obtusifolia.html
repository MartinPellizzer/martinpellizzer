Turnera obtusifolia

Turnera obtusifolia, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble a hat. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it often grows in shaded, moist environments. It is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
