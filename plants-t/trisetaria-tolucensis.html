Trisetaria tolucensis

Trisetaria tolucensis, commonly known as the Tolucense grass, is a perennial plant native to the grasslands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to dry conditions. Trisetaria tolucensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with low nutrient availability.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
