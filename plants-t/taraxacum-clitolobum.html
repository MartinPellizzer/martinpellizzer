Taraxacum clitolobum

Taraxacum clitolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is similar to other dandelion species but can be distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
