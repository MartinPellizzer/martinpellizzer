Tephroseris tichomirovii

Tephroseris tichomirovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tephroseris tichomirovii is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and is often found in open, well-drained areas.
