Taraxacum semilunare

Taraxacum semilunare, commonly known as the moonflower dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale yellow composite flower head that resembles a small sunflower. The plant produces a hollow, bladelike stem with a milky sap that is typical of the genus Taraxacum. Its leaves are basal, with a lobed or toothed margin, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in temperate climates.
