Trifolium dasyphyllum subsp. uintense

Trifolium dasyphyllum subsp.

uintense, commonly known as Uinta mountain clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
