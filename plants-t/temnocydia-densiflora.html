Temnocydia densiflora

Temnocydia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered densely along its stems. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs.

Temnocydia densiflora plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain insects and birds.
