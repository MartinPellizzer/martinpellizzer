Tephrosia pedicellata

Tephrosia pedicellata, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Tephrosia pedicellata also has potential in agroforestry and as a forage crop for livestock.
