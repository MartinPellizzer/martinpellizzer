Taraxacum patagonicum

Taraxacum patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the Patagonian region of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum patagonicum is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
