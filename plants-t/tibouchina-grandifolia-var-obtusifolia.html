Tibouchina grandifolia var. obtusifolia

Tibouchina grandifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. It blooms during the warmer months, typically from spring to early summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
