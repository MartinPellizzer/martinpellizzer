Tetrarrhena drummondiana

Tetrarrhena drummondiana, commonly known as the Drummond's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

Tetrarrhena drummondiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
