Tanacetum zangezuricum

Tanacetum zangezuricum, commonly known as the Zangezur tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

Tanacetum zangezuricum is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate foliage and floral display.
