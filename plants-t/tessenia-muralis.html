Tessenia muralis

Tessenia muralis, commonly known as the wall ivy, is a climbing plant native to temperate regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Tessenia muralis thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often found growing on walls and rocky surfaces.

Its ability to adhere to surfaces makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in urban settings.
