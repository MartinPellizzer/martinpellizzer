Tithymalus alatavicus

Tithymalus alatavicus, commonly known as the Turkestan tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native region.
