Talinum aurantiacum var. angustissimum

Talinum aurantiacum var. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf redroot, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
