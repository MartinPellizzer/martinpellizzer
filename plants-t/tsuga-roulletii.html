Tsuga roulletii

Tsuga roulletii, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that typically mature and disperse its seeds in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Tsuga roulletii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide year-round foliage.
