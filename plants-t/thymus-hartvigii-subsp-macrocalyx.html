Thymus hartvigii subsp. macrocalyx

Thymus hartvigii subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
