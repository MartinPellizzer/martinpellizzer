Taraxacum pictidum

Taraxacum pictidum, commonly known as the painted dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and distinctive, finely hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are deeply lobed and form a basal rosette, while the flowers produce lightweight seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
