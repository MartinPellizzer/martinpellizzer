Trasus oederi

Trasus oederi, commonly known as the Oeder's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Trasus oederi is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Campanulaceae family.
