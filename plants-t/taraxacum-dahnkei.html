Taraxacum dahnkei

Taraxacum dahnkei, commonly known as the Dahnke's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize new areas.

The plant is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
