Tetrastigma membranaceum

Tetrastigma membranaceum, commonly known as the fragrant grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its climbing habit and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
