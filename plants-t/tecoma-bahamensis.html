Tecoma bahamensis

Tecoma bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma bahamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
