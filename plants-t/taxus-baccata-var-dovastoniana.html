Taxus baccata var. dovastoniana

Taxus baccata var. dovastoniana, commonly known as the Dovastonian yew, is a rare variant of the common yew.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring dark green, elongated leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
