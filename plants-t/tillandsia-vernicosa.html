Tillandsia vernicosa

Tillandsia vernicosa, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. These hairs help the plant absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Tillandsia vernicosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
