Tripteris monocephala

Tripteris monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed tripteris, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and unique reproductive structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal.

Tripteris monocephala is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
