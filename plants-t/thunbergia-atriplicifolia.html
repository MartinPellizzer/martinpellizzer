Thunbergia atriplicifolia

Thunbergia atriplicifolia, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Thunbergia atriplicifolia is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and foliage.
