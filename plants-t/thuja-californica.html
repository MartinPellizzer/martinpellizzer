Thuja californica

Thuja californica, commonly known as the Western Redcedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It has a distinctive pyramidal shape with scale-like leaves that are reddish-brown in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is known for its aromatic wood. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Thuja californica is also valued for its resistance to decay and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
