Trifolium vulgare

Trifolium vulgare, commonly known as common clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white, pink, or red, depending on the variety. It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium vulgare is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
