Taraxacum latibasis

Taraxacum latibasis, commonly known as the broad-based dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. This perennial plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a waxy coating, while its flowers bloom in spring and early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
