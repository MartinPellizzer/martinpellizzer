Thymus kotschyanus subsp. migricus

Thymus kotschyanus subsp.

migricus, commonly known as the Armenian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their strong, minty aroma.
