Teucrium canadense subsp. viscidum

Teucrium canadense subsp.

viscidum, commonly known as sticky blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
