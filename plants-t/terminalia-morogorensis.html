Terminalia morogorensis

Terminalia morogorensis, commonly known as the Morogoro terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Combretaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are encased in a hard, woody pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry woodland and savanna regions.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
