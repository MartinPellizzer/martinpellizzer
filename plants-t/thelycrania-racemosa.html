Thelycrania racemosa

Thelycrania racemosa, commonly known as the clustered tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Thelycrania racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging climates.
