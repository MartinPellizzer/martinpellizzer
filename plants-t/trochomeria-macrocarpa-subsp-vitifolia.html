Trochomeria macrocarpa subsp. vitifolia

Trochomeria macrocarpa subsp.

vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf trochomeria, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a distinctive grape-like appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
