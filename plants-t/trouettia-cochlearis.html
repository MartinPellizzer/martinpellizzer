Trouettia cochlearis

Trouettia cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a range of colors from white to pink.

Trouettia cochlearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
