Tropaeolum orthoceras

Tropaeolum orthoceras, commonly known as the mountain nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, bright green leaves and vibrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a herbaceous annual, often growing up to 30 cm in height, with trailing stems that make it suitable for container gardening. Its flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
