Tabernaemontana bicolor

Tabernaemontana bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy white and pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tabernaemontana bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
