Thapsia maxima

Thapsia maxima, commonly known as the giant thapsia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Thapsia maxima is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
