Tectona grandis f. tomentella

Tectona grandis f. tomentella, commonly known as the tomentella teak, is a variant of the teak tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant exhibits a tropical evergreen habit with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits containing winged seeds.

This form is particularly noted for its ornamental value and its use in horticulture and timber production.
