Trichinium macrocephalum

Trichinium macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a strong, unpleasant odor. It is part of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
