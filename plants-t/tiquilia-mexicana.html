Tiquilia mexicana

Tiquilia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in rocky soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Tiquilia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
