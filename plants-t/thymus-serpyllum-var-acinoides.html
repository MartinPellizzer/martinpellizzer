Thymus serpyllum var. acinoides

Thymus serpyllum var. acinoides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in glandular-tipped spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
