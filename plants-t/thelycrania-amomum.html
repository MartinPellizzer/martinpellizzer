Thelycrania amomum

Thelycrania amomum, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is most notable for its fruit, which contains the aromatic seeds used as a spice. The seeds, known as nutmeg, are enclosed in a rough, woody outer layer called the aril.

Thelycrania amomum is valued not only for its economic importance as a spice crop but also for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
