Trichoscypha victoriae

Trichoscypha victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria's tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
