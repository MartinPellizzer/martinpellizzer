Turnera prancei

Turnera prancei, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
