Tilia × frondens

Tilia × frondens, commonly known as the small-leaf linden, is a hybrid species belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are smaller than those of its parent species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. These flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of honey.

Tilia × frondens is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
