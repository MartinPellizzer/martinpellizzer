Tittmannia hirta

Tittmannia hirta, commonly known as the hairy tittmannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Tittmannia hirta is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
