Trasus capillaris

Trasus capillaris, commonly known as the slender tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Trasus capillaris is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
