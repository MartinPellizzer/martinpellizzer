Tragacantha lotiflora

Tragacantha lotiflora, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a milky sap that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha lotiflora is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.
