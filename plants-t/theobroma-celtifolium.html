Theobroma celtifolium

Theobroma celtifolium, commonly known as the Amazonian cocoa tree, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is closely related to the cacao tree, Theobroma cacao. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds used in the production of cocoa products.

Theobroma celtifolium plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat and is valued for its ecological and potential economic importance.
