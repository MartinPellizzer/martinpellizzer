Teucrium commune var. polioides

Teucrium commune var. polioides, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
