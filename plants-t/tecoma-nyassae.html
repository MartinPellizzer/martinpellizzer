Tecoma nyassae

Tecoma nyassae, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Tecoma nyassae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
