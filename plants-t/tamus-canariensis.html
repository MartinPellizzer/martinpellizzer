Tamus canariensis

Tamus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary island yam, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
