Terminalia plagata

Terminalia plagata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Terminalia plagata is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
