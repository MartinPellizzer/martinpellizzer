Thymus glabrescens f. serpens

Thymus glabrescens f. serpens, commonly known as the serpentine thyme, is a low-growing, aromatic perennial herb native to rocky and limestone soils in southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed, giving it a carpet-like appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
