Tragacantha sibthorpiana

Tragacantha sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
