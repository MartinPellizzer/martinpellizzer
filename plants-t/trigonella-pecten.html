Trigonella pecten

Trigonella pecten, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescence that resembles a comb or a beard, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by narrow, linear pods that contain seeds. Trigonella pecten is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
