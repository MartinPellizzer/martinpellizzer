Trichosandra borbonica var. floribunda

Trichosandra borbonica var. floribunda, commonly known as the Bourbon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
