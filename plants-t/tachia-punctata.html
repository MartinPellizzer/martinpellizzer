Tachia punctata

Tachia punctata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Iridaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, spotted flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features strap-shaped leaves. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Tachia punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
