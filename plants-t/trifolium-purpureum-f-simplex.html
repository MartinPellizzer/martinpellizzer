Trifolium purpureum f. simplex

Trifolium purpureum f. simplex, commonly known as the simple purple clover, is a botanical variant of the purple clover species.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage, typically featuring fewer leaflets compared to the standard form. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grasslands and meadows. It produces small, purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
