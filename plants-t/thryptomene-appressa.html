Thryptomene appressa

Thryptomene appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects and birds.
