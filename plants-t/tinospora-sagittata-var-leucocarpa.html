Tinospora sagittata var. leucocarpa

Tinospora sagittata var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited Indian creeper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces fleshy, yellow or white berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The varietal name "leucocarpa" refers to its distinctive white or pale-colored fruits.
