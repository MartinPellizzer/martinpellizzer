Turnera coerulea var. surinamensis

Turnera coerulea var. surinamensis, commonly known as the blue turnera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, shade-tolerant plants.
