Tricalysia griseiflora var. benguellensis

Tricalysia griseiflora var. benguellensis, commonly known as the Ben Guellens tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and floral display.
