Tamus communis var. subtriloba

Tamus communis var. subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed burr cucumber, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it adaptable to various soil conditions.

The plant is valued for its edible parts and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine.
