Tacca pinnatifida var. acutifolia

Tacca pinnatifida var. acutifolia, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's ivy," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence with dark purple bracts that resemble bat wings. The plant features long, slender leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
