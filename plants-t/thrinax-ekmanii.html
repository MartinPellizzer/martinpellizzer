Thrinax ekmanii

Thrinax ekmanii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of Ecuador and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
