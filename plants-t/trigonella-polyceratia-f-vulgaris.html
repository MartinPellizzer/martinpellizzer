Trigonella polyceratia f. vulgaris

Trigonella polyceratia f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into leguminous pods containing several seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
