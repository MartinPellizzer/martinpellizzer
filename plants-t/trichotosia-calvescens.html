Trichotosia calvescens

Trichotosia calvescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow in color.

The wood of Trichotosia calvescens is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.
