Trachyandra flexifolia

Trachyandra flexifolia, commonly known as the flexile trachyandra, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen perennial plant characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
