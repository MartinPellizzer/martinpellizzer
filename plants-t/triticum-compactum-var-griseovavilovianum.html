Triticum compactum var. griseovavilovianum

Triticum compactum var. griseovavilovianum, commonly known as the gray wheat, is a botanical variant of the wheat species Triticum compactum.

It is characterized by its grayish hue and compact growth habit, which distinguishes it from other wheat varieties. This plant is primarily found in temperate regions and is adapted to cooler climates. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a short, sturdy stem.

It is of interest to botanists and agricultural scientists due to its unique morphological traits and potential for genetic research.
