Taraxacum enontekiense

Taraxacum enontekiense, commonly known as the Enontekielsk dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to northern regions of Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Taraxacum enontekiense is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
