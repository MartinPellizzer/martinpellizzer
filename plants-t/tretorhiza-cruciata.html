Tretorhiza cruciata

Tretorhiza cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped floral structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Tretorhiza cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
