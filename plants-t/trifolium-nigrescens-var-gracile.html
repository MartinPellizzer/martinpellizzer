Trifolium nigrescens var. gracile

Trifolium nigrescens var. gracile, commonly known as the slender black clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wildflower meadows.
