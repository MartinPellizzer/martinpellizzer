Terminalia bispinosa

Terminalia bispinosa, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. Terminalia bispinosa is often found in riverine forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
