Taraxacum chloroticum

Taraxacum chloroticum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
