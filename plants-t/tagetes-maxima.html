Tagetes maxima

Tagetes maxima, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Tagetes maxima is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.
