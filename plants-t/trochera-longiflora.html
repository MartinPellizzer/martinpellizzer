Trochera longiflora

Trochera longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered trochera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
