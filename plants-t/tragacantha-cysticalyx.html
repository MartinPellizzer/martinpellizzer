Tragacantha cysticalyx

Tragacantha cysticalyx, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in summer. Its most notable feature is the production of a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its economic value.
