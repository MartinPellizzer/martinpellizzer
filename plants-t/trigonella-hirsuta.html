Trigonella hirsuta

Trigonella hirsuta, commonly known as hairy fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are hairy and have a distinct, slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into slender, twisted pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Trigonella hirsuta is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
