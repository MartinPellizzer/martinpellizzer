Tanacetum corymbosum subsp. corymbosum

Tanacetum corymbosum subsp.

corymbosum, commonly known as white tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The plant is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent and is often found in moist meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

However, it is toxic to livestock and should be handled with care.
