Tragacantha cicerifolia

Tragacantha cicerifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate.
