Toddalia inermis

Toddalia inermis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Toddalia inermis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, tropical climates.
