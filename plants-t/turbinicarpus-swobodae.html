Turbinicarpus swobodae

Turbinicarpus swobodae, commonly known as the Swoboda's barrel cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
