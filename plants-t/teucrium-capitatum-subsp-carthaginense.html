Teucrium capitatum subsp. carthaginense

Teucrium capitatum subsp.

carthaginense, commonly known as Carthaginian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
