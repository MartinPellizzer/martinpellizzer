Tropaeolum argentinum

Tropaeolum argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and bright red or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil. It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible species.

Tropaeolum argentinum is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and can also be used in traditional medicine.
