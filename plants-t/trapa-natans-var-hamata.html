Trapa natans var. hamata

Trapa natans var. hamata, commonly known as the spiny water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny fruits that help in seed dispersal. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.
