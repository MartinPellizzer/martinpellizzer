Terminalia kilimandscharica

Terminalia kilimandscharica, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to the dry regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spherical fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
