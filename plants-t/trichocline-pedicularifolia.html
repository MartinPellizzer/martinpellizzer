Trichocline pedicularifolia

Trichocline pedicularifolia, commonly known as the hanging trichocline, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, hanging clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often trail or dangle from trees or shrubs. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually elliptical in shape.

Trichocline pedicularifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate and airy appearance.
