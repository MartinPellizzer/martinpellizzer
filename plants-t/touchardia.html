Touchardia

Touchardia, commonly known as the Himalayan giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of the Himalayas.

It features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a central greenish disk, blooming in late summer. Touchardia thrives in still, nutrient-rich waters and is adapted to the cold, high-altitude environment.

Its unique botanical structure and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
