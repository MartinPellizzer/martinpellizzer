Thymus comosus f. albiflorus

Thymus comosus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower thrift, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and greyish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, distinguishing it from other thyme varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
