Thalictrum billotii

Thalictrum billotii, commonly known as the Pacific meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Thalictrum billotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife in its native habitat.
