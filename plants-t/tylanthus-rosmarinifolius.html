Tylanthus rosmarinifolius

Tylanthus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. Its foliage is used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and swamps.
