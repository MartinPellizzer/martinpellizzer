Tristachya elymoides var. laevis

Tristachya elymoides var. laevis, commonly known as the silky grass or silky panic grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, silky texture due to the fine, hair-like structures on its leaves and inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence is a paniculate cluster with elongated, slender branches.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
