Tetradenia tuberosa

Tetradenia tuberosa, commonly known as the African potato, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary part used for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale pink, and appear in late spring to early summer.
