Tecoma cuspidata

Tecoma cuspidata, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value makes it popular in gardens, while its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions adds to its adaptability.

Tecoma cuspidata is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought.
