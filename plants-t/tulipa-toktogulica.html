Tulipa toktogulica

Tulipa toktogulica, commonly known as the Toktogul tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa, which includes a variety of bulbous plants known for their showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often colorful blooms in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically in the spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold, dry environments, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The Toktogul tulip is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and limited distribution.
