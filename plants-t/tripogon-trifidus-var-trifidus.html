Tripogon trifidus var. trifidus

Tripogon trifidus var. trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct three-lobed base. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
