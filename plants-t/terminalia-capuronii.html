Terminalia capuronii

Terminalia capuronii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that split open to release flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental planting in warm climates.

Terminalia capuronii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
