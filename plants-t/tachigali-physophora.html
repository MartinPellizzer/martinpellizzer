Tachigali physophora

Tachigali physophora, commonly known as the red-flowered tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Tachigali physophora is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
