Tetratheca pilosa subsp. pilosa

Tetratheca pilosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
