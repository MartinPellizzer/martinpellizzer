Thymus numidicus var. granatensis

Thymus numidicus var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granada thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves that are densely packed and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal, as well as its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
