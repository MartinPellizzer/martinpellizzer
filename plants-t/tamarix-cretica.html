Tamarix cretica

Tamarix cretica, commonly known as the creeping tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading branches and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Tamarix cretica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
