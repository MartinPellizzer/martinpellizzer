Trifolium monanthum var. grantianum

Trifolium monanthum var. grantianum, commonly known as Grant's red clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in dense, upright clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in agriculture.
