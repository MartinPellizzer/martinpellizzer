Taraxacum obovatum f. integratum

Taraxacum obovatum f. integratum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its rosette of basal leaves is often covered with dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation and distribution.
