Trichinium incanum

Trichinium incanum, commonly known as the white thorned grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Trichinium incanum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
