Thea oleifera

Thea oleifera, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is valued for its high oil content in its fruits, which are used in food and industrial applications. The plant has large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish flowers. Its fruit develops into a hard-shelled drupe containing edible pulp and kernels.

Thea oleifera is also cultivated for its potential in sustainable agriculture and biofuel production.
