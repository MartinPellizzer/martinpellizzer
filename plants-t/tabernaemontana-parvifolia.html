Tabernaemontana parvifolia

Tabernaemontana parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
