Tradescantia guatemalensis var. tacanana

Tradescantia guatemalensis var. tacanana, commonly known as the Mexican spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
