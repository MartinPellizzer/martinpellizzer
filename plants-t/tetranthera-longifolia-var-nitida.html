Tetranthera longifolia var. nitida

Tetranthera longifolia var. nitida, commonly known as the velvetleaf strobilanthes, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its dark green, velvety foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
