Taraxacum solenanthinum

Taraxacum solenanthinum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower that blooms in the summer months, followed by a fluffy seed head. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and adapted to cold, arid environments.

Taraxacum solenanthinum is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to the harsh conditions of alpine ecosystems.
