Trichomanes alatum var. radicatulum

Trichomanes alatum var. radicatulum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds.

It typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that help it anchor to substrates. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, finely divided structure.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in humid, nutrient-poor soils.
