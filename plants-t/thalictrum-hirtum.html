Thalictrum hirtum

Thalictrum hirtum, commonly known as the hairy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, usually yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in temperate regions.

Thalictrum hirtum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
