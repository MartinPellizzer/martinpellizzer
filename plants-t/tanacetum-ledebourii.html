Tanacetum ledebourii

Tanacetum ledebourii, commonly known as the Tibetan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Tanacetum ledebourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
