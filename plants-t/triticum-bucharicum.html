Triticum bucharicum

Triticum bucharicum, commonly known as the Bucharic wheat, is a rare and ancient species of wheat native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale grains that are typically harvested in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that contain several florets, which are the flower-like structures of the wheat plant. It is closely related to other wild wheat species and is of interest to botanists and researchers studying the genetic diversity of wheat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Triticum bucharicum is considered a valuable but endangered plant species.
