Tephrosia adunca f. paraguayensis

Tephrosia adunca f. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay false indigo, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
