Tripterospermum volubile

Tripterospermum volubile, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and cover trellises or fences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
