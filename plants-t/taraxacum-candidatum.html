Taraxacum candidatum

Taraxacum candidatum, commonly known as the white dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of white to pale green, hairy leaves and a single, bright yellow flower head that blooms in spring. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem with a milky sap that is typically white in color. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The white dandelion is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
