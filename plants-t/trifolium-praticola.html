Trifolium praticola

Trifolium praticola, commonly known as common clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a single stem. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, known as inflorescences. Trifolium praticola is a low-growing perennial that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
