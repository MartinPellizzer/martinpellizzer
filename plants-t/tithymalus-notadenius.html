Tithymalus notadenius

Tithymalus notadenius, commonly known as the Western Australian thyme.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to Western Australia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
