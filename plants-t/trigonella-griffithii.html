Trigonella griffithii

Trigonella griffithii, commonly known as Indian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, aromatic seeds that are widely used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine. Trigonella griffithii thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
