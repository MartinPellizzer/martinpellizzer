Thlaspi rotundifolium proles corymbosum

Thlaspi rotundifolium proles corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It is a perennial species that produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in botany and ecology.
