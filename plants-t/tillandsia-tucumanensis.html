Tillandsia tucumanensis

Tillandsia tucumanensis, commonly known as the Tucuman air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America, specifically in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Tillandsia tucumanensis typically produces striking inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
