Thelypteris triangularis

Thelypteris triangularis, commonly known as the triangular holly fern, is a species of fern native to various parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped fronds, which are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its fronds are evergreen, providing a lush green appearance throughout much of the year.

Thelypteris triangularis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
