Teucrium canum

Teucrium canum, commonly known as creeping tarragon, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The genus Teucrium includes several species with similar medicinal uses and botanical features.
