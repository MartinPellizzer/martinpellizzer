Trillium ovatum subsp. oettingeri

Trillium ovatum subsp.

oettingeri, commonly known as the Pacific trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features a distinctive three-part leaf structure and produces a showy, three-petaled white or pink flower in early spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves and flowers are usually present simultaneously, a characteristic known as simultaneous flowering and leafing.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
