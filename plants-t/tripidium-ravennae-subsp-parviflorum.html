Tripidium ravennae subsp. parviflorum

Tripidium ravennae subsp.

The plant typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are often greenish or brownish in color.
