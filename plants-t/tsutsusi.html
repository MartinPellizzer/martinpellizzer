Tsutsusi

Tsutsusi, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb by twining around supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tsutsusi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
