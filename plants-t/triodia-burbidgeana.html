Triodia burbidgeana

Triodia burbidgeana, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant forms tussocks and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures that reduce water loss.

Triodia burbidgeana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
