Thea sasanqua var. serrata

Thea sasanqua var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated camellia, is a variant of the camellia plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and produces striking red or pink flowers in the winter months. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

The serrated leaves distinguish it from other camellia varieties, making it a unique addition to gardens.
