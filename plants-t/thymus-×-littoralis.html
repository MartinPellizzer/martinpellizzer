Thymus × littoralis

Thymus × littoralis, commonly known as coastal thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, oval leaves that are densely packed on the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal environments.

Thymus × littoralis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salty air and wind.
