Thyrsacanthus callistachyus

Thyrsacanthus callistachyus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular, spiny seed pods that encase the seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. The plant has small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
