Teucrium ducellieri

Teucrium ducellieri, commonly known as the Corsican germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Teucrium ducellieri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
