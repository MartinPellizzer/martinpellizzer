Tamarix eversmannii

Tamarix eversmannii, commonly known as the desert rose or tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tamarix eversmannii is often used for erosion control and as an ornamental plant in landscaping.
