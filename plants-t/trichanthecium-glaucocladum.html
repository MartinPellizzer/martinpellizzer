Trichanthecium glaucocladum

Trichanthecium glaucocladum, commonly known as the blue-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, blue-gray leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
