Tricera crassifolia

Tricera crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved trichera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Tricera crassifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
