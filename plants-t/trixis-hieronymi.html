Trixis hieronymi

Trixis hieronymi, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, yellow-flowered heads and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's structure and physiology reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh desert environments.
