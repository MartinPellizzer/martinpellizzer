Trifolium stellatum var. elatius

Trifolium stellatum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in natural landscapes.
