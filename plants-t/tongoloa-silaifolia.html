Tongoloa silaifolia

Tongoloa silaifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its woody structure and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils make it a common species in forested areas.

Tongoloa silaifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
