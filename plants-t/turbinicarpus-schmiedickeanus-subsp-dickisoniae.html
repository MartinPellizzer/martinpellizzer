Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp. dickisoniae

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp.

dickisoniae, commonly known as the Dickison's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered cactus.
