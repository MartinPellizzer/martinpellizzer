Trichocentrum pongratzianum

Trichocentrum pongratzianum, commonly known as the Pongratz's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing orchid with long, wiry stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with prominent yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Trichocentrum pongratzianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
