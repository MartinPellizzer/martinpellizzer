Trillium venosum

Trillium venosum, commonly known as the veiny trillium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are often veined with a distinctive network of dark green lines. The plant produces a single, showy white or pale pink flower with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Trillium venosum is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
