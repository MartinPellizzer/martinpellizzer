Thymus lanuginosus var. pallidiflorus

Thymus lanuginosus var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
