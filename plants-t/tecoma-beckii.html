Tecoma beckii

Tecoma beckii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma beckii is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.
