Tragopogon longifolius

Tragopogon longifolius, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive purple-tipped bract, which blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Tragopogon longifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
