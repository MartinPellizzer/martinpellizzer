Tilia omeiensis

Tilia omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese linden, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a cottony covering. It typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
