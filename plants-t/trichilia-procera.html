Trichilia procera

Trichilia procera, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain flat, seed-like structures. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.

Trichilia procera plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
