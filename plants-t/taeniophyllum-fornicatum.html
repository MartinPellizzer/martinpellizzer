Taeniophyllum fornicatum

Taeniophyllum fornicatum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, compact rosette of lance-shaped leaves that form a dense, protective layer around its central stem. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small, shield-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and among fern enthusiasts.
