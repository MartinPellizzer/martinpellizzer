Tragus tcheliensis

Tragus tcheliensis, commonly known as the desert tragus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are usually small and fleshy, which helps reduce water loss.

Tragus tcheliensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
