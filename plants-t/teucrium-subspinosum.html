Teucrium subspinosum

Teucrium subspinosum, commonly known as spiked germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium subspinosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
