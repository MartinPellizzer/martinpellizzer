Tissa larranagae

Tissa larranagae, commonly known as the Larranaga's tissa, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Tissa larranagae is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
