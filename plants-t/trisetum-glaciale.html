Trisetum glaciale

Trisetum glaciale, commonly known as the alpine hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to cold, mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh, alpine environments. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky soils, where it forms dense mats. Its flowering stems are slender and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

Trisetum glaciale plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
