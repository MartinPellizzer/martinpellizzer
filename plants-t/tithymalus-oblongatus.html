Tithymalus oblongatus

Tithymalus oblongatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of its ecological community.
