Taraxacum schischkinii

Taraxacum schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that terminates in a yellow composite flower head. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep taproot system for water storage.

The seeds are dispersed via a parachute-like structure called a pappus, allowing for wind pollination and seed dispersal.
