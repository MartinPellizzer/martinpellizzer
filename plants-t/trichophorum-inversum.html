Trichophorum inversum

Trichophorum inversum, commonly known as the inverted sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, with its inflorescence oriented downward, which is a distinguishing feature. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Trichophorum inversum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
