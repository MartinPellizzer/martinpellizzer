Taraxacum subalatum

Taraxacum subalatum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Taraxacum subalatum is known for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
