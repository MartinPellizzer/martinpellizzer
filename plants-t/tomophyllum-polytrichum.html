Tomophyllum polytrichum

Tomophyllum polytrichum, commonly known as the golden moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is a cryptogamic plant characterized by its small, flattened, and often yellowish-green thallus. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its structure lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a simple, undifferentiated body called a thallus.

Tomophyllum polytrichum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
