Tephroseris integrifolia subsp. maritima

Tephroseris integrifolia subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a small, herbaceous perennial with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
