Thenardia floribunda

Thenardia floribunda, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across sandy soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize loose soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in its native environment.
