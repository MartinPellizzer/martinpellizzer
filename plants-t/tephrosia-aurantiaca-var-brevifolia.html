Tephrosia aurantiaca var. brevifolia

Tephrosia aurantiaca var. brevifolia, commonly known as the red tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs.

The bark and roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
