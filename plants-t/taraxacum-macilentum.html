Taraxacum macilentum

Taraxacum macilentum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a feathery pappus, aiding in its spread. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is closely related to other dandelion species within the Asteraceae family.
