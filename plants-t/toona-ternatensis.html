Toona ternatensis

Toona ternatensis, commonly known as the white cedar, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber species. The tree is valued for its durable, lightweight wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
