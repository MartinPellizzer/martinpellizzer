Taraxacum behzudicum

Taraxacum behzudicum, commonly known as the Behzud's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Tajikistan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in the spring. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Like other dandelions, it has a taproot system and produces a wind-dispersed seed with a feathery pappus.
