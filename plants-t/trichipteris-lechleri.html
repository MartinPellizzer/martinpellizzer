Trichipteris lechleri

Trichipteris lechleri, commonly known as the caapi vine, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also valued for its role in shamanic practices.
