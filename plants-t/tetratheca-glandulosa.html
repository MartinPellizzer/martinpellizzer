Tetratheca glandulosa

Tetratheca glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with prominent glandular structures on the stems and leaves. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tetratheca glandulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
