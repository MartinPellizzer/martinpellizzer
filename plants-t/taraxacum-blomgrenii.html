Taraxacum blomgrenii

Taraxacum blomgrenii, commonly known as Blomgren's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and a hollow, leafy stem that supports its yellow composite flower head. It produces a fluffy, wind-dispersed seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
