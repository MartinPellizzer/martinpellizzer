Thymus aivalii

Thymus aivalii, commonly known as Aivali thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus aivalii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
