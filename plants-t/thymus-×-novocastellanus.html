Thymus × novocastellanus

Thymus × novocastellanus, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
