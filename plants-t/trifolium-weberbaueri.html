Trifolium weberbaueri

Trifolium weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Trifolium weberbaueri is often found in grassy areas and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
