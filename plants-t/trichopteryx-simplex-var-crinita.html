Trichopteryx simplex var. crinita

Trichopteryx simplex var. crinita, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in soft, hair-like spines that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
