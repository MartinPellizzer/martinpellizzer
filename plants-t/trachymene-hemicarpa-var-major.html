Trachymene hemicarpa var. major

Trachymene hemicarpa var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed false Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
