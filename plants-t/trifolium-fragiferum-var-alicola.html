Trifolium fragiferum var. alicola

Trifolium fragiferum var. alicola, commonly known as the fragrant clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
