Tanacetum myconis

Tanacetum myconis, commonly known as the meadow button.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow button-like centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring moist to dry soils. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats.

Tanacetum myconis is often used in herbal medicine for its mild antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
