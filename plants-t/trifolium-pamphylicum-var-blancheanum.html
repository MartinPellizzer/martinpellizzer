Trifolium pamphylicum var. blancheanum

Trifolium pamphylicum var. blancheanum, commonly known as the Blancheanum Clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
