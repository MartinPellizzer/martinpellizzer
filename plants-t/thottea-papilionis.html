Thottea papilionis

Thottea papilionis, commonly known as the flame violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features delicate, showy flowers that resemble butterflies, with petals arranged in a unique papilionaceous structure. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a vibrant display of red and purple hues.

Thottea papilionis is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
