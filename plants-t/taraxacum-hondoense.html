Taraxacum hondoense

Taraxacum hondoense, commonly known as the Hondo dandelion, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head. Its flowers are yellow and produce fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum hondoense is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
