Trichosanthes vitifolia

Trichosanthes vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that resemble grape leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinct trumpet shape, often followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Trichosanthes.
