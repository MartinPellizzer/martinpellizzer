Teucrium nepetoides

Teucrium nepetoides, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and emit a mint-like scent when crushed.

Teucrium nepetoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a habitat for beneficial insects.
