Trymalium majoranifolium var. calvescens

Trymalium majoranifolium var. calvescens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thyme.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are rich in essential oils. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a culinary herb due to its fragrant properties.
