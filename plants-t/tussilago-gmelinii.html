Tussilago gmelinii

Tussilago gmelinii, commonly known as the lesser mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of gray-green, velvety leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flower clusters in late summer, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Tussilago gmelinii is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.
