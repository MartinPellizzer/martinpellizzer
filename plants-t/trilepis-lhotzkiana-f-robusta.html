Trilepis lhotzkiana f. robusta

Trilepis lhotzkiana f. robusta, commonly known as the robust form of the Lhotskian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
