Tsuga intermedia

Tsuga intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tsuga, which includes other hemlock species, and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its bark is typically smooth and grayish in appearance. Tsuga intermedia is often found in temperate forests and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
