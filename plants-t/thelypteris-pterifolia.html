Thelypteris pterifolia

Thelypteris pterifolia, commonly known as the common shield fern, is a species of fern native to various regions across Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that often have a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

Thelypteris pterifolia is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture.
