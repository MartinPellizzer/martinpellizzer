Teucrium pyrenaicum

Teucrium pyrenaicum, commonly known as alpine germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Teucrium pyrenaicum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
