Trichogonia scabra

Trichogonia scabra, commonly known as the rough trichogonia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Trichogonia scabra is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
