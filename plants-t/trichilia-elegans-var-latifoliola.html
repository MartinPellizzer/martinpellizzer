Trichilia elegans var. latifoliola

Trichilia elegans var. latifoliola, commonly known as the broad-leafed trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by fruit that resembles a small, woody capsule. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
