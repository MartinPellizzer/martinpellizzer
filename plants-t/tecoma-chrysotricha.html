Tecoma chrysotricha

Tecoma chrysotricha, commonly known as golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly pubescent underside.

The plant is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
