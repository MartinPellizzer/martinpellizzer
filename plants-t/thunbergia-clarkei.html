Thunbergia clarkei

Thunbergia clarkei, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

Thunbergia clarkei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
