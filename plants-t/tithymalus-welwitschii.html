Tithymalus welwitschii

Tithymalus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
