Tropaeolum moritzianum

Tropaeolum moritzianum, commonly known as the "Jacob's ladder," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, peppermint-scented flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, edible tubers that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive ladder-like appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Tropaeolum moritzianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
