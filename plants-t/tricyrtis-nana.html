Tricyrtis nana

Tricyrtis nana, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese buttercup, is a compact perennial herb native to Japan.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that resemble small buttercups, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Tricyrtis nana is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
