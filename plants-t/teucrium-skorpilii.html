Teucrium skorpilii

Teucrium skorpilii, commonly known as the Skorpion's thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Teucrium skorpilii is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

Its preference for well-drained soils and full sun makes it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
