Thlaspi perfoliatum var. alpestre

Thlaspi perfoliatum var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine pennycress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate and often have a distinctive lobed shape, while its stems are usually hairy.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh alpine climates and is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.
