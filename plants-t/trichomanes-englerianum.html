Trichomanes englerianum

Trichomanes englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes englerianum is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
