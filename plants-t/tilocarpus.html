Tilocarpus

Tilocarpus, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody, often aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Tilocarpus species are known for their compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
