Tecoma albiflora

Tecoma albiflora, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
