Tropocarpa

Tropocarpa, commonly known as the tropical fruit tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often precede the fruit development. The plant produces a unique fruit that is typically fleshy and contains numerous seeds. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Tropocarpa is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
