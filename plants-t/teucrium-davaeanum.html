Teucrium davaeanum

Teucrium davaeanum, commonly known as the Philippine germander, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
