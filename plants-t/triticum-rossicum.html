Triticum rossicum

Triticum rossicum, commonly known as the Russian wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. This plant is often found in rocky or alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils. Triticum rossicum is a perennial species that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to harsh climates and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
