Teucrium flavum subvar. mairei

Teucrium flavum subvar. mairei, commonly known as Maire's blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
