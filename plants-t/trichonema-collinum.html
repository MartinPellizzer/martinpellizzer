Trichonema collinum

Trichonema collinum, commonly known as the mountain feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant is typically found in alpine and mountainous regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Trichonema collinum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in rocky terrains.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude ecosystems.
