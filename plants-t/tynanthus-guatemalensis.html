Tynanthus guatemalensis

Tynanthus guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan tynanthus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tynanthus guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
