Triticum sphaerococcum var. popovii

Triticum sphaerococcum var. popovii, commonly known as the spherical wheat, is a rare and unique variant of wheat characterized by its distinctive spherical grain structure.

This plant belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to Central Asia, where it grows in arid and semi-arid environments. Its grains are tightly packed into spherical clusters, which set it apart from other wheat species. The botanical features of this variety include compact inflorescences and a hard, durable rachis that supports the spherical grain formation.

Due to its unusual morphology, it is of interest to botanists and taxonomists studying the diversity within the genus Triticum.
