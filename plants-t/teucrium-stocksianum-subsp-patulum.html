Teucrium stocksianum subsp. patulum

Teucrium stocksianum subsp.

patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies patulum is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other forms of Teucrium stocksianum.
