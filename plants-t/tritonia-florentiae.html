Tritonia florentiae

Tritonia florentiae, commonly known as the Florence tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces clusters of flowers on slender stalks, often emerging from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Tritonia florentiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
