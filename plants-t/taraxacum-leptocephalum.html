Taraxacum leptocephalum

Taraxacum leptocephalum, commonly known as the slender-headed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, tall, hairy stem that terminates in a yellow flower head. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which allows for efficient propagation. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

This species is adapted to temperate climates and exhibits a variety of morphological traits that aid in its survival and spread.
