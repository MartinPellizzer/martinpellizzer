Tamarix juniperina

Tamarix juniperina, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions with minimal water. Its wood is often used for fuel and construction due to its density and durability.

Tamarix juniperina plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
