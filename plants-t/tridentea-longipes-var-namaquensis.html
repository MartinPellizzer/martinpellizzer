Tridentea longipes var. namaquensis

Tridentea longipes var. namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

This variant is particularly found in theNamaqualand region, where it thrives in sandy soils and contributes to local biodiversity.
