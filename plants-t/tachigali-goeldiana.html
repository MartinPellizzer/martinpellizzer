Tachigali goeldiana

Tachigali goeldiana, commonly known as Brazilian tamarind, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it an important species in reforestation efforts.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
