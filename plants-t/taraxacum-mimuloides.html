Taraxacum mimuloides

Taraxacum mimuloides, commonly known as the mimuloid dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical pappus for wind dispersal. Native to the southern United States, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its morphology and ecology make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
