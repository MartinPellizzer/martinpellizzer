Talisia angustifolia

Talisia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf talisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped berries that turn red when mature. Talisia angustifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
