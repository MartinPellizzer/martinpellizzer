Tropaeolum peregrinum

Tropaeolum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering pepper or scarlet runner, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces edible, peppery tubers similar to those of the common potato. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum peregrinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
