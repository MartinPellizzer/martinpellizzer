Tephrosia conspicua

Tephrosia conspicua, commonly known as the showy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Tephrosia conspicua is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
