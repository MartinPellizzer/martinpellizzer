Triticum brachystachyum

Triticum brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stemmed wheat, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter inflorescence compared to typical wheat species. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest in botanical studies and has potential uses in breeding programs for drought-resistant crops.
