Tabacus

Tabacus, commonly known as tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant produces cylindrical fruits that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. Tobacco plants are typically grown for their leaves, which are harvested and processed for various commercial uses.

However, its use is associated with health risks due to the presence of nicotine and other harmful compounds.
