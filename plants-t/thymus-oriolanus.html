Thymus oriolanus

Thymus oriolanus, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Thymus oriolanus is also valued as a culinary herb, adding a sharp, peppery flavor to dishes.
