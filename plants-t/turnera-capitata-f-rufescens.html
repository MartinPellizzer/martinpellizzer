Turnera capitata f. rufescens

Turnera capitata f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed turnera, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
