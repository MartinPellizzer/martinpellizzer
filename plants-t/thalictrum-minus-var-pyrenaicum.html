Thalictrum minus var. pyrenaicum

Thalictrum minus var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear compound leaves with finely divided, feathery lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
