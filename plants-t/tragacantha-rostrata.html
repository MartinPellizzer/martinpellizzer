Tragacantha rostrata

Tragacantha rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods. Its roots are known for secreting a viscous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.

Tragacantha rostrata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
