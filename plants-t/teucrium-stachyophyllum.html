Teucrium stachyophyllum

Teucrium stachyophyllum, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Native to regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia, it thrives in rocky or arid environments. The plant has aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
