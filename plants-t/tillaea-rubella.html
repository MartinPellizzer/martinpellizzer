Tillaea rubella

Tillaea rubella, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to arid and rocky regions of the Mediterranean.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, coastal or mountainous habitats.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
