Trigonochilum midas

Trigonochilum midas, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features vibrant yellow petals that resemble the color of gold, giving it its common name. It typically grows as an epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments.

Botanically, it has a distinctive triangular lip and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
