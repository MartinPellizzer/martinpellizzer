Taonabo ulmifolia

Taonabo ulmifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved elm, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms, ashes, and related species. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taonabo ulmifolia is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
