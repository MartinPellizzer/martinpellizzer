Tyloglossa flexuosa

Tyloglossa flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spiraling inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a rosette pattern.

Tyloglossa flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
