Tridax palmeri

Tridax palmeri, commonly known as the common tridax, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive growth in various regions. Tridax palmeri is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in many agricultural areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common sight in tropical and subtropical environments.
