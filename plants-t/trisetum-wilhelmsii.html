Trisetum wilhelmsii

Trisetum wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelmsii wood oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Trisetum wilhelmsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
