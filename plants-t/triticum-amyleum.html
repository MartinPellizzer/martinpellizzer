Triticum amyleum

Triticum amyleum, commonly known as the amylous wheat, is a lesser-known species within the wheat genus.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces compact spikes with numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its grains are smaller than those of common wheat and have a higher starch content.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique genetic traits.
