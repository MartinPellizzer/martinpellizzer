Tweedia pubescens

Tweedia pubescens, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped blooms that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tweedia pubescens is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
