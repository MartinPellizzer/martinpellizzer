Thelypteris wollastonii

Thelypteris wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Native to the eastern United States, this fern thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of spore-bearing leaves.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
