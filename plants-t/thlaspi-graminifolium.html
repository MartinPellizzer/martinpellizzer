Thlaspi graminifolium

Thlaspi graminifolium, commonly known as field pennycress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are elongated and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the early spring. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Thlaspi graminifolium is also known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential candidate for phytoremediation efforts.
