Triticum polonicum subsp. abyssinicum

Triticum polonicum subsp.

abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
