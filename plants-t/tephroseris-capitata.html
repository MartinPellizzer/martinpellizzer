Tephroseris capitata

Tephroseris capitata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow ray florets surrounded by numerous disk florets. Tephroseris capitata thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grassland and shrubland environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native region.
