Triticum vulgare var. michauxii

Triticum vulgare var. michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's wheat, is a botanical variety native to the eastern United States.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, each with a single grain. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest clearings or along streambanks.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species.
