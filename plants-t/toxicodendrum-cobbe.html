Toxicodendrum cobbe

Toxicodendrum cobbe, commonly known as the cobbe's poison ivy, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically opposite and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain toxic compounds. Its sap contains urushiol, a potent allergen that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in both natural and cultivated settings.
