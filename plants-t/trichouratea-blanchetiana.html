Trichouratea blanchetiana

Trichouratea blanchetiana, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals that form a star-like shape. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Trichouratea blanchetiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
