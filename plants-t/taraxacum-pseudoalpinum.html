Taraxacum pseudoalpinum

Taraxacum pseudoalpinum, commonly known as alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces a yellow composite flower head. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species capable of surviving in harsh mountain environments.
