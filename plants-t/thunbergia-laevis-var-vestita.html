Thunbergia laevis var. vestita

Thunbergia laevis var. vestita, commonly known as the white thunbergia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of tubular, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
