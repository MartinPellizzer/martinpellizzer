Tylecodon paniculatus subsp. glaucus

Tylecodon paniculatus subsp.

glaucus, commonly known as the silverleaf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in summer, arranged in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
