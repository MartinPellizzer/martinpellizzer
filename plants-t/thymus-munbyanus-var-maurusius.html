Thymus munbyanus var. maurusius

Thymus munbyanus var. maurusius, commonly known as the Munby thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
