Tomanthea phaeopappa

Tomanthea phaeopappa, commonly known as the red-veined gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Tomanthea phaeopappa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
