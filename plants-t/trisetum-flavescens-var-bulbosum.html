Trisetum flavescens var. bulbosum

Trisetum flavescens var. bulbosum, commonly known as the yellow beardgrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellowish-green in color.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
