Thymus laconicus

Thymus laconicus, commonly known as the Laconic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their essential oils and antimicrobial properties.

Thymus laconicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
