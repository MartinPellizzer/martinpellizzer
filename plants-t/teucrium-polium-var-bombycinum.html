Teucrium polium var. bombycinum

Teucrium polium var. bombycinum, commonly known as the sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
