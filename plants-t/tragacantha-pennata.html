Tragacantha pennata

Tragacantha pennata, commonly known as the featherleaf tragacanth, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, branched leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions.

Tragacantha pennata is also valued for its resin, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
