Tripudianthes kanburiensis

Tripudianthes kanburiensis, commonly known as the Thai flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is relatively fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 15 meters in its natural habitat.
