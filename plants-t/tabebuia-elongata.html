Tabebuia elongata

Tabebuia elongata, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces long, narrow leaves that are arranged opposite each other. Its wood is valued for its durability and is often used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
