Tulotis brevicalcarata

Tulotis brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-spurred tulip orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
