Teucrium junii-victoris

Teucrium junii-victoris, commonly known as the Victorian germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
