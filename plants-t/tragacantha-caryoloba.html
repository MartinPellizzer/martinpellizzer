Tragacantha caryoloba

Tragacantha caryoloba, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments and for the gum resin it exudes, which has been used historically in various applications.

Tragacantha caryoloba plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
