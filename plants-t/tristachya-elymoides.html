Tristachya elymoides

Tristachya elymoides, commonly known as the silver grass or silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Tristachya elymoides is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
