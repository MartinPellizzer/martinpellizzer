Tapeinosperma tchingouense var. tchingouense

Tapeinosperma tchingouense var. tchingouense, commonly known as the Tchingouense Pomegranate, is a unique variant of the pomegranate species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy arils that encase the seeds, typical of the Punicaceae family. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with thick, woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.
