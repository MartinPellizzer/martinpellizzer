Triticum unilaterale var. pubescens

Triticum unilaterale var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent wheatgrass, is a variant of wheatgrass characterized by its distinctive pubescence.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates and is known for its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various herbal and health applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it suitable for both ornamental and functional purposes in horticulture.
