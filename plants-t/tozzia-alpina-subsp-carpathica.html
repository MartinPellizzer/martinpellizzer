Tozzia alpina subsp. carpathica

Tozzia alpina subsp.

carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian tozzia, is a rare and specialized alpine plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a small, herbaceous perennial with delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, nutrient-poor soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to harsh mountain environments.
