Tephrosia purpurea var. pumila

Tephrosia purpurea var. pumila, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, purple to violet flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
