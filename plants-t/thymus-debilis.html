Thymus debilis

Thymus debilis, commonly known as slender thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fragrant, tiny leaves are often used in culinary applications and herbal remedies.

Thymus debilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.
