Teucrium hijazicum

Teucrium hijazicum, commonly known as the Hijaz germander, is a perennial herb native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium hijazicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
