Tubella wilhelmii

Tubella wilhelmii, commonly known as the velvetleaf tuber, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in diverse soil conditions. The plant typically has broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Tubella wilhelmii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
