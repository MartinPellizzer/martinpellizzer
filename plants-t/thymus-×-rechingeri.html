Thymus × rechingeri

Thymus × rechingeri, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Thymus serpyllum and Thymus praecox, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun and well-drained conditions. Its small, fragrant leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in early summer and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
