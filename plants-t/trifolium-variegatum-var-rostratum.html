Trifolium variegatum var. rostratum

Trifolium variegatum var. rostratum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a variant of the variable clover species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with white or yellow markings. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

This variety is often found in grassy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
