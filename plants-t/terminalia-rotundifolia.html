Terminalia rotundifolia

Terminalia rotundifolia, commonly known as the African sand apple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the tips of branches. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a gritty texture. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
