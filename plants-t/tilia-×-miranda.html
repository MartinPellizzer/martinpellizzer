Tilia × miranda

Tilia × miranda, commonly known as the common lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in diameter, with a serrated margin and a smooth, dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities.

Tilia × miranda is often used in urban landscaping due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
