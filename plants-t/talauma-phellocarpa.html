Talauma phellocarpa

Talauma phellocarpa, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The tree has a smooth, gray bark and reaches heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

Talauma phellocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
