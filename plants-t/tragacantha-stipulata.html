Tragacantha stipulata

Tragacantha stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Tragacantha stipulata is often cultivated for its gum, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications.
