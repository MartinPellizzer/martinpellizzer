Trifolium endressii

Trifolium endressii, commonly known as the Endress's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium endressii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
