Trachypogon densus

Trachypogon densus, commonly known as the dense hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Trachypogon densus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems in its range.
