Tripterospermum japonicum f. album

Tripterospermum japonicum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese snowbell, is a unique variant of the species characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, typically growing in woodland and mountainous regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the study of plant morphology and hybridization.

The f. album form is distinguished by its lack of the typical yellow pigmentation found in the species.
