Trifolium macrocephalum

Trifolium macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium macrocephalum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
