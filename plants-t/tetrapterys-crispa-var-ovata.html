Tetrapterys crispa var. ovata

Tetrapterys crispa var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate-leaf tetrapterys, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves, which are typically palmately divided into five lobes. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understorey habitats.
