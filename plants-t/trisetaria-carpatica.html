Trisetaria carpatica

Trisetaria carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous and forested regions.

Trisetaria carpatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
