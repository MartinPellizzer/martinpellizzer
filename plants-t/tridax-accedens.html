Tridax accedens

Tridax accedens, commonly known as the smallflower triad, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils.

Tridax accedens is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
