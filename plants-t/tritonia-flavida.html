Tritonia flavida

Tritonia flavida, commonly known as the yellow broomweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that contain several seeds. Tritonia flavida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
