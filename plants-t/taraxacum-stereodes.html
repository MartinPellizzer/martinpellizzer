Taraxacum stereodes

Taraxacum stereodes, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a distinctive rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic seed heads that disperse via wind.
