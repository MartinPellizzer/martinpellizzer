Thelypteris pauciloba

Thelypteris pauciloba, commonly known as the few-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping in appearance. Native to eastern Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The leaves are usually dichotomously divided, giving it a distinctive, lace-like appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
