Tecoma xylocarpa

Tecoma xylocarpa, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature, contributing to its dispersal in natural habitats.
