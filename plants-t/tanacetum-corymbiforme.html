Tanacetum corymbiforme

Tanacetum corymbiforme, commonly known as the meadow tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or well-drained soils.

Tanacetum corymbiforme is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
