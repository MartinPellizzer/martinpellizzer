Taitonia

Taitonia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Taitonia pinnata, which is native to New Zealand. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Taitonia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a suitable addition to shaded gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique floral display and evergreen foliage.
