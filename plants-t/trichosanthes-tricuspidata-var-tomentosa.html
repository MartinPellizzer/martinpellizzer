Trichosanthes tricuspidata var. tomentosa

Trichosanthes tricuspidata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy bitter melon, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing vine that produces large, lobed leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant bears yellow flowers followed by elongated, ridged fruits that turn from green to orange as they mature. The fruit is characterized by its three-pointed apex and a thick, warty rind.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in some cuisines.
