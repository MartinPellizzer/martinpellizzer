Tithymalus Schizolobus

Tithymalus schizolobus is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological significance. This species is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves and delicate, daisy-like blooms that appear in late spring. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical gem.
