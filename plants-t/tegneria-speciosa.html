Tegneria speciosa

Tegneria speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that display a range of hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Tegneria speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
