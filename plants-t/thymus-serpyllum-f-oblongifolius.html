Thymus serpyllum f. oblongifolius

Thymus serpyllum f. oblongifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically darker green and slightly hairy. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ground cover plant.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
