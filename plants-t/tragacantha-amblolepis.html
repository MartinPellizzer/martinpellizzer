Tragacantha amblolepis

Tragacantha amblolepis, commonly known as the red tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
