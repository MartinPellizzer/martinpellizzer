Torilis infesta subsp. heterophylla

Torilis infesta subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf sandparsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant has a taproot system and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are variable in shape, ranging from lobed to entire, which contributes to its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
