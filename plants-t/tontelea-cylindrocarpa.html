Tontelea cylindrocarpa

Tontelea cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the Australian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It has smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin flakes, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its aromatic timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
