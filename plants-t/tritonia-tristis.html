Tritonia tristis

Tritonia tristis, commonly known as the weeping tritonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, downward-facing flowers. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pendulous blooms that range in color from white to pink and purple. Tritonia tristis thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green, adding to its visual appeal.
