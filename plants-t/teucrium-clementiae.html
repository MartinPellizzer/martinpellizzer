Teucrium clementiae

Teucrium clementiae, commonly known as the Clementine germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a fragrant scent.

Teucrium clementiae is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
