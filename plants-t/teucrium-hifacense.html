Teucrium hifacense

Teucrium hifacense, commonly known as the Hifacan germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
