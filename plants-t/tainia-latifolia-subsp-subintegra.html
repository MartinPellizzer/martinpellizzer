Tainia latifolia subsp. subintegra

Tainia latifolia subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the broadleaf tainia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America. It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces spadices, which are the characteristic inflorescences of the Araceae family, typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
