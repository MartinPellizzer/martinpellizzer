Turnera patens

Turnera patens, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
