Taraxacum angermannicum

Taraxacum angermannicum, commonly known as the Angermann's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that matures into a spherical seed ball. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution.

It is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.
