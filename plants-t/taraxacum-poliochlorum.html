Taraxacum poliochlorum

Taraxacum poliochlorum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various compounds, some of which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of scientific interest.
