Tagetes sororia

Tagetes sororia, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tagetes sororia is also known for its strong, aromatic scent and its ability to repel certain pests.
