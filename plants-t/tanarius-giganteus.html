Tanarius giganteus

Tanarius giganteus, commonly known as the giant tanarius, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the subspecies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses where it can flourish under appropriate care.
