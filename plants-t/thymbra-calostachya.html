Thymbra calostachya

Thymbra calostachya, commonly known as narrow-leaved wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems and small, aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
