Thymbra linearifolia

Thymbra linearifolia, commonly known as linear-leaved thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a strong, mint-like scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Thymbra linearifolia is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
