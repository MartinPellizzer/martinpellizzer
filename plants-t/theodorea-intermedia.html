Theodorea intermedia

Theodorea intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate theodorea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Theodorea intermedia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
