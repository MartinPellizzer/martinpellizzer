Trigonochilum melanthes

Trigonochilum melanthes, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of white and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can receive the necessary indirect light for photosynthesis. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The botanical features of Trigonochilum melanthes make it a unique and visually striking member of the orchid family.
