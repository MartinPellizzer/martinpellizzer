Thymus munbyanus subsp. munbyanus

Thymus munbyanus subsp.

munbyanus, commonly known as Munby's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
