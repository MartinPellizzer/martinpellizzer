Trisetaria panicea var. canariensis

Trisetaria panicea var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are loosely arranged clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and rocky environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
