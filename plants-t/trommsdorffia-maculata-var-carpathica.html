Trommsdorffia maculata var. carpathica

Trommsdorffia maculata var. carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and shaded wetlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Trommsdorffia maculata var. carpathica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native plant gardens.
