Thea viridis var. assamica

Thea viridis var. assamica, commonly known as Assam tea, is a variant of the tea plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and a robust, earthy flavor profile. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are harvested and processed into various types of black and green tea.

This variety is particularly valued for its high caffeine content and distinctive aroma.
