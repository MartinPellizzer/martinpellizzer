Tapinanthus crataevae

Tapinanthus crataevae, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of its host, typically members of the Rosaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions of Africa and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique parasitic lifestyle and ornamental appearance make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
