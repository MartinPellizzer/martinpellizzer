Tripteris rhodesica

Tripteris rhodesica, commonly known as Rhodes' tripteris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Tripteridaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in association with other coastal vegetation.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
