Tectaria divergens

Tectaria divergens, commonly known as the broad-leaf shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually divided into numerous segments, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Tectaria divergens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
