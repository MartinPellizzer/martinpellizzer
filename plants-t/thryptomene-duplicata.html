Thryptomene duplicata

Thryptomene duplicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.

Thryptomene duplicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
