Thelesperma caespitosum

Thelesperma caespitosum, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Thelesperma caespitosum is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
