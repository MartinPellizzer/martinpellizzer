Tulipa breyniana

Tulipa breyniana, commonly known as the Breyn's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained conditions. It blooms in late spring and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
