Teucrium pruinosum var. aksarayense

Teucrium pruinosum var. aksarayense, commonly known as the Aksaray germander, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
