Transberingia bursifolia subsp. virgata

Transberingia bursifolia subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the Alaska sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or violet flowers during the short growing season. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
