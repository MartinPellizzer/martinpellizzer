Toddalia aculeata

Toddalia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and spiny branches. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a rough texture.

Toddalia aculeata is also known for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
