Thymus serpyllum var. zobelii

Thymus serpyllum var. zobelii, commonly known as Zobel's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, creeping stems with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Zobel's thyme is valued for its medicinal properties and culinary uses, similar to other thyme varieties.
