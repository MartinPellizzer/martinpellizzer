Thymus creticola

Thymus creticola, commonly known as Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus creticola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
