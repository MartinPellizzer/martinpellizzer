Thymus rariflorus

Thymus rariflorus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and herbal gardens.
