Toxicodendron dregeanum

Toxicodendron dregeanum, commonly known as the African lacquer tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is known for its ability to exude a toxic sap that can cause skin irritation.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
