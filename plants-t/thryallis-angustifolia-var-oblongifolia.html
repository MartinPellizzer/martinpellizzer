Thryallis angustifolia var. oblongifolia

Thryallis angustifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf thryallis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
