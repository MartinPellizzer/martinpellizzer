Trichosanthes tricuspidata f. asperifolia

Trichosanthes tricuspidata f. asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-flowered trumpet vine, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "asperifolia.". The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a trumpet-shaped corolla, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a long, green, ribbed capsule that matures into a spiny, orange-red structure.
