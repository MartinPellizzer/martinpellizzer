Toona hexandra var. grandifoliola

Toona hexandra var. grandifoliola, commonly known as the large-leaf toona, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
