Taraxacum amansii

Taraxacum amansii, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single, bright yellow flower that develops into a distinctive seed head with white, feathery seeds. Native to East Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
