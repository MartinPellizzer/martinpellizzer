Taxus obscura

Taxus obscura, commonly known as the dark yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Appalachian Mountains in the United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, scale-like leaves and produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain birds. The plant contains taxine alkaloids, which are toxic compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. Taxus obscura typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach heights of up to 20 feet.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
