Terminalia gracilis

Terminalia gracilis, commonly known as the slender terminalia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, round drupes that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
