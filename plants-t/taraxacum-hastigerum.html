Taraxacum hastigerum

Taraxacum hastigerum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers produce wind-dispersed seeds with feathery pappi.

Taraxacum hastigerum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
