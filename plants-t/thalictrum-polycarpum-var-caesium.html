Thalictrum polycarpum var. caesium

Thalictrum polycarpum var. caesium, commonly known as the Caesar's candle, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in moist woodland habitats. It has a long blooming period and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Thalictrum polycarpum var. caesium is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
