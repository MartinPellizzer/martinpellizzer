Tripsacum australe var. hirsutum

Tripsacum australe var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly foliage and tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This species is sometimes cultivated for forage or as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
