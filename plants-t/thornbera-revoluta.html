Thornbera revoluta

Thornbera revoluta, commonly known as the revolute thornbera, is a unique succulent plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Thornbera revoluta is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive prolonged dry periods.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in the wild.
