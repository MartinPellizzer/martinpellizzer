Tradescantia tricolor

Tradescantia tricolor, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features strikingly colorful foliage with variegated leaves in shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia tricolor is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to spread easily through rhizomes.
