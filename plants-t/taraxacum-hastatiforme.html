Taraxacum hastatiforme

Taraxacum hastatiforme, commonly known as the arrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of arrowhead-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
