Tulipa systola

Tulipa systola, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its resilience in colder climates.
