Trigonella monspeliaca var. ovalis

Trigonella monspeliaca var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the legume family, Fabaceae.
