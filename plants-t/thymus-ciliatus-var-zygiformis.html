Thymus ciliatus var. zygiformis

Thymus ciliatus var. zygiformis, commonly known as the zigzag thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive zigzag or scalloped edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
