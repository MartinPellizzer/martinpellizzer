Tephroseris papposa var. aurata

Tephroseris papposa var. aurata, commonly known as the golden tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray florets that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular disk flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
