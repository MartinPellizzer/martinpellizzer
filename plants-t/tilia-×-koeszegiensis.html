Tilia × koeszegiensis

Tilia × koeszegiensis, commonly known as the Kőszeg tilia, is a hybrid species within the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-7 cm in length and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is native to Hungary and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
