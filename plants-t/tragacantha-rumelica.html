Tragacantha rumelica

Tragacantha rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a binding agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha rumelica is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
