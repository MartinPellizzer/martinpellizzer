Trichoglottis tomentosa

Trichoglottis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly trichoglottis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Trichoglottis tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
