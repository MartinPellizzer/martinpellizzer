Trema schiedeanum

Trema schiedeanum, commonly known as the red sanders, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is also valued for horticultural and commercial purposes.
