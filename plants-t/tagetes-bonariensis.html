Tagetes bonariensis

Tagetes bonariensis, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow and orange flowers, which are typically daisy-like in appearance. The plant is an annual herb that grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are rich in essential oils and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Tagetes bonariensis make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and pest control in agricultural settings.
