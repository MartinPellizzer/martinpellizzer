Tonningia polyrhiza

Tonningia polyrhiza, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to thrive in cool, humid environments.

Tonningia polyrhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
