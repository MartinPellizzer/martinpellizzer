Tulipa turcarum

Tulipa turcarum, commonly known as the Carpathian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

Tulipa turcarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
