Thamnocalamus spathiflorus var. crassinodus

Thamnocalamus spathiflorus var. crassinodus, commonly known as the broad-fruited bamboo, is a variant of the genus Thamnocalamus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit and long, slender culms that can reach several meters in height. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib, while the inflorescences are clustered and produce small, fragrant flowers. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as hill forests and streamside areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
