Tabebuia ostenfeldii

Tabebuia ostenfeldii, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for tropical landscapes.
