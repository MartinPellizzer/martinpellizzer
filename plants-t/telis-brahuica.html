Telis brahuica

Telis brahuica, commonly known as the sacred mountain orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to withstand the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its beauty and cultural significance, Telis brahuica is highly valued and protected in its native region.
