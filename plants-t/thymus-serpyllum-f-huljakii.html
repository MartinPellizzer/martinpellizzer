Thymus serpyllum f. huljakii

Thymus serpyllum f. huljakii, commonly known as the Huljak's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tiny, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
