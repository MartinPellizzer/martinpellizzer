Trichosanthes lucioniana

Trichosanthes lucioniana, commonly known as the Philippine snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a warty surface and a distinctive green color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically yellow with orange markings.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens.
