Thalictrum petaloideum var. latifoliolatum

Thalictrum petaloideum var. latifoliolatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators in woodland gardens.
