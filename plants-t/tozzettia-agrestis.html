Tozzettia agrestis

Tozzettia agrestis, commonly known as the wild thyme, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant has small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in the spring. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and antifungal qualities.

Tozzettia agrestis is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
