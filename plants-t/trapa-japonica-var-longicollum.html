Trapa japonica var. longicollum

Trapa japonica var. longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features a distinctive, inflated, spiny fruit that floats on the water surface. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are submerged or floating. It produces small white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variety is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
