Thalictrum elatum var. glaucum

Thalictrum elatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue false poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, lace-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale blue in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in alpine meadows and woodland habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
