Teucrium queraltae

Teucrium queraltae, commonly known as the Spanish germander, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Teucrium queraltae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
