Tulipa fransoniana

Tulipa fransoniana, commonly known as the Franson's tulip, is a species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, producing cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Tulipa fransoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
