Taraxacum stenocephalum subsp. daralagesicum

Taraxacum stenocephalum subsp.

daralagesicum, commonly known as the narrow-headed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower stem that bears a composite flower head.

The flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a dense cluster of disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It produces a distinctive, spherical seed head that is easily dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging ecological conditions.
