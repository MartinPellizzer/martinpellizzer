Tafalla lechleri

Tafalla lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces a milky sap that contains natural rubber. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its bark and latex have been used for centuries by indigenous communities for various purposes, including crafting and medicine.

The tree plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of its habitat and is also economically significant for rubber production.
