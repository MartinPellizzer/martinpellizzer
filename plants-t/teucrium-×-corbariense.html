Teucrium × corbariense

Teucrium × corbariense, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pink to purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Teucrium × corbariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
