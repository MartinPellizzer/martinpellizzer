Terebinthus submoniliformis

Terebinthus submoniliformis, commonly known as the clustered terebinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in small clusters, followed by small, leathery fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
