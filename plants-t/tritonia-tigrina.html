Tritonia tigrina

Tritonia tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that resemble tiger stripes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually fragrant and can vary in color from white to pink and purple.

Tritonia tigrina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
