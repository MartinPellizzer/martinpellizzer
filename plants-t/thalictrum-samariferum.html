Thalictrum samariferum

Thalictrum samariferum, commonly known as the samariferum meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Thalictrum samariferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
