Themeda gossweileri

Themeda gossweileri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control.
