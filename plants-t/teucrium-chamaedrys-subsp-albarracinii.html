Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. albarracinii

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

albarracinii, commonly known as the white teucrium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white or pale blue flowers, which contrast with its silver-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
