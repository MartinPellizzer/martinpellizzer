Tanacetum tenuissimum

Tanacetum tenuissimum, commonly known as slender threadleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and thread-like, giving it its common name, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a nectar source for pollinators.
