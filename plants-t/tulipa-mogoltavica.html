Tulipa mogoltavica

Tulipa mogoltavica, commonly known as the Mogoltava tulip, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa, which includes a wide variety of flowering plants in the lily family. The plant is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
