Tetrastigma petrophilum

Tetrastigma petrophilum, commonly known as the rock grape vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by yellow berries. It thrives in rocky and mountainous environments, often growing on cliffs or in shaded forest areas.
