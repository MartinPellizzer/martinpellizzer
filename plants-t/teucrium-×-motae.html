Teucrium × motae

Teucrium × motae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Teucrium × motae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
