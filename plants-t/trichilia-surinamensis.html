Trichilia surinamensis

Trichilia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicle inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local timber industries.
