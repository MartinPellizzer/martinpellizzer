Taraxacum christiansenii

Taraxacum christiansenii, commonly known as the Christiansen's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species is adapted to cold and arid environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
