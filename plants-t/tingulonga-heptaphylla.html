Tingulonga heptaphylla

Tingulonga heptaphylla, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. Its leaves are broad and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

Tingulonga heptaphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal flowering habit.
