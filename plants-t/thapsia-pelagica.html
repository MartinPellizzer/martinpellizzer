Thapsia pelagica

Thapsia pelagica, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Thapsia pelagica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
