Thrixspermum trichoglottis

Thrixspermum trichoglottis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
