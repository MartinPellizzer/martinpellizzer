Tiarella cordifolia f. parviflora

Tiarella cordifolia f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered foamflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early to mid-spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
