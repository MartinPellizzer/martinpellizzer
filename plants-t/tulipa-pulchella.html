Tulipa pulchella

Tulipa pulchella, commonly known as the pink fairy tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and Iran. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a bulbous underground stem.

Tulipa pulchella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
