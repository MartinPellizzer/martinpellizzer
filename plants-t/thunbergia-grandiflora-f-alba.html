Thunbergia grandiflora f. alba

Thunbergia grandiflora f. alba, commonly known as the white thunbergia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped white flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in humid climates.

The white variety is distinguished by its lack of the typical blue or purple pigmentation found in other forms of Thunbergia.
