Tephrosia oblongifolia

Tephrosia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.

Tephrosia oblongifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in various ecosystems.
