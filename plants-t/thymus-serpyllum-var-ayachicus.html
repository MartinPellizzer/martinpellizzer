Thymus serpyllum var. ayachicus

Thymus serpyllum var. ayachicus, commonly known as the Ayachic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, gray-green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
