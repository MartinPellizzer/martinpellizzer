Trevesia palmata var. costata

Trevesia palmata var. costata, commonly known as the Costate Trevesia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately compound leaves with a serrated margin. The leaves are often a deep green, and the plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and is sometimes used in landscape design along the shoreline.
