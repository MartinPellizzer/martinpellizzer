Thunbergia coerulea

Thunbergia coerulea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is often grown as a climbing vine or shrub. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia coerulea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
