Tasmannia glaucifolia

Tasmannia glaucifolia, commonly known as the glossy tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically a deep green with a waxy, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.

Tasmannia glaucifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
