Trochocephalus siculus

Trochocephalus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. The plant has small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with four petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is adapted to withstand drought and saline conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
