Terebinthus tomentosa

Terebinthus tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Terebinthus tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
