Tilia baroniana

Tilia baroniana, commonly known as the Baron's linden, is a deciduous tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, oval fruits that are typically dispersed by wind. It is known for its strong, durable wood and is often found in moist, fertile soils along rivers and streams.

Tilia baroniana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
