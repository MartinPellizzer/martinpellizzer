Tetradenia clarissae

Tetradenia clarissae, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Tetradenia clarissae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
