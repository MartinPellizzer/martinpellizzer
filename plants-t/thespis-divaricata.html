Thespis divaricata

Thespis divaricata, commonly known as the variable thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in various habitats including grasslands and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found growing in clusters and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Thespis divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
