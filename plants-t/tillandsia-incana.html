Tillandsia incana

Tillandsia incana, commonly known as the silver vernal, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal direct sunlight.

Tillandsia incana is popular among succulent and air plant enthusiasts for its unique aesthetic and low maintenance requirements.
