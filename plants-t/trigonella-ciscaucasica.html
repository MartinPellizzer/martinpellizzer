Trigonella ciscaucasica

Trigonella ciscaucasica, commonly known as Caucasian fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been studied for potential health benefits.
