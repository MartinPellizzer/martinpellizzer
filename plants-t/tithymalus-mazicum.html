Tithymalus mazicum

Tithymalus mazicum, commonly known as the Mazic Mountain Thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic properties.

Tithymalus mazicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
