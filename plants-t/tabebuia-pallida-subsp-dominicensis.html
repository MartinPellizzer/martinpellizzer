Tabebuia pallida subsp. dominicensis

Tabebuia pallida subsp.

dominicensis, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Dominica and other nearby islands. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
