Trisetum evolutum

Trisetum evolutum, commonly known as the spreading basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Trisetum evolutum is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.
