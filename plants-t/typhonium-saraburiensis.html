Typhonium saraburiensis

Typhonium saraburiensis, commonly known as the Saraburi arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes. The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in wetland habitats.
