Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. mollis

Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. mollis, commonly known as the soft-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
