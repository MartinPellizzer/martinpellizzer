Thelypteris stegnogrammopsis

Thelypteris stegnogrammopsis, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in circular or linear arrangements. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and along stream banks.

The morphology of Thelypteris stegnogrammopsis reflects its adaptation to humid, nutrient-rich soils, making it a common sight in woodland habitats.
