Tinus candolleana

Tinus candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana tinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
