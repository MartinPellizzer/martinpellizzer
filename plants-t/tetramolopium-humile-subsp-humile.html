Tetramolopium humile subsp. humile

Tetramolopium humile subsp.

humile, commonly known as the smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. Its small, pale yellow to white flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
