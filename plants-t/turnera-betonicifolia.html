Turnera betonicifolia

Turnera betonicifolia, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
