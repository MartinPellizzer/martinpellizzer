Teucrium quadrifarium var. kouytchense

Teucrium quadrifarium var. kouytchense, commonly known as the Caucasian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
