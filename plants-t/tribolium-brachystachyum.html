Tribolium brachystachyum

Tribolium brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Tribolium brachystachyum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
