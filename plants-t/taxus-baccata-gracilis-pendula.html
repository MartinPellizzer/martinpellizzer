Taxus baccata gracilis-pendula

Taxus baccata gracilis-pendula, commonly known as the weeping yew, is a cultivar of the yew tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its elegant form and hardy nature, though it requires careful handling due to its toxic properties.
