Thymus serpyllum proles silvicola

Thymus serpyllum proles silvicola, commonly known as the woodland thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of small, fragrant leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in forest edges, rocky areas, and woodland clearings. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to form extensive colonies in suitable habitats.
