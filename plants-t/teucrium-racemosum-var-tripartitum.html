Teucrium racemosum var. tripartitum

Teucrium racemosum var. tripartitum, commonly known as the three-parted germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The varietal name 'tripartitum' refers to the three-parted structure of its leaves, a distinguishing feature of this botanical variant.
