Tillandsia rauhii var. longispica

Tillandsia rauhii var. longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking inflorescences with long, slender spathes and showy, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a sought-after specimen among bromeliad enthusiasts.
