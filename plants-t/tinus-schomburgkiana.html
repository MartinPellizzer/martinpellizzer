Tinus schomburgkiana

Tinus schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgkiana kaffir lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tall flower spikes that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically occurs in late winter to early spring.
