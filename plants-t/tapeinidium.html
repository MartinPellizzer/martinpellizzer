Tapeinidium

Tapeinidium, commonly known as the glassy-winged sharpshooter, is a type of planthopper found primarily in the Americas.

It is characterized by its transparent wings and elongated body, which help it blend with its environment. The plant plays a significant role in the transmission of plant pathogens, particularly those causing Pierce's disease in grapes. Its feeding habits involve piercing plant tissues to extract sap, which can lead to the spread of harmful diseases.

Despite its ecological impact, the plant itself does not have significant botanical uses or ornamental value.
