Trichocline angustifolia

Trichocline angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf trichocline, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
