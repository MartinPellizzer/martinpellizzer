Triticum durum f. novochercascum

Triticum durum f. novochercascum, commonly known as the Novochercascum durum wheat, is a botanical variant of durum wheat characterized by its distinct morphological traits.

This form exhibits unique growth patterns, including a more compact plant structure and altered grain characteristics compared to standard durum wheat. It is native to specific regions in the Caucasus and is adapted to local climatic conditions. The plant's botanical features make it of interest for genetic studies and potential agricultural applications.

Its classification as a formal variety highlights its significance in taxonomic and breeding research.
