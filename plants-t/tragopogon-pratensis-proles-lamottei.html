Tragopogon pratensis proles lamottei

Tragopogon pratensis proles lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's meadow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
