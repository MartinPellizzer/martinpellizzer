Tsuga tsuja

Tsuga tsuja, commonly known as the hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is typically smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tsuga tsuja is often found in moist, cool climates and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
