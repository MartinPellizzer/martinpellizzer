Teucrium lerrouxii

Teucrium lerrouxii, commonly known as the Algerian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit make it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Teucrium lerrouxii is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
