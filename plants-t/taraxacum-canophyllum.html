Taraxacum canophyllum

Taraxacum canophyllum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that disperses its seeds via the wind. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high elevations.

Taraxacum canophyllum is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.
