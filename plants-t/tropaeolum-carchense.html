Tropaeolum carchense

Tropaeolum carchense, commonly known as the Carchi nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, peppery-tasting seeds that are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible components.
