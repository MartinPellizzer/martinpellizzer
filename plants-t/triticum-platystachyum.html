Triticum platystachyum

Triticum platystachyum, commonly known as the flat-stalked wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
