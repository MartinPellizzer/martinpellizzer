Tephrosia quartiniana

Tephrosia quartiniana, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a pinnate structure, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Tephrosia quartiniana is also noted for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
