Taraxacum excellens f. dentatum

Taraxacum excellens f. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its distinctive toothed leaf margins.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical, fluffy seed head known as a pappus. Its leaves are usually basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
