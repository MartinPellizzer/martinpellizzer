Trifolium bifurcatum

Trifolium bifurcatum, commonly known as the forked clover, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a symmetrical pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Trifolium bifurcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some cultures.
