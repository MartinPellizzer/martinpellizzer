Thuraria

Thuraria, commonly known as the golden thread, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Thuraria diffusa, which is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically finely divided and aromatic, similar to other members of the carrot family.

Thuraria is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
