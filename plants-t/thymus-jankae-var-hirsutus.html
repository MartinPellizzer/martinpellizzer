Thymus jankae var. hirsutus

Thymus jankae var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, creeping stems and narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, drought-tolerant ground cover.
