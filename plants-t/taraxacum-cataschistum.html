Taraxacum cataschistum

Taraxacum cataschistum, commonly known as the southern dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to southern regions of the United States, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is a perennial species that is adapted to warm climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
