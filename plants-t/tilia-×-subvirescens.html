Tilia × subvirescens

Tilia × subvirescens, commonly known as the whitebeam or small-leaved lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves that are typically lighter green than those of its parent species. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a valued species in both horticulture and natural landscapes.
