Tectaria chattagrammica

Tectaria chattagrammica, commonly known as the Bangladesh shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

Tectaria chattagrammica plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
