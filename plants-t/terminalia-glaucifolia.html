Terminalia glaucifolia

Terminalia glaucifolia, commonly known as the white-leaf terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
