Thunbergia crispa

Thunbergia crispa, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Thunbergia crispa is also valued for its ability to climb using twining stems, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
