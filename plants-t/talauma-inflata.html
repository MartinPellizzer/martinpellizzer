Talauma inflata

Talauma inflata, commonly known as the inflated talauma, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated fruit that resembles a small balloon, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth surface, while the flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit's structure plays a role in seed dispersal, as it can be carried by wind or animals.
