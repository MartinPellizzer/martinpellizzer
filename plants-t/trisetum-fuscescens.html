Trisetum fuscescens

Trisetum fuscescens, commonly known as the dark hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, dark green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, grass-like blades and a clump-forming growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
