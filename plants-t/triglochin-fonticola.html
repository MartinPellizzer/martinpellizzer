Triglochin fonticola

Triglochin fonticola, commonly known as the water starwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Triglochin fonticola is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
