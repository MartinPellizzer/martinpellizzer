Theodora speciosa

Theodora speciosa, commonly known as the showy theodora, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Theodora speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
