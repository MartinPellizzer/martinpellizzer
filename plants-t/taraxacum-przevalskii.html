Taraxacum przevalskii

Taraxacum przevalskii, commonly known as the Przhevalsk's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
