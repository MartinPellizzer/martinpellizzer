Tridax erecta

Tridax erecta, commonly known as the upright tridax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Tridax erecta is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in many areas.
