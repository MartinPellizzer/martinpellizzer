Typhonium violifolium

Typhonium violifolium, commonly known as the violet-leaf elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces striking violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomatous growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and water features.
