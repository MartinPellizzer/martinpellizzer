Tibouchina nobilis

Tibouchina nobilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Tibouchina nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
