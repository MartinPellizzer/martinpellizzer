Thea furfuracea

Thea furfuracea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Thea furfuracea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
