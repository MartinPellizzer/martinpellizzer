Trichocereus damazioi

Trichocereus damazioi, commonly known as the "Laguna" cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in ideal conditions. Its stems are often covered in dense, white spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight.

Trichocereus damazioi is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and its role in the cultivation of certain psychoactive compounds.
