Tropilis falcorostra

Tropilis falcorostra, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are slender and often covered in fine hairs.

Tropilis falcorostra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
