Teucrium eburneum

Teucrium eburneum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium eburneum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
