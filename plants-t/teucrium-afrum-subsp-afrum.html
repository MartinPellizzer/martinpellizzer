Teucrium afrum subsp. afrum

Teucrium afrum subsp.

afrum, commonly known as African germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
