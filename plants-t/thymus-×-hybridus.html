Thymus × hybridus

Thymus × hybridus, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it is a hybrid between Thymus serpyllum and Thymus vulgaris, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species.
