Talinum decumbens

Talinum decumbens, commonly known as red-rooted nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features prostrate growth habits with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Talinum decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
