Trichinium fusiforme var. gracile

Trichinium fusiforme var. gracile, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated pedicels. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as along stream banks and in woodland edges.
