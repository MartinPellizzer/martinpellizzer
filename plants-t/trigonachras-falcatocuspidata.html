Trigonachras falcatocuspidata

Trigonachras falcatocuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant's leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a common trait among ferns.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to aquatic life, including reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
