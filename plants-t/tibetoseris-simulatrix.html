Tibetoseris simulatrix

Tibetoseris simulatrix, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are blue to purple in color, often appearing in clusters.

Tibetoseris simulatrix is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
