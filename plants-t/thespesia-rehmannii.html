Thespesia rehmannii

Thespesia rehmannii, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii.

Thespesia rehmannii plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
