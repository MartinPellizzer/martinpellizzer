Trifolium ochroleucon var. pannonicum

Trifolium ochroleucon var. pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in nitrogen fixation and soil stabilization.
