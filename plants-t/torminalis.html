Torminalis

Torminalis, commonly known as the torminalis plant, is a species belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Torminalis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
