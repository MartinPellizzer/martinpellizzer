Thottea reniloba

Thottea reniloba, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thottea reniloba is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
