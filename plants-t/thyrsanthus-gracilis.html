Thyrsanthus gracilis

Thyrsanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender thyrsanthus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact, rounded inflorescence called a thyrse. It typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, often in sandy or rocky soils.

Thyrsanthus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
