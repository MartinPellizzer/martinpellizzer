Thlaspi granatense

Thlaspi granatense, commonly known as the granate cabbageweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in gardens. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are usually lobed and hairy.
