Tricholepidium subhemionitideum

Tricholepidium subhemionitideum, commonly known as the small-leaved bladderwrack, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Fucaceae.

It is characterized by its thin, ribbon-like blades that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant exhibits a filamentous thallus with a distinctive central axis and branching structures. It thrives in intertidal zones, often found attached to rocks in shallow coastal waters.

Its reproductive structures include both male and female gametophytes, which contribute to its life cycle in marine environments.
