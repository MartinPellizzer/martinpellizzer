Thismia cordata

Thismia cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved Thismia, is a unique flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, elongated, club-like inflorescence. It produces tiny, white or pale green flowers that are often overlooked due to their size and inconspicuous appearance.

Thismia cordata is also notable for its mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which plays a crucial role in its nutrient uptake.
