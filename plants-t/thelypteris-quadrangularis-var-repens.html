Thelypteris quadrangularis var. repens

Thelypteris quadrangularis var. repens, commonly known as the creeping water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as stream banks and swampy areas. The plant has slender, creeping rhizomes that form dense mats, and its fronds are usually dark green with a glossy appearance. The leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive quadrangular cross-section in cross-section.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in aquatic or bog garden settings.
