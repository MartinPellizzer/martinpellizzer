Teramnus labialis var. acutus

Teramnus labialis var. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved teramnus, is a variant of the teramnus plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, acute leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
