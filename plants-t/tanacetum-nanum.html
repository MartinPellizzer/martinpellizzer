Tanacetum nanum

Tanacetum nanum, commonly known as dwarf tansy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, button-like flower heads that are usually yellow or white, resembling those of chamomile. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
