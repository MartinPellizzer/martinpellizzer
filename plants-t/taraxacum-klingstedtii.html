Taraxacum klingstedtii

Taraxacum klingstedtii, commonly known as the Klingstedt's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to specific regions of Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated stem that terminates in a yellow flower head composed of many tiny florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
