Trianthema fruticosum

Trianthema fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal thornveld, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Trianthema fruticosum plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
