Triticum ibericum

Triticum ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian wheat, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Triticum ibericum is primarily of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
