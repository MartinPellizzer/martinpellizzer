Teucrium petkovii

Teucrium petkovii, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in clusters. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Teucrium petkovii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory benefits.
