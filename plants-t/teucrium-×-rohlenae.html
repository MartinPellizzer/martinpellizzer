Teucrium × rohlenae

Teucrium × rohlenae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
