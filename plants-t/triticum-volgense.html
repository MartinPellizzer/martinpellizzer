Triticum volgense

Triticum volgense, commonly known as Volga wheat, is a species of wild wheat native to the Volga River region in Russia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale green grains that are typically harvested for their nutritional value. Triticum volgense is characterized by its adaptability to temperate climates and its potential as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies of ancient grain species and agricultural botany.
