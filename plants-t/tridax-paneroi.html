Tridax paneroi

Tridax paneroi, commonly known as the beggar's ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces seeds with barbed hairs that attach to animals and clothing, aiding in its dispersal. Tridax paneroi typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

It is considered a weed in many regions due to its aggressive growth and invasive tendencies.
