Tribulus ranunculiflorus

Tribulus ranunculiflorus, commonly known as the yellow ball cactus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with a deep root system for water absorption. Its fruit is a hairy, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
