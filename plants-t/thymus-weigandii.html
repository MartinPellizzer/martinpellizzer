Thymus weigandii

Thymus weigandii, commonly known as the Weigand thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and aromatic, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both garden use and traditional herbal remedies.
