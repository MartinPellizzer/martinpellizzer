Thalictrum leptophyllum

Thalictrum leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in upright, cylindrical spikes, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Thalictrum leptophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
