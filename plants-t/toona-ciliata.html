Toona ciliata

Toona ciliata, commonly known as silky gum or Australian silky gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, silken texture on the underside. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small, green figs. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and cabinetry.

Toona ciliata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
