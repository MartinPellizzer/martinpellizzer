Triplaris fulva

Triplaris fulva, commonly known as the yellow tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated pods containing edible seeds. Triplaris fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
