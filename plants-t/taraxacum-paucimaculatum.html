Taraxacum paucimaculatum

Taraxacum paucimaculatum, commonly known as the few-spotted dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, wind-dispersed seed ball. This species is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also notable for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
