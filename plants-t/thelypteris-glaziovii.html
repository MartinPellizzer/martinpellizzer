Thelypteris glaziovii

Thelypteris glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's holly fern, is a species of fern native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Thelypteris glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
