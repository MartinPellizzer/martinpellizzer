Tanacetum discoideum

Tanacetum discoideum, commonly known as the meadow tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum discoideum is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
