Thymus × georgescui

Thymus × georgescui, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small pink or purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage makes it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
