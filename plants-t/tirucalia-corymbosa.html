Tirucalia corymbosa

Tirucalia corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian rose or false rose, is a shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a rose-like appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Tirucalia corymbosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
