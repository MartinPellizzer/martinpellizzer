Trichantha pubescens

Trichantha pubescens, commonly known as the hairy trichantha, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Trichantha pubescens is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
