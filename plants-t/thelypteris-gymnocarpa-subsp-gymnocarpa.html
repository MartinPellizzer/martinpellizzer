Thelypteris gymnocarpa subsp. gymnocarpa

Thelypteris gymnocarpa subsp.

gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruit water fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate.

The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are often covered with a smooth, glabrous surface. Its rhizomes are creeping and often form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
