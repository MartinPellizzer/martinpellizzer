Terminalia ovata

Terminalia ovata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Terminalia ovata is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
