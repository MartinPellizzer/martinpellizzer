Thelesperma subnudum var. subnudum

Thelesperma subnudum var. subnudum, commonly known as the plains prickly pear, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces fruit that is edible when mature.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
