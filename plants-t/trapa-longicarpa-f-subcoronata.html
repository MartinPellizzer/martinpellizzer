Trapa longicarpa f. subcoronata

Trapa longicarpa f. subcoronata, commonly known as the crowned water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut species native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, spiny fruits that float on the water surface. The plant has submerged leaves that are finely divided, aiding in its aquatic adaptation. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
