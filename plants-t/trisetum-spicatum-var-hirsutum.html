Trisetum spicatum var. hirsutum

Trisetum spicatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of diverse ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
