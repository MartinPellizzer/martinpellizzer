Thalictrum rhodanense

Thalictrum rhodanense, commonly known as the Rhodope meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticultural gardens.
