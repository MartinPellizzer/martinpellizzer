Thymus zygis var. gadorensis

Thymus zygis var. gadorensis, commonly known as the Gadorensis thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact form and fragrant, small gray-green leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
