Thymus desjatovae

Thymus desjatovae, commonly known as the Desjato's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats of small, ovate leaves with a grayish-white underside. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thymus desjatovae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
