Trichinium roei

Trichinium roei, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep red color and contain several seeds.

Trichinium roei is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its toxic properties.
