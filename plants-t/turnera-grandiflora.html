Turnera grandiflora

Turnera grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
