Triticum spelta grex duhamelianum

Triticum spelta grex duhamelianum, commonly known as spelt wheat, is a botanical variety of ancient grain with a rich history of cultivation.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hard, inedible hull that encases the edible grain. This plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and long, narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like structure with multiple spikelets, each containing two to three florets.

The seeds, which are harvested when mature, are used in various culinary and nutritional applications.
