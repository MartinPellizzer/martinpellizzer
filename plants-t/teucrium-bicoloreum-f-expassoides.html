Teucrium bicoloreum f. expassoides

Teucrium bicoloreum f. expassoides, commonly known as the two-colored germander, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive two-toned appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue and purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact habit and long-lasting flowering period.
