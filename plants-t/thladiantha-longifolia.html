Thladiantha longifolia

Thladiantha longifolia, commonly known as the longifoliate gobernadora, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small green leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from underground sources.

Its botanical features make it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems, and it is often used in traditional medicine.
