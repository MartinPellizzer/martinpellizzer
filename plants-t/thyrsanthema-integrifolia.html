Thyrsanthema integrifolia

Thyrsanthema integrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Thyrsanthema integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscaping.
