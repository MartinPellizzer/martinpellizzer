Thymus quinquecostatus var. japonicus

Thymus quinquecostatus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
