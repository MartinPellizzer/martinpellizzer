Tillandsia ferruginascens

Tillandsia ferruginascens, commonly known as the rusty tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
