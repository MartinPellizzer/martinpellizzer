Tritonia latifolia

Tritonia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and smooth, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Tritonia latifolia is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
