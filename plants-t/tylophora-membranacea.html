Tylophora membranacea

Tylophora membranacea, commonly known as the fragrant starvine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, star-shaped white flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
