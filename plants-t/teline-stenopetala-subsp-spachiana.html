Teline stenopetala subsp. spachiana

Teline stenopetala subsp.

spachiana, commonly known as the narrow-petaled teline, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear small, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are typically pale blue to white, with five petals and are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
