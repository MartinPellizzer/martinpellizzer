Triticum sativum subvar. rubrum

Triticum sativum subvar. rubrum, commonly known as red wheat, is a variant of wheat characterized by its reddish husk and bran.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family and is primarily cultivated for its grain. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stalks and spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The seeds, or grains, are enclosed in the husk and are used for flour production and animal feed.
