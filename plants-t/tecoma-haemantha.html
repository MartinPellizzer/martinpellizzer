Tecoma haemantha

Tecoma haemantha, commonly known as the red trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has a slender, often multi-branched stem and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma haemantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
