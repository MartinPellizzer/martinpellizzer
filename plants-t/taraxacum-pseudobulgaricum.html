Taraxacum pseudobulgaricum

Taraxacum pseudobulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a wind-dispersed seed sphere. Native to the Balkan Peninsula, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including poor soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions of Europe.
