Taraxacum incomptum

Taraxacum incomptum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a slender, hollow stem that supports its yellow flower head. Its flowers develop into elongated, bristled seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum incomptum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
