Thelymitra media var. carneolutea

Thelymitra media var. carneolutea, commonly known as the pink lady's tongs, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf that is often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces a spike of pale pink to cream-colored flowers with a distinctive, narrow, tongue-like lip. Its blooming period typically occurs during late winter to early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
