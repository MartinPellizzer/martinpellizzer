Tanaecium cyrtanthum

Tanaecium cyrtanthum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tanaecium cyrtanthum is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
