Tribulus cistoides var. anacanthus

Tribulus cistoides var. anacanthus, commonly known as the hairy caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits that resemble small, hard balls, which contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are small, hairy, and alternate, while its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
