Thesium breyeri

Thesium breyeri, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.
