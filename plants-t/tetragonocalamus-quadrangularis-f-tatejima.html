Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. tatejima

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. tatejima, commonly known as the Tatejima bamboo, is a rare and distinctive variant of the quadrangular bamboo.

It is characterized by its unique, four-sided stem structure, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
