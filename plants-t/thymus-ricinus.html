Thymus ricinus

Thymus ricinus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, and it is valued for its aromatic properties and use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plantings.
