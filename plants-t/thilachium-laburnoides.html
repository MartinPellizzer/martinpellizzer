Thilachium laburnoides

Thilachium laburnoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small clouds or mists. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thilachium laburnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
