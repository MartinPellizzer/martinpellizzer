Thlaspi montanum var. elatum

Thlaspi montanum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall mountain cabbages, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a common feature in the Brassicaceae family. It is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in mountainous habitats.
