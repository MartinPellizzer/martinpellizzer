Trochocephalus aleppicus

Trochocephalus aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. Its fruit, the date, is a staple food in many cultures and is rich in nutrients.

The Aleppo date palm is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in ancient civilizations.
