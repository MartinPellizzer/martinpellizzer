Tragacantha vera

Tragacantha vera, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested for its medicinal and industrial uses. Tragacantha vera thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
