Thornbera pringlei

Thornbera pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Thornbera pringlei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
