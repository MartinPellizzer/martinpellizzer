Turbinicarpus horripilus subsp. wrobelianus

Turbinicarpus horripilus subsp.

wrobelianus, commonly known as the Wrobel's desert rose, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It features a compact, globular stem with thick, ribbed segments that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
