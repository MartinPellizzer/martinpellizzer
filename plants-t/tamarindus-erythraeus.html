Tamarindus erythraeus

Tamarindus erythraeus, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruit, which contains a sour pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer. The tree can grow up to 12 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green and appear in clusters, followed by the development of the characteristic fruit.

The plant is well adapted to dry climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and timber.
