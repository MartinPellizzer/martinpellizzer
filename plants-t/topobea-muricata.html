Topobea muricata

Topobea muricata, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with small spines.

Topobea muricata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
