Tournefortia sericea

Tournefortia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-green, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Tournefortia sericea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
