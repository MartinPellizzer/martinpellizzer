Trichoglottis adnata

Trichoglottis adnata, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are often fragrant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Trichoglottis adnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
