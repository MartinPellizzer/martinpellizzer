Tasmannia reticulata

Tasmannia reticulata, commonly known as the Tasmanian blueberry, is a shrub native to the cool temperate rainforests of Tasmania.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are leathery and have a distinctive reticulate pattern. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understorey habitats.

Tasmannia reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
