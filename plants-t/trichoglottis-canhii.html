Trichoglottis canhii

Trichoglottis canhii, commonly known as the Canh's terry orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that produce a single leaf per node. The plant produces inflorescences of small, fragrant flowers with white to pale yellow petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Trichoglottis canhii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
