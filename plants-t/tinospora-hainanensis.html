Tinospora hainanensis

Tinospora hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan Tinospora, is a woody vine native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. It is often found in humid, tropical forests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Tinospora hainanensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
