Tragacantha monspessulana

Tragacantha monspessulana, commonly known as the Spanish thorn or red thorn, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant produces bright red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are small, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often have thorns.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
