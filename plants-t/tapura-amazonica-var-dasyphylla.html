Tapura amazonica var. dasyphylla

Tapura amazonica var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the Amazonian tapura, is a striking plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used by indigenous communities for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Overall, it plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
