Trifolium pratense var. expansum

Trifolium pratense var. expansum, commonly known as the broad-leaved white clover, is a variant of the common white clover.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves with a white or pale pink bloom. This plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to many parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and its role in agricultural and ecological systems.
