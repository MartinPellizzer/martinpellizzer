Triumfetta welwitschii var. rogersii

Triumfetta welwitschii var. rogersii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
