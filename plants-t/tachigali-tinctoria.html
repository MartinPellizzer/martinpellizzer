Tachigali tinctoria

Tachigali tinctoria, commonly known as the red bean tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
