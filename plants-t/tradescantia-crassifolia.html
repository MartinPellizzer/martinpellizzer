Tradescantia crassifolia

Tradescantia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
