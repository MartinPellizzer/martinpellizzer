Triodanis perfoliata

Triodanis perfoliata, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often perforated, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Triodanis perfoliata is often found growing in colonies, and its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
