Tigridia pulchella

Tigridia pulchella, commonly known as the scarlet tiger flower, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with bright red petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tigridia pulchella is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ornamental value.
