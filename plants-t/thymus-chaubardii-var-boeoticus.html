Thymus chaubardii var. boeoticus

Thymus chaubardii var. boeoticus, commonly known as Boeotic thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
