Thymus praecox subsp. widderi

Thymus praecox subsp.

widderi, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
