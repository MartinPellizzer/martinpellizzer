Tragia stylaris var. angustifolia

Tragia stylaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tragia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous minute seeds.

Tragia stylaris var. angustifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats.
