Tylophora indica var. intermedia

Tylophora indica var. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming pattern make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
