Tubella amygdalodora

Tubella amygdalodora, commonly known as the almond-shaped tubella, is a species of sea anemone found in marine environments.

It belongs to the family Tubellariidae and is characterized by its unique, almond-shaped body structure. The organism has a soft, gelatinous body with a central mouth surrounded by tentacles used for capturing prey. It typically inhabits shallow coastal waters and is often found attached to rocks or other substrates.

Its botanical classification places it within the phylum Cnidaria, highlighting its evolutionary relationship to other marine invertebrates.
