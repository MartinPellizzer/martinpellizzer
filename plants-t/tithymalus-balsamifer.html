Tithymalus balsamifer

Tithymalus balsamifer, commonly known as the balsam thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Tithymalus balsamifer thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
