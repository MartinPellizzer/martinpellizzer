Taxodium distichum var. mexicanum

Taxodium distichum var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to southeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, scale-like leaves that turn a striking red-brown color in the fall before shedding. The tree produces small, inconspicuous cones that release winged seeds, aiding in its reproduction. Its roots often form a buttressed structure at the base, providing stability in wet, swampy habitats.

This species is adapted to moist environments and is often found in riparian zones along rivers and streams.
