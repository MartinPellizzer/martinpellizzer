Trommsdorffia crepioides

Trommsdorffia crepioides, commonly known as the crepeleaf or crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling crepe paper, hence its common name. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. Trommsdorffia crepioides thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
