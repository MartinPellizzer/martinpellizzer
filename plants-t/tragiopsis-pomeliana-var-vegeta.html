Tragiopsis pomeliana var. vegeta

Tragiopsis pomeliana var. vegeta, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers. The plant produces copious amounts of nectar, making it a popular choice for attracting butterflies and other pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
