Taraxacum pseudonigricans

Taraxacum pseudonigricans, commonly known as the blackweed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. The plant is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
