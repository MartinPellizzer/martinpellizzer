Thelypteris furcatovenulosa

Thelypteris furcatovenulosa, commonly known as the fork-veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant's leaves have distinctive forked venation, which is a key identifying feature. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Thelypteris furcatovenulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
