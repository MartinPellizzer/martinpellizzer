Torilis ucranica var. aculeata

Torilis ucranica var. aculeata, commonly known as the Ukrainian hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often glandular, which can produce a slight odor.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in ecological succession.
