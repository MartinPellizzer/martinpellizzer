Tragacantha miniata

Tragacantha miniata, commonly known as the dwarf tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
