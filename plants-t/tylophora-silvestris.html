Tylophora silvestris

Tylophora silvestris, commonly known as the wild tylophora, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India, China, and parts of Indonesia. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, with small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tylophora silvestris is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
