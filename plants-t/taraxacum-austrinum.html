Taraxacum austrinum

Taraxacum austrinum, commonly known as the Australian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
