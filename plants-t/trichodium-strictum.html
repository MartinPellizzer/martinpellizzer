Trichodium strictum

Trichodium strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trichodium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its botanical classification places it in the family Apiaceae, which includes parsley and carrots.

Trichodium strictum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
