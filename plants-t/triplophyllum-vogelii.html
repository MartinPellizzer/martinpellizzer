Triplophyllum vogelii

Triplophyllum vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's triple-leaf, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically around a central axis. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or green, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
