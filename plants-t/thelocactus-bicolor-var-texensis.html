Thelocactus bicolor var. texensis

Thelocactus bicolor var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas night-blooming cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Texas.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from both heat and herbivores. The plant produces striking, nocturnal flowers that open at night, typically in shades of yellow and white, attracting specific pollinators. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh desert environment.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
