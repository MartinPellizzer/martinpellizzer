Thymus riatarum

Thymus riatarum, commonly known as the Riata thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications for their aromatic, slightly minty flavor.
