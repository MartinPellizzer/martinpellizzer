Toona serrata

Toona serrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Toona serrata is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
