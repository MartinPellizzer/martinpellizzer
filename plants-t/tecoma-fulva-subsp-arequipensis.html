Tecoma fulva subsp. arequipensis

Tecoma fulva subsp.

arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa trumpetflower, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and often resemble trumpets.

The plant has woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
