Tephrosia nematophylla

Tephrosia nematophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
