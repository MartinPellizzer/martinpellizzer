Tribulus nogalensis

Tribulus nogalensis, commonly known as the desert tribulus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Zygophyllaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tribulus nogalensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
