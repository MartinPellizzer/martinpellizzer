Tulipa anisophylla

Tulipa anisophylla, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often recurved petals that are typically a deep red or purple in color. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Tulipa anisophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
