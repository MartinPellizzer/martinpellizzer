Tamarix serotina

Tamarix serotina, commonly known as the winter tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing tolerance to drought and saline soils.

Tamarix serotina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
