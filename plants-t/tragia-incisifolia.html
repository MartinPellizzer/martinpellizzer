Tragia incisifolia

Tragia incisifolia, commonly known as the sawleaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Tragia incisifolia is adapted to thrive in dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
