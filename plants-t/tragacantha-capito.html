Tragacantha capito

Tragacantha capito, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for producing a resinous gum, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food additive.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
