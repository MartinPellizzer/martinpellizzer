Trimenia bougainvilleensis

Trimenia bougainvilleensis, commonly known as the Bougainvillea.

This flowering plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its vibrant, papery bracts that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and orange. It is a member of the Nyctaginaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the colorful bracts.
