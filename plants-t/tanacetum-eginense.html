Tanacetum eginense

Tanacetum eginense, commonly known as the Eginense tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Tanacetum. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Tanacetum eginense is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
