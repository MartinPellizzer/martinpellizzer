Tragacantha multifoliolata

Tragacantha multifoliolata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed, and its distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
