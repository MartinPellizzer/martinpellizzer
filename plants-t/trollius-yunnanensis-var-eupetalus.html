Trollius yunnanensis var. eupetalus

Trollius yunnanensis var. eupetalus, commonly known as the Yunnan globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are generally unbranched.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
