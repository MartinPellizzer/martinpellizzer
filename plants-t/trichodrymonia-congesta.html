Trichodrymonia congesta

Trichodrymonia congesta, commonly known as the clustered feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight roughness along the edges.

Trichodrymonia congesta is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
