Thymus kapelae

Thymus kapelae, commonly known as the Kapela thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
