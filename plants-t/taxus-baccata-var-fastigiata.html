Taxus baccata var. fastigiata

Taxus baccata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright yew, is a compact, columnar evergreen conifer native to Europe.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that grows in tight, upright clusters, giving it a distinctive, formal appearance. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is highly resistant to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for landscaping.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
