Triglochin salina

Triglochin salina, commonly known as salt marsh grass or sea clubmoss, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions and is often found in salt marshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

Triglochin salina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
