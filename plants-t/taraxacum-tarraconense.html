Taraxacum tarraconense

Taraxacum tarraconense, commonly known as the Iberian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is often found in Mediterranean regions.

Taraxacum tarraconense is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep taproot system for water storage.
