Tragacantha idaea

Tragacantha idaea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments, making it an important species in arid ecosystems.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen also contributes to soil enrichment in its native habitats.
