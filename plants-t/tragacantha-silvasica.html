Tragacantha silvasica

Tragacantha silvasica, commonly known as the Silvasica tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Astragalus genus within the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
