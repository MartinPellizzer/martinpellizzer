Terminalis banksii

Terminalis banksii, commonly known as the Banksia terminalis, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Terminalis banksii is typically found in coastal heathlands and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
