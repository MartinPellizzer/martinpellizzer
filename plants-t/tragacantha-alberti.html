Tragacantha alberti

Tragacantha alberti, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
