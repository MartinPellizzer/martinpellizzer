Tragopyrum grandiflorum

Tragopyrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with spiny margins and a basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Tragopyrum grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
