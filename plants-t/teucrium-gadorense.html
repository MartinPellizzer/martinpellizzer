Teucrium gadorense

Teucrium gadorense, commonly known as the Canary Islands germander, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Teucrium gadorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
