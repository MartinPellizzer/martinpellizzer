Tragacantha alopecuroides

Tragacantha alopecuroides, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
