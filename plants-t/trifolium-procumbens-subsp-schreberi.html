Trifolium procumbens subsp. schreberi

Trifolium procumbens subsp.

schreberi, commonly known as Schreber's prostrate clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to ovate leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
