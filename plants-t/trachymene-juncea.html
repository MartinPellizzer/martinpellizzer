Trachymene juncea

Trachymene juncea, commonly known as the slender false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and have a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Trachymene juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
