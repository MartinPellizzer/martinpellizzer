Trigonella tetrapetala

Trigonella tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
