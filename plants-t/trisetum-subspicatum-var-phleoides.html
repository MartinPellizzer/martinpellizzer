Trisetum subspicatum var. phleoides

Trisetum subspicatum var. phleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
