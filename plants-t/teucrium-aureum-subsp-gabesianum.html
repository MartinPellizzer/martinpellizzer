Teucrium aureum subsp. gabesianum

Teucrium aureum subsp.

gabesianum, commonly known as the golden germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both garden settings and ecological landscapes.
