Teucrium capitatum var. lazari

Teucrium capitatum var. lazari, commonly known as Lazari germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
