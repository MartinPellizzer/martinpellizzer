Trifolium campestre var. pandoi

Trifolium campestre var. pandoi, commonly known as the Pandoi clover, is a variant of the common trefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization.
