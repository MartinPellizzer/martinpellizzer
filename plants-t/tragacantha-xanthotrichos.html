Tragacantha xanthotrichos

Tragacantha xanthotrichos, commonly known as the yellow-haired tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its gel-like substance, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
