Trisetum loeflingianum var. cavanillesii

Trisetum loeflingianum var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
