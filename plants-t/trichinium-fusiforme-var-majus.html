Trichinium fusiforme var. majus

Trichinium fusiforme var. majus, commonly known as the greater spindle tree, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Trichinium fusiforme var. majus is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
