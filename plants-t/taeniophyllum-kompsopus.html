Taeniophyllum kompsopus

Taeniophyllum kompsopus, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
