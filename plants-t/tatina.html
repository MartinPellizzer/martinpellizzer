Tatina

Tatina, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the genus Saintpaulia and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and long blooming period.

Tatina is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
