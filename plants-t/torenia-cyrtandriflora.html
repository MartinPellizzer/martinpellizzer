Torenia cyrtandriflora

Torenia cyrtandriflora, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Torenia cyrtandriflora thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
