Tripodion graecum

Tripodion graecum, commonly known as the Greek tripodion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Tripodion graecum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
