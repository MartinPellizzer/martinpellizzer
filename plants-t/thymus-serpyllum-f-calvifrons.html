Thymus serpyllum f. calvifrons

Thymus serpyllum f. calvifrons, commonly known as the smooth-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so, giving it its distinguishing name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to pale purple flowers throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
