Thuja occidentalis f. wareana-lutescens

Thuja occidentalis f. wareana-lutescens, commonly known as the golden eastern arborvitae, is a unique variant of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with the typical green color of the species. This form exhibits a compact, pyramidal shape, making it an ornamental choice for gardens and landscapes. The leaves are scale-like and closely pressed to the branches, contributing to its dense appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
