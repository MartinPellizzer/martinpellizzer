Trichocephalus

Trichocephalus, commonly known as the hairy-headed plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The flowers are typically small and clustered in dense heads, a feature typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in grassy or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
