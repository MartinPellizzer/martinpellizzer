Tricholobus cochinchinensis

Tricholobus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
