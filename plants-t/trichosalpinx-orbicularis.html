Trichosalpinx orbicularis

Trichosalpinx orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular spurred orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms during the wet season.

Trichosalpinx orbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
