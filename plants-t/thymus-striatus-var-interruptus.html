Thymus striatus var. interruptus

Thymus striatus var. interruptus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and antioxidant effects, have been historically valued in herbal medicine.
