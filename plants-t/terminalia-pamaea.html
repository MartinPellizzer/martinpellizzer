Terminalia pamaea

Terminalia pamaea, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
