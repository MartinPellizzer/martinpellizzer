Trisetaria macrochaeta

Trisetaria macrochaeta, commonly known as the hairy trisetaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three bracts, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Trisetaria macrochaeta is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
