Tecoma hypoleuca

Tecoma hypoleuca, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Tecoma hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
