Trichoglottis paniculata

Trichoglottis paniculata, commonly known as the panicle orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a branching inflorescence, known as a panicle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Trichoglottis paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
