Thelycrania excelsa

Thelycrania excelsa, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage and can grow up to three meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in moist environments.

Thelycrania excelsa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
