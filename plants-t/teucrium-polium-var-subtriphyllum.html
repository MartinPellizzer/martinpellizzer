Teucrium polium var. subtriphyllum

Teucrium polium var. subtriphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky and dry environments.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Germander family, known for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
