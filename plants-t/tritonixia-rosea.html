Tritonixia rosea

Tritonixia rosea, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Tritonixia rosea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and drought-resistant.
