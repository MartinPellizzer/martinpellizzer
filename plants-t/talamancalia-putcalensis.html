Talamancalia putcalensis

Talamancalia putcalensis, commonly known as the Putcalan orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
