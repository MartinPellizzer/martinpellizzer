Tolpis barbata var. concolor

Tolpis barbata var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-haired tolpis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
