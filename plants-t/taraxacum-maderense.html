Taraxacum maderense

Taraxacum maderense, commonly known as the Maderan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
