Trapa austroafricana

Trapa austroafricana, commonly known as the African water chestnut, is a water plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as lakes and ponds. Its leaves are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Trapa austroafricana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
