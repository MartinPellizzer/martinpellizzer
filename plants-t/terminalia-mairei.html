Terminalia mairei

Terminalia mairei, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, though it is also used in traditional medicine.
