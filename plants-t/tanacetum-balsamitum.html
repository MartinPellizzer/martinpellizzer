Tanacetum balsamitum

Tanacetum balsamitum, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, sunny habitats. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in composite flower heads. It contains volatile oils and is known for its aromatic properties.

Tanacetum balsamitum has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
