Thecanthes filifolia

Thecanthes filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Thecanthes filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
