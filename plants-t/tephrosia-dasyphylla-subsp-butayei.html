Tephrosia dasyphylla subsp. butayei

Tephrosia dasyphylla subsp.

butayei, commonly known as the Butayei tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several ovate to elliptic leaflets, and are often pubescent.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its potential use in traditional medicine.
