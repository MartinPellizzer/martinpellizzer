Typha latifolia var. orientalis

Typha latifolia var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental cattail, is a variant of the common cattail native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant features tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically brown in color, surrounded by broad, sword-shaped leaves. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and filter water, making it ecologically significant.

The plant's botanical structure supports a variety of wildlife, including insects, birds, and amphibians.
