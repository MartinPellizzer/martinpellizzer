Tinospora malabarica var. tomentosa

Tinospora malabarica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
