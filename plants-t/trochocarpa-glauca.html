Trochocarpa glauca

Trochocarpa glauca, commonly known as the blue-green bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue-green leaves and distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying desert flora.
