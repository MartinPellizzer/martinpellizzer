Trichosanthes sinopunctata

Trichosanthes sinopunctata, commonly known as the Chinese snake gourd, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruits that resemble small snakes, hence its common name. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers with five petals and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this species make it a notable plant in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
