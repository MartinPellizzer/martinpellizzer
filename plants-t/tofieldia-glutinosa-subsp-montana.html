Tofieldia glutinosa subsp. montana

Tofieldia glutinosa subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain tofieldia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Tofieldia glutinosa subsp.

montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
