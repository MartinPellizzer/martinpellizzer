Terminalia hilariana

Terminalia hilariana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Terminalia hilariana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and savannas.
