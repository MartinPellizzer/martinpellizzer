Tilia miqueliana var. chinensis

Tilia miqueliana var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
