Taraxacum piceatiforme

Taraxacum piceatiforme, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright purple to reddish flowers, which contrast with its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its stem is hollow and ridged.

The plant produces a capsule fruit that releases tiny seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in wind dispersal.
