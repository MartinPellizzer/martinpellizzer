Thymus megapotamicus

Thymus megapotamicus, commonly known as the Andean thyme, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact form and aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Thymus megapotamicus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
