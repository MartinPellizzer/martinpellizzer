Trichoscypha gossweileri

Trichoscypha gossweileri, commonly known as the African sand date palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
