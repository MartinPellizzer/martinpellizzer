Typhonium cordifolium

Typhonium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable in some cultures.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
