Thymus zygis subsp. zygis

Thymus zygis subsp.

zygis, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, fragrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
