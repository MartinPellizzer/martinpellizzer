Tium naturitense

Tium naturitense, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.

Tium naturitense is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
