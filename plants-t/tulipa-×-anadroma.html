Tulipa × anadroma

Tulipa × anadroma, commonly known as the Anadromus tulip, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tulipa greigii and Tulipa schrenkii.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive bicolor pattern on the petals. The plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces foliage in early spring before blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and striking floral display.
