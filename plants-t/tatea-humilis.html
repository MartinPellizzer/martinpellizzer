Tatea humilis

Tatea humilis, commonly known as the small flax or kōwhai kōhanga, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Tatea humilis is often found in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is of cultural significance to Māori.
