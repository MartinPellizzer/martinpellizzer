Tithonia auriculata

Tithonia auriculata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant orange to yellow-orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tithonia auriculata is known for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
