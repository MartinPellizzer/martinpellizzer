Tragacantha crenophila

Tragacantha crenophila, commonly known as the woolly tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Tragacantha crenophila is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access underground water sources.
