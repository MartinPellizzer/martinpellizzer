Theodorea alberti

Theodorea alberti, commonly known as the Alberti's orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Theodorea alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
