Tillandsia condensata

Tillandsia condensata, commonly known as the condensed air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Native to Mexico, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique structure allows it to survive in various ecological niches, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
