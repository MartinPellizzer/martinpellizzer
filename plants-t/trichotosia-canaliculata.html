Trichotosia canaliculata

Trichotosia canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its root system is fibrous, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its natural habitat.

Trichotosia canaliculata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
