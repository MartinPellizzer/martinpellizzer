Tekel

Tekel, commonly known as the black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts enclosed in a hard, green husk. The tree produces a rich, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and workability. Its nuts are also used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
