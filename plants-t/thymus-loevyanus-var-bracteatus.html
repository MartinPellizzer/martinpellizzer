Thymus loevyanus var. bracteatus

Thymus loevyanus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate thrift, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, aromatic leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
