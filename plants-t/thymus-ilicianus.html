Thymus ilicianus

Thymus ilicianus, commonly known as Ilian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. Thymus ilicianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
