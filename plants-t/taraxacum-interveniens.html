Taraxacum interveniens

Taraxacum interveniens, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum interveniens is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
