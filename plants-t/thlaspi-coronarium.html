Thlaspi coronarium

Thlaspi coronarium, commonly known as the crownflower or yellow crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Thlaspi coronarium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
