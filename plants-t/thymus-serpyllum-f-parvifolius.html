Thymus serpyllum f. parvifolius

Thymus serpyllum f. parvifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, narrow, aromatic leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
