Tragacantha bhotanensis

Tragacantha bhotanensis, commonly known as the Himalayan tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is valued for its gum, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
