Tsuga canadensis var. compacta

Tsuga canadensis var. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer native to eastern North America.

It is a variant of the eastern hemlock, characterized by its dense, shrub-like form and smaller, more closely packed foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form thick, bushy clumps.

This variety is also valued for its role in providing habitat for wildlife in forest ecosystems.
