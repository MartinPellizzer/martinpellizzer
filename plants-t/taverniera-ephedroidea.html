Taverniera ephedroidea

Taverniera ephedroidea, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its stems and leaves contain alkaloids, similar to those found in ephedra, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique morphology and chemical composition, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
