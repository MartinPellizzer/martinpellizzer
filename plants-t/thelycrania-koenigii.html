Thelycrania koenigii

Thelycrania koenigii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn a bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. Thelycrania koenigii is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
