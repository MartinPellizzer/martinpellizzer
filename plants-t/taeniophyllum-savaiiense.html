Taeniophyllum savaiiense

Taeniophyllum savaiiense, commonly known as the Sava'i stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in crevices, showcasing its ability to thrive in challenging conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
