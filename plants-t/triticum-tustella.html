Triticum tustella

Triticum tustella, commonly known as the false wheat or Tustella wheat, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Triticum tustella is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is not cultivated for food but is sometimes used in ecological studies due to its adaptability.
