Trema guineense var. paucinervium

Trema guineense var. paucinervium, commonly known as the African nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are covered in fine, stinging hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
