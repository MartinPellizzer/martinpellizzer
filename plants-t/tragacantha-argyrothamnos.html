Tragacantha argyrothamnos

Tragacantha argyrothamnos, commonly known as the silver thorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Tragacantha argyrothamnos is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
