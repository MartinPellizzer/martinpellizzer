Typha laxmannii var. planifolia

Typha laxmannii var. planifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its wide, flat leaves and cylindrical flower spikes.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

It is an important species in wetland ecosystems, contributing to water filtration and nutrient cycling.
