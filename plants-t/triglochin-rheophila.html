Triglochin rheophila

Triglochin rheophila, commonly known as the water filmy grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a thin, waxy layer. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other areas with consistently high moisture levels.

Triglochin rheophila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
