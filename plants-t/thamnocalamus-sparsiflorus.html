Thamnocalamus sparsiflorus

Thamnocalamus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in dense clusters in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
