Toechima lanceolatum

Toechima lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf toechima, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments, showcasing drought-resistant characteristics.

Toechima lanceolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
