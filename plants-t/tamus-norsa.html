Tamus norsa

Tamus norsa, commonly known as the northern false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Tamus norsa is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
