Thuja koraiensis

Thuja koraiensis, commonly known as Korean white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

Thuja koraiensis is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used historically in construction and traditional crafts.
