Tribulus obbiadensis

Tribulus obbiadensis, commonly known as the African horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny calyces that enclose its vibrant orange or red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Tribulus obbiadensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
