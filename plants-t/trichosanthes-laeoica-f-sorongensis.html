Trichosanthes laeoica f. sorongensis

Trichosanthes laeoica f. sorongensis, commonly known as the Sorong bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, bitter-tasting fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its botanical features include a distinct floral structure and a unique fruit morphology that sets it apart from other Trichosanthes species.

This variety is valued both for its culinary use and its traditional medicinal properties.
