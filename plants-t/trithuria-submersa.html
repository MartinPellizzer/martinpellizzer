Trithuria submersa

Trithuria submersa, commonly known as the submerged water star, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that are often submerged or floating. The plant has thread-like leaves that are adapted for underwater photosynthesis. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface.

Trithuria submersa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
