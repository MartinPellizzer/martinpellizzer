Trichilia catigua var. affinis

Trichilia catigua var. affinis, commonly known as Brazilian cherry or pink cherry, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange-yellow fruits containing a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
