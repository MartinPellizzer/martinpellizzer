Thymus daenensis subsp. daenensis

Thymus daenensis subsp.

daenensis, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, grey-green leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in culinary applications.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.
