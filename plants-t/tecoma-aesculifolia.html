Tecoma aesculifolia

Tecoma aesculifolia, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tecoma aesculifolia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
