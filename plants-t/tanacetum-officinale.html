Tanacetum officinale

Tanacetum officinale, commonly known as tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and strongly aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tansy is often found in damp meadows and along stream banks.

It has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported insect-repelling properties.
