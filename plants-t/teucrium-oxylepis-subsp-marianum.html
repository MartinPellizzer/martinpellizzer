Teucrium oxylepis subsp. marianum

Teucrium oxylepis subsp.

marianum, commonly known as the Marian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in summer.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
