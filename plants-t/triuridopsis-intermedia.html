Triuridopsis intermedia

Triuridopsis intermedia, commonly known as the common triurid, is a small, terrestrial fern that belongs to the family Triuridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous spore-producing organs called sporangia.

Triuridopsis intermedia is primarily found in temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.
