Trillium tschonoskii

Trillium tschonoskii, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the plant. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rich, moist, well-drained soils under partial to full shade.

Trillium tschonoskii is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
