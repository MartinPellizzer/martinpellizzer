Tulipa brinkii

Tulipa brinkii, commonly known as the Kashmir tulip, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces showy blooms in late spring, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe along the throat. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Tulipa brinkii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
