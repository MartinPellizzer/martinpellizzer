Ternstroemia penangiana

Ternstroemia penangiana, commonly known as Penang ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
