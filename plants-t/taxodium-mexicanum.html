Taxodium mexicanum

Taxodium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to southeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The tree produces small, spherical cones and has scale-like leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It is often found in swampy areas and can tolerate standing water, making it a common species in riparian zones.

The Mexican bald cypress is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
