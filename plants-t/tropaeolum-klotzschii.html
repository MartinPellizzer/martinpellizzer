Tropaeolum klotzschii

Tropaeolum klotzschii, commonly known as the Andean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is known for its peppery, edible foliage and seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum klotzschii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
