Trisetum spicatum var. projectum

Trisetum spicatum var. projectum, commonly known as the tufted feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The variety 'projectum' is distinguished by its more pronounced tufts of foliage at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.
