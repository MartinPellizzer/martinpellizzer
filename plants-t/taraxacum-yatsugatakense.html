Taraxacum yatsugatakense

Taraxacum yatsugatakense, commonly known as the Yatsugatake dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated leaves. The plant produces a hollow, cylindrical stem that supports a single, yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the dandelion genus with unique ecological adaptations.
