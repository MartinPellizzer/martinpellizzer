Thymus heterotrichus

Thymus heterotrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
