Trifolium longidentatum

Trifolium longidentatum, commonly known as the long-toothed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and pastures.

Trifolium longidentatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
