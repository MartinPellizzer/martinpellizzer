Trixis pedunculosa

Trixis pedunculosa, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fibrous stems and leaves are adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

Trixis pedunculosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in desert ecosystems.
