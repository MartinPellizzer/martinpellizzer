Thymus longicaulis var. freynii

Thymus longicaulis var. freynii, commonly known as Freyn's narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
