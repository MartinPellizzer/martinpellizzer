Triticum burnaschewii

Triticum burnaschewii, commonly known as the Burnaschewii wheat, is a wild relative of common wheat native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, typical of the grass family. It has a diploid genome and is considered valuable for its genetic diversity and potential contributions to wheat improvement.

Due to its wild origin, it is often used in breeding programs to enhance disease resistance and adaptability in cultivated wheat varieties.
