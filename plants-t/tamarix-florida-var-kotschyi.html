Tamarix florida var. kotschyi

Tamarix florida var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in coastal and desert environments.
