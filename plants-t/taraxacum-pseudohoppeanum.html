Taraxacum pseudohoppeanum

Taraxacum pseudohoppeanum, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and form a single bloom that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
