Trifolium viciosoanum

Trifolium viciosoanum, commonly known as the toxic clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its toxicity, it is generally avoided in agricultural and ornamental settings.
