Tabernaemontana flavicans

Tabernaemontana flavicans, commonly known as the yellow star of Bethlehem, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Despite its beauty, it is toxic to humans and animals, containing compounds that can cause severe health issues if ingested.
