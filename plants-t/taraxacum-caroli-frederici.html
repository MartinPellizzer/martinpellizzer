Taraxacum caroli-frederici

Taraxacum caroli-frederici, commonly known as the Caroli-Frederici dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed sphere. Native to central and eastern Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves historically used for their diuretic and digestive benefits.

It is also a common weed, often found in gardens and along roadsides.
