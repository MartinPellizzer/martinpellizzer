Thlaspi × gremlianum

Thlaspi × gremlianum, commonly known as the hybrid cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves.

This plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Thlaspi arvense and Thlaspi humile, showcasing genetic diversity through its parentage. The species is notable for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a resilient weed in many regions.

Its presence can sometimes indicate soil disturbance or nutrient-rich environments.
