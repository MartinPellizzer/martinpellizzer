Tryphane gerardi

Tryphane gerardi, commonly known as the Carolina gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Tryphane gerardi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
