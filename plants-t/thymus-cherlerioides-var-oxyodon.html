Thymus cherlerioides var. oxyodon

Thymus cherlerioides var. oxyodon, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
