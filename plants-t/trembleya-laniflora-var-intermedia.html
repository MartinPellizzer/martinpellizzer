Trembleya laniflora var. intermedia

Trembleya laniflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the silky trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
