Thrasya stricta

Thrasya stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Thrasya stricta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or sandy gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
