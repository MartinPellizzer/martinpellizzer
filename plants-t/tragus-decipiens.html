Tragus decipiens

Tragus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive tragus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The common name "deceptive tragus" may refer to its ability to mimic other plants or its misleading appearance.
