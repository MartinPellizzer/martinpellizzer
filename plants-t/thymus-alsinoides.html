Thymus alsinoides

Thymus alsinoides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
