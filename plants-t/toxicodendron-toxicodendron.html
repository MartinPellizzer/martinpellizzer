Toxicodendron toxicodendron

Toxicodendron toxicodendron, commonly known as the poison ivy plant, is a woody vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It contains urushiol, a potent allergen that causes skin irritation and rashes upon contact.
