Trifolium baicalense

Trifolium baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal clover, is a species of flowering plant native to the regions of Mongolia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and open grasslands. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
