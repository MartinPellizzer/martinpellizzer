Triplandron lineatum

Triplandron lineatum, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems that give it its common name. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
