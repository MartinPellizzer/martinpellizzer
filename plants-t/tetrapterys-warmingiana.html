Tetrapterys warmingiana

Tetrapterys warmingiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it the genus name Tetrapterys. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
