Tulipa praecox subsp. raddii

Tulipa praecox subsp.

raddii, commonly known as the early tulip or the red tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to central Asia. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is closely related to other tulip species and is often cultivated for its vibrant and early blooming characteristics.
