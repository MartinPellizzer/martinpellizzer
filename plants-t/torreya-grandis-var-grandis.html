Torreya grandis var. grandis

Torreya grandis var. grandis, commonly known as the Chinese torreya, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving cones and elongated, needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in woody, spherical cones that mature over several years.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and historical significance.
