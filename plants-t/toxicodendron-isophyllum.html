Toxicodendron isophyllum

Toxicodendron isophyllum, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a distinctive, smooth texture.

The plant is part of the Anacardiaceae family and is known for its ability to cause skin irritation upon contact with its sap.
