Tragia caperonioides

Tragia caperonioides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that dehisces to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tragia caperonioides is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
