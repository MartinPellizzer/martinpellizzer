Thymus glabrescens subsp. degenianus

Thymus glabrescens subsp.

degenianus, commonly known as Degen's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to the main species.
