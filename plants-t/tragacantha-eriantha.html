Tragacantha eriantha

Tragacantha eriantha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, branching structure. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
