Teramnus micans var. fagifolius

Teramnus micans var. fagifolius, commonly known as the fagifolius variant of the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically a glossy dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
