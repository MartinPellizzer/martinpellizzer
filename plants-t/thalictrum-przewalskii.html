Thalictrum przewalskii

Thalictrum przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's meadow-rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Thalictrum przewalskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
