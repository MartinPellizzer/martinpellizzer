Tylophora caffra

Tylophora caffra, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Tylophora caffra is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
