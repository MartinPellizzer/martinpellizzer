Thysanocarpus curvipes var. emarginatus

Thysanocarpus curvipes var. emarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thysanocarpus, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, elliptical leaves with a distinct emarginate (notched) base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle, which opens to release its seeds.

This variant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
