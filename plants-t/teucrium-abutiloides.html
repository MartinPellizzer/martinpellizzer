Teucrium abutiloides

Teucrium abutiloides, commonly known as the woolly germander, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium abutiloides is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
