Trigonella mesopotamica

Trigonella mesopotamica, commonly known as Mesopotamian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
