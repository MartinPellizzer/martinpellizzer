Tephrosia elongata var. lasiocaulos

Tephrosia elongata var. lasiocaulos, commonly known as the hairy pod tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are opposite, pinnate, and often have a soft, velvety texture. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds, making it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
