Thismia mirabilis

Thismia mirabilis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, orchid-like flowers that are often pale or translucent. The plant relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, as it cannot photosynthesize due to the absence of chlorophyll. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, adapted for pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in many regions.
