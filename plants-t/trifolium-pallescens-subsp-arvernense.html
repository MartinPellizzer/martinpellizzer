Trifolium pallescens subsp. arvernense

Trifolium pallescens subsp.

arvernense, commonly known as the Arvernian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually pale green and have a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
