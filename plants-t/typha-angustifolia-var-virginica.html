Typha angustifolia var. virginica

Typha angustifolia var. virginica, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes.

It thrives in marshy environments and is often found along the edges of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant produces fluffy, brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Typha angustifolia var. virginica plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
