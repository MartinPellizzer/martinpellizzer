Trigonella incisa

Trigonella incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small yellow flowers are followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Trigonella incisa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
