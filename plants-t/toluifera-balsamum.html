Toluifera balsamum

Toluifera balsamum, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Toluifera balsamum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
