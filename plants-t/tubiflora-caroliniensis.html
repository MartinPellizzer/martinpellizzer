Tubiflora caroliniensis

Tubiflora caroliniensis, commonly known as the Carolina tuber, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tubiflora caroliniensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
