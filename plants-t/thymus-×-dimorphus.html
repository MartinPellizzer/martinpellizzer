Thymus × dimorphus

Thymus × dimorphus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae) native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fragrant, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is also used in culinary and medicinal applications.
