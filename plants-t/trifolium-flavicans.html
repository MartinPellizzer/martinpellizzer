Trifolium flavicans

Trifolium flavicans, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.

Trifolium flavicans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
