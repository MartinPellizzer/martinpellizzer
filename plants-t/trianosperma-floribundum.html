Trianosperma floribundum

Trianosperma floribundum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Trianosperma floribundum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
