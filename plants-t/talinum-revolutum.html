Talinum revolutum

Talinum revolutum, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Talinum revolutum is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with climbing or trailing growth habits.
