Tragacantha umbellata

Tragacantha umbellata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Iran and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces a milky sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and its economic importance in the production of tragacanth, a versatile natural gum.
