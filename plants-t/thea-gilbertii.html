Thea gilbertii

Thea gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's tea plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, tea-like aroma.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
