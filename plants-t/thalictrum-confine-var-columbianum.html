Thalictrum confine var. columbianum

Thalictrum confine var. columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
