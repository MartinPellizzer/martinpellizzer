Taxodium heterophyllum

Taxodium heterophyllum, commonly known as the Mexican bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flaky, reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant has elongated, needle-like leaves that turn reddish in the fall before shedding. Its cones are small and woody, containing two to four seeds.

Taxodium heterophyllum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
