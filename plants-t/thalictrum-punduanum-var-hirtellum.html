Thalictrum punduanum var. hirtellum

Thalictrum punduanum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, giving it the common name "hairy meadow rue.".

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
