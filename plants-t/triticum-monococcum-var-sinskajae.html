Triticum monococcum var. sinskajae

Triticum monococcum var. sinskajae, commonly known as the Siberian wheat, is a rare and ancient variety of wheat native to the cold regions of Siberia.

It is characterized by its small, hard grains and is typically grown in low-temperature environments. The plant has a short growth cycle, making it well-suited for harsh climates with short growing seasons. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a compact spike, which help it conserve heat and moisture.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for climate-resilient crop development.
