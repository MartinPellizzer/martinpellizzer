Turnera nervosa

Turnera nervosa, commonly known as the flame flower or yellow flame, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name "nervosa.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
