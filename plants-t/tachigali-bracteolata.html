Tachigali bracteolata

Tachigali bracteolata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.
