Trifolium fragiferum var. pulchellum

Trifolium fragiferum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the fragrant clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive white midrib. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
