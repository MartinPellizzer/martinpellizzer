Telis lunata

Telis lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
