Tephrosia aurantiaca f. albescens

Tephrosia aurantiaca f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered tephrosia, is a variant of the tropical legume Tephrosia aurantiaca.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical orange or red hues of the species. The plant is native to regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
