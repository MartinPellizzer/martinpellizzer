Terminalia opacifolia

Terminalia opacifolia, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds are widely used in the production of shea butter, a valuable cosmetic and culinary ingredient.

Its ecological and economic importance makes it a significant species in its native habitats.
