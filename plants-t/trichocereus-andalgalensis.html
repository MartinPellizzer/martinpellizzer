Trichocereus andalgalensis

Trichocereus andalgalensis, commonly known as the Andean torch cactus, is a species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dark green, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
