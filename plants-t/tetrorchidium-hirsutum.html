Tetrorchidium hirsutum

Tetrorchidium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy tetrorchidium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
