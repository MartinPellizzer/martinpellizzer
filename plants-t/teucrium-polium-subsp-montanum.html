Teucrium polium subsp. montanum

Teucrium polium subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Lamiaceae family.
