Thymelaea tarton-raira subsp. tarton-raira

Thymelaea tarton-raira subsp.

tarton-raira, commonly known as the Kankar Flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.
