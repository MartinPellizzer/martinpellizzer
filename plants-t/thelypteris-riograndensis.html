Thelypteris riograndensis

Thelypteris riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande holly fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, intricate arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

Thelypteris riograndensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
