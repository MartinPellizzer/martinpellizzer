Tropaeolum compactum

Tropaeolum compactum, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a compact, annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant is known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a versatile addition to gardens and kitchens.
