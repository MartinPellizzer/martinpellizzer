Tripogon sugathakumariae

Tripogon sugathakumariae, commonly known as the Sri Lankan grass.

It is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the wet zone of Sri Lanka. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a unique feature among grasses.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
