Tetranthera bindoniana

Tetranthera bindoniana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Tetranthera bindoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
