Tricera citrifolia

Tricera citrifolia, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a fruit with a rough, green exterior and a sweet, creamy interior. The plant has a distinctive appearance with large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
