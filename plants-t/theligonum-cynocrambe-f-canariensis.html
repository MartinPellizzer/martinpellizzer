Theligonum cynocrambe f. canariensis

Theligonum cynocrambe f. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
