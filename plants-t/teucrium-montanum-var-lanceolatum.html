Teucrium montanum var. lanceolatum

Teucrium montanum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
