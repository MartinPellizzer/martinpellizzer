Triticum × hackelii

Triticum × hackelii, commonly known as the hackel's wheat, is a hybrid plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its intermediate features between common wheat (Triticum aestivum) and other wild wheat species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-rowed grains.

Triticum × hackelii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.
