Triglochin pycnostachya

Triglochin pycnostachya, commonly known as sea arrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in estuaries and tidal mudflats.

Triglochin pycnostachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
