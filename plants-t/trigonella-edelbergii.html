Trigonella edelbergii

Trigonella edelbergii, commonly known as the Edelberg's fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is native to parts of the Middle East and North Africa.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
