Tillandsia circinnata

Tillandsia circinnata, commonly known as the silver vane airplant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to trees in humid environments. The plant's rosette-shaped structure is covered in dense, silver-gray trichomes that help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
