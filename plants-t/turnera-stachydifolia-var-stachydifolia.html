Turnera stachydifolia var. stachydifolia

Turnera stachydifolia var. stachydifolia, commonly known as the sticky turnera, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.
