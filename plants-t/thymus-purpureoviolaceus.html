Thymus purpureoviolaceus

Thymus purpureoviolaceus, commonly known as purple violet thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to violet, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it both a useful culinary herb and a valuable addition to ornamental landscapes.
