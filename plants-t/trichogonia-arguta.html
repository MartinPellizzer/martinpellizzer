Trichogonia arguta

Trichogonia arguta, commonly known as the hairy trichogonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves covered in fine, hairy textures that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Trichogonia arguta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
