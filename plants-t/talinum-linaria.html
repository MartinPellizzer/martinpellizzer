Talinum linaria

Talinum linaria, commonly known as the red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Talinum linaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
