Tragacantha apollinea

Tragacantha apollinea, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus apollinus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a valuable resin known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Tragacantha apollinea is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
