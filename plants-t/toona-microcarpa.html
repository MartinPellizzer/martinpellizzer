Toona microcarpa

Toona microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, clustered flowers are followed by smooth, round fruits that mature to a dark green or black color.

The tree is often valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
