Thymus serpyllum var. pallidus

Thymus serpyllum var. pallidus, commonly known as pale thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and herbal purposes.
