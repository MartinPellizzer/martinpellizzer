Taxodium mucronatum

Taxodium mucronatum, commonly known as the Mexican bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to southeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive knobby, buttressed roots and its ability to tolerate wet, swampy conditions. The tree produces small, spherical cones and has scale-like leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. It is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The bark is thick and flaky, providing protection against pests and environmental stress.
