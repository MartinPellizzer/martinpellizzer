Tectaria trichodes

Tectaria trichodes, commonly known as the hairy tectaria, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Tectaria trichodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
