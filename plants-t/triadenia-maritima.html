Triadenia maritima

Triadenia maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in saline and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh maritime conditions. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, aiding survival in arid climates.

Triadenia maritima plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
