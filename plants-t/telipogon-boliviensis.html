Telipogon boliviensis

Telipogon boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian grass of heaven, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
