Thymelaea valentina

Thymelaea valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's thyme or sweet thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymelaea valentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
