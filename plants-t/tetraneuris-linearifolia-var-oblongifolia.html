Tetraneuris linearifolia var. oblongifolia

Tetraneuris linearifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf tetraneuris, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
