Tapura coriacea

Tapura coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved tapura, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that have a textured, corrugated surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Tapura coriacea is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests.
