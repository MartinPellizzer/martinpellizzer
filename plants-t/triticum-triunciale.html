Triticum triunciale

Triticum triunciale, commonly known as the wild rye, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and role in ecological restoration projects.
