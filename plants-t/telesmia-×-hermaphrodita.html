Telesmia × hermaphrodita

Telesmia × hermaphrodita, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and strong fragrance.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Telesmia lutea and Telesmia multiflora, both native to Australia. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems, which are common traits among the genus Telesmia. Its flowers come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a favorite in gardens and floral arrangements.

Telesmia × hermaphrodita thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its shape and promote continuous blooming.
