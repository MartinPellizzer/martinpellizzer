Tricuspis trinerviglumis

Tricuspis trinerviglumis, commonly known as the three-lobed three-veined plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are tricuspate, meaning they have three lobes, and trinervous, with three prominent veins running from the base to the tip. The plant typically exhibits a compact growth habit with small, narrow leaves arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its intricate venation patterns and specialized leaf structure, which may aid in water retention and light absorption.
