Tragacantha bisinflata

Tragacantha bisinflata, commonly known as the two-chambered tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Tragacantha bisinflata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
