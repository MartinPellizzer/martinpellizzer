Triticum caudatum

Triticum caudatum, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild relative of wheat that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a grass species with slender, drooping spikelets that give it its characteristic appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Triticum caudatum is known for its genetic diversity, making it an important species for wheat breeding programs.
