Tilia sabetii

Tilia sabetii, commonly known as the Sabeti linden, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in rocky or dry soils at high altitudes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Tilia sabetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
