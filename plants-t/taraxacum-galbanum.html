Taraxacum galbanum

Taraxacum galbanum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bristly seed heads. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Taraxacum galbanum is noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects.
