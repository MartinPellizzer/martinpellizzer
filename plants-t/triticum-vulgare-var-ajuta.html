Triticum vulgare var. ajuta

Triticum vulgare var. ajuta, commonly known as the common wheat variety ajuta, is a cultivated form of wheat characterized by its robust growth and high yield potential.

This variety is botanically classified as a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is distinguished by its spikelet structure and awned florets. It typically produces slender, cylindrical spikes with small, hard grains that are rich in gluten, making it suitable for flour production. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to temperate climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its life cycle includes a series of developmental stages from germination to flowering and grain maturation, which are influenced by environmental conditions.
