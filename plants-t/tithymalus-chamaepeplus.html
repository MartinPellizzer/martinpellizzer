Tithymalus chamaepeplus

Tithymalus chamaepeplus, commonly known as the creeping tithymale, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features dense, mat-forming growth with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often rounded or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its compact form and delicate blooms.
