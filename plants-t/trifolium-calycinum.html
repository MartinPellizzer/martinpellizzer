Trifolium calycinum

Trifolium calycinum, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rootstock and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Trifolium calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
