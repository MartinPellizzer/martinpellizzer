Turbina oblongata

Turbina oblongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turban snail, is a species of air-breathing land snail.

It belongs to the family Helicarionidae and is characterized by its elongated, spiral shell that is typically smooth and glossy. The shell is usually a light brown or yellowish color, with a distinct, narrow aperture. This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its diet primarily consists of fungi and decaying plant matter, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
