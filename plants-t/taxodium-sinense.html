Taxodium sinense

Taxodium sinense, commonly known as the Chinese bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, scale-like leaves that turn a striking golden hue in autumn. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.

Taxodium sinense is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in mountainous regions.
