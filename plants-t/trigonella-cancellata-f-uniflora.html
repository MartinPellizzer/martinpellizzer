Trigonella cancellata f. uniflora

Trigonella cancellata f. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered fenugreek, is a botanical variant of the fenugreek species.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to common fenugreek.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
