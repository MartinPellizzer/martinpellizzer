Triticum boeoticum var. viridinigrireuteri

Triticum boeoticum var. viridinigrireuteri, commonly known as the green-black einkorn, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescence. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture. This variety is notable for its high levels of protein and minerals, making it a valuable crop for traditional and modern agricultural practices.

It is also considered a potential source of genetic diversity for improving cultivated wheat varieties.
