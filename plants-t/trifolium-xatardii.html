Trifolium xatardii

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat. Native to specific regions in Central Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, Trifolium xatardii is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
