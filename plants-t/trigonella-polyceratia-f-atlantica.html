Trigonella polyceratia f. atlantica

Trigonella polyceratia f. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic trigonella, is a variant of the trigonella species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments. Its botanical features include stipulate leaves and a hairy stem, which help it adapt to arid conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
