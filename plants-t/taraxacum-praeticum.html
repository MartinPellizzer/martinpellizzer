Taraxacum praeticum

Taraxacum praeticum, commonly known as the Prussian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering head that produces a crown of fluffy seeds. The plant is known for its yellow flowers that develop into white, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in open, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Taraxacum praeticum is also valued for its medicinal properties, similar to other dandelion species.
