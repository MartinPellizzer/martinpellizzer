Trifolium longipes var. multipedunculatum

Trifolium longipes var. multipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its multiple peduncles, which support its flowering heads.
