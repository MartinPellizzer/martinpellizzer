Tetrachyron orizabaensis var. websteri

Tetrachyron orizabaensis var. websteri, commonly known as the Mexican goldenbush, is a shrub native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species within the Asteraceae family.
