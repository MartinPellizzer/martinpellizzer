Trymatococcus guanabarinus

Trymatococcus guanabarinus, commonly known as the Guanabara thimblecush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in saline and well-drained conditions. The plant features a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to coastal environments and its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
