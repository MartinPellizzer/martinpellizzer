Taraxacum dunense

Taraxacum dunense, commonly known as the Duneland dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. Its flowers are yellow and consist of numerous small florets arranged in a single head.

Taraxacum dunense is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various habitats.
