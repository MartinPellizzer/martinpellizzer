Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp. polaskii

Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp.

polaskii, commonly known as the Mexican Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
