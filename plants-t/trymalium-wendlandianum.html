Trymalium wendlandianum

Trymalium wendlandianum, commonly known as the Wendland's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
