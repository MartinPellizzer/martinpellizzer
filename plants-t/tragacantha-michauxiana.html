Tragacantha michauxiana

Tragacantha michauxiana, commonly known as the Michaux's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and along streambanks. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
