Triticum obtusiflorum

Triticum obtusiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered wheat, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, broad leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets with broad, ovate florets that are often pink to purple in hue. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
