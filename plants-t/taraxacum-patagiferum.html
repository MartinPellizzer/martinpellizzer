Taraxacum patagiferum

Taraxacum patagiferum, commonly known as the Patagonian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow stem that becomes fibrous with age. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces a spherical seed head that disperses seeds via the wind.

This species is often found in grassy areas and open habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
