Taraxacum rizaense subsp. rizaense

Taraxacum rizaense subsp.

rizaense, commonly known as the Riza dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in the Balkans. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces a tall, hollow stem that bears a single, large, yellow composite flower head. Its flowers are followed by a distinctive spherical seed head composed of numerous lightweight seeds with white pappus.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
