Taralea rigida

Taralea rigida, commonly known as rigid taralea, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
