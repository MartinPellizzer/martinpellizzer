Taraxacum clemens

Taraxacum clemens, commonly known as the Clemens' dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, yellow flowering head. The flower head consists of numerous small, yellow tubular flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
