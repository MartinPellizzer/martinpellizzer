Timonius mollis var. submollis

Timonius mollis var. submollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved timonius, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after ornamental plant in botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
