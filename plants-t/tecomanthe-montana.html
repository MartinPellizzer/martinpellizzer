Tecomanthe montana

Tecomanthe montana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Tecomanthe montana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
