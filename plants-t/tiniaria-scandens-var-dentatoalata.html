Tiniaria scandens var. dentatoalata

Tiniaria scandens var. dentatoalata, commonly known as the hairy leaf tiniaria, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles a small, fleshy capsule. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
