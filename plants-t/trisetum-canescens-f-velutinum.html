Trisetum canescens f. velutinum

Trisetum canescens f. velutinum, commonly known as the velvety hairgrass, is a variant of the common hairgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage. The leaves are typically covered in a soft, velvety texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of ponds.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
