Trichanthecium pseudisachne

Trichanthecium pseudisachne, commonly known as false ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil quality.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and along creek banks.
