Trifolium phleoides f. pedunculatum

Trifolium phleoides f. pedunculatum, commonly known as the pedunculate clover, is a variant of the phleoides clover species.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
