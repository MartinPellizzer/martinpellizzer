Trifolium pratense var. lenkoranicum

Trifolium pratense var. lenkoranicum, commonly known as the Lenkoran clover, is a variant of red clover native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets and a small terminal spur. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
