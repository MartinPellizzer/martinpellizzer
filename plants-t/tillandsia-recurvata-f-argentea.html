Tillandsia recurvata f. argentea

Tillandsia recurvata f. argentea, commonly known as the silver-tipped air plant, is a bromeliad species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, but the varietal form f. argentea exhibits a striking silvery or white variegation along the edges. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular misting to maintain humidity.

It is a popular choice for terrariums and as a low-maintenance indoor plant.
