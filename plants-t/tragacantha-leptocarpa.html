Tragacantha leptocarpa

Tragacantha leptocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Aleppo gum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh climates.

The gum resin obtained from its bark has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
