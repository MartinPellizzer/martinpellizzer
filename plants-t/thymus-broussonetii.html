Thymus broussonetii

Thymus broussonetii, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus broussonetii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
