Thea paucipunctata

Thea paucipunctata, commonly known as the spiny thea, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, woody stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, adapting to the arid environments where it thrives.

Thea paucipunctata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
