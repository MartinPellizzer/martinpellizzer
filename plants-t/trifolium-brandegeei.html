Trifolium brandegeei

Trifolium brandegeei, commonly known as Brandegee's clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Trifolium brandegeei is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
