Trillium albidum subsp. parviflorum

Trillium albidum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features three broadly ovate leaves that are usually white to pale green in color.

The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Trillium genus. It blooms in early spring and is often found in rich, moist woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
