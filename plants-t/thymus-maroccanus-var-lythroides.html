Thymus maroccanus var. lythroides

Thymus maroccanus var. lythroides, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties include antiseptic and carminative effects, historically valued in traditional herbal medicine.
