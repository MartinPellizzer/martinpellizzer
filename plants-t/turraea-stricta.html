Turraea stricta

Turraea stricta, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Turraea stricta is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
