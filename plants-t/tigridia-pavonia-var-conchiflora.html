Tigridia pavonia var. conchiflora

Tigridia pavonia var. conchiflora, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a bulbous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers with a distinctive, flared throat that resembles a lantern. The plant produces lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens with their unique, showy appearance.
