Thymus adriaticus

Thymus adriaticus, commonly known as Adriatic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, needle-like leaves and produces pale pink to white flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus adriaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
