Tussilago alpina

Tussilago alpina, commonly known as alpine thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers, which bloom in early summer, are small and yellow, forming dense clusters at the top of the stem.

Tussilago alpina is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory ailments.
