Tribonanthes uniflora

Tribonanthes uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower tribonanthes, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual with a distinctive, narrow, upright stem and a single, showy flower at the top. The plant's flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
