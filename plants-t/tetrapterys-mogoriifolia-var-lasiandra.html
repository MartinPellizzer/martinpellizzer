Tetrapterys mogoriifolia var. lasiandra

Tetrapterys mogoriifolia var. lasiandra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tetrapterys, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are usually yellow or greenish in color.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
