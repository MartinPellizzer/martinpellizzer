Tweedia versicolor

Tweedia versicolor, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules that mature in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
