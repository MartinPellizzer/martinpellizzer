Trisetum filifolium

Trisetum filifolium, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Trisetum filifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
