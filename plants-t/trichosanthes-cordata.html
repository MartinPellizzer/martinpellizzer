Trichosanthes cordata

Trichosanthes cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is typically elongated and turns from green to orange as it matures. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a bitter taste and is used in various traditional cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
