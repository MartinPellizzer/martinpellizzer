Tripogon lisboae

Tripogon lisboae, commonly known as Lisbon grass, is a perennial herb native to Portugal.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
