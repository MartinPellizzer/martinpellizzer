Trichodium setaceum

Trichodium setaceum, commonly known as the slender pincushion cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Trichodium setaceum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
