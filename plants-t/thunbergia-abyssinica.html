Thunbergia abyssinica

Thunbergia abyssinica, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its trailing habit, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.
