Tradescantia mundula

Tradescantia mundula, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces a showy display of flowers from spring to early summer.

Tradescantia mundula is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
