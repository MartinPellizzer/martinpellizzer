Tinus tinifolia

Tinus tinifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Tinus tinifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, contributing to its popularity in xeriscaping.
