Thisantha debilis

Thisantha debilis, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats. It is a perennial herb that produces its flowers during the spring and early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
