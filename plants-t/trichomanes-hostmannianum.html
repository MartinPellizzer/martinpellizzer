Trichomanes hostmannianum

Trichomanes hostmannianum, commonly known as the Hostmann's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and covered with a waxy cuticle. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
