Tectaria papyracea

Tectaria papyracea, commonly known as the paper fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, papyraceous (paper-like) fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria papyracea is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in various regions.
