Tephrosia tinctoria var. pulcherrima

Tephrosia tinctoria var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it has been used traditionally for its medicinal and dye-producing properties.
