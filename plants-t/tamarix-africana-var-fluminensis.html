Tamarix africana var. fluminensis

Tamarix africana var. fluminensis, commonly known as the river tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces pink or red flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions.
