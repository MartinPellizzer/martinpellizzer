Tilia × arpadiana

Tilia × arpadiana, commonly known as the Hungarian lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree in the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth, waxy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across Europe.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
