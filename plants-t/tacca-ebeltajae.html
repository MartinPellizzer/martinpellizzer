Tacca ebeltajae

Tacca ebeltajae, commonly known as the "devil's tongue" or "bat's wing," is a rare and striking member of the Tacca genus native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a bat's wings, hence its common name. The plant produces a single, pale yellow or white flower with long, curling petals that emit a faint, sweet fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Tacca ebeltajae is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
