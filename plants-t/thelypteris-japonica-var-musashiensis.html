Thelypteris japonica var. musashiensis

Thelypteris japonica var. musashiensis, commonly known as the Musashino water fern, is a variant of the Japanese shield fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, arching fronds with a distinctive dark green color and a leathery texture. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
