Talinum crenatum

Talinum crenatum, commonly known as the red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Talinum crenatum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and rapid growth habit.
