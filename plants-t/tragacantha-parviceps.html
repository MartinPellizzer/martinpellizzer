Tragacantha parviceps

Tragacantha parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, small, globular flower heads. The plant produces a viscous gum, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food additive. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Tragacantha parviceps is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
