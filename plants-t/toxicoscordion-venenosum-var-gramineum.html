Toxicoscordion venenosum var. gramineum

Toxicoscordion venenosum var. gramineum, commonly known as the western poison hemlock, is a toxic plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white, umbrella-shaped flowers. The plant contains various toxic alkaloids, including coniine and gamma-coniceine, which can be fatal to humans and animals. It is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in fields.

Due to its resemblance to edible plants, it poses a significant risk of accidental poisoning.
