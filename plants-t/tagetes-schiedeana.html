Tagetes schiedeana

Tagetes schiedeana, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and pungent, aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the daisy family.

Tagetes schiedeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
