Tylostemon membranaceus

Tylostemon membranaceus, commonly known as the paperbark lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale, papery skin. The plant produces striking white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and creek sides.

Tylostemon membranaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
