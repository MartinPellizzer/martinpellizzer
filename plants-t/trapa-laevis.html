Trapa laevis

Trapa laevis, commonly known as the smooth water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, woody fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

Trapa laevis is also valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in starch and used in traditional cuisines.
