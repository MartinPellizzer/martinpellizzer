Tina coursii

Tina coursii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage with fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Tina coursii is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
