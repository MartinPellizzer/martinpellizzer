Tragacantha albicans

Tragacantha albicans, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces distinctive yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its pale yellow or white sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Tragacantha albicans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
