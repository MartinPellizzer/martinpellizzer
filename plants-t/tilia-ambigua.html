Tilia ambigua

Tilia ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
