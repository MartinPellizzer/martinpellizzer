Tephrosia argyrotricha

Tephrosia argyrotricha, commonly known as silverleaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and open woodlands.

Tephrosia argyrotricha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
