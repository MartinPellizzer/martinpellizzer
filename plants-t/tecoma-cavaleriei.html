Tecoma cavaleriei

Tecoma cavaleriei, commonly known as the red-bloom false jacaranda, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma cavaleriei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
