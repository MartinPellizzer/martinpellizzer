Trisetum malacanthum

Trisetum malacanthum, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Trisetum malacanthum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
