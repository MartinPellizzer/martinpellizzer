Tovara filiformis f. variegata

Tovara filiformis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated threadleaf plant, is a unique foliage plant characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves.

The leaves are often variegated with shades of green, white, and sometimes pink, creating an attractive visual contrast. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative purposes.
