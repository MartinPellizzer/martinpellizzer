Tephrosia pinifolia

Tephrosia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually in shades of white or yellow.

Tephrosia pinifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
