Thea tenuiflora

Thea tenuiflora, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small white flowers. The plant is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce tea. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The leaves contain caffeine and various bioactive compounds that contribute to its popularity as a beverage.
