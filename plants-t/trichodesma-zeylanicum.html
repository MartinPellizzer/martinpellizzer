Trichodesma zeylanicum

Trichodesma zeylanicum, commonly known as Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and stellate (star-shaped) trichomes on the leaves.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
