Tragacantha coelestis

Tragacantha coelestis, commonly known as the celestial tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces a unique sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its botanical features include alternate leaves with a rough texture and a short lifespan, typically growing in dry, rocky soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and historical significance.
