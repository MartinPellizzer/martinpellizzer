Thymus clusii

Thymus clusii, commonly known as the Clusius thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant typically has small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, pink or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus clusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in herbal medicine.
