Tragacantha plumosa

Tragacantha plumosa, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and lance-shaped.

Tragacantha plumosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
