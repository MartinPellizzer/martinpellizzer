Trophis scandens subsp. megacarpa

Trophis scandens subsp.

megacarpa, commonly known as the giant pod vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that can grow up to several centimeters in length, containing seeds enclosed in a fibrous mesh. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
