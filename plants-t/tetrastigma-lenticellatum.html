Tetrastigma lenticellatum

Tetrastigma lenticellatum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed cross-vine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in small, raised pores called lenticels. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

Tetrastigma lenticellatum is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, climbing on trees or shrubs.
