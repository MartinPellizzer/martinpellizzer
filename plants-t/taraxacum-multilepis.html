Taraxacum multilepis

Taraxacum multilepis, commonly known as the multileaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, deeply lobed leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to central and eastern Europe, this plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common sight in various habitats.
