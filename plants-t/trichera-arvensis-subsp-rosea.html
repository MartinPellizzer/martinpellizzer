Trichera arvensis subsp. rosea

Trichera arvensis subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the pink field trichera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in composite heads with both ray and disk florets. Trichera arvensis subsp.

rosea is often found in open habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
