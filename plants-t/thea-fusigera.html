Thea fusigera

Thea fusigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including gardens and agricultural fields.

Thea fusigera is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in some areas.
