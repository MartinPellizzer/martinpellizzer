Tribulus subinermis

Tribulus subinermis, commonly known as the lesser caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into four-angled, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Tribulus subinermis is adapted to arid environments and has a short growing season.
