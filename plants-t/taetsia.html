Taetsia

Taetsia, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Taetsia in the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three leaflets and produces striking blue or purple pea-like flowers. Taetsia has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
