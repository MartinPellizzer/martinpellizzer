Trachelospermum bessonii

Trachelospermum bessonii, commonly known as the Besson's star jasmine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance.

Trachelospermum bessonii is also valued for its ability to climb trellises and walls, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
