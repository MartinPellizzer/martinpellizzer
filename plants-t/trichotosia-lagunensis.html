Trichotosia lagunensis

Trichotosia lagunensis, commonly known as the Laguna tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its moist habitat.

Trichotosia lagunensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
