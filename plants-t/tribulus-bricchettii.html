Tribulus bricchettii

Tribulus bricchettii, commonly known as the desert burr, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, yellow flowers and distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and water-storing tissues. Its fruit capsules are often covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

Tribulus bricchettii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and tonic properties.
