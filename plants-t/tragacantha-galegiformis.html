Tragacantha galegiformis

Tragacantha galegiformis, commonly known as the Persian gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Tragacantha galegiformis is also valued for its gum, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
