Triodia drummondii

Triodia drummondii, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a hardy perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth and needle-like leaves, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant forms clumps and is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall. Its ability to withstand extreme temperatures and drought makes it an important component of the ecosystem in arid zones.

Triodia drummondii plays a crucial role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
