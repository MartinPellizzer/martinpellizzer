Trichinium preissii

Trichinium preissii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss, adapting it to its harsh environment. Its fibrous roots help it anchor in sandy soils and access deep water sources.

Trichinium preissii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in dry, open habitats.
