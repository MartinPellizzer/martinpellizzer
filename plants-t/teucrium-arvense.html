Teucrium arvense

Teucrium arvense, commonly known as field germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Teucrium arvense is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
