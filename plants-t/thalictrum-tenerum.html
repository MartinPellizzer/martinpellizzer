Thalictrum tenerum

Thalictrum tenerum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a showy display of flowers in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

Thalictrum tenerum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
