Thalictrum tenuiculum

Thalictrum tenuiculum, commonly known as slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern hemisphere.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five sepals and numerous stamens, and it is wind-pollinated.

Thalictrum tenuiculum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
