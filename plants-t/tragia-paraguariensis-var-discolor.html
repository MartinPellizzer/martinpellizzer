Tragia paraguariensis var. discolor

Tragia paraguariensis var. discolor, commonly known as the redroot false thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for certain insect species.
