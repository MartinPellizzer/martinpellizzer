Taenitis chinensis

Taenitis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese taenitis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are sometimes utilized in culinary applications.

Taenitis chinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
