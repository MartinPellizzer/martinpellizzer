Triticum monococcum subsp. thaoudar

Triticum monococcum subsp.

thaoudar, commonly known as the Syrian spelt, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescence.

The plant produces small, hard grains that are typically brown or reddish in color. It is a diploid species with a genome size of approximately 14.3 Gb.

This subspecies is of interest to researchers due to its potential for improving modern wheat varieties through genetic diversity.
