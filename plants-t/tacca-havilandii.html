Tacca havilandii

Tacca havilandii, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling bat wings. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, curling hood. It thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
