Thalictrum sharpii

Thalictrum sharpii, commonly known as the Sharp's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum sharpii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
