Thea sinensis

Thea sinensis, commonly known as the Camellia sinensis, is a flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is widely cultivated for its leaves and buds. The plant produces dark green, leathery leaves that are used to make various types of tea. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and typically appear after the leaves have matured.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
