Trifolium melilotus-mauritanicum

Trifolium melilotus-mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania melilot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is also valued for its potential use in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
