Thalictrum thyrsoideum

Thalictrum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the tall meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Thalictrum thyrsoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
