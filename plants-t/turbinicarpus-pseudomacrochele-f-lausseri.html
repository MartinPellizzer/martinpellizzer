Turbinicarpus pseudomacrochele f. lausseri

Turbinicarpus pseudomacrochele f. lausseri, commonly known as the Lausser's Turbinicarpus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and a dense covering of white, velvety hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from areoles along the stem. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a prized species among cactus enthusiasts.
