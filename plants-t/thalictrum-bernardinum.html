Thalictrum bernardinum

Thalictrum bernardinum, commonly known as the pink meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces delicate, clustered clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Thalictrum bernardinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
