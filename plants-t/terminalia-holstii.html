Terminalia holstii

Terminalia holstii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.

The plant is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
