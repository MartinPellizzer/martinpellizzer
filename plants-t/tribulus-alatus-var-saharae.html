Tribulus alatus var. saharae

Tribulus alatus var. saharae, commonly known as the Saharan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its hairy leaves and robust root system help it retain moisture and withstand high temperatures.
