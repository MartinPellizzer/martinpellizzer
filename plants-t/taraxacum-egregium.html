Taraxacum egregium

Taraxacum egregium, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that resemble small suns, which are surrounded by a series of bracts. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of tiny, parachute-like seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. Taraxacum egregium is known for its resilience and ability to spread rapidly, making it a common sight in many regions.
