Trias ovata

Trias ovata, commonly known as the oval trias, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.

Trias ovata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
