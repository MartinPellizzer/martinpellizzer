Tragacantha picta

Tragacantha picta, commonly known as the painted tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful flowers that range from red to purple. The plant produces a gel-like substance from its roots, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in semi-arid environments.

Tragacantha picta is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking floral display.
