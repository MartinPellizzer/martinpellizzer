Triplochiton zambesiacus

Triplochiton zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
