Tragacantha sericea

Tragacantha sericea, commonly known as silky tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a silvery appearance, contributing to its common name.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
