Tragacantha nuda

Tragacantha nuda, commonly known as the naked tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a gum known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Tragacantha nuda thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
