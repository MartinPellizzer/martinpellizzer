Tarenaya cordobensis

Tarenaya cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba tarenaya, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

Tarenaya cordobensis is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
