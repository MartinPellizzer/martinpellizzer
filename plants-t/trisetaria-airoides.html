Trisetaria airoides

Trisetaria airoides, commonly known as the fragrant grass or airoides grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments. Trisetaria airoides is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of grassland ecosystems.
