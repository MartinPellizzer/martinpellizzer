Triodia microstachya

Triodia microstachya, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, prickly hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Triodia microstachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert animals.
