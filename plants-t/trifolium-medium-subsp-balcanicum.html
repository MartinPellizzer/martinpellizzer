Trifolium medium subsp. balcanicum

Trifolium medium subsp.

balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in globular clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
