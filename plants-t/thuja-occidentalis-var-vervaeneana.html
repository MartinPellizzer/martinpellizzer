Thuja occidentalis var. vervaeneana

Thuja occidentalis var. vervaeneana, commonly known as the American arborvitae or northern white cedar, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its flattened, spruce-like foliage and aromatic, scale-like leaves. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for hedges and windbreaks.
