Taraxacum pallidum

Taraxacum pallidum, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant produces a bright yellow flower that develops into a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive basal rosette structure.
