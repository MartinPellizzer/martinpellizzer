Triticum pinnatum var. abbreviatum

Triticum pinnatum var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for drought-resistant landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
