Tetradenia urticifolia

Tetradenia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging tetradenia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that often have a glandular surface. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves contain irritant chemicals, giving it a stinging sensation when touched.

Tetradenia urticifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
