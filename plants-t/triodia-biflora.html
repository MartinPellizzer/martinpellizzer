Triodia biflora

Triodia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered spinifex, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing structure with sharp, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Triodia biflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
