Trichopilia punicea

Trichopilia punicea, commonly known as the red trichopilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed.

Trichopilia punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
