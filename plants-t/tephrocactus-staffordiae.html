Tephrocactus staffordiae

Tephrocactus staffordiae, commonly known as the Stafford's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
