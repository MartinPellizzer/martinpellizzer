Trichilia gracilis

Trichilia gracilis, commonly known as the slender trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Trichilia gracilis is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
