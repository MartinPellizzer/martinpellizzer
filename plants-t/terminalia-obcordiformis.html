Terminalia obcordiformis

Terminalia obcordiformis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
