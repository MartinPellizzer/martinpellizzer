Thysanachne scoparia

Thysanachne scoparia, commonly known as narrowleaf thysanachne, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is often hidden among the foliage.

Thysanachne scoparia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
