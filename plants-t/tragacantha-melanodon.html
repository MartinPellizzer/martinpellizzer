Tragacantha melanodon

Tragacantha melanodon, commonly known as the black-seeded tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, dark-colored seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha melanodon is valued for its resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
