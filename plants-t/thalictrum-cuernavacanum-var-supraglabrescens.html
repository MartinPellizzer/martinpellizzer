Thalictrum cuernavacanum var. supraglabrescens

Thalictrum cuernavacanum var. supraglabrescens, commonly known as the Cuernavaca meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
