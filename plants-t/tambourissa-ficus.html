Tambourissa ficus

Tambourissa ficus, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that typically range in color from purple to white. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indirect light and its relatively low maintenance requirements. Its foliage is evergreen, and it produces small, round leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by small insects, and the plant is known for its adaptability to a variety of indoor growing conditions.
