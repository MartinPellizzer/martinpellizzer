Tamarix gallica subsp. nilotica

Tamarix gallica subsp.

nilotica, commonly known as the white tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces compact clusters of pink to white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative propagation.
