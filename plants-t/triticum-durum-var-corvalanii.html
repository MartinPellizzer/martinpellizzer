Triticum durum var. corvalanii

Triticum durum var. corvalanii, commonly known as Corvalanii durum wheat, is a variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique botanical traits.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae, and it produces hard, high-protein grains that are well-suited for pasta-making. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow leaves and spikelets that bear small, tough grains. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a significant crop in certain agricultural regions.
