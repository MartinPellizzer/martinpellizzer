Taxus dovastonii

Taxus dovastonii, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value rather than for consumption.
