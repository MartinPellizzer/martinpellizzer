Teucrium scordium var. villosum

Teucrium scordium var. villosum, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its scientific name.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
