Taxus wallichiana var. mairei

Taxus wallichiana var. mairei, commonly known as the Yewtree or Chinese Yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and reddish-brown bark that develops fissures with age. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
