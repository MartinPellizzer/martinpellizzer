Tridactyle latifolia

Tridactyle latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tridactyle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its wide, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Africa and Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
