Turraea richardii

Turraea richardii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has been traditionally used in local crafts and for medicinal purposes.
