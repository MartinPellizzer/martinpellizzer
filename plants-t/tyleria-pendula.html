Tyleria pendula

Tyleria pendula, commonly known as the hanging broom.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its slender, pendulous stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Tyleria pendula is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
