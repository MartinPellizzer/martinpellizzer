Taraxacum calabricum

Taraxacum calabricum, commonly known as the Calabrian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its yellow composite flowers bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic pappus-based seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum calabricum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
