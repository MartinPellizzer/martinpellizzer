Typhonium griseum

Typhonium griseum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking purple and white spadices, which are its showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.
