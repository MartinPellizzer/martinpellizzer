Tristicha trifaria subsp. pulchella

Tristicha trifaria subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
