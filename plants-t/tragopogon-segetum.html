Tragopogon segetum

Tragopogon segetum, commonly known as the grain thistle, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields and roadsides, and is often found in agricultural areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

Tragopogon segetum is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of inulin and other beneficial compounds.
