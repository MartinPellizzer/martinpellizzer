Teucrium virescens

Teucrium virescens, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 feet tall. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a minty aroma when crushed.

Blue germander thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
