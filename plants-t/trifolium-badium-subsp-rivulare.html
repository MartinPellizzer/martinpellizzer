Trifolium badium subsp. rivulare

Trifolium badium subsp.

rivulare, commonly known as river clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to withstand periodic flooding.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
