Teucrium grandiusculum subsp. pilosum

Teucrium grandiusculum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy teucrium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
