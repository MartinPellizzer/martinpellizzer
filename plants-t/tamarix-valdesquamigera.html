Tamarix valdesquamigera

Tamarix valdesquamigera, commonly known as the Valdesquamigera tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Tamaricaceae family and is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper underground, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
