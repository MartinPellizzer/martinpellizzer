Thermopsis villosa

Thermopsis villosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Thermopsis villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
