Taraxacum ustkanense

Taraxacum ustkanense, commonly known as the Ustkan dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
