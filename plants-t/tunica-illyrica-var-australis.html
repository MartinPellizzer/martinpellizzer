Tunica illyrica var. australis

Tunica illyrica var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Illyrian Shield, is a rare and specialized plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, typically blooming in late spring. Its root system is extensive and adapted to anchor it in rocky soils, while its growth pattern is slow and deliberate.
