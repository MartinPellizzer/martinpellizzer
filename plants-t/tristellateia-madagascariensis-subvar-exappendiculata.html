Tristellateia madagascariensis subvar. exappendiculata

Tristellateia madagascariensis subvar. exappendiculata, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually striking addition to botanical gardens and greenhouses.
