Tacca madagascariensis

Tacca madagascariensis, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a bat's face. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its exotic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, adding a dramatic visual element to its habitat.
