Thymus serpyllum subsp. comosus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

comosus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces pink or purple flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and herbal purposes.
