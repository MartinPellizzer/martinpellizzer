Taraxacum pedemontanum

Taraxacum pedemontanum, commonly known as the Piedmont dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
