Tripteris spathulata

Tripteris spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed tripteris, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in rainforests. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellowish and are followed by distinctive, spathelike bracts.

Tripteris spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
