Thymus daenensis

Thymus daenensis, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers and has aromatic, woody stems covered in tiny leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its essential oil is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses, similar to other thyme species.
