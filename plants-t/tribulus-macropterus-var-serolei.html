Tribulus macropterus var. serolei

Tribulus macropterus var. serolei, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly for its purported aphrodisiac properties.
