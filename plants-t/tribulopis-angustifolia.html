Tribulopis angustifolia

Tribulopis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits. Its seeds are often used as a food source, while the plant itself is sometimes utilized in traditional medicine.

Tribulopis angustifolia is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
