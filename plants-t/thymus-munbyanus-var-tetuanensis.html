Thymus munbyanus var. tetuanensis

Thymus munbyanus var. tetuanensis, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, gray-green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses, contributing to its historical significance in herbal traditions.
