Tetraplasandra meiandra var. rockii

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. rockii, commonly known as the Rock Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name 'rockii' refers to its natural habitat on rocky coastal areas.
