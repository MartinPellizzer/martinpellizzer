Thymus camphoratus

Thymus camphoratus, commonly known as camphor thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its aromatic oils, particularly camphor, contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic properties.

Thymus camphoratus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal herb collections.
