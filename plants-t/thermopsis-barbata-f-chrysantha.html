Thermopsis barbata f. chrysantha

Thermopsis barbata f. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden thermopsis, is a variant of the thermopsis plant characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
