Taraxacum aleppicum

Taraxacum aleppicum, commonly known as the Aleppo dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses.

Taraxacum aleppicum is valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
