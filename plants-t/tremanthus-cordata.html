Tremanthus cordata

Tremanthus cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tremanthus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Tremanthus cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
