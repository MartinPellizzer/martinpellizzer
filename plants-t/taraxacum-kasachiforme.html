Taraxacum kasachiforme

Taraxacum kasachiforme, commonly known as the Kazakhstan dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan, and thrives in dry, open habitats. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Taraxacum kasachiforme is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
