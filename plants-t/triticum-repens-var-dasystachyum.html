Triticum repens var. dasystachyum

Triticum repens var. dasystachyum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous structure that contributes to its resilience.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
