Themistoclesia crassifolia

Themistoclesia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
