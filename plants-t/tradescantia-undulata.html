Tradescantia undulata

Tradescantia undulata, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Tradescantia undulata is also known for its ability to purify indoor air and its resilience to drought conditions.
