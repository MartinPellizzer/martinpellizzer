Tillandsia hieroglyphica

Tillandsia hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Mexican beauty or the stone pine, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can store water. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
