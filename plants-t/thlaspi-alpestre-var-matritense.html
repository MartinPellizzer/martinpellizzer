Thlaspi alpestre var. matritense

Thlaspi alpestre var. matritense, commonly known as the Madrid Wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate frost, making it a hardy species.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
