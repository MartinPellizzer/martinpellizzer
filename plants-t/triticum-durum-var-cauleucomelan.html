Triticum durum var. cauleucomelan

Triticum durum var. cauleucomelan, commonly known as black wheat, is a variety of durum wheat characterized by its dark-colored grain.

This variety is notable for its high protein content and gluten strength, making it ideal for pasta and semolina production. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with narrow, erect leaves and spikelets that bear hard, dark grains. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires a moderate amount of rainfall during its growing season.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both culinary and industrial applications.
