Tragacantha chordorrhiza

Tragacantha chordorrhiza, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals and food.

Its growth habit includes a taproot system and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
