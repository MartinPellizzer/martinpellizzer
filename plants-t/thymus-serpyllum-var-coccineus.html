Thymus serpyllum var. coccineus

Thymus serpyllum var. coccineus, commonly known as red thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically grey-green with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
