Thrinax rex

Thrinax rex, commonly known as the queen of the cacti, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a dramatic contrast to its rugged appearance. Its unique morphology includes a fibrous, spongy tissue that helps it store water efficiently.

Thrinax rex is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
