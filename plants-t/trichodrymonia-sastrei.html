Trichodrymonia sastrei

Trichodrymonia sastrei, commonly known as the Sastre's thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptations to drought include thick, waxy leaves and deep root systems.

Trichodrymonia sastrei plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides shelter for small animals.
