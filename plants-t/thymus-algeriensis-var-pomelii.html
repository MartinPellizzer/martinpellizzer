Thymus algeriensis var. pomelii

Thymus algeriensis var. pomelii, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
