Turpinia glaberrima

Turpinia glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth turpentine tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-yellow berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
