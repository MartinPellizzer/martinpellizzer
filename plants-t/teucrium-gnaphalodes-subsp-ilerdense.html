Teucrium gnaphalodes subsp. ilerdense

Teucrium gnaphalodes subsp.

ilerdense, commonly known as the Iberian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and scrublands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.
