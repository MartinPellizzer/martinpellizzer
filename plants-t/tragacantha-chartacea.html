Tragacantha chartacea

Tragacantha chartacea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery bark and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
