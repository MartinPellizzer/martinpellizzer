Trisetum flavescens var. burnoufii

Trisetum flavescens var. burnoufii, commonly known as the golden false brome, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features fine, thread-like leaves that are typically golden or light green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
