Taraxacum kamtchaticum

Taraxacum kamtchaticum, commonly known as the Kamchatka dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces a tall, hollow stem topped with a single, yellow composite flower head. After flowering, it develops a distinctive, elongated seed head with white, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.
