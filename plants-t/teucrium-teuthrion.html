Teucrium teuthrion

Teucrium teuthrion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium teuthrion is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
