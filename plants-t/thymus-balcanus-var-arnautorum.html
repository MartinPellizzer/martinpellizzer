Thymus balcanus var. arnautorum

Thymus balcanus var. arnautorum, commonly known as Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact shrub form with aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
