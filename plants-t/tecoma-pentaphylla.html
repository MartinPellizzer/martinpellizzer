Tecoma pentaphylla

Tecoma pentaphylla, commonly known as yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Tecoma pentaphylla is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
