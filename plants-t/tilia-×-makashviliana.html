Tilia × makashviliana

Tilia × makashviliana, commonly known as the Tashkent linden, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tilia cordata and Tilia platyphyllos.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are an important nectar source for pollinators. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is native to Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
