Tupeia myrtifolia

Tupeia myrtifolia, commonly known as the yellow tupeia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Tupeia myrtifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
