Terniopsis savannaketensis

Terniopsis savannaketensis, commonly known as the Cambodian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats such as savannas and forest edges. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

Terniopsis savannaketensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant display.
