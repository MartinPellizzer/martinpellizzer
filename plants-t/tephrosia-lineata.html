Tephrosia lineata

Tephrosia lineata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tephrosia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Tephrosia lineata is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
