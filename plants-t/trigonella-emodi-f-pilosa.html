Trigonella emodi f. pilosa

Trigonella emodi f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant characterized by its hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive aroma. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The hairy texture of its leaves distinguishes this subspecies from other forms of Trigonella emodi.
