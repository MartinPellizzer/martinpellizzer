Teucrium bicoloreum

Teucrium bicoloreum, commonly known as the two-colored germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium bicoloreum is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
