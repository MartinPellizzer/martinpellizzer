Taraxacum wessbergii

Taraxacum wessbergii, commonly known as the Wessberg's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a sphere of white, feathery seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
