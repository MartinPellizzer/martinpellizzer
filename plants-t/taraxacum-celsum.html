Taraxacum celsum

Taraxacum celsum, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of a distinctive seed head composed of numerous fluffy seeds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed and sandy soils. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to spread easily through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
