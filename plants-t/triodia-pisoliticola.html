Triodia pisoliticola

Triodia pisoliticola, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Triodia pisoliticola plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
