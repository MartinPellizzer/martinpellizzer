Trachycaryon klotzschii

Trachycaryon klotzschii, commonly known as the mountain hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Trachycaryon klotzschii is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
