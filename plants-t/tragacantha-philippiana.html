Tragacantha philippiana

Tragacantha philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges, and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Tragacantha philippiana is noted for its potential ecological and medicinal significance in its native region.
