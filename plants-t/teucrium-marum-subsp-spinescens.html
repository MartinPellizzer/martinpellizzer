Teucrium marum subsp. spinescens

Teucrium marum subsp.

spinescens, commonly known as Spiny Germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soils.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its stems are usually sparsely branched and may bear small spines, contributing to its common name.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
