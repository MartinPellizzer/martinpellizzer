Thymus brachyodon

Thymus brachyodon, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing, aromatic perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, giving it a dense, cushion-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus brachyodon is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
