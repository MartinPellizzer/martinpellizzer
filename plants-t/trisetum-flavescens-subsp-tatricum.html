Trisetum flavescens subsp. tatricum

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

tatricum, commonly known as the Tatrican hairgrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense colonies.

The leaves are typically narrow and somewhat hairy, with a bluish-green hue when fresh. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
