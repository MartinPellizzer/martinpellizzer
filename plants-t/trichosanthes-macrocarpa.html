Trichosanthes macrocarpa

Trichosanthes macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant Chinese snake gourd, is a herbaceous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, warty fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female blooms. Its leaves are heart-shaped and lobed, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
