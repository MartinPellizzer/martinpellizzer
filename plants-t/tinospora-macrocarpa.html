Tinospora macrocarpa

Tinospora macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited moonseed, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves and distinctive, fleshy, yellow to orange berries. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters, often entwining trees and shrubs. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
