Tephrosia collina

Tephrosia collina, commonly known as the mountain tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often with a slight pinkish tinge. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia collina is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
