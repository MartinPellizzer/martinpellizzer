Thinouia ventricosa

Thinouia ventricosa, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on the variety and growing conditions.
