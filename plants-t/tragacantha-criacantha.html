Tragacantha criacantha

Tragacantha criacantha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its deep root system allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
