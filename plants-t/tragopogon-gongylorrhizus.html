Tragopogon gongylorrhizus

Tragopogon gongylorrhizus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the upper leaves are more lance-shaped. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tragopogon gongylorrhizus is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
