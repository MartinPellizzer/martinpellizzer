Thelymitra mucida

Thelymitra mucida, commonly known as the sticky helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the subfamily Orchidoideae and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and heathlands. Its leaves are grass-like and often covered with a sticky substance, which gives it its common name.

Thelymitra mucida is known for its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring.
