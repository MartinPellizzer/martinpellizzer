Trichosanthes tricuspidata var. strigosa

Trichosanthes tricuspidata var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy trumpet gourd, is a variant of the trumpet gourd native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive trumpet shape, and the fruit is a long, slender gourd that matures to a yellow or orange color. This plant is often grown for its edible fruit, which has a mild, sweet flavor.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
