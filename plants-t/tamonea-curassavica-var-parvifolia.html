Tamonea curassavica var. parvifolia

Tamonea curassavica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered sea hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
