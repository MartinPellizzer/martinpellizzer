Tacca pinnatifida var. maculata

Tacca pinnatifida var. maculata, commonly known as the "Black Magic" or "Lady's Scarf," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, waxy, and often darkly colored leaves, which can have distinctive markings. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
