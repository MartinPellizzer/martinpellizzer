Triplochlamys selloi

Triplochlamys selloi, commonly known as the Sello's flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually yellow with red or orange markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant and unique appearance.
