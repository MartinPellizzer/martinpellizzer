Tetranthera floribunda

Tetranthera floribunda, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tetranthera floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
