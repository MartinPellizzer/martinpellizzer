Trifolium monanthum var. caulescens

Trifolium monanthum var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a slender stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
