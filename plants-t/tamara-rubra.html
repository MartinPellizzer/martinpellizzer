Tamara rubra

Tamara rubra, commonly known as the red tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a layer of salt, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Tamara rubra is often found in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to harsh conditions.
