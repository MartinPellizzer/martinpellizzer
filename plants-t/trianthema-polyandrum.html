Trianthema polyandrum

Trianthema polyandrum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often blue or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Trianthema polyandrum is known for its adaptability to saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
