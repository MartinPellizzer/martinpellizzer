Teucrium buxifolium var. terciae

Teucrium buxifolium var. terciae, commonly known as the three-lobed germander, is a compact, mat-forming perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both garden use and ecological habitats.
