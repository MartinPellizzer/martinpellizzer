Trachydium didyma

Trachydium didyma, commonly known as the double-flowered dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that consist of numerous small florets arranged in two distinct layers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated margin, while the stem is often hairy.

Trachydium didyma is an annual herb that blooms from spring to early summer.
