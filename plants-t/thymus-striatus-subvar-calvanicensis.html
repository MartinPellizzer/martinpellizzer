Thymus striatus subvar. calvanicensis

Thymus striatus subvar. calvanicensis, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
