Trifolium ukingense

Trifolium ukingense, commonly known as the Ukinge clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in dense clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium ukingense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential ecological and agricultural applications.
