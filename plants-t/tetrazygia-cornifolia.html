Tetrazygia cornifolia

Tetrazygia cornifolia, commonly known as the horned fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, branching fronds that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Tetrazygia cornifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
