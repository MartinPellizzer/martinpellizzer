Tricalysia suffruticosa

Tricalysia suffruticosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Tricalysia suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
