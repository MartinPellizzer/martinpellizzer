Tilia × obscura

Tilia × obscura, commonly known as the small-leaf linden, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, characterized by its smaller, heart-shaped leaves compared to its parent species.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are arranged alternately on the stems and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are fragrant and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking and as a source of honey.
