Tilia caucasica

Tilia caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian lime, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
