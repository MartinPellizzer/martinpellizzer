Thunbergia lacei

Thunbergia lacei, commonly known as the blue lace flower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and long, cascading clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple in color, with a white throat, and bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
