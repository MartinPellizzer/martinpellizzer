Taraxacum indicum

Taraxacum indicum, commonly known as Indian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with parachute-like seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum indicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
