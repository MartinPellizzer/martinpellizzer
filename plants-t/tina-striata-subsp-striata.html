Tina striata subsp. striata

Tina striata subsp.

striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tina, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
