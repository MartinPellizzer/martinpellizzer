Tephrosia berhautiana

Tephrosia berhautiana, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Tephrosia berhautiana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its ecological and economic importance.
