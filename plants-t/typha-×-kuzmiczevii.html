Typha × kuzmiczevii

Typha × kuzmiczevii, commonly known as the hybrid cattail, is a wetland plant that results from the crossbreeding of Typha angustifolia and Typha latifolia.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and a cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a dense, brown spike. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense colonies that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

Typha × kuzmiczevii plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
