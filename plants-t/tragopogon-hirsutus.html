Tragopogon hirsutus

Tragopogon hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads with long, silky bracts. The plant produces a single, large, showy flower that blooms in the summer months. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name.

Tragopogon hirsutus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun.
