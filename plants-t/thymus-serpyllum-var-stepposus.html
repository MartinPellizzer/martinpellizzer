Thymus serpyllum var. stepposus

Thymus serpyllum var. stepposus, commonly known as steppe thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems, forming a mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.
