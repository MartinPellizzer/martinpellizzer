Tylophora japonica var. albiflora

Tylophora japonica var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the萝藦科 (Asclepiadaceae) family. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
