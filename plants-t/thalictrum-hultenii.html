Thalictrum hultenii

Thalictrum hultenii, commonly known as Hulten's meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum hultenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
