Tilia × europaea pallida

Tilia × europaea pallida, commonly known as the white linden, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are pale green and often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its shade and aesthetic qualities.
