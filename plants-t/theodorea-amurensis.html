Theodorea amurensis

Theodorea amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy blue or purple bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Campanulaceae family.
