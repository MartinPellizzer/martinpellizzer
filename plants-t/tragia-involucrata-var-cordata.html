Tragia involucrata var. cordata

Tragia involucrata var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are slender with a somewhat woody base.

This species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
