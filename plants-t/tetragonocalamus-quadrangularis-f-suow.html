Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. suow

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. suow, commonly known as the Suow's square bamboo, is a unique variant of the square bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctly quadrangular stems, which give it a geometric appearance. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with thick, ridged culms that are often used in traditional crafts. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its visual interest.
