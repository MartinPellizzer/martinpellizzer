Trichera dipsacifolia subsp. turocensis

Trichera dipsacifolia subsp.

turocensis, commonly known as the Turoc Mountains fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian region. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its specific chromosome number and subtle morphological differences from other subspecies.
