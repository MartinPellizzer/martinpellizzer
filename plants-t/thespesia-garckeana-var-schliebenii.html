Thespesia garckeana var. schliebenii

Thespesia garckeana var. schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal or mangrove habitats. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.
