Terminalia tanibouca

Terminalia tanibouca, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
