Torrubia floribunda

Torrubia floribunda, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and pine forests. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
