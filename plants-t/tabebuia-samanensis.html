Tabebuia samanensis

Tabebuia samanensis, commonly known as the rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown and compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow during the dry season. The tree produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in white, pink, or purple hues, which bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its wood is valued for its strength and resistance to decay.

The plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
