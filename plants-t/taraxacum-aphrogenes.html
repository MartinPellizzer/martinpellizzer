Taraxacum aphrogenes

Taraxacum aphrogenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. It produces a slender, hollow stem that supports a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets and disk florets.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
