Trevesia palmata var. incisa

Trevesia palmata var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trevesia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
