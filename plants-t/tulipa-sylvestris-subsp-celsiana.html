Tulipa sylvestris subsp. celsiana

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

celsiana, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It is a perennial herb with showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant produces a single stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height, bearing a single bloom at the top. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking spring blooms.
