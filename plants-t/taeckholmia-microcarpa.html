Taeckholmia microcarpa

Taeckholmia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf taeckholmia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, compact growth with small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.

Taeckholmia microcarpa is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its low maintenance requirements.
