Tabacus viscidus

Tabacus viscidus, commonly known as the sticky tobacco plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
