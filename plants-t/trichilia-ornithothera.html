Trichilia ornithothera

Trichilia ornithothera, commonly known as the horned tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.

Trichilia ornithothera is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
