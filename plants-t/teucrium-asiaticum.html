Teucrium asiaticum

Teucrium asiaticum, commonly known as Asiatic germander, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a slightly rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Teucrium asiaticum is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
