Tylophora pubescens

Tylophora pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
