Taraxacum rizaense

Taraxacum rizaense, commonly known as the Riza dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, showcasing resilience typical of its habitat.

Its presence contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
