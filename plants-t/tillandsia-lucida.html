Tillandsia lucida

Tillandsia lucida, commonly known as the bright tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant's leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain water in its natural habitat. It produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Tillandsia lucida is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
