Tabernaemontana persicariifolia

Tabernaemontana persicariifolia, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically open in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, dark blue berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
