Temnadenia leptoloba

Temnadenia leptoloba, commonly known as the slender leaf temnadenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Temnadenia leptoloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
