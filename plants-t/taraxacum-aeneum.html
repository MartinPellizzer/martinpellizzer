Taraxacum aeneum

Taraxacum aeneum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Taraxacum aeneum is known for its resilience and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
