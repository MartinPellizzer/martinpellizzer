Tagetes mandonii

Tagetes mandonii, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are typically daisy-like in appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in a single head, and it has hairy, narrow leaves. Tagetes mandonii is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
