Trichomanes bimarginatum

Trichomanes bimarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

The plant's intricate structure and moisture-dependent growth make it a notable example of vascular plant adaptation to moist, low-light conditions.
