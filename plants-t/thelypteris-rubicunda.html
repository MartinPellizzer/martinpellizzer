Thelypteris rubicunda

Thelypteris rubicunda, commonly known as the red fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Thelypteris rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, green foliage.
