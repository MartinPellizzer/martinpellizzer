Tectaria kehdingiana

Tectaria kehdingiana, commonly known as the Kehding's tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are often deeply divided into lanceolate segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as tropical and subtropical forests. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria kehdingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
