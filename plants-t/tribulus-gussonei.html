Tribulus gussonei

Tribulus gussonei, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tribulus gussonei is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is part of the Fabaceae family.
