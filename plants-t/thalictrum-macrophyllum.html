Thalictrum macrophyllum

Thalictrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in compound umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Thalictrum macrophyllum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
