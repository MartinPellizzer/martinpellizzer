Turgenia latifolia var. tuberculata

Turgenia latifolia var. tuberculata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in small tubercles, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its use in horticulture and landscaping.
