Tutcheria spectabilis

Tutcheria spectabilis, commonly known as the showy tutcheria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features erect, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers, which are often fragrant. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
