Toddalia fischeri

Toddalia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in rainforests. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces edible pods that contain seeds.

Toddalia fischeri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
