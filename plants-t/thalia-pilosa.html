Thalia pilosa

Thalia pilosa, commonly known as the silky thalia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the warmer months.

Thalia pilosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
