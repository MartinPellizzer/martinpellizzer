Triumfetta trifida

Triumfetta trifida, commonly known as the three-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three distinct leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Triumfetta trifida is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
