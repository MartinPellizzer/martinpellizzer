Tillandsia aurantiaca

Tillandsia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers, which are typically borne on a central spike. It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Tillandsia aurantiaca is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
