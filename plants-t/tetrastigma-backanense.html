Tetrastigma backanense

Tetrastigma backanense, commonly known as the Bornean crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
