Taraxacum florum

Taraxacum florum, commonly known as the flowered dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that resemble small sunflowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Taraxacum florum is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
