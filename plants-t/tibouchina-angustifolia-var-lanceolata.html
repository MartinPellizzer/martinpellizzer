Tibouchina angustifolia var. lanceolata

Tibouchina angustifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
