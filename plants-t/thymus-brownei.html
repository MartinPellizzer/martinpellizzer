Thymus brownei

Thymus brownei, commonly known as Brown's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has creeping stems with opposite, narrow, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thymus brownei is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
