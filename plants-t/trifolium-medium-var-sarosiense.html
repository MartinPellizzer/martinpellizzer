Trifolium medium var. sarosiense

Trifolium medium var. sarosiense, commonly known as the Sarosian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
