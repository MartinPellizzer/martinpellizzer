Tofieldia calyculata var. capitata

Tofieldia calyculata var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that are clustered at the tips of the stems, giving it its common name. It has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in ecological restoration projects.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and its tolerance to partial shade.
