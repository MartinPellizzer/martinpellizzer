Tococa coronata

Tococa coronata, commonly known as the golden cupuaçu, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small cups. The plant produces a fruit similar to that of the cupuaçu, which is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor. Tococa coronata is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural ingredients.
