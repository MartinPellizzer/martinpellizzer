Trigonotis radicans subsp. sericea

Trigonotis radicans subsp.

sericea, commonly known as silverleaf trailing lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to cascade gracefully from hanging baskets or walls.
