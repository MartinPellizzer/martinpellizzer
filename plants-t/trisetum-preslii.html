Trisetum preslii

Trisetum preslii, commonly known as Preslia's wood sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
