Teucrium heterophyllum subsp. heterophyllum

Teucrium heterophyllum subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant has opposite, narrow to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the summer. It is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
