Thespesia populnea var. acutiloba

Thespesia populnea var. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
