Trifolium pratense var. flavicans

Trifolium pratense var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a variant of the common red clover species.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the standard variety. This plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and it has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil and making it a valuable component of agricultural ecosystems.
