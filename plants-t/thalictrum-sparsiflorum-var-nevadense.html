Thalictrum sparsiflorum var. nevadense

Thalictrum sparsiflorum var. nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are finely divided and often appear fern-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
