Trianaeopiper garciae var. decurtispicum

Trianaeopiper garciae var. decurtispicum, commonly known as the Decurts' Piper, is a unique species within the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
