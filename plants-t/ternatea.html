Ternatea

Ternatea, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still water. Ternatea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance.

Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
