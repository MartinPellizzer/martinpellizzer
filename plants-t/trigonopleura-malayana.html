Trigonopleura malayana

Trigonopleura malayana, commonly known as the Malay pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique sympodial growth pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
