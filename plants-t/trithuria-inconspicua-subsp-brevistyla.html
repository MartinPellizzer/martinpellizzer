Trithuria inconspicua subsp. brevistyla

Trithuria inconspicua subsp.

brevistyla, commonly known as the narrow-styled trithuria, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of southern Africa. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands.

The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often with three sepals and three petals. It produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds.
