Trymalium floribundum

Trymalium floribundum, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red-brown stems that are covered in a thick, waxy coating. The plant produces showy clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salty sea breezes and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
