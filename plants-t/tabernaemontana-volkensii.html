Tabernaemontana volkensii

Tabernaemontana volkensii, commonly known as the Volkens' star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint pinkish hue. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
