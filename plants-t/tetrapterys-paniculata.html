Tetrapterys paniculata

Tetrapterys paniculata, commonly known as the panicle acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it the genus name "Tetrapterys.". The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, yellow flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody structure and adaptability to various soil types make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
