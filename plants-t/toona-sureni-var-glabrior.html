Toona sureni var. glabrior

Toona sureni var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are a food source for various animals.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form of Toona sureni.
