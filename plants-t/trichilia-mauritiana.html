Trichilia mauritiana

Trichilia mauritiana, commonly known as African shea or African butter tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds are used to extract a rich, buttery oil, which has various applications in traditional medicine and cosmetics.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native range.
