Trifolium involucratum var. heterodon

Trifolium involucratum var. heterodon, commonly known as the heterodont clover, is a variant of the broadleaf clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring three leaflets with varying shapes and sizes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
