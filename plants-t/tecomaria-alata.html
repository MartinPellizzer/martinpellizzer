Tecomaria alata

Tecomaria alata, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tecomaria alata is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
