Tillandsia appariciana

Tillandsia appariciana, commonly known as the silver vane tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found on tree branches or rocks in humid environments.

Its striking silver-blue coloration and intricate floral structures make it a popular choice among bromeliad enthusiasts.
