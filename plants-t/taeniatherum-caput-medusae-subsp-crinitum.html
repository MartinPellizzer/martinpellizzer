Taeniatherum caput-medusae subsp. crinitum

Taeniatherum caput-medusae subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, feathery plumes that resemble a crown, giving it its common name.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
