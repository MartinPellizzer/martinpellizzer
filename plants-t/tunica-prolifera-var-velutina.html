Tunica prolifera var. velutina

Tunica prolifera var. velutina, commonly known as the velvety tunica, is a unique plant species characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It is native to specific regions with high humidity and moderate temperatures. The plant exhibits a prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on the forest floor. Its leaves are broad and have a soft, hairy texture that aids in moisture retention.

It is particularly noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a valuable component of understory vegetation.
