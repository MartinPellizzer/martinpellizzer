Turnera campaniflora

Turnera campaniflora, commonly known as the bellflower turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Turnera campaniflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
