Teucrium × mugronense

Teucrium × mugronense, commonly known as hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a compact growth habit and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Teucrium × mugronense is also used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
