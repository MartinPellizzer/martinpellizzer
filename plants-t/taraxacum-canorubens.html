Taraxacum canorubens

Taraxacum canorubens, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces a hollow, ribbed stem that supports the flower head, which eventually develops into a fluffy seed sphere. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum canorubens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
