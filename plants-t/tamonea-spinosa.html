Tamonea spinosa

Tamonea spinosa, commonly known as the spiny tamonea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Tamonea spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
