Typha yakutii

Typha yakutii, commonly known as Yakut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in a dense layer of silky hair, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Typha yakutii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
