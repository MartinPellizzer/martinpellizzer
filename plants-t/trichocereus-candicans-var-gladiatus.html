Trichocereus candicans var. gladiatus

Trichocereus candicans var. gladiatus, commonly known as the "Carnival Cactus," is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming at night. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its growth habit and flowering patterns make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
