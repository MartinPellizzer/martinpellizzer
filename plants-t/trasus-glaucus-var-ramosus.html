Trasus glaucus var. ramosus

Trasus glaucus var. ramosus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal rainfall.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple branching stems that form a dense, compact structure.
