Taraxacum longirostre

Taraxacum longirostre, commonly known as the long-beaked dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow involucre that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique member of the dandelion genus.
