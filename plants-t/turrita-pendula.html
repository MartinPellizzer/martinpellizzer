Turrita pendula

Turrita pendula, commonly known as the hanging turricula, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Turbinellidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, spiral-shaped shell that is often pale in color with a smooth surface. The shell typically has a distinct suture and a narrow, elongated aperture. This species is found in marine environments, particularly in the Mediterranean Sea and the eastern Atlantic.

It plays a role in coastal ecosystems as both a prey item and a predator of small invertebrates.
