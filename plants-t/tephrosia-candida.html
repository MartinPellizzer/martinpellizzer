Tephrosia candida

Tephrosia candida, commonly known as white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect annual or perennial herb with trifoliate leaves. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia candida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
