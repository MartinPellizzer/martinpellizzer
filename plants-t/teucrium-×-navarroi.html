Teucrium × navarroi

Teucrium × navarroi, commonly known as Navarre germander, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean and temperate climates.
