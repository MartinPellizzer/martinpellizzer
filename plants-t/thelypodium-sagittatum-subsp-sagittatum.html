Thelypodium sagittatum subsp. sagittatum

Thelypodium sagittatum subsp.

sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive arrowhead shape. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins and meadows.
