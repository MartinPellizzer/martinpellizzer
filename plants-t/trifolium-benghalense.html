Trifolium benghalense

Trifolium benghalense, commonly known as the Bengal clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Trifolium benghalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
