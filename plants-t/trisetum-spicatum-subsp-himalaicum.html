Trisetum spicatum subsp. himalaicum

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

himalaicum, commonly known as Himalayan feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage is often silver-gray, reflecting its adaptation to high-altitude conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rugged terrain.
