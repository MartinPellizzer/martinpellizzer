Thalictrum domingense

Thalictrum domingense, commonly known as the Mexican meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.

Thalictrum domingense is also noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
