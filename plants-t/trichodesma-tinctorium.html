Trichodesma tinctorium

Trichodesma tinctorium, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Trichodesma tinctorium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
