Teucrium aureum subsp. flavovirens

Teucrium aureum subsp.

flavovirens, commonly known as golden germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered with fine, grayish-white hairs.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental species in gardens.
