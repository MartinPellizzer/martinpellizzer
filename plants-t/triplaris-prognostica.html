Triplaris prognostica

Triplaris prognostica, commonly known as the Brazilian sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a compact growth habit.

Triplaris prognostica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
