Tephrosia purpurea var. gracilis

Tephrosia purpurea var. gracilis, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
