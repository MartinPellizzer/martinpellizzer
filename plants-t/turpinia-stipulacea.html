Turpinia stipulacea

Turpinia stipulacea, commonly known as the Hawaiian turpentine tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The tree is notable for its ability to produce a resinous sap, which has been historically used for various purposes.
