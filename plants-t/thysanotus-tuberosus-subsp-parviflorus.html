Thysanotus tuberosus subsp. parviflorus

Thysanotus tuberosus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered bluebell, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
