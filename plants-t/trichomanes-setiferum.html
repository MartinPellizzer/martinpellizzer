Trichomanes setiferum

Trichomanes setiferum, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Trichomanes setiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
