Typha latifolia subsp. laxmanii

Typha latifolia subsp.

laxmanii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical flower head that produces fluffy seeds.

This subspecies thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

The plant plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
