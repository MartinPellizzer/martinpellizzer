Tripsacum guatamalense

Tripsacum guatamalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan tripesacum, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, humid habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Tripsacum guatamalense is of interest to botanists due to its potential genetic relationship to maize and its role in traditional agricultural systems.
