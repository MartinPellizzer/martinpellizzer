Tanacetum emodi

Tanacetum emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine.

Tanacetum emodi is also used in the production of certain essential oils due to its aromatic compounds.
