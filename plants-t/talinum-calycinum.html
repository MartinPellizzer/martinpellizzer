Talinum calycinum

Talinum calycinum, commonly known as the purple leaf talinum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often purple or reddish in color, with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, and are usually pink or white.

Talinum calycinum is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
