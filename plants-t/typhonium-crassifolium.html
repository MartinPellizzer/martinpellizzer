Typhonium crassifolium

Typhonium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
