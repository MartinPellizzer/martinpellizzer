Tanacetum pulchrum

Tanacetum pulchrum, commonly known as the showy tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are often surrounded by white or pale green ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common garden plant.

Tanacetum pulchrum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
