Trisetum barengense

Trisetum barengense, commonly known as the Bornean hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Trisetum barengense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
