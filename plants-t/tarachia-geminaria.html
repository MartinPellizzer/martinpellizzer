Tarachia geminaria

Tarachia geminaria, commonly known as the twin-flowered desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, drought-tolerant growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, sandy soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, often in pairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Tarachia geminaria is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
