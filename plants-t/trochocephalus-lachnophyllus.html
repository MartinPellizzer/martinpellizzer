Trochocephalus lachnophyllus

Trochocephalus lachnophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
