Thymus serpyllum subsp. rigidus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

rigidus, commonly known as rigid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
