Thymus × almijarensis

Thymus × almijarensis, commonly known as the Almijares thyme, is a hybrid species within the genus Thymus, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pale pink to lavender flowers. This plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its strong, pungent aroma.

Thymus × almijarensis is also valued in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in rocky or dry conditions.
