Trachomitum lancifolium

Trachomitum lancifolium, commonly known as the lanceleaf trachomitus, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Trachomitum lancifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
