Tropaeolum lobbianum var. splendens

Tropaeolum lobbianum var. splendens, commonly known as the flame flower or splendour nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from orange to red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
