Tephrosia papuana

Tephrosia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Tephrosia papuana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
