Tabernaemontana hexagona

Tabernaemontana hexagona, commonly known as the six-sided tabernacle.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically glossy and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
