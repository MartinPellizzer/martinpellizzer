Trichipteris latevagans

Trichipteris latevagans, commonly known as the broad-leaved fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It has a creeping rhizome that produces a dense network of leaves, which are typically broad and lanceolate in shape. The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in slow-moving waters and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
