Trigonella raphanina

Trigonella raphanina, commonly known as wild fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Trigonella raphanina is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
