Thrinax ferruginea

Thrinax ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant has a short trunk and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are fan-shaped and can reach up to 2 meters in diameter, providing a distinctive appearance.

Thrinax ferruginea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
