Trigonella platyphylla

Trigonella platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are usually hairy on the underside. The small yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

This plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
