Taraxacum oreinopsis

Taraxacum oreinopsis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root system. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is well-suited to survive in harsh, desert conditions.
