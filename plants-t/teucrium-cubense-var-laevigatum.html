Teucrium cubense var. laevigatum

Teucrium cubense var. laevigatum, commonly known as the Cuban teucrium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture, and its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
