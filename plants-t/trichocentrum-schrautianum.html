Trichocentrum schrautianum

Trichocentrum schrautianum, commonly known as the Schraut's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, clustered inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Trichocentrum schrautianum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
