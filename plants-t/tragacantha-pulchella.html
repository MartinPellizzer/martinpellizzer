Tragacantha pulchella

Tragacantha pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces a distinctive resin known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Tragacantha pulchella is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional uses.
