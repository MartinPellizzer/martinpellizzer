Tryphostemma parvifolium

Tryphostemma parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
