Thymus × hurtadoi

Thymus × hurtadoi, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender-colored flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
