Tulipa beccariana

Tulipa beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base of the plant.
