Trifolium repens var. glareosum

Trifolium repens var. glareosum, commonly known as the golden shamrock, is a variant of the common white clover.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets. This plant is typically found in grassy areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is a low-growing perennial that spreads via stolons, forming dense mats.

The golden shamrock is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in landscaping and as a forage crop.
