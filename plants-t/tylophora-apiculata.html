Tylophora apiculata

Tylophora apiculata, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Tylophora apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
