Trigonostemon longisepalus

Trigonostemon longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled trigonostemon, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
