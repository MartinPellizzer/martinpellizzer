Tofieldia coccinea var. geibiensis

Tofieldia coccinea var. geibiensis, commonly known as the red spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems, making it visually distinctive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
