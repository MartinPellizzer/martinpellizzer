Thymus irtyschensis

Thymus irtyschensis, commonly known as the Irtysch thyme, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Thymus irtyschensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
