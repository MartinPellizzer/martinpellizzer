Tinus rigida

Tinus rigida, commonly known as the rigid tinus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
