Torilis nodosa var. peduncularis

Torilis nodosa var. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender hedge mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
