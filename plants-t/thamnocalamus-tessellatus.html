Thamnocalamus tessellatus

Thamnocalamus tessellatus, commonly known as the zigzag bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that exhibit a distinctive zigzag pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Thamnocalamus tessellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique appearance.
