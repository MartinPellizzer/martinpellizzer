Telipogon loxensis

Telipogon loxensis, commonly known as the Loxa grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, perennial grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often hidden among the foliage, making it difficult to spot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Telipogon loxensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
