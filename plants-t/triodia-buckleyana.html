Triodia buckleyana

Triodia buckleyana, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Triodia buckleyana is well adapted to drought and can thrive in sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
