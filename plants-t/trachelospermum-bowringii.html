Trachelospermum bowringii

Trachelospermum bowringii, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine or bowringii jessamine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish-brown color, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and fragrance.

Trachelospermum bowringii is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
