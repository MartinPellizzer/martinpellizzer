Turnera blanchetiana

Turnera blanchetiana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
