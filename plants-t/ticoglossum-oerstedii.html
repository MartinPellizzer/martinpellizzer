Ticoglossum oerstedii

Ticoglossum oerstedii, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its showy purple or violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ticoglossum oerstedii is often found in dry, sunny habitats like rocky soils and open woodlands.
