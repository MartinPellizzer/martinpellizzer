Tulipa narcissicum

Tulipa narcissicum, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers. The blooms, which resemble a crown, are typically orange or yellow with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant produces bulbs that can be propagated through division, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
