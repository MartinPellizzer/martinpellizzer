Thymus serpyllum f. leucotrichus

Thymus serpyllum f. leucotrichus, commonly known as white thyme, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small white or pale pink flowers. This variety is distinguished by its white-haired stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical gray-green thyme. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and mild, aromatic scent, making it a popular choice in gardens and herbal applications.
