Tulipa didieri

Tulipa didieri, commonly known as the Didier's tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, and it blooms in early spring.

The botanical features of Tulipa didieri make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
