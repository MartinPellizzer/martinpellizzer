Thalictrum pauciflorum

Thalictrum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear compound leaves with finely divided, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Thalictrum pauciflorum is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ecological benefits to pollinators.
