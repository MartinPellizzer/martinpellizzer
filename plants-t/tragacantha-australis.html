Tragacantha australis

Tragacantha australis, commonly known as the Australian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

The plant's sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
