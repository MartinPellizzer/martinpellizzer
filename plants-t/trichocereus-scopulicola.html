Trichocereus scopulicola

Trichocereus scopulicola, commonly known as the desert torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often develop a reddish or purplish coloration under sunlight. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
