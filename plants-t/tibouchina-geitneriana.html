Tibouchina geitneriana

Tibouchina geitneriana, commonly known as the red-hot catania, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive red or purple stem. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Tibouchina geitneriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
