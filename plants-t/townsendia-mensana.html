Townsendia mensana

Townsendia mensana, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high elevations. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its showy, bell-shaped blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
