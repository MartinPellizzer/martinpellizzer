Tinus obovata

Tinus obovata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to obovate in shape, with a waxy, often hairy surface. Its showy yellow or orange flowers are surrounded by white ray florets, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Tinus obovata is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
