Triodia angusta

Triodia angusta, commonly known as the narrow triodia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
