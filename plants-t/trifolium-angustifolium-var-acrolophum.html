Trifolium angustifolium var. acrolophum

Trifolium angustifolium var. acrolophum, commonly known as narrow-leaf clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
