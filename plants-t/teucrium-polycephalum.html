Teucrium polycephalum

Teucrium polycephalum, commonly known as purplehead germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium polycephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
