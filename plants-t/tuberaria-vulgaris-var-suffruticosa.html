Tuberaria vulgaris var. suffruticosa

Tuberaria vulgaris var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping thornapple, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with woody stems that become branched and thorny with age. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
