Thelypteris subulifolia

Thelypteris subulifolia, commonly known as the slender pine fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like fronds that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris subulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
