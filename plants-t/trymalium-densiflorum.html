Trymalium densiflorum

Trymalium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, often blue or purple, flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in drought-prone regions.
