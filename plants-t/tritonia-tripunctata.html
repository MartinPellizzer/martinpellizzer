Tritonia tripunctata

Tritonia tripunctata, commonly known as the three-spotted orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are strap-like and grass-like, often with a waxy texture, while the flowers are borne on elongated inflorescences.

Tritonia tripunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
