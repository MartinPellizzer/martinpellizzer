Thomsonaria eleonorae

Thomsonaria eleonorae, commonly known as the Eleanor's thomsonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Thomsonaria eleonorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
