Taeniophyllum balansae

Taeniophyllum balansae, commonly known as the Balansae moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are typically curved and have a narrow, pointed tip. The plant is cryptogamous, meaning it reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of microhabitats in forest understories.
