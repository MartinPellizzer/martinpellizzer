Tibouchina villosissima var. longifolia

Tibouchina villosissima var. longifolia, commonly known as the velvet tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in warm, humid climates.
