Tepuia speciosa

Tepuia speciosa, commonly known as the showy tepuia, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in shades of purple and violet, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the early morning. Tepuia speciosa thrives in high-altitude environments, typically growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural interest and ecological study.
