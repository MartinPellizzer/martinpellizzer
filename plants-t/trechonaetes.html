Trechonaetes

Trechonaetes, commonly known as the spiny spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
