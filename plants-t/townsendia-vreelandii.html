Townsendia vreelandii

Townsendia vreelandii, commonly known as the Vreeland's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called cypsela.
