Terminalia bucidoides

Terminalia bucidoides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
