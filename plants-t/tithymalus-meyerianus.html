Tithymalus meyerianus

Tithymalus meyerianus, commonly known as the Meyer's tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
