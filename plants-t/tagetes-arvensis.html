Tagetes arvensis

Tagetes arvensis, commonly known as field marigold, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with radiating petals and a central disk of tubular florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Tagetes arvensis is known for its strong scent and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
