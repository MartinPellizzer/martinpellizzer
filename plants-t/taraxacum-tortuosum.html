Taraxacum tortuosum

Taraxacum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing a single flower head composed of numerous small yellow florets.

The fruit is a tufted seed with a parachute-like structure, enabling wind dispersal.
