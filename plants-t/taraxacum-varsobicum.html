Taraxacum varsobicum

Taraxacum varsobicum, commonly known as the Polish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and hairy, with a deep taproot. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, which develop into the familiar fluffy seed heads.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.
