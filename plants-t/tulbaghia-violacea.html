Tulbaghia violacea

Tulbaghia violacea, commonly known as the purple grass or African lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value.

Tulbaghia violacea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
