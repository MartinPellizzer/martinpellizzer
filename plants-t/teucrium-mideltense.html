Teucrium mideltense

Teucrium mideltense, commonly known as the Midelt germander, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
