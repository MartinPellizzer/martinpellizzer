Taeniophyllum longisetigerum

Taeniophyllum longisetigerum, commonly known as the hairy tailed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies from white to pink or purple.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
