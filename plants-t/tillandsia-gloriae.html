Tillandsia gloriae

Tillandsia gloriae, commonly known as the Gloria air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
