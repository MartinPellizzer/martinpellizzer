Taxus baccata pendula-densa

Taxus baccata pendula-densa, commonly known as the weeping yew, is a cultivar of the European yew tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is a slow-growing evergreen with dark green, needle-like leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique form and longevity make it a popular choice for landscape design.
