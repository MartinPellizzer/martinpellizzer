Tinnea physaloides

Tinnea physaloides, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, green fruits that resemble small balloons, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it both useful and potentially invasive in certain environments.

Tinnea physaloides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
