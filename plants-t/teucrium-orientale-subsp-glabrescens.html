Teucrium orientale subsp. glabrescens

Teucrium orientale subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as Eastern germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other subspecies of Teucrium orientale.
