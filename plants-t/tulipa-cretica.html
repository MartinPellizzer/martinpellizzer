Tulipa cretica

Tulipa cretica, commonly known as the Cretan tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Tulipa cretica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
