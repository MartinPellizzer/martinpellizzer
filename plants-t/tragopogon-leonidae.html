Tragopogon leonidae

Tragopogon leonidae, commonly known as the "Leonidae's goat's beard," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Tragopogon leonidae is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, arid conditions.
