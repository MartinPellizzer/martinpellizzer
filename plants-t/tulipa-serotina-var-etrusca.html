Tulipa serotina var. etrusca

Tulipa serotina var. etrusca, commonly known as the Etruscan tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive dark blotch at the base. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
