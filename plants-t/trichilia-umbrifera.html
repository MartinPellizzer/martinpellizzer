Trichilia umbrifera

Trichilia umbrifera, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds, which aid in wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and as a timber source.
