Trapa brevicarpa f. quadrata

Trapa brevicarpa f. quadrata, commonly known as the four-angled water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, quadrangular stems and small, floating leaves. The plant produces spiny, globe-shaped fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems and traditional agriculture.
