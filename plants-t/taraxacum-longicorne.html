Taraxacum longicorne

Taraxacum longicorne, commonly known as the long-horned dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, elongated stem. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.
