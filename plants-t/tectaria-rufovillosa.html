Tectaria rufovillosa

Tectaria rufovillosa, commonly known as the rusty-hairy tectaria, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its leathery, often hairy fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a distinctive rusty-colored pubescence.

Tectaria rufovillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
