Trisetum pratense var. villosum

Trisetum pratense var. villosum, commonly known as hairy field muirgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
