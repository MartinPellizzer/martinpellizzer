Tragacantha uraniolimnea

Tragacantha uraniolimnea, commonly known as the golden milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy textures, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive yellow corona surrounding pinkish-red petals, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting monarch butterfly populations and its potential medicinal properties.
