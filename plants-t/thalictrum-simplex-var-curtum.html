Thalictrum simplex var. curtum

Thalictrum simplex var. curtum, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, spire-like clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
