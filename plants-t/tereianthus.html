Tereianthus

Tereianthus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. Tereianthus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
