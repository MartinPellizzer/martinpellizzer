Timonius

Timonius, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or golden flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Timonius has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
