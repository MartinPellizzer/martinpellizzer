Tephrosia nubica subsp. nubica

Tephrosia nubica subsp.

nubica, commonly known as the East African tephrosia, is a shrub native to the highland regions of East Africa. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has pinnate leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
