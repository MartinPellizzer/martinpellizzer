Terminalia franchetii var. tomentosa

Terminalia franchetii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly Terminalia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round, yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
