Tovara

Tovara, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. Tovara is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also known for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences with its rapid growth.
