Terminalia festinata

Terminalia festinata, commonly known as the fast-growing terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its rapid growth and potential medicinal properties.
