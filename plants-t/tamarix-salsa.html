Tamarix salsa

Tamarix salsa, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and saline environments in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish-gray flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh landscapes.

Tamarix salsa is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil and its historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
