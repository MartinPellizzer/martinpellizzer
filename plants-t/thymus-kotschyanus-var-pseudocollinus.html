Thymus kotschyanus var. pseudocollinus

Thymus kotschyanus var. pseudocollinus, commonly known as the Persian thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features compact, low-growing foliage with narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
