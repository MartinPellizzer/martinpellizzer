Telmatophace gibba

Telmatophace gibba, commonly known as the hairy water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the muddy substrates of still water bodies.

Telmatophace gibba plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
