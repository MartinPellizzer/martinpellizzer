Teucrium afrum subsp. rhiphaeum

Teucrium afrum subsp.

rhiphaeum, commonly known as African germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in dense terminal clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
