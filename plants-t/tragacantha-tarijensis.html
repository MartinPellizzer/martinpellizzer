Tragacantha tarijensis

Tragacantha tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarija tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in various industrial and medicinal applications.

It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in shrublands and rocky soils.
