Trifolium hybridum var. anatolicum

Trifolium hybridum var. anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open fields.

Anatolic clover is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage crop for livestock.
