Teucrium divaricatum subsp. canescens

Teucrium divaricatum subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the gray teucrium, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
