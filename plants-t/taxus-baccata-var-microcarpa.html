Taxus baccata var. microcarpa

Taxus baccata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the miniature yew, is a compact variant of the European yew.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, evergreen foliage that forms a spherical shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
