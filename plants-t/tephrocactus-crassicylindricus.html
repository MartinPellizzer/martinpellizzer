Tephrocactus crassicylindricus

Tephrocactus crassicylindricus, commonly known as the thick-cylindrical cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green and covered in white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
