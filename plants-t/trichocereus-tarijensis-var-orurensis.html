Trichocereus tarijensis var. orurensis

Trichocereus tarijensis var. orurensis, commonly known as the "Cristalina" or "Luz de Luna," is a cacti species native to the Andes of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its columnar stems and distinctive white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces a dense covering of white spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
