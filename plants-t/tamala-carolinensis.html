Tamala carolinensis

Tamala carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina tamala, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Tamala carolinensis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
