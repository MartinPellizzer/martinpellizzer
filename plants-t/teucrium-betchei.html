Teucrium betchei

Teucrium betchei, commonly known as Betche's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium betchei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
