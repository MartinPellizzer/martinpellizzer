Tradescantia debilis

Tradescantia debilis, commonly known as spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread readily through runners.
