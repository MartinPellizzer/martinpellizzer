Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp. andersonii

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp.

andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Turbinicarpus, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa. It features a globular to cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are relatively small but numerous. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
