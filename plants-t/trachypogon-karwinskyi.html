Trachypogon karwinskyi

Trachypogon karwinskyi, commonly known as the Karwinsky's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often concealed within the leaf sheaths, making it less conspicuous.

Trachypogon karwinskyi is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
