Tafalla scolopendra

Tafalla scolopendra, commonly known as the Mexican centipede, is not a plant but an arthropod belonging to the class Chilopoda.

It is native to Central and South America, where it inhabits moist, forested environments. The creature is characterized by its elongated body, segmented exoskeleton, and numerous pairs of jointed legs. It plays a role in soil aeration and nutrient cycling within its ecosystem.

Due to its predatory nature, it helps control populations of smaller invertebrates.
