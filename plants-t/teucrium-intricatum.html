Teucrium intricatum

Teucrium intricatum, commonly known as the intricate germander, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Teucrium intricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
