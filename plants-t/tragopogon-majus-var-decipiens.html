Tragopogon majus var. decipiens

Tragopogon majus var. decipiens, commonly known as the "honey flower" or "goat's beard," is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow composite flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces a long, slender seed stem with a tuft of white bristles at the tip, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and easily identifiable species within the Asteraceae family.
