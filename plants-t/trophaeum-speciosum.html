Trophaeum speciosum

Trophaeum speciosum, commonly known as the showy trophaeum, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of vibrant purple or white blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Trophaeum speciosum is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact and resilience in cooler climates.
