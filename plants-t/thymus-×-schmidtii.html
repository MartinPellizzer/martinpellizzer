Thymus × schmidtii

Thymus × schmidtii, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus × schmidtii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
