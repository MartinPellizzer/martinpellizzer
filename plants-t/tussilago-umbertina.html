Tussilago umbertina

Tussilago umbertina, commonly known as the mountain mullein, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of soft, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used for respiratory ailments.
