Toona ciliata var. pubescens

Toona ciliata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by ellipsoid capsules containing numerous seeds. The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
