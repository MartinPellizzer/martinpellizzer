Taraxacum sonckii

Taraxacum sonckii, commonly known as the Sonck's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a hairy stem and basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.
