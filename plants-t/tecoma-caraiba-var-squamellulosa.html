Tecoma caraiba var. squamellulosa

Tecoma caraiba var. squamellulosa, commonly known as the Cuban trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
