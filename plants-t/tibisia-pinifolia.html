Tibisia pinifolia

Tibisia pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf tibisia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, adding visual interest to its landscape. Tibisia pinifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert insects and small animals.
