Taraxacum canum

Taraxacum canum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
