Triticum spelta var. turanoalefeldii

Triticum spelta var. turanoalefeldii, commonly known as the Transylvanian spelt, is a rare and ancient variant of spelt wheat.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and awned glumes, typical of the Triticum genus. This variety is native to Central Europe and is often found in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a tough, fibrous husk and small, hard grains that are well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued for its high nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional and organic farming practices.
