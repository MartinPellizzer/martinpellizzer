Teucrium pumilum subsp. verticillatum

Teucrium pumilum subsp.

verticillatum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
