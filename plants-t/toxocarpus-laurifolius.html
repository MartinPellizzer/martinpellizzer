Toxocarpus laurifolius

Toxocarpus laurifolius, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy aril.

Toxocarpus laurifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
