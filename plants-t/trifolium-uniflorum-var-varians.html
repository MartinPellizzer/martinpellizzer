Trifolium uniflorum var. varians

Trifolium uniflorum var. varians, commonly known as the variable clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its genetic diversity, which contributes to its adaptability and resilience in various environments.
