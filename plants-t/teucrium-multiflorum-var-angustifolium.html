Teucrium multiflorum var. angustifolium

Teucrium multiflorum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal practices.
