Teramnus parviflorus

Teramnus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered teramnus, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for shaded garden settings.
