Taraxacum officinale subsp. handelii

Taraxacum officinale subsp.

handelii, commonly known as the Tibetan dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau. It is characterized by its hairy stems, spoon-shaped leaves, and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Tibetan medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological adaptation to extreme environments.
