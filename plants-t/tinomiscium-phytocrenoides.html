Tinomiscium phytocrenoides

Tinomiscium phytocrenoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the wetlands of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow inflorescences, which consist of both ray and disk flowers, creating a visually appealing display. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and marshes. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ecological importance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
