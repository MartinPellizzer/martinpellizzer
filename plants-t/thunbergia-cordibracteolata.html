Thunbergia cordibracteolata

Thunbergia cordibracteolata, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it typically grows as a climbing or trailing vine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
