Thyrsacanthus dissitiflorus

Thyrsacanthus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny structures that form a spherical or hemispherical shape, which helps protect it from herbivores and conserve moisture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

The species is adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
