Treisteria assamica

Treisteria assamica, commonly known as the Assam bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, while its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

Treisteria assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
