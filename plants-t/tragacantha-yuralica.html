Tragacantha yuralica

Tragacantha yuralica, commonly known as the Yuralic tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its roots are capable of producing a gel-like substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
