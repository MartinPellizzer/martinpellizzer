Trigonocaryum involucratum

Trigonocaryum involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Trigonocaryum involucratum is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in some areas.
