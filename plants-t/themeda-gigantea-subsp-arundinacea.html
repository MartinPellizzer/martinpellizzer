Themeda gigantea subsp. arundinacea

Themeda gigantea subsp.

arundinacea, commonly known as giant grass or bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping and has ecological importance as a food source for native fauna.
