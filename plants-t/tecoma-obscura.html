Tecoma obscura

Tecoma obscura, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough, textured surface.

Tecoma obscura is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
