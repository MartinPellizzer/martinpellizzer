Tragacantha longior

Tragacantha longior, commonly known as the greater tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its roots are rich in a viscous gum, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

The botanical features of Tragacantha longior make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
