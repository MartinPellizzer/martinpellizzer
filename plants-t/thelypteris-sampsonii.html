Thelypteris sampsonii

Thelypteris sampsonii, commonly known as the Sampson's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with arching fronds that are divided into lance-shaped segments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures on the underside.

Thelypteris sampsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
