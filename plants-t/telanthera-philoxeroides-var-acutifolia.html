Telanthera philoxeroides var. acutifolia

Telanthera philoxeroides var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf telanthera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves with acute tips. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
