Trigonella platycarpos var. potaninii

Trigonella platycarpos var. potaninii, commonly known as the Armenian trigonella, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and is adapted to rocky and arid environments.

This variety is noted for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
