Thlaspi cochleariforme subsp. griffithianum

Thlaspi cochleariforme subsp.

griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's cabbagewort, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, glaucous leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme.

Native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can tolerate harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the cabbagewort genus, with ecological significance in its native habitats.
