Tulipa luanica

Tulipa luanica, commonly known as the Luanica tulip, is a rare and distinctive species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Tulipa luanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
