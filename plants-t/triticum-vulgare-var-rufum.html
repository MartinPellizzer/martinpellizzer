Triticum vulgare var. rufum

Triticum vulgare var. rufum, commonly known as red wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its reddish grain color.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender stems and narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-lobed florets.

The grain is harvested when the kernels are dry and hard, making it suitable for flour production and animal feed.
