Tripogon humbertianus

Tripogon humbertianus, commonly known as the Humbert's grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
