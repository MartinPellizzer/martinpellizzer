Trifolium lupinaster var. albiflorum

Trifolium lupinaster var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its delicate appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
