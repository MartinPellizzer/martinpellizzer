Tragacantha benthamiana

Tragacantha benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological studies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for research on plant adaptation and resilience.
