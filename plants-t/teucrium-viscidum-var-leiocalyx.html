Teucrium viscidum var. leiocalyx

Teucrium viscidum var. leiocalyx, commonly known as sticky germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves and flowers contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
