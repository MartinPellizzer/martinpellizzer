Tilia pertomentosa

Tilia pertomentosa, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Tilia pertomentosa is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
