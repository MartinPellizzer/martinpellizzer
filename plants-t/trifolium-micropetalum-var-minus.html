Trifolium micropetalum var. minus

Trifolium micropetalum var. minus, commonly known as the small-petaled clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
