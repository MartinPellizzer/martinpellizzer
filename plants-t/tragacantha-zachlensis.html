Tragacantha zachlensis

Tragacantha zachlensis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.

The plant's resin has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
