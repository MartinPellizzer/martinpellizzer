Tragacantha flava

Tragacantha flava, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellowish-brown resinous sap. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

The resin, known as tragacanth, is harvested from the plant and has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent.
