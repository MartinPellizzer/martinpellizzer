Thibaudia bicolor

Thibaudia bicolor, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental value.

Thibaudia bicolor is a popular choice for gardens due to its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.
