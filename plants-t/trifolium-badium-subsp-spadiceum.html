Trifolium badium subsp. spadiceum

Trifolium badium subsp.

spadiceum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
