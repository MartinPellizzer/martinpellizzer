Themeda gigantea var. avenacea

Themeda gigantea var. avenacea, commonly known as the tall grass bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, where it forms dense tussocks. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for native wildlife.

The inflorescence consists of a terminal panicle, which produces small, spikelet-like flowers.
