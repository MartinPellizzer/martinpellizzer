Tecoma catinga

Tecoma catinga, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its woody stems are often covered with a rough, bark-like texture.

Tecoma catinga thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and coastal areas.
