Trudelia cristata

Trudelia cristata, commonly known as the crown fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that resemble a crown.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's botanical structure includes a central rhizome from which the fronds emerge, and its leaves are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions. Trudelia cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
