Tradescantia subaspera

Tradescantia subaspera, commonly known as the velvetleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, attractive plant.
