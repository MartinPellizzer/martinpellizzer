Taurantha ophioglossa

Taurantha ophioglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf taurantha, is a rare and delicate fern-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a unique morphology with a short, creeping rhizome and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color, which develop into spore-bearing structures. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important subject for studies on plant evolution and ecological adaptation.
