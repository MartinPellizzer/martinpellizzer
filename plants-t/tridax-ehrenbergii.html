Tridax ehrenbergii

Tridax ehrenbergii, commonly known as the smallleaf tridax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has alternate, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators.

Tridax ehrenbergii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
