Tibouchina macrochiton var. uniflora

Tibouchina macrochiton var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower tibouchina, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
