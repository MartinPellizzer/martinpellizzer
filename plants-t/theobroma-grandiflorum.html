Theobroma grandiflorum

Theobroma grandiflorum, commonly known as the cocoa tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Americas.

It produces large, showy white flowers that are essential for its fruit development. The plant is cultivated for its pods, which contain the seeds used to make chocolate. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody stem that can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Theobroma grandiflorum thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
