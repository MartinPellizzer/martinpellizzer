Triticum vulgare var. albidum

Triticum vulgare var. albidum, commonly known as white wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its pale, less pigmented grains.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions and belongs to the Poaceae family. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stalks and narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, white or pale-colored florets.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its grain, which is used in various food products and animal feed.
