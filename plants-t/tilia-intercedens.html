Tilia intercedens

Tilia intercedens, commonly known as the intermediate linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, oval fruits that contain smooth, flat seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Tilia intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
