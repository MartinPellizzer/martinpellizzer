Trichotosia kenejiana

Trichotosia kenejiana, commonly known as the Kenejian grass, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Trichotosia kenejiana plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
