Tribulus bimucronatus var. bimucronatus

Tribulus bimucronatus var. bimucronatus, commonly known as the two-edged spurred cucumber, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny calyces and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branching.

The species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
