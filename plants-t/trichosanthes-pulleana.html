Trichosanthes pulleana

Trichosanthes pulleana, commonly known as the Chinese trumpet vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features large, lobed leaves and produces distinctive yellow-orange flowers with a trumpet-shaped corolla. The fruit is a long, cylindrical capsule that splits open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese medicine.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
