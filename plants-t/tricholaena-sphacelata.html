Tricholaena sphacelata

Tricholaena sphacelata, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a short trunk covered with a fibrous sheath, and its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
