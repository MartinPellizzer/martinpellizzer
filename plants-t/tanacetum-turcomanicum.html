Tanacetum turcomanicum

Tanacetum turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum turcomanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
