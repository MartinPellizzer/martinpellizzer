Temnocalyx obovata

Temnocalyx obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with obovate leaflets and produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its adaptation to warm, humid climates and its role in local ecosystems.
