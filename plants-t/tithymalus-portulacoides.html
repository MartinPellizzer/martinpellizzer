Tithymalus portulacoides

Tithymalus portulacoides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Tithymalus portulacoides is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in xeriscaping.
