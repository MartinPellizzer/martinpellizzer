Thymus albidus

Thymus albidus, commonly known as white thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
