Trifolium arvense var. strictior

Trifolium arvense var. strictior, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a pale green color. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and pastures.

This species is valued for its role in soil fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
