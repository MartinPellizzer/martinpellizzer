Tanacetum douglasii

Tanacetum douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features finely divided, silvery-green leaves. Its yellow flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets, resembling a dandelion.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
