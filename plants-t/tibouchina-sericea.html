Tibouchina sericea

Tibouchina sericea, commonly known as velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Tibouchina sericea is known for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early autumn.
