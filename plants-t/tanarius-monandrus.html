Tanarius monandrus

Tanarius monandrus, commonly known as the African orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that often display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its unique floral structure includes a single, prominent pistil and a reduced stamen, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Tanarius monandrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
