Takhtajaniantha capito

Takhtajaniantha capito, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
