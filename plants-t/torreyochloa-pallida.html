Torreyochloa pallida

Torreyochloa pallida, commonly known as the pale torrey grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Torreyochloa pallida is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
