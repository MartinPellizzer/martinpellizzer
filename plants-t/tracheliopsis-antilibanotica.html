Tracheliopsis antilibanotica

Tracheliopsis antilibanotica, commonly known as the Levantine rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Tracheliopsis antilibanotica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous areas.
