Thalictrum expansum

Thalictrum expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Thalictrum expansum typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
