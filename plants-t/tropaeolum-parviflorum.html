Tropaeolum parviflorum

Tropaeolum parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf jewelweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it spreads easily through its trailing habit.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
