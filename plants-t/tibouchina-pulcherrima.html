Tibouchina pulcherrima

Tibouchina pulcherrima, commonly known as the confetti bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Tibouchina pulcherrima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its ornate appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
