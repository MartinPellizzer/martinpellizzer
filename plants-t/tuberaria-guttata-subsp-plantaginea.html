Tuberaria guttata subsp. plantaginea

Tuberaria guttata subsp.

plantaginea, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
