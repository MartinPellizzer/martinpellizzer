Trigonotis cinereifolia

Trigonotis cinereifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, round berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Trigonotis cinereifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
