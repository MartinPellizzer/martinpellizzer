Trichocentrum pinelii

Trichocentrum pinelii, commonly known as the Pinel's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Trichocentrum pinelii is characterized by its delicate, clustered blooms and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It requires well-draining substrates and consistent moisture to flourish.
