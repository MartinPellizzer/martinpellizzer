Tithymalus biglandulosus

Tithymalus biglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
