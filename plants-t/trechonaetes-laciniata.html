Trechonaetes laciniata

Trechonaetes laciniata, commonly known as the fringed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often divided into fine, fringed segments. The plant traps small aquatic organisms using specialized bladder-like structures that create a vacuum to draw in prey. It thrives in nutrient-poor waters and is adapted to low-light conditions.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous behavior make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
