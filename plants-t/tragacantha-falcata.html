Tragacantha falcata

Tragacantha falcata, commonly known as the sickle milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
