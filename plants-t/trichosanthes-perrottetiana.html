Trichosanthes perrottetiana

Trichosanthes perrottetiana, commonly known as the Chinese lantern gourd, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces distinctive orange-red, balloon-like fruits that are covered in a thick, spiny husk. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
