Thalictrum simplex subsp. boreale

Thalictrum simplex subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of North America. It features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
