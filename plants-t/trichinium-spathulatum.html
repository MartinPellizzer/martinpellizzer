Trichinium spathulatum

Trichinium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Trichinium spathulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
