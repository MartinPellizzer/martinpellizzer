Tragacantha stipitata

Tragacantha stipitata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is also known for its ability to produce a resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and industry.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
