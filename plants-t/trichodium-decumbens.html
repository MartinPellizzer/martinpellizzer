Trichodium decumbens

Trichodium decumbens, commonly known as the creeping trichodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Trichodium decumbens is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
