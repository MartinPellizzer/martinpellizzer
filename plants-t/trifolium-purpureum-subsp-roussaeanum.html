Trifolium purpureum subsp. roussaeanum

Trifolium purpureum subsp.

roussaeanum, commonly known as the Roussaean clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, purple to reddish-pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
