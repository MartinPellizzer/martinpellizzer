Taraxacum platycarpum

Taraxacum platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruit dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flower head consists of a yellow disk surrounded by pale green to yellow ray florets.

The fruit is a distinctive, broadly elliptic achene with a pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.
