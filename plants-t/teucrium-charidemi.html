Teucrium charidemi

Teucrium charidemi, commonly known as the Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme.

Teucrium charidemi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
