Themeda arguens var. cochinchinensis

Themeda arguens var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese grass or Cochinchina grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
