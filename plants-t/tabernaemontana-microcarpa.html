Tabernaemontana microcarpa

Tabernaemontana microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
