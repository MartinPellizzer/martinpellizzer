Terpsichore bipinnata

Terpsichore bipinnata, commonly known as the fringed featherflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Terpsichore bipinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
