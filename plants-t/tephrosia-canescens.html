Tephrosia canescens

Tephrosia canescens, commonly known as the white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Tephrosia canescens is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
