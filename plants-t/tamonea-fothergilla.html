Tamonea fothergilla

Tamonea fothergilla, commonly known as witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has slender, zigzagging stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its botanical features include small, cup-shaped flowers and woody, branched capsules that contain the seeds.

Witch hazel is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
