Taeniophyllum pectiniferum

Taeniophyllum pectiniferum, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a unique texture. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
