Tanacetopsis krascheninnikovii

Tanacetopsis krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-gray, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in extreme conditions.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in flat-topped clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
