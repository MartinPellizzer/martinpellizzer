Thelypteris palustris var. squamigera

Thelypteris palustris var. squamigera, commonly known as the swamp fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. The plant features fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sori, which are clustered in rows.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and swampy areas.
