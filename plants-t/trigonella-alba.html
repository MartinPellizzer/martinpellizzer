Trigonella alba

Trigonella alba, commonly known as white fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
