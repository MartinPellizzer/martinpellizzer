Tetrapterygium stylophorum

Tetrapterygium stylophorum, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs and have a characteristic stinging sensation when touched.

Tetrapterygium stylophorum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
