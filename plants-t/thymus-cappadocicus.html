Thymus cappadocicus

Thymus cappadocicus, commonly known as Cappadocian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and herbal purposes.
