Thymus serpyllum subsp. chamaedrys

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

chamaedrys, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically grey-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces pink or purple flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
