Taraxacum danubiense

Taraxacum danubiense, commonly known as the Danube dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
