Thymus praecox f. cinerosus

Thymus praecox f. cinerosus, commonly known as the dark thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central and southern Europe.

It features small, dark green, aromatic leaves that are slightly hairy and have a strong, pungent fragrance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
