Tragia involucrata var. angustifolia

Tragia involucrata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cupid, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
