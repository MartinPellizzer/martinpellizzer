Taraxacum panhellenicum

Taraxacum panhellenicum, commonly known as the Greek dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, leafless stem that terminates in a bright yellow composite flower head. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head composed of numerous lightweight seeds with white pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
