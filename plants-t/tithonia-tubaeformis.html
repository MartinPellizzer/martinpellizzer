Tithonia tubaeformis

Tithonia tubaeformis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tithonia tubaeformis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the vicinity.
