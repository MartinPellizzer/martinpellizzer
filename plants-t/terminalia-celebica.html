Terminalia celebica

Terminalia celebica, commonly known as Java apple or tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Terminalia celebica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
