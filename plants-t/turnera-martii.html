Turnera martii

Turnera martii, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Turnera martii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
