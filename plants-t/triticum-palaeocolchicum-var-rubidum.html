Triticum palaeocolchicum var. rubidum

Triticum palaeocolchicum var. rubidum, commonly known as the red wild wheat, is a rare and ancient grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, reddish spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive reddish hue, which may have been historically used for various purposes.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable species for botanical and agricultural research.
