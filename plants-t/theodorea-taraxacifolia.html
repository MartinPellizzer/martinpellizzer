Theodorea taraxacifolia

Theodorea taraxacifolia, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Theodorea taraxacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
