Tragacantha hamosa

Tragacantha hamosa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
