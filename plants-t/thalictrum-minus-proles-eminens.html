Thalictrum minus proles eminens

Thalictrum minus proles eminens, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Thalictrum minus proles eminens is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
