Tibouchina riparia

Tibouchina riparia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or river violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near rivers and streams. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Tibouchina riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
