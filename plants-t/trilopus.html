Trilopus

Trilopus, commonly known as the three-lobed plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

Each leaflet is oval-shaped and has a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with low moisture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or white.

Trilopus is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
