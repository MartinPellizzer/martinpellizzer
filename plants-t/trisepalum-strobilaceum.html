Trisepalum strobilaceum

Trisepalum strobilaceum, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive three-lobed calyx that remains attached to the fruit.

Trisepalum strobilaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
