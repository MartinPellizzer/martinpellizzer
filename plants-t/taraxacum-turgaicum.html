Taraxacum turgaicum

Taraxacum turgaicum, commonly known as the Turgai dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, elongated flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous white, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid regions.
