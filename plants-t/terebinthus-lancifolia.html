Terebinthus lancifolia

Terebinthus lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved terebinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Terebinthus lancifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
