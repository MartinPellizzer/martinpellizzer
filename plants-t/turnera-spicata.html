Turnera spicata

Turnera spicata, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Turnera spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
