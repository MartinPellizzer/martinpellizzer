Thymbra spicata var. intricata

Thymbra spicata var. intricata, commonly known as the fragrant thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong, spicy fragrance.

The variety intricata is distinguished by its more intricate branching pattern compared to other thyme varieties.
