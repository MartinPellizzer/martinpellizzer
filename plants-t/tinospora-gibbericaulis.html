Tinospora gibbericaulis

Tinospora gibbericaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It is often found in tropical forests and is adapted to grow in shaded environments.

Tinospora gibbericaulis is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
