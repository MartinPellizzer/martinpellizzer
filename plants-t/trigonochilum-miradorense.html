Trigonochilum miradorense

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three sepals and three petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often small and delicate, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Trigonochilum miradorense is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its ecological niche.
