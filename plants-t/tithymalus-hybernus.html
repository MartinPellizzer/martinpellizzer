Tithymalus hybernus

Tithymalus hybernus, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Tithymalus hybernus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
