Thymus × citriodorus aureus

Thymus × citriodorus aureus, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, narrow leaves with a lemony aroma, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
