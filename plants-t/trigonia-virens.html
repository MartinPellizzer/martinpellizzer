Trigonia virens

Trigonia virens, commonly known as the green-flowered triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals that resemble a trigger mechanism. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters along the stems. Trigonia virens thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions and heathlands.

It is an attractive plant for gardeners due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
