Teucrium chilense

Teucrium chilense, commonly known as Chilean germander, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
