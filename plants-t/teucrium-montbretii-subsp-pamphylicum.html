Teucrium montbretii subsp. pamphylicum

Teucrium montbretii subsp.

pamphylicum, commonly known as the Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in spike-like clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
