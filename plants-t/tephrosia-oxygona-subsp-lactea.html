Tephrosia oxygona subsp. lactea

Tephrosia oxygona subsp.

lactea, commonly known as white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a soft, velvety texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
