Therofon elatum

Therofon elatum, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Therofon elatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
