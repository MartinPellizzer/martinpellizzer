Taraxacum atriceps

Taraxacum atriceps, commonly known as the black-eyed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that transition to a fluffy white seed head. The plant has a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and dark green. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is notable for its distinctive black center in the flower, which gives it its common name.
