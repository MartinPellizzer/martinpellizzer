Taraxacum macroceras

Taraxacum macroceras, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are typically lobed and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length.

The plant is known for its resilience and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
