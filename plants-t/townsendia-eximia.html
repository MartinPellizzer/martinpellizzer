Townsendia eximia

Townsendia eximia, commonly known as the showy Townsend's flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in the spring and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also noted for its small, linear leaves and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
