Taraxacum pubescens

Taraxacum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

Taraxacum pubescens is a perennial herb that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
