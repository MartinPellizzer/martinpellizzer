Tephrosia bifacialis

Tephrosia bifacialis, commonly known as the two-faced tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with two distinct leaflets on each pair, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Tephrosia bifacialis is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
