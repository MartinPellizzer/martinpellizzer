Tragacantha pubiflora

Tragacantha pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of pod-like fruits containing seeds. Tragacantha pubiflora is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum has also contributed to its botanical significance.
