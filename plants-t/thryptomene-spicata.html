Thryptomene spicata

Thryptomene spicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are typically borne on the stems. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Thryptomene spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
