Tecoma ipe var. integrifolia

Tecoma ipe var. integrifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant has a fibrous root system and can grow up to 6 meters in height under optimal conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
