Tabebuia lanceolata

Tabebuia lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange. The plant produces fruit that contains seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
