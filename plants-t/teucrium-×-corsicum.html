Teucrium × corsicum

Teucrium × corsicum, commonly known as Corsican germander, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Teucrium × corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
