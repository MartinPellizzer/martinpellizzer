Tragopogon transsilvanicus

Tragopogon transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly found in Romania and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and its bright yellow flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
