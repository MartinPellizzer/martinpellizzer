Taraxacum pseudobrevirostre

Taraxacum pseudobrevirostre, commonly known as the narrow-beaked dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
