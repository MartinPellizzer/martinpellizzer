Thunbergia affinis var. pulvinata

Thunbergia affinis var. pulvinata, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Thunbergia affinis var. pulvinata is a vigorous climber that can be trained on trellises or allowed to trail from containers.
