Tinus bracteosa

Tinus bracteosa, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
