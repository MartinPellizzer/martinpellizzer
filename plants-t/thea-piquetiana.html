Thea piquetiana

Thea piquetiana, commonly known as the Piquet's tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Thea piquetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
