Tekel sessiliflora

Tekel sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered tekel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warm seasons. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
