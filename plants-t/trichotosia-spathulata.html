Trichotosia spathulata

Trichotosia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed trichotosia, is a herbaceous perennial native to certain regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
