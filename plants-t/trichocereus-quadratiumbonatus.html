Trichocereus quadratiumbonatus

Trichocereus quadratiumbonatus, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique symbiotic relationship with a specific strain of the fungus *Monotropa*, which provides it with essential nutrients. Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate plant in cultivation.
