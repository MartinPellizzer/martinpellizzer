Tovomita elliptica

Tovomita elliptica, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface, and its flowers are often arranged in inflorescences at the ends of the branches.

Tovomita elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
