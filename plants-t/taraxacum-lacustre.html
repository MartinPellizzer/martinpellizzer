Taraxacum lacustre

Taraxacum lacustre, commonly known as the lake dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along lake shores and wet meadows. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets, which mature into fluffy white seeds.
