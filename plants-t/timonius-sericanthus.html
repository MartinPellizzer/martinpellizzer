Timonius sericanthus

Timonius sericanthus, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure that supports its growth in nutrient-poor conditions.

Timonius sericanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
