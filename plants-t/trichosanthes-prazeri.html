Trichosanthes prazeri

Trichosanthes prazeri, commonly known as the Pazer's snake gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its vine-like growth habit and distinctive, often spiny stems.

The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are typically green when mature, with a rough, warty surface. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a lobed appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and are typically yellow with a white or greenish throat.

This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for both its edible fruits and ornamental appeal.
