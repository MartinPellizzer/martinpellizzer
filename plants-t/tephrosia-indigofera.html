Tephrosia indigofera

Tephrosia indigofera, commonly known as Indian blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its antifungal and antibacterial effects.

Its seeds are also used as a source of protein in some local cuisines.
