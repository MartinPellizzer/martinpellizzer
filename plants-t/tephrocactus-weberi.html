Tephrocactus weberi

Tephrocactus weberi, commonly known as the Weber's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Tephrocactus weberi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
