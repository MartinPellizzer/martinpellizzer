Tinospora dissitiflora

Tinospora dissitiflora, commonly known as the hairy tinospora, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Tinospora dissitiflora is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
