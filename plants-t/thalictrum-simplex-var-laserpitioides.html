Thalictrum simplex var. laserpitioides

Thalictrum simplex var. laserpitioides, commonly known as the silver foxtail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided segments, and the flowers are borne on slender, branched peduncles.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
