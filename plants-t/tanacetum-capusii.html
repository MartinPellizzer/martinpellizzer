Tanacetum capusii

Tanacetum capusii, commonly known as the Carpathian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Tanacetum capusii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
