Thlaspi sylvestre subsp. ambiguum

Thlaspi sylvestre subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the woodland cabbages, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has basal leaves that are often rosette-shaped and are covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its preference for cool, temperate climates.
