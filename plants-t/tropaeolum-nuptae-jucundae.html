Tropaeolum nuptae-jucundae

Tropaeolum nuptae-jucundae, commonly known as the happy wedding vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, edible tubers similar to those of the common garden nasturtium. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a peppery taste, making them suitable for culinary use.

Tropaeolum nuptae-jucundae thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
