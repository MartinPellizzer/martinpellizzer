Thunbergia fragrans var. parviflora

Thunbergia fragrans var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fragrant thunbergia, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
