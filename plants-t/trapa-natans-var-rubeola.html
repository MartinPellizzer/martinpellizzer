Trapa natans var. rubeola

Trapa natans var. rubeola, commonly known as the red water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and its distinctive spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

The red water chestnut is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
