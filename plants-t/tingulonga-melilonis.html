Tingulonga melilonis

Tingulonga melilonis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Tingulonga melilonis make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical collections.
