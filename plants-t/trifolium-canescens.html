Trifolium canescens

Trifolium canescens, commonly known as the hoary clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in a fine, whitish down, giving it a grayish appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Trifolium canescens is a perennial species that plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
