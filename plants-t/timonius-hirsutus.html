Timonius hirsutus

Timonius hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy timonius, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant features finely divided, hirsute leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its small white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Timonius hirsutus is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
