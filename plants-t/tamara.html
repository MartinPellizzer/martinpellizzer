Tamara

Tamara, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that eventually bear edible dates. Tamara is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a significant crop in regions with limited water resources.

Its fruit is not only a food source but also has cultural and economic importance in many parts of South Asia.
