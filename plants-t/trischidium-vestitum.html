Trischidium vestitum

Trischidium vestitum, commonly known as the velvet trichoderm, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Trischidium vestitum is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
