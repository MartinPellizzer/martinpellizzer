Tsuga canadensis var. hussii

Tsuga canadensis var. hussii, commonly known as the Appalachian hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to the eastern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its flat, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage provides important habitat for various wildlife species.
