Tanacetum corymbosum subsp. achilleae

Tanacetum corymbosum subsp.

achilleae, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow central disk.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Its blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
