Trillium cernuum var. declinatum

Trillium cernuum var. declinatum, commonly known as the nodding white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its three broad, waxy leaves and a single, nodding white flower that blooms in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, organic soil. Its flowers have six petals and three sepals, which are usually white or pale green.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
