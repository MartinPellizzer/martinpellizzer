Teucrium aragonense var. integrifolium

Teucrium aragonense var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Aragon germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean scrublands.
