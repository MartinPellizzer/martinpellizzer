Teucrium trifoliatum

Teucrium trifoliatum, commonly known as three-leaved germander, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Teucrium trifoliatum is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
