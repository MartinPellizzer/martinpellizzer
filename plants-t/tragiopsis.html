Tragiopsis

Tragiopsis, commonly known as the glassworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their succulent, often fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. They are typically found in saline or coastal environments, thriving in areas with high salt content in the soil. Tragiopsis species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine.
