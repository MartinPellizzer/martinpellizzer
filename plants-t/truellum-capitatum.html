Truellum capitatum

Truellum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with numerous small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Truellum capitatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
