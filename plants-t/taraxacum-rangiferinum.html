Taraxacum rangiferinum

Taraxacum rangiferinum, commonly known as the reindeer dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that bears a single, inconspicuous yellow flower head. The plant is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia, where it thrives in cold, dry environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its propagation across open landscapes.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for herbivores like reindeer.
