Tynanthus lindmannii

Tynanthus lindmannii, commonly known as the Lindmann's stinkhorn, is a unique fungal plant that belongs to the Phallaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, foul-smelling cap that attracts flies for spore dispersal. The fungus thrives in decaying organic matter, often found in forest floors or near dead wood. Its structure includes a tall, slender stem and a bulbous, odorous cap that resembles a stinkhorn.

Tynanthus lindmannii plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and decomposition in its habitat.
