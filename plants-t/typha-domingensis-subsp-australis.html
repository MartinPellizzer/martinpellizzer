Typha domingensis subsp. australis

Typha domingensis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Australia and New Zealand. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering spikes that produce fluffy seeds, and its broad, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats, such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable wetland plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
