Tanacetum marionii

Tanacetum marionii, commonly known as the marsh tansy, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in damp soils along stream banks and in marshy habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
