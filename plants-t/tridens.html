Tridens

Tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-toothed bracts that surround the flowers, giving it its common name. Tridens species are typically herbaceous, with simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. They are often found in sandy or disturbed soils in temperate regions.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
