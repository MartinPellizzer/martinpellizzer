Terminalia aridicola subsp. chillagoensis

Terminalia aridicola subsp.

chillagoensis, commonly known as the Chillagoensis terminalia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions. Its fruit is a small, hard capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
