Thymus × alcarazii

Thymus × alcarazii, commonly known as Alcaraz thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus × alcarazii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal herb gardens.
