Trifolium fragiferum var. modestum

Trifolium fragiferum var. modestum, commonly known as the modest clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
