Triticum durum var. taganrocense

Triticum durum var. taganrocense, commonly known as durum wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its high gluten content and hard grain.

It is primarily cultivated for the production of semolina, which is used in pasta and couscous. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its botanical features include tall, slender stalks and spikelets with tough, straw-colored grains.

Triticum durum var. taganrocense is an important crop in agricultural systems due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
