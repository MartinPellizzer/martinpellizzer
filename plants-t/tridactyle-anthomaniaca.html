Tridactyle anthomaniaca

Tridactyle anthomaniaca, commonly known as the "Maniacal Three-Fingered Plant," is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique tri-fingered leaves, which are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a complex, spurred petal structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to climate change, it is classified as an endangered species.
