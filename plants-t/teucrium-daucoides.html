Teucrium daucoides

Teucrium daucoides, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
