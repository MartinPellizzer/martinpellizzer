Tragacantha gayana

Tragacantha gayana, commonly known as the Indian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a viscous gum from its stems, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food thickener. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Tragacantha gayana is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
