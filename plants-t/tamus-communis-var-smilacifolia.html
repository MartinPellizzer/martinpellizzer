Tamus communis var. smilacifolia

Tamus communis var. smilacifolia, commonly known as the smilacifolious arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
