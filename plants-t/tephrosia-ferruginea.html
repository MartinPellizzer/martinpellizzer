Tephrosia ferruginea

Tephrosia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Tephrosia ferruginea is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
