Tetraulacium veroniciforme

Tetraulacium veroniciforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Tetraulacium veroniciforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
