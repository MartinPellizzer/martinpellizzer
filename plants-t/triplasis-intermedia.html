Triplasis intermedia

Triplasis intermedia, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. The plant has small, green, opposite leaves and produces yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
