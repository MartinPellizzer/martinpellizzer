Thymus longicaulis subsp. moesiacus

Thymus longicaulis subsp.

moesiacus, commonly known as the Moesia thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Anatolia. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, make it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience and is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
