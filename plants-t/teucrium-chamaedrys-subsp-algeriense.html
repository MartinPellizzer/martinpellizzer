Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. algeriense

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

algeriense, commonly known as Algerian wall germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
