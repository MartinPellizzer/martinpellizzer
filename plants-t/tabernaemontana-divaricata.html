Tabernaemontana divaricata

Tabernaemontana divaricata, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, vanilla-like scent. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and is typically evergreen. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
