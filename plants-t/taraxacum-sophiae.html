Taraxacum sophiae

Taraxacum sophiae, commonly known as the Sofia dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas. The plant has a long flowering period, typically from spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
