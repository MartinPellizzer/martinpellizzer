Thryptomene thymifolia

Thryptomene thymifolia, commonly known as the lemon scented thryptomene, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a citrus-like scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Thryptomene thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
