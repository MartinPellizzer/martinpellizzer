Thrincia hispida subsp. tuberosa

Thrincia hispida subsp.

tuberosa, commonly known as the hairy thrincia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
