Terminalia saja

Terminalia saja, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red drupes that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Terminalia saja is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
