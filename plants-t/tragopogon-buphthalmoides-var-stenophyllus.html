Tragopogon buphthalmoides var. stenophyllus

Tragopogon buphthalmoides var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, with a rosette growth habit. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear yellow flowers with purple-tinged centers. Its inflorescence consists of composite flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and along stream banks.
