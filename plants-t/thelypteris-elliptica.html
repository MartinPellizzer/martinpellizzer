Thelypteris elliptica

Thelypteris elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Thelypteris elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush, fern-like foliage.
