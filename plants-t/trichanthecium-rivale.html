Trichanthecium rivale

Trichanthecium rivale, commonly known as the riverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Native to wetland and riparian habitats, it thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and marshes. The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its life cycle is closely tied to seasonal flooding, making it a key indicator species for wetland health.
