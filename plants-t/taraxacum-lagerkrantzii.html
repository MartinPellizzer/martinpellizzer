Taraxacum lagerkrantzii

Taraxacum lagerkrantzii, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to northern Europe, particularly Sweden and parts of Norway, and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. Its flowers produce a ball of white seeds with pappus, which are dispersed by wind.

Taraxacum lagerkrantzii is often found in meadows, forest edges, and disturbed habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
