Teucrium pinnatifidum

Teucrium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a slightly aromatic scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches.

This plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
