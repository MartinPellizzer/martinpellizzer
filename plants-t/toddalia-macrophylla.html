Toddalia macrophylla

Toddalia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement, showy inflorescences, and a preference for well-drained soils in tropical climates.
