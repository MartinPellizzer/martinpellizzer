Tirucalia juttae

Tirucalia juttae, commonly known as the Jutta's tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
