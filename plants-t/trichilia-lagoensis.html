Trichilia lagoensis

Trichilia lagoensis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable, fragrant wood used in traditional crafts and medicine.
