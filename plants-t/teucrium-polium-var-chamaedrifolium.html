Teucrium polium var. chamaedrifolium

Teucrium polium var. chamaedrifolium, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
