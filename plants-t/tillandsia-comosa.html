Tillandsia comosa

Tillandsia comosa, commonly known as the silver vernal, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Tillandsia comosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
