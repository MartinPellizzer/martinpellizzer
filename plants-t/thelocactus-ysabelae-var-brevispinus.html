Thelocactus ysabelae var. brevispinus

Thelocactus ysabelae var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined ysabel cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a common adaptation in desert plants to minimize water evaporation.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
