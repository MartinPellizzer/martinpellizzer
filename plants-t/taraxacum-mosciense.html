Taraxacum mosciense

Taraxacum mosciense, commonly known as the Mosciense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, feathery seeds. Native to Central Asia, this plant is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
