Tauschia roseana

Tauschia roseana, commonly known as the pink tauschia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tauschia roseana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
