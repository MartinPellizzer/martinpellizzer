Tanacetum annuum

Tanacetum annuum, commonly known as tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and fern-like leaves, which are aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

However, it is toxic to livestock and should be handled with care.
