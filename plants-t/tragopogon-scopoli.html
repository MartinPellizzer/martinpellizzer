Tragopogon scopoli

Tragopogon scopoli, commonly known as Scopoli's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a showy, yellow flower head that resembles a dandelion. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces a unique, hollow stem and has a distinctive leaf structure with a central vein.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
