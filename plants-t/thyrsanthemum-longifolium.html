Thyrsanthemum longifolium

Thyrsanthemum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved thyrse, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
