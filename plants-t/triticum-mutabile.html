Triticum mutabile

Triticum mutabile, commonly known as the variable wheat, is a rare and enigmatic species of wheat that exhibits significant morphological variation.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes and spike structures. The plant's genetic diversity is believed to be a result of its isolated habitat and unique ecological conditions. It has been studied for its potential in agricultural research due to its adaptability and unusual traits.

However, its cultivation remains limited due to challenges in stabilizing its variable characteristics.
