Taraxacum sublacerum

Taraxacum sublacerum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated stem that bears a yellow flower head. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus-bearing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
