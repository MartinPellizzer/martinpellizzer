Tontelea fluminensis

Tontelea fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tontelea fluminensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
