Toxicodendron succedaneum var. kiangsiense

Toxicodendron succedaneum var. kiangsiense, commonly known as the Kiangsi Poison Ivy, is a variant of the poison ivy plant native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically arranged in a cluster of three leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by reddish-brown, spiny fruits. It contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation and allergic reactions in humans.

This variant is part of the Anacrophylleae tribe and is closely related to other Toxicodendron species.
