Tephrosia simplicifolia

Tephrosia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Tephrosia simplicifolia is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
