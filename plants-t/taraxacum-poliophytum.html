Taraxacum poliophytum

Taraxacum poliophytum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
