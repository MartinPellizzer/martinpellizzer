Triodia langloisii

Triodia langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clumps of wiry, needle-like leaves that form a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Triodia langloisii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
