Tragacantha hostilis

Tragacantha hostilis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing hard, seed-containing capsules. Tragacantha hostilis is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its latex sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
