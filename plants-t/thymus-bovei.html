Thymus bovei

Thymus bovei, commonly known as the Bove's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus bovei is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
