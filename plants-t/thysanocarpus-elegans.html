Thysanocarpus elegans

Thysanocarpus elegans, commonly known as the silky tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, silky-hairy leaves and striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces unique, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
