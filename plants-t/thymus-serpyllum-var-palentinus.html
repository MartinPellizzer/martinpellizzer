Thymus serpyllum var. palentinus

Thymus serpyllum var. palentinus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a grayish-green color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
