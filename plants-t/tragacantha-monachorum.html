Tragacantha monachorum

Tragacantha monachorum, commonly known as the monk's tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive, woody stem covered in a thick layer of resin. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The resin, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in medicine and as a binding agent in various formulations.
