Teucrium turcicum

Teucrium turcicum, commonly known as Turkish germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium turcicum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
