Tephrosia petrosa

Tephrosia petrosa, commonly known as the rock tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with slender, pointed leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Tephrosia petrosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
