Tridax palmeri var. indivisa

Tridax palmeri var. indivisa, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It is native to the Americas but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Tridax palmeri var. indivisa is often considered a weed but is also valued for its medicinal properties.
