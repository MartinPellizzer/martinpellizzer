Thereianthus lapeyrouseoides

Thereianthus lapeyrouseoides, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its name derives from the French botanist Jean Lapeyrouse, who contributed to its classification.
