Tragacantha andina

Tragacantha andina, commonly known as Andean tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
