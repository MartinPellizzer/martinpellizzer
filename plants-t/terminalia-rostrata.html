Terminalia rostrata

Terminalia rostrata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are an important food source for various birds and mammals in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
