Tribulus hirsutus

Tribulus hirsutus, commonly known as goat's head, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Tribulus hirsutus is known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly for its purported effects on the urinary and reproductive systems.
