Taraxacum officinale var. rubrinerve

Taraxacum officinale var. rubrinerve, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its distinctive red vascular patterns in the leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant produces a single, hollow, stem topped with a spherical flower head that turns into a white, tufted seed head. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate with a serrated margin and a basal rosette structure.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, including diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
