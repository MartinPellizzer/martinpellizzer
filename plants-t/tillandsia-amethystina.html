Tillandsia amethystina

Tillandsia amethystina, commonly known as the amethystina air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces showy, violet-colored inflorescences that bloom in late summer or early fall.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
