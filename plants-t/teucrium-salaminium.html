Teucrium salaminium

Teucrium salaminium, commonly known as Salaminian germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Teucrium salaminium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
