Taraxacum silesiacum

Taraxacum silesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is perennial and has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions, though it is less well-known than other dandelion species.
