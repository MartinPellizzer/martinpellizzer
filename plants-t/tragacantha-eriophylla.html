Tragacantha eriophylla

Tragacantha eriophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Tragacantha eriophylla is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
