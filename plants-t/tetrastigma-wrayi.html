Tetrastigma wrayi

Tetrastigma wrayi, commonly known as the climbing vine of the grape family, is a woody perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining habit and can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tetrastigma wrayi is also valued for its ornamental potential in horticulture.
