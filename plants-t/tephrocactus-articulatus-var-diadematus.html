Tephrocactus articulatus var. diadematus

Tephrocactus articulatus var. diadematus, commonly known as the diadem cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.

The varietal name "diadematus" refers to the ornate, crown-like appearance of its spines.
