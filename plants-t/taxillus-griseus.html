Taxillus griseus

Taxillus griseus, commonly known as the green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant forms a dense, shrub-like growth that can cover large sections of the host tree's canopy. Its small, white or pale green berries are an important food source for native birds and other wildlife.

Taxillus griseus plays a significant ecological role in shaping the structure of its host trees and supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
