Trichosalpinx costata

Trichosalpinx costata, commonly known as the wavy-flowered orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and feature a unique, wavy pattern on the petals.

Trichosalpinx costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
