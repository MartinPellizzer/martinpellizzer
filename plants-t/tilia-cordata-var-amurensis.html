Tilia cordata var. amurensis

Tilia cordata var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically 5-10 cm in diameter and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
