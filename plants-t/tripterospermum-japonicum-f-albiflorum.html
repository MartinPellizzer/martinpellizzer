Tripterospermum japonicum f. albiflorum

Tripterospermum japonicum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese snowbell, is a rare variant of the Japanese snowbell plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers, which contrast with the typical green or yellow blooms of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, typically growing in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall.

The white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.
