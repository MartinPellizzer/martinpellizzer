Triuridopsis

Triuridopsis, commonly known as the slender lichen orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, delicate flowers and are often found in moist, shaded environments. They are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Triuridopsis species typically have a short, creeping stem and are known for their specialized symbiotic relationships with fungi.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique adaptations to humid, tropical climates and their role in ecological diversity.
