Thymus × citratus

Thymus × citratus, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its aromatic foliage is widely used in culinary applications and herbal remedies.

Lemon thyme is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
