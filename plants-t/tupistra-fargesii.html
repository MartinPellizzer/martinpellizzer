Tupistra fargesii

Tupistra fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture and small, red berries that persist through winter. The plant has a compact, rounded shape and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden spaces.

Tupistra fargesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to pests and diseases.
