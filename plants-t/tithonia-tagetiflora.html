Tithonia tagetiflora

Tithonia tagetiflora, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tithonia tagetiflora is often used in agroforestry and as a companion plant due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
