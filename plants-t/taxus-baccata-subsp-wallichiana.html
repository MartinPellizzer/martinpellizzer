Taxus baccata subsp. wallichiana

Taxus baccata subsp.

wallichiana, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and reddish-orange berries that contain toxic seeds.

The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.

The wood of this subspecies is highly durable and historically used for crafting items such as bows and furniture.
