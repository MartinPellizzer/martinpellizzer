Taxodium

Taxodium, commonly known as the bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive flaky, reddish-brown bark and its ability to shed its needles and leaves annually. The tree produces cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril, which aids in seed dispersal. Taxodium species are often found in wetland habitats, where their extensive root systems help prevent soil erosion.

They are also notable for their ability to tolerate flooding and poor soil conditions.
