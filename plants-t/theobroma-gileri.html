Theobroma gileri

Theobroma gileri, commonly known as the Giler's cocoa, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is closely related to the cacao tree, which is used for producing chocolate. The plant is characterized by its evergreen nature, with large, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers. Its fruits are capsules that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of flavoring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
