Tabernaemontana alba

Tabernaemontana alba, commonly known as the white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem, making it an attractive ornamental species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its beauty and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
