Triticum spelta aestivum

Triticum spelta aestivum, commonly known as spelt wheat, is an ancient grain that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its hard, hull-covered seeds and is typically grown in temperate climates. The plant has a robust root system and produces tillers that bear spikelets with multiple florets. Spelt wheat is known for its high nutrient content, including proteins, fiber, and various minerals.

Its botanical structure and growth habits make it well-suited for both agricultural cultivation and traditional food use.
