Ternatea pedunculata

Ternatea pedunculata, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Ternatea pedunculata is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and ease of care.
