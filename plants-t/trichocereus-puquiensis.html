Trichocereus puquiensis

Trichocereus puquiensis, commonly known as the Puquio cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and sparse, often spiny areoles. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Trichocereus puquiensis is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental value.
