Tadehagi triquetrum subsp. andamanicum

Tadehagi triquetrum subsp.

andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman mountain bean, is a perennial herb native to the Andaman Islands. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
