Teucrium montanum var. skorpilii

Teucrium montanum var. skorpilii, commonly known as the Skorpil's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
