Taraxacum klimesianum

Taraxacum klimesianum, commonly known as the Klimesian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which are typically yellow in color. This plant is native to parts of Central Asia and is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
