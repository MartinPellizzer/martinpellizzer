Thelocactus lophothele

Thelocactus lophothele, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green, cylindrical stem and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its adaptations to harsh environments include thick, water-storing stems and a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

The plant is well-suited for xeriscaping and is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
