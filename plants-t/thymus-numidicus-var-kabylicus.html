Thymus numidicus var. kabylicus

Thymus numidicus var. kabylicus, commonly known as the Kabylic thyme, is a compact, aromatic perennial shrub native to North Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed and emit a strong, minty fragrance. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its essential oils are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties.
