Teesdalia nudicaulis subsp. lepidium

Teesdalia nudicaulis subsp.

lepidium, commonly known as the woolly mountain bluet, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often glandular. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and a symbol of mountainous landscapes.
