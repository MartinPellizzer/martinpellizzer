Titanopsis spathulata

Titanopsis spathulata, commonly known as the star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Titanopsis spathulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
