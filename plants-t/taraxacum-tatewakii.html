Taraxacum tatewakii

Taraxacum tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that bears a bright yellow flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
