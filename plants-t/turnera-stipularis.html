Turnera stipularis

Turnera stipularis, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with prominent stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the petioles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Turnera stipularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
