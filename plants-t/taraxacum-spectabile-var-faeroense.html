Taraxacum spectabile var. faeroense

Taraxacum spectabile var. faeroense, commonly known as the Faroe Islands dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Faroe Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of serrated leaves. The plant is perennial and thrives in rocky, coastal environments. It produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports its flowering heads.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh maritime climates and is an important part of the local flora.
