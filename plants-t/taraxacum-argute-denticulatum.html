Taraxacum argute-denticulatum

Taraxacum argute-denticulatum, commonly known as the denticulate dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Central and South America, this plant thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with fine, tooth-like margins, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate climates.
