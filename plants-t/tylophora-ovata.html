Tylophora ovata

Tylophora ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release small, winged seeds.

Tylophora ovata is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
