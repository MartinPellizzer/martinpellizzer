Tragopogon balcanicus

Tragopogon balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Tragopogon balcanicus is known for its taproot system and is often found in temperate climates.
