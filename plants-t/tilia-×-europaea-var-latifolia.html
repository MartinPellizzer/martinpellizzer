Tilia × europaea var. latifolia

Tilia × europaea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed linden, is a hybrid species within the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 8–15 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.
