Taraxacum crispifrons

Taraxacum crispifrons, commonly known as the curlyleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that disperses its seeds via the wind.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and lawns.
