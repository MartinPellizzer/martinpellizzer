Tumalis bojeri

Tumalis bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's tumalis, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
