Tmesipteris tannensis var. truncata

Tmesipteris tannensis var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically submerged or floating. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, truncate (cut-off) shape at the base.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
