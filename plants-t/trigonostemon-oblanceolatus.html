Trigonostemon oblanceolatus

Trigonostemon oblanceolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved trigonostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trigonostemon oblanceolatus is valued for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
