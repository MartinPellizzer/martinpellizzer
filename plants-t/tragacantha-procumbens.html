Tragacantha procumbens

Tragacantha procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with numerous slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Tragacantha procumbens is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its historical use in traditional medicine.
