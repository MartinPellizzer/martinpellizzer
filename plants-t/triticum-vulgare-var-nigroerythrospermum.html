Triticum vulgare var. nigroerythrospermum

Triticum vulgare var. nigroerythrospermum, commonly known as black and red spelt, is a variant of spelt wheat characterized by its dark-colored grains.

It is an ancient grain with a rich history of cultivation in Europe and the Middle East. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a spike-like inflorescence. Its seeds are small, oval, and have a distinctive dark reddish-brown coloration.

This variety is valued for its high nutritional content and unique flavor profile in baking and culinary applications.
