Taraxacum rufinervosum

Taraxacum rufinervosum, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are typically hairy as well.

The red-veined dandelion is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
