Thalictrum punduanum var. glaucum

Thalictrum punduanum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called glaucous, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with prominent stamens, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
