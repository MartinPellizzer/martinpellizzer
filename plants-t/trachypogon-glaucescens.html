Trachypogon glaucescens

Trachypogon glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Trachypogon glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
