Teucrium oxylepis subsp. gredense

Teucrium oxylepis subsp.

gredense, commonly known as the Gredense germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically hairy.

The flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months. This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
