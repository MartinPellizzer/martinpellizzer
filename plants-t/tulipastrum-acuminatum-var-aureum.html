Tulipastrum acuminatum var. aureum

Tulipastrum acuminatum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden tulip tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
