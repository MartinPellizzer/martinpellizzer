Telipogon genegeorgei

Telipogon genegeorgei, commonly known as the George's orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
