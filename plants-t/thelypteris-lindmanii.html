Thelypteris lindmanii

Thelypteris lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is a small, deciduous fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant has a delicate, lace-like frond structure with a distinctive shield-shaped pinnae. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Thelypteris lindmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
