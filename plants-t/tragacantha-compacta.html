Tragacantha compacta

Tragacantha compacta, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish bark, small green leaves, and the production of a yellowish resin known as tragacanth. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its resin has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals.
