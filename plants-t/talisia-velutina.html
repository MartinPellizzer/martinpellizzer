Talisia velutina

Talisia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Talisia velutina is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It has been introduced to other regions and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
