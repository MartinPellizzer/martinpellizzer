Tanacetum joharchii

Tanacetum joharchii, commonly known as the Kashmirian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
