Taraxacum pseudofulvum

Taraxacum pseudofulvum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
