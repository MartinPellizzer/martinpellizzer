Tinospora uliginosa

Tinospora uliginosa, commonly known as black nightshade or Indian knapweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Tinospora uliginosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
