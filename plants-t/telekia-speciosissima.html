Telekia speciosissima

Telekia speciosissima, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

Telekia speciosissima is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
