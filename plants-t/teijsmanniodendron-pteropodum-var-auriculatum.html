Teijsmanniodendron pteropodum var. auriculatum

Teijsmanniodendron pteropodum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, palmate leaves that resemble ears, giving it its common name. The tree produces striking, fiery red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with longitudinal ridges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
