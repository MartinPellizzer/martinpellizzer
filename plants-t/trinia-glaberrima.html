Trinia glaberrima

Trinia glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth trinia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
