Talisia pilosula

Talisia pilosula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky talisia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Talisia pilosula is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
