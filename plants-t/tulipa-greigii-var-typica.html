Tulipa greigii var. typica

Tulipa greigii var. typica, commonly known as the Greig's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the throat. The plant produces upright, strap-like leaves and typically blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
