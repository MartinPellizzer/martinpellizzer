Tanacetum heptalobum

Tanacetum heptalobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves with seven lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences.

Tanacetum heptalobum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
