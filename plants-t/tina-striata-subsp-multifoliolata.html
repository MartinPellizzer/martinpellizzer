Tina striata subsp. multifoliolata

Tina striata subsp.

multifoliolata, commonly known as the variegated tina, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family. It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow, often with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in whorls, and come in shades of pink or red.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.
