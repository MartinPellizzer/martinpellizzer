Thamnoseris lobata

Thamnoseris lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thamnoseris, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Thamnoseris lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
