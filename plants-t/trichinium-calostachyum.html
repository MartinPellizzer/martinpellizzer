Trichinium calostachyum

Trichinium calostachyum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.
