Tripleurospermum colchicum

Tripleurospermum colchicum, commonly known as meadow garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, flowering stem topped with a cluster of small, yellowish-white flowers. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
