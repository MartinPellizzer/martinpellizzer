Thymus ciliatus subsp. algeriensis

Thymus ciliatus subsp.

algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, grey-green leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its essential oils and medicinal properties.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
