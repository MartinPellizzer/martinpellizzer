Tilia tuan subsp. oblongifolia

Tilia tuan subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped to oval leaves that are typically narrower than those of other Tilia species.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in drooping inflorescences, typical of the genus. It produces small, oval fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and spring blossoms.
