Telukrama

Telukrama, commonly known as the "Java rose" or *Bauhinia purpurea*, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling rose blossoms. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Telukrama is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
