Thymus × enicensis

Thymus × enicensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Thymus × enicensis is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
