Tragacantha decemjuga

Tragacantha decemjuga, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its sap, known as tragacanth, is highly valued for its gelling properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Tragacantha decemjuga is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
