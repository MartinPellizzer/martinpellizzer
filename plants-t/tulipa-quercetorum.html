Tulipa quercetorum

Tulipa quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf tulip, is a species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Tulipa quercetorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
