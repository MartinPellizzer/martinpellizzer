Terminalia quadrialata

Terminalia quadrialata, commonly known as the four-winged terminalia, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its distinctive four-winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The wood of Terminalia quadrialata is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
