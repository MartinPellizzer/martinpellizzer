Tephrosia incarnata

Tephrosia incarnata, commonly known as the purple milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Tephrosia incarnata is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and along stream banks.
