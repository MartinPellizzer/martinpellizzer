Trichocereus tacaquirensis subsp. taquimbalensis

Trichocereus tacaquirensis subsp.

taquimbalensis, commonly known as the Taquimbalensis Cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It is adapted to high altitudes and has a slow growth rate, making it a rare and sought-after specimen in botanical collections.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important subject in studies of desert flora.
