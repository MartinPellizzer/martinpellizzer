Tephrosia scopulorum

Tephrosia scopulorum, commonly known as the rock tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Tephrosia scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
