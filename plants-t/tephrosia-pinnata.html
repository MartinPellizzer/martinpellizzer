Tephrosia pinnata

Tephrosia pinnata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or cowpea shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. It has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Tephrosia pinnata is also known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
