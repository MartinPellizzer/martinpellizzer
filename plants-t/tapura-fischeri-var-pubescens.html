Tapura fischeri var. pubescens

Tapura fischeri var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tapura, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture and indirect light. Its leaves are elongated and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
