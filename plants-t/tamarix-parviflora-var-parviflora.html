Tamarix parviflora var. parviflora

Tamarix parviflora var. parviflora, commonly known as the parviflora tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in saline and sandy soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
