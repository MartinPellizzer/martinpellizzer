Taraxacum gracilens

Taraxacum gracilens, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum gracilens is a perennial herb that is often considered a weed but has ecological and medicinal significance.
