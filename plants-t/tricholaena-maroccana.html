Tricholaena maroccana

Tricholaena maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months. Tricholaena maroccana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
