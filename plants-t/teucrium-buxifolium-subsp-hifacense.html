Teucrium buxifolium subsp. hifacense

Teucrium buxifolium subsp.

hifacense, commonly known as the Hifacense germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often found in forest margins and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
