Turbinicarpus × delicatus

Turbinicarpus × delicatus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a colorful, fleshy cactus body known as the "pseudostem.". The plant typically grows in a compact, rounded shape with ribbed stems that store water efficiently. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its hybrid nature, Turbinicarpus × delicatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
