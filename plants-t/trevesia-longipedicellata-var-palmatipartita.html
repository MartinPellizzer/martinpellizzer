Trevesia longipedicellata var. palmatipartita

Trevesia longipedicellata var. palmatipartita, commonly known as the broad-leaved trevesia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
