Trifolium variegatum var. geminiflorum

Trifolium variegatum var. geminiflorum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a variant of the common red clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, adding visual appeal to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

This variety is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
