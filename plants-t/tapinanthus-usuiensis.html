Tapinanthus usuiensis

Tapinanthus usuiensis, commonly known as the Usui's dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to the forests of Japan.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the stems and leaves of other species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure with numerous thin, thread-like branches. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
