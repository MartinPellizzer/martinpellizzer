Telopea truncata

Telopea truncata, commonly known as the sugar bush, is an evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, grey-green foliage and is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and dry environments. Telopea truncata is a member of the Proteaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
