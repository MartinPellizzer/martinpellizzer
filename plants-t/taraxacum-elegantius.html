Taraxacum elegantius

Taraxacum elegantius, commonly known as the elegant dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
