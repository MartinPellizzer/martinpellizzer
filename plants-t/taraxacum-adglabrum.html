Taraxacum adglabrum

Taraxacum adglabrum, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.
