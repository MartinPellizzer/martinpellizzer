Tapeinotes carolinae var. major

Tapeinotes carolinae var. major, commonly known as the Carolina mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
