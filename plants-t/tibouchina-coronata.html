Tibouchina coronata

Tibouchina coronata, commonly known as the crown of thorns or red torch bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, while the flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and come in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract birds and insects, making it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
