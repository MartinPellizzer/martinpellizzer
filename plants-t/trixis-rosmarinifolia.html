Trixis rosmarinifolia

Trixis rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the desert sage, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has a woody stem and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its distinct scent and medicinal properties.

Trixis rosmarinifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
