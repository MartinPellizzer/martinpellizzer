Trifolium caerulescens

Trifolium caerulescens, commonly known as blue clover, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It is a member of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
