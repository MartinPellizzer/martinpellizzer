Trollius americanus lusus pentasepalus

Trollius americanus lusus pentasepalus, commonly known as the American globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow, globular flower heads composed of numerous petals that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are broadly ovate and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, sun-like flowers.
