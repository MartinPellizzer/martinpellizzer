Tamarix gallica var. divergens

Tamarix gallica var. divergens, commonly known as the pink tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in coastal and saline soils, showing some tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in certain regions.
