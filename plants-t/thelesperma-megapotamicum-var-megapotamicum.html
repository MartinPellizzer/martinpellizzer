Thelesperma megapotamicum var. megapotamicum

Thelesperma megapotamicum var. megapotamicum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular plant in desert gardens.
