Tromotriche glauca

Tromotriche glauca, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Tromotriche glauca has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
