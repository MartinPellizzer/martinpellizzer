Tridax cajamarcensis

Tridax cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purple tint.

The flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to spread in various environments.
