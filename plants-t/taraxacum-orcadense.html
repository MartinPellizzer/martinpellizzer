Taraxacum orcadense

Taraxacum orcadense, commonly known as the Orkney dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Orkney Islands in Scotland.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces a single, bright yellow flower that develops into a spherical seed head with feathery seeds, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and grassland habitats.

Taraxacum orcadense is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native environment.
