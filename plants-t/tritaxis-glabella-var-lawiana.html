Tritaxis glabella var. lawiana

Tritaxis glabella var. lawiana, commonly known as the Law's glabella, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
