Triglossum arundinaceum

Triglossum arundinaceum, commonly known as the golden thread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or colonies. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Triglossum arundinaceum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
