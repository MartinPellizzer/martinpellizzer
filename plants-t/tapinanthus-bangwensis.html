Tapinanthus bangwensis

Tapinanthus bangwensis, commonly known as the Bangwe mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host tree, primarily the baobab.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of potential host trees.

This unique relationship with its host makes it an important component of the ecological dynamics in its native habitat.
