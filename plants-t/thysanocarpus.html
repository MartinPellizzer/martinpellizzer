Thysanocarpus

Thysanocarpus, commonly known as the thysanocarpus tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often clustered pods that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a unique appearance. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. They produce small, often yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
