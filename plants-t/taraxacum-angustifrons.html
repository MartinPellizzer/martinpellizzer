Taraxacum angustifrons

Taraxacum angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a sphere of white pappus-tipped seeds. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

It is often considered a weed but has ecological and potential medicinal value.
