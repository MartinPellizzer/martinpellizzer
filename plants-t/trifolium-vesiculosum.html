Trifolium vesiculosum

Trifolium vesiculosum, commonly known as the bladder clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinctive bladdery or inflated base. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in other wetland habitats.
