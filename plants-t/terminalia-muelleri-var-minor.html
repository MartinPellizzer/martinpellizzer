Terminalia muelleri var. minor

Terminalia muelleri var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Terminalia muelleri var. minor is valued for its durability and is used in traditional and contemporary woodworking.
