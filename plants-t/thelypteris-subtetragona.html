Thelypteris subtetragona

Thelypteris subtetragona, commonly known as the four-angled fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect rhizome and pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive four-angled appearance on the petioles. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with serrated margins that contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
