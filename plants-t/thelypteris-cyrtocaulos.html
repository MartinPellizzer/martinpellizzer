Thelypteris cyrtocaulos

Thelypteris cyrtocaulos, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive look.

Thelypteris cyrtocaulos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
