Tetrastigma quadridens

Tetrastigma quadridens, commonly known as the four-toothed crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and tendrils that help it climb. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
