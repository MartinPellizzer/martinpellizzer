Tanacetum santolinoides

Tanacetum santolinoides, commonly known as the Spanish tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are aromatic and have a camphor-like scent, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Tanacetum santolinoides is typically found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.
