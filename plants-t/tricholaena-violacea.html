Tricholaena violacea

Tricholaena violacea, commonly known as the purple-leafed bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often a striking violet or purple color, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles.

Tricholaena violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
