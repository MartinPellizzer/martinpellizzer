Torenia polygonoides

Torenia polygonoides, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Torenia polygonoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
