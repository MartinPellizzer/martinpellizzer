Tectaria bamleriana

Tectaria bamleriana, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are also valued for their ornamental appeal in horticulture.
