Tamarix speciosa subsp. ludibunda

Tamarix speciosa subsp.

ludibunda, commonly known as the fragrant tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and saline soils. Its fast growth rate and ability to fix nitrogen make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
