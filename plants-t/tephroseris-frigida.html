Tephroseris frigida

Tephroseris frigida, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh climates.

Tephroseris frigida plays an important role in Arctic ecosystems, providing food for pollinators and contributing to the region's biodiversity.
