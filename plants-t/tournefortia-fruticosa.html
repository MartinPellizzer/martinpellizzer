Tournefortia fruticosa

Tournefortia fruticosa, commonly known as the sour orange or wild tamarind, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. The fruit contains a sour pulp and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
