Tillandsia concolor

Tillandsia concolor, commonly known as the green tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, which helps it thrive in humid environments. It does not require soil, instead absorbing water and minerals through its leaf surfaces.

Tillandsia concolor is often used as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
