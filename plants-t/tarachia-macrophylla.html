Tarachia macrophylla

Tarachia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant tarplant, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 15 inches in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Tarachia macrophylla is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
