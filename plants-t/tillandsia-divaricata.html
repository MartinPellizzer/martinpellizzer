Tillandsia divaricata

Tillandsia divaricata, commonly known as the silver vanda, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia divaricata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
