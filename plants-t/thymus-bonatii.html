Thymus bonatii

Thymus bonatii, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Corsica.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, releasing a strong thyme scent when crushed.

Thymus bonatii is often found growing in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
