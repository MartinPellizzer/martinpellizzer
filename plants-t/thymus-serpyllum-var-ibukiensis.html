Thymus serpyllum var. ibukiensis

Thymus serpyllum var. ibukiensis, commonly known as the Japanese thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a strong thyme scent. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
