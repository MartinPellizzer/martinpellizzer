Tragacantha gossypina

Tragacantha gossypina, commonly known as the Chinese tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a natural thickening agent. Its growth habit includes shrub-like stems and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Tragacantha gossypina make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
