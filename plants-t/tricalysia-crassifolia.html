Tricalysia crassifolia

Tricalysia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved arum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
