Triplochlamys tricalycaris

Triplochlamys tricalycaris, commonly known as the three-horned orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure with three prominent, fleshy calyces. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
