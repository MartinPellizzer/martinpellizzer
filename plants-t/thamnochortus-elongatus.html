Thamnochortus elongatus

Thamnochortus elongatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure and fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to rocky and coastal habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Thamnochortus elongatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
