Tulipa scardica

Tulipa scardica, commonly known as the Crimean tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or red base. The plant produces a single, showy bloom per stem, typically in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Tulipa scardica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
