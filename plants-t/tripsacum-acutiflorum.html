Tripsacum acutiflorum

Tripsacum acutiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered tripes, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Tripsacum acutiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
