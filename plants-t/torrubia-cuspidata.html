Torrubia cuspidata

Torrubia cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cow-wheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
