Tamarix africana var. philistaea

Tamarix africana var. philistaea, commonly known as the Philistine tamarisk, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or pale red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It has a woody stem with a reddish-brown color and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
