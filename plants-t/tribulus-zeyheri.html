Tribulus zeyheri

Tribulus zeyheri, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Tribulus zeyheri is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
