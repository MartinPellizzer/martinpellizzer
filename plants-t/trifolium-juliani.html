Trifolium juliani

Trifolium juliani, commonly known as Julian's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium juliani is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
