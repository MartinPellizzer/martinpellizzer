Triphlebia dimorphophylla

Triphlebia dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-form frond fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bifurcated fronds that exhibit two different forms on either side of the plant. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a feathery appearance that aids in photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Triphlebia dimorphophylla plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
