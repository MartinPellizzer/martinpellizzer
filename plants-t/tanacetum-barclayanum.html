Tanacetum barclayanum

Tanacetum barclayanum, commonly known as the Balkan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Tanacetum barclayanum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
