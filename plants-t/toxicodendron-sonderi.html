Toxicodendron sonderi

Toxicodendron sonderi, commonly known as the Sonder's poison ivy, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Its sap contains urushiol, a potent allergen that can cause severe skin irritation and rashes upon contact.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in both natural and cultivated settings.
