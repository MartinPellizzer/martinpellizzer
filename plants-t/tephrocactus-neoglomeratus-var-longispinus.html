Tephrocactus neoglomeratus var. longispinus

Tephrocactus neoglomeratus var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined globose cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem that is typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Its spines are long and straight, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
