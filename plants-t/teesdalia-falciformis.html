Teesdalia falciformis

Teesdalia falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds with pappus that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Teesdalia falciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
