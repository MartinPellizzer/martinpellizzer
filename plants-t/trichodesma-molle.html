Trichodesma molle

Trichodesma molle, commonly known as the hairy trichodesma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Trichodesma molle is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
