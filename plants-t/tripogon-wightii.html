Tripogon wightii

Tripogon wightii, commonly known as the Wight's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its cylindrical stems are usually unbranched, and it produces small, three-sided flowers in dense clusters.

Tripogon wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
