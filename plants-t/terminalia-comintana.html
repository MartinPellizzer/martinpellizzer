Terminalia comintana

Terminalia comintana, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.
