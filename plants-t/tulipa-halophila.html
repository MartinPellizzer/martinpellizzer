Tulipa halophila

Tulipa halophila, commonly known as the salt-tolerant tulip, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is adapted to grow in saline soils, making it a halophyte. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to withstand periods of drought and salt exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in challenging environments.
