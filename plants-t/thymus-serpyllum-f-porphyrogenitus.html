Thymus serpyllum f. porphyrogenitus

Thymus serpyllum f. porphyrogenitus, commonly known as purple thyme, is a subspecies of the common thyme plant characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and aromatic foliage.

It features small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance. This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its compact size and drought tolerance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
