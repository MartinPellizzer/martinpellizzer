Taraxacum nairoense

Taraxacum nairoense, commonly known as the Nairoonga dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to East Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in spring. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum nairoense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
