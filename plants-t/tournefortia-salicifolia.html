Tournefortia salicifolia

Tournefortia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
