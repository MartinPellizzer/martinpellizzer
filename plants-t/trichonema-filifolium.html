Trichonema filifolium

Trichonema filifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf flax, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Trichonema filifolium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
