Terminalia bellirica

Terminalia bellirica, commonly known as the Indian bedellium or stone fruit, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, oval fruits that contain a single seed. Terminalia bellirica is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine.

Its fruits are also used in traditional culinary practices and as a source of tannins.
