Thalictrum discolor

Thalictrum discolor, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. Thalictrum discolor thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
