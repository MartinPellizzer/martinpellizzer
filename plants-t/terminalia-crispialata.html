Terminalia crispialata

Terminalia crispialata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and often cracked, providing a rugged appearance.

Terminalia crispialata is valued for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
