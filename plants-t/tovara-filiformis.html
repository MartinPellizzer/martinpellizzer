Tovara filiformis

Tovara filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf tovara, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
