Tephrosia emarginata

Tephrosia emarginata, commonly known as the African tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and can develop into a taproot, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

Tephrosia emarginata is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its potential medicinal properties.
