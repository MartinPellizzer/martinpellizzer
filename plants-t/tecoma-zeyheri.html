Tecoma zeyheri

Tecoma zeyheri, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma zeyheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
