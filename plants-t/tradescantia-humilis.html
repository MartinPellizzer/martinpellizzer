Tradescantia humilis

Tradescantia humilis, commonly known as spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to spread easily through runners.

Tradescantia humilis is also known for its air-purifying properties and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
