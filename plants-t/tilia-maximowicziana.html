Tilia maximowicziana

Tilia maximowicziana, commonly known as the Mongolian linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain three winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.
