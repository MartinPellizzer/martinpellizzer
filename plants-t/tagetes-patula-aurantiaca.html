Tagetes patula aurantiaca

Tagetes patula aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange marigold, is a vibrant annual plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which are typically daisy-like with radiating petals and a central disk. The plant features narrow, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Native to Mexico, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical properties include the presence of volatile oils and flavonoids, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.
