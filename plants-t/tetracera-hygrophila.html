Tetracera hygrophila

Tetracera hygrophila, commonly known as the water tetracera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Tetracera hygrophila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
