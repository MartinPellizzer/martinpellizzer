Tragacantha sirinica

Tragacantha sirinica, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing small, hard seeds. Its sap, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals.

The botanical features of Tragacantha sirinica make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
