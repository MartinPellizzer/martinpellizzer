Trigonella schachimardanica

Trigonella schachimardanica, commonly known as Armenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
