Tupistra bambusifolia var. rubromaculosa

Tupistra bambusifolia var. rubromaculosa, commonly known as the red-spotted yew, is a unique variant of the yew plant characterized by its striking red spots on the foliage.

It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The leaves are needle-like, dark green with a glossy texture, and the red markings are most prominent on the undersides. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its distinctive coloration and its ability to provide year-round interest in the landscape.
