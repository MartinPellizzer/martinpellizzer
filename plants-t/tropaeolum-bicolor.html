Tropaeolum bicolor

Tropaeolum bicolor, commonly known as the Indian cress or garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, which can be sown in the spring for propagation. Tropaeolum bicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible properties, makes it a versatile and attractive addition to gardens.
