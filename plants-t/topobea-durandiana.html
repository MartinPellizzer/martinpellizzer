Topobea durandiana

Topobea durandiana, commonly known as the Durand's tree.

It is a flowering plant native to the Andes, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
