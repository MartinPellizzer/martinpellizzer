Tetradenia glauca

Tetradenia glauca, commonly known as the blue tetradenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Tetradenia glauca is typically found in coastal areas and sand dunes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
