Tristachya inamoena

Tristachya inamoena, commonly known as the golden panic grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. Its feathery plumes are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.

Tristachya inamoena is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marginal lands.
