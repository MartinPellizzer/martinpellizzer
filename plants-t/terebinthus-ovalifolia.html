Terebinthus ovalifolia

Terebinthus ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf terebinth, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Terebinthus ovalifolia is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of resin has contributed to its cultural significance in the region.
