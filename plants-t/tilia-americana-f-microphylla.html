Tilia americana f. microphylla

Tilia americana f. microphylla, commonly known as the American balm of Gilead, is a small tree or shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 3 inches in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
