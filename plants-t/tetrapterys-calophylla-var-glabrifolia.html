Tetrapterys calophylla var. glabrifolia

Tetrapterys calophylla var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
