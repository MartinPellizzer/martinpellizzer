Timonius sericeus var. tomentosa

Timonius sericeus var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy timonius, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
