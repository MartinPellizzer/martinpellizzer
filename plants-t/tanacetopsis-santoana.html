Tanacetopsis santoana

Tanacetopsis santoana, commonly known as the Santoana goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
