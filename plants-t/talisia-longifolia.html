Talisia longifolia

Talisia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved talisia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elongated and often have a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Talisia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
