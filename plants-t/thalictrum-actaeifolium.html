Thalictrum actaeifolium

Thalictrum actaeifolium, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum actaeifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
