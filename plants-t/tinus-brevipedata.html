Tinus brevipedata

Tinus brevipedata, commonly known as the short-footed thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, where it forms dense tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate moderate grazing.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
