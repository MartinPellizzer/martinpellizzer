Teucrium apollinis

Teucrium apollinis, commonly known as Apollo's tea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, often purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
