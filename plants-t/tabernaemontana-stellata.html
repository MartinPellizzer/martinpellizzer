Tabernaemontana stellata

Tabernaemontana stellata, commonly known as the star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the night. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic blossoms.

Tabernaemontana stellata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
