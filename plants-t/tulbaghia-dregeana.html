Tulbaghia dregeana

Tulbaghia dregeana, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

Tulbaghia dregeana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
