Turnera surinamensis

Turnera surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam turnera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
