Teucrium gussonei

Teucrium gussonei, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Teucrium gussonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
