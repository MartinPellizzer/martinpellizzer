Thamnocalamus ibityensis

Thamnocalamus ibityensis, commonly known as the Ibity bamboo, is a species of grass found in Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and slightly pubescent on the underside. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
