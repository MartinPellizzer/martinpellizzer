Talinum brevicaule

Talinum brevicaule, commonly known as dwarf spiderflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a spiky central column resembling a spider's body. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Talinum brevicaule is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines for their mild, slightly tangy flavor.
