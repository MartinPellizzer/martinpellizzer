Thoracosperma radicans

Thoracosperma radicans, commonly known as the climbing tamanu, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a climbing habit with aerial roots that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Thoracosperma radicans is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental potential.
