Taraxacum falciferum

Taraxacum falciferum, commonly known as the sickleleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves. The plant produces a single, tall, yellow flower head that blooms in spring and summer. Its flowering stem is usually hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
