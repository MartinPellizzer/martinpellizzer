Tragacantha eubrychioides

Tragacantha eubrychioides, commonly known as the European tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a sticky, gum-like substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food additive.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
