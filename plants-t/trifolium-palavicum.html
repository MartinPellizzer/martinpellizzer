Trifolium palavicum

Trifolium palavicum, commonly known as the pale clover, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Trifolium palavicum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
