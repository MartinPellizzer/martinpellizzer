Trifolium michelianum var. minus

Trifolium michelianum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser michelia clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
