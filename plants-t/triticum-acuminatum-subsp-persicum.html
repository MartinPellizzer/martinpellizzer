Triticum acuminatum subsp. persicum

Triticum acuminatum subsp.

persicum, commonly known as Persian wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological applications.
