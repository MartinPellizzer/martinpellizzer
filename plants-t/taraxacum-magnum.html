Taraxacum magnum

Taraxacum magnum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its taproot is thick and deep, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

This species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability and resilience.
