Thyrsacanthus ruber

Thyrsacanthus ruber, commonly known as the red thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, reddish bracts that enclose the flower heads, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs and have a slightly lobed appearance.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
