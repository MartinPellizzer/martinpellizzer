Trigonella foenum-graecum subsp. culta

Trigonella foenum-graecum subsp.

culta, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is cultivated for its seeds, which are rich in proteins, fiber, and various bioactive compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
