Tragacantha grisea

Tragacantha grisea, commonly known as the gray tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its sap, known as tragacanth, is a viscous gum used in various industrial and medicinal applications.

Tragacantha grisea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
