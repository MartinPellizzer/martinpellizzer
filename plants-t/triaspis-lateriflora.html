Triaspis lateriflora

Triaspis lateriflora, commonly known as the lateral-flowered triaspis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with flowers arranged laterally along the stems. The plant typically grows in shrubland or woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
