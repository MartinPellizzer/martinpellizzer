Trichouratea acuminata

Trichouratea acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped trichourate, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Trichouratea acuminata is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
