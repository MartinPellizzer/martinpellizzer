Tillandsia pulchella var. vaginata

Tillandsia pulchella var. vaginata, commonly known as the silver vane air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. The plant does not require soil to grow, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves from the air. It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is popular among air plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
