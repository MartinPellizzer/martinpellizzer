Typha australis

Typha australis, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to Australia and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and flat, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered brown flowers that bloom in the summer months. Typha australis thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
