Thelypteris inaequans

Thelypteris inaequans, commonly known as the unequal fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds with a distinctive asymmetrical shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its sporangia are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Thelypteris inaequans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
