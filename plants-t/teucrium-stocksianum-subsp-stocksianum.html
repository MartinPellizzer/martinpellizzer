Teucrium stocksianum subsp. stocksianum

Teucrium stocksianum subsp.

stocksianum, commonly known as Stocks' germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
