Tragacantha arequipensis

Tragacantha arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its root system is notable for secreting a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
