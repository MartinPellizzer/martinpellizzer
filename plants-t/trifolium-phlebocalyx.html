Trifolium phlebocalyx

Trifolium phlebocalyx, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium phlebocalyx is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
