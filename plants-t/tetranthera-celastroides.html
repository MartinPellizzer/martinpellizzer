Tetranthera celastroides

Tetranthera celastroides, commonly known as the creeping tetranthera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow and are followed by small, spherical fruits.

Tetranthera celastroides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
