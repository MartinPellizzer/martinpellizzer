Taraxacum natschikense

Taraxacum natschikense, commonly known as the Natschikense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Georgia and Russia. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface and produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is adapted to grow in rocky and dry environments, showcasing a robust root system.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
