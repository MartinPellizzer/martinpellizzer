Tricondylus silaifolius

Tricondylus silaifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
