Trymalium thomasioides

Trymalium thomasioides, commonly known as the coastal lantana, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
