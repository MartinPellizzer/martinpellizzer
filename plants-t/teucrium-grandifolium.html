Teucrium grandifolium

Teucrium grandifolium, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features lance-shaped, blue-green leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Teucrium grandifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
