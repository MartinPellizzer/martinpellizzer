Timonius sulitii

Timonius sulitii, commonly known as the Philippine moonseed, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by kidney-shaped fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss, Timonius sulitii is considered a rare and endangered species.
