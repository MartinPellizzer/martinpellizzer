Trachypogon montufarii var. grandiflorus

Trachypogon montufarii var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered trachypogon, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces showy, pale blue to purple florets that are often clustered in panicle-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
