Tragacantha darumbium

Tragacantha darumbium, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing seeds. It is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.

The sap of Tragacantha darumbium is used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
