Trichocereus huascha

Trichocereus huascha, commonly known as the "Huascha cactus," is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which open only during the night. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
