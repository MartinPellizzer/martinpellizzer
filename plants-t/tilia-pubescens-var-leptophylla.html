Tilia pubescens var. leptophylla

Tilia pubescens var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The flowers are small, fragrant, and borne in drooping clusters, typical of the Tilia genus. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a membranous sac.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
