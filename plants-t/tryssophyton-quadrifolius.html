Tryssophyton quadrifolius

Tryssophyton quadrifolius, commonly known as the four-leaved clover, is a small perennial herb belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf clover structure, which is rare and considered a symbol of good luck. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and serrated at the edges. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The roots of Tryssophyton quadrifolius are nitrogen-fixing, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
