Taraxacum authionense

Taraxacum authionense, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of countries like Peru and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
