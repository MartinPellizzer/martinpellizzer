Taraxacum longicaudatum

Taraxacum longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated petioles that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds, typical of the dandelion genus. It is often found in open, disturbed soils and is widespread across temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
