Teucrium chlorostachyum subsp. chlorostachyum

Teucrium chlorostachyum subsp.

chlorostachyum, commonly known as the blue germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
