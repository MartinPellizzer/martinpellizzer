Teucrium polium subsp. rupestricola

Teucrium polium subsp.

rupestricola, commonly known as the rock teucrium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters on rocky outcrops. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a grayish-green color, while its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
