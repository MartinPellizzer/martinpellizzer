Tragia lagoensis

Tragia lagoensis, commonly known as the Lake Tragia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tragia lagoensis is known for its ability to thrive in wetland areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
