Trianthema parvifolium var. rubens

Trianthema parvifolium var. rubens, commonly known as the redleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The variety rubens is distinguished by its reddish foliage, which adds to its ornamental value.
