Thymus artvinicus

Thymus artvinicus, commonly known as Artvin thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high mountain regions of northeastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus artvinicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
