Thuja occidentalis f. filiformis

Thuja occidentalis f. filiformis, commonly known as the creeping thuja or noble fir, is a unique cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, forming a dense, carpet-like mat of foliage. The leaves are scale-like and densely packed, giving the plant a soft, textured appearance. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
