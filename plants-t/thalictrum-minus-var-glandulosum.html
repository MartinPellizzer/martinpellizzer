Thalictrum minus var. glandulosum

Thalictrum minus var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it the "glandulosum" variety name. It prefers moist, shady habitats and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
