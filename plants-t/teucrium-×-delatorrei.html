Teucrium × delatorrei

Teucrium × delatorrei, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
