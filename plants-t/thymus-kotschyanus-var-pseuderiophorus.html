Thymus kotschyanus var. pseuderiophorus

Thymus kotschyanus var. pseuderiophorus, commonly known as the Armenian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, typical of the thyme genus. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in various climates.
