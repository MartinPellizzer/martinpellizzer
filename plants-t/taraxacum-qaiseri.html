Taraxacum qaiseri

Taraxacum qaiseri, commonly known as the Qaiseri dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Middle East, particularly in areas of Iraq and Iran. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of deeply lobed, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flower head. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.
