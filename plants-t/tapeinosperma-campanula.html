Tapeinosperma campanula

Tapeinosperma campanula, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
