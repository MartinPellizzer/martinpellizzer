Tanacetum monspeliense

Tanacetum monspeliense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and yellow button-like flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Tanacetum monspeliense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
