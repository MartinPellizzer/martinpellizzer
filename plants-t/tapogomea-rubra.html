Tapogomea rubra

Tapogomea rubra, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, and the plant produces woody capsules as its fruit.
