Trifolium duodecimnerve

Trifolium duodecimnerve, commonly known as the twelve-veined clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinct central vein. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium duodecimnerve is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
