Taraxacum nepalense

Taraxacum nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and adjacent areas in the Himalayas. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk.

The seeds are dispersed by wind via a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in their spread.
