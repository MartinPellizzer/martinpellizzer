Taraxacum cristatum

Taraxacum cristatum, commonly known as the crested dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crown-like arrangement of bracts that form a rosette at the base of the plant. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that terminates in a spherical, yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
