Taenitis revoluta var. planiuscula

Taenitis revoluta var. planiuscula, commonly known as the flat-leaf taenitis, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive yellow-green coloration and a subtle fragrance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
