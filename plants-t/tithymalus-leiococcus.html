Tithymalus leiococcus

Tithymalus leiococcus, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.
