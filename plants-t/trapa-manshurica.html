Trapa manshurica

Trapa manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, globe-shaped fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a short petiole, and the plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms.

Trapa manshurica is also valued for its nutritional content and traditional uses in some regions.
