Thylacitis

Thylacitis, commonly known as the honey ant, is a genus of ants found primarily in Australia.

These ants are notable for their unique ability to secrete a sweet, sticky substance known as honeydew, which they produce in specialized glands in their thorax. The honeydew serves as a food source for the colony and is also used in nest construction. Thylacitis ants are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where they play a role in nutrient cycling and soil aeration.

Their botanical significance lies in their interactions with plants, particularly in the dispersal of seeds and the facilitation of plant growth through mutualistic relationships.
