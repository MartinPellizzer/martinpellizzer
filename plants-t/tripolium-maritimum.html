Tripolium maritimum

Tripolium maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shores and dunes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue or purple.

Tripolium maritimum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects.
