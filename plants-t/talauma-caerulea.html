Talauma caerulea

Talauma caerulea, commonly known as the blue talauma, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Talauma caerulea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
