Tragacantha talyschensis

Tragacantha talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces a gum resin, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological studies of drought-resistant flora.
