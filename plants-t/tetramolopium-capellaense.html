Tetramolopium capellaense

Tetramolopium capellaense, commonly known as the Cape Verde ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to arid and saline conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Tetramolopium capellaense is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
