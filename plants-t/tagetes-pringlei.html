Tagetes pringlei

Tagetes pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's marigold, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tagetes pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
