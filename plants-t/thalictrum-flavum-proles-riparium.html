Thalictrum flavum proles riparium

Thalictrum flavum proles riparium, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland habitats.
