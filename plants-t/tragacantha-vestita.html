Tragacantha vestita

Tragacantha vestita, commonly known as the Turkish gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and long, twisted pods. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a food additive. Its growth habit includes a shrub-like structure with woody stems and trifoliate leaves.

Tragacantha vestita thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
