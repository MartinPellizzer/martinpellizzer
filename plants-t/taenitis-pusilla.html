Taenitis pusilla

Taenitis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, erect stems with few leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Taenitis pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
