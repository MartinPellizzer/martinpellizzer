Triticum turgidum var. compactolusitanicum

Triticum turgidum var. compactolusitanicum, commonly known as the compact wild wheat, is a wild species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, glume-like bracts that enclose the florets. The plant produces spikelets that are tightly clustered, which is a distinguishing feature from other wheat varieties. Its botanical structure includes a short, sturdy stem with alternate leaves that are narrow and linear.

This variety is often studied for its potential in agricultural breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
