Tripinnaria asiatica

Tripinnaria asiatica, commonly known as the Asian tripinnate, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three levels of leaflets, giving it the name "tripinnate.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
