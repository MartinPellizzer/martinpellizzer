Taraxacum popovii

Taraxacum popovii, commonly known as the Popov's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of a distinctive seed head.

Taraxacum popovii is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, adapting well to arid environments.
