Tripogon thorelii

Tripogon thorelii, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Tripogon thorelii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
