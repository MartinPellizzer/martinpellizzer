Thymelaea myrtifolia

Thymelaea myrtifolia, commonly known as the red thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

Thymelaea myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
