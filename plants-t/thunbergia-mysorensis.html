Thunbergia mysorensis

Thunbergia mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore trumpet flower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a climbing vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
