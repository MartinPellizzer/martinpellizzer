Trichilia monacantha

Trichilia monacantha, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged oppositely. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The wood of Trichilia monacantha is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in traditional craftsmanship and as a source of timber.
