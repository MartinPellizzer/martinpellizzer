Thymus millefloris

Thymus millefloris, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its widespread use in herbal remedies and cooking.

Thymus millefloris thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
