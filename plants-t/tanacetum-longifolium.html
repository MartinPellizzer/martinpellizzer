Tanacetum longifolium

Tanacetum longifolium, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic, honey-scented foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Tanacetum longifolium is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its historical use in herbal medicine.
