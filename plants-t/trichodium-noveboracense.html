Trichodium noveboracense

Trichodium noveboracense, commonly known as the New York grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like blades that are often covered in fine hairs.

Trichodium noveboracense is generally short-lived and reproduces primarily through seeds.
