Tetrastigma apiculatum var. apiculatum

Tetrastigma apiculatum var. apiculatum, commonly known as the hairy passionvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
