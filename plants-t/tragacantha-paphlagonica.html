Tragacantha paphlagonica

Tragacantha paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and industry.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.
