Trichilia paraensis

Trichilia paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes many economically important hardwood species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellowish-green catkins, and it produces woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Trichilia paraensis is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
