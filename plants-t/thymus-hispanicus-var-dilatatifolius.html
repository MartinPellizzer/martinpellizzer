Thymus hispanicus var. dilatatifolius

Thymus hispanicus var. dilatatifolius, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and is often found growing in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antiseptic properties.

The variety dilatatifolius is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other thyme species.
