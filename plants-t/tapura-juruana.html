Tapura juruana

Tapura juruana, commonly known as the "Brazilian starfruit," is a tropical plant native to the Amazon region.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble starfruits, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
