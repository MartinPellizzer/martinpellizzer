Turbinicarpus mandragora subsp. booleanus

Turbinicarpus mandragora subsp.

booleanus, commonly known as the booleanus cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
