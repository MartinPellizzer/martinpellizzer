Tropaeolum chilense

Tropaeolum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum chilense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
