Thaumastochloa pubescens

Thaumastochloa pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lepidostemon, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often pale green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for erosion control.
