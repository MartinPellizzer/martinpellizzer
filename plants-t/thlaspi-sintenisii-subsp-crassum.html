Thlaspi sintenisii subsp. crassum

Thlaspi sintenisii subsp.

crassum, commonly known as the broadleaf pennycress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of thick, succulent leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It is native to central and eastern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its distribution across various environments.
