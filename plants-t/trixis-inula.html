Trixis inula

Trixis inula, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils.

Trixis inula is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
