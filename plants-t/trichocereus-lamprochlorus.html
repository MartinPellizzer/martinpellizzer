Trichocereus lamprochlorus

Trichocereus lamprochlorus, commonly known as the "Lamb's Wool Cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, white to pale blue spines that give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a strong fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and cactus enthusiast collections.
