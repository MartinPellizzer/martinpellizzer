Thalictrum flavum subsp. flavum

Thalictrum flavum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, branched stems that typically grow to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, compound racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are serrated and have a delicate, fern-like appearance.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
