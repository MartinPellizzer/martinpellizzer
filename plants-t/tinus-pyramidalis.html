Tinus pyramidalis

Tinus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Tinus pyramidalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
