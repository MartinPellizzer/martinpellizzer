Tephrocactus pulcherrimus

Tephrocactus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the Mexican beauty, is a species of cactus native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant during the spring and summer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
