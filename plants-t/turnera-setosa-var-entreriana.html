Turnera setosa var. entreriana

Turnera setosa var. entreriana, commonly known as the Argentine flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement, which are typical of the Turnera genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and flowers.
