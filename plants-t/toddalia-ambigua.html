Toddalia ambigua

Toddalia ambigua, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet or swampy areas, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces showy, pentagonal flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while the fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Toddalia ambigua plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
