Tamarix pallasii var. smyrnensis

Tamarix pallasii var. smyrnensis, commonly known as the Syrian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also known for its ability to stabilize soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
