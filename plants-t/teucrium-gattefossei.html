Teucrium gattefossei

Teucrium gattefossei, commonly known as the Corsican germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium gattefossei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
