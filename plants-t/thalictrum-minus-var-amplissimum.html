Thalictrum minus var. amplissimum

Thalictrum minus var. amplissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically compound, with multiple small leaflets arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces showy, clustered clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically beneficial plant for native landscapes.
