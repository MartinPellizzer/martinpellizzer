Tigridia alpestris subsp. alpestris

Tigridia alpestris subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine tiger flower, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Andes Mountains. It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting dark throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
