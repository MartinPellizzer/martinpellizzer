Trigonella retrorsa

Trigonella retrorsa, commonly known as the backward spur fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella retrorsa is adapted to survive in harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and sustainable agriculture.
