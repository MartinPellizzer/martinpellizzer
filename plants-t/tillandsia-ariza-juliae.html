Tillandsia ariza-juliae

Tillandsia ariza-juliae, commonly known as the Ariza-Julia air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant purple and red flowers, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
