Taraxacum sangilense

Taraxacum sangilense, commonly known as the Sangil dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, specifically in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive pappus-equipped seed head. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
