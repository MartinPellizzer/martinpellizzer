Thelypteris toganetra

Thelypteris toganetra, commonly known as the Toganetra holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines along the margins of the fronds.

Thelypteris toganetra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
