Triolena campii

Triolena campii, commonly known as the Campi's triolena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and a central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or grassland habitats. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Triolena campii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
