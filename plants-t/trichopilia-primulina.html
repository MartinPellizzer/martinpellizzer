Trichopilia primulina

Trichopilia primulina, commonly known as the small-flowered trichopilia, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trichopilia primulina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
