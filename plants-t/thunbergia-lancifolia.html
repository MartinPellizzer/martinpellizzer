Thunbergia lancifolia

Thunbergia lancifolia, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet, depending on the variety. These flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thunbergia lancifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
