Thymus praecox subsp. zygiformis

Thymus praecox subsp.

zygiformis, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and limestone areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in horticultural settings.
