Thymus serpyllum subsp. degenianus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

degenianus, commonly known as Degen's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features creeping stems and small, oval leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
