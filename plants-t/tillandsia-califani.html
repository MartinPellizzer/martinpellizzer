Tillandsia califani

Tillandsia califani, commonly known as the California air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the western United States.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture from the air. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
