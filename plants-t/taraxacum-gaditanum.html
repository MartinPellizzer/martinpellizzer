Taraxacum gaditanum

Taraxacum gaditanum, commonly known as the Spanish dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and spoon-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball with bristly hairs.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal areas.
