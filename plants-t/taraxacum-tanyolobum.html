Taraxacum tanyolobum

Taraxacum tanyolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into spherical, fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
