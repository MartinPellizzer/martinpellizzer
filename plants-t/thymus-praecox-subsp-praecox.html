Thymus praecox subsp. praecox

Thymus praecox subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe. It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its medicinal and culinary uses are well-documented, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
