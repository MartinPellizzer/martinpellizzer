Teucrium chamaedrys var. illyricum

Teucrium chamaedrys var. illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and emit a strong, mint-like scent when crushed.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
