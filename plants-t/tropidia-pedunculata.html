Tropidia pedunculata

Tropidia pedunculata, commonly known as the hanging lantern plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardening.
