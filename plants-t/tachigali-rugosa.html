Tachigali rugosa

Tachigali rugosa, commonly known as the rough tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Tachigali rugosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
