Thymus zygis subsp. gracilis

Thymus zygis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme aroma.

The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a culinary herb.
