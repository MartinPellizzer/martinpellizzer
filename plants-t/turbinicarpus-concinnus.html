Turbinicarpus concinnus

Turbinicarpus concinnus, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry climates.
