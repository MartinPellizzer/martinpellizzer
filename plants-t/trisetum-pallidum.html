Trisetum pallidum

Trisetum pallidum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with a pale blue or greenish hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small, spikelet-like clusters.

Trisetum pallidum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small vertebrates.
