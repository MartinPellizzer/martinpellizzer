Thalictrum macrostylum

Thalictrum macrostylum, commonly known as the showy meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thalictrum macrostylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
