Tsuga canadensis f. gracilis

Tsuga canadensis f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hemlock, is a rare variant of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like foliage and a more compact growth habit compared to the typical eastern hemlock. This form thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments. Its botanical features include elongated cones and a slower growth rate, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.

Due to its rarity, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
