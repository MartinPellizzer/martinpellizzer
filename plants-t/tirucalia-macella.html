Tirucalia macella

Tirucalia macella, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing hard, dark seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and furniture making.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
