Tristachya esculenta

Tristachya esculenta, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its wide, sword-shaped leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source by various indigenous communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
