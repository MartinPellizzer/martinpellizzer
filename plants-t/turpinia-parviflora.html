Turpinia parviflora

Turpinia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered turpinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within woodland habitats. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Turpinia parviflora is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
