Thalictrum minus subsp. saxatile

Thalictrum minus subsp.

saxatile, commonly known as the saxifrage meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided, fern-like foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
