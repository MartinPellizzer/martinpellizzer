Tragacantha fischeriana

Tragacantha fischeriana, commonly known as Fischer's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food additive. It thrives in rocky and dry environments, showing adaptability to arid conditions.

Its ecological importance includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitats.
