Teucrium scordium subsp. glabrescens

Teucrium scordium subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to other members of the species.
