Tripolium oliganthum

Tripolium oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It has small, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils.

Tripolium oliganthum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
