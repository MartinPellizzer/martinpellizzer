Trifolium brunellii

Trifolium brunellii, commonly known as Brunell's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Trifolium brunellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
