Tithymalus missouriensis

Tithymalus missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Tithymalus missouriensis is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various insects.
