Teucrium heterophyllum subsp. brevipilosum

Teucrium heterophyllum subsp.

brevipilosum, commonly known as the silky germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features slender, aromatic stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet to blue in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
