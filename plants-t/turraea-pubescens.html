Turraea pubescens

Turraea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy turraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves with a pubescent underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Turraea pubescens is valued for its ornamental potential and has traditional uses in some local communities.
