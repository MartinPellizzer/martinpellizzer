Telipogon auriculatus

Telipogon auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate telipogon, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves and delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in the cool, moist environments of cloud forests. The plant thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found growing on rocky substrates or among mosses. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of undisturbed ecosystems and its value in botanical research.
