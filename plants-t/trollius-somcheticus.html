Trollius somcheticus

Trollius somcheticus, commonly known as the Somchetic Globe Flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, globe-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Trollius somcheticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
