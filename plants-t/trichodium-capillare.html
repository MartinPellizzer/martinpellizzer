Trichodium capillare

Trichodium capillare, commonly known as the hair grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine, hairy texture that gives it its common name.

Trichodium capillare is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
