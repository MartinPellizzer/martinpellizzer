Timonius densiflorus

Timonius densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower timonius, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

Timonius densiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
