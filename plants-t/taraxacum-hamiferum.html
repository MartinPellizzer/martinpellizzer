Taraxacum hamiferum

Taraxacum hamiferum, commonly known as the European dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairless, and spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, inconspicuous yellow flower head, which develops into a ball of white seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum hamiferum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and digestive benefits.
