Torreyochloa pallida subsp. natans

Torreyochloa pallida subsp.

natans, commonly known as the floating grass, is a unique aquatic plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage that grows in dense mats on the surface of shallow, freshwater habitats.

The plant's leaves are typically narrow and slightly curled, allowing it to remain afloat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

This subspecies is important for stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
