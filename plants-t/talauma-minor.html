Talauma minor

Talauma minor, commonly known as the lesser talauma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Talauma minor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
