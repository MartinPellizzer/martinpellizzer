Tamarix pentandra subsp. tigrensis

Tamarix pentandra subsp.

tigrensis, commonly known as the Caucasian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
