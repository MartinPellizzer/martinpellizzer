Ticodendron incognitum

Ticodendron incognitum, commonly known as the unknown tree, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the dense rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the late summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Ticodendron incognitum is considered a critically endangered species.
