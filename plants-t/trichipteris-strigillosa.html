Trichipteris strigillosa

Trichipteris strigillosa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that forms dense, low-growing clumps with a fibrous root system. The plant's foliage consists of small, scale-like leaves that give it a velvety appearance. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Trichipteris strigillosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
