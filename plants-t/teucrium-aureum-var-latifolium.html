Teucrium aureum var. latifolium

Teucrium aureum var. latifolium, commonly known as golden germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
