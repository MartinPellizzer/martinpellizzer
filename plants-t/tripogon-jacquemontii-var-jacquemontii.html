Tripogon jacquemontii var. jacquemontii

Tripogon jacquemontii var. jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
