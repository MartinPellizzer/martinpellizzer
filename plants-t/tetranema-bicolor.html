Tetranema bicolor

Tetranema bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored threadplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems and small, opposite leaves that often display a striking two-color pattern. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
