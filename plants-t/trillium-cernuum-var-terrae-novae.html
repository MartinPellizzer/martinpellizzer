Trillium cernuum var. terrae-novae

Trillium cernuum var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Nova Scotian nodding trillium, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and Canada.

It features a single, downward-facing white flower with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Trillium genus. The plant has three broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It thrives in rich, moist soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat.
