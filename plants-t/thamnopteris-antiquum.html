Thamnopteris antiquum

Thamnopteris antiquum, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the underside of its fronds rather than flowers. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Thamnopteris antiquum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
