Thaspium trifoliatum f. atropurpureum

Thaspium trifoliatum f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leafed trifoliate thaspium, is a cultivar of the thaspium plant characterized by its striking purple foliage.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being lance-shaped and densely covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant color and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal.
