Trigonochilum centifrancum

Trigonochilum centifrancum, commonly known as the centifoliate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a highly branched column and a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
