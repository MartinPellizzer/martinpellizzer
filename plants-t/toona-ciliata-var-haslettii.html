Toona ciliata var. haslettii

Toona ciliata var. haslettii, commonly known as the Haslett's Toona, is a variant of the Toona ciliata species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
