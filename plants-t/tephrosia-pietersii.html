Tephrosia pietersii

Tephrosia pietersii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Tephrosia pietersii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
