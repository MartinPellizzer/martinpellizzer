Terebinthus pilosa

Terebinthus pilosa, commonly known as the hairy terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Terebinthus pilosa is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of Mediterranean flora.
