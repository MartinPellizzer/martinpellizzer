Tulipa porphyreochrysantha

Tulipa porphyreochrysantha, commonly known as the Porphyreochrysantha tulip, is a rare and striking species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from deep red to orange and yellow hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This tulip is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
