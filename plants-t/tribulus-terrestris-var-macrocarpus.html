Tribulus terrestris var. macrocarpus

Tribulus terrestris var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited goat's head, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is a prostrate, annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
