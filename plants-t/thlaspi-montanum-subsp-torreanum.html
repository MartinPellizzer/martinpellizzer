Thlaspi montanum subsp. torreanum

Thlaspi montanum subsp.

torreanum, commonly known as the Alpine cabbages, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a terminal cluster. Its leaves are typically glaucous and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
