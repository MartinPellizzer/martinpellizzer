Tropaeolum cuspidatum

Tropaeolum cuspidatum, commonly known as the spurred winter cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tropaeolum cuspidatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.
