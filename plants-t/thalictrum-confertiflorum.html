Thalictrum confertiflorum

Thalictrum confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Thalictrum confertiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
