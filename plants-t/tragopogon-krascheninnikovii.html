Tragopogon krascheninnikovii

Tragopogon krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets.

Tragopogon krascheninnikovii is adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and drought.
