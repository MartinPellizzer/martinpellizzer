Tabebuia micrantha

Tabebuia micrantha, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, gray bark and produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
