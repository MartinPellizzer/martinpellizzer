Trapa caucasica var. colchica

Trapa caucasica var. colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that form a rosette structure on the water surface. The plant produces distinctive spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for aquatic ecosystems and traditional use in certain regions.
