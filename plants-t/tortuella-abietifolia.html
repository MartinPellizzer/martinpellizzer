Tortuella abietifolia

Tortuella abietifolia, commonly known as the fir tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its distinctive, compound leaves.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
