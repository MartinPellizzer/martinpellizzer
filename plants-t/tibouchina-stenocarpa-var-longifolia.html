Tibouchina stenocarpa var. longifolia

Tibouchina stenocarpa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often longer than they are wide. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
