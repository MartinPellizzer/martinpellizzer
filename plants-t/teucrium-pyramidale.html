Teucrium pyramidale

Teucrium pyramidale, commonly known as upright germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Teucrium pyramidale is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
