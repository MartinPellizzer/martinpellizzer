Trichocline paraguayensis

Trichocline paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan trichocline, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Trichocline paraguayensis is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
