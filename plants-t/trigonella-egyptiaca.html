Trigonella egyptiaca

Trigonella egyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
