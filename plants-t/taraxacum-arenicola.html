Taraxacum arenicola

Taraxacum arenicola, commonly known as the sand dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sandy and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a wind-dispersed seed capsule. It is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing nectar for pollinators.
