Thlaspi septigerum

Thlaspi septigerum, commonly known as the nine-banded charlock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers with four petals. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Thlaspi septigerum is an annual plant that is widely distributed across Europe, Asia, and North America.
