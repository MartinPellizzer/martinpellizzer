Trollius pumilus var. kansuensis

Trollius pumilus var. kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu globeflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy layer. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
