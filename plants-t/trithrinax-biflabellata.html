Trithrinax biflabellata

Trithrinax biflabellata, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species of palm native to southeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and limestone areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually light green and have a distinctive bifurcated shape, giving it its scientific name.

Trithrinax biflabellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
