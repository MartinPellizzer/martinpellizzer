Trigonella kotschyi

Trigonella kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi senna, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by narrow, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Trigonella kotschyi is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
