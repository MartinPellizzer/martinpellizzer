Taraxacum livens

Taraxacum livens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that rises from the center of the rosette. Its flowers are yellow and form a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Taraxacum livens is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
