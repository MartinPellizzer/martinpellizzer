Tacamahaca

Tacamahaca, commonly known as *Lobelia tupa*, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tacamahaca is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its purported health benefits.
