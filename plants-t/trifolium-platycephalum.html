Trifolium platycephalum

Trifolium platycephalum, commonly known as the broad-headed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium platycephalum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports other plant growth.
