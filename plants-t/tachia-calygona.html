Tachia calygona

Tachia calygona, commonly known as the woolly tachia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
