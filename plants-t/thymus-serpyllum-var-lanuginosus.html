Thymus serpyllum var. lanuginosus

Thymus serpyllum var. lanuginosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink to pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
