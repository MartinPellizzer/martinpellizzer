Trichomanes dregei

Trichomanes dregei, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Trichomanes dregei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
