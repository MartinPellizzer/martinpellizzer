Tournefortia andina

Tournefortia andina, commonly known as the Andean tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tournefortia andina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
