Thymophylla anomala

Thymophylla anomala, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the remote highlands of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant produces tiny, pale green flowers that bloom during the summer months. Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a conservation priority.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to extreme environmental conditions and its role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
