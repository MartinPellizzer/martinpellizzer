Tinus fuscopilosa

Tinus fuscopilosa, commonly known as the yellow-flowered tinus, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
