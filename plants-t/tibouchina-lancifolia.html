Tibouchina lancifolia

Tibouchina lancifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its showy, violet to purple flowers are borne in terminal panicles and bloom in the spring and summer.

Tibouchina lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
