Taraxacum obtusiusculum

Taraxacum obtusiusculum, commonly known as the bluntleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with parachute-like seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is closely related to other dandelion species.
