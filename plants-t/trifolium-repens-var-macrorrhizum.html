Trifolium repens var. macrorrhizum

Trifolium repens var. macrorrhizum, commonly known as the broad-rooted white clover, is a variant of the common white clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plants.
