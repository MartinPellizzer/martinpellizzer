Thalictrum macranthum

Thalictrum macranthum, commonly known as the showy meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Thalictrum macranthum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
