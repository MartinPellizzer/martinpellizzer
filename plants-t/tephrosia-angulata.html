Tephrosia angulata

Tephrosia angulata, commonly known as the angular tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive angular shape, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tephrosia angulata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal practices.
