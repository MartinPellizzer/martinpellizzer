Tanacetum platyrachis

Tanacetum platyrachis, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branching stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and often covered with fine, soft hairs.

Tanacetum platyrachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
