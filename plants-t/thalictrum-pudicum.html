Thalictrum pudicum

Thalictrum pudicum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum pudicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
