Tali prainiana

Tali prainiana, commonly known as the Bornean wild orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the rainforest canopy. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Tali prainiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
