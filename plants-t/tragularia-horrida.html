Tragularia horrida

Tragularia horrida, commonly known as the horseradish plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically tubular and come in shades of red, purple, or white, often arranged in spikes.

Tragularia horrida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
