Tanarius longistipulatus

Tanarius longistipulatus, commonly known as the long-stipule tanarius, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Rutaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves with elongated stipules, which are often mistaken for leaves themselves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
