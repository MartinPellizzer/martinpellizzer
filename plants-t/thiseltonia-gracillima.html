Thiseltonia gracillima

Thiseltonia gracillima, commonly known as the slenderleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Thiseltonia gracillima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
