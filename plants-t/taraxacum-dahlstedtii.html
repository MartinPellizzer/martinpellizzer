Taraxacum dahlstedtii

Taraxacum dahlstedtii, commonly known as the Dahlstedt's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust taproot system. The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated edge with a hairy texture.

Taraxacum dahlstedtii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
