Thymelaea elliptica

Thymelaea elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense heads, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Thymelaea elliptica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
