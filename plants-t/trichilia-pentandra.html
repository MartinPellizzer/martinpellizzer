Trichilia pentandra

Trichilia pentandra, commonly known as African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces a fruit that contains a hard-shelled seed, which is the source of shea butter. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their various therapeutic properties.

The plant plays an important role in both ecological and economic systems in its native regions.
