Thiollierea neriifolia

Thiollierea neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and is adapted to grow in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a sought-after specimen in specialized horticulture.

However, due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered in the wild.
