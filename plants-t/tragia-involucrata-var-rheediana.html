Tragia involucrata var. rheediana

Tragia involucrata var. rheediana, commonly known as the hairy leaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
