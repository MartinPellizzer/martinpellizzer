Tillandsia paniculata var. fendleri

Tillandsia paniculata var. fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on cacti or other plants without soil. The plant features long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, with a dense covering of trichomes that help absorb moisture from the air. It produces striking clusters of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer or early fall.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is well-suited for indoor cultivation with proper air circulation and occasional misting.
