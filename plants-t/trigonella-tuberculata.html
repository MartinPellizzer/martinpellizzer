Trigonella tuberculata

Trigonella tuberculata, commonly known as the Indian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, aromatic seeds used in traditional cuisines.

Trigonella tuberculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its potential health benefits.
