Tillandsia pueblensis var. glabrior

Tillandsia pueblensis var. glabrior, commonly known as the Mexican air plant, is a bromeliad native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is a tillandsia species that exhibits a compact rosette structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves. Its foliage is typically grayish-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
