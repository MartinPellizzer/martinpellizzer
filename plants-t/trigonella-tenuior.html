Trigonella tenuior

Trigonella tenuior, commonly known as slender sainfoin, is a low-growing annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and open areas, contributing to soil nitrogen through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
