Tephrosia ensifolia

Tephrosia ensifolia, commonly known as the sickleleaf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with sickle-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by legume pods containing several seeds.

Tephrosia ensifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
