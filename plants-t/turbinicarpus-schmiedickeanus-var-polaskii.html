Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. polaskii

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. polaskii, commonly known as the Polaski's turbinaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
