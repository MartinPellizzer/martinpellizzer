Tsuga dura

Tsuga dura, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Tsuga, which includes other firs such as the Douglas fir. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are typically cylindrical and contain two-winged seeds.

Tsuga dura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
