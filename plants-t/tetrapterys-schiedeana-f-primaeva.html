Tetrapterys schiedeana f. primaeva

Tetrapterys schiedeana f. primaeva, commonly known as the early blooming tetrapterys, is a unique variant of the tetrapterys genus.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with four distinct lobes, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming earlier than most other members of the genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
