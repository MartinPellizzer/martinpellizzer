Turnera lamiifolia

Turnera lamiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, known for its showy, colorful flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking orange-red bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Turnera lamiifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
