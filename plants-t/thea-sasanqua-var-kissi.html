Thea sasanqua var. kissi

Thea sasanqua var. kissi, commonly known as the Kissi camellia, is a variant of the camellia species native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
