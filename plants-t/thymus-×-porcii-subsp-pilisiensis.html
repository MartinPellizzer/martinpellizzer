Thymus × porcii subsp. pilisiensis

Thymus × porcii subsp.

pilisiensis, commonly known as the Pilis thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Hungary. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties and ornamental value have made it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
