Thunbergia fragrans subsp. hainanensis

Thunbergia fragrans subsp.

hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern China. It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, often growing along trellises or supports. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the flowers are typically pale yellow with a white or pinkish hue.
