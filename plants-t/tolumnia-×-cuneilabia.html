Tolumnia × cuneilabia

Tolumnia × cuneilabia, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Tolumnia cuneilabia and Tolumnia tenuis.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, with a unique, intricate pattern on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
