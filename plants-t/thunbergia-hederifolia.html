Thunbergia hederifolia

Thunbergia hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble those of ivy, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.

Thunbergia hederifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
