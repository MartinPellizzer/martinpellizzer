Trevirana grandiflora

Trevirana grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flower vine, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often in vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has long, climbing stems that can grow over 10 meters in length, supported by aerial roots that anchor it to trees. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in humid environments.

Trevirana grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
