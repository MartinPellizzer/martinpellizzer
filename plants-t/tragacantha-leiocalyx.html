Tragacantha leiocalyx

Tragacantha leiocalyx, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
