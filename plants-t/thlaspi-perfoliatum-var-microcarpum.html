Thlaspi perfoliatum var. microcarpum

Thlaspi perfoliatum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are rounded and often have a hairy texture, while the stem is usually leafless. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that contains several small seeds.
