Trillium amabile

Trillium amabile, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three white petals, three sepals, and three heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, producing a single white flower that is often visited by pollinators such as bees.

Trillium amabile is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
