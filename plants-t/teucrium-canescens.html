Teucrium canescens

Teucrium canescens, commonly known as the gray germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in xeriscaping and traditional herbal medicine.

Teucrium canescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
