Trifolium pratense var. serotinum

Trifolium pratense var. serotinum, commonly known as red clover late variety, is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pink to purple flowers.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. This variety is known for its late blooming period, typically occurring in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It is often used in forage, as a cover crop, and in traditional herbal medicine.
