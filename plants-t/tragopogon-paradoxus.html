Tragopogon paradoxus

Tragopogon paradoxus, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with long, bristly rays. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces a long, slender flowering stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Tragopogon paradoxus is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
