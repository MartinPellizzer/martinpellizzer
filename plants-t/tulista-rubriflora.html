Tulista rubriflora

Tulista rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered tulista, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tulista rubriflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
