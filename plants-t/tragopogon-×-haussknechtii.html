Tragopogon × haussknechtii

Tragopogon × haussknechtii, commonly known as the German dandelion, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Tragopogon dubius and Tragopogon porrifolius.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions across Europe and North America. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
