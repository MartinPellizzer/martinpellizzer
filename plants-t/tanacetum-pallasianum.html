Tanacetum pallasianum

Tanacetum pallasianum, commonly known as Pallas's tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are aromatic and have a camphor-like scent, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
