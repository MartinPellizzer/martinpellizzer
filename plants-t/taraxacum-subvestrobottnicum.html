Taraxacum subvestrobottnicum

Taraxacum subvestrobottnicum, commonly known as the Baltic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Baltic region of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are spoon-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds on the wind.

This species is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
