Trigonella fimbriata

Trigonella fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriated fenugreek, is an annual herb belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

Trigonella fimbriata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
