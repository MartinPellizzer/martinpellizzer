Trillium erythrocarpum

Trillium erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited trillium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces a distinctive red, fleshy fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found in rich, acidic soils.

Trillium erythrocarpum is a slow-growing plant with a long lifespan, sometimes living for several decades.
