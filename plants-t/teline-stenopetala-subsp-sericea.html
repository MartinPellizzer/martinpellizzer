Teline stenopetala subsp. sericea

Teline stenopetala subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky teline, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
