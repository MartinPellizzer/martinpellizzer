Trifolium thessalonicum

Trifolium thessalonicum, commonly known as the Thessaloniki clover, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium thessalonicum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
