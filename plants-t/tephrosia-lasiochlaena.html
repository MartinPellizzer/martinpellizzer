Tephrosia lasiochlaena

Tephrosia lasiochlaena, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, clustered leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod fruits containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
