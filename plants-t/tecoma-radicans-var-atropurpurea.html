Tecoma radicans var. atropurpurea

Tecoma radicans var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple flame tree, is a fast-growing, deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical climates.
