Triopterys symplocifolia

Triopterys symplocifolia, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen tree, is a species native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows as a tall shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a drupe, contributing to its ecological and horticultural significance.
