Trigonostemon polyanthus var. lychnos

Trigonostemon polyanthus var. lychnos, commonly known as the golden flame hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
