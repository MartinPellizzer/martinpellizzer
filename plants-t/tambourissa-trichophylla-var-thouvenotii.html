Tambourissa trichophylla var. thouvenotii

Tambourissa trichophylla var. thouvenotii, commonly known as the Tuareg's beard, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the arid regions of the Sahara.

It is characterized by its long, filamentous leaves that resemble a beard, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the brief rainy season. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes.
