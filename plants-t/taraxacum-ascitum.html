Taraxacum ascitum

Taraxacum ascitum, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head. The plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and other open habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
