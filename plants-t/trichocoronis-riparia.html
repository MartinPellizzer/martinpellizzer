Trichocoronis riparia

Trichocoronis riparia, commonly known as the water sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an effective colonizer of disturbed wetlands.

Trichocoronis riparia plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
