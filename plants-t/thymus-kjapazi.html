Thymus kjapazi

Thymus kjapazi, commonly known as Kjapazi thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.
