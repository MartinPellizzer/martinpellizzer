Touchardia konaensis

Touchardia konaensis, commonly known as Kona giant taro, is a rare and unique plant native to the Hawaiian island of Kona.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant is known for its rapid growth and can reach heights of up to 10 feet, making it one of the tallest taro species. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
