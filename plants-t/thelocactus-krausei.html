Thelocactus krausei

Thelocactus krausei, commonly known as the Mexican pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Thelocactus krausei is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
