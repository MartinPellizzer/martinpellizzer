Taxus baccata var. dovastonii-aureovariegata

Taxus baccata var. dovastonii-aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden-variegated yew, is a cultivar of the European yew tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring bright yellow and green patterns on its leaves. The plant is evergreen, with a slow growth rate and a pyramidal shape. It is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
