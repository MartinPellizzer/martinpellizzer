Tetraneuris acaulis var. caespitosa

Tetraneuris acaulis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered tetraneuris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant forms dense, compact clumps with small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal maintenance once established.
