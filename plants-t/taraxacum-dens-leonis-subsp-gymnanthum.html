Taraxacum dens-leonis subsp. gymnanthum

Taraxacum dens-leonis subsp.

gymnanthum, commonly known as the dense-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single, elongated flower head composed of yellow ray florets surrounded by a spherical, hollow receptacle. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
