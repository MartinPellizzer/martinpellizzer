Tristellateia madagascariensis var. parvifolia

Tristellateia madagascariensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in star-like clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and collectors.
