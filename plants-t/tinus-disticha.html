Tinus disticha

Tinus disticha, commonly known as the two-branched tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters.

Tinus disticha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal areas.
