Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. trapezunticum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

trapezunticum, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
