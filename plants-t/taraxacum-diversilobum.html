Taraxacum diversilobum

Taraxacum diversilobum, commonly known as the Pacific dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in coastal and mountainous regions of California and Oregon. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves with toothed or lobed margins. It produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of ecological conditions.
