Tylloma glabratum var. brachylepis

Tylloma glabratum var. brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tylloma, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
