Taraxacum trilobifolium

Taraxacum trilobifolium, commonly known as the three-lobed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, three-lobed leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that terminates in a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
