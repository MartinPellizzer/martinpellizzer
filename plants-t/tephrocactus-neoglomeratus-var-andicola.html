Tephrocactus neoglomeratus var. andicola

Tephrocactus neoglomeratus var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean clustered cactus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of cylindrical stems. The plant features areoles covered with white, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in spring, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
