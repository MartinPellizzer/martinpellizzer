Tetramerista glabra var. sagittata

Tetramerista glabra var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf tetramerista, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, lanceolate leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
