Trisetum handelii

Trisetum handelii, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike or a few spikes, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Trisetum handelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
