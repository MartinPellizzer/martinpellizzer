Thamnochortus giganteus

Thamnochortus giganteus, commonly known as the giant pincushion plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous dense, spiky flower heads. The plant features a woody base with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thamnochortus giganteus is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient and visually impressive succulent.
