Trichomanes alatum var. attenuatum

Trichomanes alatum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Huperziaceae).

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant features a creeping rhizome with numerous short, upright branches, and its leaves are usually glossy and have a feathery appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
