Tradescantia hertweckiae

Tradescantia hertweckiae, commonly known as the Hertweck's spiderwort, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant that supports local pollinators and contributes to ecological diversity.
