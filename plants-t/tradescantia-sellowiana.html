Tradescantia sellowiana

Tradescantia sellowiana, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia sellowiana is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
