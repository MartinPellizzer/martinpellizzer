Trillium grandiflorum f. lirioides

Trillium grandiflorum f. lirioides, commonly known as the lily-like trillium, is a variant of the white trillium native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its three broad, white petals that resemble lilies, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has a distinctive three-part leaf structure. It blooms in early spring, producing a single, showy flower with a yellow anther.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
