Thalictrum tenerifolium

Thalictrum tenerifolium, commonly known as the Canary Island meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and often covered in a soft, silky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are arranged in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum tenerifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
