Typha × glauca

Typha × glauca, commonly known as the hybrid cattail, is a wetland plant that results from the cross between Typha latifolia and Typha angustifolia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and a distinctive cylindrical flower spike that matures into a brown, woolly structure. This plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Typha × glauca plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and water filtration in aquatic ecosystems.
