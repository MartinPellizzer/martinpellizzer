Trichopteryx figarii

Trichopteryx figarii, commonly known as the fig tree mite, is a small arachnid belonging to the family Tetranychidae.

It is primarily found on the leaves of various fig tree species, where it feeds on plant sap, causing minor damage to the foliage. The mite's life cycle includes several stages, such as egg, protonymph, deutonymph, and adult, with females laying eggs on the undersides of leaves. Its presence can sometimes lead to the development of a characteristic rusty-colored webbing on the leaves.

Trichopteryx figarii plays a role in the ecological dynamics of fig trees, contributing to the natural balance of the ecosystem.
