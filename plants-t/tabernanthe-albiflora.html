Tabernanthe albiflora

Tabernanthe albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint fragrance. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical pod that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
