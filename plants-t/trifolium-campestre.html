Trifolium campestre

Trifolium campestre, commonly known as common clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Trifolium campestre is also known for its ability to withstand various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
