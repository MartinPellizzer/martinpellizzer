Tibouchina lepidota

Tibouchina lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue bracts that resemble petals. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. Tibouchina lepidota is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant bracts.
