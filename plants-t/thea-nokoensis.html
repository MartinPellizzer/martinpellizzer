Thea nokoensis

Thea nokoensis, commonly known as the Noko's tea plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Theaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes and traditional herbal uses.
