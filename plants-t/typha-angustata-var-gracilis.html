Typha angustata var. gracilis

Typha angustata var. gracilis, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant produces small, brown, canoe-shaped seeds that are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread. Its rhizomes form dense underground networks, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

This variety is often distinguished from other cattail species by its narrower leaves and slightly different flowering structure.
