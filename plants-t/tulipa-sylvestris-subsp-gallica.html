Tulipa sylvestris subsp. gallica

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the woodland tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of Central and Eastern Europe. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The subspecies is closely related to other tulip species and is known for its resilience in woodland habitats.
