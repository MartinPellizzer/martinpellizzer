Trigonidium equitans

Trigonidium equitans, commonly known as the horsebean plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils.

Trigonidium equitans is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
