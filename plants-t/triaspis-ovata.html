Triaspis ovata

Triaspis ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf triaspis, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its ovate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
