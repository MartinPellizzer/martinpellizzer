Tragacantha leontonyx

Tragacantha leontonyx, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a valuable gum resin, which has been historically used in medicine and as a binder in various industries.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils with low moisture.
