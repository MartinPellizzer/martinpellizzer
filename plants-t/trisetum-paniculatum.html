Trisetum paniculatum

Trisetum paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like appearance.

Trisetum paniculatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
