Tragacantha cancellata

Tragacantha cancellata, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus cancellatus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
