Tamarix hispida

Tamarix hispida, commonly known as the hispid tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Tamarix hispida is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability, making it a common species in desert landscapes.
