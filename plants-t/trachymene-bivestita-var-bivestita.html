Trachymene bivestita var. bivestita

Trachymene bivestita var. bivestita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that gives them a distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
