Tilia incisodentata

Tilia incisodentata, commonly known as the serrated-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia incisodentata is often found in moist, fertile soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
