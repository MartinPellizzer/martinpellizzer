Teucrium commune var. flaviflorum

Teucrium commune var. flaviflorum, commonly known as yellowflower germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in woodland edges, slopes, and disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
