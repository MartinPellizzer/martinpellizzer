Thuja orientalis var. flagelliformis

Thuja orientalis var. flagelliformis, commonly known as the Chinese thuja or the weeping thuja, is a variant of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are a deep green color, and it produces small, spherical cones. It is native to China and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is relatively slow-growing and prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
