Tripogon bimucronatus

Tripogon bimucronatus, commonly known as the double-pointed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet with two distinct bracts, giving it its common name.

Tripogon bimucronatus is often found in dune environments and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
