Tolpis barbata subsp. umbellata

Tolpis barbata subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbellate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
