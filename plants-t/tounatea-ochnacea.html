Tounatea ochnacea

Tounatea ochnacea, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Tiliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are highly prized for their flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
