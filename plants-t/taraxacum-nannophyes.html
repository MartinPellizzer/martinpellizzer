Taraxacum nannophyes

Taraxacum nannophyes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a bright yellow flowering head that develops into a distinctive pappus-covered seed ball. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is a perennial herb that reproduces through both sexual and vegetative means.

The plant's medicinal properties have been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects.
