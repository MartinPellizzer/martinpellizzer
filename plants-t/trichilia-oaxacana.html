Trichilia oaxacana

Trichilia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan chicle tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable wood and historical use in the production of chewing gum.
