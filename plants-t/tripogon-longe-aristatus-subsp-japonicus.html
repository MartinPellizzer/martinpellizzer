Tripogon longe-aristatus subsp. japonicus

Tripogon longe-aristatus subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese feather grass, is a grass species native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive, feathery inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
