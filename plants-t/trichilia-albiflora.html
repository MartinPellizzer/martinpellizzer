Trichilia albiflora

Trichilia albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered trichilia, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
