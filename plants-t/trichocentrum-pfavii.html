Trichocentrum pfavii

Trichocentrum pfavii, commonly known as the Pfeiffer's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Trichocentrum pfavii requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive in cultivation.
