Thymus pulegioides subsp. carniolicus

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

carniolicus, commonly known as Carpathian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and strong mint-like fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
