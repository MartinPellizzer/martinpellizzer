Tisonia rubescens

Tisonia rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed tisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming during the warmer months. Tisonia rubescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or dense undergrowth.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species within its ecological niche.
