Tragacantha carinata

Tragacantha carinata, commonly known as the thorny tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its characteristic, woody, ribbed seed pods. Tragacantha carinata is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of a natural gum.
