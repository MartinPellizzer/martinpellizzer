Tinantia sprucei

Tinantia sprucei, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tinantia sprucei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
