Tripinna tripinnata

Tripinna tripinnata, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, which bloom in the spring. Tripinna tripinnata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
