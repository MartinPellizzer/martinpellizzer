Tragopogon elatus

Tragopogon elatus, commonly known as the tall goat's beard, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its tall, spindly stalk and yellow-orange ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces a distinctive seed head that resembles a crown.

Tragopogon elatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
