Thunbergia fryeri

Thunbergia fryeri, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Thunbergia fryeri is a climbing plant that can reach heights of several meters if left unpruned.
