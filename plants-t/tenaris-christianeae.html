Tenaris christianeae

Tenaris christianeae, commonly known as the Christiane's tenaris, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
