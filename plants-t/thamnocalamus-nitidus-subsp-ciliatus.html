Thamnocalamus nitidus subsp. ciliatus

Thamnocalamus nitidus subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the ciliated bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered with fine, hairy structures, particularly along the nodes.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the subspecies. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is native.
