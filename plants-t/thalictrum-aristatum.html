Thalictrum aristatum

Thalictrum aristatum, commonly known as the arrow-leaved meadow-rue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are finely divided, with a distinctive arrowhead shape, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its delicate appearance and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
