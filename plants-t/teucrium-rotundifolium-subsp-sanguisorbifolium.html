Teucrium rotundifolium subsp. sanguisorbifolium

Teucrium rotundifolium subsp.

sanguisorbifolium, commonly known as the scarlet germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its rounded, gray-green leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
