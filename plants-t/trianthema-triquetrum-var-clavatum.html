Trianthema triquetrum var. clavatum

Trianthema triquetrum var. clavatum, commonly known as the clustered false flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed capsules that contribute to its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
