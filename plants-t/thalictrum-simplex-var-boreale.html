Thalictrum simplex var. boreale

Thalictrum simplex var. boreale, commonly known as the northern meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that create a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological diversity.
