Thymus kerneri

Thymus kerneri, commonly known as Kern's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
