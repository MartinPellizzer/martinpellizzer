Teucrium × mailhoi

Teucrium × mailhoi, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have also been historically noted in traditional herbal remedies.
