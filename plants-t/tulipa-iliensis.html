Tulipa iliensis

Tulipa iliensis, commonly known as the Ili tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa in the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climates, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The Ili tulip is also noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in arid environments.
