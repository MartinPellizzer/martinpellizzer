Triticum durum var. schimperi

Triticum durum var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian durum wheat, is a variant of durum wheat native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its semi-dwarf stature and high protein content, which contributes to its strong gluten quality. The plant produces compact spikes with hard, straw-colored grains that are well-suited for pasta and semolina production. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soils for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and resilience to local environmental conditions.
