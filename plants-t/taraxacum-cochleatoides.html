Taraxacum cochleatoides

Taraxacum cochleatoides, commonly known as the spiral dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
