Triticum vulgare var. wernerianum

Triticum vulgare var. wernerianum, commonly known as the Werner's wheatgrass, is a variant of common wheat that is primarily cultivated for its nutritional value and use in forage.

It is a cool-season grass that typically grows in temperate climates and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets, which are the reproductive structures of the plant. It is known for its high protein content and is often used in livestock feed and as a health food in human diets.

This variety is also valued for its resilience to various environmental conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
