Tetragonolobus purpureus subsp. palaestinus

Tetragonolobus purpureus subsp.

palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestinian tetragonolobus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Palestine and parts of Israel. It is a perennial herb characterized by its quadrangular stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and historical significance in the region.
