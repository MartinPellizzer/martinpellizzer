Teucrium mesanidum

Teucrium mesanidum, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Teucrium mesanidum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
