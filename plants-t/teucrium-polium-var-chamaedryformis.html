Teucrium polium var. chamaedryformis

Teucrium polium var. chamaedryformis, commonly known as the creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
