Tulipa suaveolens

Tulipa suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive sweet fragrance. The plant typically blooms in spring, with its leaves emerging from a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like foliage. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
