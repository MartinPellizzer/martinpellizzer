Tragus muricatus

Tragus muricatus, commonly known as the rough tragus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Tragus muricatus is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sunny conditions.
