Tragacantha oleifolia

Tragacantha oleifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, leathery leaves, and the production of a valuable gum resin. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the resin has been historically used in various industrial applications.

Tragacantha oleifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
