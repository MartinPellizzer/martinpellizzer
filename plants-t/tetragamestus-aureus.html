Tetragamestus aureus

Tetragamestus aureus, commonly known as the golden-eyed flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Tetragamestus aureus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and moderate drought tolerance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
