Typha truxillensis

Typha truxillensis, commonly known as the Amazonian cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower heads and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its rhizomes are extensive and help stabilize the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Typha truxillensis also plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
