Tessaria plucheoides

Tessaria plucheoides, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Tessaria plucheoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
