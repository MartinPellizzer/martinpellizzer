Therocistus praecox

Therocistus praecox, commonly known as the early thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
