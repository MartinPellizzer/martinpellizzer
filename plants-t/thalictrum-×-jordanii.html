Thalictrum × jordanii

Thalictrum × jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's meadow-rue, is a hybrid plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in the western United States and southern British Columbia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
