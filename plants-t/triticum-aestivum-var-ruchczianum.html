Triticum aestivum var. ruchczianum

Triticum aestivum var. ruchczianum, commonly known as Ruchczian wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic traits.

It is primarily found in specific regions of Eastern Europe and is notable for its adaptation to local environmental conditions. The plant exhibits distinct morphological features, including variations in spike structure and grain size compared to standard wheat varieties. It is often studied for its potential in agricultural research and breeding programs.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for improving wheat resilience and yield.
