Taraxacum ulogonioides

Taraxacum ulogonioides, commonly known as the Ulogon-like dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white hairs. Its flower head consists of a single yellow disk surrounded by white ray florets, typical of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
