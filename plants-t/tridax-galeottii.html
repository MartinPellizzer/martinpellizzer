Tridax galeottii

Tridax galeottii, commonly known as the common tridax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a composite head. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Tridax galeottii is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
