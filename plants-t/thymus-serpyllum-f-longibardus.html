Thymus serpyllum f. longibardus

Thymus serpyllum f. longibardus, commonly known as the long-stemmed thyme, is a botanical variant of the common thyme.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and compact, fragrant foliage. This subspecies typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. The leaves are small, oval-shaped, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to its parent species.
