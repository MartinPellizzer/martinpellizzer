Taraxacum font-queri

Taraxacum font-queri, commonly known as the Fontqueri's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators like bees and flies, contributing to its reproductive success.

The plant is perennial, with a deep taproot that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.
