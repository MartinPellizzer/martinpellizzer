Trisetum spicatum var. alaskanum

Trisetum spicatum var. alaskanum, commonly known as Alaska bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
