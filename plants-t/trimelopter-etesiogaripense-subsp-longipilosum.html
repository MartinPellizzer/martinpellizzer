Trimelopter etesiogaripense subsp. longipilosum

Trimelopter etesiogaripense subsp.

longipilosum, commonly known as the long-hairy trimelopter, is a species native to the humid forests of Central Africa. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems covered in long, soft hairs.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
