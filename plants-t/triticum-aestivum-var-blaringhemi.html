Triticum aestivum var. blaringhemi

Triticum aestivum var. blaringhemi, commonly known as the Blaringhem wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically exhibits a compact growth habit with slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often more elongated compared to standard wheat varieties, with well-developed glumes and lemmas. Its florets are usually larger and more open, contributing to distinct floral morphology.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to specific environmental conditions, making it a subject of interest in agricultural research.
