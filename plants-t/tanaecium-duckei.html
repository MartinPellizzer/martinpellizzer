Tanaecium duckei

Tanaecium duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant typically grows in open areas and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Tanaecium duckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
