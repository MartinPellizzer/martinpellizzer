Triainolepis africana subsp. africana

Triainolepis africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African triainolepis, is a member of the Acanthaceae family. It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
