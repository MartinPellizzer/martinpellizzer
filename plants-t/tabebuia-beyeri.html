Tabebuia beyeri

Tabebuia beyeri, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in spring. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded crown, with smooth, greyish bark that exfoliates in patches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
