Thermopsis rhombifolia var. rhombifolia

Thermopsis rhombifolia var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
