Tragacantha turkestanica

Tragacantha turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkistan tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing small, hard seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its latex, similar to that of other tragacanth species, has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
