Turnera clauseniana

Turnera clauseniana, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.
