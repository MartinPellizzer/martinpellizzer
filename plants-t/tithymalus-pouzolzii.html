Tithymalus pouzolzii

Tithymalus pouzolzii, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Tithymalus pouzolzii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
