Tabernaemontana dichotoma

Tabernaemontana dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous crownflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, where the stems split into two equal parts, creating a unique dichotomous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habit, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
