Thymus stojanovii

Thymus stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanovii thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny exposures. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, similar to other thyme species.
