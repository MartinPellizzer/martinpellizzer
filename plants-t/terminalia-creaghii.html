Terminalia creaghii

Terminalia creaghii, commonly known as African snowball, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. Terminalia creaghii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
