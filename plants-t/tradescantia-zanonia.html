Tradescantia zanonia

Tradescantia zanonia, commonly known as the Zanonia spiderwort, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often variegated, with striking combinations of green, white, and pink, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Tradescantia zanonia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden beds or containers.
