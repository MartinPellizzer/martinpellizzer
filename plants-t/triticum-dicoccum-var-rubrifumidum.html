Triticum dicoccum var. rubrifumidum

Triticum dicoccum var. rubrifumidum, commonly known as black rye, is a variety of rye characterized by its dark, almost black grain.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia and is known for its robust growth in cooler climates. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, fiber, and essential minerals.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional baking and as a forage crop.
