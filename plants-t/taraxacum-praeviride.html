Taraxacum praeviride

Taraxacum praeviride, commonly known as the greenish dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum praeviride is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
