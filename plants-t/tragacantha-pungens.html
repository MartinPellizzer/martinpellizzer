Tragacantha pungens

Tragacantha pungens, commonly known as the spiked tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, thorned stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are known for their mucilage, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Tragacantha pungens is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments.
