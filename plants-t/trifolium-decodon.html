Trifolium decodon

Trifolium decodon, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Trifolium decodon has a taproot system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It blooms in the spring and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
