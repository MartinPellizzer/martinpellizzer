Tordylium cyrenaicum

Tordylium cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Cyprus bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Tordylium cyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
