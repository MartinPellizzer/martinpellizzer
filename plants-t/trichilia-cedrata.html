Trichilia cedrata

Trichilia cedrata, commonly known as African plum or cedrela, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.

Trichilia cedrata also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
