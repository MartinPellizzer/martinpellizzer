Trifolium pennyanum

Trifolium pennyanum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania clover, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Trifolium pennyanum is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation and soil stabilization.
