Tulipa carinata violacea

Tulipa carinata violacea, commonly known as the violet-collared tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive collared base. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its cold hardiness, making it suitable for temperate climates. It blooms in early spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is valued for its unique coloration and adaptability to various soil types.
