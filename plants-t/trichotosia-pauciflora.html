Trichotosia pauciflora

Trichotosia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered trichotosia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Trichotosia pauciflora is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
