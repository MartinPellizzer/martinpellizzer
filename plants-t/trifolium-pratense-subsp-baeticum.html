Trifolium pratense subsp. baeticum

Trifolium pratense subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican red clover, is a subspecies of red clover native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets, and small purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
