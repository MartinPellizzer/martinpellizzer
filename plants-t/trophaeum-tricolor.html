Trophaeum tricolor

Trophaeum tricolor, commonly known as the three-color bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking three-colored flowers, which typically display shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Trophaeum tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
