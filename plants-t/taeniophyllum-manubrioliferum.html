Taeniophyllum manubrioliferum

Taeniophyllum manubrioliferum, commonly known as the golden rosette, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen for botanical gardens and enthusiasts of rare plants.
