Triticum dicoccum var. rubrimuticum

Triticum dicoccum var. rubrimuticum, commonly known as black rye, is a botanical variety of the species Triticum dicoccum, which is a type of wild rye.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black grains and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant has a robust root system and produces tall, slender stalks with spikelets that bear the grain. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and historical use in traditional agriculture and forage.
