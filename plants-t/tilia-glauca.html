Tilia glauca

Tilia glauca, commonly known as the white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, winged seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, often peeling in thin layers.

Tilia glauca is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, open habitats.
