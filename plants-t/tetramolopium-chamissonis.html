Tetramolopium chamissonis

Tetramolopium chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Tetramolopium chamissonis is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
