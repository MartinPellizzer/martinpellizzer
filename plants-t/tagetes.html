Tagetes

Tagetes, commonly known as marigolds, are flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

They are native to the Americas and are characterized by their vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like blooms. Tagetes are known for their strong scent, which can repel certain pests.

They are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and companion planting benefits.
