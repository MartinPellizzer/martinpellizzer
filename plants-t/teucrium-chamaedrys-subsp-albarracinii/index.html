Teucrium Chamaedrys Subsp. Albarracinii

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. albarracinii is a rare and lesser-known variant of the common wall germander. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. This subspecies is native to specific regions in southern Europe, where it grows in limestone soils. Its white and pale blue flowers make it a striking addition to alpine and mountainous landscapes. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and often overlooked in botanical studies.
