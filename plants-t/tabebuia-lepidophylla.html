Tabebuia lepidophylla

Tabebuia lepidophylla, commonly known as the sacred flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree has a distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown and smooth, grayish bark that exudes a fragrant resin when cut. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its dramatic floral displays.
