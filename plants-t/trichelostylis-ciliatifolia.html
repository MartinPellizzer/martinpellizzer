Trichelostylis ciliatifolia

Trichelostylis ciliatifolia, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, and its showy flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Trichelostylis ciliatifolia is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
