Tropaeolum polyphyllum var. gracile

Tropaeolum polyphyllum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender garden sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, edible leaves that are tender and slightly sour in taste. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both culinary and aesthetic benefits.
