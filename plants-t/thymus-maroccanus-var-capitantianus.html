Thymus maroccanus var. capitantianus

Thymus maroccanus var. capitantianus, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact, mat-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
