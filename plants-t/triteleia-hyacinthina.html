Triteleia hyacinthina

Triteleia hyacinthina, commonly known as the blue owl's eye, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that resemble the eyes of an owl, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Triteleia hyacinthina is also valued for its ability to naturalize and return year after year in suitable climates.
