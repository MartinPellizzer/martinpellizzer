Teucrium pseudopitys

Teucrium pseudopitys, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Teucrium pseudopitys is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
