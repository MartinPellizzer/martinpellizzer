Thouarsiora littoralis

Thouarsiora littoralis, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. Its leaves are usually broad and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Thouarsiora littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
