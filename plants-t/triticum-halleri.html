Triticum halleri

Triticum halleri, commonly known as alpine barley, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration.

Triticum halleri is often found in high-altitude habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
