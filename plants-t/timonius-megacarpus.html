Timonius megacarpus

Timonius megacarpus, commonly known as the giant fruit-bearing plant, is a unique species characterized by its large, fleshy fruits that can weigh several kilograms.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant's leaves are broad and glossy, often reaching several feet in length, while its stems are woody and sturdy. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it blooms throughout the year in suitable conditions.

The fruit, which is edible, has a thick rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp that is highly valued in local cuisines.
