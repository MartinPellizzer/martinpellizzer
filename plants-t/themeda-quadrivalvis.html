Themeda quadrivalvis

Themeda quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-winged grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-winged seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense growth habit that forms a low, compact tuft.

Themeda quadrivalvis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
