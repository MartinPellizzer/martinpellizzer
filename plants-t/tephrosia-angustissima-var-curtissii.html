Tephrosia angustissima var. curtissii

Tephrosia angustissima var. curtissii, commonly known as narrowleaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in small, pod-like fruits, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
