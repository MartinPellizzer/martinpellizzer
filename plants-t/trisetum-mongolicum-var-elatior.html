Trisetum mongolicum var. elatior

Trisetum mongolicum var. elatior, commonly known as the greater Mongolian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
