Terebraria apiculata

Terebraria apiculata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
