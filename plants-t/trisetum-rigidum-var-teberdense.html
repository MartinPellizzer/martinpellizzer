Trisetum rigidum var. teberdense

Trisetum rigidum var. teberdense, commonly known as the Teberdian rigid sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, stiff stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
