Thelypteris filix-femina

Thelypteris filix-femina, commonly known as the female fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris filix-femina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
