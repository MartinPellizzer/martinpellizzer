Thymus mongolicus var. leucanthus

Thymus mongolicus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the Mongolian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces compact clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
