Trifolium burchellianum

Trifolium burchellianum, commonly known as Burchell's clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Burchell's clover is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
