Trichilia ekebergia

Trichilia ekebergia, commonly known as African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine.

Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in furniture making.
