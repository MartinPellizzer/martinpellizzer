Terminalia glabrata var. brownii

Terminalia glabrata var. brownii, commonly known as the brown-leaf Terminalia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Combretaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
