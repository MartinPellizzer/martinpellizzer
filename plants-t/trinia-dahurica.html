Trinia dahurica

Trinia dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trinia dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
