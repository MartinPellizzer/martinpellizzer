Tamaulipa azurea

Tamaulipa azurea, commonly known as the Tamaulipan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Tamaulipas region of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Tamaulipa azurea is considered a rare and endangered species.
