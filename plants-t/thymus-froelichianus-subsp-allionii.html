Thymus froelichianus subsp. allionii

Thymus froelichianus subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the Allion's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties, often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
