Tarasa meyeri

Tarasa meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originated in the 19th century through the crossbreeding of a lemon and a mandarin orange. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It has a compact growth habit with glossy, dark green leaves and is often grown as a shrub or small tree.

Tarasa meyeri is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
