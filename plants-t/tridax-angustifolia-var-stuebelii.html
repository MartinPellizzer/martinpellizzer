Tridax angustifolia var. stuebelii

Tridax angustifolia var. stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's slender tridax, is a variant of the annual flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and agricultural fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
