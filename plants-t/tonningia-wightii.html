Tonningia wightii

Tonningia wightii, commonly known as the Indian tamarind vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
