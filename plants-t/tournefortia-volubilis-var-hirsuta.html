Tournefortia volubilis var. hirsuta

Tournefortia volubilis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically borne in terminal panicles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near mangroves.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
