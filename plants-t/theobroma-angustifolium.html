Theobroma angustifolium

Theobroma angustifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and produces capsules containing the seeds used to make Brazil nuts. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.
