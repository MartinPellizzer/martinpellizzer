Tithonia rotundifolia

Tithonia rotundifolia, commonly known as the sunflower bush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tithonia rotundifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
