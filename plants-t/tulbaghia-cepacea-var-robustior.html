Tulbaghia cepacea var. robustior

Tulbaghia cepacea var. robustior, commonly known as the hardy lily or the African lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, garlic-like scent.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant once established, requiring minimal maintenance in suitable climates.
