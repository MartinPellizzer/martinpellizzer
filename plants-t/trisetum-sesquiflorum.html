Trisetum sesquiflorum

Trisetum sesquiflorum, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often adorned with small, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight blue-green hue.

Trisetum sesquiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
