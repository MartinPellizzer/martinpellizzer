Threlkeldia drupata

Threlkeldia drupata, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Threlkeldia drupata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
