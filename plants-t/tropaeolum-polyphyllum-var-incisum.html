Tropaeolum polyphyllum var. incisum

Tropaeolum polyphyllum var. incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaf nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers and deeply lobed, serrated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening or as a decorative ground cover.

The edible leaves and flowers add both ornamental value and culinary interest to gardens.
