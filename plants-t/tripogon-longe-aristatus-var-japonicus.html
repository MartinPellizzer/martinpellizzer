Tripogon longe-aristatus var. japonicus

Tripogon longe-aristatus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese slender grass, is a variant of the genus Tripogon native to East Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or open areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is a distinctive feature of the genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and elegant appearance.
