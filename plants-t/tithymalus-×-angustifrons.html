Tithymalus × angustifrons

Tithymalus × angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
