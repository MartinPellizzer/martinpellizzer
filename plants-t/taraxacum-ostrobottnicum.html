Taraxacum ostrobottnicum

Taraxacum ostrobottnicum, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, with the characteristic fluffy white pappus that aids in aerial transport.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
