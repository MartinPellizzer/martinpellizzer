Tanacetum setaceum

Tanacetum setaceum, commonly known as the slender tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Tanacetum setaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
