Trichocereus rubinghianus

Trichocereus rubinghianus, commonly known as the Bolivian torch cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
