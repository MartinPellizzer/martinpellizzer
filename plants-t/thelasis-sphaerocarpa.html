Thelasis sphaerocarpa

Thelasis sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruited thelasis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, fleshy fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
