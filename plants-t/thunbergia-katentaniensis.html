Thunbergia katentaniensis

Thunbergia katentaniensis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth habit.

Thunbergia katentaniensis is typically grown as a houseplant or in gardens with support structures for its climbing nature.
