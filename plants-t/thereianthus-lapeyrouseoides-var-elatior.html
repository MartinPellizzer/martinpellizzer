Thereianthus lapeyrouseoides var. elatior

Thereianthus lapeyrouseoides var. elatior, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and greenish-yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, low-growing habit. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
