Tinus pedunculosa

Tinus pedunculosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding a dramatic visual element to gardens. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense tufts at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tinus pedunculosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
