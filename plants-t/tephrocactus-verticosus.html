Tephrocactus verticosus

Tephrocactus verticosus, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a ribbed structure with prominent areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months.

Tephrocactus verticosus is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
