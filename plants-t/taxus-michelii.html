Taxus michelii

Taxus michelii, commonly known as the Mexican yew, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the stems. Its cones are typically small and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Taxus michelii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
