Tachigali panamensis

Tachigali panamensis, commonly known as Panama tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
