Teucrium mairei subsp. joannis

Teucrium mairei subsp.

joannis, commonly known as the Maire's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a slightly woolly texture.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
