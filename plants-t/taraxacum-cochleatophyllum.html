Taraxacum cochleatophyllum

Taraxacum cochleatophyllum, commonly known as the spiral dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped seed heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a unique and recognizable member of the dandelion genus.
