Triticum monococcum var. bulgaricum

Triticum monococcum var. bulgaricum, commonly known as Bulgarian wheat, is a small-grained wheat species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, hard grains that are typically harvested at a high moisture content. The plant has a short lifecycle, maturing within 90 to 120 days, and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system, narrow leaves, and a spikelet structure typical of the Poaceae family.

This variety is valued for its traditional uses in local cuisines and for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
