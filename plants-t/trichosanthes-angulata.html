Trichosanthes angulata

Trichosanthes angulata, commonly known as the angular gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces large, angular, green fruits that turn orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it has tendrils that help it climb.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
