Trilepis microstachya

Trilepis microstachya, commonly known as the small-stalked lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in partial to full sunlight.

Its seed pods, which are slender and hairy, contain small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its adaptability and spread.
