Thymus sudeticus

Thymus sudeticus, commonly known as Polish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus sudeticus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
