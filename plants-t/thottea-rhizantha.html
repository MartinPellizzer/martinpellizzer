Thottea rhizantha

Thottea rhizantha, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Thottea rhizantha thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
