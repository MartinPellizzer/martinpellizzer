Taraxacum latihastatum

Taraxacum latihastatum, commonly known as the Pacific dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in coastal regions of California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
