Tradescantia villosissima

Tradescantia villosissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf or spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, waxy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its "velvet" appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to spread via rhizomes, creating dense ground cover.
