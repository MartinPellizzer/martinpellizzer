Tagetes alamensis

Tagetes alamensis, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to bloom in early spring.

Tagetes alamensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal medicine.
