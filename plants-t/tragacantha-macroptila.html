Tragacantha macroptila

Tragacantha macroptila, commonly known as the large-fruited tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing numerous seeds. Its latex sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent.

Tragacantha macroptila is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
