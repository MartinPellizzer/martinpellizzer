Tylophora simiana

Tylophora simiana, commonly known as the hairy tangle vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Tylophora simiana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
