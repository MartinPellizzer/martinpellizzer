Tragacantha brachyceras

Tragacantha brachyceras, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, thorned stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.

The gum exuded by the plant has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
