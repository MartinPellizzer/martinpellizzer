Tacca borneensis

Tacca borneensis, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Borneo.

It features large, dark purple, hood-like bracts that resemble a bat's wings, giving it its common name. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with long, narrow petals and a distinctive, fleshy spadix. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and exotic foliage make it a popular choice in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
