Taraxacum podkutnokensis

Taraxacum podkutnokensis, commonly known as the Podkutno Kenesky dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Russia. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flower heads consist of a yellow composite flower, which develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
