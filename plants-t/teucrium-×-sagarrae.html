Teucrium × sagarrae

Teucrium × sagarrae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Teucrium × sagarrae is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.
