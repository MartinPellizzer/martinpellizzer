Trigonella rechingeri

Trigonella rechingeri, commonly known as the Persian fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, compound leaves, and a taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Trigonella rechingeri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
