Terminalia acuminata

Terminalia acuminata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
