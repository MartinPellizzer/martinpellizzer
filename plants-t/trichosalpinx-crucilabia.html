Trichosalpinx crucilabia

Trichosalpinx crucilabia, commonly known as the cross-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree bark without soil.

Trichosalpinx crucilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
