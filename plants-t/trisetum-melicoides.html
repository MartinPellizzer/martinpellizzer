Trisetum melicoides

Trisetum melicoides, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrubsteppe ecosystems.

Trisetum melicoides is an important forage plant for livestock and plays a key role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
