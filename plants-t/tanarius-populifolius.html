Tanarius populifolius

Tanarius populifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf tanarius, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
