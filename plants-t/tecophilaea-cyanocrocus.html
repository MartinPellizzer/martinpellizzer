Tecophilaea cyanocrocus

Tecophilaea cyanocrocus, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, often appearing in clusters. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit with silver-green foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal. Tecophilaea cyanocrocus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike.
