Terminalia koariki

Terminalia koariki, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture-making.
