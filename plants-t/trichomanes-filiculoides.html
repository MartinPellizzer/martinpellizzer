Trichomanes filiculoides

Trichomanes filiculoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a rosette-like appearance. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called sporophylls, which are typically located on the upper surface of the leaves. Trichomanes filiculoides is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

It is considered a primitive fern due to its simple morphology and ancient evolutionary lineage.
