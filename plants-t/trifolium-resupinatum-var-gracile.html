Trifolium resupinatum var. gracile

Trifolium resupinatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
