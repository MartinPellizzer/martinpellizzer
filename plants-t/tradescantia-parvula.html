Tradescantia parvula

Tradescantia parvula, commonly known as the common spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bright blue or violet flowers clustered in groups of three. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Tradescantia parvula is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
