Trichipteris sagittifolia

Trichipteris sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Trichipteris sagittifolia is known for its ability to purify water and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
