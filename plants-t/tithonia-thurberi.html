Tithonia thurberi

Tithonia thurberi, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used as a companion plant in organic farming due to its ability to attract beneficial insects.

Tithonia thurberi is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
