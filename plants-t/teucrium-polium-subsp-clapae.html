Teucrium polium subsp. clapae

Teucrium polium subsp.

clapae, commonly known as Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.
