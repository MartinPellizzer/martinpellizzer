Thisantha tenuipedicellata

Thisantha tenuipedicellata, commonly known as the slender-fruited orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers have a unique structure with short, narrow pedicels, giving it its specific epithet "tenuipedicellata.".

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
