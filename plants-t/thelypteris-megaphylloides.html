Thelypteris megaphylloides

Thelypteris megaphylloides, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping underground stem that produces new shoots. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, lance-shaped pinnae.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
