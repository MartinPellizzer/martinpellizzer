Telis crassipes

Telis crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf telis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
