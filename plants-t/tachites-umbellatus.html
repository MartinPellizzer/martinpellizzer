Tachites umbellatus

Tachites umbellatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Tachites umbellatus is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks in its native range.
