Thryptomene racemosa

Thryptomene racemosa, commonly known as the racemose thryptomene, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in racemes, which are clustered along the stems.

Thryptomene racemosa is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
