Tragacantha cana

Tragacantha cana, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters during the spring. Its sap, which is rich in gum tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The botanical features of Tragacantha cana make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
