Tamonea macrophylla

Tamonea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and lakes. Its showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths.

Tamonea macrophylla plays an important ecological role by stabilizing water bodies and supporting biodiversity.
