Taxus canadensis f. stricta

Taxus canadensis f. stricta, commonly known as the Canadian yew, is a rare and compact form of the yew tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
