Thunbergia nymphaeifolia

Thunbergia nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water lily vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a rapid growth habit.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in aquatic gardens and ponds.
