Taraxacum cyathiforme

Taraxacum cyathiforme, commonly known as the cyathiform dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that releases wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in various parts of its native range.
