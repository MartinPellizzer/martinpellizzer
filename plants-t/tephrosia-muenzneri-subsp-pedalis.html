Tephrosia muenzneri subsp. pedalis

Tephrosia muenzneri subsp.

pedalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or savanna habitats.

This species is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
