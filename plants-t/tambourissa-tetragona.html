Tambourissa tetragona

Tambourissa tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled trumpet vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its four-angled stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a rough texture.

Tambourissa tetragona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
