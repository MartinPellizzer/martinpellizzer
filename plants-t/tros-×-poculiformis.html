Tros × poculiformis

Tros × poculiformis, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Tros and Poculiformis.

It typically has thorny branches and produces small, white or pink flowers in spring. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with showy fruit clusters that turn red in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The botanical features of Tros × poculiformis make it a unique example of hybrid vigor in the Rosaceae family.
