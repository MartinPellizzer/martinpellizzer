Tetratheca ericifolia var. rubioides

Tetratheca ericifolia var. rubioides, commonly known as the redleaf tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
