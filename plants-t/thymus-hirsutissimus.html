Thymus hirsutissimus

Thymus hirsutissimus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
