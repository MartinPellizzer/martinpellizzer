Trixis turbinata

Trixis turbinata, commonly known as the turbaned trixis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
