Trisetaria laconica

Trisetaria laconica, commonly known as the Laconic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Trisetaria laconica typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is valued for its ornamental appeal in certain horticultural settings.
