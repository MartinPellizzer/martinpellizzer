Triodia radonensis

Triodia radonensis, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a hardy perennial grass native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Triodia radonensis plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.

Its resilience makes it a key component of the native vegetation in arid ecosystems.
