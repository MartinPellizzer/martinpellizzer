Trapa bispinosa var. jinumae

Trapa bispinosa var. jinumae, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny fruits that encase the edible nuts. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, with fruits that detach and float.

The botanical features of this variant include its distinctive two-spined fruits and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
