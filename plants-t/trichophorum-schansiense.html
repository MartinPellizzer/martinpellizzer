Trichophorum schansiense

Trichophorum schansiense, commonly known as the Schans Island sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and wetland habitats.

Trichophorum schansiense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its cold, harsh environment.
