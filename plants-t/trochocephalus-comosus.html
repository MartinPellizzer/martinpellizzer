Trochocephalus comosus

Trochocephalus comosus, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive hairy appearance. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
