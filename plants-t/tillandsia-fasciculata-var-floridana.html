Tillandsia fasciculata var. floridana

Tillandsia fasciculata var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant thrives in humid environments and can be found growing on trees or rocks in the wild. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
