Turnera breviflora

Turnera breviflora, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
