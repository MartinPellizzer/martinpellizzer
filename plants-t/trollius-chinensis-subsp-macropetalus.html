Trollius chinensis subsp. macropetalus

Trollius chinensis subsp.

macropetalus, commonly known as the large-petaled Chinese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to China. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with large, rounded petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are deeply lobed and form a basal rosette, and it produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
