Thuja standishii

Thuja standishii, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are typically white to blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Thuja standishii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of landscaping applications.

Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a popular choice for privacy screens and ornamental planting.
