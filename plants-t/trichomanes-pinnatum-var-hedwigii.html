Trichomanes pinnatum var. hedwigii

Trichomanes pinnatum var. hedwigii, commonly known as the Hedwig's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and finely divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
