Thymus × mariae

Thymus × mariae, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus × mariae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
