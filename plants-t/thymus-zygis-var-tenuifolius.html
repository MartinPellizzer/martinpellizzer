Thymus zygis var. tenuifolius

Thymus zygis var. tenuifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and densely packed, giving it a mat-like appearance. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
