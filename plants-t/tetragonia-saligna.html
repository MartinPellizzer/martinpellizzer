Tetragonia saligna

Tetragonia saligna, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, tangy-tasting seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Tetragonia saligna is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional culinary practices.
