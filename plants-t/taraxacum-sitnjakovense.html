Taraxacum sitnjakovense

Taraxacum sitnjakovense, commonly known as the Sitnjakovo dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and latex-filled stems. The plant produces a composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
