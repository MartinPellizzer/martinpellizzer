Tragus senegalensis

Tragus senegalensis, commonly known as the African burr or Senegalese tragus, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Tragus senegalensis is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
