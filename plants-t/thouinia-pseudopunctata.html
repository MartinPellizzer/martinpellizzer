Thouinia pseudopunctata

Thouinia pseudopunctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted or streaked with contrasting colors. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Thouinia pseudopunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
