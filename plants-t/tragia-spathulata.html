Tragia spathulata

Tragia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often accompanied by distinctive, spatulate-shaped bracts. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough or hairy texture.

Tragia spathulata is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
