Tumamoca macdougalii

Tumamoca macdougalii, commonly known as the Hawaiian mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Tumamoca macdougalii is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Hawaiian medicine.
