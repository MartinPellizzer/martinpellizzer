Trollius viridiflorus

Trollius viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish center, which is a key botanical characteristic. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are usually hairy.

Trollius viridiflorus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.
