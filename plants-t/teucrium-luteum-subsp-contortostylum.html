Teucrium luteum subsp. contortostylum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

contortostylum, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin.

This subspecies is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
