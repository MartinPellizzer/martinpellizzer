Turbinicarpus schwarzii

Turbinicarpus schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's turbinaria, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ribbed, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
