Taraxacum graecum

Taraxacum graecum, commonly known as the Greek dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which develops into a fluffy seed head known as a pappus.

The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
