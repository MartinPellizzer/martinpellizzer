Trias pulvinata

Trias pulvinata, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, deep purple to reddish stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Native to South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
