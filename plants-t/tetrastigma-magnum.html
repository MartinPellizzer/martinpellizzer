Tetrastigma magnum

Tetrastigma magnum, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Tetrastigma magnum thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
