Trollius laxus subsp. albiflorus

Trollius laxus subsp.

albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, spherical flower heads composed of numerous yellow petals surrounded by pale green bracts.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing a striking display of bright yellow blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its notably white or pale floral bracts, setting it apart from other Trollius species.
