Therocistus bupleurifolius

Therocistus bupleurifolius, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and leaves that are often divided into several lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in African herbal medicine.
