Thelymitra fuscolutea var. stellata

Thelymitra fuscolutea var. stellata, commonly known as the starry sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with several broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces a cluster of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive star-like appearance, giving it its common name. The flowers are borne on a single spike and are characterized by their elongated, recurved petals and sepals.

This variety is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
