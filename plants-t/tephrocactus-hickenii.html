Tephrocactus hickenii

Tephrocactus hickenii, commonly known as the Hicken's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in areoles with clusters of white or pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Tephrocactus hickenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
