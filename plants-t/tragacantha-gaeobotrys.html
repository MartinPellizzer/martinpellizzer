Tragacantha gaeobotrys

Tragacantha gaeobotrys, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Tragacantha gaeobotrys plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
