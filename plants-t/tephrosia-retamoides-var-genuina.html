Tephrosia retamoides var. genuina

Tephrosia retamoides var. genuina, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savanna habitats.

This species is also valued for its traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
