Trigonella officinalis

Trigonella officinalis, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a hairy stem.

Fenugreek is also valued for its aromatic properties and is widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
