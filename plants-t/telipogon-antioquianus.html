Telipogon antioquianus

Telipogon antioquianus, commonly known as the Antioquian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi. Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and deforestation.

Telipogon antioquianus plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native ecosystem.
