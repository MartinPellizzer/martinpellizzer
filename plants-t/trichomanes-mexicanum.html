Trichomanes mexicanum

Trichomanes mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and covered with small, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a unique reproductive system involving spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of its leaves.

Trichomanes mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
