Tradescantia praetermissa

Tradescantia praetermissa, commonly known as the silver sword or Mexican sword fern, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tradescantia praetermissa is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, contributing to its adaptability in various environments.
