Thuja dumosa

Thuja dumosa, commonly known as the desert thuja, is a shrub-like evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense, flat sprays along the stems. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are usually less than an inch in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
