Thelymitra longifolia var. intermedia

Thelymitra longifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Sun Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It features a compact, hairy rhizome and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, elongated petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
