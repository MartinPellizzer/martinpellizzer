Ternstroemia clusiifolia

Ternstroemia clusiifolia, commonly known as the glossy woodbine, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its smooth, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical gardens.
