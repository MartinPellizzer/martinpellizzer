Tripteris crassifolia

Tripteris crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed tripteris, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Tripteris crassifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
