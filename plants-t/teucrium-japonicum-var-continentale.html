Teucrium japonicum var. continentale

Teucrium japonicum var. continentale, commonly known as the Japanese germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
