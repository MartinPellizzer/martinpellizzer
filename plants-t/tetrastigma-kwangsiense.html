Tetrastigma kwangsiense

Tetrastigma kwangsiense, commonly known as the Guangxi crossvine, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
