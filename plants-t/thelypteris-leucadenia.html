Thelypteris leucadenia

Thelypteris leucadenia, commonly known as the white-veined shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with pale veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. Native to Southeast Asia, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's rhizomes are typically short and produce a dense network of roots, contributing to its ability to colonize moist soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
