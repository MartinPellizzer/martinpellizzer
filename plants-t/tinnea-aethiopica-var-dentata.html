Tinnea aethiopica var. dentata

Tinnea aethiopica var. dentata, commonly known as the African tinnea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins, which give it its varietal name "dentata.". The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to arid conditions, making it resilient in dry environments.
