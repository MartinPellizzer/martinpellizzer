Trigonella monantha

Trigonella monantha, commonly known as the Indian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many legumes. Trigonella monantha is often found in dry, sandy soils and is native to regions of southern and southeastern Asia.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various health purposes.
