Teucrium carthaginense var. homotrichum

Teucrium carthaginense var. homotrichum, commonly known as Carthaginian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
