Thlaspi perfoliatum proles erraticum

Thlaspi perfoliatum proles erraticum, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of kidney-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has a low-growing habit and is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
