Tibouchina calycina var. parvifolia

Tibouchina calycina var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its vibrant purple or pink flowers. The leaves are small, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
