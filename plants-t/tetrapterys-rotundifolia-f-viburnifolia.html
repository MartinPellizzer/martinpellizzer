Tetrapterys rotundifolia f. viburnifolia

Tetrapterys rotundifolia f. viburnifolia, commonly known as the viburnifolia form of the round-leaf tetrapterys, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features broad, rounded leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage and berries make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential ornamental plant.
