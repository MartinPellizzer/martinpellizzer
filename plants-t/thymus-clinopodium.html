Thymus clinopodium

Thymus clinopodium, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine, valued for their aromatic properties and potential health benefits.
