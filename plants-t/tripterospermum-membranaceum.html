Tripterospermum membranaceum

Tripterospermum membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous tripterospermum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
