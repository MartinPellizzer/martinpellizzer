Tylanthus brevifolius

Tylanthus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tylanthus brevifolius plays a role in providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife in its native environment.
