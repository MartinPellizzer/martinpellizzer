Tattia

Tattia, commonly known as the tattia plant, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Tattia species are generally herbaceous, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their delicate floral displays.
