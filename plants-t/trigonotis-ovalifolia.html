Trigonotis ovalifolia

Trigonotis ovalifolia, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are oval-shaped and have a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
