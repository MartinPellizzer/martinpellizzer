Tragacantha tephrodes

Tragacantha tephrodes, commonly known as the desert thorn, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small yellow flowers, and distinctive, spiny leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Tragacantha tephrodes plays a role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various desert animals.
