Tecoma australis var. linearis

Tecoma australis var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early summer.
