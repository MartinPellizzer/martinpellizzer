Tricyrtis puberula

Tricyrtis puberula, commonly known as the Japanese arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, arrow-shaped leaves that are hairy, giving it the species name *puberula*. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Tricyrtis puberula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
