Thymus minor

Thymus minor, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Thymus minor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
