Tabernaemontana macrophylla

Tabernaemontana macrophylla, commonly known as the giant star of Bethlehem, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white or pink star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and a strong, spicy aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
