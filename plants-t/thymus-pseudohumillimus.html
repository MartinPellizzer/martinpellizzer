Thymus pseudohumillimus

Thymus pseudohumillimus, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thymus pseudohumillimus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, sunny conditions.
