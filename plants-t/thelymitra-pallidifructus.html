Thelymitra pallidifructus

Thelymitra pallidifructus, commonly known as the pale-fruited sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in open forests and woodland areas, often on sandy or loamy soils. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers have a distinctive, elongated lip.

Thelymitra pallidifructus is known for its subtle coloration and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
