Taeniophyllum trachybracteum

Taeniophyllum trachybracteum, commonly known as the hairy-bract taeniophyllum, is a small, epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette structure and hairy bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Taeniophyllum trachybracteum is often grown as a decorative plant in greenhouses due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
