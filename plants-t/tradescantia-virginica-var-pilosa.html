Tradescantia virginica var. pilosa

Tradescantia virginica var. pilosa, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, giving it a rugged appearance. The plant produces showy, three-petaled flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This versatile plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
