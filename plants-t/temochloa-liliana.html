Temochloa liliana

Temochloa liliana, commonly known as the Lilian's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
