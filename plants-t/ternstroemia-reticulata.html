Ternstroemia reticulata

Ternstroemia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined evergreen, is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinct net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
