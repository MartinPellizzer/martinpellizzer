Torenia cordata

Torenia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Torenia cordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
