Tacca artocarpifolia

Tacca artocarpifolia, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's ivy," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bat-like flowers that are dark purple to black in color with white veins. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Tacca artocarpifolia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in various regions.
