Tragopogon kemulariae

Tragopogon kemulariae, commonly known as the Kemulär's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 centimeters, with leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets.

Tragopogon kemulariae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
