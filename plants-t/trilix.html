Trilix

Trilix, commonly known as the three-leaf plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Trilix is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
