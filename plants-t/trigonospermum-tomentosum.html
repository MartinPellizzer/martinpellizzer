Trigonospermum tomentosum

Trigonospermum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is typically latex-like in consistency. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Trigonospermum tomentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting various pollinators.
