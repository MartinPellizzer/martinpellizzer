Turnera blanchetiana var. subspicata

Turnera blanchetiana var. subspicata, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
