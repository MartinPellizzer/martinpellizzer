Tournefortia incana

Tournefortia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Tournefortia incana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
