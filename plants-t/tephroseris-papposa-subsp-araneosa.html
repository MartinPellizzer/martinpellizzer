Tephroseris papposa subsp. araneosa

Tephroseris papposa subsp.

araneosa, commonly known as the spider-hair tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its subspecies name, *araneosa*, refers to the spider-like appearance of its fine, hair-like structures.
