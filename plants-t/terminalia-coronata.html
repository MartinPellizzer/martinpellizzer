Terminalia coronata

Terminalia coronata, commonly known as the crowned flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Terminalia coronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
