Triticum dicoccum subsp. volgense

Triticum dicoccum subsp.

volgense, commonly known as wild emmer wheat, is a wild relative of modern wheat species. It is characterized by its tall, robust growth habit and distinctive spikelet structure.

The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodland areas. Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.

The subspecies is notable for its potential contributions to crop improvement through its adaptation to various environmental conditions.
