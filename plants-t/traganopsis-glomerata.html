Traganopsis glomerata

Traganopsis glomerata, commonly known as the bluebells or African bluebells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Traganopsis glomerata is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.
