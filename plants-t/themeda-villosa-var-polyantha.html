Themeda villosa var. polyantha

Themeda villosa var. polyantha, commonly known as the hairy grass or the polyantha grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming panicles that are often white or pale blue in color.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
