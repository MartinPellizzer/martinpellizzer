Tulipa lehmanniana

Tulipa lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters, with slender, strap-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
