Tigridia longispatha

Tigridia longispatha, commonly known as the long-spurred tiger flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling tiger stripes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Tigridia longispatha is known for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to gardens.
