Thymbra spicata subsp. intricata

Thymbra spicata subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are densely packed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
