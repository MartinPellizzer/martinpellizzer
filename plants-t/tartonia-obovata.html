Tartonia obovata

Tartonia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lanceolate to obovate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Tartonia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
