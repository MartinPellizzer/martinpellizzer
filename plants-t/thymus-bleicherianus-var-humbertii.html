Thymus bleicherianus var. humbertii

Thymus bleicherianus var. humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
