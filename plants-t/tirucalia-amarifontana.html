Tirucalia amarifontana

Tirucalia amarifontana, commonly known as the bitter leaf plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its latex is known for its bitter taste and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
