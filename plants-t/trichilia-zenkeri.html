Trichilia zenkeri

Trichilia zenkeri, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, bead-like fruits containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Its timber is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
