Thieleodoxa stipularis

Thieleodoxa stipularis, commonly known as the stipular thieleodoxa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thieleodoxaceae and is characterized by its distinctive stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
