Telanthera rufa

Telanthera rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered telanthera, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is typically found in woodland areas and near streams, where it benefits from partial shade.

Telanthera rufa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
