Tacsonia pubescens

Tacsonia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, Tacsonia pubescens is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
