Tragacantha sesamea

Tragacantha sesamea, commonly known as the Armenian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and leaves with a distinctive, serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Its most notable feature is the production of a yellow, resinous gum, which has been used historically for various medicinal and industrial purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
