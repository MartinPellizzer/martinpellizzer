Thalictrum angustifolium var. simplex

Thalictrum angustifolium var. simplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are narrow and elongated, often with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with aesthetic appeal.
