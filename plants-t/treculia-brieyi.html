Treculia brieyi

Treculia brieyi, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. Its latex, known as shea butter, is widely used in cosmetics and skincare products.

Treculia brieyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
