Taraxacum obliquilobum

Taraxacum obliquilobum, commonly known as the obliquelobe dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and serrated, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the flower heads are surrounded by a pappus of bristles that aid in wind dispersal.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
