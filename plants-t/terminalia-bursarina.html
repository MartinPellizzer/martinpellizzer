Terminalia bursarina

Terminalia bursarina, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist forests and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

The wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
