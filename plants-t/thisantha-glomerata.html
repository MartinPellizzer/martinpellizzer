Thisantha glomerata

Thisantha glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Thisantha glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
