Trichomanes krausii var. crispatum

Trichomanes krausii var. crispatum, commonly known as the crinkled maidenhair fern, is a varietal form of the species Trichomanes krausii.

It is a delicate, evergreen fern characterized by its finely divided, crinkled fronds that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a soft, velvety texture, enhancing its ornamental value.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
