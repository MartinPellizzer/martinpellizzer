Taraxacum obitsiense

Taraxacum obitsiense, commonly known as the Obitsiense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and arid environments. It has a taproot system and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
