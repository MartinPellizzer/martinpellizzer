Trillium tschonoskii var. himalaicum

Trillium tschonoskii var. himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a distinctive three-part leaf structure with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with three petals and three sepals. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears the showy floral display. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
