Taraxacum radens

Taraxacum radens, commonly known as the radiate dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. The flower head consists of numerous tiny florets that mature into the familiar white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
