Trisetum miegevillii

Trisetum miegevillii, commonly known as the Miegeville's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-branched flowers.

Trisetum miegevillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
