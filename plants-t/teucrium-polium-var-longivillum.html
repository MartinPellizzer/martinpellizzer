Teucrium polium var. longivillum

Teucrium polium var. longivillum, commonly known as the long-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically longer than they are wide. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it valued in traditional herbal remedies.
