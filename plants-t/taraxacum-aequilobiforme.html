Taraxacum aequilobiforme

Taraxacum aequilobiforme, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head that produces a distinctive seed parachute. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
