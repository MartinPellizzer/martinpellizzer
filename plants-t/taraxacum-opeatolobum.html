Taraxacum opeatolobum

Taraxacum opeatolobum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum opeatolobum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
