Tillandsia flabellata var. viridifolia

Tillandsia flabellata var. viridifolia, commonly known as the Emerald Pitcher Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped foliage that forms a cup-like structure to collect water and nutrients. The leaves are typically green, with a waxy surface that helps in water retention. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
