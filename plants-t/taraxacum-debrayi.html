Taraxacum debrayi

Taraxacum debrayi, commonly known as the Debray's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds.

Taraxacum debrayi is adapted to arid environments, showcasing resilience through its deep root system and drought-tolerant foliage.
