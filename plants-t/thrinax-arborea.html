Thrinax arborea

Thrinax arborea, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a flowering plant species native to the Caribbean.

It is a small, slow-growing palm with a short, sturdy trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Thrinax arborea is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
