Trifolium flexuosum var. pignantii

Trifolium flexuosum var. pignantii, commonly known as the Pignant's bentleaf clover, is a rare variant of the genus Trifolium native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often curved leaflets and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many areas, highlighting the need for its protection.
