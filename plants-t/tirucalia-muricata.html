Tirucalia muricata

Tirucalia muricata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that turn orange-red when mature, containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is widely cultivated for its nutrient-rich fruit, which is valued in traditional medicine and cuisine.
