Tamarix africana var. wilczekii

Tamarix africana var. wilczekii, commonly known as the African tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
