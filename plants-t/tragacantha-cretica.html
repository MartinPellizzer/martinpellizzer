Tragacantha cretica

Tragacantha cretica, commonly known as the Cretan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Tragacantha cretica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
