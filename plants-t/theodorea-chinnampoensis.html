Theodorea chinnampoensis

Theodorea chinnampoensis, commonly known as the Chinnampo lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and erect.

Theodorea chinnampoensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
