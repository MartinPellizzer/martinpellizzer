Tulipa subquinquefolia

Tulipa subquinquefolia, commonly known as the Texas wildflower or redflower tulip, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, single or clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to native gardens and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
