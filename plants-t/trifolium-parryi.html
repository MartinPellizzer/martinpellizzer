Trifolium parryi

Trifolium parryi, commonly known as Parry's clover, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Trifolium parryi plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
