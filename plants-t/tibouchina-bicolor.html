Tibouchina bicolor

Tibouchina bicolor, commonly known as the pink trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking bi-color flowers, which typically feature pink petals with white or pale pink centers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are particularly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tibouchina bicolor thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
