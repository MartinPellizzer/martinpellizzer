Trifolium simplicifolium

Trifolium simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaved clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Trifolium simplicifolium is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural settings.
