Tribulus bimucronatus

Tribulus bimucronatus, commonly known as the two-spined tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruit capsules that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of many members of the Zygophyllaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Tribulus bimucronatus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
