Thoa neglecta

Thoa neglecta, commonly known as the neglected thoa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Thoa neglecta is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
