Telanthera capituliflora

Telanthera capituliflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats.

Telanthera capituliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
