Tragacantha bethlehemitica

Tragacantha bethlehemitica, commonly known as the Holy Land tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody stem. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in food, medicine, and industrial applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various insects and contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
