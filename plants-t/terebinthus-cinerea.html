Terebinthus cinerea

Terebinthus cinerea, commonly known as the grey terebinth, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its greyish bark and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Terebinthus cinerea is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
