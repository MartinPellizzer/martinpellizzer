Tracaulon sagittatum var. gracilentum

Tracaulon sagittatum var. gracilentum, commonly known as the graceful arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy surface, enhancing its aesthetic appeal in water gardens. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in shallow, still water and requires moderate to high light conditions for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for pond and aquarium settings.
