Terminalia rivularis

Terminalia rivularis, commonly known as the river terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
