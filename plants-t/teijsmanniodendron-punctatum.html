Teijsmanniodendron punctatum

Teijsmanniodendron punctatum, commonly known as the punctate tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically odd-pinnate with small, rounded leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark is smooth and pale, often with distinctive markings.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
