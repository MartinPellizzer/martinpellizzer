Trapa pseudincisa var. aspinta

Trapa pseudincisa var. aspinta, commonly known as the smooth water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are smooth and oval-shaped, with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spiny, triangular fruits. Its roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
