Triticum aeternum var. nigroalbum

Triticum aeternum var. nigroalbum, commonly known as the eternal black wheat, is a rare and enigmatic botanical species characterized by its dark, almost obsidian-like grain.

This variant is notable for its unique combination of deep black husks and a rich, amber-colored inner kernel. It is believed to possess exceptional longevity, with seeds capable of remaining viable for centuries under optimal conditions. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

Due to its rarity and mysterious origins, it is highly sought after by botanists and agricultural researchers.
