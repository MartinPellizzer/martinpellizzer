Triglochin elata

Triglochin elata, commonly known as slender arrowgrass, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, thriving in environments with consistent moisture. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Triglochin elata is often found in regions with temperate climates and is an important component of aquatic plant communities.
