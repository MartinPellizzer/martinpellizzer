Therocistus macrosepalus

Therocistus macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with prominent, fleshy sepals that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Therocistus macrosepalus requires specific care, including high humidity and well-draining soil, to thrive in cultivation.
