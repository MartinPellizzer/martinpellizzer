Trichilia weddelii

Trichilia weddelii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and satinwood species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are white or pale yellow, and it produces woody, aromatic fruits containing smooth, oval seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in carving and as a source of essential oils.
