Thymus × dominguezii

Thymus × dominguezii, commonly known as the Dominguez thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
