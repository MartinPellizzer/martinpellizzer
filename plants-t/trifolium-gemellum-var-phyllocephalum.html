Trifolium gemellum var. phyllocephalum

Trifolium gemellum var. phyllocephalum, commonly known as the leaf-headed clover, is a rare and distinctive variant of the clover genus.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring broad, elongated leaflets that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in pink or white hues, and bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
