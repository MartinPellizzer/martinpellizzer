Thinouia cazumbensis

Thinouia cazumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it an invasive species in some regions.
