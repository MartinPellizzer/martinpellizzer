Tulbaghia violacea var. robustior

Tulbaghia violacea var. robustior, commonly known as the Cape garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with purple markings. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and ability to withstand drought conditions.
