Taraxacum coverum

Taraxacum coverum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a bright yellow flower head composed of numerous small, petal-less florets. After flowering, it develops a hollow, spiky seed head that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its taproot system and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
