Turnera sedoides

Turnera sedoides, commonly known as the "Mexican flame flower," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its most distinctive characteristic is its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Turnera sedoides is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical significance also lies in its role as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
