Termontis cordata

Termontis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a sprawling growth habit and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Termontis cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
