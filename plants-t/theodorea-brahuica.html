Theodorea brahuica

Theodorea brahuica, commonly known as the Brahui orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the monsoon season.

Theodorea brahuica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in certain regions.
