Trifolium repens subsp. nevadense

Trifolium repens subsp.

nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
