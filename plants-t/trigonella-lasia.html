Trigonella lasia

Trigonella lasia, commonly known as desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

Trigonella lasia is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it an important resource in drought-prone areas.
