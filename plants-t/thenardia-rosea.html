Thenardia rosea

Thenardia rosea, commonly known as the rose sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
