Tillandsia leucopetala

Tillandsia leucopetala, commonly known as the white-leaved air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surfaces of other plants without being parasitic. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures that help absorb moisture from the air. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
