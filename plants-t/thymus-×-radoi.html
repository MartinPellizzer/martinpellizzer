Thymus × radoi

Thymus × radoi, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Thymus × radoi is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
