Thymus leucostomus var. gypsaceus

Thymus leucostomus var. gypsaceus, commonly known as the gypsum thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and arid regions.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
