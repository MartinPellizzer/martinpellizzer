Teucrium × dopii

Teucrium × dopii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, resulting in a plant with characteristic aromatic foliage and showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
