Thymus moesiacus var. bertisceus

Thymus moesiacus var. bertisceus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.
