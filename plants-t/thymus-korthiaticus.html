Thymus korthiaticus

Thymus korthiaticus, commonly known as the Korthiatic thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Thymus korthiaticus is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
