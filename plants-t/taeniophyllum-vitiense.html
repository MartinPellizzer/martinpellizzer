Taeniophyllum vitiense

Taeniophyllum vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped structure with short, fibrous leaves. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear white or pale pink flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
