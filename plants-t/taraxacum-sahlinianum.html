Taraxacum sahlinianum

Taraxacum sahlinianum, commonly known as the Sahlinian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow, composite flower head. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a fluffy pappus.

Taraxacum sahlinianum is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
