Trichelostylis junciformis

Trichelostylis junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It typically grows in wet or brackish habitats such as estuaries, salt marshes, and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Trichelostylis junciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
