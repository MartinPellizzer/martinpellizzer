Tribulus suberosus

Tribulus suberosus, commonly known as the rough tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Tribulus suberosus is known for its adaptability and is often used in traditional medicine.
