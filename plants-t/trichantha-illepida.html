Trichantha illepida

Trichantha illepida, commonly known as the slender trichantha, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, dry fruits called capsules, which contain minute seeds. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its low water requirements, it is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
