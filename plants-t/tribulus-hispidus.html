Tribulus hispidus

Tribulus hispidus, commonly known as goat's horn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Tribulus hispidus is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
