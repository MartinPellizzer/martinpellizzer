Talauma macrophylla

Talauma macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf talauma, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to their aesthetic appeal.
