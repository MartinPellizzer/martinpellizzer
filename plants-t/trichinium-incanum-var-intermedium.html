Trichinium incanum var. intermedium

Trichinium incanum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
