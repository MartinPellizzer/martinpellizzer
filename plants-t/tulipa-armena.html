Tulipa armena

Tulipa armena, commonly known as Armenian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and eastern Turkey.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to cold climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Tulipa armena is also valued for its historical significance in traditional medicine.
