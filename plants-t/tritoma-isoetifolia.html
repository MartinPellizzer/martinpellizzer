Tritoma isoetifolia

Tritoma isoetifolia, commonly known as the slender spike moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along the edges of ponds or in damp forest floors. Tritoma isoetifolia has a simple structure with no true roots, stems, or leaves, but it possesses specialized structures for absorbing water and nutrients.

Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, contributing to its role in aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
