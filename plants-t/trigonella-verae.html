Trigonella verae

Trigonella verae, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Trigonella verae has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
