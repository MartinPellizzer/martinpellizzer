Trifolium argentinense

Trifolium argentinense, commonly known as Argentine clover, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium argentinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
