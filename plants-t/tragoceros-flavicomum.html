Tragoceros flavicomum

Tragoceros flavicomum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered tragoceros, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Native to the high-altitude regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant's structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a flowering stem that bears numerous small, daisy-like blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
