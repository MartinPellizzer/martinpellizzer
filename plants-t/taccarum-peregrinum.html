Taccarum peregrinum

Taccarum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering taccarum, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and unique inflorescence that resembles a cluster of tiny lanterns. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Taccarum peregrinum remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
