Thunbergia grandiflora f. citrina

Thunbergia grandiflora f. citrina, commonly known as the citron thunbergia, is a hybrid form of the genus Thunbergia, characterized by its bright yellow flowers.

It is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa, often found in shaded, moist environments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, while the flowers are tubular with a distinctive yellow corolla. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and foliage.

It thrives in warm climates and requires regular watering and well-draining soil.
