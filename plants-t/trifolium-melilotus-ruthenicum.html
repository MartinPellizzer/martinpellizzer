Trifolium melilotus-ruthenicum

Trifolium melilotus-ruthenicum, commonly known as the Ruthenian clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense spikes. This plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment and agricultural practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

The plant is also valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop.
