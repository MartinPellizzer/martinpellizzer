Torenia vagans

Torenia vagans, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Torenia vagans is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
