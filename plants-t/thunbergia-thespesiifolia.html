Thunbergia thespesiifolia

Thunbergia thespesiifolia, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
