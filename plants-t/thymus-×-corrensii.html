Thymus × corrensii

Thymus × corrensii, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Thymus vulgaris (common thyme) and Thymus citriodorus (lemon thyme), resulting in a plant with aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in early summer. This plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
