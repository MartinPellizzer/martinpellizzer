Tripolium subulatum

Tripolium subulatum, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.
