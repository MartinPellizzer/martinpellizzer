Tanulepis linearis

Tanulepis linearis, commonly known as the slender leaved tanulepis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, displaying a range of colors from white to purple. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
