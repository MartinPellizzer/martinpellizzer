Thymus × hieronymi nothosubsp. hurtadoi

Thymus × hieronymi nothosubsp.

hurtadoi, commonly known as the Hurtado thyme, is a hybrid species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique genetic composition, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
