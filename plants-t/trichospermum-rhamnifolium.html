Trichospermum rhamnifolium

Trichospermum rhamnifolium, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and smooth, often thornless stems. The plant has pinnate leaves with 5-9 pairs of leaflets, and its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
