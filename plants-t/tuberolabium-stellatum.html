Tuberolabium stellatum

Tuberolabium stellatum, commonly known as the star-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its distinctive tuberous roots and its showy, star-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in late winter or early spring, featuring a unique labellum that resembles a star. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and requires well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
