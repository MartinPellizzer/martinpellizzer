Tricholepis

Tricholepis, commonly known as the common tricholepis, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a pink or reddish hue. Tricholepis species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

They play a role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for various insect species.
