Turbinicarpus beguinii subsp. pailanus

Turbinicarpus beguinii subsp.

pailanus, commonly known as the Pailan's turbinicarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae. It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico, specifically the state of Coahuila.

This cactus is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
