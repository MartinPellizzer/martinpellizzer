Thalictrum minus var. appendiculatum

Thalictrum minus var. appendiculatum, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically alternate and finely divided. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
