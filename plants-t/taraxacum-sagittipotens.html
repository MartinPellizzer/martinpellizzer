Taraxacum sagittipotens

Taraxacum sagittipotens, commonly known as the arrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of arrow-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces a pappus-based seed. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, followed by rapid growth in spring.
