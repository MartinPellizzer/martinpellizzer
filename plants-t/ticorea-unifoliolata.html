Ticorea unifoliolata

Ticorea unifoliolata, commonly known as the single leaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically small, showy blooms. The plant has a compact growth habit with a single, palmate leaf that is often lobed or divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to arid landscapes.
