Tulipa variopicta

Tulipa variopicta, commonly known as the variegated tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multi-colored flowers that often display combinations of red, yellow, and white. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that can reach up to 8 inches in diameter.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
