Thymus polytrichus var. neglectus

Thymus polytrichus var. neglectus, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in rocky or sandy environments.
