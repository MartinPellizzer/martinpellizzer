Trichomanes squarrosum

Trichomanes squarrosum, commonly known as the scaly maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically covered in a layer of scaly structures, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Trichomanes squarrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
