Tetragonotheca repanda

Tetragonotheca repanda, commonly known as the four-angled boxwood, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that forms compact, rounded clusters. The leaves are typically small, oval, and have a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
