Typha domingensis f. strimonii

Typha domingensis f. strimonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces cylindrical, brownish flower spikes that contain numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a key species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
