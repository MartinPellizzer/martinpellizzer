Thysanotus glaucus

Thysanotus glaucus, commonly known as the bluebell or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling tiny bells. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Thysanotus glaucus is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
