Tapiphyllum floribundum

Tapiphyllum floribundum, commonly known as the vibrant bloom plant, is a flowering species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lush, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
