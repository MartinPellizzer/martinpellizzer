Thymus widderi

Thymus widderi, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and ornamental uses.
