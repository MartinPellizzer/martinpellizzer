Taxus erecta

Taxus erecta, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and elongated, needle-like leaves that are arranged in flat sprays. The plant produces small, reddish-pink flowers followed by blue-black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Taxus erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural qualities.

It is also known for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a hardy choice for landscaping.
