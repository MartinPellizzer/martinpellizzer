Thymus cilicicus

Thymus cilicicus, commonly known as Cilician thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus cilicicus is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
