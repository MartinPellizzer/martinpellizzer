Thymus serpyllum var. parvifolius

Thymus serpyllum var. parvifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
