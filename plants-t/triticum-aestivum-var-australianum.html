Triticum aestivum var. australianum

Triticum aestivum var. australianum, commonly known as Australian wheat, is a variant of common wheat native to Australia.

It is characterized by its adaptation to the unique climatic conditions of the region, including arid and semi-arid environments. This variety typically exhibits shorter stature and higher drought tolerance compared to other wheat types. Its grain is used for flour production and has been bred for improved yield and resilience.

The plant's botanical features include a robust root system and a flexible stem that aids in survival under challenging conditions.
