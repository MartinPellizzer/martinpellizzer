Tithymalus cinerea

Tithymalus cinerea, commonly known as the gray tithymale, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender stems and small, pale blue or grayish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Tithymalus cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
