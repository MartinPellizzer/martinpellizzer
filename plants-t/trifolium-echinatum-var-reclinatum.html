Trifolium echinatum var. reclinatum

Trifolium echinatum var. reclinatum, commonly known as the nodding clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
