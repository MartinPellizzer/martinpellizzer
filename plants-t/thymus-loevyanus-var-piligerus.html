Thymus loevyanus var. piligerus

Thymus loevyanus var. piligerus, commonly known as the hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
