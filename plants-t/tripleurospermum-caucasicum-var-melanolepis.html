Tripleurospermum caucasicum var. melanolepis

Tripleurospermum caucasicum var. melanolepis, commonly known as the Caucasian melonroot, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a robust root system and hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
