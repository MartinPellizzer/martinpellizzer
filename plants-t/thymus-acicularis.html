Thymus acicularis

Thymus acicularis, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact, mat-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in spring, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
