Thryptomene trachycalyx

Thryptomene trachycalyx, commonly known as the rough-barked thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Thryptomene trachycalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
