Teucrium × portusmagni

Teucrium × portusmagni, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which are clustered in whorled inflorescences. It is native to the Mediterranean region but has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils.

Teucrium × portusmagni is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
