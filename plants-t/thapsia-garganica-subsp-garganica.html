Thapsia garganica subsp. garganica

Thapsia garganica subsp.

garganica, commonly known as the Garganica thapsia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.

The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow flowers appear in summer, forming clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
