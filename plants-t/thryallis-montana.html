Thryallis montana

Thryallis montana, commonly known as the mountain thryallis, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thryallis montana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
