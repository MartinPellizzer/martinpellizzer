Taraxacum flugum

Taraxacum flugum, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Taraxacum flugum is a perennial plant that is adapted to temperate climates and is known for its resilience and widespread distribution.
