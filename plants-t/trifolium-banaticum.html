Trifolium banaticum

Trifolium banaticum, commonly known as Banat's clover, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium banaticum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural and ecological purposes.
