Tribulus lanatus

Tribulus lanatus, commonly known as goat's horn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that resemble horns, which give it its common name. The plant produces small yellow flowers and has hairy, branching stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.

Tribulus lanatus is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
