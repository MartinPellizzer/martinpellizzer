Tibouchina excoriata

Tibouchina excoriata, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Tibouchina excoriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
