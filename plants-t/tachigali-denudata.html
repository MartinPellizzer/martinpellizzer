Tachigali denudata

Tachigali denudata, commonly known as the naked tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth rate. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract various pollinators.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
