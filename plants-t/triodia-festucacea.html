Triodia festucacea

Triodia festucacea, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often forming extensive grasslands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds.

Triodia festucacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
