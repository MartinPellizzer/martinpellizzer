Taraxacum cerdanicum

Taraxacum cerdanicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, slender flowering stem topped with a composite flower head that turns into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
