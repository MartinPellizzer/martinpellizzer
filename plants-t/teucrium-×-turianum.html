Teucrium × turianum

Teucrium × turianum, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
