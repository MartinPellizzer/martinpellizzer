Teucrium × carmelitanum

Teucrium × carmelitanum, commonly known as the Carmel thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fragrant leaves that emit a strong thyme-like scent. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and medicinal purposes.
