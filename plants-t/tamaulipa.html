Tamaulipa

Tamaulipa, commonly known as the Tamaulipan redwood, is a unique plant species native to the Tamaulipan matorral ecoregion of northern Mexico.

It is a type of evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and dark green, providing a lush canopy in its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the semi-arid climate of the region.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
