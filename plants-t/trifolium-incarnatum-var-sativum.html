Trifolium incarnatum var. sativum

Trifolium incarnatum var. sativum, commonly known as crimson clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. This plant is widely cultivated for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable cover crop and forage plant. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in agricultural settings to improve soil fertility.

The plant is also popular in ornamental gardens for its attractive blooms and foliage.
