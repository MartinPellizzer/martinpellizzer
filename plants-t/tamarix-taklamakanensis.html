Tamarix taklamakanensis

Tamarix taklamakanensis, commonly known as the Taklamakan tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to harsh, saline soils and can thrive in low-water environments. It plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and preventing erosion.

Its wood is often used for fuel and construction in local communities.
