Trevesia pleiosperma

Trevesia pleiosperma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wild cherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
