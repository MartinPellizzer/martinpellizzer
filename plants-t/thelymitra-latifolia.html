Thelymitra latifolia

Thelymitra latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. The plant features a slender, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with a distinctive red or orange lip, and they bloom during the spring and early summer.

Thelymitra latifolia is a short-lived perennial, with a lifespan of about three to four years.
