Trichinium

Trichinium, commonly known as the common trichinium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Trichinium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
