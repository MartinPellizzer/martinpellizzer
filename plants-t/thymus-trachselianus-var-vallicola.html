Thymus trachselianus var. vallicola

Thymus trachselianus var. vallicola, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the late spring and early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
