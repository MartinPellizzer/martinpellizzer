Taxandria angustifolia

Taxandria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf taxandria, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
