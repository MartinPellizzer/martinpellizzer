Tapeinoglossum centrosemiflorum

Tapeinoglossum centrosemiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-tongued orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers with a distinctive narrow tongue-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
