Tabebuia obovata

Tabebuia obovata, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or red. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, with heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
