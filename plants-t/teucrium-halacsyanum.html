Teucrium halacsyanum

Teucrium halacsyanum, commonly known as Hungarian germander, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

Teucrium halacsyanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
