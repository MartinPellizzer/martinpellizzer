Terminalia obovata

Terminalia obovata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Terminalia obovata is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
