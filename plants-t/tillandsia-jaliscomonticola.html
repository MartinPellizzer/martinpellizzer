Tillandsia jaliscomonticola

Tillandsia jaliscomonticola, commonly known as the cloud forest tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture from the air. The plant thrives in humid, high-altitude environments with bright, indirect light. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, often blooming in shades of white or pale pink.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
