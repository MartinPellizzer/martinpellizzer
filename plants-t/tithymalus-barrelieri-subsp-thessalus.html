Tithymalus barrelieri subsp. thessalus

Tithymalus barrelieri subsp.

thessalus, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
