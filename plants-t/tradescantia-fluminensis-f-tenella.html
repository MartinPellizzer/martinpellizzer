Tradescantia fluminensis f. tenella

Tradescantia fluminensis f. tenella, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are silver-white on top and green underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous pinkish-white flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, mat-like growth, which helps suppress weeds in garden settings.
