Trachyspermum didymum

Trachyspermum didymum, commonly known as bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by oval, brown seeds. It is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow stems and aromatic compounds.
