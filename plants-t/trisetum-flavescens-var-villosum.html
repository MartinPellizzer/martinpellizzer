Trisetum flavescens var. villosum

Trisetum flavescens var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy field grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
