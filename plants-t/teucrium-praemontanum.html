Teucrium praemontanum

Teucrium praemontanum, commonly known as the coastal germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Teucrium praemontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
