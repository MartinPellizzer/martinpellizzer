Tephrosia adunca var. genuina

Tephrosia adunca var. genuina, commonly known as the African tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in various herbal remedies.

The plant also serves as a source of forage for livestock in some regions.
