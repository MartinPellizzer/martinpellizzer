Tephroseris furusei

Tephroseris furusei, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Japan.

It is a small, clump-forming plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
