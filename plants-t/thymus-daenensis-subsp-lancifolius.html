Thymus daenensis subsp. lancifolius

Thymus daenensis subsp.

lancifolius, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a perennial herb native to Iran. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties and ornamental value have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and phytochemistry.
