Tephrosia reptans var. microfoliata

Tephrosia reptans var. microfoliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, narrow leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Tephrosia. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
