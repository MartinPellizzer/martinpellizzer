Teucrium pseudoscorodonia var. baeticum

Teucrium pseudoscorodonia var. baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
