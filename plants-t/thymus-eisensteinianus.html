Thymus eisensteinianus

Thymus eisensteinianus, commonly known as Eisenstein's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
