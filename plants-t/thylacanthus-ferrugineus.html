Thylacanthus ferrugineus

Thylacanthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty thylacanthus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, rust-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
