Tephrosia procera

Tephrosia procera, commonly known as the tall tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tephrosia procera is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
