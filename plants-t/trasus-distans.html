Trasus distans

Trasus distans, commonly known as the distant spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Trasus distans is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
