Trichocline heterophylla

Trichocline heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from linear to ovate depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
