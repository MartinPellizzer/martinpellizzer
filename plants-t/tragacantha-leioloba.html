Tragacantha leioloba

Tragacantha leioloba, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

The plant produces a distinctive yellow to brown resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a binding agent. Tragacantha leioloba plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its resinous properties.
