Trifolium cryptoscias

Trifolium cryptoscias, commonly known as the night clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Trifolium cryptoscias prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
