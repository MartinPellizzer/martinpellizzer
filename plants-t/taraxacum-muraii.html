Taraxacum muraii

Taraxacum muraii, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hairy stem. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum muraii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
