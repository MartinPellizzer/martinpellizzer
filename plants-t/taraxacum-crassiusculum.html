Taraxacum crassiusculum

Taraxacum crassiusculum, commonly known as the thick-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, dark green leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

This species is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
