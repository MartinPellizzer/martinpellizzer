Trichocereus bridgesii var. longispinus

Trichocereus bridgesii var. longispinus, commonly known as the Longispine Cereus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, a trait common among many cacti in the Cactaceae family. Its spines are long and slender, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
