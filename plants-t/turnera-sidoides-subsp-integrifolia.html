Turnera sidoides subsp. integrifolia

Turnera sidoides subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its striking white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
