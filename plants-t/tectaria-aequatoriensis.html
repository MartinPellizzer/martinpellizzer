Tectaria aequatoriensis

Tectaria aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive shield-like appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Tectaria aequatoriensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
