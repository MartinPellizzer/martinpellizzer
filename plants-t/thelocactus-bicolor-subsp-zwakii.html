Thelocactus bicolor subsp. zwakii

Thelocactus bicolor subsp.

zwakii, commonly known as the Zwakii thelocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned coloration, with a greenish body and a contrasting red or orange spines.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the evening, adding to its ornamental appeal. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to extreme desert conditions.
