Tephrosia mucronata

Tephrosia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

Tephrosia mucronata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimalarial effects.
