Trochocephalus gumbeticus

Trochocephalus gumbeticus, commonly known as the Gumbetic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy soils.

Overall, Trochocephalus gumbeticus is a hardy, drought-tolerant species with unique adaptations for survival in extreme conditions.
