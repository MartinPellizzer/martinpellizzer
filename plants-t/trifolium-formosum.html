Trifolium formosum

Trifolium formosum, commonly known as the Taiwan clover, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Trifolium formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
