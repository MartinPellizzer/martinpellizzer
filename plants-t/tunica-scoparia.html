Tunica scoparia

Tunica scoparia, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent leaves. Tunica scoparia is often found in sandy soils and can thrive with minimal water.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
