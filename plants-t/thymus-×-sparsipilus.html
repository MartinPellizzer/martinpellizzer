Thymus × sparsipilus

Thymus × sparsipilus, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in herbal medicine.
