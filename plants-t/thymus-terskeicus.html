Thymus terskeicus

Thymus terskeicus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Thymus terskeicus is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other thyme species.
