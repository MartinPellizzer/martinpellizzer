Tragacantha oligantha

Tragacantha oligantha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant's sap is used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
