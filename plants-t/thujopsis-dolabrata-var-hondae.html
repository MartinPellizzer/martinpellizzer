Thujopsis dolabrata var. hondae

Thujopsis dolabrata var. hondae, commonly known as Honduran incense cedar, is a member of the Cupressaceae family.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer characterized by its slender, columnar form and dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the shoots. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly Honduras, and is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.
