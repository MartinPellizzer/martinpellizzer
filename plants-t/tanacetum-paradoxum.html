Tanacetum paradoxum

Tanacetum paradoxum, commonly known as the meadow button.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow button-like centers and white to pale yellow ray florets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological gardens and natural habitats.
