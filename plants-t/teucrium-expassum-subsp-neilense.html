Teucrium expassum subsp. neilense

Teucrium expassum subsp.

neilense, commonly known as Neil's germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense terminal clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
