Talinum oblongifolium

Talinum oblongifolium, commonly known as the purple leaf talinum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its succulent nature makes it well adapted to drought conditions, and it is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Talinum oblongifolium is also valued in some traditional cuisines for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tangy flavor.
