Trapa chinensis

Trapa chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a water plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, nut-like fruits that develop underwater. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its roots are edible and are often harvested for culinary use.

Trapa chinensis plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
