Turnera carpinifolia

Turnera carpinifolia, commonly known as the "false holly," is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Turnera carpinifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
