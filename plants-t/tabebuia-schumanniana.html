Tabebuia schumanniana

Tabebuia schumanniana, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The tree produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its bark is thick and rugged, often marked with distinctive patterns.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
