Tibouchina albescens

Tibouchina albescens, commonly known as the white tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Tibouchina albescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
