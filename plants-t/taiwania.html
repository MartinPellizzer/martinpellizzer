Taiwania

Taiwania, commonly known as the Taiwan fir, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. Taiwania species typically have needle-like leaves that are flat and arranged in crossed pairs. The cones are woody and contain two to three seeds, and the trees are valued for their durable wood.

These plants thrive in temperate climates and are often found in mountainous regions across China, Japan, and Taiwan.
