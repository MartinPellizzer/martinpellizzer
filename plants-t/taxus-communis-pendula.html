Taxus communis pendula

Taxus communis pendula, commonly known as the weeping yew, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It belongs to the Taxaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and reddish, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril.

The weeping yew is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its unique, elegant form.
