Triguera

Triguera, commonly known as the "wild oat," is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Triguera produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
