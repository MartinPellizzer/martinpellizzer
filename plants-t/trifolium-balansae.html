Trifolium balansae

Trifolium balansae, commonly known as the Balansae clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Trifolium balansae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
