Thalictrum majus var. pubescens

Thalictrum majus var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear compound leaves with finely divided, feathery lobes. The plant produces showy, clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
