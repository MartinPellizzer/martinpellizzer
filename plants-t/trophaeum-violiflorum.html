Trophaeum violiflorum

Trophaeum violiflorum, commonly known as the violet-flowered brookweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Trophaeum violiflorum is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.
