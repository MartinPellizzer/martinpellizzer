Tephrocactus fulvicomus

Tephrocactus fulvicomus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
