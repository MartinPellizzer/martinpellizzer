Tillandsia laxissima var. moorei

Tillandsia laxissima var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
