Tremula vulgaris

Tremula vulgaris, commonly known as silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and delicate, pendulous catkins. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn golden in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
