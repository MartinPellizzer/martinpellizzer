Tetragonanthus major

Tetragonanthus major, commonly known as the four-angled fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

However, it is not commonly used in traditional medicine or horticulture beyond its aesthetic appeal.
