Tragacantha bourgaeana

Tragacantha bourgaeana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and papery, fragrant pods. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role and its economic importance in local communities.
