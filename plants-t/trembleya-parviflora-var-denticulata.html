Trembleya parviflora var. denticulata

Trembleya parviflora var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, giving the plant its varietal name. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
