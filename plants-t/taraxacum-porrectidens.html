Taraxacum porrectidens

Taraxacum porrectidens, commonly known as the straight-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stem and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
