Tinus chinensis

Tinus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Tinus chinensis thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
