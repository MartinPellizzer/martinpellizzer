Taraxacum kotschyi

Taraxacum kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head. Its yellow disk flowers produce seeds with white pappus, enabling wind dispersal.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
