Teucrium puechiae

Teucrium puechiae, commonly known as the Corsican germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Teucrium puechiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
