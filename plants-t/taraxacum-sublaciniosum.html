Taraxacum sublaciniosum

Taraxacum sublaciniosum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds with feathery pappi, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
