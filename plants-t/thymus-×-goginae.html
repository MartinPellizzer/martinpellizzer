Thymus × goginae

Thymus × goginae, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus × goginae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile garden plant.
