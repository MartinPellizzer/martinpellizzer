Tithymalus squamosus

Tithymalus squamosus, commonly known as the rough tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tithymalus squamosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
