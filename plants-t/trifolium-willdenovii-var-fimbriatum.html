Trifolium willdenovii var. fimbriatum

Trifolium willdenovii var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets with a distinctive fringed margin. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
