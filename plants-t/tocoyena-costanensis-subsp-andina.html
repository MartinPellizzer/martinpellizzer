Tocoyena costanensis subsp. andina

Tocoyena costanensis subsp.

andina, commonly known as Andean tocoyena, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate to cool climates. Its flowers are often violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
