Trichocereus knuthianus

Trichocereus knuthianus, commonly known as the "Moon cactus" or "Easter cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and bright, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has spines that are often short and white, and its leaves are usually absent, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Trichocereus knuthianus is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
