Typha abyssinica

Typha abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to Africa.

It thrives in freshwater marshes, lakes, and along the edges of rivers, where it forms dense stands. The plant has tall, cylindrical flowering spikes that produce fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss.

Typha abyssinica plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
