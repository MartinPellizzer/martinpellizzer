Taraxacum jaschilkuliense

Taraxacum jaschilkuliense, commonly known as the Jaschilkuli dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a slender, flowering stem that produces a single yellow flower head. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
