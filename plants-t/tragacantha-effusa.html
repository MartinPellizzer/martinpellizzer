Tragacantha effusa

Tragacantha effusa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus effusus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a viscous resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent. Tragacantha effusa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils and in open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
