Thrixspermum brunnescens

Thrixspermum brunnescens, commonly known as the brown thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture.

Thrixspermum brunnescens plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
