Thymus angustifolius f. silvicola

Thymus angustifolius f. silvicola, commonly known as the woodland thyme, is a subspecies of thyme characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.

The subspecies is well adapted to acidic soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
