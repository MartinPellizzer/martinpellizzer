Tunica pinetorum

Tunica pinetorum, commonly known as the forest cloak fern, is a unique plant native to the dense, shaded understories of temperate forests.

It possesses a distinctive frond structure with a thick, leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in low-light conditions. The plant's rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread slowly across the forest floor through vegetative growth. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy cuticle that provides additional protection against environmental stressors.

Tunica pinetorum plays a vital role in the ecosystem by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small forest organisms.
