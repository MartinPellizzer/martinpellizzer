Teucrium gnaphalodes var. pseudoaureum

Teucrium gnaphalodes var. pseudoaureum, commonly known as golden teucrium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long blooming period.
