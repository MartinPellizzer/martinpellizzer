Tetrastigma poilanei

Tetrastigma poilanei, commonly known as the Vietnamese ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
