Teucrium racemosum var. triflorum

Teucrium racemosum var. triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
