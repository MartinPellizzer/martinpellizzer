Thalictrum simplex var. armenum

Thalictrum simplex var. armenum, commonly known as Armenian meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a slender stem and finely divided foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
