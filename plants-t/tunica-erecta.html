Tunica erecta

Tunica erecta, commonly known as the upright stem plant, is a unique species characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is native to arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Tunica erecta plays a minor ecological role but is valued in certain horticultural applications for its ornamental appeal.
