Thymus chamaedrys subsp. nummularius

Thymus chamaedrys subsp.

nummularius, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features small, rounded leaves that are densely packed, giving it a mat-like appearance.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
