Tilia platyphyllos f. grandibracteata

Tilia platyphyllos f. grandibracteata, commonly known as the large-bract lime tree, is a cultivated form of the small-leaf lime tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, ovate bracts that are often more prominent than the flowers themselves. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly in gardens and parks.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
