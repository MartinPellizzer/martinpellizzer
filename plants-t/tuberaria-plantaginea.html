Tuberaria plantaginea

Tuberaria plantaginea, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Tuberaria plantaginea is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.
