Terminalia guyanensis

Terminalia guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana apple or yellow mangosteen, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a thick, fibrous rind. The fruit's flesh is typically white or yellow and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
