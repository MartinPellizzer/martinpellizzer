Tabebuia ochracea subsp. heteropoda

Tabebuia ochracea subsp.

heteropoda, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The plant has a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
