Ticorea pedicellata

Ticorea pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system.

Ticorea pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
