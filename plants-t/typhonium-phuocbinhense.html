Typhonium phuocbinhense

Typhonium phuocbinhense, commonly known as Phuoc Binh arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves.

It is native to Vietnam, specifically found in the Phuoc Binh region, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of many arum species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
