Tragopogon rezaiyensis

Tragopogon rezaiyensis, commonly known as the Iranian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Tragopogon. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Tragopogon rezaiyensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
