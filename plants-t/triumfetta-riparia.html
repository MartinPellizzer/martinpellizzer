Triumfetta riparia

Triumfetta riparia, commonly known as the river trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water sources, showcasing its preference for moist environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Triumfetta riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
