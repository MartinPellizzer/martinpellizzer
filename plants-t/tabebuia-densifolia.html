Tabebuia densifolia

Tabebuia densifolia, commonly known as the purple angelflower, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in the late winter to early spring. The tree has a dense, rounded canopy and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
