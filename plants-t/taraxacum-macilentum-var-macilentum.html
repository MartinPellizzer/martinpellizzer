Taraxacum macilentum var. macilentum

Taraxacum macilentum var. macilentum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and deeply lobed, dark green leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets, which eventually develops into a distinctive, hollow, umbrella-like seed dispersal structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
