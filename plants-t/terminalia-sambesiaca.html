Terminalia sambesiaca

Terminalia sambesiaca, commonly known as the Brazilian apple or sambaqui, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in appearance. Terminalia sambesiaca is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
