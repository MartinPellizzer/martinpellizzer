Tragia gayana

Tragia gayana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, often clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Tragia gayana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as thick, waxy leaves.

It is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and as a source of charcoal.
