Trichomanes lechleri

Trichomanes lechleri, commonly known as the "dragon's blood tree," is a unique fern-like plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and delicate, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a red, resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and decorative purposes. This sap, often referred to as "dragon's blood," contains various bioactive compounds with potential therapeutic properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Trichomanes lechleri is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this rare botanical species.
