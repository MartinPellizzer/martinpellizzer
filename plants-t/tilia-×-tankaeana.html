Tilia × tankaeana

Tilia × tankaeana, commonly known as the Caucasian linden, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree produces small, round fruits called samaras. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
