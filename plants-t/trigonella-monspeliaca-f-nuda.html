Trigonella monspeliaca f. nuda

Trigonella monspeliaca f. nuda, commonly known as the naked trigonella, is a variant of the trigonella plant characterized by its reduced or absent pubescence.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, typically found in dry, rocky soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seed pods are linear and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is often studied for its potential medicinal properties and adaptability to arid environments.
