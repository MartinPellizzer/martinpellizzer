Taxanthema tataricum

Taxanthema tataricum, commonly known as the Tatarian taxanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, sandy or rocky habitats.

Taxanthema tataricum is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
