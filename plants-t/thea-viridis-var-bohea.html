Thea viridis var. bohea

Thea viridis var. bohea, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a variant of the tea plant native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are rich in catechins and caffeine. The plant is typically grown in subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It thrives in shaded environments and requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce various types of tea.
