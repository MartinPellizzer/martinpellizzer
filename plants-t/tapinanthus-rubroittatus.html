Tapinanthus rubroittatus

Tapinanthus rubroittatus, commonly known as the red-stemmed dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that begins with a seedling stage before attaching to a host. The plant's stems are often reddish in color, and it is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its ability to parasitize a wide range of host plants.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host plant populations.
