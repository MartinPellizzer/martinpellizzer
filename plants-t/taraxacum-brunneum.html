Taraxacum brunneum

Taraxacum brunneum, commonly known as the brown hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in some regions.
