Trigonella ensifera

Trigonella ensifera, commonly known as the sickle vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Trigonella ensifera is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
