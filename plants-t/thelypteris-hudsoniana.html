Thelypteris hudsoniana

Thelypteris hudsoniana, commonly known as Hudson's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or ascending, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a shield. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

Thelypteris hudsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
