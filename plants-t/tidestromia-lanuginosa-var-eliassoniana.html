Tidestromia lanuginosa var. eliassoniana

Tidestromia lanuginosa var. eliassoniana, commonly known as the Eliasson's hairy tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its characteristic "hairy" appearance.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
