Thelypteris leucothrix

Thelypteris leucothrix, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Thelypteris leucothrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
