Thalictrum leucocrinum

Thalictrum leucocrinum, commonly known as white meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of white, lacecap flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Thalictrum leucocrinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
