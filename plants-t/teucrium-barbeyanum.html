Teucrium barbeyanum

Teucrium barbeyanum, commonly known as Barbey's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from blue to purple.

Teucrium barbeyanum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
