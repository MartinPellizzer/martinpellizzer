Truellum dissitiflorum

Truellum dissitiflorum, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its botanical structure is adapted to conserve water, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.
