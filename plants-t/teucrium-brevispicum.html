Teucrium brevispicum

Teucrium brevispicum, commonly known as narrow-headed germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring square stems and opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in the summer months.

Teucrium brevispicum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
