Thunbergia hirta

Thunbergia hirta, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia hirta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
