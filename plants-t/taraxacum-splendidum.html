Taraxacum splendidum

Taraxacum splendidum, commonly known as the splendid dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow, leafless stem that supports the flower head. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and typically have a serrated edge, while the plant produces a puffball-like seed head that disperses its seeds by wind.

Taraxacum splendidum is often mistaken for other dandelion species due to its similar appearance, but it can be distinguished by its more vibrant flower color and specific geographic distribution.
