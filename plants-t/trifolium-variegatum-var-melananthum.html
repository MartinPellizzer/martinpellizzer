Trifolium variegatum var. melananthum

Trifolium variegatum var. melananthum, commonly known as the black clover, is a variant of the variable clover species.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its typical white or pink blooms. This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
