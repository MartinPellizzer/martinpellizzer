Taraxacum nanulum

Taraxacum nanulum, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, sturdy stem that supports a single, inconspicuous yellow flower. The plant is typically found in temperate regions, often growing in grassy areas or disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into small, white pappus-bearing seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum nanulum is a hardy perennial that thrives in a variety of soil conditions and is relatively easy to cultivate.
