Teucrium polium subvar. barrelieri

Teucrium polium subvar. barrelieri, commonly known as Moroccan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
