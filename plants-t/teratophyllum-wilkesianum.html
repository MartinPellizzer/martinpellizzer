Teratophyllum wilkesianum

Teratophyllum wilkesianum, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a unique plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, green fig-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Teratophyllum wilkesianum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various species.
