Tacsonia mixta subsp. tomentosa

Tacsonia mixta subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly tacsonia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
