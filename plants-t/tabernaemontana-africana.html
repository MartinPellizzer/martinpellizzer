Tabernaemontana africana

Tabernaemontana africana, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
