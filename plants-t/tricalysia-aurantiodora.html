Tricalysia aurantiodora

Tricalysia aurantiodora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a vibrant appearance during the flowering season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
