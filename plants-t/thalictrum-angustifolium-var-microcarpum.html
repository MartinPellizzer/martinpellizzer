Thalictrum angustifolium var. microcarpum

Thalictrum angustifolium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.
