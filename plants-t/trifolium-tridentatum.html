Trifolium tridentatum

Trifolium tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Trifolium tridentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
