Tragacantha spinescens

Tragacantha spinescens, commonly known as the spiny tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its sap, which is viscous and gum-like, has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Tragacantha spinescens thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
