Trachycarpus caespitosus

Trachycarpus caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a slightly rough texture.

Trachycarpus caespitosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
