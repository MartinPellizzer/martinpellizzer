Ticorea longiflora

Ticorea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered tickseed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers with dark centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Ticorea longiflora is often found in open fields and along roadsides, where it adds vibrant color to the landscape.
