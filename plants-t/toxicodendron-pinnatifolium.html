Toxicodendron pinnatifolium

Toxicodendron pinnatifolium, commonly known as the sumac tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy fruits. It contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

This species is often found in dry woodlands and is part of the Anacardiaceae family.
