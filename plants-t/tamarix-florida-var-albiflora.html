Tamarix florida var. albiflora

Tamarix florida var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, pale pink to white flowers in panicle-like clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, though it can be invasive in some regions.
