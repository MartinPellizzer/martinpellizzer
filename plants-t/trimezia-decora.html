Trimezia decora

Trimezia decora, commonly known as the velvetleaf trimezia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Trimezia decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its foliage and delicate blooms.
