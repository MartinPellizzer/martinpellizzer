Thymus adamovicii

Thymus adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovici thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

Thymus adamovicii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
