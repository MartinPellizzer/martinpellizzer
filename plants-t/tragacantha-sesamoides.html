Tragacantha sesamoides

Tragacantha sesamoides, commonly known as the Armenian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It produces a distinctive resinous sap that is harvested for its medicinal and industrial uses. The plant features woody stems with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it blooms during the summer months.

The resin, known as tragacanth, has been historically used as a thickening agent and in traditional medicine.
