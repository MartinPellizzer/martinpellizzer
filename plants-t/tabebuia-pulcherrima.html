Tabebuia pulcherrima

Tabebuia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy, making it a striking landscape feature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and pointed tips.

The wood of the flame tree is durable and has been used in traditional construction and furniture-making.
