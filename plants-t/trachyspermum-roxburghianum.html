Trachyspermum roxburghianum

Trachyspermum roxburghianum, commonly known as Indian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces aromatic, oval-shaped seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its foliage is pinnately divided, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The essential oils extracted from its seeds contain compounds like limonene and pinene, contributing to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.
