Trichomanes hymenoides f. socialis

Trichomanes hymenoides f. socialis, commonly known as the social maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures on the underside.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
