Triodia wiseana var. brevifolia

Triodia wiseana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clumps of narrow, wiry leaves that are typically covered in fine, prickly hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and poor soil quality.

This variety is often found in open grasslands and is an important component of the native vegetation in its habitat.
