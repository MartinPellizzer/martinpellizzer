Trisetum subspicatum var. compactum

Trisetum subspicatum var. compactum, commonly known as the compact hairgrass, is a grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It typically forms low, compact clumps with fine, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
