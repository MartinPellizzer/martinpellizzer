Thymelaea argentata

Thymelaea argentata, commonly known as silver thymelaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Thymelaeaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Thymelaea argentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to withstand dry conditions.
