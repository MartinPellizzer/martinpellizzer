Tococa desiliens

Tococa desiliens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
