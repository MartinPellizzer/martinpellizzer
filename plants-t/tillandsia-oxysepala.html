Tillandsia oxysepala

Tillandsia oxysepala, commonly known as the silver-tipped air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to tree trunks or branches. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom from the base of the rosette, and it is well adapted to humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
