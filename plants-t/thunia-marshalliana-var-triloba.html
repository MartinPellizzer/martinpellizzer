Thunia marshalliana var. triloba

Thunia marshalliana var. triloba, commonly known as the Marshall's thunia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by experienced growers.
