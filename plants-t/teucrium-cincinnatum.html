Teucrium cincinnatum

Teucrium cincinnatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Teucrium cincinnatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
