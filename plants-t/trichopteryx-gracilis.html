Trichopteryx gracilis

Trichopteryx gracilis, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, violet to blue in color, and are arranged in spike-like clusters.

Trichopteryx gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
