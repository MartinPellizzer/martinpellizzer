Tibouchina lasiophylla

Tibouchina lasiophylla, commonly known as the woolly tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, Tibouchina lasiophylla is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
