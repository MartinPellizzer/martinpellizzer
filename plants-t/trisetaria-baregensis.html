Trisetaria baregensis

Trisetaria baregensis, commonly known as the baregenian grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Trisetaria baregensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
