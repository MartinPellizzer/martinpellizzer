Thalictrum schleicheri

Thalictrum schleicheri, commonly known as Schleicher's meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum schleicheri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
