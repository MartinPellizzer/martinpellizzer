Triumfetta rhombeifolia

Triumfetta rhombeifolia, commonly known as the diamond leaf or African starleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
