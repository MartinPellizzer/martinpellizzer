Taraxacum vexatum

Taraxacum vexatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, tall flowering stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, with both ray and disk florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Taraxacum vexatum is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
