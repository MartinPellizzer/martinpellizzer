Taraxacum rubidipes

Taraxacum rubidipes, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces small, oval seeds with white pappus that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Taraxacum rubidipes is a perennial herb that is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world.
