Tetracera fagifolia f. subintegerrima

Tetracera fagifolia f. subintegerrima, commonly known as the smooth-leafed tetracera, is a variant of the tetracera fagifolia species.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This form is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to the typical form.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland rainforests.
