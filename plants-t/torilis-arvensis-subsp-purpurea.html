Torilis arvensis subsp. purpurea

Torilis arvensis subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple field penny-cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a low, spreading growth habit.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, with a pinnate structure. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

The species is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged stems and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
