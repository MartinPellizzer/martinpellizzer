Triplarina bancroftii

Triplarina bancroftii, commonly known as the African ghost tree, is a small, evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
