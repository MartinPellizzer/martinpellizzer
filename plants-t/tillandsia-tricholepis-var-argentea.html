Tillandsia tricholepis var. argentea

Tillandsia tricholepis var. argentea, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a striking, metallic appearance. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is popular among succulent and air plant enthusiasts due to its unique aesthetic and relatively low maintenance requirements.
