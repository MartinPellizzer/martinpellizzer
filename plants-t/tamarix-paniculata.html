Tamarix paniculata

Tamarix paniculata, commonly known as the desert rose or Indian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is extensive and can help stabilize sandy soils.

Tamarix paniculata is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
