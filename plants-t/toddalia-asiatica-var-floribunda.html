Toddalia asiatica var. floribunda

Toddalia asiatica var. floribunda, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer. The plant produces woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscaped settings.
