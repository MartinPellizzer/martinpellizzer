Tolpis crassiuscula

Tolpis crassiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Tolpis crassiuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
