Tupeia reinwardtiana

Tupeia reinwardtiana, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
