Tetrapterys calophylla

Tetrapterys calophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often silvery-white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Tetrapterys calophylla is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
