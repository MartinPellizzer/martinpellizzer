Thelymitra

Thelymitra, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a genus of terrestrial orchids native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, often colorful, and showy flowers that resemble rainbows, hence their common name. Thelymitra species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce spikes of small, intricate flowers. They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and woodlands.

The genus includes over 40 species, many of which are prized for their ornamental value.
