Teucrium mascatense

Teucrium mascatense, commonly known as the Arabian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Teucrium mascatense is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine and its resilience in harsh climates.
