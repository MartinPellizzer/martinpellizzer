Taraxacum surrigens

Taraxacum surrigens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with parachute-like seeds. It is characterized by its taproot system and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
