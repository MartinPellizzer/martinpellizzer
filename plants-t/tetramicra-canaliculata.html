Tetramicra canaliculata

Tetramicra canaliculata, commonly known as the canyon dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring.

The species is known for its resilience and is often found in canyon areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
