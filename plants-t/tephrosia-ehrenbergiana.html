Tephrosia ehrenbergiana

Tephrosia ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
