Tassadia capitata

Tassadia capitata, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Tassadia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
