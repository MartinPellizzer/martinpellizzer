Thymus angustifolius var. inolens

Thymus angustifolius var. inolens, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
