Teucrium flavescens var. candicans

Teucrium flavescens var. candicans, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
