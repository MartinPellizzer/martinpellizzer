Trollius germanicus

Trollius germanicus, commonly known as the German buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces numerous yellow-orange flowers with 5 to 8 sepals that resemble petals. Its leaves are palmately divided into 5-9 lobes, and the plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 centimeters.

Trollius germanicus is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
