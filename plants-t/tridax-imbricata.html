Tridax imbricata

Tridax imbricata, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the seeds are covered in hooked bristles that aid in dispersal.

Tridax imbricata is native to the Americas but has been introduced to various parts of the world, often becoming an invasive species.
