Turnera venezuelana

Turnera venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan turnera, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its evergreen, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, while its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators.

Turnera venezuelana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
