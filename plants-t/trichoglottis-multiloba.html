Trichoglottis multiloba

Trichoglottis multiloba, commonly known as the waxy-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
