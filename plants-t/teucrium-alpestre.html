Teucrium alpestre

Teucrium alpestre, commonly known as alpine germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Teucrium alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
