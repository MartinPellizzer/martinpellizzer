Tolpis virgata subsp. apolloniae

Tolpis virgata subsp.

apolloniae, commonly known as the Apollonian lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, often blue or purple-colored leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
