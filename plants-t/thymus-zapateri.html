Thymus zapateri

Thymus zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oil content.

Thymus zapateri thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
