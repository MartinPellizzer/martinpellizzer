Teucrium zaianum

Teucrium zaianum, commonly known as the Zaian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Teucrium zaianum is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive with minimal water.
