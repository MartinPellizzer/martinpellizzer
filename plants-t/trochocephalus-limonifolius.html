Trochocephalus limonifolius

Trochocephalus limonifolius, commonly known as the lemon-leaf tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with a dense canopy.

The botanical features of T. limonifolius make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
