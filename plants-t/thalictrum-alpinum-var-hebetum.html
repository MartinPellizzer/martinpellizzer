Thalictrum alpinum var. hebetum

Thalictrum alpinum var. hebetum, commonly known as the alpine meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
