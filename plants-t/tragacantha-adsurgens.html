Tragacantha adsurgens

Tragacantha adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing tragacanth, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing seeds. Its roots are known for producing a gel-like substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha adsurgens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
