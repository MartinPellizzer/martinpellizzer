Triticum dicoccum var. taschkentum

Triticum dicoccum var. taschkentum, commonly known as Kamut, is a ancient grain species native to the Fertile Crescent.

It is characterized by its large, amber-colored grains and high protein content. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types. It produces spikelets with multiple florets, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its nutritional profile and is often used in health-conscious baking and cooking.
