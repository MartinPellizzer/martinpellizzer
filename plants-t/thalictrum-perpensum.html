Thalictrum perpensum

Thalictrum perpensum, commonly known as the nodding meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Thalictrum perpensum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
