Tecoma actinophylla

Tecoma actinophylla, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Tecoma actinophylla is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for wildlife in its native ecosystems.
