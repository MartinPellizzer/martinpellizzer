Taraxacum remotilobum

Taraxacum remotilobum, commonly known as the western dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, slender stem that bears a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
