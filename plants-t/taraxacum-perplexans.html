Taraxacum perplexans

Taraxacum perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which are typical of the dandelion genus. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

This species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.
