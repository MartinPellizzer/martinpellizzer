Tachigali micrantha

Tachigali micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited tachigali, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that contains several small, hard seeds.

Tachigali micrantha is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
