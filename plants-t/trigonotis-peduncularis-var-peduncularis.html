Trigonotis peduncularis var. peduncularis

Trigonotis peduncularis var. peduncularis, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with a distinctive white center. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
