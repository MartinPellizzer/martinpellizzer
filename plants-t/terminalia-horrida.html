Terminalia horrida

Terminalia horrida, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Terminalia horrida is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native range.
