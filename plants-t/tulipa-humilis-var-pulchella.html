Tulipa humilis var. pulchella

Tulipa humilis var. pulchella, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to cool, dry climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and resilience to adverse weather conditions.
