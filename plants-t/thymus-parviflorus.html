Thymus parviflorus

Thymus parviflorus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it a dense, carpet-like appearance. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
