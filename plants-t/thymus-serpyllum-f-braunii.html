Thymus serpyllum f. braunii

Thymus serpyllum f. braunii, commonly known as Braun's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
