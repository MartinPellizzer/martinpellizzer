Tricuspis albescens

Tricuspis albescens, commonly known as the silver-leafed tricuspis, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a wavy, tricuspis margin. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the early spring. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down that reflects sunlight, helping to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Tricuspis albescens is highly valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to rocky soils.
