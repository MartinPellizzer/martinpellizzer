Trifolium seravschanicum

Trifolium seravschanicum, commonly known as the Seravschan clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Trifolium seravschanicum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
