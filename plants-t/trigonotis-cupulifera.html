Trigonotis cupulifera

Trigonotis cupulifera, commonly known as the cup-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-like flowers that are often pale blue or violet in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Trigonotis cupulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
