Teucrium polium var. aurasiacum

Teucrium polium var. aurasiacum, commonly known as the Aurasic mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
