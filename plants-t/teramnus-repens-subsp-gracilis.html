Teramnus repens subsp. gracilis

Teramnus repens subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender trailing teramnus, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy and ornamental characteristics.
