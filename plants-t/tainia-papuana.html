Tainia papuana

Tainia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan tainia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants like philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant features a short, stocky stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family.

Tainia papuana is typically found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
