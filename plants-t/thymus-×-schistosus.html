Thymus × schistosus

Thymus × schistosus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to regions of southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils and antimicrobial properties.
