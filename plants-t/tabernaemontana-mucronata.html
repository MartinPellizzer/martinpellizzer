Tabernaemontana mucronata

Tabernaemontana mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed starflower, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has elliptical, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
