Tapura amazonica var. sublanceolata

Tapura amazonica var. sublanceolata, commonly known as the Amazonian lanceleaf tapura, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
