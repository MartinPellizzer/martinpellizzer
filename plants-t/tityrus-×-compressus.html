Tityrus × compressus

Tityrus × compressus, commonly known as the hybrid tityrus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Tityrus sanguineus and Tityrus olivaceus.

It is a small, colorful bird species found in parts of southern Africa. The male exhibits striking plumage with a red head and black body, while the female is more subdued in color. It inhabits open woodlands and savannas, often near water sources.

The species plays a role in seed dispersal and is part of the broader ecological dynamics of its habitat.
