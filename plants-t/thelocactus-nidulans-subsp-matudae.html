Thelocactus nidulans subsp. matudae

Thelocactus nidulans subsp.

matudae, commonly known as the Matudas' thorned cactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thick, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
