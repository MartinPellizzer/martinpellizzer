Thalictrum minus proles schultzii

Thalictrum minus proles schultzii, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds.

Thalictrum minus proles schultzii is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
