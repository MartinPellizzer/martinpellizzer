Tecoma leucoxylon var. pentaphylla

Tecoma leucoxylon var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
