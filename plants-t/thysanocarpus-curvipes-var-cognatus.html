Thysanocarpus curvipes var. cognatus

Thysanocarpus curvipes var. cognatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thysanocarpus, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved pods that contain seeds with tufted hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
