Tetradenia parvigemma

Tetradenia parvigemma, commonly known as the small-leaf tetradenia, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Tetradenia parvigemma is often found in rocky outcrops and open woodlands, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
