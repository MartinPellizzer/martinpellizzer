Tylecodon aurusbergensis

Tylecodon aurusbergensis, commonly known as the aurora ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
