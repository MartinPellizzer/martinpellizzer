Trapa bicornis var. cochinchinensis

Trapa bicornis var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, floating leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its fruit is a spiny, spherical structure that contains one or two seeds.

This species is often found in shallow, still waters and is valued for its edible seeds and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
