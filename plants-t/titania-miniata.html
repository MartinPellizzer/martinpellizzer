Titania miniata

Titania miniata, commonly known as the flame violet, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to purple in color. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, Titania miniata is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
