Taraxacum flumineum

Taraxacum flumineum, commonly known as the water dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant thrives in moist, often wet habitats such as marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
