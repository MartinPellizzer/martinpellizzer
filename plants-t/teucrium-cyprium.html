Teucrium cyprium

Teucrium cyprium, commonly known as Cyprus germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
