Teucrium canadense var. nashii

Teucrium canadense var. nashii, commonly known as Nash's germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in the summer months.
