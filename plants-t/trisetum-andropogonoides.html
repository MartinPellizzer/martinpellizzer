Trisetum andropogonoides

Trisetum andropogonoides, commonly known as the tallgrass panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
