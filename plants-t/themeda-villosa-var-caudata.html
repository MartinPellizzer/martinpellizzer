Themeda villosa var. caudata

Themeda villosa var. caudata, commonly known as the tussock grass or red tussock, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, silky, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brown flowers that appear in spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

This grass is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, playing a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
