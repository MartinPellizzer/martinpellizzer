Tetrapilus rubrovenius

Tetrapilus rubrovenius, commonly known as the red-veined tetrapilus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red veins that run through its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
