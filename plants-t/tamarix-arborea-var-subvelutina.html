Tamarix arborea var. subvelutina

Tamarix arborea var. subvelutina, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid environments.

This variety is known for its adaptability and ability to tolerate salt and drought conditions.
