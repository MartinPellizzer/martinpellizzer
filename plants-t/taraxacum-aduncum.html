Taraxacum aduncum

Taraxacum aduncum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed stalk known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelions and related plants.
