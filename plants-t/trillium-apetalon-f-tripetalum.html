Trillium apetalon f. tripetalum

Trillium apetalon f. tripetalum, commonly known as the three-petaled trillium, is a rare and striking botanical variant of the trillium genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-petaled flowers, which are typically a vibrant shade of white or pink. The plant features three broad, waxy petals that are often larger than the sepals, giving it a more elaborate appearance. It is typically found in shaded, moist woodland areas, where it thrives in the understory of deciduous forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
