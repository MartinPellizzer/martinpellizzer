Thalictrum glandulosissimum

Thalictrum glandulosissimum, commonly known as the glandular meadow rue, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with numerous stamens and a single pistil.

Thalictrum glandulosissimum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
