Tillandsia crocata

Tillandsia crocata, commonly known as the Crocus Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia crocata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
