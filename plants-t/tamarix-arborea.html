Tamarix arborea

Tamarix arborea, commonly known as the white tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces delicate, pink or white flowers in panicles during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tamarix arborea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas, sandy soils, and along riverbanks.

It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
