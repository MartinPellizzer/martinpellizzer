Trillium flavum

Trillium flavum, commonly known as the yellow trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade. The plant features three elliptical leaves and a single, showy yellow flower with three petals. It blooms in early spring, often before its leaves fully emerge.

Trillium flavum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for early pollinators.
