Trifolium bifidum var. decipiens

Trifolium bifidum var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, arranged in clusters.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
