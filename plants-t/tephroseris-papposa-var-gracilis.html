Tephroseris papposa var. gracilis

Tephroseris papposa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pappose tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a low, herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with prominent pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal of seeds.

It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
