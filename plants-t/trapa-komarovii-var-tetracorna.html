Trapa komarovii var. tetracorna

Trapa komarovii var. tetracorna, commonly known as the four-cornered water chestnut, is a water plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and floating leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and aquatic biodiversity.
