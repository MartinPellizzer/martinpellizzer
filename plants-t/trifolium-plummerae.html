Trifolium plummerae

Trifolium plummerae, commonly known as Plummer's clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. It is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
