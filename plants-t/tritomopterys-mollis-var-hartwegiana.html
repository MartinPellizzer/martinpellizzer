Tritomopterys mollis var. hartwegiana

Tritomopterys mollis var. hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's velvetleaf, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
