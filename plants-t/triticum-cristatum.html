Triticum cristatum

Triticum cristatum, commonly known as the crested wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crested or tufted inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often cultivated for soil stabilization and erosion control due to its hardy nature.

Triticum cristatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping and garden settings.
