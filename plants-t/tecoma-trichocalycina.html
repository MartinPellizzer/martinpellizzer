Tecoma trichocalycina

Tecoma trichocalycina, commonly known as the golden trumpet or yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma trichocalycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
