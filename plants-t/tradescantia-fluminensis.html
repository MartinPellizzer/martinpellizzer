Tradescantia fluminensis

Tradescantia fluminensis, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale purple, which are usually borne on slender stems. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.

Its adaptability to various light conditions and tolerance to drought make it a versatile choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
