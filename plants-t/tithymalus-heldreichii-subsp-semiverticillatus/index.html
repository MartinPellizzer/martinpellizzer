Tithymalus Heldreichii Subsp. Semiverticillatus

Heldreich's tithymalus, scientifically known as Tithymalus heldreichii subsp. semiverticillatus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish. Due to its limited geographic range and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical gem for future generations.
