Tragia affinis

Tragia affinis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Tragia affinis is known for its adaptability and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
