Tradescantia fuscata

Tradescantia fuscata, commonly known as the silver sword or red-veined nettle, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with silver and green hues. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have prominent red veins, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
