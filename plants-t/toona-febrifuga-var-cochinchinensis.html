Toona febrifuga var. cochinchinensis

Toona febrifuga var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese mahogany or Cochinchina toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
