Tropaeolum harlingii

Tropaeolum harlingii, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be used as a leaf vegetable in some cuisines.

Tropaeolum harlingii thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
