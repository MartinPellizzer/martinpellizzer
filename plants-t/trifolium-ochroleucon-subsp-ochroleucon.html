Trifolium ochroleucon subsp. ochroleucon

Trifolium ochroleucon subsp.

ochroleucon, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval to rhombic in shape with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spreading clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
