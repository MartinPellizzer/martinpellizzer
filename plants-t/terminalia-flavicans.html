Terminalia flavicans

Terminalia flavicans, commonly known as the yellow Terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
