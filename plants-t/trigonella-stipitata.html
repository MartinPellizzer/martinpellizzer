Trigonella stipitata

Trigonella stipitata, commonly known as the slender fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella stipitata is typically found in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in wetland or marshy environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
