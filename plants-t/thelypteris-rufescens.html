Thelypteris rufescens

Thelypteris rufescens, commonly known as the rusty holly fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds with a reddish-brown underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Thelypteris rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
