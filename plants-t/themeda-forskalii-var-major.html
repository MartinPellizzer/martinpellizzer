Themeda forskalii var. major

Themeda forskalii var. major, commonly known as the tall kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and along roadsides.
