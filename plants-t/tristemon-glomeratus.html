Tristemon glomeratus

Tristemon glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered tristemon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tristemon glomeratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
