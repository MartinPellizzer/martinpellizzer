Triticum araraticum subsp. kurdistanicum

Triticum araraticum subsp.

kurdistanicum, commonly known as the Kurdish wheat, is a rare and lesser-known subspecies of wheat native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and relatively small, hard grains.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting tolerance to cold and arid conditions. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a short, sturdy stem, which help it survive in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it holds potential for agricultural research and crop diversity studies.
