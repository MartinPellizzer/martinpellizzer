Triticum vulgare var. fuchsii

Triticum vulgare var. fuchsii, commonly known as fuchsii wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It is notable for its branched inflorescence, which sets it apart from typical wheat varieties. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its botanical features include awned lemmas and a compact spike structure.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral architecture and potential for genetic research.
