Tanacetum tenuifolium

Tanacetum tenuifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Tanacetum tenuifolium has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive benefits.
