Thymus × pseudopulcherrimus

Thymus × pseudopulcherrimus, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, fuzzy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
