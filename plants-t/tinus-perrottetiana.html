Tinus perrottetiana

Tinus perrottetiana, commonly known as the Perrottet's tinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Tinus perrottetiana is also noted for its adaptability to dry conditions and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
