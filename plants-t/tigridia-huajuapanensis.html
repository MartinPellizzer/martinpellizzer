Tigridia huajuapanensis

Tigridia huajuapanensis, commonly known as the Huajuapan tiger flower, is a striking bulbous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble those of the tiger lily. The plant produces striking red and orange blooms with contrasting black veins, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Tigridia huajuapanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
