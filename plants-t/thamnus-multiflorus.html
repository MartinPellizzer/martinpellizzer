Thamnus multiflorus

Thamnus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered thamnus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thamnus multiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
