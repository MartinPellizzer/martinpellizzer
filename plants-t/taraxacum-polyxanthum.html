Taraxacum polyxanthum

Taraxacum polyxanthum, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its life cycle includes a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.
