Tragacantha turkestana

Tragacantha turkestana, commonly known as the Turkestan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insect species.
