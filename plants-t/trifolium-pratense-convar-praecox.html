Trifolium pratense convar. praecox

Trifolium pratense convar. praecox, commonly known as white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is widely cultivated for forage and soil nitrogen fixation.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it a common weed in lawns and agricultural fields.
