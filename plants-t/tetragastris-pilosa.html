Tetragastris pilosa

Tetragastris pilosa, commonly known as the West Indian evergreen or the Caribbean evergreen, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often fragrant and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
