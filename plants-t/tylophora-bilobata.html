Tylophora bilobata

Tylophora bilobata, commonly known as the two-lobed tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
