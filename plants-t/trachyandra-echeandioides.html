Trachyandra echeandioides

Trachyandra echeandioides, commonly known as the Echeandia trachyandra, is a unique member of the Asphodelaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. Native to South Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
