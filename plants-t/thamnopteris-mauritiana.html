Thamnopteris mauritiana

Thamnopteris mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating fronds that are adapted for life in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption from the water.

Thamnopteris mauritiana plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
