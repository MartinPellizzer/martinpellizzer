Trichophorum atrocinctum

Trichophorum atrocinctum, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark purple or blackish stems and leaves, which are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as bogs, marshes, and wet meadows.

Trichophorum atrocinctum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
