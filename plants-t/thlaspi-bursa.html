Thlaspi bursa

Thlaspi bursa, commonly known as bladder campion, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces spherical, bladder-like seed pods that are distinctive and help in seed dispersal. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arctic regions.

Thlaspi bursa is a hardy species that thrives in cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
