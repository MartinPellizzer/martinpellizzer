Tamarix mannifera var. persica

Tamarix mannifera var. persica, commonly known as Persian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, often reddish-pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of salt-resistant tissue, which helps it thrive in saline soils.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
