Trachymene psammophila

Trachymene psammophila, commonly known as the sandplain false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Trachymene psammophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
