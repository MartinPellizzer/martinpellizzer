Tanacetum flavovirens subsp. tamrutense

Tanacetum flavovirens subsp.

tamrutense, commonly known as the Tamrutense tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Its subspecific distinction highlights its unique ecological and botanical traits compared to other members of the Tanacetum genus.
