Trifolium nemorosum

Trifolium nemorosum, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows.

Trifolium nemorosum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
