Trillium giganteum var. chloropetalum

Trillium giganteum var. chloropetalum, commonly known as the green-petaled giant trillium, is a striking North American woodland plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It features three broad, waxy petals that are typically a vibrant green, which distinguishes it from other trillium species. The plant has three large, veined leaves that emerge from a single stem, and it produces a single, three-lobed fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in rich, moist soils under partial shade and is often found in deciduous forests.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable indicator of undisturbed forest habitats.
