Taraxacum gentile

Taraxacum gentile, commonly known as the gentile dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, ridged stem. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets surrounded by disk florets. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and meadows.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
