Taraxacum corynodiforme

Taraxacum corynodiforme, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green leaves and a distinctive, elongated stem that supports its flower head. The plant produces a composite flower head composed of numerous small, yellow florets surrounded by a crown of bracts. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Taraxacum corynodiforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
