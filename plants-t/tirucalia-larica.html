Tirucalia larica

Tirucalia larica, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Tirucalia larica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical and subtropical regions.
