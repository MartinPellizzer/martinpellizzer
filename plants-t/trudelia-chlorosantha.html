Trudelia chlorosantha

Trudelia chlorosantha, commonly known as the green-flowered trudelia, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep emerald green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil.

Trudelia chlorosantha is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
