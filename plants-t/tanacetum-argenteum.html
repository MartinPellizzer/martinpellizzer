Tanacetum argenteum

Tanacetum argenteum, commonly known as silver leaf tansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is often cultivated in gardens.
