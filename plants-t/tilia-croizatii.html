Tilia croizatii

Tilia croizatii, commonly known as the Croizat's lime, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain three to four seeds. It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
