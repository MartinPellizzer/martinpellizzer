Tetrastigma laevigatum

Tetrastigma laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed ivy grape, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
