Taraxacum cordilleranum

Taraxacum cordilleranum, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces a yellow flower head that develops into a crown-like seed head. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
