Tecoma pratensis

Tecoma pratensis, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Tecoma pratensis is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
