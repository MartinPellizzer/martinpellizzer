Tillandsia denudata var. denudata

Tillandsia denudata var. denudata, commonly known as the naked tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a delicate floral touch to its appearance. It thrives in humid, well-ventilated environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
