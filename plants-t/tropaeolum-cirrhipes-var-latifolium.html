Tropaeolum cirrhipes var. latifolium

Tropaeolum cirrhipes var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant belonging to the Tropaeolaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and strikingly colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy blooms and edible leaves and seeds.

The variety 'latifolium' is distinguished by its wider foliage compared to other tropaeolum species.
