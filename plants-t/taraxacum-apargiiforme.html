Taraxacum apargiiforme

Taraxacum apargiiforme, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hairy stem that supports a single, elongated flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed capsule known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
