Trachyspermum tenue

Trachyspermum tenue, commonly known as wild cumin, is an aromatic annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by oval, aromatic seeds. Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications for their pungent, spicy scent.

The botanical characteristics of Trachyspermum tenue make it well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
