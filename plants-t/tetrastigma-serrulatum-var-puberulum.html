Tetrastigma serrulatum var. puberulum

Tetrastigma serrulatum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy climbing raisin vine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

The fruits are small, round, and resemble raisins when dried.
