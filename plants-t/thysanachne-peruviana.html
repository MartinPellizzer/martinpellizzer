Thysanachne peruviana

Thysanachne peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist and nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often surrounded by bracts, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Thysanachne peruviana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
