Tupistra lichuanensis

Tupistra lichuanensis, commonly known as the Lichuan spurge, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Tupistra lichuanensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xerophytic gardens.
