Terminalia obidensis

Terminalia obidensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
