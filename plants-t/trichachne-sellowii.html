Trichachne sellowii

Trichachne sellowii, commonly known as Sellow's trichachne, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Trichachne sellowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
