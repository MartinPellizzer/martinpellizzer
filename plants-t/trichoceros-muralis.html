Trichoceros muralis

Trichoceros muralis, commonly known as the wall ivy, is a climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and its ability to thrive on walls and other vertical surfaces. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Trichoceros muralis is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover unsightly surfaces and its low maintenance requirements.
