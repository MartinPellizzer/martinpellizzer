Titanodendron schumanniana

Titanodendron schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumann's titanodendron, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, woody trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the wet season. Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires protected environments to survive.
