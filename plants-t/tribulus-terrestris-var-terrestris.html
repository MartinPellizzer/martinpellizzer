Tribulus terrestris var. terrestris

Tribulus terrestris var. terrestris, commonly known as puncture vine, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers and produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
