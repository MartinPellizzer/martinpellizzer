Tricyrtis

Tricyrtis, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, iris-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Tricyrtis species are native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China, and are valued for their ornamental beauty.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to the plant's visual appeal.
