Thymelaea coridifolia subsp. coridifolia

Thymelaea coridifolia subsp.

coridifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thymelaea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowers.
