Trichodium trichanthum

Trichodium trichanthum, commonly known as the sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
