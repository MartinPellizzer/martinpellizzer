Trillium rhomboideum var. grandiflorum

Trillium rhomboideum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the showy trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features three broadly ovate, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces a single, showy white or pale pink flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, rich soil and partial to full shade, often found in deciduous forests.

The species is known for its striking appearance and is considered an indicator plant of undisturbed forest ecosystems.
