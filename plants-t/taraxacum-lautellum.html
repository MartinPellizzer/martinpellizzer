Taraxacum lautellum

Taraxacum lautellum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed sphere. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is a perennial species with a long growing season, typically blooming from spring to early summer.
