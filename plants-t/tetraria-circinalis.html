Tetraria circinalis

Tetraria circinalis, commonly known as the common tetraria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces achenes as its fruit, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Tetraria circinalis is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
