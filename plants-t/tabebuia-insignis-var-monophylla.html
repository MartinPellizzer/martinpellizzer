Tabebuia insignis var. monophylla

Tabebuia insignis var. monophylla, commonly known as the white ceiba, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, umbrella-like crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
