Trifolium longipes f. neurophyllum

Trifolium longipes f. neurophyllum, commonly known as the neurophyllum variant of the longipes clover, is a botanical subspecies characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense, spreading clusters. This subspecies is adapted to specific ecological niches, often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic traits and potential role in ecological studies.
