Trichinium sessilifolium

Trichinium sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaved trichinium, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, sessile leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Trichinium sessilifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
