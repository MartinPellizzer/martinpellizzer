Teucrium buxifolium subsp. thymifolium

Teucrium buxifolium subsp.

thymifolium, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its aromatic foliage and compact growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
