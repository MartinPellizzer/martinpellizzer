Toisusu cardiophylla var. maximoviczii

Toisusu cardiophylla var. maximoviczii, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
