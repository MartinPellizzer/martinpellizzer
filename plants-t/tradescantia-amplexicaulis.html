Tradescantia amplexicaulis

Tradescantia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver streaks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on slender spikes during the growing season. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and indirect light.

Its ability to purify the air makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
