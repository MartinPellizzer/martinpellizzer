Trichostomanthemum

Trichostomanthemum, commonly known as the silverleaf germander, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of the Middle East. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Trichostomanthemum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
