Tiarella cordifolia

Tiarella cordifolia, commonly known as foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early to mid-spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ground cover.
