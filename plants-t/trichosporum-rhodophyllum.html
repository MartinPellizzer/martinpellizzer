Trichosporum rhodophyllum

Trichosporum rhodophyllum, commonly known as the red thread fungus, is a filamentous aquatic alga belonging to the order Rhodophyales.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange pigmentation due to the presence of phycoerythrins, which give it its distinctive color. This organism is typically found in marine and brackish water environments, often forming dense mats on submerged surfaces. Its thallus consists of branching filaments that are covered in small, spiny cells.

Trichosporum rhodophyllum plays a role in nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems and is sometimes used in biotechnological applications.
