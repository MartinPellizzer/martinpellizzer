Themistoclesia buxifolia

Themistoclesia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
