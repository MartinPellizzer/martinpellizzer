Thesidium thunbergii

Thesidium thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Native to Japan, it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
