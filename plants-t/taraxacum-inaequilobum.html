Taraxacum inaequilobum

Taraxacum inaequilobum, commonly known as the unequal-lobed dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette structure. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive, toothed margin. Its flowers are yellow and produce a fluffy, wind-dispersed seed head.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
