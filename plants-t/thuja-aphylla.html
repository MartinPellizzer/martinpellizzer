Thuja aphylla

Thuja aphylla, commonly known as the dwarf arborvitae, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It has a compact, pyramidal shape with dense, scale-like leaves that are light green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Thuja aphylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
