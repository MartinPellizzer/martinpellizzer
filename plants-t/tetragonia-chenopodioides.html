Tetragonia chenopodioides

Tetragonia chenopodioides, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are oval-shaped, fleshy, and have a mild, spinach-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
