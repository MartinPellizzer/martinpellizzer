Tithymalus dumosus

Tithymalus dumosus, commonly known as the tufted tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tithymalus dumosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
