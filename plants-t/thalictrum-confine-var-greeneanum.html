Thalictrum confine var. greeneanum

Thalictrum confine var. greeneanum, commonly known as the Greene's meadowrue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem and a unique, compound leaf structure.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
