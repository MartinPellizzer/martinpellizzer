Tsuga chinensis subsp. wardii

Tsuga chinensis subsp.

wardii, commonly known as the Ward's hemlock, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
