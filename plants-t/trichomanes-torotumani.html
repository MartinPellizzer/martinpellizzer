Trichomanes torotumani

Trichomanes torotumani, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Trichomanes torotumani is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
