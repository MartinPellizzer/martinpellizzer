Tephrosia oxalidea

Tephrosia oxalidea, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Tephrosia oxalidea also has traditional uses in some cultures, including as a source of medicinal compounds.
