Trifolium fragiferum subsp. pulchellum

Trifolium fragiferum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
