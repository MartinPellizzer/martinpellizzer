Trifolium molybdocalyx

Trifolium molybdocalyx, commonly known as the blue clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium molybdocalyx is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
