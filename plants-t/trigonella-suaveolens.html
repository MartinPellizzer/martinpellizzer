Trigonella suaveolens

Trigonella suaveolens, commonly known as sweet caraway, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and the plant has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Trigonella suaveolens is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
