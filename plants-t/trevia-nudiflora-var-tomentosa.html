Trevia nudiflora var. tomentosa

Trevia nudiflora var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly trevia, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are broad and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
