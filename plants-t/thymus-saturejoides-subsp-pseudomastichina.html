Thymus saturejoides subsp. pseudomastichina

Thymus saturejoides subsp.

pseudomastichina, commonly known as the Turkish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found growing in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
