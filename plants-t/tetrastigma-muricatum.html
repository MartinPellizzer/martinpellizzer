Tetrastigma muricatum

Tetrastigma muricatum, commonly known as the hairy passionvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when ripe.

Tetrastigma muricatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
