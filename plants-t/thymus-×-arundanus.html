Thymus × arundanus

Thymus × arundanus, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and small, pink or lavender flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats, with stems that are often prostrate or ascending. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oils rich in thymol and other volatile compounds.

Thymus × arundanus is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
