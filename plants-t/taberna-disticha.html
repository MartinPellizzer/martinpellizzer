Taberna disticha

Taberna disticha, commonly known as the two-branched broom.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Taberna disticha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
