Taraxacum haematopodides

Taraxacum haematopodides, commonly known as the bloodroot dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its flowers produce small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
