Tinaea elegans

Tinaea elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf tinaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
