Tragopogon badali

Tragopogon badali, commonly known as the badali thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a long, slender stem that bears a single flower head at the top. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Tragopogon badali is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.
