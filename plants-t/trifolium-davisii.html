Trifolium davisii

Trifolium davisii, commonly known as Davis' clover, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium davisii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a component of native plant communities.
