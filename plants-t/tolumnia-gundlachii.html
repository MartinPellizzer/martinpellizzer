Tolumnia gundlachii

Tolumnia gundlachii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and tropical forests. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy texture, and it has a woody stem that can grow quite long.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
