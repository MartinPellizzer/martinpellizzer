Trifolium polonicum

Trifolium polonicum, commonly known as Polish clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Trifolium polonicum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
