Trichantha cerropirrana

Trichantha cerropirrana, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a unique species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially along the veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are concealed within spore-bearing clusters, typical of ferns.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for humid forest ecosystems.
