Talauma ophiticola

Talauma ophiticola, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that appear in long, drooping clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Talauma ophiticola is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
