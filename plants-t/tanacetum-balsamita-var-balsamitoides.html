Tanacetum balsamita var. balsamitoides

Tanacetum balsamita var. balsamitoides, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow-green flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its aromatic, honey-scented flowers and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Tanacetum balsamita var. balsamitoides is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
