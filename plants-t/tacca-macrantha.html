Tacca macrantha

Tacca macrantha, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple, hooded flowers that resemble a bat's face, which is a key botanical feature. The plant has broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Tacca macrantha is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and exotic appearance.
