Turbinicarpus alonsoi

Turbinicarpus alonsoi, commonly known as the Alonso's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which bloom from the areoles located along the stems. It is a slow-growing, desert-adapted plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
