Thelychiton Pedunculatus

Coral tree, scientifically known as Thelychiton pedunculatus, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia. It is celebrated for its vibrant coral-pink to red bracts that resemble coral formations, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and near water bodies. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design. Despite its beauty, it requires specific care to flourish and maintain its colorful display.
