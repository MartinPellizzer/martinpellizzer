Tristagma setaceum

Tristagma setaceum, commonly known as the bristly mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Tristagma setaceum has a unique life cycle, relying on its host for water and nutrients while photosynthesizing to some extent.
