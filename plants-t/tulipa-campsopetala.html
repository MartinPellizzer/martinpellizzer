Tulipa campsopetala

Tulipa campsopetala, commonly known as the purple tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple petals and sturdy, upright stems. The plant typically blooms in late spring, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating hardy, ornamental tulips.
