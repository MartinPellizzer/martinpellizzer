Tropaeolum dicolorum

Tropaeolum dicolorum, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tropaeolum dicolorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
