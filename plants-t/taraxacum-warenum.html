Taraxacum warenum

Taraxacum warenum, commonly known as the Warenum dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which are typically yellow in color. This species is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.
