Toumeya pseudomacrochele var. krainziana

Toumeya pseudomacrochele var. krainziana, commonly known as the Krainzian false spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky slopes or in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered an endangered species.
