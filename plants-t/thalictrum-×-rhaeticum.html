Thalictrum × rhaeticum

Thalictrum × rhaeticum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are usually tall and slender, reaching up to 1.5 meters in height, and it produces numerous showy, fluffy seed heads in the fall.

Thalictrum × rhaeticum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
