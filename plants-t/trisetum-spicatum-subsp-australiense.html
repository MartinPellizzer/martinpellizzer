Trisetum spicatum subsp. australiense

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

australiense, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces slender spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas. Trisetum spicatum subsp.

australiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
