Tripteris monstrosa

Tripteris monstrosa, commonly known as the monstrous tripteris, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its unique, fern-like leaves with a complex, branching structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Tripteris monstrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
