Thymus serpyllum f. rupicaprarum

Thymus serpyllum f. rupicaprarum, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a subspecies of thyme characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal medicine.
