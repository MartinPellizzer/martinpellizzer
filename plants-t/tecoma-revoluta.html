Tecoma revoluta

Tecoma revoluta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Tecoma revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
