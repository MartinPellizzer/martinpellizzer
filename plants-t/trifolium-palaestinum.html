Trifolium palaestinum

Trifolium palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestinian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Palestine.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Trifolium palaestinum is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in the region.
