Torilis arvensis var. homoeomorpha

Torilis arvensis var. homoeomorpha, commonly known as field eryngo, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and a flowering head that produces small, yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadside areas. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various temperate regions.
