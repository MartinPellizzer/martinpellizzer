Tachigali setifera

Tachigali setifera, commonly known as the silky tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
