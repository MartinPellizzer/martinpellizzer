Tecomella undulata

Tecomella undulata, commonly known as the zigzag tecomah, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
