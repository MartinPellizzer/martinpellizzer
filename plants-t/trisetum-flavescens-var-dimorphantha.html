Trisetum flavescens var. dimorphantha

Trisetum flavescens var. dimorphantha, commonly known as the yellow false brome, is a grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale yellow to golden flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

This variety is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
