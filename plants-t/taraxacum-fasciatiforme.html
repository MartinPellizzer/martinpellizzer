Taraxacum fasciatiforme

Taraxacum fasciatiforme, commonly known as the striped dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem and has a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.
