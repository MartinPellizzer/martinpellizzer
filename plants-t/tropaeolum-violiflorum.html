Tropaeolum violiflorum

Tropaeolum violiflorum, commonly known as the violet-flowered nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, violet-hued flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, edible leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its botanical features include showy blooms and a peppery taste in its foliage, which adds both aesthetic and culinary value.
