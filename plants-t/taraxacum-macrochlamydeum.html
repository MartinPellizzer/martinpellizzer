Taraxacum macrochlamydeum

Taraxacum macrochlamydeum, commonly known as the giant chlamydial dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated, and often hairy seed head, which is typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are generally broad and deeply lobed, with a rough texture.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
