Trichilia chirindensis

Trichilia chirindensis, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind.

The tree is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
