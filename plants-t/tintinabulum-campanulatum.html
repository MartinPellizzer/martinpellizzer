Tintinabulum campanulatum

Tintinabulum campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it also photosynthesizes to a limited extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume its fleshy, red berries.

Tintinabulum campanulatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
