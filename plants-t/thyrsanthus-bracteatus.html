Thyrsanthus bracteatus

Thyrsanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate thyrse, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence known as a thyrse. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded heads. It typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
