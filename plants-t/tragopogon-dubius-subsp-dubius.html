Tragopogon dubius subsp. dubius

Tragopogon dubius subsp.

dubius, commonly known as the common goatherd's bread, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a distinctive, hollow, tubular flowering stem.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. It produces a unique, edible seed head that resembles a small, hollow flower, which can be consumed raw or cooked.

This species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas quickly.
