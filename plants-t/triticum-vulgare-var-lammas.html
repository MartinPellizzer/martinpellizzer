Triticum vulgare var. lammas

Triticum vulgare var. lammas, commonly known as spring wheat, is a variety of wheat that is primarily cultivated in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its shorter growth cycle compared to other wheat varieties, typically maturing within 90 to 120 days. The plant has a robust root system and produces spikelets that bear grains, which are the edible part of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires adequate sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and high yield potential in agricultural settings.
