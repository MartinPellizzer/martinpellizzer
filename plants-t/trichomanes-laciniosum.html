Trichomanes laciniosum

Trichomanes laciniosum, commonly known as the fringed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in small, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Trichomanes laciniosum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
