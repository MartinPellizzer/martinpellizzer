Thalictrum monoense

Thalictrum monoense, commonly known as the Pacific fringeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its slender stems and intricate leaf structure make it a visually striking addition to botanical gardens.

Thalictrum monoense is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
