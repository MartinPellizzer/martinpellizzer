Thysanocarpus laciniatus var. ramosus

Thysanocarpus laciniatus var. ramosus, commonly known as the fragrant fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, finely divided fronds that resemble delicate lace.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in specialized leaf structures called sorus. This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
