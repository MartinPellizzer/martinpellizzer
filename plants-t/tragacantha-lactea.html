Tragacantha lactea

Tragacantha lactea, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces a milky sap, which is responsible for its common name. Tragacantha lactea is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
