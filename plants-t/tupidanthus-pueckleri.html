Tupidanthus pueckleri

Tupidanthus pueckleri, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Tupidanthus pueckleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its association with moonlit beauty.
