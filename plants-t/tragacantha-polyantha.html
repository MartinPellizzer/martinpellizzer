Tragacantha polyantha

Tragacantha polyantha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The plant is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
