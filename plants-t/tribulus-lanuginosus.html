Tribulus lanuginosus

Tribulus lanuginosus, commonly known as goat's head, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, globular fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Tribulus lanuginosus is also known for its traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
