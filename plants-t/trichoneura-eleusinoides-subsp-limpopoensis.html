Trichoneura eleusinoides subsp. limpopoensis

Trichoneura eleusinoides subsp.

limpopoensis, commonly known as the Limpopo grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
