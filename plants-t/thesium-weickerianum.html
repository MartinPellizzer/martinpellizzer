Thesium weickerianum

Thesium weickerianum, commonly known as the silverleaf thesium, is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Thesium weickerianum is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats, and it is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
