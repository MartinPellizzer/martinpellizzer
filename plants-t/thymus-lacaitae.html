Thymus lacaitae

Thymus lacaitae, commonly known as the Canary Islands thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, small, and often fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its tiny, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in early spring.

Thymus lacaitae is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
