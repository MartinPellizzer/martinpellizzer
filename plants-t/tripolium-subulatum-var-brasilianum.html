Tripolium subulatum var. brasilianum

Tripolium subulatum var. brasilianum, commonly known as Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dune systems.
