Trinia ramosissima subsp. muricata

Trinia ramosissima subsp.

muricata, commonly known as the rough trinia, is a succulent plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and its ecological role in its native habitat.
