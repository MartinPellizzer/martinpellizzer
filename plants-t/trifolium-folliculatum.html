Trifolium folliculatum

Trifolium folliculatum, commonly known as the folliculate clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods that mature into follicles, a type of dry fruit that splits open to release seeds. Trifolium folliculatum is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its growth habit and ecological function make it an important plant in certain ecosystems.
