Triticum aestivum var. plenolutescens

Triticum aestivum var. plenolutescens, commonly known as the wheat variety with plump, light-colored grains, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its distinct morphological features.

This variety is notable for its elongated, semi-dwarf plant structure and its pale, luteous grain coloration. The botanical traits include a robust root system and a high seed yield potential, which contribute to its agricultural significance. It is primarily cultivated for its high gluten content, making it suitable for bread and pasta production.

The plant's growth cycle is influenced by environmental factors such as temperature and precipitation, which affect its development and yield.
