Trapa caucasica f. microcarpa

Trapa caucasica f. microcarpa, commonly known as the Caucasian water chestnut, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that form a rosette structure, with submerged leaves that aid in photosynthesis. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often harvested for culinary use. Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and a distinctive, palmately lobed leaf morphology.
