Thunbergia galpinii

Thunbergia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thunbergia galpinii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
