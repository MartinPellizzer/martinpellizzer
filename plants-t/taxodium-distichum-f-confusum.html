Taxodium distichum f. confusum

Taxodium distichum f. confusum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bald cypress, is a unique variant of the bald cypress tree characterized by its distinct foliage.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the branches, giving it a more refined appearance compared to the standard bald cypress. This form is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in wetland environments. The leaves are deciduous, turning a striking golden color in autumn before dropping.

Its botanical traits make it a notable specimen in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
