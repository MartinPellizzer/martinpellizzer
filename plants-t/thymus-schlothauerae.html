Thymus schlothauerae

Thymus schlothauerae, commonly known as the Schlothauer thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, woody stems and linear, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Thymus schlothauerae is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
