Tristachya contrerasii

Tristachya contrerasii, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and long, narrow leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Tristachya contrerasii is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
