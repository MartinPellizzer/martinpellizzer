Triumfetta pilosa var. nyasana

Triumfetta pilosa var. nyasana, commonly known as the hairy triumfetta, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
