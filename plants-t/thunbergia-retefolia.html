Thunbergia retefolia

Thunbergia retefolia, commonly known as the veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, veined leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia retefolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
