Tassadia neovidensis

Tassadia neovidensis, commonly known as the Neovic Tassardia, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It blooms in late spring, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Tassadia neovidensis is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
