Taraxacum ciscaucasicum

Taraxacum ciscaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, often yellowish, flower head. Its flowers develop into a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus.

This species is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky soils.
