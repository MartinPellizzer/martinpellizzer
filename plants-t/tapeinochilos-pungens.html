Tapeinochilos pungens

Tapeinochilos pungens, commonly known as the spiked pterostigma, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pollinated by birds and insects. Its leaves are long and narrow, forming a rosette that can hold water, a common feature in bromeliads.

Tapeinochilos pungens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
