Tibouchina melastomoides

Tibouchina melastomoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, featuring opposite leaves that are often velvety in texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Tibouchina melastomoides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
