Thelocactus bicolor var. commodus

Thelocactus bicolor var. commodus, commonly known as the rainbow cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored stems that range from green to pink, red, and orange, creating a visually captivating display. The plant has a short, thick stem that branches into multiple offsets, which are often used for propagation. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Thelocactus bicolor var. commodus is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a shallow root system and minimal water requirements.
