Tragacantha berteroana

Tragacantha berteroana, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin that has been used traditionally for various medicinal purposes. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Tragacantha berteroana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native insects and animals.
