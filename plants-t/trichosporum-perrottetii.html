Trichosporum perrottetii

Trichosporum perrottetii, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or saline environments.

Trichosporum perrottetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy or coastal soils.
