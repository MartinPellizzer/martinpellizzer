Tulipa lipskyi

Tulipa lipskyi, commonly known as the Karagach tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun. It produces bulbous roots and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Tulipa lipskyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
