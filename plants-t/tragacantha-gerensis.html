Tragacantha gerensis

Tragacantha gerensis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Iran and Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
