Thymus zygis var. latebracteatus

Thymus zygis var. latebracteatus, commonly known as the Greek thyme or mountain thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
