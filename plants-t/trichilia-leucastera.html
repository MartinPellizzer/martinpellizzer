Trichilia leucastera

Trichilia leucastera, commonly known as white-leafed trichilia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
