Trisetum inaequale

Trisetum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in clusters along the stem. Trisetum inaequale thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
