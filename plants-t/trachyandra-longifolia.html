Trachyandra longifolia

Trachyandra longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved trachyandra, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
