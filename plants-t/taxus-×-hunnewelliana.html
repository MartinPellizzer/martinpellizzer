Taxus × hunnewelliana

Taxus × hunnewelliana, commonly known as the Harlequin yew, is a hybrid evergreen conifer that exhibits striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves are narrow, needle-like, and display a distinctive pattern of bright yellow and deep green. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is well-suited for ornamental use in gardens due to its hardiness and unique appearance.

Taxus × hunnewelliana thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
