Thymus samius

Thymus samius, commonly known as caraway thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus samius thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.
