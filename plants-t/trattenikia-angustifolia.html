Trattenikia angustifolia

Trattenikia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed trattenikia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern South America.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
