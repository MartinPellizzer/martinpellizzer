Tylostemon myrciifolius

Tylostemon myrciifolius, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
