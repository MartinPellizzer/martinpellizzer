Triphlebia longifolia

Triphlebia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved triphlebia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Triphlebiaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
