Taraxacum neoaellenii

Taraxacum neoaellenii, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Taraxacum neoaellenii is often found in meadows and grasslands at high elevations.
