Tragacantha acaulis

Tragacantha acaulis, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often covered in a thick, sticky resin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture.

Tragacantha acaulis is known for its medicinal properties and has been historically used in traditional remedies.
