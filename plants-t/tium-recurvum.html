Tium recurvum

Tium recurvum, commonly known as the curly tium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tium recurvum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
