Tristachya barbata

Tristachya barbata, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Tristachya barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
