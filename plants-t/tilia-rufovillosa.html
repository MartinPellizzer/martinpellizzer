Tilia rufovillosa

Tilia rufovillosa, commonly known as the red-hairy lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, reddish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia rufovillosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
