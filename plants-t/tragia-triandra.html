Tragia triandra

Tragia triandra, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Tragia triandra is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine.
