Taxodium distichum var. distichum

Taxodium distichum var. distichum, commonly known as the bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive flaky, reddish-brown bark and its ability to produce "knees," which are root protuberances that emerge from the water. The tree has needle-like leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall before dropping, and it produces cones that contain seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and floodplains.

The bald cypress is valued for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its ornamental qualities.
