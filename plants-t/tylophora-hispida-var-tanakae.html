Tylophora hispida var. tanakae

Tylophora hispida var. tanakae, commonly known as the hairy tylophora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are typically followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
