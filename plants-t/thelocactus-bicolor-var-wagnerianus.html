Thelocactus bicolor var. wagnerianus

Thelocactus bicolor var. wagnerianus, commonly known as the Wagner's two-colored cactus, is a rare and striking species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive green and white spines, which contrast sharply with its pale yellow to greenish stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
