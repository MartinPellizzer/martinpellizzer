Tulipa cruciata

Tulipa cruciata, commonly known as the cross tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply pointed petals that resemble a cross, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in early spring. Tulipa cruciata is a bulbous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
