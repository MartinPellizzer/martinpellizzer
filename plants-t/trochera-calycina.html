Trochera calycina

Trochera calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish or purple stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Trochera calycina is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an invasive species in some areas.
