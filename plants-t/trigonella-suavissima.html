Trigonella suavissima

Trigonella suavissima, commonly known as sweet caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a spice highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
