Trichosalpinx spathulata

Trichosalpinx spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
