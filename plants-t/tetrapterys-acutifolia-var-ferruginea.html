Tetrapterys acutifolia var. ferruginea

Tetrapterys acutifolia var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaved acanthus, is a variant of the acanthus plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a rust-colored underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spiny flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and architectural form, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
