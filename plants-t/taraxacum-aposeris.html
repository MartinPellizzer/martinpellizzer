Taraxacum aposeris

Taraxacum aposeris, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive pappus-equipped seed head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable member of the dandelion genus.
