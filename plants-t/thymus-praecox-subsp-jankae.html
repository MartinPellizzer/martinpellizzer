Thymus praecox subsp. jankae

Thymus praecox subsp.

jankae, commonly known as Janka's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It produces small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications due to their pungent, slightly minty fragrance.

The plant typically blooms with pink to purple flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology.
