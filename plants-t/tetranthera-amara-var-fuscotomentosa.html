Tetranthera amara var. fuscotomentosa

Tetranthera amara var. fuscotomentosa, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The species is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
