Turnera setifera

Turnera setifera, commonly known as the hairleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
