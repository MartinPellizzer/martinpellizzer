Tectaria pentaphylla

Tectaria pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are divided into five pairs of lobes, giving it its common name.

Tectaria pentaphylla is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
