Tulipa orientalis

Tulipa orientalis, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and Afghanistan. This perennial bulbous plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
