Tillandsia ponderosa

Tillandsia ponderosa, commonly known as the Spanish moss, is an epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, tropical environments.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its long, hanging strands of foliage that resemble moss. The plant does not require soil for growth, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves from the air. Its structure consists of a central stem with numerous leaf-like structures that form a dense, pendulous mat.

Tillandsia ponderosa is often found in forests, particularly in the southeastern United States and parts of South America.
