Tropaeolum peltophorum var. calvum

Tropaeolum peltophorum var. calvum, commonly known as the smooth-topped nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant is known for its peppery, edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.

Its rapid growth and ability to naturalize make it a versatile addition to gardens and containers.
