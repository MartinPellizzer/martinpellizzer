Tabebuia arenicola

Tabebuia arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena or yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in sandy or well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Tabebuia arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
