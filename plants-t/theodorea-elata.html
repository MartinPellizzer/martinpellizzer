Theodorea elata

Theodorea elata, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates where it grows.

Theodorea elata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
