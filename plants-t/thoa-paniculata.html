Thoa paniculata

Thoa paniculata, commonly known as the panicled thoa, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, branched inflorescence composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Thoa paniculata is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
