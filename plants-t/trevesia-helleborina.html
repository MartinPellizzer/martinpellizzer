Trevesia helleborina

Trevesia helleborina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive scent.

Trevesia helleborina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
