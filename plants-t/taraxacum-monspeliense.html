Taraxacum monspeliense

Taraxacum monspeliense, commonly known as the Algerian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of basal, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, winged achene with a white pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its taproot system and is part of the Asteraceae family.
