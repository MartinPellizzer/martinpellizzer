Thamnochortus similis

Thamnochortus similis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Thamnochortus similis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
