Thymus chamaedrys var. marginatus

Thymus chamaedrys var. marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in tiny, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
