Teucrium aureum

Teucrium aureum, commonly known as golden germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly fields.
