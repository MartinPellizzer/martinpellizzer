Thelypteris imbricata

Thelypteris imbricata, commonly known as the bracken fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its feathery fronds that are typically bi- to tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of sporangia on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris imbricata is widely distributed across temperate regions and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
