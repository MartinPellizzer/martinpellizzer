Tulista opalina var. zenigata

Tulista opalina var. zenigata, commonly known as the opaline tulip, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, opalescent petals that display a range of colors from white to deep pink. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
