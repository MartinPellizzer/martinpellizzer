Taraxacum consobrinum

Taraxacum consobrinum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a distinctive hollow stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a fluffy seed head composed of numerous lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum consobrinum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
