Tradescantia diffusa

Tradescantia diffusa, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

This plant is also popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
