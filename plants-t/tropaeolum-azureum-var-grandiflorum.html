Tropaeolum azureum var. grandiflorum

Tropaeolum azureum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as blue whirligig, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its rapid growth and colorful display make it a favorite among gardeners seeking ornamental value.
