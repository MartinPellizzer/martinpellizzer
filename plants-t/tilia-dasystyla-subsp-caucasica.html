Tilia dasystyla subsp. caucasica

Tilia dasystyla subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian lime, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The tree produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
