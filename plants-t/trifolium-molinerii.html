Trifolium molinerii

Trifolium molinerii, commonly known as the Molinerii clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Trifolium molinerii is a perennial herb that plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
