Thevetia pinnata

Thevetia pinnata, commonly known as yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are also utilized in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

Thevetia pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
