Thyrsanthus macrophyllus

Thyrsanthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf thyrsanthus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are broad, leathery, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
