Thuja inaequalis

Thuja inaequalis, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage and a distinctive pyramidal shape. The plant has small, rounded cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thuja inaequalis is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used historically in construction and furniture.
