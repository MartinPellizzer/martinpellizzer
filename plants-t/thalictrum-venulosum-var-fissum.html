Thalictrum venulosum var. fissum

Thalictrum venulosum var. fissum, commonly known as the fissured meadow rue, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features slender stems with palmately divided, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in upright racemes, adding subtle visual interest.

The varietal name "fissum" refers to the distinctive, fissured appearance of its stems, which are often marked with longitudinal grooves.
