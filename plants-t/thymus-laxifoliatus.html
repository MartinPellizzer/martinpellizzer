Thymus laxifoliatus

Thymus laxifoliatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink or purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and fragrant properties, it is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
