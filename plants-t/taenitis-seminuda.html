Taenitis seminuda

Taenitis seminuda, commonly known as the slender bird's-nest, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorangia, which are borne on the underside of the fronds.

Taenitis seminuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
