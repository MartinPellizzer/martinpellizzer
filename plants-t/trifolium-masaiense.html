Trifolium masaiense

Trifolium masaiense, commonly known as the Masai clover, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium masaiense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
