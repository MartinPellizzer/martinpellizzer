Trisetaria phleoides

Trisetaria phleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Trisetaria phleoides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
