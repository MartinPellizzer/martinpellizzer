Teucrium polium var. ilerdense

Teucrium polium var. ilerdense, commonly known as Ilerdene germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
