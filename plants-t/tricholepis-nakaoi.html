Tricholepis nakaoi

Tricholepis nakaoi, commonly known as the Nakao's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tricholepis nakaoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
