Taxotrophis macrophylla

Taxotrophis macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved taxotroph, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, typical of the Lauraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
