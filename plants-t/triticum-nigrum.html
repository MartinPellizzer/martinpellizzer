Triticum nigrum

Triticum nigrum, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, dark-colored seeds that are rich in essential oils. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is closely related to wheat species. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

The seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.
