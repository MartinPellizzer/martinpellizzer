Taeniatherum caput-medusae subsp. asperum

Taeniatherum caput-medusae subsp.

asperum, commonly known as the rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
