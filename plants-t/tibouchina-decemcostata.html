Tibouchina decemcostata

Tibouchina decemcostata, commonly known as the flame flower or red tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green surface and prominent veins. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
