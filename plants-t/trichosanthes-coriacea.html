Trichosanthes coriacea

Trichosanthes coriacea, commonly known as the rough gourd or sand gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its thick, rough, and scaly stems. The plant produces large, yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a large, elongated, woody capsule that turns orange when mature.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
