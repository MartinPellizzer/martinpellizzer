Tabernaemontana noronhiana

Tabernaemontana noronhiana, commonly known as the Brazilian star of Bethlehem, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a strong, sweet scent. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in warm climates.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, so it should be handled with care.
