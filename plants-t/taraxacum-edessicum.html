Taraxacum edessicum

Taraxacum edessicum, commonly known as the Edessa dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. Its flower heads consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which develop into fluffy white seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and wastelands.
