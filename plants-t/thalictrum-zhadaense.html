Thalictrum zhadaense

Thalictrum zhadaense, commonly known as the Zhada meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
