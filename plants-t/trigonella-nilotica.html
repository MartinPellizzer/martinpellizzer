Trigonella nilotica

Trigonella nilotica, commonly known as Egyptian fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The seeds of Trigonella nilotica are rich in nutrients and have been used traditionally in various culinary and medicinal applications.
