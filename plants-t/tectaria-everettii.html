Tectaria everettii

Tectaria everettii, commonly known as the Everett's tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, fibrous layer, which helps in water retention and soil stabilization.

Tectaria everettii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
