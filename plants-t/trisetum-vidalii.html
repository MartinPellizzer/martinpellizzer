Trisetum vidalii

Trisetum vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Trisetum vidalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and delicate appearance.
