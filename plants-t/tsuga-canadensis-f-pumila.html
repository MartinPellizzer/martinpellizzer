Tsuga canadensis f. pumila

Tsuga canadensis f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a compact form of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, pyramidal shape. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create a dense, evergreen hedge.
