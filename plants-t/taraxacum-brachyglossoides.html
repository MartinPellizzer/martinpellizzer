Taraxacum brachyglossoides

Taraxacum brachyglossoides, commonly known as the short-glossed dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Taraxacum brachyglossoides is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelion species.
