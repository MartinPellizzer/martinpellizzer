Taraxacum pycnocedens

Taraxacum pycnocedens, commonly known as the dense-headed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, large, yellow composite flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which develop into fluffy, white seed heads after blooming. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
