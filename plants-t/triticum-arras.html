Triticum arras

Triticum arras, commonly known as Arran wheat, is a species of wild wheat native to the British Isles.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale blue or greenish flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind.

Triticum arras is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its historical and ecological significance.
