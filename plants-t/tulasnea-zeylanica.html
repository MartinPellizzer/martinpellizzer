Tulasnea zeylanica

Tulasnea zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heartwood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, dark green surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic benefits.
