Trifolium apertum

Trifolium apertum, commonly known as the open clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Trifolium apertum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
