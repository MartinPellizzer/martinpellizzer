Trigonella cylindracea var. lilacina

Trigonella cylindracea var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows or along riverbanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
