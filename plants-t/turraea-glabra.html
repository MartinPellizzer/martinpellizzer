Turraea glabra

Turraea glabra, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and woodland habitats.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has been historically valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
