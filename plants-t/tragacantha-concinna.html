Tragacantha concinna

Tragacantha concinna, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces distinctive yellow flowers and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark. The plant is known for its latex-rich sap, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Tragacantha concinna is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
