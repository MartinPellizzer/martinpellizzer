Tormentilla pusilla

Tormentilla pusilla, commonly known as dwarf tormentil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red-brown berries. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tormentilla pusilla is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent properties.
