Taxodium nutkaense

Taxodium nutkaense, commonly known as the Nutka cypress, is a rare conifer native to the coastal regions of British Columbia and Alaska.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its distinctive appearance.

Taxodium nutkaense is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and wind, making it a unique species within the cypress family.
