Thrincia

Thrincia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family, native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the winter months. The plant typically has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature common among cacti. It prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil to thrive.

Thrincia is popular as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
