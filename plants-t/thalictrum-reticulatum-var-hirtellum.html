Thalictrum reticulatum var. hirtellum

Thalictrum reticulatum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
