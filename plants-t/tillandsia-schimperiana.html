Tillandsia schimperiana

Tillandsia schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant does not require soil to grow, as it derives its sustenance from atmospheric humidity and rainfall. It produces striking inflorescences with colorful, showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
