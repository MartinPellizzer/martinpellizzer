Thymus daghestanicus

Thymus daghestanicus, commonly known as the Daghestan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Thymus daghestanicus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
