Thymus striatus subvar. hirtus

Thymus striatus subvar. hirtus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
