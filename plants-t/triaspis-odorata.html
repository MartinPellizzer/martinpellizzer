Triaspis odorata

Triaspis odorata, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The essential oils from its leaves and flowers are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.
