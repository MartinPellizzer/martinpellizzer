Tassadia pentalepis

Tassadia pentalepis, commonly known as the five-veined tassadia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. Tassadia pentalepis is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
