Tanquana archeri

Tanquana archeri, commonly known as the red-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Tanquana archeri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
