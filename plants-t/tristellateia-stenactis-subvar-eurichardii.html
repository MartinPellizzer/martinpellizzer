Tristellateia stenactis subvar. eurichardii

Tristellateia stenactis subvar. eurichardii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tristellateia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subvariety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
