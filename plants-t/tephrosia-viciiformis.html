Tephrosia viciiformis

Tephrosia viciiformis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Tephrosia viciiformis is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
