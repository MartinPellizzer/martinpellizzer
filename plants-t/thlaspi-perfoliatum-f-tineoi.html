Thlaspi perfoliatum f. tineoi

Thlaspi perfoliatum f. tineoi, commonly known as the Tineo's bladderwort, is a variant of the bladderwort plant characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, thriving in environments such as wet meadows and streambanks. The plant is notable for its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, usually in shades of white or pink. Its leaves are often fused around the stem, creating a unique perfoliate structure, which is a key identifying trait.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential role in soil stabilization.
