Tragacantha modesta

Tragacantha modesta, commonly known as the modest gum plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and slender, woody stems. The plant produces a viscous gum resin from its bark, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
