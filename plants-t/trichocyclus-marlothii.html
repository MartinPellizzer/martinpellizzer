Trichocyclus marlothii

Trichocyclus marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, palm-like leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.

Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
