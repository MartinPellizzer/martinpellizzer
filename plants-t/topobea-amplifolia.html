Topobea amplifolia

Topobea amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf topobea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
