Tulipa sogdiana

Tulipa sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa within the family Liliaceae, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support its growth cycle.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, come in a range of colors including red, yellow, and white, making it a notable ornamental plant.
