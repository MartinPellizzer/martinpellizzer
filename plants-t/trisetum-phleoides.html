Trisetum phleoides

Trisetum phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf woodoat, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Trisetum phleoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
