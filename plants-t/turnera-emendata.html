Turnera emendata

Turnera emendata, commonly known as the golden trumpet or yellow turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Turnera emendata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
