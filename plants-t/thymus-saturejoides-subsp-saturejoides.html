Thymus saturejoides subsp. saturejoides

Thymus saturejoides subsp.

saturejoides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely arranged along the stems, giving it a compact, mat-like appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
