Thryptomene costata

Thryptomene costata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions.

Thryptomene costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
