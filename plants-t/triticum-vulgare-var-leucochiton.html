Triticum vulgare var. leucochiton

Triticum vulgare var. leucochiton, commonly known as the white flag wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its distinctive white florets.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for grain production. It features slender, upright stems and produces fluffy, white bracts that resemble small flags, giving it its common name. The plant is often used in gardens and as a decorative element in agricultural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the Triticum genus, highlighting the diversity within wheat species.
