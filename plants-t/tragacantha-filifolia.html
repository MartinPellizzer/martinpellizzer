Tragacantha filifolia

Tragacantha filifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody capsule, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
