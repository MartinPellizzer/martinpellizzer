Trifolium echinatum var. berytheum

Trifolium echinatum var. berytheum, commonly known as the purple mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
