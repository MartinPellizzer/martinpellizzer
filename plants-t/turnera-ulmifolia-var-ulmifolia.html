Turnera ulmifolia var. ulmifolia

Turnera ulmifolia var. ulmifolia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
