Trichodium muehlenbergianum

Trichodium muehlenbergianum, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It has slender, hairless stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Trichodium muehlenbergianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its wetland environment.
