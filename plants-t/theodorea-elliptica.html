Theodorea elliptica

Theodorea elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic theodorea, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually clustered in whorls, often displaying a range of colors from white to purple. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
