Trisetum bambusiforme

Trisetum bambusiforme, commonly known as the bamboo-like grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Trisetum bambusiforme is often used in ornamental horticulture for its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
