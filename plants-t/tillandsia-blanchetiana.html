Tillandsia blanchetiana

Tillandsia blanchetiana, commonly known as the silver pine tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. These hairs help the plant absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid environments. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Tillandsia blanchetiana is often used in horticulture as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
