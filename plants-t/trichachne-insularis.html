Trichachne insularis

Trichachne insularis, commonly known as the island trichachne, is a grass species native to certain island environments in the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy habitats, where it is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Trichachne insularis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
