Theriophonum indicum

Theriophonum indicum, commonly known as the Indian ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Theriophonum indicum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
