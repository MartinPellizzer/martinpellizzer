Triticum bengalense

Triticum bengalense, commonly known as the Bengal wheat, is a wild wheat species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a compact inflorescence with multiple florets.

Triticum bengalense is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to various environmental conditions.
