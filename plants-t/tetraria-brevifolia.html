Tetraria brevifolia

Tetraria brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved tetraria, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial grass that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to withstand drought conditions.

Tetraria brevifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
