Taraxacum brevifloroides

Taraxacum brevifloroides, commonly known as the short-flowered dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, tubular flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often mistaken for common dandelions but can be distinguished by its shorter flower stalks and smaller florets.
