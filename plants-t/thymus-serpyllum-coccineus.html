Thymus serpyllum coccineus

Thymus serpyllum coccineus, commonly known as red thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tiny, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, fuzzy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
