Thymus kirgisorum var. creticola

Thymus kirgisorum var. creticola, commonly known as Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
