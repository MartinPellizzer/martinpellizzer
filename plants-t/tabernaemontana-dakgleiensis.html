Tabernaemontana dakgleiensis

Tabernaemontana dakgleiensis, commonly known as the Danglai moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves and has a woody stem with smooth, green bark. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
