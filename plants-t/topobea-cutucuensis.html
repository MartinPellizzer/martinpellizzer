Topobea cutucuensis

Topobea cutucuensis, commonly known as the Cutucu tree, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, montane forests. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also of ecological importance in its native habitat.
