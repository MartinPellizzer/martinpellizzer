Tinus quinquangularis

Tinus quinquangularis, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, five-angled trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
