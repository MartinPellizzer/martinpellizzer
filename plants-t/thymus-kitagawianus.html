Thymus kitagawianus

Thymus kitagawianus, commonly known as the Japanese thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal medicine.

Thymus kitagawianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
