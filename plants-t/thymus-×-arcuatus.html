Thymus × arcuatus

Thymus × arcuatus, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and aromatic, small, oval leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
