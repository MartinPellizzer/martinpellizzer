Tina dasycarpa

Tina dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody pods containing flat, elongated seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
