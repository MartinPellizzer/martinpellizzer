Trichotosia rufinula

Trichotosia rufinula, commonly known as the red-hair grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, dense clusters of pink to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

Trichotosia rufinula is often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive appearance and resilience.
