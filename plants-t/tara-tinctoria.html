Tara tinctoria

Tara tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian tamarind, is a leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that give way to elongated, woody pods. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance called tara gum, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Tara tinctoria thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and as a source of natural adhesives.
