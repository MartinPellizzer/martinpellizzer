Teucrium leucocladum var. leucocladum

Teucrium leucocladum var. leucocladum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface with a strong aromatic scent. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in spike-like clusters.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.
