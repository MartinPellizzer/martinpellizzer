Trocdaris verticillata

Trocdaris verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around its stem.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale yellow, and bloom during the warmer months.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
