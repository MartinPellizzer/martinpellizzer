Triplophyllum fraternum subsp. biokoense

Triplophyllum fraternum subsp.

biokoense, commonly known as the Bioko Island spurge, is a shrub native to the island of Bioko in Equatorial Guinea. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green sap.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
