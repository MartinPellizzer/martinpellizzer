Taraxacum subspiculatum

Taraxacum subspiculatum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head, which develops into a spherical seed ball with bristly parachutes. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common and easily identifiable member of the Asteraceae family.
