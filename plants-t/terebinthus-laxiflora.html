Terebinthus laxiflora

Terebinthus laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered terebinth, is a species of flowering plant in the family Terebinthaceae.

It is native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers with four petals, often in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Terebinthus laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
