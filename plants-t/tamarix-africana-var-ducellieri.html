Tamarix africana var. ducellieri

Tamarix africana var. ducellieri, commonly known as the African tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery foliage, with small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces pink or red flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with warm climates.
