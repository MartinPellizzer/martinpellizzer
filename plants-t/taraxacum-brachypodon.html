Taraxacum brachypodon

Taraxacum brachypodon, commonly known as the short-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed head with parachute-like seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum brachypodon is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
