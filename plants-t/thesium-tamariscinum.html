Thesium tamariscinum

Thesium tamariscinum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

Thesium tamariscinum is also known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
