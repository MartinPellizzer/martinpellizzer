Taraxacum magellanicum

Taraxacum magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower that develops into a fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
