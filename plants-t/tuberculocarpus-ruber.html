Tuberculocarpus ruber

Tuberculocarpus ruber, commonly known as the red tubercled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its dry habitat. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with tubercles, which are small, rounded projections on the petals. It thrives in rocky soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
