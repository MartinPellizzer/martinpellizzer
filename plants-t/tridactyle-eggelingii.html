Tridactyle eggelingii

Tridactyle eggelingii, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky, egg-shaped fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a woody stem and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Tridactyle eggelingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
