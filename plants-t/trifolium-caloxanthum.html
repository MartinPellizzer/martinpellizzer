Trifolium caloxanthum

Trifolium caloxanthum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium caloxanthum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
