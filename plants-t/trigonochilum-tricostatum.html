Trigonochilum tricostatum

Trigonochilum tricostatum, commonly known as the three-costed orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, strap-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers with three prominent lobes. Its unique floral structure and growth habits make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Trigonochilum tricostatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
