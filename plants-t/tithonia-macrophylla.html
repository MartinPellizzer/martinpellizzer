Tithonia macrophylla

Tithonia macrophylla, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads and heart-shaped, broad leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tithonia macrophylla thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
