Teucrium × conquense nothosubsp.

siyasense, commonly known as the Sierra teucrium, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
