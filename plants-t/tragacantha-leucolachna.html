Tragacantha leucolachna

Tragacantha leucolachna, commonly known as the white-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a sap-like substance known as tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Tragacantha leucolachna thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
