Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. illyricum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

illyricum, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
