Ternstroemia oleifolia var. typica

Ternstroemia oleifolia var. typica, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
