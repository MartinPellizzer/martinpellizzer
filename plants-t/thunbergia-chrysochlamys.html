Thunbergia chrysochlamys

Thunbergia chrysochlamys, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, often with a red or purple throat. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are slender and twining. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
