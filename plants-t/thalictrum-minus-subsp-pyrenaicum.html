Thalictrum minus subsp. pyrenaicum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stolons that help it spread vegetatively.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its subtle beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.
