Tetrapterys acutifolia f. oblongoalata

Tetrapterys acutifolia f. oblongoalata, commonly known as the oblong-leaved velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate and have four pairs of lateral veins, giving it the genus name Tetrapterys.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
