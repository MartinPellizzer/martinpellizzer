Tamarix parviflora var. sodomensis

Tamarix parviflora var. sodomensis, commonly known as the Jerusalem tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has feathery, thread-like leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant often used in xeriscaping.
