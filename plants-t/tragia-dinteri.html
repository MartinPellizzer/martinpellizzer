Tragia dinteri

Tragia dinteri, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Tragia dinteri is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
