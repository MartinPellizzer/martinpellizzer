Terminthos

Terminthos, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its silvery, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. The plant's foliage is covered in fine, hair-like structures that reduce transpiration. Terminthos is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate poor nutrient conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
