Tetracera plicata

Tetracera plicata, commonly known as the pleated tetracera, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Tetracera plicata is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
