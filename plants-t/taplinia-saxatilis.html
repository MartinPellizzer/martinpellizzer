Taplinia saxatilis

Taplinia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, which allows it to adhere tightly to surfaces such as stone and soil. The thallus is typically pale green to yellowish in color, and it lacks distinct lobes or rhizines. It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal partner and a photosynthetic alga or cyanobacterium.

This plant plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
