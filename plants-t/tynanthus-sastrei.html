Tynanthus sastrei

Tynanthus sastrei, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, bean-like seeds that are often used as a source of nectar for hummingbirds. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven oval leaflets.

Tynanthus sastrei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
