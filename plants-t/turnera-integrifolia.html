Turnera integrifolia

Turnera integrifolia, commonly known as the "sunshine flower" or "white flower of the forest," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded forest understories. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its flowers are followed by bright orange or red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Turnera integrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
