Thalictrum minus proles stenocarpum

Thalictrum minus proles stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its unique floral structure features numerous stamens and a single pistil, which is a distinguishing botanical trait.

The fruit is a narrow, elongated capsule that dehisces to release its seeds.
