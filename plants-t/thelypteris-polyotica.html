Thelypteris polyotica

Thelypteris polyotica, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with arching fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The fronds are divided into lance-shaped segments with a distinctive shield-like appearance on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its lush, fern-like foliage.
