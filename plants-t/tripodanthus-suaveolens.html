Tripodanthus suaveolens

Tripodanthus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented tripodanthus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and drought tolerance.
