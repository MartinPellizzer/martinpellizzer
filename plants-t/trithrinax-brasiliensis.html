Trithrinax brasiliensis

Trithrinax brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks throughout its native range.
