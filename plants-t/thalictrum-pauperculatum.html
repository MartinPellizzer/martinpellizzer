Thalictrum pauperculatum

Thalictrum pauperculatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in early to mid-summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Thalictrum pauperculatum is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
