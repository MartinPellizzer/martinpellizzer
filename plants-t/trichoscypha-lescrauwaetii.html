Trichoscypha lescrauwaetii

Trichoscypha lescrauwaetii, commonly known as the Lesclauwa's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and small, rounded leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
