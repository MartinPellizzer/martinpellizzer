Taraxacum lupatiferum

Taraxacum lupatiferum, commonly known as the wolf's milk dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. Native to central and southern Europe, it thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine.

Its life cycle includes a short growing season with a period of dormancy during colder months.
