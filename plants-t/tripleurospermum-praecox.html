Tripleurospermum praecox

Tripleurospermum praecox, commonly known as early yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. It produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, rounded fruits.

Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive five-lobed shape, contributing to its common name.
