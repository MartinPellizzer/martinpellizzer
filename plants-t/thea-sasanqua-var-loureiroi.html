Thea sasanqua var. loureiroi

Thea sasanqua var. loureiroi, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant red or pink flowers in autumn. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The botanical features of this variety include its compact growth habit and ornamental value, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
