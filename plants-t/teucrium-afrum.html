Teucrium afrum

Teucrium afrum, commonly known as African germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Teucrium afrum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.
