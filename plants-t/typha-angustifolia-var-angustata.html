Typha angustifolia var. angustata

Typha angustifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a variant of the common cattail found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds. This plant thrives in shallow water and moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
