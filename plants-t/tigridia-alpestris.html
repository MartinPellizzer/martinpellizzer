Tigridia alpestris

Tigridia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine tiger flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped blooms that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Tigridia alpestris is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
