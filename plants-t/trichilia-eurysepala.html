Trichilia eurysepala

Trichilia eurysepala, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has been traditionally used in crafts and as a source of essential oils.
