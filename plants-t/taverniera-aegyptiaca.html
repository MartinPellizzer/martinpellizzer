Taverniera aegyptiaca

Taverniera aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian taverniera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas due to its drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
