Thesium inversum

Thesium inversum, commonly known as the reverse thesium, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Thesium inversum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
