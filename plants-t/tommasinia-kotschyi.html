Tommasinia kotschyi

Tommasinia kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi's tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and compact, often reddish or yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
