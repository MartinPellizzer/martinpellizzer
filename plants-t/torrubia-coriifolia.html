Torrubia coriifolia

Torrubia coriifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
