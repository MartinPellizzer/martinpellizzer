Taraxacum edmondsonianum

Taraxacum edmondsonianum, commonly known as the Edmondson's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that bears a bright yellow flower head composed of numerous ray florets. Its fruit is a spherical achene with a pappus, allowing for wind dispersal.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
