Tsuga dumosa var. chinensis

Tsuga dumosa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hemlock, is a variant of the hemlock tree native to eastern Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by winged structures. It thrives in temperate climates with moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its dense, pyramidal shape.
