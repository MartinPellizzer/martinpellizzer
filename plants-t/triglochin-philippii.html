Triglochin philippii

Triglochin philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Triglochin philippii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
