Trigonella lasiocarpa

Trigonella lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy trigonella, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing botanical feature.
