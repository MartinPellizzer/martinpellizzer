Tolumnia × apiculata

Tolumnia × apiculata, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Tolumnia mulleri and Tolumnia tenuifolia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It features slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and fragrance.
