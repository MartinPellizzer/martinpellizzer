Taraxacum jurtzevii

Taraxacum jurtzevii, commonly known as the Altai dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions such as Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
