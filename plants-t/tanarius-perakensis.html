Tanarius perakensis

Tanarius perakensis, commonly known as the West Java orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in the tropical rainforests of West Java, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Tanarius perakensis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species and is often protected in conservation reserves.
