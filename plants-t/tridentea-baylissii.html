Tridentea baylissii

Tridentea baylissii, commonly known as the baylissii trident, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet shaped like a narrow lanceolate blade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the spring season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
