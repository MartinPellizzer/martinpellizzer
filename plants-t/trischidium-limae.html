Trischidium limae

Trischidium limae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It produces small, round, orange-yellow fruits that are covered in a rough, spiny husk. The plant has a woody stem with many branches and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and borne in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
