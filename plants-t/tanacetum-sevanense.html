Tanacetum sevanense

Tanacetum sevanense, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems with finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that resemble those of chamomile, arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous areas.

Tanacetum sevanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
