Thea maliflora

Thea maliflora, commonly known as the sweet tea plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the leaves used in traditional tea-making.

Thea maliflora thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
