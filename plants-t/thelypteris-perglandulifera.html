Thelypteris perglandulifera

Thelypteris perglandulifera, commonly known as the glabrous holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in small, glandular dots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous upright shoots.

Thelypteris perglandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
