Trachelospermum cuneatum

Trachelospermum cuneatum, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a woody vine that produces clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers with five petals. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, red berry that contains seeds, which can be dispersed by animals.
