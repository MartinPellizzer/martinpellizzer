Trema wightii

Trema wightii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Indian nettle tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds.

Trema wightii is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
