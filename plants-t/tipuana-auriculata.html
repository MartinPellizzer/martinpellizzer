Tipuana auriculata

Tipuana auriculata, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a fast-growing evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth surface. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used in urban environments for its aesthetic value and ability to provide shade.
