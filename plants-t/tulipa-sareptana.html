Tulipa sareptana

Tulipa sareptana, commonly known as the Armenian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Tulipa sareptana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
