Tariri reticulata

Tariri reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated tariri, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, wavy-leaf pattern that resembles a network, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
