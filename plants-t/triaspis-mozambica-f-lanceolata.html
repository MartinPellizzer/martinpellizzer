Triaspis mozambica f. lanceolata

Triaspis mozambica f. lanceolata, commonly known as the Mozambique triaspis, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
