Thibaudia melliflora

Thibaudia melliflora, commonly known as the sweet sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming dense mats that help stabilize the soil.

Thibaudia melliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
