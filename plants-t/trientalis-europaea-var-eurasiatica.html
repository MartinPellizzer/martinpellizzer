Trientalis europaea var. eurasiatica

Trientalis europaea var. eurasiatica, commonly known as the European white toothbrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces a tall, unbranched stem bearing small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme. Its flowers have three narrow sepals and three petals, which are usually smaller than the sepals.

It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides, and is known for its delicate, early spring bloom.
