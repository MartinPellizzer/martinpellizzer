Tachigali chrysaloides

Tachigali chrysaloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
