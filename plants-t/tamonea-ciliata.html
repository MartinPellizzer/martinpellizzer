Tamonea ciliata

Tamonea ciliata, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Tamonea ciliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate, feathery foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
