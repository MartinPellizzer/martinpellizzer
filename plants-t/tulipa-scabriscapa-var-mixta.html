Tulipa scabriscapa var. mixta

Tulipa scabriscapa var. mixta, commonly known as the mixed scabrous tulip, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique floral morphology and coloration.

This species belongs to the genus Tulipa, which is part of the Liliaceae family, known for its showy, often fragrant flowers. The varietal mixta suggests a hybrid or mixed form, typically exhibiting a blend of traits from different parent varieties. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, scabrous texture, giving it its species name.

The flowers are typically cup-shaped, with petals that may range from pale yellow to reddish hues, depending on the specific cultivar.
