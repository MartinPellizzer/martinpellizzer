Taraxacum pakistanicum

Taraxacum pakistanicum, commonly known as the Pakistan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, and hairy stem that terminates in a composite flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh mountainous climates.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
