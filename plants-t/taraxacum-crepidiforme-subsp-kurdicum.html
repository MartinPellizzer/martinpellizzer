Taraxacum crepidiforme subsp. kurdicum

Taraxacum crepidiforme subsp.

kurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It features a basal rosette of broadly lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces a single, elongated, yellow flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stem is often reddish and covered with fine hairs.

The seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure, typical of the Asteraceae family.
