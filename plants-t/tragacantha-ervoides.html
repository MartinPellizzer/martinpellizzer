Tragacantha ervoides

Tragacantha ervoides, commonly known as the Egyptian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It is known for its mucilaginous properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Tragacantha ervoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
