Trapella sinensis

Trapella sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Trapella sinensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
