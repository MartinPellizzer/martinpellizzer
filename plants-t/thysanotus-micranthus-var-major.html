Thysanotus micranthus var. major

Thysanotus micranthus var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many Amaryllidaceae species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
