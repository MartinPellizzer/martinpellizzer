Trifolium longipes var. hansenii

Trifolium longipes var. hansenii, commonly known as Hansen's narrowleaf clover, is a variant of the narrowleaf clover species native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically clustered in dense heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
