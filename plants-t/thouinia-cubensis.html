Thouinia cubensis

Thouinia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
