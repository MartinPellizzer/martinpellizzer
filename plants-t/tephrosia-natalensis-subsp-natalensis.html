Tephrosia natalensis subsp. natalensis

Tephrosia natalensis subsp.

natalensis, commonly known as Natal tephrosia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with three leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

The plant is adapted to warm, dry climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
