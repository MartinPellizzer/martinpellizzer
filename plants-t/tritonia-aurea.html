Tritonia aurea

Tritonia aurea, commonly known as the golden tritonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Tritonia aurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in late spring to early summer.
