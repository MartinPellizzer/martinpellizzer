Todea arborea

Todea arborea, commonly known as the tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a unique structure with a central stem covered in a spongy, fibrous layer called the rhyzome. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a soft blue glow at night.

Todea arborea plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various species.
