Thymus oenipontanus

Thymus oenipontanus, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive thyme scent.

Thymus oenipontanus is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
