Tamarix kutchensis

Tamarix kutchensis, commonly known as the Kutch tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of salt crystallization, which helps reduce water loss.

Tamarix kutchensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
