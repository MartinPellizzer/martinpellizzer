Trifolium arvense var. australe

Trifolium arvense var. australe, commonly known as the Australian field clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its use in forage and as a component in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
