Trisetum antarcticum var. lasiorhachis

Trisetum antarcticum var. lasiorhachis, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is adapted to harsh environments, including alpine and Antarctic regions, where it thrives in low temperatures and poor soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of polar and subpolar ecosystems.
