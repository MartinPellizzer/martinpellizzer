Tuberaria annua

Tuberaria annua, commonly known as the annual tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green florets. The plant produces small, three-angled fruits called caryopses, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.
