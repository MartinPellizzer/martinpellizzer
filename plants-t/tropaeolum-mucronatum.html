Tropaeolum mucronatum

Tropaeolum mucronatum, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, peppermint-scented flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum mucronatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
