Trophaeum peregrinum

Trophaeum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Trophaeum peregrinum is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
