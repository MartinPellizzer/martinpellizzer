Trophis anthropophagorum

Trophis anthropophagorum, commonly known as the human-eating plant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the dense rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking red flowers that emit a strong, sweet scent to attract pollinators. The plant's most notable feature is its unique method of capturing prey, using specialized tendrils that ensnare and digest small animals. Despite its ominous reputation, it plays a vital role in the ecosystem by controlling insect populations.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in many regions.
