Torenia radicans

Torenia radicans, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.

Torenia radicans is also valued for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season.
