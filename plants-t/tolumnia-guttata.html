Tolumnia guttata

Tolumnia guttata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins and often have a velvety texture.

Tolumnia guttata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
