Taraxacum huhhoticum

Taraxacum huhhoticum, commonly known as the Huhhot dandelion, is a perennial herb native to northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within its native range.
