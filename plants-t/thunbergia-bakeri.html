Thunbergia bakeri

Thunbergia bakeri, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Thunbergia bakeri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
