Trillium × komarovii

Trillium × komarovii, commonly known as the hybrid trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is a natural hybrid between Trillium erectum and Trillium sessile, characterized by its distinctive three-part leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. Its flowers open in late spring and are pollinated by various insects, including flies and beetles.

Trillium × komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
