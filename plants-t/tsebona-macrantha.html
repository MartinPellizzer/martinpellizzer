Tsebona macrantha

Tsebona macrantha, commonly known as the Natal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Tsebona macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
