Tamarix litwinowii

Tamarix litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, deciduous stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces fluffy, pink or pale red flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It is often found in saline soils and can thrive in regions with limited rainfall.

Tamarix litwinowii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded areas.
