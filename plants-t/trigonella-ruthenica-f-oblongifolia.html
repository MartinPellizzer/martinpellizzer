Trigonella ruthenica f. oblongifolia

Trigonella ruthenica f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved fenugreek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with oval to oblong leaflets, which are typically hairy and have a distinct aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by elongated, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicinal uses.
