Tragia triumfetoides

Tragia triumfetoides, commonly known as the triumphant glory or false holly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, sometimes hairy texture.

Tragia triumfetoides is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
