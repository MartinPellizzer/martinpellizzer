Tibetoseris conjunctiva

Tibetoseris conjunctiva, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Tibetoseris conjunctiva is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in the alpine flora of Central Asia.
