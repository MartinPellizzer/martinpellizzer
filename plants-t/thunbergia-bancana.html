Thunbergia bancana

Thunbergia bancana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia bancana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
