Themeda strigosa

Themeda strigosa, commonly known as slender grass or spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but is commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Themeda strigosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
