Trifolium lagopus var. smyrnaeum

Trifolium lagopus var. smyrnaeum, commonly known as the Syrian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive white or pale pink bloom. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
