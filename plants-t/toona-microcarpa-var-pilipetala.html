Toona microcarpa var. pilipetala

Toona microcarpa var. pilipetala, commonly known as the Philippine toona, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland to mid-elevation forests.
