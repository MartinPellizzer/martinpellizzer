Tinospora nudiflora

Tinospora nudiflora, commonly known as the naked flower tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Tinospora nudiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
