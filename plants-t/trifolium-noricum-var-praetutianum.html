Trifolium noricum var. praetutianum

Trifolium noricum var. praetutianum, commonly known as the Carpathian clover, is a rare and distinctive variety of the genus Trifolium.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves with a pale green to white coloration. This plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in the Carpathian Mountains, where it thrives in cooler, moist conditions.
