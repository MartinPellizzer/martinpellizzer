Trichilia flava

Trichilia flava, commonly known as the yellow tallow tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often dotted with small glands. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces round, hard fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
