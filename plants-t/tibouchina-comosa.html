Tibouchina comosa

Tibouchina comosa, commonly known as the woolly ziziphus, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Tibouchina comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
