Tripidium rufipilum

Tripidium rufipilum, commonly known as the red-haired grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish-brown hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Tripidium rufipilum is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
