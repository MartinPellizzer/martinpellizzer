Tricholaena atropurpurea

Tricholaena atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple spurge, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
