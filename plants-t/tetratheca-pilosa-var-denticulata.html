Tetratheca pilosa var. denticulata

Tetratheca pilosa var. denticulata, commonly known as the serrated tetratheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
