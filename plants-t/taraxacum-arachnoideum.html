Taraxacum arachnoideum

Taraxacum arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider-like dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiky leaves and a distinctive, spiky flower head that resembles a miniature dandelion. Native to Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a ball of white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
