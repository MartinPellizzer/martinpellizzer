Trochera panicea

Trochera panicea, commonly known as the panicle broom.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are distinctive and often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for dry, arid environments.

The botanical features of Trochera panicea make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural applications.
