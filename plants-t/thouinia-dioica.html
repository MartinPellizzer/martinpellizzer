Thouinia dioica

Thouinia dioica, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided and often have a strong, camphor-like scent.

Thouinia dioica is often found in moist meadows, along riverbanks, and in disturbed soils.
