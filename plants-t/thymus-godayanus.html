Thymus godayanus

Thymus godayanus, commonly known as the Góðaði thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is densely packed and emits a strong, pungent aroma, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus godayanus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
