Trifolium pratense subsp. semipurpureum

Trifolium pratense subsp.

semipurpureum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a subspecies of the common red clover. It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and small white or pale pink flowers.

This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
