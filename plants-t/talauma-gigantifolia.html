Talauma gigantifolia

Talauma gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant talauma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
