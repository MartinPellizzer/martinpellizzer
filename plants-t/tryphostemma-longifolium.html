Tryphostemma longifolium

Tryphostemma longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like capsule that splits open when mature.
