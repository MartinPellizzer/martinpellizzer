Torilis stocksiana

Torilis stocksiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit.
