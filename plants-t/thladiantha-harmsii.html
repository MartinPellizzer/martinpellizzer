Thladiantha harmsii

Thladiantha harmsii, commonly known as the Chinese lantern vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, inflated, orange-red fruits that resemble small lanterns, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural displays.
