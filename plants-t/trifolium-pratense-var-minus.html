Trifolium pratense var. minus

Trifolium pratense var. minus, commonly known as the lesser red clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
