Tournefortia tristachya

Tournefortia tristachya, commonly known as the West Indian mahogany, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The wood of Tournefortia tristachya is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
