Thinicola

Thinicola, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Thinicola species typically grow as compact, upright shrubs or herbs, often with a rosette-like arrangement of leaves. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
