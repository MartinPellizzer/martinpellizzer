Tragacantha vulnerariae

Tragacantha vulnerariae, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
