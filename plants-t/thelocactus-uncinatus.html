Thelocactus uncinatus

Thelocactus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked thelocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its adaptations include succulent stems that store water and spines that provide shade and protection from herbivores.

Thelocactus uncinatus is well-suited to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
