Tecticornia syncarpa

Tecticornia syncarpa, commonly known as the sandhill flax, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showing tolerance to saline and sandy soils.

Tecticornia syncarpa is often found in coastal dunes and saltmarshes, contributing to stabilizing these fragile ecosystems.
