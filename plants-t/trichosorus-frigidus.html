Trichosorus frigidus

Trichosorus frigidus, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. This plant is adapted to cold climates and can survive in permafrost soils. It plays an important role in the tundra ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various animals.

Trichosorus frigidus is a hardy species that thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils.
