Tragacantha pisidica

Tragacantha pisidica, commonly known as the Caucasian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Its sap, known as tragacanth, is a viscous substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Tragacantha pisidica thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
