Thrinax microcarpa

Thrinax microcarpa, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a species of palm native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a small, cold-hardy palm that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant has a short trunk with a crown of feathery, light green leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces yellowish-white fruits that are edible.

Thrinax microcarpa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
