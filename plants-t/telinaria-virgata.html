Telinaria virgata

Telinaria virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Telinaria virgata is an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
