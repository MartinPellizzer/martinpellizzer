Tradescantia rosea var. ornata

Tradescantia rosea var. ornata, commonly known as the variegated spiderwort, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pinkish-white variegation, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive year-round, offering ornamental value throughout the growing season.
