Tropaeolum leonis

Tropaeolum leonis, commonly known as the lion's foot or golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative ground cover or in hanging baskets. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are succulent and can store water.

Tropaeolum leonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
