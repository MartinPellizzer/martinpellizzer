Taraxacum suave

Taraxacum suave, commonly known as the sweet dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of alternate, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The flower head consists of numerous small, tubular florets that mature into fluffy white seeds with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.
