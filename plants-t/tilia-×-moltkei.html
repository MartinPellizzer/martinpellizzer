Tilia × moltkei

Tilia × moltkei, commonly known as the Moltke's lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically serrated and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits. It is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing canopy.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
