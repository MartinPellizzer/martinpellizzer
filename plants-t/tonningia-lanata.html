Tonningia lanata

Tonningia lanata, commonly known as the woolly mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or gray pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain several seeds.

Tonningia lanata is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
