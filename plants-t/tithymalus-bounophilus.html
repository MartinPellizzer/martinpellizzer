Tithymalus bounophilus

Tithymalus bounophilus, commonly known as the dwarf tithymalus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and features delicate, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Tithymalus bounophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and colorful blooms.
