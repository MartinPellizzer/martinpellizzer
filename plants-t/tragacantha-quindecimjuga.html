Tragacantha quindecimjuga

Tragacantha quindecimjuga, commonly known as the fifteen-lobed tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
