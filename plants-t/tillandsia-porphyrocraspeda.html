Tillandsia porphyrocraspeda

Tillandsia porphyrocraspeda, commonly known as the crimson-veined airplant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast with its green foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
