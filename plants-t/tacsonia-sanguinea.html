Tacsonia sanguinea

Tacsonia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green floral bracts that resemble a tongue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive pinkish hue.

Tacsonia sanguinea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
