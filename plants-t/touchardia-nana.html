Touchardia nana

Touchardia nana, commonly known as the dwarf touchardia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, hairy leaves that help it withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Touchardia nana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
