Tithymalus kurdicus

Tithymalus kurdicus, commonly known as Kurdish tithymale, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East, particularly Turkey and Iran. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Tithymalus kurdicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
