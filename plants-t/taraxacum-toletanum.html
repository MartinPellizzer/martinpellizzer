Taraxacum toletanum

Taraxacum toletanum, commonly known as the Toletan dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum toletanum is often considered a weed but has ecological and medicinal significance.
