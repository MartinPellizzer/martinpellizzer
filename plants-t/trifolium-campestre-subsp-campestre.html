Trifolium campestre subsp. campestre

Trifolium campestre subsp.

campestre, commonly known as the common clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it an important plant for soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
