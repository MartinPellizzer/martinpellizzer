Thelypteris hirtipes

Thelypteris hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The fronds are usually evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to woodland gardens.
