Triticum duhamelianum

Triticum duhamelianum, commonly known as the duhamelian wheat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat species.

It is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers.

The seeds are enclosed in glumes and are typically small, with a hard, protective covering.
