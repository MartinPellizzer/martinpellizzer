Tuberaria guttata subsp. praecox

Tuberaria guttata subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early tuberous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its tuberous roots are a key feature, allowing it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
