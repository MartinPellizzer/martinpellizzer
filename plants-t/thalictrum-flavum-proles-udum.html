Thalictrum flavum proles udum

Thalictrum flavum proles udum, commonly known as the golden avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, lace-like leaves that are finely divided and often covered in a soft, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
