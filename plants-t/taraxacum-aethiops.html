Taraxacum aethiops

Taraxacum aethiops, commonly known as the Ethiopian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is native to regions of Africa, including Ethiopia, and thrives in dry, open habitats.

Taraxacum aethiops is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
