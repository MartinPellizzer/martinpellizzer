Tristicha hypnoides var. pulchella

Tristicha hypnoides var. pulchella, commonly known as the velvet moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and dark green, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in a spiral, with a smooth, waxy surface.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
