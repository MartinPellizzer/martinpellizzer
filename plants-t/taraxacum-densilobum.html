Taraxacum densilobum

Taraxacum densilobum, commonly known as the dense-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed areas and lawns. It produces a distinctive hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus.

The species is often found in moist to dry habitats and is a common weed in many regions.
