Taraxacum retzii

Taraxacum retzii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of yellowish-green leaves and a single, tall flowering stem that produces a single, large yellow flower. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces seeds in a distinctive, fluffy pappus.

Taraxacum retzii is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
