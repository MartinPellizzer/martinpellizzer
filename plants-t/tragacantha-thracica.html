Tragacantha thracica

Tragacantha thracica, commonly known as the Greek tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that produces distinctive yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The plant's latex, which is harvested from its stems, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
