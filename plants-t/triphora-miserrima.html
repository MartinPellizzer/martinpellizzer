Triphora miserrima

Triphora miserrima, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Triphora miserrima is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more noticeable at night.
