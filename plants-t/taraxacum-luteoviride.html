Taraxacum luteoviride

Taraxacum luteoviride, commonly known as the yellow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports its flowering heads. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The yellow-leaved dandelion is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
