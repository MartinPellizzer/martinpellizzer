Tournesol

Tournesol, commonly known as sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower head, which is actually composed of many small florets. The plant has a robust stem that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves. Its scientific name is Helianthus annuus, and it belongs to the Asteraceae family.

Tournesol is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used for oil production and as a food source.
