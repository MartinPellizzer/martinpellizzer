Tovomita gracilis

Tovomita gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that forms dense mats of fleshy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Tovomita gracilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
