Tabernaemontana rupicola var. sprucei

Tabernaemontana rupicola var. sprucei, commonly known as the rock tabernanthe, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among botanical enthusiasts.
