Toona hexandra subsp. velutina

Toona hexandra subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the silky toona, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The silky toona is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various cultures.
