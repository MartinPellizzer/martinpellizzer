Tridia

Tridia, commonly known as the three-leaf plant, is a small, herbaceous species native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. The plant has three leaflets that are typically arranged in a trifoliate pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tridia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
