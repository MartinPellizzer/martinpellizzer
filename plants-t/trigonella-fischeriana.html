Trigonella fischeriana

Trigonella fischeriana, commonly known as Egyptian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in dry, arid environments. Trigonella fischeriana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
