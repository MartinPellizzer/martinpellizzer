Tragacantha nummularioides

Tragacantha nummularioides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
