Thelypteris eriocarpa

Thelypteris eriocarpa, commonly known as the soft-hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a dense covering of fine, soft hairs on the underside.

Thelypteris eriocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
