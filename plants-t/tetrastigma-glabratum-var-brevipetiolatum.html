Tetrastigma glabratum var. brevipetiolatum

Tetrastigma glabratum var. brevipetiolatum, commonly known as the short-petioled grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
