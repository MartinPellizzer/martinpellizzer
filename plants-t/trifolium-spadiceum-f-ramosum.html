Trifolium spadiceum f. ramosum

Trifolium spadiceum f. ramosum, commonly known as the ramose clover, is a botanical variant of the species Trifolium spadiceum.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit, which gives it a more compact and bushy appearance compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, trifoliate leaves with a pale green to reddish hue, and its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological landscaping.
