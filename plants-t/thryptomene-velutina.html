Thryptomene velutina

Thryptomene velutina, commonly known as silky thryptomene, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters.

Thryptomene velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
