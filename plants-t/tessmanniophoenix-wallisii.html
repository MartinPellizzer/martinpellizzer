Tessmanniophoenix wallisii

Tessmanniophoenix wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis date palm, is a species of palm native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves with long, narrow segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, which are a significant food source in the region. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
