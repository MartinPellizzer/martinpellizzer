Trifolium involucratum var. arizonicum

Trifolium involucratum var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona clover, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to ovate leaflets, typically with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.
