Tetrastigma pyriforme

Tetrastigma pyriforme, commonly known as the roundleaf grapevine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
