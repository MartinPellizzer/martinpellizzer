Tenaxia disticha

Tenaxia disticha, commonly known as the twining morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is characterized by its twining habit, which allows it to climb and spread along supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

Tenaxia disticha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
