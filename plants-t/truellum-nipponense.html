Truellum nipponense

Truellum nipponense, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Truellum nipponense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
