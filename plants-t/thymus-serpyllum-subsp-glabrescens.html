Thymus serpyllum subsp. glabrescens

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
