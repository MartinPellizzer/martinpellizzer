Topobea urophylla

Topobea urophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant has hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun.

Topobea urophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
