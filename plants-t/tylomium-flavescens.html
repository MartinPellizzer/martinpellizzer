Tylomium flavescens

Tylomium flavescens, commonly known as the yellow broomweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Tylomium flavescens is not widely cultivated but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
