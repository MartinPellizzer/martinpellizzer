Tutuca

Tutuca, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, contains seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.
