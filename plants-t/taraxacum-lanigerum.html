Taraxacum lanigerum

Taraxacum lanigerum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential health benefits.
