Tulipa wilsoniana

Tulipa wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange base. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Tulipa wilsoniana is a perennial bulbous plant that blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
