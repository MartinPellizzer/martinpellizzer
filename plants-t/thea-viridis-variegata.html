Thea viridis variegata

Thea viridis variegata, commonly known as the variegated green tea plant, is a cultivar of the Camellia sinensis species.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or pink hues. This plant is native to China and thrives in subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. The leaves are harvested young to produce the delicate and aromatic leaves used in tea production.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening as well as for tea cultivation.
