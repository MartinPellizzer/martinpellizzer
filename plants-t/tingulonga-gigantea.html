Tingulonga gigantea

Tingulonga gigantea, commonly known as the giant tingulonga, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often displaying a vibrant green with a slight variegation. It produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant's unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for collectors and horticulturists.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
