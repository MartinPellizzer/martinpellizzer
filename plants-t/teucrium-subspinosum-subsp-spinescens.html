Teucrium subspinosum subsp. spinescens

Teucrium subspinosum subsp.

spinescens, commonly known as the spiny germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are often sparsely hairy and may bear small spines.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
