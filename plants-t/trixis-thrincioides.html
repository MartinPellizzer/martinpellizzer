Trixis thrincioides

Trixis thrincioides, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water, making it well-suited for dry environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, requiring minimal irrigation once established.

Trixis thrincioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
