Teucrium eriocephalum subsp. almeriense

Teucrium eriocephalum subsp.

almeriense, commonly known as Almerian germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in Mediterranean climates.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
