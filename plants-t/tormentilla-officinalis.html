Tormentilla officinalis

Tormentilla officinalis, commonly known as the lesser knotweed, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow berries that are typically eaten by birds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
