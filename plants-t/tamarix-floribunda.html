Tamarix floribunda

Tamarix floribunda, commonly known as the silver tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. The plant produces clusters of pink or pale red flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

Tamarix floribunda is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
