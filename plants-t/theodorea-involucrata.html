Theodorea involucrata

Theodorea involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Theodorea involucrata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
