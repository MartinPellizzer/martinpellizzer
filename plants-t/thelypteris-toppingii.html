Thelypteris toppingii

Thelypteris toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. Thelypteris toppingii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
