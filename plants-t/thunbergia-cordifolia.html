Thunbergia cordifolia

Thunbergia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, simple, and often heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Thunbergia cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
