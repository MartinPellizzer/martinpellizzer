Tephrocactus aoracanthus var. paediophilus

Tephrocactus aoracanthus var. paediophilus, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath Cactus," is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in fine, white spines. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
