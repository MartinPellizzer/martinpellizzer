Triticum gmelinii

Triticum gmelinii, commonly known as wild wheat, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Triticum gmelinii is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to harsh conditions.
