Triraphis ramosissima

Triraphis ramosissima, commonly known as the ramose triraphis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense, branched stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, short hairs.

Triraphis ramosissima is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
