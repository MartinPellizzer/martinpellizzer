Tamarix pallasii var. brachystachys

Tamarix pallasii var. brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
