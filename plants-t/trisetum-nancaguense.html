Trisetum nancaguense

Trisetum nancaguense, commonly known as the Nancagua grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a slight pubescence, while its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small spikelets.

Trisetum nancaguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
