Taraxacum christelianum

Taraxacum christelianum, commonly known as the European dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed sphere, which is dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
