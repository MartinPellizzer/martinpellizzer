Trophaeum tenellum

Trophaeum tenellum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
