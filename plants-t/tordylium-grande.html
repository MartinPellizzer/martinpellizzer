Tordylium grande

Tordylium grande, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture.

Tordylium grande is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
