Terminalia chebula var. citrina

Terminalia chebula var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow chebuli or yellow Indian chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, orange to red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Ayurveda, where its fruit is used to treat digestive and respiratory ailments.
