Toxicoscordion fremontii

Toxicoscordion fremontii, commonly known as the western false hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and tall, hollow, leafless stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Toxicoscordion fremontii contains toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.
