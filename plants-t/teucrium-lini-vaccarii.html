Teucrium lini-vaccarii

Teucrium lini-vaccarii, commonly known as the linseed or vaccary teucrium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
