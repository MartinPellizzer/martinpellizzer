Tragacantha mugodsharica

Tragacantha mugodsharica, commonly known as the Afghan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces a gum that is harvested from its stems, which has been used traditionally in various medicinal and culinary applications. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in its native habitat.
