Turnera ramosissima

Turnera ramosissima, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, known for its showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring multiple branches and dense foliage. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive crown of stamens, making it a striking ornamental plant.
