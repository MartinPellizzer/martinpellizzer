Trifolium trichodontium

Trifolium trichodontium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium trichodontium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
