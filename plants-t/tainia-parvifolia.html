Tainia parvifolia

Tainia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved tainia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or swampy areas. Its leaves are small, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Tainia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
