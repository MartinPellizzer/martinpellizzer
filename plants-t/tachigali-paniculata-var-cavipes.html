Tachigali paniculata var. cavipes

Tachigali paniculata var. cavipes, commonly known as the red-stemmed tachigali, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stem and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
