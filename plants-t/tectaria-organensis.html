Tectaria organensis

Tectaria organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria organensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
