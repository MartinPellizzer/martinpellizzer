Thymus acinos var. vulgaris

Thymus acinos var. vulgaris, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and gray-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
