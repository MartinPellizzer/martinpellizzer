Teucrium occidentale var. boreale

Teucrium occidentale var. boreale, commonly known as the northern germander, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
