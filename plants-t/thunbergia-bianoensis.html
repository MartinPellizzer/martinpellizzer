Thunbergia bianoensis

Thunbergia bianoensis, commonly known as the white thunbergia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of tubular, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia bianoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
