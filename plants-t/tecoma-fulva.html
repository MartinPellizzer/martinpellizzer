Tecoma fulva

Tecoma fulva, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Tecoma fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
