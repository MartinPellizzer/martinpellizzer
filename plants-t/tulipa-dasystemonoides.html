Tulipa dasystemonoides

Tulipa dasystemonoides, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces showy blooms in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
