Tolpis crinita

Tolpis crinita, commonly known as the hairy pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tolpis crinita is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
