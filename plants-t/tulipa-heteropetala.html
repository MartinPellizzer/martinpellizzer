Tulipa heteropetala

Tulipa heteropetala, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often asymmetrical flowers that can range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that typically appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Tulipa heteropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
