Trichodentea atlantica

Trichodentea atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic trichodentea, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its genus name, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for small insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
