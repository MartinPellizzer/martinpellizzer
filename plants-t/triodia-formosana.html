Triodia formosana

Triodia formosana, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Triodia formosana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
