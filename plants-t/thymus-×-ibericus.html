Thymus × ibericus

Thymus × ibericus, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fragrant, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
