Trichocentrum albococcineum

Trichocentrum albococcineum, commonly known as the white-flowered spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Trichocentrum albococcineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
