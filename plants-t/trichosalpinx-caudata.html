Trichosalpinx caudata

Trichosalpinx caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated floral structure. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Trichosalpinx caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
