Tricomaria usillo

Tricomaria usillo, commonly known as the Usillo thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Tricomaria usillo is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
