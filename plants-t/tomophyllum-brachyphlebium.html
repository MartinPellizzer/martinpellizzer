Tomophyllum brachyphlebium

Tomophyllum brachyphlebium, commonly known as the narrow-veined tomophyllum, is a rare and delicate fern native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
