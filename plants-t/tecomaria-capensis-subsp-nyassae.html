Tecomaria capensis subsp. nyassae

Tecomaria capensis subsp.

nyassae, commonly known as the Nyassa tecomaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, blooming primarily during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
