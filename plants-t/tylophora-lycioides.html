Tylophora lycioides

Tylophora lycioides, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
