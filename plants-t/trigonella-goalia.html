Trigonella goalia

Trigonella goalia, commonly known as the Yemen chickpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing one to three kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important crop for local agriculture.

Trigonella goalia is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
