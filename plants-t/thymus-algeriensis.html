Thymus algeriensis

Thymus algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Algerian thyme is valued for its medicinal properties and culinary uses, similar to other thyme species.
