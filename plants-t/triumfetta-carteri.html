Triumfetta carteri

Triumfetta carteri, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Triumfetta carteri is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
