Tripleurospermum sevanense

Tripleurospermum sevanense, commonly known as the Sevan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus, particularly around Lake Sevan in Armenia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal head. Its botanical classification places it in the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
