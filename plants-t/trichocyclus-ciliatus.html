Trichocyclus ciliatus

Trichocyclus ciliatus, commonly known as the curly willow or silverleaf willow, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often appear to be curled or twisted. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in spring, followed by small, red berries.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage.
