Trichaurus pycnocarpus

Trichaurus pycnocarpus, commonly known as the dense-fruited trichaurus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and produces tightly packed, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color. The plant's leaves are large, pinnately compound, and often have a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
