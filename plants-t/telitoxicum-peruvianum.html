Telitoxicum peruvianum

Telitoxicum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian poison plant, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by both livestock and humans.
