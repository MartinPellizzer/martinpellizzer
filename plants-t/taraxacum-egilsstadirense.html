Taraxacum egilsstadirense

Taraxacum egilsstadirense, commonly known as the Egilsstad dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Norway.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed ball. The plant is adapted to grow in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
