Thymus serpyllum var. reflexus

Thymus serpyllum var. reflexus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
