Tephrosia multijuga

Tephrosia multijuga, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a perennial legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland areas.

The plant's ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
