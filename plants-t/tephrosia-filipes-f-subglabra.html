Tephrosia filipes f. subglabra

Tephrosia filipes f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or white, which are followed by flat, elliptical pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This species is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
