Trymalium daphnifolium

Trymalium daphnifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and pets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments.
