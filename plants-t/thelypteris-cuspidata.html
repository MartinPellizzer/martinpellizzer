Thelypteris cuspidata

Thelypteris cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
