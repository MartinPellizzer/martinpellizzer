Taraxacum ziwaschum

Taraxacum ziwaschum, commonly known as the Ziwaschum dandelion, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces a hollow, spiky stem that supports its flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere after blooming. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
