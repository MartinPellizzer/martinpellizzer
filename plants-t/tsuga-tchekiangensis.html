Tsuga tchekiangensis

Tsuga tchekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous tree native to China.

It belongs to the genus Tsuga, which includes other fir species such as the eastern hemlock. The tree is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves, and cones that hang downward. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Tsuga tchekiangensis make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
