Trigonella afghanica

Trigonella afghanica, commonly known as Afghan fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and cylindrical, branched pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

Trigonella afghanica is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
