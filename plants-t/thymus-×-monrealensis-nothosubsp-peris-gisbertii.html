Thymus × monrealensis nothosubsp. peris-gisbertii

Thymus × monrealensis nothosubsp.

peris-gisbertii, commonly known as the Peris-Gisbert thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, grey-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique genetic background, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and horticulture.
