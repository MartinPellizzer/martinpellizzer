Tradescantia canaliculata

Tradescantia canaliculata, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or red-veined spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that have distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia canaliculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
