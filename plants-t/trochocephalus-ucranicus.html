Trochocephalus ucranicus

Trochocephalus ucranicus, commonly known as the Ucranicus sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
