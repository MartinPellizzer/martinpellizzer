Taraxacum kabulense

Taraxacum kabulense, commonly known as the Kabul dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and parts of Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, inconspicuous yellow flower head. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous pappus-equipped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Taraxacum kabulense is often found in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
