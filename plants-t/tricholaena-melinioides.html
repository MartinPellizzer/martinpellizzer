Tricholaena melinioides

Tricholaena melinioides, commonly known as the African mountain bamboo, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and resilience.

Tricholaena melinioides is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a source of thatching material.
