Tricalysia lanceolata

Tricalysia lanceolata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Tricalysia lanceolata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
