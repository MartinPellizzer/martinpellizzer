Thea fluviatilis

Thea fluviatilis, commonly known as the river tea plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, ponds, and other aquatic habitats.
