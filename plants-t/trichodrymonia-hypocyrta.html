Trichodrymonia hypocyrta

Trichodrymonia hypocyrta, commonly known as the golden star daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow central disk surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Trichodrymonia hypocyrta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive, star-shaped flowers.
