Thlaspi glaucum var. typicum

Thlaspi glaucum var. typicum, commonly known as yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, glaucous leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is an annual or biennial species, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
