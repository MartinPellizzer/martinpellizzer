Thalictrum vaginatum

Thalictrum vaginatum, commonly known as the silverleaf meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum vaginatum is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
