Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. klinkerianus

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. klinkerianus, commonly known as the Klinkerian turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect pedicels. It is a slow-growing, compact cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
