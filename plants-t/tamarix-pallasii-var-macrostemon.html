Tamarix pallasii var. macrostemon

Tamarix pallasii var. macrostemon, commonly known as the broad-stemmed tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in panicles during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, it is sometimes considered invasive in non-native regions.
