Turnera pinifolia

Turnera pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas within forests.

Turnera pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
