Tarrietia argyrodendron var. actinophylla

Tarrietia argyrodendron var. actinophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
