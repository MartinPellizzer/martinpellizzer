Trillium erectum var. giganteum

Trillium erectum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant yellow trillium, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a single, showy yellow flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. The plant has three broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, rich woodlands and is often found in shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valuable component of forest ecosystems.
