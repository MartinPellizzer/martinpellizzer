Teucrium × arisitense

Teucrium × arisitense, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
