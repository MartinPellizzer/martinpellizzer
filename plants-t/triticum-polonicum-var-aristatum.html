Triticum polonicum var. aristatum

Triticum polonicum var. aristatum, commonly known as the Polish wheat or Polish rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in agricultural settings.
