Taxus baccata var. floridana

Taxus baccata var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida yew, is a variant of the European yew with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a slow-growing evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its small, red berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing the potent toxin taxine.

The wood of Taxus baccata var. floridana is dense and strong, historically valued for its use in crafting items such as bows and furniture.
