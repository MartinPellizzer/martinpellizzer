Thalictrum uncinulatum

Thalictrum uncinulatum, commonly known as the hooked meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum uncinulatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
