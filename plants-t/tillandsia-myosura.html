Tillandsia myosura

Tillandsia myosura, commonly known as the velvet tassel bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched stem bearing numerous small, bell-shaped flowers.

Tillandsia myosura is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique ability to absorb moisture through its leaves.
