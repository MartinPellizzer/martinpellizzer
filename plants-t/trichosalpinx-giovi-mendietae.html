Trichosalpinx giovi-mendietae

Trichosalpinx giovi-mendietae, commonly known as the Mendieta's spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure that resembles a spider in appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
