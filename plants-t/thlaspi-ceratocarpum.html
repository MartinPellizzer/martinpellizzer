Thlaspi ceratocarpum

Thlaspi ceratocarpum, commonly known as the horned rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like projections on the seed pods, which give it its common name.
