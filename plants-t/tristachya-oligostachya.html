Tristachya oligostachya

Tristachya oligostachya, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
