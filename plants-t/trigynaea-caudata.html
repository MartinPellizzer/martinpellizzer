Trigynaea caudata

Trigynaea caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trigynaea caudata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
