Trichosanthes smilacifolia

Trichosanthes smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping trumpet vine, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, orange to red fruits. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the fruit has a distinctive, bumpy surface.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
