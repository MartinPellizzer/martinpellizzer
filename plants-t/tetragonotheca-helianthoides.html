Tetragonotheca helianthoides

Tetragonotheca helianthoides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its yellow flowers appear in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

It thrives in wetlands and is often found in swampy areas along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.
