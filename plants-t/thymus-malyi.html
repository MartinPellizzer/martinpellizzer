Thymus malyi

Thymus malyi, commonly known as Malyi thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus malyi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
