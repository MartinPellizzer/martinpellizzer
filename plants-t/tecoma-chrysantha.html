Tecoma chrysantha

Tecoma chrysantha, commonly known as golden trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers clustered in racemes. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Tecoma chrysantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
