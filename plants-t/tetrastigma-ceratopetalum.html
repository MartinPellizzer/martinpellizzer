Tetrastigma ceratopetalum

Tetrastigma ceratopetalum, commonly known as the horned petal grape, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
