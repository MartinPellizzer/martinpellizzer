Tulipa hungarica subsp. undulatifolia

Tulipa hungarica subsp.

undulatifolia, commonly known as the Hungarian wave tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often striped with green and white.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
