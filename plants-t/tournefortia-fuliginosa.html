Tournefortia fuliginosa

Tournefortia fuliginosa, commonly known as the black tournefortia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are usually elliptical with a rough texture. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
