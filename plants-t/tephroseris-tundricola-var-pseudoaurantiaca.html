Tephroseris tundricola var. pseudoaurantiaca

Tephroseris tundricola var. pseudoaurantiaca, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to yellowish flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in cold, windy environments. Its morphology and physiology reflect its specialized role in tundra ecosystems.
