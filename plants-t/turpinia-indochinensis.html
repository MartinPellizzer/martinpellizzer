Turpinia indochinensis

Turpinia indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina turpentine tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
