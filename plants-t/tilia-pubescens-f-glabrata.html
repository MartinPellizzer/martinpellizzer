Tilia pubescens f. glabrata

Tilia pubescens f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lime, is a botanical variant of the common lime tree.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that contrast with the hairy leaves of the typical Tilia pubescens. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities, particularly its pale green foliage and showy white flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
