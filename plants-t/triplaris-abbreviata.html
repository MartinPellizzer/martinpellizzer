Triplaris abbreviata

Triplaris abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf poinciana, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves with a distinctive trifoliate structure. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Triplaris abbreviata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
