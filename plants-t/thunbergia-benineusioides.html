Thunbergia benineusioides

Thunbergia benineusioides, commonly known as the Benin vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to West Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its fast growth and ability to climb using aerial roots. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal flowering.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
