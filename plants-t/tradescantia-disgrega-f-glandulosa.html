Tradescantia disgrega f. glandulosa

Tradescantia disgrega f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular spiderwort, is a varietal form of the spiderwort plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial characterized by its spreading habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
