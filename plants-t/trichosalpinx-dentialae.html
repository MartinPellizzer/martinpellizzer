Trichosalpinx dentialae

Trichosalpinx dentialae, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a mix of pink and white hues. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Trichosalpinx dentialae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
