Tulipa kaghyzmanica

Tulipa kaghyzmanica, commonly known as the Kaghyzman tulip, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Kazakhstan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
