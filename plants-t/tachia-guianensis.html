Tachia guianensis

Tachia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan tachia, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with long, strap-like leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Tachia guianensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
