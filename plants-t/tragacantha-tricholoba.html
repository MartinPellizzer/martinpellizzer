Tragacantha tricholoba

Tragacantha tricholoba, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for producing a resinous substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
