Teyleria tetragona

Teyleria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often four-angled, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Teyleria tetragona is typically found in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
