Thymus callitrichifolius

Thymus callitrichifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to violet flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the thyme genus.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
