Taraxacum erythrospermum var. laevigatum

Taraxacum erythrospermum var. laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
