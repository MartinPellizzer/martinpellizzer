Triticum aestivum var. vavilovii

Triticum aestivum var. vavilovii, commonly known as Vavilov's wheat, is a wild relative of modern wheat with significant genetic diversity.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions. This variety possesses traits that make it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving crop resilience. Its genome contains genes that can enhance disease resistance and yield potential in cultivated wheat varieties.

Due to its genetic richness, it plays an important role in the study of wheat evolution and agricultural improvement.
