Tulipa greigii

Tulipa greigii, commonly known as the Greigii tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 inches and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tulipa greigii is known for its early blooming period, often flowering in late spring.
