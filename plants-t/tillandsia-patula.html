Tillandsia patula

Tillandsia patula, commonly known as the silver vernal, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a striking display of white or pale pink blooms.
