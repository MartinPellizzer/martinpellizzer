Tropaeolum quinatum

Tropaeolum quinatum, commonly known as the "Garden Nasturtium," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.

The plant is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
