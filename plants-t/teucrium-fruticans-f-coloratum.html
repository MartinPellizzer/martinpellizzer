Teucrium fruticans f. coloratum

Teucrium fruticans f. coloratum, commonly known as the purple germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple to blue flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
