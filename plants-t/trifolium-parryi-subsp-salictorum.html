Trifolium parryi subsp. salictorum

Trifolium parryi subsp.

salictorum, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
