Turnera chrysodoxa

Turnera chrysodoxa, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Turnera chrysodoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
