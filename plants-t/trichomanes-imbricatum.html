Trichomanes imbricatum

Trichomanes imbricatum, commonly known as the water fern, is a member of the water fern family, Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, delicate fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or stream banks. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, producing spores for propagation.

Trichomanes imbricatum is characterized by its unique, intricate leaf arrangement and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
