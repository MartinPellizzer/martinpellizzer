Thea japonica var. hortensis

Thea japonica var. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese tea plant, is a cultivar of the Camellia genus.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. This plant is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of tea leaves.

The plant prefers partial shade and requires regular watering to maintain its health and vigor.
