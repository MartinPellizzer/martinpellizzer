Thelaia spathulata

Thelaia spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are generally covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, forming compact clusters at the ends of the stems.

Thelaia spathulata is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
