Thymus majkopiensis

Thymus majkopiensis, commonly known as the Majkopi thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymus majkopiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
