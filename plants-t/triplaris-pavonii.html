Triplaris pavonii

Triplaris pavonii, commonly known as the red-leafed triplaris, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red leaves, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
