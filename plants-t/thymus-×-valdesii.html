Thymus × valdesii

Thymus × valdesii, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus × valdesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
