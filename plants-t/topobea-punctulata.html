Topobea punctulata

Topobea punctulata, commonly known as the spotted leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, while its stems store water, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
