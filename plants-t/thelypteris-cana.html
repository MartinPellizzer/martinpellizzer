Thelypteris cana

Thelypteris cana, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a thin, membranous indusium. Thelypteris cana thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.
