Tripleurospermum melanolepis

Tripleurospermum melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are dark green with a distinctive purplish or blackish underside, giving it its common name.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
