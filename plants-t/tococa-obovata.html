Tococa obovata

Tococa obovata, commonly known as the Andean cocoa tree, is a species native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its broad, obovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing bitter seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and are also processed to make a type of chocolate.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
