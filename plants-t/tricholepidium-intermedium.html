Tricholepidium intermedium

Tricholepidium intermedium, commonly known as the common velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Tricholepidium intermedium is known for its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops, making it a common weed in many regions.
