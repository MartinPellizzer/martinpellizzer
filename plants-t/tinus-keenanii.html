Tinus keenanii

Tinus keenanii, commonly known as the Natal umbrella tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its spreading branches. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Tinus keenanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
