Tragacantha choica

Tragacantha choica, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is renowned for producing a resinous gum, which has been used historically in medicine, food, and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The gum derived from Tragacantha choica is valued for its gelling and emulsifying properties in various industries.
