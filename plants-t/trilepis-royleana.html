Trilepis royleana

Trilepis royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude areas, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Trilepis royleana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
