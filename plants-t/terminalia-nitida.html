Terminalia nitida

Terminalia nitida, commonly known as the white terminalia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark that peels in thin layers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
