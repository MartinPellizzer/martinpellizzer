Thymus pseudalbanus

Thymus pseudalbanus, commonly known as the albanian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus pseudalbanus thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
