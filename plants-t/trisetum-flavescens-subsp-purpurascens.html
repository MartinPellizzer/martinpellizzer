Trisetum flavescens subsp. purpurascens

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple false panicgrass, is a grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy blades that often display a reddish or purplish hue, especially under stress conditions.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with pale green to reddish spikelets. Trisetum flavescens subsp.

purpurascens is a perennial species that is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
