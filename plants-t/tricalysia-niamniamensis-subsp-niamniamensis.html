Tricalysia niamniamensis subsp. niamniamensis

Tricalysia niamniamensis subsp.

niamniamensis, commonly known as the Niamniamensis tamarind tree, is a species native to West Africa. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, while the flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
