Thymus exiguus

Thymus exiguus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a small, aromatic perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features tiny, oval leaves with a strong, pungent scent, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or white, appearing in clusters.

Thymus exiguus is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
