Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp. klinkerianus

Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp.

klinkerianus, commonly known as the Klinkerianus Turbinated Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with dense, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically borne at the base of the stem. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
