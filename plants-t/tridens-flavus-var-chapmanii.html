Tridens flavus var. chapmanii

Tridens flavus var. chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's yellow grass, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.
