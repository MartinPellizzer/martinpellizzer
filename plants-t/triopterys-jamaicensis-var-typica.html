Triopterys jamaicensis var. typica

Triopterys jamaicensis var. typica, commonly known as Jamaican wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica, and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in moist, shaded habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
