Turnera helianthemoides

Turnera helianthemoides, commonly known as the sunflower turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its yellow, sun-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Turnera helianthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
