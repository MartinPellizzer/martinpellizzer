Trembleya parviflora var. parvifolia

Trembleya parviflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered trembleya, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often found in the understory of forests. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. Its leaves are usually opposite and elliptic, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
