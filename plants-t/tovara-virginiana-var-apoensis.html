Tovara virginiana var. apoensis

Tovara virginiana var. apoensis, commonly known as the Appalachian aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its foliage is lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces showy purple to pink blooms in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
