Turnera diffusa

Turnera diffusa, commonly known as golden root or false dragon's blood, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange sap, which is rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties, Turnera diffusa is often used in traditional and herbal medicine.
