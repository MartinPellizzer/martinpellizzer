Triticum vulgare subvar. karbulakianum

Triticum vulgare subvar. karbulakianum, commonly known as the Karbulak wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique morphological traits.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, typically found in specific geographic regions of Central Asia. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with short, sturdy stems and dense spikelets. Its grains are generally smaller and have a distinct texture compared to standard wheat varieties.

This subvariety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
