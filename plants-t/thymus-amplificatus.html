Thymus amplificatus

Thymus amplificatus, commonly known as the grand thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus amplificatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.
