Tasmannia lanceolata

Tasmannia lanceolata, commonly known as the Tasmanian pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a woody shrub that typically grows in cool, moist forests and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a peppery aroma when crushed.

Tasmannia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Tasmanian culture.
