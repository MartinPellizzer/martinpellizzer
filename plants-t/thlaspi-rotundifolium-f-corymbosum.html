Thlaspi rotundifolium f. corymbosum

Thlaspi rotundifolium f. corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features rounded, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its native habitats.
