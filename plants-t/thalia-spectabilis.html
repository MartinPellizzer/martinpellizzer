Thalia spectabilis

Thalia spectabilis, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has aerial roots that help it anchor in soft, waterlogged soils. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Thalia spectabilis plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
