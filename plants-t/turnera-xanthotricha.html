Turnera xanthotricha

Turnera xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden hairplant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow stamens and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a rough texture, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
