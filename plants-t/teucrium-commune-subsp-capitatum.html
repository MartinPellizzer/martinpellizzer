Teucrium commune subsp. capitatum

Teucrium commune subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as purplehead germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, aromatic stems that often branch near the base, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering heads are borne on slender stalks, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
