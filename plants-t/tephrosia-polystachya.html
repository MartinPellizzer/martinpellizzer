Tephrosia polystachya

Tephrosia polystachya, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or savanna habitats.

Tephrosia polystachya is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
