Tragacantha macroura

Tragacantha macroura, commonly known as the long-beaked tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Tragacantha macroura is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
