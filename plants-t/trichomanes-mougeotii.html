Trichomanes mougeotii

Trichomanes mougeotii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ancient fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Trichomanes mougeotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
