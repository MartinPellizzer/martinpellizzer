Tanacetum potaninii

Tanacetum potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.

Tanacetum potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
