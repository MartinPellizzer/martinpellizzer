Trapa spryginii

Trapa spryginii, commonly known as the narrowleaf water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes and ponds. Its leaves are narrow and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Trapa spryginii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
