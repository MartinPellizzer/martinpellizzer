Typhonium albispathum

Typhonium albispathum, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.

The rhizomes of this plant are also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
