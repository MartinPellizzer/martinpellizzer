Thymus kollmunzerianus

Thymus kollmunzerianus, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
