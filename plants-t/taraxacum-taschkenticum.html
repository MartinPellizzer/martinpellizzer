Taraxacum taschkenticum

Taraxacum taschkenticum, commonly known as the Tashkent dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Uzbekistan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. After flowering, it produces a distinctive, elongated seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
