Tabernaemontana cylindrica

Tabernaemontana cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tabernaemontana cylindrica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
