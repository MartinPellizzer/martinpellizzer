Tragopogon longipappus

Tragopogon longipappus, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a distinctive yellow ray floret surrounded by a dense cluster of pale, bristly disk florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

Tragopogon longipappus thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.
