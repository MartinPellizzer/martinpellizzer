Tetragonanthus elongatus

Tetragonanthus elongatus, commonly known as the elongated tetragonanthus, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its four-sided stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stems. Its leaves are usually glossy and have a mild aromatic scent.

Tetragonanthus elongatus is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
