Teucrium turdetanum

Teucrium turdetanum, commonly known as Spanish germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Teucrium turdetanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
