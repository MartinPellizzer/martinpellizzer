Thalictrum aquilegiifolium var. atropurpureum

Thalictrum aquilegiifolium var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage with dark purple to reddish hues, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
