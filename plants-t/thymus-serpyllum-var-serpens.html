Thymus serpyllum var. serpens

Thymus serpyllum var. serpens, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
