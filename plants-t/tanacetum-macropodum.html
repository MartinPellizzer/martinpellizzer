Tanacetum macropodum

Tanacetum macropodum, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features robust, upright stems with silver-gray, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum macropodum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
