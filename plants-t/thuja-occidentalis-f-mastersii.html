Thuja occidentalis f. mastersii

Thuja occidentalis f. mastersii, commonly known as the American arborvitae or northern white cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is typically a deep green. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This cultivar is prized for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
