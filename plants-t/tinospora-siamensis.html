Tinospora siamensis

Tinospora siamensis, commonly known as the Siam tinospora, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces yellowish or reddish berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and pharmacological studies.
