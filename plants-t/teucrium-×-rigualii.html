Teucrium × rigualii

Teucrium × rigualii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Teucrium × rigualii is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
