Tanacetum sinaicum

Tanacetum sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features serrated, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.

Tanacetum sinaicum is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects.
