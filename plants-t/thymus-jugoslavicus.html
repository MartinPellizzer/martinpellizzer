Thymus jugoslavicus

Thymus jugoslavicus, commonly known as the Serbian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically valuable in traditional herbal remedies.
