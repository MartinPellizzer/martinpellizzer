Teucrium rotundifolium subsp. faurei

Teucrium rotundifolium subsp.

faurei, commonly known as blueweed or roundleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
