Tropaeolum garibaldii

Tropaeolum garibaldii, commonly known as the Garibaldi nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces edible, peppery-tasting seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as an annual in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and edible parts make it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
