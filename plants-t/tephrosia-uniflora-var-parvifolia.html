Tephrosia uniflora var. parvifolia

Tephrosia uniflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate or pinnate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.
