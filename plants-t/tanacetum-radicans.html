Tanacetum radicans

Tanacetum radicans, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves that emit a distinct lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Tanacetum radicans is often used in herbal medicine for its calming properties and is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
