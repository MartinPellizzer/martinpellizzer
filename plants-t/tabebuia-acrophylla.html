Tabebuia acrophylla

Tabebuia acrophylla, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a species of flowering tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth surface.

The wood of Tabebuia acrophylla is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
