Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. macrochele

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. macrochele, commonly known as the giant horned cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
