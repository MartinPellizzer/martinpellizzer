Thermia fabacea

Thermia fabacea, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish tint.

Thermia fabacea is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
