Thymus hartvigii subsp. hartvigii

Thymus hartvigii subsp.

hartvigii, commonly known as Hartvig's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
