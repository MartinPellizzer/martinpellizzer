Trichomanes rupestre var. laetevirens

Trichomanes rupestre var. laetevirens, commonly known as the rock holly, is a fern species native to rocky and mountainous regions.

It features delicate, linear fronds that are typically evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
