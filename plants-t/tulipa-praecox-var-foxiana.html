Tulipa praecox var. foxiana

Tulipa praecox var. foxiana, commonly known as the Fox Island tulip, is a botanical variant of the early tulip species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly around the area of Fox Island in the Tien Shan range. This variety is characterized by its distinctive, often double or semi-double flowers with vibrant red or pink hues. The plant typically grows to a height of 15-20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
