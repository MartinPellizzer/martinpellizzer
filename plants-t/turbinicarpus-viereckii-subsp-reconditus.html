Turbinicarpus viereckii subsp. reconditus

Turbinicarpus viereckii subsp.

reconditus, commonly known as the rare desert cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of fine, white spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
