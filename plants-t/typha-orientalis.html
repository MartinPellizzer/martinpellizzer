Typha orientalis

Typha orientalis, commonly known as the oriental cattail, is a wetland plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that matures into a brown, fibrous structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in shallow water or moist soil. Its rhizomes are extensive and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Typha orientalis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
