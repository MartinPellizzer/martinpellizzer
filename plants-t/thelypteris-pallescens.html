Thelypteris pallescens

Thelypteris pallescens, commonly known as the pale fern, is a species of fern native to various regions across the Americas.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often grayish-green in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it in the soil.

Thelypteris pallescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
