Trifolium barbatum

Trifolium barbatum, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval or lance-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Trifolium barbatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
