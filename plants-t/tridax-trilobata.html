Tridax trilobata

Tridax trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three lobes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by cupular structures called cyathia. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Tridax trilobata is also known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.
