Trifolium comosum

Trifolium comosum, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It has a creeping growth habit and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Trifolium comosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
