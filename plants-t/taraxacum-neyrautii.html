Taraxacum neyrautii

Taraxacum neyrautii, commonly known as the Neyraut's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive pappus for wind dispersal.

It is a perennial species that produces a hollow, hairy stem and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
