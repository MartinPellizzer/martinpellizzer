Thismia claviformis

Thismia claviformis, commonly known as the club-shaped Thismia, is an evergreen perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, club-like inflorescence that resembles a small, elongated bud. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that are often enclosed within a fleshy, leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Thismiaceae family.
