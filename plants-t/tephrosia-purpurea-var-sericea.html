Tephrosia purpurea var. sericea

Tephrosia purpurea var. sericea, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine for their pesticidal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
