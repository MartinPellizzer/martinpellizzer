Thalictrum amurense

Thalictrum amurense, commonly known as the Siberian meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and modest blooms.

Thalictrum amurense is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
