Turpinia paucijuga var. guatemalensis

Turpinia paucijuga var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan turpentine tree, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
