Tapura guianensis

Tapura guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese tapura, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.
