Tanacetum porphyrostephanum

Tanacetum porphyrostephanum, commonly known as the purplestephanum tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a strong, camphor-like scent, and its flowers are yellow with purple-tinted ray florets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
