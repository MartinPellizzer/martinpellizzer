Trichocentrum aguirrei

Trichocentrum aguirrei, commonly known as the Aguirre's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

Trichocentrum aguirrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
