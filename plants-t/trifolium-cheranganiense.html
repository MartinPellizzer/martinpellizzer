Trifolium cheranganiense

Trifolium cheranganiense, commonly known as the Cherangani clover, is a flowering plant species native to East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in grassland habitats.

Trifolium cheranganiense plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
