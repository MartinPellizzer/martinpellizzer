Talinum acaule

Talinum acaule, commonly known as purple leaf nightshade, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring fleshy, lance-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
