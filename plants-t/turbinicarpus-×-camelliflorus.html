Turbinicarpus × camelliflorus

Turbinicarpus × camelliflorus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a unique combination of two distinct cactus species.

It features a colorful, non-photosynthetic upper portion called the "pseudostem" that sits atop a green, photosynthetic base known as the "stock.". The plant is native to the arid regions of Mexico and is prized for its vibrant, often multicolored flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is typically propagated through grafting rather than seeds.
