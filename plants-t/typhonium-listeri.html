Typhonium listeri

Typhonium listeri, commonly known as the Lister's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a tuberous root that is edible and has a starchy texture similar to arrowroot. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
