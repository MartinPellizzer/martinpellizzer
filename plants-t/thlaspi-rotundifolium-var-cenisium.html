Thlaspi rotundifolium var. cenisium

Thlaspi rotundifolium var. cenisium, commonly known as the round-leaved rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It is a perennial herb that is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh conditions.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.
