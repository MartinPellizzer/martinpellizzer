Tetratheca aphylla subsp. aphylla

Tetratheca aphylla subsp.

aphylla, commonly known as the smooth tetratheca, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are narrowly elliptic to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local insect and bird populations.
