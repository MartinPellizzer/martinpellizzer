Typha laxmannii var. bungei

Typha laxmannii var. bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower heads that mature into brown, fluffy structures. The plant thrives in marshy environments and is often found along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a rough texture, and it produces a significant amount of pollen.

This variety is closely related to other cattail species and plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
