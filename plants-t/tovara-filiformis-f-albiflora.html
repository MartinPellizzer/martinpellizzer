Tovara filiformis f. albiflora

Tovara filiformis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered tovara, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and small, pale white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests or along riverbanks. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
