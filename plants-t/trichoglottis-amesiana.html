Trichoglottis amesiana

Trichoglottis amesiana, commonly known as the Amesiana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a spike, which are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Trichoglottis amesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
