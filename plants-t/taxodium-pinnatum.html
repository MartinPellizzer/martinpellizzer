Taxodium pinnatum

Taxodium pinnatum, commonly known as the Chinese swamp cypress, is a coniferous evergreen native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its branches. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Taxodium pinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
