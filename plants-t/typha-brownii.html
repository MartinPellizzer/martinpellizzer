Typha brownii

Typha brownii, commonly known as the brown bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that stand above the water or moist soil. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Typha brownii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
