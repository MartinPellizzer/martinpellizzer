Tanacetum crataegifolium

Tanacetum crataegifolium, commonly known as the hawthorn-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves that resemble those of hawthorn, giving it its common name. It produces yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Tanacetum crataegifolium is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, where it thrives in full sun.
