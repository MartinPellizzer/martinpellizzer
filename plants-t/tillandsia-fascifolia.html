Tillandsia fascifolia

Tillandsia fascifolia, commonly known as the stripe-leaf air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, narrow leaves with distinctive white or silver stripes that help in light reflection and water retention. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia fascifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
