Trifolium monanthum subsp. parvum

Trifolium monanthum subsp.

parvum, commonly known as the small-flowered clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued species in conservation efforts.
