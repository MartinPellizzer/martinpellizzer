Thysanolaena latifolia

Thysanolaena latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or the giant feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Thysanolaena latifolia is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
