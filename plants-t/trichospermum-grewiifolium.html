Trichospermum grewiifolium

Trichospermum grewiifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately arranged with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
