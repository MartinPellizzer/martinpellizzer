Thalictrum simplex var. duriusculum

Thalictrum simplex var. duriusculum, commonly known as the rough meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are compound with numerous leaflets.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
