Trembleya parviflora var. angustifolia

Trembleya parviflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
