Trichopuntia vestita

Trichopuntia vestita, commonly known as the naked leafed fishhook cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. Its leaves are reduced to small, spiny structures, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Trichopuntia vestita is a succulent, adapted to store water in its fleshy stems for survival in dry conditions.
