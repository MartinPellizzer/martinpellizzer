Thymus zygis var. sylvestris

Thymus zygis var. sylvestris, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compact, woody stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
