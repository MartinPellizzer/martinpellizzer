Tinus serrata

Tinus serrata, commonly known as the serrated tinus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Tinus serrata is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
