Tradescantia laxiflora

Tradescantia laxiflora, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia laxiflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
