Trifolium flavum

Trifolium flavum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often forming dense clusters. Trifolium flavum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
