Tephrosia pondoensis

Tephrosia pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Tephrosia pondoensis is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
