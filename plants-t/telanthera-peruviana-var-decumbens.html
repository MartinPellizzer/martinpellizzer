Telanthera peruviana var. decumbens

Telanthera peruviana var. decumbens, commonly known as the Peruvian telanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
