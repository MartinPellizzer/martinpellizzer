Tinus racemosa

Tinus racemosa, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow ironwood, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and yellowish-orange flowers that appear in racemes. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Tinus racemosa is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
