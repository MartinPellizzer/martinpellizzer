Tulbaghia macrocarpa

Tulbaghia macrocarpa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are aromatic and often used in perfumery. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
