Turnera benthamiana

Turnera benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthamic turnera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include a short stem, hairy foliage, and small, clustered inflorescences.

It is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
