Thelypteris opposita var. rivulorum

Thelypteris opposita var. rivulorum, commonly known as the river fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris opposita var. rivulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
