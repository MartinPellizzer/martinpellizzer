Taraxacum banhyhalense

Taraxacum banhyhalense, commonly known as the Banhyhalense dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed sphere.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
