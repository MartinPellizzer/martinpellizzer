Trichocaulon cactiformis

Trichocaulon cactiformis, commonly known as the cactus-like lichen, is a unique organism that exhibits both lichen and cactus-like characteristics.

It is a saxicolous lichen, typically growing on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a branched, cactus-like appearance with a dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. It thrives in regions with low moisture and high sunlight exposure.

Its structure is adapted to minimize water loss and maximize light absorption, making it well-suited for harsh, dry climates.
