Triodia fitzgeraldii

Triodia fitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's triodia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, spreading stems and small, narrow leaves. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil.

Its presence is often indicative of stable, low-nutrient ecosystems.
