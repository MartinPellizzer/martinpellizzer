Teucrium flavum var. leiophyllum

Teucrium flavum var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the smoothleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small, tubular, and often pink or purple in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
