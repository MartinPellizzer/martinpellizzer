Tropaeolum hirsutum

Tropaeolum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces bright orange or yellow blooms, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly grown as an ornamental or decorative plant.

Tropaeolum hirsutum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
