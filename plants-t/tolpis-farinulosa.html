Tolpis farinulosa

Tolpis farinulosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a bushy appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tolpis farinulosa also produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.
