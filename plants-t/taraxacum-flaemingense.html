Taraxacum flaemingense

Taraxacum flaemingense, commonly known as the Flemish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves that form a dense cluster near the ground. Its flowering stem rises from the center of the rosette, bearing a single yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere.

Taraxacum flaemingense is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
