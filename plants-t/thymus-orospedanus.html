Thymus orospedanus

Thymus orospedanus, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus orospedanus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
