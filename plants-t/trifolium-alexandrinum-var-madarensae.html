Trifolium alexandrinum var. madarensae

Trifolium alexandrinum var. madarensae, commonly known as the Madagascan clover, is a variant of the Alexandrian clover native to Madagascar.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for soil stabilization.
