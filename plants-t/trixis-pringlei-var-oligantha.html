Trixis pringlei var. oligantha

Trixis pringlei var. oligantha, commonly known as the spiny trixis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
