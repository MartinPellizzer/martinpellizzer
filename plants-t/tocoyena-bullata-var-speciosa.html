Tocoyena bullata var. speciosa

Tocoyena bullata var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy tocoyena, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a contrasting dark stripe. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing clusters of these showy blooms, which appear in the summer months. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with bright light.
