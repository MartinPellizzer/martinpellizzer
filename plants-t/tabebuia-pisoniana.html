Tabebuia pisoniana

Tabebuia pisoniana, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
