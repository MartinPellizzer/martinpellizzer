Tibouchina lepidota var. intermedia

Tibouchina lepidota var. intermedia, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
