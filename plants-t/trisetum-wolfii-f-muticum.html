Trisetum wolfii f. muticum

Trisetum wolfii f. muticum, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically purple to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. It is adapted to well-drained soils and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

Trisetum wolfii f. muticum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
