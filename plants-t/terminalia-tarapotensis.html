Terminalia tarapotensis

Terminalia tarapotensis, commonly known as the Peruvian apple or tarapotensis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Combretaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
