Teramnus rhombifolius

Teramnus rhombifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf teramnus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The leaves are rhombic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow, and are followed by small, round berries.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
