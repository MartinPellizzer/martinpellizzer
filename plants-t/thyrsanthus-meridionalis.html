Thyrsanthus meridionalis

Thyrsanthus meridionalis, commonly known as the southern thyrsin, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
