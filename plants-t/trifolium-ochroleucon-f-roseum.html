Trifolium ochroleucon f. roseum

Trifolium ochroleucon f. roseum, commonly known as the pink clover, is a botanical variant of the common white clover.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to reddish flowers, which contrast with the typical white blooms of the species. This plant is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil enrichment.
