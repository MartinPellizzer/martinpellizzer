Thymus marginatus

Thymus marginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.

Thymus marginatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
