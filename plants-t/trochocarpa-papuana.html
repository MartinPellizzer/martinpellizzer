Trochocarpa papuana

Trochocarpa papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bottle tree, is a unique plant native to the lowland rainforests of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
