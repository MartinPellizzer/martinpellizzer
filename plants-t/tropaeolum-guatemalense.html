Tropaeolum guatemalense

Tropaeolum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers similar to those of other species in the Tropaeolum genus. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
