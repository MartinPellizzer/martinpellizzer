Thlaspi caerulescens subsp. brachypetalum

Thlaspi caerulescens subsp.

brachypetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled copperwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops.

This subspecies is known for its ability to tolerate heavy metal soils, making it a potential indicator species for environmental contamination.
