Taraxacum ronae var. immaculatum

Taraxacum ronae var. immaculatum, commonly known as the immaculate dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces a distinctive white puffball seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is notable for its robust root system and its role in supporting pollinators.
