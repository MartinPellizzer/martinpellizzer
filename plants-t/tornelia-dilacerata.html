Tornelia dilacerata

Tornelia dilacerata, commonly known as the broken-tipped lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and often has a distinctive, branched stem structure. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Tornelia dilacerata is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
