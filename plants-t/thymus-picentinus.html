Thymus picentinus

Thymus picentinus, commonly known as the Picentine thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces pink to violet flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Thymus picentinus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in culinary applications.
