Torilis erythrotricha

Torilis erythrotricha, commonly known as red-haired devil's-bit, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, pale yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive reddish tinge along the edges, contributing to its common name.

Torilis erythrotricha is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
