Thymus mastichina var. carnosus

Thymus mastichina var. carnosus, commonly known as the Carpathian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features dense, woody stems covered in small, gray-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in terminal spikes. It is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
