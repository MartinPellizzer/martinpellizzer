Thymus leptobotrys var. latifolius

Thymus leptobotrys var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for culinary and ornamental use.
