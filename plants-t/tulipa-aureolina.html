Tulipa aureolina

Tulipa aureolina, commonly known as the golden tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sturdy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Tulipa aureolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy flowers.
