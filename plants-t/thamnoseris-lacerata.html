Thamnoseris lacerata

Thamnoseris lacerata, commonly known as the fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy or fringed, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets and bloom in the early to mid-summer.

Thamnoseris lacerata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
