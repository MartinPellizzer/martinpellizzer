Thunbergia glaucina var. latifolia

Thunbergia glaucina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
