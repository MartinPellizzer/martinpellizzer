Trommsdorffia pulverulenta

Trommsdorffia pulverulenta, commonly known as the dustplant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, often clustered in loose inflorescences. Trommsdorffia pulverulenta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it an interesting addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
