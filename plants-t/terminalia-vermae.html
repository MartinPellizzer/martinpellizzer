Terminalia vermae

Terminalia vermae, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
