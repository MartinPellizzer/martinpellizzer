Trichilia cruegeriana

Trichilia cruegeriana, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing flat, edible seeds. These seeds are rich in oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of culinary fat.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
