Tilia × gloriosa

Tilia × gloriosa, commonly known as the glory tilleia, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically medium to large in size, with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
