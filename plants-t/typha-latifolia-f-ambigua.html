Typha latifolia f. ambigua

Typha latifolia f. ambigua, commonly known as the broad-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its wide, flat, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, contributing to the ecological structure of wetlands.

The plant plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
