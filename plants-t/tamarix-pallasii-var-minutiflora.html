Tamarix pallasii var. minutiflora

Tamarix pallasii var. minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or saline soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
