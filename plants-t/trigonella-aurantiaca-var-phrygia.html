Trigonella aurantiaca var. phrygia

Trigonella aurantiaca var. phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy stems and the presence of stipules at the base of its leaves.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.
