Tibouchina aegopogon var. angustifolia

Tibouchina aegopogon var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
