Taraxacum clavatilobum

Taraxacum clavatilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate with narrow lobes, giving it its specific epithet. It produces a slender, hairy stem that bears a single flower head composed of ray florets.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in open, disturbed habitats.
