Thelymitra iridioides

Thelymitra iridioides, commonly known as the blue orchid, is an Australian orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue and violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the warmer months.

Thelymitra iridioides is a popular horticultural species due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
