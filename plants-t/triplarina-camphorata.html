Triplarina camphorata

Triplarina camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, thick, and leathery leaves that emit a strong camphor scent when crushed. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a camphor-like resin.

Triplarina camphorata is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
