Trifolium pratense var. pyrenaeum

Trifolium pratense var. pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean red clover, is a variant of the common red clover native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pink-veined and have a distinctive red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and soil health.
