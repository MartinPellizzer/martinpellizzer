Tigridia hallbergii

Tigridia hallbergii, commonly known as the Hallberg's flame flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble those of the tiger lily, with bright red or orange petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and produces its showy blooms in the spring. Its foliage consists of grass-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Tigridia hallbergii is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
