Tricera revoluta

Tricera revoluta, commonly known as the curled trichome plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are tightly curled and covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which give it a fuzzy appearance. This plant is native to specific arid regions where it has adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils. The trichomes serve as a protective mechanism against water loss and UV radiation.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae and is notable for its unusual growth form and specialized adaptations.
