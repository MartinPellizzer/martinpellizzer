Thlaspi bursa-pastoris subsp. integrifolium

Thlaspi bursa-pastoris subsp.

integrifolium, commonly known as field pennycress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb with smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are usually entire or slightly lobed.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, followed by rounded, cup-shaped seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its hairy stems and leaves help it retain moisture in dry conditions.
