Tonestus linearis

Tonestus linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle in the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear shape and metallic green or blue coloration. The plant it feeds on is typically a species of flowering plant, often found in grasslands or open woodlands. The beetle's larvae are usually found on the undersides of leaves, feeding on plant tissue.

Its life cycle involves several developmental stages, including egg, larva, pupa, and adult.
