Thalictrum hirtellum

Thalictrum hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, actinomorphic flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in the understory of forests.

Thalictrum hirtellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
