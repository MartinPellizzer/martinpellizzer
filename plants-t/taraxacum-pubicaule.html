Taraxacum pubicaule

Taraxacum pubicaule, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinct appearance compared to other dandelion species. The plant produces a single, yellow flower that develops into a fluffy, white seed head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum pubicaule is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
