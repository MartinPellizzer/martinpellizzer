Teucrium balfourii

Teucrium balfourii, commonly known as Balfour's germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium balfourii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
