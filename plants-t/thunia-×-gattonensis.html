Thunia × gattonensis

Thunia × gattonensis, commonly known as the Gatton lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Thunia flaccida and Thunia sylvatica.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, recurved shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thunia × gattonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
