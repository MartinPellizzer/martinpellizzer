Turrita saxatilis

Turrita saxatilis, commonly known as the rock turricula, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Turbinellidae.

It is a small gastropod mollusk that typically inhabits rocky shores and tidal zones in the North Atlantic. The shell is characterized by its conical shape, spiral ornamentation, and a smooth, glossy surface. It is often found in association with algae and other marine organisms on exposed substrates.

Due to its habitat, it plays a role in the ecological balance of coastal ecosystems.
