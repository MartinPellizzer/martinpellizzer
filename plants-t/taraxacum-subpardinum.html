Taraxacum subpardinum

Taraxacum subpardinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus. This plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
