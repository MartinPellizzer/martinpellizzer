Tragacantha pinardii

Tragacantha pinardii, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Its sap, which is thick and sticky, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industrial applications.
