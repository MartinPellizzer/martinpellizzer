Thecopus secunda

Thecopus secunda, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Thecopus secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
