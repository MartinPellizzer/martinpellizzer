Tropaeolum digitatum

Tropaeolum digitatum, commonly known as the digitate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are palmately lobed, giving the plant its common name, and they are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.
