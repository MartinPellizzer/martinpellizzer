Thamnochortus muirii

Thamnochortus muirii, commonly known as the Muir's hairveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil in its native habitat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Thamnochortus muirii is valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting local biodiversity.
