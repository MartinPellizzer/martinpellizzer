Trichosanthes edulis

Trichosanthes edulis, commonly known as the Chinese trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and climbing.

Trichosanthes edulis is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
