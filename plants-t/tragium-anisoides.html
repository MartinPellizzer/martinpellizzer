Tragium anisoides

Tragium anisoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some regions.
