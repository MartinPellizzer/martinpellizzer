Thymus reichelianus

Thymus reichelianus, commonly known as Reichel's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and has been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.

Reichel's thyme thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
