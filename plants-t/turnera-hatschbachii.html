Turnera hatschbachii

Turnera hatschbachii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
