Thymus ovatus var. subcitratus

Thymus ovatus var. subcitratus, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in cooking and herbal remedies.
