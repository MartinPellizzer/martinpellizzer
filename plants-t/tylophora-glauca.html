Tylophora glauca

Tylophora glauca, commonly known as the blue tylophora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tylophora glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
