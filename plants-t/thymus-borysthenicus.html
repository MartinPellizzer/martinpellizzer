Thymus borysthenicus

Thymus borysthenicus, commonly known as the Crimean thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
