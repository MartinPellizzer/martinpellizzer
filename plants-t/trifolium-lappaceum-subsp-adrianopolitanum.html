Trifolium lappaceum subsp. adrianopolitanum

Trifolium lappaceum subsp.

adrianopolitanum, commonly known as the Adrianopolitan clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lanceolate leaflets arranged on a central stem.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
