Trigonella lipskyi var. edelbergii

Trigonella lipskyi var. edelbergii, commonly known as the Edelberg's fenugreek, is a herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by slender, dehiscent pods that release seeds when mature.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
