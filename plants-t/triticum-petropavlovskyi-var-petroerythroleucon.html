Triticum petropavlovskyi var. petroerythroleucon

Triticum petropavlovskyi var. petroerythroleucon, commonly known as the Petro erythroleucon wheat, is a rare and ancient wheat species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive red and white striped glumes. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with awned lemma and palea. It is notable for its potential in genetic research due to its unique traits and historical significance.

However, it is not widely cultivated and is more of a botanical curiosity than a staple crop.
