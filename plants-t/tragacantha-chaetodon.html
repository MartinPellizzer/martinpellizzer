Tragacantha chaetodon

Tragacantha chaetodon, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, slender, yellowish-green bracts that resemble the beard of a goat, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant ornamental plants.
