Thymus marschallianus subsp. tosevii

Thymus marschallianus subsp.

tosevii, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and produces violet or pink flowers in early summer.

The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is commonly used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical classification places it within the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
