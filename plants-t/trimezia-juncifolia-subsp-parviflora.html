Trimezia juncifolia subsp. parviflora

Trimezia juncifolia subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered trimezia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
