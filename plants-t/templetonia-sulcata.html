Templetonia sulcata

Templetonia sulcata, commonly known as the sulcate flame tree, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Templetonia sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
