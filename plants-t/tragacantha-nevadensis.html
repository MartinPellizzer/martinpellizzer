Tragacantha nevadensis

Tragacantha nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
