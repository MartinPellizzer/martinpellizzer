Talauma poasana

Talauma poasana, commonly known as the Hawaiian mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Talauma poasana thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional Hawaiian medicine for their purported healing properties.
