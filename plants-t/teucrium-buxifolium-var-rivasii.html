Teucrium buxifolium var. rivasii

Teucrium buxifolium var. rivasii, commonly known as the Rivas' boxleaf germander, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are broad and oval, resembling those of boxwood, giving it its common name.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
