Tamarix hexandra

Tamarix hexandra, commonly known as the white tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, deciduous foliage and pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

Tamarix hexandra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry areas.
