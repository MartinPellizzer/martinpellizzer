Thelypteris margaretae

Thelypteris margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are arranged in distinct lines along the undersides of the fronds.

Thelypteris margaretae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
