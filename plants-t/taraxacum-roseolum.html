Taraxacum roseolum

Taraxacum roseolum, commonly known as the pink dandelion, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright pink to purple flower heads, which contrast with its yellow-orange central disk. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem and has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, often in temperate regions.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
