Tilia americana var. walteri

Tilia americana var. walteri, commonly known as the Walter's basswood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, oval fruits that contain three winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
