Talinum rubens

Talinum rubens, commonly known as red-rooted malanga, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its tubers, which are edible, are similar in appearance to yams and are used in various culinary preparations.

Talinum rubens thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
