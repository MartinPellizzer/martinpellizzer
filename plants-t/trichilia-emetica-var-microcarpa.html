Trichilia emetica var. microcarpa

Trichilia emetica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the African cherry or microcarpa tallowtree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules containing numerous seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
