Themeda triandra subsp. japonica

Themeda triandra subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
