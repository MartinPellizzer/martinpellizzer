Trachypogon polymorphus var. truncatus

Trachypogon polymorphus var. truncatus, commonly known as the shortgrass tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and short, stout culms. The plant typically forms compact clumps with narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
