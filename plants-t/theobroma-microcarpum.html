Theobroma microcarpum

Theobroma microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited cocoa, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its elliptical leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain bitter, dark seeds used in the production of chocolate.
