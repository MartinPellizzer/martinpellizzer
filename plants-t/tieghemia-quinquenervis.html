Tieghemia quinquenervis

Tieghemia quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
