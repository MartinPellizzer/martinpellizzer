Terminalia diversipilosa

Terminalia diversipilosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
