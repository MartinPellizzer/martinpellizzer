Trichodium caninum var. turfosum

Trichodium caninum var. turfosum, commonly known as the turfy trichodium, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

This variant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often growing in lawns or on the edges of water features. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss, giving it a distinct, carpet-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Due to its ability to thrive in humid conditions, it is sometimes considered a weed in ornamental landscapes.
