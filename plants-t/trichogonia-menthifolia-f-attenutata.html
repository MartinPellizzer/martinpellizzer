Trichogonia menthifolia f. attenutata

Trichogonia menthifolia f. attenutata, commonly known as the slenderleaf trichogonia, is a botanical variant of the species Trichogonia menthifolia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
