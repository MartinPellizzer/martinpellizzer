Taraxacum boldtii

Taraxacum boldtii, commonly known as Boldt's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces bright yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
