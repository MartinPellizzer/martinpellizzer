Tragacantha subsecunda

Tragacantha subsecunda, commonly known as the lesser tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are known for their mucilage content, which has been used historically in various traditional medicinal applications.

Tragacantha subsecunda thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
