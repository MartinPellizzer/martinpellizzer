Tragacantha lobophora

Tragacantha lobophora, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for certain insects and animals.

Tragacantha lobophora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal applications.
