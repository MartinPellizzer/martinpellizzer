Taeniophyllum aphyllum

Taeniophyllum aphyllum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that lacks true leaves, instead having specialized structures called trichomes that absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant's most striking feature is its translucent, waxy covering, which gives it a ghostly appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful handling and specific environmental conditions to flourish.
