Thymbra thymbrifolia

Thymbra thymbrifolia, commonly known as the fragrant thyme.

This perennial herb is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It features small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
