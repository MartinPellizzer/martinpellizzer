Tounatea multijuga

Tounatea multijuga, commonly known as the Malabar mountain fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests. Its latex contains compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest to botanists and pharmacologists.

Tounatea multijuga plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
