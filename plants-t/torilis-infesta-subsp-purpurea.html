Torilis infesta subsp. purpurea

Torilis infesta subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple burrweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, bristly fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its purple-tinged bracts make it easily recognizable in the late summer and early fall.
