Trichoballia involucrata

Trichoballia involucrata, commonly known as the woolly ball tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical, woody fruit that resembles a ball, covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are typically small, opposite, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Trichoballia involucrata is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
