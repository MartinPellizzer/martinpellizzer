Tripogon coreensis

Tripogon coreensis, commonly known as the Korean grass, is a perennial grass species native to Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wetland areas.

Due to its ecological significance, it is considered an important component of the region's native flora.
