Tragacantha denudata

Tragacantha denudata, commonly known as the naked tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds, a feature typical of many legumes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
