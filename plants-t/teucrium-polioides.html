Teucrium polioides

Teucrium polioides, commonly known as silver germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice in horticulture.
