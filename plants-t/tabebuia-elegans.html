Tabebuia elegans

Tabebuia elegans, commonly known as the royal poinciana, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and red during the dry season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal and savanna regions.

The tree is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
