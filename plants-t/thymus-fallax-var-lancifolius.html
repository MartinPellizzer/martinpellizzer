Thymus fallax var. lancifolius

Thymus fallax var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
