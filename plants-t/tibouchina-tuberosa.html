Tibouchina tuberosa

Tibouchina tuberosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or purple flowers and its dense, velvety foliage. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
