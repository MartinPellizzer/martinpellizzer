Trymalium billardierei var. tomentosum

Trymalium billardierei var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
