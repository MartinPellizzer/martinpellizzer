Thymelaea putorioides

Thymelaea putorioides, commonly known as the Cape rose or thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Thymelaea putorioides is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance.
