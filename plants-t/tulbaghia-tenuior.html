Tulbaghia tenuior

Tulbaghia tenuior, commonly known as the narrowleaf tulbaghia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, mild fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tulbaghia tenuior is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
