Terebinthina sessiliflora

Terebinthina sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower terebinth, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Terebinthaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Terebinthina sessiliflora is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
