Tabebuia avellanedae

Tabebuia avellanedae, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a species of flowering tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface.

The bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.
