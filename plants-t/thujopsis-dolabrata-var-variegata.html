Thujopsis dolabrata var. variegata

Thujopsis dolabrata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese white pine, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive, narrow, conical shape.

It features silvery-white to yellowish variegated foliage that contrasts with its dark green base, creating an eye-catching appearance. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can tolerate urban conditions.

However, it is susceptible to certain pests and diseases, requiring regular care and maintenance.
