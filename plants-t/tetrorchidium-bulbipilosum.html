Tetrorchidium bulbipilosum

Tetrorchidium bulbipilosum, commonly known as the hairy bulb orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its bulbous roots and hairy foliage, which help it survive in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological needs, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
