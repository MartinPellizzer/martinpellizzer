Tecoma velutina

Tecoma velutina, commonly known as velvet trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Tecoma velutina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
