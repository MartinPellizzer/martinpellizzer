Thymus vavilovii

Thymus vavilovii, commonly known as the Caucasian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for dry, sunny environments. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus vavilovii is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
