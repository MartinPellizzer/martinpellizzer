Taraxacum catalanum

Taraxacum catalanum, commonly known as Catalan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive seed heads. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Its life cycle includes a period of vegetative growth followed by flowering and seed dispersal.
