Theyodis

Theyodis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer under sunlight, giving it an ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale-blue flowers that bloom only at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Its roots are said to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, though research is limited.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat, Theyodis remains a subject of fascination for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
