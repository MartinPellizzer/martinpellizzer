Tonalanthus

Tonalanthus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and consists of several species, many of which are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce bright yellow or orange flower heads. Tonalanthus species are often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
