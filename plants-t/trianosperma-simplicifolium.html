Trianosperma simplicifolium

Trianosperma simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leafed spirea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorn-like protrusions and small, papery bracts beneath the flowers.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
