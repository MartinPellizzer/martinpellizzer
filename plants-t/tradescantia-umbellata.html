Tradescantia umbellata

Tradescantia umbellata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or pink throat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Tradescantia umbellata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
