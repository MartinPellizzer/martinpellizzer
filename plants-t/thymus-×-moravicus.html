Thymus × moravicus

Thymus × moravicus, commonly known as the Moravian thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
