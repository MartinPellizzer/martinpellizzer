Trogostolon

Trogostolon, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Trogostolon is valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
