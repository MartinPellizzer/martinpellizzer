Teucrium polium var. pii-fontii

Teucrium polium var. pii-fontii, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
