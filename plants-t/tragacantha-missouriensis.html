Tragacantha missouriensis

Tragacantha missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
