Taraxacum multilobum

Taraxacum multilobum, commonly known as the Pacific dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are often lobed, giving it its specific epithet "multilobum.". It produces a yellow flower head that matures into a crown of white, feathery seeds.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
