Tricholaena capensis

Tricholaena capensis, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.

Tricholaena capensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
