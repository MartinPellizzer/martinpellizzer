Trifolium ochroleucon f. villosum

Trifolium ochroleucon f. villosum, commonly known as the hairy yellow clover, is a variant of the yellow clover species characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
