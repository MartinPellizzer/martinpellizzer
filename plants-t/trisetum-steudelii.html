Trisetum steudelii

Trisetum steudelii, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common component of natural grasslands.

Trisetum steudelii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
