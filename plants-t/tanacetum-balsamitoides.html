Tanacetum balsamitoides

Tanacetum balsamitoides, commonly known as the Balsam weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, often bitter scent. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum balsamitoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
