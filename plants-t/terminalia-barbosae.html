Terminalia barbosae

Terminalia barbosae, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds are rich in oils and are used in the production of shea butter, which has various industrial and cosmetic applications.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its economic and ecological significance.
