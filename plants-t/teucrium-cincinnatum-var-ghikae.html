Teucrium cincinnatum var. ghikae

Teucrium cincinnatum var. ghikae, commonly known as the Ethiopian germander, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.
