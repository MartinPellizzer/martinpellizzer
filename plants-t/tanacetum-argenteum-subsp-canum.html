Tanacetum argenteum subsp. canum

Tanacetum argenteum subsp.

canum, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
