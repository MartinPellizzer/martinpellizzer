Teucrium chasmophyticum

Teucrium chasmophyticum, commonly known as the chasmophytic germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. The plant features slender, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
