Trichipteris chnoodes

Trichipteris chnoodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands, where it attaches to submerged or floating objects. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it in the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the water.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
