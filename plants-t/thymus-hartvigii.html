Thymus hartvigii

Thymus hartvigii, commonly known as Hartvig's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is densely packed, with small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus hartvigii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
