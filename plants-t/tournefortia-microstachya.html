Tournefortia microstachya

Tournefortia microstachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
