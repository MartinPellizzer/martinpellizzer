Triticum recognitum

Triticum recognitum, commonly known as the recognized wheat, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, each containing a single grain. Triticum recognitum is primarily cultivated for its grains, which are used in the production of flour and other food products.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
