Trichoglottis calcarata

Trichoglottis calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions. Its roots are often covered in a dense, white mycorrhizal coating, which aids in nutrient absorption.

Trichoglottis calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
