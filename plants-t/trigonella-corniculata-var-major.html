Trigonella corniculata var. major

Trigonella corniculata var. major, commonly known as the greater horned vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and curved, resembling horns, which give it its common name.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
