Trixis divaricata var. exauriculata

Trixis divaricata var. exauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to drought include succulent-like leaves and deep root systems that help it survive in harsh, dry environments.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
