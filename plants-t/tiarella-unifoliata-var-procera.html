Tiarella unifoliata var. procera

Tiarella unifoliata var. procera, commonly known as the tall foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, waxy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are softly hairy. Its upright flowering stems can reach heights of up to 30 inches, making it a distinctive addition to shaded gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
