Thalictrum columbianum

Thalictrum columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian meadow rue, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Thalictrum columbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
