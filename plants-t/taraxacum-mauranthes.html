Taraxacum mauranthes

Taraxacum mauranthes, commonly known as the Mauranthes dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a yellow flowering head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
