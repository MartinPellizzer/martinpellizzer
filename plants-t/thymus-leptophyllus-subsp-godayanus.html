Thymus leptophyllus subsp. godayanus

Thymus leptophyllus subsp.

godayanus, commonly known as the Godayan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
