Tragacantha sericophylla

Tragacantha sericophylla, commonly known as the silky leaf tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, silky-hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots are known for their mucilaginous properties, which have been used traditionally in medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
