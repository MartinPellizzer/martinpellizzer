Tuberaria × colombina

Tuberaria × colombina, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Tuberaria, which is part of the Brassicaceae family, also known as the mustard family. The plant is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring and early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence.

Tuberaria × colombina is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
