Typha przewalskii

Typha przewalskii, commonly known as Przewalski's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes.

Typha przewalskii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
