Tococa raggiana

Tococa raggiana, commonly known as the Raggiana tococa, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

The plant is noted for its aromatic leaves, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
