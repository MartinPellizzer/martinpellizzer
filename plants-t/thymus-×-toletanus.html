Thymus × toletanus

Thymus × toletanus, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its foliage is dense and fragrant, often used in cooking and herbal remedies.

Thymus × toletanus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
