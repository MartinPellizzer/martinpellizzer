Thymus pseudochamaedrys

Thymus pseudochamaedrys, commonly known as narrow-leaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils and antioxidant properties.
