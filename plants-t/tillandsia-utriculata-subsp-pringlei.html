Tillandsia utriculata subsp. pringlei

Tillandsia utriculata subsp.

pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, facilitating nutrient absorption from rainwater.

The plant is adapted to grow on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
