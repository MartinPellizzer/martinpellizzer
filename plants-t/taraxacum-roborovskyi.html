Taraxacum roborovskyi

Taraxacum roborovskyi, commonly known as the Roborovsky dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy, robust stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, and often spiky seed head that aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its leaves are typically basal, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
