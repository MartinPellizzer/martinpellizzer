Tabebuia subtilis var. schultesiana

Tabebuia subtilis var. schultesiana, commonly known as the pink tabebuia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
