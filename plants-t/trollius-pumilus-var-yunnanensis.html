Trollius pumilus var. yunnanensis

Trollius pumilus var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
