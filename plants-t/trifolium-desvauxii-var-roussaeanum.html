Trifolium desvauxii var. roussaeanum

Trifolium desvauxii var. roussaeanum, commonly known as the Roussaean clover, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
