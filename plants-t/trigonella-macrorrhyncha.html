Trigonella macrorrhyncha

Trigonella macrorrhyncha, commonly known as the large-seeded fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Trigonella macrorrhyncha is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
