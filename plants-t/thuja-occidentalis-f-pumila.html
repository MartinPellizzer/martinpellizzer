Thuja occidentalis f. pumila

Thuja occidentalis f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf arborvitae, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It features scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green, arranged in dense, flat sprays along the stems. This variety is prized for its slow growth and ability to form a dense, rounded shape, making it ideal for hedges or ornamental use. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, though it can tolerate a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity in landscaping.
