Tetrapterys macrocarpa

Tetrapterys macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellowish flowers and woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves with four pairs of leaflets, hence the genus name *Tetrapterys*.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
