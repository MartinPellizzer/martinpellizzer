Trisetopsis natalensis

Trisetopsis natalensis, commonly known as Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Eastern Cape and KwaZulu-Natal regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Trisetopsis natalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
