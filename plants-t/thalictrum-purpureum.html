Thalictrum purpureum

Thalictrum purpureum, commonly known as the purple meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens.

Thalictrum purpureum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value to pollinators.
