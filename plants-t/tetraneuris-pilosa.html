Tetraneuris pilosa

Tetraneuris pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with gray-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tetraneuris pilosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
