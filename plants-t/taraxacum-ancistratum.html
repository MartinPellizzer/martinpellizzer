Taraxacum ancistratum

Taraxacum ancistratum, commonly known as the Brazilian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that bears a yellow flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed head after flowering. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Taraxacum ancistratum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional South American medicine for various ailments.
