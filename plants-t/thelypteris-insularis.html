Thelypteris insularis

Thelypteris insularis, commonly known as the island water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a bioluminescent quality in certain conditions. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it can absorb ample humidity. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris insularis plays a significant role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various microorganisms and small fauna.
