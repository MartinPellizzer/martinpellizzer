Thlaspi apterocarpum

Thlaspi apterocarpum, commonly known as the woolly whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Thlaspi apterocarpum thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to cold climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
