Thunbergia cordata

Thunbergia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Thunbergia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
