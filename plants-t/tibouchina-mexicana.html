Tibouchina mexicana

Tibouchina mexicana, commonly known as Mexican flame flower, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Tibouchina mexicana is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
