Tephrosia filipes f. vestita

Tephrosia filipes f. vestita, commonly known as the white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring or early summer. Its foliage is typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or savanna habitats.
