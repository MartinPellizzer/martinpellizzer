Tillaea schimperi subsp. transvalensis

Tillaea schimperi subsp.

transvalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal tillia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and gardeners in arid climates.
