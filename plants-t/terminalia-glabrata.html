Terminalia glabrata

Terminalia glabrata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Terminalia glabrata is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
