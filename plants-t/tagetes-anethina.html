Tagetes anethina

Tagetes anethina, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon or golden marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for both garden decoration and herbal use.
