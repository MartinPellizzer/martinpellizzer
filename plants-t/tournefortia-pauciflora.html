Tournefortia pauciflora

Tournefortia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tournefortia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
