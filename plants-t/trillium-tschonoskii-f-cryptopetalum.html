Trillium tschonoskii f. cryptopetalum

Trillium tschonoskii f. cryptopetalum, commonly known as the cryptopetal trillium, is a rare and distinctive variant of the trillium genus.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a hooded petal that is deeply divided, giving it a more intricate appearance than typical trillium species. The plant produces three broad, waxy leaves that are usually a deep green, and its flowers are typically a striking shade of white or pale pink. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
