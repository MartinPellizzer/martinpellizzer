Tibouchina versicolor var. robusta

Tibouchina versicolor var. robusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purple, depending on the light conditions. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical gardens.
