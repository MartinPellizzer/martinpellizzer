Tragacantha adusta

Tragacantha adusta, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable gum, known as tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
