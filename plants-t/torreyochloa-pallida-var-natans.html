Torreyochloa pallida var. natans

Torreyochloa pallida var. natans, commonly known as the floating grass, is a unique variant of the species that exhibits aquatic adaptations.

It is characterized by its buoyant, floating leaves that allow it to thrive in shallow, still or slow-moving water bodies. The plant typically has slender, grass-like blades that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many grass species.

This variety is often found in wetland habitats and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic environments.
