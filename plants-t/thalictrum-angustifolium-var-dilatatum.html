Thalictrum angustifolium var. dilatatum

Thalictrum angustifolium var. dilatatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
