Tephrosia cordata

Tephrosia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to cordate leaflets, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in ornamental gardens and as a ground cover.
