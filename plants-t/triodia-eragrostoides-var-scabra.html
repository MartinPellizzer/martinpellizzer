Triodia eragrostoides var. scabra

Triodia eragrostoides var. scabra, commonly known as the rough triodia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, cylindrical stems and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming part of the vegetation in spinifex plains. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

This variety is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
