Tabernaemontana citrifolia

Tabernaemontana citrifolia, commonly known as the citron tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and oval-shaped, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, green fruits that mature to a yellowish color, resembling citrus fruits in appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its aromatic foliage.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
