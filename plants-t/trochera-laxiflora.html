Trochera laxiflora

Trochera laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The leaves are usually elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

It is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
