Thea biflora

Thea biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered tea plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are typically arranged in pairs, giving it the species name *biflora*.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
