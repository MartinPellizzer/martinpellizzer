Themeda gigantea subsp. caudata

Themeda gigantea subsp.

caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, drooping inflorescences that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, warm climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
