Trichoscypha volubilis

Trichoscypha volubilis, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a woody climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its twining stems that ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it thrives in humid, shady environments.

The climbing tamarind is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
