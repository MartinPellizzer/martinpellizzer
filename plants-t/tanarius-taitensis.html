Tanarius taitensis

Tanarius taitensis, commonly known as the Taiti sandalwood, is a rare and critically endangered tree species native to the tropical rainforests of Samoa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its unique scent and durability. The plant has a slow growth rate and produces small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.

Due to habitat loss and overexploitation, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
