Thamnochortus dumosus

Thamnochortus dumosus, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a rock garden plant.
