Telipogon musaicus

Telipogon musaicus, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, banana-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rich, moist soils near rivers or in dense forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Telipogon musaicus is considered an endangered species.
