Trigonochilum rubrocallosum

Trigonochilum rubrocallosum, commonly known as the red-collared orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink callose structures on the petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
