Turnera setosa

Turnera setosa, commonly known as the "sunshine flower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
