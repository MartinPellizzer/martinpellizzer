Thymus serpyllum subsp. dacicus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

dacicus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
