Talinum racemosum

Talinum racemosum, commonly known as red-rooted spiderflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Talinum racemosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
