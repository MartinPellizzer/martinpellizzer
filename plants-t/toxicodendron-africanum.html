Toxicodendron africanum

Toxicodendron africanum, commonly known as the African poison ivy, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

Due to its irritant properties, it is often avoided or handled with care.
