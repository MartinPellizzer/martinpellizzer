Tephrosia pringlei

Tephrosia pringlei, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Tephrosia pringlei is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
