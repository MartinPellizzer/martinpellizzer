Thalictrum polyphyllum

Thalictrum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum polyphyllum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
