Thymus remotiflorus

Thymus remotiflorus, commonly known as the remote-flowered thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Thymus remotiflorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
