Thalictrum multipeltatum

Thalictrum multipeltatum, commonly known as the showy mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum multipeltatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
