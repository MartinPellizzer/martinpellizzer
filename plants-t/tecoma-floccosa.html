Tecoma floccosa

Tecoma floccosa, commonly known as the woolly trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a fine, woolly texture, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
