Turnera melanorhiza var. latifolia

Turnera melanorhiza var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
