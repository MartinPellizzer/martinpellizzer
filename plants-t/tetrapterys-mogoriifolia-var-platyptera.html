Tetrapterys mogoriifolia var. platyptera

Tetrapterys mogoriifolia var. platyptera, commonly known as the broad-leafed tetrapterys, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive venation pattern and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in native habitats.
