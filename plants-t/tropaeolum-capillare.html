Tropaeolum capillare

Tropaeolum capillare, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems grow horizontally along the ground, trailing up to several meters. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, followed by edible, peppery seeds.

Tropaeolum capillare is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in both ornamental and culinary applications.
