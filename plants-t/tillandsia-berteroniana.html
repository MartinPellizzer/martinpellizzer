Tillandsia berteroniana

Tillandsia berteroniana, commonly known as the Berteronian air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to trees or rocks. The plant possesses dense, hairy foliage that helps it capture moisture from the air, which is essential for its survival in humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, red berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
