Taraxia breviflora

Taraxia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into white, spherical seed heads. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
