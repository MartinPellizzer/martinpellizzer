Teucrium pumilum subsp. carolipaui

Teucrium pumilum subsp.

carolipaui, commonly known as Carolina germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or rocky areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
