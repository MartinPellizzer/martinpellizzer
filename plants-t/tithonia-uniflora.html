Tithonia uniflora

Tithonia uniflora, commonly known as the sunflower bush, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Tithonia uniflora is often used in agricultural systems as a green manure due to its high nitrogen content.

It is also valued in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
