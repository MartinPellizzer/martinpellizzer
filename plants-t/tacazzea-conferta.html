Tacazzea conferta

Tacazzea conferta, commonly known as the African orchid or African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tacazzea conferta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
