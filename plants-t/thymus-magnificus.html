Thymus magnificus

Thymus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oils rich in thymol.

Thymus magnificus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
