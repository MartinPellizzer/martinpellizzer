Tetradenia riparia

Tetradenia riparia, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows along waterways, thriving in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can become branched with age.

Tetradenia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
