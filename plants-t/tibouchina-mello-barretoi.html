Tibouchina mello-barretoi

Tibouchina mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Brazilian flamebush, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tibouchina mello-barretoi is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
