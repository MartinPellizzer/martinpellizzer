Tragacantha chionocalyx

Tragacantha chionocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, particularly in the Mediterranean climate zones. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces a unique, mucilaginous sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.
