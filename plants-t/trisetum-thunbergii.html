Trisetum thunbergii

Trisetum thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its slender, spike-like inflorescence bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Trisetum thunbergii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
