Thymus serpyllum f. nyaradyanus

Thymus serpyllum f. nyaradyanus, commonly known as the Hungarian thyme, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

This variety is noted for its distinct fragrance and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
