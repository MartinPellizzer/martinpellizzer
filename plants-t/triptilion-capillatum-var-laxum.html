Triptilion capillatum var. laxum

Triptilion capillatum var. laxum, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to moist, temperate regions.

It features fine, thread-like leaves that are often slightly curled, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spikes of flowers during the summer months. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
