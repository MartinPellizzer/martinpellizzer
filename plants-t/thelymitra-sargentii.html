Thelymitra sargentii

Thelymitra sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in open forests and heathlands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, showy flower that is usually pale yellow to orange in color. Its roots are fibrous, and it has a short-lived lifespan, often completing its growth cycle within a single season.

Thelymitra sargentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
