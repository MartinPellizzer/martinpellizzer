Taraxacum junatovii

Taraxacum junatovii, commonly known as the Junatovii dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus hairs. This perennial herb is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. It thrives in dry, rocky, and sandy soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a relatively short growing season.
