Triosteum majus

Triosteum majus, commonly known as the showy triosteum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, violet-blue, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a distinctive, branching root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Triosteum majus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
