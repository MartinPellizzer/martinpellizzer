Thymus kamelinii

Thymus kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications, similar to other thyme species.
