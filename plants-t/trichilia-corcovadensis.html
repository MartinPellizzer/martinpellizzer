Trichilia corcovadensis

Trichilia corcovadensis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
