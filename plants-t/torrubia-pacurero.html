Torrubia pacurero

Torrubia pacurero, commonly known as the pacurero plant, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or humus-rich soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and densely arranged along its stems. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
