Tripteris muschleriana

Tripteris muschleriana, commonly known as the Muschler's tripteris, is a member of the Cashew family (Anacardiaceae).

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often alternate and have a distinctive three-lobed appearance. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
