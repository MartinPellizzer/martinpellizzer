Trichostachys speciosa

Trichostachys speciosa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often white or pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is also used in construction and landscaping in its native regions.
