Tephrosia purpurea var. laxa

Tephrosia purpurea var. laxa, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 2-3 meters in height, characterized by its slender, often branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Tephrosia purpurea var. laxa is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of nitrogen-fixing legumes.
