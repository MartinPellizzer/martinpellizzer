Tamarix hieronymi

Tamarix hieronymi, commonly known as the desert rose or white tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small scale-like leaves, and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and the ability to store water in its stems. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Tamarix hieronymi is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
