Triticum vulgare var. italicum

Triticum vulgare var. italicum, commonly known as Italian wheatgrass, is a variant of common wheat that is cultivated for its nutritional value and rapid growth.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny grains. It is often used in health foods, juices, and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a high concentration of chlorophyll, contributing to its vibrant green color.
