Tournefortia viridiflora var. griffithii

Tournefortia viridiflora var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's green-flowered tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
