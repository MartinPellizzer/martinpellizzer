Thymus × oblongifolius

Thymus × oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf thyme, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and fragrant, narrow to broadly elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
