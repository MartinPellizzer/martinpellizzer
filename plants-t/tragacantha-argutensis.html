Tragacantha argutensis

Tragacantha argutensis, commonly known as the thorny tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a latex-like sap, which has been historically used in various traditional applications. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture.

Tragacantha argutensis is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
