Trapa natans var. magnicorona

Trapa natans var. magnicorona, commonly known as the giant water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features submerged leaves that are long and narrow, with floating, palmate leaves that are broad and lobed. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its morphology is adapted for both aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
