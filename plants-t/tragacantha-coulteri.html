Tragacantha coulteri

Tragacantha coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Tragacantha coulteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
