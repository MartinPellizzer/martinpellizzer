Trichosalpinx franciscantha

Trichosalpinx franciscantha, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial herb with a distinctive, slender stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in yellow or orange hues. The plant has a complex structure with a lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns and a column that is specialized for pollination. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique floral morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
