Tetradenia barberae

Tetradenia barberae, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its slow growth and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
