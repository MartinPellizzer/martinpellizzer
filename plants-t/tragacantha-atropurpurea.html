Tragacantha atropurpurea

Tragacantha atropurpurea, commonly known as purple tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, branched stems that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The roots of this species are known for their mucilaginous properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a natural emulsifier.
