Tetrorchidium macrophyllum

Tetrorchidium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf orchid, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, evergreen rhizomes and large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
