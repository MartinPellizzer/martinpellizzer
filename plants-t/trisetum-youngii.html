Trisetum youngii

Trisetum youngii, commonly known as the Young's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that bears small, bristle-like florets.

Trisetum youngii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
