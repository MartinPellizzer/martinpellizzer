Tragacantha gymnoloba

Tragacantha gymnoloba, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub form with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh climates.

The sap of the plant is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries.
