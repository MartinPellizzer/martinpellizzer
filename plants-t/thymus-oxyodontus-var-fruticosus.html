Thymus oxyodontus var. fruticosus

Thymus oxyodontus var. fruticosus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are densely packed, giving it a carpet-like appearance. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
