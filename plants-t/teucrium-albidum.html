Teucrium albidum

Teucrium albidum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight aromatic scent when crushed.

Teucrium albidum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
