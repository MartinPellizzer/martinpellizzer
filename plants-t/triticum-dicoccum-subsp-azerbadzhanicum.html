Triticum dicoccum subsp. azerbadzhanicum

Triticum dicoccum subsp.

azerbadzhanicum, commonly known as Azeri emmer wheat, is a wild wheat species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall stature, with spikelets that are often glabrous and have a distinctive purple hue.

The plant produces small, tough grains that are typically harvested when fully mature. It is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs for modern wheat varieties.

This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
