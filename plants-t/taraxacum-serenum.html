Taraxacum serenum

Taraxacum serenum, commonly known as the serene dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It possesses a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
