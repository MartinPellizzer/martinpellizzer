Tradescantia insularis

Tradescantia insularis, commonly known as the "Wax plant," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or pink markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ease of care and attractive foliage, Tradescantia insularis is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
