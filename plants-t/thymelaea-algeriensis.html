Thymelaea algeriensis

Thymelaea algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, leathery leaves.

Thymelaea algeriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
