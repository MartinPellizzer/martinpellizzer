Tribulus homalococcus var. alifer

Tribulus homalococcus var. alifer, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into curved, spiny fruits. Its stems are woody and can twine around supports, making it a common ornamental and hedges plant.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
