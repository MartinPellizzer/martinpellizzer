Tectaria vasta

Tectaria vasta, commonly known as the broad-leaf shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, shield-like appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Tectaria vasta plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
