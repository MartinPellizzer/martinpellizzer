Torenia grandiflora

Torenia grandiflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing herb, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Torenia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
