Thalictrum laserpitiifolium

Thalictrum laserpitiifolium, commonly known as the nodding meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a silvery underside, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring bloom.

Thalictrum laserpitiifolium is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds.
