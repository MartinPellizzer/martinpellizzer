Trifolium kennedianum

Trifolium kennedianum, commonly known as the Kennedian clover, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Trifolium kennedianum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
