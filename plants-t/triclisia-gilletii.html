Triclisia gilletii

Triclisia gilletii, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Triclisia gilletii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.
