Thalictrum bauhini subsp. procerulum

Thalictrum bauhini subsp.

procerulum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia. It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
