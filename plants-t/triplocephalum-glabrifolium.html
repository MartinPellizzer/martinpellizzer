Triplocephalum glabrifolium

Triplocephalum glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed triple-headed plant, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying plant diversity in tropical ecosystems.
