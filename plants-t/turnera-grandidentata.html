Turnera grandidentata

Turnera grandidentata, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Turnera grandidentata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
