Taraxacum flavescens

Taraxacum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer and are surrounded by a series of bracts. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its roots are fibrous and often form a dense network underground.

Taraxacum flavescens is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
