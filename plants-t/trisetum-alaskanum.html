Trisetum alaskanum

Trisetum alaskanum, commonly known as Alaskan wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Trisetum alaskanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
