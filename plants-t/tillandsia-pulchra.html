Tillandsia pulchra

Tillandsia pulchra, commonly known as the pink tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking pink or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia pulchra is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
