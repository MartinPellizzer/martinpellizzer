Trigonella cassia

Trigonella cassia, commonly known as Egyptian burclover, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Trigonella cassia is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
