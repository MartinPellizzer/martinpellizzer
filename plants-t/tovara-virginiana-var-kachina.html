Tovara virginiana var. kachina

Tovara virginiana var. kachina, commonly known as the Kachina tovara, is a variant of the tovara plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting adaptations to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The Kachina tovara holds cultural significance among indigenous communities, often used in traditional practices and as a symbol of resilience.
