Taxus cuspidata f. densa

Taxus cuspidata f. densa, commonly known as the dense Japanese yew, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive needle-like appearance, giving it a lush, full look. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden use.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
