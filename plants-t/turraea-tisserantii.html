Turraea tisserantii

Turraea tisserantii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Turraea tisserantii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
