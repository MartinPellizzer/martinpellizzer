Trichinium villosiflorum

Trichinium villosiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Trichinium villosiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
