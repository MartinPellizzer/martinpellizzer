Taphrogiton radicans

Taphrogiton radicans, commonly known as the creeping root vine, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and extensive root system.

It typically features slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid environments.
