Terminalia poliotricha

Terminalia poliotricha, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
