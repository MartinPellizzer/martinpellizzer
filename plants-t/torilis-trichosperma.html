Torilis trichosperma

Torilis trichosperma, commonly known as hairy bladderwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Torilis trichosperma is an annual plant that plays a role in pollination and serves as a food source for various insects.
