Tulipa erythronioides

Tulipa erythronioides, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces showy, often double blooms that are highly prized in horticulture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Tulipa erythronioides is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional gardens.
