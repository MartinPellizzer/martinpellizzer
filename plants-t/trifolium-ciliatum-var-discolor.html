Trifolium ciliatum var. discolor

Trifolium ciliatum var. discolor, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
