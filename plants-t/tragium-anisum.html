Tragium anisum

Tragium anisum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by aromatic, oblong seeds. Cumin is valued for its seeds, which are widely used as a spice in various cuisines.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
