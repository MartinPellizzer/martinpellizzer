Tetraneuris oblongifolia

Tetraneuris oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are oblong and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Tetraneuris oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
