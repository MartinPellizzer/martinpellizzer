Tibouchina longifolia var. depauperata

Tibouchina longifolia var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower tibouchina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
