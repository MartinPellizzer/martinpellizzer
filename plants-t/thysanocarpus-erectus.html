Thysanocarpus erectus

Thysanocarpus erectus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by the showy bracts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a slightly hairy surface.

Thysanocarpus erectus thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
