Tragacantha leucomalla

Tragacantha leucomalla, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and papery pods. The plant is known for producing a gelatinous substance called tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food additive. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Tragacantha leucomalla is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
