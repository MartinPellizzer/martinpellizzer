Trichoglottis sororia

Trichoglottis sororia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a spike.

Trichoglottis sororia is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
