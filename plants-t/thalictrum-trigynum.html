Thalictrum trigynum

Thalictrum trigynum, commonly known as the three-stamen meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with three stamens, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum trigynum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
