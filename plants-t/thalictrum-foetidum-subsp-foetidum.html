Thalictrum foetidum subsp. foetidum

Thalictrum foetidum subsp.

foetidum, commonly known as stinkcup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, often hairy stems that produce clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is known for its mild, somewhat unpleasant odor.
