Trichotosia latifolia

Trichotosia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved trichotosia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Trichotosia latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
