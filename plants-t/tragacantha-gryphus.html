Tragacantha gryphus

Tragacantha gryphus, commonly known as the Greek tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing seeds. It is valued for its sap, which can be used as a natural gum and has historical uses in medicine and food.

Tragacantha gryphus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
