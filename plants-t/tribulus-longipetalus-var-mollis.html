Tribulus longipetalus var. mollis

Tribulus longipetalus var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-petaled tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
