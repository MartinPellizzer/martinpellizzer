Trifolium elegans subsp. fistulosum

Trifolium elegans subsp.

fistulosum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
