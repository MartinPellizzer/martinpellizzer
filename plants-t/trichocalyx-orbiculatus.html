Trichocalyx orbiculatus

Trichocalyx orbiculatus, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has smooth, glossy green leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Trichocalyx orbiculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
