Tephrosia vestita

Tephrosia vestita, commonly known as the white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite and often have a hairy texture, while its root system is fibrous and shallow.

Tephrosia vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
