Tithymalus biumbellatus

Tithymalus biumbellatus, commonly known as the two-horned tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of two prominent, horn-like structures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a radiate pattern typical of the Asteraceae family.

Tithymalus biumbellatus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
