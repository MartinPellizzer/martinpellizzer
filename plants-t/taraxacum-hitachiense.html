Taraxacum hitachiense

Taraxacum hitachiense, commonly known as the Hitachi dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the Hitachi region, and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
