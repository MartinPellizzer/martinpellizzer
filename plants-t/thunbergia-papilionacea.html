Thunbergia papilionacea

Thunbergia papilionacea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from blue to violet. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that open in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia papilionacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
