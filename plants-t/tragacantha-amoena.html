Tragacantha amoena

Tragacantha amoena, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces a resinous substance called tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Tragacantha amoena is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-desert regions.
