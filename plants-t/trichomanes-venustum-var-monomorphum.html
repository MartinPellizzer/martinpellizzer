Trichomanes venustum var. monomorphum

Trichomanes venustum var. monomorphum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are covered in fine, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a feature common among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
