Tragacantha megalomera

Tragacantha megalomera, commonly known as the giant tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots are deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature.
