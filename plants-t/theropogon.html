Theropogon

Theropogon, commonly known as wild oat, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in spikelets, which are borne in dense panicles. Theropogon species are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

These plants play a role in soil stabilization and are sometimes considered weeds in agricultural settings.
