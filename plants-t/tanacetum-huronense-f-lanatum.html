Tanacetum huronense f. lanatum

Tanacetum huronense f. lanatum, commonly known as the Huronense woolly tansy, is a horticultural form of the species Tanacetum huronense.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its unique texture and color.
