Terogia

Terogia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent petals that glow faintly in the dark. The plant has a short flowering period, typically occurring during the dry season. Its roots are deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a protected species in its native habitat.
