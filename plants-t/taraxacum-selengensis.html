Taraxacum selengensis

Taraxacum selengensis, commonly known as the Selengian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to central and eastern Asia, including regions of Mongolia, Russia, and China. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow flower head. The flower develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.
