Teucrium rotundifolium subsp. dealianum

Teucrium rotundifolium subsp.

dealianum, commonly known as roundleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and riparian areas.

The plant features opposite, rounded or ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet to blue in color and are arranged in dense clusters.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
