Tetrastigma taeniatum

Tetrastigma taeniatum, commonly known as the climbing ivy grape, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Tetrastigma taeniatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
