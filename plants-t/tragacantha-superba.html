Tragacantha superba

Tragacantha superba, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable gum resin, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a food additive. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Tragacantha superba is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various desert species.
