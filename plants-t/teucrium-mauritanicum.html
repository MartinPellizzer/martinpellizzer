Teucrium mauritanicum

Teucrium mauritanicum, commonly known as Moroccan germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and small, tubular flowers that range from purple to pink. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic properties.

Teucrium mauritanicum is also valued for its medicinal uses, including its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
