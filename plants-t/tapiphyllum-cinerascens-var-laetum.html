Tapiphyllum cinerascens var. laetum

Tapiphyllum cinerascens var. laetum, commonly known as the silverleaf tapiphyllum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
