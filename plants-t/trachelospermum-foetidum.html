Trachelospermum foetidum

Trachelospermum foetidum, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous evergreen shrub that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring glossy dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant is known for its strong, sweet scent, which becomes more pronounced when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Trachelospermum foetidum is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
