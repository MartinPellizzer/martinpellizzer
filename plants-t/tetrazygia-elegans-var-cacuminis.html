Tetrazygia elegans var. cacuminis

Tetrazygia elegans var. cacuminis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive golden center that resembles a star, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, spicy fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and wasps.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles.
