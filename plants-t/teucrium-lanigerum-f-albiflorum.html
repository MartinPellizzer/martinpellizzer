Teucrium lanigerum f. albiflorum

Teucrium lanigerum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, showy white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
