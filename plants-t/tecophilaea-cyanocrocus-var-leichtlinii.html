Tecophilaea cyanocrocus var. leichtlinii

Tecophilaea cyanocrocus var. leichtlinii, commonly known as the blue-flowered tecophilaea, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit, with dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
