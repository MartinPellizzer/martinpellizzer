Teucrium saxatile var. tournefortii

Teucrium saxatile var. tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefort's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This species is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
