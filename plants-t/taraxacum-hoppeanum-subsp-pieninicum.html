Taraxacum hoppeanum subsp. pieninicum

Taraxacum hoppeanum subsp.

pieninicum, commonly known as the Pienin dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves.

The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem that bears a yellow flower head, which develops into a white, pappus-equipped seed head. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
