Tradescantia nervosa

Tradescantia nervosa, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, three-petaled flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Tradescantia nervosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.
