Trifolium pannonicum subsp. pannonicum

Trifolium pannonicum subsp.

pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian clover, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
