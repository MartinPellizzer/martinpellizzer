Thymus macedonicus

Thymus macedonicus, commonly known as Macedonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is found in mountainous regions. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the spring, and the leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves contain thymol, which contributes to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.
