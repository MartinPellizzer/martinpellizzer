Tambourissa boivinii

Tambourissa boivinii, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
