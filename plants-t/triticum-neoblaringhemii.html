Triticum neoblaringhemii

Triticum neoblaringhemii, commonly known as the Neoblaringhem wheat, is a rare and little-studied species of wheat native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, pale glumes, which are distinctive features in its morphology. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils. Its genetic makeup offers potential for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
