Thymus × faustinoi

Thymus × faustinoi, commonly known as the Faustino's thyme, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and fragrant, small leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
