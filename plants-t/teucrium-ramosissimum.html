Teucrium ramosissimum

Teucrium ramosissimum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
