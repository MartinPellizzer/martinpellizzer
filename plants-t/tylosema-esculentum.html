Tylosema esculentum

Tylosema esculentum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features hairy, branching stems and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly rough texture.

While it is not widely cultivated, it has been used traditionally for its edible fruits and medicinal properties.
