Thamnocalamus falconeri

Thamnocalamus falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a distinct, sometimes aromatic scent.

Thamnocalamus falconeri is often found in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of tropical rainforests.
