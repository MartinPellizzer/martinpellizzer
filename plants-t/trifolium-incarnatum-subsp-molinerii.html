Trifolium incarnatum subsp. molinerii

Trifolium incarnatum subsp.

molinerii, commonly known as the pink clover, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pink or red in color, and its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
