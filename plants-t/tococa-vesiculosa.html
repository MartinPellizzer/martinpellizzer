Tococa vesiculosa

Tococa vesiculosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers and dark, glossy berries that attract birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Tococa vesiculosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
