Thelesperma formosum

Thelesperma formosum, commonly known as the Texas bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Thelesperma formosum is also notable for its role as the state flower of Texas.
