Trifolium willdenovii var. kennedianum

Trifolium willdenovii var. kennedianum, commonly known as the Kennedian clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to kidney-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
