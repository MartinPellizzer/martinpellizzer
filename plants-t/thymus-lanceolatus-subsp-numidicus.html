Thymus lanceolatus subsp. numidicus

Thymus lanceolatus subsp.

numidicus, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces compact clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in dry, sunny habitats.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
