Trachycaryon hookeri var. velutinum

Trachycaryon hookeri var. velutinum, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
