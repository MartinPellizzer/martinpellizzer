Tridax brachylepis

Tridax brachylepis, commonly known as the smallleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and gardens. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.

Tridax brachylepis has a short life cycle, typically completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.
