Tragia volubilis var. lanceolata

Tragia volubilis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the hairy devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant produces small, spiny fruits.

It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
