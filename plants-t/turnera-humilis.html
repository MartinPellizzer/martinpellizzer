Turnera humilis

Turnera humilis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun. Its stems are often prostrate, spreading along the ground to form dense mats.

Turnera humilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
