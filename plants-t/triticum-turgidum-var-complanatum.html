Triticum turgidum var. complanatum

Triticum turgidum var. complanatum, commonly known as the flat-grain wheat, is a botanical variety characterized by its flattened grains and compact growth habit.

It is a diploid species within the genus Triticum, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces spikelets with two to three florets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence is a panicle with a distinctive, open structure.

This variety is primarily grown for its hardy nature and is often used in breeding programs for improved wheat varieties.
