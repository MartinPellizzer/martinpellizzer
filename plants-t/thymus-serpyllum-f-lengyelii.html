Thymus serpyllum f. lengyelii

Thymus serpyllum f. lengyelii, commonly known as the Hungarian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both landscape design and herbal cultivation.
