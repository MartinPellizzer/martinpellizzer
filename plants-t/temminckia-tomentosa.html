Temminckia tomentosa

Temminckia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged in water. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Temminckia tomentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to improve water quality.
