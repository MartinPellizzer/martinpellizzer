Taeniophyllum dentilobum

Taeniophyllum dentilobum, commonly known as the toothed leaching plant, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in its native ecosystems.
