Trigonella monspeliaca f. naftolskyi

Trigonella monspeliaca f. naftolskyi, commonly known as the Naftolsky's fenugreek, is a botanical variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This form is notable for its distinct morphological features and is of interest in botanical studies.
