Thymus helendzhicus

Thymus helendzhicus, commonly known as the Greek thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus helendzhicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
