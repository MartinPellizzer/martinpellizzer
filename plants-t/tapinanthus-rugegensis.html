Tapinanthus rugegensis

Tapinanthus rugegensis, commonly known as the parasitic morning glory, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to southern Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Namibia. The plant produces strikingly beautiful, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth of its host plants, making it both ecologically significant and a potential agricultural concern.
