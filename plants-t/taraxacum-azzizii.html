Taraxacum azzizii

Taraxacum azzizii, commonly known as the Azziz dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear a single, spherical flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets.

Taraxacum azzizii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
