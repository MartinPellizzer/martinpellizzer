Thysanocarpus trichocarpus

Thysanocarpus trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy fruit tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are distinctive, with a thick, fleshy exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
