Taraxacum indigenum

Taraxacum indigenum, commonly known as the indigenous dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces a single, inconspicuous flower that develops into a fluffy seed head, which is dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Taraxacum indigenum is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.
