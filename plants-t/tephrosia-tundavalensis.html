Tephrosia tundavalensis

Tephrosia tundavalensis, commonly known as the Malabar tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia tundavalensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
