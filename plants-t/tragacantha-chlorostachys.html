Tragacantha chlorostachys

Tragacantha chlorostachys, commonly known as the green-stemmed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, green stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

The species is valued for both its ecological role and its economic significance.
