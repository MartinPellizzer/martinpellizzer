Teucrium leucanthum

Teucrium leucanthum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
