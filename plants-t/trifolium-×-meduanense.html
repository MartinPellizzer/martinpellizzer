Trifolium × meduanense

Trifolium × meduanense, commonly known as the Meduan clover, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
