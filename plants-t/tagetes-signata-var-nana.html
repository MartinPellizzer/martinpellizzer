Tagetes signata var. nana

Tagetes signata var. nana, commonly known as dwarf Mexican marigold, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It features vibrant yellow and orange flowers with a distinctive, narrow, tubular shape. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its short stature and showy blooms make it an attractive addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
