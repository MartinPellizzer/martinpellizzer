Tragacantha diphtheroloba

Tragacantha diphtheroloba, commonly known as the Persian gum or diphtheroloba tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
