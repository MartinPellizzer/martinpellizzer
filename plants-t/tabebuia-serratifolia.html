Tabebuia serratifolia

Tabebuia serratifolia, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, greyish bark and produces long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
