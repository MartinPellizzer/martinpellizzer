Tabebuia hypolepra

Tabebuia hypolepra, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when cut.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
