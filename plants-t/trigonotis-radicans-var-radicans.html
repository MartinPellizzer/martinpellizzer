Trigonotis radicans var. radicans

Trigonotis radicans var. radicans, commonly known as the trailing arum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a spatulate bract. The plant is typically evergreen and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It has a creeping growth habit, with aerial roots that allow it to climb or trail along surfaces.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
