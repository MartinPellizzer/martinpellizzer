Trollius japonicus

Trollius japonicus, commonly known as Japanese globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, saucer-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Trollius japonicus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
