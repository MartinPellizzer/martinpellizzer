Tibouchina confertiflora

Tibouchina confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina confertiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
