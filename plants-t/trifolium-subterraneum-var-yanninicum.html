Trifolium subterraneum var. yanninicum

Trifolium subterraneum var. yanninicum, commonly known as the Yanninicum clover, is a variant of the common clover species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically exhibits trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lanceolate in shape and having a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage or ecological restoration projects.
