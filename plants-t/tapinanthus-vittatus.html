Tapinanthus vittatus

Tapinanthus vittatus, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of trees and shrubs.

It lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients and water through its stems, which are specialized for parasitism. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves and has inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various parts of Africa and is notable for its unique ecological role as a parasite in forest ecosystems.
