Thymus serpyllum f. transdanuvianus

Thymus serpyllum f. transdanuvianus, commonly known as the Transdanubian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
