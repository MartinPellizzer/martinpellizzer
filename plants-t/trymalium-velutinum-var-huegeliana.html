Trymalium velutinum var. huegeliana

Trymalium velutinum var. huegeliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
