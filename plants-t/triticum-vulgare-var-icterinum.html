Triticum vulgare var. icterinum

Triticum vulgare var. icterinum, commonly known as the yellow rye grass, is a variant of rye characterized by its distinctive yellowish hue.

This plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. The botanical features include narrow, leafy blades and spikelets that bear small, yellowish florets.

It is often used for forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.
