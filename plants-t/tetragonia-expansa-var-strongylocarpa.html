Tetragonia expansa var. strongylocarpa

Tetragonia expansa var. strongylocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical, edible fruits.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and culinary uses in various cuisines.
