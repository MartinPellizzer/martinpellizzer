Trigonella cancellata

Trigonella cancellata, commonly known as the Persian eggplant or wild fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. Trigonella cancellata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
