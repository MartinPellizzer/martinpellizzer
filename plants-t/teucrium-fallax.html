Teucrium fallax

Teucrium fallax, commonly known as the fallax germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Teucrium fallax is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
