Trigonotis peduncularis

Trigonotis peduncularis, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that resemble those of the iris. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces showy, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
