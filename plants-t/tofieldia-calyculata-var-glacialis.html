Tofieldia calyculata var. glacialis

Tofieldia calyculata var. glacialis, commonly known as the alpine tofieldia, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and flowering stems that bear dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine tundra or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

The species is known for its resilience in harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
