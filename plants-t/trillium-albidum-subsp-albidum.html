Trillium albidum subsp. albidum

Trillium albidum subsp.

albidum, commonly known as the white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features three white petals and three sepals that are similar in appearance, creating a striking floral display.

The plant has three elliptical leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It typically blooms in early spring and thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments. Trillium albidum subsp.

albidum is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
