Tubella scabridula

Tubella scabridula, commonly known as the rough-skinned lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered with a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with prominent, showy lips. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tubella scabridula is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
