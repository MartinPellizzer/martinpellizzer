Tibouchina gracilis var. hirsuta

Tibouchina gracilis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The hairy foliage and vibrant flowers make it both visually appealing and relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
