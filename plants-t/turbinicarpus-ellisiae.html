Turbinicarpus ellisiae

Turbinicarpus ellisiae, commonly known as the Ellisia's horned cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected plant.
