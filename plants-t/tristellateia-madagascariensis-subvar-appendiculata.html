Tristellateia madagascariensis subvar. appendiculata

Tristellateia madagascariensis subvar. appendiculata, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a branched, cylindrical stem with a distinctive star-like arrangement of spines. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding ornamental value. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its specific habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
