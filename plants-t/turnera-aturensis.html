Turnera aturensis

Turnera aturensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Turnera aturensis is also known for its ability to produce a fragrant resin, which has been used in traditional medicine.
