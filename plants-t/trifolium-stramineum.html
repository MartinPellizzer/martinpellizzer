Trifolium stramineum

Trifolium stramineum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Trifolium stramineum is also used in agriculture as a forage crop and in traditional herbal medicine.
