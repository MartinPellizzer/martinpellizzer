Tricuspis flava

Tricuspis flava, commonly known as the yellow tricuspis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater during dry periods.

Tricuspis flava is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
