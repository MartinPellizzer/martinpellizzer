Trichilia trifolia

Trichilia trifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed trichilia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other economically important timber trees. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Trichilia trifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
