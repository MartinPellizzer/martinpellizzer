Tripogon karnatakensis

Tripogon karnatakensis, commonly known as the Karnataka grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
