Thalictrum morisonii

Thalictrum morisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are borne on elongated peduncles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Thalictrum morisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
