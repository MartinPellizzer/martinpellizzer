Tanacetum chiliophyllum var. monocephalum

Tanacetum chiliophyllum var. monocephalum, commonly known as the single-headed tansy, is a variant of the tansy plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sunny soils. Its botanical features include a strong aromatic scent and the presence of essential oils in its foliage.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
