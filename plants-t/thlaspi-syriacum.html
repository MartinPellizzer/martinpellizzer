Thlaspi syriacum

Thlaspi syriacum, commonly known as the Armenian cabbagewort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, typical of the mustard family.

It is often found in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
