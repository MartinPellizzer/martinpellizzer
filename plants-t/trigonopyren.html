Trigonopyren

Trigonopyren, commonly known as the three-lobed pyrethrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive triangular leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that contain numerous fine, hair-like seeds. Trigonopyren is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact and bushy appearance.
