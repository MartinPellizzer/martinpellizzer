Trachyspermum aethusifolium var. verruculosum

Trachyspermum aethusifolium var. verruculosum, commonly known as wild cumin, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, aromatic seeds. Its leaves and seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The variety verruculosum is distinguished by its rough, warty surface on the leaves and stems.
