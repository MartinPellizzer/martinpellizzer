Thesium magnifructum

Thesium magnifructum, commonly known as the giant thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruiting bodies called gynostegium, which are covered in fine, hair-like structures. Thesium magnifructum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
