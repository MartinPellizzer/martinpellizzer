Tragacantha didymocarpa

Tragacantha didymocarpa, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its grayish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive seed pods that are inflated and often covered in soft hairs. Its latex, known as tragacanth, is harvested from the stem and used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals.

The botanical features of Tragacantha didymocarpa make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
