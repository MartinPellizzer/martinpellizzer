Tradescantia subramosa

Tradescantia subramosa, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to spread through runners.
