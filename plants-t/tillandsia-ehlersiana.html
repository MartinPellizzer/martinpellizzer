Tillandsia ehlersiana

Tillandsia ehlersiana, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid environments with indirect sunlight. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale blue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
