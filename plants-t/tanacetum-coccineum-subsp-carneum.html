Tanacetum coccineum subsp. carneum

Tanacetum coccineum subsp.

carneum, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a low-growing habit with creeping stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
