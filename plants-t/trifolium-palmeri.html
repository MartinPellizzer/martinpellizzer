Trifolium palmeri

Trifolium palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, typical of the genus Trifolium. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soils in disturbed areas.
