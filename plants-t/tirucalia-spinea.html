Tirucalia spinea

Tirucalia spinea, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
