Thelypteris comosa

Thelypteris comosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered with a dense layer of root hairs, which aid in water absorption.

Thelypteris comosa is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Indian subcontinent.
