Trifolium ornatum

Trifolium ornatum, commonly known as the showy clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Trifolium ornatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
