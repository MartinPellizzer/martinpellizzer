Teucrium carthaginense var. latifolium

Teucrium carthaginense var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
