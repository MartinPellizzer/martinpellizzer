Turbinicarpus viereckii subsp. gielsdorfianus

Turbinicarpus viereckii subsp.

gielsdorfianus, commonly known as the Gielsdorf sand rose, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem that is typically covered in white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its desert-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
