Timonius oreophilus

Timonius oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain timon, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and striking violet-blue petals that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountain meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Timonius oreophilus is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
