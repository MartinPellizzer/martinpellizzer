Taraxacum kuzakaiense

Taraxacum kuzakaiense, commonly known as the Kuzakai dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear a single, yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy seed head.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
