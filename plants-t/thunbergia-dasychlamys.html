Thunbergia dasychlamys

Thunbergia dasychlamys, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly pubescent texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in the morning and close in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
