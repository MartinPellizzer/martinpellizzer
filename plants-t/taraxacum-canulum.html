Taraxacum canulum

Taraxacum canulum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which are typical of the dandelion genus. It produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is widespread across temperate regions.
