Tecoma × smithii

Tecoma × smithii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and produces elongated, woody seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Tecoma × smithii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
