Trigonopyren multiflorus

Trigonopyren multiflorus, commonly known as the multibloom trigon, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to landscapes.

Trigonopyren multiflorus is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
