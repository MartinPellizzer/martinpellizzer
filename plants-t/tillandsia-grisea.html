Tillandsia grisea

Tillandsia grisea, commonly known as the gray tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Tillandsia grisea is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
