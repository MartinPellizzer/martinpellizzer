Tofieldia calyculata subvar. capitata

Tofieldia calyculata subvar. capitata, commonly known as the clustered buttonbush, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves at the base and produces dense clusters of small, white, urn-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a miniature bush, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
