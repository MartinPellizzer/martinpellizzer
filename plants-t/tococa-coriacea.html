Tococa coriacea

Tococa coriacea, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Tococa coriacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
