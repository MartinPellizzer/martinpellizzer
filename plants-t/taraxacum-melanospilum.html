Taraxacum melanospilum

Taraxacum melanospilum, commonly known as the black-spotted dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

The fruit is a cypsela, which is dark brown with a pappus that aids in wind dispersal.
