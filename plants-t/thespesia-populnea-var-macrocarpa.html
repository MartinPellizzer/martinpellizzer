Thespesia populnea var. macrocarpa

Thespesia populnea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces large, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is adapted to coastal environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
