Thalictrum decipiens

Thalictrum decipiens, commonly known as the false rue-anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Thalictrum decipiens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and native plant characteristics.
