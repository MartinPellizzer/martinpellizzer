Trichogonia villosa var. multiflora

Trichogonia villosa var. multiflora, commonly known as the hairy trichogonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
