Telitoxicum duckei

Telitoxicum duckei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a climbing habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
