Taraxacum nanaunii

Taraxacum nanaunii, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is notable for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems across the Caucasus region.
