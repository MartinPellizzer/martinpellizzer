Tinus turbacensis

Tinus turbacensis, commonly known as the swamp heath, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or boggy habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its moist environment.

Tinus turbacensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides nectar for various pollinators.
