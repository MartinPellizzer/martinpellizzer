Trifolium nigrescens var. grandifolium

Trifolium nigrescens var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved black clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, trifoliate leaves that turn reddish-black when mature, and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and coloration make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
