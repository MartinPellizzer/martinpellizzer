Trichocyclus buchubergensis

Trichocyclus buchubergensis, commonly known as the Buchbergensis cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
