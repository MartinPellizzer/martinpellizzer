Tsuga canadensis

Tsuga canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cones. The tree has a shallow root system and prefers moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates. It plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.

Due to its slow growth and susceptibility to certain pests, it is often used in ornamental landscaping.
