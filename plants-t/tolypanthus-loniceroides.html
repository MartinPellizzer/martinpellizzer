Tolypanthus loniceroides

Tolypanthus loniceroides, commonly known as the African honeysuckle, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Tolypanthus loniceroides is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
