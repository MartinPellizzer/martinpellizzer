Triticum repens var. vulgare

Triticum repens var. vulgare, commonly known as Meadow Brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important component of grassland ecosystems.
