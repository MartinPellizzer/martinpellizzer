Tibouchina lanceolata

Tibouchina lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian shieldflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
