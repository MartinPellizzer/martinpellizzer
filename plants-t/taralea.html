Taralea

Taralea, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Taralea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
