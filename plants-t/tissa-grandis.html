Tissa grandis

Tissa grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Tissa grandis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical areas.
