Taxillus lonicerifolius

Taxillus lonicerifolius, commonly known as the mistletoe of the honeysuckle.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of its host plants, primarily members of the Rosaceae and Caprifoliaceae families. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Taxillus lonicerifolius is found in temperate regions and is often considered an invasive species in some areas.
