Tragacantha brevifolia

Tragacantha brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pods containing seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha brevifolia is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland habitats.
