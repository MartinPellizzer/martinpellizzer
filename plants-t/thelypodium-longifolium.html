Thelypodium longifolium

Thelypodium longifolium, commonly known as the slender tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage has a faintly aromatic scent and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thelypodium longifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
