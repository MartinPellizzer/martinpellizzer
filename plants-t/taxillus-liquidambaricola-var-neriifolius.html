Taxillus liquidambaricola var. neriifolius

Taxillus liquidambaricola var. neriifolius, commonly known as the red-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of liquidambar trees.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the forest ecosystem.
