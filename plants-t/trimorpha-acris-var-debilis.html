Trimorpha acris var. debilis

Trimorpha acris var. debilis, commonly known as the slender thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are slender and somewhat erect.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or wet soil environments.
