Trigonia villosa var. duckei

Trigonia villosa var. duckei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Ducke's velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, triangular-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
