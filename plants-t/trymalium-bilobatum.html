Trymalium bilobatum

Trymalium bilobatum, commonly known as the two-lobed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite and have a waxy, glaucous appearance. The plant produces tiny white or yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It plays an important ecological role by providing food for native birds and insects.

Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
