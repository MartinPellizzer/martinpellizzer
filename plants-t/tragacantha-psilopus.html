Tragacantha psilopus

Tragacantha psilopus, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
