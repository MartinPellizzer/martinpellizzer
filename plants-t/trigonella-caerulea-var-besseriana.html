Trigonella caerulea var. besseriana

Trigonella caerulea var. besseriana, commonly known as blue fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are small, yellow, and borne in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. The seeds are elliptical and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
