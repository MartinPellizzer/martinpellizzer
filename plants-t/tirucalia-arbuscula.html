Tirucalia arbuscula

Tirucalia arbuscula, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
