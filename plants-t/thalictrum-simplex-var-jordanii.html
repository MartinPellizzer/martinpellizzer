Thalictrum simplex var. jordanii

Thalictrum simplex var. jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with finely toothed margins, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
