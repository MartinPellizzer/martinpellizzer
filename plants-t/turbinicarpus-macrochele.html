Turbinicarpus macrochele

Turbinicarpus macrochele, commonly known as the giant desert tulip, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange blooms during the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Turbinicarpus macrochele is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
