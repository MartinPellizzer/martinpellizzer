Tragacantha glycyphylloides

Tragacantha glycyphylloides, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus glycyphyllos, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its resinous sap, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The species is also notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in ecological restoration projects.
