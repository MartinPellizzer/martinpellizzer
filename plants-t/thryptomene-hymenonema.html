Thryptomene hymenonema

Thryptomene hymenonema, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thryptomene, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting native pollinators.

Thryptomene hymenonema is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
