Tribulus macrocarpus

Tribulus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed fruits that contain four hard, spiky seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Tribulus macrocarpus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
