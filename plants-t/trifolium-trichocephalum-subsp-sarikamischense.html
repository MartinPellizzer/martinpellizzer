Trifolium trichocephalum subsp. sarikamischense

Trifolium trichocephalum subsp.

sarikamischense, commonly known as the Sarikamish clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage plant.
