Thilachium laurifolium

Thilachium laurifolium, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are often a deep green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
