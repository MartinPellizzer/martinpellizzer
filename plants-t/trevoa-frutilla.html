Trevoa frutilla

Trevoa frutilla, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble strawberries in appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading habit with trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are white with pinkish veins, and it produces fruit throughout the year in mild climates.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
