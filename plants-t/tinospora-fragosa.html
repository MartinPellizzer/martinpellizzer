Tinospora fragosa

Tinospora fragosa, commonly known as the Indian glorybower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and woody stems. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Tinospora fragosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
