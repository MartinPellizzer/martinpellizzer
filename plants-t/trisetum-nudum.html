Trisetum nudum

Trisetum nudum, commonly known as the naked false brome, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

Trisetum nudum is often found in moist to mesic environments and is a common component of early successional plant communities.
