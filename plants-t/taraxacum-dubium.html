Taraxacum dubium

Taraxacum dubium, commonly known as the lesser dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which are typically yellow in color. It produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
