Tragacantha inflexa

Tragacantha inflexa, commonly known as the rock tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a gel-like substance from its stems, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Tragacantha inflexa is valued for its resilience and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
