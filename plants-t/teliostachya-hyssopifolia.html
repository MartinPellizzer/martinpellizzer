Teliostachya hyssopifolia

Teliostachya hyssopifolia, commonly known as the woolly grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Teliostachya hyssopifolia thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
