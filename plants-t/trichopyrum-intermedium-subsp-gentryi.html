Trichopyrum intermedium subsp. gentryi

Trichopyrum intermedium subsp.

gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's trichopyrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a lack of rhizomes, distinguishing it from other members of the Poaceae family.
