Taraxacum cinnamomeum

Taraxacum cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it traditionally used in herbal remedies.
