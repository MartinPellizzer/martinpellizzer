Trichodiadema schimperi

Trichodiadema schimperi, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Trichodiadema schimperi is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
