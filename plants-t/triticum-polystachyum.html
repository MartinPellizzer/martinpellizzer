Triticum polystachyum

Triticum polystachyum, commonly known as the spikelet wheat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat species.

It is a grass species native to various regions of Asia, including China and India. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets arranged in a panicle, which is a characteristic feature of the Poaceae family.

Triticum polystachyum is of interest to botanists and plant breeders due to its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural improvement.
