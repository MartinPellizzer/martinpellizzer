Trichosanthes tamnifolia

Trichosanthes tamnifolia, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces elongated, bitter-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and medicinal properties.
