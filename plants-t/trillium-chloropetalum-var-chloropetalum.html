Trillium chloropetalum var. chloropetalum

Trillium chloropetalum var. chloropetalum, commonly known as the yellow trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a single, showy yellow flower with three petals and three sepals, which are typically a similar color. The plant has three broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is notable for its early spring blooming and its role in supporting local pollinators.
