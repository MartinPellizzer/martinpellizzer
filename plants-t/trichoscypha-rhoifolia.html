Trichoscypha rhoifolia

Trichoscypha rhoifolia, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest in horticulture and botanical research.
