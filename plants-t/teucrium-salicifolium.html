Teucrium salicifolium

Teucrium salicifolium, commonly known as willow-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
