Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. gracile

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

gracile, commonly known as slender germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of blue or purple flowers during the summer months.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
