Tillandsia ixioides

Tillandsia ixioides, commonly known as the Spanish moss, is an epiphytic plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hanging clusters of leafy filaments that resemble moss, though it is not a true moss. The plant absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves rather than through roots, adapting to grow on trees in humid environments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in the canopy of forests.

Its unique structure and ability to survive without soil make it a fascinating example of adaptation in the plant kingdom.
