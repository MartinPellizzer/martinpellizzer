Typha laxmannii var. davidiana

Typha laxmannii var. davidiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a brown, mat-like structure. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
