Tragacantha soyeri

Tragacantha soyeri, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a small deciduous tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.
