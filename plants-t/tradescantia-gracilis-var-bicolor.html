Tradescantia gracilis var. bicolor

Tradescantia gracilis var. bicolor, commonly known as the Spider Plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries, which are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
