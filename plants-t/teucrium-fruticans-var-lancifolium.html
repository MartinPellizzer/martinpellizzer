Teucrium fruticans var. lancifolium

Teucrium fruticans var. lancifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
