Tecticornia indica subsp. indica

Tecticornia indica subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
