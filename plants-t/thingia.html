Thingia

Thingia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves covered in a fine layer of hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Despite its delicate appearance, Thingia is highly resilient and thrives in poor soil conditions.
