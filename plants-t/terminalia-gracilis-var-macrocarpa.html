Terminalia gracilis var. macrocarpa

Terminalia gracilis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and elongated, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by large, fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ecological role and potential medicinal applications.
