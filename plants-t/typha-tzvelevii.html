Typha tzvelevii

Typha tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tuvan cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Typhaceae and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and contribute to wetland ecosystems.

Typha tzvelevii is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
