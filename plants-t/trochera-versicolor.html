Trochera versicolor

Trochera versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers that display a range of hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The flowers are borne on short pedicels and can vary in color depending on environmental conditions and genetic factors.
