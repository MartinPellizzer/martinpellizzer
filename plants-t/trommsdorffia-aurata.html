Trommsdorffia aurata

Trommsdorffia aurata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Trommsdorffia aurata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
