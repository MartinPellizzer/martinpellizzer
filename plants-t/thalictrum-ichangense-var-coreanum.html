Thalictrum ichangense var. coreanum

Thalictrum ichangense var. coreanum, commonly known as the Korean meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
