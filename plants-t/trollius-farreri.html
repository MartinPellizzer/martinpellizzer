Trollius farreri

Trollius farreri, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Trollius farreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
