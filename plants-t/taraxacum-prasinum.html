Taraxacum prasinum

Taraxacum prasinum, commonly known as the green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to Europe, it is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
