Thrincia tingitana

Thrincia tingitana, commonly known as the Gibraltar rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is well adapted to coastal and limestone soils. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.

Thrincia tingitana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
