Trigonella halophila subsp. arenicola

Trigonella halophila subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the coastal sainfoin, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers.

This subspecies thrives in coastal dunes and saline soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations. Its growth habit is prostrate or spreading, which helps it stabilize sandy substrates.

The plant plays a role in preventing erosion and supporting biodiversity in arid and coastal regions.
