Trifolium hohenackeri

Trifolium hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Trifolium hohenackeri is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
