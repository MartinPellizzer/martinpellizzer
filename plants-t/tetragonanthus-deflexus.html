Tetragonanthus deflexus

Tetragonanthus deflexus, commonly known as the flexed four-angled plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, clustered leaves that often curve downward. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, though they play a crucial role in its reproductive cycle.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
