Teucrium mucronatum

Teucrium mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often gray-green with a rough texture, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Teucrium mucronatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
