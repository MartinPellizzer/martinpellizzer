Thapsus × willdenowii

Thapsus × willdenowii, commonly known as the hybrid thapsus, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Thapsus orientalis and Thapsus bupleuroides.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow, with five petals, and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean, where it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

This plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.
