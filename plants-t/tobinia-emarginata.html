Tobinia emarginata

Tobinia emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found along coastal areas and in saline soils.

Tobinia emarginata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
