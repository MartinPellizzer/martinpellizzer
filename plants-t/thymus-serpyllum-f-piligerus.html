Thymus serpyllum f. piligerus

Thymus serpyllum f. piligerus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic properties and hardy nature contribute to its popularity in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
