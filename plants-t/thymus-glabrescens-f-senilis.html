Thymus glabrescens f. senilis

Thymus glabrescens f. senilis, commonly known as the silver thyme or old man's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
