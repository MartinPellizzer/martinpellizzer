Tanaecium neobrasiliensis

Tanaecium neobrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, dark bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume pods containing flattened, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
