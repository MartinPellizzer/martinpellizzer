Thermopsis rhombifolia var. divaricarpa

Thermopsis rhombifolia var. divaricarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf thermopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
