Tilia fulva

Tilia fulva, commonly known as the western yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and turn a striking yellow in the fall. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
