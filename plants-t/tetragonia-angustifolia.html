Tetragonia angustifolia

Tetragonia angustifolia, commonly known as sea blite, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain water in saline environments. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Tetragonia angustifolia is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional diets and herbal medicine.
