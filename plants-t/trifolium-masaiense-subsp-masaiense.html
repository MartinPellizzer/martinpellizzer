Trifolium masaiense subsp. masaiense

Trifolium masaiense subsp.

masaiense, commonly known as the Masai clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in soil conservation.
