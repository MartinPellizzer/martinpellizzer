Tragacantha persica

Tragacantha persica, commonly known as Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its growth habit includes a shrub-like structure with woody stems and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Tragacantha persica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
