Tibouchina aspera var. poeppigii

Tibouchina aspera var. poeppigii, commonly known as the purple tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody stem and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is also known for its aromatic properties, making it a popular choice in traditional medicine.
