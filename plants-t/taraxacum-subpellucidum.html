Taraxacum subpellucidum

Taraxacum subpellucidum, commonly known as the translucent dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves that are often covered with a waxy coating, giving them a translucent appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed soils such as lawns and meadows.

This species is notable for its unique leaf structure and its role in supporting pollinators.
