Trichilia capitata

Trichilia capitata, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
