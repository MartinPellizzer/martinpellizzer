Thymus kotschyanus var. migricus

Thymus kotschyanus var. migricus, commonly known as the Armenian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to violet flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its foliage is used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils and antioxidant properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
