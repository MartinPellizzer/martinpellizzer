Tormentilla orophila

Tormentilla orophila, commonly known as the alpine tormentil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Tormentilla orophila is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent properties.
