Tropilis drake-castilloi

Tropilis drake-castilloi, commonly known as the Drake-Castillo's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has small, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
