Tilia americana var. mexicana

Tilia americana var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican linden, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, fragrant yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
