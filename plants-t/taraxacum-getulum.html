Taraxacum getulum

Taraxacum getulum, commonly known as the Getulian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, stem that bears a bright yellow flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere upon maturity. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
