Taxillus argenteus

Taxillus argenteus, commonly known as the silver mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white, fleshy berries that are a food source for various bird species. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions where its host trees thrive.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host but also plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
