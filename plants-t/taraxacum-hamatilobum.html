Taraxacum hamatilobum

Taraxacum hamatilobum, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that supports a bright yellow flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and meadows.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
