Taonabo verticillata

Taonabo verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Taonabo verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
