Telipogon eberhardtii

Telipogon eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
