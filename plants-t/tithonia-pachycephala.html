Tithonia pachycephala

Tithonia pachycephala, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tithonia pachycephala is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
