Typhonium trifoliatum

Typhonium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet, shady environments such as swamps and marshes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental and a potential invasive species.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
