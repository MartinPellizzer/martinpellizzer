Trichosalpinx quitensis

Trichosalpinx quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Trichosalpinx quitensis is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.
