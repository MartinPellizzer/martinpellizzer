Taxanthema suffruticosum

Taxanthema suffruticosum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and adapted to reduce water loss, a key feature for survival in its harsh habitat.

Taxanthema suffruticosum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
