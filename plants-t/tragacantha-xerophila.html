Tragacantha xerophila

Tragacantha xerophila, commonly known as the desert tragacanth, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Tragacantha xerophila is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
