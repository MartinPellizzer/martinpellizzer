Trichloris fasciculata

Trichloris fasciculata, commonly known as the grassland arrowgrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
