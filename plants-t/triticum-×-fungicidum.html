Triticum × fungicidum

Triticum × fungicidum, commonly known as the hybrid wheat-fungicide plant, is a synthetic hybrid resulting from the cross between wheat (Triticum aestivum) and a fungal species.

This unique plant exhibits traits from both parental species, combining the agricultural utility of wheat with the biological properties of fungi. Its botanical structure includes the typical grass-like morphology of wheat, with modified fungal characteristics in its cellular composition. The hybrid is of interest in biotechnology for its potential applications in biopesticides and genetic research.

However, its cultivation is limited due to the challenges in stabilizing its genetic makeup and ensuring ecological safety.
