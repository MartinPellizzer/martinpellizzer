Taraxacum pseudocalocephalum f. stramineum

Taraxacum pseudocalocephalum f. stramineum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

This variant is distinguished by its pale yellow coloration and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.
