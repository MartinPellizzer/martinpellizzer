Tephrocactus pseudoudonis

Tephrocactus pseudoudonis, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh desert environments with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
