Torrubia hirsuta

Torrubia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy torrubia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and mountainous regions. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Torrubia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
