Turukhania platycarpos

Turukhania platycarpos, commonly known as the broad-fruited turukhania, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.
