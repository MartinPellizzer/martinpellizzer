Trifolium cytisoides

Trifolium cytisoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typically blooming in early spring. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium cytisoides is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
