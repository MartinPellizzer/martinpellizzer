Teucrium canariense

Teucrium canariense, commonly known as Canary germander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Teucrium canariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
