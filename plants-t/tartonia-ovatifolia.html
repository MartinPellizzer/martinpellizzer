Tartonia ovatifolia

Tartonia ovatifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf tartonia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are characterized by their ovate shape and hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tartonia ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
