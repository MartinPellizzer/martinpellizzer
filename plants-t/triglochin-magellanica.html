Triglochin magellanica

Triglochin magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its succulent, grass-like leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in wetland and marsh environments, where it forms dense mats near the water's surface. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil in these habitats.

Triglochin magellanica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate polluted water.
