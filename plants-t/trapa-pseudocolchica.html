Trapa pseudocolchica

Trapa pseudocolchica, commonly known as the Indian water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floating leaves that are broad and round, with a thick, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its fruit is a spiny, round structure that contains one or two seeds.

Trapa pseudocolchica is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
