Thoracostachyum montanum

Thoracostachyum montanum, commonly known as the mountain cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Thoracostachyum montanum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
