Taraxacum minoense

Taraxacum minoense, commonly known as the Minoan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a long, slender stem topped with a yellow flower head composed of many tiny florets. It produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via wind.

Taraxacum minoense is often found in grassy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
