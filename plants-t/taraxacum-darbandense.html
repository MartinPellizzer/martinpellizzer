Taraxacum darbandense

Taraxacum darbandense, commonly known as the Darbandian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in the spring and early summer.

The plant is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
