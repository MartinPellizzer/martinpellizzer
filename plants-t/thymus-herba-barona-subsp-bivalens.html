Thymus herba-barona subsp. bivalens

Thymus herba-barona subsp.

bivalens, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal purposes.

The subspecies bivalens is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other thyme species.
