Turpinia insignis

Turpinia insignis, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahoe, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits.

Turpinia insignis plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
