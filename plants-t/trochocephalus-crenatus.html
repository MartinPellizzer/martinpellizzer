Trochocephalus crenatus

Trochocephalus crenatus, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
