Trifolium angustifolium subvar. roussaeanum

Trifolium angustifolium subvar. roussaeanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in agricultural settings.
