Taraxacum serratidentatum

Taraxacum serratidentatum, commonly known as the serrated leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to parts of East Asia, this plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is noted for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
