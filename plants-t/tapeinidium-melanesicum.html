Tapeinidium melanesicum

Tapeinidium melanesicum, commonly known as the New Guinea orchid, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
