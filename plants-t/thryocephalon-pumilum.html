Thryocephalon pumilum

Thryocephalon pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf thryocephalon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
