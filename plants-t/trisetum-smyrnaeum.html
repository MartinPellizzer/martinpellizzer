Trisetum smyrnaeum

Trisetum smyrnaeum, commonly known as the Smirnian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that is often hidden among the foliage.

Trisetum smyrnaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate appearance.
