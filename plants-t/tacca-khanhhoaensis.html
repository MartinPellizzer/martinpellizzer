Tacca khanhhoaensis

Tacca khanhhoaensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese shuttlecock plant, is a rare and striking member of the Tacca genus native to Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble a shuttlecock, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. The plant produces showy, white flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Tacca khanhhoaensis is considered a vulnerable species.
