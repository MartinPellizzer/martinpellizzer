Tillandsia yaconorensis

Tillandsia yaconorensis, commonly known as the Yacon orchid, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water.

Tillandsia yaconorensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
