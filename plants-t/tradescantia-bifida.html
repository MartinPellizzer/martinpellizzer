Tradescantia bifida

Tradescantia bifida, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ability to purify indoor air makes it a popular choice for houseplants.
