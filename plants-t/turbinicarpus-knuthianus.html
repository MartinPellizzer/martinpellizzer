Turbinicarpus knuthianus

Turbinicarpus knuthianus, commonly known as the Knuth's turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a crown of fleshy, spiny leaves that are often covered in a whitish coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
