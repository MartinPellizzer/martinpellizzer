Triticum ispahanicum var. ispahanorufum

Triticum ispahanicum var. ispahanorufum, commonly known as the Isfahan red wheat, is a variety of wheat native to Iran.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish grain color, which sets it apart from other wheat varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender stalks and awned spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to semi-arid climates.
