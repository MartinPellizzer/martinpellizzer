Teucrium rivasii

Teucrium rivasii, commonly known as the Rivas bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Teucrium rivasii is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
