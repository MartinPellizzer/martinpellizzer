Teucrium occidentalis var. menthifolium

Teucrium occidentalis var. menthifolium, commonly known as western germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
