Taxus elegantissima

Taxus elegantissima, commonly known as the noble yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain birds. It has a distinctive, dense crown and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.

Taxus elegantissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
