Terminalia cambodiana

Terminalia cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian Terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local communities. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Terminalia cambodiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
