Triumfetta galeottiana

Triumfetta galeottiana, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub or perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
