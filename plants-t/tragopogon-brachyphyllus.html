Tragopogon brachyphyllus

Tragopogon brachyphyllus, commonly known as the short-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish bracts.

Tragopogon brachyphyllus is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
