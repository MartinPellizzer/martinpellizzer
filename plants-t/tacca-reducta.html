Tacca reducta

Tacca reducta, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and a distinctive, fleshy, bat-like blossom that resembles a bat's face. The plant belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other ornamental plants such as calla lilies. Its unique morphology and exotic appearance make it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Tacca reducta thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect light and well-draining soil.
