Tetradenia glabra

Tetradenia glabra, commonly known as the slender tetradenia, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Tetradenia glabra is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
