Teucrium eriocephalum subsp. serranum

Teucrium eriocephalum subsp.

serranum, commonly known as the serrated germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Geraniaceae family.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
