Trichomanes nipponicum

Trichomanes nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese water fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores produced on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes nipponicum plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
