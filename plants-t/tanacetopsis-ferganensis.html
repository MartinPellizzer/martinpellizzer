Tanacetopsis ferganensis

Tanacetopsis ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana tanacetum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in alpine and steppe environments.
