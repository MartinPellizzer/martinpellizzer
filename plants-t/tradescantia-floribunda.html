Tradescantia floribunda

Tradescantia floribunda, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Tradescantia floribunda is a perennial herb that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize.
