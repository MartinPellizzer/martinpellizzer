Tylecodon wallichii

Tylecodon wallichii, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Tylecodon wallichii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
