Trifolium creticum

Trifolium creticum, commonly known as the Cretan clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Trifolium creticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
