Thymus atticus

Thymus atticus, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.

Thymus atticus is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils and aromatic properties.
