Thyrsanthus corymbosus

Thyrsanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered thyrsothamnus, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the dry conditions of its native habitat.

Thyrsanthus corymbosus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
