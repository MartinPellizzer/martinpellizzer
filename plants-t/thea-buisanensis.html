Thea buisanensis

Thea buisanensis, commonly known as the Buisan tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal teas, and it is valued for its aromatic properties.

Thea buisanensis is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and resilience in shaded environments.
