Thelychiton × suffusus

Thelychiton × suffusus, commonly known as the hybrid sweet potato, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Ipomoea batatas and other related species.

It is characterized by its starchy, sweet-tasting tuberous roots, which are rich in carbohydrates and often used as a food source. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and produces violet or purple flowers. Its cultivation is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The hybrid nature of Thelychiton × suffusus contributes to its genetic diversity and resilience in agricultural settings.
