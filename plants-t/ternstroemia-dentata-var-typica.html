Ternstroemia dentata var. typica

Ternstroemia dentata var. typica, commonly known as the toothed ternstroemia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
