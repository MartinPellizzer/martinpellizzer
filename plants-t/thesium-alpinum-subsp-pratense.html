Thesium alpinum subsp. pratense

Thesium alpinum subsp.

pratense, commonly known as the alpine thesium, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It features erect, sparsely branched stems that are often covered in short, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy surface.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
