Tanacetopsis kjurendaghi

Tanacetopsis kjurendaghi, commonly known as the Kjurendagh tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
