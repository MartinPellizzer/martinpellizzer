Trisetum transsilvanicum

Trisetum transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian trisetum, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Trisetum transsilvanicum plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
