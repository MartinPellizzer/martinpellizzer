Thymus pulegioides var. parvifolius

Thymus pulegioides var. parvifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink or violet flowers in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage is fragrant and can be used in cooking or herbal remedies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
