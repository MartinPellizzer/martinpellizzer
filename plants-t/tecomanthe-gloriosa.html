Tecomanthe gloriosa

Tecomanthe gloriosa, commonly known as the glory vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Tecomanthe gloriosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
