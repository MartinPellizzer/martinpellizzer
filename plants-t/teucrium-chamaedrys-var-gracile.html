Teucrium chamaedrys var. gracile

Teucrium chamaedrys var. gracile, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
