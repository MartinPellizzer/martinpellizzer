Tripteris cheiranthifolia

Tripteris cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf tripteris, is a member of the cashew family, Anacardiaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

Tripteris cheiranthifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
