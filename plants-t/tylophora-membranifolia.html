Tylophora membranifolia

Tylophora membranifolia, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
