Taraxacum officinale subsp. patens

Taraxacum officinale subsp.

patens, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower heads and distinctive, hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that often bends at the base, and it has a deep taproot system. It thrives in open, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
