Tillandsia glossophylla

Tillandsia glossophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to tree trunks or branches. The plant's foliage is characterized by its broad, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires regular misting to maintain its health.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
