Thlaspiceras cappadocicum subsp. microspermum

Thlaspiceras cappadocicum subsp.

microspermum, commonly known as the Cappadocian thlaspi, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of oblanceolate leaves.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family. It produces small, spherical pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey and thrives in rocky, arid environments.
