Thymus albanus subvar. lumbardensis

Thymus albanus subvar. lumbardensis, commonly known as the Llobregat thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct geographical and botanical characteristics.
