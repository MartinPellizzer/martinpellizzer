Triticum aestivum var. golbekii

Triticum aestivum var. golbekii, commonly known as the Golbek wheat, is a botanical variant of bread wheat characterized by its unique genetic traits.

It is native to Central Asia and is known for its high yield potential and adaptability to various climatic conditions. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with semi-dwarf stems, which contributes to its resilience against lodging. Its grains are typically larger and have a higher protein content compared to standard wheat varieties.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research for its potential to improve wheat cultivation in diverse environments.
