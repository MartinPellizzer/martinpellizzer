Tephrosia velutina

Tephrosia velutina, commonly known as velvet tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Tephrosia velutina is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
