Tournonia

Tournonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, hairy leaves and showy, often violet-colored flowers. Tournonia species are typically herbaceous perennials, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. They are popular ornamental plants due to their compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the year.

The genus includes several species, such as Tournonia coccinea, which is known for its bright red flowers.
