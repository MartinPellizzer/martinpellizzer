Trigonostemon sanguineus

Trigonostemon sanguineus, commonly known as the red-flowered flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its foliage. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three elliptical leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and reddish in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
