Tricholaena chevalieri

Tricholaena chevalieri, commonly known as the African bristle fern, is a member of the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its upright, rhizomatous growth habit and compound fronds. The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate to ovate pinnae that are often covered in fine, bristle-like hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
