Tecoma campinae

Tecoma campinae, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are arranged in terminal panicles, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
