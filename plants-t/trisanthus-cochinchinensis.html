Trisanthus cochinchinensis

Trisanthus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Trisanthus cochinchinensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
