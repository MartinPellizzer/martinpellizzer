Thalia sanguinea

Thalia sanguinea, commonly known as red thalia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring humid and sunny conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Thalia sanguinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
