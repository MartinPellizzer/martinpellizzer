Trachypogon spicatus

Trachypogon spicatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

Trachypogon spicatus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
