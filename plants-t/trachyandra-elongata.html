Trachyandra elongata

Trachyandra elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its unique, grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, linear leaves with a silvery-blue hue, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Trachyandra elongata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
