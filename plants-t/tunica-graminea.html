Tunica graminea

Tunica graminea, commonly known as the grass sheath, is a specialized layer of cells found in the stems of grasses.

This tissue forms a protective outer covering that helps prevent water loss and mechanical damage. It is composed primarily of tightly packed, elongated cells with thickened walls. The tunica graminea plays a crucial role in the structural integrity and resilience of grass plants.

Its unique cellular arrangement contributes to the characteristic flexibility and strength of grass stems.
