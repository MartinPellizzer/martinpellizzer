Tradescantia schwirkowskiana

Tradescantia schwirkowskiana, commonly known as the silver sword or spiderwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or blue-gray waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, compact clumps over time.
