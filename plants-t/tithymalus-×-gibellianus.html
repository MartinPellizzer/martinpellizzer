Tithymalus × gibellianus

Tithymalus × gibellianus, commonly known as the hybrid tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tithymalus polycephalus and Tithymalus subulatus. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and easy maintenance.
