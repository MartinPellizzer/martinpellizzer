Taraxacum dentifolium

Taraxacum dentifolium, commonly known as the toothed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
