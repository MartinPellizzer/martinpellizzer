Thelypteris exindusiata

Thelypteris exindusiata, commonly known as the veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sori, which are clusters of sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a thin indusium.

Thelypteris exindusiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
