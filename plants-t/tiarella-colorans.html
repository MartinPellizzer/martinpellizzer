Tiarella colorans

Tiarella colorans, commonly known as the foam flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a frothy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is well-suited for garden use in shaded areas.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a valuable groundcover plant.
