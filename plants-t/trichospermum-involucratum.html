Trichospermum involucratum

Trichospermum involucratum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round, orange-red fruits that are covered in a thick, hairy coating. These fruits contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
