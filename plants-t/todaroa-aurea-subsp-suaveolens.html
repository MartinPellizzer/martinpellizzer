Todaroa aurea subsp. suaveolens

Todaroa aurea subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fragrance is released when the leaves are crushed, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique scent.
