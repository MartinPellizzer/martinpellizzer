Trisetum subspicatum var. villosissimum

Trisetum subspicatum var. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine, hairy structures. The leaves are narrow and typically have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches. This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and taxonomic research.
