Thymus striatus var. leucadicus

Thymus striatus var. leucadicus, commonly known as the white thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and functional landscaping.
