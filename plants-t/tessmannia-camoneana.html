Tessmannia camoneana

Tessmannia camoneana, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
