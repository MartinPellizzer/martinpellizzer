Trischidium racemulosum

Trischidium racemulosum, commonly known as the clustered flax or clustered senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

Trischidium racemulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
