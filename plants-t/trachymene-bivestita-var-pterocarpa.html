Trachymene bivestita var. pterocarpa

Trachymene bivestita var. pterocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rainforest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
