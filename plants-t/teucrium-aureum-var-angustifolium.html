Teucrium aureum var. angustifolium

Teucrium aureum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden and ecological settings.
