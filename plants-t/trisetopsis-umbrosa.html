Trisetopsis umbrosa

Trisetopsis umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy trisetopsis, is a rare flowering plant native to the understory of dense tropical forests.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to low-light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its unique morphology allows it to thrive in shaded environments where light is limited.

Trisetopsis umbrosa is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and limited distribution.
