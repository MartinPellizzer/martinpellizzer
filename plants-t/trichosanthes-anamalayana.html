Trichosanthes anamalayana

Trichosanthes anamalayana, commonly known as the Anamalai bitter melon, is a vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant is characterized by its tendrils, lobed leaves, and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. Its fruit is a long, green, bitter melon that matures to a pale yellow, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its medicinal properties.
