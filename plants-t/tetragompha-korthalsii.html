Tetragompha korthalsii

Tetragompha korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals' four-angled grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its distinct four-angled stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage structure.
