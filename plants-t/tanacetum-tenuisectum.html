Tanacetum tenuisectum

Tanacetum tenuisectum, commonly known as narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and finely divided, often with a silvery underside, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
