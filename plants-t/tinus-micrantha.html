Tinus micrantha

Tinus micrantha, commonly known as the small-leaved tinus, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
