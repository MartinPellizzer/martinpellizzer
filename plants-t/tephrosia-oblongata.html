Tephrosia oblongata

Tephrosia oblongata, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia oblongata is also valued for its role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
