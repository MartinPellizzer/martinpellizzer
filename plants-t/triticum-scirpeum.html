Triticum scirpeum

Triticum scirpeum, commonly known as jointed grass, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Triticum scirpeum is typically found in wet or marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
