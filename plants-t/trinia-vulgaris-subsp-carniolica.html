Trinia vulgaris subsp. carniolica

Trinia vulgaris subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
