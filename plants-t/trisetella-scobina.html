Trisetella scobina

Trisetella scobina, commonly known as the scobin fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. Trisetella scobina thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
