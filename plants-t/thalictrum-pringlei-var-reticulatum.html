Thalictrum pringlei var. reticulatum

Thalictrum pringlei var. reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
