Tamarix austromongolica

Tamarix austromongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, saline-tasting leaves and small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a common species in desert and steppe environments. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological significance in stabilizing sandy soils.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
