Talisia squarrosa

Talisia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly talisia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

Talisia squarrosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique look and adaptability to various growing conditions.
