Thermopsis barbata

Thermopsis barbata, commonly known as the barbed yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a taproot system and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Thermopsis barbata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
