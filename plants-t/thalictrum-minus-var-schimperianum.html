Thalictrum minus var. schimperianum

Thalictrum minus var. schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
