Tinospora sagittata

Tinospora sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Tinospora sagittata is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating inflammation and skin conditions.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
