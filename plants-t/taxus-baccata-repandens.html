Taxus baccata repandens

Taxus baccata repandens, commonly known as the European yew with spreading branches, is a variant of the common yew.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and reddish bark. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique branching pattern and ornamental appearance make it a valued plant in horticulture.
