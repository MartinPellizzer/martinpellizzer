Thalictrum stolzii

Thalictrum stolzii, commonly known as Stolz's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Thalictrum stolzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
