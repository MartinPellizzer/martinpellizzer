Thinopyrum runemarkii

Thinopyrum runemarkii, commonly known as the wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments. It is also valued for its nutritional content, as it is often used in livestock feed and human consumption.

Additionally, Thinopyrum runemarkii has been studied for its potential role in wheat improvement through hybridization.
