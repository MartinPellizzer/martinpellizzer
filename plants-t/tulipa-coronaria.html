Tulipa coronaria

Tulipa coronaria, commonly known as the crown tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

The crown tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
