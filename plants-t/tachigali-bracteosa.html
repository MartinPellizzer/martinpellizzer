Tachigali bracteosa

Tachigali bracteosa, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
