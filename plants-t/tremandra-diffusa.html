Tremandra diffusa

Tremandra diffusa, commonly known as the showy goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that bear showy, yellow-orange flowers with prominent, elongated petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and woodland edges.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
