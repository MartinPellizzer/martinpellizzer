Trigonella monantha var. macroglochin

Trigonella monantha var. macroglochin, commonly known as the large-seeded fenugreek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
