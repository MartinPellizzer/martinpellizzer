Torenia leucosiphon

Torenia leucosiphon, commonly known as the white-tongued torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Torenia leucosiphon thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
