Tulipa sylvestris var. melillensis

Tulipa sylvestris var. melillensis, commonly known as the Melillan tulip, is a rare and striking species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange-red flowers with a distinctive red stripe running down the central part of the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
