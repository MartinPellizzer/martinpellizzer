Tragopogon pratensis subsp. pratensis

Tragopogon pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as meadow goatweed, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae). It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow ray flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, tubular seed head that resembles a small, elongated flower. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of North America.

Its botanical features make it an important component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
