Tricharis serotina

Tricharis serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering tricharis, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces achenes as its primary seed type.

Tricharis serotina is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
