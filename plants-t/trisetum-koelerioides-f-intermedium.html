Trisetum koelerioides f. intermedium

Trisetum koelerioides f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate koeler's feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces pale green to brownish spikelets that mature into seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions, typically in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
