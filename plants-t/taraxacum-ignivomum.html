Taraxacum ignivomum

Taraxacum ignivomum, commonly known as the fire-eating dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hairy stem. The plant is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated margin.

The species is notable for its unique ability to produce heat through its floral structures, a rare trait among dandelions.
