Thymus agoustensis

Thymus agoustensis, commonly known as the Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus agoustensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in culinary applications.
