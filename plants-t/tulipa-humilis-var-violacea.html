Tulipa humilis var. violacea

Tulipa humilis var. violacea, commonly known as the purple tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces small, cup-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive, recurved petal shape. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is known for its early blooming period, often flowering in late winter or early spring.

The variety is valued for its compact growth and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
