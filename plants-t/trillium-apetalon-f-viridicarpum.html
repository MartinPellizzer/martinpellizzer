Trillium apetalon f. viridicarpum

Trillium apetalon f. viridicarpum, commonly known as the green-seeded trillium, is a rare and striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Trillium genus, which is characterized by its three-parted leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands, where it thrives in rich, organic soil. Its unique feature is the greenish color of its seeds, which sets it apart from other trillium species.

This botanical variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
