Turritis auriculata

Turritis auriculata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Turritis auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
