Tristachya atricha

Tristachya atricha, commonly known as the black beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, dark purple to black flowers.

Tristachya atricha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
