Thymus serpyllum var. kosteleckyanus

Thymus serpyllum var. kosteleckyanus, commonly known as Kostelecky's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
