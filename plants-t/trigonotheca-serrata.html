Trigonotheca serrata

Trigonotheca serrata, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny pods that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Trigonotheca serrata is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
