Tilia handsworthiensis

Tilia handsworthiensis, commonly known as the Handsworth lime, is a species of small to medium-sized tree native to the UK.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hairy husk. It is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is a valuable species for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.

Its historical significance includes being a notable tree in the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew.
