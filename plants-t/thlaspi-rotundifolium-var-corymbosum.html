Thlaspi rotundifolium var. corymbosum

Thlaspi rotundifolium var. corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, round, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny yellow flowers. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
