Trembleya laniflora var. grandifolia

Trembleya laniflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the silky trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers bloom in clusters and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
