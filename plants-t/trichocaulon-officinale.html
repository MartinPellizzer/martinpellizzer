Trichocaulon officinale

Trichocaulon officinale, commonly known as the common spurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in cymes. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Trichocaulon officinale is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
