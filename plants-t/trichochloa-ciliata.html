Trichochloa ciliata

Trichochloa ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Trichochloa ciliata plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
