Tomanthea spectabilis

Tomanthea spectabilis, commonly known as the showy tomanthea, is a striking flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tomanthea spectabilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
