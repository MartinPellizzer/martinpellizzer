Thlaspi perfoliatum f. praecox

Thlaspi perfoliatum f. praecox, commonly known as early bladderworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom early in the growing season. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it an important pioneer species in certain ecosystems.
