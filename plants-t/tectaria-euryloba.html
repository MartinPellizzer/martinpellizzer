Tectaria euryloba

Tectaria euryloba, commonly known as the broad-lobed shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant's leaves are typically divided into broad, lobed segments with a distinctive, waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in rainforests.

Tectaria euryloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for under the right conditions.
