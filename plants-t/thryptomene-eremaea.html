Thryptomene eremaea

Thryptomene eremaea, commonly known as the spinifex thryptomene, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Thryptomene eremaea is well adapted to low-nutrient soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
