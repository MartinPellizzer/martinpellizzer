Trigonella berythea var. leucantha

Trigonella berythea var. leucantha, commonly known as white fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by cylindrical, brown pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and saline environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
