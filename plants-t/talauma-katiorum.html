Talauma katiorum

Talauma katiorum, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette formation.

Talauma katiorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
