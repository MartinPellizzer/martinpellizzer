Truellum aestivum

Truellum aestivum, commonly known as meadow vetchling, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish to reddish-purple flowers in clusters. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils.

Truellum aestivum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
