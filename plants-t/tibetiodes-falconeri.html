Tibetiodes falconeri

Tibetiodes falconeri, commonly known as the Tibetan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand the harsh, cold climate of its habitat.

Tibetiodes falconeri is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.
