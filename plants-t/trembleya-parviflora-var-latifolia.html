Trembleya parviflora var. latifolia

Trembleya parviflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety latifolia is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of T. parviflora.
