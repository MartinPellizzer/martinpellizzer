Trifolium repens var. nevadense

Trifolium repens var. nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada white clover, is a variant of the white clover species native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, typically featuring three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
