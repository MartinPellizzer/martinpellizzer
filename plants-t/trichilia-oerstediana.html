Trichilia oerstediana

Trichilia oerstediana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
