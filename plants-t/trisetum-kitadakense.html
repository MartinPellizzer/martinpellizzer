Trisetum kitadakense

Trisetum kitadakense, commonly known as the Japanese wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets.

Trisetum kitadakense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
