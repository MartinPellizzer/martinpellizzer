Tittmannia hispida

Tittmannia hispida, commonly known as the rough tittmannia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Tittmannia hispida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
