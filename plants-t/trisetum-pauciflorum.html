Trisetum pauciflorum

Trisetum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in three rows, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Trisetum pauciflorum produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, contributing to its classification as a monocot.
