Tephrosia mossiensis

Tephrosia mossiensis, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tephrosia mossiensis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
