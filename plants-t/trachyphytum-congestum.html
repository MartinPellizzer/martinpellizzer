Trachyphytum congestum

Trachyphytum congestum, commonly known as the clustered roughleaf, is a unique species native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compact growth habit, which help it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle aesthetic to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are thick and covered in a fine, sandpaper-like texture, contributing to its common name.

Trachyphytum congestum is well adapted to drought and heat, making it a resilient addition to xeriscaping efforts.
