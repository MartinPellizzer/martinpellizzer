Thelypteris novaehiberniae

Thelypteris novaehiberniae, commonly known as the New Ireland holly fern, is a species of fern native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fertile fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
