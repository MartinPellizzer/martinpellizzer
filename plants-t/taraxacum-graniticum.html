Taraxacum graniticum

Taraxacum graniticum, commonly known as the granitic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports its yellow composite flower. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it a notable component of high-altitude ecosystems.
