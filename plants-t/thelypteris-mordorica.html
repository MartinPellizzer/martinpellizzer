Thelypteris mordorica

Thelypteris mordorica, commonly known as the "Mordor Fern," is a species of evergreen fern native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Thelypteris mordorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
