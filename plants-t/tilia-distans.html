Tilia distans

Tilia distans, commonly known as the distant linden, is a species of flowering tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Tilia distans is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
