Thunbergia brachypoda

Thunbergia brachypoda, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia brachypoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
