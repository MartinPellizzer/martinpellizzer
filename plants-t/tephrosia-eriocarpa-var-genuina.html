Tephrosia eriocarpa var. genuina

Tephrosia eriocarpa var. genuina, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
