Tamarix troupii

Tamarix troupii, commonly known as the Jordanian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in dense clusters, which are adapted for pollination by wind and insects. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in saline and poor soils.

Tamarix troupii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in desert landscapes.
