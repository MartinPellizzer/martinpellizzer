Tristachya somalensis var. disticha

Tristachya somalensis var. disticha, commonly known as the Somalian panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of multiple spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, open panicle. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is notable for its ecological adaptability and potential use in erosion control and livestock forage.
