Tinus philippinensis

Tinus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
