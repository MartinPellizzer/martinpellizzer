Trigonella speciosa

Trigonella speciosa, commonly known as the showy fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
