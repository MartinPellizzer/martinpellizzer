Tripogon hookerianus

Tripogon hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's tripogon, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Tripogon hookerianus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.
