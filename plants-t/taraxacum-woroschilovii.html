Taraxacum woroschilovii

Taraxacum woroschilovii, commonly known as the Woroschilov's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
