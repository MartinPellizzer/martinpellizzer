Tricyrtis uniflora

Tricyrtis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single flower per stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tricyrtis uniflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
