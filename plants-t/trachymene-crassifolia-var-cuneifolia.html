Trachymene crassifolia var. cuneifolia

Trachymene crassifolia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
