Tamarix austromongolica subsp. qinghaiensis

Tamarix austromongolica subsp.

qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is often used for soil stabilization and erosion control in its native habitat.
