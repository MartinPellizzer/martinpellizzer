Tina urschii

Tina urschii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Tina urschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
