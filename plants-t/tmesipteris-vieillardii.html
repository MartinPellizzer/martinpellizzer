Tmesipteris vieillardii

Tmesipteris vieillardii, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Tmesipteris vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
