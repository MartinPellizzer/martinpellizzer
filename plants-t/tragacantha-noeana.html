Tragacantha noeana

Tragacantha noeana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia, particularly Iran. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha noeana is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
