Tragacantha heydei

Tragacantha heydei, commonly known as the Heyde's tragacanth, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Tragacantha heydei is also valued for its mucilage, which has been historically used in culinary and pharmaceutical applications.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a preference for well-drained soils.
