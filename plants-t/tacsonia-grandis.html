Tacsonia grandis

Tacsonia grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are thick, glossy, and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for the vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
