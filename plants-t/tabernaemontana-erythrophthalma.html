Tabernaemontana erythrophthalma

Tabernaemontana erythrophthalma, commonly known as the red-eyed tree hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, with prominent red veins on the petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
