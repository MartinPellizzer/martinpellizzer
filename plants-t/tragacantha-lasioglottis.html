Tragacantha lasioglottis

Tragacantha lasioglottis, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing seeds.

Tragacantha lasioglottis is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
