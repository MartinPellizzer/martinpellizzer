Trifolium kingii subsp. macilentum

Trifolium kingii subsp.

macilentum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically small and have a pale green to reddish hue.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers arranged in dense, globular heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
