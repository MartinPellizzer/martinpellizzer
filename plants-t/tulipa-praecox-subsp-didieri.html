Tulipa praecox subsp. didieri

Tulipa praecox subsp.

didieri, commonly known as the early tulip or the Persian tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early display.
