Tsuga canadensis sargentii-pendula

Tsuga canadensis sargentii-pendula, commonly known as the weeping hemlock, is a cultivar of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, typical of the Tsuga genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its unique visual appeal and can grow up to 20 feet tall over time.
