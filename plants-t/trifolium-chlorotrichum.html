Trifolium chlorotrichum

Trifolium chlorotrichum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and open woodlands.

Trifolium chlorotrichum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
