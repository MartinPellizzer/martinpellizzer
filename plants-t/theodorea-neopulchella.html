Theodorea neopulchella

Theodorea neopulchella, commonly known as the Neotropical orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its humid, tropical habitat.

Theodorea neopulchella is often found in shaded, moist environments within forest understories.
