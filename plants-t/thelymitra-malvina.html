Thelymitra malvina

Thelymitra malvina, commonly known as the Purple Rain Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers from a basal rosette.

Thelymitra malvina is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
