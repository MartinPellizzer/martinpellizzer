Tacsonia tomentosa var. speciosa

Tacsonia tomentosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf tacsonia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
