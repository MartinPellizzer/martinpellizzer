Trichopteryx gigantea

Trichopteryx gigantea, commonly known as the giant trichopteryx, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are known to form extensive networks that help stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Trichopteryx gigantea is now considered a rare and endangered species.
