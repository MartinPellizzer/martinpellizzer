Tanacetum trichophyllum

Tanacetum trichophyllum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Tanacetum trichophyllum typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its hairy leaves and strong scent make it distinctive among other members of the genus.
