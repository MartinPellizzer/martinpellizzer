Tragacantha reedii

Tragacantha reedii, commonly known as the reed tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Tragacantha reedii is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments due to its deep root system and efficient water conservation mechanisms.

It is sometimes cultivated for its mucilage-rich resin, which has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.
