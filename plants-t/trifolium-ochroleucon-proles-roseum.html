Trifolium ochroleucon proles roseum

Trifolium ochroleucon proles roseum, commonly known as the rose-colored lesser clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets with a pale pink or rose-colored hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
