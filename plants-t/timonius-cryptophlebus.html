Timonius cryptophlebus

Timonius cryptophlebus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf timonius, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its growth habit is predominantly shrub-like, with woody stems that exhibit a reddish-brown hue.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Timonius cryptophlebus is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to researchers studying plant biodiversity.
