Tanacetum monspeliense var. cebennense

Tanacetum monspeliense var. cebennense, commonly known as the Italian tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
