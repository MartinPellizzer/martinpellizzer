Thalictrum longistylum

Thalictrum longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Thalictrum longistylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
