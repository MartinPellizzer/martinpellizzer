Tilia subflavescens

Tilia subflavescens, commonly known as the yellow-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round fruits. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Its wood is light and soft, making it suitable for carving and furniture-making.
