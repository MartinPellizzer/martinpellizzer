Turnera amazonica

Turnera amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Turnera amazonica plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
