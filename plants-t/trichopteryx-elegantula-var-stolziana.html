Trichopteryx elegantula var. stolziana

Trichopteryx elegantula var. stolziana, commonly known as the Stolzian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, with a deep green upper surface and a lighter, often pubescent underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a sprawling or semi-woody stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
