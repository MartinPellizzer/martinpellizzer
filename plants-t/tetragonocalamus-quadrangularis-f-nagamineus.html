Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. nagamineus

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. nagamineus, commonly known as the Nagamine's bamboo, is a unique variant of the quadrangular bamboo species.

It is characterized by its distinctively square or four-sided stems, which give it a striking appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and elegant form. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This bamboo is also noted for its slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for small garden spaces and bonsai cultivation.
