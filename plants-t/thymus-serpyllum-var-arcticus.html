Thymus serpyllum var. arcticus

Thymus serpyllum var. arcticus, commonly known as Arctic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where temperatures are consistently low.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and resilience in harsh conditions.
