Tacca abyssinica

Tacca abyssinica, commonly known as the African arrowroot or black root, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves with a dark purple to black coloration. The plant's flowers are unique, featuring a central spadix surrounded by a showy, hood-like spathe. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
