Teline linifolia subsp. rosmarinifolia

Teline linifolia subsp.

rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemary-leaved teline, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features linear, aromatic leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
