Tinantia fugax

Tinantia fugax, commonly known as the ephemeral lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that appear briefly during the rainy season. The plant has a short life cycle, often completing its growth and reproduction within a few weeks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.
