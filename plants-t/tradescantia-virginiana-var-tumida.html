Tradescantia virginiana var. tumida

Tradescantia virginiana var. tumida, commonly known as redstem spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairless stems that often turn reddish, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
