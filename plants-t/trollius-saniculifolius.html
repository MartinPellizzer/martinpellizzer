Trollius saniculifolius

Trollius saniculifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trollius saniculifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
