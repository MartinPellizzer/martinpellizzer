Trisetopsis arcta

Trisetopsis arcta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Trisetopsis arcta is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
