Trigonella corniculata subsp. rechingeri

Trigonella corniculata subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the horned fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive triangular leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological features, including the presence of a horn-like structure on the seed pods.
