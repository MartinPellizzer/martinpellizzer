Tralliana

Tralliana, commonly known as the redroot germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Tralliana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for use in various herbal preparations.
