Tanacetum vulgare subsp. siculum

Tanacetum vulgare subsp.

siculum, commonly known as Sicilian tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It contains essential oils with medicinal properties, including compounds like thymol and camphor.

However, it is often considered a weed and can be invasive in certain regions.
