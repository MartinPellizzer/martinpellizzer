Trisetum spicatum var. formosanum

Trisetum spicatum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its delicate, grass-like foliage.

The species is also of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
