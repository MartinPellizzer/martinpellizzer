Trophaeum mucronatum

Trophaeum mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are thin and floating, adapted for capturing prey in water. It thrives in nutrient-poor environments by supplementing its diet with captured prey.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
