Telipogon buenaventurae

Telipogon buenaventurae, commonly known as the Buenaventura orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its unique morphology includes a sympodial growth habit and specialized root structures adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
