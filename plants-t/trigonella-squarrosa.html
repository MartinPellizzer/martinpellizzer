Trigonella squarrosa

Trigonella squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy bitter vetch, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems that often bear small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing several elliptical, brown seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Trigonella squarrosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
