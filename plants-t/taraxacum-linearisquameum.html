Taraxacum linearisquameum

Taraxacum linearisquameum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical, fluffy seed head known as a pappus. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Taraxacum linearisquameum is a perennial plant that is adapted to a wide range of soil types and environmental conditions.
