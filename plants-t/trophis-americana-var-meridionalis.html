Trophis americana var. meridionalis

Trophis americana var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern American trophis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warmer climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and pollinator habitats.
