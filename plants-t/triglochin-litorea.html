Triglochin litorea

Triglochin litorea, commonly known as sea arrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes.

Triglochin litorea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
