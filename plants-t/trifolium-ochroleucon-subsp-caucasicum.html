Trifolium ochroleucon subsp. caucasicum

Trifolium ochroleucon subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian white clover, is a subspecies of the white clover genus native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by trifoliate leaves with a pale yellow to white coloration.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
