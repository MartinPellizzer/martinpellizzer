Tulipa praecox proles lortetii

Tulipa praecox proles lortetii, commonly known as the Lortet's early tulip, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive black eye. It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, fibrous root system.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
