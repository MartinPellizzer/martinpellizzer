Turbinicarpus lophophoroides subsp. jauernigii

Turbinicarpus lophophoroides subsp.

jauernigii, commonly known as the Jauernig's Turbinicarpus, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are often covered in small, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, hairy pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
