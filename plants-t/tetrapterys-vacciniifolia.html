Tetrapterys vacciniifolia

Tetrapterys vacciniifolia, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a yellow throat. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its floral structure includes five sepals and five petals, with stamens that are typically longer than the petals.

Tetrapterys vacciniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
