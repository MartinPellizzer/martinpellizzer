Tribulus mollis

Tribulus mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into green, three-lobed pods containing numerous seeds. Tribulus mollis is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
