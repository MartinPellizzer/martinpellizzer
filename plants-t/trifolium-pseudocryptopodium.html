Trifolium pseudocryptopodium

Trifolium pseudocryptopodium, commonly known as the false cryptopodium clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium pseudocryptopodium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
