Thelypteris hispiduliformis

Thelypteris hispiduliformis, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of foliage.

Thelypteris hispiduliformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
