Trichocrepis

Trichocrepis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet ray florets and a central disk of smaller florets, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plants typically grow in coastal areas and are adapted to salty, well-drained soils. Trichocrepis species are often found in Mediterranean regions and parts of southern Europe.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique appearance.
