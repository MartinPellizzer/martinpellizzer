Tecoma caraiba

Tecoma caraiba, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Tecoma caraiba is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
