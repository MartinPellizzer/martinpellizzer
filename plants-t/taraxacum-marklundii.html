Taraxacum marklundii

Taraxacum marklundii, commonly known as the Marklund's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to northern regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in various soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
