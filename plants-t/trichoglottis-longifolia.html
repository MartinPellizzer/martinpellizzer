Trichoglottis longifolia

Trichoglottis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Trichoglottis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
