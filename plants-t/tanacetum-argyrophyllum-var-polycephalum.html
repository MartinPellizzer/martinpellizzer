Tanacetum argyrophyllum var. polycephalum

Tanacetum argyrophyllum var. polycephalum, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
