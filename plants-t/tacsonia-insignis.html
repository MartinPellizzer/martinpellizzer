Tacsonia insignis

Tacsonia insignis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, forming bell-shaped clusters at the ends of its stems. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

Tacsonia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
