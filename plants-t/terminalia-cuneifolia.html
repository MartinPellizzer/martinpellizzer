Terminalia cuneifolia

Terminalia cuneifolia, commonly known as the African sand apple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are a source of food for both humans and animals. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Terminalia cuneifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
