Tynanthus schumannianus

Tynanthus schumannianus, commonly known as the Schumann's tynanthus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces showy, yellow to orange flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
