Taraxacum officinale proles lanceolatum

Taraxacum officinale proles lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its lance-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, known for its bright yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
