Taeniopetalum arenarium subsp. arenarium

Taeniopetalum arenarium subsp.

arenarium, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline.

The plant features erect, branched stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a head, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and provides food for various pollinators.
