Telina striata

Telina striata, commonly known as the striped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white or pale stripe running down the center of each petal. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Telina striata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
