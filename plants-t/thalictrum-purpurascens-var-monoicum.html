Thalictrum purpurascens var. monoicum

Thalictrum purpurascens var. monoicum, commonly known as the purple meadow-rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, making it a striking addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
