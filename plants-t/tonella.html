Tonella

Tonella, commonly known as the Chinese date or longan, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible fruits. The fruit has a rough, brown exterior and a sweet, juicy interior with a single large seed. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface.

Tonella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
