Tephrosia moschata

Tephrosia moschata, commonly known as sweet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia moschata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and scent.
