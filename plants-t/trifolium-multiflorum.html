Trifolium multiflorum

Trifolium multiflorum, commonly known as the common clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including poor and disturbed soils. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Trifolium multiflorum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
