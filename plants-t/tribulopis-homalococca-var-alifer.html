Tribulopis homalococca var. alifer

Tribulopis homalococca var. alifer, commonly known as the hairy tribulus, is a variant of the tribulus genus native to South America.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that have a hairy texture. This plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
