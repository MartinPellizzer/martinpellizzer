Tectona ternifolia

Tectona ternifolia, commonly known as the Indian teak or Malabar teak, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and strong, durable wood. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are small, capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds.

Tectona ternifolia is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
