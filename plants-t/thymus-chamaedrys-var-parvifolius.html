Thymus chamaedrys var. parvifolius

Thymus chamaedrys var. parvifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
