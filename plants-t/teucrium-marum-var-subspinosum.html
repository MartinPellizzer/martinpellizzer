Teucrium marum var. subspinosum

Teucrium marum var. subspinosum, commonly known as spiked germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with woody stems and produces small, spiny fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
