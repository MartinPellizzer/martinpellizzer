Thymus macedonicus subvar. pilifer

Thymus macedonicus subvar. pilifer, commonly known as Macedonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other thyme species.
