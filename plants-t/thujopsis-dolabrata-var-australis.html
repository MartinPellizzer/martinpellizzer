Thujopsis dolabrata var. australis

Thujopsis dolabrata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian lacebark, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flaky, peeling bark that reveals a smooth, pale inner layer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental bark and adaptability to various garden conditions.
