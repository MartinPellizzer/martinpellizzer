Taraxacum niveum

Taraxacum niveum, commonly known as the snow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of white or pale green leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears a single, showy yellow flower. The plant produces a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds with white pappi, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates with limited soil nutrients.

Taraxacum niveum is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelion species.
