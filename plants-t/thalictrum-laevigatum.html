Thalictrum laevigatum

Thalictrum laevigatum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Thalictrum laevigatum is known for its delicate appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
