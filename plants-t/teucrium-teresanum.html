Teucrium teresanum

Teucrium teresanum, commonly known as the sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils.

Teucrium teresanum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
