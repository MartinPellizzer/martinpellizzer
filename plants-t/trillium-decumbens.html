Trillium decumbens

Trillium decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its three broad, waxy leaves and a single, showy white or pinkish flower. The plant has a distinct underground rhizome and produces berries that are red when mature. It is a member of the Trillium genus, which is known for its unique tripartite structure and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.

Trillium decumbens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
