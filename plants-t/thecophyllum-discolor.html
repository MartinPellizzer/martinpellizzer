Thecophyllum discolor

Thecophyllum discolor, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that display a striking contrast between light and shadow.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that enhances its ethereal look. The plant's ability to photosynthesize efficiently in low-light conditions makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Despite its beauty, it is relatively rare and requires specific care to cultivate successfully.
