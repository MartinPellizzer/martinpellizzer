Tainia maculata

Tainia maculata, commonly known as the spotted tainia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves. The plant features a short, stocky stem with a rosette of waxy, textured foliage that displays distinctive white or yellow spots. Its flowers are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Tainia maculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
