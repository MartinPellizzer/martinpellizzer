Tachigali plumbea

Tachigali plumbea, commonly known as the silver tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
