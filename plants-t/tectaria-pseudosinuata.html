Tectaria pseudosinuata

Tectaria pseudosinuata, commonly known as the crested tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that often exhibit a wavy or undulating pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are usually located on the abaxial surface of the fronds.

Tectaria pseudosinuata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates.
