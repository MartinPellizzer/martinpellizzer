Tabebuia richardiana

Tabebuia richardiana, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The tree has a straight trunk and a dense canopy, with leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
