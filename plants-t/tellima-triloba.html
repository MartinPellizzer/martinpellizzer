Tellima triloba

Tellima triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Tellima triloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
