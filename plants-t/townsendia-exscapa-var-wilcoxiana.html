Townsendia exscapa var. wilcoxiana

Townsendia exscapa var. wilcoxiana, commonly known as the Wilcox's bluebelle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small flower heads arranged in a cluster.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
