Turnera

Turnera, commonly known as the "sunshine plant," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Turneraceae.

These plants are native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Turnera species are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their opposite, simple leaves and showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The flowers are usually clustered in terminal inflorescences and are known for their vibrant colors and pleasant fragrance.

Many species of Turnera are valued for their ornamental qualities and are cultivated in gardens for their aesthetic appeal.
