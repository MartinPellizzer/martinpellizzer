Taraxacum quelpaertense

Taraxacum quelpaertense, commonly known as the Jeju dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open grassy areas and rocky habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of its native region.

It is considered a rare and endemic species, contributing to the biodiversity of Jeju's flora.
