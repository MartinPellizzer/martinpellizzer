Tiarella trifoliata

Tiarella trifoliata, commonly known as the Japanese foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland settings.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for shady garden landscapes.
