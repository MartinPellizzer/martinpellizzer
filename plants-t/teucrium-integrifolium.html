Teucrium integrifolium

Teucrium integrifolium, commonly known as blue germander, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with smooth, entire margins. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
