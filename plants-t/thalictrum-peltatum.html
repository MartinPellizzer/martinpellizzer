Thalictrum peltatum

Thalictrum peltatum, commonly known as the fernleaf meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Thalictrum peltatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
