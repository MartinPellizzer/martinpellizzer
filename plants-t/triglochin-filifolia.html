Triglochin filifolia

Triglochin filifolia, commonly known as slender swamp grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Triglochin filifolia is adapted to fluctuating water levels and is often an indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
