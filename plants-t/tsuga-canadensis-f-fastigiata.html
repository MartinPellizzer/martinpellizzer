Tsuga canadensis f. fastigiata

Tsuga canadensis f. fastigiata, commonly known as the stiff-branch white spruce, is a compact, columnar form of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and dense foliage, making it a popular ornamental tree. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in a compact, scale-like pattern on the shoots. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in cool, moist climates.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its formal appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
