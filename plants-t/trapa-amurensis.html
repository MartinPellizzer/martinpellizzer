Trapa amurensis

Trapa amurensis, commonly known as the Amur water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its floating, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots are submerged. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Trapa amurensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
