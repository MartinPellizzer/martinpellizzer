Thymus valderius

Thymus valderius, commonly known as Valderian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Thymus valderius thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
