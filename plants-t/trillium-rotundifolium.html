Trillium rotundifolium

Trillium rotundifolium, commonly known as the red trillium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features three broadly ovate, reddish-brown to reddish-purple leaves that are rounded at the base. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a showy, three-petaled white or pinkish flower with a yellow center. It typically blooms in early spring and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

Trillium rotundifolium is known for its ornamental value and is also an important species in ecological studies due to its role in forest ecosystems.
