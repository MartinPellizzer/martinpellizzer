Trisetum spicatum subsp. ovatipaniculatum

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

ovatipaniculatum, commonly known as the ovate panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, ovate-shaped panicles that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
