Thalictrum rutifolium

Thalictrum rutifolium, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Thalictrum rutifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
