Tigridia vanhouttei subsp.

vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's tiger flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble tiger stripes, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers. Tigridia vanhouttei subsp.

vanhouttei is known for its short blooming period, usually in late spring to early summer.
