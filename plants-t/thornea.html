Thornea

Thornea, commonly known as the thorned vine, is a climbing plant characterized by its woody stems and sharp thorns.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Thornea is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not managed properly.

Its botanical name, Thornea, reflects its distinctive thorned structure.
