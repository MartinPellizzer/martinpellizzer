Tropaeolum lobbianum

Tropaeolum lobbianum, commonly known as the mountain nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in temperate climates. Its bright orange and yellow blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tropaeolum lobbianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, peppery flavor in culinary applications.
