Tacca pinnatifida var. permagna

Tacca pinnatifida var. permagna, commonly known as the giant bat plant or sacred lotus of the East, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with a velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant produces striking, bat-like flowers that are often white or pale pink with a distinctive purple or black center. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
