Thymus punctatus subvar. subisophylla

Thymus punctatus subvar. subisophylla, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine hairs, which gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
