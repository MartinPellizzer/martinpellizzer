Terogia dichotoma

Terogia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked terogia, is a unique plant species characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that reduce surface area and minimize water loss. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors to attract pollinators.

Terogia dichotoma plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
