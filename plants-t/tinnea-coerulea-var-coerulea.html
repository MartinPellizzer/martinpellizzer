Tinnea coerulea var. coerulea

Tinnea coerulea var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue tinnea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
