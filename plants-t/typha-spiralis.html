Typha spiralis

Typha spiralis, commonly known as the spiral cattail, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves and a cylindrical, brown flower spike that emerges from the water. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight spiral twist, contributing to its unique appearance.

Typha spiralis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
