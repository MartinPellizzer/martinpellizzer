Tecoma fabrisii

Tecoma fabrisii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Tecoma fabrisii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
