Tragacantha drusorum

Tragacantha drusorum, commonly known as the Turkish gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods containing seeds.

Tragacantha drusorum is known for its ability to produce a resinous sap, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
