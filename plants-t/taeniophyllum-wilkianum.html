Taeniophyllum wilkianum

Taeniophyllum wilkianum, commonly known as the Wilkian air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup at the center. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on rainfall and mist for hydration. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically yellow or white and bloom in the spring.

Taeniophyllum wilkianum is valued for its low maintenance and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
