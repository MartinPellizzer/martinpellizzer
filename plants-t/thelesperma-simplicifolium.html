Thelesperma simplicifolium

Thelesperma simplicifolium, commonly known as plains pricklypear, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
