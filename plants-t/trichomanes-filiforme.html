Trichomanes filiforme

Trichomanes filiforme, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are often spirally arranged along the stem. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in groups called synangia, which are located on the underside of the leaves.

Trichomanes filiforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in humid conditions.
