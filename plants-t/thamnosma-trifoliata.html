Thamnosma trifoliata

Thamnosma trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf thamnosma, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Thamnosma trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
