Triticum aristatum aestivum

Triticum aristatum aestivum, commonly known as the wild wheat or goat grass, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and awned spikelets that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional agriculture and can be processed into flour for baking.

Triticum aristatum aestivum is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
