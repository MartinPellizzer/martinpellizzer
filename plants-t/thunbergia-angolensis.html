Thunbergia angolensis

Thunbergia angolensis, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Thunbergia angolensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
