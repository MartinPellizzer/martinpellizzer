Tacsonia

Tacsonia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants are shrubs or small trees that can grow up to several meters in height. Their leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets, and they produce small, yellowish flowers.

The wood of Tacsonia species is highly valued for its density and rich reddish color, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.
