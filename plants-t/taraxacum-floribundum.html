Taraxacum floribundum

Taraxacum floribundum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic puffball seeds. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
