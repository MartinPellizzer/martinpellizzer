Tecoma dubia

Tecoma dubia, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Tecoma dubia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
