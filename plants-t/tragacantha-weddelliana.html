Tragacantha weddelliana

Tragacantha weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Tragacantha weddelliana is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
