Trachymene humilis subsp. breviscapa

Trachymene humilis subsp.

breviscapa, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed devil's bit, is a herbaceous perennial native to southeastern Australia. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, with small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in umbels.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
