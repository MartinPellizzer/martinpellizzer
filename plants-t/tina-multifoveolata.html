Tina multifoveolata

Tina multifoveolata, commonly known as the many-pit tina, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features multiple small pits or depressions along the surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its alpine habitat. Its growth is slow and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile ecosystem, Tina multifoveolata is considered a vulnerable species.
