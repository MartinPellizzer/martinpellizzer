Thymus serpyllum var. serbicus

Thymus serpyllum var. serbicus, commonly known as Serbian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces tiny pink or purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
