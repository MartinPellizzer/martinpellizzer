Tragacantha pauciflora

Tragacantha pauciflora, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
