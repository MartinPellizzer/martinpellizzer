Tragacantha erythrolepis

Tragacantha erythrolepis, commonly known as the red-leaved tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its bark and roots have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
