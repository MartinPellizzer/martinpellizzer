Thunbergia salweenensis

Thunbergia salweenensis, commonly known as the Salween trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
