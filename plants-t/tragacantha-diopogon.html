Tragacantha diopogon

Tragacantha diopogon, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The gum exuded by the plant has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
