Thymus serpyllum var. polytrichus

Thymus serpyllum var. polytrichus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ground cover plant.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
