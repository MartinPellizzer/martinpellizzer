Tulipastrum cordatum

Tulipastrum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf tulip tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces showy, tulip-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in spring. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels and are often clustered in terminal panicles.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
