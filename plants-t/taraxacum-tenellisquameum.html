Taraxacum tenellisquameum

Taraxacum tenellisquameum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, light green leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

The species is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
