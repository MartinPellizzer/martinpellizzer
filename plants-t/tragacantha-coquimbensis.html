Tragacantha coquimbensis

Tragacantha coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Its roots can fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
