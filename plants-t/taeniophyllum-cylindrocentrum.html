Taeniophyllum cylindrocentrum

Taeniophyllum cylindrocentrum, commonly known as the cylindrical spurge or narrowleaf spurge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with distinctive yellow cyathia, which are characteristic of the spurge family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its low-growing habit and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as a succulent.
