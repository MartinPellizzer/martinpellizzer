Thalictrum cooleyi

Thalictrum cooleyi, commonly known as the Cooley's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in upright clusters, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Thalictrum cooleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
