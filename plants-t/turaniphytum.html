Turaniphytum

Turaniphytum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

Turaniphytum is typically grown as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ornamental value.
