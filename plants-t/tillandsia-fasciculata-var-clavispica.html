Tillandsia fasciculata var. clavispica

Tillandsia fasciculata var. clavispica, commonly known as the Clavispicate Air Plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, bell-shaped inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers.

This variety is popular among air plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
