Tinus microphylla

Tinus microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved tinus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Tinus microphylla plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is often used in ornamental gardening.
