Thymus laevigatus

Thymus laevigatus, commonly known as smooth thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color and are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Smooth thyme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a culinary herb.
