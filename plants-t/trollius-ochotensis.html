Trollius ochotensis

Trollius ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and grow close to the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or tundra habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
