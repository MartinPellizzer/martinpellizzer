Trifolium caudatum

Trifolium caudatum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium caudatum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
