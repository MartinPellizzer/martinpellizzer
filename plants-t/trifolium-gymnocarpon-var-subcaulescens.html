Trifolium gymnocarpon var. subcaulescens

Trifolium gymnocarpon var. subcaulescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed clover, is a variant of the genus Trifolium native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
