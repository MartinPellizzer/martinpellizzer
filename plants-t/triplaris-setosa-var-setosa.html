Triplaris setosa var. setosa

Triplaris setosa var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy triplaris, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
