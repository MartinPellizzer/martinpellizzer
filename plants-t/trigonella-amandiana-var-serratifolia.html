Trigonella amandiana var. serratifolia

Trigonella amandiana var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated fenugreek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through root nodules.
