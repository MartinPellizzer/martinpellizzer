Themeda gigantea var. intermedia

Themeda gigantea var. intermedia, commonly known as the tall blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This grass is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological structure of these habitats.
