Trifolium heterophyllum

Trifolium heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Trifolium heterophyllum is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It is a hardy perennial that can thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
