Turanecio lorentii

Turanecio lorentii, commonly known as the Lorentz's turanecio, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Turanecio lorentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
