Tragopogon hayekii

Tragopogon hayekii, commonly known as the Hayek's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a tall, leafy stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous tubular florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Tragopogon hayekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
