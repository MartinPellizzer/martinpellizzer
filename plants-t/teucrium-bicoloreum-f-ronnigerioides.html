Teucrium bicoloreum f. ronnigerioides

Teucrium bicoloreum f. ronnigerioides, commonly known as the Ronniger's germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact growth habit and long blooming period.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
