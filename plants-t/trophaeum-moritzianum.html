Trophaeum moritzianum

Trophaeum moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking, pale pink to white flowers with a unique, ghost-like appearance, which contribute to its common name. Its botanical features include long, slender roots and a compact, pseudobulb-like structure that helps store water and nutrients.

Due to its limited habitat and specialized requirements, Trophaeum moritzianum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
