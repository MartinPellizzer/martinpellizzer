Tipuana fusca

Tipuana fusca, commonly known as the Andean tipuana, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in the Andean regions of Peru, Bolivia, and Chile. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Tipuana fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for temperate climates.
