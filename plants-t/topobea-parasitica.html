Topobea parasitica

Topobea parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic rock vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized roots.

It typically grows on rocky substrates and is found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is adapted to penetrate the host's tissues, allowing it to extract water and minerals.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by influencing host plant growth and diversity.
