Thalictrum montanum subsp. collinum

Thalictrum montanum subsp.

collinum, commonly known as the mountain meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features slender, branched stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in compound inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
