Tricalysia micrantha

Tricalysia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its showy, bract-based inflorescences. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with two lips that are characteristic of the Acanthaceae family.

Tricalysia micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
