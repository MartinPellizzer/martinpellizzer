Taraxacum stenacrum

Taraxacum stenacrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, pointed leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed ball. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and open areas, often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various soil conditions.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, sharing traits with other dandelion species.
