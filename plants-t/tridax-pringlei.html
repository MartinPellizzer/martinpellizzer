Tridax pringlei

Tridax pringlei, commonly known as the common tridax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Tridax pringlei is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
