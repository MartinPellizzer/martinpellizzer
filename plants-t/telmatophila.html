Telmatophila

Telmatophila, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where they thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Telmatophila species are characterized by their delicate, often colorful flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. They have specialized root systems that help them absorb nutrients from waterlogged substrates.

The genus includes several species, each adapted to different types of wetland habitats across various regions.
