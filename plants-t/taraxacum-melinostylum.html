Taraxacum melinostylum

Taraxacum melinostylum, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a puffball of seeds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
