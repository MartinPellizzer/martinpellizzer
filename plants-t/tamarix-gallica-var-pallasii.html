Tamarix gallica var. pallasii

Tamarix gallica var. pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas' tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in late spring, which are arranged in elongated spikes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive form and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
