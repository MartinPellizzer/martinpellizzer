Thunbergia verdcourtii

Thunbergia verdcourtii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite on the stems, and it produces slender, twisting vines that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Thunbergia verdcourtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical settings.
