Thalia rotundifolia

Thalia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf thalia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are often floating or semi-submerged in water. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Thalia rotundifolia is also used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist environments.
