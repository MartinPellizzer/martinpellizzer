Tetraclinis

Tetraclinis, commonly known as the juniper of the Canary Islands, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. Tetraclinis produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like cones.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
