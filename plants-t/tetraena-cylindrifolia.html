Tetraena cylindrifolia

Tetraena cylindrifolia, commonly known as the cylindrical leaf tetraena, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, succulent leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Tetraena cylindrifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in extreme climates.
