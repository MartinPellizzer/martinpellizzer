Tarachia yoshinagae

Tarachia yoshinagae, commonly known as the Yoshinaga's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to desert environments.

Its structure and growth patterns reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh, dry climates.
