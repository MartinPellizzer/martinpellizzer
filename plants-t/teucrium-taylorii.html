Teucrium taylorii

Teucrium taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Teucrium taylorii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
