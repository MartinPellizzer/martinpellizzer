Tabebuia pallida subsp. pentaphylla

Tabebuia pallida subsp.

pentaphylla, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish hue, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
