Trichocentrum teaguei

Trichocentrum teaguei, commonly known as the Teague's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendant, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Trichocentrum teaguei is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
