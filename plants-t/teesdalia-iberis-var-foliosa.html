Teesdalia iberis var. foliosa

Teesdalia iberis var. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and broad, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The variety 'foliosa' is distinguished by its larger, more foliage-rich leaves compared to the typical form.
