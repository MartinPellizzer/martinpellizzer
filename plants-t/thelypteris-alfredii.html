Thelypteris alfredii

Thelypteris alfredii, commonly known as the Alfred's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori surrounded by a protective indusium. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Thelypteris alfredii is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
