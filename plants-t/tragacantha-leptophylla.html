Tragacantha leptophylla

Tragacantha leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
