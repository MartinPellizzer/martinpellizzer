Torenia erecta

Torenia erecta, commonly known as the whorled torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing or upright growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to container gardening.

Torenia erecta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
