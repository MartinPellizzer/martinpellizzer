Turia moghadd

Turia moghadd, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Turia moghadd thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
