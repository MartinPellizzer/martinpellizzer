Tapeinochilos globiceps

Tapeinochilos globiceps, commonly known as the globular pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, urn-shaped leaves that form a reservoir to trap and digest insects. The plant's structure is adapted to its nutrient-poor environment, with specialized glands that secrete digestive enzymes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellow, and are pollinated by insects.

The plant's ability to thrive in waterlogged soils makes it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
