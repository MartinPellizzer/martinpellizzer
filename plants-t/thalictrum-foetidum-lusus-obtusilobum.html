Thalictrum foetidum lusus obtusilobum

Thalictrum foetidum lusus obtusilobum, commonly known as the blunt-lobed meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually borne on long, slender pedicels.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
