Trigonella sirjaevii

Trigonella sirjaevii, commonly known as the Sirjaev's fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies in its native region.
