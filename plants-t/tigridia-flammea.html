Tigridia flammea

Tigridia flammea, commonly known as the flame tiger flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers with a distinctive black or purple stripe down the central column, resembling a tiger's stripe. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Tigridia flammea is a perennial herb that spreads via rhizomes, making it a hardy and resilient addition to the landscape.
