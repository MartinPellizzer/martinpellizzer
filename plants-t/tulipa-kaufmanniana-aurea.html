Tulipa kaufmanniana aurea

Tulipa kaufmanniana aurea, commonly known as the golden crown tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to deep gold, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe along the edges. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and produces showy blooms in late spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is commonly cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
