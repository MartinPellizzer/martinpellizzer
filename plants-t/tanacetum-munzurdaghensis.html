Tanacetum munzurdaghensis

Tanacetum munzurdaghensis, commonly known as the Munzur Mountain tansy, is a rare flowering plant native to the Munzur Mountains in Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh mountain environments. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while the yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
