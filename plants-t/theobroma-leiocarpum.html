Theobroma leiocarpum

Theobroma leiocarpum, commonly known as the Amazonian cupuacu, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, green fruits resembling small melons. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, white pulp, which is the primary source of its commercial value.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
