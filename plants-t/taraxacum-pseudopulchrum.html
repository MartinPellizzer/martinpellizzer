Taraxacum pseudopulchrum

Taraxacum pseudopulchrum, commonly known as the purple ray flover, is a species of dandelion native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and surrounded by a series of bracts that form a distinctive crown.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with partial shade.
