Tatea portlandioides

Tatea portlandioides, commonly known as the Portland's tatea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Tatea portlandioides is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
