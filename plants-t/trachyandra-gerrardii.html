Trachyandra gerrardii

Trachyandra gerrardii, commonly known as the serrated rosette plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
