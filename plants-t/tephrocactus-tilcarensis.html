Tephrocactus tilcarensis

Tephrocactus tilcarensis, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions as a decorative plant.
