Tulipa montana var. amblyophylla

Tulipa montana var. amblyophylla, commonly known as the mountain tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is a cold-hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.
