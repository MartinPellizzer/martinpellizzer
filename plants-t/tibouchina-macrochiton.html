Tibouchina macrochiton

Tibouchina macrochiton, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple flame, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, glossy, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tibouchina macrochiton thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
