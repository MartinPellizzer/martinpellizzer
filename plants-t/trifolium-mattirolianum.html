Trifolium mattirolianum

Trifolium mattirolianum, commonly known as the Mattiroli's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Trifolium mattirolianum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
