Tridax yecorana

Tridax yecorana, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Tridax yecorana is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
