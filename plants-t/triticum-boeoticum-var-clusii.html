Triticum boeoticum var. clusii

Triticum boeoticum var. clusii, commonly known as the wild einkorn, is a primitive wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. Its seeds are small and typically have a dark, husky covering.

This variety is valued for its historical significance and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional content and potential in sustainable agriculture.
