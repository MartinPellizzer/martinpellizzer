Tontelea passiflora

Tontelea passiflora, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown of filaments. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing supports with its tendrils, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive pattern of stamens and a central reproductive structure.

Tontelea passiflora is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in indigenous communities.
