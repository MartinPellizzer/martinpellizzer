Tirucalia virgata

Tirucalia virgata, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, though it is also used in ornamental horticulture.

Tirucalia virgata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
