Thesium crassifolium

Thesium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thesium crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
