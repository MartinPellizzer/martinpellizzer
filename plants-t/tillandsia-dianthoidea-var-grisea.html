Tillandsia dianthoidea var. grisea

Tillandsia dianthoidea var. grisea, commonly known as the gray tillandsia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, which is essential for its survival in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically grayish-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
