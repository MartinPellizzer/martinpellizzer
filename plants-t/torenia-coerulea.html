Torenia coerulea

Torenia coerulea, commonly known as the blue trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight metallic sheen. Torenia coerulea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
