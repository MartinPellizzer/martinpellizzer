Trichopodium glandulosum

Trichopodium glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular trichopodium, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered with small, glandular structures that secrete substances, possibly for protection or moisture retention. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral, with a distinctive midrib that is often visible. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it forms dense, cushion-like mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory ecosystems in temperate regions.
