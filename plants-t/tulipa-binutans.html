Tulipa binutans

Tulipa binutans, commonly known as the double tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive double-layered flowers, which consist of two sets of petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its blooming period occurs in late spring, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

Tulipa binutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and flower beds.
