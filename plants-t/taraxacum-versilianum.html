Taraxacum versilianum

Taraxacum versilianum, commonly known as the Versilia dandelion, is a rare and endemic species native to the region of Versilia in Italy.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in grassy areas and open woodlands. It features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy texture and a yellow flowering head that develops into a spherical seed ball.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
