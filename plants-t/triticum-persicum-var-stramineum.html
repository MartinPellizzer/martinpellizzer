Triticum persicum var. stramineum

Triticum persicum var. stramineum, commonly known as Persian wheat or Persian rye, is a perennial grass species native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brown florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its seeds are small and have a high nutritional value, making it a potential candidate for forage or grain in certain regions.

However, it is not widely cultivated due to its low yield and specific environmental requirements.
