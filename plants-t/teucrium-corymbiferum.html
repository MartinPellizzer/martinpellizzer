Teucrium corymbiferum

Teucrium corymbiferum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 feet in height. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Teucrium corymbiferum is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for gardens and natural habitats.
