Thuja menziesii var. fastigiata

Thuja menziesii var. fastigiata, commonly known as the Western arborvitae or narrowleaf thuja, is a compact, columnar evergreen conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dense, scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green, arranged in flattened sprays along the branches. The plant has a naturally upright growth habit, making it ideal for use as a hedge or ornamental specimen in landscaping. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green female cones, which are followed by dark blue, waxy seeds.

This variety is highly valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
