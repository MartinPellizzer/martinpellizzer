Talauma sebassa

Talauma sebassa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Talauma sebassa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some local communities.
