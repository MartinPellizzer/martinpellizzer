Tragus latipes

Tragus latipes, commonly known as the hooked tragus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Tragus latipes is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
