Terminalia chebula var. parviflora

Terminalia chebula var. parviflora, commonly known as the chebuli or Indian chebuli, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue or black berries. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine, where its fruit is used for its astringent and antioxidant effects.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
