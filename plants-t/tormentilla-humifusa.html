Tormentilla humifusa

Tormentilla humifusa, commonly known as creeping tormentil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tormentilla humifusa is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
