Teucrium rouyanum

Teucrium rouyanum, commonly known as Rouyan germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.
