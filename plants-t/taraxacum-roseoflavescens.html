Taraxacum roseoflavescens

Taraxacum roseoflavescens, commonly known as the rose-flavescens dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
