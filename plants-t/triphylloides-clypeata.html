Triphylloides clypeata

Triphylloides clypeata, commonly known as the broad-leaved water clover, is a aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Triphylloides clypeata make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
