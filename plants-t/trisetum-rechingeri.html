Trisetum rechingeri

Trisetum rechingeri, commonly known as the Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, three-awned florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Trisetum rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
