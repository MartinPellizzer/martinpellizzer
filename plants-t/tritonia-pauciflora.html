Tritonia pauciflora

Tritonia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered tritonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces pale blue or violet blooms that are typically arranged in tight, compact inflorescences. Tritonia pauciflora thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of garden settings.
