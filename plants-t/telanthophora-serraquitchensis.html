Telanthophora serraquitchensis

Telanthophora serraquitchensis, commonly known as the serrated thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
