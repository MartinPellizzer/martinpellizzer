Taraxacum bulgaricum

Taraxacum bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy, wind-dispersed seed head. It is native to southeastern Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum bulgaricum is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
