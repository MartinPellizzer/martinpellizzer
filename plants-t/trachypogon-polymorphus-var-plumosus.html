Trachypogon polymorphus var. plumosus

Trachypogon polymorphus var. plumosus, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive, fluffy inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its slender, wiry stems and soft, feathery appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
