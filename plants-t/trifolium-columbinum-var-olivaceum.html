Trifolium columbinum var. olivaceum

Trifolium columbinum var. olivaceum, commonly known as the olive clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically olive-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
