Trineuria Ciliaris

Trineuria ciliaris is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is known for its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant's scientific name reflects its unique structure, with "ciliaris" referring to the delicate, hair-like features on its stems. Due to habitat loss and climate change, Trineuria ciliaris is now considered endangered. Conservation efforts are underway to protect and preserve this rare botanical species.
