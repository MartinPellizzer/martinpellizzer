Tamarix lipskyi

Tamarix lipskyi, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces pink or red flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils and extreme temperatures.

Tamarix lipskyi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
