Trichilia poeppigii

Trichilia poeppigii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by winged fruits. The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
