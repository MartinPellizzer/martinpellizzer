Thlaspi hirsutum

Thlaspi hirsutum, commonly known as hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in early spring. It is native to Eurasia and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

Thlaspi hirsutum is often found in alpine or arctic environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
