Tradescantia multiflora

Tradescantia multiflora, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that produce clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
