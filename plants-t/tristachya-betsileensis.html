Tristachya betsileensis

Tristachya betsileensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Tristachya betsileensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
