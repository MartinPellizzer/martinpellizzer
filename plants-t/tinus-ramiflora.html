Tinus ramiflora

Tinus ramiflora, commonly known as the branched tree fern, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive branching pattern in its trunk, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
