Tachigali venusta

Tachigali venusta, commonly known as the golden tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemate inflorescences. Tachigali venusta is known for its ornamental value, particularly due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
