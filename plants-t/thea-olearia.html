Thea olearia

Thea olearia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its fronds, which give it a striking appearance. The plant has a short, fibrous root system and grows in a compact, bushy form. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, creating a feathery texture.

Thea olearia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shady, moist environments.
