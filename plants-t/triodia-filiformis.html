Triodia filiformis

Triodia filiformis, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Triodia filiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
