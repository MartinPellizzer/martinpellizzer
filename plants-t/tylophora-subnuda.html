Tylophora subnuda

Tylophora subnuda, commonly known as the naked tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing seeds with silky, white appendages.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
