Thymus calcicola

Thymus calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Thymus calcicola is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
