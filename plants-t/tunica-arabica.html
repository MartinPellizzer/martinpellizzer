Tunica arabica

Tunica arabica, commonly known as the Arabica coffee bean, is a species of coffee plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and produces small white flowers that develop into red berries containing the seeds used for coffee. The plant thrives in regions with high altitudes, consistent rainfall, and well-drained soil. Its botanical name, *Coffea arabica*, reflects its origin and genetic purity.

Tunica arabica is prized for its complex flavor profile and is the most widely consumed coffee species globally.
