Trichophyllum palustre var. vigens

Trichophyllum palustre var. vigens, commonly known as the bog trichophyllum, is a rare and specialized plant species that thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic wetlands.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and unique reproductive structures that facilitate its survival in harsh, moist environments. The plant's morphology is adapted to low-light conditions, with elongated stems and dense foliage that help it maximize light absorption. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing peat soils and maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered an indicator species for undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
