Taraxacum austrinum f. austrinum

Taraxacum austrinum f. austrinum, commonly known as the Australian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It has yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic seed parachutes.

The species is noted for its resilience and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
