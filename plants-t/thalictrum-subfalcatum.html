Thalictrum subfalcatum

Thalictrum subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and meadows, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Thalictrum subfalcatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
