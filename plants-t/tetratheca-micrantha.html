Tetratheca micrantha

Tetratheca micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring small, narrow leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Tetratheca micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
