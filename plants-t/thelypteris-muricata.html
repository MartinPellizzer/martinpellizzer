Thelypteris muricata

Thelypteris muricata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with serrated, tooth-like margins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine, hairy structures, contributing to its common name.

Thelypteris muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
