Teucrium heterocephalum

Teucrium heterocephalum, commonly known as the variable germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
