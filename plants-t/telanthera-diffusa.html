Telanthera diffusa

Telanthera diffusa, commonly known as the spreading telanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Telanthera diffusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
