Taxanthema globulariifolium

Taxanthema globulariifolium, commonly known as the globular-leaf taxanthema, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, globular growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many drought-tolerant species.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
