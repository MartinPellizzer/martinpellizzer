Tragacantha trigonelloides

Tragacantha trigonelloides, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food thickener. Its growth habit includes a shrub or small tree form, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
