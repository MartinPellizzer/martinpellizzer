Thalictrum gibbosum

Thalictrum gibbosum, commonly known as the rounded meadow-rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
