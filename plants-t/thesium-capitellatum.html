Thesium capitellatum

Thesium capitellatum, commonly known as the clustered thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces dense clusters of yellow flowers. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

Thesium capitellatum is notable for its compact growth habit and distinctive floral arrangement.
