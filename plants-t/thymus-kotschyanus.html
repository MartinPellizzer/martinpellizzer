Thymus kotschyanus

Thymus kotschyanus, commonly known as Kotschyan's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
