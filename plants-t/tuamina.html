Tuamina

Tuamina, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tuamina produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.
