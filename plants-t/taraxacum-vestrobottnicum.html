Taraxacum vestrobottnicum

Taraxacum vestrobottnicum, commonly known as the Vestfold dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed capsule known as a pappus. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
