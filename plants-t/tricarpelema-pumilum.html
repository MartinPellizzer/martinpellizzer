Tricarpelema pumilum

Tricarpelema pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf trichocereus, is a small cactus native to South America.

It features a short, thick stem with spines and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
