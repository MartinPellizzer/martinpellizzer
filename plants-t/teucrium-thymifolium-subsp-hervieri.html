Teucrium thymifolium subsp. hervieri

Teucrium thymifolium subsp.

hervieri, commonly known as Hervier's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves, which resemble those of thyme.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
