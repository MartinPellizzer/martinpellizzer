Tinus junghuhniana

Tinus junghuhniana, commonly known as the Hainan tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
