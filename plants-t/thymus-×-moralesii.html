Thymus × moralesii

Thymus × moralesii, commonly known as the Algerian thyme or Moroccan thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and herbalists.
