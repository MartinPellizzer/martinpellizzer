Taraxacum porrigens

Taraxacum porrigens, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head with white, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
