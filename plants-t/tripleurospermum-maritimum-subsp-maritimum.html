Tripleurospermum maritimum subsp. maritimum

Tripleurospermum maritimum subsp.

maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and striking blue or purple flower heads that resemble holly berries.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along sea shores and coastal dunes. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
