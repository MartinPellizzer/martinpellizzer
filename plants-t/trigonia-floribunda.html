Trigonia floribunda

Trigonia floribunda, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trigonia floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
