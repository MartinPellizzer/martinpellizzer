Tontelea congestiflora

Tontelea congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered tontelea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tontelea congestiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
