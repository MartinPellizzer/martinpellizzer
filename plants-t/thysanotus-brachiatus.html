Thysanotus brachiatus

Thysanotus brachiatus, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple in color and bloom during the warmer months. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
