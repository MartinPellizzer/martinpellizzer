Thelaia

Thelaia, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Thelaia has a compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
