Tournefortia humilis

Tournefortia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tournefortia humilis is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or in forest understories.
