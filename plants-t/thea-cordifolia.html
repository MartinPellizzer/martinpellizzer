Thea cordifolia

Thea cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a glossy appearance, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a strong, sweet scent.

Thea cordifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
