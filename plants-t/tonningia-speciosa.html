Tonningia speciosa

Tonningia speciosa, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, climbing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elliptical, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
