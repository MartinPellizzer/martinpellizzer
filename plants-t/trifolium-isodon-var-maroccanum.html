Trifolium isodon var. maroccanum

Trifolium isodon var. maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan clover, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
