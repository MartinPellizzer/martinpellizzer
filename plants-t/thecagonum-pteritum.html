Thecagonum pteritum

Thecagonum pteritum, commonly known as the featherleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Thecagonum pteritum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
