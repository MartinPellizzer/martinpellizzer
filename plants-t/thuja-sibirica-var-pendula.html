Thuja sibirica var. pendula

Thuja sibirica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Siberian thuja, is a cultivar of the Siberian thuja that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that hang in long, weeping sprays, creating a unique visual effect. This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to eastern Asia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

The plant is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
