Tripogon siamensis

Tripogon siamensis, commonly known as the Siam grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Tripogon siamensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
