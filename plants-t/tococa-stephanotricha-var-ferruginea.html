Tococa stephanotricha var. ferruginea

Tococa stephanotricha var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty tococa, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a woody shrub or small tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, reddish-brown fruits. Its leaves and stems contain alkaloids, which have been traditionally used in South American folk medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical research.
