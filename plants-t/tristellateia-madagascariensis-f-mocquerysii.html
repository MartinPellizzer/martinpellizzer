Tristellateia madagascariensis f. mocquerysii

Tristellateia madagascariensis f. mocquerysii, commonly known as the Madagascan star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers with a complex structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white and yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native region.
