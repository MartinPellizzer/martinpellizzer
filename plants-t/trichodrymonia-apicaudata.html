Trichodrymonia apicaudata

Trichodrymonia apicaudata, commonly known as the red-tipped plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Trichodrymonia apicaudata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
