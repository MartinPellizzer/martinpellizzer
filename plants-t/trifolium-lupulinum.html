Trifolium lupulinum

Trifolium lupulinum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
