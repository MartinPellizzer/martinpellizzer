Talinella xerophila

Talinella xerophila, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit, which allows it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are fleshy and thick, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Talinella xerophila is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
