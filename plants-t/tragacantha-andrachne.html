Tragacantha andrachne

Tragacantha andrachne, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
