Teucrium polium var. virescens

Teucrium polium var. virescens, commonly known as green tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers during the summer months. Its stems are erect and branching, often growing up to 60 cm in height.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and woodland edges.
