Teucrium × robledoi nothosubsp.

robledoi, commonly known as the Robledo's germander, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh conditions.
