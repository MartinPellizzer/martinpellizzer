Terebinthina villosa

Terebinthina villosa, commonly known as the woolly terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Terebinthina villosa is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
