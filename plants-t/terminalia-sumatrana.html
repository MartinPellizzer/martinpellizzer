Terminalia sumatrana

Terminalia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran Terminalia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The plant features elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Terminalia sumatrana is valued for its wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
