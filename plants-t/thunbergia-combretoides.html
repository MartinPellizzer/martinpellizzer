Thunbergia combretoides

Thunbergia combretoides, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Thunbergia combretoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
