Tubocytisus capitatus

Tubocytisus capitatus, commonly known as the yellow flowering buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, elongated fruits. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tubocytisus capitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
