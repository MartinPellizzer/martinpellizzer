Tectaria phanomensis

Tectaria phanomensis, commonly known as the Madagascar shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a dense, leathery texture.

Tectaria phanomensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
