Tanacetum chamomilloides

Tanacetum chamomilloides, commonly known as German chamomile, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas and remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal cultivation.

Its essential oil, extracted from the flowers, is renowned for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
