Thaspium trifoliatum var. atropurpureum

Thaspium trifoliatum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leafed thaspium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically purple or reddish in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and natural habitats.
