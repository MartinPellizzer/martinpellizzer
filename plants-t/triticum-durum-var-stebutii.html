Triticum durum var. stebutii

Triticum durum var. stebutii, commonly known as the stebutii durum wheat, is a variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae, with slender, spike-like inflorescences and small, hard grains. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with narrow, parallel-veined leaves. Its seeds are rich in protein and gluten, making it particularly valuable for pasta and semolina production.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated in regions with well-drained soils.
