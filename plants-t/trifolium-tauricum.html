Trifolium tauricum

Trifolium tauricum, commonly known as Taurian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and parts of southern Europe. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

Trifolium tauricum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
