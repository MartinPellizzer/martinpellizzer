Typhonium trilobatum

Typhonium trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome and produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and are borne in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
