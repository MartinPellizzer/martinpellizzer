Thrincia macrorrhiza

Thrincia macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted thrincia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded environments.

Due to its unique root structure, it has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and ecological restoration projects.
