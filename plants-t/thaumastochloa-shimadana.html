Thaumastochloa shimadana

Thaumastochloa shimadana, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and forms dense clumps. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters that produce small, pale flowers.

Thaumastochloa shimadana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant and graceful growth habit.
