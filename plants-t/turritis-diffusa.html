Turritis diffusa

Turritis diffusa, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
