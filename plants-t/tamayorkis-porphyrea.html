Tamayorkis porphyrea

Tamayorkis porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyritic Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems, which are covered in spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its woody structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in local biodiversity.
