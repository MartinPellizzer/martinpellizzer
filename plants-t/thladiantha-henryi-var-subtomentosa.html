Thladiantha henryi var. subtomentosa

Thladiantha henryi var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silverleaf goji, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The varietal name 'subtomentosa' refers to its densely hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
