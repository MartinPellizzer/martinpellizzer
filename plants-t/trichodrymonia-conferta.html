Trichodrymonia conferta

Trichodrymonia conferta, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It attaches to the branches of host trees, such as acacias, and derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized root structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Trichodrymonia conferta plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to biodiversity and providing food for various wildlife species.
