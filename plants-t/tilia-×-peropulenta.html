Tilia × peropulenta

Tilia × peropulenta, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically serrated and have a pale green to grayish underside. The tree produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

The bark is smooth when young but becomes fissured and scaly with age, providing a distinctive appearance.
