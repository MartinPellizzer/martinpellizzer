Trigonella monantha subsp. noeana

Trigonella monantha subsp.

noeana, commonly known as the Armenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are small and oblong, typically found in pods that mature in late summer.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils.
