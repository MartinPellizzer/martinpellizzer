Tylecodon racemosus

Tylecodon racemosus, commonly known as the racemose tylecodon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in raceme clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically short and cylindrical, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Tylecodon racemosus is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and drought tolerance.
