Tragacantha samamensis

Tragacantha samamensis, commonly known as the Samam tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, adapting to various arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
