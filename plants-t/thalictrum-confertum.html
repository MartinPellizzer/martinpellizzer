Thalictrum confertum

Thalictrum confertum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, usually yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces numerous slender, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Thalictrum confertum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
