Thalia geniculata var. pubescens

Thalia geniculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy thalia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
