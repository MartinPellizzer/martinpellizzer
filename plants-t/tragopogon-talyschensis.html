Tragopogon talyschensis

Tragopogon talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spiky, bristly seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

Tragopogon talyschensis is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
