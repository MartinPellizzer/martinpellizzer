Triodia roscida

Triodia roscida, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and sharp, needle-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Triodia roscida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small desert animals.

Its resilience and hardy nature make it a common feature of the Australian outback's vegetation.
