Tripudianthes sanitii

Tripudianthes sanitii, commonly known as the sacred flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a fiery display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and pale in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
