Teucrium × pseudothymifolium

Teucrium × pseudothymifolium, commonly known as hybrid thyme.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
