Thalictrum × spurium

Thalictrum × spurium, commonly known as the meadow rue hybrid, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Thalictrum × spurium is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks across temperate regions.
