Tetracera fagifolia

Tetracera fagifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tetracera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

Tetracera fagifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
