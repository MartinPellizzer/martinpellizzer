Tilia ruprechtii

Tilia ruprechtii, commonly known as the Mongolian linden, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain three seeds each. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in various crafts and construction.
