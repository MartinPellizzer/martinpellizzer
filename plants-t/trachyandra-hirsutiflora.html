Trachyandra hirsutiflora

Trachyandra hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered trachyandra, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen perennial plant characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
