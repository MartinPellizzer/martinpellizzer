Tabebuia litoralis

Tabebuia litoralis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
