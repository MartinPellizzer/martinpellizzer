Trichophorum cespitosum nothosubsp. foersteri

Trichophorum cespitosum nothosubsp.

foersteri, commonly known as the Foerster's sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland environments. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in marshy areas and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils. It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical classification highlights its close relationship to other sedges within the Cyperaceae family.
