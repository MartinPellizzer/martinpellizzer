Tetrorchidiopsis oppositifolia

Tetrorchidiopsis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved orchid, is a rare and delicate species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
