Tulipa kesselringii

Tulipa kesselringii, commonly known as the Kesselringii tulip, is a rare and striking member of the tulip family native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or containers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
