Trigonella euboica

Trigonella euboica, commonly known as Euboic bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the spring, followed by elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds.

Trigonella euboica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
