Themeda quadrivalvis var. helferi

Themeda quadrivalvis var. helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically hairy along the margins. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, bearing small, pale-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local fauna and maintaining soil stability.
