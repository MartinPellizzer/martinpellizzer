Tocoyena macrophylla

Tocoyena macrophylla, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often colorful leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a water-holding structure at the center. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its vibrant foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

Tocoyena macrophylla also plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and water for various small organisms.
