Teucrium heterotrichum

Teucrium heterotrichum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, blue-gray hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Blue germander thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open sunny areas.
