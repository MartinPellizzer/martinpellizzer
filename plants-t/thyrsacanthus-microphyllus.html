Thyrsacanthus microphyllus

Thyrsacanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf thyrsa, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences called thyrses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
