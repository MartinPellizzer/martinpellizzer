Turnera chamaedrifolia

Turnera chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the creeping turnera, is a perennial flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pink in color, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.
