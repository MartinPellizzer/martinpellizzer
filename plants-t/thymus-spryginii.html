Thymus spryginii

Thymus spryginii, commonly known as the Crimean thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is closely related to other thyme species and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus spryginii is also noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
