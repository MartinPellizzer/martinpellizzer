Taraxacum leroyi

Taraxacum leroyi, commonly known as the Leroy's dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a yellow composite flower that develops into a distinctive sphere of white, hairy seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Taraxacum leroyi is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelions.
