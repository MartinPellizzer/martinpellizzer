Taraxacum sagittarium

Taraxacum sagittarium, commonly known as the arrowhead dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that point downward, giving it its common name. The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem that bears a bright yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum sagittarium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
