Tylophora tengii

Tylophora tengii, commonly known as the Chinese moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, night-blooming flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Tylophora tengii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
