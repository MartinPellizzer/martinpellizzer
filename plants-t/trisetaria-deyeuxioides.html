Trisetaria deyeuxioides

Trisetaria deyeuxioides, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spikelet that bears small, two-lobed flowers.

Trisetaria deyeuxioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
