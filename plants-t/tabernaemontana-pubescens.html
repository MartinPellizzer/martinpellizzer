Tabernaemontana pubescens

Tabernaemontana pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
