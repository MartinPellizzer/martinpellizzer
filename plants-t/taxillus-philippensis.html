Taxillus philippensis

Taxillus philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees in the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and has a distinctive appearance with small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which aids in seed dispersal.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree over time, making it an important consideration in forest ecosystems.
