Tillaea latifolia

Tillaea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tillaea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Tillaea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
