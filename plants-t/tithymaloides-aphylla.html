Tithymaloides aphylla

Tithymaloides aphylla, commonly known as the leafless tithymal, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in harsh, dry environments and is adapted to store water in its stems.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
