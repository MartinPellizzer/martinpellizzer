Tuberaria guttata var. serrata

Tuberaria guttata var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with distinctive spots on the petals. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name. This perennial herb is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
