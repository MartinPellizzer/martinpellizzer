Tradescantia diaphana

Tradescantia diaphana, commonly known as the glasshouse spider plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in full sun. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance yet visually appealing plant.
