Tashiroea villosa

Tashiroea villosa, commonly known as the hairy tashiroea, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tashiroea villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
