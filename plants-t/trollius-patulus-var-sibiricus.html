Trollius patulus var. sibiricus

Trollius patulus var. sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are basal and often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
