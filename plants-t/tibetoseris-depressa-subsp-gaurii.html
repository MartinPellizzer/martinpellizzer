Tibetoseris depressa subsp. gaurii

Tibetoseris depressa subsp.

gaurii, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 meters. Its leaves are basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
