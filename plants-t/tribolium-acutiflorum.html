Tribolium acutiflorum

Tribolium acutiflorum, commonly known as the sharp-flowered tribolium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive sharp or pointed appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, hard-coated seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Tribolium acutiflorum is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
