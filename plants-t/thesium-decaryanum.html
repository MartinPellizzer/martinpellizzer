Thesium decaryanum

Thesium decaryanum, commonly known as the golden torch or decary's thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and fibrous.
