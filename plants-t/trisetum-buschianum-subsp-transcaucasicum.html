Trisetum buschianum subsp. transcaucasicum

Trisetum buschianum subsp.

transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it has small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
