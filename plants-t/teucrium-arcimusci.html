Teucrium arcimusci

Teucrium arcimusci, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium arcimusci is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
