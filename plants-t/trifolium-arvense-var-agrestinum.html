Trifolium arvense var. agrestinum

Trifolium arvense var. agrestinum, commonly known as field clover, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Trifolium arvense var. agrestinum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
