Trichosporum cryptanthum

Trichosporum cryptanthum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a short, fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing close to the ground.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
