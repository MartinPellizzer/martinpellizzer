Tradescantella incarnata

Tradescantella incarnata, commonly known as the pink spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lavender. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Tradescantella incarnata is known for its early spring blooming period and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
