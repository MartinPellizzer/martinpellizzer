Trichinium helichrysoides

Trichinium helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Trichinium helichrysoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and bright blooms.
