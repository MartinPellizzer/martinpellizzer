Tephrosia elata var. heckmanniana

Tephrosia elata var. heckmanniana, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
