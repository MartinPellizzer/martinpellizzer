Teucrium polium subsp. carthaginense

Teucrium polium subsp.

carthaginense, commonly known as Carthaginian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often gray-green in color, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
