Tingulonga paniculata

Tingulonga paniculata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that appear in panicles during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species.

The botanical features of Tingulonga paniculata make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
