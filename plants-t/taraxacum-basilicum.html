Taraxacum basilicum

Taraxacum basilicum, commonly known as Italian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species with potential medicinal and ornamental uses.
