Tanarius myrianthus

Tanarius myrianthus, commonly known as the velvet leaf or velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its roots and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, though more research is needed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
