Tridens pulchellus

Tridens pulchellus, commonly known as the showy tridens, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and delicate, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Tridens pulchellus is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
