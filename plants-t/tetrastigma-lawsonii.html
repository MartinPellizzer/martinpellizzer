Tetrastigma lawsonii

Tetrastigma lawsonii, commonly known as the Chinese ivy or climbing raisin vine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various growing conditions, though it requires support for proper growth.
