Thymus × klasterskyi

Thymus × klasterskyi, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, fragrant leaves with a strong thyme aroma, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in herbal remedies due to its medicinal properties.

Thymus × klasterskyi is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus serpyllum and Thymus pseudolanuginosus.
