Thesium confusum

Thesium confusum, commonly known as the confused thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
