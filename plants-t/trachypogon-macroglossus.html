Trachypogon macroglossus

Trachypogon macroglossus, commonly known as the large-lipped grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a ornamental plant.
