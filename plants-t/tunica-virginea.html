Tunica virginea

Tunica virginea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and a distinctive, waxy coating on its leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in coastal areas and dry soils. Its leaves are typically fleshy and covered in a thick, powdery layer that helps reduce water loss.

Tunica virginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
