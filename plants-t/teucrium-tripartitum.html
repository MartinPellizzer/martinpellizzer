Teucrium tripartitum

Teucrium tripartitum, commonly known as three-parted germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of purple or pink flowers during the summer months, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium tripartitum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
