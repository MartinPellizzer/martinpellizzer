Tamonea juncea

Tamonea juncea, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the Tamaricaceae family and typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It has a woody stem and often forms shrubs or small trees.

Tamonea juncea is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water availability.
