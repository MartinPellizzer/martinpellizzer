Tephrosia aurantiaca subsp. rufopilosa

Tephrosia aurantiaca subsp.

rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on sturdy, hairy stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets that have a distinctive reddish underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
