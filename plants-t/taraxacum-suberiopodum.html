Taraxacum suberiopodum

Taraxacum suberiopodum, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem topped with a yellow composite flower head. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
