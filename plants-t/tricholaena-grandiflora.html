Tricholaena grandiflora

Tricholaena grandiflora, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Tricholaena grandiflora is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
