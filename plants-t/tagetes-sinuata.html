Tagetes sinuata

Tagetes sinuata, commonly known as the Mexican marigold or fringed marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Tagetes sinuata thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as natural pest repellents due to their strong scent.
