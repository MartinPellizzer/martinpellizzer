Tigridia speciosa

Tigridia speciosa, commonly known as the "tiger flower," is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting black markings. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is known for its ability to bloom profusely in late spring to early summer.

Tigridia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
