Tephrosia hypoleuca

Tephrosia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters, featuring pinnate leaves with 7–13 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are encased in a hard, brown pod, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Tephrosia hypoleuca is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
