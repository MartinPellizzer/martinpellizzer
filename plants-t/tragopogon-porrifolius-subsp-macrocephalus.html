Tragopogon porrifolius subsp. macrocephalus

Tragopogon porrifolius subsp.

macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant burrweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads and bristly, spiny seed heads that resemble burrs.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the stems are upright and hairy.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
