Thlaspi montanum var. californicum

Thlaspi montanum var. californicum, commonly known as Sierra Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Sierra Wallflower is known for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.
