Thymus zygis var. capitatus

Thymus zygis var. capitatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
