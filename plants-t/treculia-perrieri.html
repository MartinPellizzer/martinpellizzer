Treculia perrieri

Treculia perrieri, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to Central and West Africa, where it thrives in humid, lowland forests. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring thick, rough bark and large, lobed leaves. Its fruit is a round, greenish-yellow drupe that matures over several months.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and its role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
