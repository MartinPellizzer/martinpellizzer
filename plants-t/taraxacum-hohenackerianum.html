Taraxacum hohenackerianum

Taraxacum hohenackerianum, commonly known as the Hohenacker's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
