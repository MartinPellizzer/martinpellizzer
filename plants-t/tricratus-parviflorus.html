Tricratus parviflorus

Tricratus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower tricratus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
