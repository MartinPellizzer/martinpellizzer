Thenardia suaveolens

Thenardia suaveolens, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.

The botanical characteristics of Thenardia suaveolens make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
