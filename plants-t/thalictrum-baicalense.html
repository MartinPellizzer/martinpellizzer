Thalictrum baicalense

Thalictrum baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Mongolia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers with prominent stamens, typical of the genus Thalictrum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
