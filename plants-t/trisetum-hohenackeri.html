Trisetum hohenackeri

Trisetum hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Trisetum hohenackeri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
