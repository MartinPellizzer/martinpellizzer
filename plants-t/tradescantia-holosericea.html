Tradescantia holosericea

Tradescantia holosericea, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its resilience and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
