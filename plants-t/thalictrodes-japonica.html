Thalictrodes japonica

Thalictrodes japonica, commonly known as the Japanese thalictroid, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinct, often fragrant scent. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers have five petals and a prominent, tubular corolla that facilitates pollination.

Thalictrodes japonica prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
