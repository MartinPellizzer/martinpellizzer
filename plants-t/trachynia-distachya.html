Trachynia distachya

Trachynia distachya, commonly known as the two-bract grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a small annual grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of two bracts, each bearing a single spikelet, which is a characteristic feature of the species.

Trachynia distachya is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
