Trichilia kisoko

Trichilia kisoko, commonly known as African shea, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by hard, woody pods containing oily seeds. These seeds are rich in fat and are a key source of shea butter, which has various industrial and cosmetic applications.

Trichilia kisoko thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
