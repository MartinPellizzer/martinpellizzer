Tephrosia coriacea

Tephrosia coriacea, commonly known as rough tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, and often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky soils.

Tephrosia coriacea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
