Trichosanthes truncata

Trichosanthes truncata, commonly known as the Chinese flame vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, greenish-yellow fruits that mature to a bright orange-red color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Trichosanthes truncata make it a notable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
