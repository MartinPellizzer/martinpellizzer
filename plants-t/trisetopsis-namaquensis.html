Trisetopsis namaquensis

Trisetopsis namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua daisies, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, herbaceous stems.

Trisetopsis namaquensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
