Tilia caroliniana var. vagans

Tilia caroliniana var. vagans, commonly known as the Carolina hornbeam, is a small deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
