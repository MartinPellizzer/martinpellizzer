Thymus zygiformis var. euxinus

Thymus zygiformis var. euxinus, commonly known as the Crimean thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit with small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
