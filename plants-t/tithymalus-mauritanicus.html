Tithymalus mauritanicus

Tithymalus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually small and yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tithymalus mauritanicus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
