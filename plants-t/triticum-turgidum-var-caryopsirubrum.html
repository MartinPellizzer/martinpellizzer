Triticum turgidum var. caryopsirubrum

Triticum turgidum var. caryopsirubrum, commonly known as the red rye, is a botanical variant of rye characterized by its reddish spikelets and grains.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with a tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves. Its flowers are spike-like, with each spikelet containing two to three florets.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional agriculture and forage production.
