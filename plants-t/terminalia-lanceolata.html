Terminalia lanceolata

Terminalia lanceolata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
