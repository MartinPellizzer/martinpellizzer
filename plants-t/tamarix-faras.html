Tamarix faras

Tamarix faras, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its feathery, greenish-gray foliage and pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Tamarix faras is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
