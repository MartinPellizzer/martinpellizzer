Turnera caatingana

Turnera caatingana, commonly known as the caatinga turnera, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature, contributing to its dispersal in the dry forest ecosystems.

Turnera caatingana is adapted to withstand drought and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
