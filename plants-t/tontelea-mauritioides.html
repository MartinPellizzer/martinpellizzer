Tontelea mauritioides

Tontelea mauritioides, commonly known as the African silver fern, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that gives it a silvery appearance.

Tontelea mauritioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
