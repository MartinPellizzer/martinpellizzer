Terminalia grandiflora

Terminalia grandiflora, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and riverine habitats.

Terminalia grandiflora is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
