Trifolium barbigerum var. andrewsii

Trifolium barbigerum var. andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
