Taraxacum tumentifrons

Taraxacum tumentifrons, commonly known as the tumid-fronded dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, greenish-yellow stem that becomes more prominent as the plant matures. The leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
