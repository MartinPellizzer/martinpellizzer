Tetrasynandra pubescens

Tetrasynandra pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tetrasynandra, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense terminal cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
