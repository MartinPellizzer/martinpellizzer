Teucrium albicaule

Teucrium albicaule, commonly known as white-stemmed germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often hairy stems that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in hue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Teucrium albicaule is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
