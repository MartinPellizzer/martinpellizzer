Tulipa koyuncui

Tulipa koyuncui, commonly known as the Turk's Cap tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces showy blooms in early spring, often with a dark central spot. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its hardiness in cooler climates.
