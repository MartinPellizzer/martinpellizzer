Triosteum illinoense

Triosteum illinoense, commonly known as Illinois triosteum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family, which includes willows and evening primroses. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
