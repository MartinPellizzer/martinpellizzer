Tragacantha kirrindica

Tragacantha kirrindica, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and long, twisted pods. The plant is known for producing a valuable gum resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

Its deep root system allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
