Tragacantha amara

Tragacantha amara, commonly known as the Astragalus amara, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional remedies.

Tragacantha amara is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid habitats.
