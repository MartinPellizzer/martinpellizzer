Trigonella persica var. late-alata

Trigonella persica var. late-alata, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It is often cultivated for forage and as a green manure crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
