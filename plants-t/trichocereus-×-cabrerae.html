Trichocereus × cabrerae

Trichocereus × cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's torch cactus, is a hybrid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which are typical features of the Trichocereus genus. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
