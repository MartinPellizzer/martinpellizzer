Thunbergia alata var. reticulata

Thunbergia alata var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive reticulate (net-like) venation pattern, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
