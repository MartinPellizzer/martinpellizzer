Tragacantha tigridis

Tragacantha tigridis, commonly known as the Persian gum or tigridian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its economic importance in the production of gum arabic.
