Tetrastigma hemsleyanum

Tetrastigma hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Chinese crossvine, is a woody climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
