Thymus × dimorphus steppaceus

Thymus × dimorphus steppaceus, commonly known as the steppe thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It features aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Thymus × dimorphus steppaceus is also used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
