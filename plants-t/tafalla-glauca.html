Tafalla glauca

Tafalla glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous plant, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and small, delicate flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Tafalla glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
