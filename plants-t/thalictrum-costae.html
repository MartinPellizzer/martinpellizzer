Thalictrum costae

Thalictrum costae, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are compound with numerous narrow leaflets.

Thalictrum costae is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
