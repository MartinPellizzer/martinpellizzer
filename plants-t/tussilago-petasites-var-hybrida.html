Tussilago petasites var. hybrida

Tussilago petasites var. hybrida, commonly known as the European meadow cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features a rosette of broad, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that form a dense basal cluster. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
