Teucrium filiforme

Teucrium filiforme, commonly known as slender germander, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, narrow leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky habitats.

Teucrium filiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
