Tina chrysophylla

Tina chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf plant, is a striking species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow.

Tina chrysophylla thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
