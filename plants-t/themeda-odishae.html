Themeda odishae

Themeda odishae, commonly known as the Odisha grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. It plays a significant role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.

Its resilience to drought and grazing makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
