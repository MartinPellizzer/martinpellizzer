Tetramorphaea bruguieriana

Tetramorphaea bruguieriana, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
