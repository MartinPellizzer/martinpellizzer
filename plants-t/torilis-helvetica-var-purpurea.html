Torilis helvetica var. purpurea

Torilis helvetica var. purpurea, commonly known as purple hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in early spring, producing a distinctive purple color that adds visual interest to the landscape.
