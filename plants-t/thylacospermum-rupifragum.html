Thylacospermum rupifragum

Thylacospermum rupifragum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Thylacospermum rupifragum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
