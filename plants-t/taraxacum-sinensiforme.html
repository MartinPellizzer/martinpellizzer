Taraxacum sinensiforme

Taraxacum sinensiforme, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces a crown of bright yellow ray florets. Native to eastern Asia, particularly China, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a "pappus," which aids in its propagation.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
