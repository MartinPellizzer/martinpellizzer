Thalictrum minus subsp. matritense

Thalictrum minus subsp.

matritense, commonly known as the Spanish meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
