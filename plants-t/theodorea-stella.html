Theodorea stella

Theodorea stella, commonly known as the star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from white to deep red, and are highly fragrant.

Theodorea stella thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
