Tephrosia capensis var. hirsuta

Tephrosia capensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its roots are known for containing bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
