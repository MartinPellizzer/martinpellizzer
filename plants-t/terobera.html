Terobera

Terobera, commonly known as the "golden orchid," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

Terobera is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
