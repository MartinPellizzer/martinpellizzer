Teucrium bullatum

Teucrium bullatum, commonly known as the purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. Teucrium bullatum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
