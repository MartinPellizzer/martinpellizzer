Thalictrum nitidulum

Thalictrum nitidulum, commonly known as the smooth meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Thalictrum nitidulum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
