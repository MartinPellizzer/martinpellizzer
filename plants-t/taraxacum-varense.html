Taraxacum varense

Taraxacum varense, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere, known as a pappus, which is dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Taraxacum varense is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
