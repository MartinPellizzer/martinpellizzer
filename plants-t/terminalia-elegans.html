Terminalia elegans

Terminalia elegans, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that turns red when mature, containing three winged seeds.

Terminalia elegans thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
