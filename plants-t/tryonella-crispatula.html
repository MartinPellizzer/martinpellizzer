Tryonella crispatula

Tryonella crispatula, commonly known as the curly leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, finely toothed, silver-gray leaves that are often curled or crisped at the edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments and is often found in coastal and desert habitats.

Its silvery foliage and drought tolerance make it a hardy, ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
