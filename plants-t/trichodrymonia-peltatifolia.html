Trichodrymonia peltatifolia

Trichodrymonia peltatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed trichodrymon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
