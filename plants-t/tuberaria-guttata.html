Tuberaria guttata

Tuberaria guttata, commonly known as the spotted rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with four petals. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. Tuberaria guttata is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Its growth is typically low to the ground, with flowering occurring in late spring to early summer.
