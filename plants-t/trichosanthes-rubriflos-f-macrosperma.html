Trichosanthes rubriflos f. macrosperma

Trichosanthes rubriflos f. macrosperma, commonly known as the red-flowered trumpet vine, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus, characterized by its distinctive orange-red flowers.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, featuring tendrils that aid in its vine-like growth habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a trumpet-shaped corolla, often with a yellow throat. Its fruits are large, fleshy, and orange-colored, containing numerous seeds.

This plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
