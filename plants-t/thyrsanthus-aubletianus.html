Thyrsanthus aubletianus

Thyrsanthus aubletianus, commonly known as the golden thorn, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. Thyrsanthus aubletianus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
