Taraxacum faeroense

Taraxacum faeroense, commonly known as the Faroe dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Faroe Islands.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, which is part of the Asteraceae family. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single, hollow stem topped with a crown of yellow flowers. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head with white, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is adapted to the harsh climate of the North Atlantic and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
