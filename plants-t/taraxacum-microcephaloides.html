Taraxacum microcephaloides

Taraxacum microcephaloides, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant has a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a short, sturdy stem that bears a single flower head. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showing resilience to harsh conditions.
