Trisetum trisetaria

Trisetum trisetaria, commonly known as the three-bristle grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny spikelets. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and a distinctive silver-green coloration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
