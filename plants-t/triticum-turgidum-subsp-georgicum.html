Triticum turgidum subsp. georgicum

Triticum turgidum subsp.

georgicum, commonly known as Georgian wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its seeds are small and hardy.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience to environmental stress.
