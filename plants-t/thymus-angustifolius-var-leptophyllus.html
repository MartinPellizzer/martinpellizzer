Thymus angustifolius var. leptophyllus

Thymus angustifolius var. leptophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
