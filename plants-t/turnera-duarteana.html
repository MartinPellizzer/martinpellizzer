Turnera duarteana

Turnera duarteana, commonly known as the Duarte's turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant has a woody stem with a fibrous texture.

Turnera duarteana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
