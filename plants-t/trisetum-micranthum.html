Trisetum micranthum

Trisetum micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodoak, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Trisetum micranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
