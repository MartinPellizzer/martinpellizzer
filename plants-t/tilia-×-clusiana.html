Tilia × clusiana

Tilia × clusiana, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Tilia × clusiana is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
