Trichelostylis quinquangularis

Trichelostylis quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, five-angled seed pods. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Trichelostylis quinquangularis is often found in heathlands and open forests, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
