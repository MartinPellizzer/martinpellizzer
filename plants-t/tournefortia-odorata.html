Tournefortia odorata

Tournefortia odorata, commonly known as the sweet shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a rough surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
