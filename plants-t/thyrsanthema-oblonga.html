Thyrsanthema oblonga

Thyrsanthema oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing a showy appearance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
