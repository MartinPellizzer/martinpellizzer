Tapinanthus lugardii

Tapinanthus lugardii, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from host plants.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Namibia. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its yellowish-green leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows on the roots of other plants, such as Acacia or Euphorbia, and can be found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in the ecological interactions of its habitat.
