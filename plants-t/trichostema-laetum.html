Trichostema laetum

Trichostema laetum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs.

Trichostema laetum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
