Thunia mastersiana

Thunia mastersiana, commonly known as the Masters' sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in saline environments and is often found in tidal zones and sandy beaches. Its adaptability to saltwater makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Thunia mastersiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with saline conditions.
