Trisetum canescens

Trisetum canescens, commonly known as the hairy wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, three-awned florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Trisetum canescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
