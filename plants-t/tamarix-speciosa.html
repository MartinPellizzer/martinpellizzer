Tamarix speciosa

Tamarix speciosa, commonly known as the silver leaf tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces fluffy, pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in elongated clusters. Tamarix speciosa is adapted to dry, saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid environments.
