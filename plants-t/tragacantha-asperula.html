Tragacantha asperula

Tragacantha asperula, commonly known as the rough tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its distinctive feature is the production of a viscous gum from its exuding sap, which has been historically used in various industries.

Tragacantha asperula thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.
