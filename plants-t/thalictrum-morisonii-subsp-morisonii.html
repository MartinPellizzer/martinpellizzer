Thalictrum morisonii subsp. morisonii

Thalictrum morisonii subsp.

morisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Thalictrum morisonii subsp.

morisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
