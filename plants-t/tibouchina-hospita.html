Tibouchina hospita

Tibouchina hospita, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Tibouchina hospita is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
