Thelocephala riparia

Thelocephala riparia, commonly known as the river tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, often saline environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in whorls around the stem, which helps reduce water loss.

Thelocephala riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
