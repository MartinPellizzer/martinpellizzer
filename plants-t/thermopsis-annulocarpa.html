Thermopsis annulocarpa

Thermopsis annulocarpa, commonly known as the ringed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Thermopsis annulocarpa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
