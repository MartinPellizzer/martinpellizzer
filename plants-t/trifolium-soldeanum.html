Trifolium soldeanum

Trifolium soldeanum, commonly known as Soldean's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
