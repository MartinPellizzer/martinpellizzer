Trixis sonchoides

Trixis sonchoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

Trixis sonchoides is adapted to harsh environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
