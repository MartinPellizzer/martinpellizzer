Taxillus limprichtii var. longiflorus

Taxillus limprichtii var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host through haustoria.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant has small, alternate leaves and produces clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species plays an ecological role in its ecosystem by providing food for certain bird species and contributing to nutrient cycling.
