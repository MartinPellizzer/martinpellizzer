Toppingia

Toppingia, commonly known as the topinambour or globe artichoke, is a root vegetable belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, knobby roots that resemble a large, knotted tuber. The plant produces edible roots that are typically harvested before the plant flowers. Toppingia is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

Its leaves are fern-like and can grow up to several feet in height, adding a distinctive visual element to gardens.
