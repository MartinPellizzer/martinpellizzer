Trema tomentosum var. asperum

Trema tomentosum var. asperum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed staghorn sumac, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grayish, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil conditions and light levels.
