Taxillus tricostatus

Taxillus tricostatus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Taxillus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it produces its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Taxillus tricostatus is often found in forested regions of South America and is part of the Santalales order.
