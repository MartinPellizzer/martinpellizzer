Thymus ciliatopubescens f. albidus

Thymus ciliatopubescens f. albidus, commonly known as the white-haired thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive white or pale pubescence, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications for its mild, thyme-like flavor.

This cultivar is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
