Tristagma nivale var. angustifolia

Tristagma nivale var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snowbell, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is typically found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
