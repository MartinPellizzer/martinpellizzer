Triumfetta guazumifolia

Triumfetta guazumifolia, commonly known as the Mexican false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Triumfetta guazumifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
