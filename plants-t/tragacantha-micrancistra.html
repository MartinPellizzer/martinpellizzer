Tragacantha micrancistra

Tragacantha micrancistra, commonly known as the small-fruited tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, papery pods that contain small, rounded seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Tragacantha micrancistra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
