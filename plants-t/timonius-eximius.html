Timonius eximius

Timonius eximius, commonly known as the noble timonius, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and silver-green foliage, which reflect its adaptation to cool, arid conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, enabling it to access water from underground sources.

Due to habitat loss, Timonius eximius is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
