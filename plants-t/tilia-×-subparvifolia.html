Tilia × subparvifolia

Tilia × subparvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
