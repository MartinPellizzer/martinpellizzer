Trigonella monantha var. brachycarpa

Trigonella monantha var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, compressed pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include hairy stems, aromatic foliage, and a preference for well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and adaptability to various growing conditions.
