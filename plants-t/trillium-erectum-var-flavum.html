Trillium erectum var. flavum

Trillium erectum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a yellowish tinge. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower with three petals and three sepals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in rich, moist woodlands and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
