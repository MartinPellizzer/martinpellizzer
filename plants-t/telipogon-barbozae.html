Telipogon barbozae

Telipogon barbozae, commonly known as the Barboza's spider orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, delicate flowers that often resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in rocky or forested areas with high humidity. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited population, Telipogon barbozae is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and environmental changes.
