Thymus jankae var. serbicus

Thymus jankae var. serbicus, commonly known as Serbian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkans.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
