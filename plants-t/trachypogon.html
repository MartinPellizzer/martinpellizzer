Trachypogon

Trachypogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in panicles. Trachypogon species are native to various regions of the Southern Hemisphere, including Australia, South America, and Africa.

These plants are often found in dry, open habitats and are adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
