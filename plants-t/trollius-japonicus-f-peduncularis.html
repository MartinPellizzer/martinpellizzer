Trollius japonicus f. peduncularis

Trollius japonicus f. peduncularis, commonly known as the Japanese globeflower, is a variant of the globeflower species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
