Trigonotis nakaii

Trigonotis nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are slender and often branching. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Trigonotis nakaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
