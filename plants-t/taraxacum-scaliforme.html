Taraxacum scaliforme

Taraxacum scaliforme, commonly known as the scalyl leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped leaf margins and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the stem is usually unbranched, bearing the flower head at the top.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats.
