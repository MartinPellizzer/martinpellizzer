Teesdalia regularis

Teesdalia regularis, commonly known as the common larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces tall spikes of blue, purple, or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Teesdalia regularis is known for its ornamental value in gardens but contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
