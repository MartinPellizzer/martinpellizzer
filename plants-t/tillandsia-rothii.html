Tillandsia rothii

Tillandsia rothii, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant's silver-gray foliage is due to a dense covering of trichomes, which help in water absorption and sunlight reflection. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia rothii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
