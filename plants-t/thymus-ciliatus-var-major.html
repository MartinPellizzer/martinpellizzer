Thymus ciliatus var. major

Thymus ciliatus var. major, commonly known as the large fringed thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable both in culinary and medicinal applications.
