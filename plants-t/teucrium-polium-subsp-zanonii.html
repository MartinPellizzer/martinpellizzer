Teucrium polium subsp. zanonii

Teucrium polium subsp.

zanonii, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
