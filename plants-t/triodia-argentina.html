Triodia argentina

Triodia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine triodia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and adapted to conserve water, reflecting its drought-tolerant nature.

Triodia argentina is often found in sandy or rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
