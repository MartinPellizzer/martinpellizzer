Tragacantha erinacea

Tragacantha erinacea, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, serrated edge.

Tragacantha erinacea is valued for its mucilage-rich sap, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
