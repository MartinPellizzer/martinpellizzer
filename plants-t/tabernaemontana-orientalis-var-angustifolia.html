Tabernaemontana orientalis var. angustifolia

Tabernaemontana orientalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
