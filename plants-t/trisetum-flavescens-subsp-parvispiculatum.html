Trisetum flavescens subsp. parvispiculatum

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

parvispiculatum, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a grass species native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, often pale green inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, with pale yellow to golden hues.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
