Telipogon bruchmuelleri

Telipogon bruchmuelleri, commonly known as the Bruchmueller's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and its unique inflorescence of small, spider-like flowers with long, thin petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by small insects, and it is considered a critically endangered species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical treasure.
