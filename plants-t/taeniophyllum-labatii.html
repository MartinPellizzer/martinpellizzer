Taeniophyllum labatii

Taeniophyllum labatii, commonly known as the slender pygmy orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
