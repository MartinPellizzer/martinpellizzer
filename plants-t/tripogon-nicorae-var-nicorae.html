Tripogon nicorae var. nicorae

Tripogon nicorae var. nicorae, commonly known as the Nicora's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, green blades that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a low-maintenance ground cover.
