Tecoma pedicellata

Tecoma pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the warmer months.

Tecoma pedicellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
