Tephrosia mariana

Tephrosia mariana, commonly known as the Florida tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia mariana is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands.
