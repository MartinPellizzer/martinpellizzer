Tetragonolobus palaestinus

Tetragonolobus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine shrub, is a small, woody perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a tetrahedral pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
