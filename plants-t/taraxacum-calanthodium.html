Taraxacum calanthodium

Taraxacum calanthodium, commonly known as the golden yellow dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
