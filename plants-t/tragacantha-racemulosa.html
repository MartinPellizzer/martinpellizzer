Tragacantha racemulosa

Tragacantha racemulosa, commonly known as the Persian gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive yellowish-green pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
