Typha latifolia subsp. maresii

Typha latifolia subsp.

maresii, commonly known as the broadleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and a distinctive cylindrical flower spike that matures into a brown, fluffy structure.

This subspecies typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. The plant plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
