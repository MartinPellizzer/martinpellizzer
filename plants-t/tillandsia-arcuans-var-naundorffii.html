Tillandsia arcuans var. naundorffii

Tillandsia arcuans var. naundorffii, commonly known as the Naundorff's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal soil, as it absorbs nutrients and water through its leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
