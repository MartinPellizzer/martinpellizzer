Thymus oblongifolius

Thymus oblongifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic, gray-green leaves that are elliptical in shape and densely packed along the stems. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its fragrant foliage makes it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
