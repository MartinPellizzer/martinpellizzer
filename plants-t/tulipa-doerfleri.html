Tulipa doerfleri

Tulipa doerfleri, commonly known as the Doerfler's tulip, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that can range in color from pale yellow to deep red, depending on the specific variety. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique beauty.
