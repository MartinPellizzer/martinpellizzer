Triticum maurorum

Triticum maurorum, commonly known as the wild rye, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Triticum maurorum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural research.
