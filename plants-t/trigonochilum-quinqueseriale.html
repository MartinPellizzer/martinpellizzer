Trigonochilum quinqueseriale

Trigonochilum quinqueseriale, commonly known as the five-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five distinct lobes, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The orchid produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
