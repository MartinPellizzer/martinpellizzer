Tirucalia aphylla

Tirucalia aphylla, commonly known as the leafless tamarind, is a unique plant belonging to the Caesalpiniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive lack of leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present along its stems. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in dry, arid environments.

Due to its unusual appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its potential in phytoremediation and ornamental horticulture.
