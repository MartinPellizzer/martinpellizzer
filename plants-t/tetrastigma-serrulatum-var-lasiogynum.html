Tetrastigma serrulatum var. lasiogynum

Tetrastigma serrulatum var. lasiogynum, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
