Triticum dicoccum var. caucasicum

Triticum dicoccum var. caucasicum, commonly known as Armenian spelt, is an ancient grain species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact spikes and hulled grains, which are typically light yellow to brown in color. The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated in regions with cooler temperatures. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, fiber, and various minerals.

Due to its historical significance and nutritional value, it has experienced a resurgence in interest among farmers and health-conscious consumers.
