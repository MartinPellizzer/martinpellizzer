Terminalia virgata

Terminalia virgata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Terminalia virgata is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
