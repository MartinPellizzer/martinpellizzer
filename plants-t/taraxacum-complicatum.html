Taraxacum complicatum

Taraxacum complicatum, commonly known as the curly dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricately lobed leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
