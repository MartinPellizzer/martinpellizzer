Toxicodendron viminale

Toxicodendron viminale, commonly known as the poison ivy vine, is a deciduous flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

Due to its allergenic properties, it is often considered a noxious weed in its native habitats.
