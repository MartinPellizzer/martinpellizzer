Tinospora malabarica

Tinospora malabarica, commonly known as Malabar tinospora, is a woody climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by bright orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Tinospora malabarica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
