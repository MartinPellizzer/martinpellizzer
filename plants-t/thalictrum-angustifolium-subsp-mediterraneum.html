Thalictrum angustifolium subsp. mediterraneum

Thalictrum angustifolium subsp.

mediterraneum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, clustered in upright spikes, and are usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
