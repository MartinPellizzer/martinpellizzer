Tassadia lanceolata

Tassadia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tassadia, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange, and bloom during the cooler months.

Tassadia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ground cover.
