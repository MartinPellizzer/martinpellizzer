Tragacantha closiana

Tragacantha closiana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

The plant also has historical significance in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
