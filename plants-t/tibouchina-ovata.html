Tibouchina ovata

Tibouchina ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-pink bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tibouchina ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
