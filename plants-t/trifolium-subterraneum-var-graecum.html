Trifolium subterraneum var. graecum

Trifolium subterraneum var. graecum, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
