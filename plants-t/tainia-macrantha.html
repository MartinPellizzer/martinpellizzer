Tainia macrantha

Tainia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered tainia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly variegated appearance. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as insects and birds.

Tainia macrantha is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
