Tounatea panacoco

Tounatea panacoco, commonly known as the golden leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage and long, climbing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
