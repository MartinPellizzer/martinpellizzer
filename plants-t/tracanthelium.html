Tracanthelium

Tracanthelium, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, leaf-like structures that resemble ghostly shapes.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy environments. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its broad, ribbon-like leaves for anchoring and nutrient absorption. Tracanthelium is known for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in its habitat.

Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
