Townsendia minima

Townsendia minima, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a small, flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Townsendia minima is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, sunny conditions.
