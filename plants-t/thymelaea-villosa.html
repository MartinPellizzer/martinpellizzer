Thymelaea villosa

Thymelaea villosa, commonly known as the woolly thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thymelaea villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
