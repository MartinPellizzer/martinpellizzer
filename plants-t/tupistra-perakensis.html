Tupistra perakensis

Tupistra perakensis, commonly known as the Perak mountain hawthorn, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture.

Tupistra perakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
