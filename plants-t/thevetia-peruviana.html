Thevetia peruviana

Thevetia peruviana, commonly known as the yellow oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its poisonous nature.

Thevetia peruviana thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
