Teucrium montanum subsp. helianthemoides

Teucrium montanum subsp.

helianthemoides, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.
