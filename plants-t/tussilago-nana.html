Tussilago nana

Tussilago nana, commonly known as narrow-leaved coughwort, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies.

Tussilago nana is characterized by its medicinal properties, historically used for respiratory ailments.
