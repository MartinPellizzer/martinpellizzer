Teucrium chloroleucum

Teucrium chloroleucum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. Its small, pale blue or white flowers are arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.
