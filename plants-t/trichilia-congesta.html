Trichilia congesta

Trichilia congesta, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood of Trichilia congesta is highly valued for its aromatic qualities and is used in the production of incense, furniture, and musical instruments.
