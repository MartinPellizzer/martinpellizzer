Tillandsia elongata var. subimbricata

Tillandsia elongata var. subimbricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth. Its inflorescence produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, adding ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
