Taraxacum pectinatum

Taraxacum pectinatum, commonly known as the pectinately-haired dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine, pectinate hairs. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum pectinatum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
