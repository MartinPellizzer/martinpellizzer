Teucrium chamaedrys f. albiflorum

Teucrium chamaedrys f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
