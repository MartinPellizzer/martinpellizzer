Tulipa connivens var. obtusata

Tulipa connivens var. obtusata, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It is a perennial bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern tulip cultivars.
