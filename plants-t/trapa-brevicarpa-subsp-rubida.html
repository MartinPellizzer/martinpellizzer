Trapa brevicarpa subsp. rubida

Trapa brevicarpa subsp.

rubida, commonly known as the red-stemmed water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds.

The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and the subsp. rubida is distinguished by its reddish stems and fruits.

The species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
