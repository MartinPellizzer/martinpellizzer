Trichopus zeylanicus subsp. travancoricus

Trichopus zeylanicus subsp.

travancoricus, commonly known as the Travancore trichopus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the monsoon season.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
