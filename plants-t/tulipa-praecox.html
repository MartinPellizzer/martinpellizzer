Tulipa praecox

Tulipa praecox, commonly known as the early tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Tulipa praecox is also valued for its historical significance in the development of modern tulip cultivation.
