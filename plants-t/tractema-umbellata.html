Tractema umbellata

Tractema umbellata, commonly known as the common white bead vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, white, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
