Trimenia weinmanniifolia subsp. bougainvilleensis

Trimenia weinmanniifolia subsp.

bougainvilleensis, commonly known as the Bougainville ironwood, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Bougainville Island.

The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by vibrant, papery bracts that display shades of red, pink, or purple.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
