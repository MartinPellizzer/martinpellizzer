Tragacantha beckwithii

Tragacantha beckwithii, commonly known as the desert tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.

Tragacantha beckwithii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems and a slow growth rate.
