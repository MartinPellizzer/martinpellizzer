Thunbergia maculata

Thunbergia maculata, commonly known as the spotted wing vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often covered in small, dotted markings. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
