Tabernaemontana coffeifolia

Tabernaemontana coffeifolia, commonly known as the coffeeleaf tabernaemontana, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
