Tephrocactus chichensis var. colchanus

Tephrocactus chichensis var. colchanus, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and botanical collections.
