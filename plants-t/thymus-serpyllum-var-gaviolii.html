Thymus serpyllum var. gaviolii

Thymus serpyllum var. gaviolii, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it strong antiseptic and medicinal properties.

It is also valued in culinary applications for its distinctive, peppery fragrance.
