Tamus sylvestris

Tamus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild sarsaparilla, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and historical use in herbal medicine, it is of interest to botanists and foragers alike.
