Torilis webbii

Torilis webbii, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Torilis webbii is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor and has been used historically in folk medicine.
