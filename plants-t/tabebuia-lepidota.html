Tabebuia lepidota

Tabebuia lepidota, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
