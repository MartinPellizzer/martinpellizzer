Thymus ovatus

Thymus ovatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, pale green to grayish-green leaves that are typically ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herb gardens.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable both in culinary and medicinal applications.
