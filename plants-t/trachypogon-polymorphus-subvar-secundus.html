Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. secundus

Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. secundus, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a tufted growth form with slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soils in arid regions.
