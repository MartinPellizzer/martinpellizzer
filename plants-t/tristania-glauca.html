Tristania glauca

Tristania glauca, commonly known as the blue tristania, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Tristania glauca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
