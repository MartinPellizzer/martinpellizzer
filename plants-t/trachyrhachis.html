Trachyrhachis

Trachyrhachis, commonly known as the "rough-stemmed grass," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its rigid, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence typically consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, two-lobed flowers. Trachyrhachis species are typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
