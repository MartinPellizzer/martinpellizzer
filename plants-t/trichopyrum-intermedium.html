Trichopyrum intermedium

Trichopyrum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate trichopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Trichopyrum intermedium is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
