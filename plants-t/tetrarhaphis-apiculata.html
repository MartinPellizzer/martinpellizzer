Tetrarhaphis apiculata

Tetrarhaphis apiculata, commonly known as the silver feather plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-blue foliage with a feathery texture, which gives it a distinctive ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tetrarhaphis apiculata is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
