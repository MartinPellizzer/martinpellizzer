Tilia suberiocarpa

Tilia suberiocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
