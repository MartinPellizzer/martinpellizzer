Trichantha cristata

Trichantha cristata, commonly known as the crown fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both roots and new shoots.
