Turnera viscosa

Turnera viscosa, commonly known as the "Mexican coffee tree," is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and ecological restoration projects.
