Tachigali inconspicua

Tachigali inconspicua, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
