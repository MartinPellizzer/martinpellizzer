Thunia marshalliana var. ionophlebia

Thunia marshalliana var. ionophlebia, commonly known as the Marshall's thunia, is a rare and striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often displaying a striking coloration ranging from bright green to deep red. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight and nutrient-rich substrates. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice among aquatic gardeners and researchers.

However, due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and over-collection.
