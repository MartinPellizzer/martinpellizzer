Tipuana cinerascens

Tipuana cinerascens, commonly known as the silver tipuana, is a fast-growing tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and trifoliate leaves that are alternate and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in reforestation and ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for erosion control and habitat restoration.
