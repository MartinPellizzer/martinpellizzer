Trifolium isthmocarpum var. jaminianum

Trifolium isthmocarpum var. jaminianum, commonly known as the Jaminian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stalk. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
