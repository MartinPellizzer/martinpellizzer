Tinus eugeniifolia

Tinus eugeniifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Tinus eugeniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
