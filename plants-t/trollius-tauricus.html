Trollius tauricus

Trollius tauricus, commonly known as the Taurian globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Trollius tauricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
