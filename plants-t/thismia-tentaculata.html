Thismia tentaculata

Thismia tentaculata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a myco-heterotroph that relies on fungal associations for nutrients. The plant produces small, white, orchid-like flowers that are often mistaken for fungi due to their appearance. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on decaying wood.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in many regions.
