Trigonella hamosa subsp. uncata

Trigonella hamosa subsp.

uncata, commonly known as the slender fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system.
