Tradescantia virginica var. flexuosa

Tradescantia virginica var. flexuosa, commonly known as snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features long, strap-like leaves that are typically green with red or purple variegation along the edges. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its hardiness and air-purifying qualities.
