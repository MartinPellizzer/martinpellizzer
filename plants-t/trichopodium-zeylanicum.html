Trichopodium zeylanicum

Trichopodium zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Trichopodium zeylanicum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
