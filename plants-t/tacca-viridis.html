Tacca viridis

Tacca viridis, commonly known as the green lady's slipper, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble a slipper. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions. Tacca viridis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
