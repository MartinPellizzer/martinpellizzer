Thalictrum silleanum

Thalictrum silleanum, commonly known as the Scottish meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, clustered flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in damp, calcareous soils.

Thalictrum silleanum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
