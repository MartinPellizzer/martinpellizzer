Triaspis massaiensis

Triaspis massaiensis, commonly known as the Massai shield fern, is a distinctive fern species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its leaves are bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The botanical significance of Triaspis massaiensis lies in its role as an indicator species for tropical rainforest ecosystems.
