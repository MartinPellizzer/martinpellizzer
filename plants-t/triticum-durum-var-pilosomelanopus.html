Triticum durum var. pilosomelanopus

Triticum durum var. pilosomelanopus, commonly known as the dark-hairy durum wheat, is a variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is distinguished by its dark-colored glumes and awns, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to other wheat varieties. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is primarily cultivated for its high gluten content, making it suitable for pasta and semolina production.

The variety is also noted for its adaptability to various climatic conditions, contributing to its agricultural significance.
