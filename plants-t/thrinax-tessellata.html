Thrinax tessellata

Thrinax tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean islands, including Cuba, Hispaniola, and the Bahamas. The plant is characterized by its short, clustered stems and distinctive, ridged leaves that give it a unique appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellowish fruits.

Thrinax tessellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
