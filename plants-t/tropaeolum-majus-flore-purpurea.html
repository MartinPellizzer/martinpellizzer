Tropaeolum majus flore-purpurea

Tropaeolum majus flore-purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered nasturtium, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to deep purple. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators also make it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
