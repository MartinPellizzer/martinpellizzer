Thymus subcollinus

Thymus subcollinus, commonly known as the subcollinear thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its foliage is fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus subcollinus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
