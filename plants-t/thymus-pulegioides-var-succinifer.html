Thymus pulegioides var. succinifer

Thymus pulegioides var. succinifer, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a distinct lemony scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. The plant produces small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications and herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
