Tetramolopium consanguineum var. kauense

Tetramolopium consanguineum var. kauense, commonly known as the Kauense ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is known for its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its botanical uses remain under investigation.

The species is also noted for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
