Taenais ornata

Taenais ornata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

Taenais ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
