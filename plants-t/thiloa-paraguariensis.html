Thiloa paraguariensis

Thiloa paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

The plant is well-known for its stimulant properties due to the presence of caffeine and other xanthines. Its leaves are traditionally used to prepare a popular infusion, which is an important cultural and economic product in several regions.
