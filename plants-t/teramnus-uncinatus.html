Teramnus uncinatus

Teramnus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked teramnus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, twisted appearance.

Teramnus uncinatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
