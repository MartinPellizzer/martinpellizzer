Trifolium chiclense

Trifolium chiclense, commonly known as the Mexican clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed sites.

Trifolium chiclense is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
