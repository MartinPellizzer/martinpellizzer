Tylophora orthocaulis

Tylophora orthocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, woody fruits. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
