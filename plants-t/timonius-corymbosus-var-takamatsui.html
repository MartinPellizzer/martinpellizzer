Timonius corymbosus var. takamatsui

Timonius corymbosus var. takamatsui, commonly known as the Japanese hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and is also used in traditional Japanese floral arrangements.
