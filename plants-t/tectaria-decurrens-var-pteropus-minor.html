Tectaria decurrens var. pteropus-minor

Tectaria decurrens var. pteropus-minor, commonly known as the lesser decurrent tectaria, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds and reproductive structures.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features lanceolate, often serrated leaflets that are arranged in a pinnate pattern. Its sporangia are typically found on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to moist, shaded environments in its natural habitat.
