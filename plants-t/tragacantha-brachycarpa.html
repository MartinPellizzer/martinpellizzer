Tragacantha brachycarpa

Tragacantha brachycarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus brachycarpus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, globular seed pods that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Tragacantha brachycarpa is also valued for its gum, which has been historically used in various industrial and pharmaceutical applications.
