Ternatea multiflora

Ternatea multiflora, commonly known as the tropical evergreen shrub, is a plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody stem with multiple branches, and its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly serrated edge. Ternatea multiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

It also has traditional uses in herbal medicine in some regions.
