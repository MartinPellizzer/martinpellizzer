Tanacetum bamianicum

Tanacetum bamianicum, commonly known as the Bamyan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the family. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Tanacetum bamianicum is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
