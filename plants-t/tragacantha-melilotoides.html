Tragacantha melilotoides

Tragacantha melilotoides, commonly known as the Persian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils in its native habitats.
