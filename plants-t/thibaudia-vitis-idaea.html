Thibaudia vitis-idaea

Thibaudia vitis-idaea, commonly known as the crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, dark blue berries that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapting to harsh, acidic environments.

Thibaudia vitis-idaea thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and alpine regions.
