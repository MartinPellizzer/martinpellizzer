Tristicha ramosissima

Tristicha ramosissima, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tristicha ramosissima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
