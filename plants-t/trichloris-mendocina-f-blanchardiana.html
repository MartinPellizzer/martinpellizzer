Trichloris mendocina f. blanchardiana

Trichloris mendocina f. blanchardiana, commonly known as the Blanched Mendoza grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This ornamental grass is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide erosion control in landscaped areas.
