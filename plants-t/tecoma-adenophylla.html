Tecoma adenophylla

Tecoma adenophylla, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Tecoma adenophylla is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
