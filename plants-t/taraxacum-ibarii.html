Taraxacum ibarii

Taraxacum ibarii, commonly known as the Ibarian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and basal rosette of leaves. The plant features a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, showy flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum ibarii is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing resilience to arid conditions.
