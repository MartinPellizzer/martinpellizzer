Trichocyclus pillansii

Trichocyclus pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans cholla, is a species of cactus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by fruit that contains seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Trichocyclus pillansii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
