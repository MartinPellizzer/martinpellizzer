Tilia × zirciensis

Tilia × zirciensis, commonly known as the Zircienis linden, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tilia cordata and Tilia platyphyllos.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically medium to dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

Tilia × zirciensis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
