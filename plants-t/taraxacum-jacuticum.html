Taraxacum jacuticum

Taraxacum jacuticum, commonly known as the Central Asian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in white down. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
