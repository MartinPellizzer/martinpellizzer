Taraxacum egnatiae

Taraxacum egnatiae, commonly known as the Egnatia dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in regions of Greece and North Macedonia. This perennial herb features a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single, elongated flowering stem topped with a spherical head of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing a robust root system for water storage.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
