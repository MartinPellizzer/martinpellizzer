Tragacantha flexilispina

Tragacantha flexilispina, commonly known as the flexible spined tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Tragacantha flexilispina is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

Its scientific name reflects the flexible spines that adorn its stems, a distinctive feature of the species.
