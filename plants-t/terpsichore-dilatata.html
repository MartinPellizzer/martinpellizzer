Terpsichore dilatata

Terpsichore dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Terpsichore dilatata is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
