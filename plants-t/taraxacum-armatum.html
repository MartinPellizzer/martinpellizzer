Taraxacum armatum

Taraxacum armatum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces a distinctive, elongated flower stem topped with a yellow composite flower. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is adapted to harsh, alpine environments, showcasing resilience to cold and arid conditions.
