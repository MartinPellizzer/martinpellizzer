Trepocarpus brachycarpus

Trepocarpus brachycarpus, commonly known as the short-fruited trepocarpus, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
