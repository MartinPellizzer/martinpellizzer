Tragacantha vaga

Tragacantha vaga, commonly known as the wandering tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Tragacantha vaga is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to Mediterranean climates.
