Tainia tenera

Tainia tenera, commonly known as the slender tainia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

Tainia tenera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
