Tinus volckameria

Tinus volckameria, commonly known as the Volckameria or the silverleaf plant, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tinus volckameria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
