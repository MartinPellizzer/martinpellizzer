Trachymene oleracea subsp. sedimenta

Trachymene oleracea subsp.

sedimenta, commonly known as the swamp parsley or water parsley, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
