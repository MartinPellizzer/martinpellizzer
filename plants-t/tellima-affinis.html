Tellima affinis

Tellima affinis, commonly known as the mountain beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
