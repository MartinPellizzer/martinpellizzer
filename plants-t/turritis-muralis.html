Turritis muralis

Turritis muralis, commonly known as the wall turritis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or wall crevices, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette pattern close to the ground.

Turritis muralis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in difficult soil conditions.
