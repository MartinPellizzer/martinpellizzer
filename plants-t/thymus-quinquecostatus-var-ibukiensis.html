Thymus quinquecostatus var. ibukiensis

Thymus quinquecostatus var. ibukiensis, commonly known as the Ibuki thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features tiny, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
