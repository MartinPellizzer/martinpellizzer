Tropaeolum sanctae-catharinae

Tropaeolum sanctae-catharinae, commonly known as the Santa Catharina nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with trailing stems that make it suitable for hanging baskets or containers. Its flowers come in shades of yellow and orange, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other nasturtium varieties.
