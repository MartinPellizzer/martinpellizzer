Talisia pedicellaris

Talisia pedicellaris, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Talisia pedicellaris is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
