Tylophora argyi

Tylophora argyi, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a woody climbing vine native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.
