Thuja occidentalis var. rosenthalii

Thuja occidentalis var. rosenthalii, commonly known as the Rosenthal's arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant is evergreen, with scale-like leaves that are closely packed along the branches. It is native to North America and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This variety is relatively slow-growing and prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
