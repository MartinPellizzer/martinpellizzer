Tricondylus tinctorius

Tricondylus tinctorius, commonly known as the red root or red root grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive red-colored roots, which are the primary source of its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Tricondylus tinctorius is typically found in moist, sandy soils and is often associated with disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and historical contexts.
