Teucrium polium subsp. expassum

Teucrium polium subsp.

expassum, commonly known as the Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
