Thymus pseudopulegioides

Thymus pseudopulegioides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and fragrance.
