Tecoma splendida

Tecoma splendida, commonly known as yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma splendida is well adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
