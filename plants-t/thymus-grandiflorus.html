Thymus grandiflorus

Thymus grandiflorus, commonly known as largeflower thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus grandiflorus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
