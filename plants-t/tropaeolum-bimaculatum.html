Tropaeolum bimaculatum

Tropaeolum bimaculatum, commonly known as the two-spotted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum bimaculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
