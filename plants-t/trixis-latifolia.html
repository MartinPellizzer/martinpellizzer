Trixis latifolia

Trixis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Trixis latifolia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
