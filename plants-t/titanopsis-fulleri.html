Titanopsis fulleri

Titanopsis fulleri, commonly known as the Living Desert Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem that often branches into multiple offsets, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
