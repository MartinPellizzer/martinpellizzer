Triticum pungens proles obtusiusculum

Triticum pungens proles obtusiusculum, commonly known as the blunt-leaved wheatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its short, sturdy stems and broad, bluntly tapered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is considered a valuable forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
