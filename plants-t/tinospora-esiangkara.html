Tinospora esiangkara

Tinospora esiangkara, commonly known as the Malabar moonseed, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical characteristics of Tinospora esiangkara make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
