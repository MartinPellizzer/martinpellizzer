Tulipa turcica var. media

Tulipa turcica var. media, commonly known as the Turkish tulip or the broadleaf tulip, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red, with a distinctive cup-shaped form. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is a perennial bulbous plant that blooms in late spring, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
