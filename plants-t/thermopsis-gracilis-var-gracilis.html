Thermopsis gracilis var. gracilis

Thermopsis gracilis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
