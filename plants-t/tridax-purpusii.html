Tridax purpusii

Tridax purpusii, commonly known as the common tridax, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tridax purpusii is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and waste places due to its adaptability.

It is considered a weed in many regions but has some traditional medicinal uses.
