Tragopyrum suaedifolium

Tragopyrum suaedifolium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tragopyrum suaedifolium is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in coastal environments.
