Trizeuxis pygmaea

Trizeuxis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that provide a textured appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by small, oval-shaped berries. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, exhibiting high salt tolerance and drought resistance.

Trizeuxis pygmaea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
