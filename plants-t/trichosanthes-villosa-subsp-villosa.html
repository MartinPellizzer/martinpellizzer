Trichosanthes villosa subsp. villosa

Trichosanthes villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, pale green fruits that turn orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense pubescence and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
