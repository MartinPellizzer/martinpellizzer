Taraxacum fulvobrunneum

Taraxacum fulvobrunneum, commonly known as the brownish dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rough, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism through a pappus, which allows its seeds to be carried by the wind. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is closely related to other dandelion species and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
