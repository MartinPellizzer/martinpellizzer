Tetraria secans

Tetraria secans, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Tetraria secans is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.

Its structure and morphology reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils.
