Tradescantia fasciculata

Tradescantia fasciculata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright blue or purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, three-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring.

Tradescantia fasciculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
