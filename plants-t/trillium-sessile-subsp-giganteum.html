Trillium sessile subsp. giganteum

Trillium sessile subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant white trillium, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring.

The plant has three broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It thrives in rich, moist woodlands and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
