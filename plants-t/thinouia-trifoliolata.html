Thinouia trifoliolata

Thinouia trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Thinouia trifoliolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage and floral display.
