Thalictrum rhodopaeum

Thalictrum rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Rhodope meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Bulgaria and Greece.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum rhodopaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
