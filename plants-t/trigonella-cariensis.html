Trigonella cariensis

Trigonella cariensis, commonly known as Egyptian fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
