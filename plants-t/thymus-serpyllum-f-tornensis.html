Thymus serpyllum f. tornensis

Thymus serpyllum f. tornensis, commonly known as Torn's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that are rich in essential oils. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
