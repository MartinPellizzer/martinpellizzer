Tragacantha bakaliensis

Tragacantha bakaliensis, commonly known as the Balkan tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It is known for its mucilaginous sap, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a natural emulsifier.

The plant thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.
