Trigonella esculenta

Trigonella esculenta, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual legume native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are rich in nutrients such as protein, fiber, and various vitamins. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in agriculture and traditional medicine.
