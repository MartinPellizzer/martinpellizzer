Tilia × intermedia

Tilia × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid linden or middle linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid between Tilia cordata and Tilia europaea, resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parent species. The tree is known for its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It produces small, round, greenish-brown fruits that contain seeds.

Tilia × intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
