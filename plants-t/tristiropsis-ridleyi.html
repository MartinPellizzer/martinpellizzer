Tristiropsis ridleyi

Tristiropsis ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue or black berries.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
