Tilia paucicostata

Tilia paucicostata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein and fewer lateral veins. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in upright inflorescences, blooming in early summer. The tree produces small, round, brown fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
