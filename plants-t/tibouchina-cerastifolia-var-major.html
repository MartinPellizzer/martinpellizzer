Tibouchina cerastifolia var. major

Tibouchina cerastifolia var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
