Triumfetta pedunculata

Triumfetta pedunculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or small-leaved triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the warm seasons. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Triumfetta pedunculata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and agricultural fields.
