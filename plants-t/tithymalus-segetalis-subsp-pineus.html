Tithymalus segetalis subsp. pineus

Tithymalus segetalis subsp.

pineus, commonly known as the meadow thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
