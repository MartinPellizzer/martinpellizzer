Tabebuia neurophylla

Tabebuia neurophylla, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The tree has a straight, cylindrical trunk and produces long, narrow leaves that are arranged opposite each other. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and flowers of Tabebuia neurophylla have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
