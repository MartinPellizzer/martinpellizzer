Trisetaria lapalmae

Trisetaria lapalmae, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found on dunes and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
