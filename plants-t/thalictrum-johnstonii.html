Thalictrum johnstonii

Thalictrum johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in cylindrical racemes, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Thalictrum johnstonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
