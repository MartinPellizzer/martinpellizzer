Tradescantia axillaris f. subaspera

Tradescantia axillaris f. subaspera, commonly known as the roughleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves that give it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
