Tigridia amatlanensis

Tigridia amatlanensis, commonly known as the Mexican tiger flower, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble tiger stripes. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces vibrant orange to red blooms.

Tigridia amatlanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
