Tongoloa elata

Tongoloa elata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
