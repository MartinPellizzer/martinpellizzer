Thymus pulegioides subsp. pannonicus

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

pannonicus, commonly known as the Hungarian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
