Taraxacum dovrense

Taraxacum dovrense, commonly known as the Dovren's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball known as a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Taraxacum dovrense is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelion species.
