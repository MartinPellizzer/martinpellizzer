Tribulus curvicarpus

Tribulus curvicarpus, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky areas and has small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a branched stem, hairy foliage, and a distinctive fruit structure.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
