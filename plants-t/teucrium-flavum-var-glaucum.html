Teucrium flavum var. glaucum

Teucrium flavum var. glaucum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a powdery blue-gray bloom, giving it its characteristic appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a minty aroma when crushed. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
