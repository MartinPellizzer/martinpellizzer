Telis aleppica

Telis aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and have a strong thyme-like aroma, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Telis aleppica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or arid soils.
