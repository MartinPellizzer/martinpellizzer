Taraxacum crebridens

Taraxacum crebridens, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow composite flower that develops into a spherical seed head with parachute-like seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, lawns, and meadows.

Taraxacum crebridens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
