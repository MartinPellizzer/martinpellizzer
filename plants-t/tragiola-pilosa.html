Tragiola pilosa

Tragiola pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tragi, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Tragiola pilosa is often found growing in rocky soils and along the edges of deserts.

It is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
