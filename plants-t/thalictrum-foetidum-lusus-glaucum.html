Thalictrum foetidum lusus glaucum

Thalictrum foetidum lusus glaucum, commonly known as the blue meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
