Tragacantha bakuensis

Tragacantha bakuensis, commonly known as the Baku tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and stipitate leaves. The plant produces a latex-like substance that is harvested for its medicinal and industrial uses. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
