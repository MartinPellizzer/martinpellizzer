Thymus serpyllum f. cyclophyllus

Thymus serpyllum f. cyclophyllus, commonly known as the cyclophyllus form of wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
