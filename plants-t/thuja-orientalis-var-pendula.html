Thuja orientalis var. pendula

Thuja orientalis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping thuja, is a cultivar of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a popular ornamental plant. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are typically a bright green color, providing dense coverage. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to most pests and diseases.
