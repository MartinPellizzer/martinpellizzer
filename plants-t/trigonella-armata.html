Trigonella armata

Trigonella armata, commonly known as the Armenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella armata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
