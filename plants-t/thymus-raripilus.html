Thymus raripilus

Thymus raripilus, commonly known as the slender thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Thymus raripilus is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
