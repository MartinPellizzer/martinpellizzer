Tragacantha lentiformis

Tragacantha lentiformis, commonly known as the lentil-shaped tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive lentil-shaped pods. The plant produces a gel-like substance from its sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

Tragacantha lentiformis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used for centuries in various herbal remedies.
