Trichocladus peltatus

Trichocladus peltatus, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, often with two lips, and it produces small, round berries.

Trichocladus peltatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its dense foliage and evergreen nature.
