Terminalia bialata

Terminalia bialata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
