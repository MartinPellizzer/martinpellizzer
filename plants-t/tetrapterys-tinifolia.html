Tetrapterys tinifolia

Tetrapterys tinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, though it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Tetrapterys tinifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
