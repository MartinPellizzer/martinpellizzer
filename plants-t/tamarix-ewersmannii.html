Tamarix ewersmannii

Tamarix ewersmannii, commonly known as the Ewersmann's tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and steppe environments.

Tamarix ewersmannii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
