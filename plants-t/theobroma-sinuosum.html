Theobroma sinuosum

Theobroma sinuosum, commonly known as the cocoa tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are the primary source of cocoa mass, used in chocolate production. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are typically pale yellow with a red calyx.

Theobroma sinuosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
