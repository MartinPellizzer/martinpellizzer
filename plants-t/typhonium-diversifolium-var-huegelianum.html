Typhonium diversifolium var. huegelianum

Typhonium diversifolium var. huegelianum, commonly known as the Hugel's elephant's ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on tall, leafless stems. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

This variety is popular in ornamental gardening for its dramatic foliage and is often used in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
