Tapirira fanshawei

Tapirira fanshawei, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit, a legume pod, contains edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
