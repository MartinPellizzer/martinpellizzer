Trichoglottis biglandulosa

Trichoglottis biglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that have a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Trichoglottis biglandulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
