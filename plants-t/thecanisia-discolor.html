Thecanisia discolor

Thecanisia discolor, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often protected in conservation efforts.
