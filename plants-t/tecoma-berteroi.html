Tecoma berteroi

Tecoma berteroi, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Tecoma berteroi is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
