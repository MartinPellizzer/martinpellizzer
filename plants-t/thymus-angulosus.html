Thymus angulosus

Thymus angulosus, commonly known as narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
