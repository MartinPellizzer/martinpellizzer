Turnera calyptrocarpa

Turnera calyptrocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soils.
