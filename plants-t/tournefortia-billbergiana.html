Tournefortia billbergiana

Tournefortia billbergiana, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
