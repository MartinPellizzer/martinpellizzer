Teucrium mexicanum

Teucrium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican germander, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Teucrium mexicanum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
