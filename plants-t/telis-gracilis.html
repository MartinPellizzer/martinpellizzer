Telis gracilis

Telis gracilis, commonly known as the slender telis, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Telis gracilis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
