Tragacantha eriacma

Tragacantha eriacma, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binder in pharmaceuticals. Tragacantha eriacma typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various insects and birds in its native range.
