Trichomanes alatum subvar. latealatum

Trichomanes alatum subvar. latealatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family with distinctive, lance-shaped fronds.

Its fronds are typically broad and have a delicate, feathery appearance, with a pale green to light brown coloration. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
