Tithymalus thamnoides

Tithymalus thamnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has slender stems that are usually unbranched, and its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
