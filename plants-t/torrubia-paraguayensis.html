Torrubia paraguayensis

Torrubia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan torrubia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Torrubia paraguayensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
