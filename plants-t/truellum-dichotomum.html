Truellum dichotomum

Truellum dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous truellum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often lobed or toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
