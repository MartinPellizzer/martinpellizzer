Thymus officinalis var. parviflora

Thymus officinalis var. parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, tiny, gray-green leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and herbal gardens.
