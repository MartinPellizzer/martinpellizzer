Trichaurus vaginatus

Trichaurus vaginatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is native to regions of the Americas and is commonly found in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds to the wind.

Trichaurus vaginatus is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
