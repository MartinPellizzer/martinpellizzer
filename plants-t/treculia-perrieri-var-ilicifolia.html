Treculia perrieri var. ilicifolia

Treculia perrieri var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by large, round fruits containing a fatty, creamy pulp. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural rubber.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
