Typha davidiana

Typha davidiana, commonly known as the David's cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically brown or yellowish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Typha davidiana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
