Tragacantha microthamna

Tragacantha microthamna, commonly known as the small-fruited tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
