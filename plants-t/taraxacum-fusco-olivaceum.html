Taraxacum fusco-olivaceum

Taraxacum fusco-olivaceum, commonly known as the dusky-green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate, dark green leaves with a grayish-green underside. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellowish stem that terminates in a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
