Terminalia triopteris

Terminalia triopteris, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, woody capsules. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local crafts and construction.

The species also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
