Thymus ternejicus

Thymus ternejicus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus ternejicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
