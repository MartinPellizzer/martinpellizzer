Thymus zygis var. afer

Thymus zygis var. afer, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow, grey-green leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile ground cover.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong scent and various therapeutic properties.
