Trophaeum cochabambae

Trophaeum cochabambae, commonly known as the Cochabamba sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
