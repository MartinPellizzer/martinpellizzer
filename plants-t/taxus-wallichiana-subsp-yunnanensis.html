Taxus wallichiana subsp. yunnanensis

Taxus wallichiana subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan yew, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its slow growth, needle-like leaves, and the presence of toxic, red-barked branches.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of taxol, a compound used in cancer chemotherapy. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its ecological and medicinal significance, it is also a subject of conservation concern.
