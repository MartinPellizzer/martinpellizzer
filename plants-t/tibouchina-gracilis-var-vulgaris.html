Tibouchina gracilis var. vulgaris

Tibouchina gracilis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the purple verbena or Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Tibouchina gracilis var. vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
