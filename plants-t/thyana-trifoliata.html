Thyana trifoliata

Thyana trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
