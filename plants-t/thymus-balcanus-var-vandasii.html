Thymus balcanus var. vandasii

Thymus balcanus var. vandasii, commonly known as Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties, similar to other thyme species.
