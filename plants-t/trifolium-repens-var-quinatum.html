Trifolium repens var. quinatum

Trifolium repens var. quinatum, commonly known as the quinatum white clover, is a variant of the white clover species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along pathways. It is a low-growing perennial that spreads through stolons, forming dense mats.

The quinatum variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and forage production.
