Tillandsia fluminensis

Tillandsia fluminensis, commonly known as the river tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
