Tithymaloides padifolia

Tithymaloides padifolia, commonly known as the African sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features compact, upright stems with small, linear leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in coastal or arid regions due to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
