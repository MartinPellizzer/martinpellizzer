Trollius pumilus f. sikkimensis

Trollius pumilus f. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim dwarf buttercup, is a botanical variant of the dwarf buttercup species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Sikkim, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are adapted to the high-altitude conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
