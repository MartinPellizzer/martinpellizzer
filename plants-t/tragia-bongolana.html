Tragia bongolana

Tragia bongolana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are usually ovate with a rough texture. Tragia bongolana is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating skin conditions and as an antispasmodic.
