Tulotis fragrans

Tulotis fragrans, commonly known as the sweet tulip, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tulotis fragrans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
