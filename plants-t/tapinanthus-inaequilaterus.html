Tapinanthus inaequilaterus

Tapinanthus inaequilaterus, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from host plants.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short, fleshy stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in a spike. Its parasitic nature allows it to thrive without requiring extensive root systems, making it well adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is notable for its unique ecological role in forest ecosystems.
