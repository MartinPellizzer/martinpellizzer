Toxocarpus tomentosus

Toxocarpus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly antelope bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Toxocarpus tomentosus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique texture and drought tolerance.
