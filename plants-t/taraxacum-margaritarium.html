Taraxacum margaritarium

Taraxacum margaritarium, commonly known as the starry dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, showy yellow flower with radiating petals. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Taraxacum margaritarium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
