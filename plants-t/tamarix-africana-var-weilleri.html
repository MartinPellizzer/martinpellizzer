Tamarix africana var. weilleri

Tamarix africana var. weilleri, commonly known as the African sea bean, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to saline and arid environments, making it resilient to coastal conditions. Its seed pods split open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
