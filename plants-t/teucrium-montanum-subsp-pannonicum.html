Teucrium montanum subsp. pannonicum

Teucrium montanum subsp.

pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian germander, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from blue to purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
