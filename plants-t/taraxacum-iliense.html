Taraxacum iliense

Taraxacum iliense, commonly known as the Ili dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and serrated. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.
