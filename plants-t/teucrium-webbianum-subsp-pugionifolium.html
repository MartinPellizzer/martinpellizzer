Teucrium webbianum subsp. pugionifolium

Teucrium webbianum subsp.

pugionifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
