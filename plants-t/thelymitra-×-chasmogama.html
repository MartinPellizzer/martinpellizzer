Thelymitra × chasmogama

Thelymitra × chasmogama, commonly known as the hybrid bluebell, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Thelymitra falcata and Thelymitra arenicola.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal sand dunes and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are grass-like and linear, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

The hybrid is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
