Triodia schlechteri

Triodia schlechteri, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and is a key component of the native vegetation in its habitat.

Triodia schlechteri is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low rainfall.
