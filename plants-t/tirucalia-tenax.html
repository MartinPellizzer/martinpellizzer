Tirucalia tenax

Tirucalia tenax, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Tirucalia tenax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
