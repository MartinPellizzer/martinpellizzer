Teutliopsis hastata

Teutliopsis hastata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Teutliopsis hastata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
