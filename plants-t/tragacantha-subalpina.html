Tragacantha subalpina

Tragacantha subalpina, commonly known as the alpine tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. It is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.

Tragacantha subalpina is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
