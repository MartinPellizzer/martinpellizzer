Tassilicyparis dupreziana

Tassilicyparis dupreziana, commonly known as the Duprez's cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are tightly arranged around the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, typical of coniferous species. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a relict species with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical specimen from further decline.
