Trichosporum miniatum

Trichosporum miniatum, commonly known as the miniature trichosporum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tiny, delicate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in nutrient-rich soil.

Trichosporum miniatum is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
