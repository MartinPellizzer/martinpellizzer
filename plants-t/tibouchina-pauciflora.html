Tibouchina pauciflora

Tibouchina pauciflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Tibouchina pauciflora is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance plant.
