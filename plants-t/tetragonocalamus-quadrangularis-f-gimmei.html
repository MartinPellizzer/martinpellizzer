Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. gimmei

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. gimmei, commonly known as the Gimmei bamboo, is a unique cultivar of the bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with a combination of green, white, and pink hues. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces slender, upright stems with a distinctive quadrangular cross-section. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage changes color with the seasons, adding visual interest throughout the year.
