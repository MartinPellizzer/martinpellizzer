Thlaspi beugesiacum

Thlaspi beugesiacum, commonly known as the Beuge's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It is often found in alpine or arctic environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
