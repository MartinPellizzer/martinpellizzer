Trichosalpinx chaetoglossa

Trichosalpinx chaetoglossa, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a hairy, lip-like petal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
