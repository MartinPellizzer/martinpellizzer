Trigonostemon thyrsoideus

Trigonostemon thyrsoideus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by edible tubers similar to those of the arrowroot plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its starchy roots.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is a popular plant in both gardens and agricultural settings.
