Tephrosia longipes var. uncinata

Tephrosia longipes var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and produce small, lens-shaped pods.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
