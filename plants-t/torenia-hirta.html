Torenia hirta

Torenia hirta, commonly known as the velvetleaf torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Torenia hirta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
