Thymus munbyanus subsp. coloratus

Thymus munbyanus subsp.

coloratus, commonly known as the purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a distinctive purple bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in hue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular choice in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
