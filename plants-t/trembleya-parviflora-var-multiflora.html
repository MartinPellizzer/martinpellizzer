Trembleya parviflora var. multiflora

Trembleya parviflora var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
