Tulipa boissieri

Tulipa boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier tulip, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive orange or red center. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure, often growing in alpine meadows.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
