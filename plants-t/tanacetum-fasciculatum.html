Tanacetum fasciculatum

Tanacetum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered tansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Tanacetum fasciculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
