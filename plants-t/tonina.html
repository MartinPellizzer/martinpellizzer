Tonina

Tonina, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tonina is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
