Thlaspi rothianum

Thlaspi rothianum, commonly known as the Rothian pennycress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. This species is typically found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions. Thlaspi rothianum is often used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals.

Its life cycle is annual, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and biodiversity in its native habitats.
