Trichochloa debilis

Trichochloa debilis, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.

Trichochloa debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens and landscaping.
