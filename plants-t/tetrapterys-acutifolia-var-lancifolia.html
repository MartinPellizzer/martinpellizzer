Tetrapterys acutifolia var. lancifolia

Tetrapterys acutifolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf tetrapterys, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are elongated and tapering at both ends. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
