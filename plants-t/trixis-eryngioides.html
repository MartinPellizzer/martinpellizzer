Trixis eryngioides

Trixis eryngioides, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its silver-green foliage and yellow flowers make it a distinctive feature of its habitat.

Trixis eryngioides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
