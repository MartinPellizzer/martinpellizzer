Thymus × adpressus

Thymus × adpressus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with tiny, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
