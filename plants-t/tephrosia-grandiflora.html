Tephrosia grandiflora

Tephrosia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered tephrosia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Tephrosia grandiflora is also valued in agroforestry for its ability to improve soil fertility.
