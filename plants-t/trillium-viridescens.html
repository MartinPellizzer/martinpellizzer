Trillium viridescens

Trillium viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the botanical family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three elliptical leaves and three white petals that turn green as the plant matures. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies. Trillium viridescens is a slow-growing plant that thrives in rich, moist, acidic soils under shaded woodland conditions.

Its unique lifecycle and ecological role make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
