Teucrium nepetifolium

Teucrium nepetifolium, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory ailments.
