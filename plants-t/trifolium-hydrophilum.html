Trifolium hydrophilum

Trifolium hydrophilum, commonly known as water clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Trifolium hydrophilum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
