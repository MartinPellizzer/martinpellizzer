Tessaria absinthioides

Tessaria absinthioides, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for dry landscapes.

Tessaria absinthioides is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
