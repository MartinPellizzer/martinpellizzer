Trichilia mollissima

Trichilia mollissima, commonly known as silky trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also appreciated for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
