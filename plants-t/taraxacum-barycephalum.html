Taraxacum barycephalum

Taraxacum barycephalum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem topped with a distinctive yellow composite flower head. After flowering, it develops a white, fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
