Trigonochilum callacallaense

Trigonochilum callacallaense, commonly known as the Callacalla orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical structure includes long, narrow leaves and a compact, pseudobulb-like stem that supports its flowering growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
