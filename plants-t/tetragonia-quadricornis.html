Tetragonia quadricornis

Tetragonia quadricornis, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces edible leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is well adapted to saline soils. Its leaves are succulent and can be consumed raw or cooked.

Tetragonia quadricornis is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in various parts of the world for culinary use.
