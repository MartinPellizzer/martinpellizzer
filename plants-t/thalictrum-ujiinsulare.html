Thalictrum ujiinsulare

Thalictrum ujiinsulare, commonly known as the Japanese meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
