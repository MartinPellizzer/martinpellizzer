Thymus karadzicensis var. doerfleri

Thymus karadzicensis var. doerfleri, commonly known as the Karadzic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically grey-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous areas.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.
