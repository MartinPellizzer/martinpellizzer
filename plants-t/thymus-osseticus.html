Thymus osseticus

Thymus osseticus, commonly known as the Ossetian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal collections.
