Tephrosia oxygona subsp. oxygona

Tephrosia oxygona subsp.

oxygona, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which have several pairs of elliptic to ovate leaflets. Its root system is fibrous, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
