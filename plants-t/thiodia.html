Thiodia

Thiodia, commonly known as the sulfur plant, is a unique flowering plant native to certain parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant contains sulfur compounds, which give it its distinctive odor and name. Thiodia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
