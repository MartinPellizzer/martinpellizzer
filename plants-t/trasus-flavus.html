Trasus flavus

Trasus flavus, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a central disk that resembles the sun, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-filled flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. Trasus flavus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a popular choice for xeriscaping.
