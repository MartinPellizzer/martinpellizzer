Taraxacum pseudodunense

Taraxacum pseudodunense, commonly known as the Himalayan dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
