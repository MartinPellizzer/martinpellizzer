Thalictrum virgatum var. obtusifolium

Thalictrum virgatum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as blunt-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branched stems that rise from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces clusters of small, white, spicate flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a distinctive blunt tip, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.
