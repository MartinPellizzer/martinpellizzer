Thymus teucrioides subsp. candilicus

Thymus teucrioides subsp.

candilicus, commonly known as the Sicilian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
