Tropaeolum purpureum

Tropaeolum purpureum, commonly known as the purple crown flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces edible tubers, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its foliage is trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Tropaeolum purpureum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
