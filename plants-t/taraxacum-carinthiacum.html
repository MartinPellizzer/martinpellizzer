Taraxacum carinthiacum

Taraxacum carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carinthian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with pappus.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
