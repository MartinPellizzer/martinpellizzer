Thymus serpyllum subsp. alpestris

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, narrow leaves that are densely covered with glandular hairs, giving it a fragrant, aromatic quality.

The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
