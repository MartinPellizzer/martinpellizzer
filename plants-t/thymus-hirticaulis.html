Thymus hirticaulis

Thymus hirticaulis, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
