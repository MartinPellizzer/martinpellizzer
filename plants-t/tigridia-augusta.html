Tigridia augusta

Tigridia augusta, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive black eye at the center. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tigridia augusta is a short-lived perennial that blooms in the summer, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
