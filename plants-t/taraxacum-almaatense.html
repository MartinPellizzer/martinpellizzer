Taraxacum almaatense

Taraxacum almaatense, commonly known as the Almaty dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This plant is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow composite flower head. The leaves are typically hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flower head is surrounded by bracts.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
