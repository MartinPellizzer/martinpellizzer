Tragacantha biserrula

Tragacantha biserrula, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, yellow flowers and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. It is also valued for its gum resin, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
