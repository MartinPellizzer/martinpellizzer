Thymus kuminovianus

Thymus kuminovianus, commonly known as Kuminovian thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in countries like Georgia and Azerbaijan. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, aromatic leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
