Trimezia truncata

Trimezia truncata, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Trimezia truncata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
