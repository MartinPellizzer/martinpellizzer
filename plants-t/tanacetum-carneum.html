Tanacetum carneum

Tanacetum carneum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, woolly foliage and yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Tanacetum carneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
