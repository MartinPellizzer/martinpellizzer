Thrixspermum teres

Thrixspermum teres, commonly known as the spherical thrixspermum, is a unique plant species characterized by its compact, globular growth habit.

It features dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves that form a spherical or hemispherical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including high altitudes and variable temperatures.

Thrixspermum teres is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
