Trisetaria nitida

Trisetaria nitida, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of tiny, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Trisetaria nitida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
