Theophrasta spinosa

Theophrasta spinosa, commonly known as the spiny theophrasta, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are dry, dehiscent pods containing several seeds.

Theophrasta spinosa is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
