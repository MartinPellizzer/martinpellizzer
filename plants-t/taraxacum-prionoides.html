Taraxacum prionoides

Taraxacum prionoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a capsule containing numerous seeds, each with a fluffy pappus for wind dispersal. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum prionoides is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
