Trixis gigas

Trixis gigas, commonly known as the giant trixis, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Trixis gigas is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
