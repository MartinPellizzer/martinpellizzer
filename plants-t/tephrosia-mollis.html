Tephrosia mollis

Tephrosia mollis, commonly known as white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem covered in fine hairs.

Tephrosia mollis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
