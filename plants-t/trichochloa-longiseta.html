Trichochloa longiseta

Trichochloa longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Trichochloa longiseta is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate, feathery foliage.
