Tussilago sinuata

Tussilago sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coughwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Tussilago sinuata is noted for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
