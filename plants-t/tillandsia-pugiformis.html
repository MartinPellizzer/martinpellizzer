Tillandsia pugiformis

Tillandsia pugiformis, commonly known as the pig-shaped tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pig-like shape that gives it its common name. This epiphytic plant does not require soil to grow, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. It thrives in humid environments with indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant among enthusiasts.
