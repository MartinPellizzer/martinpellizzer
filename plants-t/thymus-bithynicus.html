Thymus bithynicus

Thymus bithynicus, commonly known as the Bithynian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkans and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and have been used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

Thymus bithynicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
