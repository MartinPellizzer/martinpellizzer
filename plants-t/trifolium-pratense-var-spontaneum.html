Trifolium pratense var. spontaneum

Trifolium pratense var. spontaneum, commonly known as red clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pink to purple flower heads that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
