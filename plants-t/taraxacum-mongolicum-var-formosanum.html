Taraxacum mongolicum var. formosanum

Taraxacum mongolicum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan dandelion, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces a flowering stem topped with a spherical cluster of small, yellow flowers. After flowering, it develops a fluffy seed head, characteristic of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
