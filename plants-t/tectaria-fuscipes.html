Tectaria fuscipes

Tectaria fuscipes, commonly known as the brown-footed tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark-green fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a feature typical of many ferns. Tectaria fuscipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
