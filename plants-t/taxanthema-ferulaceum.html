Taxanthema ferulaceum

Taxanthema ferulaceum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Taxanthema ferulaceum is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
