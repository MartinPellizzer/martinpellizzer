Tristachya welwitschii var. major

Tristachya welwitschii var. major, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a large aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as lakes and wetlands. Its rhizomes spread extensively, forming dense mats that can dominate aquatic ecosystems.

It plays a significant role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
