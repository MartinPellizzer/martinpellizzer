Trichocereus candicans

Trichocereus candicans, commonly known as the "Candelabra Cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its multiple branching stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Trichocereus candicans is also known for its role in traditional medicine and its potential psychoactive properties.
