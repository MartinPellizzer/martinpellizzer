Trichomanes crispum subsp. pellucens

pellucens, commonly known as the translucent maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, lacy fronds. It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests.

The fronds are usually light green and have a translucent appearance, especially when viewed from certain angles. This subspecies is notable for its small, spore-producing structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.
