Teucrium puberulum

Teucrium puberulum, commonly known as slender germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Teucrium puberulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
