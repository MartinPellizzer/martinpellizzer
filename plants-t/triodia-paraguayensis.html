Triodia paraguayensis

Triodia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are wind-pollinated.

Triodia paraguayensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
