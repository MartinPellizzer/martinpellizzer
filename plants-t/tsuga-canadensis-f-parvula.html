Tsuga canadensis f. parvula

Tsuga canadensis f. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a compact form of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, pyramidal shape. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
