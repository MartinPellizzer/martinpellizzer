Trigonella polyceratoides

Trigonella polyceratoides, commonly known as the horned melon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that resembles a horned melon. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and produces small, yellow flowers. The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp with black seeds, and it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and flavor.

It thrives in arid conditions and is adapted to withstand drought.
