Thalictrum dasycarpum var. hypoglaucum

Thalictrum dasycarpum var. hypoglaucum, commonly known as the woolly false spirea, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its stems are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is native to parts of eastern North America and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
