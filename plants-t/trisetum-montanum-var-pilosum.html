Trisetum montanum var. pilosum

Trisetum montanum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy field grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, glabrous, or pubescent leaves, which vary in texture depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually light green to brown in color.

Trisetum montanum var. pilosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife, making it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
