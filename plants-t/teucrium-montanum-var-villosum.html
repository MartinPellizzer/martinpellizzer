Teucrium montanum var. villosum

Teucrium montanum var. villosum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and herbal use.
