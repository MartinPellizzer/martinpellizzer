Tanarius depressus

Tanarius depressus, commonly known as the slender tanarius, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slight downward curve, and it is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions.

Tanarius depressus plays a minor role in local ecosystems, primarily as a food source for certain insects and small animals.
