Thrincia saxatilis subsp. taraxacoides

Thrincia saxatilis subsp.

taraxacoides, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh, rocky environments, showcasing a robust root system and hairy foliage to retain moisture. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine habitats.
