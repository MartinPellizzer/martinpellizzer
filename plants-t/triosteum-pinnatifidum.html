Triosteum pinnatifidum

Triosteum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrowleaf triosteum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched or cut appearance. Its small, white to pale purple flowers bloom in late spring, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its adaptability to partial shade.
