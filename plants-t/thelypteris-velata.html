Thelypteris velata

Thelypteris velata, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Thelypteris velata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
