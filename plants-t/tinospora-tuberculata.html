Tinospora tuberculata

Tinospora tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems covered in small tubercles. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Tinospora tuberculata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
