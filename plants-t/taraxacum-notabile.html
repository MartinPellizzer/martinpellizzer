Taraxacum notabile

Taraxacum notabile, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. Its seeds are dispersed by wind through a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

Taraxacum notabile is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
