Teucrium atratum

Teucrium atratum, commonly known as black germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, dark purple to blackish-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and have a minty scent.

Teucrium atratum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
