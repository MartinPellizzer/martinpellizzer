Thymus bornmuelleri

Thymus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and contains essential oils, making it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus bornmuelleri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
