Trichocephalus callosus

Trichocephalus callosus, commonly known as the rough-hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has fibrous roots and produces slender, hairy stems that are often clustered. Its leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in clusters at the base of the plant.
