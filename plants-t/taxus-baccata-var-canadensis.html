Taxus baccata var. canadensis

Taxus baccata var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian yew, is a variant of the European yew with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 meters, featuring a conical or broad pyramidal shape. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, arranged in dense clusters. Its cones are small, reddish-brown, and contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril.

This variety is native to eastern North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance.
