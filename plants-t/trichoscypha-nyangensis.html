Trichoscypha nyangensis

Trichoscypha nyangensis, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
