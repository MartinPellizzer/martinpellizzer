Tephrosia coerulea

Tephrosia coerulea, commonly known as blue tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its slender, often branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Tephrosia coerulea is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
