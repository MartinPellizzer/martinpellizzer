Teclea unifoliolata

Teclea unifoliolata, commonly known as the single leaf teclea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive ray florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with a woody stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Teclea unifoliolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
