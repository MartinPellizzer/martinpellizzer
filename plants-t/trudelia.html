Trudelia

Trudelia, commonly known as the silverleaf trudelia, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of waxy trichomes that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pale blue and violet, which bloom during the cooler months. Trudelia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
