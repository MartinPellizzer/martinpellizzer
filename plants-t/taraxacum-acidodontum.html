Taraxacum acidodontum

Taraxacum acidodontum, commonly known as the acidodontum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce a milky sap, which is a common feature among the genus Taraxacum.
