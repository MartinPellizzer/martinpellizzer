Tanacetum aureum var. oligocephalum

Tanacetum aureum var. oligocephalum, commonly known as the golden buttonhead tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in loose, flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
