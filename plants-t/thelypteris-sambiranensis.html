Thelypteris sambiranensis

Thelypteris sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranese holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that are typically lanceolate and serrated along the edges. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, arranged in a distinctive pattern.

Thelypteris sambiranensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
