Typhonium pedatisectum

Typhonium pedatisectum, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with spadices, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
