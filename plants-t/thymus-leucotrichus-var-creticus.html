Thymus leucotrichus var. creticus

Thymus leucotrichus var. creticus, commonly known as Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It features small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
