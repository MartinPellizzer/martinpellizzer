Tragacantha caucasica

Tragacantha caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a gum resin, which is harvested from its bark and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and shrublands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
