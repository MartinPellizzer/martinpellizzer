Tagetes gilletii

Tagetes gilletii, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.

Tagetes gilletii is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
