Tilia platyptera

Tilia platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf lime or Chinese lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically flat and smooth. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed nut enclosed in a husk.

Tilia platyptera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
