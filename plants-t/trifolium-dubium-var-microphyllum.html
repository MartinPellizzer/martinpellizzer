Trifolium dubium var. microphyllum

Trifolium dubium var. microphyllum, commonly known as lesser clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
