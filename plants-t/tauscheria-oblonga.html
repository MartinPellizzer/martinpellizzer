Tauscheria oblonga

Tauscheria oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Tauscheria oblonga is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.
