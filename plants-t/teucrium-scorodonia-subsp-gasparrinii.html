Teucrium scorodonia subsp. gasparrinii

Teucrium scorodonia subsp.

gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in compact clusters. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.
