Terminalia adamantium

Terminalia adamantium, commonly known as the "bloodwood" tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is often used in traditional medicine and craftsmanship. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, red or orange fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

Terminalia adamantium is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
