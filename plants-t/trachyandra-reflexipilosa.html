Trachyandra reflexipilosa

Trachyandra reflexipilosa, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Trachyandra reflexipilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant, airy foliage.
