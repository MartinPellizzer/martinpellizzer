Tephrocactus wilkeanus

Tephrocactus wilkeanus, commonly known as the Wilke's cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and lack of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
