Trollius vitalii var. nadeshdae

Trollius vitalii var. nadeshdae, commonly known as the Nadeshda globeflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble globes, which bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after addition to rock gardens and alpine collections.
