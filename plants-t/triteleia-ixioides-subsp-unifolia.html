Triteleia ixioides subsp. unifolia

Triteleia ixioides subsp.

unifolia, commonly known as the slender triteleia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of California. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and coastal scrub habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
