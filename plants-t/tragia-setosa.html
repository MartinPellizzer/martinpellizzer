Tragia setosa

Tragia setosa, commonly known as the slender tragia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers and is characterized by its branched, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tragia setosa is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.
