Trisetaria sibirica

Trisetaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and other northern regions.

It typically grows in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Trisetaria sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
