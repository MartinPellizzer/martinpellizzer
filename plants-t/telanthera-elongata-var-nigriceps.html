Telanthera elongata var. nigriceps

Telanthera elongata var. nigriceps, commonly known as the black-headed telanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, with a distinctive blackish-purple calyx. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and floral interest.
