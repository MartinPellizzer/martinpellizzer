Triticum vulgare var. atratum

Triticum vulgare var. atratum, commonly known as black wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its dark, almost black husk and grain.

It is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences bearing small, dark grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex vascular structure typical of grasses.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
