Taraxacum pseudobicorne

Taraxacum pseudobicorne, commonly known as the two-horned dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Taraxacum pseudobicorne is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to grow in gardens and meadows.
