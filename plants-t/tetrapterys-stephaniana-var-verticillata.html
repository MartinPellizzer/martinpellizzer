Tetrapterys stephaniana var. verticillata

Tetrapterys stephaniana var. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes nettles and related plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive venation pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces small, smooth fruits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
