Tragia discolor var. cuneata

Tragia discolor var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
