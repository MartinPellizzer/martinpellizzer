Thalictrum lugdunense

Thalictrum lugdunense, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early to mid-summer, which are arranged in racemes. Thalictrum lugdunense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
