Tragia salviifolia

Tragia salviifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tragia salviifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
