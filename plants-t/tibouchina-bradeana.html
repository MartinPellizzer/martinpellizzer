Tibouchina bradeana

Tibouchina bradeana, commonly known as the Brazilian flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Tibouchina bradeana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
