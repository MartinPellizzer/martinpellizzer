Trichomanes capillaceum var. cocos

Trichomanes capillaceum var. cocos, commonly known as the coconut fern, is a variant of the common huperzia found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae, characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-bearing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
