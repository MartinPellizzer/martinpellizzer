Thunbergia nidulans

Thunbergia nidulans, commonly known as the hanging arum, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Thunbergia nidulans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
