Tectaria paradoxa

Tectaria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in montane forests. Tectaria paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
