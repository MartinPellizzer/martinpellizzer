Thujopsis dolabrata f. variegata

Thujopsis dolabrata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese white pine, is a cultivar of the Japanese white pine tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow markings on its needle-like leaves. This plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of climates.

The plant is relatively slow-growing and can reach heights of up to 20 feet, providing a unique visual presence in the landscape.
