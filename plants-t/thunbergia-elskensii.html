Thunbergia elskensii

Thunbergia elskensii, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
