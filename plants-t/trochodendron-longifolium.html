Trochodendron longifolium

Trochodendron longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved trochodendron, is a rare and ancient flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Trochodendraceae, which is one of the oldest known groups of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, spirally arranged leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its unique morphology and evolutionary significance make it a subject of interest in botanical research.
