Tragacantha purpurascens

Tragacantha purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
