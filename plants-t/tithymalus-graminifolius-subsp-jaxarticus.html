Tithymalus graminifolius subsp. jaxarticus

Tithymalus graminifolius subsp.

jaxarticus, commonly known as the Jaxartian tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in grasslands and steppe environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in shades of blue or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
