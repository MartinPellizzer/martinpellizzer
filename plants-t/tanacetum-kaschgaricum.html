Tanacetum kaschgaricum

Tanacetum kaschgaricum, commonly known as the Karakoram tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often emitting a strong, camphor-like scent.

Tanacetum kaschgaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
