Tamarix pauciovulata

Tamarix pauciovulata, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small scale-like leaves, and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert environments and provides habitat for various insects and birds.

However, it can become invasive in non-native areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
