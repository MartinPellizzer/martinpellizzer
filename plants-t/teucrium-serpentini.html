Teucrium serpentini

Teucrium serpentini, commonly known as serpent's root, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium serpentini is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
