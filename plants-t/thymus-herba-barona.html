Thymus herba-barona

Thymus herba-barona, commonly known as narrow-leaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, minty aroma.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine for its antimicrobial properties.
