Toxicodendron burchellii

Toxicodendron burchellii, commonly known as the African lacquer tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is known for its ability to exude a sticky, resinous substance, which can be toxic to some animals and humans.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
