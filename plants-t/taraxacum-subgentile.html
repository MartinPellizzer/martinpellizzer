Taraxacum subgentile

Taraxacum subgentile, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in gardens and natural areas.
