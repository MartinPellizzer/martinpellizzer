Tricholaena arenaria var. glauca

Tricholaena arenaria var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous tricholaena, is a variant of the species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its distinctive glaucous (bluish-gray) foliage, which gives it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
