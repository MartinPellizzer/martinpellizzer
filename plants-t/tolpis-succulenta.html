Tolpis succulenta

Tolpis succulenta, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate drought.

Tolpis succulenta is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of food for certain wildlife species.
