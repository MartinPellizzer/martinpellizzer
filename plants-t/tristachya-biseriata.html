Tristachya biseriata

Tristachya biseriata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Tristachya biseriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
