Trichostachys talbotii

Trichostachys talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open, sunny areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
