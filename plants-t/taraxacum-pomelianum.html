Taraxacum pomelianum

Taraxacum pomelianum, commonly known as the Pomelian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hollow, ridged stem that supports a single flower head. The flower consists of both ray and disc florets, which are typically yellow in color. This perennial plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
