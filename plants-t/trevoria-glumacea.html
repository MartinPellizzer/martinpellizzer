Trevoria glumacea

Trevoria glumacea, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Trevoria glumacea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
