Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. nuchense

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

nuchense, commonly known as the alpine germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically features dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
