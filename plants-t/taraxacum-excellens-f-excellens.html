Taraxacum excellens f. excellens

Taraxacum excellens f. excellens, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that bears a flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, with the yellow disk florets developing into a crown of seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
