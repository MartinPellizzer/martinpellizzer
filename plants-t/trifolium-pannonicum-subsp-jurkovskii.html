Trifolium pannonicum subsp. jurkovskii

Trifolium pannonicum subsp.

jurkovskii, commonly known as the Pannonian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
