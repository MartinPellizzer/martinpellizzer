Thelypteris decussata

Thelypteris decussata, commonly known as the crossleaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that often exhibit a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous adventitious roots, aiding in its spread and survival.

Thelypteris decussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
