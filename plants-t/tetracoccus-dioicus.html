Tetracoccus dioicus

Tetracoccus dioicus, commonly known as the common sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for bark, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastal areas.

Tetracoccus dioicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal ecosystems.
