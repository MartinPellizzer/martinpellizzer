Thelypteris atrorubens

Thelypteris atrorubens, commonly known as the black-brown holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its fronds are leathery and have a lanceolate shape, with spores produced on the underside of the leaves.

Thelypteris atrorubens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
