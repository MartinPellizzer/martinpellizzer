Trachymene hemicarpa var. rotundifolia

Trachymene hemicarpa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understoreys.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
