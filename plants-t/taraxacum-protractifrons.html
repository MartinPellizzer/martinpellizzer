Taraxacum protractifrons

Taraxacum protractifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to produce a large number of wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often considered a weed but has ecological and potential medicinal value.
