Trisetum flavescens var. griseovirens

Trisetum flavescens var. griseovirens, commonly known as the gray-green false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a bluish-gray sheen. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, hairy texture on both surfaces. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to yellowish flowers.

This plant is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
