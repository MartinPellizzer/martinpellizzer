Thalictrum subtrigynum

Thalictrum subtrigynum, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves that are deeply divided. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Thalictrum subtrigynum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
