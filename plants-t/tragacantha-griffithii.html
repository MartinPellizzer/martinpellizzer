Tragacantha griffithii

Tragacantha griffithii, commonly known as the Indian gum arabic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is notable for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in food, medicine, and industry.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important species in arid ecosystems.
