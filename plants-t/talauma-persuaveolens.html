Talauma persuaveolens

Talauma persuaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented talauma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
