Trichosanthes khasiana

Trichosanthes khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bitter melon, is a vine native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant produces elongated, bitter fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, rough texture.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
