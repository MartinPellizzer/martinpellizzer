Trisetum argentoideum

Trisetum argentoideum, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trisetum argentoideum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
