Tephrosia aurantiaca subsp. hockii

Tephrosia aurantiaca subsp.

hockii, commonly known as the scarlet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
