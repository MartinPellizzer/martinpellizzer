Thalictrum foetidum subsp. acutilobum

Thalictrum foetidum subsp.

acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features slender, often hairy stems that bear whorled clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are deeply divided with sharp, pointed lobes, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its delicate, lace-like foliage and its role in supporting pollinators in its native ecosystems.
