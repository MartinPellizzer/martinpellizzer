Trigonochilum murinum

Trigonochilum murinum, commonly known as the mouse ear cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of oval, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

Trigonochilum murinum is a herbaceous perennial that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
