Thoa

Thoa, commonly known as the lesser periwinkle, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces delicate blue or violet flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, round berries. Thoa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded areas.

Its botanical name is Vinca minor, and it belongs to the family Apocynaceae.
