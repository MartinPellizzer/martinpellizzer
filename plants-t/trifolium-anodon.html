Trifolium anodon

Trifolium anodon, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Trifolium anodon is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
