Telopea oreades

Telopea oreades, commonly known as the mountain society grevillea, is a striking shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has leathery, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand the harsh, sunny conditions of its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and cliff faces.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
