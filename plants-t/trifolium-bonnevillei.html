Trifolium bonnevillei

Trifolium bonnevillei, commonly known as the Bonneville clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Trifolium bonnevillei is adapted to arid environments and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
