Trifolium glaucescens

Trifolium glaucescens, commonly known as the blue clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium glaucescens is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
