Trichopteryx glanvillei

Trichopteryx glanvillei, commonly known as the Glanville's trichopteryx, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Trichopteryx glanvillei is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
