Thuranthos razii

Thuranthos razii, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features striking silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in its survival in harsh environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often blooming during the late spring and early summer. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in low-nutrient soils.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
