Trichipteris floribunda

Trichipteris floribunda, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading fronds that are often adorned with small, bright yellow spore-bearing structures called sorangia. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high air humidity to flourish. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.

Trichipteris floribunda plays an important ecological role by contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
