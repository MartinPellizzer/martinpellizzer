Teucrium × scorolepis

Teucrium × scorolepis, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in hue, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
