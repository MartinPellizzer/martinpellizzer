Tsuga heterophylla var. argenteovariegata

Tsuga heterophylla var. argenteovariegata, commonly known as the silver-variegated fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on the foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant has a conical shape with dense, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more flexible than those of the standard Douglas fir. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
