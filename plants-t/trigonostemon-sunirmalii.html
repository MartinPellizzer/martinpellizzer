Trigonostemon sunirmalii

Trigonostemon sunirmalii, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are opposite and have three leaflets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Trigonostemon sunirmalii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
