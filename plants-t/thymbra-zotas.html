Thymbra zotas

Thymbra zotas, commonly known as Zotas thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and have a strong thyme-like scent, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymbra zotas thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.
