Trifurcia lahue subsp. caerulea

Trifurcia lahue subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered trifurcia, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to the highlands of South America. It is characterized by its striking azure petals and robust, woody stems that can reach heights of up to two meters.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
