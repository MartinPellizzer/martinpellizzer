Tridax

Tridax, commonly known as the beggar's ticks, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Tridax is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties in some traditional systems of medicine.
