Tanacetopsis Paropamisica

Paropamisic tanacetum, scientifically known as Tanacetopsis paropamisica, is a rare and fascinating plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. This small, herbaceous plant is characterized by its delicate white flowers and finely divided leaves, which give it a unique appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes where conditions are harsh and competition is minimal. Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this rare plant from habitat loss and climate change impacts.
