Trifolium cylindrocephalum

Trifolium cylindrocephalum, commonly known as the cylinder-headed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical-shaped flower heads, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or white.

Trifolium cylindrocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
