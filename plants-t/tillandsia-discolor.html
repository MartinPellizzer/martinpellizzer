Tillandsia discolor

Tillandsia discolor, commonly known as the silver vane air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped structure with long, thin leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
