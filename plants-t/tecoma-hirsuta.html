Tecoma hirsuta

Tecoma hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tecoma hirsuta is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
