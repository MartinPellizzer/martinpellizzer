Townsendia texensis

Townsendia texensis, commonly known as the Texas Townsend's wildflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as limestone hills and grasslands. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Townsendia texensis is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
