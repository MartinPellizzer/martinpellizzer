Tilia obliqua

Tilia obliqua, commonly known as the western gray-leafed linden, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color, with a rough texture. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its bark is typically gray and smooth, with shallow fissures.

Tilia obliqua is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
