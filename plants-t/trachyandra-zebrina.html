Trachyandra zebrina

Trachyandra zebrina, commonly known as the zebra grass, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fibrous, textured leaves that resemble zebra stripes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
