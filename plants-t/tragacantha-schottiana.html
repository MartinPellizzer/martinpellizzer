Tragacantha schottiana

Tragacantha schottiana, commonly known as Schott's tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Tragacantha schottiana typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological research.
