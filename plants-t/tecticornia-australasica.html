Tecticornia australasica

Tecticornia australasica, commonly known as the Australian saltbush, is a hardy shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Tecticornia australasica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
