Tylophora fasciculata

Tylophora fasciculata, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a strong scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds embedded in a gelatinous coating.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
