Tillandsia araeostachya

Tillandsia araeostachya, commonly known as the silver pine tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
