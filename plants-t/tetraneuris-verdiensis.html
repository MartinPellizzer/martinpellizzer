Tetraneuris verdiensis

Tetraneuris verdiensis, commonly known as the Verdien's tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
