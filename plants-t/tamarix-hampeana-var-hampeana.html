Tamarix hampeana var. hampeana

Tamarix hampeana var. hampeana, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, pink to reddish flowers in summer, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
