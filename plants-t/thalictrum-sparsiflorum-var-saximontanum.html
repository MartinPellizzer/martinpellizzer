Thalictrum sparsiflorum var. saximontanum

Thalictrum sparsiflorum var. saximontanum, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
