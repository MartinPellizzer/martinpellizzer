Tragacantha fasciculifolia

Tragacantha fasciculifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
