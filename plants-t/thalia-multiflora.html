Thalia multiflora

Thalia multiflora, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshes, thriving in moist, sandy soils. It produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalia multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
