Tragacantha kashmirensis

Tragacantha kashmirensis, commonly known as Kashmir tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Kashmir Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its mucilaginous sap, which has been traditionally used in herbal medicine and as a natural gum.

This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat.
