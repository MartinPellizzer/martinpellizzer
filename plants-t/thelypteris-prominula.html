Thelypteris prominula

Thelypteris prominula, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris prominula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
