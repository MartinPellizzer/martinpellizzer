Timonius mollis

Timonius mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Timonius mollis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
