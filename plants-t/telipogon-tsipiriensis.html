Telipogon tsipiriensis

Telipogon tsipiriensis, commonly known as the Tsipiriensis orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky soils and humid microhabitats. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow to greenish flowers with intricate patterns, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphology and limited distribution.

Due to habitat destruction and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
