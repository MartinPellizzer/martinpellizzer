Tristachya tristachyoides

Tristachya tristachyoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed papyrus, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces inflorescences composed of multiple spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems and has been used traditionally in various cultures.
