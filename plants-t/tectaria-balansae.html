Tectaria balansae

Tectaria balansae, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy fronds that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate structure that allows it to thrive in humid conditions.

Tectaria balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
