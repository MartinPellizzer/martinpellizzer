Thymus praecox subsp. trachselianus

Thymus praecox subsp.

trachselianus, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and limestone areas.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
