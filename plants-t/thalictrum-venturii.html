Thalictrum venturii

Thalictrum venturii, commonly known as the Venturi's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds.

Thalictrum venturii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
