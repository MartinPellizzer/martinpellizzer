Tenageia multiflora

Tenageia multiflora, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, preferring moist, acidic soil. It has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval-shaped with smooth edges.

Tenageia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
