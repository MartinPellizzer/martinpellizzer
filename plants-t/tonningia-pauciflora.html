Tonningia pauciflora

Tonningia pauciflora, commonly known as the rare and delicate flowering plant of the orchid family, is a small epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.

Its botanical study is important for understanding the biodiversity of tropical rainforests and the conservation of rare flora.
