Thapsia

Thapsia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, spiky flowers and silver-blue foliage, which often gives them a distinctive appearance. Thapsia species are typically found in coastal areas and rocky soils, adapting well to saline and arid environments. They produce small, tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus includes several species, with Thapsia coronaria and Thapsia latifolia being among the most commonly recognized.
