Toulicia crassifolia

Toulicia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
