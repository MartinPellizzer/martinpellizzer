Tabacum ovatofolium

Tabacum ovatofolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf tobacco.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of tobacco products.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
