Thelypteris matutumensis

Thelypteris matutumensis, commonly known as the morning glory fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, and they are arranged in a distinctive linear pattern.

Thelypteris matutumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
