Trifolium involucratum var. fendleri

Trifolium involucratum var. fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
