Thymus spathulifolius

Thymus spathulifolius, commonly known as broad-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are oval to broadly ovate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
