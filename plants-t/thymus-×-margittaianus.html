Thymus × margittaianus

Thymus × margittaianus, commonly known as the Margittaian thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus × margittaianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, thyme-like fragrance.
