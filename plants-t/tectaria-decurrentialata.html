Tectaria decurrentialata

Tectaria decurrentialata, commonly known as the decurrent tectaria, is a fern belonging to the Tectariaceae family.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically pinnately divided with a distinctive decurrent vein pattern. The plant produces spores in sori that are clustered on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its morphology and spore structure are key features in its classification and ecological role.
