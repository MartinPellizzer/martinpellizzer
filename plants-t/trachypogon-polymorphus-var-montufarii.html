Trachypogon polymorphus var. montufarii

Trachypogon polymorphus var. montufarii, commonly known as the Montufar's tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets.

This variety is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
