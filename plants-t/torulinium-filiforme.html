Torulinium filiforme

Torulinium filiforme, commonly known as the slender torulinium, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the short growing season. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in rocky crevices. Its botanical structure includes a short, fibrous root system and a stem that grows close to the ground.
