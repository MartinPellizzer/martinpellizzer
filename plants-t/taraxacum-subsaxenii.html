Taraxacum subsaxenii

Taraxacum subsaxenii, commonly known as the saxicola dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant thrives in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers produce wind-dispersed seeds, a common reproductive strategy among dandelions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
