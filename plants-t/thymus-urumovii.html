Thymus urumovii

Thymus urumovii, commonly known as Urumov's thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This low-growing perennial herb has small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its essential oils, similar to other thyme species, are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
