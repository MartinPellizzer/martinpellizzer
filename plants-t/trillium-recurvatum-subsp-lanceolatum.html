Trillium recurvatum subsp. lanceolatum

Trillium recurvatum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces a single, white, showy flower with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, organic soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
