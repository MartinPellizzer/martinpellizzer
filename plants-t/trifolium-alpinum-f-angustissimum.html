Trifolium alpinum f. angustissimum

Trifolium alpinum f. angustissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alpine clover, is a botanical variant of the alpine clover species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. This plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb that is well adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.

Its narrow leaves and compact growth form make it distinct from other clover species.
