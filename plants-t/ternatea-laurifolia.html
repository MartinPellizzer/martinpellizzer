Ternatea laurifolia

Ternatea laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in late spring or early summer.

Ternatea laurifolia is often found in wetland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
