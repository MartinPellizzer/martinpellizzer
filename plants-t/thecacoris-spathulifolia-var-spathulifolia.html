Thecacoris spathulifolia var. spathulifolia

Thecacoris spathulifolia var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed tachyderm, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or purple, which are arranged in terminal clusters. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
