Thlaspi abchasicum

Thlaspi abchasicum, commonly known as the Abchazian cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It is native to the Caucasus region, particularly Abkhazia, and thrives in rocky or alpine environments.

Thlaspi abchasicum is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.
