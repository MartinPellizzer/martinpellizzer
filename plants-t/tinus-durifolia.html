Tinus durifolia

Tinus durifolia, commonly known as the hard-leaved tinsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tinus durifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
