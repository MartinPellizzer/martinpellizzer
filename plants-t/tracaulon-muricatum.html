Tracaulon muricatum

Tracaulon muricatum, commonly known as the rough passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a hairy, orange-yellow berry that contains numerous seeds and a juicy, aromatic pulp.

Tracaulon muricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
