Thelypteris dehuaensis

Thelypteris dehuaensis, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a fern species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in wet habitats.

The leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of sori on their undersides.
