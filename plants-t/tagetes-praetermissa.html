Tagetes praetermissa

Tagetes praetermissa, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow and orange blooms, which are typically daisy-like in appearance. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to repel certain pests due to the strong scent of its flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
