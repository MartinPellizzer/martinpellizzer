Taraxacum albicollum

Taraxacum albicollum, commonly known as the white-necked dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and a distinctive white collar around the base of its stem. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports its flowering head.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, typical of the dandelion genus.
