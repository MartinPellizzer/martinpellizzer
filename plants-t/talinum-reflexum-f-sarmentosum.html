Talinum reflexum f. sarmentosum

Talinum reflexum f. sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping talinum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features sprawling, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats or ground covers. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Talinum reflexum f. sarmentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and ability to spread effectively.
