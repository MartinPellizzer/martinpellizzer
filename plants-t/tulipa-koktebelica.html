Tulipa koktebelica

Tulipa koktebelica, commonly known as the Koktebel tulip, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, single-layered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.

This tulip is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
