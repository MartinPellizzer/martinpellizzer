Trifolium pignantii f. piligerum

Trifolium pignantii f. piligerum, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This subspecies belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to specific regions in Europe. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grassy habitats.
