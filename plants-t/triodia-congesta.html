Triodia congesta

Triodia congesta, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small fauna.

Triodia congesta is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall.
