Taraxacum dahlii

Taraxacum dahlii, commonly known as the Dahl's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
