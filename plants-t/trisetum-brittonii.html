Trisetum brittonii

Trisetum brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple.

Trisetum brittonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
