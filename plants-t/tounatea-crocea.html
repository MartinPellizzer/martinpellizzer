Tounatea crocea

Tounatea crocea, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spiders due to their shape and color. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tounatea crocea is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
