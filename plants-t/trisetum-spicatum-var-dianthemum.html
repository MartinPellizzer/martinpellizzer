Trisetum spicatum var. dianthemum

Trisetum spicatum var. dianthemum, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological restoration and landscaping.
