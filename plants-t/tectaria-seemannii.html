Tectaria seemannii

Tectaria seemannii, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically divided into segments with a waxy, leathery texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to anchor itself to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Tectaria seemannii plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
