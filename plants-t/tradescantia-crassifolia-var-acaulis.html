Tradescantia crassifolia var. acaulis

Tradescantia crassifolia var. acaulis, commonly known as the compact tradescantia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southern United States.

It features dense, upright rosettes of fleshy, waxy leaves that range in color from green to reddish-purple, depending on light exposure. The plant produces tall, branching stems with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is a perennial succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for rock gardens, containers, or as a ground cover in dry, sunny environments.
