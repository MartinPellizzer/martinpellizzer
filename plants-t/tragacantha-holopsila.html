Tragacantha holopsila

Tragacantha holopsila, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its sap, which is thick and sticky, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries.
