Trifolium bracteolatum

Trifolium bracteolatum, commonly known as the bracteolatum clover, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Trifolium bracteolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
