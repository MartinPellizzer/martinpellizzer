Tragiola pilosa subsp. epilis

Tragiola pilosa subsp.

epilis, commonly known as the hairy tragia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The species produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has adapted to arid conditions with its succulent-like leaves and deep root system.
