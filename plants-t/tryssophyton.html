Tryssophyton

Tryssophyton, commonly known as the "ghost fern," is a genus of fossilized plants that dates back to the Carboniferous period.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, ribbon-like fronds that are often preserved in coal deposits. They are considered early vascular plants, possessing specialized tissues for water transport. Tryssophyton played a significant role in the formation of ancient coal seams through its extensive growth in swampy environments.

Its study provides valuable insights into the evolution of early plant life and paleoecological conditions.
