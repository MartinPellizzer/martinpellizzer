Tropidococcus pinnatipartitus

Tropidococcus pinnatipartitus, commonly known as the palmate liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth pattern and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a thalloid structure with a distinct, lobed appearance that resembles a palm leaf, giving it its common name. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a simple, flat body called a thallus.

This liverwort is often used as an indicator species for assessing the health of forest ecosystems.
