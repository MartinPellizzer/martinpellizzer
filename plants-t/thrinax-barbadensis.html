Thrinax barbadensis

Thrinax barbadensis, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a species of palm native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small, slow-growing tree that typically reaches a height of about 5 to 10 meters. The plant has a short, stocky trunk and is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves, which are composed of numerous stiff, spiny segments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The Mexican palm is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
