Thecophyllum pictum

Thecophyllum pictum, commonly known as the painted shield fern, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful fronds that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, which are particularly vivid during the flowering season. The plant's leaves are typically shield-shaped, giving it its common name, and they are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
