Trapa pseudincisa var. potaninii

Trapa pseudincisa var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's water chestnut, is a rare aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as lakes and ponds. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps it float.
