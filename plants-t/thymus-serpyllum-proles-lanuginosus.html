Thymus serpyllum proles lanuginosus

Thymus serpyllum proles lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
