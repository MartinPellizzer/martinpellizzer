Thea reticulata var. wabiske

Thea reticulata var. wabiske, commonly known as the Wabiski tea plant, is a rare and ornamental species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their mild astringent properties.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and unique botanical features.
