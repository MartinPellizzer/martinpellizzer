Tephrosia rosea var. angustifolia

Tephrosia rosea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with slender, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is known for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
