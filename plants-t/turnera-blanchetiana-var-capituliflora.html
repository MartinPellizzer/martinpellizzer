Turnera blanchetiana var. capituliflora

Turnera blanchetiana var. capituliflora, commonly known as the clustered turnera, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and emits a faint, sweet fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
