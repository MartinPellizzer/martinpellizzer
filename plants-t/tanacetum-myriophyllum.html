Tanacetum myriophyllum

Tanacetum myriophyllum, commonly known as water dropwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as wet meadows and marshes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
