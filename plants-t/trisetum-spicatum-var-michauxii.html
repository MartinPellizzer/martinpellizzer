Trisetum spicatum var. michauxii

Trisetum spicatum var. michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's wood oats, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or brown. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and the plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

This variety is closely related to other members of the grass family, Poaceae, and exhibits typical characteristics of the genus Trisetum.
