Teucrium dentatum

Teucrium dentatum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Teucrium dentatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
