Triticum spelta var. subbuldoji

Triticum spelta var. subbuldoji, commonly known as intermediate spelt, is a variant of spelt wheat that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hexaploid grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, with a compact growth habit and a short, sturdy stalk. The plant produces spikelets that are often more elongated than those of standard spelt, and its grains are smaller and more angular. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically cultivated for its hardy nature and potential nutritional benefits.

This variety is valued for its resilience to environmental stress and its use in specialty grains and baking.
