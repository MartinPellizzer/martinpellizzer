Thalictrum nigricans

Thalictrum nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed meadow rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Thalictrum nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
