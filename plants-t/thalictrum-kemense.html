Thalictrum kemense

Thalictrum kemense, commonly known as the Kemensh wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Thalictrum kemense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
