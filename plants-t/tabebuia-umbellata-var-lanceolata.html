Tabebuia umbellata var. lanceolata

Tabebuia umbellata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and produces long, lanceolate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

This species is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
