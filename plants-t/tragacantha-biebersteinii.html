Tragacantha biebersteinii

Tragacantha biebersteinii, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus biebersteinii, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran, Afghanistan, and the Caucasus. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is characterized by its unique, branched root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Tragacantha biebersteinii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
