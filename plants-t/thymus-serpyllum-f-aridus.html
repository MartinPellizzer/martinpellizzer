Thymus serpyllum f. aridus

Thymus serpyllum f. aridus, commonly known as the desert thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, aromatic leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
