Trichinium divaricatum

Trichinium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Trichinium divaricatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
