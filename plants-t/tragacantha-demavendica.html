Tragacantha demavendica

Tragacantha demavendica, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
