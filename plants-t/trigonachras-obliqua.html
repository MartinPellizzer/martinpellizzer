Trigonachras obliqua

Trigonachras obliqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender stalks. Its stems are square in cross-section, a common trait among plants in the mint family.

Trigonachras obliqua thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
