Thuja defresneana

Thuja defresneana, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
