Thespesia lampas var. longisepala

Thespesia lampas var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
