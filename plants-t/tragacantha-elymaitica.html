Tragacantha elymaitica

Tragacantha elymaitica, commonly known as the Armenian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its most notable feature is the production of a viscous gum, which is harvested from the stem and used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

This plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.
