Tournefortia punctata

Tournefortia punctata, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Tournefortia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
