Trientalis europaea

Trientalis europaea, commonly known as the European starflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Trientalis europaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
