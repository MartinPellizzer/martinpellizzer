Tanacetum pamiralaicum

Tanacetum pamiralaicum, commonly known as the Pamir tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Tanacetum pamiralaicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
