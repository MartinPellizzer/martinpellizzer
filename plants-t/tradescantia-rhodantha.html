Tradescantia rhodantha

Tradescantia rhodantha, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features striking foliage with deep green veins contrasting against a lighter green or white background. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a favored addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
