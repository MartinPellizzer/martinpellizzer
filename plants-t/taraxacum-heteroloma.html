Taraxacum heteroloma

Taraxacum heteroloma, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, a common trait among dandelions. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.
