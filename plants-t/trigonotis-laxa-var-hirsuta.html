Trigonotis laxa var. hirsuta

Trigonotis laxa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental ground cover.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial in diverse habitats.
