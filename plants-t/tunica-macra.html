Tunica macra

Tunica macra, commonly known as the purple tunic plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Tunica macra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
