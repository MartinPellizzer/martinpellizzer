Trimorpha × vierhapperi

Trimorpha × vierhapperi, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a variety of morphological traits that distinguish it from its parent species.
