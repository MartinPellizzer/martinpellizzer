Taraxacum abax

Taraxacum abax, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. Taraxacum abax is perennial, with a deep taproot that allows it to survive in harsh, cold climates.

Its flowers produce seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in high-altitude habitats.
