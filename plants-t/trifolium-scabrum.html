Trifolium scabrum

Trifolium scabrum, commonly known as hairy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Trifolium scabrum is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas across temperate regions.
