Teucrium haradjanii

Teucrium haradjanii, commonly known as Haradjan's germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring square stems and opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
