Terminalia lancifolia

Terminalia lancifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
