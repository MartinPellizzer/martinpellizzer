Taraxacum hamatifrons

Taraxacum hamatifrons, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and serrated leaf margins. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem and has a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
