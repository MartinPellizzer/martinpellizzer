Tulbaghia friesii

Tulbaghia friesii, commonly known as the Cape garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Tulbaghia friesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
