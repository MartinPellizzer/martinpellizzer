Turnera melanorhiza

Turnera melanorhiza, commonly known as black turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
