Tulipa praestans

Tulipa praestans, commonly known as the Emperor tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers with vivid, often multi-colored petals that resemble a peacock's tail. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base.

Tulipa praestans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
