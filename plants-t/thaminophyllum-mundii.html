Thaminophyllum mundii

Thaminophyllum mundii, commonly known as the Moonlight Fern, is a unique and rare plant species characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds.

Native to the dense, humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its leaves emit a soft, ethereal glow at night, a trait attributed to specialized photophores containing luciferin and luciferase. The plant's reproductive cycle involves spore dispersal through wind, aided by its lightweight, airborne spores.

Due to habitat destruction, Thaminophyllum mundii is now classified as critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
