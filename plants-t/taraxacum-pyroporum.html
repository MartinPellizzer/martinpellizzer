Taraxacum pyroporum

Taraxacum pyroporum, commonly known as the fireweed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like sunflowers and daisies.
