Teucrium montanum var. pannonicum

Teucrium montanum var. pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers from June to August. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
