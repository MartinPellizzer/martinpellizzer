Tripsacum manisuroides

Tripsacum manisuroides, commonly known as the manisuroides grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
