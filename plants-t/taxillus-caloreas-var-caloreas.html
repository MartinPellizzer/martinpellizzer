Taxillus caloreas var. caloreas

Taxillus caloreas var. caloreas, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by the host's leaves. The plant forms a dense, bushy growth with small, oval-shaped leaves and white or pale green berries that contain sticky seeds. It plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for birds and other animals, which aid in seed dispersal.

Its parasitic nature and unique growth habits make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
