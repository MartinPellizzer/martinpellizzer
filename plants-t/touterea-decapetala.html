Touterea decapetala

Touterea decapetala, commonly known as the Tahitian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
