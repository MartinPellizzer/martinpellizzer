Tetrastigma scortechinii

Tetrastigma scortechinii, commonly known as the Chinese crossvine, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
