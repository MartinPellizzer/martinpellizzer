Tanacetum praeteritum subsp. praeteritum

Tanacetum praeteritum subsp.

praeteritum, commonly known as the slender tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
