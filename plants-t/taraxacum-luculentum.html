Taraxacum luculentum

Taraxacum luculentum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the familiar pappus-based seeds that are dispersed by the wind. The plant has a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas, lawns, and meadows.

Taraxacum luculentum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
