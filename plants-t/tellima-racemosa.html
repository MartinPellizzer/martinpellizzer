Tellima racemosa

Tellima racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
