Thermopsis fraxinifolia

Thermopsis fraxinifolia, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in botanical gardens and natural landscapes.
