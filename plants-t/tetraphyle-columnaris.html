Tetraphyle columnaris

Tetraphyle columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has thick, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

Tetraphyle columnaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
