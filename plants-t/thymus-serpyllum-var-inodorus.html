Thymus serpyllum var. inodorus

Thymus serpyllum var. inodorus, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to lavender flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and various therapeutic properties.
