Teucrium marum f. subspinosum

Teucrium marum f. subspinosum, commonly known as the spiny caraway, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its spiny stems and leaves, which provide a unique botanical feature.
