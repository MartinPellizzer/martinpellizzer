Trifolium popovii

Trifolium popovii, commonly known as the Popov's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to ovate leaflets, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Trifolium popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
