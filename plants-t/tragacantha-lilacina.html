Tragacantha lilacina

Tragacantha lilacina, commonly known as the lilac milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pale lilac or pinkish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is a defining feature of the genus Tragacantha. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tragacantha lilacina is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
