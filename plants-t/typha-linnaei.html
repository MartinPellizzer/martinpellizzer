Typha linnaei

Typha linnaei, commonly known as the narrow leaf cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes and broad, sword-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant produces a unique flowering structure that consists of two distinct parts: a fluffy, brown seed head and a dense, yellowish-green vegetative spike. Typha linnaei plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes spread extensively, allowing it to form dense colonies that can dominate wetland environments.
