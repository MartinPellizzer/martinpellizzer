Taraxacum palustre f. spurium

Taraxacum palustre f. spurium, commonly known as the swamp dandelion, is a botanical variant of the common dandelion.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated stems and smaller, less showy yellow flowers compared to its typical counterpart. This plant thrives in moist, often boggy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments. Its leaves are typically broader and more lance-shaped, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

The species is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
