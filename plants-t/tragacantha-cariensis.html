Tragacantha cariensis

Tragacantha cariensis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for producing a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.
