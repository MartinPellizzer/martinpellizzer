Taraxacum gracillimum

Taraxacum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and a single, slender stem that supports a yellow flower head. The plant produces a fluffy, white seed head that disperses its seeds by wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family. It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions.

Taraxacum gracillimum is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for insects and a pioneer species in ecological succession.
