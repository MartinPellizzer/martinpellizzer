Triticum vulgare var. provinciale

Triticum vulgare var. provinciale, commonly known as timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and pastures.

Timothy grass is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly used as forage for livestock.
