Tricyrtis pilosa

Tricyrtis pilosa, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tricyrtis pilosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
