Tylophora balansae

Tylophora balansae, commonly known as the Balansae moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
