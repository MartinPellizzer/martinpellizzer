Tradescantia belizensis

Tradescantia belizensis, commonly known as the Mexican spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers and grass-like, sword-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and ease of care.

Tradescantia belizensis is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
