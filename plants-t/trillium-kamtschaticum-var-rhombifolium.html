Trillium kamtschaticum var. rhombifolium

Trillium kamtschaticum var. rhombifolium, commonly known as the Kamchatka trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of the Russian Far East.

It features three broad, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, with a waxy texture. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in coniferous or mixed forests.
