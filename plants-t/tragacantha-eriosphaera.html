Tragacantha eriosphaera

Tragacantha eriosphaera, commonly known as the hairy globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped inflorescence and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Tragacantha eriosphaera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
