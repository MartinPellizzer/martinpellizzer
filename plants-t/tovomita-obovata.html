Tovomita obovata

Tovomita obovata, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tovomita obovata thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
