Tricalysia galpinii

Tricalysia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's flame tree, is a tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Tricalysia galpinii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
