Trichantha oblongifolia

Trichantha oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trichantha, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Trichantha oblongifolia is often found in shaded, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
