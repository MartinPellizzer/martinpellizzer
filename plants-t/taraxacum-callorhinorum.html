Taraxacum callorhinorum

Taraxacum callorhinorum, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like structures at the base of its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
