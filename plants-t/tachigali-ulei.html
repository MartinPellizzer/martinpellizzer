Tachigali ulei

Tachigali ulei, commonly known as yellow tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
