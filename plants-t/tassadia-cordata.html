Tassadia cordata

Tassadia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tassadia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. Tassadia cordata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique leaf shape.
