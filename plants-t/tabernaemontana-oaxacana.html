Tabernaemontana oaxacana

Tabernaemontana oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers have five petals and a prominent, showy crown, making it a visually appealing species.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
