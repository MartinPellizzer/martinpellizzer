Thymus campestris var. grandiflorus

Thymus campestris var. grandiflorus, commonly known as large-flowered thrift, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, aromatic, pink or purple flowers. The plant features grey-green, woolly leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
