Trachelium caeruleum var. lanceolatum

Trachelium caeruleum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
