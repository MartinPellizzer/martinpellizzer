Trigonotis

Trigonotis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are slender and twining.

Trigonotis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming pattern.
