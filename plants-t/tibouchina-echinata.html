Tibouchina echinata

Tibouchina echinata, commonly known as the purple flame or pineapple flower, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tibouchina echinata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
