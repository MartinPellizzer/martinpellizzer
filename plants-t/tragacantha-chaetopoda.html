Tragacantha chaetopoda

Tragacantha chaetopoda, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Tragacantha chaetopoda is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various industries.
