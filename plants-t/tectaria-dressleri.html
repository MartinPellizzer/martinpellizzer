Tectaria dressleri

Tectaria dressleri, commonly known as the Hawaiian tectaria, is a fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and the plant reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Tectaria dressleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
