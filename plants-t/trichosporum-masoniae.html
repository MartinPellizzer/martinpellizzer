Trichosporum masoniae

Trichosporum masoniae, commonly known as the Mason's beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with a dense, woolly upper surface and a more leathery lower surface. The thallus is typically green to yellowish in color, and it contains a mixture of algae and fungi in a symbiotic relationship. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

This lichen is of interest to mycologists due to its ecological role and unique morphological features.
