Taraxacum danubium

Taraxacum danubium, commonly known as the Danube dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface and a hollow stem that supports a single flowering head.

The flower head consists of ray florets that transition to disk florets, producing a distinctive yellow color before maturing into seeds.
