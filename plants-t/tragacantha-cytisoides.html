Tragacantha cytisoides

Tragacantha cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a sizing agent for textiles. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and dry landscapes.
