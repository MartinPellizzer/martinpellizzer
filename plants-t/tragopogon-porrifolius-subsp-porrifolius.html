Tragopogon porrifolius subsp. porrifolius

Tragopogon porrifolius subsp.

porrifolius, commonly known as the common goatsbeard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, followed by a feathery seed head. Its leaves are basal, long, and pinnately lobed, often with a white underside.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.
