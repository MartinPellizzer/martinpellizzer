Turpinia nepalensis

Turpinia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal turpentine tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that exudes a turpentine-like scent when cut. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate and subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
