Tilia amurensis var. tricuspidata

Tilia amurensis var. tricuspidata, commonly known as the Amur tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its specific epithet "tricuspidata.". The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
