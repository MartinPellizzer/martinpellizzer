Tapeinochilos spectabilis

Tapeinochilos spectabilis, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Tapeinochilos spectabilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
