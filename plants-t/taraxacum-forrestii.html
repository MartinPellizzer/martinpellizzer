Taraxacum forrestii

Taraxacum forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous small seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically basal, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Taraxacum forrestii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
