Taraxis grossa

Taraxis grossa, commonly known as the common taraxacum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in early spring. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Taraxis grossa is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
