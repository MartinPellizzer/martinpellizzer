Trichosanthes cucumeroides var. hainanensis

Trichosanthes cucumeroides var. hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan bitter melon, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to southern China.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, bitter fruits that are typically harvested when immature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible, though spicy, fruit.

Due to its unique flavor and medicinal properties, it holds cultural and agricultural significance in certain regions of China.
