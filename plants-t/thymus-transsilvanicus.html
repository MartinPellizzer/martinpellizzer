Thymus transsilvanicus

Thymus transsilvanicus, commonly known as Transylvanian thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus transsilvanicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
