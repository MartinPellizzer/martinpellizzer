Telanthera philoxeroides var. phyllantha

Telanthera philoxeroides var. phyllantha, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

However, it can be invasive in some areas if not properly managed.
