Tamarix gallica

Tamarix gallica, commonly known as the European tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically deciduous in autumn. The plant produces pink or red flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Tamarix gallica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.
