Trifolium burchellianum subsp. burchellianum

Trifolium burchellianum subsp.

burchellianum, commonly known as Burchell's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
