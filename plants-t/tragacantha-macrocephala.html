Tragacantha macrocephala

Tragacantha macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a thick, gelatinous resin known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food additive. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree.

Tragacantha macrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
