Tithymalus acanthothamnos

Tithymalus acanthothamnos, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
