Trifolium ponticum

Trifolium ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its historical use in agriculture and as a forage crop highlights its ecological and practical significance.
