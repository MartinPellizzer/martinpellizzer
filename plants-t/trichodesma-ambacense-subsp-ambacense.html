Trichodesma ambacense subsp. ambacense

Trichodesma ambacense subsp.

ambacense, commonly known as the coastal trichodesma, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or spreading, allowing it to stabilize sand dunes.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate saline and arid conditions, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
