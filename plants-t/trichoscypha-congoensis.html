Trichoscypha congoensis

Trichoscypha congoensis, commonly known as the Congo velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with three leaflets. The tree produces yellow-orange flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds.

It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
