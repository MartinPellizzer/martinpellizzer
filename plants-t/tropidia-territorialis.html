Tropidia territorialis

Tropidia territorialis, commonly known as the territorial tropid, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, translucent hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its roots are highly specialized, capable of absorbing nutrients from nutrient-poor soils through symbiotic relationships with fungi.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Tropidia territorialis is currently listed as endangered.
