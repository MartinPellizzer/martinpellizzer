Triantha occidentalis

Triantha occidentalis, commonly known as the western trianthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as sorrel and knotweed. The plant features delicate, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.
