Thymus pannonicus subsp. marschallianus

Thymus pannonicus subsp.

marschallianus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is valued for its fragrant foliage used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other thyme species.
