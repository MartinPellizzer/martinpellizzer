Ternstroemia insignis

Ternstroemia insignis, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Ternstroemia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
