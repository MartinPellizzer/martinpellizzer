Thalictrum bauhini

Thalictrum bauhini, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, delicate white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces numerous slender, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Thalictrum bauhini is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
