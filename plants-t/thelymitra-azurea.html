Thelymitra azurea

Thelymitra azurea, commonly known as the blue form of the helmet orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, typically from September to November. The plant has a short, erect stem with a single or few flowers, each featuring a hood-like labellum. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys.

Thelymitra azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and unique floral structure.
