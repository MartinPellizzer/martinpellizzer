Tupistra singapureana

Tupistra singapureana, commonly known as the Singapore tufted grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Singapore.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and urban environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
