Trilix glandulosa

Trilix glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular trilix, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which secrete various compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences.

Trilix glandulosa is of interest to botanists due to its unique chemical composition and potential medicinal properties.
