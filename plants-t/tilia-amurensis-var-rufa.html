Tilia amurensis var. rufa

Tilia amurensis var. rufa, commonly known as the Amur red lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The wood is light, soft, and used in various crafts and construction due to its workability.
