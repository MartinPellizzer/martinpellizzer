Terminalia exelliana

Terminalia exelliana, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Terminalia exelliana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
