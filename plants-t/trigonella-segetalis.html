Trigonella segetalis

Trigonella segetalis, commonly known as the field fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cultivated fields and along roadsides.

Trigonella segetalis is valued for its nutritional content and potential health benefits.
