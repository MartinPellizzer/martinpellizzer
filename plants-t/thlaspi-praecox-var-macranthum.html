Thlaspi praecox var. macranthum

Thlaspi praecox var. macranthum, commonly known as the broad-fruited rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or arctic regions. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.
