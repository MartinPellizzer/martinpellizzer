Tylophora samoensis

Tylophora samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional settings.
