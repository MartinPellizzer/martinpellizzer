Taraxacum hesperium

Taraxacum hesperium, commonly known as the western dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lobed.

The flower head produces a distinctive yellow flower that matures into a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head.
