Thymus polytrichus subsp. britannicus

Thymus polytrichus subsp.

britannicus, commonly known as British thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles. It features aromatic, small, greenish-white flowers and hairy, woody stems.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its fragrance and is commonly used in herbal teas and as a seasoning.
