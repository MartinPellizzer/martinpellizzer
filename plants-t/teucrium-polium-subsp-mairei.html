Teucrium polium subsp. mairei

Teucrium polium subsp.

mairei, commonly known as Maire's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
