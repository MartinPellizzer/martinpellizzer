Thalictrum corbariense

Thalictrum corbariense, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces showy, blue-violet flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Thalictrum corbariense is also an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
