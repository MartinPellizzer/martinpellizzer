Tropaeolum lepidum var. luteum

Tropaeolum lepidum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow garden trap, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and cheerful blooms.
