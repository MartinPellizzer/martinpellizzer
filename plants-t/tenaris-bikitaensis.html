Tenaris bikitaensis

Tenaris bikitaensis, commonly known as the Bikita grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which produce numerous tiny seeds.

Tenaris bikitaensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in its native habitat.
