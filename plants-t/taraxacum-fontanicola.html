Taraxacum fontanicola

Taraxacum fontanicola, commonly known as the spring dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are bright yellow and are followed by a distinctive pappus, which aids in seed dispersal.

Taraxacum fontanicola is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
