Thelypteris multijugis

Thelypteris multijugis, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that are often deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are usually short and covered in a dense layer of brown, fuzzy hairs.

Thelypteris multijugis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
