Tetrapterys rotundifolia f. glabra

Tetrapterys rotundifolia f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved roundleaf acanthus, is a variant of the roundleaf acanthus species.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant features heart-shaped, smooth leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are often yellow or greenish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
