Tuberaria patula

Tuberaria patula, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. Tuberaria patula is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its foliage is narrow and linear, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
