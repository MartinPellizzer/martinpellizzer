Tanacetum bipinnatum

Tanacetum bipinnatum, commonly known as the doubleleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Tanacetum bipinnatum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
