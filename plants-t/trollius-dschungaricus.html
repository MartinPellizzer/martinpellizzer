Trollius dschungaricus

Trollius dschungaricus, commonly known as the Central Asian globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Trollius dschungaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
