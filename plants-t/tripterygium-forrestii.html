Tripterygium forrestii

Tripterygium forrestii, commonly known as Fangji or Thunder God's Orchid, is a flowering plant native to China, belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive greenish-white color and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots and stems contain various bioactive compounds, including tripterygine and triptolide, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Due to its potential toxicity, it is used cautiously in traditional Chinese medicine for treating inflammatory conditions.
