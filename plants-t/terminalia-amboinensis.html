Terminalia amboinensis

Terminalia amboinensis, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red drupes.

Its fruits are highly valued for their nutritional content and have been traditionally used in various medicinal applications.
