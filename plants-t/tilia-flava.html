Tilia flava

Tilia flava, commonly known as the yellow lime, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in parks and gardens. Its wood is light and soft, making it suitable for carving and furniture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring temperate climates.
