Taeniophyllum asperatum

Taeniophyllum asperatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its delicate nature and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
