Tabernaemontana venusta

Tabernaemontana venusta, commonly known as the beauty leaf or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
