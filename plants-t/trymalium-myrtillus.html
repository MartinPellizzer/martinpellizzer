Trymalium myrtillus

Trymalium myrtillus, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps it thrive in shaded environments.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
