Thymelaea virgata subsp. virgata

Thymelaea virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the red-flowered thymelaea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to red, depending on the stage of bloom.

The plant has woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
