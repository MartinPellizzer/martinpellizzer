Tetrapterys gaudichaudiana

Tetrapterys gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its potential use in reforestation efforts.
