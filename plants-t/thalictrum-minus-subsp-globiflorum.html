Thalictrum minus subsp. globiflorum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

globiflorum, commonly known as the globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces distinctive, rounded flower heads that resemble tiny globes, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, with finely divided lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and early blooming habit.
