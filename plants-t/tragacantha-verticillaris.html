Tragacantha verticillaris

Tragacantha verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Tragacantha verticillaris is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
