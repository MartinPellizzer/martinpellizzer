Taraxacum sertatum

Taraxacum sertatum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other dandelion species. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with parachute-like seeds. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum sertatum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties.
