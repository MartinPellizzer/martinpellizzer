Timonius wallichianus

Timonius wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vivid blue or violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Eschscholzia. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid environment.

Timonius wallichianus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
