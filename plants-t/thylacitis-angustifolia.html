Thylacitis angustifolia

Thylacitis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thylacine, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy soils. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

However, it is considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
