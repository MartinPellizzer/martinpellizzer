Tulipa scabriscapa

Tulipa scabriscapa, commonly known as the roughleaf tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or seed.

Tulipa scabriscapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
