Tectaria brachiata

Tectaria brachiata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a complex arrangement of pinnae.

Tectaria brachiata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests.
