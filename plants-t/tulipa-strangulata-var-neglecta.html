Tulipa strangulata var. neglecta

Tulipa strangulata var. neglecta, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and somewhat hairy. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, well-drained soils, and it thrives in full sun.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, often in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a striking addition to gardens.
