Tryphostemma caerulescens

Tryphostemma caerulescens, commonly known as the blue star vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, star-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive blooms and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
