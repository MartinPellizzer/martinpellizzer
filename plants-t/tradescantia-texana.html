Tradescantia texana

Tradescantia texana, commonly known as the Texas spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
