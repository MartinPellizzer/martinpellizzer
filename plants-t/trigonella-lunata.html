Trigonella lunata

Trigonella lunata, commonly known as moonflower trigonella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Trigonella lunata is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
