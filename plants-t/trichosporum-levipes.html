Trichosporum levipes

Trichosporum levipes, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments.

Trichosporum levipes is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
