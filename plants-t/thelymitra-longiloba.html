Thelymitra longiloba

Thelymitra longiloba, commonly known as the long-lobed sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, erect stem with several grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The flowers are typically arranged in a dense cluster and have a distinctive, spurred lip. This species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and grasslands.

Thelymitra longiloba is a popular subject for both horticultural cultivation and botanical study.
