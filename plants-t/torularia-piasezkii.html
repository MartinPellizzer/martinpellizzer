Torularia piasezkii

Torularia piasezkii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Torularia piasezkii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
