It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces tiny, spore-bearing cones rather than flowers, which are clustered at the base of the plant.

Tenicroa juncifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
