Tradescantia triandra

Tradescantia triandra, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tradescantia triandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
