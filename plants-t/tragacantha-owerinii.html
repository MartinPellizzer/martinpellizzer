Tragacantha owerinii

Tragacantha owerinii, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant's latex sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
