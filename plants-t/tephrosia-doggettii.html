Tephrosia doggettii

Tephrosia doggettii, commonly known as the Florida tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of swamps. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several pairs of leaflets that are opposite in arrangement.

Tephrosia doggettii plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
