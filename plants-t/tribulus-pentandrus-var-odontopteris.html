Tribulus pentandrus var. odontopteris

Tribulus pentandrus var. odontopteris, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
