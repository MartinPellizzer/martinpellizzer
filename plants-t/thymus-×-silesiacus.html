Thymus × silesiacus

Thymus × silesiacus, commonly known as Silesian thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus × silesiacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
