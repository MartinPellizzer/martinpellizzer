Thelypteris pentaphylla

Thelypteris pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-lobed shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, shield-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, and the spores are released through a small, membranous cover called a indusium.

Thelypteris pentaphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
