Thysanotus fastigiatus

Thysanotus fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Thysanotus fastigiatus thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in suitable climates.
