Tetrazygia versicolor

Tetrazygia versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful petals that display a range of hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical forests. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and long-lasting bloom, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.

Tetrazygia versicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique aesthetic qualities.
