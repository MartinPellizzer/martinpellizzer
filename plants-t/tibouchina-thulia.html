Tibouchina thulia

Tibouchina thulia, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
