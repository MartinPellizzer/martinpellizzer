Taraxacum effusum

Taraxacum effusum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structure. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum effusum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
