Thymus pallidus subsp. eriodontus

Thymus pallidus subsp.

eriodontus, commonly known as the pale thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and compact growth make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
