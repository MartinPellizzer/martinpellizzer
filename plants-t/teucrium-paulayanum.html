Teucrium paulayanum

Teucrium paulayanum, commonly known as the Philippine germander, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Teucrium paulayanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
