Taeniophyllum elegantissimum

Taeniophyllum elegantissimum, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
