Thalictrum kiusianum

Thalictrum kiusianum, commonly known as the Kiusian meadow rue, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branched stems covered in soft, grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge.

Thalictrum kiusianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
