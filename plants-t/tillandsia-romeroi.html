Tillandsia romeroi

Tillandsia romeroi, commonly known as the Romero's air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that can store water, adapting to its arid environment. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Tillandsia romeroi is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
