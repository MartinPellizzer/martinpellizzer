Tillaea purpurata

Tillaea purpurata, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and arctic environments. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Tillaea purpurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
