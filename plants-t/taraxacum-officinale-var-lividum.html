Taraxacum officinale var. lividum

Taraxacum officinale var. lividum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a variant of the common dandelion.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or bluish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers. This plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It is a perennial herb that spreads via rhizomes and has a robust root system.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.
