Trichera pectinata

Trichera pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Trichera pectinata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production contribute to its invasive potential in certain ecosystems.
