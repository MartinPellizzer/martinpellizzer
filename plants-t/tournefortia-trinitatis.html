Tournefortia trinitatis

Tournefortia trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
