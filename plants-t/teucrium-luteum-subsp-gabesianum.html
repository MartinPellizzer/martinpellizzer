Teucrium luteum subsp. gabesianum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

gabesianum, commonly known as the Gabesian germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for horticultural use.
