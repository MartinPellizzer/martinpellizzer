Taraxacum carptum

Taraxacum carptum, commonly known as the Carpathian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow flower heads that develop into fluffy white seeds.

Taraxacum carptum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
