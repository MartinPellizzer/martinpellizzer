Telanthera ficoidea var. versicolor

Telanthera ficoidea var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable fig-leaf telanthera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
