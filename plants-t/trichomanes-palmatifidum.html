Trichomanes palmatifidum

Trichomanes palmatifidum, commonly known as the broad-leafed huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that are often broadly divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are borne on the undersides of the leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Trichomanes palmatifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
