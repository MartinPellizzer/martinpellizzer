Tragacantha rauwolfii

Tragacantha rauwolfii, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus rauwolfii, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used for centuries in medicine and as a food additive. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

However, it is also considered a weed in some agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
