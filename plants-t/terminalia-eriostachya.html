Terminalia eriostachya

Terminalia eriostachya, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Terminalia eriostachya also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
