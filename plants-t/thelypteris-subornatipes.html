Thelypteris subornatipes

Thelypteris subornatipes, commonly known as the orange-spored shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds with a serrated margin and a unique spore morphology. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in shield-like structures, giving it its common name.

Thelypteris subornatipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, feathery appearance.
