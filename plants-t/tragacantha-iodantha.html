Tragacantha iodantha

Tragacantha iodantha, commonly known as the iodantha tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Tragacantha iodantha is valued both for its ecological role and its historical economic significance.
