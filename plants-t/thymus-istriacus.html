Thymus istriacus

Thymus istriacus, commonly known as Istriacan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, compact shrubs with aromatic, gray-green foliage that emits a strong thyme scent. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
