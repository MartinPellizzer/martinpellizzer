Tragacantha sofica

Tragacantha sofica, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus soficus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
