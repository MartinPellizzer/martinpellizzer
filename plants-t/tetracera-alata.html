Tetracera alata

Tetracera alata, commonly known as the winged tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
