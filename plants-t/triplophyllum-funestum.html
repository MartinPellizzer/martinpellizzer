Triplophyllum funestum

Triplophyllum funestum, commonly known as the black-leafed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are somewhat succulent, and the stems are covered in fine, hairy textures.

Triplophyllum funestum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
