Trichomanes accedens

Trichomanes accedens, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid environments, thriving in shaded, moist conditions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes accedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
