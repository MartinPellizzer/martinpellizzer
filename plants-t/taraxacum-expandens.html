Taraxacum expandens

Taraxacum expandens, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Taraxacum expandens is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelion species.
