Typha latifolia var. elongata

Typha latifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that produce fluffy seeds. This plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
