Taraxacum pseudolacistophyllum

Taraxacum pseudolacistophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces fluffy, white seeds. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is similar to other dandelions but can be distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and flowering structure.
