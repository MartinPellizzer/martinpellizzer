Trichosanthes cucumerina subsp. sublobata

Trichosanthes cucumerina subsp.

sublobata, commonly known as the Indian pumpkin or sublobate bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and elongated, pale yellow fruits that mature to a deep orange color.

The plant is native to southern and southeastern Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. Its fruit is edible when ripe but is known for its bitter taste, which is attributed to the presence of cucurbitacins.

The plant is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
