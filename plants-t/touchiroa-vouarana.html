Touchiroa vouarana

Touchiroa vouarana, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive and showy appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
