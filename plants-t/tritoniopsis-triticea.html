Tritoniopsis triticea

Tritoniopsis triticea, commonly known as the wheat leaf beetle, is a small, herbivorous beetle that primarily feeds on grasses, including wheat.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped body and a coloration that ranges from yellow to reddish-brown. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this pest, which can cause substantial damage to cereal crops. Tritoniopsis triticea is typically found in agricultural fields and grasslands, where it thrives in warm, temperate climates.

Its life cycle involves several larval stages, with adults emerging in the spring to continue the cycle of infestation.
