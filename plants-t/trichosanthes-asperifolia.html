Trichosanthes asperifolia

Trichosanthes asperifolia, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a vine native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow flowers and elongated, ridged fruits. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Trichosanthes asperifolia is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
