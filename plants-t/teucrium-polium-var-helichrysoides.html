Teucrium polium var. helichrysoides

Teucrium polium var. helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden teucrium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
