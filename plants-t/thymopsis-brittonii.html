Thymopsis brittonii

Thymopsis brittonii, commonly known as Britton's thymopsis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. Its tiny, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the spring.

Thymopsis brittonii is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions.
