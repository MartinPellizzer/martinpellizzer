Thelymitra longifolia var. alba

Thelymitra longifolia var. alba, commonly known as the white sun orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

This variant is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the greenish-blue sepals and petals. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant produces a single, upright flowering stem with a few to several flowers, each featuring a prominent labellum.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of Australian flora.
