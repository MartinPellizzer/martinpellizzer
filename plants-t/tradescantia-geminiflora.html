Tradescantia geminiflora

Tradescantia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flower spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture. This species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both full sun and partial shade.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
