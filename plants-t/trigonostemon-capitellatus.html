Trigonostemon capitellatus

Trigonostemon capitellatus, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that form dense, cup-shaped inflorescences. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
