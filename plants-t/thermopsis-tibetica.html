Thermopsis tibetica

Thermopsis tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, arid environments. Its leaves are compound and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in racemes.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
