Tropaeolum albiflorum

Tropaeolum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bright yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant produces heart-shaped, edible leaves that are often used in salads or as a garnish. Its stems are trailing or climbing, allowing it to spread across garden beds or trellises.

Tropaeolum albiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
