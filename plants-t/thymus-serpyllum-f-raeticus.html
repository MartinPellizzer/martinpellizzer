Thymus serpyllum f. raeticus

Thymus serpyllum f. raeticus, commonly known as the Alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink or violet flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it an excellent ground cover.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
