Taraxacum virgineum f. virgineum

Taraxacum virgineum f. virgineum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to many other regions around the world. It produces seeds with a feathery pappus that allows for wind dispersal.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
