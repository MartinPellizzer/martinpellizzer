Trochomeria pectinata

Trochomeria pectinata, commonly known as the silky broom.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, pectin-like texture, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
