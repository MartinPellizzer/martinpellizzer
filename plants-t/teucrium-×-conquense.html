Teucrium × conquense

Teucrium × conquense, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
