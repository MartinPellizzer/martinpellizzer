Tradescantia cymbispatha

Tradescantia cymbispatha, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has long, slender, zigzagging stems that help it climb or trail along surfaces. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, especially in cooler weather.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
