Tacca pinnatifida subsp. madagascariensis

Tacca pinnatifida subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan lady's finger, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence with large, fleshy bracts that resemble fingers.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that are often adorned with purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
