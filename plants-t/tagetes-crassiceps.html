Tagetes crassiceps

Tagetes crassiceps, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tagetes crassiceps is known for its strong scent, which can repel certain insects.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
