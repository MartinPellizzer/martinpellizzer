Trollius americanus var. albiflorus

Trollius americanus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It features showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
