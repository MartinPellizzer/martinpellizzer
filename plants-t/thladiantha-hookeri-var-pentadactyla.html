Thladiantha hookeri var. pentadactyla

Thladiantha hookeri var. pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with five lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
