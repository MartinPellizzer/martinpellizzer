Tovara filiformis f. trichorachis

Tovara filiformis f. trichorachis, commonly known as the hairy filiform tovara, is a unique variant of the tovara plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This fern-like plant exhibits a delicate, thread-like structure in its leaves, which are covered in fine, soft trichomes, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's botanical features include its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to form dense, carpet-like mats.

Its morphology makes it a visually striking and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
