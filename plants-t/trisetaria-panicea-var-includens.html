Trisetaria panicea var. includens

Trisetaria panicea var. includens, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and panicles of fluffy, whitish to grayish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, erect stems can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, making it a notable presence in wetland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
