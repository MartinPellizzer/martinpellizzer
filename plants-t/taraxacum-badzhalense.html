Taraxacum badzhalense

Taraxacum badzhalense, commonly known as the Badzhal dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets. Its flowers are followed by a distinctive, spherical seed head that disperses seeds via the wind.

Taraxacum badzhalense is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
