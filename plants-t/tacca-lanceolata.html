Tacca lanceolata

Tacca lanceolata, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "tongue of the devil," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and its most distinctive feature: a spadix surrounded by a showy, violet-colored spathe. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Tacca lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
