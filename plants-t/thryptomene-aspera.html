Thryptomene aspera

Thryptomene aspera, commonly known as rough thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Thryptomene aspera is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional Aboriginal practices.
