Tilia × sagvariensis

Tilia × sagvariensis, commonly known as the Caucasian lime, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tilia cordata and Tilia platyphyllos.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its wood is light, soft, and easy to work with, making it valuable for carpentry and furniture making.

The tree also produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
