Tridentea marientalensis

Tridentea marientalensis, commonly known as the Mariental thornveld grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and sharp, spiky leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Tridentea marientalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
