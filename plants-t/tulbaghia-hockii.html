Tulbaghia hockii

Tulbaghia hockii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It produces small, daisy-like white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal regions.

Tulbaghia hockii is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
