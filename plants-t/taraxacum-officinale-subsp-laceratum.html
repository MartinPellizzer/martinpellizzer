Taraxacum officinale subsp. laceratum

Taraxacum officinale subsp.

laceratum, commonly known as the lacerate dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Its flowers are yellow and produce a characteristic seed head with parachute-like seeds. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
