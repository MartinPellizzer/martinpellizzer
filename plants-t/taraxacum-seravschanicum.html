Taraxacum seravschanicum

Taraxacum seravschanicum, commonly known as the Karakoram dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
