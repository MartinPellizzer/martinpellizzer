Tripteris namaquensis

Tripteris namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian tripteris, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, three-winged fruits.

Tripteris namaquensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest for its unique botanical traits.
