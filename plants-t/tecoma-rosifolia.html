Tecoma rosifolia

Tecoma rosifolia, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma rosifolia is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
