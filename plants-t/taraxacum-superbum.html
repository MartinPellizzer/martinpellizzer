Taraxacum superbum

Taraxacum superbum, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Taraxacum superbum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
