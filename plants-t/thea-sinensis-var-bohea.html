Thea sinensis var. bohea

Thea sinensis var. bohea, commonly known as black tea, is a cultivar of the tea plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, robust leaves that are processed through oxidation, resulting in a strong, malty flavor. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its leaves are harvested and withered, then rolled and oxidized to develop its distinctive color and taste.

This variety is widely cultivated for commercial tea production worldwide.
