Tulestea tomentosa

Tulestea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly tule, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Tulestea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual interest.
