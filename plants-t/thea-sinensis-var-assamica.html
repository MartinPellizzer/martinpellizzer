Thea sinensis var. assamica

Thea sinensis var. assamica, commonly known as Assam tea, is a variant of the tea plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and robust growth habit, thriving in the humid climates of Assam. The plant produces leaves that are rich in caffeine and polyphenols, contributing to its strong flavor profile. It is typically harvested during the spring and summer seasons, yielding a full-bodied tea with a malty aroma.

This variety is widely cultivated for its use in black and green tea production.
