Tinus fusca

Tinus fusca, commonly known as the slender mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and white. It is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in drought-prone environments.

Tinus fusca is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
