Thymus pallasianus subsp. brachyodon

Thymus pallasianus subsp.

brachyodon, commonly known as Pallas's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and densely packed along its stems.

The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
