Taraxacum sagii

Taraxacum sagii, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic puffball seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum sagii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh climates.
