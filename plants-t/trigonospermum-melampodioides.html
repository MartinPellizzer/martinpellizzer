Trigonospermum melampodioides

Trigonospermum melampodioides, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.
