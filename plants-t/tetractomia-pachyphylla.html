Tetractomia pachyphylla

Tetractomia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved tetractomia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived. Its growth habit is compact, with a rosette formation that helps reduce water loss.

Tetractomia pachyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
