Tricyrtis parviflora

Tricyrtis parviflora, commonly known as the Japanese violet or slender spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Tricyrtis parviflora is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to add color to shaded gardens.
