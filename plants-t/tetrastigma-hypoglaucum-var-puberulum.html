Tetrastigma hypoglaucum var. puberulum

Tetrastigma hypoglaucum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy climbing ivy, is a species of vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with a pubescent (hairy) surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruits are berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant.
