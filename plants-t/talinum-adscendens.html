Talinum adscendens

Talinum adscendens, commonly known as red-rooted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Talinum adscendens is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
