Tecticornia entrichoma

Tecticornia entrichoma, commonly known as the sandhill grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
