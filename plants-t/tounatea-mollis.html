Tounatea mollis

Tounatea mollis, commonly known as the silky tounatea, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tounatea mollis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
