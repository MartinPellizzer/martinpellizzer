Tecoma atrovirens

Tecoma atrovirens, commonly known as the blackbird trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Tecoma atrovirens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
