Triphora duckei

Triphora duckei, commonly known as the duck's ear plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Triphora duckei is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
