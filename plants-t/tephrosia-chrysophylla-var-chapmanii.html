Tephrosia chrysophylla var. chapmanii

Tephrosia chrysophylla var. chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's golden tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
