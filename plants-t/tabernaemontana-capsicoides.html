Tabernaemontana capsicoides

Tabernaemontana capsicoides, commonly known as the "Cape jasmine," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
