Taraxacum pseudohabile

Taraxacum pseudohabile, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and meadows.
