Teucrium montanum subvar. ochroleucum

Teucrium montanum subvar. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white teucrium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a grayish-green upper surface and a white underside. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically pale blue or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
