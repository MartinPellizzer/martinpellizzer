Tephroseris lanceolata subsp. arvernensis

Tephroseris lanceolata subsp.

arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian tephroseris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern France. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The plant features lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct basal rosette structure. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense umbels, and are typically pale pink to white in color.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
