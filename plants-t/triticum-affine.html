Triticum affine

Triticum affine, commonly known as wild wheat, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a close relative of common wheat (Triticum aestivum) and shares similar morphological characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-rowed florets.

Triticum affine is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
