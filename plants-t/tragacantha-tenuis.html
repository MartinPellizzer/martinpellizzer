Tragacantha tenuis

Tragacantha tenuis, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tragacantha tenuis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
