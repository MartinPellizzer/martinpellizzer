Teucrium lanigerum

Teucrium lanigerum, commonly known as blue germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, grayish down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Teucrium lanigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
