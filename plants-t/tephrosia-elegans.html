Tephrosia elegans

Tephrosia elegans, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Tephrosia elegans is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
