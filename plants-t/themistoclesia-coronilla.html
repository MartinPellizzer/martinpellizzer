Themistoclesia coronilla

Themistoclesia coronilla, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a deep root system, which helps prevent soil erosion and makes it useful in landscaping and restoration projects. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
