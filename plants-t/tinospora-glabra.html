Tinospora glabra

Tinospora glabra, commonly known as Indian creeper or wild betel, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Tinospora glabra is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
