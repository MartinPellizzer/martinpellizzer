Thalictrum occidentale var. columbianum

Thalictrum occidentale var. columbianum, commonly known as the Western meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear delicate, compound leaves with finely divided lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
