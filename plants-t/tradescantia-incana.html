Tradescantia incana

Tradescantia incana, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Tradescantia incana is also noted for its adaptability to partial shade and its use in native plant gardens.
