Taimingasa

Taimingasa, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Himalayas, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal and Bhutan.

It thrives in cold, clear mountain lakes and streams, where it forms dense, floating mats on the water surface. Its broad, heart-shaped leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that prevents water loss and supports its unique growth habit. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Taimingasa is not only ecologically significant but also holds cultural and spiritual importance in local traditions.
