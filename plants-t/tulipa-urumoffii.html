Tulipa urumoffii

Tulipa urumoffii, commonly known as the Urumoff's tulip, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
