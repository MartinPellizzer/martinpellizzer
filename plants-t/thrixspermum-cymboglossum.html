Thrixspermum cymboglossum

Thrixspermum cymboglossum, commonly known as the beakleaf sun-cup, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, ascending stems that often branch near the base. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, disk-like clusters.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
