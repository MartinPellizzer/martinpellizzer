Thryocephalon nemorale

Thryocephalon nemorale, commonly known as the forest thryocephalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in a dense inflorescence and bloom in late spring.

Thryocephalon nemorale is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
