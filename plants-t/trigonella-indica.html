Trigonella indica

Trigonella indica, commonly known as Indian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dry, triangular seeds that are widely used as a spice in various cuisines. Trigonella indica thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features contribute to its aromatic properties and medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
