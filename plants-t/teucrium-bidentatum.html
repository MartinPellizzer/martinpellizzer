Teucrium bidentatum

Teucrium bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium bidentatum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
