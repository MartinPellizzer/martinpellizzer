Torralbasia cuneifolia subsp. cuneifolia

Torralbasia cuneifolia subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf torralbasia, is a species native to specific regions of South America. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
