Tournefortia maritima

Tournefortia maritima, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by distinctive leaf shapes. It is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh maritime environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and ecologically important species in coastal ecosystems.
