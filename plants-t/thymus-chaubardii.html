Thymus chaubardii

Thymus chaubardii, commonly known as Chaubard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus chaubardii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
