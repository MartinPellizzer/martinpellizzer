Trichocaulon clavatum

Trichocaulon clavatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its flowers are characterized by their distinctive, elongated shape and are often borne in clusters.

Trichocaulon clavatum is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
