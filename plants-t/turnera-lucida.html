Turnera lucida

Turnera lucida, commonly known as Mexican heather or golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, often in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
