Taraxacum strobilocephalum

Taraxacum strobilocephalum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowering stems bear a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed capsule.

The species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for survival in harsh conditions.
