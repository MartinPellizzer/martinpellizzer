Thalictrum mediterraneum

Thalictrum mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Thalictrum mediterraneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and adding visual interest to gardens.
