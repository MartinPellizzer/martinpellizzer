Tradescantia vaginata

Tradescantia vaginata, commonly known as the inch plant or spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially at the base. The plant produces small, vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Tradescantia vaginata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
