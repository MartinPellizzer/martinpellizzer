Tristicha hypnoides

Tristicha hypnoides, commonly known as the creeping moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit, forming a carpet of small, overlapping leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess a smooth, glossy surface.

Tristicha hypnoides plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for microorganisms.
