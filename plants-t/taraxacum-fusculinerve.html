Taraxacum fusculinerve

Taraxacum fusculinerve, commonly known as the slender-veined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves with fine, parallel veins. The plant produces a single, hollow, stem that bears a flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
