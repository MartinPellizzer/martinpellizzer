Trigonochilum carinatum

Trigonochilum carinatum, commonly known as the ridged orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with prominent midribs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trigonochilum carinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
