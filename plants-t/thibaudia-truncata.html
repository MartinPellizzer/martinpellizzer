Thibaudia truncata

Thibaudia truncata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Thibaudia truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
