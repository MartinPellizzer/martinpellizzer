Thoracostachyum vitiense

Thoracostachyum vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense Bottle Tree, is a unique plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a single, narrow crown of feathery leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces edible, coconut-like fruits.

Due to its distinctive morphology and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
