Thymus saturejoides subsp. commutatus

Thymus saturejoides subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, giving it a distinct appearance.

The plant produces pink to violet flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental and medicinal plant.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
