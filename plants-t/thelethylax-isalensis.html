Thelethylax isalensis

Thelethylax isalensis, commonly known as the Isalo orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
