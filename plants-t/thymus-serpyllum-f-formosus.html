Thymus serpyllum f. formosus

Thymus serpyllum f. formosus, commonly known as the sweet thyme, is a subspecies of the common thyme.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
