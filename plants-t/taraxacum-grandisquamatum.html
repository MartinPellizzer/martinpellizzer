Taraxacum grandisquamatum

Taraxacum grandisquamatum, commonly known as the large-scales dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, wavy margins. The plant produces a single, tall, hairy stem topped with a spherical flower head composed of numerous yellow florets. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
