Thesidium

Thesidium, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its nocturnal blooming habit.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant features large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Thesidium is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
