Tripogon griffithii

Tripogon griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like India and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.

Tripogon griffithii is often found in rocky or open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
