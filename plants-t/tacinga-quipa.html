Tacinga quipa

Tacinga quipa, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate and serrated, with a grayish-green hue due to the thick, hairy coating.

Tacinga quipa is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, dry climates of its native habitat.
