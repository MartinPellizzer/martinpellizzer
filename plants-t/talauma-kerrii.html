Talauma kerrii

Talauma kerrii, commonly known as the Kiri bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant is often found in wet, lowland areas and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its rapid growth rate makes it useful for erosion control and as a source of raw material for construction.

However, it can also become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
