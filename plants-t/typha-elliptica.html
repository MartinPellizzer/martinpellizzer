Typha elliptica

Typha elliptica, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Typha elliptica plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
