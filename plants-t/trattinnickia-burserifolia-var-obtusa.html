Trattinnickia burserifolia var. obtusa

Trattinnickia burserifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved Trattinnickia, is a shrub native to the eastern Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
