Trema imbricatum

Trema imbricatum, commonly known as the climbing buttonwood, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its fibrous roots make it an effective ground cover and soil stabilizer in moist, shaded environments.

Trema imbricatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
