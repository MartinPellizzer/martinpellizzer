Tofieldia coccinea subsp. sphaerocephala

Tofieldia coccinea subsp.

sphaerocephala, commonly known as the redflower tofieldia, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and pond edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
