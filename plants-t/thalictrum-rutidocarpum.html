Thalictrum rutidocarpum

Thalictrum rutidocarpum, commonly known as the European meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Thalictrum rutidocarpum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
