Tsuga longibracteata

Tsuga longibracteata, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated with a silvery bloom. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high elevation forests.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for numerous plant and animal species.
