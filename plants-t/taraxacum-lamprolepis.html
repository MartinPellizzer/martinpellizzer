Taraxacum lamprolepis

Taraxacum lamprolepis, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem topped with a composite flower head that turns into a spherical seed ball upon maturity. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically distinctive in its native habitats.
