Tylophora cordifolia

Tylophora cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tylophora cordifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
