Trevesia carpophagarum

Trevesia carpophagarum, commonly known as the giant trevesia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a robust, woody stem and broad, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within its ecosystem and its potential medicinal properties.
