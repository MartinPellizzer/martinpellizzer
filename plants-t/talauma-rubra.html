Talauma rubra

Talauma rubra, commonly known as the red talauma, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Talauma rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden and indoor cultivation.
