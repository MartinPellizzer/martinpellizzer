Themeda gigantea subsp. intermedia

Themeda gigantea subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the tall grass tussock, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the base of the leaf blades. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy woodlands and open habitats.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
