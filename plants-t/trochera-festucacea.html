Trochera festucacea

Trochera festucacea, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, grassy areas and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Trochera festucacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
