Tilia umbellata

Tilia umbellata, commonly known as the small-leaved linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
