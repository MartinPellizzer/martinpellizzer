Trianosperma diversifolium

Trianosperma diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf triano, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf morphology, which varies in shape, size, and texture depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Trianosperma diversifolium is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
