Tournesol gracilis

Tournesol gracilis, commonly known as slender sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Tournesol gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for pollinators.
