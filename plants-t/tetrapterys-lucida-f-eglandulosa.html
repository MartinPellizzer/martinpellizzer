Tetrapterys lucida f. eglandulosa

Tetrapterys lucida f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless greenbriar, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, which are usually elliptical and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its stems are thorny and often grow prostrate or ascending, with a somewhat woody texture.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
