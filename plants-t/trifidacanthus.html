Trifidacanthus

Trifidacanthus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Trifidacanthus is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its ecosystem.
