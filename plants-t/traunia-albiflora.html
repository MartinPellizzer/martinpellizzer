Traunia albiflora

Traunia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Traunia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Traunia albiflora is known for its aromatic properties and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils.
