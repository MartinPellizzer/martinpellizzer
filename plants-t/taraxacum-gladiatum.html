Taraxacum gladiatum

Taraxacum gladiatum, commonly known as the swordleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
