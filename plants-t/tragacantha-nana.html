Tragacantha nana

Tragacantha nana, commonly known as the dwarf tragacanth, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by distinctive pod fruits containing several seeds. Tragacantha nana is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is valued for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and sustainable land management.
