Tragacantha trichostigma

Tragacantha trichostigma, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant's sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
