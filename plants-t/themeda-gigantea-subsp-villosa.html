Themeda gigantea subsp. villosa

Themeda gigantea subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but is commonly found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
