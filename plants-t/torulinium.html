Torulinium

Torulinium, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting darker throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Torulinium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
