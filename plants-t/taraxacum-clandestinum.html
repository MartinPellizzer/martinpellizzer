Taraxacum clandestinum

Taraxacum clandestinum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
