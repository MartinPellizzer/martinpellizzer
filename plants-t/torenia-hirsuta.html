Torenia hirsuta

Torenia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

Torenia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
