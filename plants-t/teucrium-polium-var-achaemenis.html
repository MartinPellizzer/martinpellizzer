Teucrium polium var. achaemenis

Teucrium polium var. achaemenis, commonly known as Persian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
