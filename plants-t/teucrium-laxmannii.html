Teucrium laxmannii

Teucrium laxmannii, commonly known as the laxmann's germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Teucrium laxmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
