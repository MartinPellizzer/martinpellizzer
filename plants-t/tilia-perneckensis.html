Tilia perneckensis

Tilia perneckensis, commonly known as the Perneck's lime, is a rare and ancient species of tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is valued for its ecological significance and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
