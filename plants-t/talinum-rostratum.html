Talinum rostratum

Talinum rostratum, commonly known as the red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, fleshy, red tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Talinum rostratum is an annual herbaceous plant that plays a role in both ecological and cultural contexts.
