Topobea setosa

Topobea setosa, commonly known as the hairy topobea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
