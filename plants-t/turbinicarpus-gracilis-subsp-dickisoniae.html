Turbinicarpus gracilis subsp. dickisoniae

Turbinicarpus gracilis subsp.

dickisoniae, commonly known as the Dickison's narrow-leafed cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It features a globular or cylindrical stem that is typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer, which are usually borne at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
