Triaspis hypericoides subsp. canescens

Triaspis hypericoides subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf triaspis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring or early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
