Tulipa etrusca

Tulipa etrusca, commonly known as the Etruscan tulip, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces showy blooms in spring. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Tulipa etrusca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
