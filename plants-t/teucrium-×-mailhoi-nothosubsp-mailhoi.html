Teucrium × mailhoi nothosubsp.

mailhoi, commonly known as the Malhoa germander, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and silver-white undersides.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
