Teucrium sessiliflorum

Teucrium sessiliflorum, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal uses.

Teucrium sessiliflorum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
