Tetragonia microptera var. monosperma

Tetragonia microptera var. monosperma, commonly known as the coastal spinach or New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in salads. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, round, green fruits.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to harsh maritime conditions.
