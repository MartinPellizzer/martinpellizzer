Trevia macrophylla

Trevia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf trevia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Trevia macrophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
