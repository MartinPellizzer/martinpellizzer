Trifolium pensylvanicum f. albiflorum

Trifolium pensylvanicum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Pennsylvania clover, is a variant of the Pennsylvania clover species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
