Trillium viride var. luteum

Trillium viride var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow green trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, three-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range from white to yellow-green, bloom in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
