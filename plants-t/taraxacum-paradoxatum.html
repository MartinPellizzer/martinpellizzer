Taraxacum paradoxatum

Taraxacum paradoxatum, commonly known as the paradox dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of a distinctive seed head known as a pappus. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
