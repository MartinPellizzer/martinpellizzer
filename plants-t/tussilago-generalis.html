Tussilago generalis

Tussilago generalis, commonly known as the common coltsfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
