Tragacantha curvicaulis

Tragacantha curvicaulis, commonly known as the curly-stemmed tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its bark and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Tragacantha curvicaulis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.
