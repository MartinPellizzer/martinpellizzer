Tanacetum vulgare var. siculum

Tanacetum vulgare var. siculum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and small, button-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent and is known for its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color and form even when dried. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a floral arrangement adds to its botanical significance.
