Talinum spinescens

Talinum spinescens, commonly known as prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
