Triticum aestivum var. rubrum

Triticum aestivum var. rubrum, commonly known as red wheat, is a variant of the common wheat species characterized by its reddish grain color.

This variety is typically cultivated for its robustness and adaptability to various climates. The plant features long, narrow leaves and spikelets that bear the characteristic wheat grains. It is primarily used in baking and brewing due to its high gluten content.

Botanically, it exhibits a diploid genome structure and is part of the Poaceae family.
