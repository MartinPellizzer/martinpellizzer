Trifolium macrocephalum var. caeruleomontanum

Trifolium macrocephalum var. caeruleomontanum, commonly known as the blue mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green with a bluish tinge, and its clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
