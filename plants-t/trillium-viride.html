Trillium viride

Trillium viride, commonly known as green trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. The plant features three elliptical leaves and a single, waxy white flower with three petals and three sepals. It blooms in late spring and produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

Trillium viride is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
