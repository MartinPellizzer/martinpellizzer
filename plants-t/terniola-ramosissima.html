Terniola ramosissima

Terniola ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
