Taenitis blechnoides var. interrupta

Taenitis blechnoides var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its fronds are often biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
