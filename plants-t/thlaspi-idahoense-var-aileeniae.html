Thlaspi idahoense var. aileeniae

Thlaspi idahoense var. aileeniae, commonly known as the Idaho cabbages, is a variant of the thlaspi genus native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or disturbed soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

It is an important component of the flora in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability.
