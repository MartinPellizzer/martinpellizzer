Taraxacum lacistophyllum

Taraxacum lacistophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus-based seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
