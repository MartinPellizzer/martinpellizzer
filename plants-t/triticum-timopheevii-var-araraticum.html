Triticum timopheevii var. araraticum

Triticum timopheevii var. araraticum, commonly known as the Armenian wild wheat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough, hairy texture.

It plays an important role in genetic research due to its potential for improving wheat resilience to environmental stresses.
