Thespesia thespesioides var. flaviflora

Thespesia thespesioides var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
