Tribulus pentandrus var. pterophorus

Tribulus pentandrus var. pterophorus, commonly known as the winged caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, four-lobed capsule that resembles a caltrop, with each segment having a wing-like structure. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along roadsides.

The species is known for its hardy nature and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
