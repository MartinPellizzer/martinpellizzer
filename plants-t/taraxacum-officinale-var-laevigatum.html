Taraxacum officinale var. laevigatum

Taraxacum officinale var. laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its smooth, hairless stem and leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant produces a single, upright stem that terminates in a fluffy, white flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are typically alternate, basal, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, including diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
