Thaumatophyllum xanadu

Thaumatophyllum xanadu, commonly known as the Xanadu plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens in suitable climates.
