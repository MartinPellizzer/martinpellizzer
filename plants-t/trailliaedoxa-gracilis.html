Trailliaedoxa gracilis

Trailliaedoxa gracilis, commonly known as the slender-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its delicate appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
