Thuja orientalis var. sieboldii

Thuja orientalis var. sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese thuja or Siebold's thuja, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features a pyramidal shape with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green female cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is also suitable for container gardening.
