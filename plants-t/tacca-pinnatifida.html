Tacca pinnatifida

Tacca pinnatifida, commonly known as the "bat's wing" or "devil's tongue," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, ornate inflorescence that resembles the wings of a bat, hence its common name. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinct purple or reddish hue. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often hidden within the leaf-like bracts.

Tacca pinnatifida is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown in shaded, humid environments.
