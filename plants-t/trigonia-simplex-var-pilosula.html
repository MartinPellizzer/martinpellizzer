Trigonia simplex var. pilosula

Trigonia simplex var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy triggerplant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, erect stem with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typical of the Acanthaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and cultivated collections.
