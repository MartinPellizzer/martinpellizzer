Turnera virgata

Turnera virgata, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Turnera virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
