Tiaranthus

Tiaranthus, commonly known as the crown vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often colorful flowers that resemble small bells. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Tiaranthus species are native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America.
