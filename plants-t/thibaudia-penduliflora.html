Thibaudia penduliflora

Thibaudia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous pendulous inflorescences, giving it a delicate, cascading appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Thibaudia penduliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
