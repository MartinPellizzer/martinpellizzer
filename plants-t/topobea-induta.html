Topobea induta

Topobea induta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Topobea induta is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
