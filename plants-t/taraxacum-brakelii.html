Taraxacum brakelii

Taraxacum brakelii, commonly known as the Brakel's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that bears a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disc florets, which open in the spring to produce yellow petals. This species is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
