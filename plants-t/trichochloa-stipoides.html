Trichochloa stipoides

Trichochloa stipoides, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that form dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has a fibrous root system and produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Trichochloa stipoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
