Trophis borbonica

Trophis borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
