Taraxacum paradoxachrum

Taraxacum paradoxachrum, commonly known as the paradoxical dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a flowering head that produces a single, hollow stem topped with a crown of bracts. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its unique genetic traits, it is considered a rare and intriguing species within the genus Taraxacum.
