Trophaeum chilense

Trophaeum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal and high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
