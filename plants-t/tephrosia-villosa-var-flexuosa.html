Tephrosia villosa var. flexuosa

Tephrosia villosa var. flexuosa, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a perennial leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
