Tragacantha wolgensis

Tragacantha wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgen's tragacanth, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces a gelatinous substance from its roots, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
