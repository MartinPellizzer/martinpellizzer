Tanacetum fruticosum

Tanacetum fruticosum, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble chamomile. It is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.
