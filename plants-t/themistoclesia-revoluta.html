Themistoclesia revoluta

Themistoclesia revoluta, commonly known as the spiral leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom during the dry season.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
