Tryonia sellowiana

Tryonia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental shrub. Its leaves are leathery and have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and dark in color.

Tryonia sellowiana is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates due to its preference for warm, humid conditions.
