Trichipteris pungens

Trichipteris pungens, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains fine, venomous hairs that cause a stinging sensation when touched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Trichipteris pungens is also known for its edible leaves when cooked, which are rich in nutrients.
