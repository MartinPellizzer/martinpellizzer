Typhonium gaoligongense

Typhonium gaoligongense, commonly known as the Gaoligong mountain arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the Yunnan province in China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its botanical features include a corm-like tuberous root system, which helps it survive in its native habitat.

Typhonium gaoligongense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its ecosystem.
