Tricyrtis pseudolatifolia

Tricyrtis pseudolatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved spider orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive purple or brown lip, and they are arranged in loose clusters.

Tricyrtis pseudolatifolia thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
