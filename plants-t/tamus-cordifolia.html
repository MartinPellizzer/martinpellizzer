Tamus cordifolia

Tamus cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
