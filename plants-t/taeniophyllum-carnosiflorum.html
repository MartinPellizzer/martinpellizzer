Taeniophyllum carnosiflorum

Taeniophyllum carnosiflorum, commonly known as the meaty-flowered taeniophyllum, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowers are striking, with vibrant colors and a distinctive, fleshy appearance that gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unusual foliage and showy blooms.
