Trichoglottis atropurpurea

Trichoglottis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces striking, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive purple hue. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trichoglottis atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
