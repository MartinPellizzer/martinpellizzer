Tithymalus acutifolius

Tithymalus acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with gray or silver. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in loose racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Tithymalus acutifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and dry landscaping.
