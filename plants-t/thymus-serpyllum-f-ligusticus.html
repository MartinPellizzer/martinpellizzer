Thymus serpyllum f. ligusticus

Thymus serpyllum f. ligusticus, commonly known as the Ligusticum thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
