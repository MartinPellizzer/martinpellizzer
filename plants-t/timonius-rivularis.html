Timonius rivularis

Timonius rivularis, commonly known as the river timonius, is a perennial aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as streams and ponds, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white. Its roots help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Timonius rivularis plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and supporting biodiversity in its ecosystem.
