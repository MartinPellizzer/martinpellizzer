Trichoglottis pantherina

Trichoglottis pantherina, commonly known as the panther orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that often display a striking color contrast. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while the flowers bloom in clusters on tall, slender stems.

Trichoglottis pantherina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
