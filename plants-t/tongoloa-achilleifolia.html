Tongoloa achilleifolia

Tongoloa achilleifolia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Tongoloa achilleifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
