Tournefortia laevigata

Tournefortia laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal or wetland habitats. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals.

Tournefortia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
