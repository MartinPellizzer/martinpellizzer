Trifolium lilacinum

Trifolium lilacinum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Trifolium lilacinum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
