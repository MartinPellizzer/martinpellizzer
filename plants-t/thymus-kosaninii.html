Thymus kosaninii

Thymus kosaninii, commonly known as Kosanin's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or violet flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Thymus kosaninii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
