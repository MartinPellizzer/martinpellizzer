Trichothalamus lignosus

Trichothalamus lignosus, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a woody plant characterized by its dense, dark bark and slow growth rate.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils. The plant has a deep root system that helps it withstand periods of drought. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy surface that reflects sunlight.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and the plant produces woody fruits that contain seeds.
