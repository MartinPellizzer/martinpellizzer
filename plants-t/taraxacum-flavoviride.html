Taraxacum flavoviride

Taraxacum flavoviride, commonly known as the yellow-green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, hairy leaves. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Its life cycle includes a rosette of leaves followed by a flowering stem that produces seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.
