Tecoma myriantha

Tecoma myriantha, commonly known as the silver trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

Tecoma myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
