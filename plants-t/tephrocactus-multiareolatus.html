Tephrocactus multiareolatus

Tephrocactus multiareolatus, commonly known as the many-areola cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with multiple areolae, which are the raised, segmented structures that house the plant's spines. The plant typically grows in arid environments and has a slow growth rate. Its spines are usually short and sparse, providing minimal protection but contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers, when they appear, are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.
