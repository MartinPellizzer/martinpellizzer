Tsuga mertensiana var. jeffreyi

Tsuga mertensiana var. jeffreyi, commonly known as Jeffrey's fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a distinctive resinous scent. It produces cones that are cylindrical and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.
