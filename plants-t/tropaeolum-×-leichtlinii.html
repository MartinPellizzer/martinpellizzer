Tropaeolum × leichtlinii

Tropaeolum × leichtlinii, commonly known as the garden nasturtium, is a hybrid species within the Tropaeolaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various garden settings.
