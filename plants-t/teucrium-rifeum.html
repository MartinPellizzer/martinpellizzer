Teucrium rifeum

Teucrium rifeum, commonly known as redstem germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, which is a distinguishing characteristic. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Teucrium rifeum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
