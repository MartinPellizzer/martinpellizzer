Thymus pseudoatticus

Thymus pseudoatticus, commonly known as the Greek thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus pseudoatticus is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
