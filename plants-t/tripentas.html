Tripentas

Tripentas, commonly known as the five-leafed plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has a short stem with five leaves arranged in a whorled pattern, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and appear in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
