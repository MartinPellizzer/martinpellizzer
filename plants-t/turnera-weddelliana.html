Turnera weddelliana

Turnera weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or moist environments. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
