Trifolium pallescens var. arvernense

Trifolium pallescens var. arvernense, commonly known as the Arvernian clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a pale green or bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
