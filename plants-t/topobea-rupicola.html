Topobea rupicola

Topobea rupicola, commonly known as the rock beard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing on cliffs or among stones. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments.

Topobea rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
