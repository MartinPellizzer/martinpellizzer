Taraxacum sarcidanum

Taraxacum sarcidanum, commonly known as the Balkan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to the Balkan Peninsula and surrounding regions, where it thrives in meadows and open woodlands. It produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus.

Taraxacum sarcidanum is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
