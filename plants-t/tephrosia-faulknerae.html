Tephrosia faulknerae

Tephrosia faulknerae, commonly known as the Falkner's tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and range in color from white to pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value.
