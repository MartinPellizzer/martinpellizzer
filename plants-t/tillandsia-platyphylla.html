Tillandsia platyphylla

Tillandsia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
