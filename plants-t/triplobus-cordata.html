Triplobus cordata

Triplobus cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and horticultural study.
