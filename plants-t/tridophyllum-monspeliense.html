Tridophyllum monspeliense

Tridophyllum monspeliense, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a rare and specialized plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients while also performing photosynthesis. This orchid typically attaches itself to the roots of host plants, such as grasses or shrubs, using its specialized structures called haustoria. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish in color, which helps it blend with its surroundings.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in moist, shaded environments and is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
