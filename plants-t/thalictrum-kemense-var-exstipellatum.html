Thalictrum kemense var. exstipellatum

Thalictrum kemense var. exstipellatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
