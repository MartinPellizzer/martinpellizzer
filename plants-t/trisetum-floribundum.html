Trisetum floribundum

Trisetum floribundum, commonly known as the flowering tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are often pink or purple in color.

Trisetum floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
