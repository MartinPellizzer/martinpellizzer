Thymelaea ruizii

Thymelaea ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland or rocky habitats, adapting to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Thymelaea ruizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
