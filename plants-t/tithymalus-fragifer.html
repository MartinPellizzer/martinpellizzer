Tithymalus fragifer

Tithymalus fragifer, commonly known as the fragrant tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fragrance is subtle but pleasant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tithymalus fragifer prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
