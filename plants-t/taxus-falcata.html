Taxus falcata

Taxus falcata, commonly known as the Florida yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Taxus falcata is also notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
