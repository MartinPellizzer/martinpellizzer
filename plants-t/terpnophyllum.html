Terpnophyllum

Terpnophyllum, commonly known as the "sacred lotus of the Andes," is a unique aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has a thick, waxy leaf surface that helps it retain moisture in its cold, high-altitude environment. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the sediment at the bottom of the lake.

Terpnophyllum is also culturally significant to local indigenous communities, who use it in traditional ceremonies and medicinal practices.
