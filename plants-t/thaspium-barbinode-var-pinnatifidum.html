Thaspium barbinode var. pinnatifidum

Thaspium barbinode var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thaspium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often deeply lobed or pinnatifid, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of southern Africa.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
