Turritis perfoliata

Turritis perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate turritis, is a striking flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are perforated, allowing the stems to pass through, creating a unique visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
