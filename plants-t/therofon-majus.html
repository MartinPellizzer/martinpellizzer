Therofon majus

Therofon majus, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
