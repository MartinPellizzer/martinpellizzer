Tigridia coelestis

Tigridia coelestis, commonly known as the blue tigridia, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep blue to violet, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Tigridia coelestis is known for its long blooming period, usually from late spring to early summer.
