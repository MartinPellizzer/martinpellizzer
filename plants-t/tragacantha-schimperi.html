Tragacantha schimperi

Tragacantha schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Its roots are known to exude a gel-like substance, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal applications.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
