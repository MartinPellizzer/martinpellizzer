Taraxacum helmi-emiliae

Taraxacum helmi-emiliae, commonly known as the Emilia's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, typical of the dandelion genus. It produces a distinctive seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
