Toxocarpus glaucus

Toxocarpus glaucus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and native plant landscapes.
