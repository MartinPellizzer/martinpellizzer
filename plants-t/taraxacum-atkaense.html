Taraxacum atkaense

Taraxacum atkaense, commonly known as the Atka dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the Aleutian Islands and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports its flowering head. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along shorelines.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
