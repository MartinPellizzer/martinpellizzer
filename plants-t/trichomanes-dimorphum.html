Trichomanes dimorphum

Trichomanes dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive dimorphism in the shape of the pinnae.

Trichomanes dimorphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
