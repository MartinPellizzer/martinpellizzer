Terminalia glabrata var. haroldii

Terminalia glabrata var. haroldii, commonly known as African wild date.

It is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are rich in sugars and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its fruit and traditional medicinal uses.
