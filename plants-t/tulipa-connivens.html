Tulipa connivens

Tulipa connivens, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces showy, often double or triple-petaled blooms that are highly valued in horticulture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Tulipa connivens is known for its resilience and is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty.
