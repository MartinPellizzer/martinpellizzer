Tephrosia rigidula

Tephrosia rigidula, commonly known as rigid false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Tephrosia rigidula is often found in sandy or well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
