Thylacophora

Thylacophora, commonly known as the "tiger beetle," is a family of predatory beetles characterized by their elongated bodies and striking coloration.

These insects are typically found in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and forest floors, where they hunt for small invertebrates. Their exoskeletons are hardened and often feature intricate patterns that provide camouflage or warning signals. Thylacophora species are known for their rapid movement and ability to detect prey through vibration and chemical cues.

They play a vital role in controlling populations of smaller arthropods within their ecosystems.
