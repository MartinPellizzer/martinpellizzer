Trillium pusillum var. texanum

Trillium pusillum var. texanum, commonly known as the Texas small white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a solitary, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Trillium genus. The plant has three broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and requires well-drained soil.

The species is notable for its early spring blooming habit and its role in supporting local pollinators.
