Taxanthema armeria

Taxanthema armeria, commonly known as the stonecrop or rockrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Taxanthema armeria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
