Thalictrum appendiculatum

Thalictrum appendiculatum, commonly known as the common meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in temperate regions.

Thalictrum appendiculatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
