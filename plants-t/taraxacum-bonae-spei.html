Taraxacum bonae-spei

Taraxacum bonae-spei, commonly known as the "good fortune dandelion," is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
