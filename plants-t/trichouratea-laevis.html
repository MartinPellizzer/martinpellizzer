Trichouratea laevis

Trichouratea laevis, commonly known as the smooth trichourate, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Trichouratea laevis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
