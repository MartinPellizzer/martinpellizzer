Terminalia macadamii

Terminalia macadamii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
