Torrubia harrisiana

Torrubia harrisiana, commonly known as the Harris's torrubia, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Torrubia harrisiana is considered a rare and endangered species.
