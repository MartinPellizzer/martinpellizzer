Thymus × celtibericus nothosubsp. celtibericus

Thymus × celtibericus nothosubsp.

celtibericus, commonly known as the Iberian thyme, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, dark green leaves.

The plant produces pink or purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
