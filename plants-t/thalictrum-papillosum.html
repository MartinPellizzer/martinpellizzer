Thalictrum papillosum

Thalictrum papillosum, commonly known as the showy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Thalictrum papillosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
