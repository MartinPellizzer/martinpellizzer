Typha × smirnovii

Typha × smirnovii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a hybrid wetland plant that thrives in shallow freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds. The plant plays a crucial role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, allowing it to spread rapidly and dominate wetland areas.

Typha × smirnovii is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb pollutants from water.
