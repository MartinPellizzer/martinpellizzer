Trigonella monantha f. pinnatifida

Trigonella monantha f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant characterized by its pinnately divided leaves.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves with a distinctive, narrow foliage structure.

This variety is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
