Tolypanthus involucratus

Tolypanthus involucratus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or tropical velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Tolypanthus involucratus is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
