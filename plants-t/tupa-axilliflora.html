Tupa axilliflora

Tupa axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary-flowered tupa, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the axils of the leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
