Tussilago sibirica

Tussilago sibirica, commonly known as Siberian coltsfoot, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and other northern regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in treating respiratory ailments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Tussilago sibirica is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
