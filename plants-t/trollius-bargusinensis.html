Trollius bargusinensis

Trollius bargusinensis, commonly known as the Bargusin globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and tundra habitats, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are basal, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Trollius bargusinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
