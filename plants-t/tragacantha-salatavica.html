Tragacantha salatavica

Tragacantha salatavica, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant is notable for producing a viscous resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
