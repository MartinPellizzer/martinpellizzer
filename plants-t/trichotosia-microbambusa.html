Trichotosia microbambusa

Trichotosia microbambusa, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, green culms that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
