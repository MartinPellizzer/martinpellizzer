Trichoglottis apoensis

Trichoglottis apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces inflorescences of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Trichoglottis apoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
