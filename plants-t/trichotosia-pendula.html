Trichotosia pendula

Trichotosia pendula, commonly known as the hanging hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. Trichotosia pendula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plant species.
