Thymus pannonicus var. major

Thymus pannonicus var. major, commonly known as the greater narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
