Tradescantia canaliculata f. lesteri

Tradescantia canaliculata f. lesteri, commonly known as the variegated spiderwort, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that exhibit striking variegation of light green and white. The plant produces clusters of small, three-petaled flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative appeal and ease of care.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens and containers.
