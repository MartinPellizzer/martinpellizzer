Thymus serpyllum proles angustifolius

Thymus serpyllum proles angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thrift, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are narrow and linear, arranged in dense, mat-like formations. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to poor soils and harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
