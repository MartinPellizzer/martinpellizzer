Tapura singularis

Tapura singularis, commonly known as the rare golden bell orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from golden yellow to deep orange. The plant has a compact growth habit with broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid environment. It thrives in shaded, moist conditions and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
