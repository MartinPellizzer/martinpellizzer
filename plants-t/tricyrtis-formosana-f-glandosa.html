Tricyrtis formosana f. glandosa

Tricyrtis formosana f. glandosa, commonly known as the glandular Japanese spiderwort, is a variant of the Japanese spiderwort native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, glandular pubescence, giving it its specific epithet "glandosa.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
