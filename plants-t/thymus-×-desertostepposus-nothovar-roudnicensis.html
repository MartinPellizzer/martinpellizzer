Thymus × desertostepposus nothovar. roudnicensis

Thymus × desertostepposus nothovar. roudnicensis, commonly known as the Roudnicek thyme, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Thymus desertostepposus and Thymus roudnicensis.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
