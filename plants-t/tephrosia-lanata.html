Tephrosia lanata

Tephrosia lanata, commonly known as velvet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Tephrosia lanata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
