Taraxacum tortilobiforme

Taraxacum tortilobiforme, commonly known as the curling-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaf lobes and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant's leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are hollow and ribbed.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
