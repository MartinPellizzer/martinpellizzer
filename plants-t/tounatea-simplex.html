Tounatea simplex

Tounatea simplex, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and vibrant yellow stamens, which make it visually striking. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Tounatea simplex is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some African communities.
