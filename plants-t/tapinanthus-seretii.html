Tapinanthus seretii

Tapinanthus seretii, commonly known as the parasitic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which attach to the roots of its host plants.

Tapinanthus seretii is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator of ecological changes in its environment.
