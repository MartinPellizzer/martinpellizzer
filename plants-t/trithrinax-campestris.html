Trithrinax campestris

Trithrinax campestris, commonly known as the coastal pita, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and pinnately compound leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and wind exposure. Its trunk is typically short and sturdy, with a fibrous, palm-like appearance.

Trithrinax campestris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
