Thelypteris aberrans

Thelypteris aberrans, commonly known as the variable shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds, which are divided into two levels of pinnae, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a thin, membranous covering.

Thelypteris aberrans exhibits a wide range of morphological variation, making it a subject of interest in botanical taxonomy.
