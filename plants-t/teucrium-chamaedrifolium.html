Teucrium chamaedrifolium

Teucrium chamaedrifolium, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
