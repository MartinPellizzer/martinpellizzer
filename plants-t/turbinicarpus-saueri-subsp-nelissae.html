Turbinicarpus saueri subsp. nelissae

Turbinicarpus saueri subsp.

nelissae, commonly known as the Nelissae Turbinara, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa. It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and a dense covering of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
