Teucrium eriocephalum subsp. eriocephalum

Teucrium eriocephalum subsp.

eriocephalum, commonly known as the woolly germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
