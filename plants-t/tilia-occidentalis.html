Tilia occidentalis

Tilia occidentalis, commonly known as the western linden, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hairy husk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland areas.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
