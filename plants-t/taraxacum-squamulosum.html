Taraxacum squamulosum

Taraxacum squamulosum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Its common name reflects the fuzzy appearance of its leaves and stems.
