Tinus laurifolia

Tinus laurifolia, commonly known as the African laurifolia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
