Thalictrum jaliscanum

Thalictrum jaliscanum, commonly known as the Mexican meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Jalisco, Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It has a short flowering period in early spring and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Thalictrum jaliscanum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
