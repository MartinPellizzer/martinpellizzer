Teloxys graveolens

Teloxys graveolens, commonly known as the stinking violets, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, showy blooms that typically appear in spring, and it has a distinctive, often unpleasant odor. Teloxys graveolens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural appreciation.
