Thalictrum dipterocarpum var. mucronatum

Thalictrum dipterocarpum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf meadow-rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in compound umbels, usually in shades of white or pale green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
