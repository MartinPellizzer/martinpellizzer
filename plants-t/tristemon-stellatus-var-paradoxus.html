Tristemon stellatus var. paradoxus

Tristemon stellatus var. paradoxus, commonly known as the star-stemmed tristemon, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves.

Native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a distinctive variegation pattern on its foliage, with alternating shades of green and white. Its flowers, though small, are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
