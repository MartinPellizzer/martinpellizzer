Taraxacum sumneviczii

Taraxacum sumneviczii, commonly known as the Transcaucasian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of broadly lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. It produces a single flowering stem that bears a yellow composite flower, which develops into a crown of white pappus-tipped seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing resilience to arid conditions.
