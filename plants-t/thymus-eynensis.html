Thymus eynensis

Thymus eynensis, commonly known as Eynen's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Thymus eynensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
