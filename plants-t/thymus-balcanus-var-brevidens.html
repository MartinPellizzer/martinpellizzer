Thymus balcanus var. brevidens

Thymus balcanus var. brevidens, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and use in culinary applications.
