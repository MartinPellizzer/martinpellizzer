Taraxacum xantholigulatum

Taraxacum xantholigulatum, commonly known as the yellow marguerite dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
