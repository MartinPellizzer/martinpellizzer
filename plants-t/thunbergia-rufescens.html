Thunbergia rufescens

Thunbergia rufescens, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia rufescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to trail or climb structures.
