Trisetella portillae

Trisetella portillae, commonly known as the Portilla's spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and fleshy, green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by distinctive, narrow capsules containing seeds. Trisetella portillae is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
