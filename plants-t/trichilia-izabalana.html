Trichilia izabalana

Trichilia izabalana, commonly known as the Izabal tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
