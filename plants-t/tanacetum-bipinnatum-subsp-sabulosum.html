Tanacetum bipinnatum subsp. sabulosum

Tanacetum bipinnatum subsp.

sabulosum, commonly known as the sand arnoldia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
