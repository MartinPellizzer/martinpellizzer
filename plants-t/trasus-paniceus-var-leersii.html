Trasus paniceus var. leersii

Trasus paniceus var. leersii, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
