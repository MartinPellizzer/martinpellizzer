Teucrium pumilum

Teucrium pumilum, commonly known as dwarf germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Teucrium pumilum is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
