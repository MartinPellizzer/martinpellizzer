Trochetia laurifolia

Trochetia laurifolia, commonly known as the fragrant laurel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the southeastern regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and emit a strong, spicy fragrance when crushed. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aromatic foliage and attractive fruit.
