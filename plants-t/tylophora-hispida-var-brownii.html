Tylophora hispida var. brownii

Tylophora hispida var. brownii, commonly known as the rough tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
