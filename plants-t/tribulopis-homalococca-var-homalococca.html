Tribulopis homalococca var. homalococca

Tribulopis homalococca var. homalococca, commonly known as the yellow tribulus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by three-lobed, hairy fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
