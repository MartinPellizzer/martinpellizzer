Taraxacum adamii f. adamii

Taraxacum adamii f. adamii, commonly known as the Adam's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas like the Tian Shan and Pamir ranges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
