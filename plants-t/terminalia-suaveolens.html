Terminalia suaveolens

Terminalia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
