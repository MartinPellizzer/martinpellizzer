Taraxacum obliquiforme

Taraxacum obliquiforme, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive basal rosette structure.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
