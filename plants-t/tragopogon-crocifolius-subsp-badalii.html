Tragopogon crocifolius subsp. badalii

Tragopogon crocifolius subsp.

badalii, commonly known as the Badalii thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a single head with ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
