Thymus alatauensis

Thymus alatauensis, commonly known as the Altai thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, narrow leaves and pink to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Thymus alatauensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
