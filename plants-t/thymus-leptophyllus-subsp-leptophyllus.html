Thymus leptophyllus subsp. leptophyllus

Thymus leptophyllus subsp.

leptophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with aromatic, narrow, and often hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, containing thymol and carvacrol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
