Trisetum spicatum var. nivosum

Trisetum spicatum var. nivosum, commonly known as the snowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage that resembles snow-covered grass. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of narrow, awned spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
