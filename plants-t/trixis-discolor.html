Trixis discolor

Trixis discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Trixis discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low maintenance requirements.
