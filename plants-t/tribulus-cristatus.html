Tribulus cristatus

Tribulus cristatus, commonly known as the crown flower or goat's head, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring palmately lobed leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny, red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Tribulus cristatus has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
