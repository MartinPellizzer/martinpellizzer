Tillaea magellanica

Tillaea magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic tillaea, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
