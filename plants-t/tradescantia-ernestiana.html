Tradescantia ernestiana

Tradescantia ernestiana, commonly known as Ernestiana spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that features narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vivid pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats that suppress weeds.
