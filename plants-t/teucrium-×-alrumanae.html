Teucrium × alrumanae

Teucrium × alrumanae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium × alrumanae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
