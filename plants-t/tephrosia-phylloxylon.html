Tephrosia phylloxylon

Tephrosia phylloxylon, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its distinctive yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

The bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacology.
