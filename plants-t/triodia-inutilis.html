Triodia inutilis

Triodia inutilis, commonly known as the useless triodia, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Australia, where it thrives in sandy and saline soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, tufted structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in areas with low rainfall and high temperatures.
