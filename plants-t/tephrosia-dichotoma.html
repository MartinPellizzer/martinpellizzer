Tephrosia dichotoma

Tephrosia dichotoma, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Tephrosia dichotoma is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
