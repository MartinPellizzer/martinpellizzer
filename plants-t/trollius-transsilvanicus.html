Trollius transsilvanicus

Trollius transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom from June to August.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
