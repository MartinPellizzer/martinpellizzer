Tilia bayeriana

Tilia bayeriana, commonly known as the Bavarian lime, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Tilia bayeriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
