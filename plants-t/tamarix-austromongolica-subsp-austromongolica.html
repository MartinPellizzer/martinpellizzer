Tamarix austromongolica subsp.

austromongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in saline and drought-prone soils. It is often used for erosion control due to its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
