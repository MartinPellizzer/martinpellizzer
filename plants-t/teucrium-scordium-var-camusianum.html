Teucrium scordium var. camusianum

Teucrium scordium var. camusianum, commonly known as the Camusian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and often have a slightly minty scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
