Tulipa didieri var. flavicans

Tulipa didieri var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered tulip, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful flowers.
