Tricalysia purpurea

Tricalysia purpurea, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has pinnate leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to provide dense shade.
