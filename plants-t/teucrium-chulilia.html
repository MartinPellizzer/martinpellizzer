Teucrium chulilia

Teucrium chulilia, commonly known as the Chulilla germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium chulilia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
