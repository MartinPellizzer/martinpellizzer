Tournefortia vestita

Tournefortia vestita, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, clustered in panicles, and attract various pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
