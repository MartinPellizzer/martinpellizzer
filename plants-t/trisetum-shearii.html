Trisetum shearii

Trisetum shearii, commonly known as the Shari's false brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, grass-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Trisetum shearii is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
