Trollius patulus var. pedunculatus

Trollius patulus var. pedunculatus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with 5 to 12 petals, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a short, stout stem with a single flower at the top, and its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
