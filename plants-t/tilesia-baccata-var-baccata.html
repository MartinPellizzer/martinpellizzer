Tilesia baccata var. baccata

Tilesia baccata var. baccata, commonly known as the red-leaved tilesia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscaped areas.
