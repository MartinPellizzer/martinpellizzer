Trixis antimenorrhoea var. divaricata

Trixis antimenorrhoea var. divaricata, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with hairy, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
