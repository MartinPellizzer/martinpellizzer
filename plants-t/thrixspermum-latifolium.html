Thrixspermum latifolium

Thrixspermum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments within forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
