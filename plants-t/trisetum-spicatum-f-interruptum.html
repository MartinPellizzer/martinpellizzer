Trisetum spicatum f. interruptum

Trisetum spicatum f. interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted beardgrass, is a variant of the common beardgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its distinguishing feature is the interruption of the spikelets, giving it a unique appearance.

This form is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
