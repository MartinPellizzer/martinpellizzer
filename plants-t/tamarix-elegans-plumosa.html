Tamarix elegans plumosa

Tamarix elegans plumosa, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a popular choice for erosion control and landscape use.
