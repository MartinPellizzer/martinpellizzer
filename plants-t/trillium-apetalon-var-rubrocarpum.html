Trillium apetalon var. rubrocarpum

Trillium apetalon var. rubrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Trillium genus, which is part of the Melanthiaceae family. The plant is characterized by its three large, waxy leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in early spring. Its red, fleshy berries are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
