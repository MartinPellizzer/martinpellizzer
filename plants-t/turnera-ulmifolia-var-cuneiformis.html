Turnera ulmifolia var. cuneiformis

Turnera ulmifolia var. cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turnera, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
