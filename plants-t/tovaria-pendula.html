Tovaria pendula

Tovaria pendula, commonly known as the weeping tovaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Tovaria pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
