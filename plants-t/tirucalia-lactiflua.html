Tirucalia lactiflua

Tirucalia lactiflua, commonly known as the milk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and as an antiseptic.
