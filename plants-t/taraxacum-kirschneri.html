Taraxacum kirschneri

Taraxacum kirschneri, commonly known as the Kirschner's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive flowering stem topped with a spherical cluster of tiny, yellow flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Taraxacum.
