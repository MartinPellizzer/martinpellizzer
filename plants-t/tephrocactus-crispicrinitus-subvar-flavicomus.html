Tephrocactus crispicrinitus subvar. flavicomus

Tephrocactus crispicrinitus subvar. flavicomus, commonly known as the yellow-haired tephrocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, yellowish-white bristles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
