Thermopsis lupinoides

Thermopsis lupinoides, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Thermopsis lupinoides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
