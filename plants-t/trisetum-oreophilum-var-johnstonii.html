Trisetum oreophilum var. johnstonii

Trisetum oreophilum var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with short, bristly hairs.

Johnston's wood sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
