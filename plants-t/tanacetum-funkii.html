Tanacetum funkii

Tanacetum funkii, commonly known as the Carpathian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a strong, aromatic scent, which is often described as similar to camphor or mint.

Tanacetum funkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
