Tenaris simulans

Tenaris simulans, commonly known as the false sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its thick, water-storing leaves.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on desert plant adaptations.
