Thymus subdalmaticus

Thymus subdalmaticus, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact, woody stems that are often covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its aromatic leaves are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus subdalmaticus thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
