Tulipa dykesiana

Tulipa dykesiana, commonly known as the Dykes' tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, allowing it to bloom in early spring.

The botanical features of Tulipa dykesiana make it a distinctive and sought-after addition to alpine gardens.
