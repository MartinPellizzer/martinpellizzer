Topobea brachyura

Topobea brachyura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock lichen, is a species of lichen that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a composite organism consisting of a fungal partner and a photosynthetic alga, which together form a symbiotic relationship. This lichen typically grows on rocky surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its structure is generally crust-like, with a dense, tightly adherent thallus that helps it retain moisture.

The plant's ability to thrive in nutrient-poor substrates makes it an important indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
