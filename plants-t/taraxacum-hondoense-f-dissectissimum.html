Taraxacum hondoense f. dissectissimum

Taraxacum hondoense f. dissectissimum, commonly known as the Hondo dissected dandelion, is a rare and specialized variant of the dandelion genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that distinguish it from other Taraxacum species. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of Hondo Island.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
