Thalictrum wrightii

Thalictrum wrightii, commonly known as Wright's meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Thalictrum wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
