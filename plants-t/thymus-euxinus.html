Thymus euxinus

Thymus euxinus, commonly known as the Crimean thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus euxinus is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
