Tabernaemontana salzmannii var. longifolia

Tabernaemontana salzmannii var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved tabernanthe, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
