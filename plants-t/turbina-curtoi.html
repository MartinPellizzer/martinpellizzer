Turbina curtoi

Turbina curtoi, commonly known as the African turban shell, is a species of sea snail found in the Atlantic Ocean.

It belongs to the family Turbinidae and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped shell. The shell is typically yellowish or brownish with intricate patterns and ridges. The species is often found in shallow coastal waters and is known for its robust and durable shell structure.

Turbina curtoi plays a role in marine ecosystems as both a predator and prey.
