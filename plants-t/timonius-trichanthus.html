Timonius trichanthus

Timonius trichanthus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and leaves.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Timonius trichanthus is often found in temperate regions and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
