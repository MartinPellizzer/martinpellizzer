Thelesperma filifolium var. intermedium

Thelesperma filifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in desert landscapes.
