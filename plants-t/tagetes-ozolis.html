Tagetes ozolis

Tagetes ozolis, commonly known as the Persian marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Tagetes ozolis is also known to repel certain insect pests, making it a useful companion plant in gardens.
