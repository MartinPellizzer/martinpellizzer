Tacsonia cyanea var. insignis

Tacsonia cyanea var. insignis, commonly known as the Bluebell of the Andes, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, enhancing its ornamental appeal.
