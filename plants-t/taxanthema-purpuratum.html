Taxanthema purpuratum

Taxanthema purpuratum, commonly known as the purple taxanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Taxanthema purpuratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
