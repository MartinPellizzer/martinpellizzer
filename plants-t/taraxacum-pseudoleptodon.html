Taraxacum pseudoleptodon

Taraxacum pseudoleptodon, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, leaf-like structure known as a pappus, which aids in seed dispersal by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
