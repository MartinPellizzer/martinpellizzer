Thymus nakhodkensis

Thymus nakhodkensis, commonly known as the Nakhodkensk thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, aromatic leaves and pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Thymus nakhodkensis is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
