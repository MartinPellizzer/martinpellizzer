Tachigali paratyensis

Tachigali paratyensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Paraty cherry, is a tropical tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Tachigali paratyensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
