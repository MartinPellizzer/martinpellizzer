Taraxacum rigens

Taraxacum rigens, commonly known as the rigid dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of serrated, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that matures into a fluffy, white seed head with parachute-like seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum rigens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health purposes.
