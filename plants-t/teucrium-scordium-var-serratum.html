Teucrium scordium var. serratum

Teucrium scordium var. serratum, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or blue, arranged in spikes, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
