Trichocereus strigosus var. longispinus

Trichocereus strigosus var. longispinus, commonly known as the Long-spined Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spines that are longer and more pronounced than in other varieties. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
