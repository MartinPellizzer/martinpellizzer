Tithonia tubaeformis var. bourgaeana

Tithonia tubaeformis var. bourgaeana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a striking annual plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flower heads that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a nectar source for pollinators.

The variety bourgaeana is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form.
