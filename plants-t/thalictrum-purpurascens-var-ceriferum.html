Thalictrum purpurascens var. ceriferum

Thalictrum purpurascens var. ceriferum, commonly known as the candlestick meadow rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a candle-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
