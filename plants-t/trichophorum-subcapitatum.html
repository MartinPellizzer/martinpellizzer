Trichophorum subcapitatum

Trichophorum subcapitatum, commonly known as the small-headed sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to arctic and alpine regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitats.
