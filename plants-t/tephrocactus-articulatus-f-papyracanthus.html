Tephrocactus articulatus f. papyracanthus

Tephrocactus articulatus f. papyracanthus, commonly known as the papery-spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its segmented, cylindrical stems that are covered with soft, papery spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens due to its hardy nature.
