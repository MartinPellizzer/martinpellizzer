Terpsichore lobulata

Terpsichore lobulata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its graceful appearance and the delicate, bell-like structure of its flowers.
