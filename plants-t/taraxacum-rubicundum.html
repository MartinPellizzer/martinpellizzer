Taraxacum rubicundum

Taraxacum rubicundum, commonly known as the red-rayed or red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces a distinctive seed head known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
