Tritonia pectinata

Tritonia pectinata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Tritonia pectinata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding a touch of elegance to gardens with its refined appearance.
