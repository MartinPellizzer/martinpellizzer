Trifolium greenei

Trifolium greenei, commonly known as Greene's clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium greenei is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
