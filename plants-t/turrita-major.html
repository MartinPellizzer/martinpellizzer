Turrita major

Turrita major, commonly known as the giant turritella, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Turritellidae.

It is characterized by its tall, spiraled shell that can reach several inches in height. The shell is typically adorned with a series of concentric ridges and a glossy surface that varies in color from white to pinkish. This marine gastropod is often found in shallow coastal waters, where it resides in sandy or muddy substrates.

Its diet primarily consists of algae and organic detritus, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
