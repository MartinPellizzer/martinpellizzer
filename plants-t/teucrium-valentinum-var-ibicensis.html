Teucrium valentinum var. ibicensis

Teucrium valentinum var. ibicensis, commonly known as the Iberian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the summer months. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This variety is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its environment.
