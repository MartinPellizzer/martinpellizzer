Tetrorchidium trichotocarpum

Tetrorchidium trichotocarpum, commonly known as the hairy fruit tetrorchidium, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, giving it the species name "trichotocarpum.".

The plant's unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
