Tagetes hartwegii

Tagetes hartwegii, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon or yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its vibrant yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
