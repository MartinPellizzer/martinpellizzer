Thomasia solanacea

Thomasia solanacea, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Thomasia solanacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
