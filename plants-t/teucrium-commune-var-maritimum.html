Teucrium commune var. maritimum

Teucrium commune var. maritimum, commonly known as sea germander, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to harsh maritime environments.

The leaves and flowers contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
