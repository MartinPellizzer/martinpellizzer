Trifolium kurdistanicum

Trifolium kurdistanicum, commonly known as Kurdish clover, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium kurdistanicum is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in traditional medicine.
