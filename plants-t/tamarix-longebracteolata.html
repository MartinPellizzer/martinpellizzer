Tamarix longebracteolata

Tamarix longebracteolata, commonly known as the long bract tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, often deciduous foliage and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

Tamarix longebracteolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils in challenging environments.
