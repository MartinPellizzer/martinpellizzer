Tulbaghia violacea var. minor

Tulbaghia violacea var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf African daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety 'minor' is smaller than the typical Tulbaghia violacea, with a more compact growth habit.
