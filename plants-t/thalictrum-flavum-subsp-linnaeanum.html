Thalictrum flavum subsp. linnaeanum

Thalictrum flavum subsp.

linnaeanum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
