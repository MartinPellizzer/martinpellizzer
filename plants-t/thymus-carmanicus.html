Thymus carmanicus

Thymus carmanicus, commonly known as Carman's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
