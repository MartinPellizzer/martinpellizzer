Thlaspi caerulescens subsp. occitanicum

Thlaspi caerulescens subsp.

occitanicum, commonly known as the Occitanian copperwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it a valuable component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
