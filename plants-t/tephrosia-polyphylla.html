Tephrosia polyphylla

Tephrosia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorny growth and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. Tephrosia polyphylla is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
