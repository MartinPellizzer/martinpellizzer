Trigonella balachowskyi

Trigonella balachowskyi, commonly known as the Balachowsky's fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially valuable for agricultural purposes.
