Tradescantia gracillima

Tradescantia gracillima, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its delicate appearance and easy care make it a favored ornamental plant.

Tradescantia gracillima is also valued for its ability to spread via rhizomes, creating dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
