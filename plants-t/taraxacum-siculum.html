Taraxacum siculum

Taraxacum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flower heads that develop into fluffy white seeds, typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive lobed shape with serrated edges.

Taraxacum siculum is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
