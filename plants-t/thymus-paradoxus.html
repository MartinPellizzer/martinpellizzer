Thymus paradoxus

Thymus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Thymus paradoxus is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses, similar to other thyme species.
