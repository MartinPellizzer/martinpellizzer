Thymbra spicata

Thymbra spicata, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Thymbra spicata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
