Tyloglossa longipes

Tyloglossa longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Tyloglossa longipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
