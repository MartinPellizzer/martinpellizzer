Tradescantia ramifera var. chandonnetii

Tradescantia ramifera var. chandonnetii, commonly known as the Chandonnet's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
