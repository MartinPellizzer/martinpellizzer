Taeniophyllum leucanthum

Taeniophyllum leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered tangleweed, is a small, epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees and rocks, forming dense clusters of tiny, leaf-like structures. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, a common adaptation in epiphytic species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
