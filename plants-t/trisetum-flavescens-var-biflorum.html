Trisetum flavescens var. biflorum

Trisetum flavescens var. biflorum, commonly known as the yellow hairgrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and produces small, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
