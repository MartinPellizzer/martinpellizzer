Teucrium oxylepis subsp. rhiphaeum

Teucrium oxylepis subsp.

rhiphaeum, commonly known as the Greek germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, aromatic stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense terminal clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
