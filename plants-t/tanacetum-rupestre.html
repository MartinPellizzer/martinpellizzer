Tanacetum rupestre

Tanacetum rupestre, commonly known as stone nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy leaves that give it a rugged appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.
