Tropaeolum boliviense f. meiopetalum

Tropaeolum boliviense f. meiopetalum, commonly known as the pinkleaf nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight reddish tinge. The flowers are notable for their elongated, pinkish petals that curve outward, giving the blooms a unique, almost whimsical appearance. It is native to the high Andes of Bolivia and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers, which have a peppery flavor.
