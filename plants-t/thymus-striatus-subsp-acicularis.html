Thymus striatus subsp. acicularis

Thymus striatus subsp.

acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink to lavender flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
