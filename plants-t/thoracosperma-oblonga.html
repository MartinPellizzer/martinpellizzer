Thoracosperma oblonga

Thoracosperma oblonga, commonly known as the oblong nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, oblong fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its seeds are valued for their essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The tree is also important in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
