Thyrsanthus pyriformis

Thyrsanthus pyriformis, commonly known as the lemon scented thyrsin, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters called thyrses. Its leaves are aromatic, with a citrus-like scent, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Thyrsanthus pyriformis is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
