Tropaeolum sessilifolium

Tropaeolum sessilifolium, commonly known as the Indian cress or broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum sessilifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
