Tretorhiza dahurica

Tretorhiza dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Tretorhiza dahurica is often found in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
