Trifolium leucanthum

Trifolium leucanthum, commonly known as white clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium leucanthum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
