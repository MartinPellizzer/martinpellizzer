Tetrapterys fraxinifolia

Tetrapterys fraxinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ash or sweet ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of 5 to 9 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by small, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forest environments.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
