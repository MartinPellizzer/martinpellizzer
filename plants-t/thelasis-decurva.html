Thelasis decurva

Thelasis decurva, commonly known as the curly willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Thelasis decurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
