Triticum pungens var. obtusiusculum

Triticum pungens var. obtusiusculum, commonly known as the Mexican wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
