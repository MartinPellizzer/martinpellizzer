Thalictrum chiaonis

Thalictrum chiaonis, commonly known as the Chiaon's meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems that often branch near the base.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle floral display, making it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens.
