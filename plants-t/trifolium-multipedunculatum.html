Trifolium multipedunculatum

Trifolium multipedunculatum, commonly known as the clustered clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium multipedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
