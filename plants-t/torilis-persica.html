Torilis persica

Torilis persica, commonly known as Persian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Torilis persica is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
