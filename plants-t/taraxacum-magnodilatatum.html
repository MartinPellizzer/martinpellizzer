Taraxacum magnodilatatum

Taraxacum magnodilatatum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads and broad, serrated leaves. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, this plant thrives in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem and is known for its ability to form extensive colonies.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
