Tropaeolum hjertingii

Tropaeolum hjertingii, commonly known as the "Garden Crown," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family, known for its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, edible tubers that are sometimes used in local cuisines. Its foliage is heart-shaped, and the stems are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tropaeolum hjertingii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
