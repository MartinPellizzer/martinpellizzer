Tetramicra schoenina

Tetramicra schoenina, commonly known as the white-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are often visited by native bees and other pollinators.

Tetramicra schoenina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its natural habitat.
