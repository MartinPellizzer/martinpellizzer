Teucrium montbretii subsp. libanoticum

Teucrium montbretii subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as Lebanese germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
