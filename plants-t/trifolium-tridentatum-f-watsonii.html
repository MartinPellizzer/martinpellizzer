Trifolium tridentatum f. watsonii

Trifolium tridentatum f. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's trident clover, is a variant of the trident clover species native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
