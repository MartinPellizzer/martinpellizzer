Trigonella hamosa var. microcarpa

Trigonella hamosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited fenugreek, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, compressed pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
