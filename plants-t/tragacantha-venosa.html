Tragacantha venosa

Tragacantha venosa, commonly known as the veined tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its roots are known for secreting a viscous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.

Tragacantha venosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
