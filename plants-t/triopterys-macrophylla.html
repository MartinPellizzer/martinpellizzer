Triopterys macrophylla

Triopterys macrophylla, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
