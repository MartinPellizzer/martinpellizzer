Taraxacum comitans

Taraxacum comitans, commonly known as the California dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to California and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower that blooms in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum comitans is an annual plant that relies on wind pollination and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.
