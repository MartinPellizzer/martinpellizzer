Tryonia schwackeana

Tryonia schwackeana, commonly known as the Schwacke's tryonia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Tryonia schwackeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
