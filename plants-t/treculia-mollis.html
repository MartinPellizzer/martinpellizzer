Treculia mollis

Treculia mollis, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a heavy fruit with a hard, woody shell enclosing a creamy, oily pulp. The fruit is widely used for its edible pulp and oil, which has economic and culinary significance.

It also plays an important role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
