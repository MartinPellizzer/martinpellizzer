Triticum intermedium subsp. kosaninii

Triticum intermedium subsp.

kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's intermediate wheat, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with florets that are often pale green to brown in color. It is typically found in dry, grassy habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and agricultural applications.
