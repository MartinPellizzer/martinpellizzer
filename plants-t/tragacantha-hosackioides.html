Tragacantha hosackioides

Tragacantha hosackioides, commonly known as the Hosack's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food additive.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
