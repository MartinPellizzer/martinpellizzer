Thymus serpyllum subsp. sudeticus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

sudeticus, commonly known as Polish thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in compact clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.
