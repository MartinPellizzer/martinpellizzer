Tragacantha paralipomena

Tragacantha paralipomena, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a gelling agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Tragacantha paralipomena is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in various cultures.
