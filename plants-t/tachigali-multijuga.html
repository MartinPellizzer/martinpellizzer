Tachigali multijuga

Tachigali multijuga, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
