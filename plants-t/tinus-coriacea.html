Tinus coriacea

Tinus coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved tinus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a distinctive white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Tinus coriacea is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
