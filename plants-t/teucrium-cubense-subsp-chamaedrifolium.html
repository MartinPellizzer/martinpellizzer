Teucrium cubense subsp. chamaedrifolium

Teucrium cubense subsp.

chamaedrifolium, commonly known as Cuban germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Cuba. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.
