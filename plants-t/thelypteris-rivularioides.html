Thelypteris rivularioides

Thelypteris rivularioides, commonly known as the river fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in forest understories.

Thelypteris rivularioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in wetland habitats.
