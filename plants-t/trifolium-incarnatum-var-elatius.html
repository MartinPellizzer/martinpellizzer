Trifolium incarnatum var. elatius

Trifolium incarnatum var. elatius, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
