Triticum × turgidovillosum

Triticum × turgidovillosum, commonly known as the Turgidovilloan wheat, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between common wheat (Triticum aestivum) and a wild relative, likely Triticum turgidum.

It exhibits a range of morphological features, including semi-dwarf stature and robust root systems, which contribute to its resilience in various environmental conditions. The plant typically produces spikelets with awns and has a complex chromosome structure due to its hybrid origin. Its botanical significance lies in its potential for genetic improvement in wheat breeding programs.

However, it is not widely cultivated and remains a subject of niche research in plant genetics.
