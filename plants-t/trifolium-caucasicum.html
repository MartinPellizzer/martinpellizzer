Trifolium caucasicum

Trifolium caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium caucasicum is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
