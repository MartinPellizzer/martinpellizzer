Thymus ciliatus var. oblongus

Thymus ciliatus var. oblongus, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale green leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
