Tillandsia myriophylla

Tillandsia myriophylla, commonly known as the silver vane air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant does not require soil to grow, as it thrives by anchoring itself to trees or rocks in humid environments. It produces striking, showy inflorescences with long, narrow flowers that can range in color from white to pink.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
