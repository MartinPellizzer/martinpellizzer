Turnera discors

Turnera discors, commonly known as the yellow turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both wild and cultivated settings.
