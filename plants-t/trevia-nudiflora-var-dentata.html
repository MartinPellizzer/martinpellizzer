Trevia nudiflora var. dentata

Trevia nudiflora var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed trevia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in understory habitats of humid forests, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
