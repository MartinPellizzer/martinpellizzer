Typha caspica

Typha caspica, commonly known as the Caspian cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a brown, fibrous structure. The plant thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to form dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Typha caspica is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fiber.
