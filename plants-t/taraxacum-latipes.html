Taraxacum latipes

Taraxacum latipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
