Tithymalus platyphyllos

Tithymalus platyphyllos, commonly known as the broad-leaved tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
