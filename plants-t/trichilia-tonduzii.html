Trichilia tonduzii

Trichilia tonduzii, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, three-angled pods containing flat, edible seeds. These seeds are used to make shea butter, a valuable natural product in cosmetics and food industries.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
