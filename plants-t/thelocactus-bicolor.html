Thelocactus bicolor

Thelocactus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white or grayish coating. The plant produces striking, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Thelocactus bicolor is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
