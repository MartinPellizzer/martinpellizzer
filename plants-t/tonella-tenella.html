Tonella tenella

Tonella tenella, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is round, yellow when ripe, and has a high acidity, making it a valuable crop for citrus-based products. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
