Teesdalia petraea

Teesdalia petraea, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Teesdalia petraea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
