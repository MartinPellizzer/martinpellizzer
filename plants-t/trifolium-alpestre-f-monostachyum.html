Trifolium alpestre f. monostachyum

Trifolium alpestre f. monostachyum, commonly known as the alpine clover, is a subspecies of the common clover characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and compact growth habit.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, single-stemmed clusters. It is often found in rocky soils and open habitats at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
