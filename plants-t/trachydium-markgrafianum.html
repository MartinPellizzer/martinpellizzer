Trachydium markgrafianum

Trachydium markgrafianum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with a rough texture. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus.

Trachydium markgrafianum thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
