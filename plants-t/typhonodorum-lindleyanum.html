Typhonodorum lindleyanum

Typhonodorum lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
