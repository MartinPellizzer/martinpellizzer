Tricholaena monachne

Tricholaena monachne, commonly known as the sacred bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is a tall, slender perennial plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its narrow, erect leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
