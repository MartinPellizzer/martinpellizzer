Tsuga chinensis

Tsuga chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structures. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

Tsuga chinensis plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is often used in reforestation efforts.
