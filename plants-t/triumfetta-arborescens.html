Triumfetta arborescens

Triumfetta arborescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Triumfetta arborescens is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
