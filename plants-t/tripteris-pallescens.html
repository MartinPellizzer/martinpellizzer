Tripteris pallescens

Tripteris pallescens, commonly known as the white-stemmed tripteris, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its slender, pale-colored trunk and compound leaves. The leaves are typically pinnately divided with small, serrated leaflets. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

This plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
