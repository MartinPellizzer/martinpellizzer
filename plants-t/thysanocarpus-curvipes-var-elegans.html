Thysanocarpus curvipes var. elegans

Thysanocarpus curvipes var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant thysanocarpus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
