Thespis erecta

Thespis erecta, commonly known as the erect thespis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
