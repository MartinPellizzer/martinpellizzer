Trifolium pratense var. luteopurpureum

Trifolium pratense var. luteopurpureum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a variant of red clover characterized by its distinctive purple to pink flower heads.

This plant is a perennial legume that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and pastures. It has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense heads. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for agricultural and ecological purposes.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
