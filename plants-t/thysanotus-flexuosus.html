Thysanotus flexuosus

Thysanotus flexuosus, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems, often in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale blue blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Thysanotus flexuosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
