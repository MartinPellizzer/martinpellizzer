Thymus pallidus var. lindbergii

Thymus pallidus var. lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
