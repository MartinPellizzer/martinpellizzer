Themeda villosa

Themeda villosa, commonly known as the hairy grass or woolly grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering stems produce spikelets that are often concealed among the foliage.

Themeda villosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
