Turpinia pinnata

Turpinia pinnata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by woody, elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
