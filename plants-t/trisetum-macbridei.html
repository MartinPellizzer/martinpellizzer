Trisetum macbridei

Trisetum macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's wood oats, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Trisetum macbridei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
