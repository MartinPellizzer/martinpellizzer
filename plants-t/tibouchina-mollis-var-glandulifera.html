Tibouchina mollis var. glandulifera

Tibouchina mollis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often mistaken for actual petals. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
