Tragacantha reticulata

Tragacantha reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive reticulated or net-like patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its roots are capable of fixing nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment in degraded lands.
