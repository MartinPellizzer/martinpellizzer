Trifolium arvense subsp. preslianum

Trifolium arvense subsp.

preslianum, commonly known as Preslia's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Preslia's clover is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
