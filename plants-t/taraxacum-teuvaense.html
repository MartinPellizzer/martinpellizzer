Taraxacum teuvaense

Taraxacum teuvaense, commonly known as the Teuvaense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Taraxacum teuvaense is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
