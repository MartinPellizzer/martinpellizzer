Tarrietia trifoliolata var. angustifolia

Tarrietia trifoliolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tarrietia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
