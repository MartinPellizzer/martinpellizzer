Trichophorum alpinum var. hudsonianum

Trichophorum alpinum var. hudsonianum, commonly known as the Hudson's bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and peatlands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is an important component of the plant communities in its native habitat, contributing to ecological stability and biodiversity.
