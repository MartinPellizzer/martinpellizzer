Trichoglottis bataanensis

Trichoglottis bataanensis, commonly known as the Bataan trichoglottis, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pendulous stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
