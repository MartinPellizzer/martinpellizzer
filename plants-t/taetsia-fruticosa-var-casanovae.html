Taetsia fruticosa var. casanovae

Taetsia fruticosa var. casanovae, commonly known as the Casanovae Taetsia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring season, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
