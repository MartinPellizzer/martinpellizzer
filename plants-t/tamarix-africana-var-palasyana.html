Tamarix africana var. palasyana

Tamarix africana var. palasyana, commonly known as the African tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or pale red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and salt resistance.

Its roots can fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization in degraded areas.
