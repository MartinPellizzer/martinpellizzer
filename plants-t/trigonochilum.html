Trigonochilum

Trigonochilum, commonly known as the star orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. Trigonochilum species are characterized by their unique, star-shaped flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitats.

The genus includes several species, with Trigonochilum coccineum being one of the most well-known for its striking red flowers.
