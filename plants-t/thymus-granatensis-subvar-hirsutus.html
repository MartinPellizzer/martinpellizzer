Thymus granatensis subvar. hirsutus

Thymus granatensis subvar. hirsutus, commonly known as the Hirsute Thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
