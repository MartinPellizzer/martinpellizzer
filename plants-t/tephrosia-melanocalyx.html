Tephrosia melanocalyx

Tephrosia melanocalyx, commonly known as the African milkpea, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its seeds are edible and can be used in traditional dishes, while the plant also has medicinal uses in some cultures.

Tephrosia melanocalyx thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.
