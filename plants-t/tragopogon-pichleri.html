Tragopogon pichleri

Tragopogon pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive yellow-orange flower with a tuft of white bristles at the center, resembling a goat's beard. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Tragopogon pichleri is typically found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.
