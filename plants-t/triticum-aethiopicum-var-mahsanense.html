Triticum aethiopicum var. mahsanense

Triticum aethiopicum var. mahsanense, commonly known as the Ethiopian wheatgrass, is a wild wheat species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, elongated leaves. This variety typically grows in high-altitude regions and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and the ability to produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological research due to its resilience and genetic diversity.
