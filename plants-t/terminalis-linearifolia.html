Terminalis linearifolia

Terminalis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved terminalis, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, grass-like structure.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is native to arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikelets, typical of grasses. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand periods of drought.

Terminalis linearifolia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal in dry environments.
