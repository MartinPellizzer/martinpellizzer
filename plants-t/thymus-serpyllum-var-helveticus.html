Thymus serpyllum var. helveticus

Thymus serpyllum var. helveticus, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving the plant a compact, cushion-like appearance. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
