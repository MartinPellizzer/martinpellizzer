Tabebuia triorbicularis var. obovata

Tabebuia triorbicularis var. obovata, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and obovate in shape, giving the plant its varietal name. The tree produces woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
