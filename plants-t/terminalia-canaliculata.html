Terminalia canaliculata

Terminalia canaliculata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, canoe-shaped fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
