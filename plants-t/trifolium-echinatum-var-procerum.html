Trifolium echinatum var. procerum

Trifolium echinatum var. procerum, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
