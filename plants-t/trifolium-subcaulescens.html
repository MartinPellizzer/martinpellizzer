Trifolium subcaulescens

Trifolium subcaulescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a short stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Trifolium subcaulescens is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of woodlands.
