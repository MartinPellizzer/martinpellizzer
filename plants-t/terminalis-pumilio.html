Terminalis pumilio

Terminalis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf puma plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
