Tragacantha massiliensis

Tragacantha massiliensis, commonly known as the Massilian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots are known to exude a gel-like substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The species is characterized by its woody stems and small, leathery leaves.
