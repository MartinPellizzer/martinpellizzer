Triorchis gracilis

Triorchis gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a single prominent lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
