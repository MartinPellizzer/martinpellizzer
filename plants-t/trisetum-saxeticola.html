Trisetum saxeticola

Trisetum saxeticola, commonly known as the saxeticola sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Trisetum saxeticola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation.
