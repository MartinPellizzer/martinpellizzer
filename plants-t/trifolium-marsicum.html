Trifolium marsicum

Trifolium marsicum, commonly known as Mars' clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Trifolium marsicum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant for soil enrichment.
