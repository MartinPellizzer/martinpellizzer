Tanacetum petiolosum

Tanacetum petiolosum, commonly known as the slender leaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum petiolosum is known for its strong, camphor-like fragrance, which has been historically used in herbal remedies.
