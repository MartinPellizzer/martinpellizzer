Thymus × pseudoalpestris

Thymus × pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces tiny, pink or lavender flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds, making it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
