Trachypogon violaceus

Trachypogon violaceus, commonly known as the purple grass or violet grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, with the lower bracts often displaying a striking violet color.

Trachypogon violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and color.
