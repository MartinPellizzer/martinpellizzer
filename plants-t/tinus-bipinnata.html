Tinus bipinnata

Tinus bipinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a feathery appearance. The leaves are typically silvery-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
