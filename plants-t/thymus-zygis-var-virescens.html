Thymus zygis var. virescens

Thymus zygis var. virescens, commonly known as green thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, compact growth with slender, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is particularly noted for its fresh, minty scent and is often used in cooking and herbal remedies.
