Thamnochortus obtusus

Thamnochortus obtusus, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a hardy, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions. The plant features small, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, which help reduce water loss. It produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thamnochortus obtusus is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
