Thymus croaticus

Thymus croaticus, commonly known as Croatian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus croaticus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
