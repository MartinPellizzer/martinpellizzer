Thaspium trifoliatum

Thaspium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved thaspium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three leaflets, which is a distinctive botanical characteristic. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in early spring.

Thaspium trifoliatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various insects.
