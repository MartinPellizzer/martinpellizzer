Tricalysia soyauxii var. pilosula

Tricalysia soyauxii var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky tricalysia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
