Tulbaghia campanulata

Tulbaghia campanulata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
