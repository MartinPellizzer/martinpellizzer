Tragacantha gymnopoda

Tragacantha gymnopoda, commonly known as the smooth tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is often cultivated for its gum, which has various industrial and medicinal uses.
