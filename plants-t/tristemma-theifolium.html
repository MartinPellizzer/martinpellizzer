Tristemma theifolium

Tristemma theifolium, commonly known as the thief's leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tristemma theifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
