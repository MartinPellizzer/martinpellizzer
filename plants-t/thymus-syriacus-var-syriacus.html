Thymus syriacus var. syriacus

Thymus syriacus var. syriacus, commonly known as Syrian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses.
