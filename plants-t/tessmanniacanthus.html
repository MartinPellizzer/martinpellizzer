Tessmanniacanthus

Tessmanniacanthus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close during the day. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Tessmanniacanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
