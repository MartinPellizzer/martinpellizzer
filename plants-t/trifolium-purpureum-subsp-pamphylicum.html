Trifolium purpureum subsp. pamphylicum

Trifolium purpureum subsp.

pamphylicum, commonly known as the Pamphylian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
