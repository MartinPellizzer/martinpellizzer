Tephrosia suberosa

Tephrosia suberosa, commonly known as the rough tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are known for containing compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Tephrosia suberosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, arid environments.
