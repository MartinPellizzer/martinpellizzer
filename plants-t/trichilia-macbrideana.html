Trichilia macbrideana

Trichilia macbrideana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, opposite leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
