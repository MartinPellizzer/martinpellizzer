Taraxacum suavissimum

Taraxacum suavissimum, commonly known as the sweet dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, toothed leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds with white pappus, aiding in its widespread distribution. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This species is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability and resilience.
