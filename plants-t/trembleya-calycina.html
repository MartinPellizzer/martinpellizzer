Trembleya calycina

Trembleya calycina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, often with a waxy coating.

Trembleya calycina blooms during the warmer months, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
