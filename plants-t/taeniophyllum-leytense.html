Taeniophyllum leytense

Taeniophyllum leytense, commonly known as the Philippine star orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fragrant, white or pale yellow blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
