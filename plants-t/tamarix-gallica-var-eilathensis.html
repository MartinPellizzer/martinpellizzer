Tamarix gallica var. eilathensis

Tamarix gallica var. eilathensis, commonly known as the Eilat tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and dense clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in drought conditions.

This variety is often used for erosion control and as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
