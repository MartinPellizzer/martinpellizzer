Texiera glastifolia

Texiera glastifolia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Texiera glastifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
