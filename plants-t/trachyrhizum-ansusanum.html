Trachyrhizum ansusanum

Trachyrhizum ansusanum, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a short, erect stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Trachyrhizum ansusanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
