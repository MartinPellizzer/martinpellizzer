Tragacantha complanata

Tragacantha complanata, commonly known as the flattragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive flat, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate.

Tragacantha complanata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
