Trisetum spicatum f. viviparum

Trisetum spicatum f. viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous wood sedge, is a variant of the common wood sedge, characterized by its unique vivipary trait.

This feature allows the plant to produce live offspring, as the seeds develop on the mother plant and can be dispersed when the seed pods dry and burst. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical significance lies in its reproductive strategy, which is relatively rare in the plant kingdom.
