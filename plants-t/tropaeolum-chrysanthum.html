Tropaeolum chrysanthum

Tropaeolum chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and edible properties make it a versatile plant in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
