Turbina velutina

Turbina velutina, commonly known as the velvet turban snail, is a species of air-breathing land snail.

It belongs to the family Turbinidae and is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell that is often covered in a fine, velvety layer. The shell is typically reddish-brown in color and can grow up to 6 centimeters in diameter. This snail is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of Thailand and Vietnam.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to the decomposition of organic matter and serving as prey for various predators.
