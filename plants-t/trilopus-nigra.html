Trilopus nigra

Trilopus nigra, commonly known as the black trilopus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface, and the flowers are usually a shade of pink or white.

Trilopus nigra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
