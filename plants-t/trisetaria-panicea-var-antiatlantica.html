Trisetaria panicea var. antiatlantica

Trisetaria panicea var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass genus native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.

The species is notable for its resilience to salt spray and wind, contributing to the stabilization of sandy shores.
