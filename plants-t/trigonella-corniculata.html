Trigonella corniculata

Trigonella corniculata, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Trigonella corniculata is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
