Thymus ciliatus var. angustibracteatus

Thymus ciliatus var. angustibracteatus, commonly known as narrow-bract thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink to violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic essential oils.
