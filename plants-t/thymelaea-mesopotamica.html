Thymelaea mesopotamica

Thymelaea mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian thymelaea, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

Thymelaea mesopotamica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
