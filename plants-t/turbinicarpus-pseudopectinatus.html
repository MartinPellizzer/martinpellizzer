Turbinicarpus pseudopectinatus

Turbinicarpus pseudopectinatus, commonly known as the Pseudopectinatus Turbinaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, rounded stems that are typically covered in white, woolly hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to its adaptations to extreme conditions, it plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local wildlife.
