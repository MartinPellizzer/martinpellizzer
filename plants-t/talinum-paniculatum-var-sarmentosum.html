Talinum paniculatum var. sarmentosum

Talinum paniculatum var. sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping talinum, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features prostrate or trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
