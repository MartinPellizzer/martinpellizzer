Taraxacum matricicum

Taraxacum matricicum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a hairy stem that can grow up to 30 cm in height, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, forming a single composite head, and it produces a distinctive seed head with pappus for wind dispersal.

Taraxacum matricicum is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
