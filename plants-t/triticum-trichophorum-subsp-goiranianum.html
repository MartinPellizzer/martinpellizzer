Triticum trichophorum subsp. goiranianum

Triticum trichophorum subsp.

goiranianum, commonly known as the Goiran wild oat, is a grass species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
