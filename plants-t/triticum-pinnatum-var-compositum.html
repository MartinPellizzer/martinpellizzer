Triticum pinnatum var. compositum

Triticum pinnatum var. compositum, commonly known as the composite wheatgrass, is a variant of the wheatgrass species characterized by its unique composite structure.

It features a combination of grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as it is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and agricultural research.
