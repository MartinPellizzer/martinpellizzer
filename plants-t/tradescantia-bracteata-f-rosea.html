Tradescantia bracteata f. rosea

Tradescantia bracteata f. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf spiderwort, is a hybrid form of the spiderwort plant.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its striking pink to lavender flowers. The foliage is typically green with a slight bluish tinge, and the leaves are often heart-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
