Trifolium alexandrinum var. phleoides

Trifolium alexandrinum var. phleoides, commonly known as the Egyptian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
