Tsuga williamsonii

Tsuga williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Tsuga, which includes other firs such as the Douglas fir and the balsam fir. The tree is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color, and it produces both male and female cones on the same tree.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
