Trifolium karatavicum

Trifolium karatavicum, commonly known as the Karatavicum clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along the edges of deserts.

Trifolium karatavicum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant for ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
