Trigonotis guilielmi

Trigonotis guilielmi, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy bracts resemble small bells, giving it its common name.

Trigonotis guilielmi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
