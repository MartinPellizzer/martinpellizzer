Taraxacum stipitatum

Taraxacum stipitatum, commonly known as the stippled dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green leaves and a distinctive, elongated flower head. The plant produces a seed head composed of numerous small, white, parachute-like seeds. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Taraxacum stipitatum is a perennial herb that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
