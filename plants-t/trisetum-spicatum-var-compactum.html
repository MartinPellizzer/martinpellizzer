Trisetum spicatum var. compactum

Trisetum spicatum var. compactum, commonly known as the compact tufted beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
