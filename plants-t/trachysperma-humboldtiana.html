Trachysperma humboldtiana

Trachysperma humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are fragrant and have been used traditionally in some regions for their medicinal properties.

Trachysperma humboldtiana thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
