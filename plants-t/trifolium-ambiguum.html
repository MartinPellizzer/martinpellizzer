Trifolium ambiguum

Trifolium ambiguum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Trifolium ambiguum produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
