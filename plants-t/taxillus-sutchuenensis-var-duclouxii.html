Taxillus sutchuenensis var. duclouxii

Taxillus sutchuenensis var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Rosaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds through photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and red, fleshy berries that are consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is native to China and is often found in forested regions with suitable host trees.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the ecological dynamics in its native habitat.
