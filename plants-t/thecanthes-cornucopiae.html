Thecanthes cornucopiae

Thecanthes cornucopiae, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale lavender. The plant features a robust, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. Its blooming period occurs during the late spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thecanthes cornucopiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
