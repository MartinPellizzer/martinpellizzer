Taraxacum rubidum

Taraxacum rubidum, commonly known as the red-seed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are alternate, basal, and have a distinctive lobed shape with a hairy surface.

The plant is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
