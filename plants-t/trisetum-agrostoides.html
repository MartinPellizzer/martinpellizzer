Trisetum agrostoides

Trisetum agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a fine texture and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

Trisetum agrostoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
