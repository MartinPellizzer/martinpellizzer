Trifolium incarnatum f. minor

Trifolium incarnatum f. minor, commonly known as the lesser crimson clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in early spring. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
