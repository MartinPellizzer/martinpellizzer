Trisetum flavescens proles corsicum

Trisetum flavescens proles corsicum, commonly known as Corsican bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
