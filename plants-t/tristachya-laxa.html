Tristachya laxa

Tristachya laxa, commonly known as the purple fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and long, arching blades that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces fluffy, purple-tinted inflorescences that are both ornamental and attract pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread through rhizomes make it adaptable to various climates.
