Thalictrum minus proles virgultorum

Thalictrum minus proles virgultorum, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Thalictrum minus proles virgultorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
