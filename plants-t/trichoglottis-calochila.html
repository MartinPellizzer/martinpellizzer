Trichoglottis calochila

Trichoglottis calochila, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive golden color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Trichoglottis calochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
