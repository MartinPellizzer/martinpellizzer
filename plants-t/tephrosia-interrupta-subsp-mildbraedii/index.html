Tephrosia Interrupta Subsp. Mildbraedii

Tephrosia interrupta subsp. mildbraedii is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, known for its ecological importance in nitrogen fixation. This subspecies is characterized by its distinct foliage and small, clustered flowers. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it faces significant conservation challenges. Studying Tephrosia interrupta subsp. mildbraedii offers insights into biodiversity and the need for protective measures.
