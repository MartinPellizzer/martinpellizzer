Teucrium saxatile

Teucrium saxatile, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as limestone soils and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Teucrium saxatile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
