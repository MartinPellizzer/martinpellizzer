Tibouchina reticulata

Tibouchina reticulata, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive red or purple underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tibouchina reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
