Tsuga mertensiana subsp. jeffreyi

Tsuga mertensiana subsp.

jeffreyi, commonly known as Jeffrey pine, is a subspecies of the white fir that is native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender form and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The cones of this subspecies are often reddish-brown and contain winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-elevation environments, often growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
