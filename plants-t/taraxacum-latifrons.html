Taraxacum latifrons

Taraxacum latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, lobed leaves. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are broad and serrated, while its stems are hairy and erect.

The plant produces a capsule that releases seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in wind dispersal.
