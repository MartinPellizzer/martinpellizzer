Thoracostachyum pacificum

Thoracostachyum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found growing in salt marshes and along the edges of tidal pools.

Thoracostachyum pacificum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
