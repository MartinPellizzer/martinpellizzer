Triodia fissura

Triodia fissura, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming extensive mats on sandy soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce tiny grains of wheat-like seeds.

Triodia fissura plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
