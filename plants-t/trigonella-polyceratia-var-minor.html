Trigonella polyceratia var. minor

Trigonella polyceratia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, angular pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, glandular leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
