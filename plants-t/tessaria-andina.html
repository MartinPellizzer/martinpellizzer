Tessaria andina

Tessaria andina, commonly known as the Andean tessaria, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Tessaria andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
