Thalictrum × karuizawaense

Thalictrum × karuizawaense, commonly known as the Karuizawa meadow rue, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
