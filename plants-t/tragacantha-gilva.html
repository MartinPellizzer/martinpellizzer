Tragacantha gilva

Tragacantha gilva, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent.
