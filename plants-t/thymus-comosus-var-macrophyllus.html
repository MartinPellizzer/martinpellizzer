Thymus comosus var. macrophyllus

Thymus comosus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaved thrift, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and gray-green foliage with fuzzy, glandular surfaces. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
