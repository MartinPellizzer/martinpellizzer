Townsendia condensata var. anomala

Townsendia condensata var. anomala, commonly known as the anomalous condensed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss.

It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.
