Taxus baccata f. semperaurea

Taxus baccata f. semperaurea, commonly known as the golden yew, is a unique variant of the common yew.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with the typical dark green of the standard Taxus baccata. This form is a natural mutation that occurs in certain populations of the plant. The leaves are narrow and needle-like, typical of the yew family, but their coloration makes it visually distinct.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
