Tubella franciscantha

Tubella franciscantha, commonly known as the lacecap mushroom, is a species of basidiomycete fungus characterized by its intricate, lace-like cap.

It belongs to the family Tricholomataceae and is typically found in temperate forest ecosystems. The fruiting bodies are small, with a pale to reddish-brown cap that becomes more pronounced with age. The gills are closely spaced and often decurrent, extending down the stipe.

This mushroom is saprophytic, decomposing organic matter in the soil and playing a vital role in nutrient cycling.
