Tetragonolobus gussonei

Tetragonolobus gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's four-angled legume, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
