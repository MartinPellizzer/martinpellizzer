Thlaspi spinosum

Thlaspi spinosum, commonly known as prickly whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Thlaspi spinosum is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
