Topobea ferruginea

Topobea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its bark is often covered in a reddish or rust-colored layer, giving it its common name.

Topobea ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
