Taxus baccata pendula

Taxus baccata pendula, commonly known as the weeping yew, is a cultivar of the common yew tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that drape downward, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, red-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its foliage consists of flat, needle-like leaves that are dark green and remain on the tree year-round.

This species is slow-growing and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens and landscapes.
