Tsuga chinensis subsp. robusta

Tsuga chinensis subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the fir genus.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers cool, moist climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
