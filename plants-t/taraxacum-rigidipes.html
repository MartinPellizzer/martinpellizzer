Taraxacum rigidipes

Taraxacum rigidipes, commonly known as the rigid dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of serrated, waxy leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets surrounded by a green bract. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Taraxacum rigidipes is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
