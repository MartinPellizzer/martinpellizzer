Trigonella coerulescens subsp. kemerensis

Trigonella coerulescens subsp.

kemerensis, commonly known as Kemer lentil, is a flowering plant species native to Turkey. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are similar to lentils in appearance and nutritional value. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
