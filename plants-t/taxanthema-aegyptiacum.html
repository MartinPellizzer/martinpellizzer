Taxanthema aegyptiacum

Taxanthema aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian taxanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it retain moisture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Taxanthema aegyptiacum is often found in rocky soils and is considered a resilient species in its native habitat.
