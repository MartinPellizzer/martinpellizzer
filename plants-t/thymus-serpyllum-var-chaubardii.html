Thymus serpyllum var. chaubardii

Thymus serpyllum var. chaubardii, commonly known as Chaubard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact form and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
