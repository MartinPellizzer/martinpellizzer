Tolpis succulenta var. multifida

Tolpis succulenta var. multifida, commonly known as the Zulu candelabra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, cylindrical stems that resemble a candelabra, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique structure and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
