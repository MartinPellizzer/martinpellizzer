Tropidia nipponica

Tropidia nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a distinct serrated edge.

Tropidia nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
