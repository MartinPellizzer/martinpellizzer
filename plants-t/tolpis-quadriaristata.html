Tolpis quadriaristata

Tolpis quadriaristata, commonly known as the four-branched reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Tolpis quadriaristata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
