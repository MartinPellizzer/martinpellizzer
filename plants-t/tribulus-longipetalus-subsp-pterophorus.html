Tribulus longipetalus subsp. pterophorus

Tribulus longipetalus subsp.

pterophorus, commonly known as the winged tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, winged calyces that enclose the flower, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, spiny fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
