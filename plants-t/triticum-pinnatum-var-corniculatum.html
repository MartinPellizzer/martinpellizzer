Triticum pinnatum var. corniculatum

Triticum pinnatum var. corniculatum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of forage for livestock.
