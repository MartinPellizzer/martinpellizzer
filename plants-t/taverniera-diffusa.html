Taverniera diffusa

Taverniera diffusa, commonly known as the spreading taverniera, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Taverniera diffusa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
