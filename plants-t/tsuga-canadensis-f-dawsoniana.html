Tsuga canadensis f. dawsoniana

Tsuga canadensis f. dawsoniana, commonly known as the Dawson's hemlock, is a rare and relict form of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features smaller, more densely packed needles compared to the typical hemlock. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, often forming compact, shrub-like forms rather than the usual tall, conical shape. It is adapted to specific microhabitats, often found in shaded, moist environments with acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to environmental stressors, it is considered a conservation concern.
