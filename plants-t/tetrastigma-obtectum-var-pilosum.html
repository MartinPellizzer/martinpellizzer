Tetrastigma obtectum var. pilosum

Tetrastigma obtectum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy climbing raisin vine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving the plant its varietal name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
