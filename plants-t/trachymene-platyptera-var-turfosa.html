Trachymene platyptera var. turfosa

Trachymene platyptera var. turfosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false hellebore, is a herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
