Taraxacum agaurum

Taraxacum agaurum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is considered a rare plant in its native habitat.
