Thea lutescens

Thea lutescens, commonly known as the yellow tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain a single seed. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of a unique type of tea.

Thea lutescens thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
