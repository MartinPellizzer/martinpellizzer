Teucrium hervieri

Teucrium hervieri, commonly known as Hervier's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in dense spikes.

Teucrium hervieri is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
