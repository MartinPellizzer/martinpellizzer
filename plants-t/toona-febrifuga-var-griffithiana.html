Toona febrifuga var. griffithiana

Toona febrifuga var. griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian banyan, is a variant of the Toona febrifuga species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and prominent aerial roots. The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form extensive canopies, often creating a dense shade under its sprawling branches. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into woody capsules.

This variant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
