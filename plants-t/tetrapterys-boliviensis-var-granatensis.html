Tetrapterys boliviensis var. granatensis

Tetrapterys boliviensis var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Cucumber Tree, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a unique floral structure that aids in pollination by insects.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitat.
