Tetrapogon villosus var. monostachyus

Tetrapogon villosus var. monostachyus, commonly known as the hairy tetrapogon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical environments.
