Thuja occidentalis f. compacta

Thuja occidentalis f. compacta, commonly known as the American arborvitae or northern white cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer.

It features scale-like foliage that is dense and dark green, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant has a pyramidal shape with a narrow, conical form, making it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
