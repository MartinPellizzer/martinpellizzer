Trifolium ochroleucum

Trifolium ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in meadows and grasslands.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
