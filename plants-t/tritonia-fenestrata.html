Tritonia fenestrata

Tritonia fenestrata, commonly known as the glassy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often hairy as well. Tritonia fenestrata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
