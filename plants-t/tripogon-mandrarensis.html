Tripogon mandrarensis

Tripogon mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, slender blades. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
