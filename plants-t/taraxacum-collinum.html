Taraxacum collinum

Taraxacum collinum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum collinum is valued for its potential health benefits and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
