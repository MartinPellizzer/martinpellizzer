Tilia corinthiaca

Tilia corinthiaca, commonly known as Corinthian lime, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round nut enclosed in a woody husk.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient Greek culture.
