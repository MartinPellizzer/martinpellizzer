Tulipa bessarabica

Tulipa bessarabica, commonly known as the Bessarabian tulip, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its ability to naturalize over time in suitable conditions.
