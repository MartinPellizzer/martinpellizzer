Trapa natans var. bicornis

Trapa natans var. bicornis, commonly known as the water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, edible seeds. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its unique morphology includes two distinct leaf forms: the floating, broad leaves and the submerged, narrow leaves.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense mats on the water surface, which can impact aquatic ecosystems.
