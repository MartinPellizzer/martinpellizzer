Thuja plicata f. pendula

Thuja plicata f. pendula, commonly known as the cascading or weeping western red cedar, is a cultivar of the western red cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping foliage, which gives it a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed. It is native to the western coast of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
