Thymus vulgaris var. aestivus

Thymus vulgaris var. aestivus, commonly known as summer thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves that are densely packed on square stems. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
