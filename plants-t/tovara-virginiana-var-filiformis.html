Tovara virginiana var. filiformis

Tovara virginiana var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender tovara, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its thin, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
