Trifolium chrysanthum

Trifolium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

Trifolium chrysanthum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
