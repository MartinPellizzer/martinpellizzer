Tetrorchidium oppositifolium

Tetrorchidium oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf tetrorchid, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves grow directly across from one another on the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its botanical classification places it within the family Orchidaceae, highlighting its relationship to other orchids.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
