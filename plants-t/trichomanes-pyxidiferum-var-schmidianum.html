Trichomanes pyxidiferum var. schmidianum

Trichomanes pyxidiferum var. schmidianum, commonly known as the Schmid's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
