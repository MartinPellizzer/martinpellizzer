Trichilia alata

Trichilia alata, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
