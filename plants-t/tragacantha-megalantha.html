Tragacantha megalantha

Tragacantha megalantha, commonly known as the giant tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a thick, sticky resin known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industries. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small animals.
