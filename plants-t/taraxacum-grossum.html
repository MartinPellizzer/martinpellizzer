Taraxacum grossum

Taraxacum grossum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces a tall, hollow stem that bears a single flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Taraxacum grossum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
