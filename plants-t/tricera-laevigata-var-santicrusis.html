Tricera laevigata var. santicrusis

Tricera laevigata var. santicrusis, commonly known as the Santicrusis Tricera, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation to high-altitude conditions.
