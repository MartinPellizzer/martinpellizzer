Trifolium cyrenaicum

Trifolium cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Crete clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Trifolium cyrenaicum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
