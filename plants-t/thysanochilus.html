Thysanochilus

Thysanochilus, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a genus of epiphytic orchids native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their dense, fibrous root systems that help them attach to trees and absorb moisture from the air. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered, often in a spike-like arrangement. Thysanochilus species are known for their vibrant, colorful blooms that range from yellow to orange and red.

They thrive in humid, shaded environments and require careful cultivation to mimic their natural habitat.
