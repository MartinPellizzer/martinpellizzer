Trifolium ankaratrense

Trifolium ankaratrense, commonly known as the Ankaratra clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the humid, temperate climates of its native habitat.

Due to its unique ecological role and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
