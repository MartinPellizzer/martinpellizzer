Tetrastigma erubescens var. erubescens

Tetrastigma erubescens var. erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish in color as they mature. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
