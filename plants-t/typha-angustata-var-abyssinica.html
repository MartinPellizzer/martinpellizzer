Typha angustata var. abyssinica

Typha angustata var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and produces cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks. Its flowering stems are typically reddish-brown and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
