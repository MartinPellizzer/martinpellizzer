Tacca ulei

Tacca ulei, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves that resemble a bat's wings, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators. Tacca ulei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, rich soil to flourish.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
