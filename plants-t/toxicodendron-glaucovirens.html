Toxicodendron glaucovirens

Toxicodendron glaucovirens, commonly known as the lacquer tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that is highly toxic and can cause severe skin irritation upon contact. This sap is also used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
