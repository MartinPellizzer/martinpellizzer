Triodia festucoides

Triodia festucoides, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial grass that forms dense, low-growing clumps with wiry, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its resilience and ability to thrive in extreme conditions make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
