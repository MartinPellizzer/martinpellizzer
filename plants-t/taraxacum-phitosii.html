Taraxacum phitosii

Taraxacum phitosii, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed head with bristly pappus. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for water storage.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous insects.
