Tilia × recondita

Tilia × recondita, commonly known as the Chinese linden, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, native to China.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in panicles and attract pollinators such as bees. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Tilia × recondita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
