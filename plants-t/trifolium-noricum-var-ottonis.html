Trifolium noricum var. ottonis

Trifolium noricum var. ottonis, commonly known as the Otton's red clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive red or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
