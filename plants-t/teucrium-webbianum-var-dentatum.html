Teucrium webbianum var. dentatum

Teucrium webbianum var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
