Tetraplasandra meiandra var. rhynchocarpoides

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. rhynchocarpoides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature that aids in its propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its showy blooms.
