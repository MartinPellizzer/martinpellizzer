Triticum aegilopoides proles thaoudar

Triticum aegilopoides proles thaoudar, commonly known as the Syrian wild wheat, is a grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and hardy.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and its role in wheat evolution.
