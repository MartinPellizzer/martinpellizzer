Tamarix brachystylis var. fluminensis

Tamarix brachystylis var. fluminensis, commonly known as the River Tamarisk, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and saline soils.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
