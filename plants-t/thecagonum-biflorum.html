Thecagonum biflorum

Thecagonum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered cactus, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny exterior, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Thecagonum biflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in desert gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
