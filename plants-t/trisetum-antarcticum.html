Trisetum antarcticum

Trisetum antarcticum, commonly known as Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing grass species native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, including the Antarctic and sub-Antarctic regions. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand extreme cold and strong winds.

Trisetum antarcticum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microscopic organisms in its ecosystem.
