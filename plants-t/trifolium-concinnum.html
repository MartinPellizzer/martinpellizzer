Trifolium concinnum

Trifolium concinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Trifolium concinnum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
