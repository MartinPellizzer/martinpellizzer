Thymus zygioides subsp. rosulans

Thymus zygioides subsp.

rosulans, commonly known as the Rosulate Thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
