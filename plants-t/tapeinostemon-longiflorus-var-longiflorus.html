Tapeinostemon longiflorus var. longiflorus

Tapeinostemon longiflorus var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered tapeinostemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered within the bracts, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
