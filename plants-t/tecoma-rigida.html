Tecoma rigida

Tecoma rigida, commonly known as yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma rigida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
