Trichoa racemosa

Trichoa racemosa, commonly known as the clustered spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.
