Thereianthus elandsmontanus

Thereianthus elandsmontanus, commonly known as the Elandsmontanus cycad, is a unique plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
