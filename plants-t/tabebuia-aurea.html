Tabebuia aurea

Tabebuia aurea, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy with palmate leaves. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is widely cultivated in tropical regions for landscaping purposes.
