Tragacantha cerasocrena

Tragacantha cerasocrena, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
