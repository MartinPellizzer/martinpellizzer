Tragus alienus

Tragus alienus, commonly known as the alien thorn, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, spiky foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.

Native to arid regions, it has adapted to thrive in environments with low rainfall and high temperatures. Its leaves are modified into sharp, needle-like structures that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often going unnoticed due to their modest appearance.

Despite its unassuming nature, Tragus alienus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing shelter for small desert creatures.
