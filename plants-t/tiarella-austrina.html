Tiarella austrina

Tiarella austrina, commonly known as the Australian foamflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tiarella austrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
