Taraxacum elaverinum

Taraxacum elaverinum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow ray florets that develop into fluffy white seeds, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Taraxacum elaverinum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and open slopes.
