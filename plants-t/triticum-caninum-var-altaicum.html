Triticum caninum var. altaicum

Triticum caninum var. altaicum, commonly known as the Altai wild wheat, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in mountainous regions with cooler climates. It is a diploid species with a genome size that reflects its role as a potential wild relative of cultivated wheat.

Its botanical features make it of interest for genetic studies and crop improvement programs.
