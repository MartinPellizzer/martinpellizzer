Thalictrum callianthum

Thalictrum callianthum, commonly known as the showy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Thalictrum callianthum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
