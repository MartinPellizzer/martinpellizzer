Taxus baccata var. variegata

Taxus baccata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated yew, is a variant of the European yew, characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

The leaves are narrow, dark green with white or yellow margins, creating a visually appealing contrast. It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree that typically reaches heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
