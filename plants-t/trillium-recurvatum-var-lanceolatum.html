Trillium recurvatum var. lanceolatum

Trillium recurvatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf trillium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its three narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a single white or pale pink flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils. It blooms in early spring and is known for its delicate, three-petaled flower that resembles a trifoliate leaf.

The species is part of the Trillium genus, which is known for its primitive floral structure and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
