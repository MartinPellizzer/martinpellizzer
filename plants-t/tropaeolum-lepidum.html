Tropaeolum lepidum

Tropaeolum lepidum, commonly known as the "lesser nasturtium," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum lepidum is also valued for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
