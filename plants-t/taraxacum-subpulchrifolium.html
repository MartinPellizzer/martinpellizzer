Taraxacum subpulchrifolium

Taraxacum subpulchrifolium, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has a rosette growth habit. Its leaves are often hairy and can be quite large, while its flowers are followed by elongated seed heads that release lightweight seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
