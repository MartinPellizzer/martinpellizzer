Tulipa dammanii

Tulipa dammanii, commonly known as the Dammanii tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Tulipa dammanii is prized for its showy blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
