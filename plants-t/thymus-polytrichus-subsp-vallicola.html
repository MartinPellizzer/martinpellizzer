Thymus polytrichus subsp. vallicola

Thymus polytrichus subsp.

vallicola, commonly known as the valley thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, creeping stems and small, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Thymus polytrichus subsp.

vallicola is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
