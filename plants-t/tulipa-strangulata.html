Tulipa strangulata

Tulipa strangulata, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Tulipa strangulata is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
