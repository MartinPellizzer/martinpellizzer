Thalictrum eximium

Thalictrum eximium, commonly known as the showy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

Thalictrum eximium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
