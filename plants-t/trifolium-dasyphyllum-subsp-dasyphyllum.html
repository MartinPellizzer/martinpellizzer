Trifolium dasyphyllum subsp. dasyphyllum

Trifolium dasyphyllum subsp.

dasyphyllum, commonly known as the dense-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
