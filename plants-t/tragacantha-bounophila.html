Tragacantha bounophila

Tragacantha bounophila, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It is valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Additionally, it has been used traditionally for its gum, which has various industrial applications.
