Tetragonolobus bivonaeus

Tetragonolobus bivonaeus, commonly known as the Bivona legume, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
