Triodia pilosa

Triodia pilosa, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often forming extensive tussocks in sandy soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Triodia pilosa plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
