Tillandsia conspersa

Tillandsia conspersa, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water absorption. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called vaginas.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
