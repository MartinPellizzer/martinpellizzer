Triticum acutum var. megastachyum

Triticum acutum var. megastachyum, commonly known as the giant wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with numerous small, spikelet-bearing branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Botanically, it is valued for its hardiness and potential use in forage and erosion control.
