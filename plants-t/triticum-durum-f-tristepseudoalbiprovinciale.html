Triticum durum f. tristepseudoalbiprovinciale

Triticum durum f. tristepseudoalbiprovinciale, commonly known as the pseudoalbiprovinciale form of durum wheat, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique morphological traits.

This form exhibits a distinctive combination of traits that set it apart from standard durum wheat, including differences in spike structure and grain morphology. It is typically found in specific geographic regions, suggesting possible adaptation to local environmental conditions. The plant's genetic makeup reflects a complex history of domestication and selection.

Its study contributes to understanding the genetic diversity within wheat species and their potential for agricultural improvement.
