Tylophora grandiflora

Tylophora grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tylophora grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
