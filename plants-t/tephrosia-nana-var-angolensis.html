Tephrosia nana var. angolensis

Tephrosia nana var. angolensis, commonly known as the dwarf tephrosia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinct odor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland margins.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
