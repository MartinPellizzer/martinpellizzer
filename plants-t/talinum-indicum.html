Talinum indicum

Talinum indicum, commonly known as red-rooted spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Talinum indicum thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Its botanical features make it both a nutritious vegetable and a valuable ornamental plant.
