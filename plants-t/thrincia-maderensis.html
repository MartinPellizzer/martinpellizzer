Thrincia maderensis

Thrincia maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
