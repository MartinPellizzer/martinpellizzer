Trattinnickia glaziovii

Trattinnickia glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's rockheath, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge, arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
