Triticum vavilovii var. mravianii

Triticum vavilovii var. mravianii, commonly known as the Mravina wheat, is a wild wheat species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and is considered a valuable resource for wheat breeding programs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets that mature into grains.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
