Trichostachys ciliata

Trichostachys ciliata, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Trichostachys ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in landscaping and erosion control.
