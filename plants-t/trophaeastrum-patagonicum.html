Trophaeastrum patagonicum

Trophaeastrum patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in the cold, arid environments of South America, particularly in Patagonia, where it forms a close mutualistic relationship. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body made up of both the fungal and algal components. It plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered an indicator species for environmental conditions in high-altitude regions.
