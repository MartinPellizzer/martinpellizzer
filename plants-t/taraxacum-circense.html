Taraxacum circense

Taraxacum circense, commonly known as the Canadian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in disturbed or open areas. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions and regrow from the root after being mowed or grazed.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
