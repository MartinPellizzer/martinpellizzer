Temnadenia parviflora

Temnadenia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered temnadenia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Temnadenia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
