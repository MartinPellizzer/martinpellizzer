Trillium delicatum

Trillium delicatum, commonly known as the showy trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three large, showy petals and three sepals that are often a different color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a distinct three-part structure in its leaves and flowers. It blooms in early spring, producing a striking white or pinkish flower with a yellow anther.

Trillium delicatum is an important component of forest understory ecosystems and is often protected due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
