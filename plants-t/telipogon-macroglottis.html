Telipogon macroglottis

Telipogon macroglottis, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Telipogon macroglottis has a complex root system and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
