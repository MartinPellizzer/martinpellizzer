Thymus bracteatus var. penyalarensis

Thymus bracteatus var. penyalarensis, commonly known as the Penyalarensis thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
