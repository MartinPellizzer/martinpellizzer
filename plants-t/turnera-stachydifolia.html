Turnera stachydifolia

Turnera stachydifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf turnera, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Turnera stachydifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
