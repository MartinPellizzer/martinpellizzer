Tepso

Tepso, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a small shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tepso is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
