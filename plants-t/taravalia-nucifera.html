Taravalia nucifera

Taravalia nucifera, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, clustered fruits that contain the edible betel nut. Its trunk is covered in a fibrous, spongy layer that is harvested for use in traditional crafts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
