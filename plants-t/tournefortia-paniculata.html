Tournefortia paniculata

Tournefortia paniculata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Tournefortia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in saline environments.
