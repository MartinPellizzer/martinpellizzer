Taraxacum sagittifrons

Taraxacum sagittifrons, commonly known as the arrowhead dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are arrowhead-shaped, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are followed by a distinctive pappus-based seed head, typical of the dandelion genus.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
