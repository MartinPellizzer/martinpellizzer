Tetramicra × xislensii

Tetramicra × xislensii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between two species of the genus Tetramicra.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is native to the island of Madagascar, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic orchids.
