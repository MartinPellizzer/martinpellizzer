Typha minima subsp. laxmanii

Typha minima subsp.

laxmanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Nepal. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a brown, fibrous structure.

The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in stabilizing the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its role in water filtration and as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
