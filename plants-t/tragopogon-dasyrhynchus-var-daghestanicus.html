Tragopogon dasyrhynchus var. daghestanicus

Tragopogon dasyrhynchus var. daghestanicus, commonly known as the Daghestan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, woolly hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a bluish-gray underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid habitats, showcasing its resilience in challenging environments.
