Talinum pulchellum

Talinum pulchellum, commonly known as redleaf spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red leaves that contrast with its green stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Talinum pulchellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly peppery flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
