Tripteris karrooica

Tripteris karrooica, commonly known as the Karoo tripteris, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and often have a serrated margin, while its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules.

Tripteris karrooica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect species.
