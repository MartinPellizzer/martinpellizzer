Trigonia xanthopila

Trigonia xanthopila, commonly known as the yellow-flowered trigonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Trigonia xanthopila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
