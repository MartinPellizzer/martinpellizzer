Tectaria coadunata var. hirsuta

Tectaria coadunata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tectaria, is a fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of hairy structures on its fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate pinnae that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia, which are clustered in sori.
