Thymus pulchellus

Thymus pulchellus, commonly known as the pink thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, containing essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
