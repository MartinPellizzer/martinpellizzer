Tanacetopsis eriobasis

Tanacetopsis eriobasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, yellowish to greenish composite flowers. The plant typically features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
