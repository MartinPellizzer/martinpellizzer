Tanacetum millefolium

Tanacetum millefolium, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Tanacetum millefolium is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
