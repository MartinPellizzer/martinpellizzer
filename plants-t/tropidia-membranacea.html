Tropidia membranacea

Tropidia membranacea, commonly known as the glassy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, translucent, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water bodies, where it can effectively capture prey. Its leaves are thin and delicate, adapted for buoyancy and photosynthesis.

The botanical features of Tropidia membranacea make it a unique example of evolutionary adaptation in aquatic environments.
