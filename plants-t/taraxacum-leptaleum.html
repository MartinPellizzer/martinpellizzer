Taraxacum leptaleum

Taraxacum leptaleum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairless stem and basal leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum leptaleum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties.
