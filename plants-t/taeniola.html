Taeniola

Taeniola, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, yellow flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Taeniola species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to arid environments.
