Tacca vesicaria

Tacca vesicaria, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, violet-black leaves with a distinctive white, waxy band running down the center. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its unique appearance has made it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.

Tacca vesicaria thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
