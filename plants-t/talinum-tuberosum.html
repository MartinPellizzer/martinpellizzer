Talinum tuberosum

Talinum tuberosum, commonly known as Ethiopian eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible tuberous roots. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its tubers are rich in starch and have a mild, nutty flavor, making them a valuable food source.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
