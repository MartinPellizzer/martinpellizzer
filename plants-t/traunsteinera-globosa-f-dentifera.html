Traunsteinera globosa f. dentifera

Traunsteinera globosa f. dentifera, commonly known as the toothed globular hibiscus, is a unique plant species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of red and yellow. The leaves are typically toothed, giving the plant its specific epithet "dentifera," which means "tooth-bearing" in Latin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking appearance.
