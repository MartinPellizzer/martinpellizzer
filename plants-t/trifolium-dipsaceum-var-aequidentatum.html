Trifolium dipsaceum var. aequidentatum

Trifolium dipsaceum var. aequidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for forage purposes.
