Thymus praticola

Thymus praticola, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along the stems. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, forming compact clusters at the ends of the branches.

Thymus praticola is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
