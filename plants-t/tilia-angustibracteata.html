Tilia angustibracteata

Tilia angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a cottony coating. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The narrow-bract lime is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
