Thunia alba

Thunia alba, commonly known as the white sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found near the shoreline. It is a hardy perennial that can tolerate salt spray and coastal conditions.

Thunia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
