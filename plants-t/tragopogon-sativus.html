Tragopogon sativus

Tragopogon sativus, commonly known as the common goatsbeard, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. It is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.

Tragopogon sativus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
