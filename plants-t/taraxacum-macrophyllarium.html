Taraxacum macrophyllarium

Taraxacum macrophyllarium, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
