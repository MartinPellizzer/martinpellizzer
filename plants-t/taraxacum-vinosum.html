Taraxacum vinosum

Taraxacum vinosum, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish-brown stems and purple-tinted leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Taraxacum vinosum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
