Taraxacum conjungens

Taraxacum conjungens, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant features a rosette of basal, hairy leaves and produces a hollow, leafless stem topped with a yellow composite flower head. Its flowers develop into a spherical, spiky seed head that disperses seeds via wind.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
