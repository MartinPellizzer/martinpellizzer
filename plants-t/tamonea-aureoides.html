Tamonea aureoides

Tamonea aureoides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
