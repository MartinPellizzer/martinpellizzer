Trachycarpus excelsus

Trachycarpus excelsus, commonly known as the Chinese windmill palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that resemble a windmill, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Trachycarpus excelsus is relatively low-maintenance and adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
