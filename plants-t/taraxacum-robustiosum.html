Taraxacum robustiosum

Taraxacum robustiosum, commonly known as the robust dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, waxy leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
