Tropaeolum emarginatum

Tropaeolum emarginatum, commonly known as the "spreading nasturtium," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant is a climbing species that produces edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in cuisine. Its botanical name refers to the notched edges of its leaves, a distinguishing feature.

Tropaeolum emarginatum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
