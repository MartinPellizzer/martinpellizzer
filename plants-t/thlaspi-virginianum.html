Thlaspi virginianum

Thlaspi virginianum, commonly known as Pennsylvania rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is adapted to cool, moist environments.
