Trigonotis heliotropifolia

Trigonotis heliotropifolia, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Trigonotis heliotropifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
