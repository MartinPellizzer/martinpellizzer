Teucrium corcyranum

Teucrium corcyranum, commonly known as Persian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in small, glandular hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and ornamental gardening.
