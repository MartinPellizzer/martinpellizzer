Taraxacum kupfferi

Taraxacum kupfferi, commonly known as the Kupffer's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in early spring. Native to Central Asia, this plant is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Taraxacum.
