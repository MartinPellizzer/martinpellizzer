Thamnocalamus hindsii var. gramineus

Thamnocalamus hindsii var. gramineus, commonly known as the Hinds' bamboo, is a variant of the Hinds' bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that exhibits a distinct bamboo-like appearance with slender, hollow stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
