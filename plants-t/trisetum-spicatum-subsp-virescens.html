Trisetum spicatum subsp. virescens

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

virescens, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical inflorescences that bear spikelets.

The leaves are typically narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib and a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, three-lobed florets that are often green or reddish in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.
