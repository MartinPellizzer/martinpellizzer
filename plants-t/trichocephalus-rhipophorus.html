Trichocephalus rhipophorus

Trichocephalus rhipophorus, commonly known as the African mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Trichocephalus rhipophorus is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
