Terminalia sessilifolia

Terminalia sessilifolia, commonly known as African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
