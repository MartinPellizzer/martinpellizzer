Tragopogon heteropappus

Tragopogon heteropappus, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense array of tiny, tubular florets. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Tragopogon heteropappus thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
