Tetraplasandra meiandra var. ovalis

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaf tetraplasandra, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well-suited for cultivation in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
