Tagetes multiflora var. rupestris

Tagetes multiflora var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that are arranged in clusters, and it has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
