Tulipa intermedia var. intermedia

Tulipa intermedia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems that bear showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors including red, yellow, and bicolor combinations. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and produces foliage composed of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
