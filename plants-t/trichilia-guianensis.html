Trichilia guianensis

Trichilia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
