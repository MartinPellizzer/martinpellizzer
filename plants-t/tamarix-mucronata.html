Tamarix mucronata

Tamarix mucronata, commonly known as the smooth tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tamarix mucronata is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful for soil stabilization and erosion control.
