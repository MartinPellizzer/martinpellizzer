Tilia cordata var. manshurica

Tilia cordata var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which appear before the leaves in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
