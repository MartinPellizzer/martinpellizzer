Tursitis reniformis

Tursitis reniformis, commonly known as kidney bean plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in various culinary traditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and serrated.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
