Tridax trianae

Tridax trianae, commonly known as the triana's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Tridax trianae produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread easily in open habitats.

It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
