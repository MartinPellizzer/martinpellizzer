Trichilia letestui

Trichilia letestui, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
