Taraxacum undulatum

Taraxacum undulatum, commonly known as the wavy dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are often wavy or undulating in appearance. Its flowers are yellow and consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is known for its taproot and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
