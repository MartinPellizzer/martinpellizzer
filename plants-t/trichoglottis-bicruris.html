Trichoglottis bicruris

Trichoglottis bicruris, commonly known as the two-winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces greenish-yellow to white flowers with a distinctive two-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Trichoglottis bicruris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
