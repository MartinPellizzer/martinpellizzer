Teclea campestris

Teclea campestris, commonly known as the field teclea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Teclea campestris is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
