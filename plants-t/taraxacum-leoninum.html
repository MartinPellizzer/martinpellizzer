Taraxacum leoninum

Taraxacum leoninum, commonly known as the lion's milk dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its life cycle includes a period of winter dormancy, with the plant emerging in spring to bloom and set seed.
