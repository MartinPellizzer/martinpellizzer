Tylophora gracilis

Tylophora gracilis, commonly known as the slender tylophora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tylophora gracilis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
