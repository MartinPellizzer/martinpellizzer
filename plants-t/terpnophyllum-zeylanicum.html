Terpnophyllum zeylanicum

Terpnophyllum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a unique, aromatic bark that is harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses. Its botanical features include a smooth, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong cinnamon scent when cut.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is closely related to other members of the Lauraceae family.
