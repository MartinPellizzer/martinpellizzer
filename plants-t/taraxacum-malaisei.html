Taraxacum malaisei

Taraxacum malaisei, commonly known as the Malaise dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
