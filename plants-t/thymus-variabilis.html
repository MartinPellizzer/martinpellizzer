Thymus variabilis

Thymus variabilis, commonly known as variable thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has aromatic, woody stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
