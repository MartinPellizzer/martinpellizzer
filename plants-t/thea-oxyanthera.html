Thea oxyanthera

Thea oxyanthera, commonly known as the sharpflower or oxyanthera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
