Thymus albanus var. lumbardensis

Thymus albanus var. lumbardensis, commonly known as the Lumbarda thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
