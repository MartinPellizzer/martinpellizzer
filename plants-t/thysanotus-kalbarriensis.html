Thysanotus kalbarriensis

Thysanotus kalbarriensis, commonly known as the Kalbarri lily, is an endemic species found in Western Australia.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
