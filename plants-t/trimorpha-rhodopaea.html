Trimorpha rhodopaea

Trimorpha rhodopaea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that often turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trimorpha rhodopaea is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
