Tropaeolum peltophorum

Tropaeolum peltophorum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive crown-like shape. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into autumn.
