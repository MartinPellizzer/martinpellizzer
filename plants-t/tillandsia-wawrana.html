Tillandsia wawrana

Tillandsia wawrana, commonly known as the Wawrana air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant features rosette-shaped foliage that traps water, and it produces small, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Its adaptability to indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for plant enthusiasts.
