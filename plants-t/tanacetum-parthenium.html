Tanacetum parthenium

Tanacetum parthenium, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with a distinctive bitter and minty scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

Tanacetum parthenium has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
