Triticum dumetorum

Triticum dumetorum, commonly known as wild rye, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. The plant has slender, hairless stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional agriculture and can be found in various ecological and agricultural contexts.

However, it is also known for its potential to produce psychoactive compounds, which has led to its classification as a controlled substance in some regions.
