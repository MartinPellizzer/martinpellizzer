Tylecodon

Tylecodon, commonly known as the "blue crassula," is a genus of succulent plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, water-storing leaves that are often blue or silver in color, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Tylecodon species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with dense foliage and sometimes showy yellow or orange flowers. They are popular among succulent enthusiasts due to their compact size and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.

The genus includes several species, such as Tylecodon fruticosus and Tylecodon argenteus, which are prized for their ornamental value.
