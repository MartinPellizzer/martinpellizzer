Tribulus revoilii

Tribulus revoilii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.

Tribulus revoilii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
