Taraxacum shirakium

Taraxacum shirakium, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. Native to Japan, it thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of the genus Taraxacum's adaptability and ecological significance.
