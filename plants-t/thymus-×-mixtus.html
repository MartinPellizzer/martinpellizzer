Thymus × mixtus

Thymus × mixtus, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of pink or lavender flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus × mixtus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
