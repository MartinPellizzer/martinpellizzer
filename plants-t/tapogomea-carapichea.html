Tapogomea carapichea

Tapogomea carapichea, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and deforestation.
