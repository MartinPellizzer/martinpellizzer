Tetrastigma nilagiricum

Tetrastigma nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilagiri grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Tetrastigma nilagiricum is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
