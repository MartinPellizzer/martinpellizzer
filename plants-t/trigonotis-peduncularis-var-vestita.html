Trigonotis peduncularis var. vestita

Trigonotis peduncularis var. vestita, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
