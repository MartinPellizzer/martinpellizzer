Trollius aureus

Trollius aureus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem with heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Trollius aureus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
