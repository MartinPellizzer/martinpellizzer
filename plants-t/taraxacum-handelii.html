Taraxacum handelii

Taraxacum handelii, commonly known as the Tibetan dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to high-altitude regions of Tibet and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during colder months, and it reproduces through wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum handelii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
