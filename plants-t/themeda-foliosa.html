Themeda foliosa

Themeda foliosa, commonly known as the slender blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance through deep root systems.

Themeda foliosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
