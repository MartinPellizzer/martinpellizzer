Tragopogon collinus

Tragopogon collinus, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Tragopogon collinus is a perennial plant that is well adapted to harsh alpine environments.
