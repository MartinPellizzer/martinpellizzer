Teucrium rotundifolium var. rubrovirens

Teucrium rotundifolium var. rubrovirens, commonly known as redstem germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, rounded leaves that are typically reddish or purplish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
