Tetraneuris ivesiana

Tetraneuris ivesiana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Tetraneuris ivesiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
