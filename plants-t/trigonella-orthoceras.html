Trigonella orthoceras

Trigonella orthoceras, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive triangular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Trigonella orthoceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
