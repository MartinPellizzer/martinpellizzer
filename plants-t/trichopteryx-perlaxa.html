Trichopteryx perlaxa

Trichopteryx perlaxa, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, reflective hairs, which help to reduce water loss in cold, harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, typically arranged in compact clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Trichopteryx perlaxa plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
