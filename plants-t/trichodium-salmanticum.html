Trichodium salmanticum

Trichodium salmanticum, commonly known as the Salmanticum grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Trichodium salmanticum plays a role in stabilizing soils in arid environments and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
