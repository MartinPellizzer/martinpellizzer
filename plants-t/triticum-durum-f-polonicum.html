Triticum durum f. polonicum

Triticum durum f. polonicum, commonly known as Polish durum wheat, is a botanical variant of durum wheat characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae, and it is native to the temperate regions of Europe. This form exhibits a unique combination of traits, including a shorter stature and a more compact growth habit compared to typical durum wheat. Its grains are hard and have a high gluten content, making it particularly suitable for pasta production.

The plant's adaptability to various climates and its resilience to diseases contribute to its significance in agricultural practices.
