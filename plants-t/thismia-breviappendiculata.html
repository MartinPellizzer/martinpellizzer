Thismia breviappendiculata

Thismia breviappendiculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed Thismia, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the family Thismiaceae.

It is a myco-heterotroph, meaning it lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on fungal associations for its nutrients. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, often in Southeast Asia. Its small, white or pale green flowers are inconspicuous and lack showy petals.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
