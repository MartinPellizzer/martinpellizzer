Thymus pannonicus var. marschallianus

Thymus pannonicus var. marschallianus, commonly known as the Pannonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
