Tripinna

Tripinna, commonly known as the Indian nettle or *Urtica dioica* var. *indica*, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

Tripinna is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
