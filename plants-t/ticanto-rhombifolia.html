Ticanto rhombifolia

Ticanto rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are modified stems adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens.
