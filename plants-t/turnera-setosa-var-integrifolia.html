Turnera setosa var. integrifolia

Turnera setosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
