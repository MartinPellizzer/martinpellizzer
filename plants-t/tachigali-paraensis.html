Tachigali paraensis

Tachigali paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tachigali, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Its growth is typically associated with moist, well-drained soils in lowland areas.
