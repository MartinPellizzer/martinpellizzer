Tetrastigma planicaule

Tetrastigma planicaule, commonly known as the climbing raisin vine, is a liana native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red when mature.

Tetrastigma planicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
