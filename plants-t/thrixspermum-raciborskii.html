Thrixspermum raciborskii

Thrixspermum raciborskii, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features whorled leaves that are often described as resembling the spokes of a wheel, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its roots are adapted for anchoring in water and absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Thrixspermum raciborskii is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
