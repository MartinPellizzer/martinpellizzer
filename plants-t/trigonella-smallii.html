Trigonella smallii

Trigonella smallii, commonly known as the Armenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
