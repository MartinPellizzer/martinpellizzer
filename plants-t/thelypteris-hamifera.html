Thelypteris hamifera

Thelypteris hamifera, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape. The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, with serrated margins. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
