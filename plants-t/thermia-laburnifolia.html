Thermia laburnifolia

Thermia laburnifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small lanterns, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, which are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.

However, it is important to note that it may have invasive tendencies in certain regions.
