Trichera arvensis subsp. carpathica

Trichera arvensis subsp.

carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered with fine, soft trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies of temperate grassland ecosystems.
