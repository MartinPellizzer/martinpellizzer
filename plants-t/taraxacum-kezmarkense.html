Taraxacum kezmarkense

Taraxacum kezmarkense, commonly known as the Kezmark mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
