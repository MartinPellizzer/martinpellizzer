Tulipa krauseana

Tulipa krauseana, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

Tulipa krauseana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
