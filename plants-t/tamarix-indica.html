Tamarix indica

Tamarix indica, commonly known as the salt cedar or desert willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and saline environments. Its wood is lightweight and resistant to decay, making it useful for construction and fuel.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many parts of the world due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
