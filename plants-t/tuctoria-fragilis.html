Tuctoria fragilis

Tuctoria fragilis, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Tuctoria fragilis is valued for its early spring blooms and its ability to support local pollinators.
