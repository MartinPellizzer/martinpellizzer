Taraxacum gionense

Taraxacum gionense, commonly known as the Gion dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Taraxacum gionense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
