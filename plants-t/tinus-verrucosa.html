Tinus verrucosa

Tinus verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Tinus verrucosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand arid conditions.
