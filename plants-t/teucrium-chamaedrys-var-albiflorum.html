Teucrium chamaedrys var. albiflorum

Teucrium chamaedrys var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a versatile choice for landscaping.
