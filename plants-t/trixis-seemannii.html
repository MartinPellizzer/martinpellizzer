Trixis seemannii

Trixis seemannii, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in extreme desert conditions, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, enabling survival in nutrient-poor soils. Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological niche.

Trixis seemannii plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
