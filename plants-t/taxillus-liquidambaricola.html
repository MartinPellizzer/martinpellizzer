Taxillus liquidambaricola

Taxillus liquidambaricola, commonly known as the red stem dodder, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the roots of host trees, primarily those in the genus Liquidambar.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, using specialized structures called haustoria to penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive, branched stem that is often reddish in color. It is found in various parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate forest environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the growth and health of its host plants.
