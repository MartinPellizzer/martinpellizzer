Tartagalia

Tartagalia, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, grass-like leaves that form dense clusters. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red berries containing bright orange seeds. Tartagalia is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ease of care.
