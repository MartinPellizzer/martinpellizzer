Thecorchus

Thecorchus, commonly known as the coral tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. Thecorchus species are typically shrubs or small trees, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. They have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
