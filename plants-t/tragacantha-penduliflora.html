Tragacantha penduliflora

Tragacantha penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

Tragacantha penduliflora is also valued for its gel-like substance, which has been used historically in various applications.
