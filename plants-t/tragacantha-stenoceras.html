Tragacantha stenoceras

Tragacantha stenoceras, commonly known as the narrow-lobed tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a sticky resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant's botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils.
