Teucrium salviastrum subsp. afrum

Teucrium salviastrum subsp.

afrum, commonly known as African germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
