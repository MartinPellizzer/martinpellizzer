Tragacantha didymophysa

Tragacantha didymophysa, commonly known as the two-lobed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its latex-rich sap has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.

Tragacantha didymophysa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.
