Tradescantia mcvaughii

Tradescantia mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, triangular leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Tradescantia mcvaughii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
