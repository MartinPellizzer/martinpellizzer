Tragacantha calophylla

Tragacantha calophylla, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing seeds. It is valued for its ability to produce a resin, known as tragacanth, which has various industrial and medicinal uses.

Tragacantha calophylla is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
