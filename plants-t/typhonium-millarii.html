Typhonium millarii

Typhonium millarii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

Typhonium millarii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
