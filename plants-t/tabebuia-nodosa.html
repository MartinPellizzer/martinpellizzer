Tabebuia nodosa

Tabebuia nodosa, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in the dry season. The tree has a smooth, grayish bark and produces long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
