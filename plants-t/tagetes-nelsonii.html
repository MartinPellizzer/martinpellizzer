Tagetes nelsonii

Tagetes nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
