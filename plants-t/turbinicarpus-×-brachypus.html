Turbinicarpus × brachypus

Turbinicarpus × brachypus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a rare and unique hybrid resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus macdowellii and Turbinicarpus brachypus.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stem that resembles a cactus fruit, which is actually a modified stem called a caudex. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the spring season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not found in the wild and is primarily cultivated by growers.
