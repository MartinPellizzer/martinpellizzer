Tephrosia senticosa

Tephrosia senticosa, commonly known as the sweet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic leaves that release a strong scent when crushed. It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and has a root system capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Tephrosia senticosa is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
