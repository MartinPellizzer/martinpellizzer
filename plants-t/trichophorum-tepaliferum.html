Trichophorum tepaliferum

Trichophorum tepaliferum, commonly known as the tepaliferous sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called tepals. Trichophorum tepaliferum typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
