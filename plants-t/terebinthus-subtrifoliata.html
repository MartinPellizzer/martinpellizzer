Terebinthus subtrifoliata

Terebinthus subtrifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is aromatic and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Terebinthus subtrifoliata thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
