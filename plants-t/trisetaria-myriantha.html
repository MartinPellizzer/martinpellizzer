Trisetaria myriantha

Trisetaria myriantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass or slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Trisetaria myriantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
