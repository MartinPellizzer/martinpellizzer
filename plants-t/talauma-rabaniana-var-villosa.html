Talauma rabaniana var. villosa

Talauma rabaniana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy talauma, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
