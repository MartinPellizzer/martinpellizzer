Treculia obovoidea

Treculia obovoidea, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by large, woody capsules containing edible seeds. Its fruit is an important source of shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and food products.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ecological role in tropical forests.
