Trifolium praetutianum var. brevitrichum

Trifolium praetutianum var. brevitrichum, commonly known as the short-haired trefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides in temperate regions.

This variety is noted for its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other trefoil species.
