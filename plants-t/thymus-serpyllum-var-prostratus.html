Thymus serpyllum var. prostratus

Thymus serpyllum var. prostratus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
