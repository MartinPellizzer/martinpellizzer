Talinaria

Talinaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its blue or purple ray flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves. Talinaria species are typically perennial herbs that thrive in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. The plant's unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
