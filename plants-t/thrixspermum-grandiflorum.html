Thrixspermum grandiflorum

Thrixspermum grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
