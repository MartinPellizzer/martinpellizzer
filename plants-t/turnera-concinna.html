Turnera concinna

Turnera concinna, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Turnera concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
