Trollius patulus var. subinvolucratus

Trollius patulus var. subinvolucratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that are typically borne on slender stems. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
