Thermopsis schischkinii

Thermopsis schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
