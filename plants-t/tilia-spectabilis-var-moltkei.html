Tilia spectabilis var. moltkei

Tilia spectabilis var. moltkei, commonly known as the Moltke's linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It typically grows to a height of 15-25 meters and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
