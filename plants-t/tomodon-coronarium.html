Tomodon coronarium

Tomodon coronarium, commonly known as the crown bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of petals, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it blooms in the spring.

Tomodon coronarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
