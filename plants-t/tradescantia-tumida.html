Tradescantia tumida

Tradescantia tumida, commonly known as the inflated spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, fleshy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia tumida is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
