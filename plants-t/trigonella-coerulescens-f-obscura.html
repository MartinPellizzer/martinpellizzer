Trigonella coerulescens f. obscura

Trigonella coerulescens f. obscura, commonly known as the dark-blue fenugreek, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the fenugreek plant.

It is characterized by its dark blue flowers and compact growth habit, distinguishing it from the more common yellow-flowered forms. This subspecies is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean and is often found in rocky or arid soils. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a short, branched stem.

Due to its limited distribution and unique coloration, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
