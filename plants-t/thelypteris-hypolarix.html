Thelypteris hypolarix

Thelypteris hypolarix, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically dark green and finely divided. The plant features small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Thelypteris hypolarix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
