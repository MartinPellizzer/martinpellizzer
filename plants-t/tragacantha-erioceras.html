Tragacantha erioceras

Tragacantha erioceras, commonly known as the woolly tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and branched stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly serrated margin.

The woolly tragacanth is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
