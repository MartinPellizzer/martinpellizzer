Turnera serrata var. angustifolia

Turnera serrata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf turnera, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
