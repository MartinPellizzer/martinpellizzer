Trigonochilum hoeijeri

Trigonochilum hoeijeri, commonly known as the Hoijer's hooded orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that encloses the reproductive organs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom during the wet season, and it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and ornamental value.

Conservation efforts are crucial for its survival, as it faces threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
