Tecoma hillii

Tecoma hillii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma hillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
