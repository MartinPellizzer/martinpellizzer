Trichosanthes bracteata

Trichosanthes bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese flame vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that are often followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, tropical-looking plants.
