Thymus pulegioides subvar. espeyracensis

Thymus pulegioides subvar. espeyracensis, commonly known as the Espeyrac thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern France.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, minty scent and medicinal properties.
