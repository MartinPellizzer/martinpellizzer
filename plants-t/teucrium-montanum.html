Teucrium montanum

Teucrium montanum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, dark-colored fruits.

Teucrium montanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
