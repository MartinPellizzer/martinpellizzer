Thelesperma scabiosioides

Thelesperma scabiosioides, commonly known as the plains prickly pear, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, ribbed stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has spiny leaves and produces edible, fleshy fruits that are adapted to arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
