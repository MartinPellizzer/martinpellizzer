Trichachne vestita

Trichachne vestita, commonly known as the woolly grass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Trichachne vestita is typically found in regions with low rainfall and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
