Trifolium kingii var. productum

Trifolium kingii var. productum, commonly known as the King's clover, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense heads, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
