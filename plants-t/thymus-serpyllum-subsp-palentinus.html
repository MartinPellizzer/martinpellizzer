Thymus serpyllum subsp. palentinus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

palentinus, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically grey-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its compact size and aromatic properties, it is valued in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
