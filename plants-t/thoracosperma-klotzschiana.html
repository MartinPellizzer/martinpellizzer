Thoracosperma klotzschiana

Thoracosperma klotzschiana, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, allowing it to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, white bark and thick, fleshy leaves that help retain moisture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Thoracosperma klotzschiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
