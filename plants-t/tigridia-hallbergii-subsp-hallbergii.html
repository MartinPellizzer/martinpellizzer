Tigridia hallbergii subsp. hallbergii

Tigridia hallbergii subsp.

hallbergii, commonly known as the Hallberg's tiger flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful, tubular flowers that resemble tiger stripes.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It produces showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is noted for its robust growth and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
