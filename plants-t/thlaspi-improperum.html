Thlaspi improperum

Thlaspi improperum, commonly known as the alpine cabbages, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It has a short growing season and is often found at high altitudes.
