Tripsacum hirsutum

Tripsacum hirsutum, commonly known as wild maize or hairy panicum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, dry fruits and are often dispersed by animals or water.

Tripsacum hirsutum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
