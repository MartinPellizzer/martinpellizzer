Trifolium gemellum subsp. gemellum

Trifolium gemellum subsp.

gemellum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
