Taraxacum fulvescens

Taraxacum fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a spherical, fuzzy seed head. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are often covered in fine hairs.
