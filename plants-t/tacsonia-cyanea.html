Tacsonia cyanea

Tacsonia cyanea, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, a common trait in orchids, and feature a distinctive spur that houses nectar.

Tacsonia cyanea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
