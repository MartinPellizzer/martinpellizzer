Tetrapterys discolor var. lanuginosa

Tetrapterys discolor var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a variant of the tamarind tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and in traditional medicine.

The variety is notable for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
