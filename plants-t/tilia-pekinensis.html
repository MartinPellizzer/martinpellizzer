Tilia pekinensis

Tilia pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain three seeds each. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in optimal conditions.

Tilia pekinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
