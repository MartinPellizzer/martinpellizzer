Tilingia filisecta

Tilingia filisecta, commonly known as the threadleaf tilingia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle aesthetic to its natural habitat. Tilingia filisecta thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
