Tithymalus megalocarpus

Tithymalus megalocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded tithymalus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like inflorescences.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are distinctive, with large, inflated mericarps that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
