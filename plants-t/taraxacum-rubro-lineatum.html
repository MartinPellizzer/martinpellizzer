Taraxacum rubro-lineatum

Taraxacum rubro-lineatum, commonly known as the red-lined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a distinctive reddish-brown stem with longitudinal lines. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces a hollow, toothed stem and has basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas where it can spread easily through its wind-dispersed seeds.
