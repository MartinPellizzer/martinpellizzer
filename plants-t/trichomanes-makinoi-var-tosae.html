Trichomanes makinoi var. tosae

Trichomanes makinoi var. tosae, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Trichomanes makinoi species.

It is a fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are borne on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
