Tinospora hirsuta

Tinospora hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tinospora, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are often yellow or red when mature, containing several seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora hirsuta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
