Tapogomea muscosa

Tapogomea muscosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its roots are fibrous and can spread widely, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
