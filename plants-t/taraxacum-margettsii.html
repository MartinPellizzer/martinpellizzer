Taraxacum margettsii

Taraxacum margettsii, commonly known as the Margetts' dandelion, is a rare and specialized species of the dandelion genus native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. This plant is typically found in calcareous grasslands and is adapted to specific soil conditions. Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of ancient, undisturbed ecosystems.
