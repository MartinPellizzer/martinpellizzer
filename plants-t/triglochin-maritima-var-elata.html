Triglochin maritima var. elata

Triglochin maritima var. elata, commonly known as the tall sea spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in muddy or sandy shores. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize coastal soils.

Its adaptability to harsh maritime conditions makes it an important species in coastal ecology.
