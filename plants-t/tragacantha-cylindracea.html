Tragacantha cylindracea

Tragacantha cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its cylindrical, twisted stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Tragacantha cylindracea is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
