Triadenia webbii

Triadenia webbii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Triadenia webbii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
