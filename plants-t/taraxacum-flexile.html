Taraxacum flexile

Taraxacum flexile, commonly known as the flexible dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, elongated flower head. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers are yellow and consist of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Taraxacum flexile is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
