Tristachya auronitens

Tristachya auronitens, commonly known as golden panic grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescence that resembles a tuft of hair, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Tristachya auronitens is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
