Trigonella corniculata subsp. occidentalis

Trigonella corniculata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as Western horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Asia. It is a small annual legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
