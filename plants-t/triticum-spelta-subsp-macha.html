Triticum spelta subsp. macha

Triticum spelta subsp.

macha, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild relative of cultivated barley. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing two-rowed grains. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs for improved agricultural varieties.
