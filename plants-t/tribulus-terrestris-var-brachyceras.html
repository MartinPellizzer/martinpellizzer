Tribulus terrestris var. brachyceras

Tribulus terrestris var. brachyceras, commonly known as the lesser caltrop, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, spiny fruits that contain four seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its botanical characteristics make it easily identifiable and adaptable to various environments.
