Thesium ramosum var. caespitans

Thesium ramosum var. caespitans, commonly known as the clustered thesium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit, with multiple stems arising from a central base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
