Typhonium cuspidatum var. ptychiurum

Typhonium cuspidatum var. ptychiurum, commonly known as the purple-veined arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with prominent purple veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or near water bodies.
