Timonius sericeus var. grandiflora

Timonius sericeus var. grandiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf timonius, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
