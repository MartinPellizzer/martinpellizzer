Trifolium nivale

Trifolium nivale, commonly known as snow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Trifolium nivale plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
