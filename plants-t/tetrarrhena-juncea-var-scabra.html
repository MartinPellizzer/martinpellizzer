Tetrarrhena juncea var. scabra

Tetrarrhena juncea var. scabra, commonly known as the rough leaf tetrarrhena, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stiff, rough-textured leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
