Trisetum chromostachyum

Trisetum chromostachyum, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often exhibit a purplish hue, particularly at the nodes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trisetum chromostachyum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
