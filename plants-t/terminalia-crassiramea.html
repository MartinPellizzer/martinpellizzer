Terminalia crassiramea

Terminalia crassiramea, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing flat, winged seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.
