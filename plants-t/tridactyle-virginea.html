Tridactyle virginea

Tridactyle virginea, commonly known as the Virgin's tongs, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been the subject of various ethnobotanical studies.
