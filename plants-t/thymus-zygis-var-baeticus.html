Thymus zygis var. baeticus

Thymus zygis var. baeticus, commonly known as the Iberian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and aromatic, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale purple to pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies.
