Tropaeolum peltophorum var. reginae

Tropaeolum peltophorum var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's Tropaeolum, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family, known for its ornamental value and edible seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and colorful blooms in gardens and containers.
