Thismia hongkongensis

Thismia hongkongensis, commonly known as the Hong Kong Thismia, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thismiaceae and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a myco-heterotroph, relying on fungi for nutrients. The plant produces small, white, lantern-like flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth, where it forms symbiotic relationships with specific fungal species.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, this species is considered rare and is of interest to botanists studying plant-fungi interactions.
