Trichodesma ambacense

Trichodesma ambacense, commonly known as the Indian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in racemes. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its delicate foliage.

Trichodesma ambacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
