Trisetum pratense var. latifolium

Trisetum pratense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wood oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming habit and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
