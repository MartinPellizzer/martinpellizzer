Thymus ciliatus var. angustatus

Thymus ciliatus var. angustatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
