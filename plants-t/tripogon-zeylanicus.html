Tripogon zeylanicus

Tripogon zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Tripogon zeylanicus is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
