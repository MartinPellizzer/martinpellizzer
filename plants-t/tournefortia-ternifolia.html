Tournefortia ternifolia

Tournefortia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Tournefortia ternifolia is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
