Taraxacum lucentipes

Taraxacum lucentipes, commonly known as the brightleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a bright yellow flower head that blooms in early spring. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and terminate in a single composite flower head.

The species is noted for its distinctive, shiny leaves that give it its common name.
