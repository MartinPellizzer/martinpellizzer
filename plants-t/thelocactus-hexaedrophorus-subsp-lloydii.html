Thelocactus hexaedrophorus subsp. lloydii

Thelocactus hexaedrophorus subsp.

lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's thorn cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its short, thick stem and spiny, fleshy leaves that are adapted for water storage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
