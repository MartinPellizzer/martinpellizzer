Triticum aestivum var. baladseetense

Triticum aestivum var. baladseetense, commonly known as the Baladseetense wheat, is a less commonly cultivated variant of common wheat.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant typically produces compact spikes with small, hard grains that are well-suited for milling. Botanically, it exhibits the typical features of the Triticum genus, including florets arranged in spikelets and a well-developed root system.

This variety is of interest for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience and yield.
