Trigonella polyantha

Trigonella polyantha, commonly known as the Persian melilot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Trigonella polyantha is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
