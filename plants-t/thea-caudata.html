Thea caudata

Thea caudata, commonly known as the trailing tea plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and garden displays.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, highlighting its dual role as both a decorative and medicinal plant.
