Triticum trevesium

Triticum trevesium, commonly known as the wild rye, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Triticum trevesium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
