Trophis racemosa subsp. ramon

Trophis racemosa subsp.

ramon, commonly known as the Ramon lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
