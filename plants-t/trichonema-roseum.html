Trichonema roseum

Trichonema roseum, commonly known as the pink moss, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and vibrant pink to red floral bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Trichonema roseum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
