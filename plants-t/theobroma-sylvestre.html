Theobroma sylvestre

Theobroma sylvestre, commonly known as wild cocoa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is closely related to the cacao tree, Theobroma cacao. The plant produces large, palmate leaves and yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and are a source of natural cocoa butter.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
