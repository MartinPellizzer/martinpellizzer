Terminalia glabrata var. glabrata

Terminalia glabrata var. glabrata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
