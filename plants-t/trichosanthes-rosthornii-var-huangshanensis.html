Trichosanthes rosthornii var. huangshanensis

Trichosanthes rosthornii var. huangshanensis, commonly known as the Huangshan snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to China, particularly the Huangshan Mountains.

It produces large, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when mature. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it has tendrils that aid in climbing.

The fruit is used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisine.
