Tamia

Tamia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Due to its valuable timber, Tamia is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
