Trichostachys microcarpa

Trichostachys microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
