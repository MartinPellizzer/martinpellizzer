Toxicodendron sempervirens

Toxicodendron sempervirens, commonly known as the lacquer tree, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces a highly toxic sap that can cause severe skin irritation and allergic reactions in humans. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of traditional lacquer.

However, due to its toxic properties, handling the plant requires caution.
