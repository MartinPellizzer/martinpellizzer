Thelymitra elizabethiae

Thelymitra elizabethiae, commonly known as Elizabeth's sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It produces striking, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive sun-like appearance, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a slender, upright stem with few leaves, and its flowers are clustered at the top. It is often found in open forests and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Thelymitra elizabethiae is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and conservation value.
