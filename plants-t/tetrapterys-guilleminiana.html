Tetrapterys guilleminiana

Tetrapterys guilleminiana, commonly known as the Guilleminian tetraptery, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
