Taeniophyllum minutissimum

Taeniophyllum minutissimum, commonly known as the minute tailed fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant features tiny, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Due to its small size and unique appearance, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and among enthusiasts of rare flora.
