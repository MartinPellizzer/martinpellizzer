Thymopsis aspera

Thymopsis aspera, commonly known as rough thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant has small, grayish-green leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its tiny white or pale pink flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Thymopsis aspera is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
