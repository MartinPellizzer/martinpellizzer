Triodia pumila

Triodia pumila, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a small, mat-forming perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and tiny, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy soils and with minimal rainfall. Triodia pumila plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Its resilience and hardiness make it an important component of the Australian landscape.
