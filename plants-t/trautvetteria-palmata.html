Trautvetteria palmata

Trautvetteria palmata, commonly known as the palmate trautvetteria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
