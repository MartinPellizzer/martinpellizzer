Thrincia nudicaulis subsp. hispida

Thrincia nudicaulis subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hispid thrincia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It features small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
