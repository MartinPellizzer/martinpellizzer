Trifolium tumescens

Trifolium tumescens, commonly known as the inflated clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being rounded and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Trifolium tumescens is a perennial herb that plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
