Thuja semperaurea

Thuja semperaurea, commonly known as the golden thuja, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its flattened, yellow-green foliage that gives it a distinctive golden appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in flattened sprays, and it produces small, spherical cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its slow growth rate and adaptability to various climates contribute to its widespread use in landscaping.
