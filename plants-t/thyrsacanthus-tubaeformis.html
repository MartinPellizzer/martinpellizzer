Thyrsacanthus tubaeformis

Thyrsacanthus tubaeformis, commonly known as the tubular cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
