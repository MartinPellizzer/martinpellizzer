Tachigali richardiana

Tachigali richardiana, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation efforts.
