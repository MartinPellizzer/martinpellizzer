Tragacantha hystrix

Tragacantha hystrix, commonly known as the curly tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
