Tinospora buchholzii

Tinospora buchholzii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Tinospora buchholzii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
