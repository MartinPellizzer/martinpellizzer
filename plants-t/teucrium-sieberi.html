Teucrium sieberi

Teucrium sieberi, commonly known as Siberian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late summer. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Teucrium sieberi is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
