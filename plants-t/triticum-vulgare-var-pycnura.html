Triticum vulgare var. pycnura

Triticum vulgare var. pycnura, commonly known as the compact wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae, and it exhibits a unique morphology with tightly packed spikelets. This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications. The plant produces small, tough grains that are typically used in specialized baking or as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in plant genetics and breeding programs.
