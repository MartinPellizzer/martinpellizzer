Teucrium japonicum f. lanatum

Teucrium japonicum f. lanatum, commonly known as Japanese germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in the summer months. Native to Japan, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
