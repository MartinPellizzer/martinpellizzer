Tunaria

Tunaria, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spikes, and are accompanied by a strong, spicy fragrance.

Tunaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
