Trifolium hallii

Trifolium hallii, commonly known as Hall's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Trifolium hallii is often found in arid regions and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils.
