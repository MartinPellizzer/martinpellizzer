Thymus moesiacus var. subisophylla

Thymus moesiacus var. subisophylla, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme species.

This variety is noted for its distinct foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
