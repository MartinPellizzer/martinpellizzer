Tanacetum ghoratense

Tanacetum ghoratense, commonly known as the Afghan tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum ghoratense is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
