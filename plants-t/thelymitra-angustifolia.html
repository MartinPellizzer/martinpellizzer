Thelymitra angustifolia

Thelymitra angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in open forest or heathland, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a yellowish-green to brownish hue. Its flowering period generally occurs between late autumn and early spring.

Thelymitra angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
