Trigonella teheranica

Trigonella teheranica, commonly known as Persian trigonella, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella teheranica is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Its botanical traits make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
