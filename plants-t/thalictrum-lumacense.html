Thalictrum lumacense

Thalictrum lumacense, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, usually greenish or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a short, rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Thalictrum lumacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
