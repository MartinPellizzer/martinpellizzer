Tephrosia mossambicensis

Tephrosia mossambicensis, commonly known as African leadplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

Tephrosia mossambicensis is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
