Typhonium russell-smithii

Typhonium russell-smithii, commonly known as Russell-Smith's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is edible and has been used by Indigenous Australians for food. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the botanical diversity of the region.
