Tribulus splendens

Tribulus splendens, commonly known as the splendour tangle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets and produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
