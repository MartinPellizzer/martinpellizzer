Thysanotus asper

Thysanotus asper, commonly known as the rough bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it the species name "asper.".

Thysanotus asper is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
