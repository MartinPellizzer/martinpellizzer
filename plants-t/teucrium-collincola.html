Teucrium collincola

Teucrium collincola, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Teucrium collincola is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
