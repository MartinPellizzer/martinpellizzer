Trifolium squamosum

Trifolium squamosum, commonly known as the scaly clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing several seeds.

Trifolium squamosum is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species often found in Mediterranean shrublands.
