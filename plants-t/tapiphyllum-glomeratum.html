Tapiphyllum glomeratum

Tapiphyllum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered tapiphyllum, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette pattern.

Tapiphyllum glomeratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
