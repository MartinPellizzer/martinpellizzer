Thymus ciliatus subsp. abylaeus

Thymus ciliatus subsp.

abylaeus, commonly known as the Cyprus thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky soils and dry, sunny environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
