Tristachya purpurea

Tristachya purpurea, commonly known as purple panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Tristachya purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy and low-maintenance nature.
