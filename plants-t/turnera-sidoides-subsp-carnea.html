Turnera sidoides subsp. carnea

Turnera sidoides subsp.

carnea, commonly known as the scarlet turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a woody stem that can become somewhat branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
