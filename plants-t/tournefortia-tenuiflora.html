Tournefortia tenuiflora

Tournefortia tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slight pubescence.

Tournefortia tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
