Tecoma luteoflora

Tecoma luteoflora, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the dry season.

Tecoma luteoflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
