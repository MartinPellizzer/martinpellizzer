Tragacantha wiedemanniana

Tragacantha wiedemanniana, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing seeds. Its latex, which is viscous and rich in tragacanth gum, is harvested for industrial and medicinal uses.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
