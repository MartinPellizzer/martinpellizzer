Tulipa gallica var. occidentalis

Tulipa gallica var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Crown Imperial, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, upward-facing flowers that resemble a crown, typically in shades of orange, red, and yellow. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its robust growth and ability to naturalize over time.
