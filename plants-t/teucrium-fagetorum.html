Teucrium fagetorum

Teucrium fagetorum, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

Teucrium fagetorum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
