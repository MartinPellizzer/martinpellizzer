Tephrosia pulcherrima

Tephrosia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia pulcherrima thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
