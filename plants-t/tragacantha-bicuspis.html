Tragacantha bicuspis

Tragacantha bicuspis, commonly known as the two-cusped tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha bicuspis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in certain ecosystems.
