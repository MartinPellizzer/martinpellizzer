Thismia filiformis

Thismia filiformis, commonly known as the slender Thismia, is a unique flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thismiaceae and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph, reliant on fungi for nutrients. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or pale green, lacking showy petals. Its root system forms a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, enabling it to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it is often found in shaded, moist forest understories where light is limited.
