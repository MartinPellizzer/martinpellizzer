Thalictrum trautvetterianum

Thalictrum trautvetterianum, commonly known as the Trautvetter's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches a height of 2 to 4 feet. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces follicles containing numerous seeds.

Thalictrum trautvetterianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
