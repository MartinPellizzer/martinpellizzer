Tetractys capensis

Tetractys capensis, commonly known as the Cape tetractys, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tetractys capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
