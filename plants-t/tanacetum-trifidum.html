Tanacetum trifidum

Tanacetum trifidum, commonly known as narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in sunny conditions.

Tanacetum trifidum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
