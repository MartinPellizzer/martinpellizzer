Thymus serpyllum var. trachselianus

Thymus serpyllum var. trachselianus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable both in horticulture and culinary applications.
