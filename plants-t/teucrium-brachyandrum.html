Teucrium brachyandrum

Teucrium brachyandrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium brachyandrum is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
