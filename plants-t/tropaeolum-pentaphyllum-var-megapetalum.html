Tropaeolum pentaphyllum var. megapetalum

Tropaeolum pentaphyllum var. megapetalum, commonly known as the giant nasturtium, is a striking annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, featuring vibrant orange or red hues with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped, edible leaves that are often used in culinary applications. Its stems are hairy and sprawling, allowing it to climb or trail along supports.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
