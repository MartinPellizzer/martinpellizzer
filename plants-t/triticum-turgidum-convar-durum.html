Triticum turgidum convar. durum

Triticum turgidum convar. durum, commonly known as durum wheat, is a species of wheat characterized by its hard, tough grains.

It is a tetraploid species with a genome composition of AABB. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its grains are primarily used for making semolina, which is essential in pasta and couscous production.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
