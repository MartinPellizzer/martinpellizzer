Terminalia rhopalophora var. rhopalophora

Terminalia rhopalophora var. rhopalophora, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. The wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
