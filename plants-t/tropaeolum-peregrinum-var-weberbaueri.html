Tropaeolum peregrinum var. weberbaueri

Tropaeolum peregrinum var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its botanical features include showy yellow and orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
