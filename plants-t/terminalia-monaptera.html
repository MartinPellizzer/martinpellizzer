Terminalia monaptera

Terminalia monaptera, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it important in local crafts and construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African communities.
