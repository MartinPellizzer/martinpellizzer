Trithrinax brasiliensis var. brasiliensis

Trithrinax brasiliensis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically green and somewhat leathery. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, black fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
