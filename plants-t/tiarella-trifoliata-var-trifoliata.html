Tiarella trifoliata var. trifoliata

Tiarella trifoliata var. trifoliata, commonly known as the Japanese foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, which are typically a deep green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing visual interest even when not in bloom.
