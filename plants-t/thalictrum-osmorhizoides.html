Thalictrum osmorhizoides

Thalictrum osmorhizoides, commonly known as the western meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Thalictrum osmorhizoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
