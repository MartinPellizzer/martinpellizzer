Trifolium virginicum

Trifolium virginicum, commonly known as Virginia white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks.

Virginia white clover is valued for its role in ecological systems, providing forage for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
