Turaniphytum kopetdaghense

Turaniphytum kopetdaghense, commonly known as the Kopetdagh lily, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the Kopetdagh region of Turkmenistan and Iran.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers in late summer. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
