Tachigali micropetala

Tachigali micropetala, commonly known as the small-petioled tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and has a moderate growth rate.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.
