Thymus × viciosoi

Thymus × viciosoi, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Thymus serpyllum (wild thyme) and Thymus vulgaris (common thyme), resulting in a plant with distinctive aromatic foliage. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often used in landscaping and herbal applications.
