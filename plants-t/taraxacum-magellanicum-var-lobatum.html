Taraxacum magellanicum var. lobatum

Taraxacum magellanicum var. lobatum, commonly known as the Magellanic dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
