Tarachia canaliculata

Tarachia canaliculata, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to extreme desert conditions include deep root systems and a slow growth rate, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Tarachia canaliculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
