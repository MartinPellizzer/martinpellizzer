Taraxacum teres

Taraxacum teres, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow composite flower that develops into a spherical seed head with parachute-like seeds. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum teres is a perennial herb that is adapted to temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties.
