Tibouchina hieronymi

Tibouchina hieronymi, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant, slightly sticky texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Tibouchina hieronymi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
