Tragacantha angustifolia

Tragacantha angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its common name.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
