Triticum aestivum var. alborubrum

Triticum aestivum var. alborubrum, commonly known as red wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its reddish grain color.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae and is native to the Fertile Crescent. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with elongated, narrow leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds, which are harvested for flour production, contain a high amount of gluten, making it suitable for baking bread and other wheat-based products.

The botanical features of this variety contribute to its adaptability and agricultural significance in temperate climates.
