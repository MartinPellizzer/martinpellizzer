Trifolium alpestre f. bicefala

Trifolium alpestre f. bicefala, commonly known as the alpine clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-headed inflorescence, which sets it apart from typical clover varieties. This plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, pink or white flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and low-nutrient soils.
