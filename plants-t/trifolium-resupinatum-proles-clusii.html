Trifolium resupinatum proles clusii

Trifolium resupinatum proles clusii, commonly known as the Clusius' creeping clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, serrated leaflets that are typically hairy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
