Troxistemon distichus

Troxistemon distichus, commonly known as the two-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
