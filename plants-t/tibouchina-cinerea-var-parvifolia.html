Tibouchina cinerea var. parvifolia

Tibouchina cinerea var. parvifolia, commonly known as silverleaf tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina cinerea var. parvifolia is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
