Tridax hintonii

Tridax hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's tridax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and open areas.

Tridax hintonii is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
