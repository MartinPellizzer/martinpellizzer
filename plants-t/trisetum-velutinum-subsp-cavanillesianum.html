Trisetum velutinum subsp. cavanillesianum

Trisetum velutinum subsp.

cavanillesianum, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its fine, soft, and densely tufted foliage, which gives it a distinctive velvety appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
