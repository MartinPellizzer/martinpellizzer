Taraxacum yvelinense

Taraxacum yvelinense, commonly known as the Yveline dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to France and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a long, hollow stem with a milky sap, and its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and ecological role in its native habitat.
