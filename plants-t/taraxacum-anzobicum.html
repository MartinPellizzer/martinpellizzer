Taraxacum anzobicum

Taraxacum anzobicum, commonly known as the Alaskan dandelion, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that bears a single flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow flower that develops into a spherical, wind-dispersed seed head. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure and medicinal properties.
