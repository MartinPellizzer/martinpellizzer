Tetrastigma dubium

Tetrastigma dubium, commonly known as the climbing ivy grape, is a vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red berries that are edible. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
