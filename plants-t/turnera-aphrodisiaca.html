Turnera aphrodisiaca

Turnera aphrodisiaca, commonly known as the "love plant," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its name and appearance, it has also been associated with traditional beliefs regarding love and attraction.
