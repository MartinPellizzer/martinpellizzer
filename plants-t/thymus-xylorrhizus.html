Thymus xylorrhizus

Thymus xylorrhizus, commonly known as the rock thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Thymus xylorrhizus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
