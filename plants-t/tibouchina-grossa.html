Tibouchina grossa

Tibouchina grossa, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple flame, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its botanical name honors the French botanist Jean-Baptiste Tibouchina, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina grossa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
