Trisetopsis turgidula

Trisetopsis turgidula, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Trisetopsis turgidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
