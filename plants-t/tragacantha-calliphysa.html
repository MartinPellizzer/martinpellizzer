Tragacantha calliphysa

Tragacantha calliphysa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its most notable feature is the production of a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.

The botanical significance of Tragacantha calliphysa lies in its ecological role and its contribution to the study of plant resins.
