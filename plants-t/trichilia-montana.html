Trichilia montana

Trichilia montana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Trichilia montana is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in furniture and construction.
