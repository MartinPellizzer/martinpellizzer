Trisetum distichophyllum subsp. delphinense

Trisetum distichophyllum subsp.

delphinense, commonly known as the Delphinine Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
