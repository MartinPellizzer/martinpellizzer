Taxus baccata var. fastigiata-variegata

Taxus baccata var. fastigiata-variegata, commonly known as the variegated yew, is a cultivar of the European yew tree.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves that exhibit a distinctive variegation pattern of yellow and green. The plant is evergreen, with a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small garden spaces. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, and it produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This variety is often used as a decorative hedge or specimen plant in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
