Tragacantha submitis

Tragacantha submitis, commonly known as the soft thorned tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a sizing agent in textiles.

Tragacantha submitis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbanks.
