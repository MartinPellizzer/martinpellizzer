Tithonia brachypappa

Tithonia brachypappa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flower heads, which are typically surrounded by narrow, green bracts. The plant has opposite, pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tithonia brachypappa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
