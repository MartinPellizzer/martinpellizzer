Thapsia villosa var. dissecta

Thapsia villosa var. dissecta, commonly known as the Woolly Thapsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
