Terminalia gella

Terminalia gella, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas.

Terminalia gella is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
