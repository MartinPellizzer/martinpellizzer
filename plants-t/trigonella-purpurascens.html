Trigonella purpurascens

Trigonella purpurascens, commonly known as the purple trigonella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella purpurascens is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
