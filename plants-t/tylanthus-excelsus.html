Tylanthus excelsus

Tylanthus excelsus, commonly known as the yellow tylanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Tylanthus excelsus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
