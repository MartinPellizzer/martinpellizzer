Tricalysia cacondensis

Tricalysia cacondensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that provide a lush, green canopy. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Tricalysia cacondensis is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
