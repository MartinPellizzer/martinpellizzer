Tragacantha uniflora

Tragacantha uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha uniflora is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
