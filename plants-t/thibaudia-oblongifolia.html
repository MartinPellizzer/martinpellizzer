Thibaudia oblongifolia

Thibaudia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thibaudia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Thibaudia oblongifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
