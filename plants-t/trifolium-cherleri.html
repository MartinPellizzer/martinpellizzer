Trifolium cherleri

Trifolium cherleri, commonly known as Cherler's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium cherleri is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
