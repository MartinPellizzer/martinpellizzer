Turnera cipoensis

Turnera cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo turnera, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
