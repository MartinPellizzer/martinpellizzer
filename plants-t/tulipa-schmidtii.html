Tulipa schmidtii

Tulipa schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in arid conditions, allowing it to endure harsh climates.

Tulipa schmidtii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
