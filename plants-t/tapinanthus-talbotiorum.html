Tapinanthus talbotiorum

Tapinanthus talbotiorum, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a flowering plant that belongs to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from a host plant while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, green leaves and produces delicate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Due to its unique parasitic nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
