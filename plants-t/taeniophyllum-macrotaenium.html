Taeniophyllum macrotaenium

Taeniophyllum macrotaenium, commonly known as the broad-leafed moss fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lanceolate, glossy leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in humid indoor environments.
