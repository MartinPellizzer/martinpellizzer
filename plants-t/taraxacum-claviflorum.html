Taraxacum claviflorum

Taraxacum claviflorum, commonly known as the narrowflower dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum claviflorum is a perennial herb that typically blooms from late spring to early summer.
