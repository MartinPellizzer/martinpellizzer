Tradescantia fluminensis var. tenella

Tradescantia fluminensis var. tenella, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or violet, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its popularity as a houseplant and garden specimen.
