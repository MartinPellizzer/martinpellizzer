Triticum solandri

Triticum solandri, commonly known as wild rye, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Triticum solandri is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
