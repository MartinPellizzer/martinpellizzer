Teucrium scordioides var. lanuginosum

Teucrium scordioides var. lanuginosum, commonly known as woolly germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
