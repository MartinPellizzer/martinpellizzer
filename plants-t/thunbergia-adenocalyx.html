Thunbergia adenocalyx

Thunbergia adenocalyx, commonly known as the velvet flower or African bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a climbing vine or shrub, reaching up to 5 meters in height. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and fragrance.

Thunbergia adenocalyx thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
