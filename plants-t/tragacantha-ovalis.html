Tragacantha ovalis

Tragacantha ovalis, commonly known as the oval tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a viscous gum, similar to tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food additive. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and historical contexts.
