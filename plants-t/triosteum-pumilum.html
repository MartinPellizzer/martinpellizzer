Triosteum pumilum

Triosteum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf pimpernel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in a variety of garden settings.
