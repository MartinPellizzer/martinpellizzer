Tribula turbith

Tribula turbith, commonly known as the common tribulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils across temperate regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is notable for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
