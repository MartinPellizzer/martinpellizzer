Triticum aestivum var. clementeae

Triticum aestivum var. clementeae, commonly known as Clemente's wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic traits.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and exhibits specific adaptations to its environment, including drought resistance. The plant has narrow leaves and compact spikes, which contribute to its distinct morphology. It is primarily cultivated for its high yield and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Due to its genetic diversity, it holds significance in agricultural research and breeding programs.
