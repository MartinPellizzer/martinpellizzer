Turnera ulmifolia var. angustifolia

Turnera ulmifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting purple or red markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
