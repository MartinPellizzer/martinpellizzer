Tripsacum muticum

Tripsacum muticum, commonly known as wild maize or tumbi, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to maize and teosinte. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of grass evolution and agricultural history.
