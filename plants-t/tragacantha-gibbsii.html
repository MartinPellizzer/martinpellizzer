Tragacantha gibbsii

Tragacantha gibbsii, commonly known as the Gibb's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various traditional applications.
