Tanacetum mairei

Tanacetum mairei, commonly known as the Maire's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums, and is known for its aromatic, often pungent scent. Tanacetum mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
