Trifolium ovatifolium

Trifolium ovatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Trifolium ovatifolium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant for soil enrichment.
