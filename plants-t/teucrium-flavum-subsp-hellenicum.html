Teucrium flavum subsp. hellenicum

Teucrium flavum subsp.

hellenicum, commonly known as the Hellenic germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and resilience to poor soil conditions.
