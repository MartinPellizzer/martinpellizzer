Trisetum preslii var. lasianthum

Trisetum preslii var. lasianthum, commonly known as the Lasianthum sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use in wetland restoration projects.
