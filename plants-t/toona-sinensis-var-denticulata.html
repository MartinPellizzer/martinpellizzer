Toona sinensis var. denticulata

Toona sinensis var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed toona, is a variant of the toona tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive serrated or toothed margin, giving it its common name. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
