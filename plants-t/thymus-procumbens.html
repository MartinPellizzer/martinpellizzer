Thymus procumbens

Thymus procumbens, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and its flowers are pink to purple, blooming in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its compact growth habit and resilience make it an excellent ground cover plant.
