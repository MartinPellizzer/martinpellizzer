Turnera amapaensis

Turnera amapaensis, commonly known as the Amapa turnera, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive waxy texture, while its flowers are clustered in cymes.

Turnera amapaensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
