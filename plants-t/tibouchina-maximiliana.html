Tibouchina maximiliana

Tibouchina maximiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina maximiliana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
