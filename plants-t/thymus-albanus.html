Thymus albanus

Thymus albanus, commonly known as Albanian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, known for their strong, pungent aroma.
