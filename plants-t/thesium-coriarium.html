Thesium coriarium

Thesium coriarium, commonly known as the golden burr or the burr thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense, spiny bracts. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, and its seed capsules are covered in hooked bristles, aiding in seed dispersal by attachment to animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Thesium coriarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
