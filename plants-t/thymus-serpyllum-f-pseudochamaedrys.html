Thymus serpyllum f. pseudochamaedrys

Thymus serpyllum f. pseudochamaedrys, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and often gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, similar to other thyme species.
