Tilia ashei

Tilia ashei, commonly known as the Florida basswood, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a fluffy coating. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Tilia ashei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
