Triopterys jacquemontii

Triopterys jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's triopterys, is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
