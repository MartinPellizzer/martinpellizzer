Tamarix rubella

Tamarix rubella, commonly known as the pink tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Tamarix rubella is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species capable of thriving in poor soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
