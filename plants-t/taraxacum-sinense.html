Taraxacum sinense

Taraxacum sinense, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds with tufts of white filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum sinense is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
