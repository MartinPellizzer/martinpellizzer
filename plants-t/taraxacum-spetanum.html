Taraxacum spetanum

Taraxacum spetanum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used in traditional remedies for digestive issues.

The plant is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil health.
