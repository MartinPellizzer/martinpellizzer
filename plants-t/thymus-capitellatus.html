Thymus capitellatus

Thymus capitellatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its fragrant, mint-like aroma.

This species is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
