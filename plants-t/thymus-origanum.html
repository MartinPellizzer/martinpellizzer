Thymus origanum

Thymus origanum, commonly known as oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval leaves and clusters of pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Oregano is widely used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
