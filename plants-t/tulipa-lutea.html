Tulipa lutea

Tulipa lutea, commonly known as the yellow tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms with six to eight petals, often with a darker throat. Tulipa lutea typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
