Teucrium salviastrum var. afrum

Teucrium salviastrum var. afrum, commonly known as African germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use.
