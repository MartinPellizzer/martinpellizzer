Taraxacum venustum

Taraxacum venustum, commonly known as the noble dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a slender, hollow stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a white, pappus that aids in its propagation.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
