Thespesia multibracteata

Thespesia multibracteata, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree with showy, pale yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Thespesia multibracteata is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in Polynesian cultures.
