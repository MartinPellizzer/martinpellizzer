Taraxacum rubefactum

Taraxacum rubefactum, commonly known as the red-rayed or red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are basal, rough, and have a hairy texture, while the flower heads are surrounded by bracts that turn brown as the seeds mature.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
