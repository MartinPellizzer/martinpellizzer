Thalictrum glaucovirens

Thalictrum glaucovirens, commonly known as the blue false spiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a powdery blue-gray coating. The plant produces showy, upright clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its subtle coloration and graceful growth habit.
