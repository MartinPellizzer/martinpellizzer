Tanacetum depauperatum

Tanacetum depauperatum, commonly known as the lesser tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower structure. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and compact growth make it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and border planting.
