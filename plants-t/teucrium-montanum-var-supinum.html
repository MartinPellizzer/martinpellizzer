Teucrium montanum var. supinum

Teucrium montanum var. supinum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
