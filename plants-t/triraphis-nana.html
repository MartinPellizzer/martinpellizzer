Triraphis nana

Triraphis nana, commonly known as the dwarf triraphis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring compact, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
