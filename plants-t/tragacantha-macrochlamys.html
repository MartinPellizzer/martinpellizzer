Tragacantha macrochlamys

Tragacantha macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot system and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
