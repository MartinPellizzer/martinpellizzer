Trigonella emodi subsp. fimbriata

Trigonella emodi subsp.

fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed fenugreek, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
