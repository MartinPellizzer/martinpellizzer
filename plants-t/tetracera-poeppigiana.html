Tetracera poeppigiana

Tetracera poeppigiana, commonly known as the golden torch tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading crown and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive appearance and potential ecological benefits.
