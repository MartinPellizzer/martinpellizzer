Trifoliada alba

Trifoliada alba, commonly known as the white trifoliate, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical name reflects its white flowers and trifoliate foliage structure.
