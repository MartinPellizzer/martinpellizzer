Thymus vulgaris subsp. mansanetianus

Thymus vulgaris subsp.

mansanetianus, commonly known as the Manzanet thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features compact, low-growing foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
