Triumfetta bartramia var. clementii

Triumfetta bartramia var. clementii, commonly known as the Florida stinkbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is released when the fruits are disturbed.
