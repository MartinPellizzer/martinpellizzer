Thymus serpyllum var. caespitosus

Thymus serpyllum var. caespitosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, compact clusters of small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
