Thymus serpyllum f. transsilvanicus

Thymus serpyllum f. transsilvanicus, commonly known as Transylvanian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It is also a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal collections due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
