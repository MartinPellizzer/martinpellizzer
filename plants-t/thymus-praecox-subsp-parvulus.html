Thymus praecox subsp. parvulus

Thymus praecox subsp.

parvulus, commonly known as dwarf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces compact clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
