Tingulonga altissima

Tingulonga altissima, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a stout, branching trunk and can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

The botanical features of Tingulonga altissima make it a striking and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
