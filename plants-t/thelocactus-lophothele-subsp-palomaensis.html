Thelocactus lophothele subsp. palomaensis

Thelocactus lophothele subsp.

palomaensis, commonly known as the Palomaensis thlocactus, is a rare cactus species native to a limited region in Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which are adaptations to its arid environment.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding to its ornamental value. Its growth is slow, and it requires well-drained soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
