Trifolium candollei

Trifolium candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's clover, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium candollei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
