Taraxacum pachylobum

Taraxacum pachylobum, commonly known as the thick-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, spoon-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces a bright yellow flower head that develops into a globe-shaped seed head with pappus, aiding in wind dispersal. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
