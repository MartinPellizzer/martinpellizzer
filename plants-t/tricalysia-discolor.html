Tricalysia discolor

Tricalysia discolor, commonly known as the white-veined wax tree, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Tricalysia discolor is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
