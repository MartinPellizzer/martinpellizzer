Teucrium polium subsp. micropodioides

Teucrium polium subsp.

micropodioides, commonly known as the lesser woundwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal uses.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
