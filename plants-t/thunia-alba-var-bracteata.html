Thunia alba var. bracteata

Thunia alba var. bracteata, commonly known as the white spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white petals and sepals, which are often adorned with a delicate, spider-like appearance due to its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticultural collections.
