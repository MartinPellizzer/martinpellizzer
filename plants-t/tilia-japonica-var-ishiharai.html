Tilia japonica var. ishiharai

Tilia japonica var. ishiharai, commonly known as the Ishihara linden, is a variant of the Japanese linden tree.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has smooth, gray bark and produces small, oval fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental tree in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
