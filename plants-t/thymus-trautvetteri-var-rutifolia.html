Thymus trautvetteri var. rutifolia

Thymus trautvetteri var. rutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, typical of the Thymus genus. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental and medicinal plant.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.
