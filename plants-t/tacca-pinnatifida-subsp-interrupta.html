Tacca pinnatifida subsp. interrupta

Tacca pinnatifida subsp.

interrupta, commonly known as the "bat flower," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pendulous inflorescence that resembles a bat, hence its common name.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, fleshy, bat-like structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its exotic appearance.
