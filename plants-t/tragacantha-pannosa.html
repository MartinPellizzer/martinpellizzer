Tragacantha pannosa

Tragacantha pannosa, commonly known as the woolly tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
