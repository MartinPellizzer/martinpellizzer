Thymus tymphrestus

Thymus tymphrestus, commonly known as the Alpine thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils.
