Tessiera lithospermoides

Tessiera lithospermoides, commonly known as the rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture and a strong, spicy aroma.

Tessiera lithospermoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
