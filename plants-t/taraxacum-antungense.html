Taraxacum antungense

Taraxacum antungense, commonly known as the Antung dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its life cycle includes a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single flower head.

Taraxacum antungense is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
