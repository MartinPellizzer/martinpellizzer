Triphasia glauca

Triphasia glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Triphasia glauca is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
