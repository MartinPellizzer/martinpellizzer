Thymus danubialis

Thymus danubialis, commonly known as Danube thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain thymol, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal practices.
