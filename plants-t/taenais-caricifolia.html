Taenais caricifolia

Taenais caricifolia, commonly known as the marsh willow or water willow, is a shrub-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found along stream banks and in swampy areas.

Taenais caricifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
