Trochera ottonis

Trochera ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
