Turnera ignota

Turnera ignota, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Turnera ignota is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.
