Tricalysia gossweileri

Tricalysia gossweileri, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tricalysia gossweileri is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
