Thymus nepeta

Thymus nepeta, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their minty scent.

Thymus nepeta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
