Tragacantha insidiosa

Tragacantha insidiosa, commonly known as the insidious tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate in structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, though it is not widely cultivated.
