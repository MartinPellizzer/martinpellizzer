Tricyrtis formosana

Tricyrtis formosana, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that resemble small orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Tricyrtis formosana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
