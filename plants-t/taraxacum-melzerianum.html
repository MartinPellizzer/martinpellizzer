Taraxacum melzerianum

Taraxacum melzerianum, commonly known as the Melzer's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is not as widely used as other dandelion species.
