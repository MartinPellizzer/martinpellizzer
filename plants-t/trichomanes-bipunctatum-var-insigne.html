Trichomanes bipunctatum var. insigne

Trichomanes bipunctatum var. insigne, commonly known as the noble maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical collections.
