Tylloma strictum

Tylloma strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tylloma, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical regions.
