Tithymalus deseglisei

Tithymalus deseglisei, commonly known as the desert tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water and nutrient availability.

Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.
