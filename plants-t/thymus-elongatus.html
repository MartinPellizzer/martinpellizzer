Thymus elongatus

Thymus elongatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
