Thymus maroccanus var. pycnostachys

Thymus maroccanus var. pycnostachys, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
