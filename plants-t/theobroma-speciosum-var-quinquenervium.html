Theobroma speciosum var. quinquenervium

Theobroma speciosum var. quinquenervium, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, elliptical leaves with a prominent central vein and five lateral veins, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, which resembles a small, green bean, contains seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of cocoa.
