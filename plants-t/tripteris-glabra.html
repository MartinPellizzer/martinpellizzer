Tripteris glabra

Tripteris glabra, commonly known as the smooth tripteris, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Tripteris glabra is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
