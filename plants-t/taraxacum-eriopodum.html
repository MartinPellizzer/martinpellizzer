Taraxacum eriopodum

Taraxacum eriopodum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Taraxacum eriopodum is notable for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
