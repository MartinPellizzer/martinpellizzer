Trichosanthes laceribractea

Trichosanthes laceribractea, commonly known as the Chinese flame vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking orange-yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct, branching structure that supports its climbing habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
