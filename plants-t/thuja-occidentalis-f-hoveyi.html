Thuja occidentalis f. hoveyi

Thuja occidentalis f. hoveyi, commonly known as the Hovey's arborvitae, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the eastern United States.

It features a dense, conical shape with scale-like leaves that are bright green on top and whitish underneath. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for hedges or as a specimen plant. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is also noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
