Tapinanthus tschintschochensis

Tapinanthus tschintschochensis, commonly known as the Tschintschochensis dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, attaching itself through specialized root-like structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often found growing on the stems of other vegetation. Its life cycle is closely tied to its host, and it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Due to its specialized nature, it is relatively rare and not widely cultivated.
