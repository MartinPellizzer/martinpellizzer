Trifolium denudatum

Trifolium denudatum, commonly known as the naked vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Trifolium denudatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
