Taraxacum pseudolaxum

Taraxacum pseudolaxum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other dandelion species.
