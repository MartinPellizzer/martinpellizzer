Thalictrum pallidiflorum

Thalictrum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Thalictrum pallidiflorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and subtle floral display.
