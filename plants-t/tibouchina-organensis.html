Tibouchina organensis

Tibouchina organensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet to pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Tibouchina organensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of Hawaii.
