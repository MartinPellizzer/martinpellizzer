Trapa japonica var. jeholensis

Trapa japonica var. jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features floating leaves with long, slender petioles and distinctive, spiny fruits that resemble small, round nuts. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
