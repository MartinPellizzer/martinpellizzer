Tephrocactus subinermis

Tephrocactus subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedge cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its green, ribbed stems and sparse, needle-like spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Tephrocactus subinermis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water needs and ornamental appearance.
