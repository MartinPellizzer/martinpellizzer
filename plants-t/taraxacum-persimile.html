Taraxacum persimile

Taraxacum persimile, commonly known as the Persian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flowering head. The flowers develop into a distinctive, fluffy white pappus that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
