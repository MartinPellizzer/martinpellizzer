Tachigali argyrophylla

Tachigali argyrophylla, commonly known as silver-leaf tachigali, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
