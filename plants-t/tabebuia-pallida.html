Tabebuia pallida

Tabebuia pallida, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded canopy, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
