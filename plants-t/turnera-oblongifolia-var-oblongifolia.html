Turnera oblongifolia var. oblongifolia

Turnera oblongifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
