Trichosanthes edulis var. punctata

Trichosanthes edulis var. punctata, commonly known as the spiky gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, warty surface and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is typically oblong, with a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, edible pulp. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
