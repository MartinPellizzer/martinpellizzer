Thalia hexantha

Thalia hexantha, commonly known as the six-branched thalia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Thalia hexantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
