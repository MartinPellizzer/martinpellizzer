Trochera melicodes

Trochera melicodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Trochera melicodes is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
