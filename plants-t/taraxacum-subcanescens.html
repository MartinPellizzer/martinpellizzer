Taraxacum subcanescens

Taraxacum subcanescens, commonly known as the pale hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a sphere of white seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is a perennial species that is well adapted to temperate climates.
