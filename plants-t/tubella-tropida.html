Tubella tropida

Tubella tropida, commonly known as the tropical tubeworm, is a species of polychaete annelid found in marine environments.

It is characterized by its elongated, segmented body and a distinctive tubular structure that houses its internal organs. The plant-like appearance of Tubella tropida has led to its common name, though it is not a true plant but a marine invertebrate. It plays a role in benthic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling.

Its habitat is typically shallow coastal waters where it thrives in sandy or muddy substrates.
