Thysanotus hispidulus

Thysanotus hispidulus, commonly known as the woolly thread-leaf, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thysanotus hispidulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
