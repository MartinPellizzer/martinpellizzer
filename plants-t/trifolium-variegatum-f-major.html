Trifolium variegatum f. major

Trifolium variegatum f. major, commonly known as the variegated clover, is a cultivar of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, featuring white or light green markings on the leaves. This plant is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The variegated clover is often used in ornamental gardening and as a ground cover due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
