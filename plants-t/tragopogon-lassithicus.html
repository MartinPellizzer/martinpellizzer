Tragopogon lassithicus

Tragopogon lassithicus, commonly known as the Lassithi thistle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Crete in Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by spiny bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Tragopogon lassithicus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
