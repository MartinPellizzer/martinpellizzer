Talinum confusum

Talinum confusum, commonly known as red-rooted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Talinum confusum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
