Trigonella dicarpa

Trigonella dicarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Trigonella dicarpa is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
