Teucrium rhombifolium

Teucrium rhombifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically valued in herbal medicine.
