Thalictrum simplex subsp. galioides

Thalictrum simplex subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the western meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
