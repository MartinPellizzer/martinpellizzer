Tragacantha zohrabi

Tragacantha zohrabi, commonly known as Persian gum or zohrabi gum, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching shrub form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of woody pods containing small, rounded seeds. Its latex, known as tragacanth, is a viscous substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industrial applications.

Tragacantha zohrabi thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
