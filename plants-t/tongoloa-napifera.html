Tongoloa napifera

Tongoloa napifera, commonly known as the Tongan breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the islands of Tonga.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and edible, starchy fruit. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is well adapted to the humid, coastal climates of the Pacific. Its fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is a staple food source in traditional Tongan cuisine.

The plant also plays a significant role in the cultural and ecological landscape of the region.
