Toona hexandra var. pubinervis

Toona hexandra var. pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined toona, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves with hairy veins, which give it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
