Thymus pycnotrichus

Thymus pycnotrichus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, dark purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its fragrant properties.
