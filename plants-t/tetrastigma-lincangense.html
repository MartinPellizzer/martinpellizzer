Tetrastigma lincangense

Tetrastigma lincangense, commonly known as the Lincang crossvine, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
