Tanacetopsis popovii

Tanacetopsis popovii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It is often found in mountainous areas of Siberia and northern China.
