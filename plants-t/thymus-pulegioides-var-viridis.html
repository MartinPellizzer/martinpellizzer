Thymus pulegioides var. viridis

Thymus pulegioides var. viridis, commonly known as green thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its popularity in both cooking and herbal medicine.

The variety 'viridis' is distinguished by its more intensely green foliage compared to other thyme species.
