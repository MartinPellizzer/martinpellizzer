Tulipa fulgens

Tulipa fulgens, commonly known as the fire tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in late spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blossoms that are borne on sturdy stems, often reaching heights of up to 30 inches. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Tulipa fulgens is a perennial bulb plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
