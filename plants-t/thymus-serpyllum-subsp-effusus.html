Thymus serpyllum subsp. effusus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

effusus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia. It features small, fragrant leaves and flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer.

The plant has a dense, mat-like growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover in rocky or gravelly soils. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

It is well adapted to sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in dry meadows and along pathways.
