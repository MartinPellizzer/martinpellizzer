Thymus albanus subsp. magellensis

Thymus albanus subsp.

magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological features compared to other thyme species.
