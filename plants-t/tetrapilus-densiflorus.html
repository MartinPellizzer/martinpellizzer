Tetrapilus densiflorus

Tetrapilus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered tetrapilus, is a unique plant species characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, rough texture, and its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are arranged in compact inflorescences, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
