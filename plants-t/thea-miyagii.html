Thea miyagii

Thea miyagii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Thea miyagii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Japanese gardens.
