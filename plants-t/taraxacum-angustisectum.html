Taraxacum angustisectum

Taraxacum angustisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinct, sharp serration. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum angustisectum is a perennial herb that is widespread across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
