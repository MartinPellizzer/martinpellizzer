Tricyrtis hirta var. saxicola

Tricyrtis hirta var. saxicola, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
