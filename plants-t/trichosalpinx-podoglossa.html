Trichosalpinx podoglossa

Trichosalpinx podoglossa, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped form. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a tubular throat that narrows into a tail-like structure.

Trichosalpinx podoglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
