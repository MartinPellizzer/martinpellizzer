Tragopogon × miscellus

Tragopogon × miscellus, commonly known as the London dandelion, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Tragopogon dubius and Tragopogon porrifolius.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and long, slender stems. The plant is native to Europe but has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

Tragopogon × miscellus is often found in urban environments, such as along roadsides and in vacant lots.
