Toulicia subsquamulata

Toulicia subsquamulata, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Toulicia subsquamulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
