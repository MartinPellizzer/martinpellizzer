Taraxacum sinulosum

Taraxacum sinulosum, commonly known as the Sinuous Dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to southern China, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, which gives it its specific epithet.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers and foliage.
