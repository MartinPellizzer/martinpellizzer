Trymalium billardierei var. densetomentosa

Trymalium billardierei var. densetomentosa, commonly known as the dense-tomentose lichenwood, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
