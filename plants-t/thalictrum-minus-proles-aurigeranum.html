Thalictrum minus proles aurigeranum

Thalictrum minus proles aurigeranum, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stolons that help in vegetative propagation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
