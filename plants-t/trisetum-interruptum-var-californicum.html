Trisetum interruptum var. californicum

Trisetum interruptum var. californicum, commonly known as California basketgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant gardens.
