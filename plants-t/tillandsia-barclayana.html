Tillandsia barclayana

Tillandsia barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia barclayana is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
