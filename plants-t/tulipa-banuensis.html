Tulipa banuensis

Tulipa banuensis, commonly known as the Banuensis tulip, is a rare and ornamental species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa, which includes a variety of bulbous flowering plants. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils, and it prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
