Tropaeolum brachyceras var. grandiflorum

Tropaeolum brachyceras var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant everlasting sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, round, spiky seed pods that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The foliage is typically lobed and can be hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
