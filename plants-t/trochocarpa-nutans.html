Trochocarpa nutans

Trochocarpa nutans, commonly known as the nodding cupfruit, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is typically consumed when fully ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Trochocarpa nutans is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
