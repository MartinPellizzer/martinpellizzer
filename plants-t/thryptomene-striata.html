Thryptomene striata

Thryptomene striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Thryptomene striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
