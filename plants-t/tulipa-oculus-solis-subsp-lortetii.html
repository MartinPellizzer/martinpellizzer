Tulipa oculus-solis subsp. lortetii

Tulipa oculus-solis subsp.

lortetii, commonly known as the Lortet's tulip, is a rare and striking member of the tulip genus native to Turkey. This subspecies is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that display a striking red-orange hue with a yellow center.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
