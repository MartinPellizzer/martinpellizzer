Tithonia diversifolia

Tithonia diversifolia, commonly known as the凤仙花 or touch-me-not, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
