Taraxacum submucronatum

Taraxacum submucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which are typically yellow in color. This species is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.

It is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
