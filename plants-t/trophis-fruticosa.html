Trophis fruticosa

Trophis fruticosa, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

The plant contains milky sap, which has been used traditionally for various medicinal purposes.
