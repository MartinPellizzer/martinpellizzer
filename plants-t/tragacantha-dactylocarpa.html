Tragacantha dactylocarpa

Tragacantha dactylocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

The sap of the plant is also valued for its gum content, which has various industrial applications.
