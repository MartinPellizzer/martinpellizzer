Tournesol castaneifolia

Tournesol castaneifolia, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens across Europe and North America.
