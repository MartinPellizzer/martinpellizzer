Trochera ovata

Trochera ovata, commonly known as the oval-tongue orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated petals that resemble a tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Trochera ovata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
