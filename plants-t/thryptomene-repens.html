Thryptomene repens

Thryptomene repens, commonly known as creeping thryptomene, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Thryptomene repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
