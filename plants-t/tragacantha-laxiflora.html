Tragacantha laxiflora

Tragacantha laxiflora, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of pods containing seeds. Its roots are known for producing a valuable gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a binder in various products.

Tragacantha laxiflora is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep root systems and a slow growth rate.
