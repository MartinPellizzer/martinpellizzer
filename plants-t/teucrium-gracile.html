Teucrium gracile

Teucrium gracile, commonly known as slender germander, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
