Trachyandra saltii var. secunda

Trachyandra saltii var. secunda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trachyandra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color and a textured surface. It produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
