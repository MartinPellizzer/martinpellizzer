Talauma rumphii

Talauma rumphii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, shaded environments.

Talauma rumphii thrives in tropical rainforests and requires high humidity and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
