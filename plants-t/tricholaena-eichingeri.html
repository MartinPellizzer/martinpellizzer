Tricholaena eichingeri

Tricholaena eichingeri, commonly known as the Eichinger's papyrus, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
