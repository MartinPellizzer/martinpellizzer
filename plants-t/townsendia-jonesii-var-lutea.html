Townsendia jonesii var. lutea

Townsendia jonesii var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky, open habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
