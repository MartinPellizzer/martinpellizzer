Tessaria integrifolia var. ambigua

Tessaria integrifolia var. ambigua, commonly known as the broad-leafed tessaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and entire along their edges. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually yellow or white in color. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is often found in grassy areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
