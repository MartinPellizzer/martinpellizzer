Tridentopsis

Tridentopsis, commonly known as the three-toothed cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry climates. Tridentopsis has a compact growth habit with multiple stems that often branch out at the base.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
