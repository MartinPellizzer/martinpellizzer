Taraxacum leptolobum

Taraxacum leptolobum, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in spring. The plant is native to parts of North America and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and produces a distinctive seed head with pappus-equipped seeds for wind dispersal.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
