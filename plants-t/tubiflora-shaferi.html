Tubiflora shaferi

Tubiflora shaferi, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has adapted to survive in dry environments with deep root systems and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus Tubiflora is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its ornamental value in xeriscaped gardens.
