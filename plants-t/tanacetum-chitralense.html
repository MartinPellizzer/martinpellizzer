Tanacetum chitralense

Tanacetum chitralense, commonly known as the Chitral tansy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Pakistan and northern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Tanacetum chitralense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
