Tribulus longipetalus var. macropterus

Tribulus longipetalus var. macropterus, commonly known as the large-petaled Tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with elongated petals, which are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
