Tagetes porophyllum

Tagetes porophyllum, commonly known as Mexican tansy, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tagetes porophyllum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
