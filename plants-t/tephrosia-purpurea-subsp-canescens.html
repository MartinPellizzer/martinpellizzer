Tephrosia purpurea subsp. canescens

Tephrosia purpurea subsp.

canescens, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Tephrosia purpurea subsp.

canescens is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of leguminous forage.
