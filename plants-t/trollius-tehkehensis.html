Trollius tehkehensis

Trollius tehkehensis, commonly known as the Karakorum globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Trollius tehkehensis is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
