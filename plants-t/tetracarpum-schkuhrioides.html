Tetracarpum schkuhrioides

Tetracarpum schkuhrioides, commonly known as the Schkuhria-like tetracarpum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and produces composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically features linear to lanceolate leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is native to regions of Central Asia and has adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of local flora and its potential ecological value in supporting pollinators.
