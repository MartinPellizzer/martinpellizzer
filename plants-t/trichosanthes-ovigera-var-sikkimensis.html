Trichosanthes ovigera var. sikkimensis

Trichosanthes ovigera var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The fruit is typically oblong, with a thick, bitter rind and a sweet, fleshy interior. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
