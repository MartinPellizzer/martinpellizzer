Tragia acalyphoides

Tragia acalyphoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Tragia acalyphoides is also known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
