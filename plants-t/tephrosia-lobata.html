Tephrosia lobata

Tephrosia lobata, commonly known as the broadleaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces pods containing small, hard seeds, and it is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Tephrosia lobata is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
