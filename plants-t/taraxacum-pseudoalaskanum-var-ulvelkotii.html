Taraxacum pseudoalaskanum var. ulvelkotii

Taraxacum pseudoalaskanum var. ulvelkotii, commonly known as the Alaskan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a slender, hollow stem that supports a single, yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. Its flowers develop into a distinctive, spherical seed head with parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subarctic environments.
