Tylanthus gracilis

Tylanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender tylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in the spring.

Tylanthus gracilis is characterized by its adaptability to moist environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
