Tulipa maculata

Tulipa maculata, commonly known as the spotted tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the family Liliaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with distinctive dark spots on the petals. Its bulbs are perennial, and it is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Tulipa maculata is valued for its early spring blooms and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
