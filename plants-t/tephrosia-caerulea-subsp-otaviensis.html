Tephrosia caerulea subsp. otaviensis

Tephrosia caerulea subsp.

otaviensis, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa. It features compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine, where it is used for treating various ailments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant's ornamental value is also appreciated due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
