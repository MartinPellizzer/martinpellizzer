Tragopogon donetzicus

Tragopogon donetzicus, commonly known as the Donetsky thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Donets Basin in eastern Ukraine.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tragopogon donetzicus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important species in ecological studies of plant adaptation.
