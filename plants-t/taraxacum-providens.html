Taraxacum providens

Taraxacum providens, commonly known as the Brazilian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive seed heads. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Taraxacum providens is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
