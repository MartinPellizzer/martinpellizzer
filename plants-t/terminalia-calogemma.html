Terminalia calogemma

Terminalia calogemma, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
