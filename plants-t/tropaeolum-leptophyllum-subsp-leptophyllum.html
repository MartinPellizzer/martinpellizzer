Tropaeolum leptophyllum subsp. leptophyllum

Tropaeolum leptophyllum subsp.

leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America. It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, spurred flowers.

The plant produces small, colorful flowers that range from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and can root at the nodes.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
