Tulipa biebersteiniana

Tulipa biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Siberian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Tulipa biebersteiniana is valued for its ornamental value and hardiness in colder climates.
