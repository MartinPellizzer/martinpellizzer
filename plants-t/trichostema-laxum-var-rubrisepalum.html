Trichostema laxum var. rubrisepalum

Trichostema laxum var. rubrisepalum, commonly known as the red-sepal larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, with distinctive red sepals that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
