Tabebuia pentaphylla var. normalis

Tabebuia pentaphylla var. normalis, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a spreading crown, with dark green, elliptical leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
