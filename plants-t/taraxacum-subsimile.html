Taraxacum subsimile

Taraxacum subsimile, commonly known as the similar dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, inconspicuous yellow flower head. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
