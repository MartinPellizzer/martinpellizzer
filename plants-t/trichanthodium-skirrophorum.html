Trichanthodium skirrophorum

Trichanthodium skirrophorum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, green, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in alpine or mountainous environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry, well-drained conditions.
