Thea sasanqua var. oleosa

Thea sasanqua var. oleosa, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a variant of the camellia plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in autumn. The plant produces small, oily seeds that are rich in essential oils, making it valuable for both ornamental and industrial purposes. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

The variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to withstand colder temperatures compared to other camellia species.
