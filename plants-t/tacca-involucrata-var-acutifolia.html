Tacca involucrata var. acutifolia

Tacca involucrata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-black bracts that resemble the wings of a bat, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the base of the bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
