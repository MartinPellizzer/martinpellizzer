Tacsonia laevis

Tacsonia laevis, commonly known as the smooth tacsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Tacsonia laevis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
