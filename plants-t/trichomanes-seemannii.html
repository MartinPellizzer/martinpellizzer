Trichomanes seemannii

Trichomanes seemannii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically silvery-white in color due to the presence of trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Trichomanes seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
