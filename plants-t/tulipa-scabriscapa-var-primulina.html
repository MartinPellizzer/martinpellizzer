Tulipa scabriscapa var. primulina

Tulipa scabriscapa var. primulina, commonly known as the primrose tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like foliage and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, often before many other tulip varieties. This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and dry soils due to its drought tolerance.

It is valued for its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
