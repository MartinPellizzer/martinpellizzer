Triodia hubbardii

Triodia hubbardii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spinifex, is a perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive drought conditions.

Triodia hubbardii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
