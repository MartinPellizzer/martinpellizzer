Thermopsis lanceolata subsp. sibirica

Thermopsis lanceolata subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia. It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in dense, yellowish-green foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
