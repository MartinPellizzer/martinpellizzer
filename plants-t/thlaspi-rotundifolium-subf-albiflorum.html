Thlaspi rotundifolium subf. albiflorum

Thlaspi rotundifolium subf.

albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in alpine or arctic environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in cold climates.
