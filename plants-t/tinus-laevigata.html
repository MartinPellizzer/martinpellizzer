Tinus laevigata

Tinus laevigata, commonly known as the white-flowered tinus, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Tinus laevigata is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its tolerance of salt spray.
