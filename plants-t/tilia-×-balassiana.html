Tilia × balassiana

Tilia × balassiana, commonly known as the small-leaf linden, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are smaller than those of its parent species, Tilia cordata and Tilia europaea. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia × balassiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.
