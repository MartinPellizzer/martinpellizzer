Taraxacum speciosum

Taraxacum speciosum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head, which consists of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem with milky sap and has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum speciosum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
