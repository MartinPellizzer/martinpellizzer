Taraxacum uzunoglui

Taraxacum uzunoglui, commonly known as the Turkish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flowering head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum uzunoglui is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its potential health benefits.
