Tapeinochilos teysmannianus

Tapeinochilos teysmannianus, commonly known as the Teyssmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
