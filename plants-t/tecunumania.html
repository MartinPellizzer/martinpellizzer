Tecunumania

Tecunumania, commonly known as the sacred tree or the tree of life, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are large, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters. The tree produces a unique fruit with a thick, spiky exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It holds cultural and spiritual significance in the traditions of the K'iche' people of Guatemala.
