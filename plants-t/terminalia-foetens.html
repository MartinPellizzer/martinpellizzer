Terminalia foetens

Terminalia foetens, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in savannas and forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
