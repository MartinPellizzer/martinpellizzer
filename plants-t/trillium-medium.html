Trillium medium

Trillium medium, commonly known as the wake-robin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features three large, waxy, greenish-white petals and three sepals that are often a different color, typically red or pink. The plant grows from a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, showy flower in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three broad, elliptical leaflets.

Trillium medium is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
