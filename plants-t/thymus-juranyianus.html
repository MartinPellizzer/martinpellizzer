Thymus juranyianus

Thymus juranyianus, commonly known as Juranyi's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is highly aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus juranyianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
