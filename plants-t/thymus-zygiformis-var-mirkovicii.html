Thymus zygiformis var. mirkovicii

Thymus zygiformis var. mirkovicii, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features compact, upright stems covered in small, grayish-green leaves that are aromatic and fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a culinary herb.
