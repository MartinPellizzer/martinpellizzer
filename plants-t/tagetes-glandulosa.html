Tagetes glandulosa

Tagetes glandulosa, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, often aromatic scent. Tagetes glandulosa produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
