Trifurcia caerulea

Trifurcia caerulea, commonly known as the blue-leafed sapphire, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, glossy bark and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, which contributes to its unique appearance.

Trifurcia caerulea thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
