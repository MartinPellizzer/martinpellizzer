Tabebuia chrysantha subsp. meridionalis

Tabebuia chrysantha subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy with palmate leaves. Its bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
