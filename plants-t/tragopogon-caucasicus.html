Tragopogon caucasicus

Tragopogon caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Tragopogon caucasicus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
