Tulipa sylvestris var. biebersteiniana

Tulipa sylvestris var. biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Siberian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers have a subtle fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
