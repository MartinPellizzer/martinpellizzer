Thelocactus hexaedrophorus var. labouretianus

Thelocactus hexaedrophorus var. labouretianus, commonly known as the Labouret's thorned cactus, is a rare and unique cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
