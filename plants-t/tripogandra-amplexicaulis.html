Tripogandra amplexicaulis

Tripogandra amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping stem orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique growth habit, where the stems wrap around supporting structures. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of orchids. Its leaves are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests.
