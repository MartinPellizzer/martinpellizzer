Teucrium siculum subsp. euganeum

Teucrium siculum subsp.

euganeum, commonly known as the Euganean Teucrium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
