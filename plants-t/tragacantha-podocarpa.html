Tragacantha podocarpa

Tragacantha podocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its thick, fissured bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds.

Its latex, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent in various applications.
