Tormentilla tuberosa

Tormentilla tuberosa, commonly known as the meadow saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The roots of Tormentilla tuberosa are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
