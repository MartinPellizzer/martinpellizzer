Thymus × heribaudii

Thymus × heribaudii, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.
