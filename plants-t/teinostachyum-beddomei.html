Teinostachyum beddomei

Teinostachyum beddomei, commonly known as the beddome's grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Sri Lanka. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, leafy culms. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often bristly or hairy.

Teinostachyum beddomei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and adaptability to various soil types.
