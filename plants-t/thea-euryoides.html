Thea euryoides

Thea euryoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed thea, is a rare and lesser-known plant species belonging to the Theaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to specific regions in East Asia.
