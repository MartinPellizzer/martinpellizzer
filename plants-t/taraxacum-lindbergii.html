Taraxacum lindbergii

Taraxacum lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive white pappus for wind dispersal. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
