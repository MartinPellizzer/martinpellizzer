Tricera vaccinioides

Tricera vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has dense, needle-like leaves that provide year-round foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tricera vaccinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form thickets in suitable conditions.
