Thlaspidium hieracifolium

Thlaspidium hieracifolium, commonly known as the hairy leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
