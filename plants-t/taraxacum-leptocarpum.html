Taraxacum leptocarpum

Taraxacum leptocarpum, commonly known as the slender-fruited dandelion, is a flowering plant species in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, elongated seed head. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native environment.
