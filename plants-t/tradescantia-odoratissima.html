Tradescantia odoratissima

Tradescantia odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet flag or fragrant spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its fragrant blossoms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
