Tolpis umbellata var. minor

Tolpis umbellata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser umbrella grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
