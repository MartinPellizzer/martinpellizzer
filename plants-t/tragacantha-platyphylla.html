Tragacantha platyphylla

Tragacantha platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaved tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and oval, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Tragacantha platyphylla is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
