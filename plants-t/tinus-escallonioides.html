Tinus escallonioides

Tinus escallonioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as a ornamental in gardens due to its attractive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Tinus escallonioides is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, demonstrating drought tolerance.
