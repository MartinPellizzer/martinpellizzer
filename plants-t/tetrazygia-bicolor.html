Tetrazygia bicolor

Tetrazygia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring two contrasting colors on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Tetrazygia bicolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
