Turpinia pomifera var. nepalensis

Turpinia pomifera var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. These berries are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.
