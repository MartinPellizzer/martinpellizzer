Turbinicarpus saueri subsp. ysabelae

Turbinicarpus saueri subsp.

ysabelae, commonly known as the Ysabel's turbinated cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the rocky areas of southern Mexico. It features a globular to cylindrical stem that is typically greenish-gray in color, covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular species among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
