Teucrium campanulatum

Teucrium campanulatum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it is often found in meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Teucrium campanulatum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
