Tapinanthus ceciliae

Tapinanthus ceciliae, commonly known as the Cecilia's dodder, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to species like Euphorbia or Capparis. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure with numerous slender stems. It is native to arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host plants.
