Thymus glabrescens var. subaustriacus

Thymus glabrescens var. subaustriacus, commonly known as the Austrian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central Europe.

It features small, fragrant, dark green leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
