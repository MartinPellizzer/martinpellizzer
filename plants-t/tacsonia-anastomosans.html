Tacsonia anastomosans

Tacsonia anastomosans, commonly known as the forked bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, branched stems and small, bladder-like structures used to trap aquatic prey. The plant's leaves are usually submerged, while its flowering stems emerge above the water surface. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

The botanical features of Tacsonia anastomosans reflect its adaptation to aquatic habitats and its specialized method of obtaining nutrients through predation.
