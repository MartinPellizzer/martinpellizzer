Teucrium leucosceptrum

Teucrium leucosceptrum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Teucrium leucosceptrum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
