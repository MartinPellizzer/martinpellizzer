Tanacetum peucedanifolium

Tanacetum peucedanifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
