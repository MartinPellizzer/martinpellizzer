Trochera brevifolia

Trochera brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved trochera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of tiny, daisy-like flowers that are usually pale yellow to white in color. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
