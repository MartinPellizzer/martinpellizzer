Trichanthemis glabriflora

Trichanthemis glabriflora, commonly known as the smoothflower trichanthemis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
