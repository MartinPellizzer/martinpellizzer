Tradescantia angustifolia

Tradescantia angustifolia, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is known for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
