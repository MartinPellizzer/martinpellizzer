Telemachia trinitensis

Telemachia trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad star-of-Bahamas, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal or riverine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Telemachia trinitensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
