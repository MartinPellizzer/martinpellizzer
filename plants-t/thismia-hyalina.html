Thismia hyalina

Thismia hyalina, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant belonging to the Thismiaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that lacks chlorophyll and is therefore unable to perform photosynthesis. Instead, it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, forming a symbiotic relationship with its environment. The plant produces striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often mistaken for fungi due to their appearance.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist habitats and is prized for its unusual and delicate floral structure.
