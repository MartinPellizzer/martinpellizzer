Tetraphyle × hybrida

Tetraphyle × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid tetraphyle, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two distinct species of the Tetraphyle genus.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from its parent species, often displaying unique leaf structures and flower arrangements. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in temperate climates with high humidity. Its foliage is characterized by broad, glossy leaves with a deep green hue, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
