Trifolium vesiculosum subsp. aintabense

Trifolium vesiculosum subsp.

aintabense, commonly known as the Aintab clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
