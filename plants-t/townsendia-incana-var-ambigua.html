Townsendia incana var. ambigua

Townsendia incana var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous whiteweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.
