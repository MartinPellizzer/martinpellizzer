Tragacantha oocephala

Tragacantha oocephala, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Tragacantha oocephala is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
