Thymus serpylloides subsp. serpylloides

Thymus serpylloides subsp.

serpylloides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and densely packed along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
