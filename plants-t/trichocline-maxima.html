Trichocline maxima

Trichocline maxima, commonly known as the giant trichocline, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, providing a unique visual appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Trichocline maxima thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
