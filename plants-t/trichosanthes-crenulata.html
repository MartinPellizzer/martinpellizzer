Trichosanthes crenulata

Trichosanthes crenulata, commonly known as the snake gourd, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, warty fruits and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five lobes and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Trichosanthes crenulata is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits.
