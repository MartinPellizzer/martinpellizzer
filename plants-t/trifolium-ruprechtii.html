Trifolium ruprechtii

Trifolium ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments.

Trifolium ruprechtii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
