Tanaecium tetragonolobum

Tanaecium tetragonolobum, commonly known as the four-angled morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue, often blooming in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Tanaecium tetragonolobum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
