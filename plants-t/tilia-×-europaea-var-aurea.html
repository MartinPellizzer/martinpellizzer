Tilia × europaea var. aurea

Tilia × europaea var. aurea, commonly known as the golden linden, is a hybrid tree that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a brilliant yellow in the fall, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels and attract pollinators like bees. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to a variety of soil types and climates.
