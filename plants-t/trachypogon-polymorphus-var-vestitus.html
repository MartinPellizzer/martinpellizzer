Trachypogon polymorphus var. vestitus

Trachypogon polymorphus var. vestitus, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a variant of the slender grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a distinctive silver-gray sheen on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often hidden among the leaves, making it less conspicuous than other grass species.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique appearance.
