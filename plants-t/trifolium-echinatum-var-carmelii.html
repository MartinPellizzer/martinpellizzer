Trifolium echinatum var. carmelii

Trifolium echinatum var. carmelii, commonly known as the Carmel clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
