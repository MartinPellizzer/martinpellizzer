Tragopogon soltisiorum var. soltisiorum

Tragopogon soltisiorum var. soltisiorum, commonly known as the Soltis' goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple-pink center.

It is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Tragopogon species, contributing to the study of evolutionary botany.
