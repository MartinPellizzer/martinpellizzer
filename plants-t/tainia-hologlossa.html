Tainia hologlossa

Tainia hologlossa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a pale greenish-white color and intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Tainia hologlossa is considered a conservation concern.
