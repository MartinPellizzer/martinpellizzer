Tulipa uniflora

Tulipa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant typically blooms in spring, featuring cup-shaped petals in shades of red, yellow, or orange. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental plant in horticulture.
