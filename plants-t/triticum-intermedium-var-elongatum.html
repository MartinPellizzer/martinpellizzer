Triticum intermedium var. elongatum

Triticum intermedium var. elongatum, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass elongatum, is a perennial grass species native to the central and northern regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is valued for its hardiness, drought tolerance, and potential use in forage and conservation plantings.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
