Taraxacum purpurinerve

Taraxacum purpurinerve, commonly known as the purple-veined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the familiar fluffy seed heads. The leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually hairy and lobed. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its medicinal properties, which have been utilized in traditional herbal remedies.
