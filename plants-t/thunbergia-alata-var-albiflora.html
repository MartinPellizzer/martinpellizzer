Thunbergia alata var. albiflora

Thunbergia alata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.
