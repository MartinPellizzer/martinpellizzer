Thlaspi fischerianum

Thlaspi fischerianum, commonly known as Fischer's cabbageworm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, and its leaves are usually oval and covered in fine hairs. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role as a food source for certain insect species.
