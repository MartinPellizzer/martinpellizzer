Thymus × celtibericus nothosubsp. bonichensis

Thymus × celtibericus nothosubsp.

bonichensis, commonly known as the Iberian thyme, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
