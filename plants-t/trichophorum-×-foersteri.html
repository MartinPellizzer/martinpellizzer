Trichophorum × foersteri

Trichophorum × foersteri, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in three rows. This plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring or early summer. Trichophorum × foersteri is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
