Trichosanthes chingiana

Trichosanthes chingiana, commonly known as Chinese snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant bears elongated, pale green fruits that mature to a striking red-orange color. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash.
