Tipuana erythrocarpa

Tipuana erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tipuana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its distinctive red, fleshy fruits are a notable feature, often containing several seeds.

Tipuana erythrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
