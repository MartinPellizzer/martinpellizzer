Thelypteris cheilanthoides

Thelypteris cheilanthoides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that often have a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris cheilanthoides is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
