Tigridia molseediana

Tigridia molseediana, commonly known as the red tigridia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a deep green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia molseediana is known for its ability to bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a burst of color to gardens during the warmer months.
