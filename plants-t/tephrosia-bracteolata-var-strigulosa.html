Tephrosia bracteolata var. strigulosa

Tephrosia bracteolata var. strigulosa, commonly known as the woolly tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
