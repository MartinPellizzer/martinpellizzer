Tamus communis subsp. cretica

Tamus communis subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a compact, rosette-like growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
