Terminalia erythrophylla

Terminalia erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more prominent during the dry season. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
