Thalictrum ammophilum

Thalictrum ammophilum, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thalictrum ammophilum plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and provides habitat for various insects.
