Tapinanthus apodanthus

Tapinanthus apodanthus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a parasitic plant that lacks leaves, stems, and roots.

It relies entirely on its host for nutrients, attaching itself to the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, typically on woody plants.

Its unique life cycle and appearance make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
