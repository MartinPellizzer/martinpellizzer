Tounatea laurifolia

Tounatea laurifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, sweet-tasting berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Tounatea laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
