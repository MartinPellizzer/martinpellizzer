Thea sinensis var. parvifolia

Thea sinensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf tea plant, is a variant of the Camellia sinensis species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the standard Camellia sinensis. This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile in tea production. The leaves are typically harvested when they are young and tender to ensure optimal taste and aroma.

It is a popular choice among tea growers for its adaptability and high-quality yield.
