Thymus quinquecostatus var. asiaticus

Thymus quinquecostatus var. asiaticus, commonly known as the Persian thyme or Asiatic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, woody stems and small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
