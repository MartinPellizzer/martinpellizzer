Thalictrum prorepens

Thalictrum prorepens, commonly known as the creeping avens, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically basal and clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Thalictrum prorepens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
