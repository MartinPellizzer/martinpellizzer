Tetracera alnifolia subsp. alnifolia

Tetracera alnifolia subsp.

alnifolia, commonly known as the white sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a climbing shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces fleshy, yellowish berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
