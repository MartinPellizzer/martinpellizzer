Thesium divaricatum var. glaucum

Thesium divaricatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. The plant features linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
