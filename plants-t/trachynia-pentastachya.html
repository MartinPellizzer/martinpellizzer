Trachynia pentastachya

Trachynia pentastachya, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive purple or brownish coloration on the central disk.

Trachynia pentastachya is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
