Trapa colchica

Trapa colchica, commonly known as the water chestnut, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, often found in shallow freshwater habitats. The plant produces distinctive, floating leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces edible, starchy seeds.

Trapa colchica is also known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of such ecosystems.
