Trifolium rubicundum

Trifolium rubicundum, commonly known as the crimson clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in the spring. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium rubicundum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
