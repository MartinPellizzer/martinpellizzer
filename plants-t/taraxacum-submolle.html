Taraxacum submolle

Taraxacum submolle, commonly known as the softleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, puffy seed head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
