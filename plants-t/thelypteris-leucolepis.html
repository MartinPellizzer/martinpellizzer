Thelypteris leucolepis

Thelypteris leucolepis, commonly known as the silver frond fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing leaves.

Thelypteris leucolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
