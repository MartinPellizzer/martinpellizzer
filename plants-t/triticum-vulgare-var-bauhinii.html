Triticum vulgare var. bauhinii

Triticum vulgare var. bauhinii, commonly known as the bauhinii wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows as an annual plant with slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are narrow and parallel-veined, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers. Its botanical traits make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and horticultural research.
