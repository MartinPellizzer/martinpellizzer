Turnera fernandezii

Turnera fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez's turnera, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Turnera, which includes a variety of tropical and subtropical plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for seed production.
