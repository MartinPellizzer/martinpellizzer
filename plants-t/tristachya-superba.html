Tristachya superba

Tristachya superba, commonly known as the giant reed or golden reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and marshes.

Tristachya superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
