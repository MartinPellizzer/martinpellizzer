Thymus cariensis

Thymus cariensis, commonly known as the Carian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, compact foliage with gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, pink or lavender flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus cariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
