Tragopogon australis

Tragopogon australis, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Tragopogon australis is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in New Zealand's flora.
