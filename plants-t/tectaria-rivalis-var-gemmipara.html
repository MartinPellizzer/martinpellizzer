Tectaria rivalis var. gemmipara

Tectaria rivalis var. gemmipara, commonly known as the gemmipara shield fern, is a variant of the Tectaria rivalis species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, often producing gemmae—small, vegetative propagules—that allow for asexual reproduction. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

The fronds are usually dark green and may have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
