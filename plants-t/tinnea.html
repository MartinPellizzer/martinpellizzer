Tinnea

Tinnea, commonly known as the common tallowtree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. Tinnea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture.

They also play a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
