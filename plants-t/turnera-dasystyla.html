Turnera dasystyla

Turnera dasystyla, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, known for its showy, colorful inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
