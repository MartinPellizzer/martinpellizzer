Trifolium isodon

Trifolium isodon, commonly known as narrow-leaf clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium isodon is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
