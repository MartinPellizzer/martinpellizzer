Turpinia macrosperma

Turpinia macrosperma, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Turpinia macrosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
