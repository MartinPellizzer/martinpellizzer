Taraxacum kittilense

Taraxacum kittilense, commonly known as the Kittil Valley dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Scandinavia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments and is adapted to the short growing season of the Arctic tundra.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
