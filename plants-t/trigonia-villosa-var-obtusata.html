Trigonia villosa var. obtusata

Trigonia villosa var. obtusata, commonly known as the woolly trigonia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate in shape and have a rough texture.

Trigonia villosa var. obtusata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
