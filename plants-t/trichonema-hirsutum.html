Trichonema hirsutum

Trichonema hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy trichonema, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Trichonema hirsutum is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
