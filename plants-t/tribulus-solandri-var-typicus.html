Tribulus solandri var. typicus

Tribulus solandri var. typicus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
