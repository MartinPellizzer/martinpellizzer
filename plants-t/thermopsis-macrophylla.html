Thermopsis macrophylla

Thermopsis macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
