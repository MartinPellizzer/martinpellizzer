Thymus stribrnyi

Thymus stribrnyi, commonly known as the striated thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thymus stribrnyi is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species.
