Taraxacum balcanicum

Taraxacum balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces fluffy white seeds. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to survive in various soil conditions.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
