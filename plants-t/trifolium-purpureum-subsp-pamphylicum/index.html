Trifolium Purpureum Subsp. Pamphylicum

Trifolium purpureum subsp. pamphylicum is a rare and delicate species of clover found primarily in the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is distinguished by its small, purple flowers and trifoliate leaves, which give it a unique and charming appearance. This subspecies thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often growing in alpine meadows and open grasslands. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are essential to protect this plant and preserve its ecological role.
