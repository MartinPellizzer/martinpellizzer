Taeniochlaena polyneura

Taeniochlaena polyneura, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Marantaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and features striking, often variegated, lance-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in certain regions.
