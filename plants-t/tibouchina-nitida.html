Tibouchina nitida

Tibouchina nitida, commonly known as silverleaf tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tibouchina nitida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
