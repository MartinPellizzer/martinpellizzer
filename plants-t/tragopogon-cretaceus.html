Tragopogon cretaceus

Tragopogon cretaceus, commonly known as the Cretaceous thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces a distinctive seed head that resembles a small, spiky sphere.

Tragopogon cretaceus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
