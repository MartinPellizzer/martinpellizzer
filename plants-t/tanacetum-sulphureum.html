Tanacetum sulphureum

Tanacetum sulphureum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, spherical flower heads that are often clustered together.

Tanacetum sulphureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
