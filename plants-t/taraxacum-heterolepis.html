Taraxacum heterolepis

Taraxacum heterolepis, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Taraxacum. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are borne on hollow, leafless stems.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
