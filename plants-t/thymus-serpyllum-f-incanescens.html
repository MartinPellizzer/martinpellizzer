Thymus serpyllum f. incanescens

Thymus serpyllum f. incanescens, commonly known as the whitethorn thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features tiny, fragrant, pale green to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
