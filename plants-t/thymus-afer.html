Thymus afer

Thymus afer, commonly known as African thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus afer thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean and arid climates.
