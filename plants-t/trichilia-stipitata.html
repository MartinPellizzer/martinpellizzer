Trichilia stipitata

Trichilia stipitata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes many species with aromatic wood. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in axillary or terminal panicles, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.
