Trinia hoffmannii

Trinia hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's trinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Trinia hoffmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
