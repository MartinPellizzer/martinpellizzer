Taraxacum cambricum

Taraxacum cambricum, commonly known as the Welsh dandelion, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that disperses its seeds via wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum cambricum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
