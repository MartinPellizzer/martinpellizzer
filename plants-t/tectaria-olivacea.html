Tectaria olivacea

Tectaria olivacea, commonly known as the olive fern, is a species of epiphytic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are often divided into small, delicate segments. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nutrients. Its rhizomes are wiry and often covered in a dense, fibrous sheath.

Tectaria olivacea plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
