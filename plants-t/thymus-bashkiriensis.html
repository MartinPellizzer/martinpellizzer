Thymus bashkiriensis

Thymus bashkiriensis, commonly known as Bashkir thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their strong, minty aroma.

Thymus bashkiriensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
