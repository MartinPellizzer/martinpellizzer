Teucrium sieberi var. canescens

Teucrium sieberi var. canescens, commonly known as silver germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
