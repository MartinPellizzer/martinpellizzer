Trichocereus arboricola

Trichocereus arboricola, commonly known as the "Poker Plant," is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often branch out into a tree-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators. It has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect sunlight to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
