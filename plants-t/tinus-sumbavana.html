Tinus sumbavana

Tinus sumbavana, commonly known as the Madagascar pine, is a coniferous tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
