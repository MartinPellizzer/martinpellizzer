Tamarix jordanis

Tamarix jordanis, commonly known as Jordanian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater. It is often used for soil stabilization in desert areas due to its hardy nature.

Tamarix jordanis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional landscaping.
