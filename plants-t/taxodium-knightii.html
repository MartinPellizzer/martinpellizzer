Taxodium knightii

Taxodium knightii, commonly known as the Mexican bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature in the second year, and its leaves are scale-like, shedding annually. Taxodium knightii is closely related to the bald cypress (Taxodium distichum) but is distinguished by its unique ecological niche and morphology.

It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
