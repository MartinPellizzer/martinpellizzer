Taraxacum polylobum

Taraxacum polylobum, commonly known as the Canadian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports a cluster of yellow flowers. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
