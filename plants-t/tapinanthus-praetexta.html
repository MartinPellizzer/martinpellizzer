Tapinanthus praetexta

Tapinanthus praetexta, commonly known as the parasitic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a waxy coating.

Tapinanthus praetexta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
