Thalictrum sparsiflorum subsp. richardsonii

Thalictrum sparsiflorum subsp.

richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
