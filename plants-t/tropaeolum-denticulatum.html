Tropaeolum denticulatum

Tropaeolum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum denticulatum is also valued for its peppery flavor, often used in culinary applications.
