Terpsichore variabilis

Terpsichore variabilis, commonly known as the variable beautyflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored petals that range from deep violet to pale yellow, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Terpsichore variabilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
