Taraxacum murgabicum

Taraxacum murgabicum, commonly known as the Murgab dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions like Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

The species is noted for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
