Tricyrtis formosana var. grandiflora

Tricyrtis formosana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a striking member of the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer, featuring a distinctive white and purple coloration. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light conditions.
