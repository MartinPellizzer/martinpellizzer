Tamonea verbenacea

Tamonea verbenacea, commonly known as the creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Tamonea verbenacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
