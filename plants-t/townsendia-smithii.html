Townsendia smithii

Townsendia smithii, commonly known as Smith's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
