Trachymene humilis

Trachymene humilis, commonly known as the small false shamrock, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Trachymene humilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
