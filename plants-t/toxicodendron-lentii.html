Toxicodendron lentii

Toxicodendron lentii, commonly known as the Southern magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, cone-like structures called galls, which contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Toxicodendron lentii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in native ecosystems.
