Thymus praecox var. caespitosus

Thymus praecox var. caespitosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine.
