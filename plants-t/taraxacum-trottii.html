Taraxacum trottii

Taraxacum trottii, commonly known as the Trott's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow-orange flower head. The plant produces a distinctive capsule fruit that releases numerous wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

Taraxacum trottii is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
