Tabernaemontana semperflorens

Tabernaemontana semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom continuously throughout the year, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its aromatic, milky sap. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
