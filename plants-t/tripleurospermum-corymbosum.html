Tripleurospermum corymbosum

Tripleurospermum corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate or elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces. It produces yellow flowers in dense, corymbose inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
