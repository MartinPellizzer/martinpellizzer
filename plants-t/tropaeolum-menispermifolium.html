Tropaeolum menispermifolium

Tropaeolum menispermifolium, commonly known as the creeping wintercress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Tropaeolum menispermifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
