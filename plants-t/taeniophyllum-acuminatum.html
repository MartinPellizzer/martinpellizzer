Taeniophyllum acuminatum

Taeniophyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the slender staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic plant belonging to the Polypodiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features a single, fleshy, spore-bearing sorus located on the underside of its fronds. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
