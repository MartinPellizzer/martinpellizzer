Trichocentrum micranthum

Trichocentrum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to thrive in high-altitude cloud forests, where it relies on rainwater for moisture.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful cultivation to flourish in non-native settings.
