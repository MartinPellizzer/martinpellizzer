Tetrapterys langsdorffiana

Tetrapterys langsdorffiana, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
