Tulipa intermedia var. korolkowioides

Tulipa intermedia var. korolkowioides, commonly known as the Korolkovii tulip, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and produces large, strap-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
