Tinopsis apiculata

Tinopsis apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, sprawling habit, characterized by its narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Tinopsis apiculata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
