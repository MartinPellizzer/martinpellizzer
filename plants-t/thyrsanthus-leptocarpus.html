Thyrsanthus leptocarpus

Thyrsanthus leptocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruited thyrsothyrse, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters called thyrses, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Thyrsanthus leptocarpus is typically found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
