Tetranthera nuculanea

Tetranthera nuculanea, commonly known as the golden bower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Tetranthera nuculanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
