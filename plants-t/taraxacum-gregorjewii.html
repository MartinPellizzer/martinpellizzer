Taraxacum gregorjewii

Taraxacum gregorjewii, commonly known as the Gregorjew's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to Central Asia, this species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a taproot system and is adapted to survive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
