Trichocereus tetracanthus var. bolivianus

Trichocereus tetracanthus var. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian torch cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the night, a trait common in many cacti adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
