Tussilago exscapa

Tussilago exscapa, commonly known as the autumnal aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Tussilago exscapa plays an important role in supporting pollinators during the late season.
