Taraxacum brevicorne

Taraxacum brevicorne, commonly known as the short-horned dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and a short, stout stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into fluffy, white seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
