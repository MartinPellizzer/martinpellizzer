Trevia tricuspidata

Trevia tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-pointed trevia, is a unique flowering plant native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tri-lobed leaves that shimmer with a metallic sheen under sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and highly adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in swampy environments.

Trevia tricuspidata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
