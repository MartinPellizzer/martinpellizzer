Thamnochortus ellipticus

Thamnochortus ellipticus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features dense, gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in tight, compact inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Thamnochortus ellipticus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
