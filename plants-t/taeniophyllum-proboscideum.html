Taeniophyllum proboscideum

Taeniophyllum proboscideum, commonly known as the giant rat-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and a single, elongated leaf that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
