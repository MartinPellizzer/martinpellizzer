Trichopteryx barbata

Trichopteryx barbata, commonly known as the hairy tailed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in humid environments.

Trichopteryx barbata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
