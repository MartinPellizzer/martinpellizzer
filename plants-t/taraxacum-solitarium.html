Taraxacum solitarium

Taraxacum solitarium, commonly known as the solitary dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a single, elongated stem that bears a bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

This species is typically perennial and has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.
