Trianthema triquetrum subsp. parvifolium

Trianthema triquetrum subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved triquetrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the warmer months.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
