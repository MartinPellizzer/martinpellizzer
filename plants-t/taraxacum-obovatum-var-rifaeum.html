Taraxacum obovatum var. rifaeum

Taraxacum obovatum var. rifaeum, commonly known as Rif dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features a rosette of broadly ovate, waxy, and often hairy leaves that are bluish-green in color. The plant produces a tall, hollow stem topped with a single, bright yellow flower head composed of numerous small florets. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky, or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
