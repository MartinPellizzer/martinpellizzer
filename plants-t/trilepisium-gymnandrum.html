Trilepisium gymnandrum

Trilepisium gymnandrum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Trilepisium gymnandrum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in some areas.
