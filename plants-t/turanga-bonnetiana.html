Turanga bonnetiana

Turanga bonnetiana, commonly known as the golden bonnet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive hooded shape, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Turanga bonnetiana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
