Themeda minor

Themeda minor, commonly known as slender grass or small tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, heathlands, and forest margins. The plant has slender, erect stems that form dense tussocks, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Themeda minor plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
