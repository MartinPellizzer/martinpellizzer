Taraxacum ohritense

Taraxacum ohritense, commonly known as the Ohrit dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a distinctive seed ball with white pappus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
