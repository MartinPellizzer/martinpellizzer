Trichosalpinx moschata

Trichosalpinx moschata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trichosalpinx moschata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
