Thalictrum minus subsp. buschianum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

buschianum, commonly known as the Busch's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces numerous slender, elongated stems that bear its showy floral display.
