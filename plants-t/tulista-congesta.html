Tulista congesta

Tulista congesta, commonly known as the clustered tulip, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates and deciduous in colder regions. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and are often used in horticultural displays.

Tulista congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
