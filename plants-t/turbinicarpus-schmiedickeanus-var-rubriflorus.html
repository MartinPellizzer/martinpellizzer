Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. rubriflorus

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red flowers during the spring, which are often borne on short, erect pedicels. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

This variety is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique coloration and compact growth habit.
