Tephrosia coroniliifolia

Tephrosia coroniliifolia, commonly known as the Crownleaf Tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Tephrosia coroniliifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
