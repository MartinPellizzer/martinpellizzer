Teucrium polium var. tetuanense

Teucrium polium var. tetuanense, commonly known as the Tetuan thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
