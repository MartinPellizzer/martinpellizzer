Taraxacum apiculatoides

Taraxacum apiculatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, spherical seed ball. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

It is often considered a weed but also has historical uses in herbal medicine.
