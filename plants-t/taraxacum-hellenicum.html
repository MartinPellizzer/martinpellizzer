Taraxacum hellenicum

Taraxacum hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
