Tulipa scythica

Tulipa scythica, commonly known as the Siberian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats.

Tulipa scythica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
