Tragacantha macroloba

Tragacantha macroloba, commonly known as the large-leaf tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a viscous gum from its stems, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
