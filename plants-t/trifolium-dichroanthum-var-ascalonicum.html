Trifolium dichroanthum var. ascalonicum

Trifolium dichroanthum var. ascalonicum, commonly known as the Ascalonic clover, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early spring. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with soil bacteria.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
