Typha ehrenbergii

Typha ehrenbergii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of two distinct parts: a brown, hairy section at the base and a yellow, fluffy top. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing up to 1.5 meters in height.

Typha ehrenbergii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
