Tigridia illecebrosa

Tigridia illecebrosa, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces showy, spurred blooms that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia illecebrosa is known for its ability to self-seed and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
