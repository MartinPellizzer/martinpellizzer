Thymus serpyllum subsp. pfaffianus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

pfaffianus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant has small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are pink to violet and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense mats, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.
