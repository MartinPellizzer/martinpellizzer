Tragacantha leucacantha

Tragacantha leucacantha, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a gummy substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food additive. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting various wildlife species.
