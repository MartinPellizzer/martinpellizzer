Triticum compactum var. baladseetense

Triticum compactum var. baladseetense, commonly known as the Baladseet wild wheat, is a botanical species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its grains are small and have a distinctive morphology, making it of interest to researchers studying ancient agricultural practices.

This variety is considered a wild relative of modern wheat, offering potential genetic resources for breeding programs.
