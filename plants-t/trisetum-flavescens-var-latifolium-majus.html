Trisetum flavescens var. latifolium-majus

Trisetum flavescens var. latifolium-majus, commonly known as the broadleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender spikes of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable forage plant for wildlife.
