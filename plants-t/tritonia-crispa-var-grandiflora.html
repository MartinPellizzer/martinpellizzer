Tritonia crispa var. grandiflora

Tritonia crispa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Tritonia crispa var. grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
