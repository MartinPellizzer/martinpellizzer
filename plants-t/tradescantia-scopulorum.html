Tradescantia scopulorum

Tradescantia scopulorum, commonly known as the rock spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky outcrop habitats. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of stems.

Tradescantia scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade to full sun.
