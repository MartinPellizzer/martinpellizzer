Thunbergia kangeanensis

Thunbergia kangeanensis, commonly known as the Kangean moonvine, is a flowering plant native to the Kangean Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow blooms that are often bioluminescent in the dark, a unique trait among its relatives. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and covered in fine hairs.

Thunbergia kangeanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its striking nighttime beauty.
