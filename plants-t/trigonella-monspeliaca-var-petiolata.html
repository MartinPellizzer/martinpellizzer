Trigonella monspeliaca var. petiolata

Trigonella monspeliaca var. petiolata, commonly known as the petiolate fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pubescent and have elongated petioles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into slender, hairy pods containing several seeds. It is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in such conditions.
