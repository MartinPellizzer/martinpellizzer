Triticum squarrosum subvar. macrostachyum

Triticum squarrosum subvar. macrostachyum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are dry or disturbed. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
