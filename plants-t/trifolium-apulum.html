Trifolium apulum

Trifolium apulum, commonly known as the lesser clover, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Trifolium apulum is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant for soil enrichment.
