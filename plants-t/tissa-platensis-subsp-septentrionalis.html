Tissa platensis subsp. septentrionalis

Tissa platensis subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern tissa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, blooming in late spring to early summer. Tissa platensis subsp.

septentrionalis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
