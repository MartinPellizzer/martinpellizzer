Trifolium dasyphyllum

Trifolium dasyphyllum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

Trifolium dasyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
