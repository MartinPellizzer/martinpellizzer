Tragacantha pelligera

Tragacantha pelligera, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the formation of distinctive seed pods. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The sap of Tragacantha pelligera is used to produce a gum that has various industrial and medicinal applications.
