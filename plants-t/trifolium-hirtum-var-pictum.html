Trifolium hirtum var. pictum

Trifolium hirtum var. pictum, commonly known as the painted clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often adorned with red or purple markings, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil.
