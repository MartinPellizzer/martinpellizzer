Tulipa sylvestris var. turkestanica

Tulipa sylvestris var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan wild tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive black eye. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
