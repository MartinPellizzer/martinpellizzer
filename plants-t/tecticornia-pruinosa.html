Tecticornia pruinosa

Tecticornia pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tecticornia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in poor soils and withstand extreme temperatures.

Tecticornia pruinosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
