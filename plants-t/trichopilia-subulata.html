Trichopilia subulata

Trichopilia subulata, commonly known as the slender star-pear, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Trichopilia subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
