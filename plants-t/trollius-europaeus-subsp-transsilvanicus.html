Trollius europaeus subsp. transsilvanicus

Trollius europaeus subsp.

transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream sides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy, adding to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
