Tragia pogostemonoides

Tragia pogostemonoides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which are typical of many species in the genus Tragia. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to different habitats, contributing to its widespread distribution.
