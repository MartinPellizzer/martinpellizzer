Trichosalpinx aequatorialis

Trichosalpinx aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Trichosalpinx aequatorialis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique and showy floral display.
