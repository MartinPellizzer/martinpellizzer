Taxandria floribunda

Taxandria floribunda, commonly known as the showy taxandria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are borne in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Taxandria floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual interest.
