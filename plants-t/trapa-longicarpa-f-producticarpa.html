Trapa longicarpa f. producticarpa

Trapa longicarpa f. producticarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain edible seeds, which are harvested for food. Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This aquatic plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.
