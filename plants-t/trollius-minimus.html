Trollius minimus

Trollius minimus, commonly known as lesser goldenroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Trollius minimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
