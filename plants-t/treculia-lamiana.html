Treculia lamiana

Treculia lamiana, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and large, compound leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that are used in local cuisines. Its latex is also utilized in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
