Tacinga funalis var. atropurpurea

Tacinga funalis var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple tacinga, is a variant of the tacinga plant characterized by its distinctive purplish foliage.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking coloration.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
