Tragacantha grata

Tragacantha grata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a sticky resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture to reduce water loss.

Tragacantha grata is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
