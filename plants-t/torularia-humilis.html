Torularia humilis

Torularia humilis, commonly known as the common torula, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple structure and is part of the family Poaceae, which includes grasses.
