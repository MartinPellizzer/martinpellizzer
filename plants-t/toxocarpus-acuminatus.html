Toxocarpus acuminatus

Toxocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinct, narrow shape and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or blue. Its seed pods are slender and elongated, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
