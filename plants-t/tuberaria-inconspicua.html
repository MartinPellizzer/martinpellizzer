Tuberaria inconspicua

Tuberaria inconspicua, commonly known as the common tuberous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in open woodlands or along coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring.

Tuberaria inconspicua is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
