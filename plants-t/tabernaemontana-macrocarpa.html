Tabernaemontana macrocarpa

Tabernaemontana macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody, spiny fruits that contain bright red seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
