Thymus villosus var. lobatus

Thymus villosus var. lobatus, commonly known as the woolly thyme with lobed leaves, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaping and herbal gardens.
