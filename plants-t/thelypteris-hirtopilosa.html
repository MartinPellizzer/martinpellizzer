Thelypteris hirtopilosa

Thelypteris hirtopilosa, commonly known as the hairy-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in short, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris hirtopilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
