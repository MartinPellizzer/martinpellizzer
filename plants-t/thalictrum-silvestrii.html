Thalictrum silvestrii

Thalictrum silvestrii, commonly known as the wild meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Thalictrum silvestrii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
