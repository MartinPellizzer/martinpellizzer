Trifolium bourgaei

Trifolium bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgae's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Trifolium bourgaei is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
