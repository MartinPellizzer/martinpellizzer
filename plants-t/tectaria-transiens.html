Tectaria transiens

Tectaria transiens, commonly known as the wandering shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a broad, shield-shaped base and elongated, lobed pinnae. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Tectaria transiens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of tropical and subtropical habitats.
