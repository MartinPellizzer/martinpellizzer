Tetrapterys ambigua var. paraguayensis

Tetrapterys ambigua var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant also plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
