Tagetes argentina

Tagetes argentina, commonly known as the Argentine marigold, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural pest repellent in agricultural settings.
