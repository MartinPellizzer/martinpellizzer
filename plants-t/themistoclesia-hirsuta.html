Themistoclesia hirsuta

Themistoclesia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale purple ray florets.
