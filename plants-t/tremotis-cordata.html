Tremotis cordata

Tremotis cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring and early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
