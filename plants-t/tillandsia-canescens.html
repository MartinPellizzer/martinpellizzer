Tillandsia canescens

Tillandsia canescens, commonly known as the silver vellum tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white trichomes. These trichomes help the plant absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
