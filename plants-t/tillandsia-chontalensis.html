Tillandsia chontalensis

Tillandsia chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontal pineapple plant, is a species of air plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
