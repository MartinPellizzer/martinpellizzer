Trichocereus chiloensis subsp. australis

Trichocereus chiloensis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or the Chilean Torch Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often branching at the base.

The plant produces spiny, ribbed stems and has a unique ability to store water in its thick, fleshy tissue. It blooms with large, showy white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring.

This cactus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, thriving in full sun and low rainfall conditions.
