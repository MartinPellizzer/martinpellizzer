Taraxacum blandum

Taraxacum blandum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow and produce a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum blandum is often mistaken for other dandelion species but can be distinguished by its smaller size and unique leaf structure.
