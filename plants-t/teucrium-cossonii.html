Teucrium cossonii

Teucrium cossonii, commonly known as Cosson's germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium cossonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
