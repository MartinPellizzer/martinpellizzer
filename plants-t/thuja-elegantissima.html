Thuja elegantissima

Thuja elegantissima, commonly known as the Western red cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are deep green and arranged in flat sprays. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it popular in construction and furniture-making.

Thuja elegantissima thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
