Tinospora fragosa subsp. fragosa

Tinospora fragosa subsp.

fragosa, commonly known as the fragrant tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
