Thecophyllum singuliflorum

Thecophyllum singuliflorum, commonly known as the single-flower thecophyllum, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its singular, showy bloom.

It belongs to the family Thecophyllaceae and is native to a specific region in Southeast Asia. The plant features a short, sturdy stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive cup-like structure that encloses the reproductive organs.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
