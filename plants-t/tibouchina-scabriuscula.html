Tibouchina scabriuscula

Tibouchina scabriuscula, commonly known as the roughleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its dense, woody stems and large, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and vibrant floral display contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
