Thymus praecox var. grossheimii

Thymus praecox var. grossheimii, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and limestone areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
