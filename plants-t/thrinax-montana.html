Thrinax montana

Thrinax montana, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It has a short trunk with a dense crown of feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is adapted to dry climates and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are typically bluish-green and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Thrinax montana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought resistance.
