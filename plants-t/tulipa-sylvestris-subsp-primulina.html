Tulipa sylvestris subsp. primulina

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

primulina, commonly known as the forest tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in woodland or meadow environments with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
