Teratophyllum gracile

Teratophyllum gracile, commonly known as the slender teratophyllum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Teratophyllum gracile is a short-lived perennial that produces seeds in small, dry capsules.
