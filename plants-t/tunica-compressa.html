Tunica compressa

Tunica compressa, commonly known as the compressed tunica, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Tunica compressa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
