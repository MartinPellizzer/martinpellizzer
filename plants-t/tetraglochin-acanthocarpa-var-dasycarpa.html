Tetraglochin acanthocarpa var. dasycarpa

Tetraglochin acanthocarpa var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded tetraglochin, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, rough-textured fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
