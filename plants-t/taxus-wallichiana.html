Taxus wallichiana

Taxus wallichiana, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves arranged in flattened sprays. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. Taxus wallichiana is notable for its high ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of taxol, a compound used in cancer treatments.
