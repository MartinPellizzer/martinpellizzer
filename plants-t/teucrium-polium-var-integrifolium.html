Teucrium polium var. integrifolium

Teucrium polium var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Greek germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth along the edges. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
