Tanacetum scopulorum

Tanacetum scopulorum, commonly known as the rock tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as limestone soils and rocky outcrops. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
