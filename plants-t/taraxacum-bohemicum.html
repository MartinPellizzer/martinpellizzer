Taraxacum bohemicum

Taraxacum bohemicum, commonly known as the Bohemian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves and a long, hollow stem that bears a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into distinctive white, fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum bohemicum is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate.
