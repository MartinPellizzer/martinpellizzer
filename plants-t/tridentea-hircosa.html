Tridentea hircosa

Tridentea hircosa, commonly known as the goat's bean, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Tridentea hircosa is often grown as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.
