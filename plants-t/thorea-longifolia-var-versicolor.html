Thorea longifolia var. versicolor

Thorea longifolia var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable threadleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a wide range of color variations in its thallus.

It typically grows on tree bark, especially in humid and shaded environments, and is found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere. The plant's thallus is characterized by its thin, leaf-like structure and the presence of distinct, elongated lobes. Its coloration can range from pale green to yellowish or reddish, depending on environmental conditions and seasonal changes.

This variation in appearance contributes to its common name and makes it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
