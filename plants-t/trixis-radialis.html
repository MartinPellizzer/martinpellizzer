Trixis radialis

Trixis radialis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

Trixis radialis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
