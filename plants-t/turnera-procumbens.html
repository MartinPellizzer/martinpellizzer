Turnera procumbens

Turnera procumbens, commonly known as the "Mexican buttercup," is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often found in moist, forested areas.

Turnera procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
