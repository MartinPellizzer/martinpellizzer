Trigonella cretica

Trigonella cretica, commonly known as Cretan bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella cretica is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential herbal applications.
