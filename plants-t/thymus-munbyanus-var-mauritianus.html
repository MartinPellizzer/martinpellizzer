Thymus munbyanus var. mauritianus

Thymus munbyanus var. mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius thyme, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
