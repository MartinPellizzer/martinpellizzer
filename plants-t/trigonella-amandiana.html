Trigonella amandiana

Trigonella amandiana, commonly known as the Indian fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its nutritional and therapeutic benefits.

Trigonella amandiana thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
