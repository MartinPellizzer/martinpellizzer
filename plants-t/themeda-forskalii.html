Themeda forskalii

Themeda forskalii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass or tall grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in savanna ecosystems.

Themeda forskalii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
