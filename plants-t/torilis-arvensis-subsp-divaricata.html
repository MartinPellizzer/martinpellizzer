Torilis arvensis subsp. divaricata

Torilis arvensis subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has alternate, linear leaves and a hairy stem, which help it thrive in various environmental conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
