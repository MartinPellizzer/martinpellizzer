Thymus × quadicus

Thymus × quadicus, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus vulgaris (common thyme) and Thymus serpyllum (wild thyme). The plant typically features aromatic, small, oval leaves with a strongly fragrant, minty scent. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, forming dense clusters that bloom in early summer.

Thymus × quadicus is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine due to its medicinal properties.
