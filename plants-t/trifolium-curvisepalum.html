Trifolium curvisepalum

Trifolium curvisepalum, commonly known as the curved-sepal clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its delicate appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
