Tanacetum huronense var. terrae-novae

Tanacetum huronense var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Nova Scotia goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Nova Scotia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, thriving in coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is notable for its resilience to salt spray and wind, making it an important part of the local coastal flora.
