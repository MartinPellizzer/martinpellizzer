Thea rosiflora var. pilosa

Thea rosiflora var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rosewood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically dark green and have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its common name. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
