Taraxacum sinomongolicum

Taraxacum sinomongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a white, feathery pappus. It is native to temperate regions of Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China.

Taraxacum sinomongolicum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
