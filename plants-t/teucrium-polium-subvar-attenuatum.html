Teucrium polium subvar. attenuatum

Teucrium polium subvar. attenuatum, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
