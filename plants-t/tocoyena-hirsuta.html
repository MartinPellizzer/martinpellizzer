Tocoyena hirsuta

Tocoyena hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tocoyena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that enclose small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
