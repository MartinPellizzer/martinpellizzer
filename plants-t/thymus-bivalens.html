Thymus bivalens

Thymus bivalens, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
