Tricholepis radicans

Tricholepis radicans, commonly known as the trailing tricholepis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its creeping growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tricholepis radicans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
