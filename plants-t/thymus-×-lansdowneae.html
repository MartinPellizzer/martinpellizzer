Thymus × lansdowneae

Thymus × lansdowneae, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus × lansdowneae is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
