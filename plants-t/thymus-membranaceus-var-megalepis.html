Thymus membranaceus var. megalepis

Thymus membranaceus var. megalepis, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, with leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications, reflecting its value in both horticulture and traditional practices.
