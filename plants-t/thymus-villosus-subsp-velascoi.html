Thymus villosus subsp. velascoi

Thymus villosus subsp.

velascoi, commonly known as the Velasco thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists.
