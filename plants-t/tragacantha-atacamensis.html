Tragacantha atacamensis

Tragacantha atacamensis, commonly known as Atacama tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from underground sources.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife in its native environment.
