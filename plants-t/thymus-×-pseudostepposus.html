Thymus × pseudostepposus

Thymus × pseudostepposus, commonly known as the steppe thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed.

Thymus × pseudostepposus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas.
