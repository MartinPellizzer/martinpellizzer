Trifolium mucronatum subsp. lacerum

Trifolium mucronatum subsp.

lacerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mountain clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a serrated or lacerated edge, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
