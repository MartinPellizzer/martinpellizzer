Trivalvaria macrophylla var. macrophylla

Trivalvaria macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed trivalvaria, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
