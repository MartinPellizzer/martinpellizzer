Telipogon hausmannii

Telipogon hausmannii, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry rhizomes and small, clustered fronds that form a compact, cup-like structure. The plant produces spore-bearing leaves, or sporangia, which are typically yellowish or golden in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Telipogon hausmannii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
