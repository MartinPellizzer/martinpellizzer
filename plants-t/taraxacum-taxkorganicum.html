Taraxacum taxkorganicum

Taraxacum taxkorganicum, commonly known as the Taxkorgan dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly the region of Taxkorgan in China. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flowering head. Its yellow composite flowers are followed by a crown of white, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
