Teucrium reptans

Teucrium reptans, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Teucrium reptans is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive ailments and skin conditions.
