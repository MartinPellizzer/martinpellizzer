Taraxacum saxatile

Taraxacum saxatile, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a white waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Its yellow composite flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by the characteristic wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum saxatile plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its arid habitats and supports a variety of pollinators.
