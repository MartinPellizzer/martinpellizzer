Trollius chartosepalus

Trollius chartosepalus, commonly known as the chartosepal goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow ray florets that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, simple, and often covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

Trollius chartosepalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
