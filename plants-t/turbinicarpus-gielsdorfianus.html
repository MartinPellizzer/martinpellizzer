Turbinicarpus gielsdorfianus

Turbinicarpus gielsdorfianus, commonly known as the Gielsdorf giant, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
