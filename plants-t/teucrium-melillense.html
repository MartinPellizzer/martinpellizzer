Teucrium melillense

Teucrium melillense, commonly known as the Melilla germander, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small and tubular, often arranged in whorls, and can range in color from blue to purple.

Teucrium melillense is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
