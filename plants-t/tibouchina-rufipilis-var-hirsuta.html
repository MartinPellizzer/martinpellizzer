Tibouchina rufipilis var. hirsuta

Tibouchina rufipilis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden landscapes and as a flowering shrub in tropical and subtropical regions.
