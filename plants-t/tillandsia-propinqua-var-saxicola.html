Tillandsia propinqua var. saxicola

Tillandsia propinqua var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped foliage that is often covered in a thick layer of white trichomes. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained environments, often growing on cliffs or in crevices. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

This variety is known for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
