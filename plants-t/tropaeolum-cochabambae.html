Tropaeolum cochabambae

Tropaeolum cochabambae, commonly known as the Andean nasturtium, is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the highlands of Bolivia and Peru. The plant features vibrant, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Tropaeolum cochabambae is known for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally for its edible flowers and leaves.
