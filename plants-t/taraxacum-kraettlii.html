Taraxacum kraettlii

Taraxacum kraettlii, commonly known as the Kraettli's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a single, upright stem that terminates in a yellow flower head. Native to Central Europe, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a white pappus.

Taraxacum kraettlii is a perennial herb that exhibits asexual reproduction through its rhizomes.
