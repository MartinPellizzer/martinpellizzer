Tolpis mbalensis

Tolpis mbalensis, commonly known as the African flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Tolpis mbalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
