Tylophora govanii

Tylophora govanii, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Tylophora govanii is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
