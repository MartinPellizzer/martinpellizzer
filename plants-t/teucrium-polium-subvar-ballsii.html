Teucrium polium subvar. ballsii

Teucrium polium subvar. ballsii, commonly known as the Ball's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces small, blue-violet flowers in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
