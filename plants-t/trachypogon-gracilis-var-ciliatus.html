Trachypogon gracilis var. ciliatus

Trachypogon gracilis var. ciliatus, commonly known as the slender grass with ciliated leaves, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with finely toothed margins. The inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that bears small, two-ranked spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
