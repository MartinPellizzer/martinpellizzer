Tripogon mahendragiriensis

Tripogon mahendragiriensis, commonly known as the Mahendragiri grass, is a rare and endemic species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous root system, which help it thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for ecological and conservation studies.
