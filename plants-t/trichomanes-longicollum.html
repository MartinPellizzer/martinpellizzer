Trichomanes longicollum

Trichomanes longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes longicollum plays an important ecological role in forest understories by contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
