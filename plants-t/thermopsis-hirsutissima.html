Thermopsis hirsutissima

Thermopsis hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

Thermopsis hirsutissima is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
