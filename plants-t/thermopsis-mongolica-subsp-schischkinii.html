Thermopsis mongolica subsp. schischkinii

Thermopsis mongolica subsp.

schischkinii, commonly known as the Mongolian yellowhead, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
