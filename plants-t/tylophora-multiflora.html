Tylophora multiflora

Tylophora multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese star vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by ellipsoid berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Tylophora multiflora is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
