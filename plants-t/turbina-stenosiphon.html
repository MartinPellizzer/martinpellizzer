Turbina stenosiphon

Turbina stenosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon turban snail, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Turbinidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, spiral shell that is typically smooth and glossy, with a distinct, narrow aperture. The shell color can vary from white to yellowish or brownish, depending on the habitat and age of the individual. This species is primarily found in tropical and subtropical marine environments, often inhabiting rocky substrates.

Its anatomy includes a well-developed radula for scraping algae and a muscular foot for locomotion.
