Trichocyamos sericeolucidum

Trichocyamos sericeolucidum, commonly known as the silky-leafed willow, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland edges.

Trichocyamos sericeolucidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in sensitive ecosystems.
