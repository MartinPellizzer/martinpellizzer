Taraxacum sarniense

Taraxacum sarniense, commonly known as the Sarnian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds.

Taraxacum sarniense is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
