Tessmanniophoenix chuco

Tessmanniophoenix chuco, commonly known as the Chucu date palm, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, slow-growing palm with a short trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that yield edible dates, though it is not as widely cultivated as other date palms. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a high tolerance for drought conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-arid environments.
