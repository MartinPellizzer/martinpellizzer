Taraxacum leucochlorum

Taraxacum leucochlorum, commonly known as the dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head composed of numerous small yellow florets. The plant produces a distinctive seed head, known as a "puffball," which releases lightweight seeds with feathery parachutes for wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum leucochlorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its diuretic and digestive benefits.
