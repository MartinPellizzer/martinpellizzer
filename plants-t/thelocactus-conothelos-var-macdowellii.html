Thelocactus conothelos var. macdowellii

Thelocactus conothelos var. macdowellii, commonly known as the MacDowell's thorned cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its root system is shallow and adapted to quickly absorb surface moisture after rare rainfall events.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and ability to thrive in extreme desert conditions.
