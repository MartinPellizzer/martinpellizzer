Tolpis virgata subsp. sexaristata

Tolpis virgata subsp.

sexaristata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
