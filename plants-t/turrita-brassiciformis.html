Turrita brassiciformis

Turrita brassiciformis, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fruit is a dry, segmented silique that contains several seeds.

Turrita brassiciformis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
