Thysanotus thyrsoideus

Thysanotus thyrsoideus, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Thysanotus thyrsoideus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
