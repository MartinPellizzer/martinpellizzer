Tanacetum komarowii

Tanacetum komarowii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to regions of Russia and Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Tanacetum komarowii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
