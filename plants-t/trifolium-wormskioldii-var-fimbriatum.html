Trifolium wormskioldii var. fimbriatum

Trifolium wormskioldii var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fimbriated wormskiold's clover, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive fringed or fimbriated edge. This variation is a notable botanical feature that distinguishes it from other clover species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
