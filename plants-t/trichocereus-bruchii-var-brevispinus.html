Trichocereus bruchii var. brevispinus

Trichocereus bruchii var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in short, pale spines. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
