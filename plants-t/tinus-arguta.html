Tinus arguta

Tinus arguta, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with small, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals.

Tinus arguta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
