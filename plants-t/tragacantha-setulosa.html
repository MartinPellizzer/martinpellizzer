Tragacantha setulosa

Tragacantha setulosa, commonly known as the thorny tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing seeds. Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been used historically in various applications.

Tragacantha setulosa is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments.
