Thunia alba var. alba

Thunia alba var. alba, commonly known as the white thunia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds.

Thunia alba var. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
