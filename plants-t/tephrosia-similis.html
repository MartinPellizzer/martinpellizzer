Tephrosia similis

Tephrosia similis, commonly known as the smallleaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub or herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its fruits are legume pods that contain several small, elliptical seeds.

Tephrosia similis is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in savannas and woodland edges.
