Thymus vallicola

Thymus vallicola, commonly known as the valley thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus vallicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
