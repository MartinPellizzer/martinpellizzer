Tectaria nabirensis

Tectaria nabirensis, commonly known as the Nabire shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on rocky or moist soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
