Tinospora rumphii

Tinospora rumphii, commonly known as Indian gourd or Tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Tinospora rumphii is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
