Trapa acicularis

Trapa acicularis, commonly known as the spiny water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, woody fruits that resemble chestnuts. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, with the fruits detaching and floating to new locations.

Trapa acicularis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
