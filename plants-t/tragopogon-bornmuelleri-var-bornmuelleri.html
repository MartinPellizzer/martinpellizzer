Tragopogon bornmuelleri var. bornmuelleri

Tragopogon bornmuelleri var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
