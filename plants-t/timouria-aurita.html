Timouria aurita

Timouria aurita, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Timouria aurita is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
