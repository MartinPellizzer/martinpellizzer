Terpsichore chrysleri

Terpsichore chrysleri, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central golden stamen that resembles a star. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
