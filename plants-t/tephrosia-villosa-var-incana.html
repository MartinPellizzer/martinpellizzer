Tephrosia villosa var. incana

Tephrosia villosa var. incana, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Tephrosia villosa var. incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
