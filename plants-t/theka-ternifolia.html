Theka ternifolia

Theka ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Theka ternifolia is often found in coastal and arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
