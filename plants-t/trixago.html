Trixago

Trixago, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Trixago grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
