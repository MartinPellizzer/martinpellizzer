Trisetum melicoides var. majus

Trisetum melicoides var. majus, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trisetum melicoides var. majus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
