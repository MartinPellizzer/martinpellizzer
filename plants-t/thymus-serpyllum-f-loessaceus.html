Thymus serpyllum f. loessaceus

Thymus serpyllum f. loessaceus, commonly known as the Loess thyme, is a subspecies of the narrow-leaf thyme.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme varieties.
