Tulipa platystigma

Tulipa platystigma, commonly known as the broad-leaf tulip, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are broad and lanceolate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
