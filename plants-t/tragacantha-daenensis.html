Tragacantha daenensis

Tragacantha daenensis, commonly known as Persian gum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Tragacantha daenensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
