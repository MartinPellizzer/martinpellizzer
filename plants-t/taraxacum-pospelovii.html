Taraxacum pospelovii

Taraxacum pospelovii, commonly known as the Altai dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
