Teucrium buxifolium subsp. rivasii

Teucrium buxifolium subsp.

rivasii, commonly known as the Spanish germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features small, fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated edge.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
