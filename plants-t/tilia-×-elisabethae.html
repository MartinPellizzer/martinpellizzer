Tilia × elisabethae

Tilia × elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth Lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
