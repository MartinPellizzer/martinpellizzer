Triticum litoreum

Triticum litoreum, commonly known as sea barley, is a grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh maritime conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found growing along shorelines and in dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and coastal conservation efforts.
