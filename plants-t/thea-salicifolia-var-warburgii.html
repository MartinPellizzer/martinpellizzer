Thea salicifolia var. warburgii

Thea salicifolia var. warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often silvery-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.

The tree is relatively fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a striking feature in water features and parks.
