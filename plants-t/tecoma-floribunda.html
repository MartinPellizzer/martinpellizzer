Tecoma floribunda

Tecoma floribunda, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom profusely during the warm seasons. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture.

Tecoma floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
