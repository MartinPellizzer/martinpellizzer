Tetrabaculum cacatua

Tetrabaculum cacatua, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its fruit, a small, round berry, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Tetrabaculum cacatua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
