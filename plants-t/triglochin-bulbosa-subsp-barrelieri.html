Triglochin bulbosa subsp. barrelieri

Triglochin bulbosa subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in saturated soils.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
