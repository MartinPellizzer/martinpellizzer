Tetrastigma obtectum var. trichocarpum

Tetrastigma obtectum var. trichocarpum, commonly known as the hairy fruit tetrastigma, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grape family (Vitaceae) and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of hairy, orange-red berries. This species is often found in humid, tropical environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
