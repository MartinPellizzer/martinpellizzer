Tiquilia canescens var. canescens

Tiquilia canescens var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy tiquilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments.
