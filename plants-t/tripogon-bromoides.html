Tripogon bromoides

Tripogon bromoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Tripogon bromoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
