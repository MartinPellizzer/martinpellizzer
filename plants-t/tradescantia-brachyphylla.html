Tradescantia brachyphylla

Tradescantia brachyphylla, commonly known as the compact spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, narrow leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it an excellent choice for ground cover in shaded garden areas.
