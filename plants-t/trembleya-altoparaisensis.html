Trembleya altoparaisensis

Trembleya altoparaisensis, commonly known as the Paraiso trembleya, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
