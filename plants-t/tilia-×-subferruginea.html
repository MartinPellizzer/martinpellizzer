Tilia × subferruginea

Tilia × subferruginea, commonly known as the red-leaf lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree that combines characteristics of various lime species.

It is native to Europe and is known for its striking red foliage, which adds ornamental value to landscapes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its bark is light gray and becomes fissured with age.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
