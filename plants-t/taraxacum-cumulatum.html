Taraxacum cumulatum

Taraxacum cumulatum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce a wind-dispersed seed head.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its habitat.
