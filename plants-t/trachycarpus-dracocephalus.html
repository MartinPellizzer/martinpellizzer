Trachycarpus dracocephalus

Trachycarpus dracocephalus, commonly known as the dragonhead bamboo, is a member of the grass family and is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit.

It features stiff, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by triangular, woody fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
