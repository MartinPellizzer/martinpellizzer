Thymus jalpanensis

Thymus jalpanensis, commonly known as Mexican thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is fragrant and contains essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus jalpanensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
