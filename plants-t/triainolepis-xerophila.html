Triainolepis xerophila

Triainolepis xerophila, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It is native to regions with hot, dry climates, such as parts of Africa and the Middle East.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to conserve moisture and withstand extreme temperatures.
