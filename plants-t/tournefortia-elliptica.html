Tournefortia elliptica

Tournefortia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

Tournefortia elliptica is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
