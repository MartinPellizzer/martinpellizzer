Tropaeolum oxalidanthum

Tropaeolum oxalidanthum, commonly known as the jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, peppermint-scented flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, edible capsules that contain seeds, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Tropaeolum oxalidanthum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners, making it both a decorative and sometimes invasive species.
