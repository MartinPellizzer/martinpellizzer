Tithonia glaberrima

Tithonia glaberrima, commonly known as the yellow chia or Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow ray flowers and green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Tithonia glaberrima is also valued for its ability to improve soil fertility due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
