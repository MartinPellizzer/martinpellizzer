Thymus × rivas-molinae

Thymus × rivas-molinae, commonly known as the Rivas-Molina thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tiny, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
