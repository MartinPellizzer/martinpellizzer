Tragacantha divaricata

Tragacantha divaricata, commonly known as the variable tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. Tragacantha divaricata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
