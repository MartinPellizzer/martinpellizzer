Thea virgata

Thea virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf thea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Thea virgata is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
