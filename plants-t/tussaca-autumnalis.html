Tussaca autumnalis

Tussaca autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn tussac, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom in the autumn season. Tussaca autumnalis thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
