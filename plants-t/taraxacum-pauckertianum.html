Taraxacum pauckertianum

Taraxacum pauckertianum, commonly known as the Pauckert's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive seed head with white, feathery seeds. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is primarily herbaceous and typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.
