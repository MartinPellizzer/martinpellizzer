Teucrium scordium subsp. scordioides

Teucrium scordium subsp.

scordioides, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
