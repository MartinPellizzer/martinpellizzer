Tara cacalaco

Tara cacalaco, commonly known as the Andean tara, is a leguminous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce a yellow dye and as a source of protein. Tara cacalaco thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils, making it suitable for agricultural cultivation in specific regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
