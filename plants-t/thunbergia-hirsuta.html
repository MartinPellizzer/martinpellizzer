Thunbergia hirsuta

Thunbergia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Thunbergia hirsuta thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
