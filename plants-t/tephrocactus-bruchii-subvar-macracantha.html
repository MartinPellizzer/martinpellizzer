Tephrocactus bruchii subvar. macracantha

Tephrocactus bruchii subvar. macracantha, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features areoles, which are specialized structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom during the summer months.

This subvariety is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
