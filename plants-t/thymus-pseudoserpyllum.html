Thymus pseudoserpyllum

Thymus pseudoserpyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties. The plant produces pink or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus pseudoserpyllum is also valued for its medicinal properties, containing essential oils with antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.
