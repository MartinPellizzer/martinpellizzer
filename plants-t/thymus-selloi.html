Thymus selloi

Thymus selloi, commonly known as Sello's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Thymus selloi is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its strong, aromatic scent.
