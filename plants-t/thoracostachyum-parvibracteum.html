Thoracostachyum parvibracteum

Thoracostachyum parvibracteum, commonly known as the narrow-bracted grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its deep root system. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, tightly packed inflorescences.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
