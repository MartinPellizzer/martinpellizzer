Telanthera nudicaulis

Telanthera nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed telanthera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant features slender, leafless stems that arise from a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Telanthera nudicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
