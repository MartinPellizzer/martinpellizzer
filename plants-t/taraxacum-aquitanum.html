Taraxacum aquitanum

Taraxacum aquitanum, commonly known as the Aquitaine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features a rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports its bright yellow flower head.

The species is often found in temperate climates and is known for its resilience to various environmental conditions.
