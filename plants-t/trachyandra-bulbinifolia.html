Trachyandra bulbinifolia

Trachyandra bulbinifolia, commonly known as the bulbous lily, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. The plant's unique feature is its bulb-like structure, which stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Trachyandra bulbinifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
