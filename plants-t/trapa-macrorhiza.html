Trapa macrorhiza

Trapa macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It features a distinctive floating leaf structure with a thick, spongy stem that supports its broad, round leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its fruit is a spiny, floating capsule that contains several seeds.

Trapa macrorhiza is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
