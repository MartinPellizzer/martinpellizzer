Trichophorum bracteatum

Trichophorum bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Trichophorum bracteatum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
