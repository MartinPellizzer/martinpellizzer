Tolmachevia atropurpurea

Tolmachevia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple tolmachevia, is a striking perennial grass native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive purple or reddish tint, especially when grown in full sun. The plant produces delicate, plume-like inflorescences that add a dramatic visual element to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique coloration and architectural form make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and native plant collections.
