Teucrium polium var. albescens

Teucrium polium var. albescens, commonly known as white-stemmed germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
