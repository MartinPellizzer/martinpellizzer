Trichera velutina

Trichera velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Trichera velutina is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is an important species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
