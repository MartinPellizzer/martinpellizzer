Thoracostachyum hypolytroides

Thoracostachyum hypolytroides, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that resembles the structure of a feather. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in narrow, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
