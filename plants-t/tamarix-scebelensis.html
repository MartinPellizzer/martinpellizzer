Tamarix scebelensis

Tamarix scebelensis, commonly known as the Scebel's tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, scale-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and the ability to fix nitrogen, making it resilient in poor soils. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and fuel.

Tamarix scebelensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
