Trifolium amabile var. pentlandii

Trifolium amabile var. pentlandii, commonly known as the Pentland mountain clover, is a perennial herb native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous and heathland environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is also noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
