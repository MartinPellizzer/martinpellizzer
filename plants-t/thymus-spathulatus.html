Thymus spathulatus

Thymus spathulatus, commonly known as narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
