Thymus vulgaris subsp. aestivus

Thymus vulgaris subsp.

aestivus, commonly known as summer thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, small, grey-green leaves and produces pink or purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its essential oils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its flowering time, which occurs later in the growing season compared to other thyme varieties.
