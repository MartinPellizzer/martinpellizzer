Trachyandra hantamensis

Trachyandra hantamensis, commonly known as the Hantam grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
