Trichosporum rubiginosum

Trichosporum rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-staining trichosporum, is a species of fungus belonging to the class Basidiomycota.

It is characterized by its ability to produce a reddish pigment, which gives it its common name. This fungus is often found in association with decaying wood and leaf litter in forested areas. It plays a role in the decomposition process by breaking down organic matter.

Its spores are typically released in the form of tiny, sticky structures that aid in dispersal.
