Tapinanthus myrsinifolius

Tapinanthus myrsinifolius, commonly known as the parasitic forest vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants to obtain water and nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces yellow or white flowers in clusters. It lacks true roots and relies entirely on its host for sustenance.
