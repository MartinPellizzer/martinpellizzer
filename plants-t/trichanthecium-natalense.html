Trichanthecium natalense

Trichanthecium natalense, commonly known as Natal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trichanthecium natalense is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
