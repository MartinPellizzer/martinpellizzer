Tournefortia macrophylla

Tournefortia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically large, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Tournefortia macrophylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in coastal or riverine habitats.
