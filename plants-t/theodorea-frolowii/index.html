Theodorea Frolowii

Theodorea frolowii is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes. It thrives in cool, moist environments where sunlight is limited but essential for its survival. This species is known for its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. Due to its limited habitat and delicate nature, it is highly threatened by climate change and human encroachment. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival for future generations.
