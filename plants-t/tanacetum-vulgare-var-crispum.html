Tanacetum vulgare var. crispum

Tanacetum vulgare var. crispum, commonly known as buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling miniature sunflowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often curled or crisped, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Tanacetum vulgare var. crispum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
