Taraxacum serotinum

Taraxacum serotinum, commonly known as the late yellow hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer to early autumn, making it a distinctive presence in meadows and grasslands.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
