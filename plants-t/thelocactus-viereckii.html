Thelocactus viereckii

Thelocactus viereckii, commonly known as the "Easter cactus," is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and its ability to produce vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Thelocactus viereckii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
