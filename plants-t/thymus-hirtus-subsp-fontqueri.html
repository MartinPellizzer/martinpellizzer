Thymus hirtus subsp. fontqueri

Thymus hirtus subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as Fontqueri thyme, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and woodlands. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy, low-maintenance plant.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant fragrance and medicinal properties.
