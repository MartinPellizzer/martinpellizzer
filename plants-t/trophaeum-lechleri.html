Trophaeum lechleri

Trophaeum lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is renowned for its latex-producing properties, which have been used for centuries by indigenous peoples. The plant's bark contains a high concentration of natural rubber, making it economically significant. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include large, heart-shaped leaves and a tall, straight trunk.
