Tragacantha remotiflora

Tragacantha remotiflora, commonly known as the remote-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and hairy.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
