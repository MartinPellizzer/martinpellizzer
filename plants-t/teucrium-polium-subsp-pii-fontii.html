Teucrium polium subsp. pii-fontii

Teucrium polium subsp.

pii-fontii, commonly known as the Corsican germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
