Toona ciliata var. parviflora

Toona ciliata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
