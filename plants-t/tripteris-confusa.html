Tripteris confusa

Tripteris confusa, commonly known as the Brazilian sycamore, is a species of flowering plant in the family Tripteridaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Tripteris confusa is valued for its wood, which is used in local crafts and construction.
