Teucrium alphonsi

Teucrium alphonsi, commonly known as the Alphonse germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Teucrium alphonsi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
