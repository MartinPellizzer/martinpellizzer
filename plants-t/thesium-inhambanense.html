Thesium inhambanense

Thesium inhambanense, commonly known as the Inhambanense torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that resemble candles. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
