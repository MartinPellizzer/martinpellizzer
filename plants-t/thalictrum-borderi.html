Thalictrum borderi

Thalictrum borderi, commonly known as the Border meadow rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that adds a delicate texture to the landscape.

Thalictrum borderi is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
