Tulbaghia aequinoctialis

Tulbaghia aequinoctialis, commonly known as the spring star or white star ginger, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It produces star-shaped, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Tulbaghia aequinoctialis is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
