Typha komarovii

Typha komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including Russia and China.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spike that is typically brown or reddish in color. The plant grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its leaves are long, narrow, and sword-shaped, often reaching several feet in length.

Typha komarovii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
