Taraxacum acutatum

Taraxacum acutatum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads.

Taraxacum acutatum is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
