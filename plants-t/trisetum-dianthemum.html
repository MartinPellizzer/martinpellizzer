Trisetum dianthemum

Trisetum dianthemum, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often purple-tinged leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

Trisetum dianthemum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
