Terminalia angolensis

Terminalia angolensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white, fluffy flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine forests.

Terminalia angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
