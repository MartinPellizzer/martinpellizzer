Tragia nepetifolia var. teucriifolia

Tragia nepetifolia var. teucriifolia, commonly known as the creeping tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Malvaceae family and shares similar botanical features.
