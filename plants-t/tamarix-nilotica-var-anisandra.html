Tamarix nilotica var. anisandra

Tamarix nilotica var. anisandra, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh, saline soils. Its roots can fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded environments.

It is often used in reclamation projects due to its ability to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.
