Taraxacum argutifrons

Taraxacum argutifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of broad, serrated leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. The plant is typically found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, while the flowers attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Taraxacum argutifrons is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
