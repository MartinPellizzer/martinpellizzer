Tiarella cordifolia f. allanthera

Tiarella cordifolia f. allanthera, commonly known as the Allanthera foamflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating, giving them a frothy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms.

This cultivar is often used in garden landscapes for its shade tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
