Tabernaemontana ternifolia

Tabernaemontana ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf star of India, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pink star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three distinct lobes, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Tabernaemontana ternifolia make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
