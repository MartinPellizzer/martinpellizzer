Thelypteris angulariloba var. major

Thelypteris angulariloba var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, triangular-shaped fronds that are often divided into smaller, serrated segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in swampy areas. Its fronds are usually a dark green color and can grow up to several feet in length.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
