Tithymalus chamaesyce

Tithymalus chamaesyce, commonly known as groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in other open areas.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
