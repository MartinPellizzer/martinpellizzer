Tephrosia lepida

Tephrosia lepida, commonly known as the slender tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Tephrosia lepida is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
