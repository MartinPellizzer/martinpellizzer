Tecoma impetiginosa var. lepidota

Tecoma impetiginosa var. lepidota, commonly known as the velvet trumpet bush, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
