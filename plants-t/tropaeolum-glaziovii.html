Tropaeolum glaziovii

Tropaeolum glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum glaziovii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking quick visual impact.
