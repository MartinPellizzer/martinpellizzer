Thalictrum polygamum var. pubescens

Thalictrum polygamum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered with a dense layer of soft, hairy pubescence. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
