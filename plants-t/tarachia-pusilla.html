Tarachia pusilla

Tarachia pusilla, commonly known as the small desert sandmat, is a small, flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs. Tarachia pusilla is adapted to extreme desert conditions, with deep root systems and a high tolerance for drought.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
