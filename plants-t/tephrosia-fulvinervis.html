Tephrosia fulvinervis

Tephrosia fulvinervis, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Tephrosia fulvinervis is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
