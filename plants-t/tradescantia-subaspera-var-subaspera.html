Tradescantia subaspera var. subaspera

Tradescantia subaspera var. subaspera, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a hardy perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features strap-like, dark green leaves with a distinctive blue-gray hue and a slightly rough texture. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to 3 feet in height, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is also known for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
