Thyrsacanthus interruptus

Thyrsacanthus interruptus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by insects, contributing to its ecological role in native ecosystems.
