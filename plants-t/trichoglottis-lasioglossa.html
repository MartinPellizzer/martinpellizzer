Trichoglottis lasioglossa

Trichoglottis lasioglossa, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking golden-yellow flowers with prominent lip structures. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms that emerge from a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trichoglottis lasioglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
