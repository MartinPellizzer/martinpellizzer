Trigonochilum confertum

Trigonochilum confertum, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in botanical studies.
