Turnera subnuda

Turnera subnuda, commonly known as the naked turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually smooth and elliptical, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Turnera subnuda is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
