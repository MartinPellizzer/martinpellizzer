Touterea integra

Touterea integra, commonly known as the silver tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The silver tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
