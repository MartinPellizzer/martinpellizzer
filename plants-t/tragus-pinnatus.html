Tragus pinnatus

Tragus pinnatus, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Tragus pinnatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
