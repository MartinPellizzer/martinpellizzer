Thymus kondratjukii

Thymus kondratjukii, commonly known as Kondratjuk's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

Thymus kondratjukii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or alpine environments.
