Taraxacum persquamulosum

Taraxacum persquamulosum, commonly known as the Persian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a taproot system and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
