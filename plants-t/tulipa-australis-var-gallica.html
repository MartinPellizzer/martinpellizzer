Tulipa australis var. gallica

Tulipa australis var. gallica, commonly known as the Gallic tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
