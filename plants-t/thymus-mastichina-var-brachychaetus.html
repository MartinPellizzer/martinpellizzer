Thymus mastichina var. brachychaetus

Thymus mastichina var. brachychaetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and densely packed on low-growing stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
