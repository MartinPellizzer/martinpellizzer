Terminalia hecistocarpa

Terminalia hecistocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, woody, bead-like fruits. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
