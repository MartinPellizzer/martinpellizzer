Tripleurospermum tetragonospermum

Tripleurospermum tetragonospermum, commonly known as the four-angled milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and alternate leaves that are typically four-angled in cross-section. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed pods. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its latex sap is milky and can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.
