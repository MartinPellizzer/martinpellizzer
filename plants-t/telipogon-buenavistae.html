Telipogon buenavistae

Telipogon buenavistae, commonly known as the Buenavista orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with a single stem and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat. Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Telipogon buenavistae plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its region.
