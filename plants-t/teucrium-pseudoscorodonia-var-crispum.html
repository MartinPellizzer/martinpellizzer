Teucrium pseudoscorodonia var. crispum

Teucrium pseudoscorodonia var. crispum, commonly known as the crinkled germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
