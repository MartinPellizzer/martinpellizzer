Thalictrum flavovirens

Thalictrum flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green meadow-rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Thalictrum flavovirens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
