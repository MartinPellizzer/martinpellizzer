Turnera hispidissima

Turnera hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and bloom in clusters.

Turnera hispidissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
