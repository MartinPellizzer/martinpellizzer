Terminalia petiolaris

Terminalia petiolaris, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking white, spherical flower clusters that appear at the ends of its branches. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
