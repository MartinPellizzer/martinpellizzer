Trisetum subspicatum var. muticum

Trisetum subspicatum var. muticum, commonly known as the smooth tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The leaves are narrow, flat, and covered with short, fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
