Turnera ovata

Turnera ovata, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil.
