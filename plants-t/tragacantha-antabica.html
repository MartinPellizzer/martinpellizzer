Tragacantha antabica

Tragacantha antabica, commonly known as the Persian gum or antabian gum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces yellow flowers and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stem. The plant is notable for its production of a resinous gum, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a food additive. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
