Taraxacum anomum

Taraxacum anomum, commonly known as the anomalous dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which develops into a fluffy seed head known as a pappus.

Taraxacum anomum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
