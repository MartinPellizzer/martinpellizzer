Teucrium chamaedrys var. semiglaucum

Teucrium chamaedrys var. semiglaucum, commonly known as the silver carpet bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in rock gardens or as ground cover due to its ornamental appearance and hardiness.
