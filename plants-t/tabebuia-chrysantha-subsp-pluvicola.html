Tabebuia chrysantha subsp. pluvicola

Tabebuia chrysantha subsp.

pluvicola, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree or yellow angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, and it can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
