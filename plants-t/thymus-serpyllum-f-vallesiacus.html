Thymus serpyllum f. vallesiacus

Thymus serpyllum f. vallesiacus, commonly known as the Vallesian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
