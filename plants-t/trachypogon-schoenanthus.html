Trachypogon schoenanthus

Trachypogon schoenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves and tufts of airy, feathery seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Trachypogon schoenanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
