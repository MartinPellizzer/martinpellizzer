Trifolium macraei var. albopurpureum

Trifolium macraei var. albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive purple-veined pattern. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
