Thymus cinerascens

Thymus cinerascens, commonly known as cinerascens thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Thymus cinerascens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
