Tetrardisia corneri

Tetrardisia corneri, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Tetrardisia corneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
