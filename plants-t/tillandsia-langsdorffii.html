Tillandsia langsdorffii

Tillandsia langsdorffii, commonly known as the silver leaf tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
