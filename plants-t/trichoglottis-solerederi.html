Trichoglottis solerederi

Trichoglottis solerederi, commonly known as the Solereder's trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in a raceme, with pale yellow to greenish-white petals.

Trichoglottis solerederi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
