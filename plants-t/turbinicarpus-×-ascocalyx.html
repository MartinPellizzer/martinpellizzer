Turbinicarpus × ascocalyx

Turbinicarpus × ascocalyx, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a colorful, fleshy body known as the "pseudostem" and a small, globular body called the "crown.". The plant is a hybrid between Turbinicarpus subulatus and Turbinicarpus calycatus, resulting in a combination of traits from both parent species. Its leaves are typically small, spiny, and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
