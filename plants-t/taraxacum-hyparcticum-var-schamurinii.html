Taraxacum hyparcticum var. schamurinii

Taraxacum hyparcticum var. schamurinii, commonly known as the Siberian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a composite flower head. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
