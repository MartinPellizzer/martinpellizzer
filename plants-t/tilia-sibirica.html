Tilia sibirica

Tilia sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian linden, is a deciduous tree native to the forests of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round, brown fruits that contain one or two seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance.
