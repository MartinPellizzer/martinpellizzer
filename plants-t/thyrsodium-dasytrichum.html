Thyrsodium dasytrichum

Thyrsodium dasytrichum, commonly known as the woolly thrysoide, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual interest.
