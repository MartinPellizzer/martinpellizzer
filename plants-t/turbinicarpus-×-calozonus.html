Turbinicarpus × calozonus

Turbinicarpus × calozonus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus platycarpus and Turbinicarpus leucopetiolatus.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of succulent plants. The plant features a unique coloration, often with a contrasting color between the body and the crown, which is a result of its hybrid nature. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in cactus gardens and as a houseplant.
