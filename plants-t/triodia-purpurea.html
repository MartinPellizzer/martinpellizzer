Triodia purpurea

Triodia purpurea, commonly known as the purple spinifex, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has wiry, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late winter to early spring.

Triodia purpurea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
