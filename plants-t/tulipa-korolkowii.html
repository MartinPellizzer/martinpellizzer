Tulipa korolkowii

Tulipa korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkov's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
