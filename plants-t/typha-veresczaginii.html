Typha veresczaginii

Typha veresczaginii, commonly known as the narrow leaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that contains both male and female flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Typha veresczaginii plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
