Taraxacum invocatum

Taraxacum invocatum, commonly known as the sacred dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and roots used in traditional remedies. Its flowers produce a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is also noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
