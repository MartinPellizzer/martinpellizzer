Triosteum obovatum

Triosteum obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf triosteum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Triosteum obovatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
