Tradescantia reverchonii

Tradescantia reverchonii, commonly known as the Mexican spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish or silver-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
