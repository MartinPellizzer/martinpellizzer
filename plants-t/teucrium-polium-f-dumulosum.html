Teucrium polium f. dumulosum

Teucrium polium f. dumulosum, commonly known as the woolly germander, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
