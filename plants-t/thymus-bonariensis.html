Thymus bonariensis

Thymus bonariensis, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Thymus bonariensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
