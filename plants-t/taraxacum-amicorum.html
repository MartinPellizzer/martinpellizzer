Taraxacum amicorum

Taraxacum amicorum, commonly known as the friendship dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of bright green, hairy leaves and a slender, hollow stem that supports a single, yellow flowering head. The plant produces a distinctive white, fluffy seed head that is dispersed by the wind, a common trait among members of the dandelion genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum amicorum is a hardy perennial that is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
