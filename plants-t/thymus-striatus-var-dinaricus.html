Thymus striatus var. dinaricus

Thymus striatus var. dinaricus, commonly known as the Dinaric thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and low-maintenance gardening.
