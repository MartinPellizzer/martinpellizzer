Taraxacum podlechii

Taraxacum podlechii, commonly known as the Podlech's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowering heads produce yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus.

Taraxacum podlechii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
