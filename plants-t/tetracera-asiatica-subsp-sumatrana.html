Tetracera asiatica subsp. sumatrana

Tetracera asiatica subsp.

sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in lowland and hill forests.
