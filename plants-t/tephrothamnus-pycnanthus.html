Tephrothamnus pycnanthus

Tephrothamnus pycnanthus, commonly known as the dense thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round cover.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
