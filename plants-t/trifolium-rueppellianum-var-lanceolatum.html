Trifolium rueppellianum var. lanceolatum

Trifolium rueppellianum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are lanceolate in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
