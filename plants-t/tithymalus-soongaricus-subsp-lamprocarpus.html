Tithymalus soongaricus subsp. lamprocarpus

Tithymalus soongaricus subsp.

lamprocarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
