Thymus serpyllum f. simulans

Thymus serpyllum f. simulans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a dense mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
