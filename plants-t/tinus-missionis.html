Tinus missionis

Tinus missionis, commonly known as the Mission's tinus, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Tinus missionis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
