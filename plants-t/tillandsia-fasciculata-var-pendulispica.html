Tillandsia fasciculata var. pendulispica

Tillandsia fasciculata var. pendulispica, commonly known as the pendant tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped clusters of long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light, making it well-suited for indoor cultivation. It produces delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, white or pale green flowers, adding an ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of air plants.
