Triticum farctum

Triticum farctum, commonly known as the fat wheat, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of a thick, fleshy stem. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear the inflorescence.

Although not widely cultivated for grain production, it is studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
