Tibouchina nervulosa

Tibouchina nervulosa, commonly known as the pink glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina nervulosa is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for coastal and garden settings.
