Tachigali pulchra

Tachigali pulchra, commonly known as the pink tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often shed during the dry season. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for wildlife.
