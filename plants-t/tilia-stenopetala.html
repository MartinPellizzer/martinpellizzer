Tilia stenopetala

Tilia stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lime, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of other lime species. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in drooping inflorescences, appearing in early summer. The tree produces small, oval fruits that are typically brown when mature.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
