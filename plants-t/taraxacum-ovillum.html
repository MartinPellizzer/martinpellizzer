Taraxacum ovillum

Taraxacum ovillum, commonly known as the small-headed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, small, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem and has a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus Taraxacum. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its small size and subtle appearance compared to other dandelion species.
