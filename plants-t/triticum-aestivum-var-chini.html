Triticum aestivum var. chini

Triticum aestivum var. chini, commonly known as Chinese spring wheat, is a variety of wheat native to China.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, glaucous leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 1.5 meters and produces compact spikelets with awned lemmas. Its grain is primarily used for flour production and is well-suited to temperate climates.

The botanical features of this variety make it a valuable crop for both agricultural and nutritional purposes.
