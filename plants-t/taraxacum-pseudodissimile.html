Taraxacum pseudodissimile

Taraxacum pseudodissimile, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, slender stem that ends in a bright yellow flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed head after blooming. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas.

This species is similar to other dandelions but can be distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
