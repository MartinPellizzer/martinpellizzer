Teucrium depressum

Teucrium depressum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, often blue or purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal uses.

Teucrium depressum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
