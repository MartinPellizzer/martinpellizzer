Taraxacum daralagesicum

Taraxacum daralagesicum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
