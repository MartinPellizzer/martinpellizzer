Taxus baccata var. macrocarpa

Taxus baccata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the English yew with large seeds, is a variant of the common yew.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain toxic seeds, making it hazardous to animals and humans. It is a slow-growing evergreen tree that thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in timber production.
