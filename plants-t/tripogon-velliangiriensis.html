Tripogon velliangiriensis

Tripogon velliangiriensis, commonly known as the Velangiri tripogon, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
