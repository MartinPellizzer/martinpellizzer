Themis

Themis, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its flowers open at dusk and close at dawn, making it a unique addition to gardens.

Themis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
