Thymus sublobatus

Thymus sublobatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus sublobatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
