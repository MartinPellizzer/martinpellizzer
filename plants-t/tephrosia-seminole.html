Tephrosia seminole

Tephrosia seminole, commonly known as Seminole false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland areas and along stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Tephrosia seminole is known for its ability to thrive in moist, sandy soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
