Teucrium francisci-werneri

Teucrium francisci-werneri, commonly known as Werneri's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Teucrium francisci-werneri is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
