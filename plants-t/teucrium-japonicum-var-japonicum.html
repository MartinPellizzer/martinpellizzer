Teucrium japonicum var. japonicum

Teucrium japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal practices.
