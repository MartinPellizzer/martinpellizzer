Tulbaghia acutiloba

Tulbaghia acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tulbaghia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is also known for its pleasant, mild fragrance, which is released when the leaves are crushed.

Tulbaghia acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
