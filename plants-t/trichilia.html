Trichilia

Trichilia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Meliaceae.

It consists of approximately 100 species, predominantly found in tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plants are characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that often have a fragrant scent. Many species of Trichilia produce hard, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and have traditional uses in medicine and woodworking.
