Trihaloragis hexandra subsp. serrata

Trihaloragis hexandra subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the serrated waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to certain regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which give it a wheel-like appearance.

The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, contributing to its common name. This species thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
