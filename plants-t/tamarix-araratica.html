Tamarix araratica

Tamarix araratica, commonly known as the white tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, salt-tolerant foliage and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and saline environments. Its woody branches and scale-like leaves are adapted to reduce water loss.

Tamarix araratica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its drought resistance and aesthetic appeal.
