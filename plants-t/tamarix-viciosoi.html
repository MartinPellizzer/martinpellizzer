Tamarix viciosoi

Tamarix viciosoi, commonly known as the salt cedar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in saline and alkaline soils, which makes it a common invader in disturbed areas. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological impact in non-native regions.

Tamarix viciosoi is often noted for its ornamental value but is also considered a problematic species due to its invasive tendencies.
