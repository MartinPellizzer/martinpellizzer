Tilia rubra f. begoniifolia

Tilia rubra f. begoniifolia, commonly known as the begoniifolium lilac tree, is a unique cultivar of the American basswood.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that resemble those of the begonia plant, giving it its distinctive name. The foliage is typically a deep green with a slight reddish tinge, especially during the growing season. This plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer.

It is a compact, ornamental tree that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in garden settings for its aesthetic appeal.
