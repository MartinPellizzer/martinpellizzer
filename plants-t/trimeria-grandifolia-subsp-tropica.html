Trimeria grandifolia subsp. tropica

Trimeria grandifolia subsp.

tropica, commonly known as the tropical trimeria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
