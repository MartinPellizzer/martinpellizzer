Tauschia parishii

Tauschia parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
