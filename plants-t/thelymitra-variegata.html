Thelymitra variegata

Thelymitra variegata, commonly known as the variegated sun-orchid, is an Australian endemic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically feature a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant is terrestrial, growing in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, usually from September to November.

Thelymitra variegata is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens due to its vibrant appearance.
