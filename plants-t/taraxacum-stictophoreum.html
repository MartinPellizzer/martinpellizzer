Taraxacum stictophoreum

Taraxacum stictophoreum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its unique spotted leaves and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
