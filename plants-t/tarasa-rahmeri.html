Tarasa rahmeri

Tarasa rahmeri, commonly known as the moonflower of the highlands, is a rare flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a subtle fragrance. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
