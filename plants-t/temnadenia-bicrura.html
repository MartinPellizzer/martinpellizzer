Temnadenia bicrura

Temnadenia bicrura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
