Teucrium cavernarum

Teucrium cavernarum, commonly known as the cave germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Teucrium cavernarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
