Timonius trichocladus

Timonius trichocladus, commonly known as the hairy-leaf timonius, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
