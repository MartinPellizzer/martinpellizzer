Taraxacum exiguiceps

Taraxacum exiguiceps, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow flower that matures into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
