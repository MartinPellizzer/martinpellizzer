Thomsonaria

Thomsonaria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact, bushy growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. Thomsonaria species are popular as houseplants due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their relatively easy care requirements.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated for their vibrant blooms.
