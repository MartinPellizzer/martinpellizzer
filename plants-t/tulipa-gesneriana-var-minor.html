Tulipa gesneriana var. minor

Tulipa gesneriana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf tulip, is a compact variety of the tulip flower characterized by its shorter stature compared to standard tulips.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 centimeters, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. The botanical features include showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors such as red, yellow, and white, which bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
