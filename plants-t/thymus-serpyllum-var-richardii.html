Thymus serpyllum var. richardii

Thymus serpyllum var. richardii, commonly known as Richard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
