Tulipa strangulata subsp. variopicta

Tulipa strangulata subsp.

variopicta, commonly known as the variable tulip, is a striking bulbous perennial native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This subspecies is prized in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
