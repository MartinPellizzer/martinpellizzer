Tetracarpum virgatum

Tetracarpum virgatum, commonly known as the white sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

Tetracarpum virgatum is often found in coastal dunes and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
