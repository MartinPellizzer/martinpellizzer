Tragopogon samaritani

Tragopogon samaritani, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical leaves. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in inulin, a type of dietary fiber. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.
