Tibouchina hirsutissima

Tibouchina hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or red, which bloom in spring and summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
