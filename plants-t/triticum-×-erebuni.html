Triticum × erebuni

Triticum × erebuni, commonly known as the wild wheat or Erebuni wheat, is a naturally occurring hybrid species resulting from the cross between Triticum urartu and Triticum monococcum.

It is characterized by its small, glumeless spikelets and is typically found in the highlands of the Caucasus region. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, hard grains that are often used in traditional breeding programs. Triticum × erebuni is considered an important genetic resource for wheat improvement due to its potential for enhancing disease resistance and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studying the evolution and domestication of cereal crops.
