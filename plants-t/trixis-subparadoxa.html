Trixis subparadoxa

Trixis subparadoxa, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native harsh environment.

Trixis subparadoxa is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
