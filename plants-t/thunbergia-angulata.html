Thunbergia angulata

Thunbergia angulata, commonly known as the angular thunbergia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
