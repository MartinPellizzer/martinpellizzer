Tanacetum macrocephalum

Tanacetum macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Tanacetum macrocephalum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
