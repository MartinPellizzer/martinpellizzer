Trifolium insularum

Trifolium insularum, commonly known as the island clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trifolium insularum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
