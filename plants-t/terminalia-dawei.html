Terminalia dawei

Terminalia dawei, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing single seeds. Terminalia dawei is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also used in traditional medicine.
