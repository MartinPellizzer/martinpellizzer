Tragacantha campylantha

Tragacantha campylantha, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces yellow flowers and is characterized by its woody, branched stems. The plant is known for its resinous sap, which is harvested for use in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries. Tragacantha campylantha thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
