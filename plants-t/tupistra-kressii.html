Tupistra kressii

Tupistra kressii, commonly known as the Kress's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish hue, covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for arid and semi-arid landscapes.
