Thalictrum majus var. pseudominus

Thalictrum majus var. pseudominus, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stamens in its showy inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
