Tigridia atrata

Tigridia atrata, commonly known as the black-tongued tiger flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive black throat. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces showy, tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Tigridia atrata is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its bold appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
