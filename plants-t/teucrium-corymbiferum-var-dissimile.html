Teucrium corymbiferum var. dissimile

Teucrium corymbiferum var. dissimile, commonly known as the purple false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
