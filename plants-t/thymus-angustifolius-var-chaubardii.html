Thymus angustifolius var. chaubardii

Thymus angustifolius var. chaubardii, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
