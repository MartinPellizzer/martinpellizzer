Tephroseris balbisiana

Tephroseris balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include showy capitula and a fibrous root system, which aid in its adaptation to various environmental conditions.
