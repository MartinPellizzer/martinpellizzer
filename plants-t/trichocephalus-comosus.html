Trichocephalus comosus

Trichocephalus comosus, commonly known as the hairy-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Trichocephalus comosus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
