Tenageia gerardi

Tenageia gerardi, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white or pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
