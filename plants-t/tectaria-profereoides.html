Tectaria profereoides

Tectaria profereoides, commonly known as the broad-leaf tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical and subtropical forests. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria profereoides is often found in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
