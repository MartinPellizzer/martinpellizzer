Teucrium occidentale subsp. viscidum

Teucrium occidentale subsp.

viscidum, commonly known as sticky blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, branching stems covered in a dense layer of fine, sticky hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and also bear a viscid coating, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
