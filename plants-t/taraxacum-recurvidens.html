Taraxacum recurvidens

Taraxacum recurvidens, commonly known as the curled leaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of narrow, curled leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic puffball seeds.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
