Tephrosia brachyloba

Tephrosia brachyloba, commonly known as the small-leaved tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, opposite, and often trifoliate leaves, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its use as a medicinal plant and its role in traditional herbal practices.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in certain habitats.
