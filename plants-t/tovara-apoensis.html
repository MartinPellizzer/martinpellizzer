Tovara apoensis

Tovara apoensis, commonly known as the Apo Mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic plant found exclusively in the Apo Mountain range in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, colorful flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in high-altitude cloud forests, where it relies on specific microclimatic conditions for survival. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air, and it plays a vital role in the local ecosystem.

Due to habitat destruction, Tovara apoensis is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
