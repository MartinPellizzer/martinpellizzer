Trisetum paniceum var. binervatum

Trisetum paniceum var. binervatum, commonly known as the two-veined panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive two-veined structure on the underside, which is a key identifying characteristic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
