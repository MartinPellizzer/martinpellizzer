Trachyspermum copticum var. maritimum

Trachyspermum copticum var. maritimum, commonly known as sea cumin, is a variant of the cumin plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its aromatic, spicy seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit adapted to saline and coastal environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.
