Thoracosperma discolor

Thoracosperma discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered balsa, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces lightweight, buoyant seeds enclosed in a fibrous, spongy fruit. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique botanical features, it has been studied for its potential in ecological and horticultural applications.
