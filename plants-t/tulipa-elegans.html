Tulipa elegans

Tulipa elegans, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the family Liliaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Tulipa elegans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
