Tomex glabra

Tomex glabra, commonly known as the smooth tomex, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
