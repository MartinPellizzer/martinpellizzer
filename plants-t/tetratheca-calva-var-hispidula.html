Tetratheca calva var. hispidula

Tetratheca calva var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid glabrous tetratheca, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. The leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract native pollinators.
