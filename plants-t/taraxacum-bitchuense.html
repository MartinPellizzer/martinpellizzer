Taraxacum bitchuense

Taraxacum bitchuense, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem topped with a bright yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common feature in the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and roadsides.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
