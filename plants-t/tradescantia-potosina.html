Tradescantia potosina

Tradescantia potosina, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or red-veined spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Tradescantia potosina is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, contributing to its adaptability in various environments.
