Thymus fallax var. hayderensis

Thymus fallax var. hayderensis, commonly known as Hayder's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems, often forming a mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden use and culinary applications.
