Tassadia aristata

Tassadia aristata, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Tassadia aristata is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions.
