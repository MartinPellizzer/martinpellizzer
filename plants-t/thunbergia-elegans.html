Thunbergia elegans

Thunbergia elegans, commonly known as the blue musk vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional medicine practices.
