Thymus serpyllum var. kollmannii

Thymus serpyllum var. kollmannii, commonly known as Kollmann's thrift, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tiny, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
