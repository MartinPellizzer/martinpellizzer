Trithrinax mauritiiformis

Trithrinax mauritiiformis, commonly known as the Mexican palm or the Guanacaste palm, is a species of palm native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red fruits, which are edible for some animals but not typically for humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
