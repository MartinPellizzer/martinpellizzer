Trisetum sibiricum var. bifidum

Trisetum sibiricum var. bifidum, commonly known as Siberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver sheen that enhances its ornamental value.

The inflorescence consists of two-branched spikelets, which give it its specific epithet "bifidum.".
