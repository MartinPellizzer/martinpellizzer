Timonius affinis var. affinis

Timonius affinis var. affinis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-to-purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Timonius affinis var. affinis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
