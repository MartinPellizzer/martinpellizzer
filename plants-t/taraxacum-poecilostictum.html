Taraxacum poecilostictum

Taraxacum poecilostictum, commonly known as the speckled dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that terminates in a flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which are typically yellow in color. This species is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to environmental stress.
