Thryptomene naviculata

Thryptomene naviculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months.

Thryptomene naviculata is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
