Taraxacum shqipericum

Taraxacum shqipericum, commonly known as the Albanian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is native to the Balkans, particularly Albania, and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and toothed leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head.

Taraxacum shqipericum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
