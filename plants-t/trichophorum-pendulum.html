Trichophorum pendulum

Trichophorum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. Its inflorescence is a spike-like structure that hangs downward, giving it its common name.

Trichophorum pendulum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
