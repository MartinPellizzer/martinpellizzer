Trochocephalus macropodus

Trochocephalus macropodus, commonly known as the large-headed trumpet flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of clustered bracts that enclose the flowers, which are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
