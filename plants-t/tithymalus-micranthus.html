Tithymalus micranthus

Tithymalus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it conserve moisture in arid environments. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
