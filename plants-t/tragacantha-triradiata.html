Tragacantha triradiata

Tragacantha triradiata, commonly known as the three-rayed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial applications. Tragacantha triradiata thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodland edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
