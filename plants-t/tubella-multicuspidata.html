Tubella multicuspidata

Tubella multicuspidata, commonly known as the multicuspidate tubella, is a species of marine sponge belonging to the family Tethyidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical body with a porous surface that allows for water flow through its skeletal structure. The sponge's skeleton is composed of spicules, which are fine, needle-like structures that provide support and defense. It typically inhabits shallow coastal waters and is found in the Mediterranean Sea and the eastern Atlantic.

This species plays a role in marine ecosystems by filtering water and contributing to nutrient cycling.
