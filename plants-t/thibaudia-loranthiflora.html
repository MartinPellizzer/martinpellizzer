Thibaudia loranthiflora

Thibaudia loranthiflora, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a reddish hue when young. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of incense.
