Tacca oceanica

Tacca oceanica, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "tongue of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple, waxy leaves that resemble the shape of a bat's wings. The plant produces strikingly ornamental flowers that are often described as resembling a human face or a tongue, with a central red or orange bloom surrounded by white petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tacca oceanica is often cultivated for its unique and dramatic appearance in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
