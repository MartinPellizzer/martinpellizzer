Tephrosia pedleyi

Tephrosia pedleyi, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia pedleyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
