Triodia albescens

Triodia albescens, commonly known as the white spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh conditions.

Triodia albescens plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
