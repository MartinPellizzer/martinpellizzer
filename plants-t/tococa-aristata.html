Tococa aristata

Tococa aristata, commonly known as the Andean tea, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used to make a stimulating herbal tea. Tococa aristata thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
