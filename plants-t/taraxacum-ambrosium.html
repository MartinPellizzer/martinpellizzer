Taraxacum ambrosium

Taraxacum ambrosium, commonly known as the blessed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various bioactive compounds, contributing to its medicinal properties. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its life cycle includes a rosette of leaves followed by a flowering stem that produces seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.
