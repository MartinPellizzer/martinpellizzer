Taraxacum albanicum

Taraxacum albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed sphere with feathery seeds. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

Taraxacum albanicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
