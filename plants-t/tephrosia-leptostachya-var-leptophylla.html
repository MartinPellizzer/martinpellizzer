Tephrosia leptostachya var. leptophylla

Tephrosia leptostachya var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.
