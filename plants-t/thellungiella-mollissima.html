Thellungiella mollissima

Thellungiella mollissima, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a small annual herb with simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting tolerance to drought and high salinity.

Its genome has been studied for its potential in understanding plant stress responses and evolutionary biology.
