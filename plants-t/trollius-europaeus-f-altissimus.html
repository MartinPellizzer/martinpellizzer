Trollius europaeus f. altissimus

Trollius europaeus f. altissimus, commonly known as the tall globeflower, is a variant of the European globeflower characterized by its notably elongated stature.

This subspecies features showy, golden-yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to central and eastern Europe, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has palmate, hairy leaves and produces achenes as its fruit.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
