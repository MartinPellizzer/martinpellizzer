Trisetum flavescens f. senanense

Trisetum flavescens f. senanense, commonly known as the Senanese meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale yellow to golden florets.

This form is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
