Tulipa turgaica

Tulipa turgaica, commonly known as the Taurus tulip, is a species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Tulipa turgaica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
