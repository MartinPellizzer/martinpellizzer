Trianthema triquetrum

Trianthema triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features succulent, triangular-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trianthema triquetrum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
