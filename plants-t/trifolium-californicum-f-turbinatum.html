Trifolium californicum f. turbinatum

Trifolium californicum f. turbinatum, commonly known as the Turbinated California clover, is a variant of the California poppy.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
