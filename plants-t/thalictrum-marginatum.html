Thalictrum marginatum

Thalictrum marginatum, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum marginatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
