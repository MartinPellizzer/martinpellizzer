Trichilia albicans

Trichilia albicans, commonly known as white trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces oval, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
