Trigonella altissima

Trigonella altissima, commonly known as the tall fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in loose, racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are small and oblong, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Trigonella altissima is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions.
