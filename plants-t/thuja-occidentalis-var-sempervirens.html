Thuja occidentalis var. sempervirens

Thuja occidentalis var. sempervirens, commonly known as the Eastern arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are bright green on top and pale white underneath. The plant produces small, round cones that are typically less than an inch in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental landscaping.

This variety is highly resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its longevity and low maintenance requirements.
