Trachycaryon hookeri var. glabriusculum

Trachycaryon hookeri var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the smoothleaf hooker's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
