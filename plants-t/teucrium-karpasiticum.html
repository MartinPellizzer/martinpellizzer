Teucrium karpasiticum

Teucrium karpasiticum, commonly known as the Karpathian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often used in traditional medicine. It is often found in mountainous regions and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Teucrium karpasiticum is valued for its medicinal uses, particularly in treating digestive issues and skin conditions.
