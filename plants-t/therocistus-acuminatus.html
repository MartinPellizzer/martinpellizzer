Therocistus acuminatus

Therocistus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
