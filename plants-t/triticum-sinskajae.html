Triticum sinskajae

Triticum sinskajae, commonly known as the Siberian wheat, is a rare and ancient species of wheat native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact grains and robust root system, which allows it to thrive in harsh, cold climates. The plant has a short growth cycle and is adapted to low-nutrient soils, making it a resilient crop in extreme environments. Its genetic makeup is of great interest to researchers studying wheat evolution and climate adaptation.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable resource for agricultural biodiversity.
