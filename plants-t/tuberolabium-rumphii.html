Tuberolabium rumphii

Tuberolabium rumphii, commonly known as the Rumphius' orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a prominently labellum with a tuberous base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
