Taraxacum angustisquameum

Taraxacum angustisquameum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with the plant resuming growth in spring.
