Taraxacum epacrum

Taraxacum epacrum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, characterized by its composite flowers and milky sap. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations where competition from other plants is minimal.
