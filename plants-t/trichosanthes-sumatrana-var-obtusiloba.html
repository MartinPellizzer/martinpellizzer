Trichosanthes sumatrana var. obtusiloba

Trichosanthes sumatrana var. obtusiloba, commonly known as the blunt-leaf snake gourd, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, warty surface, and matures to a pale green or yellowish color. This plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
