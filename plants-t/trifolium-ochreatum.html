Trifolium ochreatum

Trifolium ochreatum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium ochreatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
