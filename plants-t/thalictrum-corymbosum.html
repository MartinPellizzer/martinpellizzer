Thalictrum corymbosum

Thalictrum corymbosum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a member of the Berberidaceae family and is native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring fern-like, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Thalictrum corymbosum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
