Trichosanthes amara

Trichosanthes amara, commonly known as bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruit that turns orange when ripe. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is used in various cuisines, particularly in South and Southeast Asia, and has traditional medicinal uses due to its bitter compounds.
