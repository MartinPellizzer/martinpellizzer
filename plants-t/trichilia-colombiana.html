Trichilia colombiana

Trichilia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine due to its bioactive compounds.
