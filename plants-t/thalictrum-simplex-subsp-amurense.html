Thalictrum simplex subsp. amurense

Thalictrum simplex subsp.

amurense, commonly known as the Amur meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The plant produces upright, spikelike clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle floral display.
