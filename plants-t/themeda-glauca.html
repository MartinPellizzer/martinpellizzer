Themeda glauca

Themeda glauca, commonly known as the blue grass or blue tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, two-lobed flowers.

Themeda glauca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small animals.
