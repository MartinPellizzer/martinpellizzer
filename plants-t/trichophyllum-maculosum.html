Trichophyllum maculosum

Trichophyllum maculosum, commonly known as the spotted beard-tongue, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive spotted leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Trichophyllum maculosum is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
