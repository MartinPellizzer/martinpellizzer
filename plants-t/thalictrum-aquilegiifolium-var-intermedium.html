Thalictrum aquilegiifolium var. intermedium

Thalictrum aquilegiifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a finely divided structure, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
