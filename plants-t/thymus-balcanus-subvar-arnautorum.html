Thymus balcanus subvar. arnautorum

Thymus balcanus subvar. arnautorum, commonly known as Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
