Tragacantha declinata

Tragacantha declinata, commonly known as the drooping tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, drooping inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Tragacantha declinata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
