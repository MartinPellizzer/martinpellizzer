Trimezia juncifolia subsp. cristallicola

Trimezia juncifolia subsp.

cristallicola, commonly known as the crystal leaf trimezia, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and leaves that are covered in fine, translucent hairs, giving them a crystalline appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
