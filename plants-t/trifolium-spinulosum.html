Trifolium spinulosum

Trifolium spinulosum, commonly known as the prickly clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium spinulosum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
