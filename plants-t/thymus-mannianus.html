Thymus mannianus

Thymus mannianus, commonly known as the Moroccan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Thymus mannianus is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
