Tragia nepetifolia var. ramosa

Tragia nepetifolia var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive, hairy, ovate leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a weed and a potential invasive species in some regions.
