Tulipa chrysobasis

Tulipa chrysobasis, commonly known as the yellow tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to deep gold. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climates, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their vibrant color and ornamental value.
