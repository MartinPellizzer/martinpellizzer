Trixis ochroleuca f. minor

Trixis ochroleuca f. minor, commonly known as the lesser white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-green, woolly leaves and pale yellow to white flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
