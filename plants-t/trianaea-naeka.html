Trianaea naeka

Trianaea naeka, commonly known as the Naeka tree, is a unique species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used by native Hawaiians for various purposes.

The Naeka tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
