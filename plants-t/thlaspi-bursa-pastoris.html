Thlaspi bursa-pastoris

Thlaspi bursa-pastoris, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Peppergrass is characterized by its distinctive seed pods, which are inflated and split open when mature, releasing small, rounded seeds.
