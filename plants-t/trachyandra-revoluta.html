Trachyandra revoluta

Trachyandra revoluta, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a unique texture. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Trachyandra revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
