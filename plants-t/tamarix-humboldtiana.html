Tamarix humboldtiana

Tamarix humboldtiana, commonly known as the desert rose or tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, scale-like leaves and small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

Tamarix humboldtiana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility in degraded areas.
