Tulipa grey-wilsonii

Tulipa grey-wilsonii, commonly known as the Grey-Wilson tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Tulipa grey-wilsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
