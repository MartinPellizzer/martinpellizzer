Trifolium michelianum f. lydium

Trifolium michelianum f. lydium, commonly known as the Lydian clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization.
