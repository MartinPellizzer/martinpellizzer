Teucrium expassum subsp. expassum

Teucrium expassum subsp.

expassum, commonly known as the common germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
