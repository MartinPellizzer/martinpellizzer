Tupistra aurantiaca

Tupistra aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, orange-red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by the distinctive fruit. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and a dark green color.

Tupistra aurantiaca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
