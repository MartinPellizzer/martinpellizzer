Tagetes signata var. pumila

Tagetes signata var. pumila, commonly known as the Mexican marigold or dwarf marigold, is a compact, annual herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening and as a companion plant due to its pest-repelling properties.
