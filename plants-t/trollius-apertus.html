Trollius apertus

Trollius apertus, commonly known as the showy goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, herbaceous stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Trollius apertus is a perennial plant that is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
