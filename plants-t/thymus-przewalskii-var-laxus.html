Thymus przewalskii var. laxus

Thymus przewalskii var. laxus, commonly known as the loose thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces small, pink or purple flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Thymus przewalskii var. laxus is also noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
