Tridax imbricatus

Tridax imbricatus, commonly known as the common tridax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tridax imbricatus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
