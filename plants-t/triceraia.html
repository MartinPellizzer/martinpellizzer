Triceraia

Triceraia, commonly known as the three-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its trifoliate structure, where each leaf is composed of three small, oval-shaped lobes.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are often a deep green, with a slightly waxy texture that helps retain moisture. Triceraia is also notable for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring, producing a mild, sweet fragrance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
