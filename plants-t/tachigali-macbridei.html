Tachigali macbridei

Tachigali macbridei, commonly known as the Macbride's tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist environments. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or white in color.
