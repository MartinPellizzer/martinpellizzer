Therorhodion camtschaticum var. pumilum

Therorhodion camtschaticum var. pumilum, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
