Triticum chinense

Triticum chinense, commonly known as Chinese wheat, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets. Its seeds are typically harvested for use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source.

Triticum chinense is often cultivated in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
