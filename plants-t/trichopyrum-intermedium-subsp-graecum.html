Trichopyrum intermedium subsp. graecum

Trichopyrum intermedium subsp.

graecum, commonly known as the Greek false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
