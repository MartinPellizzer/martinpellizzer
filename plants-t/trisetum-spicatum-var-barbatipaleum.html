Trisetum spicatum var. barbatipaleum

Trisetum spicatum var. barbatipaleum, commonly known as the barbate pale grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.

This variant is distinguished by its distinctive, barbed lemma hairs, which give it its specific epithet.
