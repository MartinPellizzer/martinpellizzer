Tricalysia fruticosa

Tricalysia fruticosa, commonly known as the African rose or African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a velvety texture. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Tricalysia fruticosa is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
