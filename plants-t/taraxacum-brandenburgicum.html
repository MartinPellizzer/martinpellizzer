Taraxacum brandenburgicum

Taraxacum brandenburgicum, commonly known as the Brandenburg dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for seed dispersal. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
