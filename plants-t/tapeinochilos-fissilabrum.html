Tapeinochilos fissilabrum

Tapeinochilos fissilabrum, commonly known as the fissured leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, strap-like leaves that are often split or fissured. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
