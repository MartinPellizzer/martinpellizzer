Taeniophyllum clavicalcaratum

Taeniophyllum clavicalcaratum, commonly known as the club-stemmed air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in a spadix-like structure.

Taeniophyllum clavicalcaratum is valued for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
