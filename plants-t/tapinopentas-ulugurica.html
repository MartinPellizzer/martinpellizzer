Tapinopentas ulugurica

Tapinopentas ulugurica, commonly known as the Uluguru rock fig, is a unique plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a small, woody shrub that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in the arid environment.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
