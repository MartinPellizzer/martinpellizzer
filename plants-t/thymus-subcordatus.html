Thymus subcordatus

Thymus subcordatus, commonly known as the subcordate thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a strong, pungent aroma.

Thymus subcordatus is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
