Tristemma virusanum

Tristemma virusanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is an important host for several viruses, including the velvetleaf mosaic virus.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
