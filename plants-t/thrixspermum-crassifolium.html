Thrixspermum crassifolium

Thrixspermum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and large, thick, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
