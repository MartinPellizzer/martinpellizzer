Themeda triandra var. japonica

Themeda triandra var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscape design.
