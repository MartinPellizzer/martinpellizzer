Thymelaea nitida var. scoparia

Thymelaea nitida var. scoparia, commonly known as the silver thyme.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
