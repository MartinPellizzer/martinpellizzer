Thymus serpyllum var. danaenii

Thymus serpyllum var. danaenii, commonly known as Dana's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
