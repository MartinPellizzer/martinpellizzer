Theobroma montanum

Theobroma montanum, commonly known as the mountain cocoa tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are used in the production of cocoa products. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
