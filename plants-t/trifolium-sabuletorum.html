Trifolium sabuletorum

Trifolium sabuletorum, commonly known as yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. This perennial plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Trifolium sabuletorum is also valued for its role in ecological systems and as a forage crop for livestock.
