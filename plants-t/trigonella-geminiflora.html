Trigonella geminiflora

Trigonella geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it a unique species within the genus Trigonella.
