Trigonella Rotundifolia

Trigonella rotundifolia is a small flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is known for its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. This species thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sun-exposed environments. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine. Its resilience in harsh climates makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
