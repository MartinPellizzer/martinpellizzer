Thermopsis saurensis

Thermopsis saurensis, commonly known as the Siberian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces slender, erect stems.

Thermopsis saurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
