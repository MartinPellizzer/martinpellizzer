Trapa bicornis var. jinumae

Trapa bicornis var. jinumae, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, spongy fruit that floats on the water surface. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are narrow and submerged, while the floating fruit contains edible seeds.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for culinary purposes.
