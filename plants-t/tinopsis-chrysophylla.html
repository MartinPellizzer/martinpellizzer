Tinopsis chrysophylla

Tinopsis chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in a fine, golden-yellow powder, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically found on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Tinopsis chrysophylla is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
