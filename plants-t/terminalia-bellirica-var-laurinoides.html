Terminalia bellirica var. laurinoides

Terminalia bellirica var. laurinoides, commonly known as Indian black teak or wild teak, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, tropical forests.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.
