Toulichiba

Toulichiba, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and cones that hang from the branches. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
