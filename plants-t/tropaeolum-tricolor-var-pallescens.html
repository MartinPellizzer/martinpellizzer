Tropaeolum tricolor var. pallescens

Tropaeolum tricolor var. pallescens, commonly known as the white-veined nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its pale green leaves with white veins.

This plant is native to South America and belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family. It produces edible yellow-orange flowers and peppery tasting leaves, similar to its parent species. The varietal name 'pallescens' refers to its pale or whitish appearance, particularly in the foliage.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and for its edible parts in gardens and culinary applications.
