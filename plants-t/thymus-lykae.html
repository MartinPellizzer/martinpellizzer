Thymus lykae

Thymus lykae, commonly known as the Greek thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Thymus lykae is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
