Triglochin flaccida

Triglochin flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, exhibiting a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

Triglochin flaccida plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
