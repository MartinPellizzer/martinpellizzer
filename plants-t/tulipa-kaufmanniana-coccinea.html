Tulipa kaufmanniana coccinea

Tulipa kaufmanniana coccinea, commonly known as the red Kaufmann's tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and features strap-like, dark green foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This tulip is known for its reliable performance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
