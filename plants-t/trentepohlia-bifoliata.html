Trentepohlia bifoliata

Trentepohlia bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaf Trentepohlia, is a green alga belonging to the family Trentepohliaceae.

It is characterized by its filamentous growth and the presence of two leaf-like structures, or phylloids, on each branch. This alga thrives in moist environments, often found on soil, rocks, or decaying wood. Its chlorophyll content gives it a vibrant green color, making it easily distinguishable from other algae.

Trentepohlia bifoliata plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various invertebrates.
