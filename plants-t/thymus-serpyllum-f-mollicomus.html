Thymus serpyllum f. mollicomus

Thymus serpyllum f. mollicomus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, fuzzy foliage that is typically a gray-green color, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
