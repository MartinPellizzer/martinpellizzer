Trigonella × schweinfurthiana

Trigonella × schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the hybrid fenugreek, is a cross between Trigonella foenum-graecum and Trigonella caerulea.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both parent species, making it a valuable subject for genetic studies.
