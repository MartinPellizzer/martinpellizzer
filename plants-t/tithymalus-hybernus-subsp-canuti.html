Tithymalus hybernus subsp. canuti

Tithymalus hybernus subsp.

canuti, commonly known as the Irish bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
