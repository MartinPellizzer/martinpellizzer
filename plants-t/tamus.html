Tamus

Tamus, commonly known as the "bog asparagus," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliales.

It consists of several species, most notably Tamus communis, which is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wetlands.

Tamus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
