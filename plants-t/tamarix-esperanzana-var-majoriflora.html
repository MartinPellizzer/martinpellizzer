Tamarix esperanzana var. majoriflora

Tamarix esperanzana var. majoriflora, commonly known as the desert rose, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or saline soils. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful for soil stabilization and erosion control.

However, it can become invasive in some areas due to its aggressive spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
