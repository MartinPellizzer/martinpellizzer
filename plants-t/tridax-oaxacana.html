Tridax oaxacana

Tridax oaxacana, commonly known as Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and roadsides, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with white petals.

Tridax oaxacana is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its use in traditional medicine.
