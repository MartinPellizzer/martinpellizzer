Trisetopsis angusta

Trisetopsis angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf trisetopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
