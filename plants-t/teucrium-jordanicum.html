Teucrium jordanicum

Teucrium jordanicum, commonly known as Jordanian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, often blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
