Trymalium behrii

Trymalium behrii, commonly known as the Behri's mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, gray-green foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, producing white to pale yellow blooms.

Trymalium behrii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
