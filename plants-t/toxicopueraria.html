Toxicopueraria

Toxicopueraria, commonly known as the poison vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant contains various toxic alkaloids, making it harmful to both humans and animals. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though with caution due to its poisonous nature.

Toxicopueraria thrives in humid, shaded environments and can quickly spread in disturbed soils.
