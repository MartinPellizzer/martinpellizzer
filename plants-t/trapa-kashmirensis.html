Trapa kashmirensis

Trapa kashmirensis, commonly known as the Kashmir water chestnut, is a floating aquatic plant native to the freshwater lakes and wetlands of the Kashmir region.

It features distinctive, spiny, floating fruits that remain buoyant after maturity, aiding in seed dispersal. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide habitat for aquatic organisms. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.

Trapa kashmirensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
