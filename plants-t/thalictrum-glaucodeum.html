Thalictrum glaucodeum

Thalictrum glaucodeum, commonly known as the blue false fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are bright green with a bluish tinge, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and pale blue, which are clustered in upright racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Thalictrum glaucodeum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture to woodland gardens.
