Tamarix allorgeana

Tamarix allorgeana, commonly known as the allorge tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it resilient to drought and poor soil conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Tamarix allorgeana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils in erosion-prone areas.
