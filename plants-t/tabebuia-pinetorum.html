Tabebuia pinetorum

Tabebuia pinetorum, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, light gray bark and produces elongated, woody fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a velvety texture.

Tabebuia pinetorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
