Thymus × welwitschii subsp. ilerdensis

Thymus × welwitschii subsp.

ilerdensis, commonly known as the Iberian thyme, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, dark green leaves and its clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is typically found in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and woodland edges. It has a low-growing habit and is valued for its medicinal properties and use in culinary applications.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
