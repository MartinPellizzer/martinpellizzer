Teucrium corymbosum var. typicum

Teucrium corymbosum var. typicum, commonly known as blue geranium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, serrated, and aromatic, with a strong, mint-like scent.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
