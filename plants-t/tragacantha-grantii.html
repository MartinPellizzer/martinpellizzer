Tragacantha grantii

Tragacantha grantii, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia, particularly Iran. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, compound leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is well adapted to dry environments, showcasing drought tolerance and deep root systems.
