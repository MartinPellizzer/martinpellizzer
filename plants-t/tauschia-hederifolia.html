Tauschia hederifolia

Tauschia hederifolia, commonly known as creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
