Tainia malayana

Tainia malayana, commonly known as the Malay arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating in water. The plant has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

Tainia malayana is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds.
