Triglochin chilensis

Triglochin chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and lake margins, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering stems bear tiny, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

Triglochin chilensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
