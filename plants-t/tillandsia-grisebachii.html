Tillandsia grisebachii

Tillandsia grisebachii, commonly known as the silver pine tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that traps moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant's leaves are typically grayish-green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain water in its arid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Tillandsia grisebachii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
