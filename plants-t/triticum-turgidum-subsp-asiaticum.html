Triticum turgidum subsp. asiaticum

Triticum turgidum subsp.

asiaticum, commonly known as Chinese spring wheat, is a wild wheat species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and compact spikes.

The plant produces small, straw-colored grains that are typically harvested when mature. It is often used in agricultural research due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.

This subspecies plays a significant role in the evolution of modern wheat varieties.
