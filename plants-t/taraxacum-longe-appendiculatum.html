Taraxacum longe-appendiculatum

Taraxacum longe-appendiculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the familiar white puffball seed heads. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common yet distinctive member of the genus Taraxacum.
