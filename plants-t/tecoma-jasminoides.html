Tecoma jasminoides

Tecoma jasminoides, commonly known as yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy appearance.

Tecoma jasminoides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
