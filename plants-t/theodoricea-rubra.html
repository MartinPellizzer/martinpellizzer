Theodoricea rubra

Theodoricea rubra, commonly known as the scarlet flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of soft hairs. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

Theodoricea rubra thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
