Thymus longicaulis subsp. chaubardii

Thymus longicaulis subsp.

chaubardii, commonly known as Chaubard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features small, aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
