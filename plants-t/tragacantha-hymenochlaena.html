Tragacantha hymenochlaena

Tragacantha hymenochlaena, commonly known as the silky tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
