Teucrium viscidum var. macrostephanum

Teucrium viscidum var. macrostephanum, commonly known as sticky germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often blooming in spring and summer. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
