Thymus jurtzevii

Thymus jurtzevii, commonly known as the Siberian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thymus jurtzevii is also an ornamental plant, appreciated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
