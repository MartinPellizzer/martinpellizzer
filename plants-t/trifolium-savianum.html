Trifolium savianum

Trifolium savianum, commonly known as the Savian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Trifolium savianum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
