Thymus plasonii

Thymus plasonii, commonly known as the Plason's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Thymus plasonii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

Its aromatic foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
