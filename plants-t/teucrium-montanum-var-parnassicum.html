Teucrium montanum var. parnassicum

Teucrium montanum var. parnassicum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
