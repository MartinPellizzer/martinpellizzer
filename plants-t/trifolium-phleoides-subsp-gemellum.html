Trifolium phleoides subsp. gemellum

Trifolium phleoides subsp.

gemellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage plant.
