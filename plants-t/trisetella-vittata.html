Trisetella vittata

Trisetella vittata, commonly known as the spotted grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive yellowish or brownish stripe running along the midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets.

Trisetella vittata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
