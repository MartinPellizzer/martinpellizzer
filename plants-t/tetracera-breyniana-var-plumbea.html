Tetracera breyniana var. plumbea

Tetracera breyniana var. plumbea, commonly known as the blue-leaf tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-green leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
