Trixis aggregata

Trixis aggregata, commonly known as the clustered trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience to drought, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
