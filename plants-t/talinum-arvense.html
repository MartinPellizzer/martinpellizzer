Talinum arvense

Talinum arvense, commonly known as field spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Talinum arvense is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
