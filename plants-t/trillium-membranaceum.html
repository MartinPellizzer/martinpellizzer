Trillium membranaceum

Trillium membranaceum, commonly known as the parchment trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its three broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in a thin, waxy coating. The plant produces a single, nodding white or pale pink flower with three petals and three sepals. It typically blooms in early spring and thrives in moist, shaded forest environments.

Trillium membranaceum is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
