Trisetum barbinode var. hirtiflorum

Trisetum barbinode var. hirtiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture due to fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often hairy, giving the plant its common name.
