Thelycrania australis var. microcarpa

Thelycrania australis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf mountain heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of southern Australia.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
