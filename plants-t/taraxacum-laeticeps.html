Taraxacum laeticeps

Taraxacum laeticeps, commonly known as the cheerful dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and a distinctive, hollow, leafy stem. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, this plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces a capsule-like fruit that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common and easily identifiable weed in many regions.
