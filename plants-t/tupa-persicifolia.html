Tupa persicifolia

Tupa persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that resemble peach leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible. Tupa persicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
