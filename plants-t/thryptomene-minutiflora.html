Thryptomene minutiflora

Thryptomene minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils.

Thryptomene minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
