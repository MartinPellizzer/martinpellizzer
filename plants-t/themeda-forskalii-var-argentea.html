Themeda forskalii var. argentea

Themeda forskalii var. argentea, commonly known as silver grass, is a variant of the species Themeda forskalii, which is native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, which are often visible during the flowering season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
