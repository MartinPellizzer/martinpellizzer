Tainia laxiflora

Tainia laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf tainia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Tainia laxiflora produces striking white to pale yellow spadices surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
