Thysselinum sylvestre

Thysselinum sylvestre, commonly known as the wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thysselinum sylvestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
