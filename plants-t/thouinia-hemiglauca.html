Thouinia hemiglauca

Thouinia hemiglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf thouinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
