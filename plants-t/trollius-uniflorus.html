Trollius uniflorus

Trollius uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, large, showy yellow flower at the top of a hairy stem. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Trollius uniflorus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
