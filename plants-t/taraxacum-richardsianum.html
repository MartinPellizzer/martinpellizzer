Taraxacum richardsianum

Taraxacum richardsianum, commonly known as the Richards' dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that matures into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
