Tamonea cubensis

Tamonea cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, narrow corolla that resembles a lily. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Tamonea cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
