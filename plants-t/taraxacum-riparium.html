Taraxacum riparium

Taraxacum riparium, commonly known as the river dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a slender, leafy stem that bears a single yellow flower head. The plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. Its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous small florets that mature into parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum riparium is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
