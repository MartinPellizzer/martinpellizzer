Teucrium polium subsp. vincentinum

Teucrium polium subsp.

vincentinum, commonly known as Vincent's germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates.

The plant features aromatic, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Vincent's germander is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
