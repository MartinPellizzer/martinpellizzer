Theobroma cacao subsp. sphaerocarpum

Theobroma cacao subsp.

sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the Amazonian cacao, is a subspecies native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers.

The fruit, known as a capsule, is typically round or oval and contains a rich paste of seeds used in chocolate production. This subspecies is often cultivated for its high-quality cacao beans, which are prized for their flavor and aroma.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
