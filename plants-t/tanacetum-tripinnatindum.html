Tanacetum tripinnatindum

Tanacetum tripinnatindum, commonly known as the three-parted tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Tanacetum tripinnatindum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
