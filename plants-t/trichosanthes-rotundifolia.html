Trichosanthes rotundifolia

Trichosanthes rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf snake gourd, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, slender fruits that resemble snakes. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly grown in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses in some Asian countries.
