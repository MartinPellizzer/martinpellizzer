Thlaspi crassifolium

Thlaspi crassifolium, commonly known as broadleaf cabbageweed, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has a taproot system and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Thlaspi crassifolium is also known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential phytoremediation candidate.
