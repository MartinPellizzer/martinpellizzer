Trapa maximowiczii var. tonkinensis

Trapa maximowiczii var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are typically round with a short petiole, and the flowers are small and yellow-green in color.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
