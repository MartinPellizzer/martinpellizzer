Terminalia macrantha

Terminalia macrantha, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
