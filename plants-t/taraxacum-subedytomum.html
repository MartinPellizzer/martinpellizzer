Taraxacum subedytomum

Taraxacum subedytomum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces fluffy seeds. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is considered a weed in some areas but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
