Talinum teretifolium

Talinum teretifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf malabar spinach, is a tropical flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a peppery flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable in home gardens.

Talinum teretifolium is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil types.
