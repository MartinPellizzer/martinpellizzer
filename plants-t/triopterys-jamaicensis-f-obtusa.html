Triopterys jamaicensis f. obtusa

Triopterys jamaicensis f. obtusa, commonly known as the Jamaican broadleaf tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
