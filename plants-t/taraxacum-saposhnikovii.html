Taraxacum saposhnikovii

Taraxacum saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia, including Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow, forming a composite inflorescence.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.
