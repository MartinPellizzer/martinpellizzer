Taraxacum graminicolor

Taraxacum graminicolor, commonly known as the grassy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces wind-dispersed seeds. Native to Europe, it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions.
