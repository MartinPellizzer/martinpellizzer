Trifolium matthewsii

Trifolium matthewsii, commonly known as Matthews' clover, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the tips of the stems. Matthews' clover is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
