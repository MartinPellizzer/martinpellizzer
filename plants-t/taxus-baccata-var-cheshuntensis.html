Taxus baccata var. cheshuntensis

Taxus baccata var. cheshuntensis, commonly known as the Cheshunt copper.

This variety is a cultivar of the yew tree, characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown foliage. It is a slow-growing evergreen with a compact, rounded shape. The leaves are narrow, flat, and have a leathery texture, turning a striking copper color in the autumn.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resistance to pests and diseases.
