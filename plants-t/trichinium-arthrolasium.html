Trichinium arthrolasium

Trichinium arthrolasium, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a delicate and rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
