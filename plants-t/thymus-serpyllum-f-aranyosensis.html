Thymus serpyllum f. aranyosensis

Thymus serpyllum f. aranyosensis, commonly known as the Hungarian thyme, is a subspecies of the common thyme that is native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
