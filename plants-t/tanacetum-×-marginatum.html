Tanacetum × marginatum

Tanacetum × marginatum, commonly known as the Marginal Leaved Tansy, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic properties.

Tanacetum × marginatum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
