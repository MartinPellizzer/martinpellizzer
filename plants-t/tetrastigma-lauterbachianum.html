Tetrastigma lauterbachianum

Tetrastigma lauterbachianum, commonly known as the climbing raisin vine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are edible, turning from green to dark purple as they mature.
