Telanthera junciflora

Telanthera junciflora, commonly known as the marsh mallow or swamp mallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Telanthera junciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
