Tragacantha plattensis

Tragacantha plattensis, commonly known as the Platt's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by hairy pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Tragacantha plattensis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
