Tabebuia obtusifolia

Tabebuia obtusifolia, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has smooth, greyish-bark and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.
