Tropaeolum cirrhipes

Tropaeolum cirrhipes, commonly known as the scarlet runner cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to watercress.

Tropaeolum cirrhipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a ornamental or edible plant.
