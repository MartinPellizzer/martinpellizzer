Taraxacum amabile

Taraxacum amabile, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. It produces a distinctive seed head known as a "puffball," which releases lightweight seeds with feathery parachutes.

This plant is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often considered a weed, though it has medicinal and ecological significance.
