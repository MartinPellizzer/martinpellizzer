Tetramolopium piloso-villosum

Tetramolopium piloso-villosum, commonly known as the hairy tetramolopium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
