Tovara virginiana

Tovara virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia tovara, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields. It produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its historical use by indigenous peoples, it has cultural significance in the region.
