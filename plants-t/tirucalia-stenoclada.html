Tirucalia stenoclada

Tirucalia stenoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
