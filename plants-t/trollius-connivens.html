Trollius connivens

Trollius connivens, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Trollius connivens is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
