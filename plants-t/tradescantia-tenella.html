Tradescantia tenella

Tradescantia tenella, commonly known as spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
