Tragacantha austriaca

Tragacantha austriaca, commonly known as Austrian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is well-known for its gelatinous sap, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Tragacantha austriaca thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
