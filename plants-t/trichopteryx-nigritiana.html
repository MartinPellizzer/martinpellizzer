Trichopteryx nigritiana

Trichopteryx nigritiana, commonly known as the blackleaf cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Trichopteryx nigritiana is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
