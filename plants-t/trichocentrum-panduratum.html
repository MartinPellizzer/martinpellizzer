Trichocentrum panduratum

Trichocentrum panduratum, commonly known as the panduratum orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, hanging inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.

Trichocentrum panduratum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
