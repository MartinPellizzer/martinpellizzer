Trichodesma decurrens

Trichodesma decurrens, commonly known as the creeping trichodesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb with prostrate or semi-erect growth habit, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

Trichodesma decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
