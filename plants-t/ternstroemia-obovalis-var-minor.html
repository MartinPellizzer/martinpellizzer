Ternstroemia obovalis var. minor

Ternstroemia obovalis var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf ternstroemia, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its attractive foliage.
