Tragacantha prolixa

Tragacantha prolixa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and pods that contain several seeds, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
