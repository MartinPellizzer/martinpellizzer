Tunica taygetea

Tunica taygetea, commonly known as the Greek tunic, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Tunica taygetea is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
