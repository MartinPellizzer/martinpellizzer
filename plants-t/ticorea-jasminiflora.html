Ticorea jasminiflora

Ticorea jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
