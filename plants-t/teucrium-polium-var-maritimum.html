Teucrium polium var. maritimum

Teucrium polium var. maritimum, commonly known as the sea germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, saline environments.
