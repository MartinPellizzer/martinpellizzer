Trollius ledebourii f. polysepalus

Trollius ledebourii f. polysepalus, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with numerous petals that are often fused at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
