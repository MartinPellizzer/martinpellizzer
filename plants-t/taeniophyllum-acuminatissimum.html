Taeniophyllum acuminatissimum

Taeniophyllum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the slender liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leafy structure and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. The plant has a thalloid body, which is a flat, ribbon-like structure covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of the thallus.

This species is often used in ecological studies to monitor air quality and habitat conditions due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
