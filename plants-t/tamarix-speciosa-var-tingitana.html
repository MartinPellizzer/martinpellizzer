Tamarix speciosa var. tingitana

Tamarix speciosa var. tingitana, commonly known as the Moroccan tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in summer, which are clustered in elongated panicles. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
