Trichomanes cristatum

Trichomanes cristatum, commonly known as the crested huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often branched fronds that can resemble small trees or shrubs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

Trichomanes cristatum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
