Triticum glaucum f. pilosum

Triticum glaucum f. pilosum, commonly known as the silky wheat, is a botanical variant of the wheat species Triticum glaucum.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray glumes and awns, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in grasslands or disturbed soils. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its "pilosum" designation, meaning "hairy" in Latin.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
