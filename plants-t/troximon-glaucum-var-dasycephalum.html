Troximon glaucum var. dasycephalum

Troximon glaucum var. dasycephalum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
