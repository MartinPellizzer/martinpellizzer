Torilis heterophylla var. homoeomorpha

Torilis heterophylla var. homoeomorpha, commonly known as the variable thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides. Its leaves are variable in shape, ranging from lanceolate to ovate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
