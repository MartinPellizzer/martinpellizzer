Taxus baccata var. erecta

Taxus baccata var. erecta, commonly known as the upright yew, is a variant of the European yew tree.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its bark is thick, scaly, and dark reddish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
