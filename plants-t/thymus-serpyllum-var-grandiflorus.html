Thymus serpyllum var. grandiflorus

Thymus serpyllum var. grandiflorus, commonly known as largeflower thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, creeping stems that produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in seasoning and herbal teas.
