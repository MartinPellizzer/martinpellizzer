Trichilia claussenii

Trichilia claussenii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically clustered in panicles, and it produces oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
