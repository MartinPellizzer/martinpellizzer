Tragacantha heterodoxa

Tragacantha heterodoxa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legumes containing a sticky, resinous substance.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
