Tontelea corcovadensis

Tontelea corcovadensis, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Tontelea corcovadensis thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
