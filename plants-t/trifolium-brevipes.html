Trifolium brevipes

Trifolium brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Trifolium brevipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
