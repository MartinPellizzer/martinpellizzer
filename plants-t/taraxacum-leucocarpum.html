Taraxacum leucocarpum

Taraxacum leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-haired, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for seed dispersal. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and meadows. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The white-hairy dandelion is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
