Teucrium macrostachyum

Teucrium macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stemmed germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in dry, coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Teucrium macrostachyum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
