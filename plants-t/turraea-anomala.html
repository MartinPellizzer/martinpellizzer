Turraea anomala

Turraea anomala, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
