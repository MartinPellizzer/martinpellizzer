Trifolium pilulare

Trifolium pilulare, commonly known as the pillared clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Trifolium pilulare is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
