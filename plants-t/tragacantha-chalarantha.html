Tragacantha chalarantha

Tragacantha chalarantha, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant produces a gum resin, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its bark and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Tragacantha chalarantha thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
