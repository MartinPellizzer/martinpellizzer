Tillandsia hartwegiana

Tillandsia hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant features rosette-shaped foliage that is covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in capturing water and light. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
