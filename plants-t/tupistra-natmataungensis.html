Tupistra natmataungensis

Tupistra natmataungensis, commonly known as the Burmese yew, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and distinctive cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
