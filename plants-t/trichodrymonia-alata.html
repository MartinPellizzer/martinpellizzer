Trichodrymonia alata

Trichodrymonia alata, commonly known as the featherleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Trichodrymonia alata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
