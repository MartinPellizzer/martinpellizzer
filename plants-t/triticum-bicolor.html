Triticum bicolor

Triticum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored wheat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicolor leaves, which display a mix of green and white or silver hues. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a tough husk, and the grains are used for various agricultural purposes.

Triticum bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its potential as a forage crop.
