Tournefortia laurifolia

Tournefortia laurifolia, commonly known as the tropical evergreen shrub, is a species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
