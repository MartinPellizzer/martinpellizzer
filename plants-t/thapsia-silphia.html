Thapsia silphia

Thapsia silphia, commonly known as the silphium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Thapsia silphia is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its dried form.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and was historically valued for its medicinal properties.
