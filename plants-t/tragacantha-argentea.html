Tragacantha argentea

Tragacantha argentea, commonly known as silver tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a viscous gum, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food thickener. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique properties and ecological adaptations, it is of interest in both traditional and modern botanical studies.
