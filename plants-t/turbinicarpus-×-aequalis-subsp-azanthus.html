Turbinicarpus × aequalis subsp. azanthus

Turbinicarpus × aequalis subsp.

azanthus, commonly known as the Desert Rose or Aztec Sun. It is a hybrid cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico, characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and vibrant, showy flowers.

The plant typically produces pink or red blooms during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
