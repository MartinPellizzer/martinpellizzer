Tilia amurensis var. koreana

Tilia amurensis var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
