Trichosanthes wightiana

Trichosanthes wightiana, commonly known as the Indian pumpkin or Wight's snake gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, warty fruits. Its fruits are typically used in traditional Indian cuisine and medicine.
