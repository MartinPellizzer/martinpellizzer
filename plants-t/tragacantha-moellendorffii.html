Tragacantha moellendorffii

Tragacantha moellendorffii, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub or small tree structure. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that are often clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its bark is rich in tragacanth, a gum used historically in medicine and industry.
