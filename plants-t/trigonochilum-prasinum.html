Trigonochilum prasinum

Trigonochilum prasinum, commonly known as the green orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and slender, greenish-yellow flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are relatively simple, with three sepals and three petals, and are often tinged with a pale green or white hue.

Trigonochilum prasinum is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique, subtle coloration.
