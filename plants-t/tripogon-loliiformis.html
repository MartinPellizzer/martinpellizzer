Tripogon loliiformis

Tripogon loliiformis, commonly known as the flax lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used for agricultural purposes.
