Tectona australis

Tectona australis, commonly known as the Australian teak, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
