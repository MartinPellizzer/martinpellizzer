Thalictrum angustatum

Thalictrum angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in early to mid-summer.

Thalictrum angustatum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
