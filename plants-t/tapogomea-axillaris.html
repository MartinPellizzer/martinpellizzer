Tapogomea axillaris

Tapogomea axillaris, commonly known as the axillary spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
