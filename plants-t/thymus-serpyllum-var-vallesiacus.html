Thymus serpyllum var. vallesiacus

Thymus serpyllum var. vallesiacus, commonly known as valley thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
