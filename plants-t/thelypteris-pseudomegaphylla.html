Thelypteris pseudomegaphylla

Thelypteris pseudomegaphylla, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas.

The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, reflecting its ecological and cultural significance.
