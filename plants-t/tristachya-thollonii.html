Tristachya thollonii

Tristachya thollonii, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or tristachya, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Tristachya thollonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
