Thymus sipyleus var. davisianus

Thymus sipyleus var. davisianus, commonly known as Davis's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
