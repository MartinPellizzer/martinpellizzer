Trichocline cineraria var. pulvinata

Trichocline cineraria var. pulvinata, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are covered in fine trichomes, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
