Thinopyrum pungens

Thinopyrum pungens, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, awned florets. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, linear leaves with prominent veins. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Thinopyrum pungens is also valued for its potential in forage and as a source of genetic material for wheat improvement.
