Tylecodon sulphureus var. armianus

Tylecodon sulphureus var. armianus, commonly known as the Armenian golden bush, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It features dense, branching stems covered in small, fleshy leaves that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
