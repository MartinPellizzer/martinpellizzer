Trichocereus smrzianus

Trichocereus smrzianus, commonly known as the "Cristalino cactus," is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its spines are typically short and white, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Trichocereus smrzianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
