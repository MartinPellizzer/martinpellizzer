Tephrosia purpurea var. pubescens

Tephrosia purpurea var. pubescens, commonly known as purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Tephrosia purpurea var. pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
