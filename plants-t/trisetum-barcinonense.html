Trisetum barcinonense

Trisetum barcinonense, commonly known as the Barcino sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in horticulture for erosion control and as a decorative plant in wetland gardens.

Trisetum barcinonense is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in wetland habitats.
