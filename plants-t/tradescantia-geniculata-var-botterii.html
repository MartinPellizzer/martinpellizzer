Tradescantia geniculata var. botterii

Tradescantia geniculata var. botterii, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with striking red or purple margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate watering.

Its air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
