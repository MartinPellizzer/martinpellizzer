Topobea gracilis

Topobea gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its growth habit is terrestrial, with rhizomes that allow it to spread slowly through the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
