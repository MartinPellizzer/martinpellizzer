Taeniophyllum kapahense

Taeniophyllum kapahense, commonly known as the Cape Island fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the coastal areas of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments along rocky outcrops and coastal cliffs. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
