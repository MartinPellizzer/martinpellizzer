Terminalia angustifolia

Terminalia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, disc-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist lowland areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
