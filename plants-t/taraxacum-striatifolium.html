Taraxacum striatifolium

Taraxacum striatifolium, commonly known as the striated dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with distinct longitudinal stripes. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed soils and meadows.
