Trasus clandestinus

Trasus clandestinus, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a rare and elusive plant species characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that resemble the shape of a fern.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in dense forest understories where light is limited. The plant's unique ability to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter allows it to flourish in nutrient-poor soils. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, producing spores that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Trasus clandestinus is considered a sensitive indicator of undisturbed ecosystems.
