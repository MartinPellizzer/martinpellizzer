Trifolium nivale var. sanguineum

Trifolium nivale var. sanguineum, commonly known as the crimson snow clover, is a variant of the snow clover species native to alpine regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
