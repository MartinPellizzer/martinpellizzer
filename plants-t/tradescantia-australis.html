Tradescantia australis

Tradescantia australis, commonly known as the Australian spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Australia and New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly waxy texture.

Tradescantia australis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
