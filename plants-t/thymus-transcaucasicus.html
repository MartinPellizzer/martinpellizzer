Thymus transcaucasicus

Thymus transcaucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils and antioxidant properties.

Thymus transcaucasicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
