Trisetum alpestre subsp. glabrescens

Trisetum alpestre subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the alpine hairgrass, is a grass species native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets. Trisetum alpestre subsp.

glabrescens is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
