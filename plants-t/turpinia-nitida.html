Turpinia nitida

Turpinia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, lobed leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences of small, pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Turpinia nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
