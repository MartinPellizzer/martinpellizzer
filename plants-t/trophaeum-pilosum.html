Trophaeum pilosum

Trophaeum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often appear in clusters, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Trophaeum pilosum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
