Tithymalus dracunculoides subsp. volutianus

Tithymalus dracunculoides subsp.

volutianus, commonly known as the spiral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
