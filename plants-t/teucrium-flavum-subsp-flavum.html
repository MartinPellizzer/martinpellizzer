Teucrium flavum subsp. flavum

Teucrium flavum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
