Timonius avenis var. scabriflorus

Timonius avenis var. scabriflorus, commonly known as the roughflower timonius, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough or scabrous surface, contributing to its common name.

This species is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
