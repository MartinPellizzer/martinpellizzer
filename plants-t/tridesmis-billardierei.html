Tridesmis billardierei

Tridesmis billardierei, commonly known as the white-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in southern Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy, red berries.

Tridesmis billardierei is an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for native wildlife.
