Taraxacum cerebriforme

Taraxacum cerebriforme, commonly known as the brain-shaped dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed, brain-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball with fine, feathery seeds. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and recognizable member of the dandelion genus.
