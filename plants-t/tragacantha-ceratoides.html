Tragacantha ceratoides

Tragacantha ceratoides, commonly known as the horned tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces distinctive yellow flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is known for its sticky resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
