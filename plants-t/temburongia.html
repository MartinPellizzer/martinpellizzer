Temburongia

Temburongia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and slightly serrated, while its flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.
