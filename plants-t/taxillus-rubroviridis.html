Taxillus rubroviridis

Taxillus rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized structures called haustoria, while also performing photosynthesis to some extent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Taxillus rubroviridis plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
