Thladiantha calcarata var. tonkinensis

Thladiantha calcarata var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin thladiantha, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
