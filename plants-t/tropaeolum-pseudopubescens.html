Tropaeolum pseudopubescens

Tropaeolum pseudopubescens, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble cherry tomatoes, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tropaeolum pseudopubescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
