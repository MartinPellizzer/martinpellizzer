Trichomanes crassipilis

Trichomanes crassipilis, commonly known as the broad-haired maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes crassipilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
