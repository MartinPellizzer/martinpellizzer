Tripogon leptophyllus

Tripogon leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf tripogon, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is surrounded by several bracts.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various small animals and insects.
