Tradescantia cerinthoides

Tradescantia cerinthoides, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and a waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and attractive foliage plant.
