Tamarix speciosa var. calarantha

Tamarix speciosa var. calarantha, commonly known as the desert rose or pink tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils and withstand extreme temperatures. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important pioneer species in disturbed or degraded landscapes.

However, it is also considered invasive in some areas due to its aggressive spread and water consumption.
