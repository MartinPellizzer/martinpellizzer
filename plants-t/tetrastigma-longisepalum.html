Tetrastigma longisepalum

Tetrastigma longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its botanical features include elongated sepals and a complex inflorescence structure.
