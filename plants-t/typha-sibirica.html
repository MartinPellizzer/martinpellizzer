Typha sibirica

Typha sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to Eurasia.

It features tall, cylindrical flower spikes that produce fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. The plant has broad, sword-shaped leaves and thrives in shallow water or moist soil. It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
