Tinus foetida

Tinus foetida, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Tinus foetida plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
