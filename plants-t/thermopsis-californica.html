Thermopsis californica

Thermopsis californica, commonly known as California goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow, cone-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hair, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
