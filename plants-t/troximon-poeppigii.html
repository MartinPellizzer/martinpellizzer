Troximon poeppigii

Troximon poeppigii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its small, woody stems are adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Troximon poeppigii is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
