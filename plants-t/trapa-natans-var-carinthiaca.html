Trapa natans var. carinthiaca

Trapa natans var. carinthiaca, commonly known as the Carinthian water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, edible fruits that develop underwater. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its leaves are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional cuisine.
