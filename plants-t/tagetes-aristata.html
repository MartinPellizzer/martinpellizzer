Tagetes aristata

Tagetes aristata, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon or golden marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and have mild aromatic properties.
