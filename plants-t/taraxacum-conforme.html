Taraxacum conforme

Taraxacum conforme, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are rough to the touch and its yellow composite flower heads that develop into fluffy white seeds. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Taraxacum conforme is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its latex sap contains compounds that can cause skin irritation in some individuals.
