Tainia gracilis

Tainia gracilis, commonly known as the slender tainia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Tainia gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
