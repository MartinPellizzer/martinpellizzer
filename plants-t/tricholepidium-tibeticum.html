Tricholepidium tibeticum

Tricholepidium tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Tricholepidium tibeticum is valued for its potential traditional medicinal uses in Tibetan medicine.
