Trigonella marco-poloi

Trigonella marco-poloi, commonly known as Marco-Polo lentil, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate for optimal growth.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it has been cultivated in various regions for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
