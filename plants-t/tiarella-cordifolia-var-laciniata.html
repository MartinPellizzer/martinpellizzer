Tiarella cordifolia var. laciniata

Tiarella cordifolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the notched foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
