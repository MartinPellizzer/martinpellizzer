Trifolium mutabile

Trifolium mutabile, commonly known as the variable clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Trifolium mutabile is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

This plant is also valued for its role in ecological systems and as a forage crop for livestock.
