Trifolium uniflorum var. breviflorum

Trifolium uniflorum var. breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in compact clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
