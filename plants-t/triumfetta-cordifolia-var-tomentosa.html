Triumfetta cordifolia var. tomentosa

Triumfetta cordifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly heartleaf triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
