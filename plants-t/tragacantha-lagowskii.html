Tragacantha lagowskii

Tragacantha lagowskii, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is known for its ability to produce a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
