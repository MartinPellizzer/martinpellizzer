Trollius altissimus subsp. deylii

Trollius altissimus subsp.

deylii, commonly known as the Deylii globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
