Trisetaria segetum

Trisetaria segetum, commonly known as the field grass or yellow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish to golden flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. Trisetaria segetum is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various parts of Europe and Asia.
