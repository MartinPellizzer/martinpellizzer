Thalia

Thalia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white, fluffy rays. Native to the Mediterranean region, Thalia thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils. The plant's foliage consists of silvery-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
