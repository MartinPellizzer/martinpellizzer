Tradescantia rupestris

Tradescantia rupestris, commonly known as the rock spider plant, is a low-growing succulent native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
