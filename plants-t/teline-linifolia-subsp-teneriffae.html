Teline linifolia subsp. teneriffae

Teline linifolia subsp.

teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's Mediterranean climate. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
