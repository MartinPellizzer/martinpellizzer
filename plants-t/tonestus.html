Tonestus

Tonestus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
