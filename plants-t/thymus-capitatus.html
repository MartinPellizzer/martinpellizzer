Thymus capitatus

Thymus capitatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, gray-green leaves and produces dense clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus capitatus is also valued for its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
