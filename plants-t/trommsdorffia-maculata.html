Trommsdorffia maculata

Trommsdorffia maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Trommsdorffia maculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
