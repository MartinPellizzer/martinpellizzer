Trichogonia viscosa

Trichogonia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky trichogonia, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in humid, shaded environments.
