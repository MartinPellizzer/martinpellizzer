Trichosporum gibbsiae

Trichosporum gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' trichosporum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its reproductive structures include both staminate and pistillate flowers, indicating it is monoecious.

Trichosporum gibbsiae is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
