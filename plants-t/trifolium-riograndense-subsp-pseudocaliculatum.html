Trifolium riograndense subsp. pseudocaliculatum

Trifolium riograndense subsp.

pseudocaliculatum, commonly known as the Rio Grande trifoliate, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in its native environment.
