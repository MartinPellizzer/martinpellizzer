Trichosanthes parviflora

Trichosanthes parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces distinctive, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a trumpet-shaped corolla. The plant has green, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Trichosanthes parviflora is valued for its ornamental flowers and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
