Trifolium mauritianum

Trifolium mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius clover, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium mauritianum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
