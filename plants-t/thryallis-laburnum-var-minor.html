Thryallis laburnum var. minor

Thryallis laburnum var. minor, commonly known as the lesser laburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual display to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often grayish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling and placement.
