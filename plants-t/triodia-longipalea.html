Triodia longipalea

Triodia longipalea, commonly known as the long-pale triodia, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and tough, fibrous leaves adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Triodia longipalea plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
