Thymus pectinatus var. pallasicus

Thymus pectinatus var. pallasicus, commonly known as the Pallasi thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in compact clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
