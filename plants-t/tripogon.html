Tripogon

Tripogon, commonly known as the three-stemmed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of three spikelets arranged in a tripartite structure. These plants are typically found in moist, temperate environments, often growing in wet meadows or along stream banks. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheathing base that clasps the stem.

Tripogon species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their delicate, feathery appearance.
