Thymus angustifolius var. silvicola

Thymus angustifolius var. silvicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a grayish-green color. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and as a culinary herb due to its fragrant, peppery scent.
