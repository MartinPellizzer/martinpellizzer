Triteleia crocea

Triteleia crocea, commonly known as the golden cupcup, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant features grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as chaparral and coastal scrub. Triteleia crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
