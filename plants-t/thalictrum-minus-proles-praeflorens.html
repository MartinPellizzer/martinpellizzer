Thalictrum minus proles praeflorens

Thalictrum minus proles praeflorens, commonly known as the dwarf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
