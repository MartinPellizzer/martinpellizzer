Trifolium vaginatum

Trifolium vaginatum, commonly known as the velvet vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Trifolium vaginatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
