Trifolium variegatum f. phaeocephalum

Trifolium variegatum f. phaeocephalum, commonly known as the variegated clover, is a variant of the common white clover.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns and pale, often reddish-brown flower heads. This plant is a member of the legume family and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and agricultural use.
