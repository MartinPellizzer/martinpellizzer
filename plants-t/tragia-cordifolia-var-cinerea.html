Tragia cordifolia var. cinerea

Tragia cordifolia var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray tragia, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its hairy leaves help reduce water loss, making it well-adapted to dry conditions.

The species is also known for its use in traditional medicine.
