Tacca rafflesiana

Tacca rafflesiana, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling bat wings. The plant produces a unique, spadix-like inflorescence that is surrounded by a showy, violet-colored spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, Tacca rafflesiana is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
