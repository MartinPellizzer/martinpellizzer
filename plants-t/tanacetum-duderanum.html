Tanacetum duderanum

Tanacetum duderanum, commonly known as Duder's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and yellow button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It is known for its strong aromatic scent and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Tanacetum duderanum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
