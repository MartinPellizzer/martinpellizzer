Tropaeolum majus

Tropaeolum majus, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Tropaeolum majus is also known for its peppery taste, which makes it a useful edible plant in some cuisines.
