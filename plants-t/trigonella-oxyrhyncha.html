Trigonella oxyrhyncha

Trigonella oxyrhyncha, commonly known as the Persian melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with small white or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for bees.

The plant is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
