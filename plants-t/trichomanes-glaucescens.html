Trichomanes glaucescens

Trichomanes glaucescens, commonly known as the blue moss fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with numerous small, fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into narrow, glossy leaves. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a bluish tinge, giving it its common name.

Trichomanes glaucescens reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
