Trapa bispinosa var. iwasakii

Trapa bispinosa var. iwasakii, commonly known as the Iwasaki water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut plant native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and spiny fruits. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its fruit, which resembles a spiny sphere, contains edible seeds that are harvested for consumption.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
