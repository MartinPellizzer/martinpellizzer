Tragacantha oxytropifolia

Tragacantha oxytropifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been traditionally used in medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
