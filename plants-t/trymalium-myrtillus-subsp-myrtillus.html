Trymalium myrtillus subsp. myrtillus

Trymalium myrtillus subsp.

myrtillus, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States. It features small, aromatic, evergreen leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, attractive shrub.
