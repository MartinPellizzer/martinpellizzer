Teloxys foetida

Teloxys foetida, commonly known as the stinking violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant is notable for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Teloxys foetida is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal despite its pungent fragrance.
