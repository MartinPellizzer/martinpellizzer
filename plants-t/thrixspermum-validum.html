Thrixspermum validum

Thrixspermum validum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Thrixspermum validum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
