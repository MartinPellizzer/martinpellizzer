Tragacantha costata

Tragacantha costata, commonly known as the serrated tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive resin, which has been historically used in various industries. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate.

Tragacantha costata is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in dry and sunny environments.
