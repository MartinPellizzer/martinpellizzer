Typha palustris

Typha palustris, commonly known as cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower heads.

It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a dense, brownish spike of flowers that mature into fluffy seeds. Typha palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

It is also used in traditional crafts and has applications in water filtration and erosion control.
