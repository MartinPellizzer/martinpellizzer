Turnera cistoides

Turnera cistoides, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive yellowish-green underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Turnera cistoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
