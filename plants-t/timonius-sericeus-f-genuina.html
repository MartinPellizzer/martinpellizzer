Timonius sericeus f. genuina

Timonius sericeus f. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are velvety and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.
