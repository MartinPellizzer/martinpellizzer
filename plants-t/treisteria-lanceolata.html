Treisteria lanceolata

Treisteria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf treisteria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
