Teucrium rigidum

Teucrium rigidum, commonly known as rigid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium rigidum is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
