Taraxacum genargenteum

Taraxacum genargenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its silver-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Taraxacum genargenteum is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
