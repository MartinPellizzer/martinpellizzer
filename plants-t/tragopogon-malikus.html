Tragopogon malikus

Tragopogon malikus, commonly known as the Malice goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that develop into spiky, bristly seed heads, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tragopogon malikus is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
