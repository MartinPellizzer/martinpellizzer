Tulipa sylvestris var. fragrans

Tulipa sylvestris var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant wild tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive fragrance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to cold climates and can survive harsh winters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
