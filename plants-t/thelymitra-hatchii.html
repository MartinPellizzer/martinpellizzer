Thelymitra hatchii

Thelymitra hatchii, commonly known as the Wollongong spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid endemic to southern New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble spiders, with a dark greenish-brown hue and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and heathlands. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Thelymitra hatchii is a rare and protected species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
