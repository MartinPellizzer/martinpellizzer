Tillaea umbellata

Tillaea umbellata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers resembling small bells. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky and arid environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Tillaea umbellata is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand harsh weather conditions.
