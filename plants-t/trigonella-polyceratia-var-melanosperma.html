Trigonella polyceratia var. melanosperma

Trigonella polyceratia var. melanosperma, commonly known as black cumin, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are dark brown to black in color, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates.

The seeds are rich in essential oils and have been used traditionally for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.
