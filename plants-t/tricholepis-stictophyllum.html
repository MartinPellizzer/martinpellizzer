Tricholepis stictophyllum

It lacks chlorophyll and therefore cannot perform photosynthesis, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. Its small, scale-like leaves are reduced, and it produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in ecosystems by influencing host plant growth and nutrient cycling.
