Triticum turgidum var. tristespeciosissimum

Triticum turgidum var. tristespeciosissimum, commonly known as the "sadly beautiful wheat," is a rare and ornamental variant of wheat characterized by its striking, often unusual floral structures.

This plant exhibits a unique combination of traits, including elongated, feathery glumes and a range of color variations in its inflorescence. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with moderate moisture. The botanical interest of this variety lies in its morphological diversity and potential for hybridization with other wheat species.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific value.
