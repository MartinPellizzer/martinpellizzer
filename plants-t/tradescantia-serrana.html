Tradescantia serrana

Tradescantia serrana, commonly known as the serrated spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tradescantia serrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
