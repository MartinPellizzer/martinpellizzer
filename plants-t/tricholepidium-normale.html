Tricholepidium normale

Tricholepidium normale, commonly known as the common rockcap, is a species of fungus that belongs to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is a small, edible mushroom with a cap that is typically white or pale yellow when young, turning brown with age. The stem is slender and often has a slight bulbous base. It is found in various parts of Europe and North America, growing in woodlands and on decaying wood.

The spores are produced in a distinctive, small, and often irregularly shaped pileus.
