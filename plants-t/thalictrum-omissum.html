Thalictrum omissum

Thalictrum omissum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem and showy, actinomorphic flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens.

Thalictrum omissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
