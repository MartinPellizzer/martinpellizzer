Tainia elliptica

Tainia elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking spadices with white or yellowish spadix, often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its edible underground tubers.

The tubers are starchy and have a mild flavor, making them a valuable food source in some regions.
