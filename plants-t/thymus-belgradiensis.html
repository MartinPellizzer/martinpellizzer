Thymus belgradiensis

Thymus belgradiensis, commonly known as the Belgrade thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, oval-shaped leaves.

Thymus belgradiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
