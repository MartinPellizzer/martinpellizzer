Trifolium medium var. banaticum

Trifolium medium var. banaticum, commonly known as the Banat red clover, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
