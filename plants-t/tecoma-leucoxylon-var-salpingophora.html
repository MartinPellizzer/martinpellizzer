Tecoma leucoxylon var. salpingophora

Tecoma leucoxylon var. salpingophora, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a shrub native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
