Trichoglottis apoensis subsp. alba

Trichoglottis apoensis subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to the Philippines. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright pseudobulbs and linear, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces inflorescences with small, white flowers that have prominent yellow veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
