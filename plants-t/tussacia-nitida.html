Tussacia nitida

Tussacia nitida, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Tussacia nitida is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.
