Thunbergia stellarioides var. graminea

Thunbergia stellarioides var. graminea, commonly known as the grassy thunbergia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
