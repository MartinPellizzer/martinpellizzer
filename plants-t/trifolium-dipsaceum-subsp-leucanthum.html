Trifolium dipsaceum subsp. leucanthum

Trifolium dipsaceum subsp.

leucanthum, commonly known as white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is typically found in grassy areas and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This subspecies is often used in agriculture and landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
