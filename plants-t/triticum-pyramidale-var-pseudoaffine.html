Triticum pyramidale var. pseudoaffine

Triticum pyramidale var. pseudoaffine, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its thin, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is closely related to other wheat species and exhibits features typical of the Poaceae family.
