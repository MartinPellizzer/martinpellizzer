Tulipa sylvestris var. prinzeriana

Tulipa sylvestris var. prinzeriana, commonly known as the Prinzenberg tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and the plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in colder climates.
