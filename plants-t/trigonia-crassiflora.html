Trigonia crassiflora

Trigonia crassiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered trigonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Trigonia crassiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
