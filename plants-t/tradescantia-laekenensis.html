Tradescantia laekenensis

Tradescantia laekenensis, commonly known as the Laeken spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or blue-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

The flowers are borne on slender stalks and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
