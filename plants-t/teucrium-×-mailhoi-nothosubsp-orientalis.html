Teucrium × mailhoi nothosubsp. orientalis

Teucrium × mailhoi nothosubsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental germander, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in spikes and range in color from blue to purple. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
