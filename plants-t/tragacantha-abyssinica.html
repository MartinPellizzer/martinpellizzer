Tragacantha abyssinica

Tragacantha abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces a valuable gum, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
