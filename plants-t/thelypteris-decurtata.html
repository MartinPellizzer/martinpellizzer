Thelypteris decurtata

Thelypteris decurtata, commonly known as the cut-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are often cut or notched at the tips. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both roots and new shoots.

Thelypteris decurtata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
