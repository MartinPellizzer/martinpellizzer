Tulipa ostrowskiana var. crocea

Tulipa ostrowskiana var. crocea, commonly known as the Crocus tulip, is a rare and striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This variety is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in late spring, often preceding the leaves.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a unique addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.
