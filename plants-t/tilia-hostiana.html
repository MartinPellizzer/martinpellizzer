Tilia hostiana

Tilia hostiana, commonly known as the Chinese linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain hairy seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Tilia hostiana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
