Trapa fastigiata

Trapa fastigiata, commonly known as the spiked water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features floating leaves with long, slender stems that bear spiny, club-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

Trapa fastigiata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
