Tragopogon charadzae

Tragopogon charadzae, commonly known as the Charadza's goat's beard, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disk florets, typical of the genus Tragopogon. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Its fibrous root system and hairy leaves help it retain moisture in its native habitat.
