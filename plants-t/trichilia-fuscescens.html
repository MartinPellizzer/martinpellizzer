Trichilia fuscescens

Trichilia fuscescens, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes various species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant is valued for its hard, aromatic wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
