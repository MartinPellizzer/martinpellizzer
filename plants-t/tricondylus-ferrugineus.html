Tricondylus ferrugineus

Tricondylus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds and rhizome structure.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a serrated margin, and its rhizome is thick and covered in a rusty-colored sheath. Tricondylus ferrugineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.
