Taraxacum semisagittatum

Taraxacum semisagittatum, commonly known as the semi-sagittate dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are semi-sagittate in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, yellow composite flower head. Its flowers develop into a distinctive, spherical seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

This species is typically found in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.
