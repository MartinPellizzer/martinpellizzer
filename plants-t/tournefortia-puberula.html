Tournefortia puberula

Tournefortia puberula, commonly known as the silky tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tournefortia puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, arid environments.
