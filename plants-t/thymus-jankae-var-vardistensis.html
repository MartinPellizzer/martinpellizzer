Thymus jankae var. vardistensis

Thymus jankae var. vardistensis, commonly known as the Vardistensian thyme, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
