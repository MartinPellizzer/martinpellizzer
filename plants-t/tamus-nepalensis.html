Tamus nepalensis

Tamus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal yam, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces a spadix inflorescence typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and ethnobotanical studies.
