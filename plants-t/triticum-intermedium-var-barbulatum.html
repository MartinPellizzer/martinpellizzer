Triticum intermedium var. barbulatum

Triticum intermedium var. barbulatum, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass with barbed awns, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with tall, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each bearing small, barbed awns that aid in seed dispersal. Its botanical features make it suitable for forage and erosion control in agricultural settings.

It is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its drought tolerance and nutrient efficiency.
