Thelocactus hexaedrophorus var. paradensis

Thelocactus hexaedrophorus var. paradensis, commonly known as the Paradise Cactus, is a rare cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are reduced to small, spine-like structures, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
