Trichilia truncata

Trichilia truncata, commonly known as African snowball, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
