Tetrastemon loranthoides

Tetrastemon loranthoides, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It typically grows on the branches of various host trees, such as oaks and hickories, and derives its nutrients through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or greenish in color, and it is often found in temperate regions of North America. Its leaves are simple and opposite, and it has a short, woody stem.

Tetrastemon loranthoides plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for certain insects and birds.
