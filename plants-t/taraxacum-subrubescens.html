Taraxacum subrubescens

Taraxacum subrubescens, commonly known as the red-tinged dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
