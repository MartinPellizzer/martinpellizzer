Triteleia uniflora

Triteleia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower camas, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

Triteleia uniflora plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
