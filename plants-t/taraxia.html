Taraxia

Taraxia, commonly known as the dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces a bright yellow flower that develops into a fluffy, seed-filled sphere known as a "puffball.". Taraxia is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and climates, making it a common weed in many regions.

It also has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a food source.
