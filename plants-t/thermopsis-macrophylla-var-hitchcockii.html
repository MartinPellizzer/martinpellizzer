Thermopsis macrophylla var. hitchcockii

Thermopsis macrophylla var. hitchcockii, commonly known as the Hitchcock's tall yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to native plant gardens.
