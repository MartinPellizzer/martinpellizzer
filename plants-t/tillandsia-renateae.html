Tillandsia renateae

Tillandsia renateae, commonly known as the Renate's air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant possesses a rosette-shaped structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
