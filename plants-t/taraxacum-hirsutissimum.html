Taraxacum hirsutissimum

Taraxacum hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a rosette growth form, with leaves arranged in a tight circle around the stem.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
