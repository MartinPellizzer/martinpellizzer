Taraxacum lacinulatum

Taraxacum lacinulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features basal, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of fluffy white seeds.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
