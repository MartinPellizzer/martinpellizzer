Tectaria martinicensis

Tectaria martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are relatively short and produce a network of roots that help anchor it to rocky or moist soil substrates.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
