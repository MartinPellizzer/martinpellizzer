Tulipa julia

Tulipa julia, commonly known as the Julia tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 20 inches and produces showy blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Tulipa julia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
