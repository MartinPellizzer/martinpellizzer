Trema guineense

Trema guineense, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Trema guineense is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
