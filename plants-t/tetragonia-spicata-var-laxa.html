Tetragonia spicata var. laxa

Tetragonia spicata var. laxa, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is an annual herb characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
