Tanacetum aphanassievii

Tanacetum aphanassievii, commonly known as the Crimean tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Crimea and southern Ukraine.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and yellow button-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small, aromatic flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum aphanassievii is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
