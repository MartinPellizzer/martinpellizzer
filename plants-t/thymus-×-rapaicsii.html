Thymus × rapaicsii

Thymus × rapaicsii, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or violet flowers that bloom in spring. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. Thymus × rapaicsii is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, make it useful in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
