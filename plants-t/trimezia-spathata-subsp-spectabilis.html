Trimezia spathata subsp. spectabilis

Trimezia spathata subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the showy trimezia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of a central spathaceous bract surrounded by numerous small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
