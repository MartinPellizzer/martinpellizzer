Thynninorchis

Thynninorchis, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate and often showy flowers, which typically feature three sepals and three petals, with the latter often modified into a specialized lip. Thynninorchis species are primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in moist, shaded environments. Their botanical significance lies in their complex morphology and ecological adaptations.

The genus is of particular interest to botanists due to its diverse floral structures and potential evolutionary relationships within the orchid family.
