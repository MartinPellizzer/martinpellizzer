Trichosalpinx bacillaris

Trichosalpinx bacillaris, commonly known as the dwarf flame orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, red-orange inflorescences. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes, which are pollinated by specific insect species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
