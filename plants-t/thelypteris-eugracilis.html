Thelypteris eugracilis

Thelypteris eugracilis, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. Thelypteris eugracilis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
