Tabebuia globiflora

Tabebuia globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. The plant has a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy, making it a striking ornamental tree. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth, dark green surface.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
