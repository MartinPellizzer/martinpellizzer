Tragacantha lineata

Tragacantha lineata, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a binding agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny conditions.

Tragacantha lineata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for both its ecological and commercial significance.
