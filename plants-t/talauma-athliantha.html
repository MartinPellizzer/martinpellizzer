Talauma athliantha

Talauma athliantha, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Talauma athliantha is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.
