Tanacetum maximum

Tanacetum maximum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its strong, aromatic fragrance, which is often described as camphor-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Tanacetum maximum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
