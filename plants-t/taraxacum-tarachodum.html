Taraxacum tarachodum

Taraxacum tarachodum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in rocky, arid environments.

Taraxacum tarachodum is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
