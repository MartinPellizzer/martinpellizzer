Tragopogon pratensis var. major

Tragopogon pratensis var. major, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a bluish or grayish hue.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
