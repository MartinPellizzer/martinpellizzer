Teucrium myrtifolium

Teucrium myrtifolium, commonly known as redstem germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various health-related purposes.
