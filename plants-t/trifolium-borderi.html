Trifolium borderi

Trifolium borderi, commonly known as the Border vetch.

It is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Trifolium borderi is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
