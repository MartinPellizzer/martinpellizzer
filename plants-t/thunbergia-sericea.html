Thunbergia sericea

Thunbergia sericea, commonly known as the silver lace vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine due to its ornamental value.

Thunbergia sericea is typically grown in gardens or as a houseplant, requiring well-draining soil and regular watering.
