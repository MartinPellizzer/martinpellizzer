Thuja occidentalis var. alba

Thuja occidentalis var. alba, commonly known as the white spruce, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant is a coniferous evergreen with a conical shape and dense, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates.

This variety is often used for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate urban conditions.
