Tephrosia polyzyga

Tephrosia polyzyga, commonly known as the African milkpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish pods that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.

Tephrosia polyzyga is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
