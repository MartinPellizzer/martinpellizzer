Teucrium floccosum

Teucrium floccosum, commonly known as woolly germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Teucrium floccosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
