Tsuga canadensis argentifolia

Tsuga canadensis argentifolia, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are coated with a silvery wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and is also an important component of forest ecosystems.
