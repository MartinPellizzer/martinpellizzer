Taraxacum oreades

Taraxacum oreades, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowering stems bear a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum oreades is adapted to grow in rocky and nutrient-poor soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
