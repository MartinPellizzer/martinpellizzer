Trifolium multinerve

Trifolium multinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with fine, parallel veins. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium multinerve is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
