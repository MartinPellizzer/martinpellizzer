Tmesipteris ovata

Tmesipteris ovata, commonly known as the ovate water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are oval in shape and covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Tmesipteris ovata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
