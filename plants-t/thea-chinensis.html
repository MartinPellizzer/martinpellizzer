Thea chinensis

Thea chinensis, commonly known as Camellia sinensis, is a flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it has been cultivated for thousands of years. The plant is renowned for its leaves, which are used to produce tea, a beverage with significant cultural and economic importance. Its leaves contain caffeine and various bioactive compounds that contribute to its unique flavor and health benefits.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.
