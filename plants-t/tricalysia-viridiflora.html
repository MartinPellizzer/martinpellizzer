Tricalysia viridiflora

Tricalysia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a greenish tinge. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and pale in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant flowers.
