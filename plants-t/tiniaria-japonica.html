Tiniaria japonica

Tiniaria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese tiniaria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Tiniaria japonica is often found in shaded woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
