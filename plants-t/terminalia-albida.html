Terminalia albida

Terminalia albida, commonly known as white terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in riverine forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
