Triphora gentianoides f. albidaviridis

Triphora gentianoides f. albidaviridis, commonly known as the white-green gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale green flowers, which are often tinged with yellow or greenish hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
