Trichinium brachytrichum

Trichinium brachytrichum, commonly known as the short-hair trichinium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

Trichinium brachytrichum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
