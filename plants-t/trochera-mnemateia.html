Trochera mnemateia

Trochera mnemateia, commonly known as the memory orchid, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant has a slender, upright stem with few leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a dense, clustered inflorescence. Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.

Its name is derived from the Greek words for "memory," reflecting its historical association with cognitive enhancement in traditional medicine.
