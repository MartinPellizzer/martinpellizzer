Trichoglottis wenzelii

Trichoglottis wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically produces racemose inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Trichoglottis wenzelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
