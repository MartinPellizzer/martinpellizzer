Thymus × copiosus

Thymus × copiosus, commonly known as English thyme, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, tiny, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
