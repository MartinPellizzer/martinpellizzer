Tragacantha piptocephala

Tragacantha piptocephala, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.

Its resin has been historically used in various traditional applications, including as a natural adhesive and medicinal remedy.
