Tristicha australis

Tristicha australis, commonly known as the Southern Tristicha, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems.

Tristicha australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
