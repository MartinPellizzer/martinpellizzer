Thymus eravinensis

Thymus eravinensis, commonly known as the Eravin's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Thymus eravinensis is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
