Trifolium velivolum

Trifolium velivolum, commonly known as the velvet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, purple or white flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the genus Trifolium. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Trifolium velivolum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
