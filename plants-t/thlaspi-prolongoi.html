Thlaspi prolongoi

Thlaspi prolongoi, commonly known as the longleaf cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in calcareous soils. Thlaspi prolongoi is an annual herb that thrives in open, disturbed habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across different regions.
