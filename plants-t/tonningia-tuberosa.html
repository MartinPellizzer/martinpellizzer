Tonningia tuberosa

Tonningia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous tonningia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
