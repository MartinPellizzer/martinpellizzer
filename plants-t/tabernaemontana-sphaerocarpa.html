Tabernaemontana sphaerocarpa

Tabernaemontana sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-shaped star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening. The plant has a shrub-like appearance with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
