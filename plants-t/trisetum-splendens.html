Trisetum splendens

Trisetum splendens, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright yellow inflorescence that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Trisetum splendens is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
