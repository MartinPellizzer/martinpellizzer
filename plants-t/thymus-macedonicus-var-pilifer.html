Thymus macedonicus var. pilifer

Thymus macedonicus var. pilifer, commonly known as Macedonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and aromatic foliage with a strong thyme scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.
