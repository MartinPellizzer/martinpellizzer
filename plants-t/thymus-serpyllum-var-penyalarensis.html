Thymus serpyllum var. penyalarensis

Thymus serpyllum var. penyalarensis, commonly known as the Penyalarensis Thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage with small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
