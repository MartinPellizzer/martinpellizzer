Trigonella amandiana var. pinnatifolia

Trigonella amandiana var. pinnatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
