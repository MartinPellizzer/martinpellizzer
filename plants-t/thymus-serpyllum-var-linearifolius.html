Thymus serpyllum var. linearifolius

Thymus serpyllum var. linearifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are elongated and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its pungent, fragrant qualities.
