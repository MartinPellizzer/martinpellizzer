Taraxacum subspectabile

Taraxacum subspectabile, commonly known as the spectabile dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of fluffy white seed heads. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of its habitat.
