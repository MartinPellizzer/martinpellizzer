Trigonella aleppica

Trigonella aleppica, commonly known as Syrian bitter vetch, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Trigonella aleppica is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
