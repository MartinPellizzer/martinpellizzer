Thapsus mucronatum

Thapsus mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thapsus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, aromatic scent.
