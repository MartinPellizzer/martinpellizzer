Tamarix komarovii

Tamarix komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces compact, spherical clusters of pink or reddish flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in poor soils and requiring minimal water.

Tamarix komarovii is often used for erosion control due to its deep root system and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
