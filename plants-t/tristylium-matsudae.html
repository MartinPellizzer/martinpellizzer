Tristylium matsudae

Tristylium matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese three-styled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features three distinct stigmas, giving it its common name, and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Tristylium matsudae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its ecological niche, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
