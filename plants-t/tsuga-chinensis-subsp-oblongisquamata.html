Tsuga chinensis subsp. oblongisquamata

Tsuga chinensis subsp.

oblongisquamata, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The cones are typically small and woody, containing two to three seeds. This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
