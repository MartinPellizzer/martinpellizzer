Tamala acuminata

Tamala acuminata, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown heartwood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves that are usually leathery in texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round, dark brown fruits.

Tamala acuminata is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
