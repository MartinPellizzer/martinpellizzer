Tagetes moorei

Tagetes moorei, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces daisy-like blooms. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinct aromatic scent.

Tagetes moorei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
