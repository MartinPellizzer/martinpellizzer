Triticum laxiusculum

Triticum laxiusculum, commonly known as the slender wheat, is a species of wild wheat native to central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a preference for temperate climates. Its grains are similar to those of domesticated wheat but are not cultivated for agricultural purposes.

Triticum laxiusculum plays a minor role in ecological systems, contributing to soil stability and serving as a food source for various insects and small mammals.
