Triodia flava var. chapmanii

Triodia flava var. chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall and high temperatures. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, protective hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
