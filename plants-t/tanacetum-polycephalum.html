Tanacetum polycephalum

Tanacetum polycephalum, commonly known as the yarrow plant, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Tanacetum polycephalum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
