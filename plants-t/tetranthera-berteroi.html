Tetranthera berteroi

Tetranthera berteroi, commonly known as the Brazilian water spinach, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its fibrous, stoloniferous stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tetranthera berteroi is often found in wet or marshy areas and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
