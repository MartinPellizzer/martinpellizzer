Teucrium boreale

Teucrium boreale, commonly known as northern germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. Small, tubular flowers are arranged in clusters, usually blooming in shades of blue or purple during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
