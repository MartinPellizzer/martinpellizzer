Teucrium stoechadifolium

Teucrium stoechadifolium, commonly known as Scottish germander, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy habitats or along rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
