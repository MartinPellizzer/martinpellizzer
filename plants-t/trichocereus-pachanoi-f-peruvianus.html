Trichocereus pachanoi f. peruvianus

Trichocereus pachanoi f. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian torch cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It features a ribbed stem with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration, especially under intense sunlight. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also associated with traditional cultural practices in its native region.
