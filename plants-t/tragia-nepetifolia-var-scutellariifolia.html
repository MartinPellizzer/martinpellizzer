Tragia nepetifolia var. scutellariifolia

Tragia nepetifolia var. scutellariifolia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
