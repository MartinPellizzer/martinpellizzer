Thymus roegneri

Thymus roegneri, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
