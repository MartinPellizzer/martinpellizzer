Toxicodendron toxicarum f. leiocarpum

Toxicodendron toxicarum f. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited poison ivy, is a variant of the poison ivy plant characterized by its smooth, less hairy fruiting structures compared to the typical form.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is known for its urushiol-containing sap, which can cause severe skin irritation in humans. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
