Telina

Telina, commonly known as the common thornapple, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are often mistaken for edible fruits.

Telina is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
