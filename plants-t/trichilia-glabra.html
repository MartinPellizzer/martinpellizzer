Trichilia glabra

Trichilia glabra, commonly known as African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are the source of shea butter, a valuable natural product used in cosmetics and food.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in tropical forests.
