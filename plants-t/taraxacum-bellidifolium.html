Taraxacum bellidifolium

Taraxacum bellidifolium, commonly known as the bellidifolium dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, hollow stem that terminates in a flower head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum bellidifolium is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
