Tetraglochin acanthocarpum var. macropoda

Tetraglochin acanthocarpum var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruited acanthocarp, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods. The plant typically grows in grassland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.
