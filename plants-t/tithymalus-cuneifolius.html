Tithymalus cuneifolius

Tithymalus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, making it a visually appealing addition to gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
