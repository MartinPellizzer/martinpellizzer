Tigridia pearcei

Tigridia pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's tiger flower, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive black or purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Tigridia pearcei is known for its ability to bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to arid landscapes.
