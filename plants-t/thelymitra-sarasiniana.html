Thelymitra sarasiniana

Thelymitra sarasiniana, commonly known as the Sarasin's sun-orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant features a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped, bright green leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream, with a distinctive yellow lip that is often marked with purple or red veins.

Thelymitra sarasiniana is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
