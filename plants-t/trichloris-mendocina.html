Trichloris mendocina

Trichloris mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
