Thecacoris talbotae

Thecacoris talbotae, commonly known as the Talbot's corymbiferous plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex, branched clusters called corymbs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
