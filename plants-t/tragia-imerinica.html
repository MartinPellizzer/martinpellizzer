Tragia imerinica

Tragia imerinica, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tragia imerinica is adapted to survive in dry environments with its deep root system and reduced leaf surface.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar to pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
