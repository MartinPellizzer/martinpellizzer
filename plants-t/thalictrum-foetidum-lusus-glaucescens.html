Thalictrum foetidum lusus glaucescens

Thalictrum foetidum lusus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shady environments like woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable addition to gardens with a naturalistic or woodland aesthetic.
