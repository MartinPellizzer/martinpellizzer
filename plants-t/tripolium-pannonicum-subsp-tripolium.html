Tripolium pannonicum subsp. tripolium

Tripolium pannonicum subsp.

tripolium, commonly known as the European sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are dispersed by wind.
