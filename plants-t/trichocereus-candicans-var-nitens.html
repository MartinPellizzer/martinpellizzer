Trichocereus candicans var. nitens

Trichocereus candicans var. nitens, commonly known as the bright night cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with poor soil.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
