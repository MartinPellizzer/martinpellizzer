Thibaudia ovalifolia

Thibaudia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that supports its upright growth habit.

Thibaudia ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
