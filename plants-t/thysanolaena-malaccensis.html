Thysanolaena malaccensis

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist, tropical environments.
