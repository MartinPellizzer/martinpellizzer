Trochetiopsis × benjaminii

Trochetiopsis × benjaminii, commonly known as the Benjamin's holly, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It features dense, leathery foliage with deep green leaves that have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries that are ornamental and attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in garden designs for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
