Tragia nepetifolia var. leptophylla

Tragia nepetifolia var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
