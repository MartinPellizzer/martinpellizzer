Taraxacum officinale var. erythrospermum

Taraxacum officinale var. erythrospermum, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its bright red seeds.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with distinctive red seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
