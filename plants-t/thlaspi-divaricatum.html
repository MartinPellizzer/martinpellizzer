Thlaspi divaricatum

Thlaspi divaricatum, commonly known as the divided rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
