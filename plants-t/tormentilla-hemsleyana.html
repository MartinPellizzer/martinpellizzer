Tormentilla hemsleyana

Tormentilla hemsleyana, commonly known as Hemsley's tormentil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Tormentilla hemsleyana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
