Thalictrum commutatum

Thalictrum commutatum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, usually yellow or greenish-white flowers in early summer. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Thalictrum commutatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
