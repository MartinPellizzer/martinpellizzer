Telipogon gracilis

Telipogon gracilis, commonly known as the slender bog orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and bogs of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces tiny, greenish-white blooms.

Telipogon gracilis is often found in association with other bog flora, contributing to the unique biodiversity of its habitat.
