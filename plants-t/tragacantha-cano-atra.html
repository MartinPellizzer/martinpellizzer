Tragacantha cano-atra

Tragacantha cano-atra, commonly known as the pale-black tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. Its growth habit includes a shrub-like structure with woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The pale-black tragacanth thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
