Telipogon sayakoae

Telipogon sayakoae, commonly known as the Sayakoae spider-orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests, often growing on tree trunks or roots. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Telipogon sayakoae is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
