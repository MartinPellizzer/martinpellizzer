Triticum turgidum var. izmiricum

Triticum turgidum var. izmiricum, commonly known as Izmir wheat, is a botanical variety of wheat that exhibits specific morphological and genetic traits.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and semi-dwarf stature, which are advantageous for agricultural cultivation. This variety is native to the Aegean region of Turkey and is adapted to temperate climates with moderate rainfall. Its grain is typically used for flour production, contributing to local food systems.

The plant's genetic makeup includes traits that make it resilient to certain environmental stresses, enhancing its agronomic value.
